010 Chapter 10 First Steps Toward A Dream 1
The great training yard.
Shirone arrived on time.
Rian had come first and was waiting.
The battle had already begun in a sense.
The two looked at each other from a distance without approaching.
Rian
Let's begin.
The weapon Rian chose was the same longsword as Shirone.
Ozent swordsmanship, known for its reckless and bold style, was more suited to greatswords than longswords.
But that was only when schema was possible, so rather than accommodating Shirone, he was choosing a weapon that suited him.
Shirone was in the 'stillness in motion' stance from basic swordsmanship.
Though it was a simple movement, even to Rian's eyes, his center of gravity was much better balanced than before.
Rian
Is it really... possible?
Rian
But how can he be so calm? Isn't he afraid of death?
Even Rian, who had trained with a sword for 16 years, feared death.
That's why he pushed his body to the limit to boost his confidence.
On the other hand, Shirone didn't have the same courage as Rian.
He was a herbivore who loved peace and disliked fighting.
But intelligence always seeks the highest efficiency.
Analyzing the fear of real swords as the greatest risk factor in this duel, Shirone worked to find a solution and finally found an answer.
If you can't overcome fear, you can understand it.
In sword philosophy, this is called 'jumping off a cliff.'
Anyone can jump off a cliff—if they don't think about dying.
For instance, some types of people in the world don't feel fear.
Whether rulers, businessmen, or criminals, their occupations vary, but they share one thing in common: they don't imagine the consequences beforehand.
A serial killer only pursues momentary pleasure without thinking about execution when caught.
Some dictators leisurely enjoy banquets even as rebels breach the gates.
For them, the future doesn't exist even for 0.1 seconds—it's just an illusion.
Shirone imagined standing at the edge of a cliff.
Then he extended his right foot into the void and followed with his left.
Shirone didn't fall.
If one doesn't imagine the future, the area beyond the cliff is just the state right before falling.
Shirone
Until you fall, you haven't fallen. Until you die, you haven't died.
The reason intelligence can sometimes be more frightening than courage is because it understands the essence of danger like this.
Also, Shirone's frozen mind resulted in a dramatic increase in the stability of his spirit zone.
Shirone
Here I come!
As Shirone entered his spirit zone, Rian approached within reach.
A flash of light seemed to streak before Shirone's eyes.
It was clearly a bold swordsmanship of a different level from a mountaineer's axe swing.
But Shirone also had his spirit zone.
With his synesthetic cognitive ability clearly sensing the trajectory of the sword, he opened his eyes wide and avoided the attack.
Rian's expression changed to one of surprise.
Seeing the movement of a real sword with the eyes was absolutely impossible for a beginner.
But the techniques of the sword are also infinite.
There were clearly parts Shirone couldn't predict.
As the blade cut across Shirone's chest and passed by, his spirit zone momentarily wavered.
Shirone's heart sank.
At this rate, he would be helplessly defeated.
But Rian's subsequent attack was unexpectedly aggressive.
Thanks to this, Shirone was able to throw himself out of the way and escape death.
Shirone
Why such an attack?
It would have been over if he had simply cut the side with minimal force.
Shirone
Perhaps his understanding of swordsmanship is lower than I thought?
Shirone's thinking was accurate.
Lacking insight, Rian was skilled in swordsmanship itself but fell short in understanding its essence.
Shirone downgraded his assessment of Rian's skill.
Setting the enemy too high was foolish, giving up any chance of counterattack.
To win, he had to take risks.
As Shirone's counterattack began, the tension of death increased, rapidly depleting Rian's stamina.
After dozens of exchanges, Rian was finally shocked.
Rian
Damn it! This can't be! This can't be! 💢
The techniques the opponent was using were only basic cuts.
Yet they were hard to block.
He was targeting only the parts that couldn't be defended.
Suddenly, Rai's face came to mind.
In their duel two years ago, his brother had subdued Rian with just one hand.
Rian
Don't be ridiculous! I will become the world's greatest swordsman!
As Rian charged more recklessly, Shirone was puzzled.
What was this boy?
Did he not want to win?
Why was he being so stubborn?
Did he think at all?
Shirone
Huh? Huhh?
Shirone gradually realized he was being pushed back.
From some point, he couldn't read the opponent's movements.
Rian's sword, swung with conviction, began to approach Shirone with unpredictable complexity.
Rian
I won't lose! I will definitely surpass him!
Rian, riding on the advantage, pressed Shirone hard.
Shirone's stamina rapidly hit bottom.
He had distributed his energy efficiently until now, but an absolute difference existed anyway.
His sword felt heavy.
Rian
This is the end!
Rian, who had come running with his longsword lowered, struck upward as if to split Shirone's chin.
Shirone also swung his sword down with all his might.
With a clang, one sword soared into the sky and then stuck into the far side of the training ground.
Silence fell.
The two looked at each other, gasping for breath.
Rian slowly lowered his gaze.
He saw Shirone's sword pointing at his solar plexus.
Just before the swords collided, Shirone had twisted his wrist like a whirlwind, striking Rian's sword from below.
With the added momentum from the impact, Rian, whose strength had waned, finally lost his grip on the sword.
It wasn't a technique attempted with certainty.
Just a difference in understanding swordsmanship.
It was a risk taken with the thought that only attacks that could cut him were real.
Shirone didn't relax his tension.
He wanted to collapse right away, but he couldn't carelessly lose the life he had worked so hard to save.
Rian
Ah well, I lost.
Rian
Ah, I lost. I lost.
Though Rian scratched his head in annoyance, the murderous intent had already disappeared.
He had trained to his limit and fought his best.
That's why he had no regrets.
Rian
You won. Since I lost the duel, I have nothing to say even if you kill me.
Shirone
Are you joking right now?
Rian
What?
Shirone
If I kill the young master, do you think, what, I'd be allowed to live? How can you say such nonsense? 💢
Rian
Come on! Why does that matter? Is that what you say to someone you fought to the death with?
Shirone
Only you put your life on the line! I just fought to live! This ridiculous duel makes no sense from the beginning—how is it right to blame me when you were just hiding because you didn't want to train? 💢
Rian was taken aback.
Normally, after men fight, there should be, if not warmth, at least some heavy and intense exchange of words, right?
But this was nothing short of being eaten alive by nagging.
Shirone
Hey, you! Who's blaming you? Your actions were so petty! Even if we'd just met, in such a situation, loyalty should naturally kick in! 💢
Rian
Loyalty? You're talking about loyalty?
Shirone
