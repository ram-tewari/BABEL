Having been hurt by Shirone's refusal to give her gold, Amy felt a bit better seeing her school friend and said,
Amy
I'd like to take a look at some weapons. Mind if I browse?
Nade smiled kindly and encouraged her to shop.
Nade
Sure, take a look. We’ve got all sorts. However, I’m surprised to see people out in this weather. I was thinking of closing early because I’m feeling a bit down. Must be the rain—it makes everything seem gloomier.
Amy clasped her hands behind her back and wandered around the store, examining various weapons.
The basic equipment was all there. Most fortunately, there was no mage equipment.
Amy
If there had been items for magic users, she would have felt ridiculous clinging to her pride about not wanting to buy anything with someone else’s money. It was easier this way.
While casually browsing, Amy noticed a weapon encased in glass and displayed on the wall.
Amy
Hm? What’s this?
Below the case was a paper labeled <First Thunderstrike>.
Nade’s face lit up with pride as he spoke.
Nade
Ah, that’s the pride of our shop. When you swing it, it makes thunder sounds. Amazing, right? I tried it once and it’s so powerful. Any monster would run away in surprise!
Amy recalled a story she had heard from Rian during the carriage ride to Galliant about Shirone’s childhood.
Successfully created Thunderstrike at age twelve, and it seemed this was quite a precious memory.
Amy looked at the price tag. Her eyes widened in disbelief.
Amy
5.9 billion gold? ❗
Hahaha! Of course! It’s the pride of our shop! It would take at least that much to have it.
Armin stepped up behind Amy and added,
Armin
Probably no one can buy this through normal means. I’m not sure how the economy works in this world, but even if we combined all the gold Miss Reina and I have, it’s only 17 gold in total. No one, not even Shirone’s parents, could withdraw an amount as absurd as 5.9 billion.
Amy understood. But seemingly unable to let go, she stroked her chin and asked,
Amy
Hmm, what if a grave robber tried to steal this?
They could. Since it’s an object filled with Shirone’s intense thoughts, it would be easy to convert into an Objet. They would gather funds through illegal means or theft, I suppose. But if I were a relic hunter, I wouldn’t bother stealing this.
“Because it’s not particularly useful.”
Armin
Right. Objets are valuable when they break common sense. The idea of a thunderous sound coming from an axe isn’t particularly unique—it’s merely about successfully creating a Thunderstrike. Most items at this eleventh level are probably like this. This is the surface layer, so there’s no room for deep human desires to manifest. Therefore, rather than desire, <First Thunderstrike> is better interpreted as just a precious memory.
Amy had come to understand the nature of Objects and, with it, just how vile a person Arius was.
This world belongs to Shirone. Regardless of what others think, it’s his most precious mindscape. For Arius to commit murder just to plunder that world meant he had to be stopped.
Armin walked over to the window, peering outside as he spoke.
Armin
Anyway, as law-abiding citizens, we don’t need to be greedy. If we’re not going to rob, these are just dream items. No need to be picky. Just arm yourself enough for basic self-defense.
Amy tilted her head and asked.
Amy
Self-defense? Aren’t we choosing weapons to fight Zion and Arius? Wouldn’t we need perfect armament for that, and even that might not be enough?
Armin, still by the window, replied,
Armin
I’ll explain that once we’re outside. Just choose for now.
Reina propped her chin on her hand, lost in thought.
Ogent Family revered swords. And the sword was her most confident weapon.
Reina
Do you sell bows here, by any chance?
A smile spread across Armin’s lips.
Armin
Good choice.
Currently, it’s too dark outside to distinguish anything. In such conditions, a ranged weapon would be more practical than a melee one. Especially given Reina’s acute hearing, her accuracy with a bow would likely be excellent.
Reina examined the bow Nade selected. The draw weight was solid, and the bow’s curve wasn’t warped.
She had learned during martial arts training that bows could create variables. And being from a martial family, she had strong fundamentals from frequent hunting.
Reina
I like it. I’ll take this. And a quiver and arrows too.
“Great. I’ll give you good quality ones. Just wait.”
While Nade searched for items in the storage behind the counter, Reina turned to Amy and asked,
Reina
There don’t seem to be any magical weapons. Should we check somewhere else?
“It’s fine. Mages don’t really need tools anyway.”
Amy summoned a Jack-o’-Lantern next to her face.
Amy
Besides, I have the weapon Shirone bought me.
Amy regretted the words as soon as they left her mouth. Emphasizing that Shirone bought it would only make her feel more miserable.
But it was already spilled milk, so she pretended nothing had happened and turned her body away.
Armin leaned against the window with his arms crossed.
‘Jack-o’-Lantern. That’s ancient magic. She obtained quite a rare item.’
Having visited Heaven himself on a certain mission, Armin was familiar with the general market values in the community.
Ancient magic artifacts like the Jack-o’-Lantern could only be acquired with an elixir, and something of this caliber would likely require a White Elixir.
Shirone and Amy must be quite close.
Yet she can’t withdraw money. And that last comment reveals her hurt feelings about Shirone.
‘Hmm, so that’s how it is.’
It might be dangerous. No—within the confines of this mental world, this might be the most significant obstacle.
‘I should warn them ahead of time.’
Just as Armin made his decision, Nade returned with two 20-piece quivers.
When asked about the total price, he said 4 gold.
Amy approached Armin and whispered.
Amy
Isn’t that too expensive? A single bow set is worth half your entire trust fund!
“It is expensive. But it must be top-notch. It might even have additional capabilities. After all, the ‘Master of this World’ isn’t the type to swindle people. Of course, the boy Nade might be someone who usually scams people, but considering he was entrusted with the <First Thunderstrike>, he seems to be a friendly projection.”
The reason Armin referred to Shirone as the ‘The Master of this World’ indirectly was because Nade was present.
Having experienced at the inn how sensitive projections were to keywords, Amy immediately understood and nodded.
Amy
Yes. Nade isn’t the type to carelessly sell low-quality items.
Reina paid the 4 gold.
Money wouldn’t be needed anyway once they met Master of This World.
Reina strapped one quiver to her back and another to her thigh. She also held three arrows between her fingers, resting them against the bow’s back, ready for action.
Amy floated the Jack-o’-Lantern beside her and looked at Armin.
Amy
Are you ready? We’re going to meet ‘this world’s master’ now.
Reina asked.
Reina
But where do we start looking?
“We don’t need to go far. After all, he exists everywhere in this world.”
Armin raised his head and looked toward Nade. Amy and Reina stepped aside to clear the way.
Armin
Do you know where Shirone is?
