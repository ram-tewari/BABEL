266 Chapter 270 Terrajes Bloodline 2
Uorin glanced at the wall clock, then hastily set the cat down and stood up from her seat.
Uorin
Oh? It's time to eat. Brother, let's go.
Setting down his teacup, Zion stood up and leaned his face close to Shirone's.
Zion
Haha! Biological parents? Sure, that's important. But do you know this? Once parents have abandoned their child once, what's stopping them from doing it again?
Shirone didn't even snort in response.
Zion
Let me be clear, I expect nothing. The only reason I'm here is to meet my biological parents.
Haha! Biological parents? Yeah, that's important. But you know what? If a parent abandoned their child once, wouldn't they be able to abandon them a second time?
Shirone frowned, unable to understand.
Shirone
What does that mean?
Zion
How pathetic. Blood ties mean nothing in the face of power. You don't honestly think Father suddenly looked for you without a reason, do you?
Shirone had anticipated that there must be a reason behind being sought out after 18 years, but hearing it directly from Zion still came as a shock.
Uorin patted Zion's shoulder.
Uorin
Good grief! I told you, we need to go. We'll be in trouble if we're late.
Zion threw a sneering glance at Shirone before being dragged out by his sister.
After leaving Shirone alone in the room, Zion finally turned around and clicked his tongue.
Zion
Tch! What an annoying guy. Especially his eyes—I can't stand them. Is it because he grew up like some commoner? Instead of quivering in fear, he dares to talk back.
Uorin tried to soothe him.
Uorin
Don't be like that. I, for one, am glad to have a mage as my brother. Besides, he's no threat to you. As long as Mother is around, Kazra will be yours.
Zion spoke with a displeased expression.
Zion
Who doesn't know that? I just hate the way he acts. I can't even stand calling him Father. Orkamph, that bastard. When I become king, I'll make sure he's gone.
Uorin pouted her lips.
In any kingdom, the idea of a son killing his father is considered the height of disgrace. And here they were, in the royal castle no less. If those words were overheard, it would turn the kingdom upside down.
Yet, that was the extent of her reaction.
After all, they were Terraje's children.
The food laid out on the royal dining table was more extravagant than anything a commoner could even dream of.
Shirone couldn't quite grasp why so much money needed to be spent on daily meals, but the reality was that, even if the food wasn't eaten, extravagance was considered a royal virtue.
Royalty must surpass nobles, even in the smallest matters.
As long as the nobles indulged in all sorts of luxuries, the royal family's absurdly excessive spending would never change.
Shirone's group was seated at the lowest end of the table. Vincent, Olena, Reina, and Amy all had pale faces.
Never in their wildest dreams did they imagine they'd actually be invited to dine at the king's table.
It might even be the first time in the hundred-year history of Kazra Kingdom.
As such, their unease only grew, and they could barely tell how the food was even passing down their throats.
The atmosphere at the dining table was as cold and tense as it could be.
Orkamph's pride was hurt by the breach of protocol, and both Shirone and Zion were still feeling the tension from their earlier conversation.
However, Eliza seemed to be in a rather good mood. She was pleased with the outfit she had chosen in the dressing room. She was wearing a dress adorned with mink fur on the sleeves and accessorized with diamonds.
Eliza, who had been daintily picking at her food as if trying to break it down into molecular pieces, suddenly set down her utensils and flashed a bright smile.
Eliza
We should decorate Shirone's room—make it elegant and grand. Since you have golden hair like your father, I think a navy-blue suit would suit you. Oh, and we'll need a carriage as well. It looks like I'll be quite busy for a while.
Olena's face darkened. None of those things were things she could provide.
Shirone
I don't need any of that. Everything my parents have done for me until now is more than enough.
Huhu, my son is quite frugal. But you must remember, there's a certain dignity expected of royalty. You can't live as you did before. Now, let's see... Yes, gold suits you. Or maybe sapphire?
Shirone
Whether it's gold or jewels, that's not what's important. The parents here have given me their best. That kind of love is something I won't find anywhere else.
Eliza frowned. What could be more important than gold and jewels? It was their role to enjoy the most luxurious wealth in the kingdom. It wasn't a privilege but an obligation.
She found it disappointing that her son was reciting tales of his caretaker's love when he should be learning about royal duties as the future First Prince.
No, perhaps Shirone was a bit too affectionate. Maybe that's why he couldn't bring himself to say he'd live independently in front of his caretaker.
Eliza turned at Shirone's parents.
Eliza
Was it Vincent?
Vincent quickly set down his utensils.
Vincent
Ah, yes! My name is Arian Vincent!
You shall be compensated accordingly. You've cared for our son so well; you deserve a fitting reward. It would be too cumbersome to give you gold. Dear, how about granting them some land in Kosent Region?
Orkamph responded with a disinterested tone.
Orkamph
That sounds reasonable.
Fury surged within Shirone. He had come to the palace just as they wished, and Vincent and Olena had been anxious, terrified of losing their son. In this situation, the only gift Shirone could offer them was the pride of having raised their child well.
He had hoped that these esteemed individuals, with their royal titles, would at least say a simple 'Thank you for raising our son.' How could it be so hard for them to utter those words?
Shirone
Wait a moment. Why do you keep talking like this? They raised me for 18 years. They're the parents who taught me how to live from childhood until now, in place of my biological parents!
Eliza's expression turned fierce. Yes, she felt sorry for having abandoned Shirone. But his constant siding with those 'shallow' people was getting on her nerves.
Eliza
Is it that you think the reward is too little? Then, how about we add a small castle to the land…
Shirone's voice grew louder.
Shirone
That's not what I mean! Why can't you just say thank you, even once?
Eliza made a blank expression. At first, she wondered if something might be wrong with Shirone's mind. Why should a royal family member express gratitude to commoners?
Zion spoke with a displeased expression.
Zion
What are you doing, behaving so indecently? Do you think this is some commoner's dinner table where you used to live?
Shirone glared at Zion. Anger welled up inside him, but Zion did have a point. If he pushed too hard in a gathering of royalty, the situation could take a strange turn.
Uorin quickly intervened to defuse the tension.
Uorin
Easy, now. It's because brother Shirone is such a kind person. I think brother values emotions over material things. To him, any compensation without emotion is nothing more than a mere exchange of objects.
In that moment, Shirone even saw Uorin as a truly lovable little sister.
He was immensely grateful to her for speaking on his behalf.
Uorin met Shirone's eyes and winked. Then she turned to Vincent and Olena.
Uorin
I'm sorry if we were too harsh earlier. And thank you so much for raising our brother into such a wonderful person.
N- No, not at all. How could I…
