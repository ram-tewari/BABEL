Chapter 108: Return to Daily Life (3)
Siena smiled and glanced sideways at Etella.
She had been tormented by overwhelming guilt for the past ten days.
As expected, Etella approached Shirone with a face that looked ready to burst into tears at any moment.
Etella
Shirone.
Shirone
Teacher, I'm sorry. I've done it again….
Without even waiting to hear Shirone's words, Etella pulled him into a tight embrace.
Etella
I'm sorry. It's my fault. If only I'd been more careful. This won't happen again.
To their friends, it looked like a beautiful embrace between teacher and student, but for Shirone, who was on the receiving end, it was pure torture.
Etella hugged him so tightly that he couldn't breathe.
Embarrassment aside, he genuinely felt like he was about to suffocate.
Shirone
Uh, um, Teacher….
Etella
Hmm?
Shirone
I'm sorry, but… please let me live.
Etella
Oh my!
When Shirone's voice came out muffled, Etella hurriedly released him.
Etella
I'm sorry. I was so happy I couldn't help myself….
Shirone gasped for air, his face pale.
But even this sensation was part of the present world.
Smiling at the sheer joy of being back, he said:
Shirone
Now it really feels like I've returned. ✨
When their friends' eyes began to gleam mischievously, Shirone realized his mistake and frantically waved his hands.
Shirone
No, no, it's not like that. I mean, this is….
Amy
You little brat, what's all this 'this and that'?
Amy rushed over and pinched Shirone's cheek.
Amy
What? Do I not feel real enough to you? Huh?
Shirone
No! That's not what I meant!
Nade and Iruki burst into laughter.
Alpheas watched Shirone interacting with his friends with a fond expression before quietly slipping out of the infirmary.
From now on, he would be the busiest person.
Shirone
Headmaster.
Turning around, he saw Shirone, who had somehow slipped out unnoticed, closing the infirmary door and walking toward him.
Alpheas
What is it? You should rest for a while. Catch up with your friends. The school will likely remain closed for some time anyway.
Shirone
Yes, thank you for your concern. But… there's something I've been curious about.
Alpheas
Hmm? What's that?
Shirone recounted everything that had happened—from the moment he entered the infinite realm to his return to the original world through the black hole.
At first, Alpheas listened with interest. But as the story progressed, his expression gradually hardened.
Especially when Shirone described the woman's abilities, he closed his eyes and sighed.
Alpheas
Did that woman say she knew me?
Shirone
Yes. She asked me to send her regards to you. My memory is hazy, but I think that's what she said.
Shirone
Shirone wondered if he had brought up something unnecessary.
Alpheas's expression was far worse than he had anticipated.
Alpheas
I see. I think I know who she is.
Shirone
Huh? You know her? ❗
Shirone's eyes widened.
Alpheas was undoubtedly a great mage, but the woman existed in a realm beyond life itself. It was strange that he had met both of them, given they resided in different worlds.
But Alpheas's thoughts were different.
It wasn't a coincidence but inevitability.
Shirone meeting her was predetermined from the moment he awakened the Immortal Function.
Shirone
Who is she, really?
Alpheas
Adrias Miro. In her time, she was called the Labyrinth of Space-Time. She was my disciple, specializing in Scale Magic.
Shirone's expression turned blank.
He had never imagined she was Alpheas's disciple.
Learning that her abilities, which he had thought were divine, were actually magic came as a shock.
Shirone
What is Scale Magic?
Alpheas stroked his beard as he pondered how to explain.
Alpheas
Shirone, in the magical society, those who have attained the Immortal Function are called Unlockers.
Shirone
Yes, I've heard that.
Alpheas
Then this will be easier. Miro is also an Unlocker. Even as a child, her curiosity was extraordinary. She delved endlessly—into worlds larger than the universe and smaller than particles. Scale Magic is a form of magic only those who have comprehended the infinity of space-time can wield.
Shirone recalled his encounter with Miro.
Indeed, her primary ability, the glass orb, freely manipulated the relativity of space-time.
Shirone
Miro said she had been watching me for a long time. Is that possible?
Alpheas
Don't worry. Miro exists in a different dimension from you. But when you unlocked the Immortal Function, you momentarily reached the same dimension. She must have been waiting for that moment.
Shirone
But why me? Unlockers are rare, but according to Arcane, they're not that few in number. There must be many Unlockers stronger than me.
Alpheas hesitated, stroking his beard.
What he was about to say was a top-secret matter known only to teachers who had served for over 20 years.
Alpheas
Miro was the founding president of the Supernatural Psychic Science Research Club.
Shirone pictured Miro's face.
It was surprising enough that she had founded the club he now belonged to, but as the first president, she would have been active nearly 20 years ago, yet she seemed barely aged.
Alpheas
Miro wasn't fixated on phenomena. She believed there were too many things knowledge couldn't explain. Together with her peers, she established the research club in Istas. Even Micaea Gaold, the current head of the Thorhmia Magic Association, was one of the early members.
Shirone imagined the members of that time.
According to Nade, the master key of Istas's magic vault was created by a servant ability user similar to Iruki.
Shirone
It couldn't have been Miro or Gaold.
He could roughly guess how extraordinary the early members must have been.
As Shirone lost himself in thought, Alpheas also reminisced about events from 18 years ago.
Miro had been a good kid.
If not for that day's incident…
Suddenly, he realized he had omitted something and turned back to Shirone.
Alpheas
Shirone, do you know how many students there are in total?
It didn't seem like he expected an answer, as Alpheas continued immediately.
Alpheas
Of course, the total number changes. Some graduate, take leaves of absence, or face disciplinary action. But no matter the number, this school is always short by one person.
Alpheas turned his gaze out the window as he spoke.
Alpheas
Miro never graduated. She still resides in this school.
Shirone gulped.
It felt like hearing a ghost story passed down through the school.
Shirone
But what did it mean for her to “reside” here?
Shirone
Hadn't he just said she lived in another dimension?
At that moment, Scale Magic flashed through Shirone's mind.
Shirone
Then… could it be…?
Alpheas nodded.
Alpheas
Yes. Miro is in Istas. Of course, it's a higher dimension created by Scale Magic. Those who know call it the Upper Layer.
Alpheas placed a hand on Shirone's shoulder.
Alpheas
