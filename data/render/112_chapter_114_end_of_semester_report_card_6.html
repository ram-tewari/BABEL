112 Chapter 114 End Of Semester Report Card 6
Kuan
Where is the swordsman's center of gravity? The answer is outside. In schema terms, this is called 'external gravity.' Repeat after me—external gravity.
Cadets
External gravity! 💢
The cadets shouted in unison, but their voices were laced with anger.
A term they had never heard in an entire semester was being explained right before the exam.
Kuan
The reason I never explained it before is because I couldn't be bothered. Of course, idiots like you wouldn't understand anyway. But today, since I'm in a bad mood, I've decided to add an evaluation on external gravity. Understood?
Cadets
Yes!
The cadets' response echoed loudly.
Their voices filled with suppressed fury.
Changing the evaluation criteria now meant all their hard work over the past six months was for nothing.
Cadet
Ah, I really want to kill him. What kind of teacher is that?
Kuan
I'll demonstrate only once. Pay close attention and apply it to your evaluation.
Whatever their thoughts, the Colosseum, already quiet, fell into complete silence.
Kuan
Cut while advancing, cut while retreating.
Kuan demonstrated slashes while alternating between forward and backward movements.
His limp made it look unimpressive.
But the sound of the blade cutting through the air was terrifying.
Kuan
This is the basic movement of swordsmanship. Physical strength shifts the center of gravity, and the shift in center enhances the blade's destructive power. Moreover, if you amplify it with schema, the blade's power can increase exponentially.
The cadets gulped.
Though they had heard this countless times, their tension was at its peak with the exam looming.
Kuan
……That's what low-level thinkers like you would assume. But if you approach real combat with that mindset, you'll die within three seconds.
Cadet
What an asshole.
The cadets' glares intensified.
Kuan
Remember this. No matter how strong your destructive power is, if your movement is inferior, the outcome is already decided. Therefore, swordsmen adjust their center of gravity based on the situation. For example, like this.
Kuan stomped the ground with his foot.
Then, as he lifted one leg, his body tilted toward the ground at an angle of over 70 degrees.
The cadets stared in disbelief.
Without ropes tied around his waist, maintaining such a posture should have been impossible for a human.
Kuan
The amplified force from schema maximizes the shift in the center of gravity. Right now, my center of gravity is outside my body. This is called external gravity.
Kuan's body snapped upright like a roly-poly toy.
Kuan
With external gravity, the possibilities of movement become limitless. For example, this is also possible.
Kuan stomped the ground again and leaned backward until his back nearly touched the ground.
His body rotated in a conical motion.
Kuan
External gravity isn't actual gravity. It's just an extreme form of inertia. So, if you apply rotation to external gravity, your body will rotate too.
As Kuan extended his sword outward, the rotation slowed.
But as the tilt corrected itself, centrifugal force accelerated the spin.
Kuan, now standing vertically, spun at an incredible speed before stopping abruptly in a poised stance.
The cadets stared in awe.
If they had been in his place, their ankles would have been sliced off first, followed by their bodies being shredded into dozens of pieces by the accelerating blade.
Defense was impossible.
Just as humans couldn't stop a tornado caused by atmospheric pressure differences, this explosive external gravity was akin to a rotating blade machine.
Cadet
This is the Death Magician.
A nickname given because his opponents died without even understanding how.
Kuan
This is today's practical evaluation. The first part is obstacle clearance, and the second is urban combat movement.
Cadet
Instructor, I have a question.
Kuan
Drop. Two hundred push-ups, now.
The cadet's face paled as he dropped and began the push-ups.
He wondered what he had done wrong.
Kuan
Remember this. To get the answer you want, you must overwhelm the other person. I'm not your parent. Don't stare at me with those desperate eyes begging for praise. It's disgusting. Understood?
Cadets
Yes! We'll correct ourselves!
Kuan
Try again.
After finishing the punishment, the cadet raised his hand with a grimace.
Cadet
Instructor! I have a question!
Kuan
Speak.
Cadet
What does the urban combat movement evaluate?
Kuan
That's what I'm about to explain.
The cadet felt foolish.
His motivation for asking was simply to impress Kuan, but Kuan saw even respect as a sign of weakness.
Kuan
Urban combat movement. I'll keep it short since I can't be bothered.
Kuan entered a building with an open front, like a dollhouse, making it easy for everyone to observe.
Kuan
External gravity shines in complex terrain. Urban combat, in particular, is confined to tight spaces, so the level of movement decides the outcome.
Kuan stomped the floor but didn't use external gravity.
Kuan
Stomping the ground generates force to shift your center. This is called the 'first impact.' Of course, in real combat, you won't have time for such theatrics, but for beginners like you, stomping is advisable.
Though laced with mockery, the cadets were too awed by Kuan's skill to feel angry anymore.
Kuan
But I don't use the first impact by stomping. If I did, the floor would shatter. Schema offers various first impact methods for external gravity, but I'll use the most basic one—muscle vibration.
Kuan contracted his upper body muscles to generate vibrations, then leaped and touched the wall.
By bending his elbows to adjust inertia, he hovered in mid-air for over three seconds.
Cadets
Whoa!
The cadets gasped in amazement.
Normally, Kuan would have ordered them to drop for push-ups, but he ignored it and continued.
Kuan
Create external gravity with the first impact and leap. Of course, in this case, external gravity would act toward the wall. Applying this, movements like this are possible.
Kuan walked along the wall.
The cadets slowly raised their heads, staring in disbelief as he stood upside down on the ceiling.
Kuan
External gravity ultimately uses inertial force to cancel gravity. So, right now, gravity isn't acting on my body.
Kuan walking on the ceiling was beyond wondrous.
It was bizarre.
But this wasn't magic.
Even circus performers could run along walls without schema.
Kuan simply maximized inertia with stronger and more precise force.
When the external gravity dissipated, Kuan fell in accordance with gravity.
By the time he finished the demonstration and stepped outside, the cadets' expressions had completely changed.
Kuan
All of you, drop. Two hundred push-ups, now.
Cadets
Yes, sir!
The cadets dropped with dismay.
For regular students, most could handle schema, so two hundred push-ups wasn't impossible.
But for first-years like them, whose reinforcement capabilities were limited, it was a grueling punishment.
Kuan
Listen while you work. The first practical is horizontal obstacles, and the height decreases as the stages progress. The final stage is impossible to clear without external gravity. Time limit: twenty seconds. The obstacles are made with real blades, so failure could mean death. Of course, I doubt any of you would be foolish enough to dodge them. If injured, Instructor Kaina will treat you.
Kaina was the best surgeon at the swordsmanship school.
If mages had healers, swordsmen had surgeons who specialized in battlefield medicine.
The problem was the excruciating pain of the procedures, especially for amputees, who often passed out from the agony of nerve reconnection.
Cadet
Damn it! Hearing that just makes it scarier!
Kuan
Urban combat movement is simple. Create external gravity and rely on the wall. The longer you last, the higher your score. Understood?
