If you're prepared, follow me. Young master, you have been registered as a guest of the Ozent family and admitted through special selection. Let me introduce you.
The security guards were standing at the main gate of the magic school, just like six years ago.
Whether they were the same guards who had looked at Shirone like an insect and chased him away, he couldn't tell, but even if the people changed, the philosophy of that position would likely remain the same.
Guard
What is your business?
Temuran
This is Shirone, who will be joining the magic school. Here is his admission certificate and documentation from the Ozent family verifying his identity.
After checking the admission certificate, the guard didn't even dare to look at the identity document.
In any case, it was clear that anyone who could cross this threshold was someone far above their station.
Marco
Ah, I see. It's an honor to serve you. I'm Marco, in charge of the main gate security. I look forward to serving you.
As the guard bowed, the guard standing next to him also placed his right arm on his stomach and gave a respectful salute.
Shirone was stunned by this treatment, so different from six years ago, but soon realized the reality and smiled.
Shirone
Then I'll be going now. If you ever need anything, please find me.
Taking the documents from Temuran, Shirone took his first steps as a magic school student.
As he crossed the threshold of the main gate, his heart swelled. Alpheus Magic School, called the cradle of geniuses. What would he learn and what kind of people would he meet? The life of Shirone the magician was just beginning.
The magic school occupied an entire mountain with an area of 75.425 square kilometers. There were a total of twenty-two buildings and more than forty basic training grounds. The total number of students was 420, and there were 32 teachers.
With this scale, it could certainly be called the kingdom's finest school without exaggeration.
Entering the building where the reception was located, neat and beautiful students were walking around in groups.
Various maps, establishment history, and curriculum were posted on the walls. While waiting after submitting his documents, Shirone carefully examined them to pass the time.
Alpheus Magic School adopted a system called Class Ten. Basic classes ranged from Ten to Eight, advanced classes from Seven to Four, and graduating classes from Three to One.
The graduating class, the only one that applied relative evaluation, had a maximum of 30 students, of which only 10 would graduate.
Shirone clicked his tongue at the rigorous graduation method of Alpheus.
Considering that the graduation test was held only once a year, it meant that 20 talented individuals were expelled each year.
Headmaster Alpheus
So you're Shirone.
Shirone was surprised by his appearance, which hadn't changed at all in six years. But considering this was a magic school that defied common sense, it wasn't that surprising.
Shirone
Hello. I'm Shirone.
Headmaster Alpheus
I think we've met before? Do you remember me?
Shirone
Yes. Thank you so much for then.
Headmaster Alpheus
Well. Anyway, let's leave the talk for later and go. You need to take the entrance test.
Shirone was taken aback by the idea of having to take an exam right after enrolling. What kind of test could they be conducting when he hadn't learned anything yet?
Headmaster Alpheus
There's nothing to worry about. It's an assessment to determine your class. Can you do a Spirit Zone?
Shirone
Yes, I can.
He knew the child was talented and intelligent. But even so, mastering Spirit Zone on his own would have been difficult.
In fact, it was Alpheus himself who taught Shirone the Spirit Zone, but he didn't think a 12-year-old child would succeed after just one attempt.
That thought still held true. It was for this reason that Shirone was processed last among the incoming students.
Alpheus led Shirone to the examination hall. Since other children's tests would be in full swing by the time they arrived, his pace was leisurely.
Intending to give him a tour, they entered the building, and Shirone's mouth fell open. A completely different world from what he had seen so far was unfolding.
Numerous students were using magic. Even though it was the basic class building, the children's extraordinary feats made his eyes dizzy.
A girl floating at a height of four floors was chatting with a friend through a window, while another child had three books open in the air and was reading them simultaneously.
Alpheus glanced at Shirone's reaction and grinned like a mischievous child. From here on, things were on a different level.
Entering Class Seven, the age range of students increased significantly. They weren't using strange magic like the children, but their eyes were full of intelligence, and they were so immersed in conversation that they wouldn't notice if someone passed by.
Student 1
Energy of convection to bring the atmosphere below freezing point? Wouldn't it be more efficient to condense the refrigerant?
Student 2
But there's the disadvantage that you can't promote the reversible reaction. How about this? Instead of reversing the direction of energy, if we rotate the time axis...
Shirone became frightened listening to the students' conversation. He couldn't understand what topic they were discussing at all.
At that moment, an intense noise came from the end of the corridor. A door was torn off, and a female student ran out covering her ears.
The students looked back with surprised expressions for a moment but quickly returned to their daily routines. Judging by their reactions, this seemed to be a common occurrence here.
Female Student
Ah, Headmaster! Hello!
Headmaster Alpheus
Ho ho, yes. Seems like there's a problem.
Female Student
We were doing a sound amplification experiment, but there must have been an error in the output circuit. I think we need to redesign it.
Headmaster Alpheus
Balancing input and output is important. The key is to maintain electrical forces evenly.
Female Student
Yes, I'll try again.
The female student adjusted her glasses and went back to the laboratory. Children of the same age were gathered around a strange magic circle, discussing.
Shirone realized how small the pond he had been confined to was. Everyone seemed like a genius.
The status board listed the grades of 270 students in the advanced class. In Class Four, the highest within the advanced class, 42 students were listed, and the top spot was held by someone named Karmis Eimi.
Shirone
Wow. As expected, the Karmis family is at the top.
Based on his experience working for the Ozent family, Shirone had memorized the names of famous noble families. Karmis was a first-rank noble family with their main house in the city of Creas.
However, Shirone wasn't intimidated from the start. It was truly unlimited competition. He wanted to compete and see how far he could rise.
Student
Hey, Eimi! Let's go together!
Shirone turned his head. He definitely heard 'Eimi.' It was an opportunity to see the top student of Class Four with his own eyes. A beautiful red-haired girl was hugging a book and waiting for her friend.
Shirone's heart suddenly raced. Trauma. The emotion came before the memory. It took quite a while before he recalled who she was.
She was the girl who had bullied him in the back alley six years ago.
Shirone
H-how can this be! Why is that child here?
As Eimi walked alongside her friend, Shirone turned his head. It would be disastrous if she remembered him, as she knew he wasn't a noble.
Shirone
Please just walk by. Just walk by.
The two female students passed by without any particular reaction.
Shirone breathed a sigh of relief, but that was a hasty judgment stemming from not understanding women.
Eimi's Friend
Did you see that guy who just passed? Is he new? He seems too old for an incoming student, maybe a transfer? He's totally my type.
Even as she heard her chatty friend's words, Eimi didn't respond. Like Shirone, she too was feeling something strange.
Eimi
Hmm, strange. He definitely looked familiar. Where have I seen him?
Eimi's Friend
What? Are you interested in him? You never even glance at guys until now.
Eimi
