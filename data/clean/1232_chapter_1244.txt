Chapter 1244:
The Hall of Evil.
After hearing the story of the birth of good and evil, Shirone and the
others left Lilith's chamber and walked down the corridor.
"How much evil could possibly exist?" Cain spoke. "This hall holds
the entire history of evil—from its beginning to its end. I don't like ranking them, but... well, think
of it as a kind of list. Most evils are only recorded in a single line."
Every room along the corridor was a library, and their sizes couldn't be
guessed from the doors alone.
"Truly heinous evils are kept in special solitary chambers. Ah, here's
Havitz. Fairly high up—Rank 7. Enough to stand shoulder to shoulder with our mother."
The group remained silent.
"Normally, the rooms are ordered by era. If you want to see recent
evils, we'll need to walk a bit farther."
Shirone noticed a solitary room with a portrait hanging on the
door.
'Miss Minerva.'
Cain said he didn't rank evil, but by placement, this one was Rank
87. Iruki spoke.
"We don't have much time."
"I know. You're going to meet Giyorgi, right? He's got that woman,
Seina, with him."
As the world was being devoured by the Rami Church, Seina was
essential.
'The one who can officially confirm everything about the Papacy.'
Cain turned to them.
"Are you sure you're ready? I don't know much, but Giyorgi's been
waiting for this—for a chance to kill you."
Shirone replied calmly.
"This is my responsibility. Giyorgi is a demon born from my
emotions."
"Responsibility, huh?"
A word that didn't suit evil.
"You really are alike. No wonder Giyorgi didn't feel like a typical
demon. Well, fine. I'll just watch."
The end of an era.
"Alright, this is your era now. Let's see… One of you should be here,
right?"
Cain turned to Curtis and pointed to a room.
"Well, since you've come all the way to the Core, you should get a
reward. Why not go in and erase your criminal record?"
When Curtis didn't respond, Shirone called out.
"Mr. Curtis."
"…No, forget it. It doesn't matter anymore. Let's just go. If I deserve
punishment, let it come."
When the others remained standing in front of the door, Curtis scowled
and shouted:
"Yeah! Fine! I did it! I killed those beautiful girls! You disgusted?
Then go ahead and hate me!"
Pena asked softly.
"You… really did kill them…?"
"We were in love!" Curtis dropped to his knees and clutched his
head.
"Nia and I really loved each other. Yeah, it was an affair. I was a
married man messing around with my daughter's friend, so of course I must've seemed like scum! But my
life was—!"
"Why did you kill them?"
Shirone cut to the heart of the matter.
Curtis realized that no words could justify what he had done, and he
gave up pretending.
"Edrina, Nia, Bellita, and Daisy—they'd been friends since they were
little. I was having an affair with Nia. Then one day, my daughter found out. It was Nia's
fault. She had a small argument with Edrina, and in the heat of the moment… she blurted it out."
Maybe Nia had even wanted to get it off her chest.
"That day, they were going on a trip. I had actually planned to meet Nia
at the vacation house. I told my wife I was on regional patrol—common for detectives. But
eventually, Edrina found out everything…"
"You didn't go on the trip, did you?"
"No. She broke away from her friends and went to the plaza. She
saw the address on the bulletin board and found her way to the vacation house. And there… she
hanged herself."
"How do you know that? One of your fellow detectives said the scene made
suicide impossible."
"…The knot."
Curtis's hands trembled.
"It wasn't a hunting knot. It was tied with an extra twist, using a hook
loop. She must have stood on a platform to tie an old rope to the ceiling pipe, then hung
from it and cut the rope. After that, she moved the platform and locked the storeroom from the inside.
That's how she made a sealed room."
The scene came to life in their minds.
"Edrina probably lay down as if she had fallen. Then she tied the loop
around her neck and yanked it hard…"
The knot wouldn't come undone.
"She left like that. My daughter…"
Curtis imagined it again— His daughter lying on the cold floor of
the storeroom, choking, waiting for death…
The single tear she might have shed.
"I'm the one…"
He turned his head and wept.
"I'm the one who taught her that knot."
"Ever since she was little, Edrina admired the fact that I was a
detective. She thought I was some kind of justice warrior who beat up villains. In reality,
I was just a guy doing all kinds of jobs to get by…"
The first thing that came to mind when he found her body was a memory
from when Edrina was eight.
'Come here, sweetie.'
'Why, Dad?'
'Hold out your hands. I'll show you something fun.'
Curtis tied the little girl's pale wrists in a knot, twisted it again,
and hooked it.
'Ta-da!'
'Huh? What? Why won't it come undone?'
'Haha! What do you think?'
When he tickled her side, she burst out laughing.
'Stop! Let me go! I'll get you back for this!'
'Watch this.'
Curtis sat her on his knee and unhooked the knot with his thumb.
'Just one flick and it comes undone. It's called the "cuff knot."
Magicians use it.'
'Wow! Daddy, teach me too! Pleeease?'
'Alright. Let's do it again.'
"…She could have undone it."
Curtis felt like his soul had shattered.
"She could have easily escaped if she'd changed her mind. But why didn't
she… Why… Aaaagh!"
He slammed the ground and wailed.
Iruki, after watching him for a while, asked a question.
"The detectives didn't find the knot…"
"I untied it."
When Curtis had first found her body, his hands had instinctively undone
the cuff knot.
"I wasn't trying to hide the truth. Who cares about an affair when your
daughter's dead? I don't know. It was just… so tragic. It was just a knot. I thought if I undid it…
maybe, just maybe, she'd come back to life…"
"But you did kill them."
Nia, Bellita, and Daisy—Edrina's friends.
"When my daughter was dying… I was waiting at the vacation spot where I
was supposed to meet Nia."
Another part of the story unfolded.
"She was supposed to come alone, but she brought all her friends. I was
annoyed, but then I noticed my daughter wasn't there. I asked what happened, and Nia just said, 'It's
over.'"
Curtis turned his head.
"How do you think I felt then? A father who got caught fooling around
with his daughter's friend. I was furious. I fought with Nia, and she threatened me—said she'd destroy
my family."
Affairs always ended that way.
"I thought I needed to see my daughter first. But I couldn't just let
Nia and her friends go. So I locked them in the attic of the villa. I told them if they weren't there
when I got back, I wouldn't forgive them. They seemed a little scared."
"But even so, they could've escaped," Shirone said.
"If they were really afraid for their lives, maybe. It was the second
floor, and the window was open. But it wasn't like that. We'd spent the night together just the day
before, and we always argued. This time it just got serious because my family was involved. Nia probably
thought I'd come back. It was an expensive villa. I figured they'd spend the time badmouthing me.
Bellita and Daisy were probably enjoying the drama."
"But in the end, you didn't see your daughter again," Shirone
said.
"I went to Seaf Plaza, where we were supposed to meet. A merchant told
me he saw her anxiously checking a notice board. It was an ad for a rental villa. It was strange—it
looked vacant. But I figured it was the only place she could go. She didn't have money, and she was
bold, even reckless. Maybe because she was a cop's daughter. I called my friend Ryver. I couldn't
explain everything—I was too ashamed—but at least he could help me fake an alibi for a patrol. And then,
at that villa…"
They found his daughter's body.
"You don't need me to explain the rest. I won't use the excuse that I
wasn't in my right mind. At first, it was impulsive. But then… temptation set in. I thought maybe I
could make it disappear. That's what I am—a murderer. A murderer who knows exactly how to avoid getting
caught."
Leaning against the wall, Curtis lit a cigarette.
"Most cold cases are like that. They exist in the realm beyond
plausibility. People's attachment to life is immense. When it was declared a cold case, I thought… maybe
I could keep living. Honestly, I don't even remember how I managed. And then… a letter arrived at my
precinct."
Click. The flame lit the cigarette.
—It's not your fault, Dad.
"Phew…"
Curtis exhaled smoke toward the ceiling and spoke.
"I couldn't stand the shame. I just wanted to forget. I could've died,
sure, but… I knew that even if I disappeared, what I'd done would still remain. That unsettled me more
than anything. I wanted it erased completely. That's why I came to Melkidu."
This was the truth behind the Edrina case.
"Go. I'm staying. Don't worry about punishment. I've only got one thing
left."
He planned to erase his record and then take his own life.
Using the same cuff knot that took his daughter.
'That should be enough, right… Edrina?'
Curtis flicked his cigarette butt into the Hall of Evil.
"Life's a goddamn mess."
"Which is why you should live."
Pena approached him.
"What changes if you die? Some things can't be atoned for with death.
That's just how it is."
Curtis already knew that.
"So what do you want me to do? Rot in prison forever? Think that'll even
make me blink?"
Even the capital prison in Parme couldn't weigh more heavily than his
guilt.
"…I don't know. That's why it's a sin. Because it's something you can't
take responsibility for—it's something you shouldn't do."
Pena continued.
"How did it feel when your daughter died? Right now, you're doing the
same thing. If you die, what about us? The people you leave behind? If you're gonna kill yourself, do it
alone, somewhere no one knows you. At least have the decency to take responsibility."
She turned to Shirone and the others.
"I'll stay. Don't worry—go on ahead. I won't let him just die."
After thinking for a moment, Shirone turned around.
"Let's go."
Led by Cain, the group departed, leaving Curtis alone with Pena.
"…That was a good act."
"What?"
"Erase your record and run. This is the Core—it's the real world. Get on
a boat or something and start over."
Truthfully, she had thought about it.
"…No. I'm done with that."
"With what?"
Pena sat beside him.
"I've hurt a lot of people too. But being here made me realize—there's
no such thing as a new life. If I don't change, nothing will. It'll all just stay the same."
There was no sanctuary for demons.
"So then… what now?"
"Nothing. Let's just sit. Let's not think about doing anything. We're
the ones who ruined everything. Whatever the world decides, we'll accept it. With a heart ready to
atone. It may not change anything, but…"
Fighting back tears, Pena said,
"At the very least… we have to take responsibility for the sins we can't
take responsibility for."
Sins without redemption.
"…Yeah."
In the Hall of Evil, Curtis looked toward the sky.
'I'm sorry.'
'I ruined everything.'
'So please, God—'
'If you really exist… if you, too, have something like a heart…'
'Then please—'
'Don't forgive me.'
