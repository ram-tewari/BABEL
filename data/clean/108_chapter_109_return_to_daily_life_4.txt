Chapter 109:
Return to Daily Life (4)
The most tense time of the semester for magic school students had arrived—the end-of-term
report cards. Half a year's efforts were condensed into a single sheet of paper. The cutoff line determining
promotion or failure was as terrifying as a guillotine.
Alpheas had received a six-month suspension of his teaching license from the Magic
Association. The penalty would take effect next semester, and for now, Colley, the most senior teacher, was
acting as interim headmaster.
Alpheas, who had been weeding the flower beds, straightened up and smiled. His adorable
disciples were approaching. Shirone, Iruki, Nade—now notorious troublemakers known throughout the school,
but that only made their futures more promising.
"Hello, Headmaster."
"Hoho. I told you not to call me that. I'm just a old man killing time now."
"Come on. You'll be headmaster again in half a year."
Alpheas merely laughed. He was grateful for his students' unwavering support, but it also
made him feel undeserving.
"So, how are you holding up? Must be tough with school back in session."
Nade's face immediately fell.
"I'm going insane. I need to score over 80 in every subject left, and it's killing me."
"Heh! Trying to move up this time?"
The only way to advance from Class Five to Class Four was to score over 80 in every
subject—not an average, making it the highest difficulty.
"Of course. Isn't that what students are supposed to do?"
"I suppose so. Well, do your best."
Nade looked disappointed, having hoped for encouragement. But Alpheas's teaching
philosophy was firm. Students weren't racehorses. Left to roam the plains, they'd realize their own worth.
"Want some help?"
Shirone rolled up his sleeves and approached to help with the weeding.
"Hoho, you can't take an old man's only joy. I'll handle the weeds. You go do what you
want."
Nade's eyes filled with despair. She had been counting on weeding as an escape from
reality, but that plan was now foiled.
"Alright. Take care."
As they turned toward the dormitory, worry lingered on their faces. The other students
were no different. Since Arcane's attack, the school remained unsettled, and focusing on studies wasn't
easy.
Frustrated, Nade vented.
"Ugh! I'm losing it. I need to study! Study! But I can't focus!"
"After a big event, it's hard to concentrate on anything else. Same after the recital."
At Iruki's words, Shirone sighed deeply.
"Still, you're better off than me. I have twice as many subjects to pull up to 80. I
don't know if it's possible."
"I've only got math going for me. Humanities are a lost cause. In that sense, Nade's got
the advantage."
"What are you talking about? I don't have any strong subjects. You and Shirone can ace
practicals, but I'm stuck with the same Spirit Zone as everyone else."
Class Five's practical exams focused more on Spirit Zone application than magic. With
Immortal Function and Servant Syndrome, Shirone and Iruki could rank high in any practical. Nade had to
compete with ordinary Spirit Zones.
"All this thinking is giving me a headache. Let's just take today off and start
tomorrow."
Nade's suggestion met with neither agreement nor disagreement. Even the diligent Shirone
remained silent, his motivation clearly flagging.
"Huh? Guys, look…."
Shirone pointed toward the central park. Nade and Iruki turned, their eyes widening.
"What? How are they here?"
Canis and Arin were wandering the school grounds. Spotting Shirone's group, they
approached. Canis raised a hand in greeting.
"Hey. Classes over already? The facilities are nice, but you're coddling the kids too
much."
"You! What are you plotting now?"
Nade blocked their path. Though they had heard from Sad that the two had been acquitted,
she thought that was the end of it. Or it should have been. So why were they here?
Shirone asked.
"What's going on? Do you have business here?"
Canis ignored the question, studying Shirone. If anyone at the school intrigued him, it
wasn't Alpheas or the teachers—it was Shirone. The first person to defeat him. Without surpassing him, any
growth would be meaningless.
"Listen carefully. If we fight again, the result will be the opposite of last time."
Before Shirone could respond, Nade retorted.
"This guy's still delusional. You're a decade away from beating Shirone."
"Says the one who'd need ten years just to face me."
