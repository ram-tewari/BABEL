Chapter 666:
To Radum (1)
A man like a stray dog was approaching the back gate of Radum—a place
everyone knew of, but few dared to enter.
"Sniff sniff. Sniff sniff."
No, Shagall was more than just like a stray dog—he truly was one,
wandering in pursuit of the scent of an incident.
His genius-level physical ability allowed him to operate seven Schema,
rivaling the physical prowess of giants classified as First-Level users of the "Elixir of
Life."
Currently, he was running a sensory-based Schema build, the Original
Sense Build, which enhanced vision, smell, and hearing.
With four folds, amplifying his senses sixteenfold, and additional
partial folding to focus specific abilities, the scent of Ra Enemy became unmistakably clear.
'Is it here?'
"Halt. This area is restricted to civilians."
A guard from the Royal City garrison stationed at Radum's back gate
thrust a torch forward, halting Shagall.
Shagall continued approaching as if he hadn't heard a word. Alarmed, the
guards drew their weapons.
"Stop! Identify yourself!"
"Sniff sniff. Sniff sniff."
His dog-like sniffing gave the impression he might be insane.
Dragging a large bag and shrouded in long hair that veiled his face, his
wild eyes cut clearly through the strands—a madness that was unmistakably lucid.
"Kill him. I'll take responsibility."
At the captain's order, the guards leapt over the barricade to subdue
Shagall.
The moment over ten soldiers collapsed to the ground after simply
brushing past him, the captain's eyes went wide in disbelief.
"How… how could this be…?"
They weren't weak. That fact alone told him: this lunatic was a Schema
master of a level he had never encountered before.
'Send backup—!'
Abandoning counterattack, the captain blew into a whistle with all his
might.
But Shagall was already airborne. He extended his arm and drove a dagger
forward. Whip-whip-whip—three near-simultaneous slashes struck the captain's neck.
Before the captain's breath even reached his lips, it escaped through
the blade lodged in his throat. He crumpled to the ground, vomiting a massive spray of blood from the
wound.
The heavy bag containing the daggers thudded onto the ground, but did
not disturb Radum's eerie silence.
"Ra Enemy..."
The being who took everything from him.
"I'll kill you. As many times as it takes."
The back gate of Radum opened.
Just before midnight, twenty members of Shirone's Mercenary Unit
gathered in the Silver Ring Guild's courtyard.
They had been handpicked, and their presence alone suggested a force
capable of surviving even in hell.
Lupist and Jane still wore their masks, planning to keep their
identities hidden until they entered Radum.
Since Shirone was the unit's commander, it posed no issue in leading the
team.
"I'm Shirone, commander of the Shirone Mercenary Unit. Before we
depart, I'll explain our mission."
The mercenaries perked up, curious about the task that promised a 10,000
gold reward.
"From this point forward, we'll be infiltrating Radum."
No one flinched—yet.
"What exactly are we expected to do?" asked Wigg the Gale,
raising his hand.
After his loss to Rian, the hierarchy had been settled, and although he
accepted it, his spirits were clearly dampened.
"We're going to…"
Shirone paused, considering the best phrasing.
"Occupy Radum."
"What?"
At last, several mercenaries displayed stunned expressions.
Radum—the underbelly of Bashka, too filthy for the kingdom to
touch.
Rumors claimed it was too corrupt to bother with, but if it were filled
with just rabble, it would've been purged long ago.
"Occupy Radum? What are you talking about?"
Even if they were backed by Brooks the broker and led by an Ivory Tower
candidate, a mercenary unit was still a private force. Conquering Radum with only 20 members seemed
impossible.
'How do I convince them?'
Only a few knew that the Head of the Magic Association, Lupist, was
among them. And the existence of the Living Flower, the Iron Tower at the heart of Radum, was a state
secret that couldn't be revealed until the final mission was complete.
"I've been to Radum a few times," said Kargin, a healing mage
on the team. Despite being in his seventies, he was notorious for making lewd advances on female
mercenaries.
"Radum has three main zones: the outer slums, the inner concealment
facilities, and a central core zone. Some say the leaders of the Demi-humans live in that core, but no
one's ever gotten there. The countless hidden facilities form a natural defense maze around
it."
These concealment zones were filled with outlawed Demi-human groups and
undercover human operations, making the core inaccessible through conventional means.
"To conquer Radum means reaching the core. Do we have a method?
There must be at least 100 facilities to bypass—and the problem is each of them runs on a different
security system. No single hacker could crack them all."
Sensing the danger, Baikon, a heavily-armored, three-Schema swordsman,
leapt to his feet.
"This isn't just a fight-to-the-death mission! We know nothing
about the enemy! You don't really plan to break through each facility one by one, do you?"
"We do."
Shirone spoke plainly.
"It'll take time, but that's the only way to reach the
core."
"Tch! And who's going to handle the security systems?"
"Me and Number 29."
Kargin looked back and forth between the two.
"Just the two of you? With magic?"
"Yes. I can decipher any system."
It was the power of the Ultima System.
"I'll decode, and 29 will disable. We have more than enough
chance."
With nothing left to say, Baikon slumped back into his seat, though not
everyone was convinced.
"Who are they, anyway?" asked Bromark, a survival and trap
expert, pointing to 28 and 29.
"I'm not usually the type to nitpick after agreeing to a job, but
this doesn't sit right. Why are we hiding identities in a mercenary unit?"
All eyes turned toward Shirone, who realized he couldn't just gloss over
this.
'They're putting their lives on the line. Of course they'll question
it.'
"One hundred thousand gold."
The unexpected offer from Number 28 turned everyone's head.
"Take it in cash or deposit it to your guild account."
Kon, a mage from Silver Ring, scowled.
"Hold on. What kind of job pays 100,000 gold...?"
"Walk if you want out," 28 said coldly, sweeping his gaze over
the room.
"I'll pay 100,000 gold to everyone in the mercenary unit—on one
condition: that 29 and I wear masks. This is pre-approved by the commander."
Everyone's gears began to turn.
"Hmph. From the Royal Capital, huh..." Kargin narrowed his
eyes at 28 and 29.
Even without confirmation, their strength was beyond comparison.
Including the Ivory Tower candidate, Aria, and that amount of gold—it all aligned now.
Everyone was reaching the same conclusion.
'It's an unofficial royal mission. Something's happening inside
Radum.'
And it needed to be silenced.
'But they're also prepared for failure. That's why they're hiding their
identities.'
If it was discovered the royal capital was involved, any secret
negotiations could fall apart, or worse, the capital might be attacked.
'This isn't something a bureaucrat can take the fall for. Wait… that man
and woman… they're Lupist and Jane.'
The Head of the Magic Association and his chief of staff were both
deployed.
Mercenaries usually hated royal bureaucracy—but in this case, it brought
reassurance.
'If that really is Lupist, he already knows we've figured it
out.'
And still, they wouldn't remove their masks.
Circumstantial evidence and hard proof are worlds apart. For the
mercenaries, it was the difference between being discreetly eliminated or legally eliminated.
'Best to leave the masks on.'
"I'm in," said Bromark, drawing attention.
"If you send 100,000 gold to my family tonight, I don't care who
you are or what you're doing."
He spread his arms to the group.
"Why are we hesitating? Since when have we ever worked based on
who's paying us? Once they pay, the mask is our employer."
They were walking into hell anyway. Weakening their forces over this
would be stupid.
Even if not persuaded by Bromark's speech, no one in the room could
ignore the math of gold versus survival.
"I agree too."
A woman carrying a quiver raised her hand.
She was Joshua, the mercenary unit's only archer, known for her
lightning-fast accuracy and sharp ocular technique.
"But I want it in cash. Right now."
Kargin asked, "A hundred thousand gold? Even in top-grade coins,
that'd be at least a hundred pieces."
"I don't care. I'll put it in my Kubrick."
Joshua showed the ring on her finger.
"Heh, not much for trusting people, huh? Be careful—might lose a
finger that way."
"Try it, if you can."
Joshua's eyes gleamed like a cat's.
It was Cat's Eye, an ocular technique that allowed her to see clearly
even in complete darkness.
"I'll chase you to the ends of hell and kill you."
"Tsk, what a temper…"
Kargin clicked his tongue, though he didn't seem displeased.
'I like her,' he thought.
Lupist spoke.
"I'll take that as unanimous agreement and proceed."
No one objected, and Shirone rose from his seat.
"Then let's regroup once the payments are complete. We depart at
midnight, so try to settle things quickly."
Through Jane, Lupist arranged for the full 100,000 gold to be
distributed to every member except for the core operatives.
Those with families opted for bank transfers. The unattached ones, like
Joshua, preferred storage in their Kubricks.
As the bells of the Royal Capital rang out to mark midnight, Shirone's
mercenary unit departed Silver Ring Guild by carriage.
After a 30-minute ride, they arrived at Radum's back gate, and Joshua
was the first to notice.
"I smell blood."
Without a word, everyone readied themselves to move. Rian opened the
window and climbed onto the carriage roof.
'It's quiet…'
Joshua soon joined him, activating her Cat's Eye as she scanned the
surroundings.
"No ambush. There are fourteen bodies."
The squad disembarked in perfect sync as Shirone approached the
scene.
The alleyways of the slums provided the only path into Radum, and down
the narrow pass, guards lay strewn across the ground.
"This is…"
The killer's signature.
Now, even Shirone could recognize who was responsible.
"As expected, he got in first."
Lupist approached from behind Shirone.
"Which means Ra Enemy was here. Maybe we'll even catch something
big."
If Meirei could understand Ra's intent through his voice, then Shagall
could track his scent.
Though the mission's core objective was to shut down Radum, priorities
could shift depending on developments.
'What even is Ra Enemy?'
Some of the mercenaries still had no idea—but at this point, no one
questioned it.
The money had already been transferred.
"Shirone, I'm sensing murderous intent."
Rian gripped the hilt of his sword, detecting a foul desire rising from
the far end of the narrow alley.
"He's smelled food."
Shirone turned to Lupist.
"Food?"
"Corpses."
Lupist pointed at the bodies of the guards.
"Prime nutrition, packed with everything a human needs—because it
is human. That's why people on the outside call Radum's residents this—"
Lupist's eyes narrowed toward the shadows.
"Ghouls."
