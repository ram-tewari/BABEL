Chapter 466:
A moment of silence followed.
Though it wasn't their last chance, they knew that if this didn't work,
they would ultimately be the ones to lose.
Would it work?
It was a gamble, almost a 50-50 chance.
The biggest issue was that they were currently in a space created by the
Regulation Exception.
How much of a gap was there between the Orga in front of them and the
real fairy Orga?
If the impact was even slightly indirect, the operation would
fail.
'This is an incredibly complex and delicate Regulation
Exception.'
The entire building was materialized, with numerous Regulation
Exceptions installed in each room.
Was it even possible?
No human could distort so many objects to this extent.
If it were possible…
'The flow of consciousness.'
Shirone recalled his experience with Drimo.
Only the space they were currently standing in was materialized, while
the other rooms existed in a state of nothingness.
It was like thinking about your house in your mind, not viewing it all
at once but letting your thoughts flow as if you were moving through it.
If that was the case, it was possible to contain another Regulation
Exception within a Regulation Exception.
'Though it's not an easy task…'
Therefore, Orga's consciousness was focused on this room.
This was the hypothesis Shirone had formed.
Having organized his thoughts, Shirone proposed to Orga.
"Don't take your eyes off me for one minute."
It was a strange proposal.
But Orga had no right to change the proposal.
"Alright. Let's do that."
Shirone stared intently at Orga.
Once this act was over, it would be Orga's turn.
The outcome would be decided there.
'Valhalla Action.'
In a situation bound by the Regulation Exception, actions outside the
rules were likely prohibited.
But Valhalla Action might be possible.
The equivalent exchange of cause and effect would accept even the cause
of the prohibition and convert it into a result.
Therefore, the key was the calculation time.
Even with Ataraxia's accumulation time reduced to less than 30 seconds,
Shirone proposed one minute to buy some extra time.
As Shirone performed the proposal, Orga stared at him.
Staring meant Orga's mind was fully focused on Shirone.
Clang!
The magic circle of Valhalla Action appeared above his head. The
calculation was instantaneous.
'It's working!'
Exactly 57 seconds.
That was the time it took for Ataraxia to accumulate while under the
effect of Mirroring.
Before Orga could say anything in surprise, the cause and effect
reversed, and a dazzling Ataraxia appeared before them.
"Kyah!"
Even the head fairy of Shehakim couldn't withstand the magic circle of
the archangel, especially Ikael, the highest authority in Heaven.
In fact, the stronger the fairy, the greater the impact.
Cracks appeared on Orga's body, and light began to seep out.
The seal of light grew in size, eventually engulfing her and oxidizing
intensely.
Rumble!
As Paradise collapsed, the original scenery was revealed.
It was still Paradise, but the plaster on the walls had all shattered
and fallen to the floor.
The fragments of plaster were inscribed with various complex patterns
painted in red.
'She designed the space and controlled it with her mind.'
Plu felt relieved.
If a fairy could materialize a building to this extent with a Regulation
Exception, humanity would have no chance in this war.
"Ugh!"
Orga lay on the ground, her small, frail body writhing in pain.
The sudden Ataraxia attack was like being stabbed with a knife for a
fairy specialized in mental abilities.
"Director! Are you alright?"
Numerous fairies assisting Orga rushed to her side, shouting in
concern.
Then they turned to Shirone's group with terrifying eyes.
"They're rebel spies! Kill them!"
As the fairies flew at them, Plu stood in front of Shirone.
She didn't know how long the price of Valhalla Action would last, but
she had to fight until Shirone could move.
"Phoenix Formation!"
Plu raised the firebird high and opened the Dawkins Algorithm to face
the fairies.
The fairies of Shehakim were among the top ranks of the 72 classes, and
fighting them felt like battling a group of professional magicians from the human world.
Flames spewed out, and the different concepts of the fairies shook
Paradise.
'I can't hold out for long!'
The limitation of the Dawkins Algorithm lay in the magician's physical
abilities.
No matter how automatic the reactions were, if subjected to continuous
attacks like this, her body wouldn't hold up.
'54 seconds.'
The collaboration of the fairy of brute strength and the fairy of
suddenness was the most troublesome.
When the immense strength pressed down on Plu, the fairy of suddenness
would thrust a thorny spear in an unpredictable trajectory.
'55 seconds.'
Each time, Plu's body twisted grotesquely, and her joints screamed in
pain.
"Kill them! If we capture them, the war will end!"
The rebels' firepower was formidable even to Heaven, but capturing
Shirone, the Light of Sector 73, would crush the enemy's morale.
Orga's strategy, deducing the espionage tactics from the battle on
Shehakim's outskirts, was worthy of being called a fairy strategist.
'56 seconds.'
When the fairy of loud noise fired a sonic wave, it sounded like
hundreds of tons of steel beams were being pushed aside right next to her, disrupting Plu's
concentration.
"Ugh!"
As the Dawkins Algorithm broke, dozens of fairies rushed in from all
sides.
Exactly 57 seconds!
At the same time, Shirone sprang forward from the ground.
Just as the fairy of brute strength was about to crush Plu's head, a
blur passed by, and instead, her body exploded.
Then the surrounding fairies began to shatter into pieces.
Orga, barely regaining her senses, stared blankly at Shirone with eyes
hidden beneath heavy makeup.
The tendrils were moving so fast they were almost invisible.
While magic might be effective, fairies were vulnerable to physical
attacks.
"Ugh!"
"Director! You shouldn't move yet!"
"Request… request support."
Meanwhile, Shirone continued to eliminate all the visible
fairies.
"Over there!"
Before the fairies could request support, the giant unit charged in,
with the immortal Kergos among them.
They had once been part of the hunting squad, making their combat power
overwhelming even among their kind.
"Shirone! We should retreat for now!"
Given that the premise of stealth had been broken, it was best to follow
Plu's advice, but escaping now was impossible.
He had already experienced the Kergos' tracking skills in Heaven.
"Hmph!"
Shirone glared at the enemy.
An artificial brain eye emerged, and Akamai's Antithesis bound
them.
"Ugh!"
The immense restraining force made even the giants and fairies tremble
in shock.
Though a fallen angel, the restraining force that could immobilize the
angelic Ikael was not something mere giants could break.
With the added magic amplification trait of the true demon species
Galatomic, a photon cannon several times larger than before appeared before Shirone.
The photon cannons multiplied like shadow clones, and finally, twelve
massive spheres of light shook, emitting a brilliant glow.
"Now!"
Shirone fired all the photon cannons and turned his body.
As Antithesis was released, the enemies began to move, but the photon
cannons had already reached them.
Boom, boom, boom, boom, boom!
A deafening explosion erupted in Sector 12 of Shehakim, and light
flashed.
While the nearby enemies would likely converge, it was actually
fortunate for Shirone's group.
"Hurry!"
Shirone and Plu leaped over the Paradise buildings and fled.
"Chase them! Don't let them escape!"
The surviving Kergos quickly pursued them.
The chase lasted for 30 minutes.
Skima's physical abilities shone in urban warfare, and Shirone and Plu
managed to evade their pursuers by setting a complex route.
"Huff, huff!"
The two leaned against a wall, gasping for breath.
Arman increased their oxygen saturation, quickly stabilizing their
breathing, and Shirone's broken finger had already healed.
'Healing a fracture in just a few hours…'
It was thanks to Kenser's trait of infinite cell regeneration.
"What do we do now?"
"We go to Araboth."
"But we don't know where we are. We're lost. And…"
The enemies already knew Shirone had infiltrated.
The difficulty of the mission had skyrocketed compared to the
beginning.
"First, let's find some clothes."
Though they had been severely affected by Orga's Regulation Exception,
they couldn't walk around in their current state.
"I found you, Shirone."
At the voice from the sky, Shirone and Plu's faces turned pale.
As the voice reached them, a small creature entered their Spirit
Zone.
A small creature. A fairy.
'We have to kill it!'
Plu turned, and Shirone swung his tendrils faster.
The fairy flinched at the sudden attack.
Shirone was able to stop the tendrils because Arman had enhanced his
dynamic vision to the extreme.
The sharp needle of the tendril stopped right in front of the fairy's
eyes.
Seeing the fairy trembling with her eyes tightly shut, Shirone removed
his hood with a blank expression.
"You, you…?"
The spiral fairy, Feope, who had helped Shirone until the end during his
last visit to Heaven, slowly opened her eyes.
"Eek!"
Feope recoiled in fear at the needle in front of her.
Then, with a flushed face, she shouted at Shirone.
"What are you doing! Are you trying to kill me?"
Shirone quickly retracted the tendrils.
"Feope! It's you…"
More than anything else, Shirone felt a surge of relief and joy. In the
midst of the battlefield, seeing Feope's face brought tears to his eyes.
As Shirone moved closer, as if to embrace her, Feope hastily extended
her hands, feigning indifference.
"W-What are you doing? Why are you acting like this all of a
sudden?"
"How did you find me? How did you know I was here? Is it okay for
you to be here?"
The rational part of Shirone's mind considered the possibility that
Feope might be an enemy. But he pushed that thought aside for now.
If that assumption were true, he wouldn't be able to bear the
consequences, no matter the sacrifice.
Feope looked at Shirone with gentle eyes and a soft smile.
Though not much time had passed, her expression now held an intellectual
calmness.
"I'm glad you're here, Shirone. I've missed you."
Feope cupped Shirone's cheeks and kissed his forehead.
As the warmth of her lips spread, Shirone's mind was filled with the
memory of her voice from before.
"I love you."
Even if it was the confession of an immature fairy whose concepts hadn't
fully developed, her sincerity was unmistakable.
'Feope. It's really you.'
Shirone gently lifted Feope into his hands.
Even if everyone else wanted to harm him, he knew she wouldn't. This was
a matter of faith, not reason.
"Feope."
"Yes?"
"I have to fight against Heaven."
Feope blinked, waiting for him to continue.
"Can you take me, no, us, to Araboth?"
He said nothing more.
No matter what conversation they had, this was the question he needed to
ask, and he knew the answer would soon come from Feope.
Feope thought for a long time, but she never once avoided Shirone's
gaze.
Her eyes, filled with a mix of resignation and new excitement, slowly
began to smile.
With that expression, Feope nodded at Shirone.
"Alright. I'll take you there."
