Chapter 382:
The Meaning of Survival (4)
Stage 3: Poisonous Swamp.
The location was a swamp stretching to the horizon.
At first, everyone hovered in the air using Fly magic, but one by one,
they inhaled the toxic gas rising from the swamp and fell into it.
The real challenge began then.
Students who fell into the swamp screamed in agony, their faces twisted
beyond recognition.
It was undoubtedly excruciatingly painful.
The students still in the air gritted their teeth and endured.
But they all knew they would have to go in eventually.
The association conveyed through the survival program that the power of
nature couldn't be overcome by magic. The strength to survive until the end lay in the magician's will,
not their magic.
Students in the air began to vomit.
Those in the swamp were hit by it, but they were too overwhelmed to even
realize something was falling on them.
"Sigh."
Nade's sigh was heard. Slowly tilting, he looked back at Shirone and
said,
"See you later, Shirone."
As Nade fell, other students began to follow one by one.
Shirone couldn't hold on any longer either. As he fell, a drowsy voice
escaped his lips.
"Yeah... Nade."
Students who failed the survival test crawled out of the Virtual Zone
one by one.
With ice towels prepared in advance on their heads, they sat away from
the device to rest.
Collie checked the Virtual Zone's gauges and said,
"Stage 3, Poisonous Swamp, has ended. Currently, there are 8
survivors on the red team and 7 on the blue team."
Olivia said,
"Hmm, more survived than expected? This year's senior class is
certainly high-level."
"Class Three has some strong students, and Fermi's group is still
in. Of course, there are no weaklings in Class Two either."
Alpheus said,
"Stage 4... 'Death Scythe,' right? This will tip the
scales."
The difficulty level set by the Magic Association for certified
magicians was Stage 4.
Here, they wouldn't just experience pain but actual death.
Dozens of times.
Whoooosh!
Hearing the wind roaring from the abyss, Shirone was endlessly
falling.
If the previous stages were extreme natural environments, Stage 4 was a
mechanical space.
It was the inside of a massive chimney with an immeasurable
depth.
Hundreds of thousands of blades spun fiercely, leaving no gaps, slicing
the students into pieces.
Death was something one couldn't adapt to.
Every time horrific and tragic emotions struck their minds, their Spirit
Zones were destroyed, and dropouts increased.
Shirone gritted his teeth.
Had he been sliced and reconstituted about ten times? It was as if the
program was asking, "Can you still endure this?"
A giant blade, like a tray, was waiting, leaving afterimages. There was
probably no way to pass through it alive.
'Endure! Endure!'
Shirone instantly passed through the afterimage of the blade. However,
his body was already divided into three pieces.
What words could describe the feeling of watching your legs fall before
your face?
Even though people die, no one has experienced death, so human language
will never find an accurate expression.
Other students were in the same situation.
Stabbed by blades protruding from the walls, they momentarily stopped
falling, then continued their pitiful descent towards the next death as the blades retracted.
"Haaah!"
Reconstituted, Shirone gasped for air like a newborn taking its first
breath.
'Ugh! Just kill me, kill me!'
Seeing dozens of interlocking drills waiting like a mat, anyone would
think that.
Thud!
Shirone fell there.
Whirr, whirr, whirr, thud!
And in an instant, his body was ground up and poured down like
juice.
"Damn it!"
Nade woke up from the Virtual Zone, cursing.
Iruki, Amy, and even Fermi's group couldn't endure the Death Scythe
stage and were thrown out, rolling on the ground.
Their heads felt like they were splitting, and nausea surged. Dry
heaving and spitting out long strands of saliva were common.
"This is crazy. Why do I always get stuck at Stage 4?"
Amy's expression was grim as she wiped her lips.
Maintaining the Spirit Zone even after death was impossible without a
powerful obsession that transcended life and death.
A seeker. A religious person. A murderer.
Whatever it was, from now on, it was a world of mental realms beyond
ordinary people.
So it wasn't strange that Iruki had dropped out.
"Dead is dead. What's next?"
From the start, one shouldn't create situations where they would die.
His mind, which hated uncertainty, couldn't accept this stage.
The Death Scythe stage met the association's expectations
perfectly.
Many dropouts occurred at Stage 4, and most crawled out of the Virtual
Zone looking like death warmed over.
"Ugh, I'm dying. Hey, are you okay?"
Nade, massaging his neck, approached Iruki. Iruki's eyes changed to a
puzzled look as he turned to him.
"Don't tell me, you dropped out too?"
"Yeah. My head's killing me."
After thinking for a moment, Iruki asked again.
"Why?"
"Why? What do you mean?"
Iruki had thought Nade would pass the Death Scythe stage easily. But as
something occurred to him, he understood.
'Maybe it's a natural result?'
Nade didn't think about what came after death. It was probably a
deliberate suppression.
If his mind transcended death, it would be impossible for him to remain
a normal human being.
"Never mind. Let's get out of here. Dying too much is killing
me."
Collie looked at Alpheus and said,
"Entering Stage 5, Insect King, in 30 seconds. Shall we open the
vision?"
"Hmm, Insect King..."
Having passed the 'Death Scythe,' the standard for professional
magicians, they were now entering a world that couldn't be defined by theory.
Since there was a risk of severe mental damage, it was necessary to open
the vision to check the students' conditions.
"Open the vision."
"Yes. Activating the master device."
As Collie operated the device, a large hologram curved concavely
appeared in the air of the Virtual Zone.
In the graduation exam, a giant hologram appears on the ceiling from the
start, but the current vision also allowed a clear view of the program's interior.
-Entering Stage 5, Insect King, in the extreme cold program.
"Ugh! What's that!"
When the Insect King scene appeared on the vision, the dropouts gathered
in front of the Virtual Zone recoiled in horror.
Countless disgusting insects were writhing endlessly. The sight of
billions of insects wriggling made it look like the ground was moving.
The survivors were probably somewhere in there, buried in insects and
suffering.
Stage 5, Insect King.
It was a place filled with fear, disgust, and pain so intense that death
seemed preferable.
The sight of insects writhing together provoked primal human fear, but
this time, it wasn't just watching.
They penetrated the skin, nerves, and organs, transmitting real
sensations.
The pain of insects burrowing into every hole in the body and eating the
flesh didn't end with death.
As those who couldn't endure it increased, the insect-covered ground
began to stir.
Someone, covered in insects like a blackened corpse, suddenly stood up
and started thrashing and screaming.
"Ahhh!"
Aider, lying in the Virtual Zone, echoed the scream from the
simulation.
"Ahhh!"
Thrashing and rolling on the ground, he flailed his limbs, trying to
crawl somewhere.
"Ahhh! Ahhh! Ahhh!"
No one helped him. Approaching someone in severe shock could cause even
greater trauma.
Still, perhaps due to the talent that got him through the Death Scythe
stage, Aider quickly regained his composure.
"Huff! Huff!"
On the boundary between virtual and reality, he looked around in terror,
tears welling up.
"Ah, this is really scary."
"..."
How could it not be?
Those who dropped out at the Death Scythe stage felt relieved that it
ended there after seeing Aider's state.
On the other hand, Screamer's expression was grim.
He had brought Aider to the blue team because he had scored well in the
survival evaluation since last year.
But once again, Aider couldn't get past Stage 5, and the most dangerous
opponent, Shirone, was still in the Insect King stage.
'Aider couldn't endure it, but Shirone can?'
Nade had similar thoughts. Watching the Virtual Zone with an ice pack on
his neck, he said to Iruki,
"That kid Aider, he made it to Stage 5? Not bad for someone so
young."
"He's the youngest in the senior class. Maybe not in skill, but
definitely in talent."
The difficulty of the Insect King stage was incomparable to the Death
Scythe. Starting with Aider, students began to drop out one by one.
Canis grimaced as he sat up, and shortly after, Arin slowly opened her
eyes.
Unlike Aider, they calmly collected themselves.
"Canis, are you okay?"
"More or less. It's much harder than expected. The Spirit Zone
becomes too sensitive after Stage 4."
"There are too many insects secreting toxins. It's hard to maintain
equanimity when they're stinging your organs."
'Talking about equanimity... these guys are nuts.'
Arin's assessment was fitting for a mental specialist, but those who
dropped out before the Death Scythe stage were bewildered.
It was only natural that talking about toxins and equanimity while
insects were tearing through their intestines made them seem insane.
But that's why it's called a state of mastery.
The depth of this realm, which cannot be measured by numbers, is a
sensation that cannot be understood without reaching it.
'Everyone is amazing.'
While the dropouts gathered in the Virtual Zone, Maya remained in the
resting area.
She was the first to drop out, but her recovery was the slowest, and no
one paid attention to her.
She could understand the harshness of competition, but the fact that
they didn't even consider her a competitor made her sad.
She approached Shirone's friends. They were the only ones who showed her
any kindness in the senior class.
"Amy, are you okay? How's your mind?"
Hearing Maya's voice, Amy turned her head.
"Yeah. I think we're okay. How about you?"
"I'm feeling better now. I'm sorry. I dropped out at Stage
2."
Maya lowered her head, feeling ashamed. She felt sorry for not meeting
Shirone's expectations after he accepted her into the team.
"It's okay. Honestly, I was really shocked too. Don't worry too
much."
Amy comforted Maya. Knowing the pain of Stage 2, she couldn't blame
her.
"But... is Shirone okay? He hasn't come out yet."
Amy looked at the Virtual Zone. Everyone had dropped out at Stage 5, and
only two people were left lying there.
Shirone from the red team and Fermi from the blue team.
"At least the balance is maintained until the end. Whoever wins
there will win overall."
Maya looked at the vision floating in the air.
It was disgusting even from a distance, but seeing the screen up close
made her feel nauseous again.
'How can they endure that?'
Stage 2, Ice Prison, was terrifying enough for her. Yet someone was
fighting in such a horrific place.
'If it were me, I would have just died.'
Maya couldn't bear to watch the vision. But she didn't look away because
Shirone, who believed in her, was fighting.
"Th-there...!"
Boyle pointed at the vision with a horrified look.
The insects wriggled, and Shirone slowly stood up.
"Oh my god..."
Maya's face turned pale.
Other students were also shocked, staring at the vision with their
mouths open.
Shirone's appearance was unbearable to look at. Some parts of his body
were stripped to the bone, and other parts were swollen and rotting.
Maya couldn't believe that a human mind could endure such things.
With his strength completely drained, Shirone hung his head with his
arms limp.
Even as insects tore through his skin, he didn't move, and Alpheus's
lips curled into a smile.
"Impressive. Perfect equanimity."
It wasn't that he didn't feel pain or disgust. It was a superhuman state
of maintaining focus in any situation.
