Match Point (3)
"Fine. I'll give you one last chance. Defeat Savina and Closer
first, and then you can face me. I also defeated Boyle, so you won't complain, right? If you can beat
those two, I'll take on you then."
Closer and Savina cheered. This set the stage for Dante. The stage was
now set for Dante. Of course, they had no intention of losing to Shirone.
"No. There's no need for that."
Shirone rejected Dante's proposal.
"Hey, get a grip. You're the challenger here. If you want to face
me, you need to at least..."
"Why don't all three of you come at me at once? I want to get this
over with and not worry about it anymore."
"You madman…!"
Dante and his crew's eyes widened. Even at the Royal Magic Academy, they
had never received such an insult.
Nade, watching the situation, clicked his tongue.
"Shirone sure knows how to push people's buttons. I'd never want to
get into a verbal fight with him."
"He's got a real talent for pissing people off. It's not that he
doesn't do it, he just chooses not to."
Iruki, who had said this, voluntarily stepped into the tense scene. He
had been holding back because Shirone hadn't gotten involved, but he wasn't the type to enjoy being
ignored.
"Hey, Shirone. If it's something this fun, count me
in."
Dante glared fiercely at Iruki, who had barged in like an intruder. It
was clear that he was trying to force this feud into a team battle since he didn't think Shirone could
handle it alone.
"Iruki, this isn't your fight. I can see right through your
scheme."
"What are you talking about? This is right up my alley. I'll take
all of you on at once. If I calculate one second per person, it'll take exactly one
seconds."
Closer snorted.
"Idiot, if it's one second per person, it should be three
sec...."
He frowned as he realized the meaning behind Iruki's words.
"These guys really! Are you crazy? Who do you think we are, acting so
cocky!"
"If you're so confident, why don't you come at us one by one? The
conclusion is simple: win the fight, and that's it. What's so complicated about it? Right,
Dante?"
"You're misunderstanding something. It's not about wearing Shirone
down by making him fight up the ranks. It's about proving you're qualified to challenge me."
"We are well qualified. If you defeat Shirone, there will be no one who
will dare challenge you anymore."
Iruki pointed to the students observing the situation here.
"See? No need to overthink this. If you beat us, you're the best.
You'll have control of the advanced class. And that must be the special mission someone gave
you."
Iruki's words hit the mark. Having been at the Royal Magic Academy, he
likely understood Olivia's strategy.
Even if he didn't, he surely sensed that Olivia's involvement was the
reason for their aggressive behavior.
With the cards now on the table, Dante no longer hid his
intentions.
"Fine. Let's do a team battle. To avoid disrupting classes, we'll
have an unofficial match over the weekend. We'll have one person fight each week, and the first to win
two matches wins. If you lose two matches first, then there's no need for me to fight Shirone—I'll
automatically be the winner. Do you accept?"
"Fine. If you want to keep up appearances until the end, we'll do
it that way. But I'm going to win anyway, so you won't have to worry about avoiding a fight with
Shirone."
"Hmph, you talk big. In that case, add one more person to your
side. It doesn't matter who—bring Boyle if you want."
"That wouldn't be any fun. We have our own lineup, you
know."
Iruki turned his head. Nade pointed at himself with a bewildered
expression.
Helping Shirone wasn't the issue, but he hadn't expected to be dragged
into the battle. However, refusing now would dampen Shirone's momentum, so he had no choice but to go
along.
Dante tilted his head as Nade walked toward him.
"Nade? Are you sure about this?"
Based on what he'd observed, Nade didn't have impressive grades or
standout performance in practical evaluations.
Since Dante had been tasked with crushing Shirone, taking down everyone
associated with him seemed like a better option.
Nade, who arrived at the battlefield, whispered to Iruki.
"Hey, what are you thinking? What if I lose?"
"Just fill in the numbers. I'll take care of
everything."
While the two whispered, Dante went over to the teachers. Since they'd
been listening to the entire conversation from the start, there was no need for further
explanation.
"We'd like to settle this. I request that the Heaven's Trial
practice field be opened for us this weekend."
Sienna spoke as the representative.
"It's not our decision to make, but we'll consider it. Go back and
wait."
The students stopped their practice and waited anxiously. Shirone's
challenge was a huge event, but if the school didn't approve, the match would be canceled.
When all the dedicated teachers of Heaven's Trial gathered, Sienna
spoke.
"So, it has come to this. Should I say that it was bound to happen
at some point?"
Thad shook his head.
"I disagree. We can't let students judge each other's mistakes. We
should hold a disciplinary committee to announce the harm done to Pandora."
Ethella, who had been resting her chin on her hand and lost in thought,
said:
"Hmm, I partially agree with Teacher Thad. But calling it
'judgment' seems too harsh. Can't we see it as a form of self-regulation among the
students?"
"I'm not against the importance of autonomy, but this is a duel.
There's something strange about the way students fight each other with magic."
"Even so, Boyle and Dante have already settled their match. Many
students are dueling as we speak. I see opening Heaven's Trial on the weekend as no different from
holding supplementary classes."
Sienna thought that Thad's opinion was right. However, she also
understood Ethella's desire to leave it to Shirone. She knew that Thad's approach wouldn't resonate with
the school.
"Since opinions are divided, we need to address the practical
issue. If we refuse, Dante will likely appeal to Principal Olivia. What do you think her decision will
be?"
A sigh escaped from Thad's throat. Olivia would certainly approve it. He
couldn't fathom why, but it seemed this was her intention all along. Moreover, Alpheas had instructed
them to follow her lead without question. What was the principal thinking when he brought Olivia into
the academy?
"We are the teachers here. Are you suggesting we should trust
Shirone in this situation? Why, exactly?"
"Because Principal Olivia trusts Dante. And Principal Alpheas trusts
Shirone. In hindsight, this isn't just a problem between students."
Ethella said.
"I want to trust Shirone too. Just as I'm a teacher and a monk,
Shirone, though a student, embodies the spirit of a monk who seeks righteousness. Perhaps knowing this,
Principal Alpheas gave full authority to Principal Olivia?"
Thad was lost in thought. Thinking back now, it did seem odd. No matter
how extraordinary his mentor was, it seemed impossible to invite someone as esteemed as Jerome Olivia,
the kingdom's top teacher, to the academy.
There must have been a significant reason behind it.
'How is it that you cause more accidents than your disciples,
Master?'
Thad sighed and walked over to the students. Then he announced the
preliminary results regarding Dante's request.
"As a result of the emergency meeting, we've decided to approve the
opening of Heaven's Trial for the unofficial interpersonal combat training."
The students erupted in cheers, so loud that Thad's additional comment
about needing higher approval was completely drowned out.
Not that it needed to be mentioned anyway.
"Wow! This is a major event!"
"They're finally going to face off! Shirone and Dante! Dante and
Shirone! Woohoo!"
The students, unable to contain their excitement, jumped around
wildly.
In the midst of the uproar, Iruki tapped people's shoulders one by one,
pointing to himself.
"Hey, it's not just Shirone who fights, I fight too."
"Waaaah! Iruki! Did you hear? Shirone and Dante are going to face
off!"
"…."
News of the confrontation spread throughout the academy through word of
mouth from students.
In the shadowy circles of research groups, flyers detailing information
about the two contenders were already being distributed, fueling speculation.
The teachers' conference room was also buzzing with discussions. The
entire faculty was debating how to handle the situation.
"I'll say it again: this is dangerous. Allowing students to settle
personal grievances through physical conflict goes against our policies."
Olivia didn't even snort at Thad's words.
"It's not about fighting; it's about determining who's the best.
After all, most of them will be in the graduation class next year or the year after. You can see it as
an intensification of competition."
"But, Principal! If this continues, the academy's
reputation..."
Olivia raised her hand as if to signal that he'd said enough.
"The battle between Shirone and Dante will be an event that will draw
the attention of the entire kingdom. I will also personally suggest to the Teachers' Association that
they send a special correspondent."
The conference room became quiet. Having the inspector of the Teachers'
Association personally call for a special correspondent meant that Alpheas Magic Academy would gain
considerable attention. Furthermore, if it was published in an academic journal with Dante, the star of
the kingdom, at the forefront, it would be a great privilege for the academy.
Sweet visions danced in the minds of the teachers—of Alpheas Magic
Academy becoming the kingdom's premier institution. The best teacher there to guide students (that is
me).
Meanwhile, Thad was in a foul mood. While he always supported the
academy's rising fame, he disliked how it was being used to silence the teachers.
"Principal Olivia, may I say something?"
"Thad, proceed as planned."
"Yes, let's proceed as is… Huh?"
Thad turned his head, his face flushed.
Alpheas was standing at the back door. Given the gravity of the
situation, even Olivia hadn't dismissed him this time.
"As expected, Teacher Alpheas has great foresight. This event will bring
tremendous merit to the academy; it would be foolish to let it slip by."
"I wonder if it'll work out? Thanks to you, I gain fame in my later
years. Hahahaha!"
"Once the duel is over, there will be widespread reports of Dante's
victory. The child named Shirone will be deeply hurt, but seeing you so gleeful almost makes me pity
him."
"Hahaha! Both children attend Alpheas Magic Academy, so they are
essentially my students. But I also believe that the best must be determined."
Olivia's eyes narrowed. She was irritated by how Alpheas countered every
word she said.
This time, even Alpheas' gaze was fierce. The two teachers, who had
spent their lives in education, were now confronting each other with their philosophies, both having
found students who could mark the culmination of their careers.
An hour later, the proposal for the unofficial duel was formally
approved.
Magic Clash (1)
The students eagerly awaited the upcoming weekend.
This was the first event in nearly ten years at Alpheas Magic Academy
that had created such a stir.
Rumors spread that even a special correspondent from the Teachers'
Association would be sent to witness the duel between Shirone and Dante.
The boys joked about pulling off outrageous pranks to make their mark on
the world, while the girls fretted over what to wear.
Iruki and Nade headed to their research group. As they walked the short
distance, they heard countless stories, though they couldn't tell which were true or false. However,
none of the stories were about them.
"I didn't expect much from the beginning, but now that it's
happening, it feels a bit deflating. Are we just sideshows?"
"At least the correspondent is coming. Um... do you think it would
be better if I went first?"
Nade had been worried since the day the match was approved.
With Closer eyeing Iruki, it was likely that his opponent would be
Victor Savina.
A fearsome woman who accelerates with Haste and sprays Wind Cutters in
all directions.
Excluding the graduating class, she was a student who could be ranked in
the top 20 in the kingdom, so it was clear that it would be a tough fight regardless of the
matchup.
