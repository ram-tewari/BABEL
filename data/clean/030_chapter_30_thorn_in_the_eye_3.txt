Chapter 30:
Thorn in the Eye (3)
The classroom stirred. Class Five? This wasn't just an early promotion but a jump of two
levels at once. This was a radical decision not only from the students' perspective but also from the
school's.
"Teacher! I object. How can Shirone skip two classes? Shirone can't even use a
single magic spell."
This was news to Siina. Etela hadn't mentioned this either. Perhaps she didn't know
either, as there was no particular need to use magic in lower class lessons.
"Is that true, Shirone?"
The classmates turned to look at Shirone. Their eyes seemed to say:
Please say you can't. Say you can't.
"Yes. I don't have any magic I can use yet."
"Hmm."
Even so, there was no problem with the early promotion. Magic is taught in earnest from
the upper classes, and the lower class curriculum focuses on strengthening the Spirit Zone.
Shirone has mastered the basics of the Four Direction Formation, and the size and
durability of his Spirit Zone don't lag behind even compared to the upper classes. Therefore, Class Five is
appropriate if we talk about the exact class.
But it was unusual that he still couldn't use magic. Students typically try magic on
their own, even without being taught. After all, they entered the magic school because they desperately
wanted to learn magic.
"The teacher is also curious. However, there are no evaluation items related to
magic up to Class Six. Shirone's early promotion will proceed normally."
"I absolutely cannot accept this!"
Mark, the implicit leader of Class Seven, stood up abruptly. He was a student with a
square jaw fitting his large build.
"Why are you showing favoritism to a student? Please provide convincing evidence.
Teacher, you said that omniscience is as important as omnipotence! Applying special conditions only to
Shirone is incomprehensible from a common-sense perspective!"
"That's right! Honestly, if I competed with Shirone on grades right now, I'm
confident I would win! Shirone can't even keep up with theory lessons properly!"
"If Shirone is promoted early, I'll petition the student council to hold a
hearing!"
The students' resistance was much fiercer than expected. Though young, they had all risen
through competition since Class Ten. I had hoped they would be stimulated by Shirone this time as well, but
they were burning with hostility as if facing an enemy.
'There's something I don't know about.'
From the children's expressions, which suggested they would even go to war, Siina
realized the situation was serious. With this much resistance, it could actually have a negative impact on
the class atmosphere.
"The decision hasn't been formally made yet. The exact announcement will be made
after the teachers' meeting. That concludes today's class."
Back in the staff room, Siina rested her chin on her hand, deep in thought.
Given the nature of a specialized school, competition is inevitable. And the students
knew this fact and voluntarily enrolled.
'What is it? Those strange looks from the children?'
It wasn't simple jealousy. Early promotions had happened before, but never with such
strong opposition.
"Excuse me, Teacher Siina."
A female student named Maria came to see Siina.
The oldest student in Class Seven at 19, she had been stuck in place for 4 years without
promotion.
She wasn't completely without talent, but she was timid and fearful, making her a student
that the school watched with concern.
"Yes? What is it, Maria?"
"Actually... I have something to tell you."
After hesitating for a while, Maria spilled everything. How Shirone was being treated in
the current class, and why the children were so angry just now.
As the story progressed, Siina's brow furrowed. They were bullying Shirone with methods
too malicious to be seen as simple emotional conflicts.
If they encountered Shirone, they would pretend to vomit as if by agreement, and often
deliberately crowded around Shirone to talk amongst themselves, excluding him.
Maria finished her story, sobbing.
"Now I'm the oldest so they don't care about me, but I've actually been through it
too. Back then, I couldn't focus on studying or anything. I think Shirone is amazing. How can he remain so
calm in that situation?"
Siina understood the whole situation. One student was being ostracized by the group. If
fair competition wasn't taking place, there was no reason for the school to exist.
"Thank you for telling me. From now on, the teacher will handle it."
Shirone arrived at his dormitory around midnight. Since the bullying had intensified
after the early promotion talk, he thought it would be better to come in early and read books.
Knock, knock.
Someone knocked on his door. Checking the time, it was already past 1 AM.
'Who could it be at this hour?'
Students couldn't go out, and staff had gone home. Could it be the children coming to
harass him even here?
Feeling anxious, he couldn't approach the door readily, but then he heard a familiar
voice from beyond the door.
"Shirone, are you sleeping?"
"Teacher Siina?"
Shirone hurriedly opened the door. It really was Siina.
"May I come in?"
"Ah, yes. Please come in."
Was today Teacher Siina's duty day? Her hair was still damp, as if she had just bathed in
the dormitory.
The advanced class dormitory housed students even in their early twenties, so it wasn't a
place for a female teacher to wander late at night. But Siina had chosen this time to have a private meeting
away from the classmates.
