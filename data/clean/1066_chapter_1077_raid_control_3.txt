Chapter 1077:
Raid Control (3)
As the light faded from Ergos's eye and its legs withdrew from the cave
walls, the ground shook with its fall. The creatures, losing control, began to scatter in all
directions.
"Screech! Screech!"
Without receiving attack commands, they collided with each other and
quickly disappeared.
"Phew."
Taking a deep breath, Shirone saw a rotating, translucent box in front
of Ergos's machine.
It hadn't materialized because the priority for item acquisition hadn't
been determined yet.
'Michelangelo.'
Though he didn't know much about High Gear, he could sense that this was
the item he wanted.
Just as he reached for the ornate box, Mafia's group rushed over.
"Wait! Wait a minute!"
DexGamsung approached Shirone, looking at the box in disbelief.
"It's real. It's really a legendary grade."
Though probability is always a factor, getting it on the first try was
unbelievable.
'The only legendary item Ergos drops is Michelangelo. If we sell this,
the galaxies…'
Zeros raced through his mind.
'The West Country Free Pass is good, but Michelangelo is far more
valuable. If we get this…'
Catching Shirone's gaze, he cleared his throat.
"Ahem, the principle is to distribute galaxies earned from party
hunting equally. But items can't be divided, so they don't materialize until the priority is
set."
"Priority?"
"There are two ways. The user can designate it directly, or leave
it to chance. Usually, it's the former, but if trust is low, we draw cards. The highest number gets
priority, and if they forfeit, it goes to the next."
"Then designate me as number one. If you help me get Donatello too,
I'll give you the West Country Free Pass."
Though that was the initial agreement, Mafia's group couldn't help but
frown.
'We didn't expect a legendary grade to actually drop.'
They had seen Yahweh2's abilities but didn't think he could control
probability too.
"Why? Don't you want to give it now?"
Saying it outright made it hard to hide their reluctance.
Sona gestured in frustration.
"No, but come on! This is a huge jackpot. Isn't it a bit selfish to
take it all? We'd compromise if it were reasonable."
Mafia added, "Ergos was Type C, a chase pattern, so you could catch
it. You can see bullets and jump through space. If it were Type B, a defense form, your attacks wouldn't
have worked."
"So what do you want? I need Michelangelo. What do you want?
Galaxies?"
Fermi would buy it at any cost.
"Add galaxies to the West Country Free Pass. If Donatello drops,
we'll settle that with galaxies too."
It was unlikely to drop again.
"Alright. Let me get Michelangelo first. I'll contact Golden Wheel
outside."
Sona said, "No. We'll hold it and settle it later. Golden Wheel
might refuse, and you might take it and run to East Country."
"That applies to anyone."
Mafia sat down defiantly.
"We won't compromise. Choose wisely. Without setting priority, you
can't leave until you disconnect."
Their persistence made Shirone abandon the straightforward
approach.
"Fine, let's decide by chance. Whoever gets it will be determined
by probability."
"Huh?"
Mafia's group looked surprised.
"Why? It's fair, right? The highest card gets
priority."
"That's true, but…"
It was seven against one.
'Is he serious? Only one of us needs to get the highest card. Anyone
with a higher card than Yahweh2 will do.'
Mafia stood up.
"Accepted. It's reasonable. Everyone agrees?"
They all nodded.
"Yahweh2, touch the box. When the method appears, choose
random."
They thought he wouldn't get the item, but Shirone followed their
words.
Choosing random, augmented reality displayed cards, distributing one to
each member.
"Let's reveal."
They opened their cards, and Sona clenched his fist in triumph.
"Yes! A king!"
"Wow, high card. Congrats."
His comrades couldn't fully celebrate, not entirely trusting
Sona.
'This is intense.'
Shirone stared at the blinking card in front of him.
"What are you waiting for? Open it. We don't have
time."
In his focused state, Shirone's senses sharpened, and the truth of this
world flowed in.
'Here.'
At the point chosen by his quantum signal, he opened the card, and the
atmosphere chilled.
"An ace?"
Mafia's group leaned in, unable to believe their eyes.
"How… an ace? This is an ace…"
Knowing he still needed them, Shirone feigned a cheerful smile.
"Haha! I'm really lucky today. Maybe I'll get Donatello
too."
A system message appeared.
Item acquisition priority determined. 1st: Yahweh2, 2nd:
Sona, 3rd: …
"Acquire."
As the box materialized, a wooden gauntlet-shaped part landed in
Shirone's hand.
'Wood?'
It was a unique material in High Gear.
"You! That…"
As Sona began to speak, the cave brightened, enveloped in a pale
glow.
Event concluded.
When his vision returned, Shirone found himself back at the entrance to
Ergos's sanctuary.
Sona quickly spoke up, "Don't equip that! Once you equip it, you
can't trade it!"
It was a characteristic of legendary items.
"Equip."
"Oh…"
As Shirone chose to equip it, Michelangelo cracked and emitted a bright
light.
With a pop, the form shattered, and particles of light enveloped
Yahweh2's right arm.
"What is this?"
As the light faded, a metallic gauntlet with an ivory sheen replaced his
right hand.
Sona clutched his head.
'Oh no, once it's bound, it won't drop as loot. Is he really going to
run?'
It wasn't uncommon for users to kill others and take their loot when a
good item dropped.
"But how do you use this?"
Even such an innocent question now seemed like he was mocking
them.
"Ugh, a legendary item at level 100. You should equip it when
you're a ranker. What if you get assassinated and lose it?"
"Then there's nothing I can do. Anyway, this…"
As Shirone extended Michelangelo, Mafia's group approached with
interest.
It was their first time seeing one in person.
"Legendary items have common traits. They're not in metallic form
when acquired. Once equipped, they can't be traded. And they perform independently of
output."
Shirone nodded.
'Independent of output. That's why Fermi was obsessed with legendary
items.'
"And Michelangelo's function is…"
Holding Michelangelo with both hands, Sona examined it.
There was an engraved lens in the palm.
"Ah, this must be it. I've heard Michelangelo is a photon
regulator. It has destructive power with light."
Shirone's eyes narrowed.
'A photon cannon.'
Following the explanation, Shirone activated the part with his palm up,
and the lens lit up.
"Huh? Whoa?"
Mafia's group quickly stepped back, watching the light gather above his
palm.
"Wow."
The moving light was fascinating but didn't seem particularly
threatening yet.
Sona pointed at the light.
"This is difficult. Like all legendary items, it doesn't generate
power on its own but manipulates something. Skill is more important than level."
That's why it ignores output.
"Hmm, I see."
As Shirone shook the light, getting a feel for it, Mafia clicked his
tongue.
"That's why I said not to equip it. Now you can't hand it over to
the guild. Still, we need to settle…"
At that moment, everyone's eyes widened.
'So that's how it works.'
Recalling the feel of the photon cannon, the light quickly compressed
into a sphere.
"What's that?"
The fierce light looked ready to explode.
"I compressed the light. I don't know the principle in High Gear,
but anyway, like this…"
As Shirone aimed Michelangelo with a feeling of release, a flash struck
a distant rock.
Boom!
With a deafening roar, a shockwave of wind revealed a cratered
ground.
"Oh my."
Everyone's jaws dropped.
"Light explodes? That's a missile!"
The precision of compression would affect the power, but having no ammo
constraints was a huge advantage.
Shirone clenched his fist.
'Got it. One down.'
It was a blade to pierce the Operator.
As Mafia's group still stared at the destroyed spot, they quickly
adjusted their expressions.
"Anyway, congratulations. Your skill is undeniable. Now, let's
settle. Can you contact Golden Wheel?"
"No. We'll settle after finding Donatello. Let's move to the next
hunting ground quickly."
DexGamsung shouted, "Hey! What do you mean? We don't know when
Donatello will drop. Are you saying we should keep going until then?"
"That was the agreement from the start. You've broken everything so
far, so at least keep this. Otherwise, I won't settle either."
"Ugh!"
Now, Mafia's group was at a disadvantage.
'Damn! We're stuck. If he runs to East Country, we get nothing.'
Sona asked with a tearful expression, "Will you really settle if we
find Donatello? Promise here and now, okay?"
'They break promises like it's nothing.'
Though grumbling inside, Shirone had already gained the upper
hand.
'I don't want to clash with other West Country users.'
Having made his decision, Shirone equipped Booster from the selective
options.
"Alright. Let's head back to Democracy. I'll wait at the Mid Gear
station."
Activating Booster, the rocket engine ignited, and Yahweh2's machine
shot into the sky.
"…He's gone."
As he left without looking back, they realized the reality with sinking
hearts.
"Damn! I thought he was naive, but he's ruthless. Wait, did he run
away?"
DexGamsung gritted his teeth.
"He said he'd wait at Mid Gear, so we have to trust him. Even
settling Michelangelo can upgrade all our parts. We can't give up here."
"Really? Even with Donatello?"
As sighs escaped from all around, Sona activated Booster.
"Let's go. Our output is higher, so we won't lose him if we chase
now."
Everyone took off at full speed.
Rumble!
Looking back at the noise, Shirone saw Mafia's group following from a
distance.
'They should have been more honest.'
He stuck out his tongue playfully.
