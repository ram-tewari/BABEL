Chapter 32:
Instant Movement (2)
The relentless argument made the older teachers frown. Yet, they remained silent because
their ranks were higher.
Siena and Sad, both in their mid-twenties and recognized as 6th-class mages, along with
Etela, were the pride of Alpheas Magic School.
The debate showed no signs of cooling down. Etela stepped in to mediate, but it was like
fire and ice clashing, with neither side willing to yield.
Alpheus merely smiled. Without passion for education, they wouldn't be fighting like
this. It was just that their passions were directed differently.
Sad wanted the school's development, while Siena wanted the student's growth. As
teachers, they should side with Siena, but as an alumnus, Sad's feelings were understandable.
"I've heard both opinions clearly. I understand what you're saying, so let's stop here
and take your seats."
As soon as Alpheus spoke, Siena and Sad stopped arguing as if it were a lie. Their only
common ground was their respect for Alpheus.
"From what I've heard, both sides have valid points. Teacher Siena's argument is
logical. But we are teachers. Shouldn't we also avoid letting prejudice take root in the children's hearts?"
"That's exactly what I meant, Principal."
Sad smiled, while Siena remained expressionless. But Alpheus had no intention of ending
it there. After pondering, he smiled and proposed an idea.
"So… how about this method?"
***
A notice was posted on the senior class building's bulletin board.
Dozens of students gathered in front of the board and were shocked by the contents.
It was an unprecedented event in Alpheas Magic School's history. All members of Class
Seven would undergo an advancement evaluation.
The main points of the notice were as follows:
All Class Seven students would be given the opportunity for early advancement. The
advancement candidate would be one person, and upon passing the test, they would be promoted to Class Five.
Acknowledging the students' struggles, the evaluation would unusually be scored based on magic, not the
Spirit Zone.
The evaluation subject was instant movement.
"I-instant movement? I don't know how to do it. Do you?"
"No, of course not. It's a Class Five official designated magic."
"I tried it once. But it was so scary I couldn't bring myself to try again."
"Idiot! It's easy if you follow the formula. The problem is applying it."
The murmuring voices of the children suddenly quieted. Shironé was walking over. Under
the barrage of countless stares, he arrived at the bulletin board and read the notice. The conclusion from
the faculty meeting was a direct showdown among the students.
'Instant movement.'
Shironé engraved it firmly in his mind.
***
The so-called Class Seven bullying incident was also of interest to the senior class
students. Especially since the target was Shironé, there was much talk.
"Oh my! There was such a thing? Aymi, did you know?"
Aymi, who only checked the bulletin board at the end of the day, read the notice in
disbelief. When Seriel shared the specifics she'd heard from rumors, Aymi's fists trembled.
'He was excluded? Because of me?'
The classmates didn't even make eye contact with Shironé. Moreover, during integrated
classes, some even openly cursed when Aymi exchanged greetings with him.
"What do you think? Well, Class Seven has younger kids, so… I've had similar experiences,
but I advanced quickly, so it wasn't a big deal."
"Being young isn't an excuse. By that logic, aren't we young too?"
"Anyway, we're in Class Four. Magic involves a lot of mental aspects, so age shouldn't
matter."
Aymi didn't want to argue. Her mind was a mess, and she couldn't think straight. She was
just angry that Shironé had gone through this.
"Idiot! Why didn't you say anything?"
If he'd been honest and discussed it, they could've found a better solution. No, they
definitely could have. Regardless of popularity, she was Class Four's top student. The thought of trashy
kids giving him the cold eye for being with her made her furious, as if it had happened to her.
"I need to see Shironé."
"Going is fine, but what do you plan to do?"
"First, I'll hit him. Then I'll gather all the Class Seven kids. Since we're dating,
that's fine, right?"
"What? Are you crazy? Absolutely not!"
"Why not? Should I just leave it like this? It happened because of me!"
"Ugh, you really don't understand men. Why do you think Shironé didn't say anything? It's
because his pride was hurt. If you go and stir things up, what then?"
Aymi couldn't understand. The reason he hadn't even made eye contact was because he was
scared of Class Seven kids?
If anything, Aymi's pride was the one hurt.
"Men, you see, love to be acknowledged. They live and die by their pride. So if they're
being excluded, would they want to tell the girl they like?"
Seriel's words made sense, but only if they were truly in love. Even if it was a fake
relationship, not saying anything—how should she interpret that?
'He probably thinks he stepped in shit because of me. That's why he's avoiding me.'
Seriel viewed the school's response positively.
"Isn't it a relief? If he passes the advancement test, he can join Class Five. The
instant movement test is a really good choice. The Class Seven kids have to learn from scratch too."
Aymi thought differently. There was a difference between not having learned a specific
spell and not having learned magic at all.
And instant movement, of all things?
The most important thing when learning instant movement is overcoming fear. The speed is
on a different level from flight magic, and if you collide with an obstacle, you could lose your life.
"Thinking about it, this is an incredibly dangerous test. What are the teachers
thinking?"
"They'll adjust the difficulty appropriately. They might also be testing the students'
passion. Over half of Class Seven is apparently taking the test."
"What? So many, even though they've never learned it?"
"The reward is Class Five. Plus, everyone's learning from scratch. I'd have tried it
too."
Aymi couldn't shake her unease. The students taking the test would likely receive help
from seniors or dedicated teachers for intensive practice.
Meanwhile, Shironé had no such luxury. A dedicated teacher was unthinkable, and he
wouldn't even have friends to ask for advice.
'What if he really fails?'
She acknowledged Shironé's talent, but in the short term, this test didn't guarantee his
success.
"Right! Aymi, why don't you help Shironé? You could reconcile with him too."
"Hmph! After what happened, he didn't even consult me, and now he'll accept my help? I'll
just keep pretending not to know."
"Ugh, you two are really something. If you like each other, that's all that matters. Why
make it complicated?"
"Mind your own business. This is how we do things. Anyway, I'm not exactly free either.
The graduation class advancement test is right around the corner."
"Oh, right! That's true. Sorry."
For students, nothing is as important as advancement. Even Aymi wouldn't have the energy
to focus elsewhere with the high barrier of the graduation class looming.
"He'll figure it out. If he can't handle this much, he doesn't deserve the title of my
boyfriend."
As Aymi turned away from the bulletin board, she added silently:
'Even if it's a fake relationship.'
***
Midnight approached.
A masked figure infiltrated the boys' dormitory garden. With movements reminiscent of a
cat, their footsteps made no sound, not even rustling the grass. Noticing a light on the seventh floor, they
scratched their head irritably.
'This is absolutely not because I'm worried. I'm helping because I share some
responsibility!'
Aymi gritted her teeth. Even if she tried to ignore it, Shironé was on her mind, making
it impossible to focus on her studies.
"Just wait. I'll make you beg for forgiveness."
Aymi scaled the wall to the seventh floor. If rumors spread of her crossing the boys'
dormitory threshold at night, she wouldn't be able to show her face, but her childhood mischievousness
hadn't faded.
'Hehe, since it's come to this, I'll scare him out of his wits.'
Aymi rolled forward as soon as she opened the window. Mid-landing, her plan formed:
subdue Shironé, throw him onto the bed, then twist his arm hard enough to make him scream.
"Huh?"
But Aymi couldn't move a step. Shironé was staring at her with wide eyes. And beside him,
Teacher Siena sat in a chair.
"T-teacher?"
"You're Aymi?"
Adjusting her glasses, Siena looked closer. The red hair and distinct double eyelids
confirmed it was Aymi.
She knew Aymi and Shironé were dating, but she thought they'd been focusing on their
studies lately. Seeing this, their behavior was suspicious, to say the least.
"Don't tell me you've been meeting like this all along? Pretending not to know each other
during the day and having secret rendezvous at night?"
"T-that's a huge misunderstanding, teacher! A really, really big one!"
Siena hurriedly covered her mouth.
"Shh! The next room might hear."
Aymi glanced nervously at the neighboring room. This was the boys' dormitory, after all.
If rumors spread of a girl's voice in Shironé's room, she'd be the prime suspect. Not that she had any
excuses in this situation.
"Wait a minute! Teacher, what are you doing here? At this hour?"
Now that she thought about it, Siena was the most out of place here. There was no reason
for a teacher to visit in casual clothes at night when she could've just summoned him.
"Tell me. Why are you here at this hour?"
At Aymi's justified suspicion, Siena's cheeks flushed. But she quickly regained her
composure and spoke coldly.
"I came to give advice about the advancement test. Given the circumstances, it's better
not to draw attention."
If the advancement test was the reason, Aymi had no retort. She'd come for the same
thing.
"Um, senior."
Shironé handed Aymi a teacup, prepared for guests.
"Have some tea first…"
"Hmph."
Aymi took the cup and savored the aroma. As expected of a polite junior, his hospitality
was impeccable.
"…Yeah, right! This whole mess is because of you!"
"I-I'm sorry!"
Shironé's apology only made Aymi angrier. His overly gentle nature was probably why even
the younger kids looked down on him.
"Let's sit first. I want to finish what we were discussing."
Siena offered a seat. Aymi sat beside Shironé and turned away sharply. She'd come
because she was worried, but she was still mad at him.
"As you both know, the test's evaluation subject is instant movement. It's the
principal's direct decision."
"The principal?"
Aymi finally understood. A test unfit for Class Seven made sense if it was the
principal's idea.
"Yes. Honestly, I'm not sure of his intentions. Instant movement is too dangerous for
lower classes."
Aymi asked, "The test will probably be on the 'Uncrossable Bridge,' right?"
"It's not confirmed, but there's no other place."
