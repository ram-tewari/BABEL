Chapter 270:
Terraje's Bloodline (2)
Uorin glanced at the wall clock, then hastily set the cat down and stood
up from her seat.
"Oh? It's time to eat. Brother, let's go."
Setting down his teacup, Zion stood up and leaned his face close to
Shirone's.
"Haha! Biological parents? Sure, that's important. But do you know
this? Once parents have abandoned their child once, what's stopping them from doing it
again?"
Shirone didn't even snort in response.
"Let me be clear, I expect nothing. The only reason I'm here is to
meet my biological parents."
"Haha! Biological parents? Yeah, that's important. But you know what? If
a parent abandoned their child once, wouldn't they be able to abandon them a second time?"
Shirone frowned, unable to understand.
"What does that mean?"
"How pathetic. Blood ties mean nothing in the face of power. You don't
honestly think Father suddenly looked for you without a reason, do you?"
Shirone had anticipated that there must be a reason behind being sought
out after 18 years, but hearing it directly from Zion still came as a shock.
Uorin patted Zion's shoulder.
"Good grief! I told you, we need to go. We'll be in trouble if
we're late."
Zion threw a sneering glance at Shirone before being dragged out by his
sister.
After leaving Shirone alone in the room, Zion finally turned around and
clicked his tongue.
"Tch! What an annoying guy. Especially his eyes—I can't stand them. Is
it because he grew up like some commoner? Instead of quivering in fear, he dares to talk
back."
Uorin tried to soothe him.
"Don't be like that. I, for one, am glad to have a mage as my
brother. Besides, he's no threat to you. As long as Mother is around, Kazra will be yours."
"Who doesn't know that? I just hate the way he acts. I can't even stand
calling him Father. Orkamph, that bastard. When I become king, I'll make sure he's gone."
Uorin pouted her lips.
In any kingdom, the idea of a son killing his father is considered the
height of disgrace. And here they were, in the royal castle no less. If those words were overheard, it
would turn the kingdom upside down.
Yet, that was the extent of her reaction.
After all, they were Terraje's children.

The food laid out on the royal dining table was more extravagant than
anything a commoner could even dream of.
Shirone couldn't quite grasp why so much money needed to be spent on
daily meals, but the reality was that, even if the food wasn't eaten, extravagance was considered a
royal virtue.
Royalty must surpass nobles, even in the smallest matters.
As long as the nobles indulged in all sorts of luxuries, the royal
family's absurdly excessive spending would never change.
Shirone's group was seated at the lowest end of the table. Vincent,
Olena, Reina, and Amy all had pale faces.
Never in their wildest dreams did they imagine they'd actually be
invited to dine at the king's table.
It might even be the first time in the hundred-year history of Kazra
Kingdom.
As such, their unease only grew, and they could barely tell how the food
was even passing down their throats.
The atmosphere at the dining table was as cold and tense as it could
be.
Orkamph's pride was hurt by the breach of protocol, and both Shirone and
Zion were still feeling the tension from their earlier conversation.
However, Eliza seemed to be in a rather good mood. She was pleased with
the outfit she had chosen in the dressing room. She was wearing a dress adorned with mink fur on the
sleeves and accessorized with diamonds.
Eliza, who had been daintily picking at her food as if trying to break
it down into molecular pieces, suddenly set down her utensils and flashed a bright smile.
"We should decorate Shirone's room—make it elegant and grand. Since
you have golden hair like your father, I think a navy-blue suit would suit you. Oh, and we'll need a
carriage as well. It looks like I'll be quite busy for a while."
Olena's face darkened. None of those things were things she could
provide.
Shirone, as if he had made up his mind, began to speak.
"I don't need any of that. Everything my parents have done for me until
now is more than enough."
"Huhu, my son is quite frugal. But you must remember, there's a certain
dignity expected of royalty. You can't live as you did before. Now, let's see... Yes, gold suits you. Or
maybe sapphire?"
"Whether it's gold or jewels, that's not what's important. The parents
here have given me their best. That kind of love is something I won't find anywhere else."
Eliza frowned. What could be more important than gold and jewels? It was
their role to enjoy the most luxurious wealth in the kingdom. It wasn't a privilege but an
obligation.
She found it disappointing that her son was reciting tales of his
caretaker's love when he should be learning about royal duties as the future First Prince.
No, perhaps Shirone was a bit too affectionate. Maybe that's why he
couldn't bring himself to say he'd live independently in front of his caretaker.
Eliza turned at Shirone's parents.
"Was it Vincent?"
Vincent quickly set down his utensils.
"Ah, yes! My name is Arian Vincent!"
"You shall be compensated accordingly. You've cared for our son so
well; you deserve a fitting reward. It would be too cumbersome to give you gold. Dear, how about
granting them some land in Kosent Region?"
Orkamph responded with a disinterested tone.
"That sounds reasonable."
Fury surged within Shirone. He had come to the palace just as they
wished, and Vincent and Olena had been anxious, terrified of losing their son. In this situation, the
only gift Shirone could offer them was the pride of having raised their child well.
He had hoped that these esteemed individuals, with their royal titles,
would at least say a simple "Thank you for raising our son." How could it be so hard for them to utter
those words?
"Wait a moment. Why do you keep talking like this? They raised me
for 18 years. They're the parents who taught me how to live from childhood until now, in place of my
biological parents!"
Eliza's expression turned fierce. Yes, she felt sorry for having
abandoned Shirone. But his constant siding with those "shallow" people was getting on her nerves.
"Is it that you think the reward is too little? Then, how about we
add a small castle to the land…"
Shirone's voice grew louder.
"That's not what I mean! Why can't you just say thank you, even
once?"
Eliza made a blank expression. At first, she wondered if something might
be wrong with Shirone's mind. Why should a royal family member express gratitude to commoners?
Zion spoke with a displeased expression.
"What are you doing, behaving so indecently? Do you think this is
some commoner's dinner table where you used to live?"
Shirone glared at Zion. Anger welled up inside him, but Zion did have a
point. If he pushed too hard in a gathering of royalty, the situation could take a strange turn.
Uorin quickly intervened to defuse the tension.
"Easy, now. It's because brother Shirone is such a kind person. I think
brother values emotions over material things. To him, any compensation without emotion is nothing more
than a mere exchange of objects."
In that moment, Shirone even saw Uorin as a truly lovable little sister.
He was immensely grateful to her for speaking on his behalf.
Uorin met Shirone's eyes and winked. Then she turned to Vincent and
Olena.
"I'm sorry if we were too harsh earlier. And thank you so much for
raising our brother into such a wonderful person."
"N- No, not at all. How could I…"
Vincent trembled with humility. Never in his life had he imagined he'd
receive gratitude from royalty.
Shirone's expression softened a bit as well. He began to feel guilty,
wondering if he had misunderstood Uorin all this time.
On the other hand, Reina was taken aback. Uorin was a princess of
Terraje Family, and in the Kashan Empire, the throne was traditionally passed down to women bearing the
Terraje surname.
While Zion's limit would be ruling over a single kingdom, Uorin, if she
surpassed a few competitors, could ascend to the throne of the Kashan Empire and wield control over the
entire continent. So, in terms of actual power, Uorin would undoubtedly surpass Kazra's politics.
For someone with that kind of power to express gratitude to a commoner
was astonishing, and from another perspective, it hinted at a strong potential for being an absolute
ruler.
As Reina had anticipated, Uorin's words wielded immense influence. With
her recognizing and honoring Vincent and Olena, even Orkamph, who had remained silent until now, had to
show his support.
"Perhaps I was thinking too much from the royal family's perspective.
Indeed, we owe you a debt of gratitude."
Vincent once again bowed his head.
"N- No, it's nothing. We only did what was right."
Meanwhile, a hint of anger subtly crept into Olena's eyes. A woman's
intuition could tell. Uorin's words were sincere, but Orkamph's were not.
It was true that she hadn't given birth to Shirone. But over 18 years of
raising him, he had filled her heart with a love stronger than any blood connection.
If it meant Shirone's happiness, she could let him go, even if he chose
to leave with his biological parents. However, despite the appearance of the most privileged parents one
could imagine, she couldn't help but feel upset.
"We don't need money. We only wish for Shirone to live
happily."
Shirone turned to look at Olena, surprised.
Unlike how she had seemed intimidated by the royal family earlier, there
was now a quiet determination in her voice.
Eliza, who was always keenly aware of such nuances, didn't miss the
shift in Olena's tone. Her expression twisted into a deep frown. While she could tolerate Shirone, she
had no patience for a mere caretaker acting so audaciously.
Despite this, she couldn't dismiss Olena outright, as Uorin's words
still held considerable influence.
From Eliza's perspective, although she loathed Terraje, the woman who
had stolen her husband, she wasn't foolish enough to act recklessly, disregarding the clear difference
in power. In the end, all she could do was take her small, subtle revenge through her condescending
tone.
"Even so, take it. I don't want you to have second thoughts
later."
Olena didn't affirm or deny the offer, merely lowering her head.
However, her gaze remained fixed directly on Eliza.
Realizing that things could escalate to the worst possible outcome,
Shirone decided to bring up the real issue at this point.
"Actually, there's something I wish to say."
Perhaps sensing the need to change the atmosphere, Eliza shifted her
tone so quickly that it was almost awkward, smiling with her eyes.
"Go ahead, say anything. Whatever it is, Mother will
listen."
Hearing this, Olena felt even more offended. Eliza's words carried a
sneering implication that no matter how much Olena pretended to be a mother, Shirone was, ultimately,
royalty.
"I have no intention of becoming the heir to the Kazra Kingdom.
I've already discussed this with Zion. So please, let me stay for a while and then return to
Thormia."
The sound of utensils clattering ceased at once. Orkamph's face grew
grave, and Elia looked as if she were in shock, her mouth hanging open.
"What do you mean, Shirone? This is your home now. There's no need
to return to such a shabby place."
"I want to go back. At the very least, please allow me to graduate
from my current academy."
For now, Shirone brought up the magic academy as a reason. Truthfully,
he wanted to leave the palace immediately, but doing so could put his family in danger.
"No! That's absolutely unacceptable! You are a prince, carrying the
blood of the royal family! There are plenty of magic academies here in Kazra! I'll introduce you to the
best mentors!"
"I'll be taking my graduation exam next year. This is the most
crucial time for me. Please, just give me one year."
Of course, he had no intention of returning in a year. If he managed to
leave safely, he planned to graduate from the magic academy and then escape with his parents.
Once again, Uorin stood up for Shirone.
"I can understand brother Shirone's feelings. He must have friends
at the academy. Building a network is important, too."
"Connections with nobles? You should be associating with
royalty!"
Eliza was adamantly opposed. How could she let go of the son she had
finally found? More than anything, her pride was deeply wounded by the idea that she could be outdone by
those lowly people claiming to be Shirone's parents.
After a long moment of silence, Orkamph finally spoke.
"I'll consider it."
"Dear!"
Eliza protested fiercely. But sensing the cold aura in her husband's
eyes, she quickly fell silent.
Orkamph also believed that relinquishing his son to a mere hunter was
unthinkable. It would be a disgrace to the royal family. However, Terraje's children were present. If he
were to force Shirone to stay, it might arouse suspicion from the Terraje.
Reina finally realized Shirone's strategy.
The fact that the Terraje faction was the most dangerous also meant they
were the most powerful. Instead of opposing them, Shirone intended to leverage their strength to achieve
his goal.
'He really is clever.'
At first, Reina had been worried, thinking he might have been too
emotional. But as expected, Shirone wasn't someone who would jump into a battle he couldn't win.
Seating his parents at the royal table, bringing up his desire to leave
at such a moment—these were all moves Shirone could make precisely because the children of Terraje were
present.
In the end, Orkamph had no choice but to take a step back.
"Shirone, all indications point to you being my son. I, too,
believe that. However, the verification is not yet complete."
He was suggesting that definitive proof was needed. It wasn't something
typically mentioned in a situation like this, but Shirone had already anticipated it. This statement
would help reassure the Terraje faction.
"So, how about this? The process of verifying the first prince
should be conducted according to proper procedures. A paternity test will be carried out. If we begin
tomorrow, it will take about four days for the results to come out. Until then, let's take some time to
consider our options."
Though Shirone wasn't thrilled about delaying matters, he knew that
resolving things swiftly when dealing with the royal family was impossible from the start.
"Understood. I'll think it over carefully."
The royal dining table was laden with countless dishes that night, but
not a single person could recall what the menu was that day.
