Chapter 60:
Seina Under the Moonlight (2)
But Shiira walked silently, staring at the ground.
Seeing her despondent, Shirone felt heavy-hearted.
If not for the accident in her childhood, she and Armin might have grown closer.
Yet he couldn't blame Armin for marrying. He had left Shiira's side to ease her guilt.
Nade grumbled, sounding displeased.
"Tch. Relationships between men and women are really baffling."
"What can you do? Not everything in the world goes as you wish."
"Damn it! At school, she's so cold, but honestly, I didn't want to see her like
this."
Nade clenched his fists in indignation, then quickened his pace to catch up to Shiira.
"Teacher."
"Huh? Yeah?"
"We're hungry. Since we're out, how about treating us to a meal?"
Shiira looked dumbfounded. The home invasion they'd committed today was serious enough to
warrant disciplinary action at school. And now they were casually asking for food?
"You've got some nerve. In this situation, you're asking for a meal?"
"What's the big deal? Even if we get punished, we still need to eat. We're learning
magic to make a living anyway."
Shirone chimed in.
"Yeah, Teacher. Let's eat before heading back. I'm hungry too."
Iruki added.
"In that case, I suggest we head to the noble district first. It's close to school,
and I know a really delicious—and expensive—restaurant there."
Shiira lost even the will to get angry. Her students' nonchalant attitude was one thing,
but she vaguely understood why they were acting this way. She didn't feel like returning to school in this
mood either. After all, today was a holiday.
"Fine, alright. You must be tired from all the mischief, so let's eat first. But be
prepared for what's waiting at school."
"Hehe! Yes!"
Nade winked at his friends. While lifting Shiira's spirits was the primary goal, they
also hoped to delay their return and mitigate the punishment.
The noble district was lined with luxurious shops. Iruki led Shiira to the most dazzling
one. Shirone gaped at the six-story building.
The entrance alone was extraordinary, befitting the nobles who frequented it. Columns
carved from entire white sharks supported the balcony ceiling, and marble statues of mythical beasts guarded
the sides like sentinels.
Restaurant Gold Cupid.
The most high-end restaurant where every dish came with a premium.
"Wow! I've never been here either. Today's gonna be a real treat. Shirone, let's go
in."
"Huh? Uh, well……."
Shirone glanced at Shiira. As expected, she was staring at the sign with a sour smile. It
was understandable—this was the most expensive restaurant in the area. Unless Iruki planned to bankrupt
their teacher, there was no way he'd pick this place.
But Shiira didn't get angry. Instead, she sighed as if resigned and strode in.
"Fine. Let's eat here."
"Ooh! Our teacher! Your generosity is unmatched!"
Nade's flattery was joined by Iruki.
"Foolish. It's a mistake to judge Teacher's generosity by this. A certified
6th-class mage can come and go from a place like this like it's a bathroom. Right, Teacher?"
"Enough. Just get inside. But we're eating and going straight back to school."
For Shirone, who had never been to a noble-exclusive restaurant, the sheer scale inside
was astonishing. It was as if magic had been used—the interior felt completely different from the outside.
Tables were sparsely placed, almost wasteful, and one side featured a bar decorated with
crystal lamps of all colors.
The patrons were, of course, nobles, and their jewelry alone made it clear they were
among Creas's elite.
"Shirone! Over here!"
Shirone, lost in awe, headed to the table at his friends' call. Nade and Iruki also
glanced around occasionally but showed no signs of nervousness.
A waiter approached to take their order. With a slick, swallow-tailed appearance, his
gaze toward Shiira was uncomfortably sticky, making Shirone's group visibly displeased.
At school, they were teacher and students, but outside, a man should escort a woman
properly. Yet the waiter ignored the students entirely, focusing only on Shiira.
"Welcome, honored guests. Thank you for visiting Gold Cupid. Especially such a
beautiful lady—I'm at a loss for words."
His flirting was one thing, but deliberately ignoring Shirone's group was blatant.
'Tch. So this is the service at a high-class restaurant? How did they train their staff?
Does he really think Teacher would be interested in a playboy like him?'
Nade's anger simmered. His eyes scanned the menu prices. He'd order the most expensive
dish to teach this waiter a lesson.
Iruki casually ordered a full course. Encouraged, Nade added another course.
Shirone was still agonizing. Honestly, he didn't recognize any of the dishes. Was
"Ollaris stew" meat, fish, or monster tongue? How was he supposed to know?
But he wasn't slow on the uptake, so he calmly ordered the same as Nade. Something about
Iruki's choices felt unreliable.
As the VIP dishes arrived one after another, the waiter's face grew pale. His first
thought was whether they could pay. Every dish had a premium, but the difference between the cheapest and
most expensive was over threefold. If these kids had misunderstood, they might end up paying twelve times
the expected price.
The waiter shot Shiira an uneasy glance, but she seemed unfazed as she chose her own
meal.
"I'll have the Dasco course. And a bottle of Limunesse."
Shiira wasn't much of a drinker, but she occasionally drank cognac when feeling down.
Limunesse was a distilled liquor with a subtle vanilla aroma, expensive but popular among women for its
smooth finish.
"Huh? Teacher, you're drinking?"
"Why not? You kids pick locks and break into houses, but I can't have a drink?"
Nade laughed awkwardly.
"Haha. Fair point. Oh, right! You're off duty today, right?"
Watching the waiter click his tongue and leave, Shirone's group exchanged smug looks. But
as reason returned, unease crept into their eyes. Realizing they'd all ordered full courses, price tags
flashed in their minds. Only then did they worry if they'd gone too far, but Shiira seemed unbothered.
A certified 6th-class mage's income placed her in the top 10% of noble society. Moreover,
working at Alpheas Magic School, one of the kingdom's top five prestigious institutions, she could enjoy
life without dipping into the Olipher faction's wealth.
'Now that I think about it, she's really successful. Our teacher.'
A life chasing dreams, not money. But Shirone, the son of a mountain hunter, knew better
than anyone how hard that was.
With four courses, food kept arriving nonstop. Patrons at other tables glanced over with
disdain.
Shirone and his friends ignored them, too busy trying to steal each other's food. Though
Nade and Iruki were nobles and Shirone had learned table manners, decorum was useless among friends.
Forks and knives flew across the table, but Shiira just watched them eat with a smile.
As their stomachs filled and the fun waned, they talked about magic, as befitting magic
school students. It was enjoyable and educational for Shiira too. The laughter never stopped, and her gloomy
mood lifted—proof that Nade's plan had worked.
"Hehehe. Teacher, let this unworthy student offer you a drink."
"Hmph! Think that'll make me go easy on you? I'm just letting it slide because we're
eating. Be ready when we get back."
"Sure, sure. Here you go."
Nade's charm shone even here. Shiira didn't seem to mind, accepting the drinks he
offered. Cognac was strong, so Shirone asked worriedly.
"Teacher, aren't you drinking too much?"
"Hehe, it's fine. This much is nothing."
"Of course. A certified 6th-class mage wouldn't be fazed by mere alcohol,
right?"
Nade took everything Shiira said positively. If she called a fork a trident, he'd
probably believe her.
"Really? You're desperate. But I'm really fine. I can hold my liquor."
Under Shirone's concerned gaze, Shiira downed another straight shot.
***
One hour later.
"Hey, you! You! Nade."
"Yes. Teacher."
At the cleared table, Shiira leaned on her elbows, her face flushed as she swayed.
Meanwhile, Shirone's group sat stiffly, sweating.
The cognac bottle was empty in just an hour.
Shiira's speech was slurred, and her eyes slightly unfocused. Shirone's group, unable to
even turn their heads, exchanged glances with their eyes.
'We're in trouble. Teacher's drunk.'
Shiira, who had been mumbling, suddenly slumped forward. Nade hastily grabbed her arm
before she fell off the chair.
"Teacher, are you okay? We should head back now."
"You troublemaker!"
Shiira jerked her head up and pinched Nade's cheek mercilessly. His cheek stretched like
mochi as he winced in pain.
"Owowow! That hurts!"
"Listen to me properly. I want to be a kind teacher like Etella. Focus on your
studies, alright?"
"Yes, yes! Got it, Teacher! It really hurts!"
"And Shirone, Iruki. You two!"
"Yes!"
Shirone and Iruki straightened their already rigid postures further.
"You're no different. Don't slack off just because you're talented. I won't forgive
that. Think of me as a bad teacher if you want. I won't forgive you."
"No one thinks you're bad. So please, snap out of it."
"Huuu. Yeah, I know. Why do I put myself through this? Ugh."
Shiira's eyelids drooped, and her head lolled. Finally, she buried her face in her arms
on the table.
"Teacher, wake up. Teacher? We haven't even paid yet."
"Sleepy. Gonna sleep."
"Teacher?"
No response.
Nade looked dumbfounded at his friends. But they had no solutions either. As they racked
their brains to escape the predicament, two men from a nearby table approached.
"The young lady seems quite drunk. Shall we call a carriage?"
A well-dressed youth spoke to Shiira. Behind him, a burly man smirked.
Shirone's group's eyes turned icy. They'd noticed earlier—these two had been eyeing
Shiira and making lewd comments.
"Teacher? Teacher. Snap out of it."
The best plan was to wake Shiira and leave quickly. But as soon as she sat up, she
slumped back onto the table. Nade's use of "Teacher" caught the man's attention, and he pressed
closer.
