Chapter 6:
A Life-Risking Opportunity (2)
Vincent grabbed Shirone's shoulders and shouted.
"Shirone! Why did you agree to this? This is really dangerous! No, it's my fault!
This ignorant father of yours didn't know any better! So let's refuse!"
"It's okay, Dad. As long as I don't leak anything, right?"
"It's not that simple. How can anything humans do stay completely secret? No matter
how I think about it, this is madness! I'll take responsibility and refuse!"
Shirone smiled faintly and slowly shook his head. *I really have the best parents.*
"Father, thank you."
Vincent's face went blank. It was the first time Shirone had ever addressed him so
formally. But what surprised him even more was the fact that Shirone was shedding tears.
"Shi, Shirone…"
Shirone spread his arms wide. With a beaming smile as bright as his joy, he said:
"Father, you've given me the greatest gift in the world."
"Ugh!"
Vincent forgot all about dignity and burst into tears. Had he ever seen his son cry
before? No, had he ever seen such an expression of pure joy on his face?
"Don't worry, Shirone! No matter what happens, your father will protect you! You
just focus on doing your best! I'll definitely protect you!"
"Dad, I'll do my best."
"That's right, my son! My treasure!"
Vincent hugged Shirone and kissed his cheek. Moved by the father and son's emotional
embrace, their mother, Olina, wiped away happy tears.
***
A week later.
The common servants of the Ozent family came to take Shirone away. Though it would take
over two years to organize ten thousand books, Shirone, who had lived modestly, had only a single backpack
of belongings.
Temuran had promised Shirone 20 gold coins per month as a condition for taking him. Since
a family's monthly living expenses were 4 gold coins, this was an enormous sum.
*Will this house be a little more luxurious when I return?* Though he knew his parents
weren't the type, Shirone forced himself to imagine a happy future to shake off his reluctance.
The moment he boarded the carriage, the contract took effect.
The common servants swiftly carried out the procedures, and Vincent, dazed, had to send
Shirone off in the carriage without even a proper farewell.
Shirone remained calm inside the carriage. Though he hadn't shown it to his parents, it
was now more important to cool his head than to dwell on emotions.
Closing his eyes and entering the Spirit Zone, he sensed his parents wiping away tears as
they waited for the departure. Shirone gently touched their hearts with his thoughts.
A warm emotion seeped into Vincent and Olina's hearts. It wasn't something you could call
magic, but there was no doubt that Shirone's sincerity had reached them.
As the carriage jolted, Shirone was ejected from the Spirit Zone. But his eyes remained
closed.
Entering the Ozent family was a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity, but it could also become
the worst crisis—a double-edged sword.
*Can I survive two years among people with such overwhelming power?*
Entrusting himself to an uncertain future, Shirone sank back into the Spirit Zone.
***
The Ozent family was a renowned martial house that had produced a nationally recognized
3rd-class swordsman at the general level. Moreover, the family head, Ozent Bishop, was a nationally
recognized 4th-class swordsman who served as the military commander of the Creaas city.
When Shirone arrived at the Ozent family, he was overwhelmed by the vast scenery beyond
the main gate.
A massive road cut through the entire estate. The road, which passed through the
overwhelmingly large mansion, arched, and exited through the back gate, stretched far beyond what the eye
could see.
*So this is the Great Straight Road of the Ozent family, something I've only heard
about.*
The Great Straight Road, which divided the estate without hesitation, was a symbol of the
Ozent family's bold temperament.
A common servant in his early twenties sneered at Shirone.
"Hehe, scared already? No need to be surprised yet. The family members are even more
monstrous."
"Watch your tongue. Do you want to lose your head?"
An older servant cautioned him. But since there was no real malice, he stopped scolding
and led Shirone through the main gate.
The Great Straight Road wasn't for servants. Shirone followed a garden path beside the
road, circling around the mansion.
The servants' quarters were in a four-story circular building. Upon entering the office,
Deputy Butler Temuran was reviewing documents.
"You're here? I'll explain your tasks, so follow me."
"Yes, I'll work hard."
Temuran didn't even acknowledge the greeting and headed straight to the library.
As the old iron library door creaked open, a space filled with books unfolded before
them. Books filled the second, third, and fourth floors—nothing but books.
Breathing in the scent of old paper, Shirone clenched his fist. *This is more than worth
risking my life for.*
"We'll be moving these books to the new library. Your job is to categorize them.
I'll send you a list every week, and you'll find and gather the books accordingly. Work hours are from 9 AM
to 6 PM. After that, it's free time. You can't go outside, and you mustn't enter the mansion without special
permission. The common servants will inform you of the house rules. Understood?"
"Yes. When should I start?"
"Tomorrow. Today, unpack and rest. Use the time to familiarize yourself with the
house rules."
