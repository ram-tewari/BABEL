Chapter 221:
Memories of Galliant (1)
Kerugo Autonomous Region.
The room where Shirone's group had stayed was neatly organized.
A black sphere was born in a space filled with still atmosphere. The
sphere looked like a two-dimensional hole, and five people were expelled from it.
Amy, who had rolled onto the floor, crawled toward the portal, calling
for Shirone. She couldn't believe he wasn't coming back.
Tess and Arin grabbed her as she tried to go back into the
portal.
Amy resented the two for stopping her. She felt they shouldn't have left
Shirone behind.
"Let go! Everyone get out of the way! I'm going to save Shirone!"
"Amy! Once you go in, you can't come back! We have to wait
now!"
"What are we waiting for? You left Shirone behind in danger!"
"No! Peope…!"
Arin stopped mid-sentence and looked at the portal. From the other side,
Shirone was spinning and flying towards them.
Tears welled up in Amy's eyes.
She caught Shirone in her arms and rolled across the floor under the
power of the spiral. She was so happy she couldn't feel any pain.
Arin watched the Meta Gate portal disappear and thought of the one fairy
left in Heaven.
'Thank you, Peope.'
The portal had opened in the room provided by Chief Kerugo.
Heaven is a long way away, but since they've been through Gorphin's
Gate, there's a lack time relativity. Calculating the difference in solar calendars, it seems like about
three days have passed.
"Rian! Rian…."
Tess's tears fell onto Rian's face. Though he had returned alive, she
wasn't happy. He had always said he would become the greatest swordsman in the world, but now he had
lost his arm.
Rian's right arm was reduced to nothing but bones. They had tried to
remove the greatsword, but his fingers wouldn't open.
Tess was at a loss. How could he hold a sword when he had no muscle and
his ligaments were torn and dangling?
At that moment, the door burst open, and a man of Kerugo entered.
He stared blankly at the women who were hugging their partners and
giving them wary looks, then ran toward the exit and shouted.
"They're back! They've returned!"
Arin read the hostility in the man's eyes. Amy, who intuitively felt
that this was not the time to just cry, asked.
"Arin, what's going on?"
"I don't know. But I think we should leave quickly. There's
definitely no friendly feeling."
Amy's group, carrying the wounded, hurried out of the room. The cave
structure was complex, but no one had forgotten the way out.
The problem was that it was a one-way path.
Footsteps were heard from the other side of the cave, and dozens of
warriors blocked the exit.
"Where do you think you're going, you traitors!"
Arin interpreted language through telepathy.
"The sin of insulting Kerugo cannot be repaid even with death! For three
days and nights, we will flay your flesh and dry you under the sun until you die!"
The language, even though translated, sent chills down their
spines.
Amy couldn't understand the situation. They hadn't fulfilled Kadum's
request, but it wasn't a contract from the beginning.
Among the warriors, they saw an old man with familiar features. His face
was deeply wrinkled, and although he had lost some muscle, his figure still held some of its former
bulk.
"You… No way?"
"These damn things!"
It was the voice of Chief Kerugo Kadum.
Is it possible to age so much in just a few days? Perhaps decades had
passed while they were in heaven.
"How dare you try to destroy Kerugo! Give me back my
lifespan!"
"We don't know anything about it! Why have you become like
this?"
"Nonsense! If it weren't for your tricks, why else would my lifespan
have shortened? I could have lived another 60 years!"
Arin suddenly realized.
'Canis….'
Even if all the men had a chance to manipulate the lifespan record,
Canis was the only one who would make such a choice.
She supported his decision. Considering what Kadum had done to the
tribespeople, it was actually lenient.
But even Canis probably didn't know that things would turn out like
this.
As the warriors approached with a powerful aura, Amy instinctively
entered Spirit Zone.
She looked for Mahatu but couldn't see him.
If the Anti-Angel faction had been purged, this place was now the heart
of enemy territory. There was no one left to help.
"Capture them alive! I want to give these wretches a horrific
death!"
The Kerugo warriors advanced with their weapons. The individual strength
was impressive, but the collective fighting spirit was suffocating.
'What should I do? Should I block the path with Fire Wall? But it's
dangerous in a cave.'
Just as five burly warriors readied their spears and charged, a
commotion was heard from the exit.
A terrified Kadum turned around and shouted.
"What's going on? What's happening!"
As his body aged, his mind seemed to have deteriorated as well.
Soon, the warriors guarding the rear were thrown towards Amy's
group.
"You good?"
Amy's face went blank.
Mahatu, who she thought had been captured, was standing there. Even more
surprising were the people he had brought with him.
After finishing the interpretation and parting ways, Jis was standing
under the escort of the Parrot Mercenary, and even the leader Marsha and vice-leader Freeman were
there.
"Jis? What are you doing here?"
"No time to explain! Quickly, come this way!"
Before Jis could finish speaking, the Kerugo warriors charged
again.
From their point of view, they absolutely had to capture Shirone's
group.
The Kerugo Autonomous Region was already in the midst of a
rebellion.
The residents joined the Anti-Angel faction, and if things settled down
like this, the Angel faction wouldn't be able to avoid collapse.
"Freeman! Go get the kids!"
Marsha kicked Freeman's butt with her foot.
Freeman, who hadn't taken the Reverse Technique Tree, showcased his
swift movements.
The warriors' spears could not cut off even a single hair on his head.
It was the Gunner's Schema, prioritizing speed over regenerative power.
Thwack! Thwack! Thwack!
Freeman, who had entered the e heart of the enemy camp, fired his guns
in all directions, causing the warriors to slam into the cave walls. Soon, only Freeman remained,
surrounded by thick smoke from the magic bullets.
Amy couldn't believe this was the same man she had faced before.
Changing his Schema had completely altered his combat style.
"Let's go. We can't hold them off for long."
"Ah, yes!"
Amy's group followed the Parrot Mercenaries and left the area.
Kadum's warriors pursued them, but their numbers were no longer
threatening.
The dictator who had ruled Kerugo for centuries had lost all his power
along with his physical strength due to aging.
"No! Don't go! I don't want to die like this! Please, give me back my
lifespan!"
Kadum's desperate cries echoed through the cave.

While leaving the Toa Mountains, Shirone's group listened to Mahatu
explain the situation.
As Kadum's body aged, the priests declared martial law and imprisoned
the Anti-Angel faction.
The person who rescued them was Jis.
Having arrived in the autonomous region to settle Falcoa's debt, he
sensed the uneasy atmosphere and started investigating.
Then, realizing that Mahatu was locked up in prison, he arrived here
leading Parrot Mercenaries.
"Thank you, Jis. We wouldn't have survived without
you."
Jis's face turned red at Amy's compliment.
"This is nothing compared to what you guys did for me. But are you
all right? Why are only the men in such a state?"
Amy checked Shirone's condition. There was still no sign of him waking
up.
"It just happened that way. Can we find a healer on the island, or
should we head to the Magic Guild?"
Marsha looked at the injured. Even though Shirone and Canis were just
unconscious, Rian's condition was critical.
The flesh on his right arm was completely torn off. With a wound of this
severity, he should have died from excessive bleeding, but for some reason, no blood flowed.
"Why does Rian look like this? No, more than that, how did you deal
with him? Is he really okay like this?"
Tess said, sobbing.
"I don't know. He ate the giant's arm, and it seems to have
something to do with it. As long as As long as he's alive, we'll find a way to heal him."
"A giant? Ate an arm? What are you talking about?"
Tess could only shake her head.
As they reached the outskirts of the Toa Mountains, the Parrot
Mercenaries' carriage awaited them.
Mahatu bowed his head and expressed his guilt.
"The immorality of our Chief has put the messenger of God in danger.
Please forgive me."
The Anti-Angel faction was formed to oppose those who used the name of
God to satisfy their own desires.
From Heaven's perspective, it was heresy and a way of thinking that
could trigger the final war, but Amy couldn't blame Mahatu.
"I guess some rascal among our friend was displeased with Kadum. We are
sorry instead. It's fortunate that things turned out well, but it could have cost many
lives."
"It was something that had to happen. If Kadum had been strong,
overthrowing the regime would have required far more sacrifices. Thank you."
Amy waited for Mahatu to say something else.
But he didn't ask anything to the person who had been to heaven. He
chose independence.
Amy boarded the carriage. Just as Mahatu's priority was the well-being
of his people, so was her friend's well-being.
Mahatu did not move until Amy's group disappeared beyond the
horizon.

"Hurry! Hurry up!"
Marsha had found one physician and four healing mages from Galliant
Island and brought them to Jis's house.
The physician, seeing Rian's condition, was horrified.
He prayed that the boy had not been conscious while undergoing such
torture.
"Thi- This is..."
Tess clung to him, crying.
"Physician-nim, please heal him! You can, right?"
The physician was silent as if mute. What on earth did she want him to
do? Healing meant restoring something that was broken. He couldn't create something new where there was
nothing.
"There's nothing I can do. I'm not an excellent physician, but neither
is anyone else. If we don't amputate, he might die."
"Amputate? This kid is a swordsman! He's determined to become the
world's strongest swordsman!"
"You'll have to cut off everything below the shoulder if he doesn't
want to become the world's greatest corpse."
Tess fell to her knees weakly. The physician was right. Seeing the
skeletal arm upon regaining consciousness would drive anyone mad. The bleeding had stopped, but it could
start again at any moment.
"Ugh! This is… not a matter to tease."
The healing mages paired up to cast healing spells on Shirone and
Canis.
They were using healing to enhance biological functions and purification
to clear the mind, but it felt like pouring water into a bottomless pit.
"What on earth happened? Who are these children? I've never treated
someone for this long and not seen them wake up."
Amy replied.
"They're students of the Magic Academy. It'll be harder for them
than for others."
"Ugh! If we keep this up for more than ten minutes, we'll need an hour's
rest. We're already beyond our limit!"
"Please, just hold on for ten more minutes! We'll pay you whatever
you want."
"At this age, I have money. How about a date instead? You look just like
my first love."
Amy opened her eyes wide and quickly nodded.
"Yes? Okay, I'll do it. Just please keep trying."
"…Don't make an old man's wit sound dirty. Anyway, they must be
important to you. I'll do what I can."
"Thank you! Thank you so much!"
As Amy repeatedly expressed her gratitude, the mage casting Purify
clicked his tongue and scolded his colleague.
"You, you shouldn't make such harsh jokes."
"Who knew it would turn out like this?"
"No, I do remember clearly that your first love was the ugliest of the
ugly."
The two old men burst into laughter. They were unofficial 9th-rank mage
who had never seen the light of day in their entire lives, but for the first time in a long time, their
blood was boiling.
"Shall we go full power for the first time in a while?"
As they amplified their healing magic, Shirone's body began to glow
brightly.
The mages on Canis' side, who had developed a competitive spirit, also
strengthened their magic, not wanting to be outdone.
The physician, ready for surgery, instructed Tess.
"Allright, lay him down over here. There will be a lot of blood, so
prepare plenty of towels."
Tess moved Rian's body to the center of the bed, her eyes watering. The
physician then pulled out a large, menacing saw from his bag.
With Rian unconscious, there was no need for anesthesia. Even if he were
awake, the pain would be unbearable with just local anesthesia.
