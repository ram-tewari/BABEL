Chapter 869:
Groggy (4)
Iruki was a successful magician.
"We'll arrive in an hour."
He had risen to the position of Chief Commander in the Valkyrie command,
where the best minds gathered.
A thinker like Ex Machina.
The gray matter inside Iruki's skull had the most advanced functions in
the world.
The number of troops he commanded exceeded one million elite soldiers,
including grand magicians and grand swordsmen.
"I need to rest. Wake me in 30 minutes."
The carriage, guarded by 200 certified second-class knights, offered the
utmost comfort through the pinnacle of technology.
'Is this really the right thing to do?'
He had made a quick decision due to the urgency, but doubts
lingered.
'With the spiritual realm closed, the number of demons has become
objective. A weapon of mass destruction is needed, but...'
The weapon Iruki designed was an elemental bomb, first conceived during
his time at Alpheas Magic School.
Its power would far surpass any magic, potentially leading to humanity's
annihilation.
'It was a weapon that would have been created eventually. If we don't
eliminate the demons, humanity will perish anyway.'
As he consciously relaxed, fatigue washed over him, making his nose
tingle.
As Iruki's breathing steadied, Aromi, the leader of the escort team,
carefully flipped through the documents.
She had an intellectual appearance with horn-rimmed glasses and had led
the think tank of Jaive.
"We're heading to the dwarf ruins in the southwestern Arka
Mountains of the Kingdom of Tormia. A state-certified excavation team is already working there. Attach
all necessary documents to avoid any backlash."
The leader of the legal department spoke.
"They can't sue us anyway. The Kingdom of Tormia has already
approved it."
"Still..."
The fact that the excavation team included Iruki's former classmates was
a concern.
"Cough! Cough!"
As Iruki coughed, Aromi spread a blanket over him.
"It's getting worse lately."
Suddenly, Iruki's eyes snapped open, and he sat up, covering his mouth
with his hand.
"Ugh! Ugh!"
With a rough breath, a handful of blood seeped through his
fingers.
"Chief Commander!"
Aromi supported him and shouted.
"Call the medic! Quickly!"
"I'm fine. I want to go quietly."
"No. If the Chief Commander's condition is critical, I have the
authority to make all emergency decisions, including canceling the mission."
Iruki smiled weakly through his bloody lips.
"But you won't do that, will you?"
Aromi, maintaining a stern expression, softened and coaxed him.
"So please get examined. You are someone humanity cannot afford to
lose."
Only Ex Machina's thinking could counter Habitz, who was beyond
probability.
But the overdrive was steadily consuming Iruki's life.
"I'll come in."
The medic, waiting in the auxiliary carriage, boarded and examined
Iruki.
Even as his blood pressure was checked and eye movements were monitored,
Iruki lay limp.
If he stopped overworking his brain now, he could live a normal lifespan
like others...
'Don't worry, Father. I know what you'll say.'
Like the extreme of combustion is an explosion, Iruki intended to burn
his life in an instant.
'Please, just hold on until the war ends.'
As Iruki's eyes regained focus, Aromi bowed her head with a heavy
heart.
"Thank you."
It was the only thing she could say to someone facing death.
The Arka Mountains held the ruins of a dwarf civilization that thrived
2,000 years ago.
The craftsmanship of the dwarves was known to be more precise than that
of current humanity.
Its archaeological value was as high as its artistic value, and a
successful excavation would be immensely profitable.
"Center! Center! No, a little more to the side!"
A team was excavating an 80-meter-high, 320-meter-wide cliff.
The team consisted entirely of magicians who had attended the same
school.
Rumble! Rumble!
A massive piece of equipment, 13 meters tall and weighing over 70 tons,
was digging into the cliff.
It had continuous tracks for legs, and its humanoid upper body had two
arms ending in giant drills.
"That's it! Dorothy! Keep turning!"
The woman operating the equipment was Dorothy, who had dropped out of
Alpheas Magic School last year.
"Here we go!"
The mimetic ability, which allowed the magician to share their mind with
the equipment, greatly enhanced operational efficiency.
Clang! Clang! Clang!
As the two giant drills spun, sparks flew, creating petal-like light
trails.
"Keep it up! Hickory 19!"
The Hickory 19, improved through countless modifications, responded to
Dorothy's mind and increased its output.
Bang! Bang! Bang!
Suddenly, a dull impact shook the equipment, causing it to jolt.
"Stop! Stop! It's stuck!"
Sabina, who had attended magic school with Dante, waved the stop
flag.
"Damn it!"
Dorothy slammed the control panel and climbed out of the cockpit,
frowning.
"What is it? More iron ore?"
"Yeah. There's too much mineral content. I knew something was off
when they easily approved the excavation."
The Arka Mountains had historically been a difficult area to
mine.
"I'll check it out."
Closer, who specialized in earth magic, approached the drill of Hickory
19.
The heat was intense enough to cause burns.
"Phew! It's completely blocked. It'll take two days to clear this.
How about we go back to the first excavation site?"
Canis, who had joined the excavation team with a background in
archaeology, approached with Arin.
"That's not an option. If we keep drilling with that crude
equipment, the dwarf ruins will be destroyed."
Dorothy glared.
"Hey! You called it crude equipment! If I wanted, I could use this
for air juggling!"
"Anyway, we can't. If the ruins or artifacts are damaged, all our
efforts will be wasted."
Closer examined the cave's terrain using his expertise.
"If we clear this spot, we should be able to go down easily.
Judging by the flow, it's not buried too deep."
"Let's take a smoke break and try again."
Dorothy lit a cigarette and jumped down from a height of 10
meters.
Though they had dropped out due to the war, as magicians, they weren't
worried about getting hurt.
"Phew, nothing comes easy. I gave up school and staked my life on
this."
Dorothy threw her helmet to the ground and exhaled a long plume of
smoke.
Not wearing a bra, the chest area of her black tank top
protruded.
Closer, holding a water bottle, spoke.
"Still, we're better off. Did you hear about Prings? He graduated
and became a kindergarten teacher."
Sabina frowned.
Prings, who specialized in ice magic, was remembered as a talented but
voyeuristic pervert.
Dorothy burst into laughter.
"Haha! A kindergarten teacher? Well, considering his personality,
it might be the perfect job."
He was probably asking the kids what they had for breakfast, flaring his
nostrils.
"We've changed a lot too. School was really fun."
Dorothy reminisced about her senior year.
Duel combat, the strategic battles of Scramble Royale, the intense
graduation exams, and...
Smiling faintly as she recalled someone, she snapped out of it and
shouted.
"Alright! Let's get back to work! Let's do it right this
time!"
"Got it!"
As everyone returned to their positions, Arin looked into the distance
and spoke.
"Canis, look over there."
A large carriage, escorted by armed knights, was quickly descending the
hill.
"What's that? The kingdom?"
"No, the mark is different."
The entire excavation team cast air magic to compress the air, making
the carriage's emblem clear.
"The Holy War?"
It bore the insignia of the Valkyrie combat unit.
As they waited with tense expressions, the carriage stopped, kicking up
dust.
The door opened, and Aromi stepped out.
Adjusting her glasses, she watched the excavation team with a composed
demeanor, then walked over like a cat.
"Hello. I'm Aromi, the chief secretary of the strategic command of
the Valkyrie unit under the Holy War."
Closer spoke bluntly.
"That's a long name. What do you want?"
Aromi got straight to the point and handed over a document.
"This is the approval from the Kingdom of Tormia, transferring
excavation rights of the Arka Mountains to the Valkyrie unit as of now. Please review and
sign."
"What..."
Before Dorothy could respond, Canis shouted.
"What do you mean?"
He roughly snatched the document and quickly reviewed the ten-page
contract transfer.
"Don't talk nonsense!"
He threw the document to the ground and glared at Aromi.
"Until I investigated, we couldn't even find evidence of the dwarf
ruins. We planned and started this project, and now you're taking it away? I won't allow
it!"
Aromi remained calm.
"It has nothing to do with the dwarf ruins. This is a matter of
global importance beyond nations. We will compensate you."
"Who are you to put a price on the ruins? The dwarf artifacts
are..."
"Wait a moment."
Dorothy, the team leader, stepped forward.
"You said it has nothing to do with the ruins? Then what is the
Valkyrie unit planning to do here? Build a military base?"
"It's classified."
"Of course. But shouldn't you at least provide a rationale we can
accept?"
"Why should we?"
Aromi didn't give them any grounds to argue.
"The Holy War requested it, and the kingdom approved it. This isn't
a matter for a private excavation team. Please step aside."
This time, Dorothy couldn't hold back.
"Who are you? I need to speak to the highest authority. The chief
secretary isn't the decision-maker, right?"
"Again, I must insist..."
"It's me."
Before Dorothy could finish speaking, Iruki stepped out of the carriage
and approached them.
"I-Iruki?"
They had heard through the magician community that he had left Tormia,
but they hadn't expected to see him here.
"It's been a while."
Forty knights flanked Iruki, forming a protective formation around
him.
As magicians, they recognized that these knights were exceptional, not
easily found in society.
Sabina asked,
"Iruki, what's going on?"
Aromi stepped aside and spoke.
"If you are citizens of Tormia, please show respect. This is the
Chief Commander of the Valkyrie unit under the Holy War."
"Chief Commander?"
Closer blinked.
"Then he's the leader?"
Iruki spoke nonchalantly.
"Yes. I'm sorry to interrupt your work, but we need to use this
area."
As magicians, they quickly grasped the situation, and Sabina asked
pointedly,
"So you knew everything before coming here? Then you must know our
personalities well. Even if it's the Holy War, taking someone else's project is too much."
Dorothy felt the same sense of betrayal.
But because it was Iruki, she suppressed her emotions and tried to
talk.
"What are you planning to do here?" Dorothy's eyes
sharpened.
"Alright, I get it. You're in a high position, so people like us
don't even register..."
"We're going to make a very dangerous weapon."
"Chief Commander."
Iruki ignored Aromi's signal.
"For that, we need a special mineral. Our investigation shows that
this mountain range contains it in large quantities."
It was confidential information that shouldn't have been disclosed, and
Dorothy's mind was conflicted.
'Iruki.'
He was the protagonist of her most cherished memories from
school.
