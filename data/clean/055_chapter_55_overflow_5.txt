Chapter 55:
Overflow (5)
The tragedy might never have happened. He'd been as honest and pure as Shirone.
But he'd lost his light. The genius beloved by the gods had fallen because of a coward
like her.
"I see your point."
Siena emerged from her memories.
"I won't pressure Shirone to withdraw."
"Before that, when will you respect the president's opinion?"
"Hmph. Save the wordplay. You have a month. Without credible research, the club
dissolves. The legacy of countless graduates ends with you."
Neid's heart dropped. This wasn't just about being kicked out.
The club's alumni, despite their troublemaker reputations, held key positions in the
magical society. The current Magic Association chairman, Michael Gaold, was their pride. If the club ended
with them, they'd have no face to show their seniors.
'Damn it. What do we do?'
A month was just political lip service to ease the dissolution. The club had never held a
formal presentation—how could they prove the existence of ghosts or gods? Cornered, Neid abandoned pride and
begged.
"Professor! Give us a chance! Isn't there another way besides a presentation? You know
our club's value lies in its existence! We're the only ones challenging the logic-obsessed magical society!"
Siena remained unmoved.
"Then challenge it. No compromises. The faculty's decision is final. Make the right
choice."
As Siena left, the iron door clanged shut. The kids slumped onto the sofa, dazed. The
Isthas mechanism whirred, returning the lab to its original location.
Gauge Symmetry (1)
A leisurely holiday.
Normally, Shirone would spend his days off in the library, but today he sat blankly in
the central park. Iruki and Neid sat beside him, listlessly sipping juice.
"What time is it?"
"Dunno. Not night, though."
"Genius."
Silence fell. Shirone sighed, followed by two more sighs. Finally, he stood and glared at
them.
"What's the plan for the club? Shouldn't we brainstorm presentation topics instead of
lazing around?"
"Right. Let's think. What research could the school approve?"
Neid shook his head.
"As far as I know, we've never held a presentation. Sure, we've caused plenty of
incidents, but the school would never recognize those as research."
Supernatural psychic science couldn't be elevated to a professional field. If it could,
it would've spread as science long ago.
Sitting back down, Shirone's mind went blank. He glanced at passing girls' legs and
averted his eyes from well-developed women, but his thoughts were empty.
"Huh? Guys, look."
Neid pointed casually. A stunning woman—never seen at school—headed for the gates. She
wore an ivory trench coat and heels, her permed purple hair cascading to her waist.
"Biologically well-designed. Who is she?"
"A senior? Kids our age don't dress that mature."
