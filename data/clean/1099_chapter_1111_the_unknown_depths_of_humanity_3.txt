Chapter 1111:
The Unknown Depths of Humanity (3)
"Kanis, how long are we going to stay here?"
In the narrow chamber of the pyramid, Kanis and Arin sat side by side,
leaning against the wall.
A considerable amount of time had passed, but neither had the will to
move.
"It can't be," Kanis muttered.
"This insane space is trying to drive us mad. Think about it—twins?
Do you and I look anything alike?"
They did.
Not identical, but as fraternal twins, it wasn't impossible.
Arin lowered her head.
"What's going to happen to us?"
"There's no point in thinking about it! Nothing changes. Arin,
think about the years we've spent together!"
Someone more precious than life itself.
'I want you. I wanted to spend my entire life with you. And now you're
telling me we share the same blood?'
Overcome by anger, Kanis grabbed Arin's shoulders and kissed her
roughly.
It was bold, but if it were Arin, she might have accepted it
willingly.
"No!"
But she pushed him away, and Kanis fell back onto the ground, looking
stunned.
"Why… why did you do that?"
Reading Kanis's emotions through her empathy, she began to cry.
"I'm sorry. I love you too, but… just in case…"
"No! That's impossible! We agreed to live believing it wasn't
true!"
Though they'd never spoken the words aloud.
"We did, but now we know. At least, until we're completely
sure…"
"You…"
Kanis was about to shout in frustration when—
"There you are."
From the darkness emerged a pair of glowing red eyes.
"Ares," Arin murmured.
"Is everyone safe? We need to find Shirone."
Muttering as he approached, Ares paused, surprised by the looks on their
faces.
"What happened? Why do you both look so pale?"
Neither of them answered.
"I see. It got to you too."
"Ares, did you… see the past as well?"
"Yes. I keep resetting it with my ability, but it's a horrible
task. The timeline in this place is warped. The fact that we're all separated is proof of
that."
Kanis muttered, "Why is this happening to us?"
"There's no reason," Ares replied.
He examined the glowing runes running along the wall.
"This is just that kind of place. It seems all causality is
dismantled here, regardless of time. It's almost like…"
"God's perspective?" Arin asked.
Ares nodded."Exactly. From outside the universe, even time
would look like just one thread. Maybe there really is a god living here."
"Let's go," Kanis said, standing up.
"I'm going to meet them myself and find out why they're doing this
to us. I'll fix it. If there really is a god…"
He clenched his fists."I'll kill them if I have
to."
Each member of the World Health Organization delegation had been
assigned a private room.
Seriel, placing her large bag on the desk, was frustrated that her mood
had been ruined so early in the morning.
"Annoying," she muttered.
The face of Fermi flashed in her mind.
A small incident from their school days—one she'd never spoken about to
anyone.
'If Amy found out, she'd probably try to kill him.'
Her cheeks burned at the thought, but another crossed her mind.
'Would she, though?'
Everyone has secrets.
If one were to dismantle the causes and effects of a person's life, it
would reveal countless impulses, shame, and fleeting joys beyond anyone's imagination.
'Back then, we had no idea the world would turn out like this.'
Sitting on the edge of her desk, she lit a cigarette.
"Why did I start smoking?"
Perhaps that, too, was just another…
Cause.
It was during Seriel's final year at school.
The day after the 19th week of training, they were given a rare day
off.
Amy and Seriel were close friends, but during their final year, free
time was a luxury they couldn't afford.
"Amy, what are you doing on our day off?"
"Strengthening my Spirit Zone. What about you?"
Unlike the tireless Amy, Seriel was an ordinary person who needed
rest.
"I'm thinking about grabbing some coffee."
"Alright, then… let's go to the café."
Amy agreed readily, though a hint of hesitation flickered in her
eyes.
Noticing it, Seriel clapped her hands as if she'd just remembered
something.
"Oh, right! Today's the release day for The Primal Snare sequel!
Sorry, Amy, I'll have to pass on coffee."
"Got it. Go to the bookstore."
Not wanting to interrupt Amy's training, Seriel found an excuse to slip
away.
"Haha, no way. It's a dangerous book. Someone without immunity like
you would be in trouble. See you tomorrow!"
"Hey! Seriel!"
Amy called out urgently, but Seriel was already rounding the corner of
the hallway.
Could she have stopped her?
For a moment, the thought crossed her mind, but the tasks ahead left no
room for distraction.
"…Back to training."
Meanwhile, Seriel, relieved not to have become a nuisance, sighed as she
stepped outside.
"I'll just have some fun on my own."
Feeling free, she left the school and arrived at a run-down
bookstore.
"Hello."
Far from the noble district and occasionally receiving banned books,
this was her little hideout.
"It's over there, in the corner."
The bookstore owner, despite her face being hidden by large glasses and
a scarf, recognized her immediately.
Bowing slightly, Seriel's eyes gleamed behind her glasses.
'There it is. The sequel to The Primal Snare.'
A dark love story between a nobleman and a maid, devoid of any
romance.
It was classified as adult material due to its decadent and explicit
descriptions, but for Seriel, a lifelong reader of romance novels, it was nothing shocking.
From the very first page, the characters from the previous book were
engaged in an intimate encounter.
Swallowing hard, she read on, completely engrossed in the story.
'The roles have reversed.'
The maid, now holding a whip, approached the nobleman bound in
chains.
'Her breath made every hair on the man's body stand on end. Her tongue,
moist like jelly slipping between her red lips, licked his nape…'
"Hi there."
Startled by the sudden voice, Seriel slammed the book shut.
"Ah! Wh-who is it?"
Turning, she saw a familiar face she didn't want to see.
"Fermi?"
As she lowered her large glasses to confirm, Fermi waved
cheerfully.
"Hello."
That same mischievous smile on his face.
