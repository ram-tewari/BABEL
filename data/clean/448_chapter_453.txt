Chapter 453:
Two kilometers from the First Rebel Command.
Gaold was walking through the dark forest.
With his hands behind his back, his eyes were fixed straight ahead, but
they didn't take in the scenery.
Twenty years ago, he met a woman.
She was so different from him that, even though he disliked her, his
curiosity made him look at her.
Human connections are truly mysterious. The heart has no substance, so
you can't predict where it will flow.
Perhaps Gaold was regretting it.
Maybe he hated this reality, where all that was left was hell, because
he had given everything to that woman.
But he couldn't cut off the heart he had given away, nor could he take
it back. So he walked through this hell and arrived at the center of a quiet clearing.
In the silence, where not even the sound of birds could be heard, Gaold
looked up at the open sky.
Countless stars were shining.
It had been a long time since he had seen the sky.
Miro wasn't among those stars. She was somewhere connected to the ground
he was standing on, alive.
Thump, thump. Thump, thump.
Calming his pounding heart, Gaold spoke into the deep forest.
"Come out already. There's no need to waste time since I'm
alone."
As soon as he finished speaking, 21 figures emerged from the darkness of
the forest.
Cage B Team of the Tormia Magic Association.
Once, they had all been Gaold's subordinates, but few of them were
familiar faces.
However, as the former head of the Magic Association, he knew well how
powerful they were.
Of course, none of them could surpass Gaold in individual combat, but
just as scissors beat rock, and the sea beats the wind, there are always unique magical
affinities.
Also, if they were a team sent to eliminate him, they would have
included irregulars.
Team leader Rose stepped forward.
Her face was marked with a dense killing intent that Gaold had never
seen before.
"When did you realize?"
Rose, a certified Level 4 scent mage. They had known each other since
his time as the association head.
"Ha, did I need to realize? If you're targeting me, it's
obvious."
It was also why Gaold had come alone.
The 21's goal was to eliminate Gaold, and they would come with the
determination that it didn't matter how many of them died to achieve that goal.
If Gaold wasn't alone, they would never move and would wait for an
opportunity to strike from behind.
It was something that had to be finished before the project they had
prepared for 20 years could begin.
"Surround him."
At Rose's command, the 20 members intertwined like a net and
teleported.
Gaold, still with his hands behind his back, only looked at Rose.
He had no feelings about avoiding the fight or not wanting to kill his
former comrades.
But Cage B Team knew Gaold, and Gaold didn't know Cage B Team.
Even if he did know them, the tactical and strategic variations created
by the combination of 20 different individuals were nearly infinite.
All he could do was think about what he could do.
Rustle, rustle.
Even the sound of grass brushing against each other sounded like
thunder. A few people moved behind him.
Carnivore addict Benifis drew his meat cleaver and looked at his
comrades on either side.
Beam of insect magic nodded, and Reed of curse magic combined
omniscience and omnipotence.
Facing the mage with the greatest physical strength in Tormia, everyone
had the same thought.
'This won't take long.'
"Hey, juniors."
At the sound of Gaold's voice, all 21 of them flinched
simultaneously.
"Why are you taking so long to prepare? You came here to die."
Killing intent filled the mages' eyes.
As if 21 flames were burning, smoke seemed to rise from their
bodies.
A ritual of burning their remaining lifespan, igniting their
will.
In Gaold's absence, Sein and eight others gathered in a circle around a
torch in the clearing behind the command.
Sein's expression was more relaxed than usual.
Perhaps because he was recalling memories from 20 years ago, before he
became known as the ruthless mage of the Black Line.
"About the Judgment of the Twenty…"
Sein began to speak.
"There aren't many people in the world who know the details. But the
story involving Miro, Gaold, and me is known only to the three of us."
The group waited eagerly for him to continue.
But Sein's mouth didn't open easily.
It was a burden to reveal a very small and personal secret that had
occurred within the greatest event in human history.
"I loved Miro."
No one could refute the words he suddenly blurted out, so Sein
continued.
"And Gaold loved Miro too."
Sein looked up at the sky, just like Gaold.
"Yes, it may not seem like an important story. But to us, it was
everything."
This is the story.
"Twenty years ago, the coldest man in the world, the most outstanding
woman in the world, and…"
A man who wanted to live only in obedience to God, but ended up hating,
cursing, and mocking God, diving into hell in terrible sorrow.
"The kindest fool in the world was attending Alpheas Magic
School."
Sein's story began.
The Tormia Yore Order.
The Yore Order is a global religion that, even during humanity's dark
ages, never waged a single war and only bestowed mercy.
There are no ranks among them, and they don't distinguish between
commoners and nobles.
The church is inhabited only by the 'Yora,' who preach the order's
doctrines. They live in poverty, relying solely on the volunteer work and donations of the believers,
earning the love of all.
'Thank you, Yore God.'
A young man knelt under the sunlit order, holding a pendant with a small
circle and cross, the symbol of Yore, and praying.
'Today, I hope all the unfortunate people in the world find happiness.
Make me your servant. Give me the courage to love even evil.'
The young man had a white face and skin as clean as a child's.
He had a kind impression, filled with nothing but purity. This was
Gaold, 20 years ago.
"Are you giving thanks for advancing to the graduation class?"
Gaold finished his prayer and opened his eyes.
Moved, his eyes were slightly wet with tears.
"Yora."
Gaold, blushing with embarrassment, wiped his tears and stood up.
"Graduation classes start today."
Mikael Gaold.
He was an exceptionally kind child, even among the Yore
believers.
It was surprising when he said he wanted to enter a magic school.
Magic is power, and power begets violence. But Gaold wanted to learn
magic for that very reason.
Even though divinity and magic have similarities, mages deny God.
To understand them, he thought he had to understand magic
himself.
"Well, I'm off."
Gaold headed to Alpheas Magic School.
He had barely made it to the graduation class through hard work. Fierce
competition awaited, but he was determined not to lose to anyone.
'Yore God, give me strength.'
While the entrance ceremony for the advanced class was taking place,
Gaold, who was walking in the park, spotted the person he least wanted to see.
Sein, his rival since Class Seven.
But he couldn't pretend not to see him, so Gaold extended his hand for a
handshake.
"From today, the real competition begins. Let's do our best."
Sein didn't even look at him.
"Why should I shake hands with you? If you want to shake hands with me,
bring your God here."
Gaold, feeling insulted, raised his voice.
"I've never forced my beliefs on you. Why are you always picking a
fight? Your manner of speaking is really…"
Another tedious argument began.
About ten minutes later.
"Hey, you two over there!"
A woman's voice called out from a distance. Sein's eyes sparkled as he
turned his head.
Adrias Miro.
Currently the most notable graduation student at Alpheas Magic
School.
She had been suspended for a year for burning down a laboratory for
personal reasons, but her skills were already famous in other countries.
"Can't you hear me? Come over here."
As the two approached, Miro put down the box of experimental materials
and wiped her sweat.
"Phew, this is really heavy. You two are new students, right? Move this
to my lab."
Sein raised his head and said,
"Why should we? I heard there's no seniority concept in the graduation
class."
"Well… you two seem to be wasting your energy. It would be nice if you
could save your strength and help me."
Gaold, finding her words reasonable, stepped forward willingly.
"That's true. I'll help…"
"Wait. I'm not helping."
"Okay. But I will."
"No, I don't understand why you would help. Our argument is pointless?
Everything in this world is a fragment that comes from the clash of opinions."
Miro frowned.
"You're really stiff. Are you always like this?"
Gaold laughed heartily.
"Ha ha! Well, this guy is…"
"Sein. You've probably heard the name, even in the graduation
class."
As Sein introduced himself, Miro clapped her hands in
realization.
"Oh, you're Sein? The Savant?"
Sein continued speaking without changing his expression, as if it was
natural for her to know him.
"I've heard you're very skilled. But now that I'm here, I'll be number
one. You'd better be prepared."
Miro didn't seem to listen to Sein's words.
"So, you're a cold-blooded human? Do you really not get excited? Can you
even be called a person?"
"It's not that I don't get excited, I control it. The most important
thing for a mage is composure. I don't waver under any circumstances."
"Hmm, I don't believe it. If you're human, you naturally have emotions
and should follow your heart."
'A mage talking about emotions? Maybe the rumors were
exaggerated.'
Sein set aside his disappointment and made a suggestion.
"Then how about a test? If you can make me even a little angry, I'll
carry this box for you."
"Really? Alright, then… hmm…"
Miro tapped her lips and rolled her eyes before snapping her
fingers.
"You can really control it, right?"
"Of course. A Savant…"
Before Sein could finish his sentence, Miro squatted down. She grabbed
Sein's pants and yanked them down, underwear and all.
"…"
Sein looked like his soul had left his body.
But Gaold, standing next to him, wasn't in a normal state of mind
either.
"Are you crazy…?"
Miro stared at what was in front of her and made a measuring gesture
with her thumb and forefinger.
"Hmm, so this is what it looks like."
Miro looked up and asked,
"How about now? Still no emotional disturbance?"
"Of course not. I never…"
Sein couldn't finish his sentence.
But Miro didn't seem to care and stood up.
"Wow, that's impressive. I didn't think someone like you
existed."
Then she clapped her hands as if she had remembered something.
"Oh right! The Pasca root! I forgot it!"
Miro pointed at Gaold.
"What's your name?"
"G-Gaold."
Gaold, not in his right mind, answered out of habit.
"Help me move this to my lab. It's dangerous, so be careful.
Please."
"Wait! Where is your lab…?"
Miro didn't listen and ran out of the park.
Gaold smacked his lips and looked at Sein.
Though they were rivals, he could sympathize with what Sein was feeling
as a fellow man.
"Hey, are you okay?"
Sein slowly pulled up his pants. Still in a daze, he turned and walked
away.
Gaold clicked his tongue sympathetically.
"How could she pull down his underwear? She's really strange."
That was Gaold's first impression of Miro.
