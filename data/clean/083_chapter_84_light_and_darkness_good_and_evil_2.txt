Chapter 84:
Light and Darkness, Good and Evil (2)
- Canis. My heart rate has increased by 1.7 times. Rising adrenaline levels cloud my
judgment. If you're angry because of what I said, I apologize.
The consideration of the magical creature to prevent a loss in combat power calmed
Canis's heart. As long as the cold and composed Harvest was by his side, he was confident he wouldn't lose
to anyone.
- No, Harv. I'm sorry. Your judgment was right. Let's begin.
=======================================
Canis erased his distracting thoughts and glared at Shirone. The latter's flawless
appearance, as if he had never experienced hardship, made Canis's insides twist.
'Hmph. You probably think I'm the villain. But…….'
Dark power blossomed from Canis's shadow. Even as it was chipped away by the illumination
magic, its regeneration speed was astonishing.
'To me, you are the evil one!'
As the dark power surged forward, Shirone cleansed the ground with photon output. Then,
he threw the photon cannon mounted on his right hand. Harvest intercepted it, taking the hit with his body.
The photon cannon dug into his torso, sending his massive frame flying into the forest. But instead of
showing any sign of impact, Harvest wiggled his long fingers, taunting Shirone.
"Kukuku. Come on, come on."
Though the forest was a home ground for a dark mage, Shirone charged in without
hesitation. Dozens of photons rained down from above, crisscrossing left and right.
Ratatatatat! Ratatatatat!
A hundred illumination bullets pierced through the forest canopy, lighting up the sky as
if it were broad daylight. Nade and Iruki stared at the scene in disbelief.
"That guy's really gone all out."
"He must know what he's doing. If Shirone can't stop him, we'll lose this game."
Iruki slowly turned his gaze. Lucas was following Shirone into the forest. Spotting gray
smoke rising in front of him, Lucas swiftly dodged as an atomic bomb exploded with a bang. Retreating 10
meters in an instant, he glared at Iruki with terrifying eyes.
"You bastard……."
"Don't waste your energy. We're thinking the same thing you are. Just stay put."
"This is absurd."
No matter how reckless, a magic academy student daring to face a B-class criminal was
unthinkable. As Lucas drew his twin swords, a watery sound echoed. The aura transmitted through his Spirit
Zone was like burning firewood, creating a heat haze.
The potential for physical enhancement through Schema was limitless, but most used
verified builds to avoid severe side effects from imbalance.
An unbalanced build could ruin the body. For example, over-enhancing the nervous system
would cause muscles to tear as they couldn't keep up with the speed.
Lucas was currently using the Mitochondria Build, which enhanced the energy factories of
cells to boost energy efficiency momentarily.
Though it drained stamina quickly, the simultaneous enhancement of all physical aspects
was appealing, making it a staple for most users.
'This could be dangerous…….'
Iruki checked Nade's condition. His cold gaze at Lucas reminded Iruki of a summer five
years ago. On a day of pouring rain, the two had clashed. That Nade could still smile now was nothing short
of a miracle. He would likely never show his former face again—the face resembling the person he loved most
in the world, which he now hated the most.
"Be careful, Nade. That guy's a Schema user."
"I know. Let's overwhelm him with firepower early on."
Lucas found it ridiculous. Words like "be careful" were for avoiding puddles or crossing
shaky bridges, not for situations where one's head was about to roll.
"Haah. Kids these days……."
Lucas scratched his head, his figure blurring. Simultaneously, a golden flash erupted
between Nade and Iruki. A teleportation light flickered, and the two reappeared 10 meters away from Lucas.
Iruki checked his chest—his clothes were sliced open, and blood seeped from a cut.
"You okay?"
"Not a fatal wound. But he's really fast."
Even if magic was transcendent, a mage's body was still human. Without magical effects,
their ability to react and counter a Schema swordsman's movements was inherently limited.
But the same applied to swordsmen. The power of magic couldn't be withstood by Schema
alone. Even a student-level spell could be fatal if it landed cleanly.
Nade and Iruki concentrated their firepower. Chains of atomic explosions snaked forward
as Nade calculated Lucas's trajectory and fired predictive shots. But Lucas's movements were nothing like
the mechanical patterns they'd trained against in school.
'Damn! These kids are no joke!'
Lucas wasn't having an easy time either. His stamina was dropping by the second due to
the Mitochondria Build's nature. Maximizing his metabolic rate, his speed skyrocketed. Circling a wide
radius in an instant, he leaped from Iruki's flank and swung his twin swords. Nade, rushing from the
opposite side, extended both hands. An electric shock spell struck Lucas's abdomen, vibrating his body 60
times per second.
"Iruki! Now!"
Iruki was already prepared. Gray smoke rose from Lucas's face as he watched the
detonation reaction unfold before his eyes. Lucas tried with all his might to move, but his muscles felt
charred, unresponsive.
'Damn it! Damn it!'
To think he'd face death against mere brats. No, was he really about to die? The
unexpected situation made it feel surreal.
Iruki was certain it was over. The detonation reaction had already begun—it wasn't a
hasty judgment. But suddenly, the Spirit Zone vanished. Before he could figure out why, his legs began to
numb. Someone else's shadow had connected to his feet—undoubtedly Arin's handiwork.
'Damn it all!'
Lucas mustered every ounce of strength to break free from the electric vibrations. He
then lunged at Nade, who had humiliated him. His anger, having brushed against death, peaked as his swords
gleamed with raw emotion.
"Nade! Run!"
Even while stunned, Nade generated electricity in both hands. Lucas spread his twin
swords for a dual strike. As the blades tore through the wind, two thick ice pillars unexpectedly erupted on
either side of Nade.
Clang! The swords embedded themselves into the ice. Lucas's pupils shook in shock. His
swordsmanship could split boulders, yet mere ice had stopped him cold.
Slowly turning, Lucas saw a familiar face—the teacher he'd thrown off the cliff earlier.
"Ms. Siena!"
Nade cheered, his face brightening. But Siena remained silent, her emotionless eyes
fixed solely on Lucas.

"Haah. Haah."
Etella, leaning against a tree trunk, looked ready to collapse at any moment. She had
been in a 30-minute chase, yet couldn't shake Arcane completely.
'The poison is spreading. I can't delay treatment any longer.'
Sitting down, Etella tore the waist of her robe. Black blood oozed from a wound above her
navel—a cut from the Poison Cutter. Her breathing technique slowed the poison's spread, but it was only a
temporary measure. She needed to suck out the venom, but the wound's location made it difficult.
Etella interlaced her fingers behind her back, gripping the tree trunk. Bending forward,
her spine creaked. Stretching her spine with her erector muscles, her hips lifted off the ground as her back
arched sharply. Twisting her body into a spiral, her lips finally touched the wound below her navel.
She forced all the toxins to the wound and sucked them out. The moment the poison left, a
searing pain overwhelmed her. Grimacing, she spat out the black blood, which withered the grass instantly.
Color returned to her face as she threw her crooked glasses aside and wrapped the torn robe around her fist.
"Hoo. I might live now."
Her fluctuating Spirit Zone regained durability, sensing the surroundings through
synesthesia. Her eyes widened as she kicked off the ground, dodging dozens of inky hands rising like spilled
ink.
'He's already here…….'
That Arcane could chase her relentlessly, even as a master of detection, proved how agile
a dark archmage could be in the shadows.
"You've removed the poison, I see. I like you more and more."
Arcane emerged through the dark curtain. But Etella no longer needed to run.
"It won't be easy from now on."
"Ready to get serious? Then I'll enjoy this without holding back."
As Etella clenched her fist, the Yin-Yang Wave Fist's energy surged. Arcane immediately
noticed her change—she had completely abandoned her Spirit Zone.
Mages wield the Spirit Zone; swordsmen use Schema. But monks train both simultaneously. A
monk's purpose isn't to climb higher but to embrace the world with the broadest heart.
Arcane disliked this. Had she focused on one path from the start, her name would be known
across the continent.
'A pity. With talent to stand at the world's peak, why choose enlightenment? Well, it's
pointless to say this to someone who doesn't even aspire to climb.'
Arcane extended his hand and chanted a spell. Against a Schema user, he had a special
countermeasure.
"Dark Minions."
Countless spiders, each the size of a human head, crawled from the forest shadows. The
sight alone was enough to send shivers down one's spine.
Sssssssssk!
But the true horror of the Dark Minions was yet to come. A dark golem, over 3 meters
tall, rose beside Arcane.
Its face was buried in its shoulders, its limbs swollen like wrapped yarn. Its arms were
long, its legs short.
Dark Minions was a shadow-summoning spell that converted 100% of mental power into
physical force. Unlike dark energy, which absorbed external energy, this was qualitatively different. Though
not immune to light like magical creatures, at night, there was no stronger combat weapon.
"Go, my minions."
The dark spiders opened the assault. Their numbers were so overwhelming it was impossible
to track individual movements.
Etella jabbed, deflecting spiders in all directions. A straight punch sent a cluster
flying with a bang.
Grrraaaah!
Amid the scattering spiders, the dark golem charged. Etella leaped like a flying squirrel
as its thick arm smashed the ground. The earth rippled like water before exploding, rivaling the eruption
spell in power. Etella paled—the archmage's mental power, fully converted into physical force, was beyond
imagination.
The golem, its hands planted on the ground, turned its head. Swinging its arms like
hammers, it attacked again.
Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!
The relentless flurry of punches forced Etella into a desperate retreat. The dark golem
was the ultimate weapon—fast, flexible, and devastating.
Knowing she couldn't retreat forever, Etella finally planted her feet and faced the
golem.
Grrraaaah!
Diving into the golem's embrace, Etella drove a fist into its side.
Wham!
The massive golem staggered. The exchange sent a shockwave back to Arcane's mind.
