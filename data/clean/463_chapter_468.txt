Chapter 468:
The Second Heaven, Lakia.
On the inner wall of the Hall of Corruption stood Kariel, Uriel, and
Surt, the leader of the fire giants.
Since the place wasn't designed for war, the inner wall was higher than
the outer wall, offering a clear view of the endless plains beyond.
A massive drawbridge, 1 kilometer wide and 4 kilometers long, spanned
the great river crossing the plains, branching into seven paths symbolizing the seven sins, leading to
all the gates of Lakia.
Gaold's party would likely enter through one of these seven
paths.
The end of these paths was exactly 10 kilometers from this point.
But in some ways, this calculation was meaningless.
No one here believed Gaold would actually show up.
"It's still peaceful. Well, that's to be expected. How are the
troops distributed?"
When Kariel turned and asked, Surt immediately knelt on one knee and
replied.
"Ten percent of the troops are in the Plains of Lamentation outside
Lakia, twenty percent in the capital, thirty percent in the Kespa Mountains, and the remaining forty
percent are gathered at the drawbridge. Even if they cross the Kespa Mountains, they will never breach
the drawbridge."
"So they say."
Kariel looked down at Miro, who was kneeling at the edge of the
wall.
Her hands were bound with cuffs inscribed with henna, and she gazed at
the river with a calm expression, as serene as the flowing water.
A collar was around her neck, and at the end of the long chain, Arius
knelt, exhausted, with his eyes closed.
"How does it feel to be treated like a pet dog?"
Miro did not respond.
She had given her heart to no one, so even the relationship between
master and servant was meaningless.
Her gaze was fixed on the distant Gaold, who was coming.
Miro's space-time had disappeared, and the war with Heaven had finally
begun.
If Heaven won, the next event would be the destruction of
humanity.
'Don't come, Gaold.'
Gaold must not come here.
Currently, only Kariel and his remnants, who had denied Ra's will,
remained in Lakia.
Gaold's combat power should be used to win the war, not to save
her.
'Don't come. Think rationally. Go to the temple now. Destroy the Akashic
Record.'
Kariel, looking only at Miro's back, seemed to guess her thoughts and
smiled.
"What if he does come here?"
Kariel's power was significantly depleted from suppressing Miro's
abilities.
Even Brahma had temporarily disappeared due to Miro, so Uriel was
guarding him.
"Come forth, Shiva."
When Uriel invoked the law of his followers, a black sphere appeared,
and the three-horned Mara emerged.
A man with a bluish body, fangs protruding from his lips, and numerous
crescent-shaped swords around his slender waist.
In the human world, he was known as the god of destruction,
Shiva.
Even Surt, the greatest swordsman of purgatory, swallowed hard at his
appearance.
"Shiva, at the archangel's command."
"Guard the outer gate. Let no one approach."
Shiva's eyes flashed, and he immediately leaped into the air.
With a single bound, he arced towards the outer gate, and Kariel smiled
in satisfaction.
"All preparations are complete. Now the festival
begins."
Whether Gaold arrived first or Heaven won first, it didn't matter to
Kariel.
'Just a little more…'
Breaking through the wall of fire created by the giants' bodies, Gaold
increased his speed.
Fallen angels attacked from all sides, and their followers trailed
behind.
The eight members of Gaold's party were like a single arrow piercing
through everything.
Etella and Zulu guarded the rear, Armin was in the center, and Kuan and
Siena ran on either side.
Ahead of them, Kangnan and Sein ran side by side, with Gaold alone at a
distance twice as far as the others.
'Go, Gaold.'
Zulu cheered for Gaold in his heart.
A pool of emotions that had stagnated for 20 years.
But it was densely compressed with pure anger, free of any
impurities.
A space and time that could only be reached by focusing on a single
goal.
'Pour everything out, Gaold. You deserve it.'
"Raaaah!"
Gaold clenched his fist and unleashed his power.
Pain sensitivity increased by 2 million times.
"Aaaah!"
The immense pain drove Gaold's mind into hell.
The great inferno of hell unfolded, and the hungry spirits
rampaged.
As the demon of hell screamed across the sky, embodying Gaold's pain,
even the fire giants couldn't withstand the heat and writhed as they burned.
As the plains ended, Kangnan looked back and said,
"We're about to enter the city."
The capital of Lakia spread out magnificently over the vast land.
The city gates opened, and numerous giants poured out.
These were higher-ranking giants, different from those they had faced
before.
But more serious was the increasing number of fallen angels.
Armin's stop magic and Zulu's various summons quickly responded to their
abilities, but it was a miracle they had held out this long against the bizarre and powerful abilities
of the fallen angels.
"Flank!"
As Kangnan shouted, fireballs flew towards Siena.
The massive flames transformed into giants, cornering Siena.
As Siena spread powerful cold around her, an ice wall with sharp spikes
surrounded her.
Boom!
The giant's fist shattered the thick ice wall.
Even though fire giants were weak against cold, they were still
giants.
The power of Skima wielded by higher-ranking giants was incomparable to
that of humans.
"Siena!"
Armin, running in the center, leaped towards Siena.
With twenty fallen angels bound by stop magic, he didn't have much time
to act, but nothing was more important to him than Siena's safety.
As he cast flicker magic to pull Siena out, more fireballs
arrived.
Buried in the enemy, with no one able to help, the two were
cornered.
Every time the giants' flaming blades wove a complex net, Armin's
clothes singed.
"Ugh!"
As a blade passed in front of his bandaged eyes, another giant charged
with a greatsword.
Siena cast freezing magic, but the giant, determined to die together,
lunged and thrust the sword.
Clang!
At the sharp metallic sound, the giant's trajectory was
deflected.
Kuan, who had been running on the opposite side, appeared and deflected
the giant's sword, diving into the enemy.
"Watch out!"
Ignoring Siena's warning, Kuan spread external force in all
directions.
As he freely moved through space, every swing of his sword began to
dismember the giant.
Even in the dire situation, Siena and Armin watched in a daze.
"H-How?"
His skills had improved significantly since they first arrived in
Heaven.
Armin could guess the results achieved in such a short time.
'It's not enhancement. It's enlightenment.'
His speed and power hadn't necessarily increased, but the fluidity of
his movements was beyond imagination.
His genius movements, which completely broke human thinking, were so
unpredictable that even Siena couldn't follow his trajectory.
'Not yet.'
Kuan cut down everything in his path.
The transcendent trajectory, achieved by ignoring death, flowed through
the giants' blind spots, and they died without knowing what hit them, as if fighting an invisible
ghost.
'My grave…'
Kuan felt extreme joy and equal sorrow as he soared.
'This isn't the place!'
Boom, boom, boom!
As centrifugal force was repeatedly added, the sword wind began to
rage.
The sharp sword wind, which even blew away the giants' flames, nearly
annihilated the unit on Siena's flank.
Sein, displeased, turned and shouted.
"Hold your position!"
He had instructed them not to worry about others' safety when they first
charged, but Kuan's decision to cross to the opposite side, ignoring his position, displeased
him.
Kuan heard Sein's voice but smiled faintly.
The external force, which had shot out in sixteen directions, curved
back, spinning his body even faster.
As he kicked off the ground with that rotational force, his body flew
back to its original position at a terrifying speed.
In the panoramic view of the overlapping scenery, Kuan could clearly see
Siena's face in Armin's arms.
She would probably never forget Armin.
'That's why I can die.'
Unable to win Siena's heart, unable to express his feelings, he could
die happily.
Childish stubbornness. Or perhaps even more childish jealousy.
It didn't matter.
'For you…'
Boom, boom, boom, boom!
As he spun at incredible speed, the scenery was engulfed in
darkness.
Dozens of tornadoes rose around Kuan as he returned to his
position.
"Raaaah!"
The 12-meter-tall giant fell step by step, as if descending
stairs.
He cut the giant's ankle, knee, calf, then waist, chest, and
neck.
Everything was swept away by Kuan's seated, spinning sword wind.
'Tch! I can't even complain.'
Having confirmed this, Sein looked forward again.
The capital's main gate was now in sight.
Kangnan increased his speed to catch up with Gaold.
'President.'
For 20 years, Kangnan had watched Gaold's back as he chased Miro.
'Just once… We've come this far, so just once…'
Could he run alongside him?
There would never be another chance to run while looking at his
back.
Boom!
Before Kangnan's thoughts ended, the wall ahead flew inward.
Then all the walls within sight began to collapse backward.
'This is insane. He's already surpassed human limits.'
Even Kangnan couldn't gauge Gaold's pain threshold anymore.
And as the distance closed, the phenomenon intensified.
'How much pain is he in?'
Kangnan finally caught up with Gaold.
"President, now…"
But when she looked at Gaold's face, she couldn't say anything.
"Sob! Sob!"
He was crying.
Neither the face of a demon nor a madman could be seen now.
It was pure, genuine emotion.
Finally, he had arrived.
It was the catharsis of releasing all the pent-up frustration of 20 long
years.
"Sob! Sob!"
Gaold gritted his teeth and ran.
The tears streaming down his face flew off and touched Kangnan.
'Yes.'
Kangnan slowly reduced her speed, biting her lip.
With a bitter smile, she watched Gaold's back come into view
again.
'Go, Gaold.'
"Raaaah!"
Gaold released the pent-up frustration he had never been able to
express.
Buildings collapsed as if trampled by a god, and all the giants charging
at him turned into a handful of flames and were buried in the ground.
The distance to Miro was now 19.4 kilometers.
