Chapter 789:
When the dancers of Aracne moved, the feathers on their costumes swayed
in dazzling patterns.
Amidst the mesmerizing scene that left onlookers in a trance, someone
suddenly appeared at Shirone's side.
"Isn't it… simply exquisite?"
It was a man in his thirties, with hair sticking out like a bird's nest
and a patchy, short beard.
"Ah, yes," Shirone replied.
"Since I was old enough to pay for my own travels, I've never once
missed this event. Is it the eyes that are pleased? No, it's the mind. Though it has one fatal
flaw."
"A flaw?"
The man gave a sorrowful expression.
"I never got married."
As Shirone tried to step away, the man grabbed his arm and pulled him
close, whispering,
"They're not just beautiful. The women who enter this competition are
the ultimate brides—intelligent, graceful, and strong. How could I marry anyone else after seeing them?
People tell me to stop dreaming, but isn't that what dreams are for?"
This self-proclaimed beauty pageant fanatic seemed to have endured a
lifetime of frustration.
"Want to know my dream? It's shockingly realistic. I just want to go on
a date with one of them. I even started saving up."
Shirone realized he'd been caught in the wrong conversation.
"They appear in my dreams every night. If I could go on one date with
the most beautiful woman in the world, I'd be fine dying the next day. That's what a dream is! The
grandest dream a man could have!"
"Um, sorry, I've got something urgent…"
"You have to come to the competition. Take this."
As the man unzipped his crossbody bag and rummaged inside, Shirone waved
his hands.
"No, no need, really—"
He thought the man was offering a ticket, but Shirone could come and go
as he pleased if he wanted to.
"Here, take it."
What the man pulled out wasn't a ticket, but a generic flyer anyone
could grab off the street.
"…Ah. Thanks."
The man turned like a robot and cupped his hands around his mouth to
shout at the parade.
"I love you, Miss Märchen!"
Silently slipping away, Shirone found a quiet spot and let out a
sigh.
There really are all kinds of people in the world.
On the road toward the royal palace, he glanced at the flyer and noticed
portraits of previous winners.
Wow, they're really beautiful.
Everyone has different tastes, but it was clear these women had
exceptional looks.
"Huh?"
But what caught Shirone's eye wasn't the portraits—it was the list of
singers invited from various countries.
"Maya?"
Even squinting closely, the name of the singer from Tormia was
unmistakably Maya.
Come to think of it…
He recalled Iruki mentioning she had signed with a famous agency. Now
his interest in the pageant was piqued.
I should check this out.
If it really was the Maya he knew, Shirone decided he'd make time to go
see her perform.
I hope it's her.
Just as he resumed walking with that hopeful thought, a commotion broke
out at the tail end of the parade.
"Cancel the beauty pageant!"
People in black masks and clothing were marching with signs raised
high.
"Stop commodifying human beauty! The Aracne government must repatriate
all contestants immediately!"
Just reading the slogans made it clear what this was about.
They're causing a stir here, too.
Tormia had its own beauty pageant, and every year it drew criticism from
certain groups.
I understand the sentiment, but…
One thing Shirone had realized in his life: there are so many kinds of
people in this world, and there's no system that can please everyone.
"Ugh. My head hurts."
Humans…
Even Buddha and Yahweh couldn't solve that riddle.
At Aracne's royal palace, the officials who had worked overtime were
hoarse from shouting since dawn.
"Faster! There's still dust on this!"
A record-breaking cleaning operation was underway because they had
received intelligence that one of the Five Stars of the Ivory Tower was coming.
This is killing me! We didn't even do this much when actual kings
visited.
Even low-ranking nobles were armed with dusters, and the maids were
drenched in sweat.
"Nggh!"
Few had escaped the labor, but those who had were no better off
mentally.
"Why does it have to be a Star?"
King Faronica of Aracne slammed the armrest as he sat in the Grand Hall,
dressed in full formal attire.
He wore gold earrings that hung down to his shoulders and had a firm,
commanding aura—blood from both the continent and the south.
"Your Majesty, please be patient. A Star is not someone you can host
lightly. Their presence is symbolic."
Though Aracne was part of the Seven Star Alliance, it wasn't
particularly stronger than the other Ten Island Kingdoms.
The name "Kartsia" referred to all ten, with Aracne as their
representative.
"Well, yes, but still…"
So having a Star visit would boost Aracne's stature across the entire
archipelago.
"I'm a practical man."
Everyone knew the Valkyries of the Holy War were fiercely resisting the
expansion of the Gustav Empire.
This must be war-related.
If the Holy War was involved, Aracne would have the right to request
appropriate compensation.
So that's why they sent a Star. This way, there's no way we benefit
directly.
Uorin was always a clever negotiator.
"And besides…"
The steward spoke again.
"There's a risk the kingdom's biggest event may be disrupted. But if
it's a Star… we may be able to gain something in return."
"You mean the terrorist threats? Hm."
"I've heard that Shirone, this Star, isn't as strict as the others.
There's a rumor that he accepted a massive sum from South Aymond in exchange for giving them control
over the north. So he's not entirely immune to material incentives."
"But this isn't South Aymond. We can't afford that kind of money just
for this."
"Of course, Your Majesty. But perhaps we can offer something other than
money to win him over."
"Something else… I see. Is it possible?"
The steward gave a sly smile.
"Leave it to me."
He exited the Grand Hall and made his way to the office of the director
overseeing the World Beauty Pageant.
"Welcome, Steward. I know how busy you must be—I'm honored you've come
here personally…"
"I have a request."
An uneasy feeling crept in.
"A request, sir?"
"Miss Aracne. Barho Langie. We'd like to use her for something…
important."
The unease turned into dread.
"…Is this for the Star of the Ivory Tower?"
"You catch on quickly. I like that."
The director shook his head.
"That's exactly why I object. Having Miss Aracne entertain a foreign
dignitary would lower our national dignity."
"Who do you think made her Miss Aracne in the first place? It was His
Majesty's order. Prepare her. Now."
"Isn't there anyone else? That girl clawed her way here, enduring
humiliation the entire way, just for that title. She's never been asked to do this before."
"Listen here."
The steward's eyes turned cold, and the director instinctively shrank
back.
"Am I asking you to die? To pluck a star from the sky? This is doable.
Are you waiting for a sword to your throat before you act?"
The director bit his lip and lowered his head.
"I'll do my best to convince her. But if she refuses…"
The steward turned his back and opened the door with a chilling
remark.
"She'd be wise to listen while it's still a request."
Meanwhile, Shirone had arrived at the royal palace and was being greeted
by nobles and officials as he stepped into the Grand Hall.
"Welcome, oh great Star of Wisdom."
When Faronica rose from his seat, his towering build—easily a head
taller than Shirone—was fully revealed.
"Yes, it's an honor to meet you. I'm Shirone."
Even from the brevity of his introduction, it was clear he was quite
different from the other Stars.
"Haha! As expected, your spirit is admirable. Come, this way. A modest
banquet has been prepared."
Though a 10-meter-long table was laden with all kinds of dishes, only
two people were there to enjoy the meal.
Shirone sat down without comment. There was no point in nitpicking the
small details.
"As you probably saw on your way here, the parade is in full swing. If
we had known in advance, we would have gone to greet you personally."
"No need. It was fun seeing something so lively after a long time. It
looks like a wonderful festival."
There was nothing Shirone hated more than fighting demons.
"That's a relief to hear. The whole world is uneasy because of the war
with the demons, but that's all the more reason to live with joy."
The World Beauty Pageant was a major cultural event that brought huge
revenue to the Kingdom of Aracne.
"The reason I came here is…"
Shirone cut to the chase, explaining everything except the most
sensitive classified details.
"I'd like the Aracne government to assist in accessing asylum
records."
"Of course. We'll gladly help. However, with only a name and a physical
description, it will take some time. We'll need to review all refugee records from the past ten
years."
"We're short on time. How long do you think it'll take?"
"As you may know, Aracne supports open asylum. Still, I'll mobilize all
available personnel and have it ready by tomorrow morning."
That was an acceptable timeline—but the fact that they were offering
sensitive state information so readily raised some suspicion.
This is going way too smoothly.
Still, since Aracne had promised their full cooperation, waiting
patiently was the best course.
"You're free to use any facility in Bengol. We've reserved the hotel's
suite for you—please make yourself comfortable."
"Thank you. I appreciate the hospitality."
While Shirone traveled to the hotel by private carriage, the manager of
the beauty pageant arrived at the residence of Miss Aracne.
"Langie, I'm coming in."
As he opened the door, a breathtaking woman—who could easily be mistaken
for a brown-skinned angel—was brushing up her eyelashes.
Miss Aracne, Barho Langie.
Born to a mid-level military family, she had studied history before
stepping into the world of beauty pageants.
"What's going on? You're just showing up out of nowhere?"
Langie's indifferent tone as she focused on her makeup made the manager
abandon all attempts at subtlety.
"You're going to be entertaining a guest."
Her hand froze mid-application of mascara. But that was the only sign of
a reaction—no drama, no outcry.
"Why? Is it some president from another country?"
The manager clenched his teeth.
"We wouldn't even ask this for a president."
Langie resumed her makeup.
"If I don't want to, I can say no, right? I mean, of course I don't want
to. But I'll need to hear it myself to be sure."
"What about the aftermath?"
"You're Miss Aracne. If you say no, no one can do a thing. But if this
gets out, the kingdom's biggest project will be humiliated."
The manager let out a heavy sigh, understanding how difficult this
situation truly was.
"Then I'll take that as a refusal and—"
"I'll do it."
Langie stood, her makeup finished.
"Are you serious?"
"Let's not kid ourselves. I know better than anyone how terrifying
politicians can be. Better to just shut your eyes and get it over with."
As she walked past the manager, she tossed her hair back.
"So, where am I going? Got a profile or something?"
Embarrassed that he had already prepared it, the manager handed over the
file with a strained voice.
"Just… don't leave any marks. The pageant's coming up."
That was the most he could offer in terms of 'consideration.' Langie's
lips curled coldly as she flipped through the dossier.
"Hm?"
Expecting someone of high rank from an empire, she blinked at the
profile.
"A… mage?"
