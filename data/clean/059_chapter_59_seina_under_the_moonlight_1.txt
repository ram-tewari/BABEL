Chapter 59:
Seina Under the Moonlight (1)
Armin reassured Shirone with a gentle smile.
"It's only natural. If you could control it from the beginning, you wouldn't have
opened the Immortal Function. The surreal feeling you're experiencing is what you feel toward yourself. It's
because you don't yet understand the enlightenment gained through that vast expansion."
"How can I understand it?"
"You'll realize it naturally. You just can't yet put a name to what you've already
attained."
"But……."
Seeing Shirone's uneasy expression, Armin was reminded of his own past. No matter how
much someone told him he'd understand in time, it was hard to believe it would ever feel real.
"Alright. Then I'll tell you. The name of what Shirone has realized is gauge
symmetry."
"Gauge…… symmetry?"
"This world should be perfectly balanced. But in reality, symmetry is broken. You've
directly experienced that dissonance. That's likely the cause of your surreal feeling."
Shirone repeated the term "gauge symmetry" like a mantra. But nothing magical
came to mind.
Armin patted Shirone's head and spoke.
"There's no need to worry about anything. It doesn't mean you should do nothing, but
you'll eventually reach it. When that time comes, you'll understand what I mean."
Shirone took a deep breath and nodded. Though his mind was still unsettled, he knew
today's meeting would become a major turning point in his future.
"Thank you so much. If I hadn't met you, Armin, I might have fallen into Overflow as
Shiira-sensei warned."
"Unlockers share a deep bond because they resonate on the same wavelength. Though
sometimes that bond leads to big fights. Still, I'm glad I could help."
Armin smiled warmly at Shiira.
"I think it's time to leave the Room of Time now. I need to finish the painting
before my wife returns."
"Ah, now that you mention it, you said today was the last day?"
"Yes. Though I have a grandiose title, my main profession is still a painter. I'll
start packing today and leave in a few days. I won't be able to see Shiira for a while."
"Wouldn't it be better to talk to her directly……?"
Armin shook his head.
"Shiira is still suffering. Even if I hadn't given up magic, her lost eye wouldn't
grow back. Besides, I'm already married. I want to help her live her own life."
Shirone understood Armin's feelings. He didn't want to see his beloved younger sister
trapped in the past.
"Alright. I'll keep it a secret too. Thank you so much for today."
"I enjoyed it too, Shirone."
Armin covered his eyes with the cloth again. As he sat back at the canvas, time began to
flow. The sounds of Nade and Iruki's breathing returned, and life returned to Shiira's smile.
Shirone felt as if he had awoken from a dream. It was hard to believe he had just been
talking to Armin.
Time flew by in his heightened emotions. Armin finished the painting and turned the
canvas toward Shiira.
"How is it? Does it look good?"
"Yeah, it's pretty."
"Thanks for putting up with me. It was an honor to paint you."
"Oppa. We're not strangers……."
A fleeting look of regret passed through Shiira's eyes. Then, hearing footsteps from
downstairs, she startled up without even a chance to say goodbye. Was his wife already back? It was earlier
than usual, but she didn't have the presence of mind to dwell on it.
"Oppa. I'll get going now. You all should leave too."
Shiira tried to flee as if running away. Seeing her, who was always so dignified at
school, cower just because Armin's wife was coming, made Shirone's group feel sour inside.
"Darling, are you painting?"
Keira peeked her head in. Contrary to expectations of someone as calm as Armin, she was a
lively woman in her early twenties. Her expression turned startled when she met Shiira's eyes.
"Oh my, Shiira? Now that I think about it, today was your day to visit."
"Hello, Keira. It's been a while."
"It has. Why is it so hard to see you? Are you avoiding me on purpose? Hohoho!"
Shiira clung to her fading composure.
"Ah, I'm done with Oppa's work. I'll be going now."
"Since you're here, why the rush? Stay for dinner."
"Thank you for the offer, but I have plans……."
"Hmm. That's suspicious. Darling, did you do something shady with Shiira while I was
gone?"
"Keira, watch your words."
Armin's brow furrowed. Even Shirone thought it was uncalled for. The teacher had been
careful with her demeanor from start to finish—how could she accuse her of something shady?
Shiira's face flushed red as she half-heartedly put on her coat and left the room.
Shirone's group, feeling upset, glared at Keira as they followed. But whether she was oblivious or just
good-natured, Keira waved with a teasing smile.
"Bye, kids. Next time you come, I'll make something delicious for you."
Of course, there was no response. Their cold attitude made Keira tilt her head and
mutter.
"Huh? Did I make a mistake? Why are they like that? Did you really do something
weird here, darling?"
"Stop joking and come down. I want to say goodbye properly."
"Tch. You're hopeless when it comes to your little sister."
Armin quickly descended the stairs. Shiira, feeling it wouldn't do to leave like this,
waited outside the door.
"Sorry about that, Shiira. My wife's always been a handful."
"It's fine. Anyway, if we part now, who knows when we'll meet again. Take care. Stay
healthy."
"Yeah. You too, work hard. Seeing you get along with your students puts my mind at
ease. Honestly, I was worried if you'd do well. Hahaha!"
Shiira let out a small laugh. Armin was probably the only person in the world who could
treat her like a child. He was the only one who shared memories of her foolish younger days.
Shirone's chest tightened. He faintly understood why the kingdom's recognized genius had
decided to become a teacher, why she was so obsessed with Overflow, and why she kept men at arm's length
even after earning the title "Frozen Shiira."
"Then I'll be going. Write to me if you have time."
"Sure. I'll let you know once I'm settled. And Shirone, Nade, Iruki. Do your best.
I'll be listening from afar for news of your growth as great mages."
"Yes. Sorry for today."
Shirone's group expressed their sincere gratitude. Without Armin's consideration, things
wouldn't have ended so smoothly.
Armin stood there until Shiira was out of sight. Keira, who had been leaning against the
door with her arms crossed, spoke with a cold expression.
"Armin, snap out of it. If the higher-ups find out you're lost in sentimentality,
who knows what'll happen."
Armin also turned to Keira with a displeased expression. Though they had lived as a
married couple for three years, he couldn't bring himself to like her.
"Whatever feelings I have are my own. Just focus on your mission report. Is the
investigation done?"
"Perfectly. We have no more business in Creas. We need to reach Calberra within 15
days. We'll pass through dangerous areas, but with Armin the Radiant, it shouldn't be a problem,
right?"
"No need for unnecessary chatter. I'll keep my promise."
Armin cut her off, but Keira wasn't reassured. Though he had been scouted for his
exceptional insight and genius-level magic, he was a free spirit who could leave the organization anytime.
"You didn't tell Shiira, did you?"
Armin didn't answer.
"I don't want to harm you. Of course, I know no method can trap the Eternal
Contemplator, but you're still just human. The moment you betray the organization, everyone you love will
experience hell. Shiira, even the Olipher faction."
It was a chilling threat—and the truth. The organization Keira belonged to had more than
enough power, authority, and cruelty to make it happen.
No matter what, he had to protect Shiira. In that sense, meeting Shirone had been
fortunate. Today's unexpected variable, which no one could have predicted, would someday turn the tide in
his favor.
"Don't worry."
Armin smiled at Keira.
"Shiira knows nothing."
***
Shirone's group, returning to school, anxiously watched Shiira's demeanor.
While the mood had been fine with Armin, there was no telling when her feelings might
change.
