Chapter 7:
A Life-Risking Opportunity (3)
From the next day onward, Shirone began working at the library. Once he started, he
realized why this job had to be done alone.
The document Temuran handed him listed hundreds of titles, and the categorization
criteria were subjective.
For example, should *The History of Swordsmanship* be classified as history or
swordsmanship?
Though Temuran had provided a basic system, dividing ten thousand books required many
arbitrary judgments.
Shirone decided to prioritize. History was broad, while swordsmanship was specialized, so
*The History of Swordsmanship* was categorized under swordsmanship. Just establishing these standards took
two weeks.
But even this task was beneficial to Shirone.
While creating the categorization system, he realized that all knowledge is
interconnected. Music and mathematics, chemistry and the mind, art and science—they weren't so different.
Once the system was in place, the work gained momentum. As he began meeting the targets
on time, Shirone finally turned his attention to studying.
Knowledge was the greatest divider between nobles and commoners. The knowledge he'd gain
here would be immensely helpful in the future.
Shirone decided to use his time efficiently. With ten thousand books before him, even two
years wouldn't be enough to read them all.
*Where should I start? Should I just read whatever comes my way?*
A good idea struck Shirone, and he slapped his knee. Since all knowledge is connected,
mastering one field would make it easier to supplement others later.
"Then which field should I focus on?"
After much thought, Shirone's lips curled into a smile.
"Study history."
If human life is history, then knowledge has also evolved alongside it. Learning history
first was like building the spine of a creature.
In his mind, Shirone drew a horizon of history. Zooming in, that line contained all human
knowledge—religion, science, magic, mythology, literature, art, politics, war, economics—arranged
chronologically.
"Mastering history will allow me to supplement other knowledge anytime. No, it'll
make combining them much faster. History is the spine of knowledge."
The grand library held roughly 850 history books. Shirone made a decision. *In two years,
I'll read all 850 of these books. I'll build the spine of knowledge!*
***
From that day on, Shirone stayed in the library to read even after work ended.
Though he skipped roll calls under the pretext of working overtime, no one except Temuran
cared about a temporary worker like Shirone.
Reading history books was honestly grueling. Having lived in the mountains for 16 years,
he had no context for the continent's unfamiliar place names, people, or the events tied to them.
He considered memorizing everything but soon shook his head. *Memorization records in the
mind, but understanding records in the heart. Even a single line of events must be fully understood to build
the spine of knowledge.*
Yet, understanding history was backbreaking labor.
It wasn't just about events—he had to meticulously analyze why these events were
inevitable and how they influenced future ones.
His efficiency dropped drastically, and at one point, he couldn't finish a single book in
a week. He often slept in the library, collapsing unconscious more often than not.
4 AM.
The iron door opened, and Temuran entered, holding a crystal lamp. Shirone didn't notice,
but Temuran had been coming at this hour every day to monitor him.
Temuran picked up the book Shirone had dropped while reading. Despite the content not
being particularly difficult, the book hadn't changed in half a month.
Temuran scoffed. *If he'd been frantically studying practical books, I'd have considered
banning him from entering outside work hours.*
But now, it's clear he's completely clueless. Just because he can read a little, he's
dreaming absurd dreams. What a fool.
"As expected. What success could a lowborn possibly achieve?"
With a lighter heart, Temuran turned away. To rise through studying, you need luck, but
more importantly, brains. Shirone, with his grand dreams, was already doomed.
***
Three months had passed since Shirone began working at the library.
As he grew accustomed to the categorization work, his time management became more
relaxed, and he learned many things from the servants.
The most shocking revelation was that the nobles didn't care at all whether the books
were leaked or not.
After all, the family's secret techniques were only passed down directly, and nobles had
so many responsibilities that they couldn't care less about a few missing books.
That didn't make Shirone any less at risk. The nobles' leniency was built on the loyalty
of rigid commoners. The servants could be ruthless to prevent even the slightest mistake.
Shirone felt firsthand how vastly different the mindsets of the two classes were. What
was trivial to nobles could be a life-or-death matter for commoners.
At noon, the library door suddenly burst open, and a boy's voice shattered the silence.
"Ugh, damn old man. What's wrong with taking a break on a day like this?"
Shirone blinked at the uninvited guest.
It was the family head's youngest son, Ozent Rian. True to his martial lineage, he had a
robust southern face and stood a head taller than Shirone, despite being the same age.
A loud shout came from outside the library.
"Rian! How dare you run away? If I catch you, it's an extra hundred laps in the
garden!"
"Yikes! Damn it!"
Rian fidgeted, darting between bookshelves before spotting Shirone. Without giving him
time to react, Rian shook him and yelled.
"Hey! Don't tell anyone I'm here. Got it?"
Before Shirone could reply, Rian hid under a bookshelf. Simultaneously, the door opened,
and an old man's roar shook the library.
"Riaan! I know you're hiding here!"
Though white-haired, the old man had broad shoulders and stood as tall as a giant. His
white beard reached his chest as he glared at Shirone with menacing eyes.
"You! Did a green-haired peanut-sized brat come in here?"
Shirone wondered if he knew such a person. He knew a blue-haired boy, but if that was
considered peanut-sized, how did the old man perceive him?
"Judging by the description, it sounds like the young master. Excuse me, but who are
you?"
Shirone planned to buy time by invoking the family's authority. But the old veteran, who
had commanded battlefields for decades, saw right through him.
"Swordsmanship instructor! The only one who can beat that peanut into shape! Stop
wasting time and answer me. Unless you want a beating yourself."
Shirone swallowed his words. To those who sought knowledge, a teacher was as revered as
the heavens. Especially one entrusted by the family head—no servant's wordplay would work on him.
"Ah, I see. The young master is hiding under that bookshelf."
"Hey! Are you crazy? Want to die?"
Rian crawled out, scowling. But when he saw the swordsmanship instructor right in front
of him, he trembled like a leaf.
"Master, this is a misunderstanding!"
"You little—!"
Grabbed by the collar, Rian was slammed to the ground.
As Rian's body flew in an arc, Shirone winced. Rian sprawled on the floor, limbs
twitching.
"Follow me, you brat. Forget training—you need to rebuild your mental discipline
first."
Dragged away by his leg, Rian craned his neck to look at Shirone. He mouthed something
silently.
Though no sound came out, Shirone heard his voice loud and clear.
*You're dead.*
