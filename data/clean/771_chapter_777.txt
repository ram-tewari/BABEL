Chapter 777:
Upon arriving in Zion, Miro convened a meeting among the monks
representing each sect.
With the Buddha no longer present, it was the perfect opportunity to
strike against evil, and the operation needed to be swift and strategic.
Since it marked the beginning of a war with no end in sight, the meeting
took a considerable amount of time.
Meanwhile, Gaold and his companions were briefed on Zion and reunited
with Armin and Kuan.
"Is that Gaold?"
All the monks' attention was focused on him.
The human who defeated the Buddha.
They'd hear the full story from Miro eventually, but the younger
acolytes couldn't suppress their curiosity.
Though their minds were unclouded and their grasp of the Law strong,
they were still learning the true depths of enlightenment.
"What lies beyond the Buddha's enlightenment?"
At first, Gaold didn't even acknowledge them. But as their numbers kept
growing, he finally stopped walking.
It's happening!
The space became so quiet, even breathing was imperceptible.
"Sit on the toilet," Gaold said. "And push your stomach with all your
strength."
The monks leaned in, determined not to miss a single word.
"What comes out… is enlightenment."
Turning coldly, Gaold walked off, leaving the young monks scratching
their heads and whispering to each other.
"This is… quite a profound Zen teaching."
A bald boy rested his chin on his hand, murmuring.
"Hmm… sit on the toilet, strain your stomach, and what comes out is
enlightenment…"
Then he perked up and said,
"Poop?"
Enlightenment is poop.
"Maybe it means something like this: Consider enlightenment as trivial
as poop. In other words, don't become attached to it."
They were determined to interpret the words of the one who had defeated
the Buddha in the most meaningful way possible.
"Right. Release the enlightenment within you like poop—then you will
attain true enlightenment."
The children were amazed.
"Ooh, wise insight indeed! So… one must cast off the Buddha to truly
embrace the Buddha?"
As the murmurs of the acolytes filled the room, Kangnan, who had been
deep in thought, asked Gaold:
"What did you actually mean by that?"
Gaold, clearly annoyed, muttered,
"What else? I meant go take a damn dump."
"Ahahahaha!"
Kangnan's laughter rang through the cold sky.
"Uh? What's up with her?"
As the young monks all turned to look at Kangnan, Liria—a senior of
Zion—approached.
"Gaold was joking."
She was a totemist from the Archeanis Order, who had helped seal Optros
alongside Dante at the ancient ruins.
"Joking? You mean he lied?"
As the monks pouted and looked toward Gaold, Liria smiled with her
eyes.
"That's not the point. What matters is that you came to your own
realization through his words."
"……"
"Enlightenment isn't something grand or glamorous. It's simply seeing
what was always there."
To a child of Zenan, even breathing is a massive revelation.
"Gaold already understands that. So whether he speaks truth or
falsehood—it no longer matters."
In the end, all paths converge into one.
"Enlightenment is… poop…"
As the monks gazed at Gaold in awe, Liria quietly stepped away.
They'll become great powers in this world.
With that thought, she entered one of Zion's small sanctuaries—only to
bump into Miro, just leaving the meeting.
"Thank you for your hard work, Miss Miro."
Miro returned the polite bow.
"Yes. Where's Gaold?"
"He just returned to his quarters. A formidable man. I'm sure the forces
of evil will continue to weaken."
Miro let out a soft sigh.
"Is something troubling you?"
"Well… actually…"
Realizing this wasn't something to say in public, Miro gently grabbed
Liria's hand and led her somewhere quieter.
Miro wasn't one to bare her heart to others—but the issue with Gaold was
an exception.
If it's Liria…
Most of Zion's monks were close to the state of Virtua—there was no need
for secrecy.
"I see."
After listening to their complicated circumstances, Liria nodded with a
sympathetic expression.
"You feel guilty, don't you?"
"I don't know. This whole thing is so tangled. I need Gaold's help… but
this doesn't feel right."
"Of course not."
Liria was firm.
"What separates good from evil is courage. Miro, I think you need to
tell him."
The shadow on Miro's face lifted slightly.
"Though… maybe it's easy for me to say as an outsider…"
"No. I need to go see him now."
Liria smiled and raised her hands in a shrug.
"Enjoy your time."
No matter how much one fights for the world—how can one be human without
love?
"Thank you, Liria."
As Miro left, Liria crossed her legs at the now quiet table and rested
her chin in her hand.
"Sigh… I want to fall in love too."
A lifetime of chasing down evil, and she'd never properly been with a
man. Dante's face drifted through her mind.
He's probably forgotten about me. I hope he's doing okay…
"Whatever. Just go. Straight ahead."
She had made her decision—no more hesitating—but Miro's heart pounded as
she approached Gaold's room.
"I can't stand this pressure."
She was going to open up, pour her heart out, and make him choose—her,
or the world.
And if he chooses me…
Miro froze in her tracks, face burning.
But what if I'm… totally weird?
Sure, love is about feelings—but showing them openly like this was a
first for her.
The more she ran simulations in her head, the more frustrated she
became. Finally, she marched forward again.
Whatever! I'll just turn off the lights!
But when she reached Gaold's room, she found Kangnan, Zulu, Armin, and
Sein all gathered outside.
"Huh? What's going on?"
"Miro."
Armin scratched his head awkwardly.
"Why are you all standing here? Where's Gaold?"
Sein answered.
"He says he's leaving. He's packing now."
Miro's eyes widened.
"At this hour? Where is he going?"
"The battlefield."
Even before Miro had a chance to present him with a choice, Gaold had
already made his decision.
"It's because of you."
Kangnan glared.
"You didn't say a single kind word to him since we got here. How can you
be so selfish? Is that what you call goodness?"
Goodness is goodness. Love is love.
"No one dates out of compassion! I'm just following my feelings. And if
I don't choose Gaold, isn't that even better for you?"
"Ghk!"
Unable to argue, Kangnan bit her lip. Miro closed her eyes, regretting
her harsh words.
This is why I hate emotions.
Unlike beliefs, they cloud everything.
"I'm sorry. I know I've been awful. But I've made up my mind. I'll tell
him."
Grabbing the doorknob, Miro said:
"Everyone… would you give us a moment?"
That was explanation enough. Kangnan turned away with a sad expression,
and the others followed.
"Phew…"
Steadying herself, Miro opened the door—and there was Gaold, packing his
bag.
"What the hell? No knock?"
"Hmph! Like I care what you were doing!"
Gaold zipped up his bag and placed it on the bed.
"Don't bother. You have something you need to do. Just do that—don't
think about anything else."
Miro, closing the door behind her with her back, asked,
"Then what about you?"
"……"
"You're going to keep fighting for me like this? Enduring horrible pain?
And for what?"
"It's not your fault."
For the first time, Gaold looked her in the eye.
"It's not your fault that I'm in pain. That I've become like this. Let
go of me from your heart. That way, you can be complete."
"That's why I'm asking! What happens to you?"
"I'll be fine."
His gaze was warm.
"I'm not the same useless guy I used to be. Now I can fight for you.
That's all there is—take down the evil in this world."
Miro couldn't hold back her emotions anymore.
"...And if you die?"
The tears came, unstoppable.
"What if you just die like that? After suffering your whole life—what
was your life even for? What kind of life is that?"
"I won't die."
Even his smile looked painful.
"Until you can laugh with joy—until the day you look at me with
love—I'll never die."
Miro sat down on the bed and gently cupped Gaold's hair in her
hands.
Yeah, that's right.
That's the kind of person Gaold was.
A follower of Yora.
A naive young man who just wished for everyone's hearts to be full of
love.
I should've known back then…
But it was that talentless young man who survived the hell of Extreme
Heat and confessed to her.
At the very least, I should've known then.
She should have realized the day she said goodbye to the world, when
even with his limbs torn apart, he shouted her name to the very end.
God, how stupid am I?
Why did she ever think this man was a fool?
After fighting through twenty years of unrelenting agony, he was the one
who pulled her from Heaven's grip.
He killed God…
And killed again.
And killed again!
Even Buddha had fallen before him—and now, he was saying he'd kill all
the evil in the world for the woman he loved.
"That's what makes you who you are."
Miro pulled Gaold's face into her arms and cried out through her
tears,
"Because you're the strongest person in the whole damn world!"
And so—
"Until this war is over… I…"
Miro looked up at the ceiling and declared,
"I will never love you. I'll erase you completely from my heart."
From within her arms, Gaold replied,
"…Okay."
"But I'll make a promise."
Sitting up, Miro gently stroked Gaold's cheek.
"If I can't love you, then I won't love anyone. No one else will ever
have my heart."
So—
"You're free to meet someone else. If you want to give up on a woman
like me, then do it—anytime. If there's even a moment you can be happy, don't hesitate. Take that
happiness. Promise me."
"…Okay."
If that's what it takes for you to forget me.
"Gaold… Gaold…"
Miro, gently brushing his closed eyes, wiped away her tears and fled the
room.
Only after the door slammed shut did Gaold slowly open his eyes.
For a brief moment, sorrow surged so powerfully it nearly burst from
him—only to cool like a dud firework.
"All I have to do… is end the war."
For her, and her alone.
With that one clear objective embedded in his heart, Gaold stood like a
machine, slinging his bag over his shoulder.
It was already dawn. As he stepped outside, the air was cold enough to
freeze the breath in his lungs.
"……"
Under a lit lamppost in the middle of the glacier, Zulu and Kangnan
stood, already prepped for departure.
Gaold approached and said,
"From now on, this is my fight."
Kangnan smirked.
"As if it was ever not your fight. Seriously… annoying jerk…"
Ugly duckling.
Gaold smiled and patted her shoulder, then turned to Zulu, who had
prepared Kaidra.
"Where's the fiercest battle?"
Zulu replied simply, as she'd been told:
"The South."
The demons weren't particularly strong there, but humanity's forces were
especially weak.
"I see."
Gaold turned around and said,
"Let's go, mutt."
Kangnan, radiating the fiery spirit of a Ram-Muai warrior, curled up her
lips in a grin and strode forward.
"Now that's clean."
No matter who Gaold loved, she was the only one who could cross the
River of Death by his side.
