"What have you done to shout like that? Do you think there's a man

"Are you okay? You look tense. Are you worried Amy might

"If she fails, she fails. It just means she's not good enough. I'm

The special talent of Carmis's red eyes was something others envied, but

"It's been a while, Shakora."
Iruki's father, Albino, approached with Argannes.
"Yes, how have you been?"

"Sorry, my wife is very cheerful."
"Oh, it's fine. I'm a fan of Isis too. I was so sad when you

"How is Amy? Is she well prepared?"

"Haha, you're too modest. After all, she's a Carmis."

"What's wrong with our son? If he looked like me, he'd be the best

"I heard Shirone is a commoner supported by a patron. That woman

The four of them spotted Reina but couldn't find anyone who looked like

"Ugh! Why are they so late?"
Reina checked the time and tapped her foot impatiently. Just then, the

The assistant butler, Temuran, opened the door, and Vincent and Olina

Seeing the crowd of nobles, their faces turned pale, but Reina

"Welcome, Father. I'm glad you're not late."
"Thank you for inviting us. But are you sure we should be
here…"
"What are you talking about? Shirone is taking his graduation exam.
Of course, you should be here. The participants will enter soon."

"Oh? We're already here."
Reina glared and shouted.
"Hey! When did you go to get our parents? We almost missed
Shirone!"
"Well, we made it on time. Stop nagging…"
Rian, with a greatsword on his back, got out.
With blue hair, sharp eyes like blades, and a perfectly trained body, he
exuded an overwhelming presence.
'Shirone…'
Rian looked up at the top of the colosseum where Shirone's future would
be decided.
'You really did it.'
The reality of the situation, unimaginable when he took his knight's
oath, was thrilling.
"Oh? Isn't that greatsword from the Ogent family? It looks like the
youngest son, Rian, is here."
"They call him the Knight of Mach. He's quite noted among
swordsmen."
As the nobles recognized Rian, Reina slowly lowered her pointing
finger.
"Looks like you've made a name for yourself. People recognize
you."
When he first left for knight training, it felt like they were just
sending off a younger brother.
"I don't care about my reputation. If it weren't for Shirone's
graduation exam, I wouldn't have come home."
"You brat! We spoiled you, and now you look down on the
family!"
Reina kicked Rian's thigh, but it felt like hitting a rock.
"Ouch! Are you wearing armor under your pants?"
Rian looked at her disdainfully.
"When will you grow up? You need to control your temper. Who would
want to marry you?"
Tears welled up in Reina's eyes as she rubbed her toes.
'Now he's trying to beat me with strength. It's so sad.'
The days of pulling her little brother's ear and bossing him around were
over.
"So, will you leave again after the graduation exam?"
"I have to. My life is just beginning."
"You're so busy. Where are you going this time?"
"Of course…"
Rian looked at the school and smiled.
"Wherever Shirone goes."
A loud bell rang in the dormitory hallway.
"The graduation exam is about to begin. Participants, please
proceed to the colosseum."
"Whoo!"
Shirone, who had been sitting cross-legged, opened his eyes.
He had stayed up all night, but his mind was the clearest it had been
all year.
Consciously and unconsciously, his mental preparation for today had
reached its peak.
'It's starting!'
Shirone jumped up and headed to the hallway, as if he would never
return.
His determination was absolute.
At the same time, students poured out from all directions.
Iruki and Nade walked down the hallway and nodded at Shirone.
They were friends who meant more than anything, but for now, there was
no need for words.
"Look, the graduating seniors are coming!"
As they arrived at the colosseum, parents eager to see their proud sons
and daughters gathered around.
"Mother, there's Shirone."
"Sniff!"
As soon as Olina saw her son, she burst into tears, leaving Reina
flustered.
"Mother, why are you crying? The exam hasn't even started
yet."
"I don't know… The tears just…"
Her emotions were too complex to explain.
"Try to hold back a little. Shirone needs to stay
strong."
Olina quickly wiped her tears and pressed her lips together.
'Mother. Father.'
Shirone looked at his family waiting by the south gate.
'Reina. And…'
Rian stood protectively beside them.
'You've come back.'
In a perfect mental state, free of distractions, Shirone's mind focused
solely on the facts.
With a cold expression, he turned and entered the colosseum. Rian
smiled.
"He's well-prepared."
Reina thought the same.
When they were humiliated in the Kazra Kingdom, he still seemed like a
child, but his gaze just now was no different from that of the official mages she encountered in
society.
"Parents, please enter through the east gate! Advanced class
students, use the west gate!"
Following the guide's instructions, the crowd in front of the colosseum
quickly flowed into the building.
In the waiting room, the participants used their own methods to relax,
and no one spoke.
"Whoo."
Shirone also put on the 2000 bracelet given by the staff and took deep
breaths, waiting for the time.
'It's been a while, 2000.'
The large colosseum, built to Red Line standards, was equipped with the
latest 2000 system, with a 100% synchronization rate.
The magic's power coefficient accurately impacted the mind, making it no
different from real combat.
The reason for using the 2000 system was to quantify the participants'
states, minimize physical damage during deadly magic exchanges, and ensure the safety of the
spectators.
"In 10 minutes, you will enter the colosseum."
Hearing the guide's words, an unprecedented excitement churned in
Shirone's stomach.
The fastest 10 minutes of his life passed, and the door to the colosseum
opened.
'Let's go.'
As participant number 27, Shirone was at the end of the line, but the
cheers for the leaders made it clear they were in a different world.
"Please give a round of applause to support the proud students of
Alpheas Magic School!"
"Wow!"
The applause was like a downpour of sound.
Under the golden sunlight, the audience blurred into a single
mass.
"Everyone, stand!"
As the students lined up, Principal Alpheas took the stage.
"I commend all of you who have reached this point."
While the opening speech continued, Shirone scanned the audience.
Advanced class students and parents were seated, with teachers on the
opposite side, and scouts from around the world in the VIP section.
'So this is the graduation exam.'
The six evaluation categories were: Combat, Special Skills, Hill
Occupation, Magic Utilization, Tactical Strategy, and Survival (Enemy Annihilation). Special Skills and
Magic Utilization were non-combat, while the other four were combat-related.
The 4:2 ratio showed that the Red Line valued combat ability slightly
more in their evaluations.
After Olivia's explanation, the final event, the oath, began.
"One! We are children of intellect! One! We never give up under any
circumstances!"
In the height of exhilaration, Shirone couldn't even hear his own
voice.
'Finally! Finally!'
His journey from climbing over the Alpheas Magic School wall at twelve
to today flashed before his eyes.
"We will now begin the graduation exam!"
The 30 students took positions 15 meters apart along the colosseum's
inner wall.
A massive hologram covered the sky, displaying the six categories on a
roulette wheel. The central pointer spun rapidly.
As the category was decided, the 2000 system activated, and Shirone
prepared to enter the Spirit Zone at unprecedented speed.
'Combat. Special Skills. Hill Occupation…'
Tracking the categories with his eyes, the pointer finally
stopped.
"Wow!"
The cheers shook the colosseum, and like a switch had been flipped,
Shirone and everyone else had eyes like predators.
'It's starting!'
