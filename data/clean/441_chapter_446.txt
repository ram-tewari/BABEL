Chapter 446:
Two iron wheel eyes interlock to form the world, and within it,
everything changes.
This is the Sun and Moon Halos.
It is the technique that made Sein the strongest in the Black Line
mental series, allowing him to perfectly control the environment within the range of the equalizing
horizontal value.
In other words, if the horizontal value is 100, it is possible to reduce
speed to 1 while increasing strength to 99.
The transformation values are infinite depending on the settings, so the
actual mechanism is not as intuitive, but it holds formidable potential to defeat even far superior
opponents depending on how it is handled.
New program detected. Scanning.
Babel's halo immediately recognized the changes in the world.
The biggest change was that Sein's speed now surpassed hers.
Sein hadn't gotten faster, nor had Babel slowed down.
To be precise, it was the result of equalizing the relative speeds of
the two entities.
Babel's lethal response methods, which had numbered 2.7 billion,
suddenly dropped to a mere 2 million.
It had been a long time since Babel had calculated this much for a
single entity, especially a human.
Of course, there were still 2 million ways for Babel to instantly kill
Sein.
But what made Babel truly terrifying was that it was a completely
emotionless program that didn't even allow for a sense of relief.
No complacency, no anxiety.
Babel simply repeated its calculations, relentlessly pressuring
Sein.
'This is really tough.'
Sein, who had equalized hundreds of environments to his advantage, had
to reduce his mental strength to match the horizontal value.
Even though his Spirit Zone was as vast as a Joner's, it had now shrunk
to a mere 20 square meters.
Babel wasted no time in engaging in close combat.
If the number of lethal response methods increased by 830,000, there was
no reason to hesitate.
'It's a monster. There's not a single gap.'
Even Sein, who had faced countless strong opponents, couldn't help but
be amazed by its power.
It wasn't a transcendent ability, but the combat power derived from its
flawless algorithm was comparable to that of an angel.
Kiing! Kiing!
Every time Babel's knife-hand grazed Sein's cheek, a sharp noise pierced
his eardrums.
'I'm already caught in a trap.'
Sein felt that Babel had started to become certain of something.
He couldn't hold out.
If this continued, Sein's head would be severed in 10 seconds.
Lethal response methods: 32,564.
Causes produce results, and those results become the causes of new
results.
All the causality that had occurred since the start of the battle
contributed to the current result, exponentially reducing the number of response methods.
And that meant only one thing.
'I'm going to die!'
Even as Sein realized this, the rebels couldn't move.
Only Sein was aware of the imminent death.
Boom!
A massive steel pillar struck down before him.
Before he could confirm Babel pinned to the ground, Sein looked
up.
A 30-meter-tall armored figure had struck the ground with its
arm.
It was a Titan, part of the third stage of the Mecha combat
system.
The rebel command had only three of these, each weighing nearly 3,000
tons and equipped with the most powerful heat flash cannons created by magnetic pulses, the pinnacle of
Mecha technology.
Producing one required hundreds of thousands of elixirs and thousands of
tons of minerals, and even the system's program couldn't be designed by just anyone.
If a piper was needed to operate a Guroi, a Titan couldn't be controlled
without boarding a Guroi.
It took 10 tons of force just to move one of the Titan's arms.
The power was transmitted in the order of piper, Guroi, and Titan,
making it an extremely sensitive piece of equipment despite its size, and its combat power varied
greatly depending on the pilot's skill.
The person piloting Titan No. 1, which had pinned Babel, was the genius
pilot and rebel commander, Crude.
He hadn't intervened because he knew Sein was in danger. He was
confident that he could defeat Babel now.
But that confidence was shattered moments later by the overwhelming
force that surged through the controls.
"Grrr!"
With a scraping sound, Babel lifted Titan's massive hand with both of
its own.
As Crude pulled the controls, the plasma engine roared with a powerful
noise, transmitting force.
The more he did, the faster Babel's halo spun.
Eventually, it spread like a nova, pushing the impossible weight aside
and pulling its body out.
Boom!
As Titan's hand fell again, the ground cracked, and a column of earth
shot up dozens of meters high.
Sein slid back, surveying the battlefield.
Guroi's fire net filled the sky, and the remaining two Titans fired heat
flash cannons at Babel as it flew through the air.
Among the frantically fighting soldiers were Kanya and Lena, who had
lost their father.
"Die!"
Kanya fired her weapon with tears streaming down her face.
But hitting something faster than a bullet was impossible.
Babel Program: Engaging Wide-Area Combat System.
Target: All.
Combat Pattern: Massacre.
Lethal Response Methods: 7.
With a new algorithm, Babel sped across the battlefield at a terrifying
speed.
Every Guroi in its path exploded and fell.
Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!
Every time the three Titans struck the ground, the vibrations reached
Sein's feet, dozens of meters away.
'Guroi can't defeat it. It's beyond the realm of comparable
force.'
Crude skillfully maneuvered Titan to follow Babel.
Though it was too fast for the human eye, Babel's trajectory was still
visible on the radar.
But it was only measuring inertia; hitting the target was entirely up to
the pilot.
'Here!'
Boom!
Titan's shoulders opened, revealing dark, cave-like barrels that fired
heat flash cannons.
Evasion Probability: 0%. Entering defensive mode.
Babel stopped flying and crossed its arms in an X. Simultaneously, the
immense flash slammed the metal body into the ground.
Boom!
As the ground melted like lava, Babel knelt, emitting smoke.
'Damn it! What is it made of?'
It was definitely not pure mineral.
But he couldn't think of any alloy that could have such heat
resistance.
'Ten seconds.'
If he could concentrate the energy of the heat flash cannons for more
than ten seconds, even Babel would melt.
But Crude smiled bitterly, having calculated that far.
Considering what Babel could do in ten seconds, it was obvious.
As time passed, casualties mounted.
Even though Sein had equalized all environments to Babel's disadvantage,
he was still being pushed back.
'Something's wrong. Really wrong.'
Sein realized this only after stepping back from the battle.
That machine was not made by humans.
It only looked like a machine; its internal program was far beyond human
comprehension.
An angel's creation? Or something even greater?
The strangeness Sein felt from Babel was to that extent.
Changing Combat System.
As the number of rebels decreased to the set limit, Babel finally
stopped moving.
"Now! Everyone, attack!"
Crude immediately ordered, and thousands of bullets struck Babel.
Babel's body, sparking with fire, looked like flames themselves.
Activating Map Weapon. Codename: Babel.
Finally, Babel revealed its true purpose.
As it bent its upper body like a bow, its neck detached and fell back,
and its chest opened.
A barrel embedded with a glass sphere was revealed, and with its upper
body bent back, its arms and legs anchored to the ground as supports.
Rumble!
An electrical phenomenon occurred in front of Babel's glass sphere,
aimed at Titan.
"Heat flash cannons! Direct hit!"
Sensing the danger, Crude focused the six barrels of the three
Titans.
Directly targeting the ground with wide-area weapons was not in the
combat manual, but there was no choice now.
As the six beams of the heat flash cannons converged on Babel, a massive
photon cannon fired from Babel's glass sphere.
When the two energies collided, the entire command center lit up like
midday, and the world was filled with the sound of burning.
Crude's Titan, with a hole in one shoulder, staggered as if it would
collapse.
"Grr! Damn it!"
Babel test fire complete. Engaging rapid fire.
Babel's barrel fired photon cannons in all directions, ignoring the
previous power.
It was literally a map weapon.
In less than a minute, the entire command center would be
devastated.
"Damn it! No choice!"
Sein ran towards Babel, attempting his last resort.
As he embedded the equalizing of the Sun and Moon Halos into Babel's
system, his mind vibrated, and his consciousness blurred as if in a dream.
'What is this…'
Even he, who had trained his mind countless times, was overwhelmed by
the vast and complex system.
At the center of the countless circuits, a blue light pierced
through.
"Huff, huff."
In the frozen silence of Niflheim, only the guide's breathing could be
heard.
Even he, who had contracted with the spirit of ice, couldn't hold out
much longer.
Even the 2nd-tier monster Salamander could no longer sustain its
flames.
They weren't frozen to death yet, but the shadow of frostbite was slowly
creeping up their ankles.
Kangnan barely managed to open her frozen lips.
"Where is Hell? Are we lost?"
The guide answered in a dying voice.
"I just followed what I learned. Beyond that, I don't…
know…"
The guide was paying a harsh price for his greed.
The idea of becoming the ruler of the mainland with a mountain of Black
Elixirs was nothing but a mirage in the face of reality.
"I can't go on any longer…"
Just as the guide was about to utter his last words, Zulu's steps came
to a halt.
The harsh cold had driven her body to a dreadful state, but her face
remained calm.
"Gaold, over there."
Zulu pointed to a massive shadow moving beneath the ice.
Small shadows from all directions converged and rose, forming a dome
shape that emerged above the ice.
An enemy?
Regardless, it was the first sign of change since they had dealt with
the Grim Reaper horde.
This was the reason Gaold had patiently followed the guide to this
point.
For a moment, no one moved.
It was a natural scene in Niflheim, but as the rising darkness began to
take shape, everything changed.
Rumble!
As the ice shook, Kangnam steadied herself and glared at the
darkness.
Something wearing a black robe with white stripes, standing 10 meters
tall, was rising into the sky.
Its legs were not visible, and the ends of its robe split into dozens of
tendrils that fluttered.
'This is… incredible!'
Kangnam gritted her teeth and took a combat stance.
Even the sun was darkening.
Hiss!
Hearing a sound from below, she turned to see the Salamander's flames
completely extinguished and starting to freeze.
"W-what?"
The guide let out a startled cry.
Euphoria surged from his toes, but by the time it reached his head, he
had already turned into a frozen corpse.
"Such immense magic power."
Zulu erased the Salamander's corpse and focused her mind.
What spell she would cast depended on the nature of the opponent.
"I am Hel, the god of death."
Gaold's eyebrows twitched.
"Wanderers of Niflheim, I will claim your lives."
"Kekeke."
Even in the face of an opponent far beyond the Grim Reaper, Gaold walked
forward confidently.
With the Salamander gone, she would now have to ignite her own body to
create fire.
"So that's what it was. It wasn't the region."
The king of Niflheim, known as the land of death.
That was the true identity of Hel.
