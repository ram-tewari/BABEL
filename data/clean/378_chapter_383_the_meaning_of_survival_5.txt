Chapter 383:
The Meaning of Survival (5)
Crack. Crack.
Hearing the sound of insects being crushed, Shirone slowly lifted his
head.
Fermi was walking towards him with his hands in his pockets. His face,
twisted into a smile, had already turned more than half into a skull.
As he chewed on the insects in his mouth and spat out the shells, the
students watching through the vision grimaced.
"Ugh!"
Even seeing it on the screen was hard to stomach.
"Fermi is no pushover either."
At Olivia's words, Collie nodded.
"Yes. Last year, he made it to Stage 5 and became the final
survivor. But with Shirone here this time, I can't guarantee the outcome."
"Hmm..."
Olivia's gaze remained cold.
She knew Fermi had colluded in ways unrelated to graduation. However,
without evidence, she couldn't confront him.
'He's definitely a deviant.'
If recognizing an apple as an apple is normal, recognizing an apple as a
strawberry is the deviant's perception.
Cognitive distortion to the point of denying reality.
If he wasn't an Immortal Function user like Shirone or an extreme
ascetic like Gaold, he could only be seen as a twisted human from the start.
Smack!
Shirone's right eye burst, and disgusting maggots wriggled out.
Fermi watched calmly, chewing and spitting out insects.
Yet his throat continued to itch.
'This is certainly not a pleasant feeling.'
Honestly, he hadn't expected Shirone to endure this long.
During his time in the senior class, Shirone was the first to follow him
to the end of Stage 5.
'I thought it was nothing...'
During the ghost incident, he was just a rookie. But in a year, he had
grown tremendously to reach this point.
'Shall we go a bit further...?'
Fermi had never gone beyond Stage 5. He didn't care about winning or
points, so stopping here was fine.
But it was different with Shirone.
Fermi couldn't rest until he removed any obstacles to his business by
any means necessary.
"We won't settle this here. Let's move on. What's waiting for us
next?"
Spitting out another insect, Fermi spoke.
"......"
Shirone remained silent.
The moment his natural state wavered even slightly, his mind would be
overturned like a stormy sea.
Smack!
Shirone's remaining eye burst. And with that, Stage 5, Insect King,
ended.
"Stage 6! Entering Extreme Heat!"
The usually calm evaluation teacher, Collie's voice trembled with
excitement and anxiety.
In his long teaching career, this was only the second time a student had
reached this point.
The depth of enlightenment was unrelated to the level of magic or age.
But Extreme Heat was a dangerous realm even for ascetics.
"Th-that is..."
The students' eyes reflected the shadows of flames. In a world made
entirely of fire, Shirone and Fermi faced each other.
Their clothes began to burn, and soon, fierce flames engulfed their
bodies.
"Ugh..."
The students recoiled in horror.
The sight of 3-meter-high flames completely consuming a person was
enough to feel the heat without entering the Virtual Zone.
If pain is felt through nerves, fire directly stimulates the nerves,
causing extreme pain.
The more horrifying fact was that this was a virtual world where the
body was constantly reconstituted.
Whoosh! Whoosh!
Like candles on a candlestick, the two burned. Their expressions didn't
change, but their minds screamed in agony.
Surviving the brink of death didn't mean transcending it. Humans aren't
that strong.
Pain coursed through their bodies, and fear meshed with their minds like
gears.
'This is bad. This is really dangerous.'
Shirone's face began to contort. Even with a mind of steel, it was hard
to maintain composure.
What kind of life had Gaold lived to freely create such a hell?
But even those thoughts were swept away by the overwhelming pain.
The students watching through the vision no longer cheered.
It was painful just to watch, so how must the participants feel?
Cheering them on would make them seem like psychos.
"Heh heh."
Fermi watched Shirone through the flames. Was he really trying to endure
to the end against him?
'Arrogant fool.'
Stage 6 was tough for Fermi too. If he had known it would be this hard,
he might have reconsidered.
'That's why it's even more annoying.'
Points didn't matter anymore.
The only thing that mattered was money.
He had to finish Shirone here to ensure his business for the year would
succeed.
"You're holding up well. This sauna isn't bad."
Shirone couldn't respond. He wished this were reality so his body would
burn away quickly.
But cruelly, his body was reconstituted in real-time, and his nerve
thresholds reset every moment.
"Why not give up? It's only the fourth week."
Shirone's face twisted.
His mental state had reached the peak of irritation. He hated everything
and was sick of being alive.
Just talking to someone was enough to make him want to leave his Spirit
Zone.
Fermi, in the same space, knew this. That's why he was subtly provoking
him.
So he had to respond in kind.
"Who... are you?"
The students watching through the vision gulped.
Shirone's question encompassed all the doubts he had while in the senior
class.
"Who knows? Who could I be?"
Fermi wasn't in a normal state either. But he mirrored his condition to
further irritate Shirone.
"Why did you attack Amy? Why don't you graduate? Why does someone
with your skills stay in school?"
"I don't know what you're talking about. I just have a strong
mentality and endure well."
The students were dumbfounded by their conversation.
Talking about the senior class while burning? They both seemed
insane.
'It's coming.'
Fermi sensed his limit. Pretending to be calm, his inner soul was
raging.
He couldn't endure with a grimace. Would customers flock to a suffering
signboard?
'Promotion is key in business.'
Activating the timer in his mind, Fermi approached Shirone, offering a
final chance.
"Whatever you think, it's not true. I know I'm suspected. But in
the senior class, the one who stands out gets hit first. It's the natural order of
competition."
Shirone glared at Fermi with burning eyes.
'I know. You're using that.'
He concealed his crimes within the students' competitive spirit. Like a
guard beating a thief out of anger, unnoticed.
It wasn't pleasant, but it was a brilliant strategy.
"As a senior, let me give you some advice. Standing out isn't good.
You'll be eliminated early."
Shirone's nose wrinkled. Flames from his eyes and mouth seemed to
represent his anger.
"Are you threatening me?"
"Threatening? It's just advice. 20 points is a lot. But is it more
important than doing another year in the senior class?"
It was a threat.
If he didn't give up, Fermi would ensure he failed the graduation exam,
just like he did to Amy.
He knew what happened to those marked by Fermi. Even Amy, the great, was
eliminated early without a chance to prepare.
No matter the subject, he'd be lynched by everyone. A year's effort
would be in vain.
Conversely, if he offered 20 points, Fermi would ensure he
passed.
The question of whether 20 points were more important than another year
meant he wouldn't have to do another year.
'Give up, you damn rookie. Stop interfering with my business and get out
of school. I'll make sure you graduate.'
Shirone clenched his fists. Hot air seeped through his clenched teeth,
sticking them together.
Despicable Fermi.
'Because of people like you...'
He now understood how much Amy had struggled and endured to become a
magician.
Fermi had ruined it. He made her cry in sorrow all night.
"Eliminate me? Try it if youxx can. I won't lose to someone like
you."
Even in the hell of extreme heat, Fermi looked down at Shirone with
lifeless eyes.
The negotiation was over.
And Shirone would never become a magician as long as he was in
school.
"Fine, then look forward to it."
Fermi turned away. He had reached his limit.
"Wait. I haven't finished..."
As Shirone spoke, Fermi disappeared. He had deactivated his Spirit
Zone.
Fermi's eyes snapped open in the Virtual Zone. The pain of extreme heat
was still vivid, and he couldn't move a finger.
Yet the timer in his mind continued to tick accurately.
'...2 seconds. 1 second. Now it begins.'
The guide's voice was heard.
-Entering Stage 7, Void.
'My gift to you, Shirone.'
Having experienced Stage 6, Fermi knew that even a deviant couldn't
endure Stage 7.
Also, if only one person in Alpheus Magic School's history had passed
this difficulty, Shirone wouldn't survive either.
'If he gives up, that's fine. But will he...?'
Collie headed to the device to turn off the Virtual Zone. Even if the
team won, challenging higher stages for personal evaluation was possible, but not Stage 7, Void.
'This stage wasn't made because humans could do it. It's just the lid
defining the limit of the survival test. It must end here.'
Collie placed his hand on the Virtual Zone switch and looked at Alpheus,
declaring,
"I will shut down the Virtual Zone."
Fermi, who had risen, pointed at the vision.
"No. I think he'll continue."
"What?"
Collie's head snapped around. Shirone was standing in the darkness on
the vision.
'Despicable Fermi.'
For the first time, Shirone found peace.
All the previous stages had felt endlessly long, but the past was now
just a series of events that had already happened.
'I will never bow to someone like you.'
Shirone did not deactivate his Spirit Zone.
Even though he had become the final survivor, he didn't feel good
because Fermi had played dirty tricks until the end.
From today, Fermi had marked Shirone as his target. It was clear that he
would use his manipulative skills to pressure him whenever he could.
'Then I will pressure you, Fermi.'
That was Shirone's strategy to counter Fermi's tactics.
He would never give up the initiative. If they maintained a balance of
power, manipulation would be impossible.
Stage 7 of survival was undoubtedly a dangerous challenge, but that made
it all the more worth attempting.
'But where is this place?'
Shirone looked around.
It was strange to say he could see darkness, but in reality, there was
nothing but darkness around him.
Rumble, rumble, rumble!
He felt vibrations. The entire space was trembling, making it impossible
to discern the direction.
"What? What is this?"
Shirone reached out with a sense of foreboding. Darkness pressed in from
all sides, starting to crush him.
'Is this suffocation?'
His arms bent towards his body. Even in that state, the darkness
continued to increase its pressure.
