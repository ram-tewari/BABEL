Chapter 525:
Supporter (1)
In the Kingdom of Tormia, any family ranked within the top 20 of the
business world could be said to possess a merchant guild recognized across the continent.
For example, Dante's House of Airhain established the company Airhain
Shipping, constructing a transportation network that reached not only across the continent but also the
South and the Middle East—controlling 7% of the world's logistics. This made them the 14th-ranked
powerhouse in the business world.
Of course, this was backed by Tormia's long-standing military and
political efforts to maintain diplomatic neutrality, but among such families, there was one that had
grown in a particularly unique way.
That was the House of Ardino—where Miro was currently headed.
Unlike other houses that built extravagant mansions in the city,
Ardino's main estate was hidden deep within the subterranean mountain range encircling the northern part
of the capital, Bashka—a fact that alone hinted at their air of secrecy.
"It's really deep in there," said Arius, sticking out his tongue as he
followed behind Miro, carrying Shirone on his back.
Armin and Kuan were off on other missions suited to their skills, so
Arius was the only one accompanying her.
"They weren't originally a merchant family," Miro replied.
As they approached the ancestral estate, Miro's expression grew
increasingly sullen—something unusual for someone like her, especially one who had reached a state of
mindless transcendence.
"Is it bothering you? That's surprising."
"The Ardino family is different from other merchant houses."
"I've seen their publishing seal often while studying magic. Still, it's
impressive that they made it into the top 20 on book sales alone."
"They don't just publish. They translate, edit, and revise all private
texts outside of the official association publications. That alone means they possess a massive
knowledge base in magic."
"Which is good news for Shirone."
Miro kept walking up the mountain path.
"True. But that's not what I meant by 'different.'"
Arius raised his brows.
"The Ardino family branched off from the House of Adrias a long time
ago."
"Adrias… as in your family?"
"Yeah. Mine."
The House of Adrias, known for its long and austere history, was famous
for its ascetic lifestyle.
Though nobility, they never hoarded wealth. Their retainers lived in the
mountains with only the barest essentials—water and food.
That a genius like Miro came from such an environment wasn't
strange.
What they pursued was ultimate stability beyond desire and
suffering.
Their insights pierced through the nature of the world, transcending
borders and race, offering teachings free from discrimination.
That's why the kingdom, despite their lack of contribution to national
affairs, still conferred them a noble title—just to ensure they were claimed as part of the
kingdom.
"But one day, someone from the previous generation began questioning the
ascetic life."
Arius, one of the Seven Magical Sins who had indulged in worldly
pleasures, could relate.
"Understandable. I don't think I could live that way."
"Of course, the entire family opposed him unanimously. So he left, cast
off the Adrias name, and formed a new one—blending into society. That's the origin of the House of
Ardino. They used the exclusive knowledge and insight of the Adrias line to amass wealth and eventually
became a business titan. But in truth, most of their income now comes through shadow routes."
"Shadow routes… you mean?"
"Magic. The most lucrative industry in the world today—and one that's
not open to just anyone. The publishing business is just a façade."
Arius realized why Miro had looked so displeased.
"In other words, they're your relatives. Blood ties, after all."
"We never had a bad relationship. Sure, the public threw criticisms at
them, but we never had much friction. Worldly philosophy has its place, and back then, the Ardino family
hadn't yet tapped into dark funding."
"But now it's different?"
Miro clicked her tongue and narrowed one eye.
"I only found out recently, but ever since I entered Istas and built the
dimensional wall, everything's gone off track. When people talk about the Judgment of the Twenty, they
tend to focus on me—but behind the scenes, far more was happening."
"Of course. That event determined the fate of humanity."
Just because it wasn't more important than Miro didn't mean it was
insignificant. The ripple effects of the Judgment of the Twenty must have been enormous.
"When the narrative became 'the House of Adrias saved humanity,' Ardino
took the brunt of the backlash. Gossips, business interference, political pushback… it must've been
relentless. Greedy traitors who sold their souls. Runaways who betrayed humanity. I suppose they finally
cracked under the pressure and started accepting dark money."
"Do you think they resent you?"
"Who knows? I haven't seen them in nearly twenty years. I guess I'll
find out soon enough."
After about 30 minutes of hiking, the two arrived at the Ardino
estate.
The building, harmonized with nature, was the last remaining sign of
Adrias's influence before the branch.
The iron gate was neglected, overgrown with vines, and not even
locked.
Miro walked up to the weathered wooden door and knocked hard on the iron
handle.
Not long after, the door opened, revealing a vacant-looking middle-aged
man.
"What is it? We don't have any appoi—"
The man went pale as he saw Miro smiling at him.
"You… you…"
"Long time no see, Enrique."
Ardino Enrique. Current head of the House of Ardino, and Miro's
cousin, with whom she had played as a child.
"Miro… is that really you?"
Enrique's shock slowly gave way to calm as he steadied his gaze.
"I figured something like this might happen someday. I just didn't
expect it this soon."
"Oh, please. You never had a shred of foresight."
Enrique clicked his tongue.
"Yeah… truth is, I thought you were dead. But look at you—not a day
older."
"And you've gotten rounder. Geez, look at this belly jiggle. What're you
gonna do about all this?"
"H-Hey!"
As she poked at his belly, Enrique's embarrassed, flushed face briefly
overlapped with the image of his younger self.
But time was time. Once the reunion passed, distance returned to his
gaze.
"What brings you here?"
"That's a cold way to greet someone after twenty years."
"Exactly. It's been twenty years."
Even under the unspoken pressure to leave, Miro held her ground so
firmly that it left Enrique awkward and speechless.
Eventually, he sighed, turned, and motioned her inside.
"Come in. At least have a meal."
As she stepped into the house, Miro let out a dry chuckle. Nothing had
changed in twenty years.
"This is it? So much for a merchant mogul."
"It's not easy. The guild is under surveillance now—even the kingdom is
watching us. With Adrias gone, we're next."
A hint of sorrow passed briefly over Miro's face before fading.
As Enrique settled his large frame into a worn leather couch and lit a
cigarette, a maid brought in tea.
"I'm here for a reason."
"Miro…"
Enrique stubbed out his cigarette after just one puff and leaned
forward, nervous.
"Can't you just eat and go? I'm only talking to you because it's you.
The rest of the family can't stand you. Please—I'm begging you, just leave."
"But you're the head, aren't you? If the head says so, who's going to
object?"
"Sigh…"
Enrique sank back into the couch, exhausted.
"You've always done whatever you wanted. No matter how much I tried, you
never listened."
"But wasn't I fun to be around?"
Looking up at the ceiling, Enrique chuckled under his breath.
"Yeah… So, what do you want?"
"Where's 'Goldie'? It's vacation now, isn't it?"
Enrique raised an eyebrow.
"My son? Why are you asking about that brat? He hasn't even graduated
yet."
"I heard. So I thought maybe his aunt could give him a little
talk."
While Enrique made a thoughtful "hmm," Miro's smile turned
meaningful.
He might be the head of the Ardino family, but with his connections and
resources, there was no way he didn't already know that Miro had returned from Heaven.
"I don't know what this is about, but he's in the second-floor room.
Hasn't come down in days."
"I'll go see him."
Now that she had gauged the atmosphere in the Ardino family, Miro
figured there was no point in wasting time.
As Miro headed for the stairs with Arius, the sound of a lighter
flicking came from the sofa.
"…What are you planning to do now?"
Standing in front of the stairs, Miro turned around.
Though she couldn't see it, Enrique's face behind the veil of cigarette
smoke was more serious than ever.
"Miro, you know it now, don't you? No matter how strong you are, in the
end, the world will only use you."
Enrique hesitated for a moment before continuing.
"Change your name. Come into the Ardino family. If you do, I'll take
full responsibility and protect you."
Miro turned away, clearly uninterested.
"It was my choice."
"You're still rationalizing it like that?!"
Enrique shot to his feet.
"To be honest, I was shocked when you came to me! Not because you
returned—because you must've looked into it! The entire Adrias bloodline was purged! Just because they
broke the one promise you made!"
"That too was a choice my family made."
Enrique clutched his chest in frustration.
"How can you be so cruel? You don't even feel the desire for revenge, or
resentment toward the world? Ever since you saved humanity, our family has been under pressure too! Do
you know why? Because the world is afraid of us! They used you to survive, but deep down, they're
terrified of a second or third Miro! That's why the House of Adrias vanished, and why our house had to
go underground!"
He stepped out from behind the sofa and approached her.
"You can't expect too much from humanity. When they need you, they'll
flatter you with sweet words. But once you're no longer useful, they become more ruthless than any other
creature. And you don't regret sacrificing yourself for people like that?"
"I don't."
Miro slowly lifted her head, smiling as she turned to face him.
"Because that's what it means to be Adrias."
Enrique's strength left him all at once.
They were of the same blood—how could they be so different?
"…I'll have some meat pies baked. Come down when they're ready."
It had always been Miro's favorite food.
As Enrique disappeared into the living room, changing the subject, Miro
ascended the stairs and entered a sharply turning hallway.
The wooden floorboards smelled faintly of antiques.
Recalling old memories, she arrived at the fourth room. She raised her
hand to knock—but stopped, grinning mischievously.
"Goldie! What are you doing in there?"
She swung the door open, and inside, a tall young man was rinsing lab
equipment at a makeshift workstation.
He turned his head calmly at the unexpected guest, but the moment he saw
Miro, his eyes briefly wavered.
Seeing how tall he'd grown, Miro for the first time truly felt the
weight of the 20 years she had missed.
"Wow, you've really grown, huh? Do you remember me?"
"Well, look who it is."
He gave a soft tap to the Erlenmeyer flask in the sink, then turned with
a smile as dazzling as a toast.
"It's been a while, Aunt Miro."
He was Fermi Ardino—top-ranked student at Alpheas Magic Academy.
