Chapter 10:
First Steps Toward a Dream (1)
The great training yard.
Shirone arrived on time. Rian had come first and was waiting.
The battle had already begun in a sense. The two looked at each other from a distance
without approaching.
"Let's begin."
The weapon Rian chose was the same longsword as Shirone.
Ozent swordsmanship, known for its reckless and bold style, was more suited to
greatswords than longswords.
But that was only when schema was possible, so rather than accommodating Shirone, he was
choosing a weapon that suited him.
Shirone was in the "stillness in motion" stance from basic swordsmanship.
Though it was a simple movement, even to Rian's eyes, his center of gravity was much better balanced than
before.
'Is it really... possible?'
Rian once again saw Rai in Shirone. Suddenly, this thought occurred to him: maybe that
boy really hadn't learned swordsmanship.
'But how can he be so calm? Isn't he afraid of death?'
Even Rian, who had trained with a sword for 16 years, feared death. That's why he pushed
his body to the limit to boost his confidence.
On the other hand, Shirone didn't have the same courage as Rian. He was a herbivore who
loved peace and disliked fighting.
But intelligence always seeks the highest efficiency. Analyzing the fear of real swords
as the greatest risk factor in this duel, Shirone worked to find a solution and finally found an answer.
If you can't overcome fear, you can understand it.
In sword philosophy, this is called "jumping off a cliff." Anyone can jump off
a cliff—if they don't think about dying.
For instance, some types of people in the world don't feel fear. Whether rulers,
businessmen, or criminals, their occupations vary, but they share one thing in common: they don't imagine
the consequences beforehand.
A serial killer only pursues momentary pleasure without thinking about execution when
caught. Some dictators leisurely enjoy banquets even as rebels breach the gates. For them, the future
doesn't exist even for 0.1 seconds—it's just an illusion.
Shirone imagined standing at the edge of a cliff. Then he extended his right foot into
the void and followed with his left.
Shirone didn't fall. If one doesn't imagine the future, the area beyond the cliff is just
the state right before falling.
'Until you fall, you haven't fallen. Until you die, you haven't died.'
The reason intelligence can sometimes be more frightening than courage is because it
understands the essence of danger like this.
Also, Shirone's frozen mind resulted in a dramatic increase in the stability of his
spirit zone.
"Here I come!"
As Shirone entered his spirit zone, Rian approached within reach.
A flash of light seemed to streak before Shirone's eyes. It was clearly a bold
swordsmanship of a different level from a mountaineer's axe swing.
But Shirone also had his spirit zone.
With his synesthetic cognitive ability clearly sensing the trajectory of the sword, he
opened his eyes wide and avoided the attack.
Rian's expression changed to one of surprise. Seeing the movement of a real sword with
the eyes was absolutely impossible for a beginner.
But the techniques of the sword are also infinite. There were clearly parts Shirone
couldn't predict. As the blade cut across Shirone's chest and passed by, his spirit zone momentarily
wavered.
Shirone's heart sank. At this rate, he would be helplessly defeated.
But Rian's subsequent attack was unexpectedly aggressive. Thanks to this, Shirone was
able to throw himself out of the way and escape death.
'Why such an attack?'
It would have been over if he had simply cut the side with minimal force. But Rian seemed
not to notice Shirone's wavering and came with a large movement to decide the match.
'Perhaps his understanding of swordsmanship is lower than I thought?'
Shirone's thinking was accurate. Lacking insight, Rian was skilled in swordsmanship
itself but fell short in understanding its essence.
Shirone downgraded his assessment of Rian's skill. Setting the enemy too high was
foolish, giving up any chance of counterattack. To win, he had to take risks.
As Shirone's counterattack began, the tension of death increased, rapidly depleting
Rian's stamina. After dozens of exchanges, Rian was finally shocked.
'Damn it! This can't be! This can't be!'
The techniques the opponent was using were only basic cuts. Yet they were hard to block.
He was targeting only the parts that couldn't be defended.
Suddenly, Rai's face came to mind. In their duel two years ago, his brother had subdued
Rian with just one hand. Rai's face with his sneering lips was still vivid in his mind.
"Don't be ridiculous! I will become the world's greatest swordsman!"
As Rian charged more recklessly, Shirone was puzzled. What was this boy? Did he not want
to win? Why was he being so stubborn? Did he think at all?
'Huh? Huhh?'
Shirone gradually realized he was being pushed back. From some point, he couldn't read
the opponent's movements. Rian's sword, swung with conviction, began to approach Shirone with unpredictable
complexity.
"I won't lose! I will definitely surpass him!"
Rian, riding on the advantage, pressed Shirone hard. Shirone's stamina rapidly hit
bottom. He had distributed his energy efficiently until now, but an absolute difference existed anyway. His
sword felt heavy.
"This is the end!"
Rian, who had come running with his longsword lowered, struck upward as if to split
Shirone's chin. Shirone also swung his sword down with all his might.
With a clang, one sword soared into the sky and then stuck into the far side of the
training ground.
Silence fell.
The two looked at each other, gasping for breath.
Rian slowly lowered his gaze. He saw Shirone's sword pointing at his solar plexus.
Just before the swords collided, Shirone had twisted his wrist like a whirlwind, striking
Rian's sword from below. With the added momentum from the impact, Rian, whose strength had waned, finally
lost his grip on the sword.
It wasn't a technique attempted with certainty. Just a difference in understanding
swordsmanship. It was a risk taken with the thought that only attacks that could cut him were real.
Shirone didn't relax his tension. He wanted to collapse right away, but he couldn't
carelessly lose the life he had worked so hard to save.
"Ah well, I lost."
Rian graciously admitted defeat.
"Ah, I lost. I lost."
Though Rian scratched his head in annoyance, the murderous intent had already
disappeared. He had trained to his limit and fought his best. That's why he had no regrets.
"You won. Since I lost the duel, I have nothing to say even if you kill me."
It wasn't a childish statement. But that made Shirone even more displeased. Could someone
really be so immature?
"Are you joking right now?"
"What?"
"If I kill the young master, do you think, what, I'd be allowed to live? How can you
say such nonsense?"
"Come on! Why does that matter? Is that what you say to someone you fought to the
death with?"
"Only you put your life on the line! I just fought to live! This ridiculous duel
makes no sense from the beginning—how is it right to blame me when you were just hiding because you didn't
want to train?"
Rian was taken aback. Normally, after men fight, there should be, if not warmth, at least
some heavy and intense exchange of words, right?
But this was nothing short of being eaten alive by nagging.
"Hey, you! Who's blaming you? Your actions were so petty! Even if we'd just met, in
such a situation, loyalty should naturally kick in!"
"Loyalty? You're talking about loyalty?"
"What's with you?"
Shirone was incensed. For a month, his mind had been too occupied with the problem at
hand to think about anything else, but now that it was over, he couldn't contain his anger.
"Is that loyalty? Have you ever thought about the burden a mere butler has to bear
between the master's son and the sword instructor? Did you know that and still make such a request to
me?"
"Uh... well..."
Rian blinked his eyes. For Rian, who had learned through daily beatings, Shirone's
nagging was an unfamiliar attack.
"If you were truly a man of loyalty, you should have felt ashamed about burdening a
mere butler and faced the sword instructor yourself. Wouldn't that have been a truly cool and loyal
action?"
Rian was silent for a long time. From the way he repeatedly smacked his lips, Shirone
could gauge his character.
He wasn't the type to make up words.
He wasn't speaking because he had nothing to say.
But Shirone, who found that even more annoying, gave up even nagging and sat down.
"Sigh, I really almost died."
Rian looked intently at the grumbling Shirone. The formidable opponent who had overcome
the fear of death and pushed him back was gone, and just a kid seemed to be sitting there.
Who could he be? He was clearly a genius. But he wasn't Rai. Unlike his brother, who had
knocked away his sword with one hand and sneered, this boy had faced him with his whole body.
"You, what's your name?"
Shirone raised his head. Looking up from a sitting position, Rian's build seemed even
bigger.
"Arian Shirone."
"I'm Ozent Rian."
It was funny enough for a noble and a commoner to exchange introductions, but what was
even more absurd was this: did he really think he didn't know the name of the youngest son of the family?
Ignoring Shirone's dull expression, Rian walked over. Shirone's head tilted up even more.
Rian, holding out a hand as big as a pot lid, said in a deep voice:
"Let's be friends. How about it?"

First Steps Toward a Dream (1)
It had already been a year since he entered the Ozent family.
Shirone had turned 17, and over seven thousand books had been taken out of the great
library.
Temuran clicked his tongue at the amazing work pace, but Shirone had already foreseen it.
It was natural for efficiency to accelerate as classified books decreased.
In his thinking, everything in the world seemed to follow acceleration rather than
constant velocity. Even if the start was slow, if one persevered without giving up, one could reach the goal
faster than expected.
Of the target 850 history books, only about 200 remained. His average reading was over
three books a day, and in a few months, the dream-like backbone of knowledge would be completed.
Besides that, he had experienced swordsmanship and understood the essence of fear. Thanks
to this, he was able to dramatically increase the durability of his spirit zone.
Thus, the year he spent was fulfilling and beneficial.
But no benefit could compare to the true friend Shirone made for the first time.
"So many damn books. I wonder if everyone in our family has read all these?"
Rian, sitting on the floor, muttered as he glanced at a book. Actually getting to know
him, Shirone found that Rian had an affectionate and cute personality. Maybe that's what it meant to be the
youngest in a noble family?
Shirone, organizing books on a ladder, said:
"Of course. Both the head of the family and the second young master visit the
library often."
"Hmph, it must be nice to be smart. To know how to do so many things."
As Rian threw away the book he was reading, Shirone came down from the ladder, picked it
up, and scolded him.
"Don't throw it carelessly. If even one book is damaged, I'm dead."
"I don't like that either. How can a book be worth more than a human life? Even if
you burn down the library, I'll protect you, so don't worry."
"That will never happen as long as you stay still, so please be careful."
Shirone had never said anything embarrassing. He was firm and strict, but not cold. Rian
liked that about Shirone.
"I like being with you, but it's suffocating being cooped up in the library."
"Why are you hanging around here anyway? What about your training?"
"Just basic training. I told them I wanted to study a bit in the library."
Shirone trembled with frustration.
"Then you should hurry up and study! What are you doing?"
"I'm bored! Bored! Bored!"
Rian flailed his arms and legs like a child throwing a tantrum.
Shirone turned away dismissively and headed to the desk. He wasn't particularly worried
about Rian's laziness. If he was water, Rian was fire. A fire that, once ignited, grows uncontrollably.
***
After gathering a history book, Shirone nagged again.
"Get up and bring a book. Let's study."
"Ungh, training has been too intense lately."
After sending Rian to the study, Shirone sat down and opened the history book. Recently,
field work finished faster than paperwork, so he had quite a bit of free time.
Once he started concentrating on the book, the pages turned instantly.
Upper left, lower left, upper right, lower right. With four eye movements, two pages were
done. Moreover, he rarely lost rhythm until he finished a whole book.
This meant that the knowledge in his head surpassed the scope of the books. If he had
mastered 650 history books, he could digest the remaining 200 as easily as drinking water.
"Hey, Shirone. I found an interesting book here."
Rian approached, waving a book. But soon he discovered Shirone turning pages at an
incredible speed and didn't dare disturb him. For instance, he once took more than 10 hours to turn a single
page. He was definitely reading a book, but when he opened his eyes, it was morning.
Taking the seat next to Shirone, Rian opened the book he had brought. The title of this
supposedly interesting book was:
Swordsman VS Magician.
"Introduction. You've probably asked this question at least once: Who would win if a
swordsman and a magician faced off?"
As Rian read aloud, Shirone's page-turning hand paused. It was content that would perk up
the ears of an aspiring magician.
"To find that answer, I traveled around the world. Even now when I close my eyes, I
see it. The Artusna Mountain Range, 6,000 meters above sea level. There, carnivorous trees that eat mountain
birds..."
Rian closed the book.
"Boring as hell."
"What? I find it interesting!"
Startled by Shirone's outcry, Rian jumped. He had been pretending to be disinterested,
but apparently had his ears perked up like a fox.
"What's interesting about it? There are no pictures and no talk of
swordsmanship."
"How can you say that when you haven't read even one page?"
"The author should have some consideration for readers. Start interestingly, end
movingly."
"This isn't a novel. Give me the book. So who wins?"
"Who cares who wins? Would a magician and a swordsman be crazy enough to duel? If
the situation is favorable, you attack; if not, you run away."
Shirone opened the middle part of "Swordsman VS Magician" and flipped through
the pages.
"There might be cases where they have to fight. If so, who would win?"
"Well, if I had to guess..."
"Obviously, the swordsman would win."
A sharp voice came from beyond the bookshelf. Then a man with a cold impression appeared.
He wasn't as robust as Rian, but he was much taller, and his limbs were sleekly long.
The second son of the Ozent family, Ozent Rai.
Even though they were brothers, his atmosphere and appearance were quite different from
Rian's. First, his hair was pitch black.
In the Ozent family, two bloodlines came down undiluted: the blue hair and the black
hair.
The head of the family, Bishoff, the eldest son, and the second son inherited black hair,
while the aqua-colored hair was passed to the second daughter and the youngest, Rian.
Additionally, Rian's grandfather, a state-certified third-class swordsman, also had a
bluish hue.
Of course, family members are connected by tight bonds.
But it's interesting that they sometimes take sides according to bloodline. Which
bloodline is superior changes with each generation, but this time, the black hair lineage from Bishoff to
Rai was the mainstream.
"Ah! Hello."
Shirone quickly bowed his head. No one in the family knew that he was friends with Rian.
If he had heard the previous conversation, he would deserve a beating.
"So the swordsman wins? How can you be so sure? Because you're a swordsman,
brother?"
Knowing this fact, Rian used a mocking tone to try to hold Rai's attention.
"Is there a need to attach grand words like 'certainty'? It's obvious from just
knowing what positions magicians and swordsmen hold on each continent."
Fortunately, Rai didn't seem concerned about his relationship with Rian, but Shirone
couldn't be at ease. Every time he visited the library, he felt that this man was hard to read.
"Regardless of position, this is about a direct fight. Surely you're not suggesting
that in a battlefield you'd say, 'I outrank you, so step back,' are you?"
Rian laughed triumphantly as if he had scored a point.
But Rai didn't even care and turned to Shirone with indifferent eyes.
"What do you think, kid?"
"Well... if we assume they're at the highest level, magic has tremendous power, so
it could inflict fatal wounds on a swordsman. But swordsmen have superior physical abilities in return, so
if they rush in before magic can be cast..."
"No, you're wrong. A magician cannot defeat a swordsman."
Shirone was upset. Though he was still just an aspirant, being a magician was his dream.
It was unpleasant to hear that his dream was absolutely inferior to something else.
Feeling angry that his friend was being disrespected, Rian shot back:
"Who are you to say such things, brother? Do you think you represent all swordsmen
or something?"
Rai curled up his lips. It was the sneer that Rian absolutely hated.
"The pride of a swordsman isn't something anyone can acquire. But a magician's
knowledge can simply be bought with money."
There was a barb in Rai's words. In reality, swordsmen had a way to neutralize magic.
An artifact known as "Anti-Magic."
Anti-Magic generates a special wave that disrupts a magician's spirit zone. When the
spirit zone wavers, the power of magic inevitably drops sharply.
Anti-Magic artifacts are processed in the form of crystal balls and mounted on armor,
shields, or swords. Since a dragon's heart, which emits magic wave pulses, is needed, premium artifacts were
so expensive that even decent nobles couldn't afford them.
Moreover, having ten artifacts with 10% efficiency doesn't mean you can defend against
magic 100%.
Since waves resonate, even if you add a new wave to an Anti-Magic wave, overlapping parts
will inevitably occur.
Eventually, the more artifacts you equip, the lower the efficiency becomes, and the key
is how much you can suppress that.
The best artifact in existence would be the "Five Divine General Armor Set"
made by Arch-Magician Yakra. Artifacts are embedded in the helmet, armor, gauntlets, boots, and shield, each
exhibiting 20% efficiency, which is the maximum for a single piece.
According to research, the suppression power obtainable when wearing the complete Five
Divine General Armor Set is a whopping 65%. For a magician, having magic power reduced by 65% is like being
tied hand and foot.
Another troublesome thing is the "Elemental Essence." These essences,
crystallized in minute quantities in nature, come in four types: fire, water, wind, and earth. When
equipped, they not only activate magic but can also perfectly block the corresponding attribute.
The number of essences discovered so far is 17 worldwide—2 fire, 3 water, 5 wind, and 7
earth—with the value of one exceeding the budget of a small kingdom.
Shirone imagined: If a schema master equipped with the Five Divine General Armor Set and
the four elemental essences swung a sword, what magician could stand against him?
Of course, this was only theoretically possible. The Five Divine General Set was
scattered worldwide with unclear origins, and elemental essences were beyond the reach of anything less than
a national entity.
Equipping multiple Anti-Magics was also problematic. Even Yakra, a master of wave
equations, spent 10 years calibrating the Five Divine General Armor Set.
Carelessly stacking Anti-Magics would only waste money. Without a comprehensive
understanding of wave equations, resonance frequencies, and mana interactions, efficiency wouldn't increase.
The saying that raising Anti-Magic by 1% could cost half a noble's fortune wasn't just a
casual joke.
The swordsman's dilemma starts here. Rather than investing vast sums to improve
Anti-Magic, it's much cheaper to have a magician who knows Anti-Magic magic accompany you.
These implications were contained in Rai's words. Magicians are subordinate to swordsmen.
He also said this: a magician's knowledge can be bought with money.
The one who developed Anti-Magic as an artifact wasn't a swordsman but a magician.
Countless magicians are still researching the effects of dragon mana on the spirit zone. If they achieve
results, they can gain tremendous wealth and honor.
Rai was pointing out the irony that magicians who make a living from magic research ways
to weaken magic. He was saying that magicians who sell themselves for money cannot defeat swordsmen.
"Well? You seem to like books. Magic can be blocked with Anti-Magic. But a
swordsman's body is different. Our physical abilities can only be obtained through pain."
Shirone understood Rai's words. But he didn't agree with his thinking.
"That's exactly what a magician is."
"What?"
Rai was puzzled when Shirone readily agreed. If a boy who liked magic, he would naturally
be upset.
"A magician is someone who persistently delves into the harmony of all things.
Therefore, it's not strange that they research ways to suppress magic. What they value is not whether they
win or lose in a fight, but whether they know it or not. As a result, artifacts are also the product of that
boundless intellect. To say you can defeat a magician with that is fundamentally wrong, I think."
Rai wasn't as straightforward as Rian. If he could win against an opponent, he was the
type to subdue them with any logic.
Nevertheless, the reason he was clenching his jaw tightly was that he couldn't find a
rebuttal to Shirone's logic.
Rian tried hard to suppress his laughter. Seeing his brother, who always talked as if he
knew everything, become speechless at Shirone's words, he couldn't have been more delighted.
'Puhahaha! Serves him right. My friend's got some tongue, doesn't he?'
As time passed, Rai's expression grew more distorted. His brow furrowed, and veins popped
on his temples. But that didn't make any new thoughts emerge.
"Ideals differ from reality. Regardless of intention, the fact that you can't win in
a fight remains unchanged."
Rai turned coldly and left the library. Seeing this, Rian covered his mouth, his cheeks
puffing with laughter.
'Different from reality? Is that all you can say? Oh my, what a fool. Go on, hurry up and
leave.'
