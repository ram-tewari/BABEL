Chapter 90:
Crowd (6)
"Indeed. It must be the first time someone has spoken to me first."
Alpheus bowed politely and greeted her.
"My name is Mirhi Alpheus, a mage. I was so captivated by your beauty that I forgot
to introduce myself sooner. If you don't mind, would you be my partner for tonight's ball?"
Erina stared blankly, her jaw dropping slightly. A piece of apple rolled out of her
mouth. Anyone could see the confusion written all over her face, as if she were thinking, "What on
earth is happening?"
Suddenly, Erina bowed her head deeply. Without a word, she turned and walked past
Alpheus.
Alpheus was stunned. To put it bluntly, he had been "rejected." Had he
approached her too normally by conventional standards? Regaining his composure, he quickly moved to block
her path.
"W-wait a minute. Was I too forward? I'm sorry. I just wanted to get closer to you,
Erina."
Erina still didn't respond. She just stared at the ground, shuffling her feet as if
searching for an escape route.
But this time, Alpheus didn't give up. He blocked her at every turn, persistently trying
to engage her in conversation.
"Alright, if you don't want to dance, let's just talk. Let's have a conversation.
I'll tell you a really interesting story. Do you like constellations?"
"I want to go. I want to go back to my room. Mom will scold me."
"Why would your mom scold you? No one's going to scold you. You're at a party, and
I'm just asking you to be my partner."
"She told me not to talk. She said anyone who talks to me is a bad person. I want to
go to my room."
Alpheus felt frustrated. No, more than that—he was anxious. All the techniques he'd used
to win over countless women had vanished, leaving only the desire to meet Erina's gaze one more time.
"I'm definitely not a bad person. Look, just look at my face. Does this look like
the face of a bad person?"
Alpheus grabbed her shoulders with both hands. At the same time, Erina flinched and let
out a scream.
"Kyaaah! No!"
Every noble in the hall turned to look at Erina. To anyone watching, it seemed like
Alpheus was doing something terrible.
"Mom! Help me! Mom!"
"Erina! Look at me! I'm not trying to hurt you!"
"Mom! Mom!"
Among the invited mages was Sarof, Alpheus's rival. Watching Alpheus's absurd behavior,
he was dumbfounded. He'd known the guy was crazy, but he never imagined he'd be this much of a lunatic.
"After sweeping through all the women in Vashka, now he's bothering a simpleton?
He's really desperate for success."
Most of the nobles shared the same thought. Even if she was intellectually lacking, she
was still the eldest daughter of the Bastard family. If he could win her over, his future would be set.
The only one who understood Alpheus's true feelings was Klump, but even he gave up when
things reached this point.
'Whatever, you psycho. Clean up your own mess.'
Erina was thrashing around, almost like she was having a seizure. Any sane person would
have let her go in front of her family, but Alpheus stubbornly held onto her shoulders.
"Erina, wait. Look at me. Look at me."
"Mom! I'm scared, Mom!"
"Erina!"
When Alpheus shouted, Erina's movements froze for a moment. But the trembling that
followed was even more intense than before.
"Please, just look at my face once. I'm begging you."
Erina slowly turned her head, her expression filled with terror. She saw Alpheus's
burning eyes. A searing energy seemed to pull her in, heating her heart until it felt like it was on fire.
"See? Nothing bad is happening. I'm not a bad person. I won't do anything you don't
like. But it's a party. Don't you want to dance?"
An unmistakable flutter appeared in Erina's expression. The shimmering particles of light
refracting through crystals, the musicians' melodies, the chatter of countless people mingling—she just
wanted to laugh like everyone else.
"Erina. I want to be with you. Please be my partner tonight."
There was no answer, but he didn't need one. Taking Erina's hand, Alpheus led her to the
center of the hall. She followed hesitantly, like a toddler taking her first steps. And for the first time
in her life, she was held in the arms of someone other than family.
The quick-witted musicians resumed their performance, and Alpheus began to dance with
Erina, guiding her steps. The nobles were dumbfounded. Even Erina's parents were at a loss for words as they
watched.
Alpheus didn't care. Because Erina didn't care. It was as if only the two of them
existed, lost in the music. Their dance was far from elegant. But it was instinctive and passionate. They
spun like tops, often losing balance and bumping into others. Each time, Erina's laughter grew louder. By
the time ten minutes had passed, Erina had become the star of the party for the first time.
***
Playful laughter echoed through the garden. Having nearly caused a scene at the party,
Alpheus and Erina had fled into the woods, running breathlessly.
When they reached the edge of the hill, Alpheus bit his tongue and gasped for air.
Humiliatingly, Erina didn't seem tired at all. To think his stamina was weaker than a woman's—he resolved to
cut back on drinking as he looked up at the sky.
It was a night so clear it seemed like stars would pour down endlessly.
Erina was unexpectedly quiet. But Alpheus, known throughout Vashka for his talkativeness,
kept the conversation flowing without pause, ensuring the mood never cooled.
As time passed, it almost felt like Erina was drawing the words out of him. No matter the
topic, she listened silently. Whenever she smiled as if she understood, Alpheus grew even more excited, his
words pouring out like a waterfall.
Eventually, the conversation turned to his recent struggles with the identity of photons.
Was light a wave or a particle?
Sarof had defined it as a wave, but Alpheus was certain he was wrong. Yet he wasn't
advocating for particle theory either.
Honestly, he didn't know. He just believed that admitting ignorance was the starting
point.
"It's strange, isn't it? Light has such bizarre properties. In interference
experiments, it shows wave-like behavior, but on the other hand..."
Alpheus stopped mid-sentence, realizing he'd gotten too carried away. In his excitement,
he'd forgotten he was talking to Erina. She was crouched on the grass, gazing down the hill. But the smile
on her lips was still beautiful.
"Hahaha! Did I talk too much? I think this is the first time I've talked for so
long. Usually, people get bored."
"You're a good person."
It was the first thing Erina had said. And Alpheus blinked as if he'd never heard such
words before. Suddenly, his heart raced for reasons he couldn't understand.
His mind was in chaos.
Why am I here? What do I even want from this woman?
It was an unknown world. A place where the desire to explore and the fear of the unknown
coexisted, making it impossible to judge anything.
Alpheus turned away. Pretending he hadn't heard her, he continued his earlier train of
thought.
"Haha! It's embarrassing to admit, but as a mage, I'm supposed to write a thesis.
But I don't know anything. I can't decide which direction to take. What do you think, Erina? Is light a wave
or a particle?"
Erina didn't seem hurt by his attitude. Standing up, she looked at the stars in the sky
and spoke.
***
"Light is beauty."
"What? What did you say?"
Alpheus asked again. It was natural that she didn't know photon theory, but this answer
was unexpected.
"Light is beautiful. Flowers are beautiful too. And so are we. But the light you're
talking about... doesn't sound beautiful."
Alpheus felt like he'd been hit on the head with a hammer. It was a direct strike of pure
thought, impossible to shape into words.
"I-I see. But why is light beautiful?"
To Alpheus, light was just photons. But Erina answered without hesitation, as if she knew
everything.
"Light shows us the darkness. Without light, how could we see the night sky?"
The world fell silent. Like the universe itself.
Alpheus was speechless. The thoughts he'd truly longed for cascaded over him like a
waterfall.
Why had he tried to separate the two? Stepping back, it was so clear.
Because it's beautiful.
Any contradiction ceases to be a contradiction.
"Beauty. It's so beautiful, Erina."
Erina turned her head, smiling with childlike innocence. The moment Alpheus saw that
smile, he made a decision.
He would remember this moment for the rest of his life.
Alpheus knelt on one knee before Erina. With the most respectful gesture, he extended his
right hand.
"Please be my light."
***
That night forty years ago.
The confession under the moonlight became Alpheus's happiest memory, one he wouldn't
trade for the world. As the curtain of Abyss Nova lifted, a quiet smile touched his lips.
