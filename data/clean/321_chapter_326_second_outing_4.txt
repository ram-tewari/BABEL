Chapter 326:
Second Outing (4)
"Ten snowballs for ten silver? That's one silver each? That's
pretty expensive."
Having grown up in the mountains and never experienced festivals,
Shirone found it culturally shocking.
But Amy took it as a matter of course.
"Tourist attractions always have a premium. Besides, that Ice Queen
plush doll is quite famous. It's strategically made by the autonomous district and can only be obtained
during the winter festival. The types of dolls change every year, so there's even a
collection."
"Oh, I see."
Shirone's eyes sparkled.
If it's a festival limited edition, it's natural for the price to
increase over time.
If he could win the game, paying ten silver for ten snowballs wouldn't
be a bad deal.
"Alright, I'll give it a try. Watch me."
Shirone paid ten silver.
"Ten snowballs, please."
"Here we have another brave participant. May the blessing of the
Ice Queen be with you."
The blessing of the Ice Queen didn't seem like a good thing, but since
her character was a tourist attraction, he let it slide.
Shirone received the box of snowballs.
Entering the game area, he saw someone else attempting the challenge.
While waiting, he picked up a snowball from the box and found it was mixed with water, making it hard
and heavy.
'Hmm, this should have a decent range.'
Throwing it wasn't the problem.
However, he noticed that the snowball had to be thrown through a window
in the wall to reach the opposite tower.
The throwing angle was restricted, making it difficult to generate power
and significantly reducing accuracy.
"Whew, this is harder than I thought."
The previous challenger wiped his sweat and stepped back after
failing.
People who visited the winter festival every year knew how difficult it
was to win the Ice Queen collection, so they weren't disappointed.
Following the announcer's instructions, Shirone entered the game area,
set the box down, and picked up a snowball.
He rolled it between his fingers to gauge the distance, then opened his
stance and threw the snowball with all his might.
The snowball passed through the window, fell due to gravity, and
shattered against the castle wall below the watchtower.
The tourists laughed cheerfully.
Normally, Shirone would have laughed it off too, but with Amy watching,
his face turned red.
He didn't dare look back at her and quickly picked up the next snowball,
analyzing his failure.
'I can't increase the angle, so I can't generate power. This is much
harder than I thought.'
Well, a game with a festival limited edition prize wouldn't be designed
for just anyone to win.
"Grr...!"
Shirone gritted his teeth and threw the snowball.
He barely managed to get the distance right, but the snowball missed the
soldier target and fell elsewhere.
"Whew! Whew!"
Determined, Shirone threw the snowballs one after another.
Having succeeded in Thunder Strike in one go, he thought he could
eventually succeed by reducing the margin of error bit by bit.
But after throwing five snowballs, he realized.
'This is harder than Thunder Strike.'
The rules were simple, but it was not a game made haphazardly.
It was designed to look easy to succeed, but the throwing angle and
distance were cleverly adjusted so that only those with natural strength or professional skills could
succeed.
After throwing ten snowballs, Shirone was panting.
Despite the cold weather, his body was hot, and sweat formed on his
forehead. He couldn't take off his coat because he was wearing Armand.
Shirone sheepishly stuck out his tongue and returned to Amy.
"Uh, sorry. I failed."
"There's nothing to be sorry about. It's just a game. But that was
really hard."
Through Shirone's challenge, Amy guessed that the game's difficulty was
top-notch.
Analyzing with her red eyes, she calculated that there weren't many
routes to hit the target considering the force, gravity, and air resistance.
"Haha! You failed spectacularly. And you're supposed to be a magic
school student. How embarrassing."
Shirone and Amy turned around simultaneously. A boy with messy hair was
approaching with a sneer.
Behind him stood a tall, curly-haired young man and a heavily made-up
girl, standing arrogantly as if guarding the rear.
"Long time no see, Amy."
When Jokre extended his hand, Amy stood still, lost in thought.
Since entering magic school, she hadn't known anyone outside of her
school friends.
Especially not any boys.
"Um, sorry... but who are you?"
Jokre's face twisted.
How could she not remember him?
Even if she had rejected him, being confessed to should be an
unforgettable memory.
At least, that's what Jokre thought.
He had never been confessed to by a girl, but if it ever happened, he
was confident it would be a lifelong bragging point.
"Do you not know, or are you pretending not to? Jokre. Alpheas
Magic School. You really don't remember?"
"Jokre? Jokre..."
Mumbling, Amy's eyes widened as she pointed at Jokre.
"Oh, oh! Wow! It's been a long time."
Though she didn't seem genuinely pleased, her exaggerated surprise made
Jokre puff up with pride in front of his friends.
Being recognized by none other than the famous Karmis Amy was
something.
But he hadn't approached to reminisce with an old classmate, so he
raised his chin and looked down at her.
"Hmph, shouldn't you greet me properly?"
"Huh? What greeting?"
Jokre swept his arm over his friends behind him and pointed his thumb at
himself.
"I hear you're still in school, but we're official mages. That
means we're unofficial 10th-grade mages."
Official mage.
Amy's heart fluttered. Since entering Alpheas Magic School at twelve, it
was a term that had never left her mind.
Praying her face wasn't stiff, Amy extended her hand to Jokre.
"I see. Congratulations. You finally graduated."
At the time, Amy was thirteen. She barely remembered Jokre, but she
clearly recalled looking down on him when he transferred as if fleeing.
A smile spread across Jokre's face.
Amy's poker face was perfect, but he could see her heart without her
opening up.
How frustrated must she be, watching him from the position of a genius
from the Karmis family who had failed her graduation exam?
'Hmph, how does it feel? When you rejected me, it was much more
humiliating than this.'
Jokre turned his gaze to Shirone, who was standing idly.
"Seeing a famous person here. Arian Shirone. The kingdom's top
prospect, right?"
Shirone remained silent. He could tell from the tone that Jokre wasn't
approaching with friendly intentions.
"Want some advice from a senior? A prospect is just a prospect. The
real competition starts when you become an official mage. Well, for a commoner like you, you're doing
well. Haha!"
Amy shouted angrily.
"Jokre! What are you saying? Apologize to Shirone right
now!"
"Why should I? I'm not wrong. Oh, was that a public secret? But
it's better for your mental health to give up. It's already been published in the journal. Every student
knows."
"Really? Then should I say whatever comes to mind too? Are you okay
with that?"
Jokre's eyes narrowed. He didn't know what she was going to say, but he
couldn't help feeling pricked.
'Amy...'
After five years, Amy had grown into a much prettier girl than she was
as a child.
Her hair was permed, something he hadn't seen even in the journal, and
she wore bright, cute clothes. It was clear she had dressed up with care.
'Tch! I guess this look is only for Shirone?'
Amy was the girl most coveted by the boys at Eins Magic School.
Across the kingdom, the number would be much higher.
Thinking that she dressed up and put on makeup just for one person made
him feel like he had lost to Shirone without even fighting.
'I'm disappointed, Amy. How can you date a commoner unless you're out of
your mind? No matter how skilled he is, he's still just a student.'
Seeing Shirone frown without retorting after being insulted as a
commoner only made Jokre more annoyed.
'He should at least argue, but he doesn't say a word? Is he ignoring me?
No, he must be scared of the term "official mage." Pathetic, hiding behind a girl... tsk
tsk.'
Jokre was mistaken.
To Shirone, who had even fought against Bosun, a certified 4th-grade
mage, the title of official mage wasn't much of a threat.
The reason Shirone was frowning wasn't Jokre but the Armand he was
wearing.
A person's mind flows like water.
And Armand's precise control was a delicate function that synchronized
almost 100% with its owner's mind.
If he couldn't perfectly control his emotions, Armand would move before
he could calm himself.
That's why it was called a cursed sword.
Of course, it wouldn't have mattered to its previous owner, Zion. He
could just kill as his emotions dictated.
But Shirone had to continue his school life normally.
As a commoner with no one to rely on, committing murder would put him in
an irreversible situation.
The problem was that after returning from Kazra, it was harder to
control his emotions than before.
Though the demon Behemoth was trapped in the sealing formation, it was
connected to Shirone's mind through the keyword "anger."
If his emotions amplified to an uncontrollable state, Armand would
decapitate his opponent without a second thought.
'I guess I shouldn't have worn it. I'll have to be more careful in the
future.'
Seeing Shirone unable to retaliate, Jokre clicked his tongue and pointed
to the game area, speaking to Amy.
"It seems you can't believe everything in the journal. You couldn't
even win a doll for your girlfriend. Mind if we show you how it's done?"
Amy shrugged.
"It's a game anyone can play if they pay. Go ahead if you
want."
Jokre's group entered the game area.
Vivian, seemingly aware of Shirone's challenge, spoke loudly on
purpose.
"Guys, if you win the Ice Queen doll, you have to give it to
me."
"Let's see. Well, it should be easy for Rudvans."
The announcer handed over the box of snowballs. Although Jokre paid, it
was Rudvans who received the box.
Jokre looked back at Shirone and Amy, pointing his thumb at
Rudvans.
"Rudvans is our school's 'Tuku' representative player. You can
expect great things."
Tuku is a combat sport derived from ancient warfare, where teams of five
compete in an arena with obstacles, trying to hit opponents with rubber balls.
While amateurs, including children, can enjoy it, at the
semi-professional level of school competitions, the speed of fastballs can reach up to 160 kilometers
per hour.
Additionally, tactics using three-cushion shots and various types of
breaking balls have been developed, making it an exciting yet injury-prone sport.
Rudvans was the ace of his school, and the Eins Tuku team was strong
enough to reach the quarterfinals in last year's national competition.
"Well... let's start with a trial shot."
Rudvans' stance was clearly different from that of an ordinary person.
Each movement had a mechanism, and they connected smoothly to create the best throwing power.
As he swung his arm forward, the snowball flew with a whoosh.
It hit the target precisely, and Vivian cheered.
"Great! A success on the first try! As expected of our school's
ace!"
