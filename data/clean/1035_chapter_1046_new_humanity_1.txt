Chapter 1046:
New Humanity (1)
In the imperial city of Yeomna in Jincheon, many lights were still on as
midnight approached.
Jingang called Shirone.
"Come in."
Entering the emperor's residence, Shirone found Jingang sitting alone,
sipping from a cup.
"Come in."
As the door closed and Shirone sat down cautiously, Jingang lifted a
porcelain bottle of liquor.
"Seems like you're busy."
Receiving the drink, Shirone replied,
"Jincheon is vast. Thanks to your help, many issues are being
resolved quickly."
Jingang was a strong ally of Shirone, which would be crucial in the
upcoming Holy War Council.
Breaking the awkward silence, Shirone spoke.
"Currently, Seongeum is…"
"Don't misunderstand. That's not why I called you. A true man waits
if he has made a promise."
Though he was curious, Jingang was not one to let personal feelings
influence important matters.
"The people of Jincheon are grateful. Without you, the damage from
the demonic realm would have been much worse."
The Cheongryong Mountains, where Bassago had opened the demonic realm,
were now completely isolated.
"I just did what I had to do. Fortunately, the range of the mental
broadcast wasn't too wide."
A radius of 300 kilometers wasn't small, but it was localized compared
to Jincheon.
Jingang got to the point.
"Have you heard of the New Humanity?"
"Yes. It's the name given by the intelligent beings among the
mutants in the Cheongryong Mountains. They're isolated now, but once the situation in hell is resolved,
we can find a way."
"They've started a rebellion."
Shirone narrowed his eyes.
"I just received the intelligence. They have broken out of the area
designated by the imperial city and decimated the forces. The northern and eastern gates of the
mountains have been breached, and the mutants are attacking nearby cities."
"Is that… possible? The northern gate is guarded by Yucheong, and
the eastern gate by Hwang Iseong."
The reason the Five Dragon Generals were dispatched was due to the high
risk.
"The scale of the rebellion is enormous. I don't know how they've
increased their numbers. But if they suddenly appeared like this, it's one of two things. Either it was
planned long ago, or…"
"An unexpected variable occurred."
"Exactly. Both are possible. After all, the New Humanity is
different from humans."
'Different from humans.'
That was true.
"Please quell the rebellion. If their forces expand, there's no
guarantee they won't reach the imperial city."
"Yes. I'll go there myself."
For Shirone, maintaining Jincheon's imperial power was essential to
fight in the Holy War.
Given the urgency, he packed up and left the imperial city
immediately.
The Earth Dragon Gaitan and the White Dragon Asraiker were
waiting.
"Are we leaving right away?"
"Yes. We're heading to the Cheongryong Mountains. Let's adjust our
speed to arrive by morning."
Flying into the sky, Shirone spread his wings as he looked towards the
mountains.
'Biological mutation.'
With the demonic realm open for quite some time, they would be entirely
different from what he imagined.
"Let's go."
As Shirone explosively propelled himself forward, the apostles followed
closely behind.
Dawn broke faintly beyond the Cheongryong Mountains.
"The sun is rising."
The scene below was far more severe than Shirone had initially
expected.
Walls built solely by labor had crumbled like sawteeth, and corpses
filled the fields.
Asraiker's eyes changed.
"Shall we enter immediately?"
If they had rebelled under the banner of the New Humanity, there must be
a leader.
'Crush the head swiftly.'
It made sense, but seeing the extent of the damage on the ground,
Shirone changed his mind.
"Let's assess the situation first. I have a bad feeling. There
might be something we don't know."
Flying to the northern gate, which had suffered the most damage, Shirone
landed in the ruins.
"Great Star!"
Soldiers searching for survivors in the collapsed buildings all bowed
their heads.
"Where is the general?"
"Resting in the medical ward. He sustained serious injuries but is
fortunately not in mortal danger."
"Serious injuries?"
Did that mean the Great Swordmaster Yucheong of the Five Dragon Generals
was hurt?
"Gaitan, stay here and gather information. Be thorough about last
night's events."
"Understood."
Asraiker couldn't do this task.
Following the soldier's guidance, they entered the medical ward, where
Yucheong struggled to sit up.
"Great Star."
He was bandaged from chest to abdomen.
"Just lie down."
"No. For a warrior, this is nothing… Ugh!"
He wanted to maintain his pride, but a groan escaped
involuntarily.
'What a disgrace.'
Asraiker stood silently in the corner of the medical ward, avoiding eye
contact.
Since being scolded by Shirone, she avoided meeting people's eyes as
much as possible.
Yet, in Yucheong's heart, she was everything.
'She's truly beautiful. It's a shame I can't be her lover.'
Even the pain from his nearly shredded abdomen was now
unnoticeable.
"Who did this to you?"
But as soon as Shirone asked, the feeling of walking through a flower
field turned into a nightmare.
The fear was so intense that even his feelings for the White Dragon were
overshadowed.
Yucheong's body trembled.
"It wasn't… human."
He could still see it in his mind.
"No, it looked human. But it felt different. Not an animal, not a
human, not a plant, not an insect. I can't explain it…"
"Is that the leader of the rebellion?"
"I don't know. Somehow, they infiltrated the gate. Then they
revealed their true nature and started killing indiscriminately. When I came to my senses, the soldiers
were all torn apart like paper."
As Yucheong clenched his fists, blood slowly seeped through the
bandages.
"My sword didn't even graze it. Of course, it never came
close."
"Then how did you get these injuries?"
"Its eyes changed."
Yucheong's face turned pale.
"Not just a feeling, its entire eyes turned black. That was the
end. When I regained consciousness…"
He was lying with his abdomen split open, limbs spread out like a
dissected frog.
Aaaah!
As his scream tore through the veil of memory, Yucheong curled
up.
"Damn it! Damn it!"
As the truth he had desperately avoided surfaced, his mind broke.
"I don't know! How am I alive? I didn't even feel pain then. Damn
it, what did that monster do to me? That monster…"
As Shirone stepped back, Asraiker, understanding his intention, patted
Yucheong's back.
"It's okay. You did your best. Leave it to us and
rest."
"A… alright…"
Tears of gratitude streamed down Yucheong's face as he gradually closed
his eyes in drowsiness.
Asraiker gently laid him down, but seeing the blood-soaked bandages, it
seemed further treatment was needed.
"New Humanity."
While the doctor treated the wounds, Shirone pondered in the corner of
the medical unit.
Asraiker approached.
"Messiah, eye techniques can affect the mind but can't have
physical effects."
That was the part Shirone was also questioning.
"Right. Just looking can't split someone's abdomen. If it's
possible…"
A technique of the mind.
"Could it be using quantum signals?"
"I don't know. There were demons in hell with mutant abilities.
They gained transcendent intelligence through fourth-generation mutations, but to use quantum signals,
they'd need at least a seventh-generation mutation."
It was an evolution that might not occur even if all humanity mutated
for a thousand years.
Gaitan entered.
"Messiah, I have a report."
"Go ahead."
"The mutants of the New Humanity moved east at dawn. The nearest
city is Bukjeon, with a population of about 700,000."
Shirone nodded.
"Let's go to Bukjeon. We need to identify the New Humanity first
and then strike their base."
"Yes. But…"
Gaitan added.
"The administrator said they called in an expert from the Jincheon
Emergency Bureau. They should have arrived in Bukjeon by now. I thought I should report this
first."
Shirone frowned.
"An expert? The generals of the Five Dragon Generals couldn't
handle them, so what expert?"
"Well…"
Gaitan's expression turned awkward.
"They're people you know, Messiah."
Residents of the Ivory Tower arrived at Bukjeon's city gate.
"Haha! It's been a while since we've been deployed. Nothing beats
the thrill of being in the field."
A giant over two meters tall, wearing a judge's robe, waved a fan and
shouted.
It was Tanju Ra, a 1-star resident and chief judge of the Law
Department.
"Feeling the breeze like this clears the mind. Don't you agree,
Borbor?"
A woman as thin as a toothpick, dressed entirely in black, walked beside
him.
Her half-open lips and unfocused pupils showed she had no
thoughts.
Morai Borbor.
A 2-star  resident of the Balance Department, nicknamed the
Mollusk.
"Borbor?"
Borbor suddenly turned her head.
Staring at the smiling Tanju Ra, she burst into laughter.
"Hahaha! Hahaha!"
"Hohoho! Right? Hahaha!"
The laughter abruptly stopped, and Borbor turned forward as if nothing
had happened.
'Chilling. Truly chilling.'
The nickname Mollusk came from her mental fluidity, surpassing human
limits.
Tyrant, murderer, seductress—there was no mental preference she couldn't
adapt to.
"Halt. Who goes there?"
As the city gate guards stopped them, Tanju Ra waved his fan and spoke
heartily.
"We're merchants looking to buy local specialties. We've come to
see the famous products of Bukjeon. May we enter?"
"Merchants?"
The guards exchanged glances and shook their heads.
"Bukjeon is under martial law. The governor has ordered that no one
is allowed in. You can either leave or camp until the lockdown is lifted."
"Hmm, martial law."
As Tanju Ra clicked his tongue, Borbor's eyes momentarily
focused.
'They've already infiltrated.'
It seemed they were attempting to take control from within, just as they
had when they destroyed the northern gate.
'The most efficient way to occupy a specific area is to destroy the
command structure. Even with unknown power, they resort to such tedious methods because…'
They can't tolerate inefficient means.
'New Humanity, huh.'
"Actually, we are…"
As Tanju Ra was about to show his Ivory Tower badge, Borbor raised her
hand to stop him.
She approached the guards with a pitiful expression and whispered
something in their ears.
"Hmm."
The guards' eyes wavered, and they spoke to each other in hushed tones
for a while.
"Alright. Let's check it out first."
Two guards led Borbor to a temporary post set up next to the city
gate.
As Tanju Ra tapped his foot and waited, he heard giggling from
inside.
"What are they doing?"
Suddenly, there was a loud gasp, followed by a wail, and then sorrowful
crying.
Moments later, the two guards emerged, heads bowed, wiping away
tears.
"Go ahead."
The guards opened the gate without a word, looking as if they had lost
their souls and had no future.
Following Borbor, Tanju Ra asked,
"What did you do to them? They look like they're ready to kill
themselves."
"Nothing."
Borbor's eyes lost focus again.
"They just realized what kind of people they are."
