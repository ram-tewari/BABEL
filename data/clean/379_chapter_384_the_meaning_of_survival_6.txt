Chapter 384:
The Meaning of Survival (6)
The students' eyes were filled with tension. They were witnessing the
seventh stage, something they had only heard about in rumors.
"What is this? He's being crushed continuously. Surely he won't die
by being squashed like that, right?"
"No, it can't be that. Otherwise, it wouldn't be different from the
Death Scythe."
Collie's face turned serious.
Void wasn't just about suffocation. It was a terrifying stage that could
potentially end a magician's career.
'As this is an official evaluation, we must respect the student's
decision. But Shirone, you've already won. You don't need to go that far to become a magician. You might
lose everything.'
Unable to ignore his conscience, Collie, unusually for an evaluation
teacher, requested reconsideration.
"Principal, should we proceed with this? If we miss the timing,
even deactivating the Virtual Zone might not get him out."
The students murmured at Collie's words. Was it really possible that he
couldn't get out even if they turned off the device?
Amy asked with wide eyes.
"What do you mean he can't get out? Shouldn't you have told us that
beforehand?"
"I didn't say he couldn't get out. I said he might not be able to.
And information about the survival program is classified. Our school isn't the only one using this
program."
Currently, the survival evaluation was being conducted with the same
program in all countries within the same time zone, based on the universal standard time.
Even if they had known in advance, they couldn't have said anything. But
honestly, they hadn't expected anyone to reach this point.
'It's been 19 years since I've seen this...'
Having exercised his right as an evaluation teacher, the decision now
rested with Alpheus.
"Is there a decision to be made? The student wants to do it. They
should already know how to exit the Virtual Zone, right?"
"Yes, but Void is..."
"It's on a different level. But that's why there are stages. If you
put any student in Stage 7, it would be murder, but Shirone reached it by clearing all the difficulties.
He deserves to be evaluated. How can one become a magician without being able to judge their own
life?"
It seemed like a harsh educational philosophy, but the students, who had
already overcome many dangers to get here, silently agreed.
"Ugh!"
In the vision, Shirone was pushing against the darkness with all his
might. But eventually, he couldn't hold on, and his body was flattened.
Collie sighed in resignation.
'So it's happening.'
Now, the Void stage began.
'What happened to me?'
Shirone was trapped in a two-dimensional square. Having lost time and
height, he couldn't move a finger.
'What am I supposed to do here...?'
The bottom of the square folded up like paper.
Then it folded from left to right, top to bottom, and right to left in
an endless pattern.
It was shrinking.
It was becoming infinitely small, like a one-dimensional point.
While it's impossible to fold actual matter infinitely, the Magic
Association implemented the concept of Void through a virtual program.
'Ugh...'
A massive shock struck his head. Even Shirone's Spirit Zone was on the
verge of collapse.
Was he shrinking, or was the world growing?
As the scale difference became transcendental, pressure filled his mind,
and his eyes rolled back.
With each cycle, he shrank to one-sixteenth of his size. He kept
shrinking, smaller than molecules, smaller than atoms.
'No, I can't endure this.'
The students looked blank. They couldn't understand what kind of pain
this was.
The vision's focus was on Shirone, so all they could see was the
repetitive folding pattern.
But even if they understood the mechanism of Void, their perception
wouldn't be different from now.
A realm that couldn't be understood until experienced. That was the
profound truth of Void.
"Shirone!"
Amy shouted with a pale face. Shirone's back was arching and twitching
in the Virtual Zone.
Collie clenched his fists tightly.
'Shirone, give up! If you give up now, you can live! Deactivate your
Spirit Zone!'
The Void program hammered the maximum scale difference, expressible in
cosmic units, into the mind.
Humans, confined to a single planet, didn't even have the sensory organs
to feel this kind of fear.
If it continued, even turning off the device wouldn't stop the mind from
being sucked into one dimension, eventually becoming a vegetative state, never to awaken.
Shirone's back arched off the ground.
His body, which had been fine even in extreme heat, was now violently
convulsing, indicating a dangerous state.
"Move! We need to save Shirone!"
As Amy ran towards the Virtual Zone, Nade hurriedly grabbed her
arm.
"Wait! If you interfere, both you and Shirone will
fail!"
"I don't care! We can take the exam next year! If something happens
to Shirone, I'll never forgive myself!"
Nade saw the burning determination in Amy's eyes. She was
serious.
After a moment of hesitation, he turned around.
"You're right. Let's save him together."
Then Iruki stopped them.
"Don't make hasty decisions. It's Shirone."
"What are you talking about! If Shirone...!"
"There's some change, however small."
Amy immediately closed her mouth and turned to the vision.
The same pattern was still repeating.
"Change? What change?"
"The folding is slowing down. Slightly, but surely."
Amy checked with her Red Eyes.
Indeed, the process was slower than before. And... it was getting even
slower.
'Ugh!'
Shirone had entered the Immortal Function state.
He was trying to escape the infinitely deep pit of Void by expanding his
mind into the infinite realm.
But unless he expanded his mind faster than he was shrinking, he
couldn't escape the eternal cycle.
'This isn't enough. I need a greater expansion...'
His mental cohesion began to loosen. An ordinary Unlocker would have
already lost their identity, merging with everything.
But Shirone never lost himself.
Mental transcendence.
Breaking the illusion of scale, the Immortal Function escaped the realm
of Void, and the folding stopped in the vision.
"It stopped."
The students murmured. Then, Shirone began to unfold in reverse
order.
"What? Why is he doing that?"
Something even more astonishing happened in the Virtual Zone.
The students slowly stepped back. Shirone, who had been lying down, was
staggering to his feet.
"How is he moving? He's in a semi-conscious state."
"Look! The vision!"
The students' eyes turned back to the vision. The Shirone on the screen
was standing in the same posture as the one in the Virtual Zone.
With his head bowed, he raised his arms and began to push the walls of
darkness aside.
"That... that is...!"
Collie trembled in disbelief.
The real Shirone and the one in the vision were perfectly synchronized
in their movements.
Slowly lifting his head, Shirone's eyes snapped open. Simultaneously,
the darkness in the vision disappeared, revealing the real scenery.
The same space, the same person.
Two Shirones, one in reality and one in the virtual world, stood with
their arms outstretched, glaring fiercely ahead.
"He broke out of Void on his own..."
-Stage 7, Void program has ended. Deactivating the Virtual Zone extreme
cold system.
As the machine hummed and shut down, Shirone slowly lowered his arms and
stepped out of the Virtual Zone.
The students flinched and made way for him.
Even those who had underestimated Shirone instinctively knew not to mess
with him at this moment.
He was the one who had passed Stage 7 of the survival evaluation.
As everyone's eyes followed Shirone's path, he stopped in front of
Fermi.
"I have something to say since you ran away."
Fermi's eyebrows rose.
"I will become a magician, no matter what. I don't care what you
plot, but if you interfere with my dream, I'll drop everything and crush you first."
The students' faces hardened.
It was a declaration of war against Fermi and a message to everyone
present.
Don't interfere with my dream.
Faced with his resolve to fight to the death, everyone's minds became
complicated.
'Damn. Fermi alone is troublesome enough...'
Anyone in the senior class knew Fermi had some control.
They couldn't oppose him because the first one marked would fail the
graduation exam.
Even now, Fermi's influence was significant, but Shirone's presence
began to subtly change the atmosphere.
'The second person in school history to master the survival
evaluation.'
That meant he had a 100% chance of passing if the survival subject was
in the graduation exam.
'Until now, Fermi targeted those he marked. What should I do? If I get
swayed, will I be the only one in trouble?'
Now, all eyes were on Fermi. They would have to reassess based on his
reaction.
Fermi forced a fake smile. Whatever he said would sound like a loser's
excuse.
'I'll back off today.'
As Fermi turned and walked away without a word, the students were
shocked.
It was the first time they saw him, who always led situations to his
advantage, remain silent.
'Damn rookie. From now on, I'll consider you my greatest enemy. You'll
regret making me think this way for the rest of your life.'
Fermi's face, as he walked away, showed an anger no one in the senior
class had ever seen.
"Final survivor, Arian Shirone. Red team gains 20 points, blue team
loses 20 points."
As Collie announced, the students finally returned to the team
competition, not just Shirone versus Fermi.
"We did it! We won! 20 points!"
"Plus, we earned those points by beating Fermi! This feels so
satisfying! I knew my judgment was spot on!"
Amy and Nade hugged each other and jumped up and down, while Shirone
high-fived Iruki, celebrating their victory.
"Wow..."
Maya, watching from a distance, couldn't help but exclaim in
admiration.
Though she was a veteran in the senior class, she had never seen anyone
stand up to Fermi directly before.
But soon, her eyes filled with a sense of melancholy.
'It's nice to be strong...'
She had never even dreamed of defying Fermi. Not that he would care if
she did.
For her, trying to become a magician to lift her tribe out of poverty,
Shirone seemed like someone from another world.
"Shirone! That was amazing! I can't believe you passed Stage
7!"
"I knew it! No matter how great Fermi is, he can't beat an
Unlocker!"
Shirone, Shirone, Shirone.
Even students who usually kept their distance joined in to praise
Shirone's performance today.
Shirone had climbed at least two stages higher than any other
participant, leading his team to victory. Thanks to him, 15 people gained 20 points each. On a day like
this, being cold would only harm their relationships.
After comforting the blue team members as they left the training ground,
Alpheus finally turned to look at Shirone. His feelings were mixed.
'Is this really the same boy I knew?'
The boy who had climbed over the wall at twelve, asking to be taught
magic, had now become a student who broke through Void.
He was proud of his student's growth, but also worried about how quickly
it had happened.
It was no coincidence that memories from long ago overlapped in his
mind.
Looking at Shirone, Olivia said,
"Mental transcendence, or extreme asceticism. Without one of these,
it's impossible to escape Void on your own."
