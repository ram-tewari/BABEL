Chapter 335:
Midwinter Night (6)
4 AM
The Ice Queen's Castle
A brown liquor bottle shattered against the castle wall.
"Come out, you bastards! Fight me!"
Drunk, Jokre shouted at the top of his lungs. His pupils were completely
dilated, and he staggered in a zigzag pattern.
Rudvans and Vivian supported him from either side.
"Jokre, stop it. What if we get caught? We're not supposed to be
here at this hour."
His gloomy mood had turned to anger through the alcohol.
Jokre needed someone to fight, and his defiance against the world led
him to trespass into the restricted area.
"Haha! Who's going to stop me? I'm a mage! An official mage! Bring
it on! I'll blow away any guards with a single spell!"
Jokre shook off his friends and stumbled forward.
Rudvans and Vivian couldn't stop him. They were also drunk and barely
coherent.
Rudvans, who was the most sober, grabbed Jokre again and led him into
the castle.
"It's cold. Let's go inside. If you keep shouting, we'll be in big
trouble."
"Drinks! Rudvans! Let's drink! Let's drink to our hearts'
content!"
"Alright, just be quiet and follow me."
The hall, now empty of people, felt eerie.
Objects they had passed by indifferently during the day now seemed to
emit a creepy aura, as if they had come to life.
The Ice Queen's figure, illuminated by the cold moonlight through the
window, was especially chilling.
Vivian shivered and said,
"Can we get some light magic?"
Jokre was too drunk to cast a spell, so Rudvans and Vivian attempted the
light magic.
After several failed attempts due to their inebriation, they finally
succeeded.
With a pop, two lights floated to the ceiling, instantly brightening the
hall.
Jokre crawled up the slippery ice stairs.
Reaching the 1.5th-floor corridor, he picked a fight with the wax figure
of the Ice Queen encased in ice.
"Hey, Ice Queen? Are you asleep? Want to have a drink with
me?"
There was no response.
"Haha! See? Even legendary mages are nothing special. Just stuck in
ice, sleeping! Is that even magic?"
Magic. Law that twists phenomena. Ice Queen. Mage.
The mental entity absorbed Jokre's information.
Materialized by Lyria's necromantic summoning, Optros instinctively fled
to the place where the most familiar thoughts were concentrated.
That place was the Ice Queen's castle.
Among the information spewed by tourists, the Ice Queen held the most
significance.
Optros quickly adapted to this world based on the vast amount of
information.
At some point, the mental entity, which had only existed as a sense,
developed cognition.
As sense and cognition communicated, the preservation of simple
knowledge began to transition into the stage of thought.
I think, therefore I am.
Optros concluded that it needed a form to prove its existence. It sought
a special vessel to materialize its thoughts.
The Ice Queen's eyes snapped open.
Jokre wasn't surprised. He knew that stepping on the wax figure's
pedestal would make its eyes open.
The Ice Queen's beautiful appearance made his wounds, carelessly
inflicted by a woman, throb even more.
Jokre knocked on the ice as if knocking on a door.
"Hey, Queen. Want to date me? Hahaha."
Vivian looked at Jokre's drunken antics with disdain and clicked her
tongue.
"He's completely lost it. Hey, get a grip. If you pass out here,
you'll freeze to death."
Jokre rolled up his sleeves and took a fighting stance against the Ice
Queen.
"Alright, let's do this. Mage versus mage. If I win, you date
me."
The information Optros had gathered was categorized as experience, and
it reached the stage of judging the world by its own standards.
What is strength? Superior traits. Control of properties.
Alteration, transformation, change of the physical world.
Then a question arose.
Then... am I strong?
"Enough already. Let's go back. We need to sleep. How long are you
going to keep this up?"
Seeing Jokre's half-closed eyes, Rudvans grabbed him from behind and
dragged him away.
Vivian, who had been sighing with her arms crossed, glanced at the Ice
Queen and then followed Rudvans.
But as she took a step, she turned her head sharply in shock.
"W-what?"
The Ice Queen's eyes, which should have been looking straight ahead,
were now staring at her.
Vivian thought she was still drunk.
But no matter how much she tried to clear her head, the Ice Queen's gaze
was unmistakably turned sideways.
It felt like her hair was standing on end. Her heart pounded as if it
would burst, and she was in shock.
"Guys, over here..."
Jokre's voice faded down the stairs.
"Date me... huh?"
Don't leave me!
Vivian was too terrified to scream.
The Ice Queen's wax eyes, which were clearly made of wax, now moved
freely, scanning the surroundings.
It seemed to be analyzing the material that confined it.
Vivian felt like she was going to die, even though she wasn't in
pain.
No, it was scarier than dying.
As she felt the distance from her friends growing, her desire to live
filled her lungs with air.
Vivian screamed louder than she ever had in her life.
"Heyyyyy!"
Her scream echoed through the hall.
Rudvans, who was descending the stairs, turned his head in shock as a
flash of light burst from the Ice Queen's location.
"What the...?"
Rudvans left Jokre and ran up the stairs.
The Ice Queen was enveloped in a radiant glow, and Vivian, pale-faced,
watched in terror.
"R-Rudvans... this, this..."
The wax skin of the Ice Queen cracked, and light seeped through the
gaps.
As her body vibrated, the ice block cracked and split into thousands of
pieces, ready to explode.
'We need to get out. We need to get out...'
The ice block exploded with a deafening noise. Simultaneously, Rudvans
grabbed Vivian and leaped away.
Ice shards scattered to the ends of the hall.
Jokre, who was on the stairs, covered his face as the shards flew
towards him. Then he looked up in shock.
Even as she fell on her backside, Vivian couldn't take her eyes off the
Ice Queen.
Inside the shattered wax figure was a golden, glowing
phosphorescence.
It slowly moved towards the railing.
As the light gradually faded, the Ice Queen emerged, far more vibrant
than before.
Vivian muttered in disbelief.
"The Ice Queen... has resurrected?"
Having witnessed the Ice Queen's resurrection up close, she saw the wax
skin break like a mold, revealing a new Ice Queen inside.
Jokre, who had reached the opposite corridor, started to run towards
Vivian but stopped abruptly.
Like Vivian, his face turned pale.
"The Ice Queen?"
Was the legend true?
If this wasn't a dream, he was now facing the mage who had brought death
to countless people.
"Haaa."
The Ice Queen opened her mouth and exhaled. Frost escaped, causing the
air to freeze with a cracking sound.
"This is the freezing phenomenon."
Her voice was beautiful.
But as Jokre met the Ice Queen's lifeless eyes, he felt his blood freeze
in his veins.
The Ice Queen, looking at Jokre with indifferent eyes, spoke.
"If you defeat me, I will date you."
"U-ugh!"
Jokre's nerves raced towards death.
Conversely, his desire to live instantly sobered him up, and he deployed
the magic he had trained in the longest.
A fireball formed in his palm.
Jokre specialized in close-range fire magic.
Though not the fastest, he prided himself on its power.
"Jokre! No!"
Vivian shouted. From a third-party perspective, it was a reckless
action.
But if she had met the Ice Queen's eyes, she would have done the
same.
"Die, die!"
Jokre's fireball hit the Ice Queen's head directly.
No, it seemed she had no intention of dodging. With a pop, her head
burst into flames.
"Ahhh!"
Vivian screamed, imagining the wax figure as a human candle.
The damage was done.
Rudvans, the most rational, carefully observed the Ice Queen's condition
and muttered.
"Is she dead?"
The Ice Queen didn't move. All her nerves were concentrated in her
brain.
The sensation of experiencing pain for the first time in her new form
was intriguing.
Having transformed the law into a biological form, the pain of her face
burning was excruciating.
But to her, who couldn't clearly define pain, it was just an abnormal
sensory state.
'Is this heat? Absorb and suppress the energy.'
As she absorbed the energy through the law, the fire vanished, and the
surrounding air froze.
The Ice Queen's grotesquely distorted face was revealed.
Jokre, who had caused this, felt his legs give way and fell on his
backside.
"Please, spare me..."
The Ice Queen savored the spasms of her facial nerves.
Pain was an unpleasant sensation.
'The brain...'
As the Ice Queen approached Jokre, her face began to regenerate.
"I almost melted."
If the brain melts, you die.
That is a living being.
"Please, spare me! I'm sorry!"
Jokre prostrated himself, begging for his life. Facing a mage who
survived even with a burnt face was madness.
"Jokre!"
Vivian and Rudvans ran to block Jokre.
Vivian and Rudvans rushed to stand in front of Jokre.
Though they couldn't hope to match the Ice Queen, Jokre felt his friends
were like saviors, protecting him.
Vivian knelt and pleaded.
"Please, spare us! My friend is drunk. He never intended to
challenge you, Your Majesty."
"Drunk?"
Alcohol. Brain. Disruptive substance.
"I see."
Realizing that the reason for her pain was a brain-disrupting substance,
the Ice Queen lost interest.
"Consuming alcohol doesn't seem beneficial for living
beings."
"Yes! Yes! We're very sorry."
"So, what will you do now?"
The Ice Queen asked with genuine curiosity.
She had taken on a human form and met humans. She was curious about what
they would do to her.
Pure curiosity and madness often go hand in hand, and the Ice Queen's
honest question filled Jokre with fear.
"We'll do anything you ask. Just please spare our
lives."
"Anything I ask?"
"Yes, anything..."
The Ice Queen tilted her head.
"Why?"
Jokre looked up at the Ice Queen in bewilderment.
As he regained his senses, he realized her manner of speaking and
behavior were strange.
But if the legend was true, she had been in hibernation for 800 years.
It was likely she had lost her memory.
"Of course, Your Majesty is a ruler. A great mage. So you should
rule again."
"Rule?"
The Ice Queen tilted her head further, then frowned as if something
displeased her.
Though she had gained individuality by taking on a human form, her
information processing speed was significantly slower.
But she accepted this neural discrepancy as a characteristic of living
beings, not as something unpleasant.
"I see. I will rule. Because I am strong."
Jokre sighed in relief.
The Ice Queen was still the Ice Queen. If he had tried any tricks, he
would have lost his life.
"Absolutely right. We are nothing compared to you."
Jokre groveled endlessly.
He had worked his whole life to achieve his first success.
If he died a meaningless death here before enjoying life as a mage, he
wouldn't be able to rest in peace even as a corpse.
