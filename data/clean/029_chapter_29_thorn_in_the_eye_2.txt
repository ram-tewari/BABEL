Chapter 29:
Thorn in the Eye (2)
Etela touched her lips thoughtfully.
"Then would you like to demonstrate in the Image Zone?"
"What? Now?"
"Why? Not feeling well?"
"No, that's not it, but..."
Etela blinked in confusion. The enthusiastic student who had wanted to use the Image Zone
from the first day of class had recently become passive in lessons.
'What's happening? He doesn't look good. The children seem to be antagonizing him—is that
why? He's more sensitive than I thought.'
Etela patted his shoulder and said:
"If you're not feeling well, we can do it next time. You're the only one in Class
Seven who can change the Four Direction Formation, so I just wanted to see it once."
"No, I'll try it. Please evaluate me."
Shirone changed his mind. Even if his classmates criticized him, he wouldn't miss the
opportunity to receive guidance from Teacher Etela for such a reason.
As Shirone entered the Image Zone, insults flew at him.
"Oh? Looks like he's going to do it again? So desperate for attention."
"Probably wants to impress his girlfriend. I mean, that's all he can do, right? He
can't do any magic at all."
In contrast, the senior students stopped their practice and turned to watch. A month had
passed since he succeeded with the defensive form of the Four Direction Formation, so they were curious
about how much he had grown.
"Teacher, I'm starting."
"Alright. Let's go one by one."
Shirone demonstrated his specialty, the defensive form. A massive cube was created with a
strong appearance.
The senior students exclaimed in admiration.
"Just looking at the defensive form, he's upper class level. That's almost armor
level."
Meanwhile, Class Seven students glared at Shirone as if he were a common enemy. Yet they
couldn't complain because his level was truly impressive.
Shirone deployed the Four Direction Formation in offensive form. The zone's size
decreased, but numerous spikes protruded menacingly.
Next was the target form. The length of the cross was considerable, but the rotation was
weak for practical use. Nevertheless, it was not an exaggeration to describe it as rapid progress.
Etela smiled with satisfaction. Soon he could be promoted early to Class Six.
Shirone didn't stop there and attempted the departure form. It was important to fail too
if he wanted to receive advice from Etela.
Predictably, he encountered obstacles. The Spirit Zone's shape would change, or even when
he moved the center, the zone would burst just before departure.
Etela's eyes lit up with surprise. She hadn't even expected him to try, given their
opposing inclinations, yet he was getting fairly close.
Meanwhile, the children of Class Seven, who didn't even think of attempting the departure
form, each breathed sighs of relief.
"Hehehe, what's he doing? Is that supposed to be a Four Direction Formation? Just
child's play."
"Some genius he is. Not even good at one thing. How does he expect to become a
wizard like this?"
Shirone felt upset. Not a single person in Class Seven could manage the departure form.
He couldn't understand the mindset of applying standards to others that they themselves couldn't meet.
As he lost his composure, his Spirit Zone wobbled. This only encouraged his classmates,
who didn't stop badmouthing him in an effort to bring him down.
Amy, watching the demonstration, frowned. The formation conversion had been satisfactory,
but suddenly the zone was shaking.
"What's wrong with him? What is he doing?"
"The departure form is naturally difficult. He seems too concerned with the
children's reactions."
"No way. He's not the type to be shaken by that. Is something really going on?"
"Oh my, defending him? Well, of course, he is your man after all."
Amy silenced Seriel with a red face and fell into thought. Something was definitely
wrong. Where was the endurance that had overcome Anti-Magic? His Spirit Zone was wobbling like a drunk
person.
'Is there really something I don't know about?'
Shirone struggled to suppress his emotions. But anger, once it arises, grows by consuming
itself, making it difficult to control.
'Why do they hate me so much? What did I do wrong? I just want to learn hard and do
better. What's wrong with wanting to become a wizard?'
His Spirit Zone began to fluctuate violently, as if about to burst at any moment.
"Hey, you're thinking too much."
Shirone suddenly awoke from his thoughts. Someone from the Class Five area had spoken.
"If there's something you want to do, just do it. You came here to learn magic,
right?"
Shirone finally looked at his state coolly. Once he came to his senses, he painfully
realized how foolish his thoughts had been.
'What was I doing?'
Having cleared his distractions, Shirone attempted the departure form. Whether he failed
or succeeded, it was fine. What mattered was taking a step forward through the attempt.
The 40-meter diameter zone slowly began to move away from its center. As the center
distanced, the diameter decreased exponentially.
Which comes first, departure or dissolution?
All the advanced class students stared intently at the scene.
By the time the zone had shrunk to a diameter of 2 meters, the surface of the Spirit Zone
completely detached from Shirone's body.
Class Seven students were indescribably shocked. He had succeeded at a technique
difficult even for advanced class seniors.
The Spirit Zone began to rapidly orbit around Shirone. Every moment, new information
flooded in. The massive amount of information nearly made his head explode. When he blinked his eyes wide
open, the Spirit Zone popped and disappeared.
"Haa, haa."
He was out of breath despite having done nothing.
"I did it. I really... succeeded with the departure form."
The upper class began to stir.
It was an achievement incomparable to succeeding with the defensive form. Not only in
Class Seven but also in Class Six, no one had succeeded with the departure form.
Seriel shook Amy's arm excitedly.
"Amy, Shirone did it! He really did it!"
Amy was smiling too. Regardless of her feelings, it was a talent worthy of respect as a
colleague pursuing the same dream.
'His growth speed is incredible. No, it's way too fast. If he can succeed with the
departure form in a month, surely...'
A talent matching mine. Or even beyond.
Amy felt a bit frustrated. Shirone must have trained his mind every day without fail. But
what about herself? She had been wandering back alleys causing mischief.
'Why... did I waste precious time so futilely?'
After succeeding with the departure form, Shirone looked around the Class Five area for
the owner of the advice. But he couldn't tell who it was by voice alone since he hadn't had the chance to
talk with seniors.
'I wanted to thank them...'
Etela, watching Shirone receive applause, fell into thought. Everyone was stimulated by
Shirone's growth. She thought of a way to further invigorate the class atmosphere.
"Hmm, it came earlier than expected."
Recently, Shirone had one private concern. He didn't know how to use any magic at all.
The knowledge needed for magic wasn't something that lower class children could easily
acquire. That's why the school only began practical magic training from Class Five.
But reality was different. Nobles received education from birth, so even lower class
students knew how to use at least one magic spell. In contrast, Shirone's study period was only the 1 year
and 6 months he spent with the Ozent family.
Shirone's dilemma was here. If he focused on one discipline, he might be able to use
simple magic. But he could never surpass students who had been educated from childhood if he followed the
same approach.
That's how he arrived at the strategy of studying entire fields and their derivative
disciplines together.
As a result, he had to borrow so many books that these days he practically lived in the
library.
Students ridiculed Shirone for this. They thought it was stupid to study so haphazardly
when he couldn't even keep up with current classes.
But Shirone didn't change his mind. With a backbone of knowledge, any discipline could be
arranged according to time sequence. This meant the probability of forgetting content was significantly
reduced.
'The immediate results might be minimal. But at least 2 years. If I can study like this
for 2 years, all the core knowledge in each field will connect.'
This would be the framework of knowledge. It meant having an entire encyclopedia of
information in his head, accessible for any information.
Shirone immersed himself in books with renewed determination. When he regained awareness,
it was well past midnight. The only person left in the reading room was a girl from Class Six.
As Shirone nodded slightly while packing his bag, she returned a shy smile.
When he left the library, it was 2 AM.
Even if he went in now, he could only sleep for about 4 hours. But when he thought about
how he was behind in studies compared to children from noble families, even sleep seemed wasteful.
At such times, Shirone recalled a book he had once read called "Dialogues of Great
Scholars."
Particularly impressive was an interview with a biologist named Torkoklin. He who claimed
that people must get at least 6 hours of sleep, made this joke at the end of the interview:
"Of course, I only slept 4 hours during my school days."
The author of the book asked:
"Then isn't your research finding wrong? You became an outstanding scholar despite
sleeping only 4 hours."
Torkoklin's answer was:
"Haha! Teenagers are not human. They are superhumans. Sleeping or not probably makes
no difference to them."
For Shirone, who was pressed for time, these words were a great encouragement.
'Right! Let's work hard. If I do my best, results will follow.'
The graduation class building had its lights on. The windows flickered, perhaps from
late-night magic experiments. What new magic was being created in there?
Shirone's gaze was already set on the graduation class.
As lunch time was ending, Etela came looking for Siina. At 26 years old, they were the
same age and had worked together for 4 years, but they still spoke formally to each other.
"Teacher Siina, could you spare some time?"
"Yes. But I have a Class Seven lesson scheduled, so I won't be able to talk for
long."
Discussing magic often took more than an hour, so Siina apologized in advance.
"Ah, I see. I wanted to discuss something about Shirone."
At the mention of Shirone, Siina also showed interest. She had heard that while he was
mediocre in the theory classes she taught, he excelled in Etela's practical sessions.
"How's he doing these days? I saw his practical scores were quite high."
"They certainly are. Last time, he even succeeded with the departure form of the
Four Direction Formation."
"Oh, I see."
Siina smiled contentedly. As Shirone's homeroom teacher, it was pleasant to receive
praise from another teacher.
"So I wanted to make a suggestion to you, Teacher Siina."
"A suggestion?"
It was quite rare for Etela to make recommendations to another teacher about a student.
Expecting something dramatic, Siina blinked her eyes.
The blackboard in Class Seven's classroom was filled with chemical theory. The students
busily moved their eyes and hands, taking notes. The lesson seemed faster than usual today. As expected,
Siina ended the class about 10 minutes early and stepped down from the podium.
"That's all for today. Instead, I have an announcement."
The students were magnanimous. If she just ended the class early, they would listen even
if she talked about ghosts eating rice.
"An early promotion candidate has been decided. Arian Shirone, please stand
up."
Shirone was startled by the sudden announcement of early promotion. Meanwhile, his
classmates wore displeased expressions. Early promotion wasn't particularly rare. But the problem was that
Shirone was the candidate.
"Student Shirone will be promoted to Class Five. From tomorrow, please attend
classes according to their schedule. I hope all of you will also strive harder for opportunities. That is
all."
