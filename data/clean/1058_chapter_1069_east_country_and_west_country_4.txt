Chapter 1069:
East Country and West Country (4)
The entrance to Atogram was a 30-meter-high archway.
Despite its massive size, it didn't feel out of place because the city's
walls were even taller.
User code confirmed. Entry permitted.
Unlike reality, everything here was automated, and the enormous iron
gates opened to the sides.
Just the first sight made Shirone's head spin.
"Wow, there are so many!"
Various advanced machines moved through the futuristic city.
The neon signs on the buildings flashed in so many colors that it was
dizzying.
"We're meeting Yolga's Son in the evening, so let's hunt in the
meantime. We need to level up," said Death Princess.
"How about getting some parts first? Using just rifles is a bit
annoying," she added.
A team passing by saw Shirone's group and burst into laughter.
"Ha! Look at those guys. Isn't that an old helmet? Do they still
have those?"
"Reminds me of the old days. We used to catch Metal Rats with
those. Are the Tenmen still around?"
Destroyer707 stomped the ground.
"Damn it! If I hadn't cleaned my codename, they wouldn't even talk
to me."
Shirone asked, "You said you were level 180, right? Maybe those
users are higher. This place gathers high-level users."
"No, just by looking, they're barely level 100. You can tell by the
parts. They're from Spread Corp, which only deals with mass-produced items. If they're over level 100
and still using those, it's a joke."
Strongest Codename said, "When you reach Atogram, you usually
gather about 20,000 galaxies. The standard is to buy a package of parts."
Shirone asked, "What about selective options? It keeps blinking
that I can buy something from the 'Electronic Store.'"
Death Princess said, "Turn off the beginner tips. They're annoying
while hunting. I'll explain. First, go into the selective options."
Following her instructions, a slot appeared with an 'Electronic Store'
indicator below it.
"You can buy functions for the selective options from the
Electronic Store. But right now, there's only 'Communication,' which costs 20,000 galaxies. It's less
efficient than buying a package unless you're doing organized activities like the Tenmen."
"Well, we can communicate verbally. But there's only one slot. Do I
add functions there?"
"Yes. You'll get one more slot at level 20 and another at level 30,
for a total of three options. From level 40, new functions will be registered in the 'Electronic Store'
every 10 levels. Then you can choose options based on your preference, situation, or
mission."
Strongest Codename said, "If Yolga's Son is supporting you, you can
buy communication, but it's a matter of preference. We have time until evening, so let's buy a package
and hunt."
Destroyer707 looked at Shirone.
"By the way, are you sure Yolga's Son will help you? Will you get
galaxies too?"
"Probably. I don't know how much he can help, but he'll have to
give everything he's got."
It was a matter of humanity's future.
"Really? You must be really close."
There seemed to be a slight misunderstanding, but there was no need to
explain in detail.
"Haha, well…"
Destroyer707 pointed to the city.
"Let's go to 'Maron.' It's the best for beginners."
Entering the commercial district specializing in parts, they found it
crowded with people.
"There are so many."
"The most important thing in High Gear after levels is parts. There
are 270 companies with shops here. Some are even run by users."
"Users?"
"Of course. Now users can exchange all items. Some sell parts at
stalls, and some buy shops with galaxies and open stores."
Death Princess added, "Be careful with user-made parts. They often
have unique or excellent functions but are weak in balance. My right arm is from Megamix, and if it's
not compatible, it's a waste of money."
"That's your story," Strongest Codename said, making Death
Princess bristle.
"That's why I'm telling you! If we had plenty of galaxies, we could
experiment with anything, but we're poor. We have to buy carefully."
When they arrived at 'Maron,' a humanoid machine with visible metal
frames approached them.
"Welcome. How can I help you?"
"We want to buy parts. The beginner Maron package. It's still
20,000 galaxies, right? When's the sale period?" Destroyer707 asked.
The friendly guide robot brought a box larger than a person.
"They put a lot of effort into the packaging."
The box featured a cute girl winking as if begging to be bought.
"All commercial parts come in boxes. Once opened, they're
non-refundable. Some users collect them for specific models."
"So we can't check the contents. Well, it's a program, so there
shouldn't be defects."
"There are no defects. But you can check the contents. There's an
AI simulation mark on the box. Touch it for a virtual experience."
Swallowing the words "This is already virtual," Shirone
reached for the box.
An electric signal scanned Shirone, and he was suddenly equipped with
the parts.
"Huh?"
The appearance was almost the same, but bearings were in every joint,
and the seams were visible on the face.
"Hehe, interesting, right? You can experiment and buy if you like
it. But as Death Princess said, user-sold items don't have this function, so be careful."
"Joints…"
Shirone rotated his elbow 360 degrees and turned his neck to see his
back.
"It's really strange."
Death Princess said, "It'll feel unfamiliar at first. But ball
joints are very convenient in combat. Of course, durability issues arise later. Anyway, let's buy. We
can't afford anything else."
Shirone placed his palm on the 'Purchase' in the box and paid the
galaxies, just like his companions.
"Choose immediate replacement. You'll experience it later anyway,
but it's a bit strange."
Curious about body replacement, but still feeling more resistance,
Shirone agreed.
"Okay, immediate replacement."
After the transaction, the replacement process was skipped, and the four
of them became androids.
"Let's hunt now. You'll get used to the parts with a bit of play.
So, where should we start?"
Strongest Codename said, "Since we don't have a nationality, we
should go to the neutral zone. It'll be annoying there too, but…"
Exiting Atogram's north gate, they saw an abandoned city 1 kilometer
away.
Creepy creatures inhabited it, and some users were happily
hunting.
Spatial Information: Destroy-033.
"Destroy is a basic hunting ground in High Gear. Since we're level
10, let's target the war robots. See that one?"
A machine with triangular legs stomping the ground and looking around
caught their attention.
As the auto-aim locked on, Shirone's right arm underwent a mechanical
transformation.
Ching! Ching!
In less than a second, his hand had transformed into a gun barrel about
a hand's length.
"Whoa!"
"It looks weird, but bear with it. For beginner parts, this is
pretty good. The main firepower of these parts is energy bullets. See the vertical gauge on the side of
your view?"
"Yeah, it says 1,000/1,000."
"Energy bullets use a battery. One battery can fire 1,000 shots.
Other companies sell parts that use real bullets, but galaxies can't cover that. It's less efficient
too."
"How much is a battery?"
"500 galaxies. That's affordable even if you mess up hunting. Let's
start."
Gunfire echoed through Destroy-033.
The speed and firepower were incomparable to when they were in bio
mode.
Bang! Bang!
Every time the four of them concentrated their energy bullets, the war
robots shattered with a satisfying sound.
"Five hundred shots left."
"But we've already gathered 2,000 galaxies. A bit more, and we'll
level up."
Shirone was achieving the highest scores.
"Over there! And there!"
He destroyed war robots, rotating his elbow and neck joints.
'…His adaptability is amazing. He said it was gross before.'
One thing was clear.
'He said it's his first time in High Gear, but this guy has fought a
lot.'
He had watched rankers' raids via satellite broadcasts.
All the strategies, tactics, reflexes, and judgment showed that skills
were more than just functions.
'His level is low, but…'
Watching Yahweh2's combat, the thrill he felt from the rankers came
rushing back like déjà vu.
'Who is this guy?'
For the first time, he was curious about his identity, but Destroyer707
shook his head.
He wouldn't like it if someone tried to uncover his real
identity.
'Let's just hunt.'
With that thought, he refocused, but suddenly Shirone shouted
loudly.
"Dodge!"
At the same time, sparks flew from Death Princess's body.
"Ugh!"
As the remaining three turned their heads, they saw someone smirking 250
meters away.
'User codename…'
Chaos5MinutesBefore.
'West Country.'
Shirone had sensed it through intuition, but there was nothing he could
do except warn them verbally.
Death Princess shouted, "Hey! What are you doing? Do you want to
die?"
"Haha! Sorry, my mistake. I was aiming for the wolf over there and
missed… Sorry."
Chaos5MinutesBefore apologized, but the group was still
displeased.
'Yeah, right. A mistake.'
Auto-aim doesn't make mistakes, and the parts covering his joints
indicated he was over level 20.
'A level 20 hunting here?'
They considered fighting but didn't want to lose their newly bought
equipment, so they turned away.
"Ignore him. He's just bored and picking on beginners."
Shirone checked Death Princess's side.
"It's broken."
"Yeah. Durability is 80/100. It's a Maron bolt gun. Let's just
avoid him. His armor is better than ours."
Since she said so, Shirone also backed off, glaring at
Chaos5MinutesBefore.
He grinned and waved.
About ten minutes later, while hunting again, Shirone felt that annoying
sensation once more.
"Dodge…"
Bang!
Death Princess's left arm was shattered.
'Penetration judgment.'
It was clear he had aimed deliberately, and Shirone turned around with a
fierce look.
Chaos5MinutesBefore rubbed his head.
"Haha! Sorry! Another mistake. I'll be really careful from now on,
so please forgive me."
Shirone didn't believe him.
"Let's fight. He's a jerk."
"If we retaliate, it becomes hostile action. If we die in that
state, we lose our loot. That's what he's aiming for…"
Before he could finish, Shirone kicked off the ground and fired his
energy gun at Chaos5MinutesBefore.
"Haha! You shot? Now it's hostile action!"
The level 23 AI was automatically dodging Shirone's bullets.
'Heh, Maron parts are easy.'
He didn't care much about the loot but wanted to see the user's
frustrated reaction.
"This is what a bolt gun is."
His energy bullets fired with a much heavier sound than Shirone's
weapon.
And Shirone…
"Huh?"
He kept dodging the barrage of bullets and continued to counterattack.
The distance was closing.
"W-What?"
Chaos5MinutesBefore's evasion probability was rapidly dropping to
zero.
