Chapter 652:
The Air of Life (1)
"H-How…?!"
Austin was a veteran.
He had completed countless missions over thirty years and was still
actively working in the field—that alone proved his credentials.
It hadn't been a life of grand adventures, but it was precisely the vile
and revolting tasks that hid beneath the veil of an ordinary life.
If his life were written into a book, most people would mistake it for
the diary of a madman or a sleazy novel. But everything he had experienced had been real, and very
little could stir his heart anymore.
If a friend of ten years stabbed him for no reason, he wouldn't hesitate
to slit the man's throat with the dagger tucked up his sleeve.
A monster?
When hundreds of eyes burst forth from Shirone's robe, Austin felt his
heart race for the first time in years.
His ribs hurt, and a strange nervous reaction made him dizzy.
"Ghhh... What is this…?!"
As the members of the Spear and Death bandit crew, who had been writhing
on the ground, began to shakily rise to their feet, Shirone realized something.
They're moving even under Anti-thesis?
Though he had diffused the effect into hundreds of weaker projections,
normal bandits shouldn't have been able to move at all.
"There! Kill that one!"
More spearmen leapt down from the opposite ridge.
Just by the height of their jumps, it was obvious they were skilled
Skima users.
Austin conjured flames in his hands and shouted:
"Careful! It's the Spear and Death bandits!"
That group—composed entirely of spearmen—were elite operatives trained
in secret by Tormia's National Intelligence to infiltrate Kazra. After becoming a ghost unit due to
political fallout, they had turned into battle-hardened outlaws.
"Shut those eyes down!"
With the focus of soldiers trained to produce results, they found a
solution—brute force. Gritting through the effects of Anti-thesis, they formed a living wall of
bodies.
As the second wave pushed their spears through gaps between their
comrades, twenty spears crisscrossed like latticework and pierced Shirone.
Damn it. It's over.
Just as Austin, who had already retreated, thought it was the end, a
blinding curtain of light exploded from within the spearmen's formation.
"Uwaaaaaah!"
Caught in a multi-layered optical blast, their bodies twisted violently,
and as Shirone crouched low, massive wings of light unfurled from his shoulders.
"Fire! Pour everything on him!"
Shirone's gaze turned upward to target the Ear of the Deep bandit crew
in the sky.
Specializing in terrain-based guerrilla warfare, their mana-charged
arrows raining from the sky posed a serious threat.
As the mana rounds poured down like rain, the mountain path was
shredded, and the explosion consumed the entire landscape—until Shirone soared upward, his radiant wings
flaring.
"Fire! Fire! Fire!"
The Ear of the Deep's captain unleashed a barrage as Shirone approached,
followed by dozens more mana rounds.
Shirone stopped midair, folding his wings forward. The air pressure
hardened nearly into a solid barrier, causing the rounds to explode before reaching him.
"What the hell is that?!"
The shockwave rocked even the hang gliders, and then dozens of Photon
Cannons stretched out like blinding spears of light.
Boom! Boom-boom-boom!
With impact force like cannonballs, the bandits died before they even
hit the ground, and it took less than a minute to wipe out a force of sixty.
"What is that magic…?"
Austin, staring dumbstruck at the blinding aerial assault, suddenly felt
both feet lift off the ground.
A claw swept past his eyes—Claw, of the Beast Lynx bandit crew, who
fought like an animal.
Looking back, he realized Rian had grabbed him by the waist and was
sprinting at full speed.
"Put me down! I need to fight too—!"
"Stay still. This is way beyond you."
The Beast Lynx, who made their lairs in the harsh zones of the
underground mountains, moved with terrifying beastlike agility—lethal for any mage.
Rian, wielding his massive sword with one hand, swung it through the
air. Each time the blade sliced the wind, it was followed by the sound of flesh and bone being torn
apart.
"Kill him! Don't let him escape!"
Despite their feline-like leaps and graceful arcs, Rian moved in
defiance of inertia, backstepping and killing each one with terrifying precision.
When he had retreated nearly 100 meters, a trail of corpses from the
Beast Lynx formed a clean line along the slope.
Such overwhelming difference in ability made the whole fight look
effortless—and the results shook Austin to his core.
This is just… slaughter.
"Uoooooooh!"
A thunderous roar made Austin snap his head up as Alphasa, the
vice-captain of the Red Blade Bandits, charged with his massive axe.
This one's different.
He didn't rely on gimmicks. His was a pure, traditional Skima fighting
style.
Rian raised his sword to meet the blow, and Alphasa bellowed again,
ramming forward like a rhinoceros.
"Everyone, push!"
Ten more bandits pressed behind Alphasa's back, piling their weight.
Rian's arms bent, and his feet scraped across the ground.
"Ghhh!"
When the charge ended, Rian had been forced back a full 20
meters.
What kind of monster is this guy?!
Even while bearing the weight of eleven people, Rian didn't lean against
anything—just stood his ground, and Alphasa was awestruck.
That's not human strength.
Though Alphasa had never been overpowered in raw strength, this time, he
couldn't even feel resistance to admitting it.
As Austin prepared a spell, Alphasa's gaze snapped toward him.
"Urgh!"
Eye Technique: Pressing.
As expected of the vice-captain of a top-tier bandit crew, his power was
immense—and so the strength contest resumed.
"Push harder! Keep going!"
At someone's shout, the bandits activated Skima Folding—compressing
their base Skima to double their physical strength.
"Ghhhhh!"
Rian was clearly in pain—but Austin was the one truly terrified.
Damn it! How's he holding on?!
Then he finally understood—it might not be physical strength at
all.
He's pushing back.
As Rian's transcendent will overtook his body, his arms began to
straighten.
Watching him push back eleven folded Skima bodies with one arm made
Austin's spine go cold.
He's going to cut them down!
Crack! A tooth from Alphasa's axe shattered.
And finally, Rian's blade began descending—slowly splitting the axe head
in two.
In his mind, Alphasa screamed.
No wayyyyy!
Is it even physically possible to cleave through another weapon when
they're locked together?
Why is it just my axe breaking?
If the strength were equal, Rian's sword should've chipped too.
But like slicing paper slowly, the massive blade parted the axe and
crept closer. Alphasa's mind blurred.
Facing death, every sense exploded—he began to see a vision, not with
his eyes, but with pure instinct.
He's not cutting the axe…
Rian disappeared. What Alphasa saw now was an infinite world, splitting
apart on both sides and rushing toward him.
The world… is being cut in two.
Divine Transcendence – World Severing.
"…S-stop…"
As Rian's blade passed more than halfway through, the division of that
world crashed down on Alphasa, and with a hoarse voice, he screamed—
"Don't push! You idiots!"
Maha.
And just as Alphasa saw the will of a god cleave the world, his body was
split in two.
Thunk! Rian's greatsword dropped to the ground, and two more bandits
were bisected on the spot.
Then, gripping the sword in both hands, Rian slashed diagonally—and the
remaining eight bandits were diced into pieces in unison.
"Fuuu…"
As Rian exhaled, Austin stared at him, pale-faced.
"W-Who are you…?"
Even if Rian had said "a ghost," Austin would've nodded in agreement.
But Rian only rested his sword over his shoulder and watched as Shirone's battle with the Red Blade
Bandits came to an end.
It's over.
The strategist Banker was lying flat with his face gone, and only the
captain, Coldpark, remained, swinging his sword in vain.
"What kind of mage…?!"
Could someone truly dodge Skima strikes with such ease?
With his nervous system enhanced by Armand and his metabolism boosted by
Kuzen, Coldpark's blade couldn't even graze Shirone's robes.
The remnants charged in and surrounded them—but Shirone had already
moved from his spot.
As he spun and stepped back, a Photon Cannon condensed in his right
hand, its power amplified by more mass. A glowing sphere the size of a human torso formed in his
palm.
When he swung his arm toward the direction of the scattered bandits, the
thick beam of light curved through the air and crashed into the ground.
BOOOOM!
A tremor rippled through the earth. In the place where debris flew, no
one remained standing.
"Now! Attack!"
From atop the cliff, the Blood Pillar Bandits, who had been watching the
battle, took aim at Shirone with seven mages and forty-eight archers—the core of their strength.
Their arrows, each twice as heavy as average, struck the ground first.
As Shirone ascended into the air, the mages raised their hands.
"Wh-what is that?!"
The blazing magic of fire and ice weakened before even being cast,
suppressed by the massive radiant angel emerging from Shirone's body.
Incarnation Technique – Angel's Judgement.
"Shoot! I said shoot, you idiots!"
The Blood Pillar's commander shouted, but the angel's massive arm,
wielding a divine spear, moved faster.
"Ah…"
Austin saw it—clearly.
A single flash, the sharpest light, scattering like dust.
BOOOOOOM!
The explosion knocked the senses from his body. For a while, he couldn't
even think.
And when he came to, he realized—the entire cliff that had stood before
him was gone.
Some may have escaped, but of the bandit forces numbering over 300, more
than 90% had been reduced to corpses.
Am I dreaming?
His thirty years of survival instinct—the years he lived only to
endure—couldn't accept what had just happened as real.
The Red Blade Bandits were one of the kingdom's three great bandit
crews. The Ear of the Deep, Beast Lynx, Spear and Death, and Blood Pillar Bandits were all A-grade
criminal syndicates by capital standards.
Was it truly possible for just two people to annihilate five such
massive factions?
—Mr. Austin, have you ever seen an Arch Mage before?
Estera's voice surfaced in his mind. It wasn't a coincidence.
No, you were wrong.
The Arch Mage he had just witnessed far exceeded even her
imagination.
Estera… we made a mistake. There's no way to "hold onto" that
boy.
Not unless they offered the entire guild to him.
While Austin was lost in thought, Rian walked over to Shirone and placed
a hand on his shoulder.
"Shirone, are you okay?"
"Yeah. I'm fine."
Shirone glanced around at the corpses strewn across the
battlefield.
By now, he should've been used to it. But every time a battle ended, the
same question echoed in his mind—Why?
"Such devastating power… it's not like the one from the second
trial."
He looked toward the crater where Angel's Judgement had struck.
Summoning his Incarnation and hurling a lance of light—it had become
second nature to him.
"They were clearly targeting me."
One of the spearmen had mentioned the 6 billion gold bounty.
"It seems word about Armand has spread. If I had known earlier, we
might've avoided this battle."
He didn't think about the people those bandits had hurt—or would have
hurt—before or after.
As always, Shirone believed no human had the right to judge
another.
The right to judge and the necessity to act were entirely different
matters.
"I've got something to tell you."
As Shirone and Rian turned, Austin pulled a small note from inside his
coat.
"Looks like my mission ends here."
