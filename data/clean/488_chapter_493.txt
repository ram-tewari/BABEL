Chapter 493:
Under the noon sun shining on Aravot, Shirone and Ikael stared at each
other in silence for a long time.
The Valhalla Action was still spinning above Shirone's head, and Ikael's
eyes watching it were intensely cold.
"Destroy heaven?"
According to the magical theory Shirone explained, it was not an
impossible task.
Then, this time, she must kill him.
Ikael's delicate fingers tightened, curling like a predator's
claws.
Yet, she couldn't strike because Shirone's direct perception read her
intent as soon as she revealed her killing intent.
"No, that's not it."
Shirone said that if she revived Feope, he wouldn't destroy
heaven.
It wasn't a difficult task for Ikael, and the Shirone she knew wasn't
one to go back on his word.
Accepting the proposal was a wiser choice than betting on an uncertain
assumption.
"No, that's not it either."
Ikael frowned.
Shirone's Ataraxia was a power she had directly imparted to him through
possession.
While synchronizing with Shirone's mind, she felt admiration, pride, and
a deep, poignant emotion.
"I don't remember."
Most things were clear in her mind, but the problem was the emotion of
poignancy.
"What do I want to do with that boy?"
Her mind began to waver again.
Ikael couldn't define her feelings towards Shirone in clear
terms.
Love?
Simply calling it love felt too shallow.
Unable to delve deep into her emotions, Ikael finally made a
decision.
"He must die."
She couldn't trust Shirone.
No, she couldn't trust herself.
Trying to analyze someone with imperfect memories was never a good
idea.
"I just need to do what I can."
Having made her decision, Ikael spoke.
"Alright. I will revive Feope. But you must keep your
promise."
"As long as I can save Feope, I don't care."
Shirone looked at the small fairy in his hand with pity.
He still wasn't sure if this was the right decision.
But Feope had wasted an almost eternal lifespan to save him and had
shriveled into a pitiful state.
"I must save Feope."
For now, that was all that mattered.
"Place the fairy in front of me. Amplifying life force is a highly
sensitive task."
This was the main reason Ikael didn't choose to negotiate.
Once Ataraxia began, she would have to focus all her mind on
Feope.
If Shirone, with his direct perception, found an opening, he could
deliver a fatal blow.
It wasn't a strategy to employ in front of an untrustworthy
opponent.
"Don't worry. I won't do anything until Feope is
revived."
"Please…"
As Ikael responded in a dry tone, Shirone cautiously approached without
lowering his guard.
"Kill. Kill. Kill."
Watching Shirone close the distance, Ikael repeated the same thought
over and over.
If he got just a bit closer, even if he sensed her intent, he wouldn't
be able to react before his heart was ripped out.
"Now!"
Shirone walked calmly.
"Now!"
Even as he approached and slowly bent down to place Feope on the ground,
she couldn't move.
"Now! Now! Now!"
Ikael finally realized.
"Now…"
The thoughts swirling madly in her mind were just that—thoughts.
"You can't kill me."
Placing Feope down and stepping back slowly, Shirone looked at Ikael
with sad eyes.
It wasn't that he didn't guess her intent.
Maybe he did… That's why he didn't lower his guard, but he also thought
it didn't matter.
If Ikael killed him, the reason he risked his life to come to heaven
would disappear.
But in the end, Ikael couldn't kill Shirone, and Shirone was satisfied
with that.
Just to meet her, many people had already sacrificed themselves, and he
had killed many lives.
"Yes, this ends it."
Contrary to Shirone's thoughts, Ikael was even more confused.
"How do you know that? Why can't I kill you?"
The words he had held back rose to his throat, but Shirone swallowed
them.
If Ikael had already lost her memory, saying anything would only hurt
her.
If there was one, a single hope…
"I don't know."
"But you…"
"I really don't know. That's why I came to meet you. But you can't
tell me anything."
Shirone gritted his teeth, suppressing the rising sadness.
"Why don't you try to remember me? You can do it."
Ikael remained silent.
It wasn't that she hadn't considered what Shirone thought.
If Anke Ra had only cut off her memory instead of resetting it, maybe
Ikael could regain it.
But that would mean directly defying Anke Ra's will, and it wouldn't end
with just a backlash.
Seeing Ikael's silent figure, Shirone felt another small wound and
stepped back, looking down at Feope.
Saying nothing was the right choice.
"It's enough. Save Feope. Then I'll do whatever you
want."
Ikael's gaze turned to Feope.
If she couldn't kill him and wouldn't kill him, making a contract with
Shirone was the best choice.
"…Alright."
Kneeling slowly in front of Feope, Ikael placed her hands on her knees
and closed her eyes.
Chiiiiiiing!
As her halo expanded, Ataraxia, Ikael's symbol, spun brilliantly with
five-colored light.
Shirone watched in awe with half-closed eyes.
He could also use Ataraxia, but no one could match the beauty of the
original.
"Amplify the life force…"
Reviving Feope.
Reviving a fairy on the brink of death after exhausting its entire
lifespan was as difficult as giving new life, but it wasn't impossible for Ikael, who handled
amplification as a fundamental concept.
"It will still take time."
Ikael emptied her mind and reached out to Feope.
"Shirone will keep his promise."
As she made up her mind, a strange calmness came over her.
Her mind was still calculating all the variables, but trusting Shirone
filled her with a serene and happy feeling, making her smile slightly without realizing it.
But her expression soon turned cold, and she focused on activating her
power with emotionless eyes.
Rumble!
At that moment, the ground began to shake from the west of
Aravot.
Shirone, tense, quickly turned to look, his eyes shaking with
shock.
An indescribably dark and gloomy aura was approaching from afar.
Feeling the aura through his direct perception, Shirone shuddered with
fear.
Black dots, like pixels, swirled like a vortex, spreading fear and
casting a murky darkness around the one who spread it.
"Faiel?"
The Archangel of Annihilation, Faiel.
Even Ikael hadn't expected this, and her eyes wavered with
anxiety.
If Ikael, the Archangel of Amplification, was the first to be born among
the eight great concepts, Faiel, the Archangel of Annihilation, was the last.
Thus, it was extremely rare in the history of heaven for two archangels
to clash over a single event.
And the law of outcomes always favored annihilation.
"Faiel! Don't come any closer!"
Faiel's purpose was singular, and his intent in coming here was
clear.
Victory or defeat in the war didn't matter.
Faiel was coming to end everything.
"Archangel Ikael…"
Even from a distance, his voice echoed through the black vortex around
Aravot.
Shirone desperately steadied his mind to suppress the rising
fear.
Faiel's appearance was unimpressive.
At 1.7 meters tall, he could be called a dwarf among angels, and though
he wore a white robe, his face hidden by the hood was pure darkness.
"From now on, I deny you."
Whoooosh!
Faiel's face finally emerged from the hood.
Or could it be called a face?
Only the wickedly slit eyes and thorn-like protruding lips were etched
in light within the darkness.
Whoooosh!
As the surrounding darkness distorted and the face within the hood was
projected, his halo turned black and began to spin like spilled ink.
"Huuu!"
Just being exposed to the aura felt like his life force was draining
away, and Shirone's teeth chattered.
Armand's tendrils withered like dried leaves, and a chill like ice water
flowing through his bones pierced his entire body.
"Annihilate, Ikael."
As Faiel lunged, Ikael leaped away.
Amplification and annihilation.
There was no need to clash to know which was superior.
As Ikael watched from 100 meters away, Faiel glanced at her briefly
before turning to Shirone.
"Foolish Nephilim. Trying to destroy heaven with mere human
desires."
As Faiel raised his hand, Feope's body slowly floated up.
Reaching a terrible realization, Shirone spun the Valhalla Action's halo
faster and shouted.
"No! Let go of Feope!"
"All life is annihilated. It is only following the natural order.
The same goes for you."
"Don't touch Feope. I'll kill Ra, whom you cherish so
much!"
Faiel tilted his head slightly, as if looking at Shirone's halo.
He had already confirmed through his vision what result was equipped in
the Valhalla Action.
But he had no attachment to the destruction of heaven.
As the Archangel of Annihilation, he simply couldn't watch the Archangel
of Amplification be ruined any longer.
"The only result for me is annihilation."
"Nooooo!"
As Shirone screamed, Faiel clenched his fist.
Black smoke seeped into Feope's body, turning it into black ash that
scattered like the remnants of a fire.
"Fe-Feope…"
Shirone stared blankly at the ashes.
Feope had been annihilated.
Without any chance of resurrection or even the dignity of a proper
death, Feope had vanished from the world.
"Ugh! Ugh!"
Shirone clenched his fists, biting his teeth.
He didn't think about holding back his tears or appearing weak in front
of the enemy.
What began to sprout deep in his heart was intense anger.
And that anger quickly expanded, filling Shirone's mind.
"I won't forgive you."
Judicial Halo - Valhalla Action.
As Valhalla Action activated, Ataraxia unfolded in an instant, and
Shirone's body disappeared from his spot.
"Foolish Faiel! Are you trying to endanger Ra?"
Faiel, who had been watching Shirone's movement, turned his head at
Ikael's shout.
His face in the darkness still wore a smile, but his voice was cold and
solemn.
"The foolish one is you, Ikael. Do you still not understand what
awaits at the end of this war?"
"What do you mean…!"
Flash!
Before Ikael could finish speaking, a massive red light shot up into the
sky from afar.
Estimated power: 2.19 million kilobusters.
The divine punishment, the most powerful destructive spell in Shirone's
arsenal, had begun to activate.
