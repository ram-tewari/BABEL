Chapter 675:
The Secret of Radum (3)
Recalling Klump's face, Shirone thrust the greatsword into his back. Rian stared blankly
and asked,
"Rian? Are you really recovered?"
"As you can see, no problem."
The moment Rian raised his thumb, a fist struck his solar plexus.
Of course, the impact was blocked by his muscular barrier, but that only irritated
Shirone more.
"You idiot! What were you thinking, doing something so reckless? Do you even know what
that is? It's an ancient weapon!"
"Weren't you the one trying to do something about that ancient weapon?"
"Huh?"
Shirone blinked.
"I saw you preparing to counter. You never planned to dodge from the start, did you? If
you hadn't bought time, everyone caught in the explosion would have died."
Lupistra might call it foolish, but Shirone had his own unyielding principles.
"Yeah. The mission is important, but the mercenary band I built…"
Rian shook his head.
"It's fine. No matter what decision you make, I'll just follow as your sword. What I
really want to say is, there's nothing more shameful for a knight than failing to protect."
If Rian said it was what he truly wanted to convey, it meant he would carry it out no
matter the circumstances.
That was the conviction of a knight.
Perhaps Rian had grown far beyond what Shirone knew.
'Sorry, Rian.'
As if reading Shirone's thoughts, Rian smiled and pointed toward the core of Radum.
"Let's go. We have a journey to continue."
***
The ancient weapon, Bioflare.
A strategic defensive weapon that mimics biological mechanisms, collecting photons to
grow its own body.
With roots powerful enough to supply energy to all of Radum, it now served as a fortress
for the high-ranking members of Spectrum, the largest coalition of non-human races.
After Venice left Radum, only three of the four wills remained at the Round Table: the
heads of the Combat, Defense, and Internal Affairs Departments.
But they were acutely aware of the presence of Ra Enemi sitting in the empty seat.
Raika, the Internal Affairs Minister of Spectrum and a pureblood vampire, was the first
to speak.
A handsome man with neatly combed black hair, his pale face looked as if powdered, and
his wide lips were vividly red.
"The kingdom has made its decision. It was expected. Though I'm surprised the Magic
Association Head came personally."
"Lupistra is a strong human."
Florino, the Defense Minister of Spectrum and a member of the Flower Clan, spoke.
Her eyelashes were unusually long, her red lips glossy, and her body emitted a fresh
floral scent, untouched by chemical processing.
The ancestors of the Flower Clan were said to be lotus flower spirits who attained
enlightenment over 40,000 years.
Though they were a remarkable race skilled in floral arts, their population now numbered
fewer than 30 worldwide.
The reason for their near-extinction was their "extreme passivity," a unique racial
trait.
Without the instinct to choose mates, they were genetically predisposed to submit to the
desires of all races.
To them, accidental union was a beautiful probability, and they held a noble heart that
cherished every seed.
But humans wouldn't leave them alone, and their possessive, monopolistic desires
gradually withered them to this state.
"He's also a dangerous human. Worse than Gaold."
When Michael Gaold was the Association Head, the pressure on Radum wasn't as severe.
"That's why I said Lupistra was no good. Thinking you could negotiate with such a
conservative human was foolish."
Spectrum had also pressured the Kingdom of Tormia through its channels, but Gaold's
mistake had been too great.
"It doesn't matter. Now we have our chance. A chance to reclaim Vashka."
A man with half his face covered in reptilian scales spoke.
Draker, the Combat Minister of Spectrum and a Dragonkin.
The Dragonkin were a race created through dragon bioweapon experiments. Though their
creators, the dragons, deemed them failures, their unmatched combat prowess allowed them to conquer vast
territories and establish themselves as a race.
"Venice was foolish. If you can't avoid it, use it."
At Raika's words, Florino nodded.
"We will overthrow humanity."
Draker glanced at the empty seat and said,
"You want that too, don't you?"
In the minds of the three, the memory of Ra Enemi smiling and nodding was freshly etched.
***
"Joshua! Snap out of it, Joshua!"
Shaken awake, Joshua slowly opened her eyes and immediately sat up, drawing her bow.
Though it was a trained motion, she forgot to nock an arrow, leaving the bow empty. But
Cargin wasn't in the state to care.
"Woah, woah! Calm down! It's me, me!"
Staring at Cargin, who stood with his hands raised and a pale face, she finally regained
her senses and lowered the bow.
"What happened?"
"We must've been blown away by the explosion. I don't even know where we are."
Cargin looked terrible.
Assuming she was no better, Joshua tried to stand.
"I'll scout around. Ow!"
Wincing in pain, she fell back. Cargin checked her ankle—swollen and twisted.
"Damn, you're hurt. I was too out of it to notice."
As he cast a healing spell, a burning pain surged through her.
"It hurts!"
"Hang in there. It'll be over soon."
Healing magic was divided into high-speed activation, which boosted natural recovery, and
regeneration, which rapidly repaired cells. Cargin specialized in the latter.
"Be thankful I'm a regeneration expert. Believe it or not, I was called 'Hand of God' in
my youth…"
"Shut up and heal me. My head's already a mess."
Though Joshua openly mocked him, Cargin didn't mind—perhaps because he sensed her
underlying concern.
"Even at the fastest, it'll take ten minutes."
Regeneration still took time, especially for fractures.
"That's too long. We don't even know where we are…"
As she looked around, a guttural voice echoed from the path.
"Kekekeke! Found 'em, found 'em!"
Joshua aimed her bow from her seated position. This time, she didn't forget to nock an
arrow.
Goblins emerged from all sides, brandishing crude weapons with their agile movements.
"Damn it! There's too many."
Though her arrow was tipped with a flame-enchanted magic stone, taking on over sixty
enemies was impossible.
"Gurgle gurgle! Wait! The woman's ours!"
Hearing the voice behind them, Cargin looked up while maintaining his spell. Pig-faced
Pidu tribesmen wielding massive axes approached.
"Oh… damn it!"
Both races were terrifying to humans, given their carnivorous nature.
The Goblin Union Speedkillers were annoyed that the Pidu Union Gluttons were trying to
steal their prey.
The Goblins' cruel eyes gleamed as their leader, Marta, made a throat-slitting gesture.
"No. Both humans are ours. Back off quietly, and we'll give you a leg."
"Gurgle gurgle! You think one leg will fill our bellies? We're starving!"
Pidu leader Pugo protested vehemently, but Marta only found his desperation amusing.
"Kekekeke! That's your problem. We found 'em first. If you're unhappy, run faster next
time."
With only an arrow to rely on, the two watched the standoff between the groups,
swallowing hard.
'Maybe things will work out.'
If they could provoke the Goblins and Pidu, they might find a chance to escape.
But as if mocking their thoughts, Pugo made a horrifying suggestion.
"Gurgle gurgle! Then let's do it your way! No complaints then, right?"
"Hoh? The Goblin way? You think you can handle it?"
Marta rested her twin-bladed staff on her shoulder, eyes gleaming.
"Fine. Whoever catches 'em, eats 'em."
"Meat! Meat!"
With Marta's words as the signal, Goblins and Pidu charged from all sides.
At the same time, Joshua's arrow loosed, and the magic stone exploded.
"Yeehaw!"
The Goblins, true to their warlike nature, even enjoyed their prey's resistance.
"The guts are mine! The eyeballs too!"
"I'll take the… kehehehe!"
Cargin grabbed Joshua and cast Teleport, but with no place to hide except underground,
escape was impossible.
"Kikiki, humans before slaughter are even grosser than pigs."
"Don't call us pigs! Gurgle gurgle!"
Even while teleporting, Joshua's shots were precise, but the Goblins' innate agility
matched that of quadrupedal predators.
"Gwooooh!"
As Glutton members leaped from buildings, the teleportation flashes weaved through them.
But they couldn't evade the Goblins' attacks forever, and eventually, the repeated spells
broke, sending the two tumbling to the ground.
"Guhhh!"
Seeing Joshua's ankle, which he'd healed 30%, worsen, Cargin's face paled.
"Gotcha."
Turning around, the Speedkillers and Gluttons formed a semicircle, sealing their escape.
"I'll make you a promise. We won't kill you. Just eat you."
Watching their expressions change, Marta clutched her stomach and laughed.
"Puhahaha! Look at their faces! They're totally terrified."
Cargin, holding Joshua, trembled violently.
'How can I die?'
Though he'd often thought about dying, now that the time had come, he couldn't think of a
way.
"Escape."
Joshua nocked a smoke arrow and said,
"Time it right and teleport out."
"B-but…"
Summoning all his courage, Cargin asked,
"What about you?"
He feared she'd say, "What about me?"
"My life's been over since Leo died."
She couldn't say she'd loved him, but he'd been a cute kid.
When he laughed and called her "Mom," she could forget her miserable life, if only
briefly.
"Kids! Dinner time!"
As Goblins and Pidu charged competitively, Joshua fired the smoke arrow.
"Aaaah!"
Cargin teleported upward, dodging blades with the greatest focus of his life, escaping
the enemies' radius.
"Gurgle gurgle! Should we chase?"
"Forget it! Take the woman first! She's tastier!"
Like most last stands, Joshua didn't care about her broken leg, leaping and firing every
magic stone she had left.
Boom! Boom!
As the explosions faded, so did Cargin's running speed.
'What have I done?'
His legs gave out, and he collapsed.
Hadn't he lost Marian the same way and lived in despair ever since?
'I should go back. What's left of my life anyway?'
He turned his head toward where Joshua was, but his heart wouldn't move.
"Ughhh!"
He didn't want to die.
Even if it meant living as a coward, he wanted to live.
Cargin punched his own legs.
"Move! Move, damn it!"
Though it hurt as if his legs would break, he knew the truth.
Hurting himself like this was better than dying.
"Aaaah! Please move! Move!"
***
'Did they escape safely?'
Her quiver was empty, and her ankle was horribly twisted.
She'd taken down over thirty enemies alone and even eliminated Pugo, the Gluttons'
leader, with a clever tactic.
It was a record for her life, but what remained was rightful retribution.
"Don't think we'll kill you cleanly."
Marta's eyes burned with malice, and the Gluttons, having lost their leader, drooled at
the thought of devouring her.
'Should've saved one arrow.'
Even an arrowhead would be better than their teeth.
"Start with the legs."
As a Goblin raised a glaive to strike her leg—
"You monsters!"
Everyone turned to see Cargin, looking a decade older, charging with a club.
"Aaaah! Just kill me, you bastards!"
His disheveled white hair made it clear he'd lost his mind.
Marta tripped him, sending him crashing into Joshua.
"What the hell, old man! What're you gonna do now?!"
"Ugh! I can't… live like this."
Tears and snot streaming down his face, Cargin spoke.
"I can't live like this."
"……"
Faced with such foolish words from someone who'd come to die, Joshua realized she felt
the same.
"Kekeke! What's this? We let you go, and you crawl back? You wanna be eaten that badly?"
'What have I done?'
Cargin came to his senses again.
How many times did he have to wake up to pretend to be human and live in this world?
"Good. One woman wasn't enough."
"Kids, it's a feast!"
The pudgy Pidu charged first, bypassing the Speedkillers.
"Gwoooh! The arms are mine…!"
Then, in the next moment, a flash streaked in—and his face exploded.
"What the—?!"
Looking around, there was no one.
"Found them, Rian! Over here!"
At the voice above, they looked up to see Shirone calling Rian from a rooftop.
"Huh! Ugh!"
Tears finally welled in Joshua's eyes, and without thinking, she blurted out a honorific.
"Captain!"
