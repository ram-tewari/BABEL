Chapter 687:
Incident Occurrence (2)
It seemed like it had all been an illusion. The landscape where Dinah had disappeared was
stitched back together, and the only thing that had changed was Draker's body.
"How dare you... me...?"
His voice split left and right, coming from both sides, until finally, Draker, now split
into two, collapsed to the ground.
"Huuuuh."
Rian, leaning on the hilt of the Great Straight Blade to catch his breath, slowly
straightened his back and stood up, rolling his neck.
"Am I now the strongest in Radum?"
There was no need to assign any special meaning to it.
The race known as the Dragon Demons was nothing more than a failed creation of the
dragons' experiments.
'This isn't enough to become a Dragon Slayer.'
Just by looking at Draker's case, one could indirectly sense how powerful the dragon race
was.
'But now, I don't doubt it.'
The Schema he had obtained after 20 years gave him the confidence to overcome any
obstacle that might come his way.
"By the way, where's Shirone?"
With a serious expression, Rian looked up at the dust cloud rising from the center of
Radum.
The fact that dust was rising through the rain was enough to guess the magnitude of the
impact that had occurred there.
'Who would do something so absurd?'
He had seen Saeghwa collapsing with his own eyes, but he had been too focused on the
battle to analyze it properly.
"Well, they must have had a big fight."
Thinking was tiresome.
As Rian slung the Great Straight Blade over his shoulder and prepared to head to the next
battlefield, a voice called out from above.
"Did you finish him off?"
Kuan, holding Venezia by the waist, was looking down at the ground from the rooftop of a
half-destroyed building.
After carefully examining Draker, Rian reported, "I'm not sure if he's finished off,
but I did split him in two."
"...Looks like lightning struck from the sky."
Having worked as an investigator in Radum, Kuan knew the extent of the Three Pillars'
strength.
While Plarino had never left Saeghwa, Draker, whom he had observed through espionage
missions, was by no means an easy opponent.
"Pfft."
Rian let out a hollow laugh and shrugged. "Why such a disappointing remark? Do you
think a Dragon Demon could be taken down so easily? It was a strike stronger than lightning."
'What's wrong with him? Did he lose his mind during the fight?'
Though he was foolish, he wasn't usually this lighthearted. Kuan tilted his head at
Rian's unusually buoyant demeanor.
"I've unlocked my Schema."
Finally understanding, Kuan stared at Rian without even blinking.
"......"
The fact that it wasn't some imagined Schema, as before, was evident from Draker's
corpse.
"Is that so?"
Carrying Venezia, Kuan jumped down from the building and walked toward Rian.
From the Kaizen Swordsmanship School to his battle with Lai, and then his departure for
knight training—countless memories flashed through his mind. But Kuan simply surveyed the scene at Saeghwa
and said briefly, "Let's go."
Rian, savoring his words, nodded with a satisfied smile. Those words resonated more than
any others.
"Yes."
As he answered, the two swordsmen kicked off the ground and soared into the air.

"Waaaah! Save me, Goblin!"
Shirone snatched Kido, who was flailing as he fell, like a swallow catching its prey.
"I told you I'd catch you. Why are you making such a fuss?"
"Dammit! I hate the sky!"
For Kido, who had grasped the principles of the earth, the sky—with nothing to hold
onto—was an uncomfortable place.
"By the way, that human is really going all out. I didn't expect him to bring it
down."
The seismic waves from Saeghwa's collapse had destroyed more than half of Radum.
"Good riddance. I never liked ancient weapons anyway."
Map-based weapons, which judged based on maps, never bothered to look at what was
actually there. They only calculated damage and gains based on strategy and tactics.
That was why Shirone restrained himself from using large-scale destructive magic, despite
its immense power.
"That's just your opinion. Do you know how expensive that thing was?"
After setting Kido down on the ground, Shirone looked around.
The impact of Saeghwa's fall had caused the ground to bounce like a rubber sheet, kicking
up dust that obscured visibility entirely. The only saving grace was that the rain would soon clear the air.
'Both Spectrum and Saeghwa have been annihilated. What is Ra Enemi thinking?'
Shirone turned to Mei Rey.
"How are you? Is the voice still...?"
Shirone felt his heart stop as he met her gaze.
"Who... are you?"
Her appearance hadn't changed, but the person before him was no longer Mei Rey.
If the face was a mirror of emotions, then her expression—lips pursed sharply as she
stared at Shirone—held an emotion he had never seen in any human before.
"......"
When no words came from Mei Rey, Shirone opened his Bakji.
Behind every substance lies the concept that defines it.
A sword is a collection of atoms, but for it to be recognized as a sword, the concept of
a sword must precede it.
Thus, if a sword is preceded by an indestructible concept, it remains unbreakable in
reality. This was the true nature of Rian's Great Straight Blade, <Idea>.
Soul, Idea, Concept.
No matter how it was defined, there were two ways for a human, a being of the five
senses, to step into this realm.
One was to transcend the physical body (the vulgar brain).
The other was to perceive the hidden world from the realm of reality (Bakji).
Using the latter ability to examine Mei Rey, Shirone felt a chill run down his spine.
'What... is this?'
Mei Rey's conceptual form was unlike that of a human.
Her body was as thin as a thorn, standing over three meters tall, with a crown that
spread out like a fan. Her arms were slender and long, her back hunched, and her pupils were covered with a
glossy black retina, devoid of irises.
"Who the hell are you...?"
As Shirone spoke, Kido grabbed his wrist.
"Enough. Leave her be for now."
"Kido, what's going on?"
Though Kido couldn't perceive Bakji, he had some idea based on the taste of memories.
"I realized when I drank Mei Rey's blood. Her memories aren't human."
Mei Rey's memories were something a goblin's imagination couldn't analyze.
"At any rate, it's clear she intends to fight Ra Enemi. That makes her an
ally."
"Why didn't you tell me?"
"That's..."
Mei Rey spoke to Shirone.
"Long time no see, Hexa."
Though it wasn't human language, the Ultima System translated it.
"You know me?"
No, more precisely, Hexa.
Though Shirone had recalled Hexa's memories through Igor's ability, it still didn't feel
like his own.
Mei Rey looked around and said, "There's too much noise. That must mean the time has
come. Wait a little longer. You'll understand everything soon."
"Wait? For what?"
Mei Rey raised her head and pointed a finger at the sky.
"The Event."

"Grrr! Damn it!"
The collapse of Saeghwa had caused countless casualties, regardless of race, but Raika, a
pureblood vampire, had miraculously survived.
"You think I'd vanish from something like this?"
Crawling out from the wreckage of Saeghwa, Raika trembled as she stood up.
The tower was so massive that even in its fallen state, it resembled a standing cliff.
'I need blood right now... but recovery comes first.'
Vampires were a unique race, existing between half-body and half-soul.
Straddling the boundary between matter and concept, they could choose to destroy or
penetrate walls, but neither functioned perfectly.
For example, wood was easier to destroy than stone, making it easier for vampires to
penetrate.
Rufist's Iron Wall was so durable that even purebloods would struggle to break through,
and after being struck by the Golden Tsunami, Raika's body and soul were both in tatters.
"Still alive? I'll give you credit for that."
Rufist stepped forward, his feet sinking into the mud formed by the rain and dust.
'Damn it.'
His tenacity in tracking Raika down amid a disaster of this scale was commendable.
"Glad you're alive. I wasn't planning to give you a clean death."
Raika bared her fangs. "Hahaha. How pathetic. Do you think bringing down Saeghwa
will bring that dead woman back? You didn't even retrieve the seed. Your mission is a complete
failure."
She knew full well that the Magic Association had sent its leader to the royal capital
for Saeghwa.
"None of your concern, since you'll be dead soon."
A gurgling sound rose from Raika's throat.
"A lowly human... dares to kill a vampire?"
Rufist let out a long sigh. "Human, human. Is that all you mongrels can say?"
"Because it's the truth. It's absurd that a weak race rules the world."
"Yeah, weak."
Rufist readily agreed. "Generally speaking."
Raika's eyes sharpened.
"Let me tell you an interesting story. Two men fell into hell with their
families."
Rufist held up two fingers. "They decided to escape hell. But one succeeded, while
the other couldn't."
He bent one finger, leaving only one standing.
Though Raika recognized the human insult, she suppressed her anger.
"The failed man couldn't abandon his family. He lifted his wife when she fell,
carried his child when they stumbled. In the end, the gates of hell closed on him."
Rufist smirked. "The successful man, on the other hand, cared for no one. Whether
his wife fell into a pit of fire, his parents screamed in agony, or his child wept tears of blood, he just
ran and ran until he escaped before the gates closed."
He spread his arms. "Strange, isn't it?"
"...What's your point?"
"It's a metaphor. A metaphor for the system of human society. The more
responsibilities you have, the less competitive you become. Yet humans choose responsibility over
competition. Those 'generally weak' humans you mentioned."
A murderous glint flashed in Rufist's eyes.
"Most people react the same way to this story. They pity the failed man and condemn
the successful one. So hardly anyone asks this question: Why did the successful man have to run, leaving his
most beloved people behind in hell?"
Rufist raised a finger. "Because it's efficient. The moment you take responsibility
for someone, everyone dies. So what about the mindset of the man who abandoned his family? Could he really
be called sane?"
He shook his head. "Absolutely not. Obsession with success, madness. I call this
psychological murder. Whether by innate lack of guilt or suppression through reason, victory depends on how
many loved ones you can kill in your heart. And the upper echelons of the current human system are teeming
with such lunatics. So, do you still think humans are a weak race?"
Rufist's smiling face turned cold. "The opponent standing before you now is beyond
the scope of 'general' humans. Everyone in Radum carrying out their missions is a ruthless psychological
murderer. To become a mage, to spread goodwill, to reach the pinnacle of swordsmanship—they had to kill
their most beloved people. They killed, and killed, and killed, climbing higher and higher."
Friends, family, even themselves—the only thing gained from such slaughter was... the
true self.
"Don't mistake us for the kind-hearted humans you've been feeding on. There isn't a
single sane person here. Only madmen who will do anything to achieve their goals, swarming at the top of the
world. This isn't a system where inferior races like vampires can meddle."
"Kieeeeeee!"
Raika let out a shriek and charged.
Though her soul hadn't even half-recovered, her pride as a race couldn't endure such
humiliation.
'Jane...'
Though he had poured out his inner thoughts, Rufist felt no relief.
Perhaps the reason a genius mental mage like Jane had met her end in a den of non-humans
was because she had chosen to take responsibility.
'You should have killed. You should have lived.'
Maybe Jane had wanted to escape the guilt of psychological murder and seek a better life.
**Steel Magic—Golden Steel Water.**
Steel materialized behind Rufist's shoulders, transforming into the massive arms of a
beast that pinned Raika down.
"Grrr!"
Like a mouse caught in a lion's grip, Raika flailed helplessly.
Even her half-soul abilities couldn't free her, proving the Golden Steel Water's
durability surpassed that of a vampire's powers.
"Shall we have some fun now?"
Raika's face twisted grotesquely. "Inferior...!"
The Golden Steel Water's claws penetrated her body.
"Gyaaaaaa!"
The vampire's screams lasted a full ten minutes.
"Guhhh..."
No predator would leave its prey in such a mangled state.
As Raika died, her body withered away, turning to dust as her black soul dispersed into
the air.
'Is this the end?'
Raika had perished, and with Saeghwa destroyed, Plarino would be powerless.
Of the Three Pillars, only Draker remained, but since he hadn't shown himself, it seemed
Shirone's mercenaries had handled him.
"Found you at last."
The sound of splashing water drew Rufist's attention. Shagal, his head dripping like a
mop, approached with a hunched posture.
The first thing Rufist checked was the bag Shagal was dragging.
'He retrieved the dagger.'
His only weakness was gone.
"I'll kill you."
Shagal quickened his pace, but Rufist didn't flinch.
As Shagal closed in, Rufist slowly turned his body, and Shagal dashed past him.
"Ra Enemi!"
His rapid thrusts stabbed at empty air with terrifying speed.
"Wherever you are!"
Shagal sniffed the air and leaped toward the opposite side of where Saeghwa had fallen.
"I'll find you and kill you!"
Rufist took Nemesis from his cube.
'If his sense of smell has caught it, that means everyone will gather soon.'
Ra Enemi had begun to move.
