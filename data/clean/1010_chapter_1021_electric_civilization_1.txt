Chapter 1021:
Electric Civilization (1)
A fleet of seventy warships was swiftly advancing through a windless
zone on the sea.
"Commander, it was a dud."
The ship, unparalleled in size, was equipped with all kinds of
weapons.
"No need to report every little thing. This is just a greeting. Fire a
shot to get them riled up."
The Jakra Pirates.
A famous pirate group in the South Pacific, led by Jakra, the younger
brother of the King of Dionas.
"But it was a dud."
With fiery red hair and wickedly slit eyes, he gazed beyond the
sea.
"This is awkward. Open all the gunports."
Iso Jakra.
One of the world's top 100 dangerous individuals, a master of skima with
no equal in naval battles.
"Open the gunports!"
The ship slowly rotated to the left, and 120 gunports opened on the
side.
Cannons extended ominously, pointing forward like thorns.
"Power level 3. Fire."
The crew member operating the device turned his head.
"Uh, Commander, if we fire 120 electric guns simultaneously, our ship
might blow up too."
"It's fine, fire."
It wasn't fine at all, but Jakra didn't care about the aftermath.
The deputy commander spoke.
"How about luring them first? The king ordered us to avoid direct
confrontation if possible."
Even for Dionas, making a deal with Arachne wasn't a losing
proposition.
The problem was Langi.
While treasures could be stolen again, humans were irreplaceable.
The Jakra Pirates' task was to provoke a battle and abduct her.
"If we take Langi, we won't be at a disadvantage in negotiations. The
opponent is Yahweh. Isn't it unnecessary to provoke him?"
"Yahweh, huh."
Jakra had heard of him.
"Well… saving the world and all that? Why doesn't a guy like that come
to me? Honestly, aren't I the most pitiful human?"
The deputy commander got to the point.
"He's strong. His record is impressive."
"Yeah, he must be incredibly strong. Meanwhile, I'm just a cowardly,
ruthless, and dumb pirate."
The deputy commander didn't deny it.
"Hahaha! Fine! Let's show them a pirate's battle. Kill the weak ones
first! Torment and threaten them!"
Jakra stood up and pointed forward.
"King or whatever, on the sea, I'm the god! If I say it, it happens!
Fire!"
The deputy commander sighed and nodded to the crew member, who entered
the coordinates.
"Opening 120 electric guns. Power level 3. Estimated projectile speed…
Mach 4.2."
Those who hadn't yet left the deck watched Poine with blank
expressions.
The cannonball that fell with a thud was half-crushed by finger
marks.
"What? She caught a cannonball with her hand…"
Only a few on the ship knew that Poine was a dragon.
"Messiah."
Poine approached Shirone, who was still watching the enemy fleet.
"The projectile speed exceeded the speed of sound. If I hadn't been
watching, there would have been damage."
That was why Shirone stayed in position.
"How's your hand?"
Even with a dragon's durability, there were limits to biological
cells.
Poine looked at her right hand.
"It's fine."
Her fingertips trembled involuntarily, and the skin on the back of her
hand was ruptured.
"But I can't guarantee the next one. We should strike first."
If Shirone allowed it, she wanted to fly up and boil the sea with
poison.
While she was lost in thought, people evacuated, and Arachne's ship
approached.
Knights equipped with flying artifacts soared vertically and landed on
the deck.
"What's going on?"
Pushing past the knights, Foreign Minister Cairns approached.
"Pirates. Probably under orders from the King of Dionas, but there's no
way to confirm."
Cairns didn't feel betrayed.
"Damn! That's just like them. By the way, where's Benahar?"
He was probably hiding somewhere on the evacuation ship, but searching
three hundred ships was impossible.
Shirone spoke.
"This is just the beginning. If a battle breaks out, the damage will be
uncontrollable. Retreat."
"We'll fight too. We've prepared the elite knights for this."
Having already lost to Shirone in negotiations, Cairns couldn't afford
to lose this battle.
'If we lose the initiative here, even Maika's artifacts will be taken by
Shirone.'
Shirone looked at Arachne's ship and met Langi's eyes on the
deck.
"…Do as you wish. But if you decide to fight, I'll classify you as a
combatant."
It meant he could be a strategic sacrifice if necessary, and Cairns
agreed.
"Of course."
As the knights moved to the front of the deck, Poine wore a serious
expression.
"They don't seem interested in talking."
In the giant light lens Shirone created, a warship with over 100 open
gunports appeared.
"Th-that's…"
Hot air shimmered above the warship, pushing the sea away at tremendous
speed.
'Faster.'
The knights saw 120 cannonballs approaching faster than sound…
'Evade.'
It was impossible to react.
Not being able to dodge something at close range was a familiar pattern
to the brain.
'Huh? Huh?'
When something approached at incredible speed from a distance, the
nerves screamed.
Boom!
When the loud noise arrived, the knights felt as if time had
reversed.
'Hand of God.'
It seemed like the cannonballs had already arrived, and then Shirone
moved.
"Grrr!"
Shirone scattered the 120 cannonballs he had swept up with the Hand of
God into the air.
"Poine!"
As if waiting, Poine's lips twisted wickedly, and her skin split.
"Roar!"
Flying into the sky, she transformed, and the knights stared
blankly.
"A dragon…"
She had a much thinner and longer body than the dragons typically
imagined.
"Move back!"
Sensing the seriousness, the knights retreated with Cairns to the
captain's cabin.
The poison seeping from between her scales made it hard to
breathe.
"Insignificant humans…"
That was why Shirone restricted the apostles' transformations; once
transformed, they lost all humanity.
'She's really angry. I can't stop her.'
Given her personality, she would act even if it meant being killed by
the Messiah.
"Follow me!"
Spreading his wings, Shirone flew towards the pirates, with Poine
following.
"Grrr!"
A boiling sound came from the poison dragon's throat, and thick green
smoke billowed out.
"Roar!"
Three hundred meters from the pirate fleet, Poine unleashed her breath,
the Mist of Despair.
The smoke, like a meteor's tail, quickly filled the sea.
The pirates' screams were heard.
"Gas! Gas!"
The recharge time was short for one of the Twelve Apostles, only two
hours, but the duration exceeded two hours.
"Ahhh! My flesh is melting!"
Hearing the screams from the giant green cloud, Shirone checked his
fleet.
'This distance is safe.'
If it weren't a windless zone, the toxic smoke would have killed his
allies too.
"Haha, the smell of rotting flesh is delightful."
Shirone glanced at Poine, who was craning her neck to look below, and
sighed.
"What are you doing, using your breath? We can't identify the
enemies."
"The Messiah allowed the transformation. Without a one-hit kill, our
allies would have suffered."
They were indeed handling dangerous weapons.
"Huh?"
Suddenly realizing the change in the air, Shirone quickly turned his
head.
The green cloud, which had been floating, began to spin faster.
"What?"
Huge electric bolts shot out from the cloud, striking like
lightning.
'Magnetic field?'
The green smoke dispersed, revealing the warship surrounded by a
magnetic field.
"Hahaha! Is this all? A dragon is nothing special, right?"
Jakra's words made Poine's eyes bloodshot.
"I'll chew you up myself."
"Wait."
Something was off about the enemy formation.
"There are fewer ships. Over thirty are missing."
Realizing the situation, Shirone looked back and saw the missing pirate
ships chasing the evacuation ships.
'I didn't see them?'
It was as if the ships had magically moved through space.
"Save the world, Yahweh!"
Jakra, pointing his finger at the sky, laughed.
"Huh? Or maybe not? They'll all burn to death in the 'Birdron' soon.
Hahaha!"
Poine's body emitted poison, but compared to Shirone's killing intent,
it was nothing.
"…Wait here."
As his wings flapped, Shirone's body shot across the sea like a
flash.
"Grrr!"
Flying at incredible speed, Shirone confirmed the pirate ships'
position.
'Even flying like this takes over two seconds. It's physically
impossible.'
Thousands of shadows rose from the thirty pirate ships like birds taking
flight.
'Is that Birdron?'
They were small spheres with vibrating metal wings, scattering in all
directions as soon as they locked onto a target.
"Damn…"
As Shirone moved, thousands of Birdrons drew a massive trajectory and
struck his fleet.
Boom!
The massive chain explosion was visible even from a distance.
"Hahaha! What's this? Yahweh is nothing special? Who will you protect
now?"
Ignoring the dragon in the sky, Jakra's attitude made his crew
tremble.
'He's our leader, but he's really crazy.'
Despite ignoring the power balance, he always led them to victory, which
was amazing.
"Hey! If you're angry, come down! I've always wanted to catch a
dragon!"
When Jakra drew his sword and taunted, Poine turned her head from
watching the smoke.
"It is not I who will punish you."
At that moment, a crew member scanning the horizon with a telescope
shouted in surprise.
"Commander! Over there…!"
Frowning, Jakra pressed his temples, and his eyes lit up.
Where the Birdron's smoke had cleared, there was not an evacuation ship
but the Hand of God.
"What? Where did they all go?"
As if to show him, the Hand of God opened its palm, revealing
nothing.
Jakra's expression turned cold as the Hand of God quickly waved.
Then, in the next moment, it struck the sea, revealing nearly three
hundred evacuation ships.
Neither the pirates nor the refugees, nor even Poine, could understand
what had happened.
"Hand of God," Shirone said.
"Sleight of Hand."
