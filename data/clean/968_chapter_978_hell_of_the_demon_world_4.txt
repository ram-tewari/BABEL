Chapter 978:
Hell of The Demon World (4)
The Jincheon Empire was one of the countries that suffered the most from
the demons.
However, as it had boasted the world's largest population before the
war, it still had billions of survivors.
"Satan."
Basago, with multiple horns hanging down his back like hair, spread his
four arms wide towards the sky.
"Cover the whole world in evil."
As the Demon Realm opened, his muscular body began to crack and turn
gray like stone.
Despite the numerous cracks, his body held together as if glued, but
then it crumbled in the breeze, and the 3rd Legion Commander of the Hell Army was annihilated.
In the quiet mountains, where there were no humans or even demons,
unbelievable changes began to occur.
The grass turned black and grew over tens of meters high, breaking the
surrounding trees.
Winged insects fled, but they too rapidly changed form for unknown
reasons and fell to the ground.
Animals began to scream, and the entire mountain landscape transformed
into an impossible harmony.
The chaotic energy that made up Basago spread like radiation, causing
biological mutations.
"We're late."
The earth dragon Gaitan looked down at the scene of the entire mountain
moving like a giant monster.
Shirone spoke with a regretful expression.
"Basago's ability is mental radiation. It spreads the mind like
waves, changing the cells of living beings. Now that the Demon Realm has opened, this is just the
beginning. Countless mutants will enter human cities."
Even as he spoke, the mental radiation surged, and sparks flew from the
Miracle Stream's defensive barrier.
The white dragon Asraiker, paired with Gaitan, looked at the city beyond
the mountains and said,
"First, we need to control human access. We should check the extent
of the radiation."
"Yes."
Without knowing what monsters awaited them, Shirone and the apostles
descended the mountain.
"Argh!"
When the 4th Legion Commander Gamagin opened the Demon Realm, the entire
continental plate of the Ten Kingdoms of the Archipelago began to shake.
"Damn it!"
By the time Shirone grabbed him with the Hand of God, the life force had
already drained away.
"Messiah! Get back!"
As if coordinated, the legion commanders around the world were
simultaneously opening the Demon Realm.
'They have no intention of fighting.'
With their focus on opening the Demon Realm, the only way to stop them
was persuasion, but from the experience in Gustav, Shirone knew it was nearly impossible.
The power of destruction caused a clear earthquake, bouncing all
land-dwelling creatures into the air.
Landslides occurred in the mountains, and a tsunami could be seen
rushing in from the reddish sunset.
'Damn it!'
The massive volume of water was enough to cover and then recede from the
Ten Kingdoms of the Archipelago.
As the Hand of God expanded to its maximum, it pressed down one side of
the wave, but the tsunami was coming from all directions.
At the same time, the 5th Legion Commander Marbas in the Middle East
opened the blazing Demon Realm.
"I will burn everything!"
Although it was just morning, the desert temperature soared, drying up
the oases.
People were already suffering from the heat, and cries of despair could
be heard from the dried-up oases.
Back at the royal castle in Bashka, Shirone was waiting for the dawn to
break. Friends, drunk and asleep, snored or talked in their sleep at the table, while only Rian sipped
his drink in a steady rhythm.
"The situation doesn't look good."
Shirone didn't want to burden his friends with more information than
necessary, but he trusted Rian.
"While we were drinking, terrible tragedies occurred worldwide.
About 1.8 million people died. The Ten Kingdoms of the Archipelago suffered the most. There will likely
be an emergency in the morning."
"...I see."
Rian followed the principle of not thinking about the sword.
"Shirone, you should rest too. I'll take all our friends to their
lodgings."
"No, let's do it together."
Rian carried the men on his back, while Shirone used the Miracle Stream
to lift the women into the air.
The fact that no one showed signs of waking up indicated their
exhaustion but also their trust in the safety of the place.
'I love that trust.'
Praying that they could rest a little more comfortably, Shirone and Rian
left the dining hall.
The situation the next morning was as Shirone had expected, and
honestly, it was worse.
Even though the central continent was unaffected, the officials were in
a panic, muttering the same thing.
"The Demon Realm has fully opened."
An emergency meeting was convened, and everyone except the
still-unconscious Great Star and Zulu gathered at the royal castle.
The succession ceremony was scheduled for the afternoon, but given the
urgency, Pony took the throne.
The first thing she did with her temporary royal authority was to look
at Shirone.
"Did you know?"
"Yes. Rather than knowing, I was there. I'm sorry. I tried, but it
was impossible to stop the Demon Realm."
"According to the reported time, it was when we were together
yesterday! Why didn't you say anything?"
Pony fell silent.
Knowing wouldn't change anything, and Shirone was the only one who could
take action.
"And there's a mistake in the report. The Demon Realm hasn't fully
opened."
Shirone said,
"I instructed the 12 apostles to prioritize locating the legion
commanders. That way, we could respond to simultaneous events. Some were on time, some weren't, but
there's one commander we haven't found."
"1st Legion Commander Baal."
At Miro's words, Shirone nodded.
"Yes. And Baal's Demon Realm is the Deadly Cross, a meteor
collision. Unlike other Demon Realms, once the meteor collides, the planet itself is
destroyed."
The Great Star can no longer protect the planet.
Sein asked,
"How can you be sure Baal hasn't opened the Demon Realm? Meteor
collisions take time. He could have opened it and disappeared already."
"We have an expert in this field. Someone who can observe the space
domain with their Spirit Zone."
Amy looked incredulous.
"Sending a Spirit Zone outside the universe? Who is
it?"
Shirone raised his index finger.
"Arnold Lampa, a 3-star resident of the Ivory Tower."
Lampa was on an uninhabited island in the middle of the sea, 200
kilometers east of Galliant.
"The Demon Realm is not a phenomenon. It's a pre-existing
system."
There, Shirone discussed the current Demon Realm, specifically Baal,
with Lampa.
"It's like twisting the law. In that sense, it's similar to the
hidden code of the Underworld that the Great Star mentioned. But it's a much larger and more complex
code. It's like a world with its own laws within the overall law."
"Yes. Jincheon, the Ten Kingdoms of the Archipelago, the Middle
East, and Gustav are no longer the environments we knew. We've requested cooperation from the World
Climate Organization, but we don't know how effective it will be."
"What I want to say about that is this. I can also use a detached
Spirit Zone to draw in meteors from near space. It's a spell called Meteor."
Lampa's assistant, who was listening from a distance, corrected
him.
"It would have been possible 30 years ago."
Lampa clicked his tongue at his student's lack of awareness, but Shirone
smiled at him.
"If Meteor directly draws in meteors, Baal's Demon Realm likely
changes the course of meteors by altering the law. But even so, it can only use meteors within the solar
system."
"How many are there?"
"There are 7,543 meteors within 30 days' distance of impacting the
planet. Of those, 1,411 have the mass to cause fatal impacts. Additionally, I can't move these 1,411
meteors. They're beyond Meteor level."
"They'll probably all be affected. When the Demon Realm opens, the
course of the meteors will subtly change."
"Yes. We've already checked, and so far, there's no significant
movement. The Demon Realm hasn't opened yet."
"Alright. Keep monitoring. If you notice anything unusual, let me
know."
Lampa and his assistant bowed.
Shirone, in Bashka, relayed the conversation to everyone.
It was fortunate that the Demon Realm hadn't opened yet, but everyone
looked exhausted.
"1,411?"
Even a single Meteor spell could destroy an entire city.
"It's okay. I'll look into it further. We'll coordinate with Pony
and the World Climate Organization."
Hearing this, Shirone's friends understood why he only shared the
necessary information.
'It's not my field. It's an unsolvable problem. If we listen to all
this, we won't be able to do anything.'
As if reading Nade's thoughts, Shirone nodded.
"Let's have a meeting about what we can do. First, the succession
to the throne. Once Pony takes full control of Tormia, I can effectively communicate my voice to other
countries."
An official asked worriedly,
"Which country will listen to us? The demon invasion has already
depleted our forces."
"But we have the Supreme Commander of the Holy War. Only Iruki
knows the core technology of the elemental bomb. If we use it strategically, we can gain support from
other countries."
"But that means other countries will also be able to use elemental
bombs. Isn't that dangerous?"
Although he had a point, his tone was unpleasant, given that he was a
close aide to Adolf XIII.
"Elemental bombs are crucial in war. The light of Agape suppresses
demons, but to unleash powerful force like over Bashka, we need the explosive power of elemental bombs.
We'll make the bombs but only use them with Agape."
This time, Pony asked,
"Can we control it?"
"We have to. Let's create a task force. Mr. Lupist can manage it.
Also, Nade's group will have a monopoly on bomb manufacturing. These two measures will allow us to exert
influence on other countries."
As the official fell silent, Miro asked,
"What about the heavenly army?"
"They're still scattered around the world. They're slowly
regrouping, but they won't be able to act quickly."
"Why? Because Nane has disappeared?"
"That's part of it, but there's also internal conflict. The biggest
issue is that Ikael has sided with humans."
Pony's eyes widened.
"Really? How did you recruit the enemy's leader?"
"I didn't recruit her. She's my biological mother."
A hush fell over the hall.
Amy already knew, but the other friends were stunned into
silence.
"An archangel, no, the archangel is your mother?"
Even the officials held their breath as Shirone made eye contact with
Amy and smiled.
