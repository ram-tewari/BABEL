Chapter 814:
Dark Clan (2):
The mansion was not much different from before.
'Is there something wrong with my eyes?'
But when Rian swung his sword, the wall split, revealing the night
scenery.
And eventually, even that scenery split, filling his vision with a
single flash of light.
"Ugh... why?" The vampire's eyes wavered in shock. If it
was something that could be destroyed, it could penetrate even the semi-spiritual body. But Rian's
object had an indestructible nature, making it a natural enemy for the vampire.
"Step back."
Rian pointed his sword forward.
"It's not over yet."
Blood oozed between the vampire's upper and lower body, rejoining
them.
'Rapid regeneration.'
It was one of the pureblood abilities, using blood to quickly heal
the body.
If a stake was driven into the heart, regeneration was impossible,
but if the stake was removed, vampires could revive.
"You... you're not human."
Rian was human.
"My sword will kill you. Surrender peacefully."
"Nonsense."
History had shown what happened to vampires captured by
humans.
The vampire briefly glanced around and spotted the knights behind
him.
"Heh, I'll kill at least one more."
Starting from his legs, the vampire split into numerous bats and flew
towards the knights.
"Aaah! What is this!"
Rian leaped into the swarm of bats, seeing the terrified
knights.
"Get out quickly!"
Swinging his greatsword, he scattered the bats, but the knights
couldn't even lift their heads.
"S-Save me...!"
Grabbing a knight by the collar, he threw him outside, where he
rolled on the ground.
"Tonight, you're the sacrifice!"
The vampire, dissolving his lower body into smoke, charged at a
well-dressed knight.
'Damn! No choice!'
As Rian pushed the knight away with all his might, the vampire's palm
struck.
"Impact Wave!"
The vampire's technique, delivering a shock in a semi-spiritual
state, struck Rian's chest.
"Ugh!"
As Rian's heart stopped and he crashed into the wall, Irene's face
turned pale.
"Mister!"
Crash!
A nimble figure broke through the third-floor window and landed in
the first-floor hall.
"Step back!"
It was a woman's voice, and she had silver hair.
Swiftly ascending the stairs, she swung a sharp rapier.
The blade sliced through the vampire's neck.
"Argh! This is!"
Even though he transformed into a semi-spiritual state, the sword
accurately inflicted a wound.
"Hunter!"
Seeing the gleaming sword the woman held, the vampire's doubt turned
to certainty.
Having killed Rian, his escape route was open.
"Damn! I'll remember this!"
As the vampire fled through the wall, the woman quickly changed
direction.
"Where do you think you're going!"
"Mister! Mister!"
Hearing Irene's sobbing voice, the woman hesitated and turned
back.
Rian was slumped against the wall with his head tilted.
"It's too late. An Almas-level Impact Wave doesn't just stop the
heart; it explodes it."
Seeing no reason for emergency resuscitation, the woman leaped out of
the broken window.
Irene didn't give up and shook Rian.
"Don't die! What will I do if you die! Get up
quickly!"
"Ugh, noisy."
Rian slowly opened his eyes.
"Oh my god!"
Though Irene had begged for him to live, she was shocked as if seeing
a ghost when he actually woke up.
"W-What? That woman said your heart..."
"Yeah, it stopped."
The durability of the Yacha preserved his form, so recovery didn't
take long.
'Still regenerating?'
Smiling's hallucination echoed in his ears.
"Don't worry. This isn't enough to kill me."
She had heard his heart had stopped.
"...Then what does it take to kill you?"
"I don't know."
Thinking he might not be much different from a vampire, Rian looked
up at the third-floor window.
"Who was that?"
Even in the chaos of life and death, Irene remembered the crucial
details.
"It was a silver-haired woman, and the vampire called her a
hunter."
"Ah."
Not all hunters were strong, but Rian knew they used all the methods
vampires hated.
'I understand why he ran.'
If she was specialized in fighting vampires, even an Almas rank would
find it tough.
"No choice but to wait for the next chance."
It was an unavoidable choice to save the knight, but it was better
than having more casualties.
"What happened? Could it be..."
The mansion's lights turned on late, and Irene's mother rushed in
with soldiers.
"Mom!"
Hugging Irene, she looked at Rian with a frightened
expression.
She would normally be working late, but she had returned home two
hours earlier due to the warning letter.
"What happened?"
"A vampire targeted your daughter. It escaped. It won't attack
again tonight."
"You lost it? Even with all these soldiers?"
Rian didn't want to explain the significance of the Almas rank in
vampire society.
"Mom, Rian... I mean, brother protected me."
Since Irene only called certain people brother, her mother
relaxed.
"Oh, I'm sorry. I got sensitive without realizing
it."
"It's fine. You must be worried about your
daughter."
"Yes. If you need anything, let me know. I'll spare no
support."
Rian looked around the hallway.
"First, disband all the soldiers."
"What?"
It was a surprising suggestion, but if the opponent was an Almas,
those soldiers would only become hostages.
"It's easier for me to protect alone. I'll explain in detail
when the security chief arrives tomorrow morning."
"Mom, do as brother says."
Irene's voice hinted at something only the two of them
understood.
"Alright, then."
As the butlers cleaned up the bodies and tidied the house, Irene ran
over.
"Brother, what should I do?"
Rian was firm.
"Go to bed quickly."
The next morning, Security Chief Benoff, who had been informed the
night before, visited the Bark family.
"You had a fight?"
"Yes. The culprit is indeed a vampire, an Almas
rank."
Hearing it was one of the few purebloods in the world, Benoff
scratched his head.
"Damn! A big shot has come."
"There were casualties. I'm sorry."
"I heard. It's not your fault. If you hadn't sacrificed
yourself, there would have been more deaths."
"What about the security?"
"We'll move the entire family to the royal palace. You can join
our task force. You're not planning to back out, are you?"
Though he wasn't as benevolent as Shirone, Rian was a swordsman who
had vowed to take responsibility for all deaths.
"Of course not. Any progress?"
"Confirming it's a vampire is progress. We'll press harder from
now on."
"Where?"
"There are places we couldn't access due to unclear guidelines.
The Rodenin Blood Bank. The royal palace will soon grant access."
If the culprit was a vampire who took large amounts of blood,
investigating the blood bank was essential.
"Isn't it too late?"
"The blood bank is under the royal palace. Other departments
can't access the records until a warrant is issued."
"I see."
"We'll deploy the royal palace guard 'Shinjang.' Even a
pureblood vampire won't stand a chance."
If it was just one Almas rank, that would be true.
'Something's off.'
The amount of blood taken was too much for one vampire.
'Did they form an organization around the Almas?'
Especially if it was a royal institution, the possibility of an
internal spy couldn't be ruled out.
"Can you give me some time?"
"Why? The task force hasn't been formed yet..."
"I have something I want to investigate."
With that, the Bark family moved to the royal palace, and Rian
wandered the city alone.
'Here it is. The Rodenin Blood Bank.'
He examined the seven-story building occupying a triangular corner
and entered an alley.
As night fell, as expected, several groups wandered the maze-like
alleys.
'There's no way to confirm.'
One reason it was hard to eradicate vampires was that they were
indistinguishable from humans.
'They're vulnerable to sunlight, but...'
That was only fatal to hybrids, and purebloods were said to endure
using rapid regeneration.
"Hey, you guys."
When Rian called out, a group of five stopped and turned.
"Who are you?"
The medium-height man who seemed to be the leader pulled out a
knife.
"I have a question. Have you seen anyone entering or leaving
this blood bank at this hour?"
The leader's eyes visibly wavered.
"Who are you? Security?"
"Swordsman."
"Swordsman?"
The leader twirled his knife and approached.
"How do you like my sword?"
Even without showing hostility, just stabbing with the knife showed
his experience.
"Huh?"
As Rian swiftly twisted his upper body and punched his abdomen, the
leader's feet lifted off the ground.
"Ugh..."
As he knelt and vomited stomach acid, the group behind him
charged.
"You bastard, you want to fight!"
There was no need to draw his greatsword.
"Ugh! Ugh!"
With jabs learned from Benoff, he tapped their necks, causing them to
collapse.
"S-Spare us! We don't know anything!"
'They're not vampires.'
But they didn't seem completely ignorant either.
"Speak honestly. As I said, I'm not security. If you make the
wrong decision..."
As Rian drew his greatsword, the leader's face turned pale.
"I really don't know! We just transport things! We didn't do
anything wrong!"
"Transport? What?"
"B-Bags! We wait in front of the blood bank at dawn, and someone
comes out and gives us bags and money. We deliver the bags, and the money is ours."
"Who gives you the bags?"
"It's too dark to see clearly." Rian pointed his greatsword
at the man's neck.
"He wears glasses! He has a sharp look, is about a hand's width
taller than me, and has a pale, slender face."
'He's not from the Bark family.'
Rian asked, "Where do you deliver the bags?"
"The destination changes every time. Sometimes it's Block 13,
other times Block 47."
They wouldn't reveal their hideout.
"Are there others doing this job besides you?"
"These days, it seems like everyone is doing it. Different
groups take turns on different days. Honestly, it feels shady, but they pay a lot. We're risking our
lives for this."
Rian checked the time.
"Go back. And don't come near this place again." The
leader, looking confused, saw Rian's fierce eyes and shuddered.
"Do you want to die here?"
"N-No!"
The leader, not even picking up his dropped knife, ran deep into the
alley, followed by his group.
'Brokers frequenting the blood bank. The security team must already
be monitoring them.'
That's why Rian had to act.
After waiting for two hours where he had sent the thugs away, the
area became completely deserted.
In the pitch-black night, with even the street lights off, a man in a
fedora appeared in front of the blood bank.
'He's here.'
After surveying the surroundings, the man headed to the back door.
Only then did Rian step out and start tailing him.
