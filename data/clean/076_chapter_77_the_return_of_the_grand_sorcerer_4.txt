Chapter 77:
The Return of the Grand Sorcerer (4)
The debate ended at 7 p.m.
As the evening party hosted by the Teachers' Association began, the fervor of the
discussion vanished as if it had never existed. The attendees were busy sipping wine and exchanging
conversations with notable figures from various fields.
Around Reina, high-ranking individuals had gathered—people whose names even those
uninterested in the Teachers' Association would have heard.
*'So this is what they mean when they say beauty is a burden.'*
Sade waited for his turn to speak, eavesdropping on their conversation. Most of their
words were either unbearably authoritative or teasing jokes.
Reina maintained a warm smile and responded kindly. This was unexpected for Sade. Her
bright personality didn't match the infamous reputation of the 99th Council.
Art was a technique for handling emotions, and its power to captivate the masses was
something even those in authority couldn't ignore.
Given art's critical nature, many artists were stubborn individuals. But Reina, as if
completely detached from such rumors, mingled cheerfully with people from all walks of life.
*'Haah, if only Ms. Siena had even half of her personality…'*
As he sipped his wine, Reina finished her conversation and walked toward him.
"Hello, Teacher Sade."
"Ah, yes! Huh? Do you know me?"
"Of course. You're a teacher at Alpheas Magic School. The Ozent family's main house is in
Creas."
"Aha! That's right. I forgot for a moment. Nice to meet you."
Delighted to meet someone from their hometown, the two talked for a long time. As Sade
had expected, Reina was bright and fun to be around.
"By the way, how is Shirone doing?"
"Huh? How do you know Shirone? Ah, right."
Sade, who had been the grading teacher for Shirone's entrance exam, recalled that Shirone
had listed the Ozent family as his origin.
"Shirone was a guest of the Ozent family. As expected of a prestigious family, your
discernment is exceptional. Shirone is doing well. Though he did cause some trouble recently."
"Huh? Trouble? What kind?"
When Reina's eyes widened, Sade realized his mistake. If she found out that a student
backed by the Ozent family had been suspended, her pride would be hurt.
"Ahaha! It's nothing serious. He's such an outstanding student that even minor incidents
become big news. That's all."
"Ah, I see."
Reina sighed in relief. Just from Sade's words, she could guess how Shirone was living.
"I'm glad he's doing well. Does he have many friends?"
"Of course. He's popular with both boys and girls. Sometimes I'm even jealous. Hahaha!"
"Oh? Then, perhaps…"
Reina shook her head, stopping herself from asking if he had a lover. As a sponsor, it
was a question she could ask. But the questioner in her heart wasn't borrowing the sponsor's name.
"Huh? Is there something else you're curious about?"
"No. I'm just glad he's doing well."
"Haha! Don't worry. It's a bit presumptuous of me to say this, but Alpheas Magic School
is a prestigious institution. You can rest assured."
"Hehe, that's true. I was being presumptuous. Sorry."
"Not at all. If anything, I was the rude one. How about joining me on the way to Creas?
We're leaving in the morning. I'll treat you to a splendid lunch."
Reina shook her head regretfully.
"I'm sorry. I have a performance schedule, so I need to return to Vashka."
"Ah, I see. That's too bad…"
Sade couldn't hide his disappointment. Normally, he'd let it go without a second thought,
but she was someone he truly didn't want to part with.
"It can't be helped. But I'd love to see your performance. Could you tell me the
schedule? I'll come to Vashka."
"Oh, I'd be honored. Actually, I was worried. With the recent trouble in the capital, I
was afraid there'd be too many empty seats."
"Ah, what happened in Vashka?"
"A dark sorcerer attacked Inferno and helped a B-class wanted criminal escape. The
capital's security is almost under martial law now."
"Dark… sorcerer?"
Sade's brow furrowed. Among countless news stories, this one stood out as particularly
unusual.
Dark sorcerers were rare in this day and age. And to attack Inferno, of all places? Just
to free one B-class criminal?
Dark sorcerer, Inferno, B-class criminal.
A scene from history flashed through Sade's mind.
***
"Could you tell me more details?"
"The incident happened a week ago. The escaped prisoner is a man named Lucas, formerly
the vice-captain of the Parrot Thieves. The captain is still wanted."
"Do you know anything about the person who attacked Inferno? Their appearance, perhaps?"
"Hmm, I'm not sure. I only heard rumors. An elderly man with white hair, I think. He had
two attendants. They used dark magic to free the prisoners. Something about darkness… what was it?"
"The Authority of Darkness?"
"Yes, that's it! As expected of a magic school teacher."
Reina's praise fell on deaf ears. Dark magic—and a high-level technique like the
Authority of Darkness—used by an elderly man to attack Inferno.
*'No way…'*
Sade's head snapped up.
"Sorry. I need to go."
"Huh? Ah, okay."
His politeness was almost instinctive. Sade no longer saw the party or the beautiful
woman before him.
*'Damn it! Of all times, when I'm away on business!'*
The escape had happened a week ago. If they moved nonstop from Vashka, they'd have had
enough time to reach Creas.
"Carriage! A carriage!"
Leaving the mansion, Sade jumped into the first carriage he saw. He opened the window to
the driver's seat and shouted,
"To Creas! As fast as possible!"
"S-sir, all carriages here are reserved. If you're not on the list, I can't…"
These carriages were meant for high-ranking officials, and no ordinary sum would sway the
driver.
Sade rummaged through his pockets and pulled out all the money he had.
"Here! Take it. Are you going or not?"
The driver counted the gold coins scattered on the box seat and immediately changed his
expression, preparing to depart.
"We'll leave at once. We'll go through the night?"
"No sleep, no breaks! Just go fast!"
The sound of hooves clattered against the night air.
*'Damn it! I can't be late.'*
Sade leaned back and pressed his forehead. He wanted to use spatial teleportation, but
jumping repeatedly in an unfamiliar place without knowing the coordinates or environment would be suicidal,
even with ten lives.
The helplessness brought him back to his senses. As he assessed the situation, doubts
arose.
*What's the plan behind his return?*
Sade had never met or seen him. But the terrifying connection he'd heard from Alpheas was
vivid in his memory.
"Viltor Arcane…"
Sade's eyes burned as he stared ahead.
***
Past 4 a.m., Kanis and his group entered the training ground on the mountainside of
Alpheas Magic School.
Though they'd never attended the school, they could tell it was a top-tier facility,
befitting the disciples of the Grand Sorcerer.
A frivolous voice broke the silence.
"Wow, this is well-built. Looks like it's covered in gold coins. How much is the land
worth?"
Kanis frowned and turned his head. Lucas, the B-class wanted criminal, had a pair of
swords with parrot engravings slung over his shoulder.
"Keep it down. The guards might be patrolling."
"What's there to worry about? We can just cut them down. Or bury them."
Kanis's mood worsened. Though they were traveling together under their master's orders,
he found nothing likable about the man.
"Don't get it twisted. You're hired help. If you disrupt the plan, you'll be the first to
go."
"Ooh, scary. Kids these days are lucky. Meeting a great master and talking back to
adults."
Lucas flopped onto the training ground. Since he didn't interfere further, Kanis
suppressed his anger.
*'The most important thing is Master's revenge. No matter how much I dislike him, I have
to endure.'*
Lucas yawned and asked,
"By the way, why are we waiting here? We'll have to go down to the school anyway."
Arin answered with undisguised disgust.
"Master is going to cast a spell. It's a wide-area spell, so if we're in range, we won't
survive either. If you're confident, you can go down first."
Lucas shrugged. Avoiding Arcane's magic was his honest preference.
"Heh, don't be so angry, little girl. Your cute face is getting ugly."
"Hmph, I don't care how others look."
"Don't be like that. How about dating this old man? I can teach you something more fun
than magic."
As Arin's face turned red and she prepared to yell, Kanis cut in.
"Quiet. It's starting."
The group looked up. Arcane was floating in the center of the moon, using Fly Magic.
Fly Magic was a fusion of two branches of Air Magic: Press and Blow. First, Press Magic
created a solid air pressure around the caster, then Blow Magic generated wind to produce lift. Classified
as overpowered due to its flight capability, it required delicate control as the wind changed in real time.
As darkness gathered, covering the moon, Arcane's eyes snapped open. Combining
omniscience and omnipotence, he spread his limbs and cast his life's masterpiece: Abyss Nova.
There was no deafening noise or terrifying aftermath, but the sight of the school being
engulfed in dark energy was spectacular.
As the effects of Abyss Nova spread beyond the school and faded, the moon reappeared,
brightening the surroundings.
"Hah… Hah…"
Arcane gasped for breath. Even for a Grand Sorcerer, this spell consumed 90% of his
mental energy.
*'It'll be hard to recover for a while.'*
He turned toward the mountain. Maintaining Fly Magic was already difficult, but his anger
burned stronger.
With Abyss Nova spread, no one could stop him. And hadn't he raised disciples precisely
for this situation?
"Hehehe, just wait, Alpheas. I'll make you taste the humiliation I suffered 40 years
ago."
The dark night passed, and a faint light rose from the eastern foothills.
