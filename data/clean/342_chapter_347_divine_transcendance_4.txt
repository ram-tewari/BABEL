Chapter 347:
Divine Transcendance (4)
So far, he has managed to hold on with just his physical abilities, but
as his competitors' levels rise, the gap will inevitably widen.
Therefore, even though the school is aware of Rian's situation, they
have not been able to create an exception for him.
"We will start the evaluation with the first group. Groups two and
three, come out and warm up, the rest can rest."
The cadets in the first group glared as they headed to the training
ground. Kuan, holding the evaluation sheet, signaled the start, and the cadets engaged in a fierce
hand-to-hand combat with monstrous shouts.
As Kuan focused on the match, the cadets relaxed. As always, Tess came
to sit next to Rian. It was good to talk to Rian before the evaluation, like being alone with a lover on
a battlefield.
But Tess's small pleasure was ruined by Bawio's approach.
With his developed jaw raised, he looked down at Rian and said,
"Hey, Rian. How about it? Do you think you can avoid last place
this time?"
"Mind your own business. It's not your concern."
"Haha! Of course. I don't care about last place. But I suddenly had
an interesting thought. It might be related to you."
"What are you talking about?"
When Rian turned around, Bawio pointed to himself with his thumb.
"How about a match with me? Wrestling. Who knows? If you beat the
strongest, the school might recognize you. You have to try everything to avoid last place,
right?"
Rian turned his gaze away, uninterested.
"I don't need anyone's recognition. I'll fight anyone if I have to,
but I don't want to show off useless strength."
Bawio smiled faintly. It was the typical talk of someone who wanted to
avoid a fight but maintain their pride.
But this time, that pride would be his downfall.
"Hmm. Can you take responsibility for what you just
said?"
With that, Bawio looked around the training ground. Seizing the moment
when the second group's match was ending, he raised his hand.
"Instructor! I have a suggestion."
"What is it?"
"I want to compete with Rian. Please allow it. We have already
agreed."
For a cadet to change the evaluation method was a bold move.
But this time, Kuan also thought deeply. If it was Rian, it would be
interesting for him too.
He was curious to see how far Rian's divine transcendence could hold
against Bawio, the most physically capable among his peers.
"I will decide after hearing the opinions of the
group."
Even if Rian and Bawio had agreed, the other group members might have
different thoughts.
But Bawio was confident.
There was no one who could beat him in wrestling, so they wouldn't
refuse the proposal to gain high scores.
As expected, Bawio's opponent agreed without hesitation.
Even if the outcome didn't affect the grades, it was hard to show one's
skills against an overwhelmingly stronger opponent.
Rian's opponent had no reason to refuse either. If Rian, who couldn't
use Schema, did well, it would be a draw at best, and if he didn't, it would be a serious loss.
With everyone's interests aligned, Kuan crossed out two names on the
evaluation sheet and changed the group members.
"Good. Rian and Bawio will join group 17."
As Bawio sat next to Rian with a satisfied smile, Tess felt an
inexplicable unease.
There weren't many who could overpower Rian in practical swordsmanship
among his peers, but Bawio in wrestling was truly dangerous.
She didn't know why he proposed such a match, but she hoped it would end
as just part of the evaluation.
Bawio looked at Rian's stiff face and said,
"How about it? You're really going to fight me now? You won't
regret it, right? If you're scared, say so now. I'll go easy on you."
Rian didn't answer. He wasn't the type to make up words if he had
nothing to say.
The evaluation proceeded smoothly after that.
When the eighth group entered the arena, Tess, who was in the tenth
group, prepared to go out. Wrestling was a strenuous activity, so stretching beforehand was crucial to
avoid injuries.
"Rian, I'm going."
"Yeah. Do your best."
Bawio added a comment.
"Your opponent is nothing. Show them your top-tier
skills."
Tess ignored Bawio's words and headed to the training ground. Bawio
didn't mind. The cold looks wouldn't last long. Once the match with Rian started, her eyes would burn
with intensity.
It was Tess's turn. Her opponent was a man named Godim, a
straightforward swordsman. He blushed as he faced Tess, maintaining the buzz cut from his trainee
days.
It was foolish to distinguish between men and women in a school that
taught killing techniques. But he thought there was no need to engage in wrestling, which involved close
physical contact.
However, once the match began, Godim's eyes sharpened like a warrior
facing a beast.
Tess charged first and collided with Godim. He lowered his center of
gravity and pulled her thighs with his arms. Her legs lifted momentarily, but Tess skillfully spun
around, neutralizing the move.
Bawio smiled in satisfaction at the sight.
"Impressive, Tess. She's really amazing, isn't she?"
Rian acknowledged Tess's talent and skill. But Bawio's words were
unpleasant. He knew Bawio's gaze was on Tess's body, not her technique.
"Why do you want to fight me?"
"You should know the reason."
Bawio sneered. It seemed he was anxious about losing Tess.
"If that's really the reason, don't involve Tess again. If you want
to fight, come to me anytime. I won't run or hide."
"Haha! Confident, aren't you? But will there be a next time for
you?"
Bawio decided to get to the point. With Tess absent, it was the perfect
time to reveal his true intentions.
"Ogent Rian. I've heard the rumors. They call your training
demonic. You've staked your life on victory, right? Fine. How about the loser of this match gives up on
Tess?"
Rian snorted in disbelief.
"Do you think Tess will choose you if you beat me?"
"That doesn't matter. You leaving and Tess choosing me are separate
issues. I'm asking about your resolve."
Rian delayed his answer and looked at Tess. Her wrestling was different
from Godim's. She disrupted her opponent's rhythm with flexibility and balance, not strength.
Tess's talent was genuine. On the other hand, he had no talent or
future. In this situation, pondering her significance was a luxury.
"I don't gamble with people's hearts."
"Hmph. Backing out because it's unfavorable?"
"But I'll tell you this."
Rian's eyes flashed as he glared at Bawio.
"Even if you kill me in this match, I won't resent
you."
Rian's determination was sincere. But Bawio, being naturally strong and
a soldier, felt more combative than fearful.
"Haha! Big words. Do you think I can't do it?"
At that moment, a thud was heard from the training ground. Rian and
Bawio turned simultaneously. Tess had thrown Godim to the ground, holding his arm.
Godim looked up at the sky, confused about what had happened, then
blushed and sat up.
"I lost. It was a good match."
"Phew. You did well too. The last move was a gamble."
She had used the opponent's pushing force to attempt a throw. If she had
misjudged, she would have been in a helpless position, but Tess, with her sensory Schema, couldn't miss
the opponent's center of gravity shift. Godim knew her words were a courtesy to the loser and accepted
his defeat gracefully.
Tess returned to Rian and made a V sign with both hands.
"Hehe. I'm done. Now it's vacation time."
But the atmosphere was different from before. Rian's expression was
colder, and Bawio's eyes were filled with hostility. Something had clearly happened while she was
away.
"Rian, why the face? Bawio, what happened?"
Bawio shrugged nonchalantly.
"Why ask me? I didn't do anything."
Tess looked at Bawio with suspicion but had no choice but to sit down.
She knew he was lying, but Rian wouldn't answer even if she asked.
When the fifteenth group's match started, Rian , who was in the
seventeenth group, entered the training ground to warm up. Bawio, pretending to follow, turned to Tess
and said,
"Tess. You'll soon see how useless Rian is. And how great I
am."
"What nonsense? No matter what you do, you'll never look good to
me."
"That's harsh. We're friends, after all. You know, Rian and I made
a bet earlier."
"A bet? What kind of bet?"
"The loser of this match gives up on you. I thought you should
know."
Tess scoffed and shook her head.
"Bawio, Rian would never gamble with someone else. That's the
difference between you and Rian."
Bawio was displeased with Tess's blind trust but didn't show it. This
was also the source of his numerous scandals in social circles.
"Really? Just watch."
The match was set, so it didn't matter. Seeing Rian humiliated in front
of the cadets would embarrass Tess too. Wrestling was perfect for this. It allowed him to slowly torment
his opponent.
When it was the turn of Group 17, Rian and Bawio faced each other in the
arena. Comparing their bare bodies, the difference in size was almost 1.5 times. Although Rian was not
small for his age, Bawio's bulk was so intimidating that it looked like a match between an adult and a
child.
The cadets fixed their eyes on the two, like people expecting to witness
a brutal feast in the wild.
Rian was going to be devoured. The disadvantage of not being able to use
Schema was fatal in every aspect.
Rian was recognized for keeping up with the progress solely through his
physical abilities, but he was never the best in terms of skill.
Bawio cracked his thick neck and said,
"Shall we start? Are you ready?"
"Anytime. Since it has come to this, let's have a good
match."
As Rian and Bawio took their wrestling stances, Kuan put down his notes
and focused on their match.
As soon as the signal to start was given, the two collided.
Once their bodies touched, anything was allowed. But Bawio charged in
headfirst, delivering a shock right from the start.
There was a loud bang. It was a sound that made one worry that a skull
might have been broken.
Kuan didn't consider it a foul. Collisions to close the distance were
common.
Rian wasn't unprepared, but when their heads collided, sparks flew
before his eyes, and his upper body was thrown back.
In such collisions, weight was everything. This was why Bawio had bulked
up so desperately.
As Rian staggered, Bawio immediately followed up, grabbing the back of
Rian's neck. Rian quickly regained his composure and did the same. They stood like two bulls locking
horns. Their faces turned red as they held their breath. Gradually, Rian's legs began to drag on the
ground as he was pushed back. Once again, weight was the deciding factor.
"Huuuuh..."
Bawio's breath escaped through his teeth. He had heard the rumors, but
facing Rian in person was shocking. Rian couldn't use Schema. Normally, he should have been knocked out
in the first collision.
