Chapter 558:
That Evening
After the briefing session ended, Iruki and Nade headed straight to the
research room.
As they opened the door, they found Shirone sitting on the sofa with a
pale and terrified face.
"What's up with your expression?" Iruki asked.
Nade rolled up his sleeves and approached.
"What do you think? He's probably imagining the slaughterhouse that's
about to unfold, scaring himself stiff. Come here. I'll spare your mouth, but the rest of you's getting
it."
"Guys…" Shirone turned his frightened face toward them.
"Something's wrong here."
"Yeah, it is wrong. This is where your corpse will end up
buried."
"Listen to me! I'm serious, something's really off!"
"Shut up! Betrayer's got a lot to say for himself!"
With Nade's heartfelt wrestling attack, Shirone felt the world
spinning.
"Ahhh! Let me go!"
This time, even Iruki didn't intervene.
"Don't worry. He won't kill you. You just might lose your ability to
function as a man."
"Haha! That's actually a good idea!"
Shirone's eyes widened in alarm.
"Wait! Hahaha! That tickles!"
"We're not done yet!"
As Nade's relentless teasing continued, Shirone thrashed about.
"Hahaha! Ow! Stop it! It really hurts!"
Only after a high-pitched scream did Nade let go, brushing off his
hands.
"Hmph, you know what'll happen if you betray us again, right?"
"Got it, got it," Shirone muttered.
Iruki patted the sofa, motioning Shirone to sit.
"Alright, sit down. Let's hear it. If you were ready to write your will,
it must've been serious."
Though Nade hadn't fully let go of his annoyance, his curiosity about
Shirone's reason was stronger.
Shirone, still sniffling from tears, set aside his thoughts about his
transcendent sense and sat down.
"Well, here's what happened…"
The explanation went on for two hours, even though it was already
condensed.
After blinking a few times in disbelief, Nade asked abruptly, "Are you
writing a novel these days?"
"It's true!" Shirone protested.
Though they knew Shirone wasn't the type to lie, it was hard to digest
his story at face value.
"Alright, fine. Hmm, so that's why you refused the evaluation. It was
definitely the right call. If you can't stand out in scores, making an impact during the graduation exam
is what counts."
Nade thumped his chest in support.
"Don't worry, Shirone. I'll give you all the insider info on the
graduating class."
Iruki scoffed.
"What info? What can you even give? You're dead last in the
class."
Shirone's eyes widened.
"What? Dead last? Nade, are you really last?"
"Haha, yeah, it just kind of ended up that way."
Though Nade had never been particularly motivated about magic, he had
always managed to stay in the middle ranks, so this result was unexpected.
"This is my fault," Shirone muttered.
While it was true Nade had fallen into despair after their trio's leader
disappeared, he wasn't the type to burden his friends with his struggles.
"No, absolutely not. The truth is, I underestimated the graduating
class. I thought I could coast through like I did in the advanced class, but that wasn't the
case."
Iruki nodded in agreement.
'Now that Shirone's back, Nade will probably get his act
together.'
Being the only one left behind while all his friends graduated was a
nightmare Nade couldn't bear.
"By the way, if Nade is last, and with me, Maya, and Aider out of the
running, that puts me at 27th. Iruki, what rank are you?"
Iruki answered briefly, "8th."
"Wow, you're in Class One? That's amazing."
"Well… someone had to balance things out when one of us lost his focus,"
Iruki replied, smirking at Nade.
Nade pouted in irritation.
"Besides, I'm nothing special. Amy's 5th."
"5th?!"
Shirone clenched his fists instinctively.
Amy had finished 5th in last year's final evaluation as well, so her
mid-term performance was impressive.
Nade chimed in.
"Since you left, three people have been newly promoted to Class One:
Amy, Iruki, and Dante."
"What rank is Dante?"
"4th."
"…"
Though Shirone had resolved to focus more on strategy than raw skill
from now on, hearing about his friends' progress filled him with unease.
"So, three people in the original Class One were bumped down to Class
Two. There's been quite a shake-up."
"Sounds like it's been intense," Shirone muttered.
Nade clenched his fists in frustration.
"Tsk, now I get it after hearing your story. That Fermi guy, staying in
1st place by buying other people's magic."
"Well, that's a skill in itself. And Shirone, you don't need to worry so
much. You've been training with that amazing Miro, haven't you?"
Shirone was reminded of his transcendent sense.
"Right, let me explain."
As Shirone began explaining his Time Explosion ability, Nade stared at
him, slack-jawed.
"What are you talking about? Perceiving time differently?"
"I'll show you."
Shirone stood up, entered the state of oneness, and summoned the avatar
of the Angel of Time.
"Is that… an avatar?"
"Yeah. It's a law that breaks time. Like this…"
As Shirone walked forward, leaving golden afterimages, he stopped and
reappeared in the position he was in one second ago.
"…"
After a long silence, Iruki spoke.
"I see. So you're not transcending time; you're perceiving it
differently."
"Exactly. And future events too…"
At that moment, a loud thud echoed from the ceiling, and Shirone's eyes
widened.
"This is it! Do you hear that? You're hearing it, right?"
His friends looked around in confusion.
"Hear what? All I hear is your shouting."
Shirone turned toward the hallway, following the sound of a scream that
had erupted outside.
"Look! Can't you hear the fighting in the hallway?"
Iruki checked the hallway and shook his head.
"There's no one out there."
Shirone deactivated Time Explosion with a frustrated expression.
"Why is this happening?"
"What exactly is going on?"
As Shirone explained step by step, Iruki's expression turned
puzzled.
"Isn't it a side effect of your ability? Perceiving time
differently…"
"No, it's not that. There's no problem anywhere else. It's only in
Istas."
"Hmm, Istas…"
Shirone looked at Nade, who had a thoughtful expression.
"Do you have any idea? You figured out the secret of my
supernatural sense before."
"No, it's not that. I just remembered something a senior once told
me. As you know, the Paranormal Psychic Science Research Group had some amazing seniors. Many
underground research groups have been eyeing this place for a long time."
Iruki interjected.
"Upper-level rumors, right? But from what Shirone said, it was just
Miro's dimension."
"I thought so too. But how do you explain the supernatural sense
Shirone felt? And the story my direct senior told me keeps bothering me."
"What story?"
Nade spoke as if he didn't need to remember.
"They said everything that can't exist in this world exists in the
upper levels of Istas. That's why so many research groups want to take over this place."
Shirone pondered Nade's words.
"What can't exist in this world?"
"I don't know. My senior didn't know either. It's a saying passed
down from their senior's senior. It could just be a rumor. Honestly, I thought Istas's secret was fully
revealed after hearing your story. But now…"
"There's something more."
Iruki showed interest, resting his chin on his hand.
"It's worth investigating. Maybe it's something hidden in specific
coordinates of the building, like Miro's dimension."
Nade's eyes lit up as he shouted.
"Yeah! Let's change the structure of the building! If it's a puzzle
involving space-time, Iruki can calculate it."
Shirone didn't doubt Iruki's mental abilities compared to Sain, but he
was worried for other reasons.
"Let's not go too deep. We're in the graduation class. We don't
have enough time even if we train hard every day."
Iruki shook his finger.
"No, we should do it because we're in the graduation class. Once we
graduate, when will we come back here? We can't let someone else have this fun."
"Exactly! Let's make new history. Let's uncover Istas's
secret!"
Iruki teased Nade's enthusiasm.
"You just don't want to attend classes."
"No, I'll work hard now. Shirone is back."
Seeing his friends' strange enthusiasm, Shirone sighed but couldn't
argue as the one involved.
"Alright. Shall we start right now?"
Shirone raised his hand to stop them.
"No, not today. I have somewhere to go."
"Huh? Where at this hour?"
"I need to talk to Amy. She'll be upset if she hears it later than
you guys."
Shirone also didn't want his friends to get too caught up in
this.
"Yeah, if you're going to get hit, it's better to get it over with.
The longer you wait, the worse it will be."
"Haha! Right. And let's solve the Istas issue on the weekend. We
have 15 weeks until the graduation exam, so there's enough time."
Iruki nodded.
"Then let's call it a day. Even if you can't enter the evaluation
hall, we'll see each other in the dining hall."
The three left Istas's warehouse, and by the time they reached the
dormitory, it was late at night.
'Is it too late?'
Back in his room, Shirone hesitated but felt certain she wouldn't be
asleep.
"Yeah, I should go."
Having decided, Shirone put on his coat and turned the doorknob.
And the next moment, he froze in place.
Amy, looking as surprised as he was, stood there.
"……"
Facing each other with just a door between them was a strange
coincidence, but Amy's eyes had already calmed.
"You were in your room. Where were you going?"
"Oh? No, actually… I was coming to see you."
"I see."
Shirone couldn't find the words to say, surprised by her unexpected
reaction. Amy pointed to his room.
"Can I come in for a moment?"
"Of course."
After giving Amy a chair and sitting on his bed, Shirone waited for the
right moment to speak in the awkward atmosphere.
"Um…"
They both started speaking at the same time, and Amy went first.
"You go first."
"Okay. I wanted to explain why I had to leave. So, I…"
"Wait. Let me go first."
Caught off guard, Shirone swallowed hard.
"Let me do this. I want to make things clear before I lose my
resolve."
Before I lose my resolve.
Amy hadn't revealed anything yet, but Shirone's heart pounded just
hearing those words.
"Shirone, no matter what explanation you give, I have no intention
of forgiving you."
"Amy…"
"I know you won't misunderstand, but let me be clear. I'm not
saying I won't see you or that I'm ending our friendship. It's just that…"
Amy, who had been hesitating, looked up again.
"When you disappeared like that, I had no choice. Should I keep
waiting for you, or should I just forget you?"
"I understand. I'm sorry."
He had no excuse, even if he had ten mouths.
He couldn't calculate the odds of life and death, but he had left on a
journey assuming he might not return.
Amy forced a smile as she spoke.
"So, that's just how it is. If you're expecting something from me
or think we can go back to how things were before you left, there's no need to explain. You're still a
good friend. Do you understand what I mean…"
Amy suddenly stopped speaking, her expression blank.
She had run countless simulations in her mind before coming in, but this
was an unexpected situation.
