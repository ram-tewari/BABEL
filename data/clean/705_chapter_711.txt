Chapter 711:
The 13th Night of the Soldier Ants.
Born during the early days when Queen Garnet was building her colony, he
was a master of battle who had annihilated thousands of enemy ants.
Currently serving as the captain of Garnet's royal guard, it wasn't
unusual for someone as battle-hardened and bloodthirsty as him to personally investigate the appearance
of a new enemy.
As the 13th Night approached, he extended his antennae toward Shirone,
carefully examining him.
While the antennae could gather an immense amount of information, they
were still just an evolutionary enhancement of the senses. Simply brushing against Shirone's body
couldn't reveal what he was thinking.
"Who are you? Why have you trespassed into Garnet's
colony?"
The pheromones from the 13th Night were decoded through the Ultima
System, but Shirone had no means to convey his intentions.
'What should I do?'
As Shirone pondered, he noticed something in the way the ant's antennae
scanned his body.
'Maybe…'
Raising his hands slowly to show he meant no harm, Shirone focused his
mind. From Armand's robe, two tendrils resembling the antennae of an ant extended outward.
"Microcosmic Adaptation."
If Armand's robe could interface with countless systems, perhaps it
could communicate with an ant as well.
"What is this now?"
The 13th Night adopted a defensive stance as the tendrils crept along
Shirone's shoulders.
'His presence is suffocating.'
The ant's killer intent was pure chaos, so raw and overwhelming that it
reminded Shirone of Neide's magical transformation.
Drawing on his prior experiences, Shirone maintained his composure and
carefully connected the tendrils to the antennae.
"Hnng!"
Even a brief touch triggered an exchange of information between two
entirely different systems, causing the 13th Night to shudder.
New language system detected. Translation program
initializing. Interface generating and deploying.
At that moment, the robe inflated and released a globular, organic
structure bristling with nerve-like filaments.
Artificial Brain Module: Language Translation
Version.
"What have you done to me?"
The alien sensation in his antennae left the 13th Night enraged. He
lunged forward, unleashing a surge of murderous energy.
"Wait! Hold on…!"
The 13th Night's jaws, as sharp as twin blades, snapped shut inches from
Shirone as he barely leaned backward to evade.
"Not bad!"
The ant curled into a ball, spinning rapidly while slashing with its
claws. Shirone's eyes widened in realization.
'This… This is like Kido's technique.'
Earth's Law—Antlion Pit.
"Kill! Kill! Destroy the enemy!"
The countless ants around them released aggressive pheromones, but
Shirone remained focused as the artificial brain module continued decoding the signals, sparing him from
sensory overload.
'The real problem is this guy.'
The spinning attack of the 13th Night created an illusion as if the
surrounding space was being sucked in. Even the landscape began to warp as though the earth itself were
tangling into threads.
'Wow…!'
Shirone couldn't help but admire the mastery of the ant, who had
achieved such a level of harmony with the earth in a non-human body.
As the ground quaked and the earth rose to block all escape routes,
Shirone realized that it wasn't merely an illusion. The Law of the World was imposing Zero Movement
through denial.
"Choeni Bardo!"
The hidden pathways of the world unfolded before Shirone, creating an
alternative route for him to escape.
"What's this?"
The 13th Night halted his spin, glancing around in confusion. He had
sealed every path with the Earth's Law, yet Shirone was nowhere to be found.
"I don't want to fight."
The pheromones carried Shirone's message, causing the 13th Night to
flinch and turn. Shirone had calmly repositioned himself behind the ant.
"……"
The other ants froze, observing silently. The 13th Night, drawing on his
sharp instincts, finally understood.
"You… you're doing the same thing as I am."
The 13th Night didn't know how he had learned to meld with the earth. It
had come naturally to him through countless battles, turning enemy ants to dust and discovering the
essence of life.
"Yes, I've lived the same way you have."
Though the ant's system was simpler than a human's, Shirone saw no
fundamental difference in their essence.
'Nothing is insignificant. We just fail to observe.'
Recalling Miro's teachings, Shirone respectfully addressed the 13th
Night.
"I come from another world. I need to find a way back to my world. Take
me to your queen."
"To the Queen?"
It was fortunate that they could communicate, but the Queen was old and
frail, and Shirone was undeniably strong.
'But he has no hostility.'
In the fleeting moment when their antennae connected, Shirone's genuine
intent had been conveyed, earning a sliver of trust.
"I have no authority to bring you before the Queen."
Only ants ranked in the top ten among the colony's royal caste were
allowed to meet the Queen.
"You could die. The royals are conservative and view intrusion by other
species as filth."
Shirone could see parallels with human societies but wasn't
concerned.
"You're strong. If you take me there, I'll convince them myself."
The 13th Night shook his head.
"You don't understand. They can't be reasoned with. The royals exist
solely to build the colony…"
His pheromones cut off abruptly.
'Perhaps Merote might permit it.'
Merote, the highest-ranked royal, was revered for her stunning beauty
and noble character. But the fierce competition among the male ants vying for her favor made it risky to
bring a foreign creature to her.
"There's one way. I'll present you as a war slave. That might stop the
royals from interfering."
It was still dangerous, but Shirone had no other choice if he ever
wanted to leave.
"Fine. I'll leave the details to you. Just get me to the Queen."
"… Follow me."
The 13th Night turned, and the worker ants parted, forming a pathway as
though responding to a silent command.
In front of Andre's entrance.
Kido was locked in battle with the Mara Bandits, unable to catch his
breath for even a second. His face had grown pale from oxygen deprivation.
'I can't hold on…'
After trading hundreds of blows, he had managed to take down a few, but
their numbers had only dwindled from 38 to 32. To Kido, it felt like their numbers were endless.
Particularly, the vice-captain's skills were undeniably superior to his own.
"Break through! Clear the entrance!"
All Kido could do was use the terrain of the entrance to desperately
hold them off.
'Bladed Hell!'
Rolling across the ground, he spun his spear, momentarily forcing the
bandits to retreat. But his stamina was nearly gone.
Seeing his movements slow, the vice-captain drew his sword and charged
forward.
"This is it! Kill him!"
"Damn it!"
In a desperate moment, as the blade came down toward his neck, Kido had
no choice but to retreat and abandon the entrance.
'This is bad… If they get in…'
The bandits now had control of a large open space, giving them the
advantage of numbers.
"If you kneel and beg, I'll make your death swift," the vice-captain
said, approaching with his blade dangling ominously.
Kido felt his strength draining away.
'I'm not afraid to die. But…'
If Kido died, so would Shirone.
"He trusted me to protect him."
His trembling hands barely held onto his spear, and tears began to flow
as he realized he couldn't fend them off.
"You fought well. But now, it's time to die."
As Shirone's death loomed closer, Kido felt something he had never felt
before: fear. The thought of Shirone's death terrified him.
'I have to protect him.'
The cold heart of a goblin now burned like a fiery coal, ignited by the
weight of protecting another life.
It was… emotion.
"Please, spare Shirone. I'll do anything. Just don't kill him. He
trusted me."
Tears dripped onto the ground.
"… How tragic," the vice-captain muttered, genuinely pitying him.
"You'll forget everything once you're dead."
Offering what little mercy he could, the vice-captain raised his blade
and walked toward Kido.
'Water… I need water.'
The thought of Shirone's life burning within him made Kido feel an
unbearable thirst.
"My throat… my throat…!"
"Die."
The moment the vice-captain brought his sword down, Kido suddenly
snapped his head up, his bloodshot eyes blazing red.
'What the—?'
Kido's figure flickered like a mirage, and the ground beneath them
rippled like waves.
Sensing danger, the vice-captain slashed down at lightning speed,
but—
"GRAAAHHH!"
The ground warped under the influence of Denial, transforming into an
illusory swamp that began pulling the bandits down.
Earth's Law—Bound Spirit.
"What the hell is this?! My feet…!"
The illusion of sinking into an endless swamp sent the bandits into a
frenzy, flailing desperately as the vice-captain shouted, "Stay calm! It's just an illusion caused by
the Law!"
The Law's effects distorted gravity, creating a sense of being dragged
into a bottomless pit.
'What incredible mastery of Axing! How could a mere goblin…?'
"GRRAAHHH!"
Kido stretched his back wide, his eyes fixed on the ceiling in a state
of overwhelming ecstasy.
'It's filling me! It's filling me!'
A rush of clarity surged into the void that had once been his cold,
empty heart, bringing an unparalleled sense of joy.
The goblin's mind, once confined to cycles of consumption and
reproduction, expanded into a greater understanding of the world—through emotion.
'This is what it means to care.'
Like parched soil soaking in rain, his heart filled with love,
amplifying the power of his Axing to new heights.
"Fall back!"
The vice-captain and his men barely resisted the illusion, but the
sensation of walking through a swamp made their movements sluggish and awkward.
A whistling sound broke the tension. Everyone turned to see Kido
spinning his spear with a crazed grin on his face.
'I get it now, Shirone. What it means to be human.'
As the whistling stopped, Kido firmly gripped his spear and gestured for
the bandits to come forward.
"Let's enjoy this dance together."
Kido had finally understood what love was.
Labyrinth Pytharos
At the entrance to a dark, foreboding cave, Nane paused, staring at the
black void before him. This was where all the impurities of the world seemed to converge.
Having ascended to the pinnacle of individual mastery, Nane felt as
though every blink erased and recreated the universe anew.
'Without me, the universe ceases to exist.'
But was that truly all there was?
'If closing my eyes is the end, why do we exist in this world at
all?'
What began as a journey through the simplest of experiences had brought
Nane to confront the ultimate question of the universe's purpose.
The moment he stepped into the labyrinth, a sensation of endless descent
overwhelmed him.
This was The Lower Plane of Laws.
Just standing at the mouth of this chasm, where the world's corrupting
energy pooled, felt like his very sense of self was unraveling.
'To fall… means…'
No naturally formed cave could be this deep. Only one conclusion could
be drawn.
"This is the Under World."
The feeling of falling suddenly ceased. Before him stretched a surreal
landscape entirely unlike reality.
Crimson flames burned without fuel, their light illuminating twisted
metal frameworks and charred iron grates that connected in chaotic patterns.
"S-save me! AAAAAH!"
"Kill me! Please, just kill me!"
The horrifying screams of others echoed through the labyrinth. Yet,
Nane's expression remained calm as he walked forward.
If this was a mental realm, physical death would not mean true
annihilation.
"No! No!"
Monks and adventurers who had ventured into the labyrinth were now
reduced to shattered psyches, subjected to unimaginable horrors.
'Is this Hell?'
Pytharos—First Floor.
This was the Hell of Lust, a realm born from the corrupt laws of
humanity's sins, one of the seven great vices.
