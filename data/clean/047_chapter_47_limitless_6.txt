Chapter 47:
Limitless (6)
"Let's have coffee. Seems like I've been living with coffee on my lips lately."
The two entered the café, each holding a cup of coffee, and sat on a bench. Without much
conversation, they idly passed the time watching birds fly by.
Unlike most girls, Amy wasn't the type to enjoy chatter. Moreover, this silence was the
greatest solace she couldn't experience in the graduation class.
The reason the usually talkative Seriel stayed quiet was because she sensed the
exhaustion of a warrior emanating from Amy. Though she had grown thinner, the unique aura of the graduation
class radiated from her.
There's a saying that class shapes a person. For Amy, every day might have been hell, but
enduring it had already set her far apart from the advanced class.
'That must be part of it. I need to advance quickly too.'
Amy, who had been noisily sipping her empty iced coffee, was the first to speak.
"How's Shirone these days?"
"I was wondering when you'd ask. Have you really not met him at all? Haven't even
heard any rumors?"
"No. No time to meet, and the graduation class has different classes than the
advanced class. I did hear that Samuel went as a teaching assistant last time."
"I see. Well, something interesting has been happening in Class Five lately."
Amy, who had been biting her straw, turned her head with a questioning look. Like a fish
in water, Seriel began chattering excitedly. The gist of the story was the thrilling rivalry between Shirone
and Iruki ahead of the speed gun test.
"He used logs to reduce the units? That's innovative. Though, should I say it's
typical of Iruki?"
"Huh? You know Iruki?"
"Of course I do. We were classmates. Though I advanced quickly, so I didn't see him
much. You know, that thing about him—the Servant Syndrome."
"Wow, you really do know him! Did you talk to him? He didn't seem like the type to
befriend people."
"Yeah. When I first entered Class Five, he came up and talked to me."
Seriel's eyes widened as she exclaimed.
"What? He bothered you? Oh no! Then this is a rivalry over a loved one! This is a
total scoop!"
"Don't be ridiculous. He's a bit weird. Some of what he says makes sense, but it's
hard to empathize. I think he was trying to do to me what he did to Shirone. He started picking a fight the
moment he saw me. I didn't engage. Maybe he lost interest because I'm a girl."
"Hmm, I see. So he's the so-called 'genius hunter'? Shirone must've been unlucky to
cross paths with him."
"Not necessarily unlucky. It's a hurdle everyone must face at some point. Still, it
must be tough. It's a targeting test, and if Iruki uses logs, he'll score really high."
Seriel glared at her with sharp eyes.
"How can you say that? Your boyfriend is in trouble. Even if you can't help, you
should at least cheer him on. What if Shirone loses to Iruki?"
"Losing is part of competition. Why are you making such a fuss?"
Amy seemed unfazed, unlike before. Perhaps because she was waging a daily war
incomparable to the advanced class.
Seriel couldn't hide her disappointment.
"You've never lost! I want to see the two undefeated legends dating! So hurry up and
tell Shirone to hang in there. I'll deliver the message myself."
"Enough. Are you going to exaggerate it again? This isn't an advancement test; it's
a practical evaluation. Shirone's goal is the graduation class too. It'll be a good experience."
"Then at least share some high-score tips for the speed gun. You hold the record,
don't you? 987 points."
"Hmm. But any advice I could give, Shirone probably already knows. It's not that I'm
indifferent—Shirone is truly exceptional. He's already surpassed 700 points, right? I just benefited from
specializing in targeting."
***
At the mention of no solution, Seriel's face fell. Having witnessed their (?) love from
the beginning, she was immersed in the situation as if Shirone were the male lead of a novel.
"Can he win, no matter what? Shirone has to win."
"Hmm."
Amy propped her chin on her hand and fiddled with her straw. Merkoda Iruki. Their first
meeting had been unpleasant, but talent was talent.
"Just focusing on targeting, it won't be easy. Servant Syndrome is a truly powerful
ability. Using logs on top of that would make it incredibly tricky to counter. And Iruki's serious, right?
If he had seriously challenged me back then…"
"If… if he had challenged you?"
After pondering, Amy pouted her lips and said.
"I might've lost."
Seriel was shocked. Amy, who had never lost first place in the advanced class, the one
who set unbreakable records in Class Five, was saying she might've lost.
Though she might've been lenient in recalling the past due to being in the incomparable
graduation class, it was still a staggering statement.
"Eek, what do we do? Our Shirone."
Seriel's face twisted into a frown.
***
The speed gun test was now a day away.
Shirone sat on the floor of his dorm, deep in meditation. He was counting numbers, but it
wasn't a sequence formula. While he had mastered techniques until now, today he sought the answer to what a
sequence formula truly was.
A journey of numbers with a starting point but no destination.
Ten hours passed without him moving a muscle. As time flowed, Shirone realized the
essence of the sequence formula in a realm most humans never reach. It was like witnessing lightning strike
right before his eyes.
When he opened his eyes, dawn was breaking outside. He hadn't eaten or slept for 24
hours, but he felt no fatigue. A light too sacred to be called emotion lingered in his eyes.
Shirone looked up at the ceiling, engulfed in sorrow.
What he had felt was a great epiphany, but also a chillingly harsh reality.
'Is this… truly the truth?'
No one answered. But Shirone felt as if he had heard one.
***
Though there were still 30 minutes left until the test, the training ground was already
packed with participants and spectators. Staff bustled about, reinforcing safety measures and installing
scoreboards around the image zone.
Not a single student from Class Seven or Six missed the event. It was a glimpse of the
hierarchical society, eager to impress their seniors.
On the other hand, only those from Class Four interested in Shirone and Iruki's showdown
made time to attend. Among them, the most notable was Seriel, one of Class Four's top students.
"Ah! Senior! Hello!"
Mark rushed over with the excitement of catching a big fish and greeted her. Seriel,
waving cheerfully, glanced around.
"Good. All the juniors are here. Well, it was the same in my time."
"Don't say such sad things. I'm here solely to celebrate Senior Shirone's
victory!"
"Oh! That's reassuring! Alright, let's fire up our cheering spirit!"
Seriel and Mark secured the best seats with a clear view of the image zone. Wherever she
went, a path opened up, making the front row practically reserved.
Most of the chatter revolved around the showdown between Shirone and Iruki. Shirone,
naturally strong in the Spirit Zone, versus Iruki, skilled in strengthening the zone through sequence
formulas. It was a battle to determine the top talent in Class Five.
The test would be taken simultaneously by both. The school had adopted this competitive
format to maximize students' potential.
For the same reason, participants were scheduled later based on their practice scores.
Naturally, Shirone and Iruki were assigned the last slot, Group 20.
As Etella activated the magic barrier, the scoreboard lit up. After a 10-minute warm-up,
the test began with Group 1.
Though technically a practical evaluation focused on individual scores, the speed gun
test was also a fun event for spectators due to its competitive nature.
Participants couldn't help but be conscious of this. Even if they broke personal records,
losing to their groupmate left them with bitter expressions as they stepped down.
It took about an hour to complete up to Group 10. The highest score so far was 533
points, while Nade finished slightly above the average with 491.
As time passed, the students' eyes grew livelier. They knew what the main event of the
day was.
Finally, Group 19 finished, and Etella called Shirone and Iruki. The clamor of cheers
fell silent in an instant.
Shirone and Iruki walked up the steps side by side, not looking at each other. Their
backs radiated competitiveness.
"Wow! Senior Shirone looks fired up! I heard Iruki's way ahead in practice."
"Pfft. Shirone's strong in actual combat! Remember the teleportation test?"
When Seriel glared, Mark laughed awkwardly and scratched his head. He had underestimated
Shirone and paid the price.
Etella explained the rules to Shirone and Iruki.
"You have one minute. The test starts when the red light turns on. The magic barrier
ensures no interference, so treat it as an individual event. Especially you two—the juniors are watching, so
don't embarrass yourselves."
As if conserving even the mental energy required to respond, Shirone and Iruki only
nodded. Etella spread her arms, and the two split off to their respective zones.
The juniors watched intently, not wanting to miss a moment of the showdown. The tension
was palpable. With both holding records above 700 points, high-speed targeting was expected from the start.
Shirone calmly gazed ahead. His heart raced, but he felt no tension—a sign of peak human
concentration. As the scoreboard's standby light turned on, he counted down in his mind.
'3, 2, 1.'
Start!
The moment the red light flashed, Shirone's eyes widened as he initiated the sequence
formula. He hit zero instantly, and numbers surged at an incredible pace.
Whoosh whoosh whoosh—Bam!
Ten photon outputs per second.
Light streaked across all 360 degrees. Every target in the first step was obliterated.
Iruki's evasive Spirit Zone also scraped through space, eliminating targets.
"Whoa! They're both insanely fast! Everything's gone already!"
Mark couldn't contain his excitement. The same happened in the second and third steps.
Targets were destroyed the moment they appeared. By the sixth step, still tied, Seriel clenched her fists.
"Yes! Keep this up, and the outcome's anyone's guess."
Shirone and Iruki matched each other up to the tenth step. But the next step had 20
targets appearing at once—a number neither could handle in one breath. The real battle began now.
The scores were 239 to 237, with Shirone slightly ahead. At this rate, surpassing 700
points was guaranteed.
'Yes! He's holding his own against a serious Iruki. Keep pushing!'
In targeting, a single mistake could widen the gap drastically. But the Flash Shirone and
evasive Iruki missed nothing.
377 to 377.
At the 30-second mark, Iruki tied the score. Sensing danger, Shirone accelerated his
sequence formula. His Spirit Zone strengthened, and the flashes chained faster.
Shirone took a 4-point lead, making Iruki restless. Unlike his usual composed self, he
was now giving his all.
'And I'm still falling behind? My calculation speed…'
Just three days ago, Iruki's victory seemed certain. Improving sequence formulas in such
a short time was impossible.
'I can do this! My strategy's working!'
Shirone matched Iruki's speed by abandoning modular limits. Instead of bundling numbers
in 100s, he now processed them randomly—232, 187, 99, 276. It was a risky trade of stability for speed, but
his defensive mental endurance kept his focus steady.
469 to 432.
For the first time, Shirone pulled ahead significantly, electrifying the crowd. Even
Etella couldn't look away, gripped by the unpredictable tension.
Iruki finally deciphered Shirone's strategy.
'He abandoned modular limits? He doesn't care about the test score. He's gambling
everything to win.'
Iruki's lips curled. He had waited so long for this moment—a rival who could push him to
his limits. He gladly accepted the challenge.
'Shirone, my gamble is on another level.'
Iruki raised the difficulty of his sequence formula. The change was immediate, leaving
students bewildered. A secondary rhythm emerged amidst the explosions.
"Huh? What's that?"
542 to 558.
Iruki not only caught up but overtook Shirone. Achieving this required more than double
the speed.
"Senior Seriel! What's happening? Did Senior Shirone make a mistake…?"
"No. Shirone's score is still rising. It's more like Iruki's targeting suddenly got
faster."
"How's that possible? He jumped 100 points in an instant."
Seriel, scrutinizing Iruki's performance, finally realized and bit her lip.
"...He created another Spirit Zone."
The students turned to Iruki. Two afterimages were now flying at different angles.
Double Spirit Zone was a unique trait of Servant Syndrome, enabling mental division. It
wasn't a technique but an innate ability, impossible to learn.
But what truly shocked Seriel was that Iruki's speed kept increasing despite the
additional zone.
Performing two separate sequence formulas simultaneously—could the human mind handle such
calculations?
Iruki proved it possible. And he was doing it with logs, no less.
'As Amy said, he's a monstrous talent. No, beyond talent—a mutant outside human bounds.'
The scoreboard reflected Servant Syndrome's power.
692 to 845.
A gap of over 100 points, with Iruki several steps ahead. Once steps diverged, catching
up became impossible—the gap only widened.
'Faster. Even faster.'
Shirone grew desperate. His modular bundles now exceeded 300 numbers. The irregularity
made calculations more complex and chaotic.
732 to 911.
Shirone broke his personal record. But Iruki wasn't just surpassing his own—he was on
track to overtake Amy's. At this rate, it was inevitable.
Only one option remained for Shirone.
A realm unreachable by mere speed—only by leaping beyond.
But was it possible? Truly?
Again, no one answered. But Shirone felt as if he had already heard.
'Transcend numbers!'
