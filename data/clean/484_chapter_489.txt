Chapter 489:
Six hours before Shirone entered Araboth.
Ethella arrived at the deep forest of the First Heaven, Shamain, under a
special mission from Sain.
"Late. If they don't come..."
Gaold had requested an item from Frankwine, the leader of Yamang, in
exchange for granting Yamang free access to the Second Rebel Command, where Racis was the
commander.
The item Gaold sought through Yamang was an ancient artifact called the
Disk.
With the collapse of the spatial maze, the coordinates had also changed.
The existing Meta Gate had become useless, leading Gaold to plan extracting new coordinates from the
Akashic Records.
When parts change, the whole remains perfect. Therefore, new and precise
coordinates should now be recorded in the Akashic Records. The Disk, known as the artifact of Goffin,
could extract these coordinates and input them into a new Meta Gate.
After Goffin's erasure, no ruins of Goffin remained in Heaven. However,
some relics used there still spread throughout Purgatory.
"If we fail to secure the Disk, we'll be stuck in Heaven until humanity
intervenes."
Ethella, experienced in many hunts within Purgatory, knew how
challenging this mission would be. Despite this, Gaold entrusted it to Yamang, the most elite
organization in the Purgatory community. If they couldn't succeed, no one could.
"So, they sent a beauty to greet me. Gaold knows how to handle
business," a voice echoed from deep within the forest.
As Ethella turned her head, the shrubs parted like a door, and Frankwine
and Mitgun approached side by side.
Wasting no time, Ethella got straight to the point."Did you secure
the item?"
Mitgun pulled a palm-sized square tablet from his inner pocket and
handed it forward. Though she had no way to confirm its authenticity, the tablet was etched with
Henna-like markings and emitted a faint red glow.
"At this point, they wouldn't have a reason to fake the deal. I must get
this to Zebul quickly."
By now, Armin, Shina, and Kuan were likely heading toward Zebul's
Inglis. Delivering the Disk to them was the task assigned to Ethella by Sain.
"Thank you. Thanks to you, the mission will—"
As Ethella stepped toward Mitgun, she abruptly stopped, her heart
sinking as a sense of lethal intent pierced her chest.A gust of wind howled, and Frankwine's fist
narrowly grazed her temple. Staggered, she retreated without finding her balance.
"What is this...?"The moment Ethella met Frankwine's gaze, she
realized."Who are you?"
"Kekeke, who knows?"Frankwine's demeanor had shifted. Though she
had sensed his malevolence from their first meeting, this was far more pronounced—a clean, sharp malice,
so extreme it felt almost pure.
"How could a human...?"
Noticing a small dot moving in one of Frankwine's pupils, Ethella
adopted a combat stance.A fly.
The fly circled in his pupil as though trapped in a glass
orb."You're not human."
"Keke, consider yourself honored. I've chosen you as the signal flare of
my resurrection."
With a deafening roar, the ground beneath Frankwine exploded as he
charged at Ethella."A Schema?"
No, it was a different kind of physical ability. While Yamang's members
came from a world apart from Shirone's, their power operated under different principles. Mitgun
distorted the fields of time, space, and mind to unleash unique abilities, while Frankwine was
classified as a warrior focused solely on enhancing physical power.
Though straightforward, his strikes targeted only vital points, leaving
Ethella with no opportunity to counterattack and forcing her to focus solely on defense.
As she retreated down the forest slope, Frankwine closed the distance
with alarming speed. Ethella raised her guard, crossing her arms, only for Frankwine to seize her
wrists, pull them apart, and lean in with a sinister grin, revealing his grotesque smile and the fly
clinging to one pupil.
"Keke, what shall I do with our lovely guest?"
With a frown, Ethella kicked Frankwine in the groin. A crushing sound
accompanied his twitching eyebrow. The two froze in place for a long moment until Frankwine's head
slowly tilted downward, revealing a wet stain spreading between his legs.
"It's over."No matter how strong he was, a blow to the ultimate
weak point should have rendered him unconscious or dead. However, her assumption was shattered when
Frankwine's head gradually lifted again.
"Keke, rougher than I expected. I don't mind that."
Still stunned, Ethella barely noticed Frankwine had let go of her
wrists. As he twisted his body, his movement looked like a vortex. Too late, Ethella attempted to block,
but Frankwine's punch pierced her guard and slammed into her abdomen.
"Urgh!"Ethella's body rocketed backward, snapping thick trees
before crashing out of sight.
"Are you all right?" Mitgun asked, tilting his head. Though expressing
concern for Frankwine, the man he revered for his strength, was audacious, he couldn't suppress human
sympathy for such a blow.
"Who cares? Let it burst or not."Frankwine shook his drenched
groin, then turned."I'll head to Zebul. Finish this and join me. We've much to do to seize
Heaven."
Mitgun watched Frankwine leave, murmuring under his
breath,"Satan…"
Eventually, Frankwine arrived at the Second Command, only to find a
middle-aged man with a monocle and a cane waiting. Introducing himself as Satan, the man offered a
deal.
"Are you content with ruling Purgatory? Sell your soul to me, and I will
give you all of Heaven."
For Frankwine, who had sold anything for profit in his previous world, a
soul was a useless currency. Without hesitation, he accepted. Satan handed him a vial containing a fly,
which was fused with Frankwine through a Fuzix Machine at the command center.
Though it should have melted into him completely, the fly remained
visible in his pupil. Mitgun realized it wasn't a flaw—it was the core concept behind taking over
Heaven.
Following orders, Mitgun approached Ethella's collapsed form. Drawing a
dagger, he aimed for her neck."Don't resent me. Everyone dies someday."
Just as the blade descended, Ethella's hands shot up, gripping Mitgun's
wrist.
The Fifth Heaven, Matei.
Amid the immense city of titanic giants that could make one's legs
tremble just by looking, the rebels advanced fiercely.
Crewed aboard Titan No. 1, Crude destroyed the walls with ten flash
cannons, supported by numerous Kuroi units.
Penetrating Araboth through a siege was impossible. The key lay in
creating an alternate route to regroup the rebels, but the giant forces, realizing this, grew even more
violent in their assault against the rebels.
The giants currently engaged in battle with the rebels were formidable
warriors, 15 meters tall, who had reached the fourth stage of Ilhwa's Elixir. Even Kuroi, classified as
heavy equipment, were no more than mere toys before the giants' size. Their iron swords shredded
reinforced plates as though they were paper.
"This is too hard to break through. We can't go any deeper."
Having reached a decision, Crude spun the Titan sharply and began
advancing toward the wall.
"We'll carve a path here! All forces, follow me!"
The troops, having received the transmission, surged forward like a
massive torrent toward the wall.
"Damn it! Damn it!"
Crude gritted his teeth. The rebels fought with everything they had at
his command for one reason—to win. To win and return home. But Crude couldn't promise them a future. As
they neared Araboth, what awaited them was the end—countless lives and the infinite possibilities they
carried with them coming to an irrevocable close.
"Fluuuuuuu!"
Unable to suppress his swelling rage, Crude cried her name.A mage?
A cold-hearted rationalist? The anger at himself for once being captivated by her flooded in too
late.
She was a witch.A witch with neither blood nor tears.
Did Crude know? In the world where Fluu once lived, mages had long ago
been called witches.
"I won't forgive you! When this war is over…!"
Death didn't scare him. But whatever death awaited him, hers would
accompany it.
The massive mechanical army poured through the shattered walls into
Zebul, creating a spectacular sight.
The Sixth Heaven, Zebul.
Despite its title, City of Angels, no angels were to be seen wandering
Zebul. At the far end of the gateway leading to Araboth, the archangels Metiel and Satiel hovered in the
air, gazing over the battlefield.
Though the walls were collapsing and the rebels were flooding in, Zebul
currently lacked the forces to repel them.
The absence of angels.
This had been the greatest factor prolonging a war that should have been
easily quelled.
The two archangels, both beautiful with flowing golden hair, did not
look at each other. Their domains were Strong Force and Weak Force, fundamental concepts so vast they
underpinned all matter in the universe.
Thus, they were both two and one.
Unlike the relationship between Cariel and Uriel, representing creation
and destruction, Metiel and Satiel's nature kept them perpetually at odds.
"Arrogant humans…"
Satiel's face twisted in disgust.
Though Metiel's temperament wasn't particularly kind, Satiel was far
more brutal and merciless.
"I can't understand it. Why were the angels forbidden from acting? If we
lose this war, we might end up subordinated to humans."
"It seems to have something to do with Ikael. They banned the angels yet
suddenly reinstated her. And Ikael is deeply tied to Shirone."
"Hmph, Shirone? What's there to fear from a mere Nephilim?"
Metiel's gaze cooled. Satiel was practically her twin, having been with
her since their creation. She knew exactly when Satiel's cold nature had turned cruel.
"Is it jealousy?"
For the first time, Satiel turned to look at Metiel, her eyes trembling
with murderous intent.
"What?"
"Don't waver. It's already over. Goffin didn't choose you—he chose
Ikael…"
BOOOOOOOM!
Satiel's Judicial Halo erupted with a thunderous roar.
"Say that again, and I'll obliterate you on the spot."
Her wide-open eyes radiated fury, void of the sanctity an archangel
should possess.
