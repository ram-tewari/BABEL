Chapter 888:
Desperate Measures (2)
The special forces that had raided Tess's unit retreated before
achieving any significant results.
Two days later.
"Urgent report! Twenty million demon troops are advancing south in
the northwest area!"
"Report! Forty million from the northern mountains..."
The bad news kept pouring in.
Iruki watched the entire demon army move simultaneously on the 3D
strategy map.
"A full-scale war."
The raid two days ago was to confirm the enemy's response and design a
perfect movement plan.
They intended to swallow the central continent.
"So far, so good."
Even from Iruki's perspective, fighting with the resolve for mutual
destruction, it was crucial to draw the enemy in.
"The problem is time."
At least ten more days were needed to deploy the elemental bombs in
actual combat.
"Overdrive." Sparks flew in Iruki's eyes as his thoughts began
to unfold at a superhuman speed.
"This method buys us 12 hours." As numerous strategies were
combined and discarded, methods to buy time emerged.
"Up to 48 hours here..." Seeing blood dripping from Iruki's
nose, his assistant Aromi bit her lip.
"It's thinking beyond human logic."
Only Iruki could do it.
The durability of his brain would rapidly decline, but he didn't
care.
"Four days bought. But still far from enough."
He needed to find a way to prevent the demon army from reaching Tormia
for at least six more days.
"This is it! This buys five days! And then..."
Lost in thought, Iruki didn't realize he was now bleeding from his
eyes.
Ten minutes later, Iruki's eyes widened, and he collapsed, coughing up
blood.
"Commander!"
Aromi rushed in with a towel to wipe the blood, but Iruki stopped her
and stood up.
"Reorganize the strategy map."
As the commanders unfolded the new strategy map, Iruki moved the units
around frantically.
Everyone who saw the results he produced was shocked and
trembled.
"This method..."
Iruki stopped abruptly.
"This gets us seven days."
The commanders swallowed hard.
"The deployment you see now will be the exact military strength on
the seventh day. This is the limit with normal methods."
"Then with abnormal methods...?"
Iruki's face twisted in pain.
"The way to buy the remaining three days is very extreme. In fact,
you might have thought of it yourselves."
Everyone looked at the map again, and the faces of a few quick thinkers
turned pale.
"No way..."
Iruki moved the large army.
"Deploy all nearby forces to the Kanian Highlands. We can hold them
there for three days."
In other words...
"But Commander, the Kanian Highlands are unfavorable terrain for
humans. That's why we've avoided it until now..."
The commander who realized this closed his mouth.
"Yes. It will be a tough battle. The expected casualties are at
least 200,000. But if we can't hold them here, the seven days we've gained will be
meaningless."
If it was a strategy from Iruki's mind, it had to be the only
way.
"The hardest part is on the Commander."
Iruki added.
"I think it's a bad strategy. No one wants to fight an unfavorable
battle. But if we can't hold them here, humanity will be exterminated by the demons."
"How about deciding by vote?"
It wasn't a vote to oppose.
"No. There will be no vote. The fewer people responsible, the
better. That's what the Commander's position is for."
"But..."
It wasn't Iruki's fault.
If there had been a better way, he would have found it even if it meant
burning out his brain.
"Nothing changes. Just because the battle got a bit tougher doesn't
mean it's not a war. We'll devise the best strategy on the premise of buying three days."
Iruki finally wiped his face with a towel.
"There will be a detailed strategy meeting in 30 minutes. Please
inform each department."
Iruki needed rest.
As all the commanders left, Aromi, the only one remaining, spoke.
"I think we should vote."
Iruki just smiled.
"No one thinks it's a unilateral decision. Your thoughts are
humanity's thoughts. You don't have to bear it alone."
If they couldn't think like Iruki, they at least wanted to share the
responsibility.
"Sharing it doesn't lighten the burden. And... there will be
personal blame to bear."
"What do you mean?"
Iruki looked at the map.
"I'm sorry."
It was just a model of the terrain, but he could see the battles that
would take place there.
"I'll take all the responsibility. But..."
Iruki spoke heavily.
"Can I request one personnel transfer?"
The 2nd Corps' Magic Division.
The order from the Valkyrie Command was so urgent that Corps Commander
Garcia personally took charge.
All officers above battalion commander gathered, and Amy took a seat at
the end.
"For the next seven days, the 2nd Corps will march rapidly to the
Kanian Highlands. Minor skirmishes can be skipped."
The pens of the commanders taking notes suddenly stopped.
"Did you say the Kanian Highlands?"
"I just said that. Are you not paying attention? And you're a
brigade commander?"
"Sorry! But the Kanian Highlands are not under the 2nd Corps'
jurisdiction, and strategically..."
Seeing the murderous look in Garcia's eyes, the brigade commander closed
his mouth and straightened up.
A cold silence followed.
"What do you want to say?"
"Nothing! I'll correct it!"
Discipline was strict, but the same thought ran through the minds of the
officers.
"A desperate measure."
It was impossible to read the Command's intentions precisely on the
ground, but this much was clear.
"They need lives to throw in. A lot of them."
Amy wasn't afraid.
"Iruki wouldn't sacrifice our troops easily. On the contrary, it
means there's a chance for a turnaround."
A strategy for humanity to win had emerged.
"I know what you're thinking," Garcia said.
"A soldier doesn't only fight winning wars. That's a mercenary. For
a soldier, life is just another combat power. We're not going to die. We're going to fight. If anyone
can't accept this, speak now."
No one raised their hand.
"Deliver the orders to each unit. Form a large formation right
after lunch. Don't miss it. It's a rapid march."
"Yes!"
As the officers left in order, Garcia looked at Amy.
"6th Battalion Commander of the Magic Division."
"Yes! Lieutenant Colonel Karmis Amy!"
"Stay for a moment."
Even as a mentor, it was unusual to request a private meeting in the
military conference room.
"Understood!"
After everyone left, Amy turned her head.
"Do you have something to say?"
"Well, there's been a request for your transfer from the
higher-ups. Pack your things when you return to the battalion."
"What? A transfer?"
Garcia nodded.
"As of now, you're no longer with the Magic Division but with the
Tormia Capital Defense Command. A teleportation magic circle has been authorized, so jump to
Bashka."
"Tormia?"
Blinking for a moment, Amy slammed the table in a surge of shame.
"What are you saying!"
It wasn't behavior she should show in front of the Corps Commander, but
Garcia understood her.
"Why am I the only one being transferred to the rear when all units
are going on dangerous missions? What's the reason?"
"The higher-ups said this: They highly value Karmis Amy's ability
to train troops..."
"Master!"
Amy shouted.
Garcia spoke in a lower voice than before.
"Someone high up in the Holy War doesn't want you to
die."
Amy thought of one person.
"Iruki!"
She gritted her teeth.
"I can't accept this! Pulling troops to the rear like this is
favoritism and overreach!"
"It could be, or it might not be." Garcia turned his hand
over.
"It doesn't matter. You're a talented person. It's a reasonable
decision not to want to waste you."
"But why me! There are plenty of people more capable than me in the
2nd Corps!"
"Telling me won't change anything... You know the
answer."
"I refuse! I won't leave! I'll lead the 6th Battalion and
march!"
"I won't allow it." Garcia spoke coldly.
"Orders don't distinguish between life and death. Just as you
follow orders to die, this is the same. A battalion commander can't defy the command
structure."
"Master. Are you telling me to be a coward? What do I tell my
subordinates? That I'll run away while they charge into the fray?"
"That's also your burden to bear. What I want to tell you is to
return to Bashka quickly. That's an order."
Amy, trembling with clenched fists, quickly turned around.
"I'll come back."
Garcia said nothing.
As the troops ate lunch, Amy came out with a backpack slung over her
shoulder.
She didn't show it outwardly, but their gazes hurt more than
arrows.
The subordinates who had put down their utensils saluted.
"Are you leaving now?"
"I'm not leaving, just going to Tormia for a bit. I'll be back
soon, so take care of the unit."
The subordinates smiled, but no one believed it was true.
"Don't worry. We've learned from you, so we'll manage
well."
They said this because they heard she was transferred to the Capital
Defense Command as a training officer.
"Alright. I'm counting on you."
Wanting to show through actions rather than words, Amy didn't make
excuses and turned away.
As she left the unit, she heard the soldiers' voices.
"Did you hear? She's the only one from the corps transferred to the
rear. And as a training officer."
"Ugh! Some go to the front lines, while others get to hang out with
young guys in a comfortable place. That's why you need a strong background."
It was now an open secret that one of the Five Great Stars of the Ivory
Tower was Amy's lover.
"Honestly, I get it. You know how cruel the demons are, right? And
the chances of winning are almost zero. Would you want to send your lover into that?"
"Who cares? If that's the case, they shouldn't have joined the
battlefield in the first place. This back and forth just lowers morale."
Listening to their words, Amy's face turned uncontrollably red.
"Iruki, you jerk!"
The reason Amy was leaving the unit so obediently wasn't just because
she was ordered to.
"I really hope it's not you."
It wasn't certain that it was Iruki.
"But if it is you, you'd better have a damn good reason to convince
me. If not..."
Amy's face flushed with anger.
"You'll find out exactly what insubordination means."
The rapid march was set for seven days.
To rejoin her unit, Amy quickly ran towards the teleportation magic
circle.
