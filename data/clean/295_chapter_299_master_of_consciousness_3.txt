Chapter 299:
Master of Consciousness (3)
"Do not threaten our peace!"
The speed of the massive axe descending towards her head was almost
impossible to follow with the eyes.
In that fleeting moment, Amy realized this was her end.
'Goodbye, Shirone...'
It was a bad ending.
But in a way, she thought it might not be a bad conclusion. This was
Shirone's mental world. She would sleep forever in his embrace.
"Raaaargh!"
A ferocious roar snapped Amy back to reality. At the same time,
something dark and fast moved past her eyes.
When she came to her senses, Ignite had been thrown 10 meters
away.
Amy felt a presence behind her and turned around.
But to see it properly, she had to tilt her head all the way
back.
It was an Egoist, covered in spiky protrusions all over its body. It
stood over 6 meters tall, in a hunched posture that was somewhere between quadrupedal and bipedal,
glaring forward.
The Egoist manifested in a specific form, borrowing the avatar's desire.
The process wasn't finished yet, and all the Egoists in the plaza turned into black smoke and were
absorbed into it. Although it didn't grow taller, the spikes on its body became abnormally long.
"Raaaargh!"
Egoist - Rage Form.
The giant shadowy monster roared and leaped over Amy.
With the obstruction gone, she saw Shirone with a fierce
expression.
'Shirone...?'
Shirone had a stronger inclination towards reason than desire. But the
current Egoist was exuding a strength on a different level than anything she had experienced
before.
'Could it be that he's trying to save me?'
Knowing the hostility Shirone's avatar held towards her, Amy avoided
jumping to conclusions.
But she couldn't help but feel a swelling hope. It had always been this
way. Whenever danger struck, Shirone always protected her.
The Egoist (Rage Form) tore through hundreds of antitheses and still had
energy to spare. Even Ignite's heavily armed subordinates were sent flying with a single kick.
"Shirone! Do you still dream of a petty life to the
end?"
Ignite, coughing up blood, grabbed his massive axe and charged at the
monster. He leaped high and swung the axe behind him.
Bending his back like a bow, he sprang forward and brought the axe down.
The Egoist's head was split in half.
A smile formed on Ignite's lips, but the expected scream didn't come.
The state of the Egoist transmitted through the handle was still angry, bubbling, and about to
explode.
As Ignite's expression hardened, the Rage Form's claw swiped across his
face.
With half his face gone, he fell to the ground, and a leg thicker than a
pillar stomped on him.
Boom!
The ground shook, signaling the end of the battle.
"Ahhh! R-run away!"
The antitheses scattered in all directions in panic. The Egoist roared
and chased after them, trying to eliminate even one more.
The plaza, now empty except for Shirone's party, was clean without a
single corpse.
Amy, who had been on the brink of death, collapsed to the ground.
Shirone approached her.
Turning her head, Amy's heart raced as she realized Shirone was there.
Was his anger gone? Had he returned to the kind Shirone of before?
"Shirone, thank you. If it weren't for you..."
"You make me angry."
Shirone spoke without even making eye contact, looking at the
ground.
"You really make me angry."
Then he turned coldly and walked away.
Even now, he couldn't understand why he had saved Amy.
Just before the massive axe split her head, he was seized by an intense
impulse. From that moment, his rage exploded, and he couldn't remember anything else.
Amy hung her head in dejection.
Reina, who had hoped for their reconciliation, patted her
shoulder.
"Don't be too disappointed. Once we save Shirone, all
misunderstandings will..."
Before Reina could finish, Amy lifted her head.
Contrary to expectations, her face was bright. She even looked somewhat
relieved.
"It's okay. Shirone saved me, after all. Maybe he doesn't hate me
to the core. That's enough for me. Haha!"
Even though she said that, Reina could guess how great Amy's sense of
loss was.
She was holding on desperately, solely to save Shirone.
"Yeah, let's keep going a little longer."
Reina looked at Armin. He was drenched in sweat from disrupting the
enemy deep within the antithesis's camp.
"Are the antitheses gone now?"
"They're not gone. But the manipulated antitheses are no more.
Still, we can't let our guard down. They are fundamentally mental entities that criticize
Shirone."
After a short rest in the plaza, they followed Shirone's lead into the
castle. Although Ignite had been there just a while ago, no projections were visible anymore.
Shirone descended into the basement. He walked through the maze-like
paths without a single hesitation.
When he opened the door to the storeroom, another staircase leading down
appeared.
On the first basement floor, Armin grabbed a torch and walked alongside
Shirone. At an old wooden door, he stopped the group and turned around.
"Beyond this door is the world of dreams, the REM realm. Before we
enter, there are a few things to note. You've all dreamed before, but different physical laws apply here
than in reality. Simply put, it's called focus."
"Is it like concentration?"
"Similar. Reality is a series of concentration and relaxation. In
an ordinary life, the relaxation phase is overwhelmingly long. But in dreams, there's only
concentration. It's like following the dramatic structure of a play. You only see what you want to see,
and other situations don't exist. So if you lose focus, you lose yourself. This is called
'rilpin.'"
Armin held up five fingers.
"There are five main types of rilpin. First, a sense of floating.
Second, meaningless mumbling. Third, repetition of the same segment. Fourth, perspective shift. Fifth,
the realization of anxiety."
Amy thought she understood. She had often experienced these things while
dreaming.
"Dreams only mimic gravity; they don't actually have it. Keep
confirming the feeling of your feet being on the ground. The moment you feel a sense of floating, you'll
keep rising endlessly. And don't speak without certainty."
"Conversations need focus too, like a script in a
play."
"Exactly. Habitual slang is also not allowed. Losing focus in
language will make you repeat meaningless words."
"So the repetition of the same segment is spatial
focus?"
"Yes. Don't turn around or run away without thinking. You'll lose
focus and repeat the same segment. Fourth, if you focus on the surrounding situation, you'll lose
yourself and become an observer. Lastly, don't have anxious thoughts."
"Because they'll become reality."
Armin nodded at Reina's words.
Amy, deep in thought, asked,
"What happens if we get caught in rilpin? For example, if we start
floating or can't stop mumbling meaningless words?"
"As long as we don't all get caught at the same time, we can pull
each other out. If you start floating, we'll pull you down. If you start mumbling, we might slap
you."
Amy puffed out her cheeks as if she had already been slapped.
Reina asked,
"What if we can't get out?"
"Eventually, you'll catch a new focus. But it won't be Shirone's
focus; it will be ours. In the end, you'll be dreaming a new dream."
"A dream within a dream."
"Exactly. This is why Drimo is called a multidimensional world.
It's fine in your own dream, but this is someone else's world. If you get deeply buried, you might never
wake up from the dream."
Everyone tensed up after hearing Armin's explanation.
"Don't worry. As long as we focus on following Shirone to the next
exit, there shouldn't be any problems."
Shirone took a deep breath and grabbed the doorknob.
"Then... I'll open the door."
With Armin's approval, the door slowly opened.
Music softly played, and soon the performance of court musicians filled
the air.
Amy squinted at the familiar scene.
"This place...?"
Many couples were dancing in the grand hall. It was the ballroom of
Kazra Castle, where they had been just a few hours ago.
Armin hurriedly explained, as if he had missed something.
"The REM realm is a fluid space. It's like multiple plays being
performed on the same stage. If the background is the grand hall, the exit should be close."
"That's the exit."
Shirone pointed to the other side of the ballroom.
Amy thought she understood why. It was the door she had exited with
Zion. Shirone must have gone through that door to find her. And then all this happened.
'It was because of me.'
Amy smiled bitterly.
If she hadn't followed Zion, if she had refused his request, would
Shirone be in a different situation now?
'Haha, no wonder he hates me.'
"Amy, don't lose focus."
Amy quickly regained her composure. She took deep breaths and maintained
a clear gaze.
"Yes, I'm ready."
"Then let's go. Shirone, walk straight ahead without bumping into
anyone."
Shirone walked carefully. He stopped and waited when couples passed by.
Taking a detour could lead to rilpin.
Amy and Reina only looked at the back of Shirone's head. Meanwhile,
Armin walked leisurely, observing the surroundings. He had obtained a lucid dream certification for a
certain mission, making this possible.
'What exactly happened?'
Given that Shirone in reality was facing a horrific death, the REM
realm's situation was likely a nightmare.
This made him tense.
At any moment, everyone in the ballroom could turn into monsters, or
people might suddenly go mad and start killing each other.
However, nothing happened as they crossed half of the ballroom.
Since the focus had already shifted from the path they had taken, it was
unlikely that anything would happen there.
'Strange. What kind of nightmare is this?'
Armin noticed something and his eyes lit up.
'So, that's what it is.'
In the center of the ballroom, a couple was dancing. The woman wore a
beautiful red dress like a rose, and the man had a strange appearance.
The man's eyes were completely slanted upwards, his nose was upturned,
and his lips were smaller than a chicken's anus. It was a human face, but one that couldn't exist in
reality.
'He must have really hated him.'
If it were just this, it wouldn't be a big deal for a third party. But
for Shirone, it was a horrific nightmare.
Armin turned his gaze and saw someone serving near a long table.
He was dressed in the attire of a steppe tribe, which didn't match the
castle's scenery at all. He wore a red hat, had a stylishly long beard, and was confidently smoking a
pipe even in a non-smoking area.
The only entity not a projection of Shirone, he was Luber, a dreamer
dispatched from Drimo.
Luber looked at Armin and winked. But Armin just glanced at him
indifferently and turned away.
He wasn't someone to greet out of mere curiosity. Currently, dreamers
couldn't be analyzed by human intelligence. They were acknowledged simply because they existed.
When Shirone reached the exit, the focus on the path they had taken
ended, and the music stopped.
Was the ball still going on in the grand hall? They were curious, but no
one looked back.
