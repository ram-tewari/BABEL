The Supreme Court of Theraphos.
Though the Chief Justices had departed, a few judges remained who
refused to back down.
"This is too hasty! The battle between good and evil isn't over yet!
Doesn't the last light still remain?"
The reason Theraphos had rushed to confirm evil's victory was because of
the fate of the universe.
"If we waste time here, the universe really will close! Judges, is that

"It hasn't happened yet. The arbiters of the universe must judge based

"Then look at the present. What is left for humanity? No user can

Only suffering remains.
"Objection! I formally appeal!"
"There's no time! All enforcers, carry out the Supreme Court's final
ruling! Any who resist will be detained!"
The doors of the court burst open, and enforcers equipped with plasma

"Judge! Are you really doing this?!"
A formal appeal against the Supreme Court's ruling—this was
unprecedented even in the history of Theraphos.
"...There's no other way, is there?"
Even if they possessed standards beyond human comprehension, they had
not reached the 11th sense.
"We must follow the grand principles of Theraphos, set by the Creator.
We are here to restore balance."
They, too, were creations of the gods.
As silence fell, the enforcers surrounded the opposing judges.
"Detain them."
The Prison of Balance—a space that isolates anything that defies the
balance of the universe. All of them vanished.

"Haa… Haa…"
Cutting through the hazy curtain, a woman with her nose and mouth
wrapped in cloth appeared.
It was Miro, the last guardian of light.
"Please save me!"

ran toward her.
A survivor.
Though 99% of humanity had perished, some had still remained on the
surface.
Not many, certainly.
"Hehehe! Get her!"
A wagon drawn by skeletal horses, carrying demons, was hunting the
girl.

"Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara."

"Huh?"

"Lightning Strike."

ground.

"Ahh…"
"Are you okay?"
Whether the one before her was enemy or ally, dream or reality, there

"Water… Can I have water…"
Miro conjured water with magic.

"Waaaah! Waaaah!"

"What now?"
"Bearer of Light."

flickering, transparent silhouette.
"A Chief Justice?"

"Hiiiek!"

"What's going on?"

unfair.
"This was your decision? Aren't you the Chief Justice?"
"I issue the sentence, but the judgment follows the three principles of

"You couldn't stop it?"
"I could've stalled. But I judged this to be the better choice than

Had he fought in court, the Chief Justice himself might've been

"Quick judgment is fine—but why?"

"There's merit to it."
"Although the odds aren't high. In fact, if they fail, the universe will

"And yet you're betting on us?"
"Ultima."

"Theraphos is superior to humanity, but we never reached Ultima. And

Miro pondered those words.
"What do you want me to do?"
"Endure."

"Bearer of Light, you cannot defeat evil alone. Good and evil, emptiness

"Winning is also evil's methodology. When good and evil, emptiness  and

Far in the distance, the Theraphos warship approached.
"Never forget—you are the Bearer of Light. If you fail to maintain that

A Theraphos enforcer dropped its stealth and approached.
"Let's go."
"…Remember. Good exists to oppose evil, not to hate it."
