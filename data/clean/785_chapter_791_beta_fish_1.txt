Chapter 791:
Beta Fish (1)
The Aracne National Intelligence Service submitted the documents.
Apparently, she was now leading a small mercenary guild in the
commercial town of Timaros, near the capital, Bengol.
"The Jaestin Mercenary Guild, huh…"
She had abandoned her surname but kept her given name. The guild had
barely ten members.
It was an ordinary life on the surface, but Shirone could imagine how
much she must have given up to find peace.
"Where is Timaros?"
Swallowing hard, Paronica answered.
"There's a small town 14 kilometers east of Bengol. I'll grant you
a clearance pass right away."
"Thank you for your cooperation. As for the terrorist threat—we'll
address that once the immediate fire is put out."
Only after Shirone left the grand hall with the documents did Paronica
let out a breath.
"Phew... what a terrifying man."
The steward bowed his head, his face pale.
"My apologies, Your Majesty. I even offered up Miss Aracne, and he
still tossed it aside like it meant nothing…"
"No, this is enough. Now that we mentioned 'terrorism,' he won't
just stand by and watch."
Still, the pressure Shirone exuded was strong enough to trample the
prestige of the Seven Kings.
'Is this the gaze of someone who bears the weight of the world?'
When they first heard Shirone would be coming as a Star from the Ivory
Tower, they had celebrated.
"But now…"
They had completely changed their minds.
"We did pretty well to get this much from one of the Five Stars,
didn't we?"
The steward nodded silently.
Shirone soared straight up from the palace and flew toward the small
town on the edge of the horizon.
As he reached the arch at the town's entrance, the gate captain, who had
already received word, saluted with a booming voice.
"Welcome, sir! We've been ordered to provide any support you need.
Do you require anything?"
"I'll just take a look around."
He wanted to handle things quietly.
"It is Timaros' honor. Please speak to any nearby guard station if
you need assistance."
Nodding, Shirone passed under the arch and saw rows of shops lining the
unpaved roads.
'Must be because of the Miss World Pageant…'
There were more foreign visitors than locals, and the market buzzed with
a festive atmosphere.
As he pushed through the bustling crowd and entered the guild district,
things became noticeably quieter.
Passing smithies, alchemy shops, potion and healing guilds, he finally
reached his destination.
Jaestin Mercenary Guild.
A building that clearly didn't see much work—small towns didn't need
more than a guard force to maintain order.
"Excuse me."
A few empty tables were sparsely arranged inside, and the front counter
was unmanned.
At a corner table, a group of people playing cards with a few bottles of
alcohol looked up.
"Who the hell are you?"
The one who spoke was a one-eyed man with slurred speech—clearly
drunk.
'Must not get many visitors…'
After a moment's thought, Shirone asked,
"I'm looking for someone. Can you help me?"
A plump woman at the far end of the table suddenly stood and
approached.
"Oh! A customer! Come on over here."
She stepped behind the counter and dropped a guest book onto the table,
kicking up a cloud of dust.
"Finding people is our specialty. Who are you looking
for?"
"Amery Jaestin."
The blood drained from the woman's face, and the card players froze
mid-movement.
She asked again.
"Sorry… who did you say?"
"Amery Jaestin. I heard the leader of this guild goes by the name
Jaestin."
The one-eyed man muttered,
"Close the door."
With a loud bang, the door shut, and a black-haired man—who had appeared
out of nowhere—drew a blade and stood guard at the entrance.
'He's strong.'
Shirone hadn't failed to sense him, but even compared to the Silver Ring
Guild, these mercs were top-tier.
"You better speak clearly, brat."
The one-eyed man aimed a magic-charged gun at Shirone.
"Who told you that surname—Amery?"
From that, Shirone could tell Jaestin had shared her past with these
mercenaries.
'So they're bound by deep trust… I'll have to be careful with my
words.'
Shirone raised his hands.
"I'm not here to fight. I came to ask Jaestin for help. Think of it
as a request, if you like."
"I warned you."
The gun clicked, and two others drew their swords, their blades
gleaming.
"Speak clearly."
At the same time, the gun fired with a magical burst, and the
black-haired man charged in with a dagger.
"Tch…!"
Shirone dodged the magic bullet via teleportation just before it
exploded against the wall.
"Careful! He's a mage!"
"A mage?!"
The swordsmen rushed from both sides, slashing in an X pattern—only to
strike empty air.
"I said I didn't come to fight!"
His voice was drowned by another explosion. Walls shattered.
'This is your own guild, you know…'
Either they didn't care—or they deemed the threat serious enough to risk
it.
"Kill him! I'll take responsibility!"
The woman shouted as the black-haired man advanced with a reverse-grip
dagger.
'He's definitely the strongest one here.'
Realizing he couldn't reason with them, Shirone summoned Shining Chain
and bound the man's body.
"Gah! What is this—?! Aaargh!"
As he was hoisted into the air, the chain extended and swept through the
guild, entangling everyone else.
"Let go! Let us go, damn you!"
He pinned the swordsmen by their ankles and wrapped the one-eyed man's
arms tightly behind his back.
"Phew…"
As Shirone caught his breath, the woman who had hidden behind the
counter pulled out a blowpipe and fired a poisoned dart.
Fwip!
Laced with deadly venom, the dart zipped toward him—but slowed in
Shirone's eyes under his Slow spell.
"......"
He casually caught the dart between his thumb and forefinger mid-air.
The mercenaries' eyes widened.
"He… he caught it with his fingers."
In their experience, catching a dart—even under a Slow spell—was
impossible.
'They say geniuses spend their lives trying to delay time by even
1%...'
Only someone like Shirone, a photon-type prodigy from Alpheas, could
make time feel this dreamlike.
"Just… who are you?"
The one-eyed man glanced sideways—his cue for the woman to load another
dart.
"Aaah!"
The Shining Chain wrapped around her and yanked her upside-down,
suspending her from the ceiling.
"Fine, I get it! I'm Amery Jaestin! So just let them go and let's
talk!"
If it was true, he still needed confirmation.
"Can you show me the scar?"
All the mercs tensed.
'He knows everything.'
Just then, the back door burst open, and a woman walked in, her hair
damp as if she'd just washed up.
"You idiots! Did you lose the deed to the place or something? What
the hell is all this—"
She had a hardened look, forged by life's battles, and slung a
sniper-modified rifle over her shoulder.
A shooting range could be seen behind her.
"What is this ridiculous scene?"
Her eyes scanned the chaos until they landed on Shirone.
"Are you the one stringing up my people?"
The one-eyed man shouted,
"Boss! Run! He knows your surname!"
Her brow twitched—but as the person at the center of the situation,
Jaestin didn't overreact.
"Let them go."
But Shirone wouldn't, not while the air still brimmed with hostile
intent.
"Are you Amery Jaestin?"
"Yeah. If you've got something to say, say it to me."
If this is also a trap, like the woman at the counter, the best way to
confirm it was with his own eyes.
"Show me the scar on your side."
The one-eyed man thrashed about.
"Captain! Run, damn it! Get this off me! I swear I'll kill you when I
get out—Aaargh!"
As Shirone tightened the Shining Chain, the one-eyed man screamed, and
everyone linked by the chain grimaced in pain.
"Grrrhhh!"
Jaestin shouted.
"Stop!"
She grabbed the hem of her shirt and pulled it all the way up to her
neck, her chest jostling as bare skin was revealed.
"Happy now? Now let my people go."
"C-Captain…"
The men looked away in embarrassment, but Shirone stared intently at her
ribs.
It was there.
A scar ran down her side, etched sharply along her ribcage—clearly once
a fatal wound.
"Want me to show you what's below too?"
Shirone released the Shining Chain.
"Ugh!"
The woman dropped from the ceiling, and the others collapsed to their
knees, groaning from the pain.
"Pathetic…"
Jaestin clicked her tongue, though she couldn't hide the concern in her
eyes.
A strong person, Shirone thought.
He could sense the spirit of a soldier who had crossed the line of
death.
"Yeah, I'm Amery Jaestin. So what now? You gonna kill me?"
"I told you, I'm not here to fight. I have no intention of hurting
you."
"Is that so?"
She didn't seem particularly moved.
"Fine, let's hear it. You want to talk one-on-one, right? I know just
the place."
It was the shooting range she practically lived in.
"Captain! We'll go with you—ugh!"
The one-eyed man tried to get up but collapsed again, wincing at the
pain in his bones.
"Just focus on healing. Honestly, such drama queens…"
After Jaestin and Shirone slipped out the back door, the woman at the
counter brought out some ointment.
"Put this on. Your skin's torn up."
While applying the ointment, the one-eyed man glanced at the
chain-shaped marks on his skin and frowned.
"Damn it. I'll have to wear long sleeves. Can't go out looking like
this—people'll think I'm into weird stuff."
One of the swordsmen looked toward the back door.
"Will she be okay? That guy's no joke. Shouldn't we follow?"
"Hmph, no joke?"
The black-haired man spoke up.
"You think someone who could take me down is just 'no joke'? Though, I
suppose you guys could manage it."
The one-eyed man bared his teeth.
"What's that supposed to mean?! I'm already pissed and you're adding
fuel to the fire."
"If he meant to kill us, he would've done it already."
Everyone fell silent.
"If he had evil intent, we wouldn't be here right now. That's why the
Captain went with him. Just trust her."
"……"
The one-eyed man blew gently on his shoulder, where he'd applied the
ointment.
"No scars, right…?"
—
A magic round boomed.
"Next!"
Every time a new target was set up halfway up the mountain, her sniper
rifle recoiled with perfect precision.
"Next!"
Shirone watched Jaestin train, deep in thought.
What exactly is she shooting at?
This level of marksmanship was near miraculous—something only possible
through relentless daily practice.
She's still fighting. Even now.
Finally, Jaestin broke the silence.
"If you're not a hitman, then why are you here?"
"I've come to ask a favor."
"A favor? From me?"
She pulled the bolt back, ejecting a shell from the side of her sniper
rifle with a metallic click.
"Please kill Havitz."
Her breath cut off. Then, like a tsunami crashing through, her shoulders
began to tremble.
"N-Next!"
She fired the next shot as soon as the target popped up, but the magic
round exploded somewhere completely off-target.
"Havitz…"
Terror swirled violently in Jaestin's eyes.
