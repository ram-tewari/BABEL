by the Parrot Mercenary Corps.

unknown uses.
It was the future from the past.
'It will take time to restore the memory chip.'
It had been buried for a long time, and the hardware itself was severely
damaged.
Fermi set the chip aside.
'Carefully, carefully.'
Complete restoration was difficult, but even a single word could be
invaluable.
Using tweezers, Fermi picked up a memo and gently held it under the desk
lamp.
Someone's diary.
"Let's see."
Or perhaps the last desperate record written before death.
"New Humanity?"
Fermi noted the term in his mind and unfolded the twice-folded paper
with the tweezers.
It was an official document, half torn as if it had been destroyed, with
Eastern characters written on it.
"Politicians, always the same…"
Even in the face of the world's end, their dedication to not sharing
information with other countries was unwavering.
He deciphered the text with flexible thinking.
'The second beast. The number of the beast 666. The three-stage filter
reaching the end. The potential rampage of Argones.'
Setting the document down, Fermi rested his chin on his hand.
'As expected, the end is unavoidable. Humanity, having reached the
singularity, cannot ignore the outside world.'
This singularity is divided into three-stage filters.
'The first stage is awareness of the outside world. The second stage is
contact with evidence of the outside world. And the third stage is… escaping the photon system.'
According to Fermi's analysis, humanity was just passing through the
second stage filter.
'The beginning of this is the New Humanity.'
More excavation would be needed for detailed information, but it was
clear it was related to the outside world.
'When the third stage is reached, the world will end somehow. But
"somehow" is a bit…'
In his thoughts, the end was as difficult as integrating humanity into
Ultima.
'It's very hard for any event to reach an extreme. Moreover…'
Hadn't Goffin already escaped the photon system?
"Hmm."
Fermi tapped the tweezers on the desk.
'Just because one escapes the photon system doesn't mean they enter the
outside world.'
He wanted to fill the gaps in his reasoning.
'Or… the concept of the outside world might be entirely different from
what Goffin or we think.'
One cannot guard against the unknown.
'A perfect end? Transcending causality? Does this world allow such a
system?'
Even when Nane tried to close the world, wasn't Shirone there to defend
it?
'That's the rule. In a world where extremes exist, there must be
corresponding extremes.'
Therefore, the end is something that transcends the world.
'It's unimaginable. Beyond the brain's function, it would be something
our brains can't process.'
What form would it take?
"The end of numbers."
Whatever unimaginable form it takes, it would ultimately come down to
numbers.

on one of the memos.
'The second beast.'
