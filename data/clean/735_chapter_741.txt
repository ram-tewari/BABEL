Chapter 741:
The Ceremony of Stars
Unlike the residents who spend their entire lives in the ivory tower,
those who go out into the world to spread their teachings are called stars.
Their authority is not given by someone else but is self-asserted, so no
grand procedures are necessary.
However, being recognized for their intellectual value by the
incarnation of a star would be an unfamiliar feeling for anyone born on this land.
"Shirone, I award you the highest five stars. Become one of the
Five Great Stars and protect this land."
As Great Star's voice gently echoed, a plaque appeared before Shirone's
eyes.
"This is..."
Only those who have received the title of a star can possess this
planet's high pass.
"Take it. It is a heavy burden entrusted to you as the master of
this land."
'The master of this land...'
There are many kings and nobles with territories in the world, but they
are not the true masters either.
"This plaque represents me. All who dwell on this land are my
children, and they will gladly assist you."
What the powerful fear is the military might of the ivory tower, but at
least the meaning was appreciated.
"Yes. Thank you."
The plaque engraved with five stars had a texture that seemed to mix
iron and wood, a sensation Shirone had never felt before.
"It's made from star fragments. Just carrying it will have
significant effects."
"Star fragments..."
If he knew the principle, he could summon it with material.
"What effects does it have?"
"I've heard it promotes blood circulation and is good for
vitality."
Shirone hung the plaque around his neck.
"Is it over now?"
"Usually, yes. But as one of the Five Great Stars, there are things
you need to know. Of course, there are special privileges too."
'Privileges...'
What could be given beyond the power that makes even a king
tremble?
"First, most stars have satellites. If you want, I can help you.
What do you think, Shirone?"
"Hmm, a satellite..."
Rian came to mind first, followed by a few others.
"I don't think I need one."
As if expecting this, Great Star nodded.
"I see. In fact, none of the Five Great Stars have satellites. It's
peculiar to consider it a coincidence."
Satellites orbit the star, providing mutual support and direction as
they orbit the sun together.
"Each star has a firm philosophy. So, they don't choose satellites
based solely on ability. Especially the Five Great Stars..."
"Yes. What I aim to achieve cannot be obtained through compromise
with someone."
It is a task that must be carried through to the end, despite numerous
oppositions at the pinnacle of intellect.
Such is the world of the stars of the ivory tower.
"That's why stars without satellites sometimes request weapons or
means of transportation. In some cases, they even search the outer world for a satellite."
"The outer world..."
Arthe's satellite, Tokei, came to mind.
"So, Shirone, why don't you ask me for something? I can provide
whatever you want."
Even the mental world, the source of objects, was under the control of
the four-star resident, Luber, so it wasn't a bluff.
"I'm not sure. Something I absolutely need..."
With the ability to use material, there was nothing he absolutely had to
have.
"I didn't explain well. The type isn't important. What's important
is registering it with the ivory tower."
Shirone, coming out of his thoughts, raised his head.
"Register it?"
"As you know, stars can register one other entity with the ivory
tower besides themselves. Usually, it's a satellite, but it can also be an object."
Great Star showed the plaque carried by the satellites.
"Having a star's plaque means receiving the absolute support of the
ivory tower's residents. For example, if a dagger that anyone can use is registered as a star's
item..."
The plaque transformed into a dagger, with a mark orbiting the star
engraved on the handle.
"If the owner loses the dagger and someone picks it up and kills
someone, and if that murderer is about to be punished by the kingdom..."
The mark on the dagger shone brightly.
"The residents of the ivory tower will do everything in their power
to erase that kingdom from the map."
Shirone swallowed hard.
"The residents of the ivory tower absolutely trust the stars. Even
if someone is killed with a lost sword, they believe there is some inevitable reason unknown to the
perpetrators."
Therefore, not just anyone can become a star.
"Nothing is completely wrong or completely right. We are always the
ones who define it. Sometimes, compromise isn't everything. Sometimes, the only way is to carry it
through."
If humanity had tried to solve all problems through compromise, they
would have perished long before Nane.
"Registering the title of a star is the minimum problem-solving
ability the ivory tower can have. In other words..."
Great Star extended the handle of the dagger.
"Even if it's wrong, the star's judgment is always
right."
"Ah..."
As Shirone grasped the handle, the dagger turned bluish and scattered
into beautiful fragments.
"Choose carefully. It can be a person, an object, or an animal. You
can register one more thing with the ivory tower besides yourself."
"Yes, I'll keep that in mind."
Satisfied that the message was properly conveyed, Great Star clapped her
hands.
"That's the basic right of a star. And the Five Great Stars receive
a special gift."
"I'll gratefully accept it."
He wasn't particularly materialistic, but since he had become one of the
Five Great Stars, he should accept what was given.
"What kind of gift?"
Great Star asked playfully, but it was a difficult question to answer
when he actually thought about it.
"Haha, who knows? Maybe a souvenir?"
It seemed fitting for the Five Great Stars, but Great Star shook his
head and pointed to the sky.
"I'll give you a star."
"...Yes. What?"
Shirone doubted his ears.
"What did you say?"
"Actually, all I have are stars. Everything else was achieved by
the residents of the ivory tower."
In his own way, it made sense, but he couldn't even guess what it meant
to own a star.
"If I own a star... does it really become mine?"
"Of course. I govern all the stars in this world, and I'm giving
you one of them."
As Great Star swung her arms wide, the glass walls of the Great Temple
brightened, reflecting the view of the universe.
"Wow..."
This time, even Shirone was a little impressed.
"Stars, especially the Five Great Stars, have a lot to think about
as leaders of stars. Sometimes, I think they need a space to contemplate alone, away from the human
world."
"But, still, on another star..."
"A star without a name is lonely."
Shirone closed his mouth.
"The universe is surprisingly empty. Not all stars fulfill their
purpose. Many stars exist just to fill probabilities. I started this because I thought it would be nice
if even one person gave a star a name."
It was a thought only the incarnation of a star could have.
"I see. I'll gratefully accept it."
Although he called it a privilege, Great Star seemed more excited as she
clapped her hands and showed the candidates for stars.
"Great! Then I'll pick a few stars where people can live. Choose a
star you like and name it."
The countless stars spread across the ceiling of the Great Temple rushed
in at the speed of light, soon illuminating a giant planet.
'Beautiful.'
It was entirely blue, with countless islands scattered across the vast
sea.
"The average temperature is 28 degrees. It's a planet similar to
the tropical climate of this planet. There are many islands, making it perfect for a resort. Although
there are no beings to name, numerous different plants and animals are evenly distributed across the
islands, making it a great place for a walk depending on your mood."
Great Star proudly introduced the masterpiece born from the
near-infinite probabilities of the universe.
"Impressive."
"I highly recommend it. And next is the star of the blue
land."
A planet with endless vegetation spread across a vast continent rotated
with blue clouds floating above.
"Many animals live here. Since there is no shortage of food, they
don't prey on each other. They are all gentle. If you like animals, I recommend this
planet."
As Great Star brought the planet closer, mermaids were singing beautiful
songs on a rock by a small lake.
'Amy would love it.'
As Shirone watched with satisfaction, Great Star introduced the last
planet.
"This one is undoubtedly a masterpiece created by
nature."
It was a planet with countless breathtaking landscapes, rare even on the
planet Shirone lived on.
"Volcanoes, typhoons, tides, tectonic movements have created the
best of the best. It has now entered a stable period, so it's safe to bring friends. Of course... you
might sprain your ankle climbing a mountain."
Mountain peaks rose above the clouds, and below the steep cliffs lay a
blue sea.
Forests were formed along the great rivers, and animals moved in herds
across the endless plains.
"Hmm... I see."
Shirone retracted the thought he had when he first heard Great Star's
words.
'I did have something I wanted.'
He really wanted it.
Even if he made it with material, he doubted he could create such
beauty, let alone the time it would take.
"Although it's not a fitting expression for a star, it is rich in
resources. There are many substances not found here. Large-scale imports would disrupt the balance of
the star, so I can't allow that, but you can collect them for personal use. If you have time, learning
alchemy would be good too."
After finishing the introduction of the stars, Great Star looked back at
Shirone.
"If none of them appeal to you..."
"I'll take that star."
When Shirone raised his hand and pointed to the ceiling, Great Star
smiled and nodded.
"Then, please give it a name."
"The name of the planet is..."
Shirone's eyes momentarily softened.
"Hexa."
May it no longer be a lonely name.
"It's a wonderful name. From now on, the planet Hexa belongs to
Shirone. I, who govern the stars of the universe, guarantee this, and no one can claim
ownership."
Great Star checked the time.
"There is a little more than a day left until the altar opens.
You've fought fiercely until now. Feel free to do what you want."
Feeling like he was being told to sort out his remaining life, Shirone
became anxious.
"No. I'd rather go back to Nane. This time, I'll settle it for
sure."
Great Star shook her head.
"If Shirone loses, no one will be able to stop Nane. Wait for the
right time. It takes time."
"Are you telling me to just watch the world head towards
destruction?"
"It won't be destroyed."
Great Star spoke with conviction.
"Shirone, humans are weak beings, but they are not fragile. If they
have overcome numerous crises so far, they won't easily give up the world."
As Great Star said this and looked down at the ground, Shirone also
turned his gaze to the blue planet beyond the glass floor.
'What is happening?'
