Chapter 840:
Depression (4)
Habitz's army entered the capital of Kashan without bloodshed.
White flags hung from every house, and the residents bowed their
heads in fear.
It wasn't just because they had lost the war.
The vanguard had already swept through, leaving corpses strewn across
the streets and the smell of fresh blood lingering in the air. Smoto, with a toothpick in his mouth,
smiled.
"They grasp the situation quickly. As expected of the Empress of
Kashan."
Balkan thought it wasn't over yet.
"I heard there was a coronation. If they've already lost the war, why
does it matter?"
"They're probably thinking of negotiating while hiding the Empress.
If the conditions are right, it's good to accept. After all, we just need to enjoy ourselves."
When they reached the entrance of the imperial city of Aganos, the
army of hell had completely surrounded it.
As the gates opened, the scene inside the imperial city was
revealed.
"Ugh..."
Over a thousand humans were bound to various torture devices,
bleeding profusely.
The torture devices were connected to mechanical devices that
resembled musical instruments scattered around.
"Zetaro made these."
As they passed through the outer gate, a demon who had made
significant contributions in the war approached Habitz and bowed.
He was a handsome man, just under 2 meters tall, wearing black armor
with spikes.
Amon, the commander of the 7th Legion of Hell.
One of the ten commanders directly under Satan, he wielded a
hell-forged sword.
None of the demon commanders were weak, but those ranked in the top
ten were on a different level of power.
"We've been waiting for you, Satan."
Habitz's appearance was human-like, but Amon could clearly see
it.
The four red triangles floating above Habitz's head were a sign that
he was an entity beyond the law.
Balkan asked.
"Where is the ruler of Kashan?"
As soon as he finished speaking, a group appeared from the direction
of the inner gate.
It was Kando, who had just become emperor, and the highest nobles who
had ruled Kashan until now.
Amon spoke to Habitz.
"To commemorate this victory, Zetaro has prepared an event. Please
enjoy it."
With that, he gave the order, and a hundred demons formed an
orchestra.
Women with red skin and hair made of tentacles stood holding sheet
music.
A short, plump conductor looked around the orchestra and waved his
baton.
As numerous instruments played,
"..."
Over a thousand humans began to scream at different pitches.
From the first note, Kando and the nobles felt their hair stand on
end.
'These guys are ruthless.'
Kando gritted his teeth and endured.
"The army of hell!"
To the music made from human screams, the choir sang, their bellies
puffed out.
Every time an instrument played, the torture devices moved, squeezing
out the humans' voices even more.
"The world of chaos!"
It was truly the song of hell, and even Balkan and Smoto were deeply
impressed this time.
"This is amazing... Zetaro is a genius."
Habitz seemed to be enjoying the music, but after listening for a
while, he just chuckled.
"Let's go."
As Amon led the way, Habitz's party passed the choir, who were moved
to tears, and approached Kando.
'It's not boring. But...'
The fact that the one welcoming him wasn't Uorin was enough to spoil
his mood.
'What I want to hear is the Empress's song.'
Although it wasn't necessary for Habitz to personally greet the
emperor of the defeated empire, he boldly approached.
"Where is Uorin?"
Kando, who had already prepared for everything, bowed
politely.
"She is in the castle. But before you meet her, I have a proposal as
the emperor of the Kashan Empire."
Balkan sneered.
'I knew it would come to this.'
"I will hand over the control of Kashan. You can use me if you want,
but if it's inconvenient, you can execute me. Just please spare the lives of the people."
The faces of the highest nobles were filled with emotion.
'I'm sorry, Your Majesty.'
It was a decision to ensure the safety of the country, even at the
cost of his own body and the honor of Teraje.
Habitz looked at Kando with indifferent eyes.
His eyes showed no intention of analyzing anything, but the endless
chaos in them made Kando dizzy.
'Mother...'
Last night, Uorin had called Kando.
"We will lose the war at this rate. Even the help of the Holy War has
its limits. Kashan will soon fall."
Kando didn't care.
Although it was a situation where he should have been angry and
desperate, for some reason, his emotions didn't fluctuate.
It was probably because of Uorin's unusually pale face.
'So even mother feels fear.'
She had never known defeat with her ability to encompass history, but
the Great Purge was an unknown territory.
Above all, facing the chaos of Habitz was the worst.
"I will find a way. With the remaining forces and the power of the
Holy War, we can find an escape route..."
"No, that won't be enough to escape Habitz. I've considered all
options. I've thought about what would lead to a good outcome. And the decision I've made is..."
Uorin looked up at Kando.
"I must live."
Her expression was like that of a frightened rabbit, but her eyes
still burned with the will to live.
"I'm not afraid of dying. Being captured by Habitz... I can endure
it. But Kando, I want to live."
Kando realized what Uorin intended to do.
'She is focused solely on survival.'
She was willing to abandon the history of Kashan, the honor of
Teraje, the lives of the officials, and the lives of the people to survive.
'That Teraje...'
He wasn't angry that she had abandoned her responsibility as an
emperor.
He disliked seeing her, who had reached the pinnacle of the world,
desperately clinging to life.
"Mother, let me..."
"Save me."
Kando couldn't say anything.
Kando, who had escaped from Habitz's chaos, spoke.
"That is all we ask. If you accept the proposal, we will open Aganos
immediately."
Balkan asked.
"What if we refuse?"
"Then Kashan will be destroyed. But the Gustav Empire will never get
what it wants."
"Uorin."
Habitz spoke.
"Where is she?"
Kando pointed to the top floor of Aganos.
A woman with white hair was looking down from a window just below the
fluttering white flag.
"What will you do?"
After staring at the spire for a long time, Habitz spoke.
"Amon."
Amon, clad in armor, approached.
"Kill them all."
As soon as he spoke, Amon's sword created a black afterimage as it
passed by.
All the nobles except Kando, who had turned into electricity and
disappeared, were split in two.
"Ahhh!"
The maids and servants who were further away screamed and ran.
"Damn it!"
Kando, who had cast a spark spell, was lying down, grimacing.
'Did he notice?'
No, he just acted on a whim.
'He's definitely the Empress's nemesis.'
With Plan A failed, Kando's eyes were fixed on Habitz, just 20 meters
away.
'If I can just kill him...'
His life was already forfeit.
Kando cast a spark spell at the army of hell and then cast lightning
in front of Habitz.
"Ahhh!"
A perfect release with no magic left.
Blue electricity spread in all directions, clawing at the air and
causing a massive explosion.
Boom!
'Missed!'
A demon of division commander rank, one of Amon's subordinates, had
grabbed Habitz and soared into the sky.
Smoto sighed in relief.
'If the demon hadn't helped, would Habitz have died?'
Balkan drew his sword and approached.
"Smoto, don't overthink it. Just remember, he doesn't die. Keep that
in your head."
"...Got it."
The Kashan guards were launching an offensive, but the 7th Legion's
direct troops were formidable.
"Was it a trap?"
Balkan shook his head.
"No. Until just now, there was no sign of any killing intent. They
really intended to surrender."
"Then why?"
Balkan smiled bitterly.
"Teraje is truly amazing."
Even if it meant killing nearly a billion people, she wanted to
live.
"Head to the spire!"
Amon quickly flew to the spire and grabbed the woman standing at the
window.
The woman with white-dyed hair was trembling in fear.
"Sa-save me..."
She cried out in tears as she begged for her life.
"Long live the Empress!"
Balkan, who had beheaded her with one stroke, looked at the exhausted
Kando and asked.
"Where is Uorin?"
Kando could only manage a weak smile.
As the five great stars maintained their silence, each adhering to
their own philosophy, the other stars engaged in debate.
Mini stood up and shouted.
"We must go to Kashan immediately! If the Three Emperors collapse,
the demons will become even stronger!"
"Why should we? Kashan tried to side with the demons. Humans will do
anything for profit."
"I agree. It's a human matter anyway. The Ivory Tower pursues
transnational goals..."
The Law Slayer let out another scream.
Ahhh!
As if by magic, the voice disappeared, and all the stars turned to
Shirone.
"Enough."
The Ivory Tower.
The pinnacle of the magical world, pursuing pure scholarship without
being swayed by any power.
"I will leave the Ivory Tower."
Minerva, surprised by the unexpected statement, asked.
"Shirone, what are you talking about?"
"Don't misunderstand. I'm not angry or disappointed. Everyone here is
right."
The problem is that everyone is right.
"Are you running away?"
Freed spoke without even turning his head.
"You were close to the Empress of Kashan. You're too soft. I don't
know how you became one of the Five Great Stars like that. We aim for the entirety of this world. If you
truly want to benefit the world, enforce it here. Overpower us."
"No."
Shirone was firm.
"Now I understand what you meant. You said you rose to this position
because you hated getting involved with humans and were tired of debates."
"Yes. That's the Ivory Tower."
"So I'm going higher."
Finally turning his head, Freed glared coldly at Shirone, who
spoke.
"I'm sick of dealing with you all."
Freed's face turned red, and Xing looked at Shirone with
interest.
Minerva let out a small laugh.
'That's more like you.'
Shirone, who had grabbed the trembling Law Slayer from the air,
approached the Grand Star.
"If we start from the North Pole, it will be too late. Let me out
from here."
"...Outside is space."
Shirone looked down at the blue planet visible through the
transparent glass floor.
"It's the fastest way."
"Understood."
Although it was a situation of unauthorized departure from the grand
assembly, Grand Star seemed oddly pleased.
'Yes, Shirone. The place you aim for is not just the top, but
something beyond that.'
And someday, you will clash with Nane for that unique
position.
'Material.'
As Shirone emitted the light of Yahweh, a transparent sphere formed
around him.
Minerva nodded.
"Ah..."
If he could just break through the atmosphere, he could go straight
to Kashan.
As Grand Star raised her hands, the glass sphere containing Shirone
floated up to the ceiling.
"Remember, Shirone. You said you were leaving the Ivory Tower,
but..."
In an instant, the glass sphere exited the Great Holy War.
"I do not permit it."
The glass sphere, pulled by the planet's gravity, began to fall
rapidly toward the desert.
