Ymir's body resided deep underground in Helheim.
Far below the icy sea, where even light couldn't reach, Suoi, the
bioluminescent creatures that coexisted with Ymir, floated around, emitting faint light.
Suoi, like other creatures in Helheim, had the properties of an
incorporeal entity, freely moving between solid molecules and transmitting all the world's information
to Ymir through quantum communication.
The only place in Heaven that could confine Ymir's body was the eternal
ice of Helheim.
But Ymir didn't think so.
No physical force could confine him.
If it could be destroyed, he could destroy it.
His law, the law of war, was the vanguard, and he knew it wasn't time
yet.
Suoi drifted away from Ymir's eyes, displaying various images on their
translucent bodies.
Ymir's eyes remained indifferent to the affairs of Heaven, the outside
world, and even distant worlds.
Then, dozens of Suoi overlapped, showing a scene of a young man, now a
youth, wielding a great sword and fighting many opponents.
Suoi's information was just information.
Ymir trusted his judgment, which was never wrong.
'Not yet.'
One corner of Ymir's mouth lifted.
Crack! Crack! Crack!
Just that caused a massive 2-kilometer crack in the abyssal ice
wall.
'Not yet.'
Ymir simply waited.

Despite being the detestable human who had opened Miro's dimension and
humiliated the angels, his anger didn't turn to hatred because he held a reverence for her.
It wasn't confidence or arrogance. It was the absence of worry because
she had no heart.
Perhaps it was the ultimate state of mind a human could reach, the state
of emptiness.
'This is the first time I've felt curiosity about a human.'

"What are you thinking?"
"Thinking? What kind of thinking?"

"All thoughts. I'm curious about the thoughts flowing through your mind
right now."
"Hmm."

"You're handsome. Do you have a lover?"

"For an angel's device, it's too crude. The aesthetics are sloppy.

"Maybe if I ask to be released, you'll be charmed by my beauty and let

Heavenly Galaxy Wheel.

"But…"

"…"

heart meant.
Yet he couldn't refute her because the more he tried to shake her, the
more he felt shaken himself.
Uriel retracted the staff and flew to Cariel. Now, he too wished for
Miro's quick demise.

"I told you, provoking her wouldn't end well. She's the one who

How many humans could infiltrate the noble mind of an angel and cause a

What was she thinking?
