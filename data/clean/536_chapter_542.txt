Chapter 542:
A metallic arm suddenly punched through the elevator ceiling.
With a clanking sound, Yoa's Silver Mirror gauntlet began slicing open a
path. The ceiling collapsed, and Shirone and Yoa jumped down.
"Not as deep as I thought."
Judging by the height, the elevator had stopped at the first basement
level.
"What if we break through the floor and keep going down?"
Since the staircase had been blocked by mucus, going too deep right from
the start felt risky.
Suddenly, sparks crackled from above, and the elevator button lit
up.
"There's still power…?"
Before they could process the surprise, the elevator doors opened on
their own.
As they stretched out into the corridor, the ceiling lights flickered on
one after another, illuminating a monotonous laboratory hallway.
Although patches of mucus clung here and there, the area was spacious
enough to move around in.
"Let's start from here."
Shirone led Yoa through the hallway of the lab.
Each room had airlock doors, and behind the glass walls, countless
electrified cages were stacked like blocks.
A containment facility. Were they doing animal testing here?
They entered the central control room, the only one with an open door,
and a foul odor immediately hit them.
Faint bloodstains remained on the walls, and mucus seemed to have seeped
in from above, dripping halfway down along the surfaces.
"There must've been a fight."
Debris was scattered everywhere, and blood stained the floor.
"No… This wasn't a fight. It was a massacre."
The scene made that abundantly clear.
"If the power's on…"
Half-expecting something, Shirone pressed the administrator button in
the central control room.
With a hum, holographic monitors flickered to life, projecting countless
three-dimensional images around them.
"U-Undergrounders…!"
Yoa hardened her arms and moved into a defensive stance, but Shirone
raised a hand.
"It's fine. They're just projections."
Shirone stared intently at one of the cylindrical holograms.
A human and an ape circled side by side, then the projection overlapped
and merged them into a single creature bearing the features of a Undergrounders.
What the hell…?
As Shirone manipulated the hologram controls, a document appeared on a
side monitor.
"Is that ancient script? What does it say?"
Recalling pre-stasis memories, Shirone began to read.
"Dynamic Human Project. Officially sanctioned by the government."
At that point, Shirone fell silent and continued reading with his eyes
alone.
He was beginning to understand why both the Children of the Sun and the
Undergrounds feared this place.
No way…
The contents of the document were shocking.
In the aftermath of nuclear war, 80% of humanity suffered from
mutations, deformities, and biological deterioration.
Ravaged by recessive genes, humans could no longer reproduce healthy
offspring. Thus, the Dynamic Human Project was launched.
By combining the genes of artificially bred primates with humans, they
aimed to create a stronger, more resilient human being.
At the very least, such beings might survive the ruined world.
"Arbiter, what does it say? Are the Undergrounders going to be
punished?"
This truly was a forbidden sanctuary, and Shirone couldn't find the
words to explain.
"Are you alright? You look pale."
Just then, mucus silently oozed from the wall behind them, and a pair of
eyes snapped open within the two-meter-high gelatinous mass.
As the eyes locked onto Shirone and Yoa, the mass surged forward,
gradually taking on a humanoid form.
"So, uh… this thing is…"
At that moment, Shirone suddenly sensed something through his Spirit
Zone and spun around.
"What the—!"
There was nothing.
Yoa's expression softened as she looked around. "What is it?"
"I'm sure something was there…"
"Maybe you're just tense from everything?"
"No. The mucus—it's different."
It had the same volume as when they first arrived, even piled up to the
ceiling, but the shape was completely different now.
However, Yoa's eyes weren't sharp enough to notice that level of
detail.
"If it bothers you that much, should we leave?"
Shirone bit his lip and thought.
Now that he understood what this place was, he couldn't simply return to
the settlement.
The answer is here. Maybe I can recover my memories, too.
Having made up his mind, Shirone said, "Let's keep exploring for
now."
The two of them left the control room and began searching for a way
deeper underground.
"The explosions stopped."
At Miro's words, Fermi sped up.
"I'll scout ahead."
Gliding over the pavement with Air Skate, he quickly turned a corner and
signaled that it was safe.
Rian, arriving a little later, looked up at the tall buildings.
"This area's the only one that doesn't seem heavily damaged."
Marsha nodded. "You're finally noticing things? Still, yeah… something
about this place is off."
As each of them moved carefully in different directions, Miro pointed
toward a building.
"See that?"
A high-rise completely covered in mucus.
"Someone went inside."
The wide smear of mucus had a crude path cut through it—clearly
manmade.
"Mucus is such a pain. Especially this much…"
There was no telling how much of it filled the interior.
"But we don't have much choice, do we?"
As Miro moved first, the others followed.
Surprisingly, the lobby was relatively clean. Only the stairway leading
upward was sealed with mucus.
"Should we carve through it?"
As Miro pointed to the stairs, Fermi rounded a corner and approached the
elevator.
"They went this way, it seems."
Marsha, stepping over, pressed her hand to the wall and peered down the
elevator shaft.
Faint light flickered from the bottom of the steel shaft.
"There's a light. Could be an Abyss Walker."
"Well, we'll find out when we get down there."
As the four of them descended the shaft, a squelching thud came from the
lobby ceiling.
A human figure formed from mucus.
Its appearance was grotesque, as if its skin had been peeled off—but its
facial features were clear, and its eyes were sharp.
It was a so-called Mucus Man.
"…"
The Mucus Man turned toward the elevator shaft. Mucus dripped slowly
down his head, and in the next moment, as if being sucked upward, it pulled his face toward the
ceiling.
Shirone took a deep breath of the crisp air as he stepped onto the
fourth basement level.
"Whoa…"
Yoa looked around with eyes full of awe.
It was a tropical-style botanical garden, with countless tree leaves
climbing the walls.
"A whole ecosystem underground? No, more than that—how has it lasted
this long?"
It would have been impossible without carefully maintained temperature,
humidity, and lighting.
"Let's just head down. I don't like the feel of this place…"
He felt uneasy exploring the entire floor with Yoa.
"There's a bigger tree over there."
But Yoa was already taking the lead on her own.
When they entered the large greenhouse sealed by glass walls, dozens of
computer devices were still running, and a massive tree stood at the center.
Shirone, following in behind, could tell from just looking at Yoa's back
that her face was frozen in disbelief.
"H-Huh…?"
In front of her stood what was unmistakably a Tree of Life—slightly
smaller than the ones outside, but clearly the same.
Fetuses, still with umbilical stubs, hung like fruit from the branches,
and the wide glass tank encasing the roots was filled with mucous.
"How could this be…? We…"
As Yoa murmured in disbelief, the mucous in the tank surged upward and
pulled in one of the fetuses.
The umbilical snapped with a pop, and the fetus dropped into the mucus.
Yoa screamed in horror, her face pale.
"KYAAAAAH!"
"Calm down! If you yell too loudly—!"
Yoa wasn't in her right mind.
"Arbiter! What is this! Why is there a Tree of Life here? What about Ra?
The Sun—!"
Shirone couldn't possibly know, but he did have a way to find
out.
He pressed the control panel's option button, and once again, a hologram
activated, continuing the explanation.
"Human Extinction Prevention Project. Governmental Unofficial
Approval." As expected…
Just like the Dynamic Humans, the Children of the Sun were also not
naturally evolved humans.
And the content in the document was even more shocking than what he'd
seen on the first floor.
Over 70% of all plant and animal life had gone extinct, and resources
had run dry. Only then did humanity realize—
They could no longer afford to let humans control energy.
Human greed had destroyed everything!
Humanity required a special caretaker—one immune to desire. Thus, the
Local Energy Circulation System, Mucus, was created.
Its purpose: to dissolve biological matter and produce energy to sustain
the ecosystem.
But when even localized energy began to run dry, the system broke
through its designed limits and began expanding.
Prioritizing ecosystem stability over the preservation of specific
organisms, it began to digest even humans.
With the planet desiccated and dying, humanity reached one
conclusion:
The human propulsion mechanism needed to be redesigned from
scratch.
The company Silence Cryonics Inc., which led the Human Survival Project
under government oversight, eventually contacted the last remaining descendants of the fairy race and
founded Fairy Biomimetics.
The result was the Tree of Life powered by solar energy.
If they could create a species that survived on nothing but
photosynthesis and water, humanity could persist—even after the world ended.
And now, the future they could never have foreseen was unfolding before
Shirone's eyes.
So the mutants were manifestations of fairy traits… Humanity's obsession
with its own survival is terrifying. But…
Shirone looked around the lab with mournful eyes.
Is this really the future they dreamed of?
Thud! Yoa dropped to her knees.
"No…"
Tears streamed down her cheeks.
"This can't be real, right? I must be dreaming, right? We… The Children
of the Sun are truly blessed by Ra, right?!"
Long ago, the Dynamic Humans and Children of the Sun must have known of
this place.
But it was something they could never be allowed to remember. That truth
had passed into instinct, and the place became a forbidden sanctuary.
"Arbiter! Please tell me! What are we? Are we really human?!"
"That doesn't matter. What matters is that your race is the one still
surviving in this world."
"But all this time we've…!"
At that moment, the Mucus under the Tree of Life began to stir, and a
Mucus Man burst out.
"Watch out!"
Shirone grabbed Yoa and rolled across the floor, just as mucus flowed
from the ceiling and coated the walls.
"What the hell is that?!"
Holding onto Yoa, Shirone turned and recoiled at the grotesque sight of
the Mucus Man.
"High-efficiency energy…"
Its arm stretched out like rubber and rushed toward them. Shirone flung
his body aside and unleashed Photon Cannons.
The Mucus Man's shoulder and abdomen exploded, but he sprang upward
again, only to be absorbed back into the ceiling's mucus.
"Damn it!"
Defeating one didn't end it—the entire building filled with mucus was
their enemy.
The Mucus Man, having reformed a new body, crawled across the walls like
a spider. Whenever Shirone attacked, it would merge back into the goo.
How are we supposed to fight this?
One lunged at him from the floor and swung a punch. Shirone dove to the
side—
At the same time, thick tendrils shot out from the wall mucus.
"Arbiter!"
As Yoa regained her senses and tried to leap forward, the greenhouse
door burst open, and a huge man ran past her.
Without hesitation, he stood before Shirone and swung a greatsword,
slashing through the tentacles with a wet splack.
"Shirone! Are you okay?!"
The man with blue hair turned his head, and Shirone stared at him in
stunned silence.
Suddenly, memories surged into his mind, flowing in and connecting with
countless others.
"…Rian?"
Shirone's memory recovery rate: 69%.
