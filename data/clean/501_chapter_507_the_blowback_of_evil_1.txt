Chapter 507:
The Blowback of Evil (1)
Satiel flew away, and Ikael turned to check on Shirone.
Shirone was hunched over, arms hanging limply.
Though standing, it was as if his mind was already lying down, and his
eyes were vacant.
"Shirone."
Ikael flew towards Shirone, who was slowly collapsing forward.
But before she could reach him, someone else ran up and caught
Shirone.
It was Plu, who had been unable to intervene in the battle that
surpassed human limits.
"Shirone! Snap out of it, Shirone!"
Plu cradled Shirone's face in her lap, slapping his cheeks, but he
remained motionless like a corpse.
The massive flash of the photon cannon had been intense even for Plu,
who had been away from the battlefield.
She didn't know how Shirone had managed to amplify the power to such an
extent, but she knew that in the world of magic, there were no rewards without a price.
Seeing no sign of Shirone regaining consciousness, Plu turned to glare
at Ikael.
"What happened? Why isn't Shirone waking up?"
Even in front of an archangel, Plu showed no fear.
Her mind had reached its breaking point, leaving only anger, but she
also sensed no malice from Ikael.
In fact, Ikael looked at Shirone with more concern than anyone.
'Shirone…'
From the moment he cast Ataraxia in its physical form, his mind had been
shattered.
But reaching the state of Prajna had delayed the mental
explosion.
If it had stopped there, there might have been some hope.
"Shirone defeated Faiel using the judicial halo Valhalla Action. He
is now paying the price."
"Valhalla Action?"
Plu looked at Shirone in shock.
Considering the scale of the final photon cannon, the price Shirone had
to pay was unimaginable.
"How long… will he be like this?"
Ikael shook her head.
No one could know except the computational trigger of Valhalla
Action.
"I don't know, but it will be much longer than you think. Fifty
years, a hundred years, maybe even two hundred years."
Plu looked at Ikael with irritation.
An archangel, and that's all she could say?
"Two hundred years? Shirone is human. Humans don't live that long.
The magic shouldn't have worked in the first place!"
That's true.
If the result couldn't be reached, the cause shouldn't exist.
But conversely, if the result was reached, it means the cause was
valid.
"Valhalla Action is just a trigger that mediates the Akashic
Records. According to human logic, the cause should precede the result, but Valhalla Action reverses
that logic. In this case, since the result exists, the cause must be possible."
Though still infuriating, Plu wasn't a foolish mage.
"Then what if Shirone dies of old age? Or gets killed? The result
is already there, so how does the cause change?"
"I don't know. But that's the Akashic Records. Changes in parts
don't matter to the whole. No matter how parts change, the whole is always perfect. If Shirone dies,
some change will occur. But it won't matter to the whole."
"God is indifferent, is that it?"
Though the concept of god differed between angels and Plu, Ikael
nodded.
Only living beings give meaning to things like a lover's death,
incurable diseases, or heinous crimes.
To the whole, Shirone's death is as significant as a pebble rolling down
a mountain.
"Exactly. And perhaps… that might be one way to bring Shirone
back."
Plu's eyes widened.
"What do you mean?"
Ikael shook her head again.
Even an archangel couldn't know what computations Valhalla Action had
performed.
She just wanted to convey the gravity of the situation.
"Sadly, it's beyond my capability. But Shirone is human, so humans
must find the answer. Isn't it human to endlessly seek answers even in despair?"
Plu started to shout something but then closed her mouth and looked down
at Shirone's face with a sullen expression.
Ikael was just as sorrowful.
'It's truly unfortunate.'
Perhaps… if Shirone had progressed in normal time, he might have found a
way to defeat Faiel within ten years.
Ikael saw that much potential in Shirone.
But there are no hypotheticals in the Akashic Records.
Thus, Valhalla Action must have calculated the exact time Shirone needed
to reach that level and demanded it as the price.
It's difficult for a human to live beyond a hundred years, but not
impossible.
In Shehakim, there are countless people over a thousand years
old.
"For now, there's no need to worry. He reached the state of Prajna
and prevented the explosion. Valhalla Action can't put a debtor of desire in a state of
moratorium."
Hearing that Shirone wouldn't die, Plu felt a bit relieved.
It wasn't easy, but there were other pressing concerns.
"Gasp!"

Time had passed significantly, even by her senses.
She shouted, filled with the fear that it might already be too
late.
"Something terrible has happened! Divine Punishment…!"

sky.
