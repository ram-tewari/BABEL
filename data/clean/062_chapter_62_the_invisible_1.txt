Chapter 62:
The Invisible (1)
"But there's no good way, is there? Did you come up with any plan to save the research
club?"
"No. But I think that's a separate issue. Honestly, today… it must have been a really sad
day for Ms. Siena. I don't know the details, but I can tell."
It was something everyone felt. She had bid farewell to someone she had cherished since
childhood. The fact that it ended with just a drink showed how strong Siena was.
"But isn't it strange to get so sentimental? What's the point of living? Without using
our heads, we're just corpses. The more we're like this, the more we need to stay calm. There's no immediate
way to save the research club, so why push ourselves?"
Iruki's words were also persuasive. If emotions clouded judgment, the future ahead would
be nothing but hardship.
Neid's heart wavered slightly.
"Hmm. Honestly, Ms. Siena is a tough opponent. She has no weaknesses. Why else would we
have gone this far? It's a once-in-a-lifetime opportunity."
Iruki nodded in agreement.
"She's sharp-tempered, meticulous in planning, and thorough in managing students.
Especially after what we heard before—how she dated Shirone to prevent an overflow and all that. How can we
do anything against such a flawless opponent?"
'These guys…!'
Siena, who had been listening with her eyes closed, felt a surge of anger. Her mood was
gloomy, and her body was exhausted, but she couldn't just let it slide.
"But now we know."
Shirone spoke.
"Why she chose the path of a teacher, why she was so obsessed with overflow. Ms. Siena
didn't want us to walk the same path she did."
"Well, that's true, but…"
"No matter what made her become a teacher, the important thing is that she genuinely
cares for us. But what about us? We're trying to attack her by exploiting her painful past. The seniors in
the research club wouldn't want to see their juniors fall this far."
This time, even Iruki couldn't refute. Magicians always pursued efficiency, but focusing
too much on it could make them overlook what truly mattered.
"…You always hit the nail on the head at times like this."
Neid, having made his decision, spoke lightly.
"Alright. Let's keep today's incident a secret among us. What do you say, Iruki?"
"If it's not connected to the research club, I have no complaints. Honestly, I think Ms.
Siena is the best when it comes to education."
"Well, we're the villains here. Teachers like Ms. Siena, who care so meticulously for
their students, are rare. And considering what we've done, she's been really lenient with us."
Iruki said.
"I'll let it slide this time. As for the research club, the three of us can put our heads
together and come up with something, right?"
Shirone spoke.
"So, is that the plan? Even if we face disciplinary action tomorrow, what happened today
stays buried. We'll never speak of it!"
"Deal! Sold!"
Free from the torture of hope, they felt relieved. Shirone and his friends walked with
lighter steps than before. None of them noticed that Siena, who was being carried on Shirone's back, had
opened her eyes at some point.
The main gate of the magic school came into view. Exhausted, Shirone stopped when he
spotted a guard holding a torch. Since it wasn't yet midnight, the night shift hadn't left.
"What do we do? If we go back like this, rumors might spread. Plus, we left without
permission."
"No choice. Let's use magic from here. I'll levitate Ms. Siena, so you support her."
"Wait. Let's put her down first. Iruki, help me."
As he bent his knees, the weight on his back suddenly disappeared. Startled, Shirone
turned around to see Siena standing upright. The drunkenness was gone, and her eyes were as clear as
starlight.
"T-teacher?"
Shirone, feeling hollow, slumped to the ground. If she could walk, what was the point of
all his effort to carry her?
"Teacher, are you really okay?"
"Of course. Did you think I was really drunk?"
Shirone exhaled roughly, on the verge of tears.
"Then why did you…?"
"You ate an expensive meal, so you should pay for it. Did you think you wouldn't have to
suffer at all? Anyway, follow me. If you want to get back quietly."
Shirone and his friends followed Siena as if possessed. The guard, recognizing her,
greeted her with sleepy eyes.
"Ah, good evening, Ms. Siena."
"Yes. Good work."
"But… did you go out with these students?"
"Yes. I took them for fieldwork."
"Ah, I see. Welcome back. Have a peaceful night."
Although students were forbidden from leaving at night, no one would doubt the word of
Siena, who was highly respected in the school.
Nervous, Shirone and his friends quickly passed through the gate, worried the guard might
call them back. Once they safely reached the central park, Siena turned and glared at them.
"Eek!"
Only then did Shirone and his friends realize it—they had returned to Alpheas Magic
School.
"I'll let today's incident slide."
Shirone and his friends blinked in surprise at her unexpected words.
"I'll give you another month starting today. That's the best negotiation card I can
offer."
They understood what she meant. Resetting the grace period was her way of telling them to
fight seriously.
"Find a way to convince the school within a month. Whether it's holding a presentation or
summoning a ghost, that's up to you. If you truly take pride in the research club, fight the school head-on
and win what you want. That's my philosophy of education and how I show my love for my students."
Shirone and his friends were moved. It was hard to believe Siena of all people would say
this to them.
"Yes! You can count on us! We'll definitely surprise the school!"
"Right! Struggling isn't our style! This time, we'll think it through properly and give
it our all!"
Siena smiled bitterly as she watched her students regain their confidence. It was true
that anxiety came first when their enthusiasm flared, but part of her also looked forward to what amazing
things they might achieve.
'Haha. Do your best, you troublesome students.'

The Invisible (1)
Shirone, who had visited the research club later than usual, raised his hand weakly upon
seeing Neid and Iruki lying on the sofa.
"Hey."
"Did you sleep well last night?"
"I don't know. I'm not even sure if I slept. What about you?"
"Same. What do we do now? We've been discussing all night, but no good ideas came up."
Shirone sat on the sofa and spoke.
"We have to decide today. Let's come up with a plan."
"So, how? Supernatural parapsychology deals with invisible phenomena. But humans don't
believe in what they can't see."
The same conversation from last night repeated. Too tired to even speak, they stared
blankly at one spot. It felt like their minds had gone blank. After some time, Shirone's eyes regained
focus.
"What if we change our perspective?"
Neid and Iruki sat up abruptly. They didn't know what he would say, but it was clearly
different from the patterns so far.
"How?"
"So, our dilemma is this: we have no way to show what's invisible."
"Yeah. That's the big problem."
"So, what I mean is, if it's invisible, we don't have to show it. We've been clinging to
the impossible, right? Maybe we can approach it from the angle of not showing it at all."
Not showing it was akin to not holding a presentation. But his friends weren't
disappointed. Instead, they seemed to have realized something and fell into thought.
"If it can't be verified, then it doesn't need to be verified."
"Exactly. The reason I joined the research club wasn't because you showed me something.
So, wouldn't others be the same?"
A faint smile appeared on Iruki's lips.
"Why didn't we think of that? Right, verification wasn't necessary from the start. We
didn't need to show what we didn't know. Rather, the opposite…"
"Make them believe in the invisible?"
Neid was convinced this was the answer he'd been seeking. He immediately got up, hugged
Shirone, and rolled around.
"Shirone! Come here! You saved our research club! You can even have my first kiss!"
"Gross! Get off!"
While Shirone and Neid tussled on the sofa, Iruki was lost in thought. The real challenge
was just beginning.
"So, what's the best way? Shirone's words are the solution, but we still need to give the
presentation some form. We need tangible results."
Neid returned to his seat. Shirone also smoothed his disheveled hair. It wasn't the time
for jokes.
"Let's think. What should we show?"
The three put their heads together and discussed. Once they had a direction, countless
methods came to mind. The key was something that would make people believe in the invisible.
"How about something auditory? It's easy to design."
"It's efficient, but not effective. We also have to consider whether the school will
accept it."
"Then let's go for something visual. If possible, auditory too. We'll pour everything we
have into it."
"Then what should we visualize? The supernatural? The paranormal?"
"The supernatural isn't mainstream. The paranormal seems better."
Neid raised his hand.
"I also vote for the paranormal. There are things like spirits, ghosts, paranormal
phenomena—which one?"
Shirone said.
"Ghosts might be better. They're impactful."
"But wouldn't that be childish?"
Neid looked skeptical. But Iruki agreed with Shirone.
"I also think ghosts are better. The key is to strongly penetrate people's minds."
"Hmm. No matter how I think about it, it feels childish."
Shirone said.
"The first thing to consider is the effect. The rest is secondary. There's no need to
give up."
