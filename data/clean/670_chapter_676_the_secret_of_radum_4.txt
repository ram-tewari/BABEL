Chapter 676:
The Secret of Radum (4)
"What the hell is that now?"
The Speedkillers and Gluttons glared at Shirone with displeasure.
How many times would their meal be interrupted?
If not for the mage who'd blown off the Pidu's head in one strike, they'd have lost their
minds already.
"Joshua, Cargin. Are you okay?"
With enemies at close range, the two nodded quickly.
Noticing Joshua's twisted ankle, Shirone locked eyes with Marta.
'So it was her…'
The strongest non-human here.
"Step back."
"What? Step back? Are you out of your mind?"
Goblins, with their far higher fear threshold, rarely cowered in any situation.
"You think we'd give up such delicious food?"
Once, Shirone had asked Flu if there wasn't a peaceful solution.
At the time, her answer—not to force her ideals on others—had felt bitter, but now he
understood.
"You're living beings too."
Marta's face twisted as if insulted.
"I don't want meaningless slaughter. But I don't know how to save my comrades without
fighting."
"So what? You gonna beg for your life?"
"Run."
A Photon Cannon materialized above Shirone's palm.
"That's fair."
"Kukukuku!"
Marta shrugged.
"Hey, human. Is this your first time?"
Shirone didn't answer.
"You think you're a dragon or something? How many races do you think can negotiate with
Goblins? Just fight. We'll handle living or dying."
Baring her fangs, Marta added,
"Be our shit."
As the Speedkillers and Gluttons charged, Cargin, holding Joshua, shut his eyes.
"Aaaah!"
A battle cry rang from the building, and Rian, defying gravity, swung his greatsword.
Four Pidu were cleaved in half, making Marta click her tongue.
"What the hell is that?"
The Goblins' unique swordplay—rolling on the ground to target ankles—unfolded, forcing
Rian to step back hastily.
'They're fast. This is a Goblin.'
In pure speed, they surpassed Schema.
"Taha! Taha! Taha!"
Dodging forward slashes, Rian twisted his wrist mid-swing, countering with the
greatsword.
"Kekeke! Too slow, idiot!"
Marta rolled under the horizontal slash, aiming for Rian's ankle—
"Huh?"
Defying physics, the sword's trajectory curved, and the reversed greatsword slammed into
her torso.
"Gaaah!"
Pinned like a bug, she couldn't comprehend it.
'How…?'
A sword technique that altered its path mid-swing was beyond imagination.
"Harder than swatting roaches."
Rian muttered, feeling the ache in his arms from forcibly reversing the sword's flow with
Divine Transcendence.
"Martaaa!"
Though most would flee after their leader's death, the Goblins' combat power soared.
"I'll stab you a hundred times!"
Despite the bold declaration, the charging Goblins froze.
Their green faces paled to a corpse-like purple.
"W-what is that?"
Centered on Shirone, the scenery began turning hellish red, distinct from reality.
Inside this boundary, buildings pulsed like hearts.
"Come forth, Igor."
From this hellish world, the Lord of Terror, Igor, emerged astride a dead horse, dragging
his spear.
"Laktas Sagaar Vero Dem."
As he plunged his blue-flamed spear into the ground, lightning spread in concentric
circles.
"Gyaaaah!"
The Goblins and Pidu's retinas flooded with traumatic memories from their subconscious.
Given their warlike nature, their trauma was on another level.
"Aaaah! Just kill me! Kill me!"
One Goblin clawed at his eyelids until blood streamed down.
The brutality they'd inflicted for fun now tormented them beyond endurance.
"Damn it! Why am I seeing this?!"
Though an eternity to them, the traumatic visions lasted only an instant.
The non-humans, broken by terror, began scattering with screams.
"T-that's impossible."
Cargin and Joshua gaped.
The creatures who'd treated them as mere food were now wailing in fear—unbelievable.
"I told you to run."
Homing Photon Cannons materialized around Shirone.
Like Seekers' lasers, they locked onto targets and curved with terrifying speed.
Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!
"The light's bending?"
As Goblins and Pidu dropped like flies, Cargin bit his lip.
'They're on another level. This isn't a battlefield for street trash like us.'
Now he understood why the reward was 100,000 gold and why nothing in life was free.
Surveying the annihilated enemies, Igor turned to Shirone.
"You summon me often."
Even if it was the reverse world, its spatial limits matched reality, making it hard to
meet outside Radum.
"Go back. I'm not in the mood for jokes."
A mage at 90% Karsuchi had no mercy, but their emotions remained human.
"…Your world's affairs, then."
Ignoring the sealing sensation, the reverse world closed, and Igor vanished.
Stabilizing his mind with Breath of the Void, Shirone jumped down and checked on Cargin
and Joshua.
It dawned on them that Shirone hadn't moved from his spot until the battle's end.
"How's the leg?"
"Ah… it's fine."
It clearly wasn't.
"Hang in there. Let's find the others first, then treat it."
With enemies potentially lurking, Shirone turned to leave—but Cargin anxiously rolled his
eyes.
"Um… Captain."
As Shirone looked back, Cargin knelt and pressed his forehead to the ground.
"I beg you! Let us go back!"
Joshua stared in disbelief.
"What's this all of a sudden…?"
But seeing Cargin's tearful expression, she fell silent.
He desperately wanted to live.
"I'll return every coin! I'll pay the penalty! Please, let Joshua and me go!"
The mercenary band had been formed to infiltrate Lupistra into Radum unofficially.
Yet the mercenaries had joined knowing the risks, so leaving now was a breach of
contract.
"A mercenary who betrays once can't set foot in this world again, as far as I know. How
will you pay the penalty?"
"That's…"
Had he thought Shirone would let them go freely as their savior?
Faced with Shirone's cold response, Cargin's blood ran cold—until Joshua crawled over on
her broken ankle and prostrated herself.
"We'll repay you any way we can. Please, have mercy. We want to live."
'Hmm, there's the fraud issue too.'
The thought crossed Rian's mind, but the decision was Shirone's.
'I'll just keep fighting.'
In a way, a swordsman's life—focused on one thing—was blessed.
