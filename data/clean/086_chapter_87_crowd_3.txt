Chapter 87:
Crowd (3)
"A horn… is it?"
"That's impossible. Shirone's Photon Cannon evolved during the battle. And yet you're
saying we can't win?"
Shirone pushed himself up from the ground and spoke.
"We can't win."
His friends doubted their own ears. Perhaps it was the first time they had heard such
words since meeting Shirone?
"What the hell? Don't talk like a weakling."
"It's not weak talk. That guy… he's seriously strong."
Shirone admitted it honestly. Had they known he was the disciple of the Grand Sorcerer,
it would have made more sense, but even without that knowledge, they could feel his will in every spell.
The will to not lose. The will to crush humanity. The will to prove he was the best.
He had maximized the power of the Photon Cannon, but Harvest, borrowing Canis's mental
strength, absorbed even that.
From then on, it was a brawl. A battle of attrition, exchanging dark powers and Photon
Cannons to whittle each other down.
In the end, Canis succeeded in countering and pushed Shirone out of the forest.
'Someone that strong… why would they want to hurt people?'
If he had chosen evil for any reason, that would be one thing. Everyone makes mistakes.
But he couldn't accept that evil was strong. Strength was the product of effort, not
mistakes.
"Why are you doing this? What did the students ever do to you?"
"So when you're outmatched in strength, you resort to words? Typical pattern of weak
nobles."
Since it wasn't true, Shirone didn't flare up.
"Answer me. What you're trying to do is terrible enough to be recorded in history. At
least tell me why."
"History? Who cares about that? The abandoned just struggle to survive each day. If I
don't kill someone, I die."
"Don't try to justify it with sophistry. No matter how hard your life was, everyone in
the world has their own unbearable pain. Everyone carries something."
"You. Have you ever eaten shit off the ground?"
Shirone fell silent. A cold wind blew.
'Canis….'
Arin's eyes filled with sorrow. Those who hadn't lived in Radum could never understand
what Radum was like.
It was another world, detached from this one.
Canis and Arin had been abandoned there. They didn't remember since when or why it was
just the two of them.
But from the moment they became aware, they were there, surviving even as other children
died or were sold off.
Canis had been sharp since childhood. Though just a powerless child, he showed firsthand
how strong a human could become when talent met malice.
In Radum, teeming with villains, Canis wanted only one thing.
To protect Arin for one more day.
In Radum, women didn't exist. To be precise, girls who had passed infancy.
In a place where food was extremely scarce, powerless girls were consumed as soon as they
were born.
But Arin had survived in Radum until the age of ten.
The only woman in Radum.
The one who protected that eerie symbol was Canis.
He always barked like a mad dog. If an enemy appeared, targeting Arin, he would charge at
them, ready to die.
With Arin by his side, he couldn't join any organization. So they survived by scavenging
through trash bins every day.
The truly absurd part was that in Radum, even trash had owners. They fought desperately,
their flesh tearing, just to claim the scraps others had discarded.
After such fierce battles, Canis might barely obtain a moldy half-piece of bread.
***
Arin hadn't eaten for three days. And as the price for her three-day fast, Canis hadn't
eaten anything for twenty days.
'Hah! Hah! I did it! I got it! I got the bread!'
Canis sprinted mindlessly through the alley. Only after shaking off his pursuers did he
lean against a wall and exhale.
His consciousness blurred, and all he felt was hunger. He couldn't even remember how long
it had been since he last ate.
At that moment, a single piece of bread caught his eye.
Canis swallowed hard, his eyes gleaming with madness.
He wanted to eat it. To put it in his mouth.
'Right, I need strength to keep Arin alive. It's okay to eat this much. Arin can hold on
a few more days.'
Thinking that, it seemed like a brilliant idea.
Could anything be more logical? Eat this, regain some strength, and then find more food.
That's all.
Canis opened his jaw and brought the bread to his mouth. His parched mouth flooded with
saliva, and his hands trembled violently.
"Guh!"
Canis clenched his eyes shut and tucked the bread into his chest. It was all a lie. What
kind of strength could this pathetic bread give him?
'I have to endure. My body breaking down doesn't matter. But if my mind shatters, it's
over.'
Canis looked around like a madman, thinking of stuffing stones into his stomach.
He crawled toward feces someone had left at the alley's entrance. Before any thought
could form, he reached out, shoved it into his mouth, and forced it down.
"Ugh! Ugh!"
His tongue, mouth, stomach—his whole body rejected it. But Canis swallowed it.
Better than stones.
Feces were better than stones.
After struggling with his stomach for a long time, Canis returned to their hideout with
weak steps. Though still nauseous, he brightened upon seeing Arin and held out the bread.
"Arin, ta-da! I got this."
"Oh, really? Wow, amazing."
"Haha, I figured out the trick. I can get more from now on. Eat up."
Arin looked at the bread with sorrowful eyes. She wanted to go out and search for food
together, but in Radum, a girl venturing outside was suicide.
She had never spoken to anyone except Canis. Though it gave her social anxiety, what did
that matter? At least she hadn't become someone's meal, digested in their stomach.
"Canis, let's eat together."
"I'm fine. I picked up stuff while wandering. Earlier, there was a big centipede—got some
nutrients from that. You can't eat that, so you need this."
"I can eat it too. How long will you treat me like a child?"
Canis gripped Arin's shoulder with tender eyes.
"Arin, I know. You're a brave kid. But you shouldn't. Understand? The only reason I can
endure this hellhole is because I can feed you like a human being. If you become like me, you might go mad.
So eat."
It was a line she had heard dozens of times before. And every time, Arin would pretend to
relent and take a bite.
But this time was different.
Arin stared at Canis's lips with trembling eyes. Something was smeared there, and a smell
lingered.
"Canis, what… did you eat?"
Canis panicked like never before.
"Huh? Ahaha! It's cake. Cream got on me while I was licking it. Sorry for not telling
you. I was so hungry…!"
*Slap!*
Canis's face snapped to the side sharply. Though he had been hit countless times by
back-alley thugs, this was the first time a slap had hurt this much.
"A-Arin…."
Arin wore an expression Canis had never seen before—terrifying.
"You bastard… How could you do this to me! Am I livestock to you? Are you raising me? And
then you tell me to eat this? What the hell am I to you!"
"Arin, it's not like that! This is my punishment! It has nothing to do with you!"
"I don't need any of this! I don't need this!"
Arin threw the bread away. Watching it roll in the dust, Canis turned back with an angry
expression.
"Arin! What the hell! Do you know how hard I worked to get this…!"
Arin pulled Canis's face close and kissed him. As she licked what was smeared on his
lips, tears streamed from her eyes.
It wasn't a sweet kiss. Nor was it a beautiful exchange of emotions between humans. It
was just two creatures, born as sinners, pitying each other.
Only then did Canis realize what he had eaten. For the first time in his life, tears
flowed. A lifetime of suppressed sorrow burst forth at once.
"Hic! Hiiic!"
"Never do that again. If you do, I won't stay by your side anymore."
"I'm sorry, Arin. Don't leave. You're all the family I have. You're the only reason I
live."
"Right. Let's live, Canis. We must live."
Overwhelmed, Canis could only nod repeatedly. But Arin didn't forgive him. That day, she
made him eat every last crumb of the bread that had fallen to the ground.
***
Canis recounted his past as if narrating someone else's life.
"We lived in hell. But our master saved us from there. He gave us food, the power to
protect Arin, and even Harvest, the pinnacle of dark magic."
People's gazes turned to Harvest. Though usually a chatty magical creature, this time it
remained silent.
"I see."
Shirone spoke.
"What kind of life you lived. But that doesn't justify killing people. Just because your
life was harder doesn't make evil right."
"Don't misunderstand. I'm not defending myself—I'm teaching you. How shallow your
so-called justice is, how hypocritical the world you live in is. It wasn't your precious justice that saved
me and Arin. I'm just acting on what I believe."
"Hurting people won't give you anything. If you don't try to understand others first,
even your past will never find solace."
"Kik! Solace? You're spouting nonsense till the end. Let me tell you the situation. Our
master will erase this school entirely. That includes your friends."
"No. You won't hurt anyone. Except yourself."
As Shirone summoned photons above his hand, Harvest spread its broad palm to shield
Canis. But that wasn't enough—it stretched its other arm to envelop Canis completely.
"What's wrong, Harv? No need to be so scared."
"Dangerous. His aura is strange."
Canis snorted. He had already gauged Shirone's strength in the forest. For a magic school
student, he was decent, but still just a greenhouse flower.
"Hmph. Anyway, to me…."
Canis's expression froze. The Photon Cannon floating above Shirone's palm was vibrating
at a terrifying rate. Its power was even greater than the one he had used in the forest. But given his
severe injuries, it couldn't be that he had been hiding his strength.
'What's going on? With this kind of power….'
Canis and Harvest's analysis was mostly correct. But there was one thing they overlooked:
Shirone was an Unlocker who had opened the Infinite Domain.
"Last chance. Release the mind control."
Canis scowled. The fact that he could no longer underestimate Shirone wounded his pride.
"Ridiculous. Even if I die, I'll follow our master's will. You can't break my
conviction."
Shirone's gaze turned icy. The power of the Photon Cannon, enhanced by Immortal Function,
was on a completely different level.
He didn't want to kill anyone. But if Canis intended to harm the students, he had no
choice.
"Return everyone's memories. If not…."
The Photon Cannon emitted light explosively, transforming into a blindingly cold sphere
of white light.
"I'll have to hurt you."
