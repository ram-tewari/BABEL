Chapter 889:
Desperate Measures (3)
Valkyrie Headquarters.
As the mining at the Ark Mountains neared completion, Dorothy brought
the approval documents to Iruki.
"93 percent of the estimated mining output. That should be
considered a successful mission, right?"
Iruki signed the documents and nodded.
"Good work."
Dorothy's lips pouted.
"Is that all you have to say, 'good work'?"
"You know my personality."
Dorothy thought Iruki was stoic but not cold-hearted.
"You tend to deceive yourself. Honestly, you wanted to say
something else, right? Like love..."
"Let's go eat something. What do you want?"
Dorothy then realized something.
"You, something's up?"
Iruki, who never showed stress no matter the situation, seemed unusually
anxious today.
Iruki turned away silently, and Dorothy persistently prodded as she
followed.
"What's going on? You can tell me. Is it because of the
war?"
"No, don't worry..."
As Iruki grasped the doorknob, a woman's voice was heard.
"I've come to see the General!"
Dorothy peeked out as a group was arguing at the end of the
hallway.
"You! Do you know where you are? Do you think a mere battalion
commander can just meet with the Valkyrie General whenever they want?"
"You could at least talk! That bastard, I mean, the General will
surely meet with me."
"Amy?"
Dorothy's eyes widened.
"What's going on? Why is Amy here?"
"I called her."
Iruki briefly responded and gestured to Aromi.
"Let her in."
Though Iruki and Amy were friends, Aromi had her responsibilities to
maintain.
"But General, this is Valkyrie Headquarters. It's a breach of
discipline for a low-ranking officer to meet privately with the General without proper
procedure."
"Then what proper procedure is needed for a Lieutenant Colonel to
have a private meeting with me?"
Aromi's eyes tensed up.
"There is no such law."
Understanding what she wanted to say, Iruki nodded and entered the
room.
"Let her in."
Ten minutes later, Amy knocked on the door and entered.
"Amy."
Dorothy greeted her warmly, but upon seeing Amy's expression, she
withdrew her hand.
After an awkward silence, Amy adjusted her posture and saluted.
"Loyalty. Lieutenant Colonel Carmis Amy. Requesting an audience
with the Valkyrie General."
Iruki did not return the salute.
"Enough, it's embarrassing."
Then he spoke to Dorothy.
"Step out for a moment."
Realizing it wasn't a military discussion, Dorothy refused to
leave.
"No. If it's not about the military, then I have a right to listen
too."
Amy spoke up.
"It doesn't matter. I won't take long."
Iruki sighed softly.
"Alright, what do you want to say?"
"You know, right?"
Amy stepped forward.
"As of yesterday noon, my position changed. I'm now a training
officer at the Capital Defense Headquarters."
"I know."
Amy's eyebrows twitched.
"Did you order this?"
"Yes."
Her fist clenched tightly.
"Why? What's the reason?"
"Education in the rear is just as important as combat at the front.
Your training capabilities among the officers are..."
"Iruki."
Amy asked again in a low voice.
"What's the reason?"
As the atmosphere grew tense, Iruki blurted out.
"You look like you want to hit me."
"I just want to know the truth. Why did you send me to the rear?
Because I'm your friend?"
"I promise. If you're honest, I won't get angry. Was the only
reason to pull me out of danger really just that?"
Iruki's mouth slowly opened.
"Yes."
As soon as the words fell, Amy punched Iruki in the face.
With a crash, Iruki rolled on the floor as Dorothy rushed to help
him.
"Iruki! Are you okay?"
As she turned her head, the fuming Amy was raising her fist
again.
"We're not done yet."
Dorothy extended her hand.
"Not the head! Besides this, he's all brain!"
Perhaps agreeing with her point, Amy lifted her foot aiming for Iruki's
abdomen.
Dorothy reached out her hand again.
"Not there either! That's mine!" Tears welled up in Amy's
eyes.
"… What do you know?"
Iruki maintained a calm expression.
"Do you know how much I trained to get here, how many deadly
situations I've overcome?"
"I don't know."
Iruki sat up straight.
"I don't need to know all that to save you. I made the decision,
and I'll take responsibility. Just stay quietly in the rear."
Dorothy finally understood the situation.
'So that's what happened.'
She could understand the shame Amy felt and the agony Iruki must have
endured in making his decision.
Amy put down her foot.
"I can't accept this. I know it's a waste of time to have come here
for this. Send me back to the front. That's something you can do."
"No. I'm the General. If you defy orders, I can send you not to the
Capital Defense Headquarters but to military prison."
"… Is that what you want? My men, my comrades are dying, and you
want me to survive cowardly?"
"Yes."
"You…!"
As Amy was about to curse, Iruki contorted his face and screamed.
"Just stay alive!"
Seeing Iruki angry for the first time since their school days was a
first.
Iruki stood up abruptly.
"I'm already stressed enough without you making it worse! Do you
know what orders I gave? You really want to know the truth? If you go there, you'll die! Do you
understand? If you go there, you're certain to die, you fool!"
Once his emotions exploded, he couldn't contain them.
"So what if it's cowardly? I took all the responsibility to save
you! Is it so bad to have a General for a friend? Endure the shame! Just shut up, and I'll handle
everything!"
"I understand your feelings. But Iruki…"
"Shut up! How can you understand my feelings? Does my head look
like a delusional brain to you? I know you'll die if you go! Have you thought about Shirone? If you die
there, terribly, do you think I could ever look Shirone in the face? Just live, will you!"
Amy saw another face hidden in the furious Iruki.
'He's not just angry.'
Tears streamed down Amy's cheeks.
'That Iruki…'
He seemed terrified.
It was the first time he had shown such fear…
'It's not a lie.'
If Iruki says she will die, she really will.
"I understand what you're saying. And I fully grasp your desire to
save me."
She couldn't assure that she wouldn't have done the same in Iruki's
position.
"But before being Shirone's lover, your friend, I am a soldier.
That's something defined by me alone. And a soldier shouldn't…"
Iruki leaned on the desk, head bowed.
"Didn't you hear me? If you go, you'll die. If you're hell-bent on
dying, do as you wish."
Amy shook her head.
"I don't want to die. Of course, I want to live. I'd love to live
happily with Shirone and my friends. But you know? As much as I want to live…"
She struggled to hold back her tears.
"Other people want to live too. Yet they fight to protect
humanity."
Iruki spoke with a hoarse voice.
"… Every time I give an order, at least thousands die. The bad news
keeps coming. Here 10,000 have died, there 100,000. It echoes endlessly like a
hallucination."
It wasn't a job one could sustain sanely.
"It's terrible. I don't want to imagine what it's like for 100,000
to die. It appears in dreams. But no imagination…"
Iruki grasped his head.
"… can compare to thinking of you dying. Whenever I think of a face
I know, a expression I recognize, a pain I'm familiar with, I feel like I'll go mad."
"Iruki."
Amy approached Iruki with a sad smile and caressed his cheeks.
Iruki looked up with tears in his eyes.
"Once you go there, I can't help you in any situation. That's what
terrifies me."
Amy spoke with sympathy.
"Iruki…"
My precious friend.
Locked in a lonely cortical cell, how much had he thought and
agonized?
'He's the most rational.'
The situation was unbearably terrifying.
Dorothy wiped her tears.
'Amy, can't you just stay here? Since it's come to this, can't you just
live?'
The words almost burst forth, but they were mere whining against Amy's
convictions.
Amy spoke firmly.
"Don't worry. I can overcome any situation. I am a soldier. There's
nothing in this world that scares me."
She didn't say she would survive.
"Send me back to the battlefield. Just like you care about me, I
care about my men. I don't want to doom them and survive alone. Do you understand?"
"You're really stubborn."
"Ha ha."
Amy took it as an answer and slowly stepped back, then saluted.
"I will return to my unit." Even though she was heading to a
deadly fate, her eyes were more alive than when she first entered. Iuki exhaled.
"… Approved."
Finally, Amy smiled in satisfaction, opened the door, and waved to
them.
"Sorry for barging in. I've been too busy, I'll go ahead. Have a
good time."
She now had to hurry to catch up with her unit's forced march.
"Amy…"
Dorothy started to say something, but Amy had already closed the door
and left.
"She didn't want to leave any emotional ties."
Iruki walked around the table and sat in a chair, burying his face in
his hands.
"Iruki, are you okay?"
"How can I be? I've known since school she had a fierce
temper."
The reason Dorothy couldn't get closer was that Iruki's shoulders were
shaking.
"Damn it…"
I thought I could do anything.
'Sorry, Shirone. I should have stopped her, even if it meant getting
killed myself…'
Initially, that had been his intention.
"To stop her!"
He couldn't break her convictions.
"Sorry. Sorry, Shirone!"
Dorothy tenderly embraced the sobbing Iruki.
"Move! Move away!"
Authorized by Valkyrie Headquarters, Amy charged towards the
teleportation magic circle.
There was still time to catch up if she hurried.
"Wait for me, guys. I'm coming." Her comrades would welcome
her warmly, and the unit's morale would surely soar.
Amy's eyes sparkled with vigor.
"I will never give up."
Even if death awaited, she wouldn't kneel to fear.
