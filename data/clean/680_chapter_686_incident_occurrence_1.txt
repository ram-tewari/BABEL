Meanwhile, Lupist left the control room in the opposite direction of Shirone's group.
As the automatic door opened and he stepped into the hallway, Plarino was standing near
the stairs.
"How does it feel to lose someone precious to you?"
Lupist sensed an alien energy pressing against his back.
Soon, black smoke billowed up, and the pureblood vampire Raika blocked his retreat.
"Inputting the primal code to reclaim the bioweapon was a good idea. The attempt was
fine, too. But you didn't anticipate the gap in ability, did you?"
Finally turning around, Lupist's eyes were bloodshot, unblinking.
Raika smiled, as if enjoying it even more, and raised his hand.
"I finished her off with this hand. Well, not completely, but close enough. Given how
things turned out, I think it's for the best. She must have died in agony."
Lupist wasn't displeased.
That wasn't why he was displeased.
"If you fear pain, you shouldn't do such stupid things in the first place."
"Heh, so you're human after all. Your voice is trembling even as you say that. Was she
precious to you?"
Plarino spoke.
"You've failed. The bioweapon's control has returned to me, and soon the royal capital
will be destroyed."
Lupist let out a sigh.
"No. I don't care about that anymore."
Knowing how meticulous the chairman of Tormia's Magic Association was, the two of the
Three Directors looked puzzled.
"You must be angry, huh? So you're human after all."
It wasn't sympathy.
What Plarino wanted was for Lupist to taste the same feelings of despair and defeat.
"Don't misunderstand. That woman was nothing to me. She was an aide, a chief of staff. A
bit eccentric, but now that she's dead, there's no need to think about her."
The air grew heavy, as if it had turned into heavy metal, and Raika and Plarino's
expressions stiffened.
"She was competent."
A woman so capable she had helped the single-minded Lupist rise to become chairman of the
Magic Association.
"The only reason I'm angry is because of that. That talented woman, that genius who was
almost impossible to replicate no matter how much we invested in education, wasted it all on the lives of
some half-breed vermin. That's what infuriates me to no end."
Beyond a feeling, actual iron began to seep into the air.
"How will you compensate for this? The countless problems she was supposed to solve while
alive. Not even a hundred of your deaths would be enough."
"How dare you look down on vampires!"
Raika, dissolved into smoke, charged forward, and Plarino manipulated the surrounding
iron walls to press in.
"…Iron Armor Wave."
As the words left his mouth, the air around Lupist rippled, and iron barriers began to
spread.
"Guhhh!"
Raika pierced through the first iron wall with his half-materialized ability, but second
and third barriers formed, pushing him back.
"Ughhh!"
Plarino, fused with the bioweapon, crouched and screamed.
"You'd break it? Isn't this flower precious to you, too?"
"Of course it is."
The ancient weapon's value was immense, rivaling more than half the kingdom's national
power.
"You should've behaved, then."
The iron barriers surged even more violently, spreading toward the bioweapon's massive
outer wall.
"You'll regret this! You!"
A cold smile formed on Lupist's lips.
"What wouldn't I regret?"
Creeeeak—BOOM!
The bioweapon's vibration, growing toward the sun, became so intense that Shirone's group

"What's happening?"
