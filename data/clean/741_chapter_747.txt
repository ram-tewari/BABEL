Chapter 747:
Kingdom of Tormia
A month had passed since the Great Purification began.
Martial law had already been declared nationwide, and even nobles needed
a pass to enter the noble district of Creas City.
The demons that appeared when the psychic realm expanded were almost
annihilated.
However, a few stragglers, such as ghouls, still lurked in dark places,
preying on women.
"These ghouls are really getting on my nerves."
Mages from the Creas branch of the Magic Association and royal special
investigators were patrolling the streets.
"They don't eat flesh. They eat the act of eating. If there's food
in front of them, they'll stuff it in their mouths even if it means bursting their
stomachs."
The body of a noblewoman found three days ago was horribly
mutilated.
"In any hierarchy, stronger entities absorb weaker ones. Eating is
a sign of superiority."
Humans eat almost everything.
"Heavier stars absorb lighter stars. The problem is that the
biological world is based on pleasure. That's probably why ghouls mutilate corpses."
Countries around the world had already assessed the scale, combat power,
and key figures among the demons.
The investigator's cold analysis was part of this effort, but the
accompanying mage didn't like it.
"Humans are no different."
The investigator smiled.
"Surprisingly sentimental. I heard mages are cold."
"Well, seeing the world in this state, I think maintaining
composure isn't always the best approach."
Everything humanity had achieved was collapsing.
Perhaps the answer lay in the heart, a sudden insight flashed through
his mind.
"Of course, I know demons are the other side of humans. They say
they're laws created by strong desires. But demons are still demons. Most humans don't rape passing
women or steal from the rich."
The investigator added quietly.
"...Though who knows what they're thinking inside."
Why do demons exist?
As he pondered this, turning a corner, the mage suddenly stopped and
raised his arm.
"Stop."
The mages following him immediately entered the spirit zone, and the
investigator narrowed his eyes and slowly backed away.
"Who goes there? Identify yourself."
The figure walking in the darkness didn't respond, but the heavy
footsteps alone were intimidating.
"Shining."
As the mages behind him cast their spells simultaneously, the
surroundings lit up, revealing a blood-soaked swordsman.
"Oh, my..."
Judging by the congealed blood, he hadn't washed for at least a week,
but his eyes were as sharp as if he had just been born.
Rian, who had crossed the Akkad Desert to reach Creas, frowned.
The dried blood crust fell off in chunks.
"Who are you?"
While the capital and key national points were well-prepared for the
altars, the altars set up in the vast desert of a large kingdom had unleashed numerous demons.
Having cut down all of them and returned home, Rian's mind was sharp
enough to cut just by touching it.
"A demon?"
The investigator's words didn't sound like a joke, and the mage showed
hostility.
"I'm a mage from the Creas branch of the Magic Association. We're
on patrol. You can't enter without a pass."
"Patrol?"
Rian looked up at the stars in the night sky.
"You're working hard. I'm Rian Ozant. I'm on my way home, so could
you let me pass?"
"O-Ozant Rian?"
The Ozant family was well-known in Creas.
But the mage was surprised not by the family's prestige but by the name
Rian.
"The Knight of Maha. Are you really Shirone's sword?"
Few people knew Shirone's face, but the world knew the names of the
five-star resident of the Ivory Tower, Shirone, and his sword, Rian.
Rian, tired and unwilling to argue, slowly moved forward, and the mages
raised their magical weapons.
"Stop! You can't pass without identifying yourself."
"If I'm Rian, I wouldn't have such a thing. Follow me to my home,
and my family will vouch for me."
It was a reasonable suggestion, but most intruders used this tactic,
which was why passes were issued.
"Then wait here. We'll go to the Ozant home to confirm. The
situation isn't good, not just because of the demons."
Rian stepped into the spirit zone created by the five mages without
hesitation.
"I cut down thousands of demons crossing the desert."
The tribes in the desert, swept by demons, were all annihilated, and
their end was too horrific to describe.
"But after cutting and cutting, I found that it wasn't the demons I
hated, but humans."
As Rian approached, the mage's heart raced.
"I don't want to waste time."
'We can't win.'
The moment they tried anything, their heads would fall.
"So please, as quickly as possible."
The tension among the mages disappeared as if it were a lie.
The young man, with a large sword on his back, looking indifferently at
the sky, might not have shown any hostility from the start.
'I was mistaken. He never intended to fight us...'
He was so strong that they were scared in advance.
"Stand down."
At the mage's command, the subordinates opened the way, and the
investigator leaned against the wall with a hum.
"Thank you for fighting for humanity."
As the mage bowed politely, Rian nodded and walked through them.
'Is my home safe?'
The blood was washed away, but the claw marks of the ghouls on the walls
weighed heavily on his mind.
"Rian! You're safe!"
As he arrived home, guided by the assistant butler Temuran, Reina ran
out barefoot.
The smell of blood on Rian's body was strong, but since it wasn't his
blood, Reina was relieved.
"I was worried something had happened! How could you not send a
single letter?"
Rian was puzzled.
"Why are you at home?"
Even without the battles, she had a mountain of duties as a court
musician in the royal palace.
"I brought her."
Clump Ozant descended the stairs.
"Grandfather."
"What happened? I heard Shirone passed the Ivory Tower
test..."
Reina interrupted.
"First, wash up. I'll prepare dinner quickly. Butler, please use
the remaining ingredients."
"Understood."
The butler, Louis, bowed with his right arm bandaged below the
elbow.
It was a wound inflicted by a demon.
Rian showed no particular emotion and asked Clump.
"Where's the eldest brother?"
The eldest son of the Ozant family, Guy Ozant.
He had passed the public service exam early and held a key position in
the Kingdom of Tormia but had become a spy due to his strong patriotism.
"I don't know. No contact for three years. He's either still active
in another country or dead."
Guy was a painful name in the Ozant family.
"I see..."
As Rian headed to the bathroom, Clump asked.
"Are you planning to wander the world again? This
hell?"
"I have to."
Clump was proud of his grandson but also wished Rian would stay in
Tormia to protect his sister.
"Parting with Shirone is unusual. Weren't you
inseparable?"
"...That's why I left."
He didn't know what the realm was, but he knew he couldn't bear
Shirone's pain.
"If I'm around, it'll be harder for him. I'll find my own way to
help Shirone."
Clump realized he couldn't hold Rian back.
"Follow me to the training ground."
Rian and Reina looked puzzled, but Clump strode out of the mansion with
his broad back.
Rian's private training ground.
As Reina watched anxiously, Rian and Clump stood ten meters
apart.
'It's been a while.'
The place where he had run a hundred laps daily, beaten by the sword
instructor Kite.
It was also where he had lost a real sword duel to Shirone, who had
become the reason for his life.
'Grandfather chose this place for a reason...'
It was a message to his grandson, who was throwing himself into an
irreversible future, to look back one last time.
"Are you ready?"
Clump exuded fighting spirit.
"Yes."
Rian had faced many swordsmen through his knight training, but it was
his first time facing a certified third-grade swordsman.
In military terms, it was equivalent to a division commander, and above
that were only two corps commanders and one general.
"Why do the men in this family always duel?"
Reina should have been used to it by now, but this time she couldn't
keep her legs still.
'Grandfather is serious.'
Even in the dim light where only the torches provided visibility, she
felt the heat waves of Clump's fighting spirit.
"You must have crossed many lines of death. For the sake of my
grandson's honor, I'll kill you with all my might."
Rian's expression remained unchanged, and he took a slow stance that
made the observer sleepy.
"..."
Clump, who had been glaring at Rian like a tiger, suddenly lowered his
sword and extended his hand.
"Give it to me."
"Give you what?"
"Idea. It's originally mine, so I'll fight with it."
Rian stepped closer with a bewildered look.
"Why is this your sword? You gave it to me, so it's
mine."
"But we're fighting now. So I should use it, not you."
"Oh, come on! Just use any sword! Does a general want to take his
grandson's weapon?"
"Fine, I won't fight! If you don't give it to me, I won't
fight!"
Reina, Louis, and Temuran, who were watching, were all
dumbfounded.
"Don't fight, then! Calling a tired person and making unreasonable
demands!"
As Rian sheathed his sword and walked towards the mansion entrance,
Clump's eyes softened.
"You've become strong, Rian. Really strong."
Not being obsessed with victory even in front of a general meant that he
had already transcended the cycle of winning and losing.
"Someone who understands what they have doesn't covet what others
have."
"I don't understand difficult words. Anyway, it's good to know
everyone is safe. I'll eat and leave immediately."
Reina was shocked.
"Tonight? Without even sleeping?"
"I'll sleep in the carriage. Leaving right now won't change the
world, but..."
It was the secret of time that Shirone had taught him.
"The enemies are also moving without rest. In a race running on the
same time, even one minute is significant."
When Rian sleeps, the enemies sleep, and when Rian fights, the enemies
fight.
In that case, the one who saves a minute wins.
'Shirone.'
According to the rumors he heard on his way back to Tormia, Shirone was
still tirelessly traveling the world, eliminating the psychic realms.
'He must be doing it to save even one unrelated person.'
He couldn't be by his side, but this was the only way to lighten
Shirone's burden.
'Oh, you really haven't changed.'
Rian couldn't even guess how heavy the burden on Shirone's shoulders was
now as he returned to the mansion.
'A few years ago, he was my meal.'
A younger brother who had no talent, was insensitive, and did things his
own way.
'But he had one thing.'
Even if a man called Buddha tried to destroy the world, Rian would never
give up until the very end.
'Go, my brother.'
Reina looked at her brother's back as he walked away and extended her
hand.
