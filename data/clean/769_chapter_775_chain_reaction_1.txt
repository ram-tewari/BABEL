Chain Reaction (1)
As the heat of the explosion faded, Miro's group and Shura stepped a
little closer to Gaold.
In this unprecedented moment—where the Buddha had been defeated—their
senses were on edge, desperate not to miss even the faintest signal.
"Nane, who reached the state of Buddha after experiencing the entirety
of the world's fortune and misfortune…"
Zulu spoke.
"But… he never suffered more than Gaold did."
He understood human pain—but not all pain. And that's where the match
was decided.
Sein said quietly,
"So unless Gaold allows it, Nane can't close this world."
That was the reason Nane's sermon had broken.
"Exactly. For now, at least," Zulu added.
Everyone turned their attention back to Gaold and Nane's
conversation.
"Does it hurt?"
Gaold looked down at Nane as he asked.
His body was shattered, bleeding from his mouth endlessly—but his eyes
looked peaceful.
"More than anything in the universe."
Gaold gave a dry laugh and dropped to the ground beside him, pulling a
cigarette from his coat.
The moment he lit it and brought it to his lips, a cough burst
out.
"Khak! Khak!"
The shock of transcending his limit—the backlash from going beyond
"fullness"—made it feel like his bones were crumbling.
"And yet you're still smoking in that broken body?"
Gaold squinted as he took a drag, savoring it like it was the finest
thing in the world.
"Compared to the pain of living, a cigarette's nothing. But you—you
won't last long."
Gaold lowered his gaze.
"Are you scared? Of dying, I mean."
It wasn't something you were supposed to ask a Buddha. But that's
exactly why Gaold wanted to hear the answer himself.
"Not really. Life and death are no different to me."
Gaold exhaled a long stream of smoke and looked toward the wide-open
horizon.
"…Don't you ever just think about living? Forget emptiness and
ideals—just get messy, argue, and mix it up with people?"
Kangnan's eyes filled with sorrow.
'Of course.'
How could anyone hate Nane?
'The truth is, he's the one who wants to die the most. And Nane…'
…was the one who understood Gaold's heart more deeply than anyone
else.
"My thinking hasn't changed. Your pain may be endless, and still you
chose life, but…"
As he recalled the suffering of mortals, blood-tinged tears welled up in
Nane's eyes.
"Not everyone is as strong as you."
Even if pain weighs the same for everyone, not everyone has the strength
to endure it.
"…Yeah."
Gaold, who had considered death with every breath he'd taken, knew that
all too well.
"Damn it. Why is it all so complicated?"
Gaold flicked away his cigarette.
"Why does other people's pain matter so much? What about your pain? Why
do you live so damn hard?"
Nane was resolute.
"Because I know it's right."
Gaold knew there was no changing his mind—and yet he sat in silence for
a long time.
"…No way."
Miro suddenly felt anxious—afraid that Gaold might actually spare
Nane.
'The chain reaction of the Law…'
If the Buddha exists, then so must Yahweh. In the end, good can never
erase evil entirely.
"I'll do it myself."
"Miro."
Zulu's voice was cold.
"You know the truth. We're not qualified."
Only Gaold had suffered more than the Buddha.
So to accept or reject the Buddha—only he had the right.
"This is about humanity's future. If we don't end the game, countless
people will keep suffering!"
"That's your belief, not Gaold's. He's not humanity. He's just a
human."
"But still—!"
"Miro, just accept it already."
Zulu turned to her with emotionless eyes.
"Humanity has won."
It was Middle Tongue.
The one who defeated the Buddha was, after all, a human.
Faced with that undeniable truth, Virtua bit her lip.
'Gaold… just listen to me…'
Nane spoke.
"Let it end now. This world you upheld—I'll entrust it to humankind and
walk away."
Gaold looked up at the sky.
"You're not a bad guy."
Miro's face twisted with worry.
'Gaold, please…'
"But."
As if by telepathy, Gaold suddenly turned and looked straight at
Miro.
"It's just… when the woman I love is living in this world, it gets hard
to do anything drastic."
"…Huh?"
Everyone turned to look at Miro, but she couldn't even lift her
eyes.
Nane smiled faintly.
"That's enough."
Humanity had won. So the standard by which the world would now be
defined… should be thoroughly human.
"It's a good thing the one you love is Virtua."
Virtua, the one who always gives.
"I don't care about any of that. I don't want to know."
To Gaold, Miro was just a reckless woman who would tear out her own
heart if it meant giving humanity a better chance at happiness.
As he raised his hand, the air sliced with a sharp shriek, compressing
into the edge of a blade.
"Farewell."
The moment Nane closed his eyes, the wind cutter shot toward his
neck.
KRAKAKAKAKA!
Sparks burst in the air as the blade halted, and moments later, a faint,
translucent shield took form before them.
"Haa… Haa…"
Behind the shield, Shura looked down at Nane in confusion.
'He's going to die.'
It hadn't been a well-thought-out decision.
'I don't know.'
When Nane's death became real, a sliver of doubt bloomed in her
heart.
She thought she needed time to sort through her thoughts—and that alone
was enough reason to act.
'I need to get out of here.'
Just as she tried to push Gaold back with her Gestalt shield, a crushing
force pinned her to the ground.
'What is this?'
Even after exhausting her strength against Nane, the weight of this
shattering power was far beyond what she could handle.
"Kyaaaaaa!"
A divine execution, assembled from hundreds of inverted crosses,
descended upon her and flattened her.
KUUUUUUNG!
The solidified air compressed the earth itself—causing a massive
explosion.
"Get Shura!"
Since it wasn't Gaold's intention, even Zulu didn't stop Miro from
flying toward the ground.
At the center of the massive crater in Jungcheon-dong, a smaller,
daughter crater formed, and Miro's group cut through the smoke.
"What happened?"
Gaold stood in the distance, eyes fixed ahead.
"Caught her."
Then pointed to the ground.
"Half of her."
Miro looked down.
"A tail cut… how sly."
Shura's lower half, cleanly severed at the waist, was flailing and
spurting blood.
Through clenched teeth, Shura gasped for breath.
"Hhh! Hhh!"
With only her upper body, she dragged herself, shoulders trembling—yet
her speed was faster than the wind.
As they crossed the cliffs of the Jungcheon-dong mountain range and
entered a tangled forest path, Nane—lying on her back—asked:
"Why did you save me?"
"I don't know! I said I don't know! I'll think about it now,
okay?!"
Nane smiled faintly.
"You're a terrible liar sometimes."
And that's what made him doubt.
'There's no such thing as eternity in this world. When you die, you die.
But… but still…!'
Shouldn't you at least know why?
"Lies! Lies! Lies!"
As Shura pushed off the ground with both arms and launched forward,
blood poured from her waist like a torrential storm.
"There is no truth in this world!"
All things are nothing more than frequencies, and what we believe to be
real is just the illusion produced by those signals.
"Everything is a lie!"
Born as a serpent, mastering Incarnation Magic, and becoming human—Shura
had lived through countless lies.
But if that's true…
Then the ultimate conclusion reached by the god of falsehood was:
"Then what is real?"
There must be something, after all.
"Hrrngh… huff…"
Clenching her teeth, Shura, having stanched the trail of blood, dragged
herself up a steep slope and reached the summit.
"Haah! Haah!"
Panting, her chin pressed to the ground, she extended her meter-long
tongue and tilted her head back.
"You okay?"
"…Not dead yet,"
Nane added calmly.
"But I will be—unless you take care of me."
Shura narrowed her eyes.
"Tch! So even the Buddha doesn't want to die, huh? So all that stuff you

"If you see with falsehood, everything is false. If you see with truth,

"In the end, there's no difference between what you see and what I

"…"
Just like when she had faced Veron, even Shura found herself unable to

Looking up at the sky, Nane smiled.
"The weather's nice."
Nane was alive.

Though she still couldn't walk properly, she filled her mouth with water

"Mm! Mm—"

"This is good water."
"Of course it is. I went and fetched fresh bedrock water especially.
Can't you just get up already? You can heal yourself with a sermon, right?"

Shura, who had been licking the dirt off her body with her tongue,
turned back to him with a glimmer of excitement.
"Did you figure out the secret of the world?"
"If I had, I would've closed it already. No—it was about why I couldn't
be perfect."
Shura looked disappointed.
"Aw, come on. That illusion's already shattered."
"In hindsight, I think it was wrong from the beginning. Righteousness

Shura blinked.
"So that's why, no matter how high you climb—through lies or truth—you

The 11th sense: Insight beyond understanding.

"Let's go. This is something I must do."

"I speak now to the entire universe."
This was Nane's final sermon.
"With the current Law, I can no longer fully save the suffering of all

"I hereby permit the breaking of the system."

In other words—Nane could no longer be the system's
administrator.

Perhaps that's why—
Even though Nane had now become a mere human, having lost his grasp of
