Chapter 834:
Ivory Tower Grand Assembly (2)
Habitz's army left Benaford and continued their advance westward
towards Kashan's royal castle.
Although their march was unimpeded, Balkan, seated in the commander's
position, wore a contemplative expression.
Two dice continuously rolled in his hand, and his gaze was
indifferent to external stimuli.
"What are you thinking about?" Natasha asked, but there was
no response.
"Twelve came up four times in a row," Balkan finally
spoke.
"Do you think Habitz received special treatment?"
Smodo and Zetaro showed interest, realizing the significance of his
words.
"Are you talking about last night's dice game?"
"Yes. It wasn't magic or trickery. Habitz almost died last night
due to high odds."
Smodo commented, "It seems the world still wants to keep him
alive."
"No, it's quite the opposite," Balkan disagreed.
"The world doesn't care about anyone in particular. Thinking
that rolling the same number on the dice a few times means being chosen is foolish. There are much lower
probabilities. The chance of a single sperm fertilizing an egg is one in billions. But we don't think
we're chosen because of that."
Zetaro grasped the point. "So, it feels like being chosen
because of the outcome."
"Think about it. Habitz has always been like that. So let's
reverse our thinking. It's not that luck follows Habitz, but because he's still alive, Habitz is
defined."
Smodo said, "So it's not a miracle."
Balkan nodded. "Habitz could have died yesterday. If he had, we
wouldn't be having this conversation. His story would have ended there. But he lived and created a story
that will become part of legend."
Smodo, deep in thought, asked, "What is time?"
"The law that drives this world. Therefore, those bound by the
law can never turn back. Of course, there's talk of the Five Great Stars of the Ivory Tower breaking
time."
Balkan continued, "It might already be predetermined.
Ultimately, what we need to consider is whether Habitz still exists at the end of time."
Natasha asked, "How can we know that?"
"No one can guarantee it. He might survive several dramatic
crises only to die meaninglessly... Habitz has become Satan."
"Perfect chaos. A being outside the law cannot be killed by any
phenomenon in this world. It's not about miracles or sudden shields. He's already reached that
outcome."
"So, stopping and last night's dice game were just predetermined
events?"
"Yes. Being outside the law is a form of breaking the System. He
leads the army of hell, but there are many strong humans, especially in the Ivory Tower. But we know
Habitz's future."
He will never die.
"No matter what happens, no matter how grotesque or heinous the
gamble, Habitz won't die. What he possesses is not brute force but an immutable future itself. We must
fully utilize this. And if my hypothesis is correct..."
Balkan looked beyond the horizon.
"We will win this war."
Twelve Mini, a 2-star resident of the Ivory Tower, visited the 400th
floor where Shirone was resting.
Usually maintaining a small stature that could fit in the palm of a
hand, today she kept a normal proportion.
With glossy black hair and a neatly dressed suit, her expression was
different from usual.
"The Ivory Tower Grand Assembly. It's my first
time."
Following the orders of the Grand Star, all key figures of the Ivory
Tower were gathering for the largest meeting.
The last Grand Assembly Mini knew of was right after the 20 Judges
regarding the dimensional wall.
Walking beside Mini was a woman with curly blonde hair tied
back.
It was Mini's satellite, Ariana.
"I heard from Araka. There was intense power struggle among the
five departments back then."
It was more than intense; the Ivory Tower almost fell apart.
"It was expected. Those with the title of Star are all stubborn.
Even Borbor is stubborn in his own way."
Ariana held her head with both hands.
"That's why it's annoying. Summoning all the Stars, even the
satellites. It's not surprising if the Ivory Tower turns upside down. What is the Grand Star
thinking?"
"It means the situation is that bad. Anyway, we must support
Shirone well so that our department isn't overshadowed."
Ariana snorted.
"He's only twenty-two? I don't get it. How does someone become
one of the Five Great Stars of the Ivory Tower right after graduating from magic school? I understand if
it was Mahagaruta..."
Mini cautioned her.
"Ariana, this concerns the honor of the Integrated Universe
Management Department. And he's as great as Mahagaruta. He single-handedly stopped Nane."
"I know he's one of the Four Superhumans of the Law. But
philanthropy, is that really needed in this world?"
"There must be issues beyond our reach. Perhaps something that
operates at the extreme of the law..."
Ariana was impatient.
"Ah, I don't know. I'm here because I like being your 'pair.' If
he's useless, I'll dropkick him. Then I'll tag with you and double backdrop him to the ground,
hehe."
She was certainly capable of it.
"And this is annoying too. Other departments are busy with the
Grand Assembly order, and we even have to pick him up? He just got the title of Star, isn't he too
arrogant?"
Mini was also puzzled by that.
"He's not that kind of person. What's going on?"
When they arrived at Shirone's house, there was no sign of anyone
inside.
"Is he not here? We were told to come here."
When knocking received no response, Mini exchanged glances with
Ariana and opened the door.
"Excuse us..."
The moment she saw the scene inside, her face turned pale.
"Great Star!"
Shirone stood in the middle of the floor, tears of blood endlessly
streaming from his eyes.
The floor was soaked with blood as Mini hurriedly approached.
"Shirone! What happened?"
Shirone didn't respond.
He just clenched his fists and glared ahead with bloodshot
eyes.
"Shirone...!"
As Mini reached out to touch him, Ariana spoke.
"Stop. Leave him be."
"But if we leave him, he'll bleed out..."
"Calm down. Come here and look closely."
At Ariana's words, Mini slowly stepped back.
Holy light was dripping from Shirone's entire body like water
droplets.
"He's purifying."
"Purifying? What?"
"Anger. He's trying to remove every trace of anger deep within
him."
Mini shook her head.
"He's a very rational person. Having reached the level of
philanthropy, what is there to be angry about now?"
"That's why he's going through this miserable
process."
Ariana looked at the blood-soaked floor.
"Even a superhuman is still human. They have loved ones and
things they want to do. Sacrificing for the world? That's the emotion furthest from
humanity."
"How long has he been like this?"
The Grand Assembly order was given a week ago, so he must have been
tormented for even longer.
"He can't hear anything."
"He's lost his reason to anger. He's purifying with Yahweh's
light, but if he fails, he'll die."
"Then we have to save him!"
"But if he returns, he'll reach a completely different level
than Yahweh now."
Mini fell silent.
"I heard the project failed. It might be a sense of
responsibility for that. Or self-reproach for not being able to protect loved ones in a world facing
catastrophe."
"I heard he has a lover."
"Accepting the philosophy of philanthropy was possible because
of the hope of winning. But when hope disappears, doubt arises about why only he has to suffer. Consumed
by cosmic anger, he destroys himself."
"In other words, there's no hope."
There's no way to defeat the extreme evil.
The concept of an immutable future that Balkan spoke of was also
suspected by the upper echelons of the Ivory Tower.
"This Grand Assembly is in the same context. They're still
trying to fight. To become stronger."
"Purifying anger?"
"Humans are defined when they realize and act. But the heart is
different. He's trying to truly become Yahweh beyond his level. And he must reach that level to
be..."
Ariana got to the point.
"Someone we can truly respect as one of the Five Great
Stars."
Mini looked at Shirone with a sympathetic expression.
'Please.'
The blood was pain, and the flowing light was the tears of Shirone's
heart.
How much time had passed?
The sphere of light flowing from Shirone's head began to
dissolve.
"What is this?"
Then it poured over his entire body, enveloping him in a bright
glow.
'Ah, I see.'
The color of the blood flowing from his eyes gradually turned
transparent, becoming tears.
'This world is not beautiful.'
Those who say it's beautiful are just the ones who rolled the dice
well and got high numbers.
But even that is just occasional luck in a cruel gamble.
'Now I understand, Nane.'
Nane, who was defeated by Gaold.
The reason he permitted the breaking of the System for all beings,
even as he laid down his status as Buddha.
'It's not because life is beautiful. It's to make it
beautiful.'
If that's truly possible.
If you believe you can realize ideals in this hellish world, it means
breaking the rules and going all the way.
'That's why we fight.'
Accepting Nane's enlightenment to reach a higher level.
Since everyone is suffering, distinguishing between you and me
becomes meaningless.
'All things are unified.'
Just one universe.
Like burning his body, light filled with love and compassion radiated
from Shirone's entire being.
"Oh, Great Star..."
Mini and Ariana stepped back.
As the cosmic anger vanished, Shirone's eyes snapped open.
Beyond belief in love, becoming a being that enforces love.
"Now I can fight."
It was a small change, but the shift in Shirone's heart was as vast
as the universe.
Ariana smiled.
"He has transcended."
There was no trace of anguish in Shirone, who now radiated Yahweh's
light like a shimmering mirage.
"Huuu."
Finally ending his long meditation, Shirone took a deep breath and
calmly looked around.
"Huh?"
Two women in neatly tailored black suits were looking at him.
'Who are they?'
He didn't recognize the blonde, but the woman with black hair seemed
familiar.
"Mini? What brings you here?"
A small, cute woman who liked wrestling and made unabashedly sexual
jokes.
'Her demeanor is completely different.'
But today, she maintained normal proportions and wore a suit, looking
every bit the elegant lady.
'Something must be up.'
Mini spoke.
"As I thought, you didn't hear. There's a Grand Assembly. Oh,
and this is my satellite. Introduce yourself."
Nervous about possibly being rude, Ariana bowed politely.
"It's an honor to meet you. I'm Ariana, Mini's satellite and a
2-star resident."
Mini sighed in relief.
