Chapter 343:
The Protagonist of Life (3)
Kangnan took off her glasses and slipped them into her breast pocket.
Then, as if emulating a wolf, she raised both fists above her face and stepped forward with her left
foot, lightly keeping rhythm.
Shirone and his companions were baffled. Although they were unfamiliar
with Ramuai, they could guess that it was some form of fighting stance. But standing before the gigantic
statue of the Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara, it seemed rather comical.
Unable to watch any longer, Liria approached with a frown.
"Hey! What are you doing…?"
At that moment, Kangnan's figure blurred. A high kick, too fast to see,
kicked up a trail of dust. Her instep smashed right across the face of the Thousand-Armed
Avalokiteshvara.
The expression on the Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara's face turned to
one of bewilderment. It blinked its eyes rapidly, then its hand movements slowed. Finally, as if sinking
into sleep, it was sucked into the deep sea of Okwangbakyojin.
"What… what is that…?"
The shock experienced by Shirone's group was beyond imagination.
Although trapped in Okwangbakyojin, the moment her kick broke its neck was so startling that Shirone
felt his heart could have leapt from his mouth.
Shirone looked down and saw her thigh exposed beneath her tight business
skirt. At the moment of impact, the stocking on her instep had burned away, rolling up to reveal her
right leg entirely. Her muscles tensed up like chiseled sculptures, but soon, the soft subcutaneous fat
returned, revealing her smooth, sculpted legs.
'Who exactly is she?'
It occurred to him that she might be a government official. If a foreign
exorcist had come calling, it was insightful to think that any department in her own country might have
anticipated the situation.
"Huh."
Kangnan breathed out freely, then pulled her glasses out from her pocket
and put them back on. Shirone, having watched quietly, met eyes with his friends and then approached
her.
"Excuse me, who are you? Perhaps from the kingdom…
ack!"
As Kangnan suddenly lifted her skirt, Shirone's face turned as red as a
beetroot. She rolled up her stockings, took out a new pair, and began changing them, unaware or
indifferent to the boys' covert glances.
"The entity you faced is a mental being from beyond the northern
boundary of heaven, codenamed 'Lawmaker'."
"Heaven?"
Dante cocked his head. It seemed there truly were all sorts of crazy
people in the world. But for Shirone and Amy, it was a serious matter. Knowing about heaven meant this
woman was no ordinary individual.
"Actually, the Lawmaker is not so dangerous. It's just a simple
phenomenon like an aurora. But it becomes a headache when the environment changes. The golden time is 72
hours from onset. After that, it evolves exponentially. You barely caught it."
Kangnan pulled her stockings up to her belly button, then smacked down
her skirt to straighten it.
"Because you judged well, I'll overlook the unilateral actions. If
the Lawmaker had expanded its range, the military would have been deployed. You saved many
lives."
Having said that, Kangnan turned around and put on her high heels. She
adjusted her outfit which had shifted during her kick.
"But could you first tell us who you are?"
As Shirone removed his hood and approached, Kangnan's eyes narrowed
slightly upon seeing his face.
'He looks younger in person. That he could defeat a Lawmaker…'
Even an initially awakened Lawmaker is ranked as a B-grade phenomenon by
Valkyrie. A regular angel is A-grade, so essentially, he fought an opponent comparable to a fallen
angel.
'But how did he block the law?'
If it were the <Magical Sword Armand> given to him as a gift by
Princess Uorin, it might have somewhat defended against the law. But he definitely could not have
defeated the Lawmaker with that alone.
'Could it be…'
Kangnan could only think of one possibility.
'Mental Transcendance? No, that can't be. An eighteen-year-old couldn't
possibly have reached that state.'
It is said that all beings ultimately progress towards awakening their
divine nature, but because the process is so lengthy, most die without discovering themselves within
their given lifespan.
The exception was heaven.
In that place, governed by Anke Lae where lifespans are determined,
there are humans who live for hundreds of years and plants that live for hundreds of thousands of
years.
This is why the art of divine awakening is called the technology of
heaven.
The time to reach enlightenment varies among species; on average, it
takes humans 220 years, cats 400 years, monkeys 600 years, snakes 700 years, and foxes 1,000
years.
If Shirone had indeed achieved a transcendental mental state, then he
would have effectively bypassed around 200 years.
'It seems there was no exaggeration in the association's
evaluation.'
Kangnan was not a person of leisure. Furthermore, she was one of the
trusted members of the association.
If it hadn't been for the direct orders of her superior, Gaold, she
wouldn't have come just to meet a magic school student.
Kangnan recalled the situation at the time.
"Are you talking about Shirone Arian of Alpheus Magic
School?"
"Yes. That miserable old school of mine. How it hasn't collapsed
yet, I'll never know."
Gaold joked, but Kangnan, catching his intent, sharply
interjected.
"Surely you're not thinking of involving him in this
project?"
As expected, Gaold didn't respond.
"I'm against it. The risk is too high. You're basing this on one
instance of Ataraxia, not to mention jeopardizing 20 years of preparation."
Gaold's expression turned serious as he finally spoke.
"I've heard he's growing fast."
Kangnan scoffed. No one in the association knew more about Shirone than
she did.
"Of course, he's one of the promising representatives of the
kingdom. He's an Unlocker who can impart mass to light. Probably, if he continues to accumulate
achievements diligently, he'll attain level 6 by the age of twenty. Excellent managers are a rarity in
any organization."
Kangnan narrowed her eyes and gauged Gaold's reaction. As expected, he
kept his lips tightly sealed.
"Are you joking now? He's just level 6. The tutors teaching him,
Romi Ethela and Olipher Siina, were certified level 6 by age twenty. But even they weren't the youngest.
Such geniuses are commonplace in the kingdom. If you're not planning to wait another ten years, it would
be right to give up on Shirone."
"I don't know. Youngest or whatever, I'm not a genius."
Gaold, with a cigar in his mouth and leaning back, looked up at
Kangnan.
"But right now, I'm the best, aren't I?"
Kangnan couldn't deny it. Although not a brilliant student, Gaold was
now indisputably the greatest magician in the Kingdom of Thormia.
"That's an exceptional case…"
Gaold raised his hand to stop her.
"Ah, forget it. Just do as I say. Whether he becomes level 6 at 20
or 40, that kind of thing isn't important."
"No. In reality, numerous scouts are measuring talent based on
performance over time. If that's not important, then what is?"
Gaold, with a cigar between his fingers, exhaled a careless cloud of
smoke, deep in thought.
"In the end, what matters is what you accomplish."
Kangnan wanted to argue but held her tongue. The man was insane, but
sometimes he rendered her speechless.
"Mongrel. Since I've reached the pinnacle of magic…"
Gaold arrogantly tilted his chin up. He was the only one who could call
the last descendant of the Wolf Tribe a mongrel.
"Being the best isn't about being number one. It's about being an
entity completely detached from the hierarchy. Sure, following the path others have paved quickly can be
considered a talent. But look at the world. Those we call the best are illuminating realms of the
unknown that humanity could not reach even in a lifetime. It's not about being first or second. It's not
even on that level. There's nothing to compare it to. It's a completely different issue from just
following an existing path quickly."
Kangnan mulled over Gaold's words several times.
"Do you think Shirone can become the best?"
"I can't guarantee it. But his growth rate is breaking norms, and
he can wield Ataraxia. If these two factors align at the right time, he might just reach that state
momentarily. If so, he could create at least one more variable."
The 20-year project was still a challenge to the impossible. In such a
scenario, one variable was worth any sacrifice.
However, Kangnan couldn't accept it. Placing baseless faith in an
uncertain subject in a matter as critical as exchanging the lives of all humanity was not
prudent.
"I understand. But you know what? The right timing, specific
circumstances, momentarily, at least. Most of what you said is based on assumptions."
Finally, Gaold seemed to relax and smiled slightly.
"I know. That's why I'm telling you to meet him. Check with your
own eyes. If there's even a 0.1 percent chance to increase the success rate, shouldn't we use everything
at our disposal?"
Snapped back from her recollection, Kangnan addressed Shirone.
"I am Ahoya Kangnan, the secretary general of the Magic
Association."
"Ma, Magic Association secretary general?"
Shirone's eyes widened in surprise. His friends watching from behind
couldn't hide their astonishment either. Being the secretary general was virtually the second highest
position in the magic association, a person one might never meet in a lifetime as a magician.
Shirone quickly bowed his head.
"Ah, hello. I am…"
Kangnan cut to the chase.
"Shirone, it's a national emergency. Someone wants to meet you.
Let's go to the capital together."
Shirone looked dazed.
The phrase 'national emergency' was shocking enough, but it was strange
that someone would seek out a mere student in such circumstances.
"Who wants to meet me?"
Kangnan spoke nonchalantly.
"Micae Gaold, the president of the Thormia Magic
Association."
"Micae Gaold?"
Shirone's heart raced. It was the most frequently heard name he had
never met in his life.
Not just for Shirone. In the Kingdom of Thormia, there was no one
involved in magic who didn't know Micae Gaold.
Dante, curious, asked.
"Why would such a high-ranking official seek out
Shirone?"
Shirone was just as curious, but a part of him had a guess.
"Is it related to the Ice Queen?"
"Correct. The Ice Queen is an entity from heaven. Do you understand
what it means for such a being to descend to this world?"
Shirone's eyes flashed. The first thing that came to mind upon hearing
that a being from heaven had descended was the ultimate war.
"Could it be?"
"Yes. There has been a fissure in Miro's Space-Time."
If the only barrier holding back the armies of heaven had been
compromised, Kangnan's mention of a national emergency made sense.
Liria interjected quietly.
"Wait a moment. This Space-Time of Miro's, is it the Barrier of Law
mentioned in the prophecies?"
Kangnan looked Liria up and down.
"Are you a scholar?"
"Akeanis."
"Ah, I see."
Kangnan nodded and continued to explain.
"For now, it's not an immediate crisis. It was a small crack, and
it has been repaired for the moment. But a crack is always the start of a collapse, and things can only
get worse from here. We need to prepare for that."
"So, is Ms. Miro right now…"
Kangnan raised her hand to cut off Shirone's question.
"That's all I can tell you for now. The rest you'll have to hear
directly from my superior. Come with me to the capital."
Shirone looked thoughtful, considering his options.
