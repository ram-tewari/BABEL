Chapter 44:
Limitless (2)
Shirone headed to the integrated training ground early in the morning. Starting today,
classes were conducted in a tense atmosphere due to exam preparations.
The exam subjects varied by class. Shirone's Class Five had a targeting test, commonly
called the Speed Gun.
"Ugh! From today, it's just Speed Gun until we die. It's my worst subject. Targeting
types have it easy. They're at such an advantage."
"Not really. Targeting types are better against a few elite opponents. The practical
exam targets pour out endlessly. Why not switch to offensive and blast everything in sight?"
"Then you'll just tire out first. Anyway, it's either offensive or targeting. No one
uses evasion. How could you keep up with so many?"
"Speaking of which, what about Iruki? Is he using evasion again this time?"
"Haha! Seriously, only an idiot would do that. I'm curious about Shirone too. He's
strong in actual combat. But since he's specialized in defense, I wonder what he'll choose."
The students' conversations occasionally mentioned Iruki and Shirone. There were others
with better grades, but the two were overwhelmingly expected to show something decisive when it mattered.
But Shirone wanted to cry. Far from being strong in actual combat, he hadn't even
succeeded in photon emission yet. Without active magic, the practical exam would be a zero.
'Ugh! What should I do? I'm really worried.'
As Shirone agonized, the sound of a dog barking came from the edge of the training
ground. The girls noticed first, then the boys rushed over.
"What's that? How did it get here?"
"Must be a wild dog from the mountains. But why is it barking like that?"
As the crowd gathered, the dog grew more wary. It bared its teeth and growled.
Dogs were generally thought to wag their tails at people. But displaying violence made
even a dog unpleasant to be around.
"Hey! Get lost! You're disgusting!"
Class Seven kids started throwing stones. Upperclassmen watched but didn't intervene.
They thought it was no different from a wild animal since it disliked people.
But Shirone was different. After listening to the barking for a while, he realized
something and ran over.
"Wait! Stop throwing stones."
Though he'd been their classmate not long ago, Shirone was now their senior, so the kids
froze. Mark, Class Seven's leader, stepped in.
"H-hey! Stop it! Senior Shirone said to stop!"
Shirone approached for a closer look. Mark followed nervously.
"Senior, it's dangerous. This is a wild dog."
"No matter how wild, dogs don't bite people. They'd rather run away."
"But it's barking like that."
"Maybe…"
Shirone fearlessly approached. The dog growled menacingly but didn't attack.
Shirone stared into its eyes and herded it toward the forest. As expected, a puppy lay
whimpering in the grass.
"It's hurt. Poor thing."
"Huh? Really? Guys, there's a puppy here, a puppy!"
"Don't call them yet. Even a dog can turn dangerous when its pup is
threatened."
"Oh, right. Hey! Don't come closer!"
"Can you find someone who knows healing magic? It'd be great if Senior Seriel comes,
but anyone from Class Five or below is fine."
"No need. Maria can do it."
Maria, who'd attended the school since childhood, knew many types of magic. Moreover,
meeting Sard had given her peace of mind, so she got along well with her classmates. When Mark called, she
came running.
"Oh my! How did this happen?"
"Probably got caught in a territorial fight. Can you fix it?"
"Yes, Senior. This is easy."
Maria cast a healing spell, and the puppy's wounds closed. Revitalized, it bounded around
the grass. The mother stopped barking and led the pup back into the mountains.
Saving a life was always heartwarming. Even the stone-throwers watched the forest with
emotion.
"You're amazing, Senior. How did you know there was a puppy?"
"Huh? Haha! I used to live in the mountains."
Shirone recalled a hunting trip with his father when he was about 11.
That day, they were lucky to find a red bear with valuable claws tangled in a rope. A red
bear's strength could snap the rope, so Vincent quickly nocked an arrow.
Shirone waited nervously. Catching the bear would mean no worries about meals for a
while.
But the arrow didn't fly. Puzzled, Shirone turned to see Vincent putting away his bow and
preparing to leave.
"Well, they say too much luck is bad. Let's head back, Shirone."
"Huh? Why not take it? Mom would love it."
Vincent crouched and pointed where the bear was trapped.
"Look there, Shirone."
Shirone looked closely. Beside the trapped mother, a cub whimpered.
"No matter how much of a hunter you are, you don't take a mother with cubs. Animals
may be beasts, but their love for their young is no different from humans'."
"But then we're going home empty-handed. That's a great catch right there."
Vincent stroked Shirone's head warmly.
"That bear has cubs just as I have you. As a parent, how could I ignore that?
Fighting to survive is the same for humans and beasts. But you mustn't do it in front of your children.
That's an unwritten rule for all living things."
That day, Shirone felt his father's denial. Perhaps Vincent hadn't spared the bear but
protected his own child. The certainty that his father would always protect him warmed Shirone's heart.
***
The students were moved by Shirone's story. They didn't know it was about his father, but
the impact was no less profound.
"I see. Shirone, you're really cool."
"Right. Shirone is so kind."
His classmates saw him differently. They'd thought he was rigid and only cared about
magic, but his warm heart was an unexpected charm.
"What foolish logic. No hunter spares prey for such reasons."
The warm mood was doused with cold water. The kids glared at the speaker. Iruki stood
with his hands behind his back, looking at Shirone.
"Not killing animals with cubs is to prevent the cubs from growing up to attack
humans. In biology, it's called imprinting. Once they see humans as enemies, their offspring inherit the
trait. That makes hunting difficult. But killing the cubs would deplete the species. It's the wisdom of
hunters who can only hunt in their own territory."
Shirone was furious. Of course, Iruki didn't know, but his words insulted his father's
denial.
"How can you say that? Sure, some hunters might think that way. But most spare
animals out of genuine compassion."
"Hearts. Emotions. These things are just packaging. I'm talking about reality. I
don't dazzle people with foolish idealism like you."
"What? You've said enough!"
Shirone jumped up and glared at Iruki. It was the second round of their argument from the
Supernatural Spiritual Science Research Club.
The students were stunned. Shirone wasn't the type to fight, and Iruki didn't get along
with anyone. Their clash sent tension skyrocketing.
"What's this? Did something happen between them? They act like they've fought
before."
"They're rivals. They're both top candidates for promotion, after all."
Shirone glared with intensity. Iruki, seemingly uninterested in fighting, looked around
distractedly.
"Hey, hey! What's with you two? You were quiet for a while. The teacher's coming, so
let's go back."
Nade's intervention prevented a full-blown fight, but Shirone was left in a foul mood.
Iruki's mindset was incomprehensible. Researching equations to wipe out cities, treating
lives lightly—everything about him was disagreeable.
'Ugh, I shouldn't engage with him anymore.'
Class began, and Etela came up the mountain. The students gaped at her unusual
appearance.
Perhaps because it was exam period? She wore a training outfit with short sleeves and
exposed thighs for ease of movement.
Most shocking was that she wasn't wearing glasses.
The boys were speechless at the beautiful woman before them. Could glasses really change
one's impression so much?
"You know there's special training starting today, right? Due to the training
ground's schedule, Class Five's Speed Gun test will be first. From tomorrow, each class will have their own
time slots, so keep that in mind. Today's class will start with a demonstration by the assistant. Samuel,
come here."
A tall, handsome blond man stepped forward. He appeared to be in his early twenties, with
unusually long hair for a man, reaching his waist.
"This assistant is Samuel, who's preparing for graduation in the senior class. His
major is incantation magic, and he's here today to demonstrate magic. Since it's related to Spirit Zone
enhancement training, pay close attention."
Whoaaaaa!
The girls welcomed Samuel enthusiastically. After feasting their eyes on the boys during
integrated classes, the appearance of a handsome man was thrilling.
"Nice to meet you. It feels like just yesterday I was in the advanced class. Time
flies. My skills are lacking, but I'll do my best to contribute to your education."
Shirone watched Samuel with interest. This was a chance to see the rival competing with
Amy in the senior class. Especially since incantation magic was an unfamiliar major.
Samuel entered the Image Zone and expanded his Spirit Zone. The students gasped. A
42-meter-diameter sphere enveloped him, similar in size to Shirone's.
"Incantation magic strengthens magical power through the power of language. First,
I'll cast Tornado without incantations. Tornado."
Samuel raised his hands, and a powerful whirlwind erupted. Some in the advanced class
could use Tornado, but Samuel cast it as easily as a basic spell.
"Remember the power of that. Now, I'll enhance Tornado with incantation magic.
Literally using the power of language. By savoring the will embedded in words, a mage's mental strength
grows."
Samuel cleared his throat and recited an eight-stanza poem.
"O land of fervor, lend me thy strength, caress my hand. The sky gazes down, and
history is etched in moments. When the power of the void dwells in my palm…"
Before the first stanza ended, the students' expressions soured. The obscure, meaningless
poem bored them. Someone yawned, but Samuel wasn't offended and finished the poem. Then he raised his hands
again.
"Tornado."
Whoooooosh!
A massive whirlwind shot skyward with a deafening roar. The students froze, battered by
wind so strong it stole their breath. Samuel's figure wavered like a mirage.
"This is the power of incantation. Language carries a mage's will. Savoring that
will strengthens magic. Speak, reflect. That's the basic framework of incantation magic. Now, let's begin in
earnest."
