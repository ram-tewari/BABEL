Chapter 647:
After resting at the Ogent estate, Shirone and Rian finished preparing
for their departure.
They couldn't rest for long as they had to face strong competitors and
take the Ivory Tower test.
During the three-day rest period, Tess, who had come down from the
capital, was making lunch boxes with Amy for the two departing.
"Are you sad, Amy?"
"……"
Tess, who had always supported Amy, was furious about the situation
where she had to part ways with Shirone.
"Don't just stay silent. Say something. Tell him you'll wait, that
he shouldn't meet other girls, or where and when you'll meet again."
What frustrated Tess the most was that no promises were exchanged
between the two.
Amy shook her head with a sad smile.
"I don't want to. It's pointless anyway."
"Why is it pointless? Even saying such things makes a man more
careful with his actions."
Amy's hands, which were making rice balls, stopped.
"Tess, do you remember when we went to Galliant?"
Of course, she hadn't forgotten.
It was a journey where they fought the Parrot Thieves and experienced
extraordinary events in heaven.
"I said back then that sometimes Shirone seemed faint."
"Yes, I remember. You said it seemed like Shirone viewed the world
omnisciently."
"In the Ivory Tower, they call that Kar. The ability to analyze
phenomena objectively, excluding oneself."
That's why the residents of the Ivory Tower place absolute trust in the
stars.
"Hmm, he certainly has that quality."
"They say the worst person in the world is the one who has harmed
you. It means humans are still trapped in their own existence. But Shirone seemed a bit
different."
"You mean he doesn't judge based on himself?"
"Yes. Of course, mages always strive to be objective, but Shirone
even denies himself. When he embraced Marsha, when he forgave the royal Pony. Or maybe this is just my
perspective, and Shirone might have been following the flow of logic from the beginning."
"That seems a bit extreme. Shirone is rational, but he's not
cold."
"Honestly, I don't know."
Amy thought of Shirone's Kar score.
"A Kar score close to 90% means only 10% of subjectivity is
involved in his judgments. It's certainly not very human. Most people act with an expectation of
emotional premium, but Shirone's actions are solely focused on achieving his goals."
Tears welled up in Amy's eyes.
"That's why I don't say anything. Words like 'I'll wait' or 'let's
promise the future' won't affect Shirone at all."
With a thud, the kitchen knife stuck into the cutting board.
"That's not true, Amy!"
Tess grabbed Amy's shoulders with both hands.
"People's hearts aren't determined by numbers. Surely, you're
within that 10%. Your feelings will fill that 10% more than the 90%."
"Do you think so?"
Amy wiped her tears with her sleeve.
"Do you think Shirone won't forget me?"
"Of course not! If you, who know Shirone best, are anxious, what
will happen?"
"Yes. I believe. I believe in Shirone."
In a situation where they had to part indefinitely and with the
evaluation of the top institution, the Ivory Tower, her heart had unknowingly weakened.
Tess, hugging Amy with her broad chest, smiled and said.
"Let's make the best food. If not for us, would those fools even
eat properly?"
Finally, Amy regained her smile and nodded.
"Haha! Yes, you're right."
With two horses waiting, Shirone received Amy's still-warm lunch box
with both hands.
"Thank you, Amy. I'll enjoy it."
"Take care. And… write to me sometimes."
Even the request to write was burdensome, but Shirone nodded
willingly.
"Yes. I'll contact you once I settle down."
Rian, checking the time by the position of the sun in the sky, turned to
the horse and said.
"Let's go. We don't have time."
Tess glared.
"Hey! You don't know when you'll see each other again, and you
don't even say goodbye?"
"We'll see each other someday. Shirone has to compete with
incredible people now. Goodbye isn't important."
Tess was taken aback by the indifferent response.
'What kind of guy is this?'
Rian added bluntly.
"Just graduate from swordsmanship school. Join us
quickly."
Finally, a smile appeared on Tess's face.
"Hmph, of course, I will. I'll graduate at the top and rise much
higher than a dropout like you."
"Hahaha! You don't know yet, but by then, I'll be a great swordsman
known all over the world."
Although they seemed to be bickering, Amy envied the two who were
talking about each other's futures.
"Oh, I almost forgot this!"
Shirone, who had put the lunch box in the bag hanging from the saddle,
took out Armand from the cubic.
Having made mental progress during the graduation exam, Armand must have
undergone some changes as well.
Amy, with her hands on her hips, said.
"Hmm, right. Now that you're a mage, you can carry
weapons."
"Yes. I'll try to wear it as much as possible."
When he activated the Diamond Armor, the blade split and wrapped around
Shirone, filling the metal frame with organic material.
Multiple focus points detected. Function reset.
Armand, having confirmed the state of quantum superposition, attempted
another change following heaven.
"Huh?"
The pristine white robe had transformed into a calm gray hooded cloak
with a slightly worn look.
The changes weren't just in appearance; the first noticeable difference
was the absence of the artificial brain.
'It's because of the quantum superposition.'
When the artificial brain operates in a state where thoughts overlap in
the force dimension, it becomes much harder to integrate into Ultima.
'The Valhalla action is still disabled.'
It seemed that Teraze hadn't paid the price yet, but according to Miro,
it wouldn't take long.
With the disappearance of the artificial brain, Akamai's antithesis
function seemed to have moved to Armand's robe.
"Hmm."
When the antithesis was activated, the robe split as if cut by a knife,
and dozens of eyes blinked.
"Ugh!"
Tess grimaced, but from a tactical perspective, it eliminated blind
spots.
When combined with Ringer's carapace ability, which reacts instantly to
shocks, it provided the best user protection.
'Huh? This is gone?'
The biggest change was that the tentacles of the devourer Kuzen no
longer appeared.
'It's because my mindset has changed.'
When the Diamond Armor was transformed in heaven, survival was the top
priority, leading to extreme trait manifestation.
Kuzen's tentacles had the ability to absorb energy by chewing and
swallowing external organisms.
While it had the advantage of delivering physical shocks, it didn't
align with Shirone's nature.
'The most important thing is that it's incompatible with quantum
superposition.'
An overly active ability would be harder to control as the number of
overlapping events increased.
"Wait, then how does it absorb energy?"
Shirone, integrated with Armand, immediately realized the change.
"Aha, it eats me."
"It eats… you?"
"The efficiency of energy metabolism has increased. So when I eat
something, Kuzen eats my body again."
It was a more stable operation than seeking energy externally.
"But it's creepy, thinking it eats you."
"Though I say 'eat,' it just accelerates metabolism. It's like
losing weight when you starve."
After all, living beings survive by digesting their own bodies.
"But it will drain your stamina. Isn't that dangerous?"
"It's fine. I decide when to activate it. Especially, Kuzen's
metabolic digestion is very useful against Skima."
Moreover, when combined with Kenser's cell regeneration ability, it
would add defense against internal shocks.
'In other words, a four-stage defense system through Armand.'
In the first stage, Akamai's antithesis restrains, and in the second
stage, Ringer's carapace protects the user.
In the third stage, Kuzen's accelerated metabolism strengthens the body,
and finally, Kenser's ability regenerates cells.
Of course, real combat differs from theory, but with such a defense
system, any enemy could be faced.
'Next is attack power.'
Galtomic's ability to enhance the law would strengthen not only the
power of magic but also the incarnation of the Light Angel.
There was also a technical change in pure magic amplification.
The magic amplifiers mounted on both gauntlets were integrated into the
right palm, increasing efficiency.
'This is better. Obsessing over balance prevents creating variables when
needed.'
This also aligned with Shirone's nature.
"I like it."
From form to function, it felt like he had finally shed the extreme pain
experienced in heaven and donned clothes that fit him.
"As long as you like it."
Rian spoke nonchalantly, but Amy was amazed at Armand's ability to
enhance the entire mind-body system.
Shirone's abilities demonstrated in the graduation exam had already
proven to create tremendous synergy, and now Armand's functions were added.
'How strong is Shirone now?'
On the other hand, Shirone liked that the style, which had been more for
decoration, had changed to a field-use design.
"I think I prefer these shabby clothes."
Honestly, the thought of walking around in a pristine white robe made
his face flush.
"The mobility has improved, and most importantly…"
Shirone fluttered his sleeves and smiled brightly.
"It's waterproof."
Amy looked puzzled, but for Shirone, who would wear it continuously, it
was an important issue.
Tess said.
"But for the most expensive clothes in the world, it's a bit plain.
Good clothes have the designer's name on them. How about adding something like that?"
"Aha, right."
Thinking the suggestion was reasonable, Shirone closed his eyes and
activated Ringer's carapace.
Crack. Crack.
As the metallic lines moved on the soft sleeve, the name Arian Shirone
was engraved.
"How about this?"
True to his modest nature, he only engraved one line, but it looked much
more luxurious.
"Yes, it looks great. Now you really seem like a mage."
Shirone looked at Amy with affection.
If he tried to express his feelings in words, a day wouldn't be enough,
so they exchanged their farewells with their eyes.
"Then, I'll be back! Take care, everyone!"
As Shirone and Rian, mounted on their horses, waved and quickly moved
away, Tess placed a hand on Amy's shoulder.
"Don't worry, Amy. Just now…"
"Yes, I saw it too."
Amy nodded, tears streaming down her face.
"Shirone."
As they left the main house of the Ogent family and entered the mountain
path, Rian asked.
"Why didn't you engrave Amy's name too? She seemed to want it. It
wasn't a difficult thing to do."
Shirone smiled contentedly and stared at the sleeve with his name
engraved.
Then, after a moment, he turned the sleeve inside out to show the
lining.
"I already did."
Inside the name Arian Shirone, another line of text sparkled in the
sunlight.
Karmis Amy.
