Chapter 36:
The Uncrossable Bridge (3)
The Spirit Zone was an extremely sensitive mental state. Even the slightest loss of focus
made it impossible to maintain. Eventually, the students in the rear group failed to harness the omnipotence
of teleportation and began to plummet.
"Aaaaah!"
"S-save us! Please save us!"
Wahahahaha!
The seniors watching from the other side burst into laughter. Having already experienced
the uncrossable bridge themselves, they found the floundering juniors utterly adorable. The teachers, too,
smiled as they meticulously recorded the evaluations of the eliminated students in their documents.
But the festive atmosphere was shattered in an instant.
Almost simultaneously, the teachers looked up with startled expressions. There should
have been plenty of time left before the air net activated, yet the siren hadn't sounded.
"What's going on! What happened! The control room!"
"I'll check it out!"
Sard activated spatial teleportation. A flash of light shot into the sky as he curved
toward the control room.
"I'll save the students first!"
Siena dove off the cliff, performing teleportation at a terrifying speed. Etella, who
had been watching from the other side, also sensed something was wrong and leaped down.
"Headmaster, what should we do? Should we halt the exam?"
Alpheus let out a sigh. With two officially recognized 6th-class mages on the scene, the
students would likely be safe. However, whether to continue the exam was another matter entirely.
From the school's perspective, canceling the exam would be the easier option. But doing
so would unfairly disadvantage the students who had fought tooth and nail to maintain their lead. As long as
safety was guaranteed, it was better to respect the participants' determination.
"Let's observe for now. We can protect about 20 students easily enough. Ending it here
would be a disservice to their passion, wouldn't it?"
From the abyss, Siena and Etella surged upward simultaneously, followed by the students
trapped in the air net magic.
"What's happening? Something must have gone wrong."
Seriel scanned the sky with a telescope as she spoke. The other students buzzed with
confusion over the unexpected situation.
But Amy remained unfazed. It was unthinkable for a fatal accident to occur under the
watch of a 6th-class teacher. Moreover, the leading students showed no signs of panic. She would have done
the same. With Class Five on the line, who cared about safety?
In contrast, chaos erupted among the middle-ranked group. Seeing the teachers leap down
to rescue students, they realized something was wrong.
'The safety measures are off?'
Until now, they had endured the fear because no matter how realistic the test was, it
wasn't real. But now, they were truly floating over a thousand-foot abyss.
"Aaaaah! No!"
"Teacher! Save me! I forfeit the exam!"
"Are we going to die here?!"
The middle-ranked group began falling one after another. A surreal scene unfolded as
teachers flashed like beams of light, rescuing the students.
Amidst this, the leading group's charge never wavered. They, too, knew the safety
measures had failed, but they cheered instead.
'This is our chance! The more who fail, the better.'
These were children who had been called geniuses unparalleled in the world before
enrolling in the magic school. Their competitive spirits were on a completely different level from ordinary
kids. The leading group had already advanced 270 meters—an impressive feat for Class Seven.
Shirone still held first place. Mark and his team alternated overtaking him, but he
always reclaimed his position in the next turn. He had chained 27 teleportations, each leap covering exactly
10 meters without the slightest error.
'Focus only on the present moment. Forget the concept of chaining.'
The mechanical routine Shirone had refined for this test was paying off. Under certain
conditions, humans could achieve precision surpassing machines—thanks to the unconscious drive of repetition
compulsion. Those who harnessed compulsion were terrifying. Once they resolved to do something, they would
break through barriers like machines.
'Damn it! He's relentless! What are the others even doing?'
Mark was frustrated. Shirone's unyielding lead left no opening for his team to interfere.
Glancing sideways with a sharp glare, he locked eyes with a female teammate, who flinched. His silent
pressure conveyed the message: it was time for the last resort.
The method was mutual destruction.
But with the safety measures off, she hesitated. Was this really the right thing to do?
'No. There's no choice. If I die here or live a failed life, it's the same either way.'
She pushed her concentration to its limit. By abandoning the goal of completing the test
and focusing solely on teleportation, she could briefly take the lead.
Chaining high-speed teleportations, she cut in front of Shirone. Simultaneously, another
teammate blocked his left path.
With his front and side blocked, she slowed down, forcing Shirone to reduce his leap
distance.
=======================================
'Sorry. We've got to survive too.'
A male teammate closed his eyes tightly and teleported toward Shirone.
If they collided, fatal injuries were inevitable.
"Ugh!"
A groan of fear escaped him, but the expected impact never came.
Opening his eyes, he saw Shirone still beside him.
'He changed direction.'
Avoiding the collision was better than mutual destruction, but the loss in distance meant
victory was now out of reach.
The female and male teammates exchanged satisfied looks as they fell, and Mark seized
Shirone's lead.
'Damn it…!'
The disruption broke Shirone's repetition compulsion. Gritting his teeth, he focused to
regain his teleportation tempo.
"Didn't their movements seem off just now?"
The question came from the advanced-class students.
"I thought so too. Maybe they got tangled up pushing too hard? They wouldn't actually try
to crash, right?"
"No, they did it on purpose."
The Class Five students turned to look.
The owner of the steely, thin voice was Melkoadin Iluki of Class Five.
Previously, he had given Shirone advice in the Image Zone. His gaunt frame, mismatched
eyes, and uneven teeth made him stand out.
"How can you be so sure? Without confirmation from the parties involved, it's just
speculation."
Iluki clenched his fist and said, "One blocked the path while the other struck from the
side. A brilliant strategy to simultaneously seal teleportation's instantaneity and spatial leap. If Shirone
hadn't dodged, the match would've ended there. Though, if it were me, I'd have calculated the motion
equations and slipped through at the last second."
His classmates turned back to the test site.
'What a show-off.'
Meanwhile, Shirone, now trailing Mark, grew increasingly anxious.
The remaining distance was 350 meters.
By the halfway point, all participants except Shirone and Mark had been eliminated.
'I've won! I've won!'
The moment Mark felt certain of victory, a thrill ran through him.
No one stood in his way, and despite nearly 40 teleportations, he felt no fatigue.
'Today, I'm different. I'm the one who'll pass!'
Images flashed through his mind: the fast-track promotion to Class Five, the envious
faces of his peers, the humiliation of the Class Six seniors.
"Hahaha! I'm first!"
Mark accelerated his teleportation tempo, widening the gap with Shirone.
'Then…'
Shirone's rhythm suddenly quickened. The sonic booms of his teleportations echoed like
drumbeats.
The students' excitement peaked.
"Shirone's catching up! This is the climax!"
"Mark! Faster! Faster, faster, faster!"
Then, a strange change occurred in Mark's vision.
The rails of the uncrossable bridge bent geometrically, rushing toward him at incredible
speed.
Even a graze would mean broken bones at minimum, and losing balance would guarantee a
fall.
-Warning. Warning. Activating the uncrossable bridge's mechanism. Level 10. Level 10.
The advanced-class students panicked.
"Level 10?"
The highest difficulty of the uncrossable bridge.
Even the top classes of the advanced division had never seen the rails bend in such a
terrifying manner.
"What's going on! Why is the mechanism activating suddenly?"
The teachers were in disarray.
Not only were the safety measures off, but the highest-level traps had been triggered.
"What's Sard doing! He went to the control room!"
"Headmaster, what should we do? The promotion exam…"
Alpheus clicked his tongue as he watched the bent rails bear down on Shirone and Mark. He
wanted to respect the students' resolve, knowing how seriously they took the graduation exam, but Level 10
of the uncrossable bridge changed everything.
'There's no discrimination at this level. Those kids' class can't possibly pass.'
Alpheus spoke bitterly. "Cancel the exam and deactivate the mechanism."
As his words faded, a streak of light shot into the sky above the test site.
It was Siena, heading to the control room to find Sard.
