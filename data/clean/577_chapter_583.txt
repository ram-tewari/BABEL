Chapter 583:
All the Scramble participants' eyes were fixed on a single point in the
sky.
Electricity generated from the ground discharged into the atmosphere,
causing lightning to strike back down from the sky.
"What is that?"
Hershey was so captivated by the spectacle that he delayed analyzing
it.
The only time he had seen such lightning in a real battle was when the
Electric Monster Lycan faced a professional mage.
'That's where Nade is.'
The lightning was so violent that he couldn't even think of using Sona
to search.
Finally, a massive light flashed as if cracking the world, followed by a
thunderous explosion from the peak.
Then, silence.
'Nade! Wait!'
Sabina quickly retraced her steps.
Even she hadn't expected Nade to possess such magical power.
'The biggest problem is…'
Once magic is cast, its effects are real, even for the mage.
If the lightning was powerful enough to devastate the mountain peak,
Nade couldn't have survived unscathed.
"Nade…!"
Returning to the battlefield, Sabina couldn't call out his name and
stood frozen.
Everything had turned to ash and crumbled, leaving only Nade standing on
the ground.
'It was like this before…'
The fear she felt when Nade had beaten her resurfaced, striking her
heart.
It wasn't just because she was weak-hearted.
What was happening to Nade was clearly some 'phenomenon,' a real and
present danger.
Where Nade walked, Frings lay sprawled with his mustache singed.
As Nade, with lightning crackling in his right hand, reached out to
finish him, Frings spoke.
"…Spare me."
The reason his short words carried a lifetime of sincerity was that Nade
truly intended to kill him.
If there were levels of murderous intent, Nade's was pure
savagery.
It was different from animals killing for food or humans killing for
possession.
'There's no purpose.'
It was like a force of nature.
Just as hurricanes, earthquakes, and lightning kill without meaning,
Nade was a predator at the top of the killing pyramid.
"Why should I spare you?"
Frings, looking at Nade, immediately averted his gaze to the sky, unable
to bear even a second of eye contact.
Conversely, Nade observed everything about Frings as if he were a prey's
corpse.
The trembling of his fear-stricken body, the desperate breaths of a
living being.
Then, noticing the bandaged fingers, Nade stepped on Frings'
hand.
"Aaaargh!"
"Don't make a sound."
The sound vanished as if cut by a knife.
"Huff! Huff! Huff!"
As Nade approached his face, Frings' nostrils flared with each terrified
breath.
"Don't breathe."
His breathing stopped.
"Yes. Die like that."
'Don't breathe!'
The reason he couldn't think of anything but not breathing, even though
he would die anyway, was that Nade was scarier than death.
'Am I going to die…?'
"Tell me. Why shouldn't I kill you?"
As he began to lose consciousness, Frings' eyes snapped open.
'There's still hope!'
The second question hinted at a trace of humanity.
'He hasn't completely crossed the line. Or is he desperately holding
back?'
He didn't know how it was possible, but it was clearly his last chance
to live.
"If you kill me… you'll be expelled. You'll go to prison. The
kingdom's powerful people will kill you."
"I don't care."
The humanity disappeared from Nade's voice again.
"Huh! Huh!"
Tears leaked from Frings' eyes.
"Think. Use your brain. Because I don't know. So convince
me."
Nade declared.
"You have three seconds."
"Huuuuh! Huh!"
Frings squeezed his eyes shut and sobbed.
'Think! You have to think!'
The brain of a creature facing death sought answers at unprecedented
speed.
The brain, nerves, and cells of his entire body coordinated, releasing
all their energy.
'There must be a reason he can't kill me.'
A unique neural pattern flashed in his brain, bringing all his knowledge
to the surface.
When he couldn't find an answer there, he began to trace back through
all his past experiences.
The day he decided to become a pervert, Overflow, his infancy sucking
his mother's milk…
He searched through his fetal, embryonic, and even the weak experiences
of being a sperm and egg, finally reaching…
"Die."
'Found it!'
Frings' eyes snapped open.
"If you kill me!"
The electric afterimage flickered before his retinas.
"You'll never come back."
"…"
Frings used all the air in his lungs to speak.
"You're holding back desperately. If you cross the line here,
you'll… never come back. So…"
The words crawled out of his throat.
"Spare me…"
The electricity in Nade's hand fizzled out.
"Take out your master card."
Frings, not even knowing how his broken fingers twisted, fumbled in his
pocket.
"Tear it."
With a snap, the card split, and Nade stood up and walked away.
With each step, Frings' body convulsed.
'Nade…'
Sabina reached out to the retreating Nade.
She was relieved he hadn't crossed the line.
'But…'
Today, she realized she couldn't get close.
No one in the world could stay by his side unless he allowed it.
"Sigh."
Sitting on a burnt rock, Nade covered his cursed face with his hands and
exhaled.
Anchal's mind projection had revealed his true face.
Having vented his rage, he regained some sanity, but what followed was
intense regret and self-loathing.
'This face. This face!'
Even without a mirror, his mother's image in his mind told him what he
looked like now.
"Damn it!"
His hands, covering his face, rose like spikes.
As his nails scratched his skin, red lines appeared on his face.
"Aaaargh!"
Just hearing Nade's scream made Frings feel like he was
suffocating.
"Huff! Huff! Huff!"
Tears welled up in his bloodshot eyes as he gasped for air.
Nade, Frings.
Retired.
