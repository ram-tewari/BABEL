Chapter 99:
Infinite (3)
'How foolish… truly foolish…!'
He was the one who reached out first, who handed her coffee, only for her to coldly
reject him. As if turning away from the regret-filled scenes, Emi tightly shut her eyes. Then, with all her
might, she screamed.
"Shirone!"
Shirone smiled. He was relieved that Emi had regained her memories. At the very least, he
could depart as Shirone, remaining in someone's memory until the very end.
"Thank you, Emi."
Shirone's tears fell onto Emi's cheek.
'Why are you crying, Shirone?'
Emi felt uneasy. Something was wrong. Then, the moment she saw the light of life
gradually fading from Shirone's eyes, she realized.
"Shirone! No!"
"I'm sorry. I couldn't keep my promise."
Shirone, holding Emi in his arms, fully unleashed the Immortal Function. As his
consciousness expanded infinitely, his sense of self began to fade, dissolving like ink in water.
'Goodbye, everyone….'
Father, mother, the members of the Ozent family, friends from school. Shirone could smile
because he had been loved by so many people. A tremendous light filled the space below the cliff. The
falling students, Iruki and Nade, even Sad—all were engulfed by the radiance and vanished.
The next moment, a massive flash of light shot vertically into the sky.
It was a mass teleportation of a staggering 400 people.
'Ah, I see.'
Akein, lying sprawled on the ground, watched the spectacle of the light splitting into
dozens of strands as it soared above the cliff. Only in his final moment did he feel true conviction. Where
he had been. And what a magnificent life he had lived.
'A playground where even a lifetime wouldn't be enough to play. Isn't that right,
Alpheus?'
And so, the legendary archmage who had once captivated the world passed away.
"Master?"
Kanis stood dumbfounded before Akein's corpse. Hadn't he been his disciple? Yet, he left
without even leaving behind a final word, doing only what he wanted until the very end.
"Was it really all for nothing? Were we just tools to you?"
Kanis rushed to Akein and shook him by the collar.
"Wake up! How could you do this? I should have been the one to die! Why did you leave me
behind?!"
Arin approached Kanis with sorrowful eyes. The reflection in her mirror showed a humanoid
figure made of a liquid resembling sewage. The form never stayed still, constantly dissolving and reforming
like a liquid.
"No, Kanis. He didn't die because of you. That man used us. He was a villain through and
through."
"Kukuku, what do you mean 'no'? It seems you've finally realized."
Harvest spoke in a hoarse voice.
"That's right, Kanis. Everyone died because of you. Akein, me, and one day, even Arin
will die."
Arin, who had been glaring at Harvest, quickly turned her head back to Kanis. As
expected, he was in shock—his human form collapsed, melting into a viscous liquid that clung to the ground.
"Why? What did I do wrong? Surviving in Radum? Being a damned bastard?"
"Because you're weak."
Harvest's conclusion was simple.
"How long will you live waiting for others to acknowledge you? There's no such thing as
'this is why you should live' or 'this is why you should die.' Akein simply lived his life as he chose."
Akein had given his life to uphold his beliefs in evil. Harvest had chosen
self-destruction to fulfill his contract as a demonic entity.
'Then… why was I trying to die?'
He couldn't find an answer. He had been nothing more than a wild colt, blind and
thrashing about. A life built entirely of empty shells.
"Like Master…."
"Yes, and like that boy."
At the center of the 400 scattered students, Shirone lay collapsed. He had vowed not to
harm anyone. And he had upheld that belief until the very end.
Kanis approached Harvest and checked his condition.
"How are you holding up?"
"What do you think? I'm dying. Without a host, I can't survive."
Arin spoke up.
"Make a master-servant contract with me. Kanis won't be able to endure after exhausting
his mental energy."
Harvest shook his head.
"Sorry, but that's not happening."
"What? Why not? You'll vanish like this!"
"My master is Kanis."
Harvest's words pierced Kanis's heart. Now, it was his turn to raise the banner of his
own beliefs. Could he endure? If he faltered, both of them would lose their lives.
"Harvest. I'll take you as my subordinate. But I won't forgive betrayal a second time."
"Kukuku. Understood, Master."
"Kanis! It's too dangerous! If something goes wrong…!"
Arin stopped herself. Kanis's liquid form was hardening as mud seeped into it. Would he
one day gain an unshakable heart? Like the image of Shirone she had seen through her mirror.
Kanis placed his hand on Harvest. As the subordinate contract took effect, Harvest began
siphoning what little life force remained. Kanis's consciousness faded into darkness as his eyes slowly
closed.
***
Alpheus stood somberly apart from Kanis and his group. Though the students had narrowly
escaped death, the losses were too great.
"Headmaster."
Etella felt the same. From this day onward, the magic school would undergo significant
changes. The students, having regained their memories, would be thrown into chaos, and Alpheus's mistake
from 40 years ago would also come to light.
"Etella, I am a sinner."
"Don't blame yourself too much. If a life where even mistakes are unforgivable is a sin,
then the very existence of humanity is sinful."
Alpheus shook his head. Everyone makes mistakes. But responsibility always follows. Like
Akein, he had no intention of running away.
"I was the one who elevated dark magic to the ranks of grand magic. Harvest could be
called revolutionary in the field of memory transfer. Abyss Nova will be registered as a regulated spell by
the Magic Association."
Before Akein's corpse, Alpheus clasped his hands in mourning.
"The eternal boy, Archmage Akein. The path of magic you walked will live on in future
generations."
When Alpheus opened his eyes, he seemed lighter. Etella, however, remained worried. With
the entire student body having faced mortal danger, this was no longer an issue the school could handle
internally.
"Headmaster, what do you plan to do now?"
"What can I say? I, too, am merely waiting for judgment. Rather than punishment,
shouldn't we first honor today's hero?"
Alpheus glanced around in search of Shirone. The boy had a knack for astonishing people.
Even Alpheus couldn't confidently say he could teleport 400 people at once.
"We have a problem!"
Emi was sobbing near where Shirone had collapsed.
Sensing the unusual atmosphere, Alpheus and Etella quickened their steps. In the
distance, Iruki and Nade were running toward them.
But before they could arrive, Emi screamed first.
"Shirone isn't breathing!"
