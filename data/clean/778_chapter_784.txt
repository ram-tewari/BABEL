"Are we training separately?" If so, it was a bit disappointing—she had

"The commander is entering!"
A massive man, his face marked with dozens of scars like badges of

"Salute the commander!"
As salute commands in various languages rang out from the recruits, a
few of them remained still.
Either way was acceptable.

"What a bunch of disorganized fools! Huh? You're all a mess!"

"You know what this is? Royal recommendation letters with the king's

"Don't need this crap! You know what demons are? They're exactly like

"Hey. You."

"You! Red hair!"
Amy turned her head and realized he was pointing at her. She stepped
forward.
"Recruit Carmis Amy, reporting!"
After a long silence, Gomez asked:
"…Why are you wearing makeup?"
Amy was briefly flustered but quickly regained her composure and lifted
her chin.
"I'm not wearing any, sir!"
"Oh yeah?"
Gomez moved in close and scrutinized her face.
"No makeup, huh? Incredible! The demons are gonna love you! What are you
doing here, trying to seduce me with that face?!"
"No, sir!"
Sparks lit up in Gomez's eyes as he swung his fist, landing a blow
square on Amy's face.
She collapsed to the ground, trying to grasp the situation—but her body
rose instinctively before her mind could catch up.
"I'll ask again! Are you trying to seduce me?!"
Amy had come to become a general.
"No, sir!"
Gomez's face twisted in a sneer as he hit her again, harder than
before.
"Get up!"
Amy bounced back up like a punching doll.
"One last time! You'd better answer right if you don't want to be the
first one eliminated! Are you trying to seduce me?!"
His voice exploded, practically a roar of rage.
"No, sir!"
She clenched her jaw, one eye squeezed shut from the impact. Gomez
stormed back to his spot, shouting as he walked.
"Listen up from now on! There's only one thing I want from you—just die!
That's our job!"
The recruits snapped to full attention.
"Who the hell wants to die? That's why we're needed! We get paid to die
for them! That's what it means to be a soldier! So do your job! Death is the highest honor!"
All 100 women had fought fiercely to make it here, but their eyes now
burned differently than when they first arrived.
"That's all! Head to your quarters and receive your supplies! Once you
finish lunch, the official induction ceremony begins!"
As Gomez made his way to where the male recruits were, the instructor
followed behind and asked,
"What do you think, sir? I was worried about the quality of foreign
recruits, but after reading their recommendation letters, they seem far better than expected."
"What do you think I'd say? War is the enemy, not people. You think
girls like that came here because they're just dying to die?"
Even Gomez, for all his bluster, was a loving husband and father of two
daughters at home.
"These are the future backbone of the Valkyries. Fighting demons is
nothing like fighting humans. Drill them until it makes their eyes bleed. Because if we don't—"
A deadly glint flashed in Gomez's eyes.
"—they'll be the ones bleeding on the battlefield."
Once the instructors left, the women finally relaxed, releasing
long-held breaths.
"I was curious what a global military would be like. Turns out it's
nothing special."
"Discipline can only go so far. What kind of training we get—that's what
matters."
Amy, rubbing her sore, swollen cheek, quietly listened to the other
recruits' chatter.
"I saw in the last Valkyrie recruitment letter—they're accepting not
just officers and noncoms, but even general-level candidates. It's a great chance to learn under the
best."
As expected, Amy was steeling her resolve, thinking there wasn't a

"Wha…!"
Her eyes widened at the sight of a familiar and unexpected face.
"Tess! You applied too?"
Tess of the Elzine family.
Rian's friend, and part of the trio alongside Amy and Arin during their

"Yeah… I think I've been marked already."
"That guy's totally a pervert. If he'd hit you one more time, I would've

Knowing Tess, she absolutely meant it.
"It's fine. At least it was a punch. If he'd slapped me… I probably
would've flattened him too."
I'll become stronger.
If she rose high enough—became a general—then she could meet Shirone

"Yeah. Let's both pass and show them what we're made of."

"But what about your swordsmanship school? What made you suddenly apply

"My father died. He was guarding a colony during the last altar opening…
He was killed by the demon army."
Amy felt the ground fall away from beneath her.
"Oh…"
"It's okay. I'm not sad. He just did his duty as a commander of an

after I saw the notice."
"A non-com? Not an officer?"
With Tess's swordsmanship, she could've easily entered the Valkyrie Army
as an officer.
She wants to fight, Amy realized.
She wanted revenge—for her father.
"What about you, Amy? Officer?"
"Yeah. The Valkyrie magic corps doesn't take non-coms. But officer or
not, we're still going to be deployed the same."
Since magic was considered a specialized field, its units were smaller
than regular infantry.
Promotion came faster, but even a lieutenant colonel often ended up in
frontline melee combat.
"By the way…"
Tess hesitated before asking.
"Have you heard from Rian?"
"No. Total radio silence. Even the Ogent family doesn't seem to know
anything. He hasn't contacted you either?"
Tess answered only with silence.
"How heartless. He could've at least sent word…"
Rian was probably the only one who could ease the grief of losing her
father, which made his silence all the more painful.
"It's fine. That's just how he is. He's gotten stronger, so wherever he

Amy, too, had a boyfriend who was hopeless when it came to understanding

"Hmph! Who needs a blockhead like that anyway? I've got you now, right?
If the nights get lonely, I'll just date you instead."

"Wait—what did you just say…?"

"Hohoho! Who knows? I might surprise even myself!"

Rian's location was top-level classified information.
