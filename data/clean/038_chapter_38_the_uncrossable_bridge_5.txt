Chapter 38:
The Uncrossable Bridge (5)
"I know you're kind. But you don't have to go that far. I can't prove it, but Mark
hired people to interfere. That's just petty."
"Shirone doesn't want to be first."
He wasn't competing to gain numbers given by others.
"He wants to pass the advancement exam and reach a higher level. He knows that
advancing without being convinced himself would be meaningless."
Amy understood Shirone's feelings perfectly, which made her all the more frustrated.
'But this time, luck wasn't on his side. It's too dangerous. This exam needs to be
stopped here.'
The Level 10 of the Uncrossable Bridge was impossible for anyone at the Class Four level
to cross.
Even though Shirone was still holding on, based on Amy's experience, the difficulty of
the traps he had faced so far was only around Level 6.
"Uwaaaaah! Uwaaaaah!"
Mark, who had been screaming at the top of his lungs, finally collapsed, his eyes rolling
back as foam spilled from his mouth.
In contrast, Shirone's focus grew even sharper.
At some point, even the teachers who had been stomping their feet in frustration and the
students who had been making a fuss fell silent, as if they had forgotten their own existence.
Even Alphaeus was repeatedly amazed, his eyes glued to Shirone's dazzling movements.
'Crisis brings out potential. But everyone has their limits. How?'
Could he still hold on?
The Uncrossable Bridge was now racing at a speed of 200 kilometers per hour.
An ordinary student would have been overwhelmed by the speed alone and lost
consciousness.
Yet, Shirone was darting through the black iron structures like lightning.
'Was there never a limit from the beginning?'
Even when riding a rising trend, the reason one couldn't soar endlessly was that every
human eventually reached a point of self-satisfaction.
Magic was no different.
Was the reason not all magicians could become the best simply a matter of skill or
talent?
The moment one reached a certain level, the self-imposed limit of satisfaction became an
inevitable psychological barrier.
'Shirone, are you dreaming of infinity in a human body?'
It seemed as if he had no such vessel to begin with.
Because he had no standard for satisfaction, his mind could endure the endlessly rising
difficulty.
Shirone, clearly in a state of ecstasy, might not even remember this moment.
But Ethela, the youngest bishop of the Carthis Council, would likely never forget
Shirone's prowess she witnessed today.
'He's finding escape routes in real time, each with odds close to infinity.'
What kind of mind could make that possible?
'Even great masters can only maintain such mental strength in a state of complete
detachment.'
The structures were now twisting into bizarre shapes, beyond complex, entering a chaotic
trap where no rules could be discerned.
'It needs to be broken down further.'
As the waves shortened to the point where moving less than a meter would be unavoidable,
Shirone's jumping distance also decreased.
If he couldn't calculate each move one by one, he had to analyze everything from the
start and dive in.
Left, right, left, left, right, left...
Shirone imprinted the overlapping ring-shaped traps in his mind and recalled their
patterns.
As he teleported, flashes of light zigzagged through the structures, but this time,
identical traps rushed in vertically.
There was no time to calculate the pattern.
'It needs to be denser!'
Time fragmented further, and the teleportation flashes curved into a parabola, bending
downward.
While students and even teachers gaped, Iruki's eyes widened as he shouted.
"Rainbow Drop! He's finally reached that level!"
The bending flashes of light swam through the center of the circle like a loach, piercing
through.
Rainbow Drop was an advanced teleportation technique, achievable only by graduation-level
specialists.
Thus, the upperclassmen couldn't comprehend how the light had bent to escape the trap.
"Is that even possible? How can teleportation bend? Light only moves in straight
lines!"
Iruki tapped his head and said.
"It's simple. If you differentiate a circle, you get infinite straight lines. He
just fragmented the teleportation intervals into thousandths."
"Thousandths? Can a human do that?"
"Even if it's impossible to calculate, you can imagine it intuitively. He's more of
an omnipotent genius than an omniscient one. In short, the opposite of me."
The students turned their heads away, as if ignoring Iruki's words.
'He's obsessed with geniuses. For a Class Five.'
Meanwhile, the Uncrossable Bridge's speed reached its peak.
The afterimages of the iron bars stretched so long that only those whose eyes had adapted
could distinguish their shapes.
Shirone's movements through the afterimages transcended technique, reaching the level of
art.
Everyone was mesmerized by the sight of him fragmenting straight light into curved
trajectories.
"Could he... actually do it?"
Only 30 meters remained to the bridge's endpoint.
Now, even the students set aside their thoughts and silently cheered for Shirone's
success.
For the first time in the magic school's history, an upperclassman might master the
Uncrossable Bridge.
"Headmaster! Look there!"
Someone pointed to the bridge's endpoint, and all the teachers who saw it turned pale.
The inevitable had finally arrived.
From the endpoint, the iron bars began twisting like burning hair, forming a massive
three-dimensional structure.
"The Dragon's Labyrinth! That can't be! Disable the device now!"
The Dragon's Labyrinth was a trap that appeared only once at Level 10, named for its
resemblance to a dragon's face when completed.
While the exterior looked similar, the internal structure varied each time, and the iron
bars used to construct it stretched a staggering 600 meters.
Without analyzing the twisting process in advance and finding an escape pattern, one
would be swallowed by the dragon and left battered.
Even Shirone, who was pushing his limits, sensed something ominous about the twisting
bars.
Forgetting even to blink, he imprinted the structure's pattern in his mind when he felt a
shake at his side.
"Uwaaaah! What the—! Save me!"
Mark, who had regained consciousness, screamed at the sight of the enormous trap that had
appeared in an instant.
To him, who hadn't cleared the traps step by step, the rapidly racing spectacle was too
overwhelming to analyze.
'It's coming!'
Despite the situation, the Dragon's Labyrinth charged at them at the fastest speed ever
recorded, its maw wide open.
***
The time to arrival was barely a second. Even Shirone couldn't help but feel his heart
drop this time.
'Don't give up!'
An electric jolt struck his brain as Shirone steadied his Spirit Zone, shaken by Mark's
scream.
This much took a tenth of a second.
Shirone wondered: Was there any leeway?
But while imagining dividing a second into ten was possible, experiencing 0.1 seconds ten
times was not.
Thus, there was only one method: keep dividing.
A hundredth of a second.
He reconstructed the Dragon's Labyrinth's formation in his mind.
Its overall shape was a seven-pointed star. Six branching paths.
A thousandth of a second.
After retracing the labyrinth's form, he found the escape route.
To break free, he needed to teleport nine times in rapid succession.
'No! It's impossible with Mark!'
If there was no gap for two people, he had to find another route.
At that moment, the Dragon's Labyrinth swallowed Shirone.
The male students turned their heads away, while the female students screamed, imagining
the gruesome outcome.
Seriel's scream was the loudest.
Whoosh—!
The sound of rushing wind in the confined space resembled the cries of Shirone and Mark.
Then, finally, silence.
A ten-thousandth of a second.
In a time too fleeting even for insight, Shirone analyzed every pattern again and found
the escape route.
The teleportation flash burst out of the Dragon's Labyrinth, and Shirone, glowing
brilliantly, crossed the finish line and landed.
"Haa, haa."
Shirone set Mark down, took a few more steps, and then collapsed to his knees.
No one made a sound. The students stared at Shirone with eyes full of terror, as if they
had seen a ghost.
Ethela teleported to find Siina, who had left to search for Sard. Shortly after, the
mechanism deactivated, and Echo's voice rang out.
"-Deactivating the Uncrossable Bridge's mechanism. Level Zero. Level Zero."
The iron bars slowed, and the structures that had grown like wooden vines returned to
their original horizontal form, restoring the bridge to its initial state.
"What... what just happened?"
The students all felt the same.
"Is this even possible?"
"It's unbelievable. Level 10. And he cleared the Dragon's Labyrinth."
Amid the murmurs, Shirone, still on the ground, finally came to his senses and looked
around.
'What? Why are they staring at me?'
Though parts of his memory were blank, it was an undeniable fact that he had crossed the
Uncrossable Bridge.
As reality settled in his mind, Shirone's face twisted in dismay.
"Ugh, I really thought I was going to die."
It was a disappointingly plain statement from someone who had just made history.
But Seriel knew this was the real Shirone—the one before he jumped off the cliff.
"Amy! Shirone did it! Your boyfriend did it!"
She could have done without the "boyfriend" part, but seeing Seriel's tearful
joy, Amy let it slide this time.
The students rushed over to congratulate Shirone.
"Congratulations! You were amazing!"
"So, are you advancing to Class Five now? Hahaha! The Class Six kids must be
fuming!"
While Shirone received congratulations, the teachers and Maria, who had gone to the
control room, arrived via teleportation.
Siina checked Shirone first.
Surrounded by students, he seemed dazed but unharmed.
'He cleared the Dragon's Labyrinth? How? Even graduation-level students practice dozens
of times for that difficulty.'
She shot a glare at Sard, who had made her miss the historic moment. He shrugged in
defense.
"As you know—or maybe you don't—I did my best."
Siina sighed and approached Alphaeus.
Just in case, if Shirone and Mark had arrived simultaneously, announcing the results
publicly would prevent any disputes.
"Headmaster, let's announce the results first..."
Alphaeus, who had forgotten, snapped back to attention and smiled warmly at the students.
"The early advancement exam's successful candidate is Arian Shirone. Shirone will
advance to Class Five. However, all participants should be proud. The courage you showed today will be the
driving force leading Alphaeus Magic School forward. I hope you continue to strive for excellence."
Applause began with the seniors and spread to the juniors.
No one had any complaints about Shirone's advancement now, not even Mark, who had faced
him directly.
'So geniuses... really do exist.'
The image of Shirone teleporting at incredible speeds through the Dragon's Labyrinth was
seared into his mind.
It wasn't a matter of talent.
In impossible situations, some would despair and retreat, while others would only think
of surpassing them.
'This leaves no room for excuses.'
Mark approached Shirone on trembling legs.
Most students had suspected some collusion in this test, but Mark, as if resolved,
publicly admitted defeat.
"Senior, I lost. It was a great match, and I learned a lot! I'll do my best from now
on!"
With Alphaeus's approval, he now had to address Shirone as his senior.
"Yeah, good work today. I'll do my best too."
Amy, who had been watching with satisfaction, turned to Seriel.
"Let's go down now."
"Huh? Why so soon? On a day like this, you should stay with your boyfriend."
"No time. Shirone did it, so now it's my turn. I need to prepare for my advancement
exam."
Amy's feet felt just as heavy, but she needed to calm her excitement by doing something.
"Amy."
Shirone called out, and Amy turned with a shrug.
When the two locked eyes, the surrounding students murmured in awe.
"Did he just call her by name?"
"Of course. They're dating."
"But even so, she's Senior Amy..."
Amy, who had never lost first place, was more than just a senior to the juniors.
Given that she could easily compete at the graduation level, the current situation was
astonishing.
Shirone ignored the reactions and smiled at Amy, holding up two fingers.
"Now, just two steps left."
"Haha..."
Amy's lips trembled.
After passing her advancement test a week ago, she had finally entered the graduation
class, leaving only a two-step gap between them.
"Let's go, Seriel."
"Huh? Oh, okay."
As Amy walked away, Seriel hurried after her down the mountain.
Except for Class Seven, the other upperclassmen also began to disperse. Finally, Sard
approached Alphaeus.
"Headmaster, I need to talk to you about Maria."
Sard reported everything that had happened in the control room.
"I see. How could such a mistake..."
Alphaeus muttered regretfully, and Maria stepped forward, bowing deeply.
"I'm sorry. I made an irreversible mistake. I'll accept any punishment."
"Hmm, for now..."
Mark interrupted.
"I have something to confess. Everything that happened today was because of
me."
All eyes turned to Mark, who stood with a crooked smile.
"What do you mean, because of you?"
"Actually, I really got under Maria's skin this morning. Honestly, even I would've
lost it if I'd heard those things. So, I'll take the punishment. I'm the cause."
Siina spoke.
"Even so, Maria's actions were problematic. And why are you saying this? You
could've kept quiet."
"I'm not a good guy, but I'm not petty enough to shirk responsibility. Besides,
everyone already knows. And by the way, I was the one who planned to counter Senior Shirone."
Mark, who had always believed winning was all that mattered, had learned something from
his battle with Shirone.
'Overwhelming skill.'
Shirone's focus on overcoming obstacles without distractions had shaken Mark's entire
mindset.
"Anyway, that's how it is. I'll accept any punishment, but go easy on the others.
Especially Maria..."
Mark glanced at Maria.
"She must've been under a lot of stress. We ganged up on her every day. Who wouldn't
snap?"
Alphaeus also had deep thoughts about Maria.
Though not academically outstanding, she had trained longer than anyone at the school,
silently persevering.
Facing expulsion for an irreversible mistake, she reminded him of his younger self.
'Master...'
Sard, who knew Alphaeus's past, watched him with pity.
The light of the Mirhi family.
Alphaeus had first made a name for himself with light magic.
Though now considered outdated theory, it was an innovative idea at the time, earning him
the Gold Circle Award from the king.
But his shining talent had led young Alphaeus into chaos.
If not for a single misjudgment that left him wandering in darkness for ten years, what
might have been?
Perhaps he would have been a great magician etched in history.
"Both of you will face the disciplinary committee. Maria illegally activated the
mechanism, interfering with the exam and creating unnecessary danger. However, since Mark was the
instigator, her punishment will be lenient."
As Maria and Mark bowed, Class Seven students began raising their hands one by one.
"Teacher, I bullied Maria. Doesn't that make me responsible too?"
"I was part of the plan to attack Senior Shirone."
Every Class Seven student raised their hand, showing the issue stemmed from the entire
class.
Though the matter would have surfaced eventually, their courage to confess was
commendable.
"Then all of you will be punished. Return and await the committee's decision."
"What about Maria?"
"The committee will consider it. The punishment will apply to the whole class, and
Maria won't bear all the blame alone."
Though not a definitive answer, the weight of the headmaster's words was undeniable.
Siina asked.
"Isn't that too risky? We don't know how the committee will rule."
"We're teachers. Our role is to guide students rightly, not judge them as
magistrates. They're the students we must teach and nurture."
Maria burst into tears.
"Thank you. I'm sorry."
Sard smiled as he watched.
'Well done, Maria.'
This moment might be the miraculous opportunity Maria would never get again.
Just like Sard when he met Alphaeus.
'Some call it luck... but you can't even get that if you give up.'
If Maria hadn't truly loved magic, could she have endured in Class Seven for so long?
Though the world was unfair, Sard believed it was worth living.
If one dreamed earnestly and persevered, opportunity would come to anyone.
