Chapter 707:
The Noskarta that originated in the southern Akkad Desert dissipated
only after reaching the northern desert. The desert tsunami swept away all traces of life, and the
desert was hotter than ever under the scorching sun.
"Shirone, water. Give me some water."
Shirone and his companions were trudging through the vast wasteland,
covered in sand.
"More water?"
Shirone used magic to fill a canteen with water, which Kido gulped
down.
"I don't know, maybe because it's made with magic? It doesn't
quench my thirst."
After drinking a whole liter, Kido's protruding belly bulged even
more.
"How much longer do we have to walk? We've been walking for two
hours since we sent Latusa. Didn't you say we were almost there?"
The power of Noskarta was immense, and by the time they emerged from the
desert tsunami, Kaidra was no longer able to fly.
"We're going in the right direction. Just a bit more. Once we see
it, we'll use magic to get there."
Rian regularly checked the path behind them.
"Still no pursuit. Could they have been wiped out?"
Considering even Kaidra was exhausted, it was unlikely that those who
had chased them on Omeks had survived.
"We can't let our guard down."
Despite this, they were worried because the strength of the Maga
Bandits' leader, a member of the Ten Demons, was beyond imagination.
"If it's that woman, she might have survived Noskarta. We don't
have time to waste. Let's walk a bit faster."
"Shirone, water."
After walking for another hour, Shirone's group spotted a fortress on
the horizon.
True to its reputation as a famous tourist spot, people were gathering
there.
"That's Andre."
Using air magic, Shirone lifted Kido and Rian into the air and flew
towards the fortress.
At the main gate, there was a ticket booth.
"Welcome! This is the labyrinth of Andre! We welcome adventurers
seeking experiences more valuable than life!"
The staff, dressed in Middle Eastern attire, switched to the common
continental language upon seeing Shirone's skin color.
"This is the place…"
One of the world's three great labyrinths, where Ra Enemy was presumed
to be.
"You've come at a great time! Due to Noskarta, admission is
half-price for the next week! Don't miss out!"
"…"
The cheerful voice momentarily relaxed them, but those arriving behind
urged them to hurry.
"Come on, move along. It's hot."
Pushed by their voices, Shirone glanced at Rian and Kido before speaking
to the ticket booth.
"Uh… two adults and one goblin."
"Hmm? Goblin?"
The staff smiled and looked at the goblin.
"There's no set price for goblins. Is he under six? Children under
six are free."
Shirone turned to see Kido scowling.
"Yes, he's under six."
Kido's eyes widened.
"Alright, that'll be 4 gold for the two adults."
Even at half-price, it was quite expensive for a world-famous tourist
spot.
"Thank you! Enjoy your visit!"
Beyond the main gate, there were numerous shops along the
well-maintained path, and even fountains despite the desert setting.
As the Empire managed it specially, there were high-end restaurants,
bars, inns, casinos, and souvenir shops.
"There are more people than I expected. I thought it would be
off-season due to Noskarta."
"It must be even more crowded normally."
Many nobles were walking around with their families, and some
strong-looking individuals were gathered in one place.
'Typical tourists or adventurers.'
Kido ran to a souvenir shop and browsed the items on display.
"Kido, we don't have time to shop now."
The Maga Bandits could arrive at any moment.
"Shirone, look at this."
As Shirone and Rian approached, Kido showed them a model of
Andre.
"Hmm, so this is what it looks like."
The structure consisted of interconnected large chambers, and through
the transparent ceiling of the model, numerous circular doors were visible.
"No way there are 19,000 doors."
The miniature could only represent a few hundred doors, but it was still
quite unique.
"It'll be different when we see it for real. Let's go."
Kido reluctantly put the model back on the display.
"Andre opens at 2 PM. Please wait in line if you wish to
tour."
Following the guide's instructions, Shirone's group joined the line of
people waiting.
"What's this? We're here to find Ra Enemy, right?"
The peaceful atmosphere wasn't bad, but Rian was wary of losing his
sharp focus.
"Ra Enemy probably isn't here."
Both Rian and Kido turned to Shirone.
"Not here? Then why are we here?"
"Because we can only meet Ra Enemy through this place."
Shirone shared his thoughts.
"What I realized during the Ivory Tower test is that finding Ra
Enemy isn't like finding a lost item."
"Because events are manipulated?"
"Exactly. I'm not finding Ra. Ra is making me find him. The subject
and object are reversed."
Shirone raised his index finger.
"So, 'I will be where you seek' doesn't mean Ra is waiting where I
go. It means I have to find where Ra is."
"A kind of hint."
"So I thought about it. Where would I be looking for Ra? The
conclusion is everywhere."
Rian looked at the entrance of Andre.
"The Prison of Time and Space. Goffin's relic."
"Right. Goffin imprisoned 19,000 worlds in separate cells. If we
break all these seals, time and space will mix again…"
"Then we can meet the real Ra Enemy."
Shirone nodded.
"We'll know for sure once we see Andre…"
At that moment, the entrance to Andre opened.
"Please enter. Viewing time is until 4 PM."
The cave was spacious, and the long line quickly shortened. Shirone's
group crossed the threshold, feeling an unusual coolness for a desert.
"Wow…"
The real thing was much more impressive than the model, and Kido gaped
in awe.
Circular steel doors, resembling eyes, were installed on the walls,
ceiling, and floor of the chamber.
In the center of each steel door was a circular panel, illuminated
despite no visible power source.
Shirone read the number 3256 on one panel.
Looking around, he saw different numbers on each door, likely up to
19000.
"According to legend…"
The guide began to explain.
"This cave once contained many overlapping worlds. Then one day, a
great wizard appeared and divided them into 19,000 worlds, sealing each behind a door."
A tourist pointed to a door and asked,
"So if we open this door, we'll enter a completely different world?
Can we go in now?"
The guide smiled but blushed slightly.
"Of course, there are other worlds. But no one who has entered
these doors has ever returned."
"Then it's a lie. How do you know there are new worlds if no one
has come back? Isn't that right, everyone?"
The people, annoyed by the interruption, frowned, and the man became
more insistent.
"Why not open one? We can check. Just don't go in."
The guide held back, knowing his salary included putting up with such
behavior.
'This job is really tough.'
According to the veteran guide's philosophy, everyone thinks they're
right, but no one actually is.
"Unfortunately, opening Andre's doors requires a magic circuit
expert and is against Imperial law."
The mention of Imperial law silenced the man, but the guide's enthusiasm
was dampened.
"Rian, this place is incredible."
Everyone turned to see Shirone examining the doors.
"Oh! You recognize its value. Yes, these doors are coded with a
special code no information wizard has ever developed…"
The guide's words faded as Shirone's thoughts took over.
'How is this possible?'
The Ultima System revealed a perfect vacuum and infinite spots
surrounding it.
'Existence and non-existence. Completely separated.'
Only Goffin could conceive of separating the number 2 of the law into
two independent 1s.
Rian asked quietly,
"Can you do it? There are 19,000 worlds. It would take thousands of
years to break each seal."
"I don't plan to live that long."
Seeing Andre firsthand, Shirone realized it was something only he could
do.
"I'll break them all at once. With the Wheel of
Infinity."
He would infinitely multiply the bubbles of events to explore all 19,000
worlds simultaneously.
Kido, who retained memories by drinking Shirone's blood, adjusted his
glasses and said,
"Are you sure? You don't know what you'll encounter in each world.
It's different from multiplying events for a single purpose."
Kido was right.
"If I turn the Wheel of Infinity, 19,000 versions of me will have
different experiences. My thoughts will change."
Rian looked worried.
"Can you come back? If all those Shirones merge, you might go
mad."
Rian's concern was valid, but what Shirone would face was beyond
madness.
"I'm not doing it because I can. I'm doing it because only I
can."
Shirone looked around the chamber.
"It was infinite at first. Then it split into existence and
non-existence. Many and infinite are different. Goffin didn't seal this place. He made it an
investigable area."
"Do you think Goffin prepared this for you? Because you could
master the Wheel of Infinity?"
It's uncertain.
"I don't know. But I feel like everything I've experienced and
realized fits perfectly with Andre, like a key and lock."
"Hexa is the key to breaking Andre's seal?"
Shirone nodded at Kido's question.
"Infinite is infinite. So the 19,000 worlds must have been divided
with Goffin's special intent. If we explore them all…"
Then perhaps…
"We might understand Goffin a bit more. And learn more about
Hexa."
Rian and Kido couldn't think of a way to dissuade him.
"How long do you think it will take to break the
seals?"
"With the Ultima System, I should be able to find a way out. It
will take at least seven days."
If the Maga Bandits survived, they could reach Andre by this
evening.
"Time is tight. So Kido and I will…"
Just then, a loud explosion echoed from outside the cave, followed by
screams.
"What's happening?"
As the tourists watched anxiously, a guard shouted from the cave
entrance.
"Everyone evacuate! Bandits are attacking!"
Kido asked in shock,
"What? Bandits?"
They had arrived much faster than expected.
