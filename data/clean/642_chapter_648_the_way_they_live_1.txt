Chapter 648:
The Way They Live (1)
After leaving the city of Creas, Shirone and Rian tethered their horses
on a mountain path and had a late lunch.
"This is good."
Unlike Amy's carefully packed rice balls, Tess's lunchbox was closer to
military rations, but both were satisfied with the meal.
"By the way, where are we going now?"
It was a rather late question, but Rian had already decided to follow
Shirone wherever he went, so the destination didn't really matter to him.
What he liked more than when he was serving as a knight was that Shirone
actually thought things through—and as expected, Shirone started to speak smoothly.
"To be honest, I don't think we can find Ra on our own."
Shirone unfolded a world map he had taken from the Ogent family.
Even just counting the nations officially recognized as countries, there
were about 70. If you included tribal and non-human territories, it would take more than a lifetime to
traverse the entire world.
"Jin Seong Eum—she said she was of royal blood, right? She'd have a much
easier time with this kind of search than we would."
"Yeah. But it's not like we've got nowhere to turn."
Though not comparable to royalty, Shirone had built a considerable
network during his time at school and in Heaven.
"For starters, there's Miro. Actually, Miro may have already met Ra by
now."
"But the problem is, we don't know where Miro is, right? Wouldn't it be
better to go find this Teraje person instead? At least if we go there, we're guaranteed to meet
her."
If it was Kashan, they wouldn't fall short of Jincheon's intelligence
capabilities.
"Yeah. Depending on the situation, we may need her help. But it's too
far to do anything right away."
Rian looked over the map.
The world could be divided into four major lands.
The North Pole, where the Ivory Tower was located; the continent, which
served as the heart of the kingdoms; the Eastern lands across the Atlantic; and the Southern lands
stretching across the equator beneath the East.
The largest territory was, of course, the continent, shaped like the
Greek letter Sigma (∑), cradling the Atlantic and South Pacific.
The continent itself was divided into the Northern, Central, and
Southern continents, as well as the Middle East. Shirone's homeland, Tormia, was one of the 21 kingdoms
of the Central Continent, located near the South Pacific.
"Kashan is in the Northern Continent, so…"
The Northern Continent was exclusively Imperial territory, where Kashan
and Gustav shared a border.
"Even if we keep going up from Bashka, we'd have to pass through the
kingdoms of Yakma, Kazra, Ferris, Biden, and Jaive to get there."
Just thinking about it made his head spin.
There was no time limit for the test, but considering the skill level of
their competitors, they'd need to find Ra within at least a year to have a chance of passing.
Shirone spoke.
"Finding Ra right away is too vague. So, my plan is to first visit the
magic school he graduated from."
"Ah."
Going to the school would at least let them confirm some basic
information.
"But we don't know which school he graduated from."
"That's why we're going to the capital. I know someone who does."
"Who?"
"Scout Baikal."
Thinking he could get help through Plu, Shirone brushed himself off and
stood up.
"Let's go. We'll sleep in Kentra tonight and get to the capital by
tomorrow."
After riding for four hours, Shirone and Rian arrived in Kentra, where
they left their horses at the stables and entered the city.
True to its reputation as a livestock town, cowboys could be seen
everywhere, and unlike the graceful ladies of Creas, bold, rowdy women were laughing and bustling
about.
'Teacher Siina said she met Kuan here.'
Recalling the distant, dreamlike days of Heaven, they made their way to
the Magic Guild.
"Silver Ring Guild."
Reading the sign, Shirone turned to Rian and explained.
"Now that I have an unofficial mage license, I should be able to use the
guild too."
"But do you think we'll find anything about Ra here?"
There was little chance a small guild would know something even the
Ivory Tower didn't.
"Probably not. But it doesn't hurt to get the experience. Looks like
they offer lodging too, so let's stay here tonight."
Since it was winter, the sun had set early and the weather was cold, so
Rian followed without complaint.
Upon opening the door, they entered a cozy space lit with torches on
every wall.
At the tables, mages were reading or playing chess, and some were
already drunk and passed out.
There was a staircase leading to the second floor, and along the path to
it was a counter.
"Welcome to the mage's sanctuary, the Silver Ring Guild."
The woman at the counter stood up with a businesslike greeting.
"Huh?"
Seeing the two young visitors, she looked at them with interest.
"You two, are you mages?"
"No, just me. He's my friend."
Rian added, "I'm a swordsman."
She nodded after spotting the Grand Rite strapped to his back.
"Alright then. I'm Estera, the manager of the Silver Ring Guild. How can
I help you?"
"We were wondering if we could stay the night."
"Hm? Is this your first time at a guild?"
Shirone nodded honestly.
"Yes, it is."
"I thought so. Lodging is free for guild members, so you can't stay
unless you join. How about signing up with the Silver Ring Guild while you're at it? It'd be convenient
if you're a mage."
Even just having an unofficial license meant you could engage in basic
work or gather information, so joining a guild was practically a must for an unofficial mage.
"Hmm…"
As seen in the story of the Parrot Mercenaries, kingdoms controlled by
the Mage Association didn't have globally renowned magic guilds.
However, according to Shirone's research, the Silver Ring Guild—along
with War Wagon and Blood Rose—was one of Tormia's top three national guilds.
They handled various tasks through local branches, so it wasn't a bad
offer, but since Shirone planned to leave the Kingdom of Tormia anyway, there was no real
advantage.
"I'm not thinking of joining a guild just yet."
"Hehe, I guess it feels like a big step. Don't worry—joining and leaving
the Silver Ring Guild is totally up to you."
A guild's main goal was always expansion, so national guilds were
fiercely competitive.
The Silver Ring Guild's strategy of allowing free membership and
withdrawal was a way to secure the largest possible talent pool.
'Free membership, huh.'
Free lodging was tempting, but he remained cautious.
"I'll think about it a bit more."
As soon as Shirone finished speaking, Rian's gaze sharpened.
He had sensed fierce hostility from the mages in the hall.
They were all likely members of the Silver Ring Guild and didn't
appreciate seeing their guild dismissed by a boy who had just entered society.
Estera felt the same deep down, but she was too professional to show
it.
"Alright, if that's what you're comfortable with. The Silver Ring
Guild's doors are always open."
In the end, they stayed elsewhere, but since they were already at the
guild, Shirone asked,
"I heard you can get a variety of information through guilds."
Estera's business-savvy eyes sparkled.
"Of course. But it'll cost you. What kind of info are you looking
for?"
After a glance at Rian, Shirone asked,
"Do you have any information on someone named Ra Enemy?"
He had nothing else worth asking.
"Ra Enemy?"
Estera tilted her head, then smirked as if a good idea had popped into
her head, raising a finger.
"One gold."
"Excuse me?"
Shirone blinked in disbelief.
"You just asked me about someone named Ra Enemy, right? If you want an
answer, you'll need to pay 1 gold."
"You're charging just to find out if the information even
exists?"
It was absurd, but this was how guilds operated.
"Any information exchanged in a guild has value. If you were a member,
maybe it'd be different. But for outsiders, nothing comes for free."
Laughter erupted in the hall.
"Little brats who don't know how the world works. If you want something
free, go ask your mom."
Rian just folded his arms and closed one eye, calmly.
'So what'll you do, Shirone?'
The principle of not judging hastily still applied here.
'Let's see… we've got 300 gold on hand.'
It was a large amount at face value, but considering the journey ahead,
it wasn't unreasonable.
More importantly, Shirone had no way of knowing whether even this type
of question genuinely required payment—or if it was just Estera's sly way of getting back at him for
hesitating to join.
"I'll pay the 1 gold."
So, Shirone handed over the money.
If he didn't clarify how things worked when he had the chance, he could
make a mistake when it really counted.
Estera hadn't expected him to hand over the gold so readily, but she
didn't hesitate to snatch up the coin with a sharp clink.
"Now, tell me. Do you have any information on Ra Enemy?"
Estera flicked the coin and gave a short reply.
"Yes."
"You do?"
Shirone's eyes widened in confusion.
"You really do?"
"You know what happens to people who lie in this business, don't you? Go
wait at the table. I'll sort it and bring it over."
As Estera disappeared into the archive room, Shirone finally looked
around the hall.
Unlike the officials from the Association, the mages here looked rough
and hardened. A mage with an eyepatch, another missing a right arm, and one with burn scars on his
face—just by their appearances, Shirone could tell what kind of lives they had led.
Once Shirone and Rian sat at a table, a guild worker came by and poured
them tea.
Even as he sipped the tea meant to clear the mind, Shirone couldn't wrap
his head around it.
Was it really possible that a local guild, not even in the capital, had
information on Ra Enemy?
"Hey, kid."
The one-eyed mage called out.
"You a magic school graduate?"
"Yes, I am."
"Keh. Thought so. It's written all over your face. You think a shabby
little guild like this has anything to offer? But listen, this world isn't as soft as you think. You may
be some noble magic school graduate, but with that attitude, you won't get anywhere."
Shirone listened in silence.
If they truly had information on Ra Enemy, then the strategy he had
built so far was completely off-base.
After an hour of enduring the disdain of the other mages, Estera finally
called him to the counter.
"Did you find the information?"
Estera laid out twenty-seven cards, each with the Silver Ring Guild's
emblem printed on the back.
"These are information cards. The emblem's different, but most guilds
use these to trade intel."
Once information is revealed, its value is diminished, so a special
format was needed to facilitate monetary transactions.
"All of these cards are about Ra Enemy?"
It was more than he expected.
"Yep. I pulled everything that includes the keyword 'Ra Enemy.'
Accuracy: 100 percent."
"How much?"
He asked without hesitation. Estera smirked.
"Two gold each."
Buying all twenty-seven cards would cost a whopping 54 gold.
"Isn't that too expensive?"
"Nope. You're actually lucky. All 27 are in 'pure' condition."
Estera explained.
"These bits of intel were originally bought from someone else. When
someone like you mentions a keyword, the guild sells everything that includes it."
With this method, intangible information could be traded like items in a
shop.
"That 1 gold you paid earlier was basically the keyword fee. And 'pure'
means the intel hasn't been viewed by anyone yet."
Since different people assigned different value to the same information,
pricing by view count was logical.
"But how can I trust that?"
"Well, you can't. There's no way to prove it. That's why the price
difference isn't that big. Pure, unread info is 2 gold. Once-viewed info is 1 gold 50 silver. If it's
been viewed too much and has no confidentiality left, it's sold for 1 gold."
After thinking it over for a moment, Shirone placed 2 gold on the
counter.
"Then I'll just buy one card for now."
"Alright. Which one do you want?"
"The very last one."
Assuming Estera had already read them while transcribing, the most
important information was likely at the end.
"Smart choice. Be careful not to let anyone else see it."
Snatching up the card quickly, Shirone checked the front—and found
himself unable to look away.
'So this is the world we're dealing with...'
Below the name of the informant, a single line was written:
– Ra Enemy is presumed to be male.
