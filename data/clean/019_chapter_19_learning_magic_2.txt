Chapter 19:
Learning Magic (2)
"If you're prepared, follow me. Young master, you have been registered as a guest of
the Ozent family and admitted through special selection. Let me introduce you."
The security guards were standing at the main gate of the magic school, just like six
years ago. Whether they were the same guards who had looked at Shirone like an insect and chased him away,
he couldn't tell, but even if the people changed, the philosophy of that position would likely remain the
same.
"What is your business?"
"This is Shirone, who will be joining the magic school. Here is his admission
certificate and documentation from the Ozent family verifying his identity."
After checking the admission certificate, the guard didn't even dare to look at the
identity document.
In any case, it was clear that anyone who could cross this threshold was someone far
above their station.
"Ah, I see. It's an honor to serve you. I'm Marco, in charge of the main gate
security. I look forward to serving you."
As the guard bowed, the guard standing next to him also placed his right arm on his
stomach and gave a respectful salute.
Shirone was stunned by this treatment, so different from six years ago, but soon realized
the reality and smiled.
"Then I'll be going now. If you ever need anything, please find me."
Taking the documents from Temuran, Shirone took his first steps as a magic school
student.
As he crossed the threshold of the main gate, his heart swelled. Alpheus Magic School,
called the cradle of geniuses. What would he learn and what kind of people would he meet?
The life of Shirone the magician was just beginning.
The magic school occupied an entire mountain with an area of 75.425 square kilometers.
There were a total of twenty-two buildings and more than forty basic training grounds.
The total number of students was 420, and there were 32 teachers. With this scale, it
could certainly be called the kingdom's finest school without exaggeration.
Entering the building where the reception was located, neat and beautiful students were
walking around in groups.
Various maps, establishment history, and curriculum were posted on the walls. While
waiting after submitting his documents, Shirone carefully examined them to pass the time.
Alpheus Magic School adopted a system called Class Ten. Basic classes ranged from Ten to
Eight, advanced classes from Seven to Four, and graduating classes from Three to One.
The graduating class, the only one that applied relative evaluation, had a maximum of 30
students, of which only 10 would graduate.
Shirone clicked his tongue at the rigorous graduation method of Alpheus. Considering that
the graduation test was held only once a year, it meant that 20 talented individuals were expelled each
year.
"So you're Shirone."
Headmaster Alpheus personally came to find Shirone.
Shirone was surprised by his appearance, which hadn't changed at all in six years. But
considering this was a magic school that defied common sense, it wasn't that surprising.
"Hello. I'm Shirone."
"I think we've met before? Do you remember me?"
How could he forget? It was the event that first made him dream of becoming a magician.
"Yes. Thank you so much for then."
"Well. Anyway, let's leave the talk for later and go. You need to take the entrance
test."
Shirone was taken aback by the idea of having to take an exam right after enrolling. What
kind of test could they be conducting when he hadn't learned anything yet?
"There's nothing to worry about. It's an assessment to determine your class. Can you
do a Spirit Zone?"
"Yes, I can."
"Oh! Is that so."
He knew the child was talented and intelligent. But even so, mastering Spirit Zone on his
own would have been difficult.
In fact, it was Alpheus himself who taught Shirone the Spirit Zone, but he didn't think a
12-year-old child would succeed after just one attempt.
That thought still held true. It was for this reason that Shirone was processed last
among the incoming students.
Since there were numerous families that began magic training from birth, many of the
incoming students were not even 10 years old. Even the oldest ones were no more than 14.
Therefore, time was adjusted so that 18-year-old Shirone wouldn't lose face.
'I'll have to teach him from the basics. It's a shame, partly due to my oversight. It
would have been better if he had started just a little earlier.'
Alpheus led Shirone to the examination hall. Since other children's tests would be in
full swing by the time they arrived, his pace was leisurely.
Intending to give him a tour, they entered the building, and Shirone's mouth fell open. A
completely different world from what he had seen so far was unfolding.
Numerous students were using magic. Even though it was the basic class building, the
children's extraordinary feats made his eyes dizzy.
A girl floating at a height of four floors was chatting with a friend through a window,
while another child had three books open in the air and was reading them simultaneously.
Alpheus glanced at Shirone's reaction and grinned like a mischievous child. From here on,
things were on a different level.
Entering Class Seven, the age range of students increased significantly. They weren't
using strange magic like the children, but their eyes were full of intelligence, and they were so immersed
in conversation that they wouldn't notice if someone passed by.
"Energy of convection to bring the atmosphere below freezing point? Wouldn't it be
more efficient to condense the refrigerant?"
"But there's the disadvantage that you can't promote the reversible reaction. How
about this? Instead of reversing the direction of energy, if we rotate the time axis..."
Shirone became frightened listening to the students' conversation. He couldn't understand
what topic they were discussing at all.
At that moment, an intense noise came from the end of the corridor. A door was torn off,
and a female student ran out covering her ears.
The students looked back with surprised expressions for a moment but quickly returned to
their daily routines. Judging by their reactions, this seemed to be a common occurrence here.
The female student covering her ears spotted Alpheus and called out happily.
"Ah, Headmaster! Hello!"
"Ho ho, yes. Seems like there's a problem."
"We were doing a sound amplification experiment, but there must have been an error
in the output circuit. I think we need to redesign it."
"Balancing input and output is important. The key is to maintain electrical forces
evenly."
"Yes, I'll try again."
The female student adjusted her glasses and went back to the laboratory. Children of the
same age were gathered around a strange magic circle, discussing.
Shirone realized how small the pond he had been confined to was. Everyone seemed like a
genius.
The status board listed the grades of 270 students in the advanced class. In Class Four,
the highest within the advanced class, 42 students were listed, and the top spot was held by someone named
Karmis Eimi.
'Wow. As expected, the Karmis family is at the top.'
Based on his experience working for the Ozent family, Shirone had memorized the names of
famous noble families. Karmis was a first-rank noble family with their main house in the city of Creas.
However, Shirone wasn't intimidated from the start. It was truly unlimited competition.
He wanted to compete and see how far he could rise.
"Hey, Eimi! Let's go together!"
Shirone turned his head. He definitely heard "Eimi." It was an opportunity to
see the top student of Class Four with his own eyes. A beautiful red-haired girl was hugging a book and
waiting for her friend.
"Huh?"
Shirone's heart suddenly raced. Trauma. The emotion came before the memory. It took quite
a while before he recalled who she was.
She was the girl who had bullied him in the back alley six years ago.
***
'H-how can this be! Why is that child here?'
As Eimi walked alongside her friend, Shirone turned his head. It would be disastrous if
she remembered him, as she knew he wasn't a noble.
'Please just walk by. Just walk by.'
The two female students passed by without any particular reaction.
Shirone breathed a sigh of relief, but that was a hasty judgment stemming from not
understanding women.
"Did you see that guy who just passed? Is he new? He seems too old for an incoming
student, maybe a transfer? He's totally my type."
Even as she heard her chatty friend's words, Eimi didn't respond. Like Shirone, she too
was feeling something strange.
But it wasn't quite a trauma. Having committed countless mischievous acts in back alleys,
she couldn't easily recall Shirone.
"Hmm, strange. He definitely looked familiar. Where have I seen him?"
"What? Are you interested in him? You never even glance at guys until now."
"No. I really have seen him somewhere."
"Don't lie! You're not being honest. You've fallen for him, haven't you?"
"Oh, really, I haven't."
In response to her friend's persistent questioning, Eimi quickened her pace. When her
friend walked even faster, Eimi started running. Male students around them looked at Eimi with pleased
expressions.
Only Shirone knew that she, who was revered as a goddess at Alpheus Magic School, was
once a troublemaker who ruled the back alleys.
Magic School. Examination Hall 3.
A mirror-smooth floor was laid in a huge auditorium. Unknown characters were densely
inscribed, and not a single chair was placed.
The only area with equipment was the north of the auditorium. Five teachers sat side by
side at a table that stretched across.
Six children were taking tests, waiting for their turn, and most of them were under 10
years old.
"The tests haven't finished yet, I see. That's good timing. Watch how those children
do it."
Shirone went to where the test was being conducted. The children who were waiting and
concentrating looked at Shirone with curiosity.
Traditionally, Examination Hall 3, which tests the Spirit Zone, only conducts entrance
exams except in special cases. However, Shirone's age was too old to be a new student.
Alpheus explained to Shirone that when you enter the zone, the characters engraved on the
floor transmit special information. Based on what one sees there, the teachers check the size, strength,
flexibility, and tendencies of the Spirit Zone.
"So, Kemi. Can you use magic?"
"I can make objects float in the air."
The examiners didn't verify each claim. In a school one enters to learn, there was no
benefit to boasting falsely, as it would ultimately only harm oneself.
"I see. Then shall we try entering the Spirit Zone?"
Kemi entered the Spirit Zone, feeling the gaze of the examiners upon him. A teacher
sitting on the right observed him intently, resting his chin on the back of his hand. He had a neat
impression with slicked-back hair and deep double eyelids.
"What's the farthest number you can see?"
"6."
"Oh! 6, you say."
The man exclaimed in admiration and recorded the number in his documents. It meant that
the diameter of the Spirit Zone was 6 meters. For an incoming student to have this size was at least the
level of entering Class Nine.
It was the turn of an elderly teacher sitting next to him to ask questions. With a shaved
head and many fine wrinkles on his forehead, he habitually widened his eyes, making him look like an owl.
"What color do you feel most vividly?"
"Um... I feel like I've entered a red world."
"Red. Divergent type. That explains why his zone is wider than his age would
suggest."
The teachers nodded at the old man's words. The third and fourth teachers also asked
questions in their respective fields.
The question of whether a huge tower was standing on the right or left was to determine
whether the brain predominantly used the right or left hemisphere, and the question about how many balls
were wobbling around was to measure the density of the zone.
"How does the temperature feel right now? Is it hot or cold?"
The last teacher to ask was a young woman. Her eyebrows were fiercely thin, and her eye
corners were elongated, giving a cold impression, but her appearance was certainly attractive.
"Um... it feels a bit hot. I'm sweating too."
If he felt heat, it meant the form of the Spirit Zone was variable. However, the more
variable it was, the lower its durability, so it wasn't a clear advantage or disadvantage.
The remaining students took the test in this manner. They seemed to be of a high caliber,
as the teachers had satisfied expressions.
When one child called out the number 12, even Headmaster Alpheus couldn't help but admire
him.
After all the students finished their tests, it was Shirone's turn. Alpheus gently pushed
the nervous Shirone's back and took him to the teachers.
"Now, don't feel pressured and show your skill."
"Yes, I'll try."
Shirone wasn't pressured. From the moment he entered this place, he had made up his mind
to study from the basics.
As the tall boy entered, the examiners carefully observed Shirone. He was a student who
had been admitted through special selection, as the headmaster had notified in advance.
His resume indicated that he was a guest of the Ozent family, an unusual status, and in
special notes, it was written that he had high potential for development.
Occasionally, there are such children. Children who accidentally unlock their abilities
later in life.
But this is a magic school called the cradle of geniuses. There were countless children
who might be called geniuses outside but become inferior students once they came here. Perhaps that's why
the teachers didn't have any particular expectations.
The man with slicked-back hair on the right asked in a lukewarm voice. Sard, the flame
magician. The only subjects he had any liking for were women.
"Arian Shirone. 18 years old. Can you use magic?"
"No. I haven't learned magic yet."
"I see. Well, one would expect to be able to do a few things even without formal
learning."
There was mockery mixed in Sard's tone. Of course, his words had some truth. The Spirit
Zone serves as a conduit to bring forth knowledge from the mind into reality.
Therefore, noble children who study various knowledge from an early age often wield
simple magic on their own.
But for Shirone, who had no opportunity to acquire academic knowledge, this was
impossible. Aware of this disadvantage, he had focused solely on reading history books, but the truth was
that his knowledge that could be applied to magic was still shallow.
"Alright. Let's see. Stand in that circle and try the Spirit Zone. Ah, you can do
the Spirit Zone, right?"
If he couldn't even enter the Spirit Zone but had been admitted through special
selection, one might question the headmaster's moral integrity.
Fortunately, Shirone nodded and headed towards the circle, avoiding any unnecessary
suspicion.
Shirone closed his eyes and concentrated. As his sense of self faded, countless
sensations spread outward.
'Wow...'
Entering the zone, Shirone was overwhelmed by the abundance of information. It felt as if
he had fallen into a different dimension rather than an examination hall.
Most information was blocked within the phase space. All he could sense was the
interaction of the magical characters inscribed on the floor.
"What's the farthest number you can see?"
Numerous numbers were orbiting. Shirone focused on the farthest visible number. Since it
was through synesthesia rather than visual perception, eyesight wasn't important.
But Shirone couldn't answer.
"Shirone, what's the farthest number you can see?"
Even as Sard urged him, Shirone's lips remained closed. The teachers thought he was
feeling shy. It was natural to feel intimidated after seeing the skills of young children.
Alpheus coaxed in a kind voice.
"Shirone, just be honest. We're not ranking you; we're trying to find an appropriate
educational method for you."
Shirone spoke in an unsure voice.
"Um... I can see numbers, but they're too long to read."
"Too long to read? Do you mean you can't see them?"
"No. I can see them, but they're too long. They keep getting longer even now."
Sard was annoyed. The magical text that calculates the size of the zone only conveys
integers. He thought Shirone was embarrassed by his lack of skill and was stalling for time by rambling.
"Then just tell me roughly what numbers are at the front."
"Um, 3.14."
The teachers' eyes widened. The number Shirone was seeing was pi. Since it's an
irrational number that can't be divided into integers, it was extending infinitely.
The woman sitting on the left pushed up her glasses and snapped. Siina of ice magic. True
to her magical inclination, she had a cold personality and was a rival to Sard.
"Shirone, I'm sorry, but there is no 3.14 among the numbers we've designated. Are
you absolutely sure?"
Shirone frowned in difficulty.
"Hmm. Is that so? But that's what I see."
'It's true...'
Siina had lied. Of course, the magical characters inscribed on the floor didn't record
pi. If pi appeared, there was only one possibility.
When the zone's range exceeded what could be measured.
That meant the diameter of Shirone's expanded zone was at least 30 meters or more.
The measurement precision in Examination Hall 3, which evaluates new students, was set
rather low. This was because showing intense scenes, hot fever, or Arctic cold to immature children could
cause psychological shock.
As part of this consideration, the range for measuring the diameter of the Spirit Zone
was also limited to 30 meters.
But even for a late enrollment, not many had exceeded this limit. A zone with a diameter
of more than 30 meters was at least Class Five level or higher.
The old man sitting next to Sard asked a question.
"What color do you feel most vividly?"
"Um, blue. Everything is dyed blue."
The old man's lips protruded. Murmurs began to spread among the teachers.
If it's in the blue spectrum, it's a convergent type. This type has advantages in
durability rather than the size of the Spirit Zone.
Yet, if the diameter of the Spirit Zone exceeded 30 meters, it wasn't a case of growth
being slow for his age.
Alpheus was also continuously surprised. He finally understood why Clump had been so
confident.
But being a magician by nature, he became more critical.
If it had been a moderate talent, he would have praised it, but Shirone was already
beyond that level. Moreover, it was still a half-talent that hadn't touched the realm of magic.
This was dangerous. Praising him too much could ruin his entire life.
Shirone answered the following questions. Brain development was the bilateral type, using
both left and right simultaneously.
This was also characteristic, but what was truly noteworthy came from the next question.
"How many balls do you see floating around?"
Due to the synesthetic experience, there was no need to count individually. Just as one
can feel when something touches the skin, he could sense the number of balls all at once.
Shirone remained silent for a moment before speaking.
"867."
"867..."
The teacher who had asked the question murmured unconsciously.
Even Siina, who had been observing with a cold attitude, finally pulled her chair in and
sat properly for the first time.
The density of the zone measured how evenly mental power was distributed.
Sensing 867 balls meant that Shirone's mental power was filling the zone with a density
of 86.7 percent.
Uniform density meant that the mind was that much more stable, which inevitably increased
the success rate of magic as well.
