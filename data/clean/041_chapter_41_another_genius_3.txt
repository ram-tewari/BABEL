Chapter 41:
Another Genius (3)
"Which teacher do you guys prefer, Etela or Siena? Ah, Shirone, you probably don't care
since you have Amy, right? Nade, what about you?"
"Hmm. My type is Etela. Of course, Siena's fine too."
"Ooh! A man of culture. Etela's the best. She's got a baby face, an amazing body, and
especially her chest… wow."
Etela was captivating. Her large glasses made her look nerdy, and her waist-length hair
was always tied up haphazardly, but she was still a beauty. Her voluptuous figure, as if her body had grown
while her face remained childlike, was the number one reason students adored her.
The passionate discussion suddenly died down as the students lowered their voices and
walked away. Puzzled, Shirone stopped his Spirit Zone training and turned his head.
A strangely dressed boy was walking toward them. It was Melkordain Iruki, the eccentric
of Class Five. His blunt personality made his peers avoid him, but Nade, the friend to all, was different.
"Hey, Iruki! Are you here to train?"
"No. Patrol duty. What were you talking about so excitedly?"
"Same old stuff. Which teacher's better, things like that."
Nade suddenly realized something and looked at Shirone.
"Oh, Shirone! This is your first time talking to him, right? This guy's Iruki. Class
Five's resident outsider."
Though it had been almost a month since Shirone joined Class Five, this was his first
time meeting Iruki. It wasn't that he had been avoiding him—Iruki simply skipped more classes than he
attended.
"Oh-ho! Arian Shirone. The genius the school's keeping an eye on, right? I'm so honored I
should write this in my diary."
Shirone understood why the others kept their distance. His analytical gaze and twisted
way of speaking were unsettling.
"Hi. I'm Shirone. Nice to meet you."
"Hehe, a genius saying 'nice to meet you'? Class Seven and up, I've been watching you.
When you succeeded in the separation type, when you crossed the uncrossable bridge."
"Huh? You've been watching me?"
Shirone realized it then. That scratchy, thin voice. It was the same one that had given
him advice when his Spirit Zone wavered under the other students' criticism.
"So you were the one who talked to me back then?"
"Just gave a word of advice. Couldn't stand seeing you get swayed by kids."
Iruki waved his hand dismissively, but Shirone was grateful. Without his advice, it
would've been much harder to break out of his slump.
"Thanks. It really helped."
Iruki stared at Shirone intently.
"You're such a boring kid, huh?"
"Huh? Boring?"
It was the first time Shirone had heard that, and it stung oddly. Sensing the mood, Nade
smoothly defused the tension.
"Hey, hey! Iruki. Even if it's a compliment, that's rude to say to someone thanking you."
Iruki shrugged and turned away.
"Was it? It was a compliment, though. Anyway, do your best. Of course, it won't be easy.
Class Five's full of stubborn idiots. Kekeke."
As Iruki walked away, Nade called after him.
"Hey, where are you going? Train with us."
"Too lazy. I'd rather sleep."
Shirone gaped in disbelief. Skipping class to sleep? Class Five was one of the
highest-tier advanced classes. He couldn't understand how someone like that had made it this far.
"He's… a bit weird, huh?"
"Sorry, Shirone. He's not a bad guy once you get to know him."
"But…"
"Haha! I know what you're thinking. Digging deeper will just tire us out. That guy's a
genius too. Just a different type from you, which is the problem."
"A genius? Iruki's a genius?"
"Ah! You wouldn't know since you just enrolled."
Nade explained about Iruki. Despite being called a genius, his grades were similar to
Nade's. But none of their classmates underestimated his abilities.
Iruki was the epitome of a lopsided person. He consistently scored zero in subjects like
literature and art, where sensibility mattered, but in calculation-heavy subjects, he could ace them
blindfolded.
At first, even the teachers were eager to take him under their wing. But his personality
was so erratic that most had given up by now.
Still, rumors said he surpassed even graduates in mathematical fields, and many teachers
predicted he'd leave Class Five soon.
Iruki's specialty was computational physics, a field only possible for those with unique
brain functions—calculating phenomena in extreme environments like temperatures exceeding 100 million
degrees.
In academic circles, those with such calculative brains were called "Savant Syndrome." It
usually manifested in autistic individuals but could also appear in normal people.
The exact cause was unknown, but the prevailing theory was that damage to specific brain
areas during fetal development, followed by natural healing, opened unique thought circuits different from
ordinary humans.
Insight also used different thought circuits, but Iruki's Savant Syndrome was distinct in
its mechanical computation.
Iruki could derive the date 40,000 years in the future within a minute. If he didn't
sleep, he could recite pi's decimal places indefinitely.
After hearing Nade's explanation, Shirone was stunned. If true, it was an incredible
talent.
"Then why is he still in Class Five? No matter how you look at it, zero points doesn't
make sense. Even guessing should get some right."
"Kekeke. That's just how he is. Like a month ago…"
Nade shared an anecdote from Iruki's last written exam. As expected, he aced natural
sciences but ran into trouble with literature.
The question was about a line from poet Gilbert's *Life is a Falling Leaf*:
*"O leaf that falls in zigzags."*
Students were asked why the leaf fell in zigzags. Most answered that it symbolized the
fear of death.
But Iruki wrote:
*- The varying air resistance acting on the leaf's surface creates differential pressure
vectors, causing movement along the path of greatest force.
See attached equations based on the standard leaf model.*
Below were incomprehensible equations. Teachers familiar with Iruki let it slide, but
Adelhia, the literature teacher, wouldn't stand for it.
A mother of three, she waddled into the classroom and slammed the test paper on Iruki's
desk.
"Iruki! What is this?"
"My test."
"Not that! Look at your answer! This is a literature exam, not math!"
"Isn't dividing math and literature proof of literature's fictional nature?"
Adelhia's plump face turned red as a roasted pig.
"What do you know, acting all high and mighty? You're a student! It'll be years before
you can act like an expert! The world has deep, profound thoughts even you can't quantify!"
"Teacher, no such thing exists. If any part of the world were illogical, how could we
live without fear? My body might turn into an orc right now."
"So you're saying everything's logical? Even Gilbert's poetry?"
"Of course. That's why I answered like that."
Adelhia fumed for a while before jabbing a finger and shouting.
"Then answer this! A thousand innocent people are about to die. But you have the power to
kill one innocent person to save them. What would you choose?"
"Kill the thousand. A thousand lives are proof; one life is a hypothesis. The equation
can't be balanced from the start."
Adelhia suppressed her rising fury with superhuman effort. She had a comeback ready.
"Does God exist? Answer!"
"He might not."
Adelhia grew even more incensed.
"Who do you like more, your mom or dad? Pick one!"
"Momdad."
"Get out."
Adelhia's chubby finger trembled as she pointed at the door.
"Get out, you brat! You don't even deserve to be in my class! Out!"
A faculty meeting was convened at Adelhia's request. The teachers concluded that Iruki's
answers mocked their authority and gave him zero in all humanities subjects.
***
"Wow! That's insane."
After hearing the story, Shirone couldn't believe Iruki was an 18-year-old student like
himself.
Challenging a teacher was one thing, but sticking to his beliefs as a student took
incredible resolve.
"Haha! Told you. That's just how he is. Really hard to get close to."
"After hearing that, I get why he called me boring. But why did he say it was a
compliment?"
"Who knows? Maybe he saw something special in you. That's why I introduced you. Thought
you two might get along."
Shirone grew curious about Iruki. Had he known, he would've talked more. As he scanned
the room for Iruki, his eyes widened in surprise.
"Huh? Nade, look over there."
Iruki, with a sleepy face, was heading toward the Image Zone. Students made way for
him—not out of dislike, but as if giving him space.
"Damn it! What's he planning now? Let's go."
Shirone followed Nade to the Image Zone. Other students began gathering too.
"Is he gonna strip and lie down again like last time?"
Someone's comment made Shirone's eyes widen. Stripping in front of female students?
Contrary to worries, Iruki properly activated his Spirit Zone—a 15-meter sphere, small
for Class Five.
But size wasn't the issue. He was freely wielding the separation type, the hardest
casting style.
"Whoa! Iruki's going all out after so long!"
"Should we stop him? Etela banned him from using the Image Zone."
"Let him be. You think he'd listen?"
The shrinking sphere darted across an absurdly wide area. Iruki locked eyes with Shirone,
then flicked his finger, summoning targets that zipped around at high speed.
Students groaned and clutched their heads. Using targets without permission was
forbidden, but that thought soon vanished as they watched, mesmerized.
"Whoa…"
Iruki's Zone chased the targets. His movements were fluid, as if his mind had left his
body. The speed was breathtaking, with the sphere slicing through the air so fast it seemed to whistle.
"Hey! Iruki! What are you doing?!"
Etela, who was instructing Class Four, ran over in a panic. But Iruki ignored her. The
Zone's center bubbled with tiny lights as it swallowed a target.
"Atomic Bomb."
"You lunatic!"
As students scrambled back, an explosion powerful enough to knock people over erupted.
The deafening blast left Shirone dizzy. Everyone fell on their backsides.
Pale-faced, Shirone looked ahead. Iruki stood with his hands behind his back, staring at
him.
"See you next time, Shirone."
***
What "next time" meant became clear shortly, as Etela dragged Iruki away by the ear.
"Iruki! One more stunt like this, and you're getting disciplined!"
"Owowow! I calibrated it so the equipment wouldn't break! You know what happens if I go
all out!"
"No remorse! You're suspended for a week! No classes!"
"Nooo! That's too harsh!"
Iruki wailed but never admitted wrongdoing. He even grinned at Shirone and Nade as he was
hauled off.
"Sigh! He never changes. Shirone, sorry. I gotta go."
Nade left first. Shirone, overwhelmed by the intensity, barely noticed. The other
students were the same, buzzing with chatter.
"That guy's amazing, huh? The detonation's faster than last time. Same explosive power,
though."
"Well, he's a savant. Of course he calculates reactions perfectly."
Shirone chimed in.
"Reaction formula? You mean the equation for the explosion?"
"Yeah. I don't know much about detonation magic, but the formula affects the blast power.
Similar to Ignite, the basics of flame magic."
"I see. With that much power, he wouldn't need flame magic."
"Nah. The applications are different. Flame magic activates instantly. Even Iruki would
take time to build up to detonation. Reducing that time is a detonation mage's goal."
If Shirone's Insight specialized in teleportation, Iruki's superhuman calculations made
detonation magic perfect for him.
A key advantage was that detonation magic could create explosions larger than the Spirit
Zone itself. The magic only triggered the explosion, which was the result. Ideal for the separation type,
where the Zone shrank with distance.
'Melkordain Iruki…'
His first impression was as explosive as his magic.
