Chapter 348:
Divine Transcendance (5)
'Arrogant brat…!'
Bawio attempted a technique. He tried to disrupt Rian's balance by
entwining his arms around him. However, Rian also swung his arms to block the move.
Annoyed, Bawio pushed Rian. Then he collided with him again. With a
bang, the second headbutt landed. This time, Rian also felt dizzy.
Finally, seeing an opportunity for a tackle, Bawio rushed at Rian,
wrapping his arms around his waist. As Rian staggered back, Bawio hooked his leg, opening up his waist.
If he could slam him down now, it would be over.
"Ugh!"
Rian barely twisted his body to escape. He still wasn't fully conscious.
He needed time.
But Bawio didn't give him a chance to recover and charged again. Having
been thwarted twice, Rian instinctively countered by thrusting his head forward. His mind flashed, but
it wasn't as bad as the first and second times.
The two stood with their foreheads pressed together, glaring at each
other. Blood was flowing from both their noses.
Bawio couldn't contain his anger. For someone aspiring to be a heavy
infantryman, strength was as important as swordsmanship. The fact that Rian had pushed him this far,
despite never losing in wrestling before, raised Rian's value.
'Hmph. I can't let it end like this.'
Bawio smirked and said,
"You're holding up pretty well. But are you okay? You look
dazed."
In reality, Rian was dazed. In a strength contest, you had to match your
breathing to your opponent's reactions. It was like a contest to see who could hold their breath the
longest underwater. With a lack of oxygen, even standing made him feel faint.
"Then let's start folding."
Rian ground his teeth. Had Bawio been fighting without using folding all
this time? As if to prove he wasn't lying, immense power emanated from Bawio's body.
"Hahaha! This is too easy!"
Bawio toyed with Rian at will. He lifted and dropped him, swung him left
and right like playing with a child. With more than double the strength difference, techniques were
useless.
'You didn't think I'd end it like this, did you?'
Bawio hugged Rian's chest and lifted him. As he applied pressure, all
the air was forced out of Rian's lungs.
"Ugh!"
The feeling of having the air forcibly expelled from his body was the
height of discomfort, but it was nothing compared to what followed.
Bawio lifted Rian's arm and slipped it under his armpit, attempting an
abdominal stretch while standing.
"What, what is that…?"
The cadets looked on in astonishment. It was an abdominal stretch, also
known as a cobra twist.
It was a legitimate wrestling move, but successfully applying it to a
standing opponent was difficult, usually practiced as a joke or for training unless there was a
significant skill gap.
And this was exactly what Bawio was aiming for.
Rian was caught in the move, his side twisted and his arm lifted. It
looked ridiculous, but it was a position where he couldn't exert any strength, making it impossible to
escape once caught.
"Ugh!"
A groan escaped Rian's mouth. His muscles were stretched to their
limits, and even a slight movement from Bawio sent pain shooting up his spine.
Bawio turned his body to show off Rian's predicament to the cadets. It
was humiliating, but he had no choice but to be dragged along.
"How about I break your back like this? Huh?"
Every slight movement from Bawio made Rian's spine creak. Satisfied,
Bawio turned to Tess. He saw her biting her lip and trembling.
'I told you not to underestimate me.'
Bawio had never considered going easy on Rian to impress Tess. A woman
in love was single-minded, no matter how impressive another man might be. To turn her heart, he had to
completely ruin Rian and remove him from her sight.
"Why don't you surrender now? Or should we play a bit more?"
Rian didn't answer. His gaze was fixed on what Bawio was looking
at.
'Tess…'
Tess looked like she was about to burst into tears. She was a righteous
person. Therefore, the most frustrated person here was not Rian, but Tess.
"Break it."
"What?"
Bawio asked, bewildered.
"I told you, I don't care if you kill me. But I will never
surrender."
"Hahaha! Why are you always so serious? This is just an evaluation.
There's no need to go all out. Besides, this won't erase your shame."
Bawio dragged Rian around again. He could feel the emotions of the
cadets. Some were fearful, some were angry, some were sympathetic. But he knew these were the gazes of
the weak looking at the strong.
"What the…?"
At that moment, a powerful force was transmitted. Bawio, startled as if
bitten by an insect, looked at Rian.
'He still has strength left?'
Rian's face didn't show any strain. His eyes were cold and lifeless, as
if time had stopped.
'Straighten your back. Straighten your back. Straighten your
back.'
Rian's gaze was inward.
As the command to straighten his back was delivered, his side began to
rise slowly, as if by magic.
"Huh. Look at this guy."
Bawio laughed dismissively but couldn't completely hide his tension. He
was subtly exerting all his strength, but he couldn't stop Rian from moving.
'What is this? Why is this happening?'
Bawio gritted his teeth and flexed all his muscles.
"Huuuuup!"
And the moment he felt the response, his heart sank. It wasn't a mistake
or complacency. Rian's upper body continued to rise, and he couldn't stop it.
His heart raced, and a chill ran down his spine. Something other than
strength was lifting Rian. A massive force, like the weight of the world, brought an unprecedented
fear.
Kuan watched with unblinking eyes. This was it. The incomprehensible
mystery of divine transcendence.
'This is impossible. How is this happening? What is moving him?'
Rian wasn't in a position to exert strength. Yet he was defying the
principles of leverage and standing up. Therefore, it wasn't strength moving him. Some rule of the world
was lifting him.
"Hey, wait! Stop!"
Finally, a desperate voice escaped Bawio's mouth. No matter how much
strength he used, he was endlessly being pushed back. As Rian's posture returned, Bawio's body twisted
grotesquely. Finally, the cobra twist was reversed, and his muscles stretched to their limits.
"Aaaargh!"
The cadets were stunned as the victim screamed. Rian neither vented his
anger nor mocked Bawio. He was solely focused on moving his body.
"Surrender! I lost! Please stop! Not my back!"
Rian didn't stop. He hadn't been angry when Bawio performed his mocking
act. He had long discarded shame in the face of his lack of talent.
But to defeat him, his opponent had to give everything. And even if it
was the folly of someone without talent, he couldn't forgive Bawio for angering Tess.
'Yes. This will do.'
Rian finally made his decision. He pressed Bawio's face down with his
armpit. There was a cracking sound as something twisted.
"Aaaargh!"
Bawio screamed as if his jaw was dislocated. With their bodies
entangled, he couldn't even fall. As his abdominal muscles tore, all strength left his body, and he hung
limp like a puppet with its strings cut, drooling.
Rian grabbed Bawio's head under his armpit and pushed him away. Bawio
twisted in pain from the second impact as he hit the ground. Tears streamed down his face from the
pain.
In the prolonged silence, the cadets' gazes changed. The image of the
absolute strongman they had seen in Bawio earlier was gone.
'Poor guy. He was supposed to go on vacation today, but now he'll be
recovering for three months.'
Bawio propped himself up with his hands. But he screamed and fell again
from the pain. He barely lifted his head and shouted in frustration.
"Don't think this changes anything! I'm just weak! You're not
strong!"
Bawio's venomous words, even as he belittled himself, showed his
malice.
But Rian heard nothing.
The aftermath of the forced physical exertion made his muscles feel like
they were tearing apart.
'Damn. I overdid it.'
Rian counted to ten in his head. As he counted, the pain gradually
subsided. It was an incredible recovery ability he had gained from breaking through his limits every
day.
'I can move now.'
As his hearing returned, he heard Bawio's voice.
"Do you think this will always work? I'll get stronger! But that's your
limit. You'll live as a loser forever!"
"That bastard…!"
Tess clenched her fists. If it weren't for the evaluation, she would
have run over and punched his torn abdomen.
Meanwhile, Rian remained calm. He returned to his seat and draped his
training uniform over his shoulder. Without a word, he turned and walked towards the exit of the
training ground.
"R-Rian?"
Tess and the other cadets looked at Rian in shock. Kuan's cold voice
pierced his back.
"Rian. Sit down. Leaving without permission is a fail."
"Then fail me."
Rian couldn't see any difference between last place and failing. No
matter what achievements he showed, the school wouldn't give points to a swordsman who couldn't use
Schema.
As Rian left the training ground, the atmosphere turned cold. No cadet
had ever defied Instructor Kuan like that, and some were already praying for Rian's soul.
'Is this the limit?'
Kuan wasn't displeased, nor was he worried about Rian. As long as he
took responsibility for his actions, he didn't care if the cadets ruined their lives.
But today, he didn't feel light-hearted. Divine Transcendence was a real
power, and Rian could wield it.
How long could he endure? One day, his body would fail to withstand the
power of his will and break.
Kuan wrote "fail" on Rian's evaluation sheet and added
"unauthorized departure" in parentheses.
After the wrestling evaluation ended, Tess went to find Rian without
even taking time to wash up. Just then, Rian came out of the dormitory. Despite the turmoil inside him,
he looked neat, as if he had even taken a shower.
"Rian! What happened? Do you really want to fail?"
Rian walked past Tess with determined strides. This made her anxious.
Once Rian's fire was lit, it spread uncontrollably.
"Please, say something. Leaving during an evaluation is serious. It's
not just about last place. You know what happens if you get marked by the instructors, right?"
"I don't care. Whether they mark me or not."
Tess understood Rian's frustration at not being rewarded for his
efforts, but this time the situation was really bad. Defying a superior was never acceptable.
"Rian, think again. You've endured well so far."
"It's not because I can't endure. I'm just sick of it."
Seeing Rian's twisted face, Tess realized he was serious. But what could
be done now? Everything was happening too quickly.
Rian arrived at the instructor's office. When he entered, Kuan was
sorting evaluation sheets. Glancing at Rian's salute, Kuan turned his gaze back to his desk,
uninterested.
"What is it? You left so abruptly."
"I have something to say."
"Go back. You failed. A cadet who acts on their own isn't worth
evaluating."
Rian placed an envelope on the desk. Seeing the writing on it, Kuan
frowned and looked up. But Rian, having already made up his mind, spoke calmly.
"It's a resignation letter. I'm leaving the swordsmanship
school."
