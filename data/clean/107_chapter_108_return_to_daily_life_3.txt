Chapter 108:
Return to Daily Life (3)
Siena smiled and glanced sideways at Etella. She had been tormented by overwhelming
guilt for the past ten days. As expected, Etella approached Shirone with a face that looked ready to burst
into tears at any moment.
"Shirone."
"Teacher, I'm sorry. I've done it again…."
Without even waiting to hear Shirone's words, Etella pulled him into a tight embrace.
"I'm sorry. It's my fault. If only I'd been more careful. This won't happen again."
To their friends, it looked like a beautiful embrace between teacher and student, but for
Shirone, who was on the receiving end, it was pure torture. Etella hugged him so tightly that he couldn't
breathe. Embarrassment aside, he genuinely felt like he was about to suffocate.
"Uh, um, Teacher…."
"Hmm?"
"I'm sorry, but… please let me live."
"Oh my!"
When Shirone's voice came out muffled, Etella hurriedly released him.
"I'm sorry. I was so happy I couldn't help myself…."
Shirone gasped for air, his face pale. But even this sensation was part of the present
world. Smiling at the sheer joy of being back, he said:
"Now it really feels like I've returned."
When their friends' eyes began to gleam mischievously, Shirone realized his mistake and
frantically waved his hands.
"No, no, it's not like that. I mean, this is…."
"You little brat, what's all this 'this and that'?"
Amy rushed over and pinched Shirone's cheek.
"What? Do I not feel real enough to you? Huh?"
"No! That's not what I meant!"
Nade and Iruki burst into laughter. Alpheas watched Shirone interacting with his friends
with a fond expression before quietly slipping out of the infirmary. From now on, he would be the busiest
person.
"Headmaster."
Turning around, he saw Shirone, who had somehow slipped out unnoticed, closing the
infirmary door and walking toward him.
"What is it? You should rest for a while. Catch up with your friends. The school will
likely remain closed for some time anyway."
"Yes, thank you for your concern. But… there's something I've been curious about."
"Hmm? What's that?"
Shirone recounted everything that had happened—from the moment he entered the infinite
realm to his return to the original world through the black hole.
At first, Alpheas listened with interest. But as the story progressed, his expression
gradually hardened. Especially when Shirone described the woman's abilities, he closed his eyes and sighed.
"Did that woman say she knew me?"
"Yes. She asked me to send her regards to you. My memory is hazy, but I think that's what
she said."
Shirone wondered if he had brought up something unnecessary. Alpheas's expression was far
worse than he had anticipated.
"I see. I think I know who she is."
"Huh? You know her?"
Shirone's eyes widened. Alpheas was undoubtedly a great mage, but the woman existed in a
realm beyond life itself. It was strange that he had met both of them, given they resided in different
worlds.
But Alpheas's thoughts were different. It wasn't a coincidence but inevitability. Shirone
meeting her was predetermined from the moment he awakened the Immortal Function.
"Who is she, really?"
"Adrias Miro. In her time, she was called the Labyrinth of Space-Time. She was my
disciple, specializing in Scale Magic."
Shirone's expression turned blank. He had never imagined she was Alpheas's disciple.
Learning that her abilities, which he had thought were divine, were actually magic came as a shock.
"What is Scale Magic?"
Alpheas stroked his beard as he pondered how to explain.
"Shirone, in the magical society, those who have attained the Immortal Function are
called Unlockers."
"Yes, I've heard that."
"Then this will be easier. Miro is also an Unlocker. Even as a child, her curiosity was
extraordinary. She delved endlessly—into worlds larger than the universe and smaller than particles. Scale
Magic is a form of magic only those who have comprehended the infinity of space-time can wield."
Shirone recalled his encounter with Miro. Indeed, her primary ability, the glass orb,
freely manipulated the relativity of space-time.
"Miro said she had been watching me for a long time. Is that possible?"
"Don't worry. Miro exists in a different dimension from you. But when you unlocked the
Immortal Function, you momentarily reached the same dimension. She must have been waiting for that moment."
"But why me? Unlockers are rare, but according to Arcane, they're not that few in number.
There must be many Unlockers stronger than me."
Alpheas hesitated, stroking his beard. What he was about to say was a top-secret matter
known only to teachers who had served for over 20 years.
"Miro was the founding president of the Supernatural Psychic Science Research Club."
Shirone pictured Miro's face. It was surprising enough that she had founded the club he
now belonged to, but as the first president, she would have been active nearly 20 years ago, yet she seemed
barely aged.
"Miro wasn't fixated on phenomena. She believed there were too many things knowledge
couldn't explain. Together with her peers, she established the research club in Istas. Even Micaea Gaold,
the current head of the Thorhmia Magic Association, was one of the early members."
Shirone imagined the members of that time. According to Nade, the master key of Istas's
magic vault was created by a servant ability user similar to Iruki. It couldn't have been Miro or Gaold. He
could roughly guess how extraordinary the early members must have been.
As Shirone lost himself in thought, Alpheas also reminisced about events from 18 years
ago. Miro had been a good kid. If not for that day's incident… Suddenly, he realized he had omitted
something and turned back to Shirone.
"Shirone, do you know how many students there are in total?"
It didn't seem like he expected an answer, as Alpheas continued immediately.
"Of course, the total number changes. Some graduate, take leaves of absence, or face
disciplinary action. But no matter the number, this school is always short by one person."
Alpheas turned his gaze out the window as he spoke.
"Miro never graduated. She still resides in this school."
Shirone gulped. It felt like hearing a ghost story passed down through the school. But
what did it mean for her to "reside" here? Hadn't he just said she lived in another dimension?
At that moment, Scale Magic flashed through Shirone's mind.
"Then… could it be…?"
Alpheas nodded.
"Yes. Miro is in Istas. Of course, it's a higher dimension created by Scale Magic. Those
who know call it the Upper Layer."
Alpheas placed a hand on Shirone's shoulder.
"Shirone, promise me this. Don't speak of today's events to anyone. The Upper Layer is
nothing you should meddle with. Never, ever involve yourself with it. It could plunge the world into chaos."
Shirone couldn't answer immediately. An unknown world. Unlockers. Scale Magic. All this
information was shaking his mind. Of course, he had no intention of recklessly stepping into danger. But
promising never to get involved would be a lie in some way.
Alpheas sighed as if he had expected this.
"If you truly wish to learn about Miro, I'll allow you to investigate the Kergo Ruins.
I'm not suggesting this to placate you. It's far too dangerous to dive straight into the Upper Layer. So
please, at least abide by this."
Finally, Shirone nodded. Alpheas wouldn't be so strict without good reason. And Shirone
himself also preferred a gradual approach.
"I understand. But knowing she's in Istas is eerie. I don't think I'll be able to sleep
in the research club. What if she suddenly appears?"
"Don't worry. Miro would never leave the space she created."
Shirone tilted his head. Such certainty implied there was proof.
"But she's an extraordinary person. Is there a special reason?"
Alpheas looked out the window with a sorrowful expression. His words weren't spoken with
absolute confidence. But he knew Miro would never return.
"For Miro… this world is far too painful."

Alpheas, Canis, and Arin left for the capital, Bashka, to undergo an investigation by the
Magic Association. According to the teachers, the school would remain temporarily closed until the
Association's decision was finalized.
Shirone was admitted to the infirmary. The medical staff decided to monitor his condition
since his heart had stopped for ten days. Shirone, too, had no desire to do anything for the time being, so
he passed the time with his friends.
"Shirone, I'm sorry. You must have felt so lonely."
Seriel spoke with a tearful expression. The thing she regretted most after regaining her
memories was pushing Shirone away harshly when he brought her coffee.
"It's fine. It was because of the magic. You didn't do anything wrong, Seriel-senpai."
"Even so, how could I forget you?"
"Really, it's okay. Please don't dwell on it."
Seriel's eyes softened like a deer's.
"Shirone, you're so kind. So, what will you do now? Will you demand my body?"
"Huh?"
Shirone's face twisted in disbelief. His friends' reactions were no different. Amy shook
her head vigorously, pressing her fingers to her forehead.
"Seriel, what kind of books have you been reading lately?"
"This one!"
As if she had been waiting for the question, Seriel pulled out a novel. The cover was
provocatively red, with the title "Primitive Noose" written on it.
"This is the hottest book in bookstores right now. It's about a married nobleman who
falls in love with a maid. There's this line: 'I've broken your plate. What will you do now? Will you demand
my body?' And then the nobleman ties her up tightly with a rope and…."
"Stop! That's enough!"
Amy raised her hand to cut her off. It felt like they shouldn't hear any more.
"What's wrong? The interesting part is just starting."
"Seriel, no matter how much you love novels, don't confuse them with reality. Those books
use sensationalism to captivate readers."
"Hmph! You're just saying that because you haven't read it. If you gave it a try, you'd
be hooked. Want to borrow it?"
As Seriel held out "Primitive Noose," Amy quickly turned her head away. If she accepted
the offer now, she'd be subjected to at least a week of Seriel's enthusiastic reviews.
Shirone leaned back against a cushion and sighed.
"Ah… Today's the last day in the infirmary. Thanks for visiting me all this time. You
too, Seriel-senpai."
"Why the sad talk? Honestly, I was dying from all the studying in the graduating class.
Amy and I were glad for the break."
Amy didn't deny it. The daily competition for rankings had pushed her stress to the
limit. The temporary school closure was a blessing for graduating students.
Seriel's expression turned gloomy.
"But I'm worried about the headmaster. Honestly, this decision was unexpected. It's
something the Creas autonomous region could handle, but he insisted on undergoing the Magic Association's
inspection."
Alpheas had voluntarily submitted to the indictment. Given his high reputation on the
continent, he could have easily smoothed things over, but he fully accepted the student council's claims. As
a result, the case was transferred to Inspection Division 3, known as the most ruthless in the Magic
Association.
"Inspection Division 3 is called the special investigation unit of the Magic Association.
They follow the law strictly, ignoring connections or favors. Even Headmaster Alpheas won't escape
disciplinary action."
Shirone thought it was for the best.
"The headmaster has carried his pain for 40 years. If this opportunity can lighten his
burden even slightly, I think accepting punishment is a good thing."
Nade chimed in.
"Let's wait and see. Who are we even worrying about? Alpheas, a certified 4th-class mage.
Once the school reopens, we'll be busy again, so let's rest while we can."
"Yeah. That sounds good."
Shirone looked out the window.
The afternoon sun cast a drowsy light.

Capital, Bashka.
Viewed from the Zije Mountains, Bashka's landscape was dominated by the royal palace
towering into the sky, with key institutions spreading concentrically around it. The capital's residents
said that the buildings visible from the Zije Mountains symbolized the nation.
One of them was the Magic Association. The 18-story building managed all matters
concerning mages registered in the Thorhmia Kingdom and handled magical society's oversight and intelligence
tasks in coordination with other institutions.
Any mage would want to visit at least once, but Sad, assigned as the escort for Alpheas,
Canis, and Arin, felt like a cow being led to slaughter.
"Master, is this really the best course?"
"It's what I wanted. My only regret is that it took so long."
Alpheas's resolve was firm. Sad hadn't asked with much expectation. But once they crossed
the Association's threshold, there would be no turning back, so he wanted to confirm his master's will.
"Understood. Then I'll wait until the trial concludes."
Alpheas wore a cheerful expression. With Canis and Arin in tow, he turned himself in to
the Inspection Division, and the three were each confined to solitary cells to await their fate.
The next day, Alpheas was the first to enter the interrogation room. Meanwhile, Canis and
Arin waited in an isolation facility lined with magical devices. The mana suppression devices made entering
the Spirit Zone impossible. But beyond that, even Harvest couldn't function.
After about three hours, the summons came.
"Canis, Arin. Come out."
Though not officially convicted yet, the Magic Association staff already treated the two
like criminals. Likely because they had heard Alpheas's testimony.
Canis and Arin silently complied. As they entered the hallway leading to the
interrogation room, they saw Alpheas walking toward them from the opposite end. Canis planned to pass by
indifferently, but just before they crossed paths, Alpheas turned to the guard.
"Sorry, could you give us a moment to talk?"
"Ah, yes. But not too long."
Canis snorted at the guard's deferential attitude, so different from how he treated them.
Alpheas received respect not for his strength but his fame. It irked him to see the man exuding the victor's
composure despite being weaker than Arcane.
Once the guards stepped away, Alpheas spoke.
"Don't be too nervous. Just answer a few questions honestly."
"You're mistaken. We're not the docile students you teach. We've fought countless
battles. No situation frightens us."
Canis, having experienced life-and-death battles, possessed something his peers lacked.
But such a life had also cost him much. Alpheas wanted to help him and Arin reclaim what they had lost.
"Actually, that's why I wanted to see you."
"What do you mean?"
"Once this is over, how about enrolling in the magic school?"
Canis felt like he'd been insulted. Did this man not understand how he had lived? After
learning magic through life-and-death struggles, was he supposed to mix with hothouse flowers?
"Don't mock me. My master didn't lose because he was weak. If not for Abyss Nova—if we
had fought properly—I could have blown you away in one hit."
"That might have been possible."
Alpheas readily admitted it.
"But if it hadn't been that way, I might have knelt first and apologized."
Canis bit his lip. The words were unacceptable, but he couldn't refute them.
"Canis, evil is like that. No matter how justified your reasons, you'll still be hated.
You can rise higher. I want to offer you a bright future, not darkness."
"Why do you care about me? Is this pity?"
"I was also Arcane's disciple. That makes me your senior. Just as time can't be reversed,
connections last forever. If you want to start a new life, I'll help."
Canis didn't respond. What kind of new life could there be now? All he knew was battle,
and thus, his fate was to live on the battlefield.
As Alpheas walked away, the guards seized Canis and Arin and dragged them to the
interrogation room. Unlike the infernal prison they had imagined, the room was neat and tidy. A diminutive
man sat at the table with his back turned. From behind, all they could see was his silver hair.
"Inspector, these are the suspects in this case, Canis and Arin."
"Ah, come this way."
His voice was like grating metal. True to his reputation as a field agent of the infamous
Inspection Division 3, his aura was razor-sharp. Canis and Arin circled the table to face the inspector.
Contrary to expectations, his appearance wasn't intimidating. He had a cat-like pointed chin and narrow eyes
that smiled.
"I'm Inspector Sakiri of Inspection Division 3. You are Canis and Arin, correct?"
"Yes."
Canis answered calmly. But no response came from Arin. Not wanting to provoke the
inspector, Canis turned to her.
"Arin, what's wrong?"
Arin trembled, her face pale. Canis deduced from her shaken eyes that she had seen
something shocking.
'What could make her react like this?'
"Canis, I can't read him…."
It was impossible for Arin, an Awakened, to fail to perceive emotions unless there were
none to begin with or they had been artificially erased.
To Arin, Sakiri's form was grotesque. His entire body was smooth like mirrored metal, and
his egg-shaped face lacked any features. His hands and feet were conical, making even the slightest emotion
impossible to discern. It was the form of an Awakened who had perfectly suppressed all emotion.
"Oh dear, the lady seems startled. It's called Awakening, right? We can block magic, but
innate traits are harder to suppress. Not that I expect you two to cause trouble."
Sakiri, perhaps intrigued, set down his pen and crossed his legs.
Arin watched as his conical legs twisted. They coiled like pretzels, then unraveled
repeatedly.
'This man is toying with us.'
He wasn't just suppressing emotions. He could control them perfectly.
"Focus."
Arin jerked her head up. The inspector's mirror-smooth face elongated into a cone,
stopping just before her forehead.
"Let's proceed. We're on official business, after all. Arin, correct?"
"Y-yes."
Terrified, Arin wasn't even sure if she had answered. The sharp cone aimed at her
forehead was likely Sakiri's piercing gaze. She had never seen an Awakened form so pristine. It made her
think no criminal could escape this man unscathed.
Canis and Arin finally faced reality. They were in the clutches of the kingdom's most
powerful and authoritative judicial body. As the two sat deflated, Sakiri's needle-like face returned to its
smooth, metallic egg shape.
"Well, we've prepared a statement based on Alpheas's testimony, but there are a few
things to confirm. Answer my questions."
Sakiri checked various points through Q&A. Arin and Canis answered honestly. After
about an hour, Sakiri tidied the documents with a satisfied expression.
"Yes. Most of the statements align. There are discrepancies, but nothing major."
His words were oddly casual for an inspector. But Sakiri continued as if it were trivial.
"Isn't this a form of brainwashing? Arcane exploited your unhappy lives to brainwash you
from childhood."
"What?"
Canis's eyes sharpened. He'd rather be called a villain. Sakiri's words negated the very
foundation of his life.
"Master never forced anything. I acted on my own. Everything was my doing."
Arin, aware of the inspector's strength, grew anxious as Canis flared up. But Sakiri's
Awakened form remained unchanged.
"A vicious method. He saved you, made you revere him, then said things like, 'I need
this, but it's not like you have to do it.'"
Canis didn't understand. He was here to atone. Now, suddenly, it was brainwashing? Were
they planning to send him to a mental hospital?
"So what do you want me to do? What changes if I admit to being brainwashed?"
"Well… you'd both be acquitted."
Canis's expression went blank. He had followed Arcane to break criminals out of Inferno.
He had endangered hundreds of lives at the magic school. Even if he wasn't the mastermind, acquittal was
impossible.
"Acquitted…? Us?"
"Ah, of course, only if you admit to being brainwashed. The Association is focusing on
that possibility."
Admit it, and they'd be free. Both of them could start new lives as if nothing had
happened.
For once, Canis felt his blood run cold. What was stranger was the Association's stance.
In a time when catching criminals was paramount, why were they so eager to let them go?
"Ah, one more thing."
Sakiri rummaged through the documents as if he had just remembered. Maybe he had. Arin
couldn't read his emotions, after all.
"Arcane's lawyer sent a formal notice. He requested anonymity, but it's been notarized.
Take a look. His asset records, will, and inheritance documents."
"Will?"
Sakiri slid the documents across the table as if no explanation was needed.
Canis checked them with trembling hands. Arcane's will was unremarkable, listing matters
to be handled posthumously in a calm tone.
Among them was this line:
Chapter Document: All assets transferred to Canis.
'Master….'
He didn't think of Arcane as secretly warm or caring. The man had been unapologetically
firm in his beliefs, so his treatment of Canis had likely been equally honest.
Arcane proved it with his will. Though he had seemed indifferent to his disciple's life,
he had considered Canis his only student.
Setting aside the will, Canis checked the asset list.
Real estate, including dungeons, magical equipment, expensive artifacts, rare herbs, and
bank deposits were neatly itemized.
'1, 10, 100, thousand, ten thousand….'
Canis counted the total at the end.
'4.8 billion gold….'
An amount befitting the wealth accumulated by the great mage Arcane over 150 years. Yet
Canis couldn't comprehend the current situation. First, they were suddenly acquitted, and now he was the
heir to a 4.8-billion-gold fortune.
Sakiri scratched his head awkwardly.
"But there's a slight problem. Arcane left his assets to you, clearly acknowledging you
as his disciple. But the Association sees brainwashing as implanting a false self. In the end, you were
deceived by Arcane. Otherwise, how could you commit such atrocities? That's why the Association is
considering acquittal. But if that happens…."
"I can't inherit? Because I'm no longer his disciple?"
Sakiri smiled warmly for the first time. Of course, his Awakened form didn't change a
whit.
"Yes. Exactly. Arcane's heir is the brainwashed Canis, not the un-brainwashed one. The
money would be seized as illegal funds and returned to the Association. So before that, we wanted your
thoughts. Admit to being brainwashed, and you'll be acquitted. Just sign here."
Sakiri slid another document forward. Canis stared at it blankly.
A waiver of inheritance.
Admitting to brainwashing would acquit them. They were merely Arcane's victims. But the
fortune would end up in someone else's hands.
In the end, the Association had chosen 4.8 billion gold over two insignificant kids.
"I can give you time, but it might hurt your case. It could imply voluntary crime.
Honestly, does it make sense? Of course you were brainwashed. Why else would you commit such heinous acts?"
Canis's mind went blank. He wasn't afraid to fight and die. But the inspector said they
were innocent. He had clearly done wrong, yet it was as if he hadn't.
'This place is no different from Radum.'
The outside world wasn't a hothouse bathed in sunlight either. It was a battlefield where
colossal desires swirled under the guise of law.
As Canis hesitated, Sakiri clicked his tongue and stood.
"Then I'll give you some time. About an hour—"
"I'll sign."
Sakiri, halfway up, paused. Then he smiled and sat back down.
"A wise choice. Here…."
He handed over his own fountain pen. Canis signed the waiver.
The pen scratched against the paper. Sakiri took it back with a pleased smile and checked
the document.
"Congratulations on your freedom. Now that it's over, let me say you made the right
choice. Hiring a lawyer would have complicated things."
A lawyer. Canis hadn't even considered it. Maybe he should have.
No, Sakiri wasn't lying.
If the Magic Association had been lenient, they wouldn't have presented the waiver in the
first place.
"What do we do now?"
"Hmm. Well, you can leave. You're free."
Canis and Arin had been thoroughly outplayed. But Sakiri, as if it were none of his
concern, gathered the documents and turned away.
"Where do we go for the release procedures…."
"There aren't any. Just leave."
The dismissive attitude made Canis realize the truth.
From the start, the Magic Association hadn't cared about them. They had only spared two
hours to reclaim Arcane's wealth.
It wasn't just his pride that was wounded. The Association's overwhelming power, which he
could do nothing about, was far more humiliating. It made him realize just how extraordinary Arcane had been
to stand against nations.
Canis regained his composure. It was clear now. He was a battle mage. And knowing how to
fight, he had no more hesitation.
"Hey, you."
Sakiri turned at the door.
"Yes?"
"You're a mage, right?"
"Hmm, you could say that. All Association staff are mages."
"What's your rank?"
"Certified 5th class, why?"
Canis gauged his opponent's level. An inspector of the Magic Association was a 5th-class
mage. That was the kind of man he was.
"This isn't the end for me. I'll never stay down."
"I see. As you should. Well, I'll be going."
"Someday… I'll make you my subordinate."
Sakiri's hand on the doorknob froze. After a moment's thought, he turned back with a thin
smile.
"You'll have to study hard. This position is highly competitive. But if by some chance
that day comes…."
Sakiri placed his right hand over his heart.
"I'll be in your care."
Canis didn't react to the inspector's uncharacteristic gesture. He only realized how
thoroughly pragmatic Sakiri was. Bowing cost nothing. The future was anyone's guess.
As Sakiri left, Arin smiled at Canis. At least they were free. Wasn't that better than
rotting in prison with 4.8 billion gold?
"Canis, you made the right choice."
"There are… so many strong people."
Canis's lips curled faintly. With a new goal, his heart pounded as fiercely as when he
had first met Arcane.
"Arin, let's get stronger."
"Yeah…."
Arin looked at the door Sakiri had exited. Canis would return here someday. Since this
wasn't their end, she too could move forward.

Leaving the interrogation room, Sakiri headed straight for the Inspector's Office. The
matter had been resolved easily given its gravity. Arcane's death had been pivotal. There were no legal
conflicts, and Alpheas's admission of guilt had helped.
The Inspector's Office was an elegant room with marble floors. A man known as the Iron
Inspector, a legend of Inspection Division 3, stood with his hands behind his back, gazing out the window.
"Inspector, the approval documents for this case."
"Leave them on the desk."
Sakiri placed the file on the desk. Normally, he would leave, but this time curiosity got
the better of him, and he turned at the door.
"Is this really alright for the Alpheas case?"
"Why? Do you think there'll be complaints from above?"
"A six-month suspension is lenient. But I doubt there'll be complaints. It's a
40-year-old case with no intent to harm, and converting 4.8 billion gold into bail seems fair."
"Then why ask?"
The question implied things were proceeding as expected. Sakiri regretted stirring
trouble but couldn't backtrack now.
"I wondered if there was an element of atonement."
The inspector was silent for a long time. Then he slowly turned to face Sakiri.
"I did feel guilty toward Alpheas. But I've never let personal feelings dictate a
criminal's punishment."
"My apologies. That was out of line."
"You may leave."
Flustered, Sakiri hurried out. Though infamous among criminals, within the Association,
he was just another mage wary of his superior.
The inspector opened the file. After carefully reviewing the contents, he took out a
fountain pen and brought it to the approval line. Countless memories flashed through his mind. Now a
white-haired old man, recalling those days made his jaw ache where Alpheas had struck him.
"Tch. I should've brought a gift."
After the incident with Erina, he had never visited Alpheas again, but he had lived
refusing to accept his defeat.
Yet over 40 years, photon theory had evolved and revolutionized, continuing to change the
world even now.
He could no longer deny how genius Alpheas's achievements had been.
"Heh. A belated gift. The Gold Circle was rightfully yours."
The inspector inscribed his signature on the document.
