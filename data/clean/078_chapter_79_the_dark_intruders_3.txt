Chapter 79:
The Dark Intruders (3)
Shirone, whose chest was pounding, was calmed down by Iruki.
"I get it. Don't get too excited. It's just how it is. Anyway, I believe you. You're
saying people have lost their memories of you."
Neide spoke.
"But we still remember. Couldn't this be a clue to the situation?"
"I think so too. There's only one reason I can think of why we haven't forgotten Shirone.
We weren't at school for the past two days. What could have happened while we were gone?"
Shirone and his group fell into thought. While they were away, someone manipulated not
only the entire student body but also the faculty's minds? It was a hard-to-swallow assumption for now.
"Could it be a supernatural phenomenon? Historically, there have been quite a few similar
cases."
Iruki was skeptical.
"Well, I think those cases are also related to magic. But if it's that kind of thing, how
do we explain why we haven't lost our memories?"
"It might be because we were in Istas. We've been here the whole time after parting ways
with Shirone."
Shirone asked,
"What does that have to do with Istas?"
"There have always been rumors about Istas having mysterious powers. Of course, it's just
a rumor, but we should consider everything we can. It's related to our safety too."
Iruki said.
"So, you're saying there's a chance we could lose our memories too."
"It's not something we can guarantee."
Silence fell. It would be sad if people couldn't remember them, but the possibility of
them losing their memories was also a problem.
Neide spoke up bravely.
"Still… we should go out, right?"
"Well, hiding won't solve anything, will it?"
Their friends were right. But Shirone, who had already been through something terrible,
didn't look pleased.
"What if you guys forget me too? I might really lose it then."
"Let's do this. We'll leave a mark in advance. Even if we lose our memories, we can avoid
the worst-case scenario."
***
Neide picked up a pen and wrote something on his palm. His watching friends also carved
the same phrase.
- I have lost my memory. Those who know the keyword are trustworthy. The keyword is
Polaris.
Shirone and his group spread their palms with satisfaction. With this mark, even if they
lost their memories, they would be able to cooperate.
"Alright, done? Let's go out now."
Regaining his confidence, Shirone left Istas.
Students whose classes had ended were moving around the school grounds. Calming down and
observing, there were more than a few strange things.
Normally, students would move around in small groups, but now they were moving
individually. The strangest thing was that despite walking busily, their paths were completely chaotic.
"Guys, those people…."
"I know. I noticed it too. They have no destination. They're just walking around
aimlessly, aren't they?"
Neide spotted someone he knew in the crowd. It was Gabe, a classmate from Class Five.
"Hey, Gabe. Where are you going?"
Gabe approached. Then, in a monotone voice as if talking to a stranger, he asked back.
"Who are you?"
Neide pouted his lips. But since he had already heard what Shirone had gone through, he
didn't mind and asked the question he was curious about first.
"Never mind that. Where are you going right now?"
"Where am I going?"
Neide tapped his chest in frustration and said.
"You were just walking continuously. What's your destination?"
"Me?"
Gabe looked down at his own two legs. Then, as if suddenly realizing something, he began
looking around.
"Where… is this?"
"Hey, snap out of it. What's wrong with you? Did something happen in the past few days?
Have you seen anyone suspicious?"
"Do you… know me?"
"Of course I know you. How could I not?"
Gabe's face turned pale. Suddenly, he grabbed Neide's shoulders and shook him violently.
"Then tell me! Who am I? No, where is this place? I… what's my name?"
As Gabe, terrified, clutched his head, Neide slowly stepped back, preparing for an
unexpected situation. Fortunately, Gabe didn't turn aggressive. But he was losing his mind.
"What is this? I hear strange sounds! Why do I keep hearing these sounds?"
Gabe looked around and shouted, but his tongue gradually began to twist. Eventually,
incomprehensible, bizarre sounds burst out.
Shirone's pupils shook in shock. He realized what state Gabe was in. On the other hand,
Iruki, who hadn't caught on yet, listened to the sounds around them.
"What sounds is he hearing? Auditory hallucinations?"
Shirone said in a trembling voice.
"No, he's hearing his own voice."
"What? His own voice?"
"He doesn't remember language. He's habitually speaking but doesn't know the meaning. So
of course he's scared."
Gabe had completely forgotten his language system. Eventually, it seemed he even forgot
syllables, screaming like a primitive person.
Shirone and his group were creeped out. Judging by the speed of mental deterioration,
memories were disappearing quite rapidly.
As time passed, Gabe's voice faded, the light in his eyes disappeared, and eventually, he
lost his motor skills. At the same time… everyone on the street stopped moving.
A terrifying silence fell over the magic school. It was as if wax figures had been placed
there, with no sign of movement from anyone.
"What on earth… happened?"
"At least we're safe. We can't be optimistic, but there's still room to investigate
further."
Shirone examined the people one by one. It felt like the world had stopped, similar to
Armin's Stop Magic. But time was clearly passing. Moreover, seeing people occasionally blink showed they
weren't frozen.
"Their thoughts… have stopped."
If it was a type of magic, it was a more dangerous effect than Stop. It could threaten
the opponent. If someone wanted to, even a child could harm everyone here.
The three of them entered the crowd and conducted a few experiments. Tickling a junior
made them burst into laughter. But when they stopped, the junior immediately returned to a stiff expression.
Threatening to poke their eyes made them blink. Their nerves were still alive.
"Hoh, so only their thoughts have stopped. This is peculiar."
"If that's the case, then their physical strength has limits too. They won't last long
standing like this. What's the point of doing this?"
"First, we need to figure out who did this. Let's look around a bit more."
Shirone and his group headed north. They wanted to check if the senior graduates and
teachers with high magical abilities were in the same situation.
When they reached the uphill path, graduating seniors were pouring out through the steel
gate's arch.
Shirone and his group hid in the bushes.
The fact that only the graduating class was moving while everyone else was frozen was
reason enough to be wary.
Among the graduating class, there were unfamiliar faces.
A boy and girl who looked around Shirone's age and a man carrying twin swords who didn't
fit in at a magic school.
Lucas whistled and made a sarcastic remark.
"Wow, I've seen a lot of miserable sights in my life, but this is the first time I've
seen such a mess. They've all turned into zombies, haven't they?"
Abyss Nova is a magic that blocks the memories of those exposed to Nova within 24 hours.
Without memories, thought becomes impossible, so it's no exaggeration to call it an
inanimate-objectification magic. The order in which memories are blocked starts from the most recent and
most memorable.
Leading those whose memories were blocked was Arin's task. Specializing in mental magic,
she brought the graduating students to the school grounds where Shirone and his group had encountered Gabe.
'Shadows of Capture.'
And there, she cast her magic again. Her shadow stretched out like tentacles, piercing
through the shadows of the people.
A unique form that doesn't exist in the Four Directions. But for mental magic users, it's
considered a standard technique called the tentacle type.
The tentacle type uses the Spirit Zone like thin threads, making it much more flexible
than the Four Directions.
However, it takes much longer, so it would take at least 20 minutes to pass through all
the shadows of the advanced class students.
While Arin worked, Lucas passed the time by critiquing the female students. Being nobles,
they all had fair skin and pleasant scents. Lucas, with his serpent-like eyes, scanned their figures and
stopped when he found a girl to his liking.
"Oh ho. Look at this."
A girl whose pupils remained clear even in her memory-lost state. Her red-tinged bangs
flowed down, covering one eye in a retro style, but her looks were the best among the students.
"She's probably got a temper. I like feisty women."
The moment Lucas reached out to Amy, Canis grabbed his shoulder and turned him around.
"Lucas, stop. Refrain from undignified acts."
"What? Lucas, stop? Are you talking to me like that right now?"
"I won't stand by if you do unnecessary things."
"Pfft!"
Lucas let out a deflated laugh. Had the status of the Parrot Thieves fallen this low?
"Hey, what's so serious? I heard this is a revenge play anyway. What's wrong with having
a little fun?"
"This is revenge to restore our master's pride. I won't tolerate lowly acts."
"What will you do if I don't stop? Want to fight here?"
"I'll terminate the employment contract."
"Haha! Kid, you seem to be misunderstanding something. I was hired by Arcane, not by
you."
"It's the same either way. Arin is casting Shadows of Capture right now. If you interfere
with the operation, you can be fired anytime. Of course, if you have a problem with that…"
A whirlpool of abyss rose in Canis's pupils.
"I'll kill you right here."
Lucas clicked his tongue and stepped back. As the vice-captain of the Parrot Thieves, he
had killed countless mages and knew how dangerous it was to show hostility in the Spirit Zone.
"Fine. Let's drop it. Anyway, if I don't get paid, I'm the only one at a loss."
"Let me make this clear. If you interfere with our work one more time, I won't hold
back."
"You're so damn uptight for partners. I don't even see a hint of this so-called master.
Where the hell is he?"
"The master is preparing something important. He meant for us to handle this simple task
ourselves."
Arcane was currently hiding and recovering his exhausted mental strength from Abyss Nova.
But since telling Lucas wouldn't do any good, Canis hid the truth.
"Tch, whatever you say."
Lucas turned away, his eyes flashing intensely for a brief moment.
'Hmph, he's probably hiding and resting.'
Lucas was well-versed in the equivalent exchange of mages. Blocking the memories of
hundreds of people was a level of magic ordinary mages couldn't even attempt. Even if he was a grand mage,
it was clear he wouldn't be able to use his full power for a while.
'Your bluffing only works now, kid. You think I'd miss such a fun opportunity?'
When Arin finished her work, the graduating and advanced class students began moving all
at once. Only after they were out of sight did Shirone and his group emerge from the bushes. From what they
saw, it seemed the intruders planned to take the entire school somewhere.
"Who are those people?"
"No time to think. It seems nothing will happen for now, but who knows what they'll do
once they reach their destination. And the teachers were captured too."
Shirone said.
"The magic that girl used seems like dark magic. Does that mean they're responsible for
the current situation?"
"The possibility is low. To cast magic of this scale, the size of the Zone alone would
have to be enormous. Two of the three are too young, and the other one is carrying weapons, so he's likely
not a mage."
"So there's a third person?"
"That's what I think. For now, let's focus on tracking them. If someone's in danger, it
won't be too late to step in then."
"Taking over the magic school. They're insane. And dark magic at that…."
With an inexplicable sense of foreboding, Shirone and his group followed the intruders.
