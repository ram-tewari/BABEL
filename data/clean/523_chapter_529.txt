Chapter 529:
Everyone in the restaurant turned to look at Rian.
It wasn't surprising, considering that the hottest topic in the area was
the "Knight of Maha." But it wasn't just that—they seemed to instinctively sense something
from Rian's massive sword and blue hair.
"Could they both be fakes? Or maybe... one of them is real?"
They weren't far from the village of Rafne, so it wasn't entirely
impossible that the real deal was present among them.
"But that young man said it himself, didn't he? That he's not the Knight
of Maha."
The man who had claimed the title of the Knight of Maha grew flustered
when he noticed the crowd's lukewarm reaction. He soon realized it was all because of the young man with
the greatsword—and the unfortunate coincidence of his blue hair.
"Hey, you. Think it's funny that I'm the Knight of Maha? What's with
spitting out your food like that?"
"The Knight of Maha, huh?"
When Rian asked back, the man took the opportunity to raise his voice
dramatically.
"Yeah! I'm the rising holy star—Rian, the Knight of Maha!"
'So that's what this is.'
Rian was well aware of how rampant impersonations were in the world.
Though, he never imagined someone would impersonate a name as obscure as his own.
"You're Rian, huh? Then what's your family name?"
The identity of the Knight of Maha was shrouded in rumor, and his
origins were unknown—so the question piqued everyone's curiosity.
"You really want to know? Fine! I'll reveal it specially today! I'm Rian
of the Lion family! Rian Lion!"
Someone in the crowd chimed in knowingly.
"I knew it! Makes sense. Only someone from the Lion family could carry
the title of Knight of Maha."
The Lion family was one of the "Seven Blades" of the Kingdom
of Baiden, known for their bold and powerful swordsmanship—just like the Ogent family.
'The Lion family...'
Rian placed his utensils down and stood up.
There was no way a swordsman from such a prestigious family would go
around impersonating someone else's name. And even if he were from that family, using a name that wasn't
his was still unacceptable.
"You're not Rian. The real Rian is nineteen, has blue hair, wields a
straight-blade greatsword—not a saw blade. And his family name is Ogent. One of the oldest sword
families in the Kingdom of Tormia."
When Rian said something so specific, the man—Alf—flinched.
'Who the hell is this guy?'
His real name was Alf. A former bandit leader who'd been betrayed by his
men. He'd since tried to redeem himself as a wandering swordsman, dreaming of fame and fortune.
"Don't spout nonsense! You think you can fool me by name-dropping some
foreign kingdom? And how would you know any of that?!"
Rian scratched his head and stepped forward.
"Because I'm Rian."
"Hah! You're Rian? What? Rian?!"
Alf's eyes went wide.
"That's right. I don't know anything about being a Knight of Maha, but
I'm Rian. So if you want to make a name for yourself, do it with your own—not by stealing someone
else's."
Alf broke into a cold sweat. He hadn't expected to run into the real
deal here of all places.
'Wait, but I'm not certain yet. I lie about who I am—why couldn't he be
doing the same? He could've dyed his hair, and that sword...'
Alf was no stranger to combat, and he could sense the object's true
nature just by instinct.
Despite its plain shape and lack of shine, the greatsword Rian carried
was something different—something that couldn't even get scratched. It had the unmistakable feel of an
Idea weapon.
"Hey, so which one's the real Rian?"
"Maybe they're both fakes? That'd be hilarious! Hahaha!"
The whole situation—two people claiming to be Rian—had become a circus
act. Truth didn't even matter anymore. The crowd was laughing at the absurdity.
'Goddamn it…'
Maybe the other guy was a faker too. But something about him felt real.
And that scared Alf more than anything.
'Give up? Just like that?'
Memories from the past few days flashed through Alf's mind like a
slideshow.
The awe-struck stares, the unnamed swordsmen who sought him out to learn
from him, the lavish treatment at the red-light district…
He'd lived like royalty ever since he became the Knight of Maha.
'It took everything I had to get this far.'
What he'd learned living under that name was this: in the end, all that
mattered was reputation.
With the same skill, a famous swordsman got to teach in safety, while a
no-name wandered the battlefield only to be slain by some general's sword.
"Shut up! I'm the Knight of Maha! I'm Rian!"
Alf decided to risk everything on this one moment.
He'd beat this no-name punk and become the true Knight of Maha.
"Calm down. I'm not trying to fight you. There's no need to apologize,
either. Just walk away—and don't use the name Rian anymore. That's all I'm asking."
But Alf couldn't accept that.
If he backed down now, it would be like admitting the truth—that the
real and fake had already been decided.
"No! I'm the real one! If you want the name Rian, you'll have to kill me
for it! What's wrong? Are you scared? Avoiding a fight is the surest proof that you're a fake!"
Some in the crowd nodded in agreement.
He had a point. Words were cheap—fists and blades settled things
better.
Roughly 200 meters from the restaurant,
Miro stood at a vendor stall, munching on one of Baiden's local
specialties—spicy grilled chicken skewers.
Every time she crossed a border, her attire had evolved. Now her braided
hair was styled high like sheep's horns, revealing her neckline, and she wore an oversized robe with red
lettering printed down the sleeves.
"Whew, spicy. But this is seriously good."
"...Miss, where exactly are you from?"
To the stall owner, her outfit was strange, but she was too beautiful to
be seen as anything but fascinating.
"I've been all over. Oh, one more of these, please."
The vendor handed her a freshly grilled skewer.
"Take it slow. Too much might upset your stomach."
"It's fine. I've eaten snakes in the desert."
"Fight! There's a fight in the restaurant alley!"
Just then, a man rushed past her yelling,
"The Knight of Maha's here! You're gonna miss the show!"
The vendor glanced after the growing crowd.
"Unbelievable. The Knight of Maha, in this town. If I weren't stuck at
this stall, I'd go watch too."
"You know of the Knight of Maha, sir?"
"Of course! He's the swordsman who not only wiped out a whole bandit
gang but also took down Lambda. I heard the rumors have already reached the capital. His name was...
Rian, I think?"
"Hmm."
Miro turned toward the alley, stuffed the rest of the skewer in her
mouth, and chewed thoughtfully.
"Swing it there! Drag it with the saw blade! Ugh, no—go for a grapple!
Close the distance!"
By the time Miro arrived, a full ring of spectators had formed like an
arena around the restaurant.
They knew it would scatter once the guards arrived, so everyone was
enjoying the mayhem while they could.
Squeezing through the crowd, Miro observed the battle—and found it
odd.
The hairy brute was swinging his savage weapon wildly, while the
blue-haired youth didn't even try to strike back.
"Hey! What're you doing?! Fight already! Are you scared? If you don't
fight, we're crowning the sawblade guy as the real Rian!"
Spectators kept yelling, trying to provoke a fight, but Rian ignored
them all.
Ever since he destroyed the Red Spear Bandits, his inner demons had
vanished. Whether to swing his sword or not—that was entirely his choice now.
'Why is he so desperate to fight? This isn't something worth drawing
swords over.'
Though Rian had once cut down anyone who raised a weapon at him without
hesitation, he hadn't become soft.
'This doesn't feel like a fight.'
That was the key.
He didn't feel any threat from his opponent's swings.
'Things changed after I cut down those hundred men.'
Without fear came clarity. And with clarity came understanding.
'I've grown stronger.'
Even now, in the heat of combat, Rian felt a flicker of joy in
recognizing his growth.
"You bastard!!"
Seeing that as mockery, Alf charged forward, swinging his massive
sawblade overhead.
Rian had been lost in thought, and by the time he refocused, the blade
was just 30 centimeters from his head.
Once, that would've made his heart plummet—but not now. Now, he saw it
clearly.
'Still okay. I've got about a hand's width of room.'
With a twist of his torso, denying the swing's inertia, the sawblade
slammed into the ground with a loud clang.
"What the hell? That looked weird just now."
The crowd murmured in surprise.
The moment Rian dodged, the scenery around him seemed to warp
slightly.
Miro's eyes sparkled.
'He's awakened. A Yaksha.'
Her conclusion was clear.
"You... you bastard...!"
Alf bared his teeth like a cornered wolf, but his legs were slowly
retreating.
The Action that distorted the laws of the body betrayed the future his
brain had predicted.
That dissonance caused a visual illusion—but for someone unaware of such
phenomena, it would feel like magic.
"I found you, Rian."
As Miro stepped into the dueling grounds, Alf scowled in
irritation.
"And who the hell are you? If you're here to swoon over the Knight of
Maha, come back later. The fight's not over yet."
"No, it is over. I'll tell you which one of you is the fake."
"What did you say? You—?!"
He barked, but couldn't fully protest. Deep down, he felt a flicker of
hope—there was still a 50/50 chance she might pick Rian instead of him.
But Miro didn't hesitate for even a second. She simply pointed at Alf's
face.
"The fake is you."
Alf's guilt flared into rage.
"What?! Don't spout crap! You got proof I'm a fake?!"
"Of course I do. From what I heard, the Knight of Maha wiped out an
entire bandit gang to save a mother and daughter in danger. A guy like that wouldn't look like some
thuggish brute. Therefore, you can't be him. No—you shouldn't be him."
The last bit of reason vanished from Alf's eyes.
"You filthy bitch!"
As he brought his sawblade sword crashing down vertically, Rian grabbed
Miro and swung his own sword.
"Oh my."
CLANG! Their blades met for the first time, and Alf recoiled in shock at
the sheer force.
"You've gotta be kidding me...!"
His massive sword, brought down with both hands, had been swatted away
by Rian's single-handed swing.
"Are you alright?"
Rian turned to Miro.
"Yes, thank you. The Knight of Maha really is kind."
"Please step outside. A third party interfering in a duel is a disgrace
to a swordsman's honor."
Miro's elegant smile faded.
"So you're Rian, Shirone's friend. You'll need to come with me
now."
Rian went pale, as if struck by lightning.
"You know Shirone? What happened?"
"I need your help. Are you coming or not?"
Without hesitation, Rian slung his sword onto his back—ready to
go.
"I'll come. Where is he?"
'Phew. Way easier than Marsha.'
After spending an entire day being interrogated under the guise of
conversation, even thinking about Marsha still gave Miro a headache.
"You bitch! You think you can just ignore me?!"
Alf lunged for Miro's back.
He was a former bandit leader—the kind who didn't care if it was a man
or woman in his way when enraged.
"I'll grind your face off with this saw!"
Sensing the bloodlust, Miro's expression turned ice-cold.
She gently pushed Rian's chest aside and turned, her incarnation flaring
behind her in the form of Avalokiteśvara.
As if the world itself slowed down, Alf experienced something
strange—like time itself fractured before him.
In the mind of a Scale Mage, space and time splinter to extremes. As her
arms moved, her palms unfolded like a fan, coming down toward him.
The sawblade clashed precisely with Miro's pressed palms. A moment
later, countless hands of Avalokiteśvara followed, compounding the force again and again.
Drrrrrrrrr!
Just from the pressure from both sides, the thick blade rippled like
rubber. Then, with a cracking burst, the metal handle shattered like glass, sending shockwaves through
the air.
"ARGHHHH!"
Alf collapsed, howling—only to stare dumbly at the smoke rising between
Miro's palms.
When she pulled her hands apart, a red-hot sheet of metal—shaped like a
palm—clanged to the ground with a metallic ring.
"Th-This is..."
It had been completely flattened like a thin film. The cooled metal was
charred black and crumbling.
Rian watched her approach, shaking off his hands casually, looking
utterly baffled.
"Just who... are you?"
Miro grinned like nothing had happened and gave him a playful push on
the back.
"Come on. I'll explain on the way."
And with that, the final piece of the puzzle for their journey into hell
was in place.
