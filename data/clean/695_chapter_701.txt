Chapter 701:
The decision to venture into a place containing 19,000 worlds left both
Rian and Kido visibly pale.
"Surely, he doesn't intend to search through all of
them?"
Even exploring just one world was an unpredictable endeavor, with no way
to estimate how much time it would take.
"Hmm, a wise choice," said Uorin.
"The conquest difficulty of Andre is the worst among the three
labyrinths. But for you…"
This was because Andre's labyrinth was designed by Goffin, and the Hexa
system within it was specifically tailored to Shirone's abilities.
"Exactly. I plan to use the Ultima System. If Goffin could do it, so can
I. If the probability of encountering La Enemy is 1 in 3 for any labyrinth, I'll bet on Andre."
Uorin stood, signaling readiness.
"Very well, let's leave immediately. Gando, prepare the Ratusa."
"Yes, ma'am."
As Gando departed, Shirone asked, "What's a Ratusa?"
Uorin chuckled. "You'll see soon enough. You can't cross the desert by
carriage, can you?"
"A specialized desert vehicle, perhaps?"
Following this assumption, Shirone trailed behind Uorin into the
corridor, only to encounter a group blocking the way.
Shirone instantly recognized who they were.
"The Wind Guards."
This was the world's most elite swordsmanship group, infamous for
turning Kazra's Magic Division Leader, Bosun, into minced meat in mere moments.
The fact that Gando only learned of their presence after the meeting
spoke volumes about how secretive Uorin had been with Shirone.
However, this secrecy seemed to have offended the Wind Guards members,
and their unspoken fury was palpable, now directed entirely at Shirone's group.
Rian examined the 30 swordsmen wearing owl masks.
"So, these are the Wind Guards."
The overwhelming presence of their unified killing intent, Gaksi,
matched their reputation as the ones who had severed Kuan's Achilles' heel.
But the true terror lay in their collective bloodlust—a level of
synchronization that bordered on the supernatural.
"Are they looking for a fight?"
Ordinary warriors would have crumbled under the pressure of their Gaksi,
but Rian's godlike transcendence of will rendered such intimidation futile.
"Impressive. They're no ordinary intruders."
While the Wind Guards Seemed unified in thought, the lead figure in the
triangular formation stepped forward and removed her owl mask.
Rian, noting her sharp, otherworldly gaze, instantly identified her as
the leader.
"That woman is their commander."
She bore the unmistakable eyes of a ghost.
"How could you, Your Majesty, admit outsiders without informing
us?"
It was audacious for anyone to express displeasure toward the Queen of
Kashan, yet this woman seemed to be the only one who dared.
Uorin's response, however, was icy.
"What are you implying? Your job is to protect me. Yet, here I am
surrounded by these strangers. What were you doing?"
"…"
None of the Wind Guards moved, yet their energy rippled like an
impending storm, distorting the air around them.
Even Kido flinched at the optical illusion of oil floating on
water.
"What a maniac, this woman."
The Wind Guards' leader bowed slightly and spoke.
"We have no excuses. However, if allowed, we can rectify our mistake
now."
Feeling the rising tension, Rian instinctively stepped in front of
Shirone. But Uorin shook her head firmly.
"No. You've missed your chance. You're all fired."
The leader of the Wind Guards hesitated, clearly struggling to read
Uorin's intentions.
"I can't figure her out. What is she planning?"
At that moment, Uorin unexpectedly wrapped her arms around Kido's neck
from behind.
"I don't need incompetent guards like you. I already have someone
better, right here."
"What?!"
Shirone and Rian looked baffled, but Kido was the most stunned of
all.
"What the hell are you talking about? Why would I be your guard?"
"I've always wanted a fascinating pet. With a leash around your neck,
you'd be far more convenient than the Wind Guards. Why don't you give up your travels and let me keep
you instead?"
"A mere human wants to tame me?"
"So what? As the Goblin King, you should feel honored I'm even offering.
You like crawling, don't you? From now on, walking on two legs is banned."
Kido's eyes turned cold and dangerous.
"… Shirone, can I kill this woman?"
At the same time, the Wind Guards' leader drew her sword, and the
remaining 29 members followed suit in perfect synchronization.
"Mind your words. If you dare lay a hand on Her Majesty, you'll
experience a pain no living being can imagine."
As the situation teetered on the edge of chaos, Uorin tightened her grip
on Kido's neck and whispered.
"Kido, you'll fight for me, won't you? Don't trust their words. I've
already fired them—they'll try to kill me."
"This woman is completely insane."
Just as Kido's fury reached its peak, Uorin grinned mischievously and
kicked him in the rear.
"Go! Take them down, my pet goblin!"
"Kido!"
Shirone's cry echoed as the Wind Guards swordsmen dissolved their
formation like smoke and charged.
Uorin laughed hysterically, but to Kido, only the sound of blades
cutting through the wind reached his ears.
'Damn it!'
Despite their human forms, the Wind Guards' movements were utterly
inhuman.
Dodging the whirlwind of blades, Kido rolled across the ground and
counterattacked with his spear.
'This is….'
The leader of the Wind Guards, observing his brief movements,
immediately understood.
"He has mastered the law of the earth."
In the narrow space, the Wind Guards wind-based techniques clashed
violently with Kido's earth-based movements, creating friction that seemed to ignite the very
air.
"Grrr!"
As Kido tumbled out of the melee, the Wind Guard members spread out like
a murder of crows and regrouped behind Uorin.
"Your Majesty, please command us to take our own lives. We cannot bear
the shame of being displaced by a mere goblin."
Uorin's laughter vanished, replaced by a frosty demeanor.
"And what about your incompetence that couldn't even handle that mere
goblin?"
The leader bit her lip, finding no excuse, while Kido's thoughts were
far grimmer.
'If that fight had lasted one more second….'
He knew he would have been sliced to pieces.
The Wind Guards' leader, desperate, pleaded with Uorin.
"Please, give us another order, and we shall—"
"Silence. Leave my sight. For the next three days, maintain yourselves
in the most agonizing state possible. Worthless fools."
The leader immediately bowed her head.
The command to maintain the most painful state for three days meant
something different to each person, but, paradoxically, this kind of clear instruction made things
easier.
"Understood, Your Majesty."
As the members of the Wind Guards lifted themselves gracefully and
disappeared down the corridor as though the hallway itself were swallowing them, Shirone asked,
"Uorin, what exactly was that about...?"
Until that moment, Uorin had maintained a cold, stern expression, but
suddenly her cheeks puffed up, and she burst into laughter.
"Ahahahaha!"
Pointing at Kido, who still looked dazed, she stomped her foot in
amusement.
"You were scared, weren't you? I mean, really terrified! Look at
that face!"
Kido, holding his spear and trembling with rage, shouted, "What the
hell was that?! Are you out of your mind, you lunatic?! I nearly died!"
Even Shirone seemed disappointed this time.
"Uorin, that was too much. Using the Wind Guards for a prank?
That's not something to joke about."
"Hmm, the Goblin King, huh?"
As Uorin approached him, Kido flinched and took a step back.
"But still, not bad. To endure a clash against a high-tier group
like the Wind Guards, even for a single exchange…"
"A single exchange?!"
To Kido, it had felt like a thousand exchanges, at the very
least.
"I didn't know much about goblins, to be honest, so I was a bit
concerned. But seeing it firsthand puts my mind at ease. Take care of Shirone. Not a single hair on his
head should be harmed, got it?"
"Shut up! Why should I protect Shirone? I'm traveling for my own
reasons!"
Shirone nodded in agreement.
"He's right. Kido isn't some kind of pet. To test him like that
just to confirm something is too much."
You don't understand, Shirone.
Uorin—no, Teraje, or perhaps Mitochondrial Eve—valued Shirone more than
anyone could fathom.
"Shirone," Uorin blurted out without thinking, "do you
think there'll ever come a day when we share the same bed?"
"What the hell are you talking about now?" Shirone's
expression mirrored his disbelief.
"That seems… unlikely," Shirone replied. "For one, I
already have someone I love. And you're the Empress of Kashan, the strongest in the world…"
"True, I am a bit regal," Uorin said with a sly smile.
"But all I'm saying is, if it ever happens, then it just happens."
Whenever Uorin spoke about life, it always seemed cryptic.
"If I kiss you right now, that's a fact—it happened. Whether it was
impossible or unlikely becomes irrelevant after it occurs."
Shirone thought he understood.
"I'm biologically sixteen and still growing. Who knows? I might
turn into someone you'd want to fall in love with."
Even now, Shirone found her stunning.
"But, of course, that's unlikely. The thought of us sharing a bed
feels absurd. Imagining us conspiring to kill each other feels more natural."
If it were between the Empress of Kashan and the Star of the Ivory
Tower, it wouldn't be impossible.
"But life is unpredictable. Someday, I might become your bride. Or
I could even end up being your daughter."
A brief silence followed.
"I get what you mean," Shirone said. "I'll keep that in
mind."
Why she brought it up at that moment in that place was something only
Uorin knew.
Don't worry, Amy, he thought to himself.
Satisfied with having planted her thoughts in Shirone's mind, Uorin put
on a bright expression and led the way down the corridor.
"All right, let's get going."
The group climbed Aganos Tower, a spire overlooking the capital. At the
tower's summit, handlers were already waiting.
"Whoa, what's that?"
Shirone pointed to a massive, featherless bird crouching at the edge of
the tower.
"It's a Kaidera," he said, recognizing it.
Kaidera was a third-tier high-speed flying creature that could cover
7,000 kilometers in a day, once summoned by Zulu in the past.
"Wait… is this a summoned creature?"
"Nope, I raised it myself. Its name is Ratusa. I take it out for
rides when I feel like catching some fresh air."
"…"
The fact that she had captured and tamed a creature usually summoned by
archmages spoke volumes about Uorin's power.
"The journey to Andre is long, but with Ratusa, we'll be there by
tomorrow afternoon."
"Tomorrow afternoon? That fast?"
"Ratusa has been trained since birth to respond to human
intentions. It won't be difficult."
After giving instructions for crossing the desert, Uorin approached the
creature. Ratusa nuzzled its beak against her cheek affectionately.
"Ugh, I'm not a fan of the skies," Kido muttered, reluctantly
following behind.
Uorin pulled a pouch of gold coins from her robes and handed it to
him.
"Here, take this. You'll need it."
Before Shirone could object, Kido snatched it.
"Well, well, seems like Her Majesty has some sense. I'll let that
pet comment slide."
"Really? It wasn't a joke," Uorin teased, pretending to tug on
an invisible leash.
"I'll make sure to get the finest collar for you."
"You…!"
Just as Kido grumbled and moved closer, Uorin smirked
mischievously.
"They call you Kido the Devourer, don't they?"
"… What of it?"
"Maybe someday… you'll get a taste of my blood too."
Something about her tone unsettled Kido, and he blinked in confusion
before brushing it off.
"Well? What are you waiting for, Kido? Get on," Shirone called
out.
"Yeah, yeah, I'm coming."
Once everyone had mounted, Shirone took hold of Ratusa's reins and
looked back at Uorin.
"Thank you. You've saved us so much time. I'll repay this favor
someday."
From the debt of Armand to today's events, Shirone felt he owed her far
too much.
"Take care, Shirone. The world is vast, and Kashan's desert even
vaster."
Shirone nodded, repeating Uorin's words to himself, and tugged the
reins.
"Let's go, Ratusa!"
With a sharp cry, the Kaidra raised its massive body and leaped off the
tower.
Kiaaaahhhh!
Their shadow against the sun grew smaller and smaller as they soared
into the distance.
