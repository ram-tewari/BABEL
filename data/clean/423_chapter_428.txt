Chapter 428:
Plu couldn't understand what Shirone was saying.
"You've been here before?"
Babel was known as the Tomb of Angels, a place that existed even before
the citizens lived in Heaven. It was impossible for nineteen-year-old Shirone to have been here.
"I'm not sure, but…"
"Hmm, maybe it's déjà vu?"
Normally, it wouldn't be something to dwell on, but considering the
steel golems had collapsed just before attacking Shirone, it wasn't something to ignore.
"Let's move to another place. This area is too
dangerous."
"Wait. Are we just going to leave that behind?"
A team member pointed to the electricity above the cone.
Even if it wasn't an electric spirit, it was too valuable to
abandon.
Plu felt the same but had a bad feeling about it.
The deceased team member wasn't weak, yet he couldn't even react before
being torn apart by the shrapnel.
"Shirone, what do you think?"
Shirone stared intently at the glowing electricity.
It wasn't clear, but a faint sense of familiarity, like a distant
memory, washed over him.
'Have I seen that before?'
As Shirone searched his memories, he muttered,
"Ultima."
"Huh? Shirone, what did you say?"
"What?"
Shirone turned with a surprised expression.
"You just muttered something. Ultima?"
"Did I?"
Plu nodded.
She was meticulous, so it must be true, but Shirone genuinely couldn't
remember.
"We should check it out."
"You know it could be dangerous, right?"
"That's why we need to confirm it. The control device for Babel's
traps might be in the control room."
It made sense.
"Alright. But I'll do the investigation."
"Senior, that's…"
"Listen to me. This is the kind of task a second-in-command
handles."
Plu approached the electricity, ready to take the risk to protect
Shirone at all costs.
She gently touched the electricity with Phoenix, and it crackled angrily
but didn't react further.
"Hmm, it doesn't seem dangerous."
At that moment, a team member leaped forward and grabbed the electricity
with his hand.
"It's mine!"
It was the man with the long philtrum, assigned by Raesis to monitor
Shirone.
Despite his cold demeanor, he was just a human driven by greed.
As the electricity flowed into his arm, he was convinced it was a spirit
and shouted, "Spirit, contract with me!"
Blue light spread through the control room as the spirit's power seeped
into him.
As the immense concept filled his mind, his eyes rolled back
involuntarily.
"This… this is… incredible…"
As he muttered in ecstasy, his face suddenly exploded with a pop.
Shirone and Plu stared in shock at the man's headless body.
"Foolish. How could he just grab it without checking?"
It wasn't a lack of patience. The Nor's desire for spirits was that
strong.
Shirone and Plu, who could cast magic anytime through the Spirit Zone,
couldn't understand that mindset.
When Plu glared at the last remaining team member, he shook his head and
raised his hands, stepping back.
Nothing was more valuable than life, so it was a wise choice.
"Shirone, do you still want to continue?"
Plu asked, suggesting they stop, but Shirone felt more certain after the
man's death.
Babel was sealed with Hena and existed before the citizens were
born.
"Those who accessed this place were likely angels or Nephilim. The
Immortal Function is fundamental here. The steel golems appeared because someone other than a Nephilim
entered."
Plu agreed that Shirone was probably right, given the
circumstances.
But with two deaths already, she had to caution him.
"Be careful. There could still be unforeseen dangers."
Nodding, Shirone reached out to the electricity.
It didn't sting, and the electricity climbed up his hand like a living
creature.
"Gasp!"
He heard a rushing sound in his head, like a drill boring into his
skull.
Ultima System - Universal Integrated Information System.
In terms of the law, it was number 1.
Unique concepts, unmatched by anything else, tore through his mind at
the speed of light.
"Ugh!"
Shirone realized why the man's head had exploded. His own head felt like
it might do the same.
As he opened the Immortal Function, an immense sense of liberation
washed over him, and the concepts of Ultima flowed into the infinite passage.
His mind emptied.
Only a thin, blue thread of light connected him to the outside of the
passage.
The last remaining team member approached, swallowing nervously.
"What… what was that spirit?"
Shirone turned with a surprised expression.
"Say that again."
"I was curious about the spirit's attribute."
Hearing the man's voice without the drone's translation mode confirmed
it.
He couldn't understand the pronunciation, but the meaning seeped into
his mind.
'Who created this system?'
It wasn't about the citizens' language. Any symbol in existence could be
integrated and decoded into a single signal.
When Shirone placed his hand on the cone, the electricity pierced his
hand, activating the machinery.
The flow of information was as clear as blood vessels.
"So that's the control device."
Nodding at Plu's words, Shirone connected to the main circuit and
explored Babel.
The structure's layout, vast enough to be called a fortress, freely
appeared in his mind.
Thud! Thud!
Opening the control room wall revealed a tube-shaped glass
chamber.
A red cylindrical light shot up, spinning rapidly.
"That's the main elevator."
The elevator, similar to the one in Aravot, transported the three of
them 100 meters underground in an instant.
"Wow, it's huge."
The control room was so vast it was hard to see the end.
A massive screen, likely ten times larger than life, floated in front,
with a conical pillar similar to the one in the control room beside it.
Shirone placed his hand on it without hesitation.
Why did he feel like he'd been here before? Perhaps the answer lay
here.
Accessing the control room records through the Ultima System, the screen
lit up.
Shirone calmly searched, but to Plu, the pages seemed to flip rapidly,
leaving afterimages.
At some point, the screen stopped, displaying strange characters,
neither Hena nor the citizens' language.
Only Shirone's pupils moved, following the text.
Through the Ultima's single signal, the meaning was conveyed in a way he
could understand.
Record: Babel Project

Heaven fell into chaos.
Omega 999 - The last Gaia (record deleted) exited the photon

'What is this…'
Shirone stared blankly.
The file recorded events between Gaia, the first humanity, and Anke Ra
before the citizens were born.
Gaia, who achieved an integrated mental system, created an ancient
weapon called Babel to kill Anke Ra.
But after losing the final war, Gaia split into three races: Kergos,
Nor, and Mecha.
The timeline was divided into percentages, so it was unclear how long a
year was, but one thing was certain.
The last remaining Gaia, the one marked as (record deleted), left this
record.
'There are two types of record deletions. One is Gaia's name, and the
other is the significant crime Ikael committed.'
The screen reset.
A single sentence, likely left by the last Gaia, flashed.
Beyond infinity.
As Shirone stared intently at it, Plu couldn't hold back and
approached.
"What are you looking at? You can't even understand
it."
Shirone shook his head. He couldn't reveal the Ultima System with the
team member listening.
"I'll explain later. There's something we need to check. Follow
me."
Activating the control device opened a path through the iron
wall.
Babel lay at the end of that path, the ultimate weapon created by the
first humanity to kill a god.
'What will it look like?'
As they passed through the corridor, they entered a chamber resembling a
womb.
A spotlight-like beam of light illuminated something kneeling
below.
It was a sleek, black metallic woman, connected to numerous wires
descending from the ceiling.
The team member's face turned pale.
"An angel…"
"No. It's Babel."
Plu asked, "Babel?"
"Babel isn't the name of this place. It's an ancient weapon that
mimics an angel's abilities."
"What are you talking about? You've been saying strange things for
a while now. Explain properly…"
Whirrrrr!
The facility's systems activated, and power flowed through the wires
into Babel, as relayed by the Ultima System.
In this area where lightning struck continuously, there was no issue
with power supply, but the output was immense.
Babel's body began to vibrate intensely as it absorbed the entire
structure's power.
It was like a fetus finally being born, having absorbed all its mother's
nutrients through the womb.
Babel's slender neck straightened, and her kneeling legs rose as if in
zero gravity.
Standing over 2 meters tall with a slender figure, her blue wings
expanded, revealing her beautiful face.
She looked eerily similar to someone Shirone knew.
"Ikael?"
Babel's eyes lit up with a red glow, and a bright white halo spun above
her head, emitting a high-pitched noise close to a roar.
"Sh-Shirone, let's get out of here."
Plu's shoulders trembled.
The power she felt was beyond imagination, truly mimicking an
angel.
'Was the chairman planning to fight something like this?'
Babel, hovering about a meter above the ground, took in the sight of the
three people below.
Babel program activated. Applying Kariel
algorithm.
Primary objective: Eliminate intruders.
Crackle!
Babel's form shifted into an electric tangle and vanished. At the same
time, a blade-like hand stopped right in front of Shirone's face.
Only after hearing the clear sound of the fingertips did Shirone realize
his situation.
His arm was frozen in place, and Babel's face slowly approached,
examining him.
Warning! Warning!
Higher algorithm deems attack impossible.
Cause analysis: Gaia Code Number 2 detected. Checking
Ultima System application. Confirmed.
Result: Absolute non-aggression target.
Babel, locking eyes with Shirone, slowly withdrew her hand and stepped
back with metallic clanks.
Reapplying Kariel algorithm.
Secondary objective: Eliminate all citizens.
Whirrrrr!
As the halo spun, Babel's body lightly floated.
Just as the terrified trio looked up, electricity enveloped Babel, and
she shot through the ceiling with a deafening roar.
"Ugh!"
The three, regaining their balance in the shaking room, could only stare
blankly at the perfectly round hole in the ceiling.
The black angel had descended.
