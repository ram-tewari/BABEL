Chapter 934:
In The Flower Field (2)
It's a sad story.
When Ikael asked Satiel to name the child, Satiel agreed and returned to
Heaven.
Goffin asked, "Are you sure? I know Satiel's character well, but
Heaven is no easy opponent."
Ikael smiled. "Have you forgotten? Who was the angel that supported
humans the most when you were struggling?"
Goffin had no response.
"I know, but..."
"Don't worry. With you and me here, who would dare harm this
child?"
Ikael, the leader of the angels, and Goffin, with powers akin to a god,
were Heaven's strongest couple.
"Are you scared?" Ikael joked, bringing a smile back to
Goffin's face.
"Haha! The only thing I'm afraid of is you. Now then... shall we
take down some strong opponents?"
As Goffin leaned forward, Ikael twisted her body to protect the
baby.
"Wait! Our baby!"
Goffin quickly stopped and checked on the baby, who was smiling brightly
at his father.
'Don't worry.'
Goffin hugged Ikael's shoulder and gently stroked the baby's cheek with
his other hand.
"Daddy will protect you."
Meanwhile, back in her quarters in Heaven, Satiel felt as if she had
just woken from a dream.
But it was reality, and she couldn't forget the tender image of Ikael
and Goffin.
"A name."
A name for the child?
At the time, she had nodded without thinking, but now everything became
clear.
'Who am I fighting for...'
The countless days she had fought for humans flashed through her
mind.
She had done her best.
Yet Goffin chose Ikael, who had always been an obstacle to him.
"I loved him first..."
Tears of light streamed down her cheeks as her halo dimmed.
"I loved him more. I fought for him more. But why? Why
Ikael?"
She hadn't thrown her heart out there.
'I should have said something. If I had told him first that I loved
him...'
Wouldn't there be a child who resembled Satiel?
Her halo dripped like molten lava onto the floor, and when she regained
her senses, she found herself in a different place.
'Where is this?'
The sanctuary of Aravot.
Turning around, she saw the door wide open and Anke Ra pulsating before
her.
"What brings you here, Satiel?"
Though she didn't remember requesting an audience, Anke Ra's calm
demeanor suggested she had.
"Ah, well..."
Anke Ra's eyelid lifted slightly, and Satiel's jaw trembled as words
caught in her throat.
Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!
In Matei, the city of giants in the Fifth Heaven, giants who had reached
higher stages of the Elixir of Life marched.
Though not tall in stature due to their condensed form, their massive
weight shook the city with each step.
"What is this..."
For the first time in Heaven's history, all archangels and Maras
gathered for this ritual.
"Is it really possible?"
Uriel asked Kariel, who was in charge, but even his expression lacked
certainty.
"We can't be sure."
Most of the giants in Matei gathered around a statue that reached into
the sky.
"It's the first attempt at reaching the 10th stage of the Elixir of
Life. It's not about probability."
"The highest so far has been the 7th stage?"
Giant Commander Girshin was the only one at the 7th stage and ruled over
Matei's giants.
"It's not impossible. The giants participating in this ritual are
all ancient beings integrated with Gaia."
The first giant born would travel to Jotunheim to receive new laws—this
was stage one.
From this point on, giving giants an identity made advancing stages
extremely difficult. Considering that using Shinmin only reached stage three, Gaia's integration power
was immense.
"But suddenly aiming for stage ten? It's like integrating 10
billion Gaia people."
"Yes. If successful, it will be the first and last giant to reach
stage ten of the Elixir of Life. It will surpass Girshin and rule all giants."
"...The king of giants."
Following Anke Ra's instructions, Kariel proceeded with the Elixir of
Life ritual step by step.
Success after success after success.
The lamps on the interconnected bronze statues continued to light up
like a pyramid scheme.
"Were Gaia people always this powerful?"
Kariel's face showed relief as he worried about losing only giants
unnecessarily.
When the lamp on the 8th stage bronze statue lit up, Giant Commander
Girshin's expression twisted.
Uriel watched him closely.
'His pride must be hurt.'
Having been at the top among giants until now, suddenly falling to
second place was natural for him to feel this way.
'But I'm also curious.'
Uriel turned his gaze to the colossal bronze statue that seemed endless
in size.
'Why do we need something like this?'
He knew Girshin's strength well enough.
But stage ten?
Uriel wondered if anything in this universe needed to be that
powerful.
"Stage nine..."
Finally surpassing a critical point, all intermediate beings melted into
liquid and were absorbed by the central bronze statue.
It took a long time to fill due to its enormous size, but those watching
lost track of time.
"It's over."
With someone's murmur ending it all, the vibrations ceased, and silence
fell over everything around them—
The sound of opening bronze echoed through an empty plaza where
countless giants had vanished.
Despite its low pitch, it felt like eardrums would burst, and everyone's
gaze turned skyward—
Only seeing an empty mold inside—
"Why?"
Boom!
As someone tried speaking—the ground shook—
Looking down again, they saw someone barely taller than a Gaia person
standing with their head bowed—
"A condensed form?"
Proving it, a wall of thick muscles impossible through normal training
was visible.
'Condensed form is difficult, succeeding right after the completion of
the Elixir of Life...'
While Kariel analyzed, the newly born giant approached Girshin—
"Newly born giant."
Following Anke Ra's instructions, Uriel moved to intervene, but Kariel
stopped him with a meaningful look—
'Even stage seven life fusion technique was considered dangerous for
Heaven, how different will stage ten be?'
It was pure curiosity.
"I am Giant Commander Girshin."
Before the newly born giant, Girshin drew his sword and continued
speaking.
"You may have been chosen as the king of giants, but the law of
giants strictly follows the principle of might makes right. Let us settle this here..."
The moment the newly born giant raised his head, Girshin, whose breath
had stopped, dropped his sword.
As the clear sound of metal echoed, the newly born giant slowly looked
around.
Kariel's face turned pale.
'A murderous aura?'
No, it wasn't a murderous aura.
It was simply that the giant's presence was so overwhelming that making
eye contact felt intimidating.
No angel showed it outwardly, but their celestial bodies trembled
uncontrollably.
"Ymir."
Girshin's voice wavered as he spoke the name, and his legs
trembled.
"I am Ymir."
While every creature has a natural predator, Ymir seemed not to be a
creature made to contend with anything.
'The king of giants. No, the king of all creatures.' Girshin immediately
prostrated himself.
"Forgive my impudence, Your Majesty. I will conquer the world
according to your command."
Instead of responding, Ymir turned to look at the pinnacle of
Aravot.
'I want to fight.'
There was no question of his birth that a creature would naturally
have.
"This world is..."
As Ymir pressed down with one foot, the stone floor collapsed under the
pressure.
"It's too weak."
As he finished speaking, a tremendous explosion erupted from where Ymir
had been.
"Ugh!"
The assembled beings were angels, maras, and mid-tier giants, so there
was no harm done, but the plaza was utterly devastated.
"Such reckless behavior!"
Kariel doubted Ymir's self-control, but Uriel was interested in the
source of the explosion.
"There was no malice toward us."
"No malice? In the presence of archangels gathered together, and he
does this...!"
"Look there."
Uriel pointed to where the entire radius had sunk into a semicircular
crater.
"Ymir didn't do anything. He just bent and straightened his
knee." Kariel stared blankly at the clearly imprinted footprint at the center of the crater.
Uriel said,
"That's the problem. Not having even a hint of malice in front of
the archangels means..."
It meant he was no different from Girshin.
Hearing Uriel's analysis, all the angels kept silent, and then an
explosion rang out from the direction of Aravot.
Bang!
With a single leap, Ymir reached Aravot and embedded his fingers into
the spire's wall.
"Hmm."
He had intended to make a brace with his head, but his strength was so
great that it crumbled to dust.
'How displeasing.'
In a universe filled with solid materials, Ymir felt as if he lived in a
world without solids.
'I feel nothing.'
Thinking it would take time to adjust his strength, Ymir leapt into the
air and stomped down.
Boom!
The pressure alone lifted his body, and soon after, the ground flattened
with a loud crash.
He crushed the walls like tofu and proceeded until the sanctuary's door
blocked his path.
For some reason, he thought he shouldn't open it carelessly.
"Anke Ra."
It was an animalistic instinct.
"I have come when called."
As the door opened, Anke Ra, composed of massive muscles, greeted
Ymir.
Seeing the oppressive entity with his own eyes, Ymir smirked and
said,
"Do you think you're stronger than me?"
"It depends on what you consider strength."
"No."
Ymir moved a bit closer.
"There is no standard. I am stronger than you. Why should I submit
to you?"
"Because I gave you the blessing of birth."
"No, it's more like a curse."
Ymir approached close enough to touch and spread his palm under Anke
Ra's eye.
"I have no achievements. Being the strongest, what's the point of
becoming stronger?"
As he exerted force, Anke Ra's flesh began to tear slightly.
"Look, just a little strength and this happens."
"Do you want to feel it?"
Ymir looked up.
"The power, the body, the sensation of impact, do you want to feel
it?"
"Can you do that?"
"It's not a question of can."
Anke Ra's tentacles moved quickly.
"McClain Goffin."
As Ymir's fingers tore away Anke Ra's flesh, they spread again, and
Ymir's body reacted to the name.
"Goffin."
It seemed like the voices of ten billion merged personalities were
audible.
"Goffin is the strongest."
Connected to Satiel's celestial body, Anke Ra emitted electricity from
his eyes and said,
"Go."
The door opened by itself.
"I will guide you to Goffin."
Slowly turning around, Ymir looked at the devastated landscape before
jumping down.
Moments later, a powerful shockwave shook the spire.
