Chapter 37:
The Uncrossable Bridge (4)
Meanwhile, unaware of the situation at the test site, Sard approached the control room
entrance, fuming.
"Damn it! Just wait till I catch whoever did this!"
Only a madman would deactivate the mechanism, so it had to be the work of troublemakers
in the school.
"Who the hell—!"
He grabbed the doorknob and twisted it roughly, but it was locked from the inside.
"You little—!"
Expanding his Spirit Zone, he sensed someone leaning against the door inside.
Flames flickered in his palm as he spoke. "Open up. I'll use Fire Strike. If you don't,
you'll get blown away too."
"I don't care. No—please, do it, Teacher."
Unexpectedly, it was Maria's voice.
Though not her homeroom teacher, Sard had been at the school before her enrollment, so he
couldn't be mistaken.
"Maria? What are you doing?"
"I'm sorry, Teacher. Please don't forgive me."
"Open the door. Let's talk face to face first."
"I'll be expelled, right? No—maybe I'll go to prison?"
"No, Maria. I don't know what's going on, but I'll listen. Just open the door."
"It doesn't matter. I don't want to live anymore. I just want to die here."
Sard extinguished the flames.
"Sigh. Fine, stay there if you want."
Given Maria's usual behavior, she wasn't in her right mind. Breaking the door would be
pointless.
Leaning against the iron door, Sard gazed at the sky of Alpheus Magic School, which he
had once found suffocating in his youth.
His restraint—not yelling at her to come out immediately—calmed Maria's heart slightly.
"Teacher."
"What?"
"Do you hate me?"
"I don't hate women."
While he generally liked women, this statement reflected his own eccentric philosophy,
distinct from that.
"Why is the world so unfair?"
"Haha! You hate unfairness? It's rare to be born as pretty as you."
"You wouldn't understand, Teacher, being a genius mage. What it's like to have no talent.
No matter how hard I try, despair always comes first. Do you know how hopeless that feels?"
"…I do."
Maria turned her head toward the door.
"You do?"
"I graduated at 22, so it's been four years since I became a teacher. You enrolled when I
was a senior. You know I'm an alumnus, right?"
"Yes."
"Then think back. Have you ever heard of a student named Sard at this school?"
Now that he mentioned it, she couldn't recall anything.
Even Shirone, who became the talk of the school immediately after enrolling, was an
exception.
"No. Never."
"Figures. Middle-to-lower grades, no talent, not from a wealthy family—just a
troublemaker always fighting outside school."
"You?"
"Yep. I took two years for every class jump. Spent three years kneeling in the senior
class. Haha! Though my situation was slightly better than yours now."
Sard imagined Maria's pout as he spoke.
"But isn't it the same? Unless you're the best, everyone breaks eventually. I was no
different. I made it to the senior class but failed every time."
"Then how…?"
"How did I become a top teacher? Luck, pure luck. I did my best, but not as much as
others, and I hated struggling to crush others. Then one day, someone told me this."
Sard recalled the moment.
"They said, 'The reason you can't win competitions isn't because you can't—it's because
you don't want to.'"
Maria quietly repeated the words.
"Maria, you don't have to beat anyone. If you love magic, that's enough. There are people
in this world who don't want to compete. Like me."
The ten-year-old girl who had enrolled in school because she loved magic was now broken
after nine years.
"Meeting that person changed my life. Thinking back, they were such a weirdo. A teacher
saying something like that."
"Was that person… you?"
"Yes. My mentor, Headmaster Alpheus."
Maria's head whipped around.
"What? The Headmaster?"
"From that day on, I became their formal student and received personal guidance. An
incredible privilege. It turned my life around. With training from a mage renowned across the continent,
even a scoundrel like me could be called a genius and live comfortably. How about it? Envious? Life's all
luck, hahaha!"
Maria grew despondent again.
"So what I'm saying is, the world is unfair. Some are born talented, some throw
themselves into competition. But even someone like me—untalented and hating competition—can sometimes
experience miracles. That's why everyone lives with hope, right?"
Sard stood up and turned away.
"Miracles can come to anyone. It's too soon to give up after all the time you've spent.
Why not try a little longer? Just a little?"
Maria buried her face in her knees.
"Should I stake my life on a miracle that might never come? What if it doesn't? What
happens to me then?"
"What are you talking about, Maria?"
Sard placed a hand on the iron door and smiled.
"Look—a miracle has already come to you."
Tears welled in Maria's eyes.
"Let's start over. You might not have soared like others, but don't define yourself yet.
Maybe you were just unlucky. Maybe the method was wrong. School isn't efficient for everyone. So, can you
trust me one more time? I'll definitely make you a mage."
Maria covered her face with her hands.
"Teacher, really? Can I really do it?"
"You can. If magic is your life, never give up."
With a clank, the lock opened. Maria emerged, her eyes swollen, and shouted urgently.
"Teacher! Hurry! The mechanism… the mechanism…!"
"Huh? The mechanism?"
Even leaping 700 meters, teleportation magic wouldn't take long.
By now, the test should be nearing its end, and the lack of news suggested the teachers
had handled it smoothly.
At that moment, defying Sard's expectations, a sharp sound pierced the air as a streak of
light crashed down.
"Teacher Sard! What are you doing!"
Siena charged forward, her eyes blazing with a mix of fury and urgency.
As she grabbed Sard's collar, she noticed Maria and froze in confusion.
"Maria? Why are you here?"
Sard asked, "What's wrong? Did something happen?"
Siena shouted again, "Deactivate the mechanism now! The uncrossable bridge's difficulty
is set to Level 10!"
"What?!"
It wasn't just the safety measures that had been tampered with?
Remembering Maria's words, Sard realized the truth and rushed toward the control room.
Just then, another streak of light arced down.
Etella arrived via spatial teleportation, panting as she ran toward Siena and Sard.
"Teacher! It's terrible, just terrible!"
Given the scale of the trap, Siena was terrified.
"What's wrong? Don't tell me there's been an accident…?"
Etella shouted with shock still in her eyes.
"Shirone… Shirone is…!"
