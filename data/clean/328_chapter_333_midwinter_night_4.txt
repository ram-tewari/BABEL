Chapter 333:
Midwinter Night (4)
"Then? Do you have any other plans in mind?"
Lyria pulled out a totem and held it out.
"While the mental entity remains, we'll isolate this area with the
law. We need to set up a golden formation, so help me out. You know how to calculate a three-dimensional
magic square, right?"
Dante shrugged nonchalantly.
"Well... it's kind of my specialty."
As someone who specialized in magic circles, he was more than qualified
to assist Lyria with her work.
However, Dante still had doubts.
Would this really be enough?
Even if they isolated the mental entity in the old ruins, trapping it in
a sealing formation afterward was a completely different matter.
"Can we finish the golden formation by tomorrow
morning?"
"It depends on our skill, but if we split the work, it should be
possible. Why?"
Dante glanced back at the still-lit shopping district.
There were probably countless couples wandering the streets, drawn to
the lights like moths.
"I think we might need some reinforcements."
After parting ways with Dante, Shirone and Amy spoke much less than
before.
But the atmosphere wasn't tense.
Amy was preoccupied with Dante's advice, pondering how to find her own
style.
Shirone, too, was deep in thought, struggling to fully control the
wrathful spirit within him.
'That was really dangerous earlier.'
Armand had broken free of his control and targeted someone.
It wasn't that Shirone lacked mental discipline. It was more that his
anger had grown stronger than before.
Of course, it's natural for one's personality to change over
time.
But this wasn't the direction he wanted.
'I need more training. I'll visit Teacher Etella during the break and
ask for advice.'
Having reached that conclusion, Shirone found the Snow Crystal Inn that
Shakora had recommended.
"This is the place I mentioned."
Amy didn't say anything, just observing the atmosphere from
outside.
Shirone examined the exterior of the building.
As Shakora had said, the ambiance was nice. The decorations were
elegant, and the view of the endless snowy landscape was pleasing.
Amy had no complaints about the inn. But she still had questions.
There was no reason for Shirone, unfamiliar with the area, to
specifically seek out this inn.
"Why insist on this place?"
"Well, I heard the atmosphere is really nice. We can relax
here."
"Alright..."
If Shirone said so, Amy had no reason to argue.
Though practical by nature, this trip was also meant to console her, so
she appreciated the effort he had put in.
Still, she felt a bit guilty.
On a day like this, it would have been nice to spend the night together,
but her mind was too conflicted.
"Welcome to Snow Crystal."
As they entered, Shirone was surprised by the bright lights.
Numerous lamps lit up the hall as if it were daytime.
Past a right-angled corridor was a spacious terrace where tourists
gathered around a hearth, drinking tea.
A minstrel's music filled the air.
The lyrics conveyed the Ice Queen's desperate feelings, freezing
everyone around her.
"Are you two together?"
The receptionist smiled as she asked.
"Yes, two of us."
While Shirone arranged the rooms, Amy stood a bit apart, looking
elsewhere.
It felt strange for a man and woman to enter such a place together,
unlike traveling with friends in a group.
"Two rooms, please."
The receptionist glanced between Shirone and Amy, then nodded and placed
the keys on the table.
"Alright, rooms 311 and 312. Take the stairs to the third floor and
turn right in the corridor. Payment is in advance."
After paying, Shirone looked at Amy, who slowly walked towards the
stairs.
As they walked down the third-floor corridor, Shirone spoke.
"Let's unpack first. If you're hungry, we can go out to
eat."
"No, I just want to rest today. Walking around in the cold has made
me feel heavy. Is that okay?"
If she was truly tired, resting was fine, but Shirone felt she might be
lying.
Maybe she was angrier than he thought.
Even if she understood Dante's advice intellectually, emotions were a
different matter.
When Dante asked for his opinion during their argument about the
graduation exam, Shirone felt he should have sided with Amy.
"Amy, about earlier..."
Reading his thoughts, Amy smiled slightly.
"Don't misunderstand. It's not that. You know I don't hold
grudges."
Shirone felt even more uneasy.
The fact that she mentioned grudges suggested she did hold one.
If Amy's feelings were already a mess, no words could make her feel
better.
In this case, what was needed wasn't conversation but time.
"Alright, see you tomorrow."
"Yeah, you rest too."
Amy unlocked her door and went inside. Shirone watched her go, then
inserted his key into the lock.
Entering his room, the first thing he noticed was the endless snowy
landscape through the large window.
In the distance, tiny torches flickered, and people were ice
fishing.
How nice it would have been to see this view with Amy.
The thought made him sigh.
"Sigh."
"Sigh."
Shirone and Amy turned to look at each other simultaneously. Then, as if
on cue, they turned towards the door.
"What is this?"
There were two doors but only one room.
And only one bed.
They stared at each other in confusion.
A moment of silence followed.
Shirone's mind was overwhelmed with thoughts.
Most importantly, he had insisted on this inn, so he needed to come up
with an excuse.
'What should I do? Should I ask to change rooms? But all the rooms here
might be like this. Should we just get a refund and leave? Would Amy be upset? Or would she prefer
that?'
As Shirone stood there, pale and unsure, Amy sighed softly.
She had thought his behavior was odd. Now that things had come to this,
the decision was hers.
Amy went to the wardrobe and set down her backpack.
"Well, we have no choice. Let's unpack."
"Oh? Oh, okay!"
Shirone was still flustered.
He started unpacking without knowing what was in his bag when Amy
casually asked,
"Who told you about this place?"
"Huh? What?"
"You wouldn't have come here knowing what it was like. Did someone
recommend it?"
Feeling embarrassed, Shirone confessed.
"Actually, your father did..."
Amy sighed, as if she had expected it.
With a spy in the family, she couldn't blame Shirone.
"He said the atmosphere was really nice. That you'd like
it..."
The night view was indeed beautiful. But it was also a risky place for
just the two of them.
After roughly organizing her clothes, Amy pointed to the door.
"Let's go down and have dinner."
When Shirone and Amy returned to the inn after dinner, it was around 11
PM.
They had spent as much time outside as possible, but they both knew this
was their final destination.
Washing up and changing clothes felt different than usual.
Having traveled by carriage since the previous afternoon, their bodies
felt heavy, but their minds grew clearer.
Neither approached the single bed.
They didn't discuss who would sleep in it.
The bed was a taboo topic.
They sat side by side, looking at the horizon where the white snow met
the black night sky.
The mysterious night view allowed them to momentarily forget
reality.
But strange noises from the next room abruptly brought them back to
reality.
When their eyes met, Shirone's face turned red.
Amy averted her gaze, desperately trying to appear calm.
"What's wrong? Why is your face red?"
"Y-yours is a bit red too."
Amy realized this wasn't right.
If this atmosphere continued, she'd rather sleep in hell.
"Alright! Let's be honest. We're not kids, so let's not act like
this. We're spending the night together in an inn, and we hear strange noises from the next room. And
there's only one bed. It's a weird situation. But now that we know it's weird, it's not weird anymore.
Agreed? Let's just sleep."
Shirone felt more at ease. But part of him was dissatisfied.
How could emotions be cut off so cleanly?
"Are you sure? I'm still a guy."
"I know. But nothing will happen, right?"
Of course, nothing would.
But feeling a bit hurt, Shirone muttered softly,
"You never know..."
Amy chuckled at his timid protest. She thought of her family, who were
probably imagining all sorts of things.
'Honestly, Dad...'
Setting up such a perfect trap to make them share a room.
No ordinary noble would think this way.
But the Karmis family, valuing individuality, was open-minded about
relationships.
Though it was uncertain if they would accept a commoner.
Still, this decision showed how much they liked Shirone.
Knowing she wouldn't have to worry about her family's opinion in her
relationship with Shirone eased her tension a bit.
'Come to think of it, he's amazing.'
The Karmis family's Red Eyes were excellent at understanding others'
temperaments.
But that also meant ordinary talents didn't impress them.
Shirone had grown at a frightening pace, earning recognition from her
strict father.
Though it was partly due to her own failure, he had kept his promise
from a year ago and stood on the same level in the graduating class.
One year.
He had closed the vast gap in just one year.
But she didn't feel resentful.
Even if Shirone started his life over, he would still end up
here.
"Honestly... I was mad at you earlier. I was upset that you didn't
take my side when Dante asked."
Shirone turned to look at Amy.
"I don't know. I just felt pathetic. When I heard Jokre had passed,
I was really jealous. I wondered if I should have transferred too. Maybe I was stubborn without the
skills to back it up. Maybe I don't have the talent for a prestigious school."
"Amy, thinking like that..."
"I know. If I think negatively, there's no end to it. That's why I
snapped at you. I was scared to admit I was wrong."
"But now you know what the problem is."
"Yes, Dante's analysis is accurate. I'm grateful for that. But... I
still don't know. Even if I develop a style, I don't know if I can graduate. I have no confidence in
anything."
'Amy...'
This is why failure is so frightening.
The graduating class is the pinnacle of the magic school.
Falling from the top feels much worse than failing a promotion exam in
the advanced classes.
The probability of someone who failed the first graduation exam passing
the second one is lower than average for this reason.
