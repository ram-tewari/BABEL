Chapter 618:
In Richard's room, filled with various mechanical parts, the sound of
machinery echoed. Surrounded by mirrors, he meticulously adjusted his body with tools, a necessary task
the day before the graduation exam.
The cover from his right chest to his side was open, revealing the core
power unit attached to his heart, glowing red. His detached right arm lay between his legs, and after
finishing the soldering, he attached the arm to his shoulder.
As the mechanical sounds, like scratching an itch, followed, his five
fingers wriggled like octopus tentacles, and the ball bearings in his elbow rotated, performing
movements impossible for a human.
"Hmm."
Richard was satisfied.
'Next, system synchronization.'
Winking one eye, he adjusted his brainwaves, causing the mechanical iris
in his eye to emit a blue glow.
In the blue-tinted vision, like being underwater, numerous measurement
data changed in real-time.
Richard's father, Ainca Marto, was the owner of Ainca Alchemy, the
world's second-largest alchemy foundation, and the head of the Bio-Robotics Research Institute under
Valkyrie.
The fact that a direct family member of such a prestigious family
willingly participated in an experiment with less than a 30% success rate indicated the family's
nature.
Even implanting a mechanical device in the brain had less than a 4%
success rate, but Marto had no hesitation in opening his eldest son's skull.
'Heh, we have many siblings.'
What he gained from modifying 35% of his body with alchemy technology
was the trust of the foundation's board and biologically impossible physical strength.
'Click correction.'
Extending his right arm and inputting a command, a crosshair appeared at
the final point his fist aimed at.
'Activate combat simulation.'
The room transformed into a forest, and numerous monsters emerged from
cover, charging at Richard.
'Equip fire magic stone.'
His elbow joint shook violently.
The magic stones Richard used were not the disposable ones sold on the
market but special products of Ainca Alchemy, advanced materials mass-produced from the "Essence of
Spirits," of which only 17 existed worldwide.
'Fire!'
As his right arm moved back and forth with a clanking sound, virtual
fireballs shot out in rapid succession, knocking down the monsters.
'Switch magic stone. Ice.'
With a hissing sound, his elbow rotated, and sharp ice spikes were
fired.
The automatic tracking function calculated the monsters' current
coordinates and acceleration, selecting the primary target, and Richard only needed to shoot
accordingly.
After annihilating 200 monsters, a mission complete signal appeared,
followed by an analysis chart.
Three monsters had approached within a 20-meter radius, and the click
error rate was 0%.
'Deactivate combat simulation.'
As the dense forest disappeared and the room's scenery returned, Richard
smiled.
"Perfect."
Ainca Richard (Final Rank: 7th in the graduating class).
Major: Alchemy Engineering.
Special Notes: Modified 35% of his body with alchemy technology. Heir to
the Ainca Foundation.
Amy and Shirone sat side by side on the bed, silent for a while.
"……"
The reason their hearts raced just by being next to each other was
because it was a special day.
'Whatever the outcome…'
Today, the possibilities they dreamed of would become harsh
realities.
"Do you think we'll pass?"
Amy, who had never relied on anyone else's judgment, genuinely wanted to
hear it this time.
"Of course."
Even if it was a lie, Shirone needed the reassurance too.
"We've done our best. Think about all the days we've worked
hard."
"But that's true for everyone. If we fail here…"
They would have to fight for another year.
No, could they really keep fighting?
"I'm not confident. I want to leave it to fate, but… it's
hard."
"Amy."
Shirone turned her to face him, holding her shoulders.
"You're not inferior to anyone in the graduating class. I can vouch
for that."
"Really?"
"Yes. You're one of the best students. Look at yourself
objectively. No matter how fierce the competition, if you don't make mistakes, you can
pass."
Thinking about Shirone's words, Amy realized he was right.
She had analyzed the graduating class for a year, and if she took the
exam normally, she could achieve good results.
"Shirone."
As the tension faded, she finally noticed Shirone's face.
'What will happen to us?'
Reading Amy's expression, Shirone said,
"We are who we are. No matter the outcome, my feelings won't
change."
Even if they both passed and moved on to a bigger world, their hearts
would remain connected.
"Yes."
Her small voice felt like the world was closing its eyes, and Shirone
slowly leaned in.
"Amy…"
As the sound faded, Amy gently lifted her chin.
They were close enough to feel each other's breath, and just before a
monumental moment in their history…
'Wait, just a moment ago…'
Amy's eyes widened.
"Shirone, wait. Time out."
"Huh? What?"
Shirone, who had been leaning in completely, quickly pulled back,
blushing.
"Why, what's wrong?"
"Well… it's just that right now might not be the best time.
Actually…"
Amy glanced at the bathroom, and Shirone realized.
"Oh, right."
From a woman's perspective, a first kiss after vomiting would be a
terrible memory.
In the awkward atmosphere, Shirone tapped his knees.
"Ahem, it can't be helped."
He couldn't hide his disappointment, and feeling guilty, Amy quickly
added,
"It's not that I don't want to. Really. So…"
"I know. It's okay."
As Shirone smiled and reassured her, Amy shyly lowered her head and then
asked hesitantly,
"Should I… go brush my teeth?"
Shirone couldn't help but laugh and fell back onto the bed.
None of the 30 students believed they would fail the next day.
No matter how the rankings were set, surprises could happen, and
everyone had a secret strategy prepared for success.
"A strategic alliance?"
In the garden behind the graduating class building, Binder, Suabi, and
Luman were having a secret conversation.
"Yes. You guys are in the utility series. My proposal isn't
bad."
Binder, who organized the meeting, adjusted his horn-rimmed
glasses.
He had barely made it into Class Two, but he was an excellent theorist,
scoring 100 in advanced class theory and perfect marks in the graduating class's implementation
evaluation.
"As you know, I scored full marks in all basic magic series. But
it's true I don't have any special strengths."
Suabi and Luman listened intently to his cold analysis.
"But if you help me, my balance could become the best weapon. If
Suabi's buffs focus solely on me and Luman's crowd control creates the right situations, we can face
anyone in the graduating class."
Elcras Binder (Final Rank: 20th in the graduating class).
Major: Magic Analysis, Implementation Series.
Special Notes: Master of 36 types of regulated magic designated by the
association.

While Suabi accepted it positively, Luman scoffed.
"Do you really think that makes sense? In the end, only you

Luman, who had gained more weight from binge eating over the year, still
had a quick mind.
"Maybe. So I have a proposal. If one of us has to drop out, I'll be

"I'm in."

"In the six graduation exam categories, four involve combat skills.

Podran Luman (Final Rank: 16th in the graduating class).
Major: Trap Magic, Crowd Control Series.
Special Notes: Binge eating. Excellent situational judgment.
