Chapter 478:
Gaold looked up at the sky and waited.
The bridge, which had become much steeper since Kangnan started, now
formed an almost vertical cliff.
Thus, the height was a staggering 2 kilometers.
It was a perfect height for a warrior like Kangnan or Miro, who couldn't
use magic, to fall to their deaths.
But that was only if they crossed the drawbridge.
Uriel hadn't taken action yet, but that fact was even more
concerning.
"Damn it."
He didn't care about Gaold's group at all.
The only thing he was interested in was Miro, the one and only in all of
humanity.
And finally, when Miro appeared over the bridge, Uriel reacted.
If he could reclaim Miro, everything would return to square one.
He didn't know how Kariel would judge, but he believed that this time,
his intellect and his intuition would align.
Boom!
As the drawbridge shook and Uriel soared vertically, Gaold followed
behind.
A new hope sprouted as Miro came back over, but that sprout was rotten
somewhere.
"Mutt…"
Kangnan didn't make it over the bridge.
She had faithfully carried out her mission by sending Miro
flying.
His heart pounded, and a strange flame rose in his chest.
It was a flame different from desire.
It was another kind of hell, different from anger or hatred, that Gaold
experienced for the first time in his life.
"Argh!"
As he crushed the atmosphere with the Air Press, Uriel's speed, which
had been soaring with his wings of light, rapidly decreased.
Screech!
Uriel turned his body towards Gaold, spinning the halo of
destruction.
To be honest, Gaold was a nuisance.
If his opponent could match him, he could enjoy the contest.
Conversely, if the enemy was weak, he could just destroy them.
That ambiguous borderline.
No matter how much he trampled them, they would stand up again, and they
couldn't harm him.
Uriel thought.
Perhaps that was the confusing essence of humanity.
Humans were a bundle of contradictions, with good and evil, life and
death, strength and weakness all mixed together, making it impossible to define any one thing.
"I see."
Uriel suddenly realized.
Why he hadn't unleashed all his power to end them.
"He was afraid."
He was uneasy about the possibility that he might not achieve results
even if he gave his all to these weak creatures.
"Anke La, the reason you were wary of humans was…"
Gaold's air gun hit Uriel directly.
It was a powerful attack that could pierce steel, but to the archangel
of destruction, it was just a sting.
In that slight unpleasant stimulation, Uriel delved deeper into his
thoughts.
It excited the pinnacle of intellect, Kariel, and made the incarnation
of destruction, himself, hesitate to destroy.
"Human chaos…"
Finally, Uriel reached a conclusion.
"It collapses the absoluteness of the world."
"Argh!"
Gaold's rapid-fire attacks struck Uriel's body more powerfully and
quickly.
Finally, a sensation that could be defined as pain, though angels had no
such definition, was transmitted, and Uriel's halo expanded to an enormous size.
"I am absolute."
Infinite Judicial Halo - Ragnarok.
As the concept of destruction accumulated in the halo, white electricity
spread in all directions.
"I can't stop it."
As the thought flashed through Gaold's mind, his hand was already
reaching out to Miro.
Pulling her with the Air Net, Gaold dived towards the ground with all
his might.
Uriel dissolved the Nirvana Shield.
Surrounded solely by the concept of destruction, his body needed no
tools, being a weapon in itself.
"Ugh!"
Uriel fell vertically.
As his body, surrounded by white electricity, became a streak of light
and crashed into the bridge, Zulu's summoning magic activated.
Space-Time Bird - Posmetery.
The frictional heat from Uriel's fall generated immense heat,
evaporating everything made of matter.
In the place where the light sphere, born and extinguished in an
instant, disappeared, only a hemispherical crater with a diameter of 5 kilometers remained.
Complete destruction, literally.
Standing at the center of the hemisphere, Uriel slowly raised his head
to look at the sky.
This was why he didn't want to do it.
"Humans…"
He had been struck from behind again.
What was it?
The giant bird made of light that appeared to Gaold's group at the
moment of destruction.
When the bird enveloped them, it was as if they had crossed into another
dimension, and the concept of destruction was not conveyed.
"Where is Kariel?"
Uriel flew up immediately and arrived at the wall of the Hall of
Corruption in an instant.
Kariel, still trapped in the Stop field, was motionless.
All the fallen angels flew in and knelt before him.
They trembled with guilt for failing to recapture Miro, but Uriel didn't
seem to care.
"What happened to that woman?"
A fallen angel at the rear dropped the unconscious Kangnan to the ground
and said,
"We've captured her for now. It seemed better to take her
alive…"
Kariel would have praised them.
He believed everything in the world had value and could create new
value.
On the other hand, Uriel, the archangel of destruction, didn't like that
approach.
It wasn't because he was simple-minded.
He was absolute destruction, and before that, any value became
worthless.
"What should we do?"
Uriel turned his body.
"I will go to Raiel."
He needed to bring the archangel of light to restore Kariel.
"Handle the humans as you see fit."
Leaving those words, Uriel disappeared into the sky.
The fallen angels, left without their commander, drooped their shoulders
with sullen expressions.
If they had achieved merit in this battle, it wouldn't have been
impossible to be reinstated as angels, but in the end, everything had failed.
"There is still a chance."
A fallen angel with golden hair cascading down like a veil to her chest
said,
"Let's take this woman hostage and negotiate."
A fallen angel with sharp fangs scoffed.
"Do you think that's possible? What's important to them is Miro.
This woman has no value as a hostage. It would be better to torment her and at least get some small
pleasure."
"That's a good method too."
A burly fallen angel interjected.
"But ignoring even the slightest possibility isn't effective. No,
maybe the solution lies here."
"What do you mean?"
"Humans have something called affection. They have a tendency to
understand others' pain as their own."
"I see."
The golden-haired fallen angel nodded and looked down at Kangnan with
cold eyes.
"The point is, as long as we don't kill her, right?"
Kangnan was still in a deep sleep.
The flash of teleportation landed in the forest with a loud
noise.
Casting it without fully understanding the terrain was akin to suicide,
but Gaold's group had no time to be picky.
"Ugh!"
As they crashed to the ground and rolled at tremendous speed, no one lay
sprawled.
They immediately regained their balance and cast teleportation spells,
delving deeper into the forest.
They had to get as far away as possible.
The terrifying power that imprinted death in the deepest part of their
brains was enough to make them flee.
If it weren't for the Posmetery they had saved until the end, no one
would have survived.
"Gaold! We have to go back!"
Miro, struggling in Gaold's grip, shouted.
"We have to go back, you fool!"
"Shut up. Stay still."
Gaold's voice was harsh.
For 20 years, he had lived with only the thought of rescuing Miro in his
mind.
And now, Miro was in his arms.
The stopped clock was ticking again, and from now on, everything that
happened was a terrifyingly pure white future for Gaold.
The expression "lost his way" was accurate.
He couldn't judge anything, and he didn't even know what the fear rising
in his heart was.
"We can save her now! As long as I'm here, they won't kill her
recklessly!"
"Shut up."
Miro was frustrated.
He couldn't possibly not know. That's why Gaold was making the wrong
decision now.
"You idiot, that girl…"
Electricity sparked in Gaold's eyes, and he opened his mouth wide enough
to dislocate his jaw and shouted.
"Shut up!"
The group's steps halted.
Gaold, rolling his eyes in confusion, roughly threw Miro to the ground
and turned around.
"Damn it!"
When he punched a tree trunk, the bark flew off with a thud, and immense
pain surged in.
Even so, one thought wouldn't leave his mind.
Kangnan. Kangnan had been captured.
What tormented him the most was the certainty that she was still alive
at this moment.
To the group, Kangnan was just a brief companion in this project, but
Gaold was different.
The moment he realized she wasn't by his side for the first time, he
understood that Kangnan had always been there.
"She is a warrior of the wolf tribe. She acted according to her
beliefs. She chose, and we must respect that."
"Don't talk nonsense."
Miro stood up from where she had fallen and grabbed Gaold by the
collar.
"Get a grip! We can save her now! This has nothing to do with
me!"
The group's feelings were the same as Miro's.
If they could save her, they should.
But no one could speak up.
Having lived in hell for 20 years, Gaold's judgment was paramount unless
they could share even a tenth of his pain.
For the first time, Sein lied for his friend.
"The resolution we made when we came here was that the project
takes priority under any circumstances. Miro, we all risked our lives to save you. Now, breaking that
rule isn't easy."
Miro glared at Sein and then turned back to Gaold.
"Is that what you think too? Is this really what you want? Are you
choosing to let that girl die just because you want me?"
Gaold remained silent.
He seemed like a newborn child, unable to extend his thoughts in any
direction.
Miro, looking at him with pity, grabbed Gaold's chin and shouted.
"Look at me! Look into my eyes!"
Gaold's gaze slowly moved to her.
His pupils held an emotion as if something had been completely drained
from him.
"Are you that scared? Afraid that everything you've done for 20
years will be in vain, so you're going to abandon that girl?"
"I…"
Before he could finish, Miro pulled Gaold's neck and kissed him.
Gaold, his eyes shaking with shock, reflexively pushed Miro away.
"What are you doing! Does this look like a time to joke
around?"
Miro didn't give up. She approached again, grabbed Gaold's wrist, and
placed it on her chest.
"Just do it here. Take me! Then go save her, you fool!"
Gaold frowned.
Everyone was making him feel like a fool.
"You, right now…"
At that moment, a beautiful voice, a mixture of many voices, spread from
the sky.
It was the ability called Gospel, spread collectively by the
angels.
"To the human with Miro. We have captured your kin. If you do not
bring Miro by noon tomorrow, we will kill her. Remember, the sooner you come, the less pain she will
suffer. This is our final message. We are…"
The Gospel repeated the same words and then disappeared.
It was a huge sound, but it ended cleanly without even an echo.
Gaold, staggering and sitting down on a rock, bit his thumbnail and fell
into thought.
Noon tomorrow.
"Time is tight."
The fallen angels were those who had broken taboos, and the Maras they
commanded were extremely ferocious.
Even at this moment, there was no telling what might be happening to
Kangnan.
Crunch. Crunch.
Gaold's teeth, which had quickly bitten off his nail, began to gnaw at
the bone of his thumb.
Crunch. Crunch.
No one could speak to him as he performed this grotesque self-harm,
amplified a thousand times by his heightened sense of pain.
