Chapter 668:
"Lemo!"
Jordic rushed to Lemo's collapsed body and shook him.
As a veteran of countless battles from the War Chariot Guild, he
couldn't accept that someone like Lemo had died just from fear.
Especially since the guild members from Silver Ring and Blood Rose were
still alive and well.
"Wake up! What kind of pathetic death is this!? Open your eyes!"
Unable to contain his anger, Jordic suddenly flinched and began slowly
stepping back.
As if gravity had disappeared, Lemo's corpse began to rise, standing
upright.
"What… is this?"
The world had twisted into something grotesquely unnatural.
Realizing this, Jordic turned to Shirone and shouted.
"What the hell is going on!? This wasn't part of the deal!"
"Anyone can die. Didn't we all join the mercenary corps prepared for
that from the start?"
Lupist's voice remained cold.
"Anyone can die? Does this look like a normal death to you? Why is the
corpse standing!?"
"Don't get agitated. You'll die."
Shirone's sharp tone made Jordic fall silent.
There had been casualties, but the most critical factor now was mind
control.
"Chairman, please explain this properly. We can't proceed with the
mission while everyone's in the dark."
At Shirone's words, Lupist removed his mask. There was no longer any
point in hiding his identity.
Jane also removed her mask, revealing her face.
Just seeing that they were the Chairman and Chief Secretary of the Magic
Association slightly calmed the group's fear.
"It's a type of human trap. And in my opinion, it was set specifically
for you."
Lupist pointed at Shirone as he continued.
"Ra Enemy manipulates the past through sensory memories, but that method
doesn't work on you. So they added another layer—manipulating someone else's past to create the present,
and using that to influence your past."
Jane added,
"The brain-in-a-vat method isn't mental magic. Simply put, it's a
completely different way of perceiving the world compared to the sensory input we've known until
now."
Hearing this, the mercenaries glanced around at their grotesquely
shifting surroundings.
The buildings had transformed into fibrous structures, pulsating like
beating hearts, and blood seeped through the cracks.
The corpses stood on tiptoe, candles were lit before demon statues, and
skulls littered the floor.
"So what? It was always like this, and we just didn't perceive
it?"
The realization that this was not a conjured illusion, but a shift in
their sensory perception of reality, made their skin crawl.
"Our senses are everything."
Lupist placed his palm on a wall of muscle, and dozens of tendrils shot
out to grab his arm.
He quickly retracted it before being seized and continued
speaking.
"Perhaps our senses have finally tuned in to the real world. The
hellscapes we've seen in stories may just be the product of the sixth sense activating."
"Bullshit! You're just making this up! Spouting creepy ghost stories
doesn't help anyone!"
"I'm telling you not to look away. If a specific signal directly
stimulates the brain, then Lemo's death can be explained. From now on, suppress your emotions and think
logically. The moment you lose reason, this hell will swallow you whole."
While the others swallowed nervously, Meirei calmly covered her
ears.
"I can't hear the voices."
Their hearing was also being manipulated by the trap of the vat-mind,
because their senses were already distorted.
"Remove the Nemesis. It's just a distraction now."
The core members opened their Cube brick slots, and the rings on their
fingers vanished like magic.
Shirone gave instructions.
"First, we focus on survival. Then we find a way out. Let's secure a
safe zone and regroup there to figure out how to escape."
With the structure of Radum altered, this was no longer the
outskirts.
The mercenary unit re-formed their formation and began moving
forward.
Looking at the grotesque landscape, they realized how futile the
memories created through their senses truly were.
"Damn it… How did I end up in this mess…"
The glass-like atmosphere, scattering the red sun's light, only deepened
the feeling of being trapped.
"Stop. Ghouls."
Three ghouls were stuck to a wall, gnawing at the muscle fibers. Their
intestines were spilling from their torn stomachs, likely due to nerve failure in their brains.
In a world like this, it might be better to go mad. Just then, the
ghouls turned their heads.
"Food… Food…"
Just as they caught the scent of fresh flesh and were about to move, a
scream came from around the corner.
"AAAAH! HELP ME!"
A flood of ghouls, all with terrified faces, began rushing toward
them.
"A demon! A demon has appeared! Guh!"
The ghoul at the front screamed and died with his eyes rolled back in
terror.
"Captain! What now!?"
As the swordsmen shouted and drew weapons, Shirone stepped forward and
fired a barrage of Photon Cannons.
The Photon Cannons, projected through the Spirit Zone, lashed out with
their familiar clean brilliance and struck the ghouls.
'Good. The Spirit Zone isn't affected by the distortion.'
The ghouls were wiped out in an instant, but before they could even feel
relieved, a heavy rumble echoed from beyond the alley.
"What the hell is that!?"
Massive beings, about 2 meters tall, with black cloths over their heads
and cleaver-like blades below their elbows, approached.
Their grotesquely swollen bellies were crudely stitched together with
metal pins, and maggots writhed in the seams.
"Urgh…"
Their appearance alone was enough to induce nausea, but Shirone focused
only on the proportion of their mass.
'Height is about 2.2 meters… For the ground to rumble like this, they'd
have to weigh over a ton.'
Meirei spoke.
"They're Gallopers. Chefs of the Underworld."
Shirone whipped his head around.
"You knew?"
"No. This is my first time seeing them too. But I've heard of them
through an oracle."
The Gallopers spotted Shirone's mercenary unit and exchanged words in an
unfamiliar language.
—"Those guys can see us? The boundary's been fading lately."
—"They're weak anyway. Good for us."
'They're coming!'
As Shirone sensed it, the Gallopers all turned and charged the
mercenaries.
—"Fresh ingredients!"
As they plowed through the ghouls and closed in, the structures around
them began to pulse in sync, as if emotionally resonant.
"Shit! What the hell are these things!?"
Tens of tons of mass clashed directly with the mercenary unit.
Etella dodged the blades and countered with a blow to the abdomen,
instantly grasping their power.
'Too tough…'
Whether it was a sensory distortion or their actual durability defied
biology, it no longer mattered in the heat of battle.
"Hiyaaah!"
With a sharp cry, she unleashed a barrage of Yin-Yang Wave Fists,
shaking the one-ton body violently.
"Latra me dakaporuga! (These guys are strong!)"
'Now!'
When Etella struck the abdomen with her Twin Palms, a massive shockwave
erupted within the Galloper's body.
"Kraaaagh!"
Its skin bulged grotesquely before bursting at the side, spilling
rotting intestines everywhere.
"Ugh…"
Shaking out her stinging wrists, Etella frantically looked
around.
'Where's everyone else?'
The core members were holding the line, but the rear was in
chaos.
With the Gallopers' absurd durability, panic began spreading among the
mercenaries.
'No! Focus!'
The moment fear consumed them, they were as good as dead.
It was like fighting on the edge of a psychological cliff.
'Fight! We can win!'
As she tried to refocus, Toto—his face twisted in madness—charged into
enemy lines, flailing his spear.
"DIEEEEEE!"
It was a full-force blow, but his accuracy was off. When the spear
struck the Galloper's stomach, it snapped like a twig.
Staring blankly at the broken weapon, Toto saw the Galloper raise its
arm and bring down a cleaver.
"AAAAAGH!"
Just in time, Rian slid forward and intercepted the falling blade with
his greatsword.
"Kh!"
The sheer impact contorted his face, but without wasting a moment, he
shouted back.
"Move! Now!"
When he looked, Toto was already standing there, eyes rolled
back—lifeless.
"Patunga'ar! Patunga'ar!"
The Gallopers, shouting in their phlegm-filled voices, all struck Rian's
sword simultaneously.
It felt like catching boulders with bare hands.
'I'll push them back!'
With Divine Transcendence, Rian lifted the greatsword, and his knees
began to straighten.
"Patunga'ar! Patunga'ar!"
Panicking at his strength, the Gallopers used both arms to bring down
their cleavers.
Even as sparks exploded in his brain, Rian stood his ground, his legs
digging into the floor.
'I'll cut you down!'
As the divine will merged with Mach-level power, the bodies of the four
Gallopers split in half simultaneously.
"Phew!"
As Rian swung his greatsword through a pile of corpses reeking of rot, a
spray of guts burst forth.
"This isn't going to be easy."
Crushing a Galloper's body with steel magic, Lupist answered Rian's
remark.
"This isn't looking good. Our allies are too close together—it's
difficult to use area magic."
"Whatever this is, the problem is the twisted rule itself. The fact that
dying from fear makes people more afraid… that's the real issue."
Lupist glanced sideways and asked,
"You're no exception either, right? This is hell."
Rian, who had been silently staring at the red sky, replied
calmly.
"...I figured I'd end up here someday."
Then, dragging his greatsword, he began to walk away from the
frontlines.
"People are the same wherever you go."
Lupist watched Rian's back for a moment, then turned away with a faint
smirk.
"Well, you're not wrong."
Thanks to the efforts of the core members, they managed to keep the
battle even—but the real problem lay elsewhere.
'They're not the ones killing us.'
Nimbly dodging a cleaver, Shirone wiped the sweat from his neck and
frowned.
'Just winning isn't enough.'
Out of the three casualties so far, two had died from their own inner
fear.
"Rian! Fall back with the rest of the squad!"
The fact that no one had broken ranks was unusual for such a hastily
assembled team. But in truth, none of them could run.
'If your will breaks, you die!'
Everyone was holding on with all they had, and the moment Shirone gave
the order, they instinctively turned back.
'This is the commander's order! I'm not retreating out of fear!'
Justifying it that way, they followed Rian's lead in retreat—while
Shirone alone stepped forward to block the alley.
"Captain! What are you doing?!"
Bikon shouted as his armor clanked, but Shirone didn't budge. His eyes
were locked on the approaching Gallopers.
'I'll end this in a single blow.'
Light flared around Shirone like burning oil, and the massive
incarnation of a Radiant Angel spread its wings.
The light haloed even the murky hellscape in a divine radiance, and the
Radiant Angel raised a shining spear.
"W-What is that?"
Incarnation of the Radiant Angel – Angel's Punishment.
A beam of light shot down diagonally, piercing straight through the
fiber-covered floor.
'Damn...!'
The ground had seemed solid enough for people to walk on, but to the
piercing Angel's Punishment, it was no better than sponge.
That thought barely had time to register before a shockwave erupted from
underground, causing the structures around them to balloon like inflating balloons.
KUUUUUUNG!
The world seemed to split in two under the immense tremor, and from all
sides, blood sprayed from the walls as the shockwave burst outward.
"RAAAAAGH!"
Just that alone was enough to tear the Gallopers' bodies—once proud of
their iron-like skin—into shreds like dried fish, leaving them scattered across the floor.
'The structures absorbed the shockwave.'
Which meant it might be possible to use area magic more freely, so long
as it didn't harm allies.
"Not bad."
