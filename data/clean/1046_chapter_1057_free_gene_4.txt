Chapter 1057:
Free Gene (4)
'How did she detect me?'
Vedium, though Lycan's father, was no longer a biological being. His
body was composed of electricity, and since he could control the movement of electric charges, he could
go anywhere in the world.
'Is it an angel's ability?'
While remaining in Amy's room in the form of free electrons, he had
overheard every conversation. Through this, he learned that Ikael was an angel, but her exact abilities
were still unknown to him.
'No ordinary human—or even an extraordinary one—could track my movements
in this state. Therefore…'
A direct confrontation would be a losing battle.
"Answer me."
As Ikael took a step forward, her face was illuminated by the moonlight
streaming through the window.
The eyes on Vedium's white mask changed into heart shapes, but the
atmosphere remained icy.
"That girl… is Shirone's lover, isn't she?"
"Shirone?"
"Yes. Shirone is my target. If I kill her, I can destabilize Shirone
emotionally."
Vedium was honest because he needed to buy time and find a way to
escape.
'No matter how quickly I react, I won't be able to escape her
senses.'
If her abilities rivaled those of a scale mage who could manipulate
space, her mobility would be formidable.
"Hmm…"
Thinking she had cornered her prey, Ikael rested her chin on her hand,
lost in thought.
Vedium remained frozen, unable to move, but he knew she wasn't mistaken
in her assessment.
"Why… do you want to kill Shirone?"
"That brat…"
The simple white mask, which usually only displayed basic emotions, now
transformed with intricate, complex lines, forming a vivid depiction of a demonic face.
"…killed my son."
"What?"
At that moment, Vedium's body disintegrated back into free
electrons.
"Damn it!"
By the time Ikael regained her focus, the flow of electric charges had
become too faint to trace.
'He planned this.'
He only needed to hold Ikael's attention for the brief moment when
electricity lingered in space.
'What a formidable human. But if Shirone really killed his son, what
happened?'
Ikael figured she could ask Shirone about it in the morning, but she
quickly revised her thoughts.
'Shirone is my son.'
In any situation, protecting her child came first.
Watching over the still-sleeping Amy, Ikael's body once again dissolved
into radiant light.
'I'll handle this myself.'
A silent explosion of light filled the room, leaving behind an eerie
calm, as if nothing had happened.
'What a strange situation.'
In his free-electron state, Vedium roamed the air, sensing Ikael's
presence. While he could read human brainwaves, he couldn't fully decipher their thoughts, especially in
terms of language.
Angels, however, were different. Through their radiant forms, he could
directly sense their thoughts.
Ikael abruptly changed direction, ignoring inertia.
'She's heading there.'
Vedium had managed to fool her senses while inside Amy's room.
'Amplification?'
He had only been able to evade her because her senses hadn't been
heightened at the time.
Now, however, Ikael's perception had become so keen that she could hear
even the faint breathing of a sleeping butterfly in the forest.
'He's gone.'
With a faint popping sound, Ikael came to a halt.
'He's erased his thoughts.'
In his free-electron state, Vedium's presence was virtually zero.
However, if a specific thought occurred, the resulting charges could still be detected.
"I don't know how you came to have such a body…"
Ikael addressed him with respect.
"The cessation of thought is akin to the cessation of existence. You
won't be able to remain in that state forever."
A breeze swept through the silence.
"Come out. I want to hear your story. Depending on what you say, I may
yield."
His son was dead.
Though she now had Shirone, Ikael understood the pain of losing a
child.
Crackle!
Sparks erupted, igniting the grass ten meters away from Ikael.
Amidst swirling ash, Vedium materialized once more, his body taking
human form.
The mask was blank, devoid of expression.
"An angel, huh? Interesting."
Despite the apparent admiration, his tone lacked any trace of
fear.
"You're a strong human, too. You said Shirone killed your son. Is that
true?"
"Yes."
"If Shirone was at fault, then perhaps—"
"No need for that."
Vedium raised a hand, cutting her off.
"My son was also an assassin. He probably tried to kill Shirone first
and got killed in return. It's just business. I have no personal grudge against Shirone."
Ikael understood the situation but didn't fully believe Vedium's
words.
"'Kill your emotions to achieve what your emotions desire,' is that
it?"
"…You're not very angelic. I thought angels would be colder and more
rational."
Ikael thought of Goffin.
"No being is truly devoid of emotions."
"So, why did you track me? Do you intend to eliminate any potential
threats to Shirone in advance?"
"I'm a parent, too. Even if there's a valid reason, grief doesn't simply
vanish. If you're willing to accept an apology, can you forgive Shirone?"
"No."
Vedium's response was firm.
"I've already been paid, and there's more money to collect after I
complete the job. Not fully grasping your abilities was my mistake, but I won't make the same mistake
twice."
The symbols in his eyes changed to dollar signs ($).
Ikael didn't give up.
"If it's money you want, I can give it to you. Name your price, and I'll
get it."
"No."
It wasn't the same as no interest.
"Why?"
After a long pause, Vedium muttered, almost to himself.
"I can't let him down…"
Lycan.
For Ikael, those words cut the deepest. She turned her body slightly,
concealing her emotions.
"Fine."
Her delicate fist was now aimed at Vedium.
"If it's a matter of emotion, then I'll act on mine. One strike. If I
can subdue you with a single blow, you'll let this go. If not, you may do as you wish."
Vedium didn't fully understand but had no other choice.
"You'll regret this."
His body began to glow with a bluish light.
"I'll show you my best technique. I guarantee it's unlike anything
you've seen before."
Even if it meant eternal annihilation.
Without a word, Ikael remained still, her fist poised.
Vedium dissolved into electricity.
At the same moment, blue sparks scattered in every direction.
Driven solely by a desire to kill, Vedium charged toward Ikael with
precision akin to nature's most primal instincts.
'Now!'
Even as time slowed to a crawl and Vedium's attack drew near, Ikael
didn't flinch.
Instead, a radiant magical field unfolded.
'Ataraxia.'
Ignoring the relativity of time, her sensitivity heightened to the
extreme, allowing her to sense even the faintest movements of electrons.
She twisted her body with blinding speed.
Thud!
Her outstretched fist twisted midair, striking Vedium's mask with
tremendous force.
Crack.
The mask fractured, and Vedium's body was hurled backward like an
electric cannon.
"Arghhh!"
His white mask, once floating alone above the plains, was now pulled
down by gravity.
An expression with drooping eyes appeared on its surface.
"That was close."
Ikael retracted her fist and spoke.
"I didn't think you'd break your promise. But I'd like to know—what
happened to your body?"
"Grrk… creak…"
For a moment, the mask emitted mechanical noises before Vedium's voice
returned.
"Why does it matter? Life's full of things no one wants to hear. Your
son's alive, isn't he? That's all that matters."
A cold wind blew past as Vedium, his voice fainter than before,
continued.
"Why do you care? My son tried to kill Shirone. You have no reason to
pity me. So why did you try to give me a chance?"
"…A long time ago, my son died. Right in front of me… his head cut
off."
"I don't know. Maybe it was self-defense. Maybe your son was in the
wrong, and most people would think so…"
Ikael's brow furrowed in sorrow.
"But emotions aren't like that. No matter how logical you try to be,
some things can't be handled rationally."
Several moments passed.
"I don't know exactly why my body became electricity. But I do
know…"
Vedium recalled the moment.
"When I learned of Lycan's death, I decided to kill Shirone. That's when
it began. Maybe it triggered something… or maybe I'm already dead, and only my memories remain."
Ikael remembered a term Shirone had mentioned.
'Fine-tuning.'
Vedium's voice had become so faint that it could barely be heard.
"When you become electricity, you learn many things. Most of them can't
be put into words, but you get a sense of it. This world… it seems to hate your son."
Ikael's expression grew serious.
'Fine-tuning is applied to those who harbor hatred toward Shirone.
Active interference from the outside world…'
The ceiling of this world was halfway open.
"Who hired you for the assassination?"
"I can't say. It's a matter of professional pride. But Shirone probably
has an idea."
Ikael didn't press further.
"I have nothing more to say. But… when will I finally disappear?"
Though his body had disintegrated, his thoughts lingered, slowly fading
like dissipating smoke.
"Your name is Ikael, right?"
She turned to face him.
"This is strange… no, curious. Let me give you some information."
He was standing at the edge of the outer world.
"For gods…"
The neon symbols on his mask flickered, forming a face with a tongue
sticking out.
"…time flows backward."
"What?"
Before Ikael could ask, the neon lights went out, and the cracked mask
dissolved into nothingness.
Even an archangel couldn't comprehend his final words.
She decided to set the thought aside for now, turning to face the
inevitable end.
'At least… may it have been a life filled with something
meaningful.'
Be it good or evil, or something else entirely.
The next morning, Ikael recounted the events of the previous night to
Shirone.
Shirone had already suspected something.
'Giorgi.'
The leader of Catacomb, an organization within the armies of hell formed
to assassinate Yahweh.
"I didn't tell you earlier because I didn't want you to worry, but there
have been other assassination attempts in different regions. However, this time…"
The fact that they had targeted someone close to him was
unsettling.
'I've been too careless. No, there's nothing more I could have done. The
real issue is…'
Fine-tuning.
'It's impossible to guard against entities with different universal
constants. That's why Nane went to the outer world…'
Ikael spoke.
"He said, 'For gods, time flows backward.' That was his final message.
Does it mean anything to you?"
"No. I can form some hypotheses, but I don't have a definitive answer
yet. We'll find out soon, though. I'm heading to the ancient ruins with Zulu."
Canis and Arin had also joined them.
'For now, Wizard…'
As that thought crossed his mind, he turned just in time to see Wizard
walking toward the training grounds.
Her eyes were sunken, and she looked gaunt, as though she hadn't slept
all night.
'You worked hard.'
If a once-in-a-generation genius had pushed herself to the brink in just
one day, what kind of results could she have achieved?
"Teacher."
Wizard approached Shirone directly, while the instructors nervously
swallowed their breaths, watching.
"You stayed up all night. Did you make any progress?"
"One day…"
Wizard's fierce expression suddenly crumpled into one of
desperation.
"Can I have just one more day?"
