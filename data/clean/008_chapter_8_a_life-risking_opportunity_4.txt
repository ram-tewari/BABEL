Chapter 8:
A Life-Risking Opportunity (4)
The next morning.
Shirone headed to the library, exhausted. He hadn't slept a wink due to last night's
events.
His troubled mind slowed his categorization work. Realizing this wouldn't do, Shirone
forced himself to regain his composure.
Fear wouldn't solve anything. He had to prepare a countermeasure within the month.
First, he investigated.
Family head Bishop had three sons and one daughter. The eldest was a nationally
recognized 6th-class swordsman, and the second son, Rai, was a prodigy expected to follow in his brother's
footsteps.
Rian's older sister also had Ozent-level talent but had given up the sword for being a
woman and now worked as a royal pianist.
One by one, it became clear that Rian was the only one who hadn't distinguished himself.
Despite the family head assigning him a dedicated instructor, reports only mentioned his lack of talent.
"*Sigh*, I messed up. I should've just taken the beating."
Having lived his life compared to his siblings, Rian's outburst at Shirone's Thunder
Strike was understandable. Not all nobles were exceptional, it seemed.
"Now what? I'm really in trouble."
Once he grasped the situation, Shirone grew restless again. Beneath the desk lay the real
sword Rian had thrown at him.
After staring at it for a long time, Shirone finally stood up and headed to the
bookshelves.
Whether he fought or not, preparation was key. Having lived in the mountains, he was
confident in his stamina. If he could learn even basic defense, he might survive.
He decided to set aside history books for now. Instead, he gathered every book related to
swordsmanship.
There were technical manuals like *Swordsmanship Fundamentals*, philosophical works like
*What Is a Sword?* and *Only Humans Fear Swords*, and practical guides like *Win with Bluffing* and
*Survivors Are the Strongest*.
Shirone opened a straightforwardly titled book, *Swordsmanship*. It covered the history
of swords and the basics of swordsmanship.
Even to Shirone, an aspiring mage, the content was fascinating. If mages had the Spirit
Zone, swordsmen had something called Schema.
Schema was a virtual body, a human blueprint. Everyone had their own mental image of
their body, and Schema was an extreme refinement of that image.
Masters of Schema could perfectly understand their bodies, down to controlling individual
cells.
Reading this far, Shirone realized Schema was the counterpart to the Spirit Zone.
While mages erased themselves to harmonize with the world, swordsmen relentlessly honed
their sense of self.
Shirone experimented.
He entered the Spirit Zone while visualizing his virtual body.
This part was easy.
But the virtual body was shadowy, its interior impossible to see.
*Lifting this dark veil entirely would create Schema. Amazing.*
He returned to the book. Methods to attain Schema varied by family, as did the aspects
they emphasized.
Some families achieved Schema through mental discipline, while others pushed their bodies
to the limit.
Some families strengthened muscles, others speed, and others the nervous system.
Shirone recalled the red-haired girl from the alley four years ago. At the time, he'd
been too busy fleeing to notice, but her movements were superhuman.
*She must've mastered Schema. And she was around my age. What incredible talent.*
Admiration wouldn't help now. Shirone closed the book and pondered. Rian likely hadn't
mastered Schema either. But attempting it himself posed a problem.
Just as the Spirit Zone wasn't magic, Schema wasn't swordsmanship. As knowledge was vital
to mages, a strong body was indispensable to swordsmen. Without physical prowess, Schema's enhancements
would be limited.
"I already have the Spirit Zone. Rather than learning a conflicting concept, it's
faster to find a solution within swordsmanship."
He had to find a way to survive within a month.
Shirone picked up *Swordsmanship Fundamentals* and turned to the first page. Though it
was sudden, with his life on the line, his focus had never been sharper.
