Chapter 11:
First Steps Toward a Dream (3)
When the library door closed, he couldn't hold back and burst into laughter.
"Hahaha! Shirone, you're the best! I've never seen my brother make that kind of
expression before!"
Even as Rian hugged Shirone like a doll and shook him, Shirone couldn't take his eyes off
where Rai had left.
Swordsman versus Magician.
The single book Rian had brought raised many questions for Shirone. Perhaps, as Rai said,
reality might be different.
But Shirone believed.
That the spirit of a magician who fearlessly puts even his weaknesses on the experimental
table is the very reason intelligence exists in this world.
The grand library felt eerily empty. The bookshelves once filled with knowledge were now
vacant, and the glow lamps that used to illuminate the place like daylight were turned off.
A single beam of golden sunlight poured in from the fourth-floor window, developing on
the floor. Shirone turned the pages while basking in that sunlight.
One page, then another. And finally... the last page ended, and the cover closed.
Shirone caressed the back of the orange-red cover. Flipping a book is an easy task. But
making it through hundreds of pages of valleys to finally flip it over wasn't something just anyone could
do.
The history of 850 volumes.
Shirone had read all the history books in the grand library.
'It's over. This is the backbone of my knowledge.'
It felt warm. Like light was filling him up.
Shirone could now understand the world. Though he was a hunter's child, he could discuss
general worldly matters with any noble without issue.
It was the result of selection and focus. Talent merely suggests an efficient direction;
anyone who reads 850 history books can realize where they stand.
The library relocation project had also been completed after a major undertaking of one
year and six months. He could go home anytime now, and no longer had to worry about his family dying.
It felt like a dream. Numerous memories flashed through his mind like fragments of a
dream.
***
"Hey, Shirone. You're still here? I thought the work was finished?"
Rian, who opened the door and came in, coughed at the rising dust. Shirone greeted his
friend with a gentle smile.
"What's with that expression? Did something good happen?"
Rian was perplexed by his friend's unusual demeanor. But what Shirone said next was even
more shocking.
"Rian, show me around your house."
"Huh? What did you say?"
Rian's eyes widened. For Shirone, who was always principled and never made careless
mistakes, to suddenly ask to see his house was surprising.
But then he noticed a single book placed beside Shirone and understood.
"You... you did it?"
"Yes. There's nothing left to read, at least not in this library."
Nothing left to read.
Rian didn't like books, but he could feel how impressive that statement was.
In this moment, Shirone looked like the embodiment of intellect. Even the sunlight
pouring through the window seemed to bless him.
"Whahaha! You really did it? Man! You're truly amazing!"
Rian rejoiced as if it were his own achievement. In his own aggressive way, of course.
After hugging Shirone tightly and swinging him around for a while, he snapped his fingers as if he'd had a
good idea.
"Alright! If you want to come to our house, you're always welcome! This is perfect
timing. I've prepared an awesome gift to celebrate the end of your studies."
"What gift all of a sudden? I don't need anything."
"Just follow me, I tell you. You'll love it! Hahaha!"
Shirone felt uneasy. It was strange that Rian claimed to have prepared a gift when he'd
just learned about the completion of the reading. Moreover, the bulging laugh lines under his eyes suggested
it definitely wouldn't be a gift Shirone would appreciate.
Seeing the mansion up close for the first time, its imposing size was overwhelming. He
understood why ancient people chose massive structures to represent the divinity of gods.
"Rian, let's go in quietly. What if your family catches us?"
"What are you worried about? The contract's over anyway, right? Besides, everyone in
the family is out. Ah, this way. Hurry, hurry!"
The place Rian took him to was a woman's room with a fragrant scent. Pink curtains hung
over the balcony window, and various musical instruments, including a piano, were arranged around the room.
"Wait! This is your sister's room, isn't it? Let's get out! What are we doing
here?"
"Hehehe. Just wait a bit. It should be somewhere around here."
Rian rummaged through the dresser drawers as if it were his own room. Searching the
bottom shelf, he grabbed something and threw it at Shirone with a triumphant cheer.
"Found it! Here, Shirone, a gift!"
Something fragrant and soft covered Shirone's face. When he held it up with both hands,
it was a tiny piece of cloth.
Shirone's puzzled face turned red. Judging by its shape and size, it was clearly his
sister's underwear.
"Hey! Are you crazy? Why are you throwing this at me!"
"Hahaha! Look at how red your face is. You're already 17. You should be able to look
at panties boldly. That's why I'm giving it to you as a gift."
"Shut up! What's bold about looking at panties! Take it back quickly!"
"Oh? You're excited. Shirone is finally excited!"
Rian clutched his stomach and stomped his feet in laughter. In truth, nine-tenths of him
just wanted to tease Shirone, but one-tenth was genuinely concerned.
Having been cooped up in the library for a year and a half, Shirone's skin was pale
white, and his face still couldn't shed its childish appearance.
Shirone was too hungry for knowledge. It wouldn't be a bad idea to use shock therapy once
in a while to bring out the man inside him.
But it didn't work on Shirone. After returning the panties, Shirone sighed and gave his
usual nagging.
"When will you ever grow up? Playing such pranks. It's your sister, of all people.
Put it back quickly. What if we get caught?"
"She's a court musician and only comes home once or twice a year. We won't get
caught anyway. Are you sure you don't want to keep it? Don't blame me if you regret it later."
"Following you is my life's biggest regret! Put it away quickly."
Rian crumpled the underwear and stuffed it back into the drawer.
"Hey, we'll get caught if you do it like that."
"The dedicated maid will organize it when she has time. Anyway, it's no fun since
you're not excited."
"I'll cut ties with you if you do this one more time."
"Hahaha! Alright. It was just a joke. Anyway, let's go to my room."
Rian's room was at the end of the corridor. He said since most rooms were vacant anyway,
he picked the one he found comfortable.
Befitting a passionate swordsman, wooden swords were stacked in his room, and the
bookshelf contained books related to swordsmanship.
However, there weren't many professional books; most were novels about mythical
swordsmen.
After sitting in the room and chatting about various things, the two played a card game
when they got bored.
They both bet the same amount of chips, but the game ended in just 30 minutes. Rian had
lost completely.
As Shirone collected the chips, he asked, "One more round?"
"No way! Damn it. Gambling is supposed to be about luck, but I guess I'm extremely
unlucky."
"You seem to have a habit of moving your cards outward when you get an ace. And the
time you take to bet is different when you have a pair versus when you don't. Just knowing those two things,
anyone could easily beat you."
Rian was dumbfounded, as if he'd been scammed.
"How do you find out such things?"
"Observation is surprisingly easy. You don't see because you're too fixated on
preconceived notions. Let go of the idea that you must find something, and feel the whole picture. Then you
can spot it."
"Hmm, so don't obsess over what you want to find, right? This might help in finding
the schema too, don't you think?"
"Probably? It had a big influence on my understanding of the spirit zone."
Rian repeated Shirone's words countless times. His mind felt ticklish, as if he was close
to understanding something, but he just couldn't grasp the core.
Perhaps from thinking hard, which was unusual for him, his body felt sluggish and
drowsiness overwhelmed him. Stretching widely, Rian lay down and said, "There's nothing to do, so let's
sleep for a bit. I ran a hundred laps this morning too, so I'm dead tired."
"Go ahead and sleep. I'll read a book."
"Mm-hmm."
Rian fell asleep as soon as he put his head down. Shirone chuckled at his carefree friend
and picked up a novel. After reading only difficult history books, time flew by as he immersed himself in
adventure stories.
When Rian didn't wake up even after an hour, Shirone closed the book and tidied up the
room. It seemed best to leave now, as the house would become bustling when the sun set.
As he walked along the corridor, he heard piano music.
Shirone stopped in shock. For someone raised in the mountains, sheet music inspired by a
composer stirred his heart in a way different from magic.
The sound was coming from Rian's sister's room. Moving his feet unknowingly, Shirone saw
Rian's sister for the first time.
A pure face like the dawn dew. A woman with blue hair flowing like waves was playing the
piano.
Ozent Reina, the second daughter of the Ozent family.
The melody Reina played was truly heavenly. Though not the youngest, she was recognized
for her talent and entered the royal palace at the age of 19, so Shirone's assessment wasn't exaggerated.
The harmonies building up. The rush of those harmonies. And then variation. Shirone's
mind followed Reina's fingertips like a marionette caught in the melody.
Only after the performance ended did Shirone realize where he was. Perhaps the emotion
lasted too long. By the time he came to his senses, Reina had already turned around and was waving at him.
"Hello? How was my piano sonata?"
He had read about it in books, but this was his first time hearing it. Shirone couldn't
dare offer an opinion on Reina's performance.
"You can come closer and look. I haven't seen your face before. Are you a new
butler?"
"Yes. I was in charge of the library classification work. It's finished now,
though."
"Ah! I've heard about you. You must be very smart. Not just anyone can do
classification work."
Perhaps because she was an artist, her words and actions weren't authoritative at all;
rather, they were considerate.
For the first time, Shirone envied Rian for having a sister. Then he remembered the
inappropriate crime he had unintentionally committed. His cheeks flushed as her face overlapped with what he
had seen.
"Ugh!"
"Are you feeling unwell? Why does your face look like that?"
"No, no, it's nothing."
Shirone awkwardly entered the room. They say the most perfect lie is one that deceives
even yourself. Shirone tried to change the subject, hypnotizing himself that he hadn't seen anything.
"Is this what they call sheet music?"
"Hmm? Yes, that's right. It's Schraiman's Piano Sonata, 3rd Movement, 'In the
Darkness.' Have you ever played the piano?"
"No, never."
"Really? Sit over here. I'll teach you. It's not a difficult piece."
Shirone took a liking to her kind and affectionate nature. Of course, he was also
bewildered, having heard from Rian that she was a witch with a nasty temper.
Shirone pressed the first chord, paying attention to the fingering as instructed. Then he
moved at intervals to connect to the second chord.
"Wow! You have very quick eyes. For someone playing for the first time, your
sight-reading is really good."
Sight-reading refers to playing a score for the first time. It requires technical talent
rather than sensitivity, and someone with excellent sight-reading can be considered a fast thinker.
Shirone was pleased with Reina's compliment. But he forgot his fascination with Reina and
became engrossed in the piano. It was an insatiable curiosity.
"Excuse me..."
"Yes?"
"Could you play it once more for me?"
At that moment, Reina discovered Shirone's unique temperament. The idea that artists like
talent is a lie fabricated by critics. Rather, artists love passion.
"Alright. Listen carefully."
Reina encored 'In the Darkness.' Even to an amateur like Shirone, it was a much more
delicate performance than the first. This was the power of the schema. Her nervous system, which understood
her own body, was twice as sensitive as an ordinary person's.
After the performance, Reina turned her head with a satisfied face. But Shirone didn't
express any reaction, as if he hadn't heard anything, and was lost in thought.
"May I try?"
"Hmm? Oh, sure."
"Please understand if it's hard to listen to."
"Hehe, of course. Everyone starts that way."
Shirone's hands gently rested on the keyboard. Just as Reina thought his posture was
decent, Shirone's fingers began to tap the keys.
As unfamiliar music played, Reina smiled awkwardly. But as it progressed to the middle
section, she seemed to realize something and leaned in to listen.
'What is this child? What is he trying to do?'
All the notes were off. Completely wrong. But amazingly...
It was perfectly similar.
It couldn't be explained specifically, but that was the fact. Clearly, Shirone lacked the
technical proficiency to play sheet music.
But excluding the technical part, it was as if he had become Reina, imitating everything
about her.
This wasn't a performance for someone else. In this child's mind, there was only the
piano.
'A little more. Just a little closer.'
Shirone was desperately trying to penetrate the music. Maintaining the rhythm made the
development too fast. With his sight-reading skills, he couldn't hit the exact notes.
He compressed his concentration, which fluctuated every second. His state of selflessness
began to be pulled into the spirit zone.
Reina swallowed hard. Pleasant harmonies were occasionally interspersed among the
discordant sounds.
And finally, during the climax that went "du-du-du-du!" the performance matched
the score perfectly for exactly 1.7 seconds.
Reina felt a thrill and held her breath. Shirone maintained that state and entered the
authentic cadence.
After striking the tonic, he raised both hands and forcefully hit the dominant. Then he
pressed the final tonic with the greatest passion of his life.
Kuuuuuuung.
The harmonies that had continued from four minutes in the past dissipated into seven
notes, spreading infinite reverberations.
