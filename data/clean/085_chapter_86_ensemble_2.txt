Chapter 86:
Ensemble (2)
"Brr. It's freezing. What's going on? Why is Ms. Siena casting magic?"
"Maybe she's regained her senses? She might have awakened after falling off the cliff."
"Doesn't seem like it. What kind of magic is this? It's unlike her. At this rate, we'll
all freeze to death."
Nade was equally puzzled. Hundreds of students were here. It was unlikely they could
survive sub-zero temperatures for long without memories. But the blizzard's pressure on Lucas was
undeniable. Cooling cells would weaken his Mitochondria Build.
"Damn it! This is insane!"
Lucas, battered by the blizzard's winds, felt his extremities necrotizing. Mages often
started battles with blizzards—not for lethality but for the constant chill, a staple against swordsmen.
"Ugh. Mages are the worst."
Lucas hated their tricks. But giving up wasn't an option. And Siena's state was clearly
abnormal.
An ice spear pierced the blizzard. Lucas dodged but, weakened, took a graze to his side.
"Damn it!"
Most creatures slowed in the cold. Pushing through, his fingertips showed frostbite.
'It's getting colder. Does she really plan to kill us all?'
A Schema user getting frostbite meant students should've frozen already. Yet only Lucas
was trapped in the blizzard, which now homed in on him.
"You're insane! How long will you keep this up——?"
Lucas froze. Siena's eyes were still empty—no emotion, no thought, not even
self-awareness.
"Don't tell me……."
His shoulders trembled. Her body remembered the magic.
Literally, muscle memory.
Just as swordsmen drilled techniques through repetition, Siena had engraved spell
patterns into her brainwaves, even without memories.
Endless repetition. She must've driven herself to the brink of death, over and over.
'How much has she trained?'
His hair stood on end. Beyond skill, her path of suffering was unimaginable.
'Damn it! This is worse than stepping in shit.'
He had to retreat. No bounty was worth his life.
Lucas fled with all his might. If he escaped the blizzard, she wouldn't chase. But his
hope was delusional. Turning his back on a 6th-class mage came at a price.
"Gyaaaaah!"
Siena pointed, and Lucas screamed as he was buried in snow. No pain, but his legs were
gone—frozen solid, utterly numb.
'No way…….'
Absolute Zero.
A supreme ice spell halting particle motion through preservation and condensation.
Siena had mastered it by 20, earning her 6th-class rank.
But even she could only achieve localized effects due to the immense mental strain.
'Damn it! This "mediocre school" is full of monsters!'
Siena emotionlessly cast her next spell—Glacier Bombing, a high-tier ice spell derived
from Absolute Zero. Dozens of massive ice blocks formed in the sky, draining the color from Lucas's face.
With no legs, escape was impossible. His fate was in the heavens' hands.
Nade and Iruki rushed to the students. Though not area-wide, the ice blocks were massive
enough to be deadly. As they collided, one almond-shaped glacier began falling toward the students.
Nade grabbed Arin, tackling her to the ground.
"Eek! What are you doing?!"
"Evacuate the students! Or they'll all die!"
"No! There's no time!"
Iruki stepped forward, shouting.
"I'll handle it!"
"How?"
Iruki clenched his teeth. Was it possible? The glacier weighed dozens of tons. Even if he
shattered it, fragments would rain down. It needed to split into over 1,000 pieces to avoid fatalities.
'No choice…….'
Momentum could be calculated mathematically. If he split, split, and split again, he
could destroy it before impact.
"Here I gooooo!"
His detachable Spirit Zone pierced the glacier's core. An atomic bomb triggered,
splitting it into seven chunks.
Iruki activated Double Spirit Zone, madly firing atomic bombs to shatter the ice.
Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!
Ice flowers bloomed. Fragments scattered like fireworks.
Nade and Arin stared, dumbfounded. Hail-like shards fell, but no one was hurt.
Arin's eyes wavered. Arcane had always belittled magic academy students—weak, spoon-fed,
lacking will. But their strength far exceeded her expectations.
'So this is the magic academy… Huh?'
Arin realized she was pressed against a stranger's chest. Shoving Nade away, she yelled.
"Get off! What are you doing?!"
Nade, sprawled on his rear, flushed red.
"W-what! After I helped you!"
"Why help me? We're enemies!"
"Says who? Hurry and lift the mind control! Do you really want to kill them all? Are you
sane?"
Arin bit her lip. She'd become a mass murderer in history books, but she felt no fear.
Without meeting Canis in Radum, she'd have fallen into despair anyway.
"I don't care. For Canis, even if the whole world dies."
Nade saw the emptiness and bitterness in her eyes. What kind of life had she lived to
harbor such a gaze?
"Hup."
Iruki carried Siena on his back. Lucas, half-buried, had lost his legs and his jaw hung
slack. His thieving days were over—back to Inferno's gloomy prison in seven days.
Boom!
A deafening roar drew their attention. A massive beam shot through the forest.
"Whoa. What's that?"
"Photon Cannon. Did he have an epiphany?"
The forest shook as Shirone was flung out, battered.
Arin brightened—Canis must've gained the upper hand.
But her hopes were dashed.
Harvest knocked down trees, revealing Canis limping. His state was worse than Shirone's.
Iruki muttered in disbelief.
