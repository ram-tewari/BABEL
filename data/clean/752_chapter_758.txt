Chapter 758:
The Underworld that Shirone saw seemed to be filled entirely with the
things humans most despised.
After following a trail of intestines and passing through fields of
villi, a grotesque statue appeared—one commonly seen in religious imagery.
"This is unexpected…"
Tracking Baseto through the Underworld had gone well at first, but at
some point, the scenery of the real world completely disappeared.
"Miro…"
Zion's Law, designed to compress the spiritual barrier, was putting
pressure on the entire world.
What had once been a thin, easily torn boundary had transformed into an
iron wall.
"I support you."
If it was for the sake of humanity, it had to be endured. In fact,
Shirone was in awe of her mastery.
"I can't follow him any further."
Having lost track of Baseto, Shirone decided to return to the real world
as quickly as possible.
"Something's wrong. Seriously wrong."
By the time he had arrived here, Shirone had already counted over a
thousand demons.
"There's no way out of the Underworld from here. Most forces should be
stationed inside the spiritual barrier…"
In every nation, the area surrounding the spiritual barrier was a fierce
battlefield. This was highly unusual.
"The spiritual barrier is the frontline between demons and humans.
Unless one side completely gives up, this is impossible…"
Or perhaps—both sides had given up.
Grrrgle…
Water trickled along the intestines, and the outer wall where Shirone
was began to tremble violently.
"Damn it…!"
Wind rushed in from the path he had taken, followed by the stench of
decay.
SKREEEE!
With a repulsive noise, a flood of putrid sludge burst in. Shirone
quickly took to the air.
"Urgh!"
"Save me! Save me!"
Skeletons swept up in the current reached out with bony arms as they
were carried by the flow.
Compassion.
"Take my hand!"
But the moment he grabbed one, the shoulder detached, and the arm,
separated from the torso, turned to black ash.
Spiritual cleansing.
Even though he'd realized through sealing various shrines that it was
futile, Shirone still couldn't ignore the hands reaching for help.
"But I've never seen a case this severe…"
The minds of the living, when they died, seeped into the Underworld and
were recycled as energy for demons.
"Sexual orgies of witches, demonic cult gatherings…"
Collective consciousness born in such places was powerful and
stimulating enough to numb the brain.
But sometimes, an individual's will became just as powerful.
When?
At the moment of death.
"They're already dead."
Few could overcome the fear of death unless they were devout believers
in divine salvation or ascetic seekers who had embraced the void of life.
That overwhelming fear, when it exploded, would suck the consciousness
into the Underworld, casting it into Hell.
"I'm sorry."
Thinking of the fate of those who would fall into the "frying pan" and
be melted in hellfire, Shirone couldn't help but think of Nane.
"Maybe you were right."
Fighting off the rising tears, Shirone shook his head to stay
focused.
"I can't break down."
Yahweh's domain loved all that existed, but the residents of the
Underworld had no true form.
"They're just a hell we created."
True salvation wasn't in sealing the world shut, but in all of humanity
practicing love to liberate these souls.
After completing the most dreadful soul-cleansing he had ever
experienced, Shirone returned to the ground.
The grotesque statue's female face had changed; it was now wide open in
a ghoulish grin.
"…"
Its mouth was stuffed with the remnants of souls.
Then the statue's eyes locked on Shirone and began chewing the remains
with a hideous laugh.
"Kyahahahaha! Kyahahahaha…!"
Bang!
A photon cannon struck the statue's face, which cracked and tumbled to
the floor.
"Haaaaa…"
Who am I fighting for?
Sometimes—no, often—when he looked upon the world humans had carelessly
ruined, Shirone struggled to contain his rage.
"How dare you damage the sacred statue of purification!"
The vile demons, hearing the noise, came swarming into the rotting
intestines.
"Answer me."
O human heart.
"How can you come to understand love?"
This question had been posed before, by seekers who had come even into
Hell's depths, so the demons took time to respond.
"There is one way."
A demon with bull-like horns stepped forward and spread his arms
wide.
"Submit to me. Worship me. And I will love you."
Because only I matter.
"Puhaha! Brilliant answer! The correct answer! Seeker of the Way, why do
you anguish over something so simple?"
All the demons spread their arms.
"Worship me!"
Shirone tightly closed his eyes, and when his eyelids—heavier than the
world—opened once again…
"Kill him! The flesh of a seeker tastes divine!"
Yahweh's light burst forth in a storm.
"Hahaha! It drives you mad, doesn't it? Expecting compassion from
humans! You're the foolish one! Your light is strong, it burns us, but…"
Even amidst the cacophony of demonic voices in the heart of Hell,
Shirone did not cease the light.
"I'm not afraid! So I won't yield! Even to the brink of complete
annihilation—we will fight!"
Incinerating all the demons of that region with Yahweh's light was the
only way to seal the shrine.
"So wretched! So foolish, foolish—!"
Yahweh.
In the space where screams and roars had once filled the air, a void as
vast as Yahweh's radiance opened.
"Nothingness."
It was neither dark nor transparent.
It was simply absence—so alien to human thought that only Shirone could
endure it.
Others couldn't bear the emptiness.
"Let there be light."
Shirone had formed the habit of filling the sealed shrine's space with
Material afterward.
"Still far from perfect…"
Just by recreating the statue of the archangel from his primal
instincts, in a pure white space, his heart was eased.
"Ikael…"
He recalled the vision from Hexa's memory, of her weeping with her hand
clutched to her chest.
"It must have been such a tragic sight…"
So why was it that whenever he thought of this memory, he felt a sense
of peace washing away his inner rage?
"Laktas Vera Imera! (Submit to fear!)"
Startled by a familiar voice, Shirone looked beyond the edge of the
Material domain—into the landscape of Hell.
"Igor."
The Lord of Terror, Igor, approached with his spear of blue flame
trailing behind him, mounted on a black horse with no eyes.
"This isn't your territory, is it?"
By coordinates in the real world, he should've been in Radum.
"I've come to retrieve the Mark of Submission."
Igor halted before the pure-white boundary newly filled with
Material.
It had nothing to do with him—but even so, emotionally, he must've found
it revolting to step past it.
"Hand it over. I fight for Satan."
The necklace in Shirone's possession began to glow with blue starlight
and floated toward Igor.
"So that's how you found me."
Just before it reached his hand, the starlight stopped abruptly,
refusing to go any further.
"…You're resisting?"
Shirone was still the necklace's master.
"What happens if I refuse?"
"Satan will erase me. But even if that happens, another terror will
simply emerge under a new name."
The more frequently humans experience an emotion, the faster it revives
in the Underworld.
"A new name, huh…"
The name of North Aymond's president was also Igor.
"Is this related to him?"
"Not particularly. There are many Igors in the human world. And some of
them wield fear as their weapon."
The Lord of Terror, Igor, was born when that emotion fused with souls
melted in the fires of Hell.
"He came first. I came after. Don't confuse us."
If his emotion was powerful enough to become a commander in Hell, then
one could guess what kind of man the human Igor was.
"Tell me what's happening where I am."
Igor stayed silent.
"That's the condition for releasing your vow of submission."
Since he still wanted to fight for Satan, Igor gave a heavy nod.
"The one who gave me my individuality—Igor—no longer exists in this
world."
"You mean he died?"
"He was absorbed. Currently, half of the army stationed in your kingdom
isn't human—they're demons."
"Demons?"
Shapeshifting means altering not just form but also function—something
even demons struggle with.
If it were just illusion magic, Shirone would have noticed. As someone
who had reached Yahweh's domain, it was impossible for him not to.
"How can that be…?"
Then it hit him.
"Garas?"
"Yes. A hybrid of demons and Garas has taken over the military. Garas…
they're quite the species. Ever since they crossed over into the kingdom, a tremendous energy has been
pouring into the Underworld. The demons born from that energy overthrew the existing commanders and took
command. Even you might not win against them."
Shirone had never faced a foe he was sure he'd defeat, so his heart only
beat a little faster.
"You already know demons are born from the energy of the 'frying pan.'
The reason each is different is because they inherit specific traits from Satan. Which means…"
"Satan is the embodiment of all evil."
Demons call themselves evil, but just as humans find beauty in good,
they believe evil is truth.
"How far has the infiltration spread in North Aymond?"
"All the way. The kingdom you're in has already been taken over by
demons. It was a choice made by the human Igor himself."
"By his own will?"
"He truly wished to become fear itself. So he willingly gave his body to
something far greater than himself. As such, the one ruling that country now is, in reality, a demon—one
of the few capable of transforming into human form."
"…A Legion Commander."
If shapeshifting was something only the top-ranked dragons could
achieve, then that spoke volumes about the commander's power.
"Satan's 72 Legions. The 24th Commander—Maraduke."
Maraduke, the Demon of Destruction.
"We are all parts of Satan. The commanders inherited the most powerful
of his aspects. Think of it like this—if someone like me was forced into submission, even Yahweh's light
may falter."
As Shirone sank into thought, Igor spoke again.
"That's all I can tell you. If you want to know more, you'll have to
destroy me."
"…Then I'll return your necklace."
At last, the blue starlight sank into Igor's chest.
"I wouldn't have minded if you'd just erased me."
"I know. But…"
Even the possibility of sharing something—anything—with a demon meant
too much to Shirone.
"You helped me."
Igor turned his black horse.
"It won't happen again."
As Shirone watched Igor retreat into the infernal landscape, the demon
suddenly reined in his horse.
Was this really the right thing to do?
Even if only briefly, the emotions Shirone had shared through the
necklace might have left an impression.
"It's not impossible."
Igor's voice was like a mutter to himself…
"For a demon to repent."
But to Shirone, it struck like thunder.
"Heh. The problem is, it's almost impossible."
Drawing a clear line, Igor vanished without looking back.
Shirone stood frozen for a long time.
"There was one."
His fists clenched tight.
"I can keep fighting. I wasn't wrong!"
As if his chest were on fire, he couldn't breathe—hot tears spilled down
his cheeks.
"I'll never give up. No matter how evil humanity may be…"
That didn't mean all humans were evil.
