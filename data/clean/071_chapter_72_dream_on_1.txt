Chapter 72:
Dream On (1)
The Photon Cannon.
The mass contained within the photons was minuscule, but the rapid rate of photon output
created an impact force no ordinary monster could withstand.
The eyes of the Wulke chieftain burned with fury. Having fought countless battles against
humans, he knew exactly how dangerous a mage could be.
"That's it! They're scared now! Shirone did it!"
Martin shouted. But Altol's thoughts were different. What they were facing wasn't just a
beast—it was a monster. Even if they had reason, their savagery was nothing to scoff at.
"This isn't over. No matter how strong Shirone is, he's still just human. Beasts that
hunt in packs don't retreat just because of one or two casualties."
Just as Altol had predicted, the Wulke chieftain had no intention of backing down. While
the mage's power was destructive, the pack's violence wasn't intimidated by a single individual.
"Grrrr, kill… him…"
"Rooooar!"
The Wulke's monstrous roar shook the heavens and earth.
'I can do this. If I just keep my focus, I can win.'
Shirone teleported into the air and bombarded the ground with his Photon Cannon. Flashes
of light rained down like hail. Even those not directly hit by the Photon Cannon were struck by the
shockwaves erupting from the ground.
Yet, the Wulke's ferocity didn't waver in the slightest. When gathered in numbers, they
were a warlike race that even the ogres—known as the kings of the forest—would yield to.
Ignoring the casualties among their own, the Wulke leaped at Shirone, slashing with their
claws. Shirone hastily landed on the ground, only to find seven more waiting for him.
Shirone transformed his Spirit Zone into an omnidirectional offensive form. The moment
the Spirit Zone's thorns touched the Wulke, a barrage of Photon Cannons fired. A blinding flash of light
erupted, sending the Wulke struck by the photons flying back.
"Whoa, that's insane… Shirone."
The children stared in awe. A human fighting dozens of Wulke on equal footing? They had
always thought tales of heroes were just exaggerated rumors. But watching Shirone fight made even the
wildest stories seem believable.
"Haa, haa."
Shirone's breathing grew ragged. Firing the Photon Cannon in rapid succession made him
feel like his consciousness might slip away. Even with the mental fortitude to sustain photon output for
over ten minutes, it was nearly unbearable. The Photon Cannon was fusion magic, combining light and mass.
A mage only had one slot for a spell. It was the same principle as how humans couldn't
focus on two things at once. Someone like Iruki, who had the Servant Syndrome, could dual-cast, but barring
such special cases, even the highest-ranking mages could only use one spell at a time.
Thus, mages who specialized in pure, single-attribute magic were rare in the professional
world. Most combined two, or even three, attributes to maximize the effect of a single spell.
Examples included Siena's Ice Tornado, which combined cold and wind, or Sad's Fire Sun,
which fused light and flame.
Fusion magic could maximize efficiency in an instant, but it required immense mental
focus. Having cast such spells repeatedly, it was no surprise Shirone felt exhausted.
'This is bad. I don't think I have many shots left. I doubt I can take them all down.'
Wulke were nothing like street thugs. A beast's keen sense of smell could detect even the
slightest hint of fatigue in its prey. If he showed any sign of weakness, they'd tear him apart in an
instant.
True to form, the Wulke chieftain still bared his fangs despite losing a third of his
pack.
The Wulke's second wave charged, sensing Shirone's exhaustion. Their numbers were similar
to the first wave, but the feeling was entirely different compared to when Shirone was at full strength.
In desperation, Shirone fired his Photon Cannon. A mass of photons buried itself in the
belly of the lead Wulke.
"Rooooar!"
The massive body was sent skidding across the ground, but unlike before, it didn't fall.
It dug its legs into the earth and held its ground. Shirone's heart sank. Had his power dropped this much
already?
Emboldened, the Wulke chieftain ordered a full assault.
"Roar! Roar! Roar!"
The Wulke, sensing victory, surged forward.
'In that case…'
The only thing left for Shirone was the Immortal Function.
But could he endure it?
This was on a completely different level from when he had used photon output before.
Casting fusion magic—which consumed vast amounts of mental energy—in an infinite domain was uncharted
territory. There was no telling what backlash might follow.
"Grrrr!"
The moment he activated the Immortal Function, pressure built in Shirone's mind. It felt
like his head might explode.
"Aaaah!"
Shirone steadied himself and opened his mind. As his sense of self loosened, an
overwhelming power rushed in.
The moment his Spirit Zone filled with light, it exploded violently. A mass wave sent the
Wulke flying, scattering golden fragments everywhere.
Shirone, holding a sphere of light in his palm, unleashed it forward with all his might.
The Photon Cannon shot out as a white flash, devastating the area. Firing at full power without restraint,
the Wulke were swept away like dust under a broom.
But he couldn't maintain this state forever. If infinity was an ocean, Shirone's mind was
a stream. The Immortal Function was the dam between them. He was drilling a hole to draw seawater, but if
this continued, the dam would collapse.
Different spells required different amounts of mental energy, so mages chose the most
cost-effective spell for the situation.
Spells whose effects far outweighed their cost were called Overpower in magical society.
Notable examples included the time magic Stop, the invisibility magic Invisibility, the alchemy magic
Injection, and the healing magic Heal of the Almighty.
So, what about the Photon Cannon?
Shirone considered it close to Overpower.
Its exceptional attack speed, versatility between single shots and rapid fire, and even
the ability to form beams—it had everything an offensive spell needed.
But it was only efficient relative to its cost. The Photon Cannon was inherently
expensive. Firing indiscriminately like this could lead to catastrophic consequences.
'No more! I'll die if this continues!'
Shirone stopped casting the Photon Cannon. The barrage had lasted only about three
seconds, but dust filled the air, obscuring everything.
When the wind swept the dust away, the children trembled at the sight before them. The
area swept by the Photon Cannon was utterly devastated. The Wulke were embedded in the ground as if chewed
up, and even those who fled were too terrified to mourn their fallen.
Shirone realized the true power of the Photon Cannon. Nothing remained where the light
had passed. If even the caster was awed, how much worse must it have been for the targets?
The Wulke chieftain, his face twisted in fear, had no choice but to accept reality.
That mage was unbeatable.
"Grrr, we… leave… Let us… go…"
Shirone was completely exhausted, incapable of casting any more spells. But he forced
himself to stand firm.
"Don't ever come back here. If you set foot on this mountain again, it won't end like
this."
The Wulke chieftain conveyed his submission in clumsy human speech.
"Not… come. Strong… mage."
"Go."
Shirone waved his hand, and the Wulke chieftain left first. The others followed without
hesitation, disappearing into the forest.
Only after the Wulke were gone did Shirone's tension ease. Staggering, he closed his eyes
and collapsed.
"Shirone! Shirone!"
"Shh! Quiet. They might come back."
Altol's warning made the children cover their mouths. He then checked on Shirone. The
sight of him sleeping soundly made Altol chuckle.
The children of the slash-and-burn village had grown over time, but Shirone seemed
unchanged, still the same as in his youth.
'They say mages don't age.'
At first, he'd thought Shirone was just some mountain hunter's kid—an adopted one at
that. Shirone looked nothing like Uncle Vincent, which made him all the more easy to underestimate.
But he'd been wrong. Perhaps Shirone was like the ugly duckling from fairy tales.
A human fundamentally different from a mountain hunter.
Altol lifted Shirone effortlessly. Despite his strength, Shirone felt light as a feather,
but there was no pride in it.
He now knew that no matter how light Shirone's body was, the weight of the intellect
within it was something he could never lift.
"Let's go back. The hunt is over."

"Gah!"
Shirone woke with a start, bolting upright.
He was in someone's house.
Placing a hand on the sofa's armrest, he looked around and saw a cozy living room.
Warmth emanated from a fire in the hearth, and the sound of cooking came from the
L-shaped kitchen.
"Huh? Shirone's awake. Rumina, Shirone's up!"
Martin's shout was followed by a clatter from the kitchen. Soon, Rumina peeked out from
behind the wall, her face flushed.
"Where…?"
Then it came back to him. This was Rumina's house, where he'd often stayed with his
father as a child.
The children rushed downstairs, still dressed in their hunting gear, as if they'd stayed
by Shirone's side instead of going home.
"Shirone, are you okay?"
Altol asked worriedly. He'd checked Shirone's condition as soon as they arrived and found
nothing wrong. But his ignorance of magic left room for concern.
Shirone ran a few mental tests to ensure his mind was intact. His memories were fine, and
his consciousness seemed clear.
"I'm fine. I must've passed out from exhaustion."
"Whew, that's a relief. We thought something was seriously wrong."
Shirone felt guilty for worrying them, though the children felt otherwise.
"I feel refreshed after a long sleep. The fatigue's finally gone."
Despite long rests at the dorm, his exhaustion had never truly lifted. But now, he felt
great. The persistent sense of dread was gone.
He'd been confident he could overcome it. But now that it was gone, he realized just how
hard he'd been pushing himself.
'Mr. Armin was right. As soon as I mastered the Photon Cannon, my senses returned. No
nightmares, either.'
His stomach growled. It had been a while since he'd felt this hungry. The savory aroma
from the kitchen only made it worse.
Shirone licked his lips and asked Rumina, "What's cooking?"
"Pork stew. Mom bought some meat."
Rumina's mother emerged from the kitchen, ladle in hand.
"Shirone, hello! Long time no see."
"Hello, ma'am. Sorry for the trouble."
"Oh, don't say that. My daughter's been waiting for you."
Rumina's face turned bright red. But then her expression suddenly saddened, and she bowed
her head.
Altol understood. He felt the same, and the other children likely did too.
Shirone couldn't be their leader. He wasn't one of the slash-and-burn village kids, nor
was he the mountain hunter's son.
Shirone was a mage.
As the mood soured, Altol boomed, "Alright, alright! I'm starving. Let's eat first."
"Yay! Meat after so long! Meat! Meat! Meat!"
Rumina's mother placed a large pot on the table. The pork stew sloshed, filled to the
brim.
Rumina's family only had four members, but it was common in the slash-and-burn village
for multiple households to eat together, so there were plenty of utensils.
