Chapter 81:
The Mage's Battle (2)
Neide and Iruki teleported in front of the Uncrossable Bridge's cliff. Their eyes burned
with fury after witnessing Shina's humiliation.
"How dare you lay a hand on our teacher…!"
"Oh? There were some sane ones left? Typical mages…"
Lucas was equally annoyed. A so-called grand mage had made the mistake of missing his
targets. Moreover, the ones still sane were now his responsibility to deal with.
"Was that woman your teacher? My bad. She's probably almost reached the bottom by now.
The ground, I mean. Heh."
The moment Lucas finished speaking, Shirone, holding Shina in his arms, teleported up
from below the cliff. His gaze was no different from his friends'. How depraved did one have to be to throw
someone off a cliff?
"I won't forgive you."
Lucas didn't even bother listening. The situation escalating was just annoying.
"Hey. What's the plan? Is this part of the operation?"
Lucas made the responsibility clear. It might come up during the final payment. Canis
didn't evade it either. As the leader in Arcane's absence, it was his duty.
"Arin, stay back."
"But Canis…"
"You don't need to intervene. Focus on controlling the people. I'll handle this."
Maintaining mental control over hundreds while fighting was impossible. As Arin hid among
the crowd, Canis approached Shirone's group, unburdened.
"Who are you? How did you break free from the mental control?"
Iruki spoke.
"Dark magic is outdated. There are plenty of counters."
"Nonsense. If it were that easy, the ones behind us wouldn't be affected."
'True…'
Canis looked up at the sun. At the peak, the light was still strong. But as a grand
mage's disciple, there was no need to negotiate with students.
Lucas, arms crossed, shouted.
"Hey, what's the plan? This wasn't in the contract. I'll handle it for double the pay."
"I don't need help. I'll do it alone."
"Suit yourself."
Lucas clicked his tongue and stepped back, though his true intent was the opposite. He'd
pretend to stay out of it, then strike from behind once the fight started.
"Come at me. I'm busy."
Shirone and Canis closed the distance. Shirone sensed Canis's Spirit Zone—a 50-meter
sphere, similar to his own.
"I'll handle this. You stop that swordsman."
Iruki glanced at Lucas.
"That guy? He doesn't seem like he'll interfere."
"You never know. He's vicious enough to throw Shina off a cliff."
Shirone's cold gaze flicked back.
"Don't believe a word he says."
