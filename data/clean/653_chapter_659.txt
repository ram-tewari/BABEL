Chapter 659:
A massive blaze burst out from the Silver Ring Guild's main building,
causing pedestrians to throw themselves flat on the ground.
"Damn it! What's going on?"
People struck by flying brick fragments winced and scrambled to
safety.
Had the explosion occurred anywhere else, it might have caused chaos,
but since it happened in the guild district, the disturbance wasn't as extreme as expected.
Conflicts between factions sometimes escalated to violence, so people
simply evacuated quickly, with no intention of intervening.
Inside the guild, however, it was a disaster.
The smell of burning wood and stone filled the air as guild members
slowly emerged from under tables to assess the aftermath.
"What the…!"
Hellfire's immense force had blasted all objects within a ten-meter
radius outward, and Rian had already thrown himself into a corner.
Only Shirone remained standing where the inferno had passed.
No one had seen the exact moment of the explosion, so it was unclear
whether he had dodged or blocked it.
'Did he use the principle of light?'
Ackman recalled the information he had reviewed about Shirone in
preparation for their meeting.
No one knew exactly how it worked, but there were rumors that he had
deceived even the 2000's system during the graduation exam by manipulating time.
'A divine gift, perhaps?'
Even as an unofficial fourth-rank mage who feared no one in combat,
Ackman still held resentment from a lifetime of being compared to officially certified prodigies.
"That was dangerous. Was this really necessary?"
Judging by the power of Hellfire, it had clearly been cast with lethal
intent.
"That's such a rookie way to think. This isn't a school. Every
guild test is the real deal."
"And who gave you the right to test me?"
Ackman no longer hid his true feelings.
"Because I can't accept it. I doubt the other guild members can
either."
He pulled a red crystal orb containing fire from his robes.
"You're not giving us a certification or accepting the position of
master, so prove your skill. Do that, and I'll give you what you want."
Shirone's gaze grew serious.
Ackman's offensive Spirit Zone pierced forward like dozens of spears,
and Shirone responded by casting teleportation.
Teleporting through the complex interior of a building was highly
dangerous, but for mages of their caliber, the risk was negligible.
'Impressive.'
Shirone's movements were clearly exceptional, but Ackman's experience
wasn't to be taken lightly.
"Let's get serious."
Lifting both feet, Ackman backed away as if luring Shirone, and the back
door burst open.
Shirone arrived in the training yard behind the building, where Ackman
stood ready.
"The Flame Robe and the Fire Prison."
Ackman held out the crystal orb as he explained his magical
artifacts.
"The Flame Robe boosts fire-based mana by two hundred percent. And
this crystal orb..."
Flames whirled around him, forming a four-meter-long serpent made of
1,000°C fire. The flame flicked its tongue as it loomed over Shirone.
"It traps the spirit of fire in physical form."
Ackman's Fire Snake rushed at Shirone, its body expanding
dramatically.
Now four times larger, the flames engulfed Shirone, while second and
third waves of fire coiled and closed in on him.
As twelve flame serpents pursued Shirone through the orb's power, all
the guild members arrived in the courtyard to witness the battle.
The spell itself wasn't complex, but the firepower drawn from an
unofficial fourth-rank mage's mental strength was tremendous. Even with magical equipment, controlling
twelve flame entities so freely was impressive.
'This is the strength of a fourth-rank mage.'
In terms of stats alone, he surpassed even Plu's similar fighting
style.
And from an experience standpoint, Ackman clearly outmatched
Shirone.
'The technique is simple, but fast and precise.'
With enough firepower and speed, there was no spell more effective at
incinerating a target.
As Shirone began to fall behind, the guild members let out cheers as if
they had been waiting for this.
"That's our Master! The Ivory Tower candidate's all talk and
struggling now!"
"Defeating a bunch of bandits? Our Master could do that
too!"
Like Ackman, the guild members also seemed to harbor some inferiority,
but Rian stayed silent, recognizing there wasn't much wrong with what they were saying.
'He's a mage who relies heavily on a single technique. If he can't find
a workaround, he won't be able to handle even bandit-level threats.'
Kon shouted toward Rian like he had already won.
"Hey! Maybe you should stop this! Your buddy's about to end up
roasted!"
Rian snorted.
He acknowledged Ackman's skill, but considering what Shirone had shown
during the graduation exam, something didn't add up.
"This really is different from school. It won't be easy,
Shirone."
Kon, misunderstanding the tone, burst into laughter.
"Only now realizing that? Even now—"
"It's not the 2000's system."
"Huh?"
Rian turned to Kon.
"He's trying to find a way to win without killing."
To a bystander, it might have sounded absurd, but Ackman could feel it
in his bones.
'Why hasn't he attacked yet?'
More than five minutes had passed, and Shirone had yet to cast a single
offensive spell.
'Damn it, this is tricky.'
Shirone had considered many strategies, but none of his spells could
avoid inflicting fatal damage.
And his support magic like Shining Impact or Shining Chain wouldn't be
enough to subdue someone like Ackman.
'He's not this worn out already.'
If stalling wasn't helping, then brute force was the only option
left.
'Time to decide.'
Ackman didn't show it outwardly, but his instincts sensed that Shirone
was shifting gears.
'Is this it? Come on, show me.'
Just before the twelve flame serpents completely sealed off his escape,
Shirone burst through the fire.
'That again.'
Time Explosion extended one second from the past and future into the
present, but it couldn't handle anything faster than one second ahead or behind, making it less than a
perfect evasion tool.
'I have to end this.'
Wiping away all stray thoughts, Shirone leapt into the air and cast
Photon Cannon.
The dent left where Ackman dodged was proof enough of its destructive
power.
'Still, if it doesn't land, it's meaningless.'
Controlling the Fire Snakes while expertly dodging attacks, Ackman
stayed calm.
This strategy had brought him many victories—single strikes wouldn't
work.
"In that case..."
Shirone gritted his teeth and fired Photon Cannons in rapid
succession.
'Can you dodge this too?'
Beams of light streaked toward Ackman like lightning. He dodged fluidly,
circling the yard with ease.
Meanwhile, Fire Strikes rained from above, relentlessly pressing
Shirone.
'So this is all he's got?'
Realizing Shirone wasn't so different from other mages he'd faced,
Ackman's pride returned.
'No, he has potential. But still...'
The Ivory Tower was nothing but a hollow title.
'I'm the strong one!'
Ackman's eyes blazed as all twelve flame serpents rushed Shirone at
once.
Quantum Superposition—300 layers.
The number of flashes pouring down increased beyond what the Spirit
Zone's synesthesia could even count.
"What the…!"
A thunderous roar shook the ground, making people squeeze their eyes
shut. The vibrations, like an earthquake, rippled through the earth.
Only Rian watched through one squinting eye, his gaze locked on the
blazing rays of light.
'So the answer was overwhelming firepower after all.'
Though the output had clearly been restrained, the sheer number of
Photon Cannons fired by 300 versions of Shirone was terrifying to behold.
"M-Master…"
Dust kicked up by the Photon Cannons blanketed the field and engulfed
Ackman's figure, clouding him from view.
'Can he dodge this too?'
Shirone was unleashing his spells without restraint, but Elysion had
pinpointed Ackman's location with precision.
A single physical hit would be fatal. Not one of the Photon Cannons
touched Ackman's body.
'Then I'll destroy his mind.'
The barrage, which seemed like it couldn't possibly get any faster, grew
even more intense, drowning out even the screams of the guild members.
The moment the Fire Snakes began to decrease in number was when those
watching, helpless from a distance, first realized a change was happening.
Seven left. Then six.
The fire serpents that had been writhing through the air as if blind
began disappearing one by one, signaling that Ackman's mind was succumbing to fear.
"Gahhhhh!"
Within the blinding dust cloud, Ackman could do nothing but curl up and
endure.
The deafening booms nearby felt like they would rupture his eardrums.
The vibrations through the ground crawled up his bones.
His nerves were in disarray. He couldn't tell whether he was buried in
the ground or if his body had already been destroyed.
He felt like a bug trapped in a jar, being violently shaken by a
lunatic.
"They're vanishing."
Rian counted the remaining Fire Snakes.
Three. Two. And finally, the last one flickered and faded.
His Spirit Zone had collapsed under the mental pressure.
Though Shirone had stopped the bombardment, the guild members didn't
realize it until much later, still reeling from the ringing in their ears.
A massive cloud of dust was swept away by the wind, falling like blood
and drifting over nearby buildings like fog.
"What… is this…?"
Everyone trembled at the sight.
In the center of the yard was a crater so deep its bottom couldn't be
seen. On a small remaining patch of ground, like the top of a lone pillar, crouched Ackman.
Twenty meters away, Shirone landed and steadied his breathing.
'I'm pretty exhausted.'
If it had been Angel's Punishment, this destruction would've happened in
an instant. But this time, he'd had to fight while imposing limits on himself.
"Master! Are you alright?!"
Manager Michella shouted, but Ackman, his hearing still paralyzed,
didn't move.
Still, being an unofficial fourth-rank mage, his mental recovery was
fast. Once the tremors stopped, he slowly raised his head.
His eyes, dazed as he stared at the surreal landscape, gradually filled
with melancholy.
'So this is what it means to be a star…'
It wasn't about proving superiority on a human scale.
'There's simply no comparison.'
It wasn't a matter of who was faster or stronger—it was like comparing
someone with three arms, or five eyes, or wings.
"I lost."
The one suited to be a candidate of the Ivory Tower was not himself, but
Shirone.
"You said you had something you wanted from the guild. I'll cooperate,
no conditions."
Still wanting to save face, Ackman cast Fly despite his trembling legs
and floated over the crater.
"There's only one thing I want from the guild."
Shirone was about to explain, since he hadn't even begun acting on
Lupist's orders yet, when applause rang out from the direction of the building.
"Heeheehee! That was a spectacular fight. It feels like a decade of
frustration just melted away."
The expressions of the guild members instantly soured as they turned
toward the back entrance.
"What the hell are those smug bastards doing here?"
The masters of War Chariot and Blood Rose—two of the kingdom's top three
magic guilds—stood there, sneering.
