Chapter 388:
One Man Team (4)
Amy pointed her finger at Screamer.
"Who else would I be talking to? Honestly, the martial artist's
killer strategy failed, didn't it? I could understand up to that point, but why take it out on an
innocent team member?"
Screamer's face went blank.
No matter how emotional he was, he usually considered the
context.
In contrast, Shirone's group was pushing forward recklessly without any
plan.
'Are they all crazy?'
If you let emotions take over, you'll only be used.
And Screamer immediately found a way.
This was an opportunity.
If he could easily gain 20 points at the cost of losing 5, it wasn't a
bad deal.
'Any team with Maya will lose.'
Having made his decision, Screamer made a proposal.
"Fine, if you're so confident, how about we test it?"
"Test it?"
"You know there's an overall evaluation in two weeks, right? Hill
Capture. If you can beat me with Maya on your team, I'll acknowledge it."
Hill Capture involves placing flags in both camps and balancing offense
and defense to capture the opponent's flag.
In the graduation exam, it's 15 vs. 15, but in the group evaluation,
it's a 5 vs. 5 mini-game.
'As expected of the senior class. They really know how to dig
in.'
Shirone saw through Screamer's strategy.
He planned to include Maya, whom he considered a weak link, in their
team to secure 20 points.
As Shirone hesitated to answer, Screamer grinned. His red tongue flicked
out.
This was why you shouldn't rush in blindly. Whether they refused or
accepted, he would win.
"What? Are you scared now that it's time to do it? After all that
big talk?"
After a long pause, Shirone spoke.
"It's true that Maya's sound magic is below average."
"Haha! Finally admitting it! So, you realize you're at a
disadvantage..."
"But."
Shirone's face twisted in disdain.
"She's still better than having someone like you on the
team."
Screamer closed his mouth and wiped his expression. His eyes, which once
made gangsters tremble, glared at Shirone.
"Does that mean you're accepting?"
Shirone turned to his friends.
"Guys."
Iruki, Nade, and Amy stepped forward, flanking Shirone.
Amy wiggled her fingers and said,
"Yeah, let's do it. You'll need the best team to stand a chance.
But you'll still get crushed."
The one who was most taken aback by the situation was Maya.
She had failed the evaluation and been scolded. Normally, that would be
the end of it, but now it had escalated beyond her control.
She was grateful to Shirone's group for standing up for her, but she
didn't want to be a burden.
"Wait, I didn't mean for this to..."
Before she could finish, Nade pulled her into a hug.
As Maya's face turned red and she tried to speak, Nade raised his fist
and shouted at Screamer.
"Let's settle this!"
Nade's voice echoed loudly.
There were no regrets. It felt like all the pent-up frustration from the
senior class was being released.
'Yeah, this is how Shirone's group should be.'
Maya, on the other hand, turned pale. But she couldn't dampen the fiery
spirit of Shirone's group.
Screamer controlled his anger.
Logically, it was as good as a victory. They would lose 20 points, and
he would gain 20.
"See you in two weeks."
Screamer left the training ground. The students were stunned, unable to
believe what had just happened.
Not only had they challenged Fermi's group, but now they had also taken
on Screamer, a well-known figure in Class Two.
'What are they, a bunch of brawlers?'
Sabina, standing next to Dante, sighed. She couldn't ignore it since
Nade was involved.
"This is crazy. Why do they keep causing trouble? With Maya,
they're obviously at a disadvantage. Now Nade's going to lose 20 points. Shouldn't Shirone have stopped
them?"
"You didn't know?"
Dante smirked.
"Shirone is the leader of the troublemakers."
Shirone's group gathered at the Paranormal Science Research Club for a
relaxing time.
With training and Gaold's mission, Shirone barely had enough time in a
day, but he joined his friends to clear his mind.
Lying on the sofa, staring at the ceiling on a holiday felt incredibly
refreshing.
Why hadn't he appreciated such moments before, always complaining about
boredom?
Shirone recalled the events from a few days ago.
"So, Maya is a commoner. I didn't know."
Nade said,
"Me neither. I looked into it after that day, and it turns out
she's an outsider."
"An outsider?"
"She's from the Mystique Tribe, a small ethnic group from the
south. About 200 of them settled in Tormia, cultivating their traditional herb, Kapura, for medicinal
use. But 20 years ago, it was classified as a drug, and they lost everything. They couldn't integrate
into society, burdened with debt and the stigma of being a drug tribe."
"I see. So Maya's family must be struggling."
"Probably. She has seven siblings. The tribe pooled their money to
support her. If she becomes a magician, it would improve the Mystique Tribe's reputation."
"But how did she get into the magic school? I wouldn't have made it
without the Ozente family."
"Teacher Collie recommended her. I don't know the details, but
there must have been some connection, like with you."
Though Collie was a strict head teacher in the senior class, he was
kind-hearted and likely helped Maya.
"There were rumors at the time. But when her grades turned out to
be unimpressive, it just faded away."
Was that a blessing in disguise?
Shirone also wouldn't have faced much resentment if he hadn't stood out
in the entrance test.
"Anyway, it's really unfortunate. She must feel immense pressure.
The tribe's expectations are on her, and she can't graduate. Seeing her gulping down meat with water to
increase her volume was pitiful."
Shirone felt the same.
"Yeah. Especially since Maya is a girl. Who wouldn't want to look
pretty? She'd probably look great if she lost weight. But in the senior class, there's no
choice."
Iruki said,
"Want to see what Maya used to look like?"
"Huh? How?"
"Back then, Nade collected a lot. Remember? The picture
book."
Shirone suddenly remembered.
"Oh, the one published by the Women's Anatomy Research
Club?"
Nade sat up from the sofa.
"That's right! We're in the senior class now. Most of the repeaters
would be in there."
Without waiting for a response, Nade dove under the desk.
"Iruki, give me the issue and page number."
Iruki, who never forgot, said,
"The latest issue is the most accurate. Issue 48, page
8."
"Issue 48? That's last year's. Maya would have been overweight
then."
"You'll see. Just get it."
Nade rummaged through the picture books piled in the safe.
Since Shirone joined, he had lost interest, but he couldn't bring
himself to throw them away.
"Let's see. Issue 48, Issue 48... Ah, here it is."
Nade placed the picture book on the table, and Shirone and Iruki leaned
in.
Page 8 showed Maya's previous appearance.
"Wow..."
For a work by the Women's Anatomy Research Club, it was modest and
portrayed her in a sacred manner.
But that wasn't why they were surprised.
"Is this really Maya? She's so pretty."
Though different from Tormia's urban standards, she was a fresh and
bright girl anyone would fall for.
Nade nodded.
"I remember. I wondered who she was when I saw this
picture."
Iruki explained,
"Maya started gaining weight in her second year of the senior
class. But the Women's Anatomy Research Club seems to only draw her previous appearance."
"That's strange. Do they like Maya?"
"Maybe. But it feels more like respect than affection."
Shirone was intensely curious.
"Who are they, the Women's Anatomy Research Club?"
Nade said,
"I don't know. If they're caught, they're expelled immediately. But
they must be in the senior class. They couldn't draw the senior class while being in the advanced
class."
Thinking about it gave him chills. It meant they saw each other every
day during evaluations.
Nade said,
"Let's check the others. Based on last year, there should be girls
in the senior class now."
Iruki said,
"Page 14 has Pony, page 16 has Suabi, and the last page has
Dorothy. Those three are in the senior class now."
Shirone looked at Iruki.
"You really remember this because of your servant,
right?"
Nade pointed to the page.
"Hey, it's Pony."
Pony's appearance was the same as now.
Her symmetrical part emphasized her high forehead, and her golden hair
framed her face.
Wearing a dress that exposed her shoulders and collarbone, she exuded
royal elegance.
"Oh, here's Suabi."
Kneeling by a stream, Suabi was covered with a blanket over her bare
body.
A bird perched on her cupped hands, and she wore a beautiful smile never
seen at school.
"It's Dorothy? She looks so different here."
Dorothy wore a black woolen knit, pulling up the hem with a rebellious
expression.
Her slender waist and cute belly button were exposed, and she wore only
underwear, highlighting her long, giraffe-like legs.
Her tin doll, which she never parted with, was hugging her neck.
"I've always thought, but these drawings are amazing. They look
just like the real thing."
Nade closed the picture book with a snap.
"Man, there are so many beauties at this school, but how come none
of them are our partners?"
"Shirone has one."
"N-no! I mean, I don't really..."
Nade turned to him with a sharp look.
"Wait, didn't you say you went on a trip with Amy? Did nothing
really happen?"
"Huh? Nothing?"
Shirone's face turned red. Nothing had happened, but why was his face
heating up?
"Seriously? That sounds suspicious. You better come
clean!"
Shirone glanced around. Realizing no one would help him, he resorted to
his last option.
"Ugh, whatever! I'm going to sleep! I'm so tired these days. Wake
me up in an hour!"
Sleep was the best way to clear his mind.
When Shirone woke up, he got up from the sofa. His friends had already
left for the dormitory.
"What time is it?"
A note was left on the table.
"We left early. You looked tired, so get some rest and meet us at
the dining hall later. And whatever you do, don't look at the red book in the safe under the table. It's
amazing."
Yawning, Shirone crumpled the note and stood up.
"Still, that nap felt great."
He gathered his toiletries and headed to the senior class building. He
planned to wash up at school and then go to the dining hall.
As he finished brushing his teeth and washing his face, the bell rang,
signaling 5 o'clock.
"Yawn."
Yawning away the remnants of sleep, he walked down the hallway when he
heard music from somewhere.
"What's that?"
Someone was singing in the music room at the end of the hall. It wasn't
a classical piece but a popular song.
"Wow, they're really good."
The singing was impressively skilled.
'Could it be a professional singer?'
When he opened the music room door, he saw Maya playing the piano and
singing.
It was a lively rhythm and blues song called "Nothing is More
Beautiful Than You," which she used to hum while wandering through the village.
As soon as he opened the door, her voice hit him, sending chills down
his spine.
Nothing is more beautiful than you
It wasn't just that she sang well. Even though he knew nothing about
music, he was certain of it the moment he heard her.
Maya's singing was something special.
