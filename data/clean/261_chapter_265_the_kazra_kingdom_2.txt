Chapter 265:
The Kazra Kingdom (2)
After arriving at the academy, Shirone and Reina continued to chat as
they walked toward the dormitory.
Shirone's face turned red at Reina's mischievous joke. Seeing this,
Reina found him even more adorable and laughed gleefully.
The male students looked at Shirone with envious eyes.
Reina was not only beautiful but also a famous pianist who could be
considered the idol of the nobles.
For such a person to be walking arm-in-arm with Shirone made his status
appear much higher.
Amy and Seriel stopped walking when they saw the two walking side by
side toward the dormitory.
They, too, were on their way to greet Shirone at the dormitory, but
seeing the two of them so close, they couldn't bring themselves to say hello.
Amy knew that Reina was Rian's older sister. However, the atmosphere was
completely different from three days ago.
At first, they seemed distant, but now they had grown so close that they
were walking with their bodies touching.
Seriel, acting exaggeratedly, tapped Amy on the shoulder.
"Oh my, oh my! Amy, do you see that? Are you sure there's nothing
going on between those two?"
"How would I know? She's Rian's sister, so they must be close. They
probably lived together when Shirone was working as a butler."
"No, that's not the case. I looked into it, and during the 1 year
and 6 months that Shirone worked as an ordinary butler for Ogent Family, Reina was studying as an
apprentice under a famous pianist. In other words, they didn't have any time to grow close. For them to
have gotten this close in such a short period… could it be?"
Amy pushed Seriel's back.
"Oh, enough already. Just go."
Judging from the fact that she even did background checks, it seems that
she was quite curious about the relationship between the two.
Amy didn't feel great about it either. However, since Shirone's
situation was more important right now, she suppressed her emotions and followed behind.
The boys' dormitory was off-limits to girls, but on the day Shirone
left, an exception was made. The fact that this event could result in the birth of the First Prince of
the Kazra Kingdom was impactful enough to override school rules.
In front of the dormitory, a carriage from Ogent Family was
waiting.
The teachers chatting at the entrance nodded casually when Amy and
Seriel greeted them and resumed their conversation.
Even before reaching Shirone's room, they could hear Reina's laughter
coming from inside. Amy felt a pang in her chest but forced herself to ignore it as she continued
walking.
Upon entering the room, they found Iruki and Nade giggling while Shirone
and Reina were dancing, embracing each other.
It seemed Reina was teaching Shirone how to dance in preparation for the
ball at the royal palace. If Amy thought of it as just that, it would be fine, but seeing Shirone's
flushed face made her feel strangely twisted inside.
Shirone's dance steps were incredibly awkward.
Of course, it makes no sense that the greatest aspiring mage in the
kingdom has no athletic ability. It was clear that his body had frozen under Reina's affectionate
gaze.
"Huh? A- Amy."
Seeing Amy enter, Shirone quickly pulled himself away from Reina.
Amy pretended not to notice and stepped into the room. She could see the
foolish duo, Nade and Iruki, swallowing their saliva, but she neatly ignored them.
Reina smiled warmly and raised her hand.
"Oh my, Amy's here. Hello?"
Amy gave a formal but polite greeting.
"Hello. Today is the day you're leaving, right?"
"Yes, I was having one last practice with Shirone. Surprisingly, he
doesn't have much talent for dancing."
Shirone waved his hands in protest as if feeling wronged.
"No, that's because I was too nervous…!"
Reina covered her mouth and laughed.
"Ohhoho! Nervous? Could it be because you were practicing with
me?"
When Shirone hesitated and couldn't respond, Amy felt her irritation
spike.
Even if their relationship was just a fake romance, they were still
supposed to be a couple. He had even told Reina this, so it would be normal for him to act accordingly,
at least formally.
Yet, Shirone was glued to Reina and couldn't seem to pull himself
away.
There seemed to be some special bond between the two, but Amy couldn't
figure out what it was, nor did she want to.
Amy put the thought aside and asked Shirone.
"Are you all set?"
"Yes, but I'm worried. I feel like I'm going to make a
mistake."
"What's there to worry about? They'll laugh off any small
mistakes."
For a brief moment, Amy caught the trace of anxiety that flickered
through Shirone's eyes.
She thought he was just absorbed in Reina and not thinking clearly, but
it seemed he was aware to some extent.
On the surface, it was a case of his biological parents finding their
child, but if you thought about it deeply, there were certainly many strange aspects.
Reina patted Shirone on the shoulder and reassured him.
"It'll be fine. Since the Kazra Kingdom officially announced it,
there shouldn't be any real danger. Kazra isn't particularly powerful compared to Thormia, and our
family won't just stand by either."
Just hearing that made Shirone feel a bit more at ease.
Reina was sensible, knowledgeable about the kingdom's political
landscape, and well-versed in the dynamics between royalty and nobility.
If she took responsibility and led, it seemed unlikely that anything
strange would happen.
"Yes, I'll trust you, sister."
When Shirone answered with a smile, Reina stroked Shirone's head with a
pleased face.
Then, as if something had crossed her mind, she turned to look at Amy.
As expected, Amy's face didn't look good.
'I do feel a bit sorry for her.'
During the three-day preparation period, Reina had meticulously checked
on the people around Shirone.
Overall, he had good relationships with his friends, and Iruki and Nade,
in particular, seemed like they would be good companions for Shirone in the future.
The problem was the relationship between Shirone and Amy.
They claimed to be lovers, but judging by their conversations, that was
far from the truth.
When lovers are together, a unique sense of intimacy was bound to
emerge. However, that particular feeling was missing between Shirone and Amy.
Yet, despite this, they had maintained the act of being fake lovers for
over a year.
Unless they were experts at balancing their emotions on a tightrope,
such situations usually end with either a complete breakup or growing closer together.
Reina had seen countless cases where time passed, and people ended up as
just ordinary friends in such situations.
In the end, from her perspective, the two had no relationship
whatsoever.
Of course, it was clear that there was a chance that things could get
heated up at any time if sparks were ever ignited, but given their personalities, the chances of that
happening were extremely low.
Reina felt the urge to say something out of frustration, but the thought
of them actually getting together made her feel so irritated that her desire to help completely
vanished.
Reina realized that this was also a form of jealousy, but she made a
plan to postpone the thought, thinking that if there was no competition, there was no need to
worry.
'Hehe, come to think of it, I'm in no position to talk about
others.'
Checking the time, Reina spoke to Shirone.
"Then let's get going. We need to leave quickly if we're going to
bring your parents."
Amy intervened.
"Wait. Let me talk to Shirone for a moment."
"Huh? Why not talk on the way? It'll take time to get the carriage
ready anyway."
Amy knew time wasn't on their side, but there was something she
absolutely had to hear from Shirone.
"It will only take a moment."
Amy directly demanded a short time, and Reina, blinking her eyes,
replied as if she had no choice.
"Then I'll go ahead and prepare. Shirone, come down when you're
done talking."
As Reina left, Nade and Iruki bowed politely.
She doesn't know what kind of vixen thing Reina did, but it seemed like
the three fools fell head over heels for her.
Amy responded with a snort and pushed everyone out except
Shirone.
Shirone, who had been packing clothes into his backpack, turned around
with one strap over his shoulder.
"I'll be back, Amy. Make sure you prepare for the
exams."
Amy didn't beat around the bush and went straight to the point.
"Something just doesn't sit right. You know that too, don't
you?"
Shirone smiled bitterly.
"Yes, but… I don't want to think about it. If they're truly my
biological parents, I feel I shouldn't have such thoughts."
Amy understood Shirone's feelings. While everyone was focused on the
possibility of him being the First Prince of the Kazra Kingdom, for him, it was a journey to meet his
blood relatives.
Even if they truly were his biological parents, it would still be a
problem. They had abandoned him, leaving an unhealable scar on Shirone's heart.
"Are you really okay with this?"
"I don't know. I don't know anything right now. I'll figure it out
when I get there, how I really feel. But for now, I don't want to make any judgments."
Sadness and anxiety simultaneously crossed Shirone's face. Watching him
quietly, Amy mustered her courage and spoke.
"Should I come with you?"
"Huh? What are you saying?"
"It would be a lot more helpful if I went with you. There are no classes
anymore anyway."
Shirone felt a warmth in his heart but shook his head. He couldn't
selfishly take away the most important time in Amy's life.
"It's okay. Sister Reina is with me."
Shirone felt the coldness in Amy's eyes and flinched. Fortunately, that
coldness vanished quickly, but her words had a strangely sharp edge to them.
"Fine, I get it. Let's go then. That Sister Reina must be
waiting."
Amy left the room without looking back. Shirone, still bewildered, was
too overwhelmed by his situation to consider her feelings deeply.
Outside the dormitory, Reina was holding a file and checking the luggage
on the carriage one by one.
Shirone exchanged farewells with his friends, who were there to see him
off. Although the journey was only supposed to take about ten days, it somehow felt like a final
goodbye.
Nade said.
"Have a good trip. When you become the king, make sure to buy me an
extra-large cream bread from the store."
"Haha! Sure. But when I come back, it'll be the
vacation."
Reina watched Shirone chatting with his friends with a pleased
expression.
Just like Rian, Shirone had changed a lot in just one year.
Above all, defeating Dante, the greatest talent in the kingdom, was a
tremendous source of pride for Ogent Family.
Rian's eyes were not wrong.
And neither had her own.
As Reina looked at Shirone with affectionate feelings, Seriel, seething
with rage, clenched her fists.
"Shirone is really disappointing. At times like this, a man should
draw the line clearly."
Seeking agreement, Seriel glanced at Amy and was startled.
Until now, Amy had always brushed things off casually, but now she was
glaring daggers at Reina.
"Seriel, I'm going."
Seriel's face turned pale. Although they had discussed it, she hadn't
expected Amy to make a decision.
"Amy, are you serious?"
Seriel's eyes soon filled with concern.
In just about two weeks, the graduation exams would take place. It
wasn't a time when they could afford to be distracted by anything else.
"It's fine. The classes are over anyway, so I might as well take a
trip abroad to clear my head."
Seriel knew better than anyone that this wasn't true.
In a way, she felt envious of Amy, who could throw herself fully into
this situation.
If Amy had decided to go, the least Seriel could do was send her off
wholeheartedly, ensuring she wouldn't look back, leaving no regrets behind.
Seriel said, pushing Amy's back.
"Go on, then. This big sister grants her permission."
Amy added hastily.
"I- It's not because I'm worried about Reina or anything! I'm just
concerned about Shirone, that's all..."
"Hohoho! Got it! Who's saying anything? Go show her what you're made
of!"
With Seriel's encouragement, Amy walked toward the carriage.
Just then, Shirone, who was getting ready to board, turned and bid her
farewell.
"Amy, good luck! Make sure you pass. I'll do my best
too."
"Will you please step aside?"
"Huh?"
Shirone didn't understand. Amy waved her hand to the side as if
annoyed.
"Move. Don't you know 'Ladies First'?"
Pushing Shirone aside, Amy climbed into the carriage, and Reina's face
took on a bewildered expression. Amy, however, ignored this, bent forward, and got inside.
"Hello. Please take care of me on the way."
"Oh my, where are you going?"
Reina, now thoroughly irritated, feigned ignorance despite knowing.
Nothing would change anyway, but she didn't want to admit this situation with her own mouth.
"Well, I just couldn't let him go alone. I feel like I need to look
after him myself."
Shirone opened the carriage door and poked his head inside.
"Amy! What's going on? Are you really planning to go?"
"That's why I got on. Sit down quickly. We don't have
time."
Reina's insides burned like a furnace.
Carmis Amy.
From what she had observed over the past few days, Amy seemed to have a
cautious personality, making it fun to tease her. But now, it seemed she was just as hot-blooded as
Rian.
Regret crept in, feeling like she had unnecessarily lit a torch, but it
was already water spilled.
