Chapter 46:
Limitless (5)
"A-Amy did…?"
Shirone blankly recalled her face. As a targeting specialist, her speed was undoubtedly
incredible.
But 987 points was a record that seemed unbreakable.
"Senior Amy exhibits incredible concentration in real combat. Well, that part's
similar to you. No wonder she never lost first place until graduation."
Just how many monsters existed in the world? No—this was magic school. It felt like the
whole world was filled with talented people.
"No need to aim for the highest record. Before 987, the record was in the 700s. Even
the teachers said it wouldn't be broken for another decade."
A significant gap. Shirone set his first goal at 700 points, refusing to despair. Three
weeks remained until the exam. He had to find a way to improve his score by then.
'I won't lose. Absolutely not!'
Shirone clenched his fists tightly.
***
Shirone devoted every spare moment to the numerical sequence. Unless he could count
faster than Iruki's computations, the outcome was predetermined.
Currently, he could group 20 numbers at once. But this was no match for the logarithmic
compression of units.
'Damn it. What do I do?'
Shirone trained through the night, casting photon output in the practice hall. His
zero-to-hundred had improved dramatically, but he could only count to 10,000 in a minute.
'Iruki hit a million on the first day. I need to count faster.'
He needed to understand the exam format too. If targets spawned infinitely, scoring 1,000
would be easy, but there was a pattern.
The first step spawned 10 targets. Only after eliminating all would the second step spawn
11. The steps continued rising for a minute, increasing the number of targets.
Thus, failing to clear early steps quickly made high scores impossible, even with late
efforts.
'It's all about the zero-to-hundred. Whoever clears steps first can gain hundreds of
points later.'
Practice ended at dawn. Sleeping like a log for a few hours before returning to the
practice hall became his routine.
During exam prep, integrated classes were canceled, and each class took turns, leaving
the practice hall quiet.
Students shared tips or practiced numerical sequences while waiting for their turn on the
Image Zone. But Shirone found even waiting in line wasteful, focusing solely on the sequence until his turn
came.
Finally, he stepped onto the Image Zone and poured out all his efforts. His jagged Spirit
Zone flashed with photon output as targets were hit.
Adapting to active magic allowed him to cast photon output without magical actions,
enabling him to hit targets without looking.
Final score: 567 points. Higher than Iruki's demonstration, restoring Shirone's
confidence. Meanwhile, his classmates' confidence waned.
"He improved by over 200 points in a week. What's this? Even with talent, this is
cheating."
"He's adapted to offensive Spirit Zone. Magical actions can boost power but disrupt
flow. Looks like he's casting at least four times per second."
As fewer students used the Image Zone, Iruki finally appeared, having idled in a corner
until now.
"My turn now?"
Smirking at Shirone, he activated the device. His evasion-mode Spirit Zone shrank
further, now fist-sized.
The dazzling Spirit Zone eliminated targets. While Shirone's photon output was flashy,
Iruki's speed was equally thrilling.
After a minute, Iruki left without checking his score. Final tally: 568 points. Exactly
one point above Shirone.
It could've been coincidence, but Shirone sensed the Servant Syndrome's calculations at
work.
The pattern held. Shirone improved rapidly, but Iruki always outshone him by one point.
Spurred on, Shirone redoubled his efforts. His modularization surpassed 100 numbers. The
zero-to-hundred became instantaneous, and his Spirit Zone's performance skyrocketed. He realized faster
zero-to-hundred shortened the Zone's duration, but his endurance could sustain it for a minute.
His scores rose daily, surpassing Class Five's average of 500 and reaching the 600s.
But Iruki always won. The one-point lead was so dramatic the students grew more excited
for his demonstrations than Shirone's.
The final training session before the exam arrived.
Since final checks often reflected actual exam performance, the students' focus peaked.
Shirone gritted his teeth and eliminated targets. His body seemed to glow as flashes shot
everywhere.
The light show ended, and the students erupted.
Final score: 700 points. Nearing Class Five's highest record, second only to Amy's.
"700 points is really possible. I never thought I'd see it."
"You have to clear the tenth step within 10 seconds to hit 700. How many is he
destroying per second?"
Shirone clenched his fists upon seeing the score. He'd improved by 70 points since last
class, finally reaching his 700-point goal.
As he descended, Iruki brushed past him.
"Congrats. 700 is impressive. Your growth is terrifying."
"I don't want to lose to you."
"Kukuku, I see. But don't get careless. The exam hasn't even started."
Shirone snorted and stepped off the Image Zone. When he turned, Iruki had already begun
his speed gun demonstration.
The students gasped.
"Whoa! Shrinking the Zone that small is a skill."
Iruki reduced his Spirit Zone to the size of a bead. Its speed made even afterimages hard
to see.
Bangbangbangbangbang!
Targets seemed to explode on their own, detonating around Iruki at the Zone's center.
When it ended, Shirone paled. Final score: 701 points. Once again, Iruki had surpassed
his best by one point.
The students cheered, forgetting their own exams in the heat of the rivalry.
'701? One point again?'
Shirone grew anxious for the first time. His palms sweated, mouth dry. If Iruki had gone
all out, he wouldn't have been so tense.
But another one-point lead meant Iruki could score even higher.
The exam was next week, but Class Five's schedule was first, leaving only three days.
Could he close the gap? Swallowing his unease, Shirone left the practice hall dejected.

The students' favorite holiday. Normally, young couples would pair off, but with exams
looming, the central park was empty.
Meanwhile, Seriel strolled through the park, humming as if perfect for a walk. Having
submitted her graduation application, advanced class exams were no longer her concern.
"Amy! Over here!"
Amy was reading by the fountain. Still beautiful, but up close, dark circles under her
eyes and a thinner frame were noticeable.
"Wow, you've lost weight. Graduation class must be tough."
Amy smiled faintly and handed over documents.
"Welcome to hell. Here's the graduation class data you asked for."
Records of graduates' grades and specialties. Since all graduation students could access
them, it wasn't cheating.
"Thanks. Let's sit. Want something to drink?"
