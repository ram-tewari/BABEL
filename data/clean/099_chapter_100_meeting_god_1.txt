Chapter 100:
Meeting God (1)
Shirone expanded infinitely and simultaneously faded. Having forgotten even his lingering
attachments to the world, he seeped into everything. Then, a powerful force pulled him back into a single
point. If there was something beyond life for humans, this event could be called a hijacking of the soul.
"Gasp! Gasp!"
As Shirone's consciousness reassembled, his memories from before activating the Immortal
Function returned. Trembling briefly at the fear of death, he exhaled sharply and checked his physical
state. Aside from being naked, there seemed to be no abnormalities.
"Where is this?"
The space was entirely white. In this monochromatic expanse stretching infinitely, he
couldn't perceive distance. Even as he slowly walked forward, he didn't feel like he was moving at all.
'This is bizarre. It's like my senses are numb.'
Suddenly, Shirone had an odd thought. Experimentally, he slowly bent his knees and
stepped forward as if climbing stairs. To his surprise, his body floated upward.
"Huh?"
Shirone continued climbing the endless stairs. When he paused to look around, he was
speechless.
"……."
He didn't feel like he had ascended to a great height. No matter where he moved, the
white scenery remained unchanged.
"There's no distance."
Without measurable distance, space couldn't exist. To test his hypothesis, Shirone tried
descending this time. His prediction was correct—no movement made any difference.
Just as he lost the will to move further, a woman's voice spoke.
"Impressive. Grasping the meaning of space so quickly. You truly deserve to be here."
Shirone didn't turn around. In this place, even the concept of direction didn't exist.
"Who are you? Am I dead?"
A beautiful woman stepped out from a gap in the white scenery. As distance formed between
them, the scenery transformed instantly. A colossal temple loomed over Shirone, as if pressing down on him.
"Wow……."
The scale was beyond imagination. Even ancient temples weren't this massive. Hundreds of
pillars supported the ceiling, each roughly two kilometers tall.
"Where is this place……?"
"Hello, Shirone. I've been waiting for you."
The woman had silver hair that cascaded down to her ankles. Shirone had never seen anyone
so beautiful in his life. It wasn't about appearance. It was the value of her existence. Just as a man in
love sees his lover as the most beautiful in the world, her worth here was absolute.
"Oops!"
Shirone hurriedly covered himself. He had been standing there boldly, unaware he was
naked.
"Hehe. No need to be shy. This isn't your world. Your body remains in your original
world."
With a wave of her hand, the woman clothed him. Shirone stared blankly at himself before
resigning to his fate. Only one thing came to mind.
"Is this the afterlife?"
"No. This isn't the afterlife. It's just a different spot from where you lived."
Shirone tilted his head. A spot was a one-dimensional point without space. A point moves
to form a line, a line moves to form a plane, and a plane moves to form three-dimensional space.
"Wait……?"
Shirone recalled the white space he had been in earlier.
"That's right. That's the spot. It's not that space doesn't exist—it's compressed
infinitely."
Of course, it made sense that he couldn't perceive distance. But even if he were trapped
in one dimension, his body was three-dimensional. Couldn't he measure the distance between his right and
left arms? Wouldn't that mean space existed?
"Ah. So a three-dimensional being exists within one dimension."
"Your insight is remarkable. Yes, that's the essence of space-time. No matter how vast
the world, from afar, it's merely a point. Conversely, no matter how small the point, an infinite world
unfolds within it."
Shirone finally calmed down. As long as logical conversation was possible, there was no
need to fear, even if this was the afterlife.
"Then who are you?"
The woman smiled like an innocent child. Though eerie, Shirone found even that beautiful.
Here, she was the most valuable existence.
"I am God."

The magic school was in chaos.
It took a full eight hours just to recover Akein's body and return the students to their
dormitories.
The students, still under memory suppression, lay in their respective rooms. The faculty
could regain their memories to expedite the process, but considering the turmoil once the entire student
body woke up, it was a headache-inducing prospect.
To make matters worse, the suspects—Kanis and Arin—had vanished, leaving only Nade,
Iruki, and Emi to tend to Shirone in the infirmary.
"Tending" wasn't quite accurate. Shirone wasn't breathing, and his heart had stopped.
Yet, no one dared to utter the word "death."
