Chapter 297:
Master of Consciousness (1)
The carriage carrying Armin and his party sped towards the castle
gate.
The wheels splashed through knee-deep rainwater, sending water flying
outside.
The carriage bounced over the rough mountain path, hitting rocks. Amy
and Reina gripped the handles tightly with both hands.
"It looks like we'll arrive soon. It's fortunate that it's not
far."
Amy stuck her head out the window. Beyond the sky covered with dark
clouds, a massive pillar of light seemed to pierce through.
As they entered that place, the sound of rain disappeared, and a warm,
sunlit meadow spread out before them.
Armin and his party got off the carriage and looked back at the path
they had taken. Between the dark clouds and the horizon, almost touching, lightning like static
electricity was flashing.
Even in the sunlight, the coachman's face was not visible. Amy thought
he must be a nobody, as even Shirone had no particular impression of him.
Shirone was sitting on the meadow below the hill, looking at
flowers.
Although his face couldn't be seen as he had his back turned, there was
no one else who could exist alone in the realm of the Unbreakable Diamond.
Following a fluttering butterfly, he turned his head, and his profile
showed a beautiful smile.
Reina, now understanding the mental world, was amazed.
Even in the horrific reality, Shirone's mind was unwavering. She seemed
to understand why he could make precise judgments in numerous crises.
'But... should I talk to him?'
Reina couldn't easily approach.
Strictly speaking, it wasn't Shirone but his avatar. Since it integrated
even intimate emotions, an unexpected response could hurt him.
"Let's go. We have nowhere else to retreat."
Armin pushed Reina's back and approached Shirone. Sensing their
presence, Shirone turned his head.
Reina swallowed hard.
"Oh? Mr. Armin? And Sister Reina?"
Shirone greeted them with a bright expression.
"It's been a while. How have you been?"
Armin scrutinized Shirone.
'Truly remarkable.'
Shirone's avatar was almost indistinguishable from his usual
self.
Among the avatars Armin had met, there were famous personalities from
around the world. But even they often revealed disappointing natures at the avatar stage.
It wasn't their fault.
That's human nature.
But Shirone was different.
While anyone might deviate when unseen, Shirone's mind had reached a
pure state, unaffected by others' gaze.
It was a form of avatar that could only emerge from refining and
cultivating one's inner self from childhood.
'The rumor that he's the most promising talent in Tormia wasn't
unfounded.'
Even after parting a year ago, Armin had consistently heard about
Shirone's achievements. His abnormal, wide-ranging progress, which couldn't be explained by talent
alone, was supported by this mental completeness.
"So, what's going on?"
The avatar knew this was the mental world. However, it couldn't separate
it from the dimension of reality. It was like not realizing one is dreaming in a dream.
Reina asked Armin.
"Does Shirone not know why we're here? We've been feeding him
keywords all this time."
"It's hard to draw a clear line on what he knows. The avatar
governs the whole but isn't the whole itself."
"It's like a core code."
"Exactly. While all this is the avatar's doing, it doesn't embody
the avatar's will."
"Um..."
Amy's voice was heard.
When the two turned their heads, they saw the Egoist, over 2 meters
tall, holding Amy with four arms. The upper right arm had turned into a rope, strangling her
neck.
'There shouldn't be any projectiles?'
Reina looked around and realized the coachman had disappeared.
But why did the Egoist manifest? Wasn't Shirone supposed to be friendly
to them?
"Shirone, what's wrong?"
"I just... get annoyed when I see Amy."
Armin sighed.
What he feared had happened.
Connecting cause and effect is the realm of reason. Conversely, emotions
are true to the moment's feelings.
In reality, the two are strictly separated, but the mental entity, the
avatar, couldn't do that.
"Shirone, you need to suppress your emotions as much as possible
now. You might feel it, but your life is in danger. You need our cooperation. And of course, Amy's
cooperation too."
Armin's persuasion didn't make Shirone understand, but he had no choice
but to follow since his life was at stake.
Suppressing his emotions, the Egoist became blurry and then turned into
black smoke.
Amy, rubbing her neck and coughing, looked at Shirone with sad eyes. She
had been prepared, but being treated like this still hurt.
"What's the reason? You have to tell me so I can fix
it."
Shirone didn't know the reason either. Memories related to Amy surged
like a fountain, but the avatar, unable to arrange cause and effect in order, could only feel the
emotions of those situations.
Shirone distanced himself from her and turned to Armin.
"What should we do now?"
"First, we need to penetrate deeper into Shirone's mind. If we
don't catch the intruder, there's no way to recover the situation."
"But I don't know the way down."
"No, you do. The reason Shirone can accept the current situation
without discomfort is because he knows. It's just that the logical system is broken, so he can't draw
conclusions. Think about going down. Then tell me the first place that comes to mind."
Lost in thought, Shirone pointed to the western mountains.
"It's not exact, but..."
"That's enough. Let's go."
Armin sat in the coachman's seat in place of the disappeared
coachman.
They traveled for about an hour in the direction Shirone pointed to and
arrived at a small village.
It was also pouring rain there, but as soon as Shirone entered, the
weather cleared up as if by magic.
Shirone led them to a shabby stable. When he hugged a bunch of soft
straw on the floor and moved it aside, a door leading underground appeared.
"Oh, there really is a door?"
"Rather than that, it's more accurate to say it was designated. The
door exists where Shirone thinks it is. Remember this sense. At this speed, we can catch up with Arius
and Zion."
Hearing the names of the two, Shirone's face twisted fiercely.
Armin flinched and looked back. Though it had disappeared, he felt a
momentary, otherworldly killing intent.
'What was that just now?'
Armin realized he needed to revise his thoughts.
Shirone's avatar seemed no different from his usual self, but perhaps
that wasn't all there was to it.
Shirone's Mental Stage 8
Arius independently gathered information and delved deeper.
The current place he was exploring was a landfill with a stench so foul
it was hard to breathe.
As the unbearable smell wafted on the wind, Zion covered his mouth with
a handkerchief.
'What on earth is this smell? It's not even the smell of a
corpse.'
The fear Shirone felt was manifested as a smell. It was the scent of
death that couldn't exist in reality.
Arius, already accustomed to the stench, grabbed a passing man and
asked,
"Where is the nearest library?"
The man, covering his nose, pointed in a direction and hurried on his
way.
Arius headed to the place the man indicated. As soon as he entered the
library, the stench disappeared, and he felt a sense of relief.
Zion shook his handkerchief and, deciding it was beyond saving, threw it
to the ground. He looked around and said,
"Is it a library this time?"
Arius ignored Zion's words and focused on examining the books. The books
were categorized by subject, but the titles on the covers wriggled like worms, making them
unreadable.
However, some sections had clear titles.
They were books related to Shirone's major, photonics.
Arius walked along the shelves, glancing at the covers. Then he picked
up a book titled "The God Particle."
When he tapped the wall with the book, a rectangular crack appeared. The
wall dissolved into the air, revealing a staircase leading down.
"I've been curious for a while, how do you find these
things?"
"You trace the flow connected to Shirone's unconscious. Every
object has its own inherent meaning. It's a field that requires a certain level of study. You need an
excellent understanding of psychoanalysis."
"I see."
By the middle of Arius's explanation, Zion had already lost
interest.
As always, complicated matters were best left to experts. He just wanted
to extract Ataraxia and get out of there as soon as possible.
When they opened the door to the 7th stage, a plaza unfolded before
them. There was no torrential rain, bone-chilling cold, or stench that could rot one's nose.
Zion adjusted the leather strap of his armament at his waist and
mentally prepared himself.
"At least it's livable here."
As soon as Zion finished speaking, a man fell from above. He landed
face-first, his brain spilling out as he died.
Looking around, people were climbing onto rooftops.
There were numerous suicides. From a window in one building, a woman was
seen standing on a chair, preparing to hang herself.
"What the hell is this?"
Arius, watching from behind Zion, spoke.
"The 7th stage is the gateway just before entering the REM realm.
It's a layer with strong rationality. Projections facing death exhibit symptoms of nervous
breakdowns."
"Ahhhhh!"
Two sisters falling from the 4th floor screamed as if they were being
torn apart.
Zion frowned.
He didn't care about the sisters' deaths or the gruesome corpses. He
just wished they would die quietly without making a fuss.
"Let's move quickly. This place is annoying."
"No. We'll stay for a while. If they meet Shirone's avatar, it will
be troublesome. They could reach the deeper layers much faster than us."
"Hmm, in that case?"
Arius pointed to a castle beyond the city.
"We'll meet the ruler of consciousness here. Shirone's superego.
Divers call it the antithesis."
"Antithesis?"
"It's the consciousness that criticizes Shirone. It's as strong as
the Egoist but significantly weakened due to being on the brink of death. That will buy us some
time."
Arius turned north. They had to be careful with their actions as the
nerves of the passersby were so sensitive that even brushing against them could provoke murder.
The place they arrived at was a castle so small it seemed like a
miniature compared to reality. But it had a quaint charm. Beyond the walls, seven red spires stood, and
guards were stationed at the entrance across a moat that was more like a stream.
As they approached the archway, the guards crossed their spears to block
the entrance.
"Stop. Who are you?"
"We're here to meet the ruler of this place."
"Lord Ignite is on sick leave. Go back."
Arius gave a meaningful smile.
The ruler's name was usually the most impressive concept in rationality.
And Ignite was the name of an ignition spell.
"That's precisely why we're here. I have medicine that can cure the
illness."
The guards were confused. They exchanged glances, and one guard went
inside.
After a while, he returned and allowed Arius to enter.
Once inside, it was far from luxurious. It looked like a military camp
stationed at the front lines.
Like the citizens, the soldiers were also on edge, but under Ignite's
control, they performed their duties without faltering.
At the end of the hall, covered with a red carpet, sat Ignite, resting
his chin on his hand. He was nearly two meters tall, with a stylish brown beard that stood out.
