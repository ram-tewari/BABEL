Chapter 68:
At the Summit (3)
That night.
"Ugh… hng."
Shirone tossed and turned, sweating coldly. The nightmares that hadn't come during his
deep sleep now rose to the surface of his consciousness as his rest grew lighter.
"Ugh…."
Shirone grimaced and shook his head. In the vast universe, humans were nothing. That
hollow sense of deprivation struck him with intense shock.
Once again, the nightmare sucked him into a single point. Time flowed backward, reaching
the beginning of the universe. Light appeared, swaying like threads, then transformed into bubbles that
filled Shirone's vision. Only light. And the next moment, a powerful explosion erupted in the void of
nothingness.
"Huh—uhk!"
Shirone sat bolt upright. His wide eyes stared at the afterimages lingering in his
memory. Only when the dampness of his sweat-soaked pajamas registered did he slowly turn his head toward the
window.
It was morning, with birds chirping outside.
"Phew. Thought it might pass this time."
The nightmare had come without fail. A strange sensation enveloped his body, making him
shiver.
But Shirone was no longer afraid.
He had learned the cause from Armin and had even willed himself out of the Infinite
Domain during the presentation.
Now that he could control his power to some extent, he calmly observed the phenomena
occurring within him.
'The universe in my dreams. It must represent the beginning of the universe. And gauge
symmetry.'
Though he lacked logical information, he felt like he was grasping the threads. Perhaps
it was his subconscious organizing the data. Remembering Armin's words about understanding it naturally, his
heart settled, and the eerie sensation faded.
Shirone headed to the bathroom. After days of deep sleep, his condition was remarkably
good. As he brushed his teeth, he recalled Neid and Iluki's visit.
'Was it a dream? Or did they really go?'
The image of the two boasting about hitting the jackpot at the casino floated hazily in
his mind.
'Maybe I should've gone. What do I do alone now?'
While brushing, Shirone suddenly stopped and turned to look at the bed. Tiny dust
particles were floating and spinning above it. The window was closed, so it wasn't a draft.
Forgetting to rinse his mouth, Shirone rushed over. Upon closer inspection, they were
definitely moving. He reached toward the center of the rotation. Just as he thought he felt nothing, an
intense eerie sensation surged through him.
"Ugh—hk!"
Shirone bent over, spitting out toothpaste foam.
What was this?
It felt like touching the exposed nerve of a pulled tooth—like confirming something he
already knew but couldn't name.
'This is it! Something I know but can't name. But what is it?'
Shirone waved his hands frantically in the air, but the strange sensation didn't return.
Calming himself, he went back to the bathroom, showered under hot water, and emerged. All the while, he kept
thinking about the phenomenon he'd discovered.
'Not wind, not air pressure, not temperature. So how were the dust particles moving?'
They weren't just floating. They were clearly rotating under some force.
After over an hour of deep thought, Shirone grew frustrated and changed clothes to head
outside. With classes in session, no students were around.
Feeling oddly out of place, Shirone walked toward the main gate. With a week's
suspension, the next four days were his to spend as he pleased. Since the damage was done, he decided to
treat it as a rest period after his intense training.
'But where should I go?'
The first thought was home. The image of his parents made him long to see them.
'No, that's ridiculous.'
A carriage ride would get him there by midday, but he had no excuse. He couldn't just
show up and say, "Mom, Dad, I got suspended." Visiting the Ozent family without Rian or Ra Enemi would only
be awkward.
"Ah, right!"
Shirone snapped his fingers as his destination clicked.
A bookstore.
The school library had more books than he could ever read, but buying a book had been a
long-held dream.
A memory surfaced—his father buying him a worn-out book as a child, and his joy at
receiving it. But deep down, he'd always wanted more.
Now, he could. Thanks to the allowance Temuran sent monthly, he had plenty saved up.
If he bought books in the commoner district, even after spending modestly, he could fill
a basket.
'This'll be my first time. My very own book.'
Borrowing from the library wasn't bad, but Shirone loved books and had a strong desire to
own them.
With his decision made, he quickened his pace. Soon, he passed through the noble district
and entered the commoner district.
The streets he'd often visited as a child in his father's cart remained unchanged.
Shirone strode confidently into the bookstore, jingling his coin pouch. The shopkeeper recognized him—the
boy who'd spent hours browsing every time he came to the city.
"Welcome… Oh my, isn't that Shirone?"
"Hello, ma'am."
"My, how you've grown. Your father hasn't been around lately, so I thought he'd moved to
another city. He always bought a book or two when he came."
"He won't be coming anymore. So I'm here to buy my own."
The shopkeeper's eyes widened.
"Oh? Still love books, do you? Pick whatever you like."
"Thanks. I'll look around."
Shirone browsed happily. Price tags showed that well-preserved books cost more,
regardless of content.
Few commoners could read, but in a large city like Creas, business was steady.
Unsurprisingly, novels outsold academic works, and the few scholarly books available
covered only basic topics. Compared to Alpheas Magic Academy's library, these were far less advanced. But
Shirone loved all books and eventually settled on a novel about a mage protagonist.
It was popular, with multiple copies in stock, priced by quality—high, medium, and low.
With money to spare, Shirone chose the cleanest new book and handed over a gold coin. Books were expensive,
especially new ones, costing ten times the lowest quality.
"Oh my, such an expensive one? Are you running an errand for someone?"
"No. It's my first time buying a book. I wanted a clean copy, even if it's a bit much."
"How diligent. Not many spend money on books. Study hard—it's the only way to get smart.
A sharp mind keeps your future wife from suffering."
Unaware Shirone attended a school for geniuses, the shopkeeper lectured him with pride.
But Shirone listened cheerfully, happy even for that, before leaving with his book.
'This is mine.'
Not borrowed, not someone else's—his very own book. The weight in his hands made his
heart race like a child's.
"Hey, isn't that Shirone?"
A voice called out. Shirone turned to see a group of kids gathered around a cart. Their
faces looked familiar, and after a moment, his eyes widened.
"Huh? Altol? Martin? Even Lumina?"
They were the children from the slash-and-burn village he'd played with as a kid.
"Wow, it really is Shirone!"
The kids swarmed around him. Most were his age, with a year or two difference at most.
But their builds had changed drastically. Altol, the leader, had grown into a hulk even adults would
struggle to handle. Only Martin, who'd always been small, was still around Shirone's size.
Altol wrapped an arm around Shirone's head.
"You brat! Where've you been? Not a single visit in years! Even Uncle Vincent just said
you were living in the city."
"Hahaha. Sorry. It just happened."
The familiar rough greeting made Shirone's head spin, but he was happy to see them.
Martin, with his rabbit-like front teeth, eyed Shirone's clothes admiringly.
"Whoa, you've made it big. You look like a noble."
"Really? I hadn't noticed."
The comment surprised Shirone. He'd never considered himself noble-like. But living with
the Ozent family and attending the magic school had left its mark.
Lumina, the only girl in the group, spoke up.
"Shirone always looked like a noble anyway. Glad to see you're doing well."
"Thanks, Lumina. You've gotten prettier."
Lumina's face turned red. Shirone remembered her as a chubby, freckle-faced kid. Now, her
skin was slightly tanned, her freckles gone, and her face had slimmed into a proper young woman's.
Altol burst out laughing.
"You've gotten even smoother, Shirone! Though you were always a silver-tongued brat.
What's so pretty about this tomboy?"
"Shut up! Who are you to judge me?"
Lumina's rare outburst left Altol scratching his head.
"I was just joking…"
"Whatever. I don't want to talk to you."
Shirone smiled, reminded of their old dynamic. This was how the slash-and-burn village
kids always were—seeming to fight but actually close.
Altol hoisted Shirone onto the cart.
"Good to see you, Shirone! Let's drink till our noses bend tonight!"
