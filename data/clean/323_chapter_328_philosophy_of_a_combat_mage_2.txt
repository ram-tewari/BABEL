Chapter 328:
Philosophy of a Combat Mage (2)
"Ouch!"
The snowball was hard enough to make his head ring. Moreover, he was
standing on slippery ice.
Unable to keep his balance, Jokre fell forward.
The shock was brief, and anger surged. Who dared to play such a
prank?
"What the... Who did that?"
Jokre touched the back of his head and turned around.
A gray-haired boy stood outside the skating rink, casually tossing
snowballs. In his other hand, he held an iced coffee, quite out of place in the cold weather.
"No matter how unpopular you are with girls, you shouldn't mess
with a couple on a date."
Jokre squinted at the familiar face.
Vivian muttered in surprise.
"Isn't that... Dante? Airhain Dante."
Dante entered the rink with his shoes on, gliding smoothly on one leg as
he pushed off the ice. Rudvans and Vivian instinctively stepped aside to let him pass.
'No way. It's really Dante?'
Vivian couldn't believe her eyes.
Since she entered magic school, Dante had never lost his position as the
kingdom's top student. When the news of his defeat by Shirone was published in the journal, every
student in the kingdom was shocked.
Jokre, too, had once clipped articles about Dante and pasted them in his
notebook, fueling his determination.
But how long could he cling to memories of his school days?
Above all, being hit on the back of the head before even introducing
himself was a lifetime humiliation.
"Hey, you..."
Dante ignored Jokre and approached Shirone.
"Long time no see, Shirone. And Amy."
Amy crossed her arms and glared at him.
"Oh, so now you're calling your seniors by name?"
"Haha! We'll be in the same graduating class next semester
anyway."
Amy wasn't really upset.
During the 2000th duel, Dante had treated her with utmost respect, so
his friendly attitude now felt a bit awkward.
But Dante's personality was so cool that he seemed to have already
erased those memories from his mind.
Anyway, meeting a schoolmate outside of school was a pleasant surprise,
even if they were rivals at school.
Shirone smiled and said,
"Fancy seeing you here. What brings you?"
"As you can see, I'm here to have fun. I travel every vacation.
It's my first time in Creas. Someone recommended the winter festival."
"I see. Did you come alone? What about Closer and
Sabina?"
"They started special training as soon as vacation began. By now,
they must be realizing what hell is. They're exhausting to hang out with."
Dante shrugged, and Shirone burst into laughter.
He had been wondering if it was okay to have fun, but seeing Dante's
relaxed demeanor made his own worries seem insignificant.
'Dante, you're amazing as always.'
Sometimes, when he recalled the 2000th duel, Shirone was astonished that
he had defeated Dante.
Beyond magic, Dante had star quality. The ability to captivate people's
attention wasn't something that could be achieved through effort alone.
As expected of someone from the capital, Vashka, he dressed well.
He wore a blue coat, black skinny pants, and a purple scarf, looking
both understated and radiant.
He even had a piercing in one ear. For someone like Shirone, who was
clueless about fashion, it was surprising.
But Jokre thought differently.
He had been completely ignored.
In front of Amy, no less. Or rather, in front of Amy's boyfriend,
Shirone.
What angered him the most was how bright Shirone, Amy, and Dante looked
together.
'Geniuses only hang out with other geniuses? Seeing them together is
really annoying.'
Jokre took a deep breath and spoke.
"Hey, Dante."
"Hmm?"
Dante turned around, sipping his drink, seemingly having forgotten what
he had done earlier.
"If you hit someone, shouldn't you apologize? Especially to a
senior mage."
"A senior? Who are you?"
"Jokre, a graduate of Eins Magic School. I'm not just some
promising student like you."
Dante blinked, still sipping his drink.
The longer Dante remained silent, the more anxious Jokre became.
He couldn't help but imagine what Dante might be thinking.
Of course, Eins Magic School was Jokre's beloved alma mater, and he had
no shame in choosing to transfer.
But Dante was from the Royal Magic School.
The kingdom's top institution.
Although he was now attending Alpheas Magic School to avenge his defeat
by Shirone, it was still one of the top five schools.
Moreover, he had been the direct disciple of Olivia, a certified
second-grade archmage, so Jokre knew he wouldn't be noticed.
"Sorry, never heard of you. And you're the one bothering a couple
having fun."
"You...!"
Jokre's eyes flared. The words "never heard of you" pierced
his heart like a thorn.
Rudvans and Vivian, who had hoped their names were known in the minor
leagues, also looked coldly at Dante.
"Dante, don't be mistaken. Do you think you're something special?
You're just a student. And you were thoroughly defeated by Shirone. Shirone, who is a commoner. And Amy,
who was hailed as a genius, is just a repeat student."
"You really know a lot."
Dante's expression didn't change as he spoke.
"That's the difference between you and us. We're featured in the
journal, and you buy it to read about us."
Shirone's face burned with embarrassment.
If he liked witty remarks, Dante mercilessly trampled on his opponent's
weaknesses.
It was cool if you were on his side, but hell for the other
party.
As expected, Jokre's group's faces twisted, and their eyes showed a hint
of murderous intent.
If they intended to provoke, it worked perfectly, but knowing it wasn't
their intention made Shirone worry.
'Damn it! Damn it! Damn it!'
Jokre was furious. Why was he being treated like this?
Logically, the roles should be reversed.
The successful graduate should be the one flaunting, while they should
look at him with jealousy and envy.
'Hmph! Pathetic fools! Do they think a school's reputation will live
their lives for them?'
Jokre glared at Shirone's group, wondering how to make them
submit.
'Huh? What's that...'
He noticed something shiny under Shirone's coat. Upon closer inspection,
it was a sword sheath.
'Why is he carrying a sword at a festival? And isn't he a commoner? Is
he allowed to carry a weapon?'
Jokre pondered the reason. Then, realizing something, he burst into
laughter.
"Hahaha! So that's it. You're just the same."
As Jokre's demeanor suddenly changed, Dante stepped back, seemingly
intimidated.
Ignoring him, Jokre pointed at Shirone.
"Acting all confident, but you're just insecure. Why else would a
commoner carry a sword? I guess you wanted to match your noble girlfriend."
Vivian looked and gasped.
"Oh, it's true? But isn't that illegal?"
"Of course it's illegal! How can a commoner carry a sword? And you
think you can talk big?"
Jokre approached Shirone and opened his coat.
Since it was a serious crime for a commoner to carry a sword, he didn't
hesitate.
As the buttons tore off and the coat opened, the silver sheath of Armand
was revealed at his waist.
"Just as I thought. Pathetic. Do you think this will make you a
noble?"
Shirone's eyes turned cold.
"Step back. And compensate for my coat."
Shirone desperately tried to control his emotions. The inner monster,
sensing his anger, was trying to emerge again.
But Jokre, feeling victorious, continued talking.
"No decorations and short. It must be cheap. Well, where would
someone like you get an expensive sword..."
Jokre reached for the handle.
Since it was short, the blade would be unimpressive. He planned to draw
it and repay the humiliation he had suffered.
But his thoughts ended there.
With a swishing sound, Jokre's movements stopped. His arms slowly
rose.
His eyes trembled with shock, and his skin prickled with cold.
"This... this is..."
Rudvans and Vivian's faces turned pale with shock. Shirone's sword had
drawn itself and was now pressed against Jokre's throat.
"What... how did the sword..."
Shirone was frowning fiercely.
Not because he was angry at Jokre. If he had been even a moment late in
controlling his emotions, Jokre's head would have been rolling on the ice.
'I was too complacent.'
The precise control was an impressive feature, but its synchronization
was so perfect that it was influenced by the demon's actions in the first layer of his mind.
Shirone was a mage who had trained his mind to remain calm in any
situation. But that didn't mean he had no emotional changes.
In a fleeting moment of anger, Armand had drawn itself.
His disappointment in himself made Shirone even angrier.
Sensing its master's anger, Armand trembled, and Jokre heard the sword's
wailing through his eardrums.
Jokre gritted his teeth and glared at Shirone.
"You...!"
"This is your final warning. Just leave. If you act rudely again, I
won't hold back."
Jokre, feeling the pressure of death, wobbled on the ice, unable to keep
his balance.
He would have fallen if Rudvans hadn't come to support him.
"Let's go. We're official mages. If we cause trouble here, we'll be
the ones at a disadvantage."
Though meant to boost Jokre's morale, it was also a practical
statement.
"Hmph! Arrogant brats! Let's see how well you do."
Jokre continued to curse until the end.
But as Rudvans pulled him away by the collar, he only looked more
pathetic.
It was only after Jokre's group had completely left the skating rink
that Shirone sheathed Armand.
"Hey, no matter how angry you are, you shouldn't swing a
sword."
Shirone's eyes narrowed at Dante's words.
"You're kidding, right? This is all your fault! Why did you have to
provoke them? What about my coat? You have to pay for it!"
"You can just sew the buttons back on. By the way, what's with the
sword? Is it magic too?"
Amy, glancing around, spoke up.
"Let's move somewhere else. People are watching. The guards might
come."
For now, people didn't know Shirone was a commoner, so they saw it as a
minor incident. But if the guards came and investigated, things could get complicated.
"How about we go to a café and have some coffee? Since I helped,
you guys can treat me."
"Why should we buy you coffee? And aren't you already drinking
coffee?"
"This is iced coffee. Let's go get something warm."
"Are you a coffee addict?"
Dante winked, holding his iced coffee.
"No, I'm a coffee enthusiast."
Shirone and his friends found a café called 'Cozy Teahouse' in the
shopping district and went inside.
As the warmth enveloped them, their cheeks turned rosy.
Shivering to shake off the cold clinging to his clothes, Shirone sat at
a table and took out a sewing kit.
Dante picked up the menu.
"When you travel, you should try the local specialties. Let's see,
'Warm Ice Queen Coffee'? That's a strange name."
Amy waved her hand dismissively.
"Skip the weird ones. I'll just have orange juice."
"Alright? Then I'll have the Warm Ice Queen Coffee. Shirone, what
about you?"
Shirone, focused on sewing the buttons, replied.
"I'll have hot cocoa."
