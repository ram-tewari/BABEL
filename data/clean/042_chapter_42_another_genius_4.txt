Chapter 42:
Another Genius (4)
"Ha ha! It's fine. We have everything here. As long as we have the items in the
warehouse, we can make anything. Ah, you must never tell anyone. I'm only telling you because I trust
you."
"Gasp! Really?"
Nade, who seemed like an ordinary student at school, was secretly using the warehouse
items illegally. Stunned, I followed Nade into the building. The structure was complex, with multiple
entrances. Moreover, nearly 100 buildings were interconnected, making it feel like navigating a maze.
"Hehe! Cool, right? But there's no need to observe it closely. We change the
building's layout whenever we have time."
"Change the building's layout? Is that possible?"
"This place's official name is the Magic Warehouse Istarus. The building itself is a
magical device. It's much faster to move the building to bring items to the entrance than to search and
retrieve them. It's an engineering design that considers both storage and transportation efficiency. So, on
days when the school has practical training, the structure often changes. That's why no one can find us no
matter what they do."
As Nade finished speaking, she giggled. A hideout where no one could find us. Hearing
this, Shirone's heart began to race.
"Actually, the way to navigate is simple. But since you're not a member yet, I can't
tell you."
Nade walked through the complex paths without hesitation. After passing twenty buildings,
we arrived at a blue-painted area. One building had about ten large and small warehouses, and Nade's
research club occupied the largest one.
"Ta-da! We're here. This is the place."
Shirone approached with a tense expression. What kind of research could possibly require
hiding in such a place?
Upon reaching the iron door, a crooked sign hung on it:
- Supernatural Spiritual Science Research Club -
"Spiritual science?"
Among the countless fields Shirone had considered, spiritual science had never even
crossed his mind.
"How about it? Our club is amazing, right? We literally research supernatural
phenomena. Ghosts, souls, the afterlife, aliens, hell, gods. Stuff like that."
Shirone's bewildered expression gradually faded. Thinking about it, the topic was oddly
intriguing.
'The existence of gods.'
Throughout history, countless people had invoked the name of God, but God had never once
revealed Himself directly to the world.
"Well, this sounds interesting. It might not be magical, but it's worth
researching."
"Haha! Right? I knew you'd get it! Come on in. I'll show you around."
Nade grabbed Shirone's hand and opened the door. Contrary to the expectation of a neat
space for philosophical contemplation, Shirone was greeted by a cloud of dust.
Shirone frowned at the dust bombardment. For this much dust to rise from just opening the
door forcefully was abnormal.
The warehouse was about 15 square meters. The desk had a broken leg, and the table was
littered with bread crumbs and crumpled paper, remnants of meals from who-knows-when. They had supposedly
converted the warehouse into a lab, but it seemed the only thing they'd modified was the sign.
"A bit dirty, right? You'll get used to it. Come in, come in."
Shirone couldn't understand why they didn't clean the warehouse instead of expecting him
to adjust to the mess. But such thoughts vanished the moment he saw the blackboard hanging ahead.
The blackboard, as crooked as the sign, was densely covered with equations that could
make one dizzy. A boy was still rapidly scribbling with chalk.
"Huh?"
Shirone was startled when he saw the face of the boy who turned around after finishing
his writing. It was Iruki, who had been absent from classes for days due to disciplinary action.
***
"Uh? Huh?"
As Shirone stood speechless, Nade awkwardly smiled and explained.
"Haha! Sorry. Actually, our club has a bit of a notorious reputation, so we can't
reveal our members."
That was why the eccentric Iruki was friends with Nade. The problem, however, was the
nature of the club. It was hard to believe that someone as rational and cold as him was part of an
unscientific research club.
"Did Iruki have an interest in this field?"
"Quite the opposite. He approaches it from a critical standpoint. Iruki believes
there's nothing in the world that isn't logical. So, what better prey is there than this?"
Iruki seemed unaware of Shirone's presence as he continued mumbling and pacing. Then,
after briefly glancing at Nade, he approached with a surprised expression.
"Huh? Shirone!"
Without giving Shirone a chance to respond, Iruki strode over, grabbed his shoulders, and
shook them.
"You came at the perfect time. I've just come up with something incredible! So,
here's the thing: splitting the smallest matter. You might wonder how you can split something that's already
the smallest, right? That's exactly my point! If you split it, it's no longer matter! Then what is it? I've
been calculating for three days. And amazingly, the world! There's an incredible amount of energy contained
within. If this is possible, what do you think would happen? Huh? What do you think? Tell me quickly."
His words were so fast that Shirone couldn't catch them properly. But faced with Iruki's
blazing eyes, Shirone felt he had to say something.
"W-well? Energy would be released, right?"
"That's it! An enormous explosion! An explosion powerful enough to wipe out an
entire city! Can you imagine it? Huh? You can imagine it, right?"
Shirone was dumbfounded. Wipe out an entire city? Beyond whether it was possible, the
idea was horrifying.
"But people would die. Everyone living in the city would die."
"What are you talking about? It's an explosion, an explosion! The city would be
blown away!"
"That's exactly it! What would happen to the people living there if the explosion
happened?"
"Huh? The people living there?"
Iruki tilted his head as if he didn't understand the question. Shirone finally realized
and asked again.
"You… don't mean you've never even thought about it, do you?"
"Of course not. What matters is how much explosive power we can generate. Do we
really have to think about that too?"
Shirone felt a chill. It would have been slightly more understandable if Iruki had said
he didn't care if people died. But the fact that he hadn't even considered it was terrifying.
"How can that be? Does it make sense for a mage not to think about the consequences
of their magic?"
"What foolish words, Shirone. That's something for anthropologists to think about. A
genius's duty is to push their capabilities to the limit."
"A genius who can't help but harm others is worse than a fool."
"It's the few geniuses who feed the many fools. History proves it. And so does the
reality we live in. Shirone, I hate it when people spout idealism."
"No, your logic is soaked in defeatism. Ideals can absolutely be realized!"
"They're ideals because they're ideals."
As Shirone and Iruki growled at each other like sworn enemies, Nade awkwardly smiled and
intervened.
"Hey, hey! Let's stop. Discussion is good, but conflict isn't. Let's sit down calmly
and talk about this issue."
"No. I have nothing more to say. Nade, I'm leaving!"
Shirone turned sharply and stormed out of the research club. Nade hurriedly chased after
him.
"Shirone! Wait for me! How will you find your way alone?"
"Leave me alone. The structure hasn't changed, right? I can remember the way back.
If I can't even do that, then I'm truly an idiot."
By the time Nade stepped outside, Shirone was already gone. Returning inside, Nade
frowned.
"What was that about? You could've convinced him in a way he'd understand."
"Who knows. Maybe it's because we're rivals. One of us has to fall."
It was rare for Iruki to use the word "rival." His arrogant personality played
a part, but more than that, he simply had no interest in competition.
Neither women, wealth, nor fame could move him. The only thing he was obsessed with was
equations.
"Shirone is your rival? Are you serious about that?"
Iruki sat on the sofa. As his bottom sank in, dust puffed up.
"You saw it too, right? The teleportation test. The bridge that couldn't be
crossed."
"I saw. That's why he became our classmate."
"The first person to learn teleportation was Level 10. And to escape the Dragon's
Maze, how fast do you think you'd have to think?"
Iruki tapped his head and spoke.
"According to my calculations, the smallest unit of time Shirone split in that
moment was at least 1/10,000th of a second."
"1/10,000th of a second…"
Nade felt dizzy. Just imagining that minuscule gap in time made her head feel like it
would explode.
Iruki let out a metallic laugh.
"That's the mystery of insight. Faster than effort, more precise than knowledge. No
conscious calculation can match the speed of intuition. But hey, Nade."
Madness swirled in Iruki's eyes.
"I wonder… what about me?"
"…"
Shirone's insight versus Iruki's calculation. Which would be faster? Recalling their
clash, Nade shuddered and swallowed hard.
