Chapter 75:
The Return of the Great Archmage (1)
The spot where the light had struck was sunken in a broad, flat depression. Without a
moment's hesitation, the two of them rushed over to examine the traces.
"Wh-what is this? Magic? Or real sorcery?"
"Strange. To imbue light with impact… I thought photon-based fusion was impossible.
Shirone, what's going on?"
Shirone wore a triumphant expression as he spoke.
"It's a new weapon I've mastered this time. Not photon output—it's a photon cannon."
"A photon cannon. Sounds impressive. What's the principle behind it?"
"Well… explaining it would take a while."
As Shirone hesitated, Neid hurried over, his face flushed with excitement, and shook
Shirone's shoulders.
"Out with it! How did you do it?! Embedding physical force into light—this is absolutely
impossible!"
Light and electricity were cousins, so Neid understood exactly what Shirone had
accomplished.
"I'll tell you. But first… how about you come somewhere with me?"
***
Shirone's group entered a restaurant in the noble district. While filling their stomachs
with seafood pasta, Shirone recounted how he had come to master the photon cannon.
The whirlwind of dust that had appeared over the bed, the moment when Ulk was thrown off
the cliff, and the realization of the photon cannon's principle in that instant.
Even without mentioning Armin, the photon cannon was Shirone's own idea, so explaining it
was straightforward.
Neid and Iruki were so engrossed they barely noticed whether the pasta was going into
their mouths or their noses.
After an hour-long explanation, Iruki, who had been lost in thought, set down his fork
and spoke.
"I've heard of gauge symmetry from my father. The gist is this: there's a separate
particle that conveys mass, right? And you've learned to manipulate that particle."
Shirone was once again reminded of how incredible Iruki's father was. To teach his son
about gauge symmetry, of all things—truly fitting for the head of the Dragon Thunder, someone who seemed to
grasp the cutting-edge theories across the entire magical society.
Neid chimed in.
"So, in short, the result of the immortal function is mass. That's why your supernatural
senses disappeared after mastering the photon cannon."
"Yeah. And I don't have nightmares anymore."
Iruki and Neid fell into thought. A particle that conveys mass to matter—Shirone's
realization was a monumental discovery.
The photon cannon, in particular, was a unique magic only Shirone could perform. No one
else could replicate it because it required direct experience from the realm of infinity. Simply
understanding the theory wasn't enough to cast it.
"This is amazing, Shirone. This might be far more significant than we imagine. If you
patent it, you could be sitting on a fortune right now!"
Neid's mind was already buzzing with countless inventions that could be derived from
Shirone's theory.
"That's why I have a favor to ask. Would you come to the library with me?"
"Huh? The library?"
"The school library is off-limits because of my disciplinary action. So I'm thinking of
going to the noble library. I want to research the photon cannon further. But I can't get in alone, so I
need you guys to take me."
"That's no problem. It'll help us too. But why can't you go alone?"
Commoners weren't allowed in the noble library. Once his suspension was lifted, he could
use the school library, but after his trip to the firefly village, he'd realized something. Denying his
identity wouldn't change anything. At the very least, he wanted to be honest with his friends.
"Actually, I'm not a noble."
Neid, who had been chewing pasta, froze with his mouth open. Iruki also seemed shocked,
staring intently at Shirone.
"I'm a commoner. I was abandoned in a stable as a child and raised by my current parents.
My father is just an ordinary mountain hunter."
Though he'd entered the world of magic late, no one would've guessed he was a commoner.
Especially the fact that he'd been abandoned by his birth parents—it didn't match Shirone's untroubled
demeanor at all.
"I'm sorry for not telling you until now. I'll accept any criticism. But I didn't want to
lie anymore."
Neid and Iruki exchanged glances. The emotions they sensed from each other were the same.
Iruki was the first to speak, turning to Shirone.
"It's shocking, honestly. But I don't really feel any different. You're still Shirone,
aren't you?"
"Same here. If I'd known from the start, maybe it'd be different, but hearing it now
doesn't change anything. The time we've spent together doesn't just disappear."
Shirone had hoped his friends would understand, but that was just a personal expectation.
Hearing their acceptance made his heart swell.
Iruki lowered his head and chuckled.
"So that's why you were a special case. Learning magic late because you weren't a noble.
Well, talent isn't something you're born with based on lineage. That's really impressive."
"Thank you. For saying that."
Seeing Shirone's moved expression, Neid waved his hands dismissively.
"Hey, hey. No need to be so serious. I'm actually relieved. I'm glad I became friends
with you without any preconceptions. If I'd known earlier, something like the research presentation a few
days ago would've been impossible."
Iruki shook off his thoughts and stood up from the table. Dragging this out would only
dampen the mood.
"Alright, let's head to the library. I was bored to death of lazing around anyway—this is
perfect. Let's ignite our academic passion properly!"
Shirone and Neid jumped to their feet and cheered.
"Let's do it!"
***
The capital of the Tormia Kingdom, Vashka.
Deep within the underground mountain range surrounding the royal palace lay a dungeon, 70
meters below ground, completed a century ago. Known as the Labyrinth of Darkness, this place had once been
the hideout of the great archmage Viltor Akein, who once ruled the world.
Akein, an authority on dark magic, had clashed with numerous nations before vanishing
without a trace 40 years ago due to a certain incident.
At the time, Akein's mage rank was an unofficial 3rd class.
Once a mage reached 3rd class or higher, regardless of official recognition, they were
granted the title of "Great Archmage."
Mage ranks were determined by achievements and fame, managed by the Magic Association
through a special scoring system.
To rise from 10th to 9th class required 1,000 points combined from achievements and fame.
But to go from 4th to 3rd class required a staggering 280 million points.
In other words, a 3rd-class mage was someone who had amassed unimaginable achievements
and fame.
For comparison, clearing a danger level A dungeon awarded 500 points, while receiving an
invitation to the royal palace and an audience with the king awarded 100 points in fame. This gave a glimpse
of how much Akein had shaken the world.
Akein had reached the rank of unofficial 3rd-class mage at the age of 89, 40 years ago.
At the time, his score was 100 million in achievements and 180 million in fame.
His fame outweighed his achievements because he'd been a troublemaker who turned the
continent upside down.
From forbidden experiments to founding dark organizations and spawning anarchists, he'd
caused enough chaos to destabilize nations.
Even now, elderly men in their seventies shuddered at the mere mention of Akein's name—a
testament to his infamy, even without the 280 million points.
"Huff. Huff."
Inside a crystal tube filled with green liquid, Akein opened his eyes. Naked, his skin
sagged with the wear of 149 years.
'At last… at last, the essence of darkness is complete.'
It was the power of darkness, gathered over 40 years. Akein planned to absorb it and
reclaim his former glory.
Humans aged and died because their cells reached the limit of self-replication. But Akein
had suppressed cell division using a life-support device.
His gleaming eyes turned to the crystal on the desk. Once shining like a diamond, it was
now pitch black. This was the result of injecting it daily with dark magic to accumulate power.
'Alpheas, wait for me! I will repay this humiliation.'
After suffering a fatal wound from Alpheas in his early twenties, Akein's magical power
had sharply declined.
But clinging to vengeance, he had survived. And today, he would regain his former power,
rising once more as a great archmage.
As the green liquid drained from the life-support device, Akein's back hunched further.
His body was so decrepit it seemed ready for a coffin.
The front of the crystal tube split open, and Akein crawled out, gasping for breath.
"Canis! Arin! Are you here?"
A boy and girl who looked youthful entered. Their gloomy aura betrayed their mastery of
dark magic.
"You called, Master."
"Clothes. Bring me my clothes."
