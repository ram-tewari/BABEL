Chapter 14:
First Step Toward the Dream (6)
Kite untied the practice sword from his waist and handed it to Rian. As the real sword
approached, Rian's heart pounded. Though he didn't know what he was supposed to do with it, the intention
behind it was clear.
It was a message: if you want to save your friend, you must be prepared to draw your
sword.
"If you can enter the mansion with this sword, I'll send you off. But if you're not
prepared for that much, don't move from here. It's already out of your hands."
"Master."
A hot smile appeared on Rian's lips as he looked at Kite. He bowed respectfully and
extended both hands, and Kite silently handed over the sword.
"Thank you. I won't forget this kindness."
"Remember this. Abandoning your friend is abandoning another part of yourself."
"Yes!"
Rian fastened the sword to his waist and ran toward the mansion. Kite watched the scene
with satisfaction. This was the end. The time for farewell had come a little early, but he was glad they
could part ways with a manly spirit.
May good fortune follow his disciple's future.
***
A chilling atmosphere filled the mansion. Work had stopped, and the butlers and maids
stood in place without making a sound.
Behind the sofa where the direct family members sat, Head Butler Louis adjusted his
glasses. Next to him, Sub-Butler Temuran glared at Shirone with a stern gaze.
Shirone knelt before the direct family members, enduring countless hostile and
contemptuous stares. It felt like he had stepped into the middle of a colosseum.
"Head Butler, report."
"Yes, Head of the Family. Temuran hired Shirone, who has resided here for a year and
six months, and is now accused of disrespecting the authority of the Ozent family. Many servants have
witnessed Shirone associating with the youngest young master, and I myself have heard Shirone speaking
casually to him. The detailed evidence will be attached in the testimony documents prepared by
Temuran."
Bishop silently moved his hand behind his back. As he received the documents Louis handed
him, he flipped through them roughly with sharp, deliberate motions.
While his father read the documents, Reina quietly approached Shirone and whispered in
his ear.
"Shirone, don't worry too much. I'll try to persuade Dad. Nothing will happen."
Shirone slowly raised his head, his face eerily calm. It was so unsettling that it sent
shivers down Reina's spine. Even in this situation, there wasn't a trace of fear in his expression.
'He's not pretending to be calm. He genuinely isn't afraid.'
Shirone wasn't afraid. His mind was currently floating outside the cliff. The future was
nothing more than an illusion that didn't exist yet. Nothing had been decided.
After skimming through the documents, Bishop spoke. With his pale complexion befitting
the black-haired lineage, his voice carried a chilling aura.
"Arian Shirone, is the content of these documents true?"
"Dad, Shirone and Rian—"
"Reina, I wasn't asking you. I'll give him a chance to speak. Stay quiet for
now."
Reina thought it wouldn't be easy. Bishop, who based his authority on principles and cold
rationality, wasn't someone who could be persuaded by emotions.
"There's one thing I'd like to ask."
Shirone spoke in a calm voice. The abnormal composure surprised the watching servants,
but it had no effect on Bishop.
"Speak."
"If I and the youngest young master are friends, what happens to him?"
Even in a life-threatening situation, Shirone's first concern was his friend. Reina's
face softened affectionately at his kindness.
"It won't affect him at all. This gathering is to interrogate you, a commoner. No
matter what crime Rian has committed, as long as it's a family matter, we will protect and shield him."
Bishop's words were laced with thorns. It was a warning not to think that a noble's son
would be treated the same as a commoner.
But Shirone was satisfied with that. Shedding the last of his worries, Shirone smiled
brightly and readily admitted the truth.
"Then that's a relief. It's true that Rian and I are friends. We've built a good
friendship over the past year and will continue to do so."
No one showed it outwardly, but Shirone's words caused a huge stir. Especially for the
servants who had lived with him, the shock was immense when something they had doubted turned out to be
true.
A noble family's child and a hunter's son had been friends for a year. It was the kind of
thing you'd only see in a play, yet it had actually happened.
"The family's authority has fallen to the ground. Shirone, as the head of the Ozent
family, I will execute you."
"Dad! Wait, listen to me! You said you'd give him a chance to speak!"
"He confessed himself. Dragging this out further is pointless. Head Butler."
"Yes, Head of the Family."
"Carry out Shirone's execution. And this matter must never leak outside."
"Understood."
Reina grew frantic. She was almost resentful of Shirone for remaining so calm in this
situation.
But how could she stop it? Her father had never once reversed a decision he made as the
family head.
"Who dares to execute him without my permission!"
Rian burst into the mansion, kicking the door open as if to break it. Just his entrance
alone seemed to raise the temperature in the mansion.
Everyone knew Rian's temperament, and that was precisely the power of fire he possessed.
"Who's trying to execute my friend without my permission?"
"I am."
Bishop cut him off sharply.
"Why are you doing this? He hasn't committed a capital crime—he just became friends
with me!"
"You don't need to commit a capital crime to die. A noble's circumstances are
complicated. It's because we handle those complicated circumstances that you've grown up enjoying privileges
as a noble's son."
"If those privileges come at the cost of killing a friend, then I refuse them!"
"How naive, Rian. A friend is someone you share mutual feelings with on equal
footing. Do you think that child considers you a friend? How can you believe that? For example, what if he
approached you for money? Even then, you wouldn't know. And yet, you still believe in him?"
"Shirone isn't the kind of person to do something so low!"
"That's exactly what I'm asking. How can you believe that? Show me proof that
Shirone is that kind of person."
Reina, who had been listening to the conversation, bit her lip. Rian was being dragged
into Bishop's rhetoric. There was no proof that Shirone was a true friend. Whether he had approached with
ulterior motives or genuine friendship, Shirone's attitude wouldn't have shown any difference.
Rian also felt a chill at his father's logic. It was cold. Asking for proof of
friendship. It was so cold it felt like he might freeze.
Rian gripped the real sword at his waist. His master's heat seemed to transfer through
his hand.
"Hahahaha!"
And then he laughed heartily. He had finally understood his master's true intention. The
sword hadn't been given to him to throw a tantrum.
"Dad, you're really something. I almost fell for it. Proof? I believe in him. Even
if Shirone doesn't consider me a friend, as long as I believe in him, Shirone is my friend. That's the
clearest proof there is!"
This time, Bishop grew heated. After all, Rian's words were also true.
"So you're defying the family head's orders? This isn't just a matter between father
and son. If you disobey the family head's command, you could be disowned."
Bishop had drawn a line in the sand. Disowning his own son. The butlers, and even the
direct family members, couldn't help but be taken aback by this statement.
But Rian didn't even blink. As if facing a superior officer rather than family, he
expressed his will with unprecedented formality.
"If disownment is the price I must pay to protect my friend, I'll gladly accept it
with a smile."
Bang!
Bishop stomped his foot on the floor. It was unacceptable. His 17-year-old son was
willing to be disowned? It was a tantrum. A disrespectful act. He was exploiting the weakness of the
parent-child relationship to play hardball.
"Draw your sword. If you're willing to be disowned, it means you're ready to abandon
even your family, right? If you truly stand for your friend, stake your life on it."
Rian drew his sword. That action only angered Bishop further.
"Are you really pointing a sword at your own father?"
"This sword isn't meant to cut you down. It's a sword to protect my beliefs."
"Since when have you cherished friends? You didn't even mingle with noble peers.
Because they shone too brightly. You're using a commoner inferior to you as a sacrifice to comfort
yourself."
"No. This time, you're wrong, Dad. Shirone is a friend who shines brighter than me.
I want to help Shirone shine even more."
Bishop was dumbfounded. What had that child done? What kind of words had he used to
bewitch his simple-minded son into completely submitting?
"Was it you? Did you convince Rian to become like this? Did you persuade him that
nobles and commoners can be friends?"
Even as the family teetered on the brink of disaster, Shirone remained calm.
"Yes. I told him that."
Rian stepped in front of Shirone and spoke.
"Shirone, stay back. I'll handle this. No matter what happens, I won't let
you—"
"Rian."
Shirone cut him off. The cold tone, unlike anything Rian had heard before, made him turn
around with a bewildered expression.
"You must take responsibility for what's yours to take."
His voice, tone, demeanor, and intent—everything was as cold as Bishop's, if not more so.
Even Rian felt the chill, let alone the family.
"Huh? Oh, yeah. Of course."
"But..."
Shirone continued.
"You don't need to take responsibility for what isn't yours. We've always done
everything together to get this far. This time, we'll overcome it together too."
As if it had never happened, Shirone's face softened warmly.
"Even if it means death, I don't regret becoming friends with you."
Shirone's final words exploded in Rian's already burning heart. Tears welled up. At this
point, he could no longer see clearly.
"That's it! Even if I'm cast out, we're friends!"
The butlers began to murmur. Even those who had initially sneered now couldn't decide
whose side to take.
***
'How impressive, Shirone.'
Reina, who had been watching Shirone with her chin propped on her hand, smiled. With a
single strict word, he had cowed the adults in the room. Moreover, he had made Rian's fiery temper burn even
brighter.
In a way, he might be the most similar in personality to their father. Yet, the reason
Shirone still shone was that his words were filled with sincerity, not calculated strategy.
"Bwahaha! This is a masterpiece! To think I'd get this excited in my later years!
Youth really is the best!"
The laughter was so loud it seemed to shake the mansion, making the maids scream. In
contrast, the direct family members sighed and turned around upon hearing the voice.
A blue-haired old man with a buzz cut stood on the second-floor balcony.
Shirone stared blankly. Even if you called him Rian's final evolved form, it wouldn't be
an exaggeration. His massive frame and bulging arm muscles seemed ready to burst.
It was Ozent Clump, Rian's grandfather.
"Grandfather!"
Reina stood up with the expression of someone who had just received divine
reinforcements. She and Rian alone hadn't been enough to persuade the black-haired family head, but with
their grandfather here, things would be different.
"You've arrived, Father. Reina said it would take a few more days."
"Kehehe! My friend ran off with a young bride, so I came early. But this place is as
lively as ever. Youth really is something."
"This isn't a good thing. The family's dignity has fallen to the ground."
"Is that so? From what I've been watching, everyone seems to have a lot to say. Why
don't we hear everyone's opinions before deciding whether our dignity has fallen or not?"
Reina cheered inwardly, 'Grandfather is the best!' Though Clump had already passed the
family head position to his son, his status as a publicly recognized third-grade swordsman was enough to
earn the family's respect.
"Then I'll speak first. I only met Shirone yesterday, but he was clever and
talented. Also, as a contract worker, he fulfilled all his duties and was supposed to leave the family
today. Given these circumstances, it's hard to believe Shirone had any ill intentions toward Rian, and thus,
I don't think he has disgraced the family's honor."
Reina gave a short but firm opinion and sat down. But the tide hadn't turned yet. At
best, the scales had balanced.
Though the blue-haired bond was stronger than the black-haired one, Clump, who had once
been the family head, was fair-minded. He gave the opposing side, Rai, a chance to speak as well.
"Then what does our second grandson think?"
Rai answered in a cold tone.
"I dislike that child."
The butlers began to murmur. This was none other than the family's most promising member
speaking.
"But I don't want to bother with him. It's none of my business."
Bishop asked, puzzled.
"What do you mean? You dislike him but don't want to bother with him?"
"It's exactly as I said. I don't like that child. But I don't want to lie about my
feelings just because I dislike him. I said I don't want to bother with him because I don't want to bother
with him. No matter who Rian befriends, I just want to end this matter quickly and go to the royal
palace."
Reina clicked her tongue. Even if he was her younger brother, his self-centeredness ran
deep. But this time, his personality might work in Rian's favor.
Clump chuckled like a boy. He enjoyed seeing each family member's unique personality.
Moreover, this was precisely the strength of the Ozent family, with its two mixed bloodlines.
"Then lastly, let's hear the opinion of the sub-butler who has observed Shirone the
longest."
Temuran stepped forward. Louis gave him a sidelong glance, signaling him to choose his
words carefully. The family could say whatever they wanted, but for a butler, the most important person was
his employer, Bishop.
"Shirone has perfectly carried out all the tasks I assigned him over the past year
and six months."
Louis's brow furrowed unpleasantly. All he had to do was fabricate one sentence. This was
why he could never rise above being a sub-butler.
"Did you know they were friends?"
"I knew the youngest young master often went out with him, but I didn't know they
were friends. But in my humble opinion, if he had been a reckless child without discernment, he wouldn't
have been able to organize ten thousand books."
Shirone's eyes widened in surprise. He had never dreamed Temuran thought of him that way.
Head Butler Louis asked angrily, "So you're taking Shirone's side now?"
Temuran, seasoned as a butler, bowed with an obsequious smile.
"Not at all. I, too, was shocked that a contract worker like Shirone had befriended
the youngest young master. I am merely stating the facts as a sub-butler."
With this, the verdict was effectively over. Bishop was the only one still insisting on
punishing Shirone. No matter how much authority the family head had, he couldn't go against the united voice
of the family.
Rian, his tension easing, let out a deep breath. Shirone also felt relieved and smiled at
Rian.
But not everything was over yet.
"I cannot accept this."
Clump's final decision.
"Father."
"Grandfather!"
Bishop and Rian called out to Clump simultaneously, but the emotions in their voices were
polar opposites.
"I understand that the two of you acknowledge each other as friends. But that's as
far as it goes. If this relationship continues, rumors will spread, and other nobles might make a meal of
it. So, Rian and Shirone, think of this as a good memory and end your friendship here."
Reina was disappointed in her grandfather but had no rebuttal. Family matters were
important. Saving Shirone's life was already a good outcome.
Clump met Rian's fiery gaze with a cheerful smile. He knew how much his grandson adored
him, but Rian also had a duty to protect the family's honor. The fact that no one objected to this decision
proved it.
"Shirone, what do you say? I'm grateful you taught my foolish grandson about
friendship, but can you let him go now? It's hard for nobles and commoners to mingle as friends."
Shirone, who had been staring at the marble floor, spoke.
"I can't do that."
The unexpected bold answer made Bishop, Reina, Rai, and even Clump raise their eyebrows
in surprise. But no one was more shocked than Rian.
"Shi, Shirone..."
"If I, a commoner, am an obstacle in Rian's path, I don't mind never seeing him
again. If you still doubt me, you can even kill me. But that doesn't mean we weren't friends. Because Rian
and I are friends."
Tears streamed down Rian's face. They were tears of shame. A part of him had been
relieved just that Shirone wouldn't die. In the end, it was he who had lacked trust.
Finally making up his mind, Rian raised his sword and shouted.
"I hereby declare!"
The family's expressions darkened with unease. This wasn't like Shirone's case. Whenever
Rian got this excited, unexpected disasters always followed.
Their unease was spot on. Rian turned to Shirone. And before he could utter a word, he
reversed his grip on the sword and plunged it into the floor in a flash.
The long sword pierced through the marble floor. Shirone stared blankly at his reflection
in the blade.
At that moment, Rian knelt on one knee and bowed his head to Shirone.
"Ozent Rian of the Ozent family, from this moment onward, shall become Shirone's
sword."
"What is this, you fool!"
Bishop forgot his dignity and stood up, pointing angrily. Rian's actions were utterly
absurd. Even Reina, who had been on Rian's side, looked incredulous.
"Are you... making a knight's vow?"
A knight's vow.
Nobles pledge loyalty to the royal family in exchange for stipends, but if you go back in
time, royalty itself was once just nobility. Thus, royal authority could always be overthrown, and a
knight's loyalty was limited to the stipends and honor granted by royalty.
But a knight's vow was different. It was a decision to reduce oneself to a mere sword, a
pledge to become the sword of the lord one would serve for life.
A noble who defies the king is just a rebel. But a knight who breaks a knight's vow is
not only stripped of humanity but is also recorded in history as a stain on their family for generations.
Once in a lifetime. Rian had just made that weighty pledge to Shirone.
Bishop's blood boiled. Once a knight's vow was made, it couldn't be undone. Even without
emphasizing the gravity of the vow, breaking it would undoubtedly brand him as the world's biggest fool.
"Rian, what are you doing?"
Shirone asked, sounding shocked. But Rian couldn't raise his head out of guilt. He had
wavered for a moment. Compared to Shirone, who had staked his life to protect the name of friendship, all he
had done was talk.
"What do you think? Now there's no going back, right? We're in this to the
end."
Compared to Shirone's resolve, a knight's vow was nothing. Rian looked around at his
family with a satisfied expression, sword in hand. He had never seen such faces before. It was a huge mess,
but it felt exhilarating.
Clump stroked his massive chin, lost in thought. Honestly, he hadn't expected such
foolishness. But he didn't feel displeased—perhaps because Rian took after him.
'Well, you have to be this foolish to be truly foolish. Hehehe.'
Burying his mixed feelings deep, Clump asked solemnly.
"Rian, if you're willing to become his sword, does that mean that boy is worthy of
it?"
"More than worthy."
"Then answer me. Is the standard of that worthiness the boy's greatness or your own
inadequacy?"
Rian pondered. Was Shirone exceptional? Or was he lacking? No matter what conclusion he
reached, it wouldn't satisfy the Ozents. Why had his grandfather asked such a question?
"A sword does not judge. It only protects."
Clump was satisfied. As Rian said, a sword does not think. It only needs to cut through
in the sharpest way its master desires.
"You have a way with words, for someone so lacking in swordsmanship. I pity your
friend for getting a sword like you."
"Grandfather!"
Rian glared and shouted. But the tension from before was gone. Partly because he had
already taken an irreversible path, but mostly because everyone was too exhausted to care.
Clump, true to his straightforward personality, wrapped things up cleanly.
"Enough. This ends here. Help your master up. Shirone, Rian is still an unpolished
sword, but he won't cut his master's feet. Take good care of my grandson from now on."
Shirone bowed silently. Humility here would only hurt the Ozent family's pride.
"He is a friend far beyond my worth. Thank you for allowing it."
"That boy did it on his own. What can I do? I heard your contract has ended. You
were planning to return home, right?"
"Yes. My parents must be waiting."
"You must miss them. But now that you two have pledged to be friends for life, it
would be a shame to send you back like this. Hey, Head Butler."
"Yes, Elder."
"Send the carriage Shirone was supposed to take back now. Bring his parents
here."
"Understood."
The head butler left the mansion immediately after answering. Whether it was the family
head or anyone else, this matter was now far beyond his hands.
Louis once again realized how unfathomable noble thinking was.
***
Shirone chatted with Rian in his room. From being summoned in the morning and made to
kneel, it was now noon. It felt like too much had happened in less than half a day.
"Kehehe, did you see our family's faces earlier? It was so satisfying."
"By the way, Rian, are you really okay with this? That knight's vow thing. I feel
like you might have gone too far for my sake."
Rian's playful expression turned serious. Even as foolish as he was, Rian understood the
weight of a knight's vow.
"Shirone, I didn't make that vow just because we're friends. You're different from
my brother. You have a magnetism that draws people in. I've entrusted my life to someone like that."
Shirone felt Rian's sincerity. But he still hated awkwardness. Rian thought the only way
forward was to deploy the underwear strategy again.
"Alright. I'll make the suggestion. But the condition will probably be that you
enroll in the swordsmanship school. Dad exploits weaknesses thoroughly."
"Tch! Whatever. I'll think about it."
Rian knew his father's personality all too well. For Shirone's sake, he could easily
enroll in the swordsmanship school.
Come to think of it, he needed it for himself too. He had lost to a magic aspirant with a
sword. At this rate, he'd become a burden to Shirone rather than his sword.
Someone knocked on the door, interrupting their conversation. A butler nervously opened
the door.
"Young Master Shirone's family has just arrived. They are to be brought to the
mansion, but the family head ordered me to inform you in case you wish to greet them."
"Oh! Shirone's parents? What do you want to do, Shirone?"
It was a needless question. Shirone's eyes were already fixed on the mansion's unseen
front gate. He was practically bouncing in his seat, ready to run out immediately.
"Hehe. Go greet them, Shirone. We'll wait in the parlor. Escort Shirone,
please."
"Understood, Miss."
Shirone followed the butler out of the mansion.
The butler's attitude, which until yesterday had been "Hey, you!" had changed
completely, much to Shirone's discomfort. He seemed overly worried about Shirone tripping on a pebble.
"Um, you can act like usual. It's not like I've become a noble or anything."
"Not at all! How could I be so rude to the master of the youngest young master?
Please don't say such things. My head will roll."
Shirone, who had once staked his life on a single book, understood that feeling better
than anyone. But that was precisely why he didn't want such treatment.
"I won't tell anyone even if you make a mistake, so please don't do this."
The butler nodded repeatedly, moved. A commoner becoming a noble's master. To the
servants, Shirone was nothing short of a hero from legend.
At any rate, Shirone didn't pay it any more mind. No, he couldn't. At the end of the path
symbolizing Ozent, a carriage had just arrived.
Two people stepped out. At that moment, Shirone's vision blurred.
His parents, whom he hadn't seen in a year and six months, were there. Memories of
enduring hardships and staying up all night in the library flashed through his mind, and tears of sorrow
poured out.
His legs moved on their own, driven by overwhelming emotion. Not even hearing the
butler's calls, Shirone ran toward the mansion's iron gates.
"Mom! Dad!"
