Chapter 518:
The Nameless Swordsman (2)
Before Fox's menacing gaze, Rian slowly averted his eyes.
One of the things he'd learned from his time on the battlefield was that
avoiding fights you can avoid is often the best course of action.
Actually, that's a lie.
He had seen many men who were unafraid of death and who maintained their
convictions until the end.
'What exactly is the problem?'
It wasn't the simple fear of pain or death.
It was the uncertainty in his sword.
The anxiety of constantly throwing himself into an uncertain future was,
at times, more terrifying than the thought of losing his life.
Fox, observing Rian's stiff face, seemed to realize something and burst
into laughter.
"Heh, a wet-behind-the-ears brat."
It was a common scenario.
Young men who had learned a bit of swordsmanship and used their puny
strength to act tough in front of ordinary people.
Though, even among thieves, there were those who didn't act this way.
But Fox's skills were on an entirely different level.
Above all, he could wield the Skima.
"For now, we'll settle the score later…"
Fox mumbled as he suddenly turned his head and glared at Sylvia.
"What's going on, Sylvia? After all I've done for you, you bring in
someone else? Looks like you're just like every other woman after all."
Sylvia's face crumpled in disgust at the vile misunderstanding.
"You're despicable. I don't want to hear such words from someone
trying to steal another man's wife."
Fox's face flushed red.
Since he had given up being a soldier and become a thief, he had let go
of everything. But when he thought of Sylvia's husband, his last shred of conscience always gnawed at
him.
"Shut up! He's dead! He's already gone from this
world!"
Fox summoned the magic words to shake off his guilt.
"And I've liked you since I was young. I want to love
you."
"Having interest in a married woman with a daughter isn't love.
It's just filthy obsession."
"Filthy obsession?"
Fox's face turned as cold as if he were a different person.
"Is that what you think? I could have had you any time. But you
call it filthy obsession? Fine, from now on, I won't hold back."
Fox's malevolent energy radiated as he approached, but Liz stepped in
front of Sylvia.
"Don't bother my mom! Dad won't forgive you!"
Fox's cold gaze pierced Liz.
"Liz, I'm not trying to bother your mother. I'm trying to save her
from this hellish life. Your father will surely thank me for it."
Sylvia was on the verge of dizziness from the shameful words.
"You're out of your mind. You're crazy."
"Heh, maybe I am. But what's changed if I haven't gone crazy? If I
could have you, I'd rather go mad."
Realizing there was no way to convince Fox, Sylvia shoved Liz out the
door and screamed.
"Liz! Run!"
She then grabbed a soup pot from the table and threw it at Fox.
"Ugh!"
Fox slammed the pot with his thick fist, and in that moment, Sylvia
suddenly darted in front and stabbed a fork into his abdomen.
Fox flinched slightly but calmly inspected the wound.
It wasn't enough to pierce through his steel-like muscles with a
household utensil.
"Arrogant..."
Fox slapped Sylvia across the face, sending her crashing to the
ground.
"Mom! Mom!"
Fox ignored Liz's cries.
"Don't blame me. You brought this on yourself."
"Stop it."
Fox turned his gaze, and Rian, who had been sitting with an annoyed
expression, stood up from his chair.
Rian had a well-balanced body, and seeing him stand, it was clear that
his size wasn't to be underestimated.
"Are you talking to me?"
"No matter how crazy you are, you shouldn't be doing this in front
of a child."
"... You're right."
Fox twisted his neck as he spoke.
"First, I'll deal with you. How about it? If you're confident, come
at me."
The moment Rian accepted the challenge, the weight of responsibility
fell on his shoulders.
"..."
Rian looked at Liz.
Her wide eyes filled with hope, an expression of selfishness only a
child could show.
"Let's go. I don't want to stain this house with
blood."
Rian proposed the fight, and Fox's mouth twitched into a smirk.
It was clear that Rian's size couldn't be ignored.
"Ha, you want to play tough. Fine, let's go. Let's show the
villagers who the real owner of this house is."
As they stepped outside, the villagers, having heard the commotion, hid
out of Fox's line of sight, peeking out to observe the situation.
Rian surveyed the twelve thieves surrounding him.
There would be no fair fight or equal numbers.
'Twelve against one, huh?'
Fox swung his large sword and shouted.
"Hey, you rookie swordsman! What are you waiting for? Are you
waiting for someone to ring the bell for you?"
Striking first is always effective, but unless you're ready to die,
there's no need for the side with the advantage in numbers to rush in.
Knowing that, Rian slowly pointed his great sword at Fox.
The heavy iron blade extended far beyond its center of gravity, and the
thieves' faces turned pale.
It was a lethal display of strength.
"Deputy, this guy, is he actually that strong?"
Fox's face twisted in frustration as he overheard his subordinate's
whisper.
Saying such things in front of the enemy would only lower morale.
"Shut up. He's alone. If twelve of us rush him, anyone would
die."
Despite showing his overwhelming strength, Rian's heart was filled with
unease.
When he knew nothing, he had rushed into battle, believing victory was
all that mattered. But as his understanding of the sword deepened, he realized how reckless and insane
his actions had been.
'It's all been luck so far. My sword isn't perfect. Maybe... I could
die.'
Even with a band of veterans on the battlefield, Rian's skill was enough
to overcome them.
But could he really?
The endless questions about his sword filled his mind.
With no confidence, he couldn't act. And because he couldn't act,
mistakes happened in critical moments.
'Is my sword truly right? Can I really... defeat them?'
As the thieves watched Rian's face pale, their expressions changed to
confusion and then laughter.
"Ha! What's this? Looks like he's scared! Just a big fool with
strength after all."
"Hey, what are you going to do? You're going to die
now."
As Rian's momentum faltered, the thieves closed in, ready to
strike.
"Brother..."
Liz's face showed disappointment, and Sylvia, who had been praying for
Rian's victory, also grew pale.
"Kill him!"
As the thieves charged, Rian instinctively looked behind him, noticing a
sword coming at his head.
"Ugh!"
Blocking with his great sword, Rian's balance was pushed back, and
without checking the position of his opponent, he swung his sword wildly.
'This is a childish sword, Rian.'
Divine Transcendence is a technique of the Yaksha that draws its power
from rage.
When uncertain about his sword, Rian found it difficult to focus his
will entirely on one action.
"Why aren't you attacking?! Just go and stab him!"
Several swords fell above Rian's head, some aiming at his
abdomen.
'I can't dodge!'
With that certainty, Rian twisted his body and raised his great sword
above his head.
Contrary to his expectations, his defense and evasion were perfectly
executed.
But still, Rian wasn't satisfied with the outcome and fell into
self-loathing.
'It's just luck. This isn't skill.'
As if throwing a lasso, Rian swung his great sword, decapitating two
thieves with a sharp crack.
'This isn't the right angle. It's just luck.'
The thieves didn't see what Rian saw.
Even Fox, who could wield the Skima, couldn't understand the simple
truth hidden in the high level of swordsmanship Rian was displaying.
"Dammit! That was a lucky strike!"
As Rian stepped forward, he swung his great sword diagonally, and two
thieves were sliced into pieces with a loud crash.
As the number of thieves decreased, Fox, realizing the danger, raised
his sword and rushed in to decapitate Rian himself.
With the leader joining in, the thieves' momentum overwhelmed Rian, but
the fight remained tense.
'I've gone too deep. This isn't swordsmanship.'
Though Rian didn't even feel like he had cut anyone, the number of
thieves was steadily dwindling.
There was no feeling of winning.
He wanted to fully unleash the trajectory in his mind, but there was no
way to prove its reality.
He wasn't even sure if he was practicing swordsmanship or merely
flailing like a madman.
Clang!
The sharp sound of steel snapped Rian back to reality, and he saw that
11 thieves were dead, leaving only Fox standing.
"Dammit!"
Rian's relentless assault had pushed Fox back.
Despite massively enhancing his physical abilities through the Skima,
Fox could only focus on avoiding Rian's attacks.
The difference in power between someone using the Skima and someone
without it was as large as the gap between species.
Not feeling that difference meant that the technical gap was
immense.
'What is this guy? He's no ordinary swordsman.'
What was even more frustrating was that, despite his skills, Rian's eyes
held no glimmer of hope.
'No! I'm wrong! Both this and that, they're all wrong!'
Rian saw what Fox could not.
That's why every strike felt like a desperate, life-or-death
gamble.
'No.'
He didn't possess the genius insight that would allow him to strike with
the shortest distance.
The only way for him to advance to the next level was to continually
refine his technique, adjusting the slightest margin of error after hundreds or thousands of
swings.
'This isn't it.'
But he could see it.
An ideal state that couldn't be explained with words.
Because that vision lingered in his mind, all his standards became
muddled.
"This isn't it!"
As Rian erupted in frustration, Divine Transcendence activated for the
first time.
With a single slash, he cut through Fox's raised sword, cleaving his
torso vertically before the great sword finally stopped.
An eerie silence, as if the world had paused.
As Fox's body fell sideways with a dull thud, the villagers, who had
been watching from outside, slowly began to enter the building.
Rian had single-handedly cut down 12 thieves.
In a village where there was not a single blade of grass to eat, such a
master would never be seen.
"Ha! Ha!"
Rian, for the first time since the battle began, felt a sense of relief.
But no matter how much he tried to recall, he couldn't remember the position he was in just moments
ago.
'What the hell was that…?'
Sheathing his sword, Rian looked around, and aside from Sylvia and Liz,
no one else was around.
'Did I win?'
No, he had survived.
By sheer luck, by the grace of chance, he had survived.
But how long could he keep enduring this way?
"Ah..."
When Sylvia's eyes met Rian's, she involuntarily let out a sigh.
Her gaze held the weight of every sorrow in the world.
But Rian, as if not wanting pity, turned his gaze back to the bodies
scattered around.
'I've really done it now.'
He couldn't even take care of himself, and now he had to deal with this
mess.
"Clean up the bodies."
Rian told the villagers, who would likely be the ones to hear it.
"If you don't want to take responsibility, it's better to do it
now."
No one wanted to take responsibility for today's events, so Rian didn't
bother to explain further.
"Let's go. Tell me everything."
Rian said this as he started walking back to his house, with Sylvia
following behind.
Meanwhile, Liz remained where she was, staring blankly at the horrific
scene Rian had left behind.
"That brother... he really is strong, isn't he?"
