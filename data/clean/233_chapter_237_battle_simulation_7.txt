'Summoning magic. That's definitely tricky.'
Summoning mages can summon creatures through a unique and difficult
magic called Yearning (상사/相思/Sangsa).
The first entity that a summoning mage learns to summon is
themselves.
This is referred to as a doppelgänger, and once this is successful, the
doppelgänger has the potential to transform into a third entity.
Summoning something means you have a complete understanding of that
entity.
This is the essence of Yearning, which involves three stages.
The first is the Engraving Stage, where the mage uses all five senses to
observe the entity they intend to summon, imprinting the image in their mind.
Since the summoner must perfectly remember the appearance of the
summoned creature, summoning mages possess observational skills that far exceed those of ordinary
people.
A representative exercise for this is called the Arrangement
Method.
This involves mixing 30 sticks that differ in length by only one
millimeter each and arranging them in order of length within one minute.
The second stage is called the Empathy Stage, where the mage studies the
behavior of the entity to gain complete confidence in their understanding.
Although some mages can do this with their vast knowledge, most mages
tend to develop this understanding as they live together.
If any living creature spends more than a year in an isolated place, its
characteristics are bound to be imprinted in your mind.
The simpler the creature's intelligence, the easier it is to grasp and
clear this stage.
The third stage is the Extinction Stage, the most crucial part.
In this stage, the mage kills the imprinted entity, erasing its physical
existence.
However, if the entity appears in reality afterward, the summoning mage
can manifest it through the doppelgänger.
Killing the entity is not always necessary. The key is to create a state
where they can 'never be seen again'.
The more this state is reinforced, the more vivid the summoned familiar
becomes.
It was a gruesome feeling to kill an entity with which you had built a
deep connection over many years, but keeping it alive was also risky.
For instance, there was a case where a female mage hesitated during the
Extinction Stage when trying to summon Opoi, a monster that could cast healing magic.
In the end, she managed to clear the Extinction Stage by selling Opoi to
a monster collector. However, five years later, she heard through some channels that Opoi was still
alive, and as a result, she lost her ability to summon Opoi.
Summoned familiars are categorized from 10th tier to 1st tier based on
the level of Yearning achieved. According to the Magic Association's research, there are 13,872 types of
familiars in the lowest 10th tier, but only three are listed in the 1st tier.
It is a well-known fact that one of the 1st tier familiars is the Lich,
the archmage of the undead monster world.

The reason this caused such a stir is that to clear the Extinction

ability to die.
Some speculated that Zulu herself became the Lich and merely summoned a
