Chapter 322:
Fleeting Rift (4)
As they ascended to the top floor, Ordan's mouth did not stop moving.
Although most of what he said was trivial, Kangnan listened without showing annoyance.
"What percentage of Thormia's population are graduates from magic
schools?"
"I believe it's about 72 percent."
"Ah, similar to Yakma then. That's a good sign. School graduates
are reliable. Those who come from the streets know only one thing, their adaptability is low, what good
would hiring such people do?"
Kangnan was displeased when someone unrelated to magic commented so
freely on the association's matters.
"But perhaps improvisation in real-world situations could be
considered higher, couldn't it?"
"Ha! Improvisation? That's just pettiness."
'Dirty bureaucracy.'
Kangnan's lips twisted.
She too wasn't a school graduate. Indeed, how could one who only sees
the numbers on paperwork understand reality?
Upon entering the reception room, Kangnan sighed as she looked around.
Even though a foreign diplomat had arrived, someone who was supposed to be on the same floor hadn't yet
appeared.
"Please wait here. I will bring the association
president."
As if it were the predetermined course of action, Kangnan diffused the
situation and exited the reception room.
Her pace quickened from that moment.
The sound of her high heels, full of killing intent, stopped abruptly.
She turned precisely at a right angle and knocked on the door of the president's office.
"This is Kangnan. I'm entering."
No response came, not that she expected any. Kangnan forcefully turned
the doorknob and entered.
The room, filled with stifling heat and the smell of sweat, assailed her
senses. The sight of the man sitting on the sofa, his groin indecently accentuated by tight square-cut
briefs and his legs shamelessly spread, offended her.
The man, a magician who should abstain from tobacco, was clenching a
particularly potent cigar between his teeth while gazing at the ceiling. He looked as if he had just
finished exercising, his chest heaving.
His wide chest and lean waist, uncharacteristic of a typical magician,
his thighs as solid as rock, and his calves sharply defined, did not fit the mold.
However, if any woman other than Kangnan saw the man's body, they might
scream. It was covered in scars, looking as if hundreds of worms had writhed over him.
"What do you want, mongrel?"
Kangnan's expression grew fierce.
She had met this man when she was wandering the world alone after the
annihilation of the Wolf Tribe and had started a new life with him, but now he seemed not a benefactor
but a sworn enemy.
"What do you mean what do I want? I told you that an urgent
emissary from Yakma has arrived. They are waiting in the reception room; what are you doing
here?"
The man's head slowly rose from behind the sofa.
Despite his muscular body, his face was rugged, with pronounced
cheekbones. A scar ran vertically over his left eye, and his lower jaw was also covered in scars,
indicating his long sufferings.
Micae Gaold, the president of the Thormia Magic Association.
He was a first-class grand magician of the Red Line and a founding
member of the Alpheus Magic School's Supernatural Psychics Research Society.
"An emissary... came?"
"Don't you remember? I told you just an hour ago! Go and receive
them now."
Gaold seemed lost in thought.
He couldn't remember. Lately, it seemed he was forgetting more
frequently.
What did it matter?
He deeply inhaled his cigar and said curtly,
"No."
Kangnan closed her eyes. She had to be patient.
Thinking of it as saving a pitiful wretch, she reopened her eyes and
pulled out an official document sealed with the royal emblem.
"His Highness Adolf himself issued this order. The matter is
serious and requires your cooperation regardless of the situation. If you still refuse, I will proceed
according to the law."
Gaold leaned back on the sofa, unconcerned, continuing to puff on his
cigar with annoyance.
"It's damn troublesome. Can't even be left alone for a
second."
"Receiving an emissary is a matter of courtesy every second. Please
stop acting like a spoiled brat."
After finishing her sentence, Kangnan muttered under her breath,
"Like a duckling…"
Gaold lifted his head.
"What did you say?"
"…to stop acting like one."
"That's what I said earlier!"
"I was emphasizing."
Kangnan adjusted her glasses with a prim expression and walked towards
the wardrobe. Reluctantly, Gaold got up as she pulled out his official robes.
"Damn! Just let it be something trivial, or I'll show
you."
He roughly wiped off the sweat and changed his clothes.
Throwing his cigar on the floor, Gaold strode out of the room and headed
toward the reception room.
Opening the door, Ordan greeted him brightly. He couldn't treat a
secretary and the president of the association the same way.
After the pleasantries, Ordan got straight to the point. Even as the top
emissary, his eyes changed once business began.
An aide brought a portable video recording device to the table. Then, a
small safe arrived, and Ordan unlocked it himself. Inside the safe was Obscura B.
"Currently, anomalies are occurring around the world including in
Yakma. Let's watch the video first."
Once Obscura B was connected, a video appeared. It showed a provincial
city still retaining ancient features.
Gaold, who had been watching indifferently, suddenly showed interest as
his eyes lit up.
People with tongues multiplying inside their mouths were seen eating
others.
Following screams, the guard was dispatched. However, the situation
escalated uncontrollably as the guards bitten by the mutants underwent the same mutations.
"This happened in a region called Kubelin. Besides Yakma, we
haven't obtained videos from other countries. However, rumors say similar diseases have spread. In some
areas, there are reports of ghosts."
Gaold focused solely on the video.
Eventually, the kingdom's special forces intervened and subdued the
mutants. Some of the magicians collecting samples were ones Gaold knew by name.
Magicians collected samples and set the entire village ablaze with a
massive flame.
"After the incident, we performed autopsies on the deceased. Any
kind of mutation is based on existing properties. However, the mutations in the deceased did not match
the characteristics of any known creature here."
Gaold returned to his indifferent attitude and yawned lazily.
"So what, are you saying this came from space?"
The video Ordan showed must be top-secret information from Yakma.
Showing it to a foreign magician and sharing key information meant they expected something in
return.
Ordan, Yakma's top diplomat, couldn't have missed Gaold's
reaction.
But today wasn't about setting up a negotiation table. It was a
transnational matter, with the world's eyes on this meeting.
"It seems something has gone wrong with Miro's
Space-Time."
Gaold, who seemed weary, suddenly showed a spark of interest in his
eyes, but it was a fleeting change, and his face soon returned to its usual calm.
"So what? What do you want me to do?"
"We'd like to investigate Miro's Space-Time. As you know, it can
only be accessed through the Kingdom of Thormia... Of course, I'm not asking for it to be independently
opened. The Valkyries have already approved it."
"That would be through the Alpheus Magic School,
precisely."
Gaold showed his displeasure.
Even if he had become a reclusive amnesiac, there were memories he could
never forget. Vivid memories of reality still torturously whipped his mind and body.
Gaold leaned back and crossed his legs, manners long forgotten from his
mind.
Kangnan sighed, but he didn't care.
"I'll think about it. You can go back now."
Faced with an unexpected response, Ordan's expression turned
incredulous.
"Think about it? Do you think I came here to ask you for a favor?
This is about international cooperation. I understand His Highness Adolf has already
agreed..."
Gaold's facial muscles twisted abnormally. As if a demon's soul had
possessed Gaold's face, two extreme expressions appeared simultaneously.
"Do you think mentioning the king will make me back down? Adolf?
Who the hell is he? No one goes without my permission."
The air heated up, and the room's furnishings began to tremble. The
emotional conflict between wanting to kill Ordan and restraining himself moved the objects.
This was an extreme beyond human limits. Even Kangnan couldn't intervene
this time and swallowed hard.
Ordan hallucinated.
The reception room disappeared, and only he and Gaold sat facing each
other, surrounded by hellish flames. The smell of sulfur was intense, and beyond the wall of fire, the
screams of the damned could be heard.
What kind of life had this man lived? Having met countless harsh people
in the world, this was the first time someone had truly terrified him.
'They say the president of the Thormia Magic Association is a
madman…'
Kangnan understood why she hadn't been expelled from the association
despite beating many nobles. The head of the association himself was an unparalleled lunatic.
"Get out. Before I kill you."
A man who referred to his own king in such terms. Gaold's words made
Ordan realize there was not an iota of falsehood in them. Screaming, he fled the reception room as urine
ran down his trousers.
The incident had already occurred, so the reception room was enveloped
in heavy silence.
As Gaold's killing intent subsided, Kangnan sat in Ordan's place.
"Did it really have to go this far? You could have sent him away
nicely."
"The bastard deserved it. You enjoyed it too, didn't
you?"
Kangnan couldn't deny it.
Gaold pulled out a cigar from his pocket and flicked his fingers,
igniting a flame that lit the cigar.
In the association where magic controls were active, Gaold was the only
one who could wield magic.
"You want me to investigate Miro's Space-Time? Might as well stick
my neck out and ask you to smash the back of my head, right?"
"But what really happened? If there's an issue with Miro's
Space-Time, Thormia should be the first to show signs."
"Probably. It just hasn't materialized yet. You said the tongue
monsters appeared in Kubelin, right? Where the ancient Colosseum was."
"What does that mean?"
"It's not a creature. More like... a concept, I suppose. That
concept has crossed over here and settled in areas where human thoughts are concentrated, maybe
something like that?"
Kangnan nodded compliantly.
If Gaold said so, it must be true. No one in Valkyrie's command knew as
much about the Labyrinth and heaven as Gaold did.
"Why did such phenomena occur?"
"The creatures couldn't cross over. The most likely assumption is
that Miro's Space-Time has suffered significant cracks. The Labyrinth could repair that extent. But it
won't hold out for long."
Gaold leaned back, fitting the cigar between his fingers. The acrid
smoke from the tobacco filled the reception room.
"Kangnan, you'll have to go."
Kangnan straightened up, placing her hands on her knees.
"Give your command."
"Something similar will happen here soon. Investigate everything.
Start with places where ancient thoughts might congregate—historical sites, Colosseums, old church
grounds."
Kangnan noted Gaold's orders in her notebook and sprang to her feet,
heading for the door. If it was a solo mission, she wouldn't need a carriage, so no matter where an
incident occurred in the kingdom, she wouldn't be late.
"Ah, and while you're at it, swing by Creas too."
As Kangnan turned to leave, she paused and looked back.
"The place with the magic school? Do you have a message for
Alpheus?"
Gaold tapped on the sofa, lost in thought.
It might seem premature, but if there was an issue with Miro's
Space-Time, there was no time to waste. It was time to enact a plan he had devoted his life to.
"Meet a kid named Shirone."
