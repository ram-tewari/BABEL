Chapter 613:
As Shirone walked down the hallway to the 18th floor of the Magic
Association, memories from the past came flooding back. The atmosphere was slightly more solemn, likely
due to the change in leadership.
When he reached the association head's office, he knocked but received
no response. Unsure whether to enter, he waited until a staff member approached him.
"Go on in. The head is in a meeting but will be with you
shortly."
"Okay."
Shirone slowly opened the door and entered Lupist's office. True to his
nickname, "The Steel Archmage," everything in the room, from the furniture to the decorations,
was made of metal. It made sense, considering the omnipotent nature of magic.
Sitting in a chair, Shirone anxiously rubbed his palms together as he
waited. Two hours later, the door finally opened, and Lupist entered with his chief secretary,
Jane.
It was natural for Shirone to see Gaold and Kangnan in Lupist's place,
but his first impression was that Lupist was much colder than them.
"Hello, I'm Arian Shirone."
Lupist walked past Shirone indifferently and said, "Leave
us."
Not knowing who he was addressing, Shirone stood still until Jane closed
the door and left.
"Sit down. We've met before, haven't we?"
Shirone had noticed Lupist's gaze at the plaza.
"Yes. It's an honor to be called."
"No need for that. I know you're on Gaold's side."
Although they had carried out missions together in Heaven, Shirone had
not formed an alliance with Gaold.
"No, not exactly…"
"Right, Gaold doesn't have allies. He tries to do everything alone.
But it's true that you're emotionally closer to Gaold than to me."
Since Lupist seemed to know everything, there was no need for Shirone to
explain.
"Are you confident about the graduation exam?"
"I'll do my best. I will become a mage."
It sounded like a rehearsed answer, but Shirone's voice naturally gained
strength in front of Lupist.
"You think I'm your enemy."
"Am I not?"
Having already faced Gaold, Shirone wasn't intimidated, even in front of
the association head. Lupist smirked.
"Maybe. You're on Gaold's side, and I'm the head of the
association, playing power games with other countries. If the association takes you in, Tormia will face
opposition from powerful nations."
Hearing it from the head himself made Shirone's heart sink, even though
he had expected it.
"I won't be used. I'll decide my own future."
"Of course. But no choice is truly free. You pick between red and
blue and think you're free, not knowing who offered those choices. That's the system."
The part cannot surpass the whole.
"Isn't there something you wanted to tell me?"
Shirone's voice rose in the face of the unpleasant truth.
"Let's make a deal. I'll guarantee you maximum freedom and
safety."
It wasn't a bad offer.
"What do I need to do to get that?"
"Fail the graduation exam early."
"What?"
"Of course, you might have your own solutions. But none will be as
easy as my offer. Fail. And fail miserably. Make it clear to everyone that you're insignificant. Some
might know it's a show, but people believe what they see. That alone will remove many
risks."
"But if I do that…"
"I'll guarantee everything afterward. You'll get your mage license,
and I'll ensure you get certified by the king. Then you can join the association and work for the
kingdom. I reinstated Plu and Isabel. You don't need to worry about your treatment."
Failing the graduation exam would actually grant him everything.
"Make a wise choice. If you accept my offer, it's over. Forget the
exam, go home, rest, and come back. I guarantee you won't get a better deal, no matter how well you
perform in the exam."
The truth in Lupist's words pained Shirone.
"You're a ticking time bomb that could cause global tragedy. But if
you're going to explode, it's natural to want to manage you. And I have the ability to do that, if you
help a little."
Failing the graduation exam miserably.
"I refuse."
Lupist wasn't surprised.
"Why?"
"You said no choice is truly free. I don't believe that. Choosing
nothing is also a choice."
"That's denying the whole."
"Then I'll fight the whole."
Lupist leaned back in his chair, half-closing his eyes.
"…Do you think you can win?"
"Winning isn't important. Doing what I believe is right, that's
what being a mage is about."
Shirone wanted to become a mage.
"If that's your decision, so be it. If you change your mind, don't
hesitate to fail. The exam hasn't started yet."
"Thank you for your concern."
Maintaining his composure, Shirone left the room.
"Whew."
Standing in the hallway, he finally felt the reality of the
situation.
'Did I do the right thing?'
If he messed up the graduation exam, he'd feel even worse, but he
wouldn't regret his decision, no matter the outcome.
"Are you done?"
Jane approached.
"Yes."
"Then go to the annex on the first floor. There's a dinner party at
5:30."
She didn't ask any questions.
"Thank you. I'll go."
Jane watched Shirone turn the corner before entering the head's
office.
One look at Lupist's face told her the negotiation had failed.
"I told you it wouldn't work."
Lupist muttered, resting his chin on his hand.
"Surpassing the whole as an individual. Is it
possible?"
Jane glanced at the door.
"He's Ultima."
"But Gaia failed."
As Lupist stood up, Jane turned back to him.
"What will you do? He's too valuable to lose to another
country."
"No rush. He's a difficult meal to digest. Watching for now isn't a
bad idea."
Approaching the door, Lupist looked back at Jane.
"It should have started by now. The video recorders?"
"Set up with no blind spots. There won't be any major incidents, if
they have any sense."
"Where can I watch?"
"Try the central control room. But why watch the students'
fun?"
Lupist smirked.
"After all the trouble, it would be a shame to send them off
without a proper farewell."
Though called an annex, the association building was so large that even
with all 150 students inside, the central area felt spacious.
Under the light of hundreds of chandeliers, delicacies from around the
world gleamed.
Some ate, some enjoyed themselves, but most of the students representing
the kingdom's prestigious schools were engaged in invisible psychological battles with students from
other schools.
In the midst of the complex web of emotions, Alpheas Magic School
received the most attention.
"Is it true that Baikal is going to Creas this year?"
"I heard that too. He's been exclusively with the Royal Magic
School for over ten years."
The rumor spread quickly, and even students who didn't know began
glancing at the Alpheas Magic School graduates.
Among them, the most attention was on Pony.
"Miss, we've prepared a gown for you. Please accept our request. A
party in the same attire as the nobles."
'Annoying.'
Surrounded by attendants from the royal castle, Pony didn't even know
where she was standing.
"Please leave. I'm here as a representative of the magic school,
not as royalty. I have no special privileges."
"Miss, royal blood cannot be denied."
But it was a weak lineage, far from the line of succession.
"Why soil your noble body? If you wish, I'll call the head of the
association right now to get you the highest mage certification."
Pony looked at the attendant with disdain.
"A mage certification is the same for everyone."
"Then I'll order a special one made. A top-tier certification
befitting royalty. You'll like it."
Knowing the attendant was sincere only made her more irritated.
Talking to people living in a reality beyond reality made her feel like
a fool.
'I won't rely on my lineage. I'll become the best with my own
strength.'
Alfred Pony (Final Rank: 11th in the graduating class).
Major: Water Magic, Torrent Series.
Special Notes: Royal bloodline.
"Ugh, annoying."
Feeling a headache coming on, Pony walked away quickly, with the
attendants following in a rush.
"In a way… it's a bit pitiful."
Maya, watching with a drink in hand, muttered.
"There's nothing more unfortunate than a predetermined
fate."
Turning to the voice beside her, she saw Kaiden.
It seemed to be the first time he had spoken to her since they started
school.
"Good evening, Maya. Shall we toast?"
Maya stared at the trembling liquid in Kaiden's glass.
"Why are you so nervous? Your hand is shaking."
"Really? I didn't notice."
Though he said that, the trembling worsened as he became aware of
it.
"Strange. It looks like you're shaking…"
"What are you talking about? Why would my hand shake?"
Maya pointed to the glass, which seemed to be shaking as Kaiden held
it.
"The drink is spilling over."
"Strange. I don't feel it."
"…"
Watching Kaiden not even glance at the glass, Maya awkwardly
smiled.
"Well, have a good time."
She quickly turned and left, almost running away.
'What a strange personality. Is he teasing me? I should avoid
him.'
As the trembling stopped, Kaiden put down the glass with a sad
smile.
"After all… the Red Cross Star isn't working?"
"Stop talking nonsense about the Red Cross Star!"
Amy pinched Kaiden's side.
"Ouch! What was that for?"
"Now I see, you're actually a softie. If you act like that, what
girl wouldn't think you're crazy?"
Kaiden grumbled, rubbing his sore side.
"Why are you interfering? You know it won't work
anyway."
"Let's start with the assumption that you're normal. Come on, go
apologize to Maya for the joke."
Kaiden spotted Maya standing alone in the crowd.
"Do you think… it'll work?"
"Are you proposing or something? And don't take the drink. You
can't even finish one glass."
"Alright, I'll try."
Taking a deep breath, Kaiden approached Maya again.
"Hey…"
Seeing the fear in Maya's eyes, he realized the reality.
"Sorry, it was just a joke earlier. I just wanted to talk to
you."
Maya's face brightened immediately.
"Oh, I see! I thought you had a strange personality. But it was
fun."
When the imagined fear didn't materialize, Kaiden regained his
confidence.
"Are you ready for the exam?"
"Yeah. I feel good about it. Iruki's advice in the first half was
really helpful."
Though lacking in magical talent, Maya's artistic sense was so
exceptional that even Kaiden of the Red Cross Star couldn't compare.
"This time, I'm going to do it my way. I might hit a limit someday,
but for now, my goal is to graduate."
The future of the Myst Tribe rested in Maya's hands.
Myst Maya (Final Rank: 28th in the graduating class).
Major: Sound Magic, Song Series.
Special Notes: Innate omnipotence in singing.
"Yeah. You'll definitely pass."
"Huh?"
Maya questioned his confident statement, but Kaiden answered in his
heart.
'Because I'll be by your side.'
