Chapter 1158:
The Five Major Systems
Etella pressed her stomach firmly.
This is bad.
According to the structure of the Law, this place was where illogical
demons flowed in.
It's reacting.
Every time she heard the crying, it felt like a knife was stabbing into
her abdomen. But more than the pain, it was fear.
At this rate… I'll have a miscarriage.
It was most likely Shagall's child. The child of the man who had cast
her, a follower of Goodness, into Hell.
That's why she was so confused.
I don't know… What should I…
It was still difficult to make a decision, but her womanly instincts
were screaming at her to get away from this place.
For now, I have no choice but to protect it.
Even the thought of saying it aloud was embarrassing. Just as she was
trying to gather her courage, Shagall stepped forward.
"Tch! What the hell is this?"
Everyone turned to look at him.
"They're just the same wretched souls. Starving ghouls clinging to
life when they should already be dead. I came here to break these damn chains. I don't want to waste my
time in a place like this."
It was not a sight one could stomach for long. Shirone's group quickly
followed Shagall out.
Only Etella remained behind for a moment, silently absorbing the
emotions transmitted through their chains.
On the way up to the 12th floor, Venistov explained the Fire Management
Corporation's system.
"The Under World, as managed by the Fire Management Corporation, is
divided into three major sectors. First is the corporation's subsidiary branches, second is the cities
where demons reside, and lastly, the logistics warehouses controlled by the Hokseung."
Shirone and Rian had already experienced all three firsthand.
"What makes the Company special is the Flesh Brain, the Central
Processing Unit you saw earlier. Emotions may be illogical, but they are not without causality. While
reality operates based on events, we handle emotional causality. This is what we call
karma."
Shirone asked, "If the Flesh Brain is purified, will Jin Seong Eum
be freed?"
"Hmm… The Under World would be paralyzed. What happens after that,
I cannot say. But this is only hypothetical. In reality, the Flesh Brain cannot be
purified."
"Why not?"
"The **fetuses that make up the Flesh Brain are classified as the
Limbo race. They have no karma. Therefore, they cannot be purified."
Venistov raised a finger.
"Not all Limbo are aborted fetuses. The criterion is karma. A child
who was born but never developed logic also lacks karma. Such Limbo drift outward in search of
sustenance. You can see them in Baramaon's Seventh District."
When Etella and Shagall first fell into Hell, they had seen them in the
Graveyard of Spines.
"It's best to be cautious. They may not be strong, but they are
cruel. Or rather, they don't even possess the concept of cruelty. The only driving force behind Limbo is
their obsession with life."
Shirone asked again, "What if we destroy the Flesh
Brain?"
Sometimes, understanding the target requires brutal thoughts.
"Limbo, like Hell Prison, is part of the Law. Even if the
individuals disappear, the concept remains. Burning the fetuses would only lead to the creation of a new
Flesh Brain. Of course, a major system error would occur, but as a system expert, I strongly advise
against it. What Yahweh wishes to achieve will not happen."
Venistov's tone became firm.
"Destruction is not a good solution. Especially when too much is at
stake. If you break both reality and the Under World, what remains? Would you take a hammer to a
delicate machine in the hopes that it might fix itself? Sure, maybe you get lucky. But in my opinion,
the odds are much higher that it turns into useless scrap. There's no turning back from
that."
Shirone fell deep into thought.
He's not lying. If it weren't true, they wouldn't have shown us the
Flesh Brain so easily.
But… something feels off.
It was that enigmatic feeling, as if the most important piece was still
being hidden.
The moment they reached the 12th floor, any complex thoughts were blown
away by the sheer noise.
"Worship Satan!"
It was a grand, coliseum-like space, packed with people on the 10th and
11th floors.
A demon, holding a microphone embedded with multiple human ears, raised
his hands and shouted.
"Ohhh! Lord Satan! Grant us joy! Grant us pleasure! Grant us
success!"
His voice boomed through the hundreds of mouths mounted on the
walls.
Below the altar, a crowd of thousands shed tears of emotion and chanted
after him.
"We believe! We believe in Satan!"
Shirone asked, "This place…?"
"A Satanic Mass. The followers gathered here are humans who have
made contracts with Satan."
In other words, living humans who had cast their souls into Hell.
There were about six thousand of them—every one a candidate for Hell
Prison.
"Submit! Desire with all your heart! Satan will answer
you!"
The crowd roared with fervor, raising their hands.
Each of them was longing for something, but to Shirone, it all sounded
like meaningless screams.
The demon stretched out his hand.
"I hear it!"
Like magic, the noise vanished. The demon's long fingers pointed to
someone in the crowd.
"The grief of a mother who lost her child… I hear you,
Marie."
"It's me! It's me!"
A woman clutched a dead infant in her arms, jumping up and down.
"Come forth. You, who submit to Satan, have the courage to do
so."
With tears of joy, Marie climbed onto the stage. The demon accepted the
child's corpse.
"This child will live again. Do you believe?"
"Yes! I believe! I… I believe in Satan! If only my child can be
brought back…!"
"There is no condition! Do you believe?!"
The woman screamed.
"Yes! I believe!"
"Good! In Satan's name…!"
With both hands, the demon lifted the corpse high into the air and
slammed it onto the ground with all his might.
"Come back to life!"
A dull thud rang out as the child's body crashed onto the floor.
The entire congregation erupted into thunderous applause.
"Praise the greatness of Satan!"
Shirone felt like he was watching a sick joke, but what came next was
even more horrifying.
"Ohhh! My baby!"
Even the mother—holding her dead infant—danced with joy.
"Come here, my child."
She kissed the cold, blue corpse.
Shirone's group was filled with dread.
Shirone whispered.
"What… the hell is this?"
The demon continued.
He called forth a soldier who had lost a leg and an elderly woman who
had gone blind.
"Walk properly!"
The soldier, filled with ecstasy, hobbled onto the stage on his one
remaining leg.
He took one step before collapsing, but he kept picking himself back
up.
"My body is healed!"
With another loud crash, he fell again.
"Look! I can walk just fine now! I'll get my revenge on those
bastards!"
Once again, she collapsed.
"I can see! I can see now! My checks! No one will steal my checks
ever again!"
The old woman flailed her hands, wandering endlessly in circles.
Shirone clenched his teeth.
"… A fanatic."
Lethe spoke.
"That's right. This is how Hell Prison is born. A blind faith so

contradictions."
She turned her head with a sympathetic look.
"I don't think you're underestimating this. But if it's Jin Seong

didn't you?"
Shirone didn't answer.
"This is a colossal desire. To what extent must one's emotions be

Lethe was right.
As the demon sent the worshippers back to their seats, twenty

"They are the most prominent candidates for Hell

They weren't as frenzied as the other believers, but their despair was

"Behold, everyone! Thanks to my faith in Satan, I have become

From his pocket, garbage continuously spilled onto the floor.

"Hello, everyone. I am Mortasinger, one of the final twenty

That woman… is Mortasinger?!
