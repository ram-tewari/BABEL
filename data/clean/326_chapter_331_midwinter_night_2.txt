Chapter 331:
Midwinter Night (2)
The way she was dressed, it seemed like she could freeze to death at any
moment, making her an amusing sight for the drunken onlookers.
However, the woman paid no attention to the stares and continued to
preach her message to the people.
"The end of the world is approaching. This place is dangerous.
Everyone, leave the ruins quickly."
'The end of the world? What is she talking about out of nowhere?'
Dante didn't take it seriously.
Every ancient ruin has its share of people who get so caught up in cults
that they give up on life.
The Ice Queen herself was a legend fabricated for the festival, so the
idea of danger was absurd.
The tourists seemed to think the same, as no one was listening
seriously.
"If it's the end of the world, it doesn't matter where we go,
right? If we leave here, where should we go? Surely you don't mean we should die in the arms of our
nagging wives at home? Hahaha!"
"Now that you mention it, that's true. That would be the worst.
Miss, if you need attention, why not stop talking nonsense and dance instead?"
The woman, seemingly accustomed to such disregard, didn't change her
expression and retorted while looking straight at them.
"It's not a lie. It's an oracle from the goddess Akeanis, who
governs the melody of time."
"Akeanis? Never heard of that religion."
"You must believe me! Soon, the birth pangs of the end will occur
here. Everyone will die!"
"Alright, alright, we believe you. But let's go inside first. We
can talk over a drink."
When a large man put his arm around the woman's shoulder, she pushed him
away with both hands and shouted.
"Don't lay your unholy hands on me!"
"Hey! This girl... Come on, let's go inside. I'm not a bad
guy."
The woman reached into a cylindrical container slung horizontally under
her backpack and pulled out something, pressing it against the man's neck.
At first glance, it looked like a quiver, but the contents were thin
iron rods about 50 centimeters long.
The ends were sharp like awls, and a small bell dangled from a ring on
the opposite end.
Feeling the genuine threat, the large man quickly withdrew his hands and
stepped back.
"Whoa, what a temper."
As the situation escalated, the tourists who had been indifferent began
to gather with interest.
Dante overheard the men standing behind him exchanging crude
jokes.
"She's crazy. She looks fine, though. What do you think, should we
have some fun with her tonight?"
"Forget it. You might get hurt dealing with a woman like that. Who
knows what could happen?"
"But it's a shame. She's a rare beauty."
Dante observed the woman closely.
"Akeanis..."
Even Dante, who had a keen interest in the supernatural, had never heard
the name before.
Well, there were thousands of religions in the world, so it was
impossible to know the names of all the gods they worshipped.
The woman kept repeating the same message like a parrot.
"The end is near. Leave the old ruins quickly! If you don't,
everyone will die. I'm telling the truth!"
"Will leaving solve the problem?"
The people's attention turned to Dante.
He was the first to take the woman's words seriously, so he drew more
attention.
Dante stepped forward and spoke.
"If the end is really coming, there must be a way to solve it,
right? Running away won't fix anything."
The woman, realizing Dante was younger than her, turned her head away in
disappointment and addressed the crowd again.
"I am Lyria, a totemist and mage of the Akeanis Shrine. Tonight, an
exorcism will be attempted here. If it fails, it could be dangerous. So please leave this place
quickly."
Dante was more interested in the fact that she was a mage than in being
ignored.
Mages worked in exorcism circles, and their sealing formations were
similar to mages' magic circles.
That's why Dante, who specialized in information magic, had studied
exorcism to some extent.
While mages materialize phenomena through equivalent exchange, mages
strengthen an aspect of logic through the power of will.
The two are similar but different.
In a situation where a fire needs to be extinguished, a mage would
create water. But a mage would strengthen the will of water to suppress the will of fire.
Controlling the balance of the source.
Mages call this the law.
It doesn't have the sharpness of magical change, but it can encompass a
broader range of phenomena.
Despite this, it doesn't receive much attention because the ability
itself isn't logical.
Mages, specifically white mages, fight to eradicate the world's
evil.
But good and evil are artificial distinctions created after humans were
born.
So how can mages seal evil?
Due to this ambiguity, people often dismiss the law as a derivative or
alternative form of magic.
"There is no time! Leave this place before the day ends. Your lives
are at stake!"
Lyria pleaded earnestly, but the people paid no attention.
Seeing no one else stepping forward, Dante approached her again.
"Can I help?"
Lyria sighed softly without looking at him.
'Kids these days.'
She had encountered boys hitting on her like this while traveling for
exorcisms more than once.
The job of an exorcist seemed to spark quite a bit of curiosity among
young people.
Judging by the boy's appearance, he was handsome and stylish. He even
had a piercing, suggesting he was quite experienced.
'But this time, you picked the wrong person, kid.'
Lyria glared at Dante with cold eyes.
"This isn't something a kid like you should get involved in. Go
home quickly if you don't want to cause your parents any trouble."
As Dante was about to respond, someone in the crowd spoke up.
"Hey, isn't that Dante? The kingdom's top mage prospect, Airhain
Dante."
The middle-aged man recognized Dante's face because he worked for a
company that supplied magical materials.
Even if they weren't mages, people working in magic-related fields often
subscribed to journals.
Hundreds of new magical tools are developed each year, and analyzing
students' needs helps them capture the market for popular products.
"Huh? That boy's a mage? He doesn't look like it."
"No, he's still a student. But I've heard even professionals can't
mess with him."
To the general public, mages were mysterious beings.
Moreover, the title of the kingdom's best in any field wasn't given
lightly, so people paid attention.
Lyria finally adjusted her expression and looked at Dante.
As a mage, she knew much more about the magical society than the average
person, so her feelings of disregard disappeared.
"The kingdom's top student?"
Dante smacked his lips and shrugged.
"Well, maybe second best now?"
The man from the magic supply company knew that a boy named Shirone had
defeated Dante.
But the nostalgia for the champion doesn't fade easily.
Especially since Dante had boasted an undefeated record since childhood,
his popularity didn't wane quickly.
Lyria didn't care whether Dante was first or second. What mattered was
that he was a knowledgeable mage and the first to take her seriously.
Dante pointed to the bar on the third floor.
"This isn't the best place. How about we go inside and have a
drink?"
Lyria looked at Dante with suspicion.
Even if he was a skilled mage, if he was hitting on her like this, it
was a different story.
"If you're trying to hit on me, find someone else."
"What are you talking about? Hit on you?"
"Then why are you trying to help me?"
"My specialty is magic circles. You deal with sealing formations,
right? I'm interested. It might help my studies."
"Magic circles?"
If the boy was telling the truth, he could indeed be helpful.
And if he was the kingdom's top prospect, it meant he was the best at
handling magic circles at the student level.
"Alright. Let's go inside. But I won't drink."
"Suit yourself. I just need a beer."
Lyria finally shook the snow off her head and followed Dante into the
bar.
"Damn... lost to a kid who hasn't even dried behind the
ears."
The old ruins were not only a date spot for couples but also a place for
lonely singles to find connections.
The many young men who had tried to hit on Lyria watched the two with
envious eyes.
8 PM.
The old ruins were eerily quiet.
Moonlight glistened on the ice statues.
Even the laughter from the distant shopping district sounded reverent
here.
Breaking the night's silence, two figures nimbly jumped over the
crumbling walls and entered the deserted ruins.
Dante's expression was serious.
If what he had heard in the bar was true, the old ruins were far from
safe.
'Seeing the future? Is that even possible?'
According to Lyria, the head of the Akeanis Shrine was a 70-year-old
woman named Poriter.
She had the ability to see future events like déjà vu since
birth.
In her childhood, there were rumors that she was mentally ill. She
herself suspected delusions of grandeur.
But how often would a randomly imagined future scene come true by
coincidence?
One day, when Poriter was eighteen, she saw her village burning at the
hands of bandits, just as she had imagined. That's when she realized.
She had the ability to see the future.
She didn't know how future events came to her mind, but she said it
wasn't foresight.
Rather, it was like someone walking through the future, recalling past
events, with sudden impressions like old memories.
"Such a person said a disaster would strike the old
ruins?"
Lyria nodded with a look of trust.
"And it's not just any disaster. She said a being that could bring
about the end of the world would appear."
"The end of the world, huh? If the opponent is that formidable,
there's nothing we can do, right?"
"No. That's why High Priestess Poriter is amazing. The future can
be changed. We don't know what kind of disaster it will be, but we can deal with it before it manifests.
It's like removing an egg before it hatches."
"I see. Do you have a plan?"
"We'll use necromantic summoning. Right now, its energy is seeping
into this place. We'll materialize it and then immediately trap it in a sealing formation."
"But will it be that easy? What if it's stronger than
expected?"
Lyria reached into the cylinder at her waist, pulled out a needle, and
shook it, making a bell ring.
"That's why I, a totemist, was sent. This totem has a core of birch
wood. By strengthening the power of the law with this, we can definitely capture it."
Lyria planted the totem in the ground. Then she moved to another spot
and pulled out another totem.
"The sudden oracle left us short-handed. There's much more evil in
the world than people think. Most exorcists, including myself, can't even go home and wander the
world."
Lyria used 20 totems to draw a large circle with a diameter of 20 meters
and walked to the center.
Dante followed and asked,
"I've always been curious, how does it work?"
"Hmm? What do you mean?"
"I can design a magic circle that imposes physical restraints. But
a sealing formation isn't like that, right? It only reacts to evil. When something enters the sealing
formation, how do you distinguish if it's good or evil? What's the definition of evil?"
