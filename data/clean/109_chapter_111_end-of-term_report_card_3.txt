Chapter 111:
End-of-Term Report Card (3)
Nade's expression twisted. The emotions he felt when Shirone was teetering between life
and death resurfaced, and a murderous intent flashed through his mind. Not wanting to cause unnecessary
commotion, Iruki stepped in to defuse the situation.
"Enough. Never mind that—why are you here?"
"Why am I here? I'm checking out the place where I'll be living from now on."
"What?"
Shirone and his group's eyes widened at the unexpected response.
"Exactly as I said. A magic school or whatever—I thought it'd be good to experience
it."
Nade rolled up his sleeves and fired back.
"You think you can just waltz into a magic school like it's nothing? Who'd even
accept someone like you?"
Canis raised a finger and pointed.
"Senior."
"What?"
"Call me 'Senior.' I looked into it—you're all Class Five, right? I'm Class
Four."
Shirone's group was stunned. It was likely Alphaeus who had arranged for the two to be
admitted. But starting at Class Four right after enrollment? While not unheard of, it was certainly rare.
"What's so surprising? We're the disciples of the Grand Magus. We've already
mastered the basics of magic. It's laughable to be treated the same as slackers like you."
Shirone's group seethed but kept their mouths shut. Having spent over a decade mastering
magic under Arcane, they couldn't help but feel dissatisfied with being labeled Class Four.
"So, from now on, I expect you to address me respectfully as 'Senior.'"
"Ridiculous! You'll only start attending next semester anyway! By then, we'll have
advanced to Class Four too!"
"Oh? I heard your grades are surprisingly terrible. Honestly, I'm disappointed. To
think I lost to a bunch of incompetents like you."
The first thing Canis had investigated about Shirone's group was their grades and social
standing at school. Contrary to his expectations of them being top contenders, they were Class Five—middle
of the pack. It had been a shock.
Yet, no one in the advanced classes looked down on them. The consensus was that they
could rise anytime if they put their minds to it—something he'd heard ad nauseam.
As Shirone's group gritted their teeth in silence, Canis turned away with a satisfied
smirk.
"Anyway, do your best. If you manage to join as juniors, I'll be sure to take good
care of you. Hahaha!"
Canis's shadow stretched toward Shirone's group as he walked away, and Havist raised both
hands, flipping them off with both middle fingers. The three trembled with rage.
Shirone broke the heavy silence.
"Cancel today's plans."
"Agreed. The thought of Canis being our senior… I'd rather drop out than endure that
humiliation."
"Tch, why drop out? We should advance and crush his pride instead."
Their minds were now solely focused on advancing. But how? The end-of-term evaluation was
a different beast compared to overcoming obstacles. It was the culmination of half a year's effort—no amount
of last-minute cramming could guarantee success.
"I've thought about this before…"
Shirone's expression turned serious as he began.
"The probability of me advancing was around 30%. That's if I gave it my all in
studying."
Though not as mathematically precise as Iruki's analyses, Shirone's insights were highly
reliable, so his friends took his words seriously. Nade's expression darkened again.
"Honestly, even after all that bravado, it's not easy. If Shirone's at 30%, I'd be
around 50%. Iruki, maybe 60%?"
"No. I'm at 58.7%. Advancement is based on end-of-term grades, but frankly, I
haven't attended a single class."
The three sighed in unison. If things continued like this, they'd have no choice but to
bow their heads to Canis and Arin next semester.
"So, here's an idea… What if we try this?"
At Shirone's suggestion, Nade's eyes lit up as he turned to him.
"What? Got a good idea?"
"Honestly, if we stick to our usual study methods, our chances are too low. We need
to adjust our strategy."
Iruki chimed in.
"Change our approach? But how?"
"The 30% I mentioned assumes we study hard. But if we focus solely on the exams, we
can push it past 50%. That means you two could hit 70% or even 80%."
"Hmm. Target the exams? I see. You mean prioritize and focus?"
"Exactly. We only need to score above 80. No need to waste time on subjects we're
already good at or topics that won't be tested. The sixteen subjects can be broadly categorized into four
fields: humanities, mathematics, sciences, and practical skills."
Shirone counted them off on his fingers.
"The good news is, we can each easily pass at least one field. And most importantly,
our strengths don't overlap. I'm humanities, Iruki's math, Nade's sciences. Practical skills are a wild
card, so we're on our own there."
Iruki nodded in understanding.
"In short, form a study group. Since we don't need to study our strong subjects, we
can use that time to cover each other's weaknesses, right?"
"Right. If we share the core tricks to scoring above 80, our chances will skyrocket.
This could push us past 50%."
Iruki's lips curled into a smile. Seeing Shirone this desperate showed how much the idea
of Canis and Arin being his seniors bothered him.
Still, it was a solid plan. And if all three advanced together, it'd be the best possible
outcome.
"I'm in. Let's head to the research club and start planning. What do you say?"
"Let's do it!"
The three boys, burning with determination, sprinted toward Istas.
***
Shirone devoted himself to studying day and night.
Time had to be managed wisely. To the unaware, an hour passed in a blink, but to those
who divided it into ten-minute increments, it felt much longer.
Shirone broke down every remaining minute until the end-of-term exams into one-hour
blocks. By keeping time firmly in mind, he prevented any waste.
With this efficiency, he targeted the exams.
Rather than understanding processes, he conquered problem types, analyzing the intent
behind questions and their solutions. In this regard, the study group was effective. Compensating for each
other's weaknesses allowed Shirone to raise his mock exam average above 70.
Of course, it wasn't all smooth sailing. Studying revealed that certain subjects were
weak not due to laziness but sheer lack of talent.
Iruki's disparity between humanities and math was especially stark. As he frantically
solved a problem set Shirone had prepared, he set down his pen and stretched after just 30 minutes.
"Done."
"Let me see."
Shirone checked each of Iruki's answers. His hands trembled as he confirmed the answers.
Now he understood why humanities teachers recoiled at the sight of Iruki's face.
Shirone pointed at the test paper with a baffled expression.
"How does 'You eat pasta, I'll eat pasta' make sense? I explained auxiliary verb
usage!"
"Why doesn't it? 'You eat pasta! I'll eat pasta!' It's clear, isn't it? It
emphasizes each as an independent subject."
"Huh? Oh, well…"
For a moment, Shirone was taken aback—it did sound plausible. But he quickly regained his
composure and shouted.
"That's not it! Language isn't math! It's a tool for expression, not proof! You
don't split sentences—you read between the lines!"
"Damn it, what's that supposed to mean? Math is supreme!"
"Shut up! I know you're being stubborn! Admit it—even you think it's weird,
right?"
"Of course it's weird. But that's just sensory. Mathematically, there's no issue
with the sentence."
Iruki's pride in math was immense. How else could humanities teachers justify giving his
tests zero marks?
Shirone resorted to his last resort.
"Do you want to advance or not?"
"Ugh! Damn it!"
Even Iruki had to relent at that. He'd rather die than become Canis's junior.
As Iruki returned to solving problems, Shirone sighed deeply.
'If he does this in the actual exam, it'll be a disaster.'
Nade suddenly looked up from his book.
"Oh, right. The practical exam is next week. Have you two practiced much?"
Shirone and Iruki responded in unison.
"What exam?"
"Damn it. You didn't even know? It's the Spirit Zone endurance test."
"Do we need to practice? Shirone and I can easily score above 80. Why, are you
worried?"
"No. But practical exams are unpredictable. Unlike other subjects, it's graded on a
curve. If others do better, our scores drop. Don't you think we should at least go over it once?"
"Relax, relax. We don't have time."
Iruki ignored him and focused on problem-solving. At the Class Five level, how much could
they even do with Spirit Zone? He assumed the practical exam was a guaranteed pass.
Shirone spoke while solving math problems.
"I'll pass too. I don't have the energy to worry about that."
Nade was confident, but it was also true that scoring higher than the other two would be
tough.
'Hmm. What to do?'
If it really seemed impossible, he could ask for help. But stealing his friends' time
just for his own anxiety would be exploiting the rules.
'Whatever. As long as I make the top 10, it's fine.'
Nade pushed the thought aside and started studying. If any of them failed to pull their
33.3% weight, the study group's efficiency would collapse.
They cooperated for mutual benefit, but results were something no one could give you.
***
Image Zone training grounds.
Normally, this was the time for integrated advanced class training, but only Class Five
students were present today.
It was the infamous practical exam period that had cornered Shirone multiple times. With
the end of the term approaching, the students' tension was higher than ever.
Ethella, as always, unfolded a 20-meter Spirit Zone and began explaining.
"Today's evaluation is the mental pulse test. It measures how long you can sustain
changes in your Spirit Zone."
She pointed to black pillars of unknown material towering in the distance, their surfaces
carved with cryptic symbols.
"The benchmark is a diameter of 20 meters. If your Spirit Zone falls below that, you
can't take the test. Not that any of you would fail to meet it."
Ethella contracted her Spirit Zone. When its volume dropped below 2 cubic meters, a
buzzer sounded.
"The buzzer marks one cycle. The measurement time is 30 seconds, and your score
depends on the number of pulse cycles. Since you rarely practice with the counter on, I'll demonstrate
first."
At her signal, the Image Zone's mental pulse mode activated. Simultaneously, Ethella's
Spirit Zone expanded and contracted repeatedly, pulsing like a heart.
"Whoa…"
The sight of a 20-meter Spirit Zone shrinking below 2 meters and instantly expanding
again was spectacular. Though the cycles weren't fast, the repeated contractions and expansions were as
intense as explosions.
Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!
The Image Zone's sound machine calculated the expansion speed, producing explosive
sounds.
This wasn't just for flair. Engaging all five senses with the intangible Image Zone
maximized training efficiency.
After 30 seconds, the scoreboard displayed 30 points—one pulse per second.
According to seniors, the cutoff for advancement was at least 90 points—three pulses per
second. The kids used Ethella's demonstration to finalize their strategies.
Ethella concluded, hands behind her back.
"In real combat, you'll often need to rapidly transform your Zone into various
forms. Mental pulse is foundational training, so keep at it even after the exam. Let's begin the
evaluation."
Based on their previous speed gun test results, students were called from the rear. As
expected, improving in just a month or two was tough—most rankings stayed the same.
With the lowest score at 48, it was Nade's turn. Having placed mid-to-upper tier in the
speed gun test, he had to go much earlier than Shirone and Iruki.
"Nade, do your best."
"Whew, I'm nervous."
Encouraged by his friends, Nade stepped into the Image Zone, closed his eyes, and
focused. At the starting signal, he opened his eyes and expanded his Spirit Zone. It pulsed rapidly, like
the heart of a sprinter.
"Whoa! What's gotten into Nade?"
The kids' eyes widened. He'd already passed 50 points in just 10 seconds. Five pulses per
second was overwhelming to watch.
"Wasn't he mid-tier before? This'll easily put him in the upper ranks."
"Did training with Shirone boost him this much? Maybe we should've joined the
research club."
"Right. They've been studying like crazy lately. Even Iruki, who used to skip class
daily, hasn't missed a single lesson."
After 30 seconds, Nade's score appeared: 173. Accelerating in the last 10 seconds had
helped.
"Good job, Nade."
Ethella smiled warmly as she recorded the score. Seeing her student improve so
dramatically pleased her.
'Well, I expected this much from the start.'
Nade returned, sticking out his tongue sheepishly. After high-fiving his friends, he sat
down.
"Whew, I thought I'd die from nerves."
"Well done. One down. Just the two of us left."
Perhaps inspired by Nade, the remaining participants gave their all. But no one surpassed
173.
With Nade holding first place, Iruki entered the Image Zone second to last. Calm as ever,
he activated his Servant ability at the start.
Pre-designing the expansion-contraction mechanism and repeating it mechanically was
useless in real combat but perfect for exams.
His score: 210.
The kids stared in disbelief. Only a machine-like seven pulses per second, without any
speed variation, could achieve that.
"Wow, he's insane. Is his mind an equation too? Bet he wasn't even trying."
"What can you do? He's a Servant. Even Shirone might struggle to top this."
Then, a voice dripping with disdain cut through the excited chatter.
"Tch, making a fuss over such mediocre scores. Class Five really is a gathering of
pathetic losers."
The kids turned, their expressions freezing as if they'd seen a ghost. Canis and Arin,
set to enroll next semester, were watching the exam.
Ethella, recording scores, asked,
"Hmm? You two, what's the matter?"
"The headmaster said we could observe. We came to see how the exams work."
"Oh, I see. Then come over here. You'll have a good view."
Canis frowned. Was this woman really the one who'd rivaled his master? In battle, he
hadn't noticed, but now she seemed foolishly docile.
'Is she pretending? Or is she really this dumb?'
Still, he was genuinely interested in the exam. Canis and Arin stood at the back of the
participants.
"So, Arin? What do you think?"
"We've trained mental pulses to death. Master insisted on pushing our limits. I
could easily clear 250."
Arin was a mental-type mage, specializing in the most advanced Zone form—tentacles—so her
Spirit Zone endurance was unmatched.
'As expected of Arcane's disciples. They're on another level.'
The students shrank at Arin's words. Though they were nobles, any aspiring mage dreamed
of becoming a Grand Magus's direct disciple. Even if they came from slums, Canis and Arin were elite among
elites.
'Tch. But we have our own secret weapon.'
When Shirone, the last participant, entered the Image Zone, the students cheered in
unison.
They knew Shirone had defeated Canis after regaining his memories. They hoped he'd show
his true power again.
Shirone took a deep breath at the center of the Image Zone. With Iruki at 210, scoring
near that would suffice for advancement.
But Shirone's eyes were different now. With a clear goal, he had no intention of
half-hearted effort, even without Canis's provocation.
At Ethella's signal, Shirone started pulsing his Spirit Zone at full power from the
outset.
Unlike Iruki's precise design, dividing each second evenly was hard without rhythm—a
precise internal clock.
Shirone synchronized his Zone's pulses with rhythm and accelerated. The sound machine
erupted with drumline-like bursts.
BamBamBamBamBamBamBamBoom!
The kids' jaws dropped. Even without blinking, they could barely catch one pulse per
second.
"How… How many per second is that?"
Multiple layers of Spirit Zone moved simultaneously, making it impossible to count. They
waited for 10 seconds. The scoreboard then displayed an insane number: 120.
"Twelve per second…"
Could they even imagine a 20-meter sphere contracting twelve times per second? They could
only watch, clueless about what was happening inside. The rapid-fire explosions and soaring numbers were the
only proof of Shirone's pulsating mind.
Canis and Arin were stunned. Arin, especially, looked incredulous. At that speed, the
mind should collapse before the Zone.
The core of pulsing was how fast you could expand from the smallest to largest sphere and
how much contraction shock you could endure.
In that sense, Shirone's demonstration was the pinnacle—maximizing both speed and
endurance.
'Indestructible Diamond.'
Ethella recognized Shirone's level at a glance.
'Truly remarkable. His potential is beyond measure.'
She could no longer gauge how far Shirone had come. The moment she thought he'd reached
one point, he'd already surpassed it.
