Chapter 1071:
The Only Chance (1)
The silence that seemed endless suddenly turned into a noisy
murmur.
"What, what's going on? Why is Aegis here?"
In High Gear, it was very rare for a user to actually bow like
that.
Even if threatened by a ranker, users would rather clean their codename
than sell their pride.
"Who's Yahweh2? Have you heard of him?"
"No, it's my first time. And his parts are a mess. A basic Maron
package with a bolt gun…"
As people were stunned by the appearance of someone who looked like they
were selling scrap metal, Shirone asked, "I thought he would come himself."
He had guessed Aegis's identity when she referred to Fermi as
"boss."
'So, it's the real Golden Wheel.'
Aegis bowed again.
"I'm sorry. The boss thought it would be better to handle Yahweh2's
issue quietly."
Shirone understood.
"Alright. Let's go now."
Aegis turned military-style and walked towards the northern exit of the
square.
Shirone didn't mind the stares, but Destroyer707 and his group felt
differently.
'This is the first time I've gotten this much attention. But who is
Yahweh2, really?'
They had thought he wasn't an ordinary user from the start, but that was
within High Gear.
'What's happening? Who is this boss? Are we getting involved in
something huge beyond our virtual world?'
'Is it okay to go along with this?'
Destroyer707 turned to see the same concern on his friends'
faces.
"Ugh, oh…"
When they came to their senses, they were in an alley in the northern
slums of the city.
Junkies, high on drugs, were rolling on the ground, making strange
noises.
Shirone asked, "Who are they?"
"They're addicted to drug programs. High Gear is primarily a battle
simulation, but being in the undercoder, its applications are vast. Also, being a floor coder makes it
hard to track. The boss conducted clinical trials here too."
Angel's Tears, it was called.
"Yaaah!"
A bald couple slumped against a wall stuck out their tongues at Shirone,
making strange noises.
Electricity sparked from their seven mechanical eyes.
'Here, drug reactions can be quantified. It's more effective than
biological experiments.'
Shirone could guess why Fermi chose High Gear.
Soon, they saw a shabby motel with half-lit neon signs, and Aegis went
straight to room 307.
"Boss, I've brought them."
Entering the room, they saw a man with a chilling appearance sitting on
the bed.
"Fermi?"
User codename, Yolga's Son.
He had half-shaved red hair and tattoos that made his face look like a
monster with black dye.
His eyes were white without pupils, and he wore a leather fur coat that
devil worshippers might wear.
"…What is he, a villain boss?"
In reality, Fermi played the role of a neat businessman, but his inner
self seemed different.
"Is that really Yolga's Son?"
Unlike the bewildered Shirone, Destroyer707 and his group couldn't
believe their eyes.
'Awesome. A flawless ATX eye from Genosa. And it's matte.'
The appearance alone was intimidating, but the lack of pupils was
excellent for close combat.
Fermi gestured to the table.
"Sit down. By the way… you have company?"
Forgetting it was a virtual world, Destroyer707 was at a loss.
"Uh, hello. We are…"
Shirone briefly said, "They're my friends."
Fermi nodded as if that was enough and offered them seats too.
"Nice to meet you. Have a seat."
"Uh, okay."
Even sitting far from the bed, Destroyer707 and his group were
dazed.
'What's going on?'
Codenames can be cleaned. So, meeting new users from large guilds
required caution.
'Can we not ask anything?'
Even without a clear definition, it felt like the balance of power was
in Yahweh2's favor.
Fermi started the conversation.
"So, then…"
"Hey! You!"
Shirone pointed a finger.
"You didn't tell me anything! The Tenmen, the Electronic Wasteland,
it's huge! You did it on purpose, didn't you?"
"Hey, why get mad over that? Honestly, it wasn't a big deal,
right?"
Looking back, it wasn't.
"You have to go through that to be called Yahweh. Listen, I wasn't
just playing around. About defeating the Operator, you haven't changed your mind, right?"
Destroyer707 and his group exchanged glances.
'He's really doing it?'
They had heard it, but Yahweh2 knew nothing about High Gear.
'Now the leader of Golden Wheel is speaking.'
The weight was entirely different, and they couldn't even imagine how it
was possible.
Shirone said, "I have no choice. I have to beat the Operator to get
things done."
"Just checking. Now that you understand the system, you know how
difficult it is, right?"
"…Yeah."
Shirone couldn't deny it.
"Then this will be easier. That's why I didn't tell you. Your
insight is much faster than my explanations. Have you heard of the Ascension Guild?"
"No."
"It's the top-ranked guild in the West Country, led by the
Operator. They have many rankers and famous people like me. They rarely engage in public activities.
They hunt at the highest difficulty levels inaccessible to regular users."
Fermi pointed west.
"She's difficult, probably a woman. She won't come to meet you. So,
you'll have to find her in the hunting grounds. But I think you'll die instantly, not just from the
guild members but from the creatures. This isn't about pride…"
"It doesn't hurt my pride."
Shirone interrupted.
"Just tell me how. How can I meet her?"
"The Solar War."
Destroyer707 and his group groaned.
"Ah…"
"It's the most crucial event in High Gear. You know the basic lore,
right?"
"Dr. Martin stole the sun."
"Yes. More precisely, the Solar Palace. It's the only place in this
world with a Low Gear."
"Low Gear?"
"A device that converts solar energy into electrical energy. The
electricity produced there flows through the entire continent via Mid Gear. Mid Gear is a capsule-like
device that charges High Gear's energy."
Fermi pointed at Shirone.
"You might not feel it now because your level is low, but once you
surpass level 100, energy management becomes challenging. You either recharge at a station with Mid Gear
installed or buy auxiliary devices. Think about it. Every user in High Gear relies on it."
Shirone understood.
"And the country that wins the Solar War…"
"Yes, they get it all. Especially the guild that controls the Solar
Palace can recharge High Gear for free until the next Solar War. Conversely, other countries have to pay
fees to buy energy. As you know, High Gear is the heart of the users. In reality, it's a life-and-death
battle."
"The Operator will definitely participate."
"Every guild participates. This is the only way you can meet the
Operator."
"Um…"
Death Princess raised her hand.
"Can I ask a question?"
"The princess's interest is always welcome."
She giggled.
"I've watched a lot of Solar War broadcasts. The tactics and
strategies are intense. The Operator will definitely participate, but Yahweh2 won't be able to meet her.
Honestly… unless you're at least near ranker level, you can't even enter the Solar Palace."
"That's our problem too."
Fermi nodded.
"The win rate between East and West is close, but the West won the
last Solar War. So we're attacking, and they're defending. To enter the Solar Palace, you have to get
through a massive crowd and face rankers. Especially the first team of the Ascension Guild, which you
can't break through."
Strongest Codename added, "They operate command centers for guild
tactics and strategies. It's impossible to carry out a personal mission in a battle where one mistake
can lose the sun. I don't think it's a good idea."
"I can do it."
Shirone didn't give up.
"Solving problems is your job. You have a plan, right? What should
I do?"
Fermi sighed and looked up.
"We give up the Solar War."
Destroyer707 and his group's expressions hardened.
"Honestly, it's impossible to carry you while winning the war. So
we give up. We'll use all the guilds in East Country to transport you near the Solar Palace. After
that…"
Fermi glanced at Aegis.
"The Golden Wheel guild members will distract the first team of the
Ascension Guild. Even if we can't win, we can do it if it's just to keep them away from
you."
Destroyer707 looked shocked.
"But that's betraying the guilds of East Country. The guild will
collapse, even if we leave the Solar War."
Fermi didn't budge, and neither did Shirone.
"What's the probability?"
"100 percent. Or zero percent. We'll keep marking the first team of
Ascension with our bodies. Even if we lose everything, we'll get you into the Solar Palace. That's my
responsibility. After that, it's up to you. Catch the Operator by any means necessary."
Destroyer707's mouth opened and closed.
"But, why go that far?"
He glanced at Aegis, who also had a determined expression.
Shirone asked, "How strong do I need to be? Even if the probability
is low, how strong do I need to be to make it possible?"
"Hmm. Have you noticed anything strange since you got here? Like
your senses being a bit different."
"I still have my intuition. It's not the same as reality, but the
signal system is identical."
"Heh, I see."
As if he had expected it, Fermi quickly calculated.
"At least level 300. Higher is better, but it's hard to reach that
before the Solar War. You don't want to take more time either."
In reality, every day was critical.
"From level 300, the experience required to level up is absurd. So
300 is the best, and the rest depends on parts."
"But parts are also affected by level. This bolt gun is already too
much for my output."
"Not with prototypes. You need special legendary or artisan parts
to complement energy efficiency. No matter what you do, your win rate won't exceed 5 percent. It's about
how close you can get."
Fermi stood up.
"Similarly, there's a limit to what I can give you now. I'll leave
you with your friends until you reach level 100. Get there in two days."
Destroyer707's eyes widened.
"What? Reach level 100 in two days?"
"Equip the best parts and hunt high-level creatures. Strong
creatures aren't always the answer, but you know this phase better than I do."
Of course, they did.
"But if we do that, we'll have to keep buying new parts as we level
up. What about the existing parts?"
"Discard them. I'll send you a credit card approved by Golden
Wheel. Buy everything you need. Use unlimited galaxies to get Yahweh2 to level 100 in two days. Can you
do it?"
Destroyer707 looked at his friends in a daze and then nodded.
"…Yes."
