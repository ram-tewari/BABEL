Chapter 823:
Smile, Smile (3)
Omega Year 938
It had been 1,500 years since Anke La dismantled the Ultima System
with the Elixir of Life.
In heaven, a few Gaia people, led by Maclaine Goffin, were still
resisting.
Many giants had ventured to other planets and created new humans
there.
Some of them returned and became the Celestials, and the River Tribe,
to which Ogent belonged, was one of them.
"Brother."
A woman with long, clear blue hair down to her waist, characteristic
of the River Tribe, approached Ogent.
"Are you alone again today?"
"Smile."
Ogent, who never showed emotions to anyone in the tribe, sometimes
gave a shy smile in front of his younger sister.
"You have no friends because you only practice swordsmanship. If
a person doesn't engage in social activities, they become a recluse."
"I prefer being alone."
Ogent didn't know his exact age.
Of course, he was also a Celestial, but his first memory was
wandering in the mountains like a stray dog.
The person who took pity on young Ogent and adopted him was Smile's
father, Damian.
"Go back. If Father finds out you're here..."
Smile pushed against Ogent's chest.
"Come on! Let's talk about that later. Come with me to the
Marang River. We can swim and catch some food."
If Damian found out, the atmosphere would become tense again, but no
one in the tribe could break Smile's stubbornness.
"We have to be back before sunset."
"I know."
The Marang River, which they reached after crossing a small ridge,
was as wide as the sea.
'Beautiful.'
Ogent liked looking at the river.
"Come on! Let's show off our skills. Father hasn't let me swim
lately."
He didn't like the idea of his daughter, who was about to get
married, swimming in the river.
"Well, you're an adult now... Ugh!"
As Smile threw off her top, Ogent's gaze wavered, unsure where to
look.
"What are you doing?"
"What's wrong? Everyone catches fish like this."
Of course, the River Tribe were good swimmers, and it wasn't the
first time he'd seen them catch fish dressed like this.
The problem was Smile, but she didn't understand Ogent's
discomfort.
"Even though we're siblings, we're old enough to be careful. I
understand why Father worries."
"Ha ha! Brother, you're funny. We used to bathe together when we
were kids. What's the big deal? You have a strange obsession. That's why people say you're
creepy."
"Creepy?"
To the River Tribe, Ogent was shy, emotionless, and obsessed only
with swordsmanship.
'Maybe they're right.'
Showing emotions to others was extremely difficult for him.
"That's why you should meet girls and make friends. You practice
swordsmanship every day, so you get embarrassed seeing your sister's body." Smile lifted her arms
and pushed up her chest.
"Well, I do have a nice body."
Then she laughed heartily.
"Don't make terrible jokes."
Ogent felt sad.
'It's like we're really becoming siblings.'
Smile's bold personality made her oblivious to the subtle changes in
Ogent's feelings.
Perhaps because Ogent was extremely shy, his emotions were thoroughly
hidden.
'I'm a coward.'
He couldn't approach her, nor could he leave her coldly.
"Anyway, get ready and come in. I'm going first!"
Taking a deep breath, Smile dove into the river from a low cliff with
graceful movements like a fish.
'I hate getting wet.'
Ogent sat down.
He watched Smile swim vigorously in the river, reflecting the
sunlight.
Just looking at the river made him happy.
After swimming for about 30 minutes, Smile began diving to hunt
fish.
Though she had no talent for swordsmanship, her perseverance was
unmatched, and she wouldn't come up until she caught one.
"Smile, let's go back now."
"Just one more!"
As she shouted and dived again, the river's current suddenly
changed.
'What is this?'
Only the hypersensitive Ogent could notice the change.
Soon, a fin emerged from the water.
'A sea creature?'
A Celestial creature weighing over 10 tons, which couldn't exist in
heaven's ecosystem.
"Smile! Get out!"
But Smile was already deep underwater.
"Damn it!"
As Ogent grabbed his sword and leaped from the cliff, Smile realized
the danger.
Fish chased by the sea creature were swimming towards her in a
group.
'I have to get up!'
As she surfaced with all her might, she saw the gaping mouth of a
giant fish.
"Smile!"
Just before she was swallowed, the fish's head was severed from its
gills and shot upwards.
"Brother!"
Grabbing Smile's hand, Ogent used external gravity to soar into the
sky.
Dozens of fish followed, casting shadows over their faces.
"Hold on tight!"
As he emitted external gravity in all directions, Ogent's body
vibrated violently.
As the sea creatures lunged, his sword swung like a whirlwind.
"Wow..."
Watching the fresh chunks of fish fall, Smile swallowed her
saliva.
'Looks delicious.'
Meanwhile, Ogent, exhausted, finally reached the cliff.
"Haa. Haa."
Smile gave a thumbs up.
"Brother, you're the best!"
"You idiot! You came to the river without knowing it's the sea
creatures' spawning season? Where's your mind?"
"You didn't know either."
"Why should I know that? Anyway, I..."
I'm not part of the River Tribe.
But saying that would make him feel like he was truly nothing.
"Brother, I'm hungry. Let's eat something."
Ogent had no choice but to close his mouth.
"Haa, you and your appetite."
He lit a small campfire and roasted the cleaned fish on
sticks.
"Delicious."
"We're already late, so eat slowly. You'll get
indigestion."
Smile nodded but didn't slow down her eating.
"Brother, about earlier. Flying through the air, is that
swordsmanship too?"
"It's a technique to expel inertia from the body. It's quite
difficult. Why?"
"Can you teach me that?"
Ogent chuckled.
"You? You haven't even mastered basic martial arts. How could
you?"
There were many skilled warriors in the River Tribe, but for some
reason, Smile had no talent.
"No, I'll work really hard. Please teach me."
"You have to awaken all your senses."
Ogent opened his palm.
"Anyone can open and close their fist. But to master your body,
you have to go deeper."
"How?"
"Move extremely slowly. Watch while you eat."
As Smile chewed, Ogent's eyes calmed.
At first, she didn't understand, but after about 10 minutes, she
began to feel it.
"Ah?"
Ogent's five fingers were barely moving.
"Got it? Slowly. Even slower. Break down the time it takes to
clench your fist into ten hours."
"I want to try."
Smile opened her palm.
"Hmm..."
Watching her closely, Ogent added,
"If you stop, it's over. Keep moving slowly without
stopping."
"Got it. Slowly."
Smile's expression was serious.
"Ah! I can't do it! I stopped again. This is harder than I
thought. Where did you learn this, Brother?"
"I didn't learn it. I developed it by thinking alone. It's about
breaking down the body's senses into the smallest units."
Ogent had no teacher.
"If I had to name a teacher..."
Smile blinked, waiting for an answer, but Ogent prepared to
leave.
"It's late. Let's go back."
As the sun set, Smile quietly packed up.
"Smile."
An old man with neatly tied blue hair stood with a cane at the
entrance to the village path.
"Father."
Damian, the chief of the River Tribe, watched Ogent and Smile walk
together.
"I'm sorry."
As Ogent avoided his gaze and bowed, Damian sighed.
'Haa, what a headache.'
He never showed it.
Though not his biological son, Ogent was also his child and the
greatest warrior of the River Tribe.
Or should he say warrior?
Compared to the domineering swordsmanship of the River Tribe, Ogent's
movements were delicate and precise.
'Even with different blood, they grew up together, yet they turned
out so differently.'
Ogent's isolation was partly due to his unparalleled talent with the
sword.
"Let's go home."
Without a word, Damian turned, and Ogent's heart grew heavier.
Dinner time, usually a harmonious occasion for the food-loving River
Tribe, was heavy with tension tonight.
"Ogent, the pre-wedding ceremony is tomorrow. The Mountain Tribe
will come, including Smile's fiancé."
"I know."
It was customary for the bride's family to invite the groom's family
for a seven-day feast before the wedding.
"I wonder what my fiancé is like. Even if he's not handsome, I
hope he's strong. Like you, Brother."
"Smile."
Ogent warned her, but Damian was already irritated.
"Ogent, what are your plans? The River Tribe has always
protected the river under the strongest warrior's command."
Ogent was the strongest, but his individualistic nature meant few
would support him as leader.
"I'm leaving."
This time, even Damian was surprised.
"Leaving?"
'I have no reason to stay here anymore.'
It was common for the chief's daughter to marry into another tribe,
but it felt too soon.
'It's because of me.'
"I'm not telling you to leave. You're a strong warrior and my
son. But protecting the river..."
"The River Tribe has always done that. I'm not qualified. I have
other plans."
"Plans?"
"I want to travel and improve my skills."
"You want to become a wanderer?"
With Ogent's skills, he could survive even in purgatory, but as a
father, it pained him.
'In the end... is he running away again?'
He was that kind of child.
Even if he wanted something, he couldn't say a word, and even when he
was angry, he just kept it to himself.
Such a son was reliable, but knowing his feelings for Smile now made
it all the more pitiful.
'Well, maybe this is the natural order of things.' If he hadn't been
adopted in the first place, the two could never be together.
"Brother! You're leaving suddenly? You've never said anything
like this to me before!"
"I'm sorry."
Smile raised her voice.
"Is 'sorry' enough? You're always like this! You never tell me
what's really important. I don't know what you're thinking."
What am I thinking?
'Damn it!'
If it was something that couldn't be undone anyway, he wanted to
shout it out before leaving.
'Smile, I...!'
But he couldn't.
Feeling the rising anger, Ogent put down his utensils.
"I'll get up first."
"Where are you going? Talk to me."
Ignoring Smile's words, he walked outside, and even Damian didn't
stop him.
'I must endure.'
Rational and cold, Ogent didn't have the courage to take a single
step away from the taboo.
'The sword, I must hold the sword. Control the anger.'
Anger was his only teacher.
