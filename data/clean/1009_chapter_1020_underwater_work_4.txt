'I have to leave.'

this ship.

"Chief Secretary, what's going on?"
"It's over. It's all over."
He stuffed the expensive watch he had bought into the depths of his bag
and zipped it up.
"You should pack quickly too. In the worst-case scenario, this ship
might sink."
The Chief Secretary wasn't one to talk nonsense, but this time it didn't
make sense.
"The ship will sink? Is it the demons? Or is a tsunami coming?"
Benahar hesitated.
Could Yahweh be more dangerous than the demons or a tsunami?
"Ugh…"
The memory of Shirone's gaze from earlier in the day made his metabolism
go haywire again.
How narrow is the human brain?
'I couldn't even imagine it until I felt it with my whole body.'

"Loving all humanity, do you really think that's something a human can

Benahar's hand trembled.
"That gaze. That mind. The moment I thought it didn't matter if I blew

It wasn't murderous intent, hostility, or anger.
"Yes, deprivation. At that moment, Yahweh clearly felt that I was just
'unnecessary.' No matter how you think about it, that's not a human emotion."
It was closer to divine logic.
"When that happens, your mind goes blank. You can't think of anything.
Negotiations, deals… those are things humans do in the human world."
If he could save all humanity, Shirone didn't care if he wasn't
human.
"A world where common sense doesn't apply. But I want to be human. I
want to make money, meet women, and live boasting even fake honor. That's what we're all trying to do.
I'm leaving."
Without waiting for the attendant's response, Benahar opened the door
and left.
The door slammed shut, leaving the room as chaotic as his mind.
