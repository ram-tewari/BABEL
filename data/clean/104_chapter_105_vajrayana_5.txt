Chapter 105:
Vajrayana (5)
When Siena opened the window, the protesters fell silent in an instant.
"What is all this commotion? Is it the student council's job to incite students and shake
the school?"
Amira walked toward the window.
"This isn't incitement. We didn't want it to come to this either. That's why we haven't
submitted the lawsuit yet. But there are too many unanswered questions. It's already been seven days since
Shirone died. Yet, not a single teacher has come forward to announce anything."
"Shirone isn't dead. He's just in a vegetative state."
"A vegetative state without a heartbeat is impossible. And there's no explanation for why
his body isn't decomposing. Couldn't it be Principal Alpheas's magic?"
The student council seemed to have reached that conclusion. It was an accurate guess.
But there are things in this world that shouldn't be judged solely by the outcome. The
student council didn't understand why Shirone had to be preserved.
The root of the problem was the Immortal Function. Even students of a magic school knew
nothing about the state of Nirvana, so their emotional response was inevitable.
"Think what you will. Right now, Shirone's body is being preserved with Slow Magic."
Siena admitted it outright. The student council, as well as the opposing students
blocking the entrance, erupted in murmurs.
"Are you admitting to the allegations?"
"That's hasty, Amira. The term 'allegations' can only be used if the teachers were
planning to use Shirone as a shield to escape responsibility."
"You're right, teacher. But we haven't heard any contextual evidence to judge that. The
student council can't understand the teachers' handling of this situation."
"The faculty hasn't acknowledged Shirone's death. Nor are we trying to escape
responsibility. That's why we're asking for your understanding."
"Teacher, Shirone's death is also a sad event for us. But the student council doesn't
want our beloved school to fall into ruin like this."
"I understand that you don't trust the teachers right now. But we've also deliberated
carefully and made the best decision we could. Whether you believe that or not is up to you."
Amira pondered Shirone's words. She couldn't rule out the possibility that the teachers
knew something she had overlooked. Humans are beings who believe what they think is right.
But no matter how much she thought about it, it didn't make sense. Shirone was already
dead. No, honestly, whether he was dead or alive, the fact that all the teachers were protecting him only
made her feel more resentful.
'Is this the right path? Am I being rational? What if Shirone comes back to life? But
that's absolutely impossible…'
If she weren't the student council president, Amira wouldn't have wanted to go as far as
filing a lawsuit. But if she backed down now, the students would bear the brunt of the consequences.
Fermi, who had been watching, didn't like seeing Amira struggle like this. Worried that
the situation might spiral out of control, he decided to step in.
"So, there's no room for compromise, is there? Then we have no choice but to resort to
our final option."
Siena looked at Fermi with displeasure. In her four years as a teacher, she had never
encountered a student like him. He was a person with exceptional talent but even more skilled at concealing
it.
He worshipped lies and enjoyed using that power to control situations. It might have been
a necessary skill for a magician, but it wasn't a quality a student should possess.
"Hmph, go ahead and file your lawsuit if you want. In the end, you'll be the ones who
regret it."
"Fine, we'll do that! First, we'll retrieve Shirone's body and proceed with the lawsuit!
Come on, let's go! We'll rescue Shirone with our own strength!"
Fermi led the graduating class toward the infirmary. The crowd, already swept up in mob
mentality, was beyond stopping.
"Let's hold Shirone's funeral and sue the teachers! The school belongs to the students,
not the teachers!"
"Wait! Stop this!"
Maria and the other students from Shirone's faction tried to block them, but their
efforts were futile, and the path was quickly cleared. Fermi's side consisted mostly of the revered seniors
from the graduating class. If it came to a physical confrontation, numerical superiority wouldn't matter.
"Isn't this going too far? A fellow student's life is at stake here!"
Mark stood firmly in their way with his characteristic resolve. But to the graduating
class, a junior from Class Seven wasn't even worth talking to.
At that moment, a third-floor window shattered. As the glass shards fell, the students
scrambled back. Neid jumped down from the building. The wind from his Fly Magic blew dust off the ground,
making the protesters grimace.
Neid slowly raised his head, his eyes blazing with killing intent.
"All of you, get lost. Before I kill you. No one touches Shirone."
Seeing the situation escalate, Amira abandoned her hesitation and took action. The damage
was already done, and if she lost momentum now, public opinion would turn against her.
"What is this? Do you really think violence will solve anything? And threatening to kill
your seniors? Where did you learn such manners?"
Neid didn't hear a word she said. His only thought was to protect Shirone.
"You want to take Shirone? Then come at me. But I can't guarantee your lives."
A bluish bolt rose above Neid's head and unleashed powerful lightning around him. It was
the Lightning Magic spell. But even more terrifying was the plasma emanating from Neid.
Amira stepped back in shock.
'How can an advanced class student use plasma…?'
To cast Plasma Magic, one had to either construct an ultra-high-temperature battery or
manifest a virtual ultra-high temperature with omnipotence.
The former method hadn't been developed yet, so all plasma magicians belonged to the
latter category. Since it couldn't be learned through theory, it was an exceptionally difficult realm to
reach.
Iruki looked down at Neid with displeasure.
'You fool. After holding back for so long…'
Iruki was the only one in the school who knew Neid's past. Five years ago, Neid had been
a completely different person. But he had vowed never to show his true face again and wore a mask. Now, he
had removed that mask—all to protect Shirone.
'Now I'm the one feeling guilty.'
Iruki smiled bitterly and turned away. Then, as if he had never been there, he slipped
out of the infirmary unnoticed.
***
The protesters were visibly shaken. The graduating class included Raiken, known as the
"Electric Monster," so they understood how dangerous plasma could be.
Neid glared at Amira and spoke.
"Last warning. Get lost."
"Why are you going this far? What does protecting a dead friend mean to you?"
"Shirone isn't dead."
Neid's eyes widened as tears streamed down his face.
"What do you know? How dare you spout nonsense about Shirone!"
Seeing Neid's tears, Amira pitied him. But she had no intention of backing down.
"If you want a physical confrontation, I can't just stand by. Before the students get
hurt, I'll subdue you first."
Amira took a step back and clenched her fists at her dantian. A grayish cloud swirled
around her, forming a mist. It was a type of weather magic—Cloud Magic. It had excellent compatibility with
electricity. No, at Amira's level, she could even absorb Neid's lightning and reflect it back.
"Anyway… why do something you can't handle?"
Iruki walked out from the entrance. Neid frowned. What was he here to nitpick about now?
But Iruki stopped not next to Amira but beside Neid.
"What? Don't interfere. This is my fight."
"But from now on, it's my fight too."
"Weren't you on their side?"
Neid's tone was sharp. There was no bad blood between them as friends, but Iruki, who
believed Shirone was dead, had no reason to join this battle.
***
"Are we kids picking sides? I'm serious too. I don't want them taking Shirone away."
Amira made a baffled expression. It was admirable that they wanted to protect their
friend, but they were still just advanced class students. There was no way they could stand against the
graduating class.
"Do you really think you're something special because we've been lenient? Do you think
you can win against us?"
Amira and the entire protest group entered Spirit Zone. The overwhelming pressure from
the graduating class's zone reached Neid and Iruki through their shared senses.
'Heh. Hehehe.'
Fermi, watching their standoff, secretly shrugged his shoulders. The current situation
was laughably absurd.
'Good. Fight, fight. Destroy yourselves.'
Fermi knew that all profit was born from chaos and conflict. In that sense, war was the
ultimate blue ocean. Without the exploitation of the many by the few, wealth accumulation was impossible.
Fermi knew how to win wars. Remove emotions. Humanity's weakness was ultimately emotion.
Those who manipulated others' emotions ruled the world.
'Grieve. Rage. Shout that you alone are right. That's how you grow the pot. Of course,
I'll be the one to take all the winnings.'
Siena, observing the situation from the infirmary, fixed her gaze on Fermi.
'This is all your doing, you wretched brat.'
She was furious but couldn't blame him. Fermi's philosophy wasn't half-baked either.
Perhaps he was even exploiting the situation Shirone was in.
Alpheas approached Siena.
"There's nothing we can do now."
"Principal."
"It seems this is as far as we go. We'll have to accept the student council's demands."
Aimi shouted.
"Principal! No! What about Shirone?"
"Aimi, I'm sorry."
"How can you do this! You know Shirone isn't dead yet!"
"No. The truth is, I don't know."
Stunned, Aimi froze. Alpheas placed a hand on her shoulder and spoke.
"Aimi, no one can determine Shirone's condition. We're just believing. I, too, believe
Shirone will return."
"But why…?"
"But look. Even unrelated students are fighting now. How we got here doesn't matter. I
can't let my students keep fighting like this."
To Alpheas, the other students were just as precious as Shirone. Now, they were
unleashing Spirit Zones to kill each other. In this situation, prioritizing only Shirone would be selfish.
"I'll go down and speak to them myself. We'll inform Shirone's parents and proceed with
the funeral. Disciplinary actions can be discussed afterward."
Aimi slumped into a chair, her strength leaving her. Seriel approached with teary eyes.
"Aimi, what do we do? Shirone, Shirone is…"
Aimi felt the same. In the end, Shirone hadn't returned. The moment Alpheas acknowledged
it, it felt like Shirone had disappeared from her heart too.
"Shirone…"
***
The atmosphere in front of the building was on the verge of exploding. Everyone had
activated their Spirit Zones, and at the slightest provocation, lethal magic could be unleashed.
Amira felt doubt creeping in again. Was this really the right path? Could fellow students
pointing Spirit Zones at each other and preparing to kill ever be justified by any logic?
She bit her lip and alternated her gaze between Neid and Iruki. Neid was already in full
combat mode, but Iruki seemed to leave room for dialogue.
"You're Iruki, right? The Servant ability user."
Iruki didn't answer.
"Do you really have to do this? Your friend is too emotional. But you're different,
aren't you?"
"Sorry, but I'm emotional right now too."
"Liar. No, it doesn't matter. You know, don't you? Do you really think your friend is
alive? Is that your conclusion?"
"Hmm, conclusion? Then the answer is simple."
Iruki, who had been lost in thought, glared at Amira.
"Shirone isn't dead."
Neid turned to Iruki in surprise. Just moments ago in the infirmary, he had insisted
Shirone was dead. Why the sudden change?
"Iruki, you…"
Iruki kept his gaze forward. Amira's disappointment grew. She had expected more from him,
considered the most rational person in the school, but he was just another brat from the advanced class.
"If you really think so, then you must have some logic to convince us, right?"
"Of course."
Amira blinked in surprise. Until she heard it, she couldn't believe the situation.
"Then state your evidence. Why do you think Shirone is alive?"
"It's 4 o'clock now… I'll leave it at that."
Ding. Ding. Ding. Ding.
The clock tower chimed.
Iruki had measured the time without looking at a clock because he was a Servant. The
method was simple: count the seconds from a fixed point in time.
Neid realized why Iruki had been so quiet lately.
'He was counting the seconds. But since when?'
Amira stared blankly at the clock tower. Then, realizing something, she turned her head.
"Now I get it. You're just insane. What does time have to do with Shirone's death?"
Iruki didn't answer. He had expected some margin of error, but it was taking much longer
than anticipated.
'4:07. 4:08. 4:09. What the hell is taking so long?'
Amira made up her mind. Her judgment was correct. Trying to reason with the emotional
Neid and the already insane Iruki was a waste of time.
"We're taking Shirone. Let's see how long you can hold out against the graduating class."
The overwhelming aura of the school's top students filled Neid's eyes with murderous
intent. Then, an unfamiliar yet familiar voice rang out.
"Looks like you're having fun. Mind if I join? This is my specialty, after all."
Everyone looked around. But the owner of the voice was nowhere to be seen. Moments later,
two figures—a man and a woman—emerged from the shadows of the building. It was Canis and Arin.
Amira's eyes sparked with anger.
"You two! How dare you show your faces here! We'll arrest you on the spot!"
"Try if you can. Not that we plan to run, though."
Canis snorted and walked toward Iruki.
Iruki frowned in annoyance.
"You're late."
"You said to come by 4."
"You're 27 seconds late."
Canis bristled but suppressed his anger. Settling the score came first.
"Here, the thing you asked for."
Iruki quickly took the document Canis handed him and flipped through the pages. Neid,
finally curious, peered at the document.
"Iruki, what's that?"
"A report from Yongrae. I sent them samples of Shirone's tissues."
"What? When? No, how did you get here from the capital in five days?"
Even by carriage, it would take four days. And Yongrae needed time to analyze the
samples, so the actual round trip would have to be within 48 hours.
"I asked Canis for help. He knows the geography of Bashuka well."
Canis gritted his teeth, recalling the ordeal. He had made it on time, but it had been a
journey fraught with mortal danger. He traveled by carriage during the day and used Dark Port at night.
Nights were faster, but exhausting his magic to the limit had caused him to lose consciousness more than
once.
"Anyway, this settles my debt to Shirone."
Canis felt no particular guilt over Shirone's death. But as a disciple, he did feel a
shred of responsibility for Shirone's sacrifice due to Arcane's atrocities.
"Not that it's my business. I'll pass the message along to Shirone, though."
The strange turn of events made Amira uneasy. Especially since Yongrae was the kingdom's
top organization, led by Iruki's father.
"What kind of trick is this? Do you think we'll back down just because of Mercodain?"
Mercodain was undeniably intimidating. But the families of the students gathered here
were no pushovers either. With numbers on their side, it was a fight they could win.
"Don't worry about that. I used a pseudonym. If my name were on it, who knows what the
old man would do. More importantly, it's the content of this document."
"Hah! Does it say something about magic to revive the dead?"
Iruki closed the document and began reading from the start.
"Title: Report on Immortal Function and Suspended Biological Activity."
The protesters flinched at the title and fell silent. Even those in the third-floor
infirmary pricked up their ears at Iruki's next words.
"Analysis results of the subject's tissue cell samples. Non-dynamic vegetative state. No
organic deformation found in samples taken 24 hours post-mortem. Data attached."
Iruki turned to the next page.
"Based on the above data, the subject's biological activity is suspended. However,
extending this state to functional suspension—i.e., absence of life—is premature."
Iruki emphasized this critical point.
"Yongrae classifies cardiac arrest into three cases. First, a state where heart function
has ceased. Second, a state where heart function is temporarily suspended. Third, a state where the heart
appears to have stopped but hasn't functionally."
Amira made a baffled expression.
"What nonsense is this? The first case makes sense, but the others don't!"
Iruki, as if expecting this, turned the page.
"Examples. While a state of ceased heart function can be considered death, the Immortal
Function introduces two new possible cases."
In the hushed atmosphere, Iruki's voice grew louder.
"Case 1. If mental expansion is delayed due to a special event, the body may temporarily
suspend function until the mind reaches a conclusion. Report attached."
Iruki nodded and continued.
"Case 2. Even if mental expansion concludes, the observer's time may differ. That is,
biological activity may be normal but unobservable externally due to spacetime barriers. Report attached."
Iruki felt no need to read further and skimmed to the end.
"Based on the above magical analysis, the subject—currently lying in the infirmary—"
He held up Yongrae's report for the protesters to see.
"Arian Shirone cannot be considered deceased."
The protesters, who had been vocal until now, fell silent. They, too, were magicians.
They knew no one could refute Yongrae's analysis.
"Iruki. Since when…?"
Iruki smirked at the dazed Neid.
"So? The method to revive the dead that I found."
Neid pouted sheepishly. It was real. A method to bring back the dead. While everyone else
wallowed in grief and false hope, Iruki had fought alone.
"Here, see for yourself."
Iruki handed the report to Amira. She silently took it and checked the first page.
Yongrae's seal was unmistakably stamped there.
Further conflict was pointless. The kingdom's top academic institution had proven the
teachers right. Even if they filed a lawsuit, the court would likely side with the school.
"You've worked hard, Amira."
Alpheas appeared at the building's entrance.
"Principal."
