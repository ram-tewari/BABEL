Chapter 1164:
Arius spoke.
And just like that, as Sein's family gathered, the objects floating in
midair slowly descended.
"I'm sorry."

"I was the one who taught Miro. She's not at fault. Please don't be too
angry."
"How the hell did you do that? Floating objects in midair… Is it some
kind of supernatural ability?"
Sein already knew the answer.
A power he had seen in a dream.
No, now these incidents were forcibly imprinting themselves into his
mind even while awake.
Miro stepped in front of Sein.
"Shirone didn't do anything wrong! I just copied what I saw him
do."
Just from watching?
Sein's mind went blank.
Is that how it works?
Luber had called their situation a spot-the-difference puzzle, where
neither version could be identified as the original.
There are two different worlds. But we can't say which one is
real.
However, in the dream world, Miro had been a seeker of truth with the
deepest understanding of human thought.
Shirone, Miro—this isn't right. Something is forcing them to
awaken.
Sein spoke.
"Let's go see Gaold."
"I already went."
Gaold entered through the door, tossing his house keys and cash onto the
table before taking off his jacket.
"You're back?"
Kangnan took his clothes and asked.
"How was it today?"
"The same as always. It was collection day, so I barely made any money.
Those bastards were more aggressive than usual."
Kangnan had never criticized Gaold's lack of business skills.
"It'll get better. The merchant alliance is moving. Sooner or later,
they'll clash with the Tamo Syndicate."
Kangnan was part of the merchant alliance.
"We're going to raid the armory and hit their hideout. We outnumber
them."
"Hah! Those bastards aren't even—"
"Don't underestimate them. Everyone is fighting for survival. And we're
no different."
"They think this world is all there is. They're not playing a
spot-the-difference puzzle like us."
"Yeah."
Kangnan gave a bitter smile.
"In the dream world, we were incredible. But we can't live in a dream
forever."
Kangnan wrapped her arms around Gaold's waist.
"What's wrong? Didn't we decide to live on? At least until Miro grows
up…"
"Enough."
Gaold felt guilty.
"That's not it. I'm sorry. I'm just… confused. Lately, more than
ever."
Kangnan felt the same.
"I know what you're thinking. Sometimes… I get anxious. Like I'm a
burden to you."
"A burden?"
No matter which world was the real one, Kangnan was the most precious
person to Gaold.
As their gazes met and their lips slowly drew closer—
"Old man!"
The door suddenly slammed open as Miro burst in.
"Huh?!"
Seeing Gaold and Kangnan abruptly pull away, she blinked in
confusion.
Something about it felt complicated, but the girl quickly smirked
playfully.
"What? So you and Kangnan— Ow!"
Sein smacked her on the head.
"How many times have I told you to knock before entering someone's
house? Apologize."
"This isn't 'someone's house!' Auntie, I'm here!"
Miro threw her arms wide and ran toward Kangnan, who laughed and hugged
her.
"Alright. What brings you here at this hour?"
In reality, Gaold and Kangnan were sworn enemies, but Miro was unaware
of this, and Kangnan was living with Gaold.
A perfect compromise.
"Where's Luber?"
Lately, Luber and Monga had been absent, though no one had really needed
to seek them out.
"They're not here. What's going on?"
Sein looked at Shirone, and Gaold popped open a beer bottle, raising it
like a toast.
"You're here, scholar?"
Kangnan elbowed him in the ribs, but Shirone's expression remained as
unreadable as ever.
"Hello."
Sein spoke.
"There's been an incident. Something's happening to Shirone and Miro… We
need Luber."
"What kind of incident?"
"Shirone, can you show them here?"
Shirone hesitated, clearly reluctant. Sein walked up and patted his
shoulder.
"It's okay. No one's blaming you. We're trying to help you. Just show
us."
"… Alright."
As Shirone extended both hands forward, Gaold laughed.
"What is this? A magic trick?"
The moment he finished speaking, the beer bottle in his hand lifted into
the air and floated toward Shirone.
Gaold and Kangnan's jaws dropped in shock, while Miro shouted
excitedly.
"Auntie, Auntie! I can do that too!"
Gaold, staring dumbfounded at the bottle Shirone had returned, finally
looked up.
"What the hell? Goddamn…"
"They've both awakened."
Monga spoke.
As the twilight dimmed along the coast, they all turned back toward
Gaold's house.
"They've found the hidden code through an immortal function. Ever since
the Tamo Syndicate arrived, it's been a storm."
"Hah, well, the Five Stars reject the 1.5th layer. So they must have
projected the feeling of reality into this world. And Miro saw it and came to the same
realization."
Monga spoke with concern.
"If the compromise starts to break down like this… the 1.5th layer will
eventually be destroyed. We need to keep an eye on their changes—"
"Watch Gaold. He'll be the one to decide whether the 1.5th layer
continues to exist."
"Huh?"
Monga tilted his head in confusion.
"But he's the most lost one here. It feels like he's being pulled in
every direction."
"That's what it means to be human. They waver endlessly, but the moment
they decide, that becomes everything."
That's why conviction exists.
"If in reality, the Five Stars, Miro, and Gaold collide, there's no
telling who will force their will through. Not because it's a close match—but because the outcome is
beyond imagination."
Monga asked seriously.
"What happens if Gaold awakens?"
"Probably…
It'll be a complete mess."
Gaold sat on the couch, chugging a beer.
"Ahh, refreshing."
Arius lay sprawled on the rug while Shirone and Miro floated toys in
midair, spinning them around.
"Hmmm."
At the dining table, Sein spoke to Kangnan.
"In three days, we'll have the right amount of supplies. The issue is

Kangnan examined a schematic of explosives.
"We've secured three hundred people. But the ones guarding the warehouse

"The first strike is what matters. If we seize the armory, we'll have

Miro interrupted.
"Dad, I have a great idea."
They had kept her in the dark, but she already knew everything.
Just like Shirone, she claimed that when she focused, she could sense

"Stay out of this."
Even so, Sein had no intention of involving his daughter.
"Just listen first! The problem is throwing the bombs, right? The

Miro caught a toy Shirone tossed into the air and placed it on the

"This is the bomb."
The toy slowly floated across the air and gently landed between Sein and
Kangnan.
"Boom."
Sein's pupils trembled.
"What do you think? If we do it like this, we can blow up the warehouse
without getting shot, right?"
Silence fell over the room.
"No."
Gaold spoke.
"This isn't a kid's game. Moving objects around isn't enough to win a
fight."
Miro snapped.
"What do you know? You just keep getting pushed around by the Tamo

Gaold chugged his beer, avoiding the question, while Sein asked,
"You're really not going to do it?"
"I'm not."
"Why?"
Gaold pointed at Shirone.
"Are you all idiots? Can't you see what's happening? You're using
strange abilities. And you're talking about just throwing bombs?"
"… What's your point?"
"Spot-the-difference. At this point, isn't it obvious which one is
real?"
This world was a lie.
"If we let the Tamo Syndicate run loose, they'll bring this world to an
end. And when that happens, we'll wake up."
"That's possible. But if your so-called 'real world' is as you say, then

A life spent writhing in the ultimate threshold of pain.
"You wouldn't last a minute. It'd be pure agony. You're saying you'd

"Yeah."

"So, this isn't just about 'seeing her one last time before you die.'

"Gaold can't do it."

nothing."
"I see."
"The pain of reality is something that already happened. But when given

to improve themselves. But Gaold? He can't. The pain he has to endure surpasses the limits of living
beings."
Gaold muttered.
"So… I don't really care. Merchant alliance, whatever—they can handle

Miro shouted,
"I'm disappointed in you! You said you liked me! But I hate

Gaold downed his beer.
"Well, who knows? Maybe if you were grown up and an adult, I'd care. But
I'm not interested in little kids."
"You're an idiot! A moron! A worthless jellyfish!"
That night.

ominous feeling.
A rotten nightmare.

the dream as a nightmare.
"Are you sleeping well?"

on Miro's room.
And the moment he opened the door—
"Gaold!"
His voice thundered through the house.

"Thirty bombs are missing."

"Can we call the merchant alliance?"
"I don't know. I doubt we could summon them immediately, and more

As Kangnan was about to slide a knife into her boot, Gaold stepped
forward and snatched it.
"What the hell are you doing?"

Thud!

"Idiots."

"What's your plan? Miro's still a kid, and Shirone is unstable. We need

"Yeah. But Miro's the one who went."
As he recalled the feeling of wanting to kill even a god, Gaold pulled

"All this talk about market economy and city survival—you're going about

He turned to face them.
"Do you even know what fight you're in? Stop talking and follow me.
We'll go get them back."
Kangnan and Sein realized.
That's right.
Gaold always fights for himself.
But us…
As if bewitched, they had followed him all the way here, always looking
at his back.
