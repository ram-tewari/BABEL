Chapter 422:
Shirone and Plu headed to the 2nd Headquarters.
Clove and Gardrak guided them, and as they closed the distance using the
light spots, they received various pieces of information.
"Research suggests that the angels' 'overlooking' isn't about the
observer's gaze leaving but rather pulling the desired location in front of them. It's like opening a
drawer in a room and rummaging through it. So how did the rebels avoid being overlooked?"
Gardrak's words were translated through the drone they received from the
headquarters.
The drones produced in Heaven didn't have the language of the land, but
those from 'Yamaeng' were programmed with the languages of many border guards.
"Hmm, putting a lock on the drawer?" Plu suggested.
Gardrak nodded. "That's the Mecha tribe's way. They use a
self-developed magnetic field to disrupt the scenery, making it impossible to focus."
Shirone added, "Then the second way is to create many
drawers?"
"Correct. The 2nd Headquarters we're heading to operates in a
decentralized manner. It's a classic concealment strategy, but it significantly reduces
risk."
Plu asked, "What kind of place is the 2nd
Headquarters?"
"I've never been to the headquarters myself. I worked in the 13th
Battalion before being transferred to the 1st Headquarters. Unlike the Mecha, who prioritize command,
the Nor prioritize individual ability. They believe the strongest should be the commander."
Shirone said, "Then the commander must be very strong."
"I've heard so. The commander is a woman named Raesis, and rumors
say her Spirit Force is as high as 32%."
Spirit Force is an indicator of affinity with essence, a mental state
much closer to the essence than the Spirit Zone.
Plu asked, "How impressive is 32%? If it reaches 100%, can you
control essence freely?"
"That's impossible. It's just affinity. At 32%, it's like providing
water for 100 people with a single drop. The average Nor's affinity is only 7-8%. Even a 1% difference
exponentially increases magical power."
Shirone vaguely imagined the 2nd Headquarters commander in his
mind.
"Anyway, I agree with this journey. The Mecha are strong against
giants but weak against fairies. On the other hand, the Nor are strong against fairies. An alliance is
essential for survival."
As they talked, Shirone's group arrived at the light spot.
Above a sparkling, transparent stream, particles of light floated
gently.
Gardrak waded into the stream, extended his fist to the spot, and
prepared the light magic Elizer.
Plu watched the light seep into his fist and asked, "Do we have to
go further? This is the third time already."
"This is the last one."
He had saved this information in case they were attacked on the way and
the information was leaked.
Plu nodded and approached Shirone.
She felt more at ease than when surrounded by her illustrious seniors,
but now she had to protect Shirone entirely.
She recalled the conversation with Gaold after finishing negotiations
with Crude and leaving the bunker the previous night.
"Plu."
"Yes, Chairman."
Gaold glanced at Shirone and then spoke.
"I'm worried. Other teams have veterans mixed in, but you and
Shirone are different."
Plu readily acknowledged this.
Of course, she knew Gaold was concerned not about their safety but about
the project's success.
"I fully understand. I will risk my life to ensure we don't let our
seniors down."
"In case things go wrong…"
Gaold paused.
To Plu, it was a remarkable moment.
The fact that he, who could be compared to a machine with broken brakes,
hesitated, already felt like a reward.
Plu smiled softly and said, "Don't worry. If a life must be lost in
this project, I know it will be mine. As a supporter, no matter what happens, I will ensure Shirone
returns safely."
Shirone, who joined the team as the Terminator, was the master key of
the task force, meant to survive until the end even if most team members died.
As a magician, it was shameful to weigh her life against that of such an
important figure and supporter.
Gaold looked at Plu silently for a moment, then turned with a wicked
grin.
"Hehe, I'll trust you."
His last words deeply resonated with Plu.
She watched Gaold's broad back with a dazed expression, then saluted
with a bright smile.
'I don't mind dying. I'm part of the best team.'
"Um…"
At that moment, Crude, who had been watching from a distance, cleared
his throat and approached.
Even in wartime, he believed humans should live humanely, so he
cautiously expressed his feelings.
"If there's anything uncomfortable…"
"No."
Plu turned coldly, her smile gone as if it had never been there.
Shirone was playing with minnows, stirring them with his feet.
If not for the mission, he was just an ordinary
nineteen-year-old.
She would do her best to protect Shirone.
But in reality, the word "best" had no meaning in actual
combat.
Only completing the goal by any means necessary mattered, and for that,
Shirone also needed determination.
"Listen. Now that you're here, you're a magician too. You haven't
graduated yet, so you're an unofficial magician."
Shirone looked at Plu in confusion.
"So from now on, I'll treat you as a professional. You must stay
focused on the mission and not be swayed by emotions. I'm not asking you to do anything unreasonable. In
fact, you might not even be able to distinguish what's unreasonable. That's war. If confusion arises,
focus solely on the mission."
Shirone understood.
His seniors, who were like gods to him, would gladly sacrifice their
lives to protect him, who didn't even have a magician's license.
What was most nerve-wracking was knowing this and still having to send
them into danger without hesitation.
'I must survive. That's my mission.'
Shirone nodded firmly.
"Yes. Don't worry."
No one wants to take on an unpleasant task.
If he didn't do it, someone else would have to bear the burden.
Shirone resolved, or rather prayed.
No matter what situation arises, please let me not hesitate.
"Let's go."
A massive flash of Elizer shot into the sky.
As the world was enveloped in warm light, the forest scenery
shimmered.
When the light faded, the scene changed from the valley with the stream
to the middle of a dense forest.
Plu looked around and asked, "Is this the
headquarters?"
"No. The gatekeeper of the headquarters will lead us. We probably
have to go much further."
It was a measure to avoid being followed. It showed how thoroughly they
were hiding.
Gardrak put his fingers in his mouth and whistled.
As the sound magic Peri was cast, it sounded like hundreds of birds
chirping, flying into the forest sky.
After a while, a man appeared from behind a tree.
"Where are you from? I wasn't informed of any visitors
today."
He was a man with a cold impression.
He had blue hair, sharp eyes, and the left side of his head was shaved.
The right side of his hair, which reached his shoulders, covered one eye.
Gardrak pointed to Shirone and said, "We're from the 1st
Headquarters. You've heard the rumors, right? This boy is…"
"Ah, I see."
The man raised his hand and cast a spell.
The forest magic Oprica.
The trees behind them shook, and numerous vines grew rapidly, flying
towards Shirone's group.
"Ugh!"
Clove and Gardrak, terrified, lost their balance and fell, while Shirone
and Plu remained still.
The trajectory of the flying vines wasn't aimed at them.
Anyone could have figured it out if they had stayed calm.
The vines struck the ground and grew into an arch.
In an instant, Shirone's group was trapped in an arch-patterned wooden
prison.
'This is ancient plant magic.'
From the remnants of his battle with Bosun in Kazra, Shirone knew that
even a certified 4th-level magician couldn't produce such rapid growth.
He didn't think the man was stronger than Bosun, but the advantages of
ancient magic using essence affinity were clear.
'This won't be easy.'
He was concerned that groups that displayed force from the start rarely
negotiated properly.
The gatekeeper cast Oprica again, drawing more branches to aim at the
group.
"Why have the traitors of Nor come to us?"
Clove grabbed the prison bars and shouted, "What are you doing? Do
you know who this is? This is Shirone, the light of Sector 73!"
The more hostile the relationship, the more active the espionage.
The gatekeeper also knew that Shirone had returned.
"So what?"
"So what? Don't you want to fight the war? Don't you know how much
the rebels' morale will rise with Shirone?"
A sharp branch pierced through the prison bars and stopped in front of
Clove's nose.
"Ugh!"
Clove hurriedly backed away, hitting the opposite side of the
prison.
Oprica was a magic that couldn't be cast without more than 10%
affinity.
With only 7% affinity, Clove couldn't resist the man's power.
"What does the light of Sector 73 matter? When did you criticize
and denounce Nor, and now you want to fight together? We don't need the power of the Mecha tribe. How
can we fight angels with those who rely on machines?"
"How do you know that?"
Shirone spoke.
"We came to see your leader, not you. I don't think you have the
authority to decide the fate of the entire Nor tribe."
The man turned his gaze to Shirone.
Shirone's exploits were well-known, but the Nor, who had absolute
standards of power, were not easily swayed by mere myths.
If the light of Sector 73 thought that defeating a fairy chief like
Igirin would be enough to impress here, he was either quite naive or an overconfident youngster.
"That might be true. But as the gatekeeper, I have authority.
Whether it's the Nephilim or the light of Sector 73, what matters in war is power. I don't know how it
is in Mecha, but whining in a wooden prison won't make the Nor even snort."
"Is that so?"
Shirone placed his palm on the prison.
As light began to concentrate in his hand, the gatekeeper quickly
stepped back.
From what he had heard, Shirone could manipulate light freely,
regardless of affinity, and could even destroy objects with it.
"Hmph! Even so…"
The gatekeeper, a skilled practitioner of ancient magic entrusted with
guarding the rebel headquarters, gathered all the surrounding vegetation, twisted the vines into sharp
spears, and prepared to counterattack.
'What's this?'
But the opportunity to counterattack never came.
The light in Shirone's hand gradually darkened, and the vines began to
twist grotesquely.
Dark Light.
The sight of the thick vines contorting left the gatekeeper
stunned.
Crack! Crack!
With a sound like bones being crushed, the hemispherical prison was torn
from the ground.
Crack! Crack! Crack!
Eventually, it twisted towards a void that had formed, being sucked in
completely.
Like a glutton swallowing a living creature whole, the trees struggled
from their roots but were ultimately consumed, leaving no trace.
Clove and Gardrak's faces turned pale.
Even when Shirone had shattered a rock with light before, they hadn't
been this shocked.
"W-what kind of magic is that, Master?"
"……"
Gardrak had no idea. The gatekeeper likely didn't either.
All the Nor could only stare at Shirone in a daze.
