Chapter 829:
Breaking the System (1)
At the start of the event where the black sphere approached, Ogent
felt dizzy.
It wasn't as fast as light, but it was the limit of the law that a
material with mass could produce.
It felt that way.
'Cut.'
Time flowed slowly, and the afterimage of the black sphere overlapped
vividly like a real object, revealing its trajectory.
At the exact point where mind and body met, Ogent's two arms, holding
nothing, fell down.
Idea.
To Ogent, who had reached the pinnacle of swordsmanship, this was a
concept that did not exist in this world.
As the entire law distorted, the scenery behind Ogent split left and
right.
'What is that?'
In the time delay phenomenon where the halo rotated at high speed,
Kariel saw it clearly.
As the three-dimensional scenery compressed into two dimensions, the
end of the world opened up right before his eyes.
As if the world he had experienced so far was just a picture drawn on
a single curtain.
'The universe is...'
It was opening.
A concept penetrating from outside the universe engraved a sword into
reality and collided with the black sphere.
'It's the end.'
Heaven would be destroyed.
Seventh Heaven, Araboth.
The giant mass of flesh pulsating at the top of the spire, Anke Ra,
opened its huge eyes.
'Breaking the System!'
Even a fleeting moment was just a concept that could stretch into
eternity for Anke Ra, who dreamed of this world.
From the state of non-being, he closely observed the strongest law in
this world and the point of breaking that law.
'A mere creature is tearing the universe.'
If asked how that was possible, Anke Ra could only answer this
way.
'Isn't that a very human doubt?' The real question should not be 'how
is it possible,' but 'what is this world where that is possible.'
'I am the whole.'
Therefore, he questioned.
'Can something that transcends me exist?'
As the curves of thought crossed mountains, Anke Ra's pupils moved
rapidly.
Since time was almost at a standstill, the shaking of his pupils was
akin to the speed of light...
'It doesn't exist anywhere.'
After calculating the Akashic Records an almost infinite number of
times, Anke Ra finally concluded.
'It must be concluded that it does not exist.'
The conclusion he reached was not logical, as it was in the realm of
belief rather than certainty.
Therefore, it was a clear error.
But since the Akashic Records were the entirety of this world, a
paradox arose that they could not be wrong.
'Breaking the System is impossible.'
It must be made so.
'I have awakened, and everything is mine.'
Anke Ra manipulated the Akashic Records, detaching a part of the
Great World War to a distant place in the universe.
'As far as possible!'
Even in the unit of thought that divided one second infinitely, the
space he could send was only a sphere with a diameter of about 40 meters.
"Aaah!"
Following Ogent's shout, a vertical shockwave collided head-on with
the material cannonball.
With a roar, the trees in the dense forest were all uprooted and flew
away, turning into a wasteland.
"Huff! Huff!"
Ogent, whose arms had disappeared, staggered and knelt, glaring
forward with terrifying eyes.
Kariel sat on the ground with his left shoulder cut off.
"You..."
Even Kariel could not analyze the power that surpassed the concept of
an archangel.
"Smile."
From Ogent's shoulders, where his arms had disappeared, bones and
muscles began to regenerate rapidly.
'Cell regeneration? No, this is...'
Even with the power of a giant, physical restoration was possible,
but what was happening to Ogent was on a different level.
'Information is being restored.'
Signals from outside the universe were perfectly reconstructing his
information.
'To destroy that human, I must use the same method.'
But for an archangel relying on Anke Ra's law, breaking the System
was never permissible.
Thinking he needed to buy time, Kariel looked around.
'Where is this?'
It was a place he had never been to, and beyond the radius destroyed
by Ogent, the forest stretched endlessly.
"Give me Smile."
Ogent took a step toward the glass sphere filled with black
liquid.
As the space fell, the Elixir of Life stopped, but there was enough
time for a person to suffocate.
Kariel awkwardly stood up and blocked the way.
'Heaven may have been saved from destruction, but if I can't stop
this guy, it's all the same.'
Swinging the Idea wouldn't immediately end the universe, but it
wasn't theoretically impossible either.
"Will you stop me?"
Ogent slowly raised his hand.
"Then die."
Just as another breaking of the system was about to occur, the space
between Kariel and Ogent twisted like a vortex.
"What...?"
Anke Ra had sent something else here, but before the phenomenon
ended, the space tore apart.
A huge figure emitting white steam from its body flew at
Ogent.
"Ha!"
As the fist exploded, Ogent's body disappeared from the spot.
Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!
Kariel, who was watching Ogent fly endlessly through the forest,
turned his head.
"Who are you...?"
"Hehe, it's been a while since I've had a fun
fight."
Anke Ra, who recognized breaking the System as an error, chose not an
archangel but the king of creatures, Ymir.
Ymir, who had been frowning at the shock of crossing space, looked
back at Kariel.
"You got hit properly."
Since Kariel was an archangel, a higher being by law, he showed a
displeased expression.
"Don't get cocky. The reason Ra sent you is..."
"Aaah!"
A huge shout was heard from afar, and Ogent's fist struck Ymir's
face.
"Ugh!"
Ymir's pupils shook from the impact, but his body didn't
budge.
Instead, the ground he stood on crumbled like a cookie, and another
shockwave erupted.
The glass sphere containing Smile flew far away, and Ogent's gaze
followed it.
"Smile..."
"Is this all?"
Ymir's fist, creating a strong wind, swung in and struck Ogent
again.
'It's nothing special.'
Contrary to Anke Ra's order to eliminate a human with the pride of a
giant, it was surprisingly bland.
"Ugh..."
But then, a strange light appeared in Ymir's eyes.
'Did he block it?'
The fist, driven to the limit a creature could reach, was blocked by
Ogent's chin.
"Smile!"
And Ymir saw it.
The enormous wave of law flying in from the distant end of the
universe.
'Ah...'
The moment he received Ogent's second attack, all the common sense he
had held shattered.
'It's this guy.'
Even Ymir, who could overcome gravity, was so dazed he didn't know
where he was flying.
'The reason I exist.'
The pinnacle of a creature integrated from ten billion Gaia people
dreamed of self-destruction.
'This guy can destroy me.'
Rumble!
As Ymir, who had braked inertia, planted his feet on the ground, two
mountains were born as the earth pushed aside.
Ymir had also reached the level of breaking the System.
And Ogent flew in and sharply struck the center of his huge
body.
"Hahaha!"
As the indiscriminate battle, where there was no need to distinguish
between heaven and earth, raged on, the forest was devastated.
All the animals cried out in fear, and a bat watched the battlefield
from right in front.
While other bats instinctively sensed the disaster and left the cave,
one bat still hung upside down from the ceiling, watching the world closely.
Naturally, a bat without reason had no ability to think.
But just the scenery it took in with its eyes was enough to shake the
animal's inner self.
'What is that?'
It had experienced natural disasters a few times in its life.
But watching this, which was different, like the world itself was
being torn apart, sent endless chills down its spine.
'It exists.'
It can be done.
Creatures recognize their limits from birth, but at this moment, the
bat that broke those limits would become something entirely different.
"Move! Get out of the way!"
As Ogent grew more anxious searching for the glass sphere containing
Smile, time passed.
'How can this be?'
Even the level of Idea, obtained at the bottom where a creature could
fall, was useless against a giant.
Every time fists crossed, Ogent's body broke.
But more serious was that even the Idea that made him up was being
destroyed.
"If this is the end..."
Ymir raised his fist with a smirk.
'In the end, it's like that.'
Destroy Ogent, destroy Heaven, and destroy this entire world.
"Smile..."
A voice emerged from beneath his broken jaw.
"Smile!"
The moment Ogent struck Ymir's face, a force incomparable to before
was transmitted.
"Ugh!"
As his molars broke, he fell diagonally to the ground, and a
shockwave erupted upon impact.
Boom!
With a blow strong enough to create a huge crater, Ymir followed and
fell.
Momentarily shocked, it was the first time in Ymir's history that he
lost control of his body.
'It's truly strange.'
It seemed like the end, but it wasn't.
'What is the mind?'
Though it was trivial because he already had it, was there a means to
create something greater?
With a thud, Ymir fell and had to wait until his body
recovered.
'If I endure, I win.'
He realized something from the previous blow, but Ogent didn't come
to Ymir.
"Smile! Smile!"
Kariel blocked the way in front of the glass sphere.
"Stop. This woman..."
Before he could finish speaking, a punch sent Kariel flying hundreds
of meters away.
"Please! Please!"
The glass sphere shattered, and the black liquid, the Elixir of Life,
poured out.
Smile was no longer alive.
Seeing her half-melted body, tears of blood flowed from Ogent's
eyes.
"Aaah!"
He had believed a miracle would happen, but the world was just cold
and indifferent.
Ymir, who had recovered, walked over, shaking the ground.
'Is it that woman?'
An individual's Idea.
"Let's keep fighting."
Ymir's words were polite, but Ogent didn't respond.
He was in a daze, staring at the ground with soulless eyes,
mumbling.
"Smile."
His body, hardened like a fossil of anger, began to melt in the
Elixir of Life.
"Is she that precious to you?"
"You said you wanted to fight me?"
Ogent, whose lower body had melted, slowly turned his head.
"Protect Smile."
Knowing what was happening, Ymir asked with a regretful look.
"What's your name?"
"Ogent."
As his upper body melted, the Elixir of Life flowed into Smile as if
it had a life of its own.
"I may disappear, but..."
Ogent looked up at the sky.
"My will shall continue through Smile."
Tears flowed from his face, now only a head.
'I see, Smile.'
The sky he saw at the end of his life was dazzling.
'Life is so beautiful.'
Ogent's body completely disappeared, and the black liquid began to
restore Smile's body.
'I am Ogent.'
Only his consciousness whispered quietly.
'I am just a swordsman who neither saved the world nor achieved a
great cause, but...'
His genes transcended many generations.
'I have certainly passed it on.'
Finally, it reached a man.
'Ogent... Rian.'
Rian, holding the Idea diagonally, looked up and glared at
Faust.
"Hoo."
The battle would be decided in a single strike.
