Chapter 395:
Hill Capture (4)
The fourth round went to Shirone's team. The Scatter Movement was a
strategic weapon, and Iruki's Cancellation completely blocked Suavi.
The current score was 3 to 1.
Shirone's team had the upper hand, and unless there were any significant
changes, the current momentum seemed likely to continue.
"Huh? The fourth round just ended?"
Students were coming out of the third battlefield. Canis and Arin were
among them.
Their opponent was Fermi's team, and the result was a 1 to 5 loss for
Canis and Arin's team.
It was a disappointing result for Canis and Arin, but maintaining their
extreme dislike for cooperating with others meant they had to accept this kind of evaluation.
Moreover, even with the best team, there was no guarantee of victory
against Fermi's team, so neither of them looked particularly disheartened.
Fermi checked the score between Shirone's team and Screamer's team from
a spot where he could see the side of the second battlefield.
"Hmm, 3 to 1."
"Shirone's team did better than expected. Initially, it seemed like
it would be a close match."
Fermi thought similarly. However, it was just a matter of probability,
and there was always the possibility of other variables arising.
"The insight to create variables. That's Shirone. If they get just
one more point, this will likely be over."
Saying this, Fermi looked at Screamer's team.
'Of course, it won't end like this.'
Fermi's lips twisted into a wicked smile.
Although the fourth round had ended, Suavi was still staring at Iruki
from Shirone's team's camp.
'Merco Dine Iruki...'
If it weren't for the top-tier overpowered magic called Cancellation
used by the Servant Syndrome, no one at the student level could have frustrated her this much.
Iruki, with his hands behind his back, calmly accepted Suavi's
gaze.
'She must feel very wronged.'
He didn't want to provoke her. After all, in terms of calculation speed,
everyone in the graduating class was beneath him.
To beat a Servant, you need intuition, not calculation. The highest
level of intuition, on par with a Servant.
"Suavi, let's go."
At Screamer's call, Suavi finally turned around.
A fire burned inside her. She knew there were many strong people in the
graduating class and had failed last year's graduation exam. But having her major nullified was a
completely different kind of humiliation.
Screamer gathered his team and spoke.
"Let's stick together. We'll break through the central line in the
shortest distance. Pony, you go on the offensive now. If we lose this round, a comeback will be nearly
impossible."
If the score became 4 to 1, defeat was almost certain. Shirone's team
had Ataraxia, which could guarantee them at least one point.
"No. Pony, keep using Refresh."
Suavi took off her glasses and spoke.
"This time, I'll do it right."
Screamer tilted his head.
They had known each other for over a year, but Suavi had never shown
such determination except during the graduation exam.
'Hmm, she's really pissed off?'
As a martial artist, Screamer had no complaints. He couldn't guess how
to counter Cancellation, but since Suavi said she was fine, he decided to trust her.
"Alright, let's consider this the final round. I'll show them what
I'm made of."
As soon as the fifth round started, Screamer's team, except for the
guard, gathered on the central line.
It was a rush-and-rush tactic with a high comeback rate.
As the combat package hit Screamer, Iruki twitched his eyes and sent out
a detached Spirit Zone.
'If it's Cancellation anyway...'
Bang! Bang bang bang bang bang!
At that moment, buff effects exploded in succession. The combat package
was hitting all team members except the guard.
Iruki blinked in surprise, but Suavi gritted her teeth and glared at him
even more.
'How about this? Can you still do it?'
The number of reserved cores in her mind was a whopping 137. That was
the accumulated number in a situation where eight were being consumed per second.
'Tikitikitakitoki tok tok tikitaki tak tak tikitokitakitok!'
Every time the unique rhythm pounded in her brain, the cores in her mind
bounced in all directions.
"Hahaha! Yes! This is it!"
Filled with power, Screamer charged into the center of Shirone's team
and struck all four members repeatedly. Then, he took a bold finishing stance like in the first
round.
"Kyaaaaaa!"
Screamer's team's morale soared. Feeling Suavi's determination, the
wingers also began to reveal their hidden cards.
'We just need to cut Shirone.'
Luman slowly raised his hands as if drawing energy from his
soles.
'Everyone hates me.'
But he didn't care.
For humans, harmony is just a second-best option born from the fear of
distrust. If he could control it, others' feelings didn't matter.
'Yes, I am the king of crowd control (self-proclaimed).'
Luman's cheeks quivered, his whites showed, and his mouth stretched to
his ears.
"I am the king of the battlefield!"
His secret weapon, the Spiral Hunter, revealed itself.
Rumble!
The ground, swirling in a spiral, turned mushy like mud and pulled
Shirone down. Then it solidified into rock, gripping his waist tightly and not letting go.
"Now!"
As Screamer charged, Suavi's concentration soared to its highest
level.
Countless cores stormed through her mind like a hurricane.
It was the realm of intuition beyond calculation, and every time a core
hit another, her tongue flicked rapidly.
'Tikitakitokitakitakitakitokitakitokitakitikitaki!'
Reserved cores: 372.
Iruki's lips curled slightly.
'I'm glad I joined the graduating class.'
He had never wanted to beat someone before. But now he vaguely
understood.
He hated losing even more.
'You're amazing, Suavi.'
Countless talents claimed their specialties like stars.
'You must have practiced every day. To hold onto your drifting mind,
biting your hand every night, you must have trained hard.'
Iruki lowered his stance and clenched his fists. It was his way of
responding to Suavi's sincerity.
'This will shorten my lifespan, but...'
Since entering the magic school, this was the third time, after the
bloody battle with Nade in the rain six years ago and getting excited by Canis's provocation in the
infirmary hallway a year ago.
His brain cells would be destroyed, but did it matter? It was a matter
of pride for Shirone's faction, beyond the battle.
'You will be a great magician. But not now. Only two people in the world
can stop me. One is Shirone, and the other is...'
Sparks flew in Iruki's pupils. It was a phenomenon where electricity
generated in the cerebral cortex was projected onto the retina.
'It's me, Merco Dine Iruki!'
In the virtual image in Iruki's mind, data amounting to 100 million
joules per second surged like a tornado.
As the information processing capacity to handle all events in a city at
once was reached, the double Spirit Zone flew through Screamer's team at an astonishing speed, canceling
all buffs.
"Unbelievable..."
Suavi was stunned. The overwhelming calculation speed drained her will
to fight.
Boom boom boom boom boom!
Shirone, who destroyed the Spiral Hunter with Radiance, led his team in
a counterattack.
With the buffs gone, Screamer's team weakened rapidly and was soon on
the defensive.
"Hah, we're losing after all."
Aider sighed.
The downside of the martial artist killer strategy was that there was no
Plan B. If they lost this round, there would be no chance for a comeback.
The youthful look disappeared from Aider's face. He took out a
black-and-white patterned chip and swallowed it.
'It feels wrong, but does it matter?'
He could give up graduation if he could enjoy it. No, he couldn't give
up graduation. He had to become a magician to enjoy it even more.
A core was installed in his mind. According to the dealer, it was a
high-level air-type technique called 'Ghost Movement.'
Even without understanding the internal principles, he could cast the
spell because it was similar to Shirone's Ataraxia.
"Ah, ah..."
Aider's eyelids trembled.
The days he had restrained himself from tasting humans. But now, there
was no need for that. With the deal made, his graduation was practically guaranteed.
"Hahahahaha!"
Aider threw off his mask and flew straight into Shirone's camp. It was
classified as a reverse position tactic, but it wasn't agreed upon with Screamer's team.
While his allies looked on in astonishment, Shirone's team intercepted
with their respective spells.
The first to reach him was the Photon Cannon. But just before being shot
down, Aider's movements twisted bizarrely. Like a willow branch, his trajectory wavered, dodging all the
Photon Cannons.
"Huh? Ghost Movement?"
Dorothy, watching from outside the battlefield, noticed.
It wasn't a spell a student could use. But Aider's movements couldn't be
explained without the AT algorithm.
'Did he join Fermi?'
It was hard to believe he had mastered it in just a few months. The AT
algorithm, which controlled turbulence to automatically evade approaching objects, was a core even
professionals struggled with.
Nothing could stop Aider. Reaching the hill, he ran towards the
flag.
But contrary to everyone's expectations, Aider targeted Maya instead of
the flag. Wham! A powerful kick twisted Maya's jaw, sending her rolling down the hill.
"What...!"
Aider sat on Maya's stomach. His facial muscles twitched with the
thought of releasing a year's worth of pent-up desire.
"Sis, this might hurt a bit. But bear with it, okay?"
Aider's fists pounded on Maya. A senseless laugh escaped his
lips.
Seeing her terrified expression, a rush of pleasure overwhelmed his
reason.
"Ahaha! This is so fun, I'm going crazy!"
Aider's pounding fists, raised like a gorilla's, made him look inhuman.
His eyes showed the whites, and drool dripped from his gaping mouth.
His teammates, as well as the students watching from outside, were

"Remember the Truth or Dare game when you asked me a
question?"

"The ugliest person? At first, I was puzzled. I can't distinguish

According to the mental classification, he's 'evil.'"
Canis listened silently.
"Only those who understand evil can act good. That's the law of

Canis didn't think much of it. Every human in Radum was like

"I asked the question to test you. When you denied the truth, I

"Yes. I thought I could use it as a bargaining chip. But today's

"Your presence must have influenced him. With a first impression

Canis let go of his regrets lightly.
There were still dozens of cards left. It was good to know how much
Arin's first impression could pressure the graduating class.

Shirone's eyes lit up. Seeing Maya curled up and being beaten, his mind

"Hehehe! Does it hurt? How much does it hurt? Aren't you mad? You

Aider swung his fists with unfocused eyes.
He didn't care about Shirone's team rushing in from all directions. With

Zap!
Blue electricity flashed before Aider's eyes.
The AT algorithm activated late, pushing his body over 10 meters away.
Nade's face, scratching with his nails, remained on his retina. When he came to his senses, he was
already at the bottom of the hill.
Fear mixed with the ecstasy on Aider's face.
'What was that? What did I just see?'
He thought he saw Nade's face, but he couldn't understand what it was.

'Am I scared? Me?'
Nade, the first to arrive, blocked Maya. The face he had when he swung

"Screamer, the flag."
"Ah..."
At Pony's words, Screamer snapped out of it and hurriedly ran to pull

Shirone approached Maya. Seeing her still trembling and curled up, his

"Maya..."
Maya was never suited to be a magician. Her talent for entering the

singer.
"Maya, get up."
Shirone spoke in a cold voice.
Maya had a family to support and the honor of her tribe to uphold. If

"Get up. We're still in the evaluation. If you get disqualified

Maya's shoulders twitched.
That cursed 20 points. But everyone in the graduating class was risking
their lives for those 20 points.
'I'm... so scared, Shirone. Everyone here, their coldness is
terrifying.'
She wanted to give up. She had endured for her tribe and fought for her
family, but seeing Aider's eyes, she realized.
This was not where she belonged.
'Shirone...'
Maya slowly stood up.
She had fallen in love with Shirone. It was more desperate than the

girl.
"I'm fine... I can still fight."
Seeing Maya trying to hold back her tears, Shirone felt tears welling up
instead.
He looked at Aider, who was laughing in the enemy camp. A terrifying
rage filled his eyes.
