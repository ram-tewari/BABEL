Chapter 462:
Second Heaven, Lakia.
The city of fallen angels.
All the buildings were white, but the walls shimmered with a red light
like a mirage.
The streets were empty, and from the thousands of spires came sounds
filled with mixed emotions.
As many sounds as there were fallen angels.
There were no taboos for them, and their indulgent minds had lost
control, racing towards countless pleasures.
Kariel arrived at the building housing Lakia's executive branch.
The massive brick building was as complex as a castle, with three spires
piercing the sky.
The Hall of Corruption.
In a place as vast as the Great World War, countless plaster statues of
fallen angels were enshrined like heroes.
The poses of the statues revealed numerous acts, some easily understood
by humans, others incomprehensible by human standards.
Kariel threw Miro and Arius towards the altar in the Hall of
Corruption.
Arius, who rolled on the floor, bared his teeth like a dog deprived of
its prey.
"Growl! Growl!"
Kariel ignored him and looked at Miro.
Her intelligent eyes showed she was trying to grasp the current
situation.
"There's no need to struggle. You'll die one way or
another."
"Heh, you're the one trembling. Your face was quite a sight
earlier."
Kariel's expression twisted.
The reason Ikael was feared was not because she was strong.
Pain always comes to the one who gives their heart, and betrayal cuts
deeper, making the world feel like it's collapsing in the face of her anger.
"Do you think you've defeated Heaven's army? No, you're just
someone's successor. And now, even that power has vanished."
When Kariel snapped his fingers, the plaster statues in the hall glowed
red, and hundreds of Maras sprang out, rushing towards Miro.
"Roar!"
Countless grotesque monsters bared their teeth at Miro's face.
"Despicable woman! I'll chew your bones to bits!"
"I'll devour your soul! I'll make you wander the fires of hell
forever!"
The chilling sounds came from close by.
Arius, filled with such hostility that he wanted to claw and tear at her
flesh, restlessly circled Miro.
In contrast, Miro didn't even blink.
She gave her heart to no one, which is why everyone threw their hearts
at her.
Miro's detachment placed her above all emotions, allowing her to feel no
fear.
"What are you plotting? It seems your plan has failed."
Ikael's shout of anger reached Miro as well.
And if this was the city of fallen angels, it meant Kariel was already
an outsider to Heaven, with his limbs cut off.
Kariel was also thinking about that.
With Ikael as the archangel, the other archangels wouldn't move.
Suddenly, a thought crossed his mind: maybe ending it here was the best
option.
But then his mind changed.
'Ikael.'
The stronger she became, the more he feared her, the greater his desire
to stand above her heart grew.
'Is that all there is?'
Without answering, Kariel turned his body.
The numerous Maras retreated from Miro, showing their submission.
"Radiant Archangel, where are you going?"
Given Lakia's nature, it was impossible to station Heaven's forces
here.
In that case, there were those who fit perfectly.
"I'm going to Muspelheim."
Kariel transformed into light and flew away.
To the place where the fallen giants were.
"Ikael?"
The rebels were thrown into confusion just by Ikael's appearance.
Her name had more destructive power than any physical air raid.
Once, they too had been citizens of Heaven.
Ikael's exploits, which had eradicated countless heresies and destroyed
entire civilizations, were already the most shining part of myth.
Gaold observed Ikael's demeanor.
Just by looking, he couldn't measure her objectively.
Combat is something you can't understand without experiencing it, as it
involves numerous variables.
'I can't win against that.'
Gaold acknowledged his opponent's capabilities.
The fully awakened archangel Ikael was not someone to be analyzed in
terms of combat.
She was simply power.
Amplification itself existing in nature.
That's why she came alone.
It could only be seen that Ikael herself knew she could annihilate the
rebel's first command alone.
'Ikael…'
While complex thoughts ran through everyone's minds, only Shirone's mind
went blank.
Before coming to Heaven, and even after arriving, he never imagined he
would meet her like this.
His heart pounded, and his fingertips trembled.
But he couldn't find the beautiful smile she once had.
Only the authority of the archangel commanding Heaven was reflected in
her expression.
A sharp metallic noise was heard as Babel flew in from the
armory.
Babel, newly equipped with an algorithm by Shirone, immediately detected
the overwhelming presence of the archangel and maximized its combat power.
"This, this can't be…"
The rebels were shocked.
It only became real when the two beings were in the same space.
Ikael and Babel were perfectly alike.
Of course, Babel's beauty couldn't compare to the real thing, but from
height and body curves to the fingertips, they were identical.
'Babel…'
Ikael looked at the machine that resembled her.
She knew it was the only one of its kind in the world, and she knew who
made it, but all conversations with that person had already been erased.
Yet, her eyes held a faint emotion.
Even if the memories were erased, the events themselves hadn't
disappeared.
Her body, everything except her memories, felt a nostalgic connection to
Babel.
Babel moved quickly, charging towards Ikael.
Eliminating angels was Babel's purpose.
As the sharp iron claws aimed at Ikael's forehead, Shirone activated the
Ultima system.
'No! Stop!'
Babel stopped its movement, aiming at the capital.
But there was nothing standing there anymore.
Ikael had already flown past Babel's back and was walking towards the
rebels.
Sein, who had experienced Babel's speed firsthand, got goosebumps just
seeing Ikael's movement.
'Do we have to fight that monster to rescue Miro?'
Ignoring human thoughts, Ikael addressed everyone.
"Follow Ra's will. If you cease the war from this moment, Ra will
also liberate you."
"Liberate? Does that mean we win?"
The rebels murmured.
Even with large Tagis, the outcome of the war was uncertain, so it was
unexpected for Heaven to propose a truce first.
Commander Crude stepped forward.
"What exactly does liberation mean?"
"State your conditions. If it's within my power, I will accept
anything."
Crude looked at Sein.
Honestly, from the moment Ikael appeared, he thought everything was
over, so he didn't expect her to come out so submissively.
Sein signaled with a nod.
Of course, he came here to rescue Miro, not to liberate the rebels, but
given the situation, it was best to listen.
"Let's move."
When Crude pointed to the command control room, Ikael shook her
head.
"No, you're not the one to negotiate."
"Then who?"
When Ikael looked at Shirone, all eyes focused on him.
Of course, Shirone was a symbolic figure for the rebels as the Light of
Sector 73.
But in terms of deciding something at the negotiation table, his
experience and position were ambiguous.
No one knew why Ikael specifically pointed out Shirone.
"Let him go."
Gaold said.
If Shirone's condition for activating divine punishment was a one-on-one
meeting with Ikael, it was the right tactical choice.
Crude, who trusted Gaold's party, eventually agreed, and Shirone and
Ikael walked side by side down the corridor of the command control room, guided by an officer.
Shirone's mind was still in turmoil.
What to say first, why she was so cold to him, many thoughts swirled,
but it wasn't time to speak yet.
He could only assume that she felt the same way.
"This is the only place where eavesdropping is
impossible."
The officer opened the door to the most secure meeting room in the rebel
command and let the two inside.
The door closed, and even the guards left.
Ikael's order was to keep a 100-meter radius clear.
Considering Shirone's danger level, it was an unreasonable demand, but
they knew she could do anything if she wanted to.
Shirone and Ikael sat facing each other at the table in the meeting
room, silent for a while.
Unable to bear the tension, Shirone smiled and spoke first.
"It's been a while."
Finally, Ikael showed a hint of a smile.
"Indeed. I'm glad you returned safely."
Shirone clearly remembered the situation when Ikael taught him
Ataraxia.
If it weren't for her, they wouldn't be meeting here again.
Another awkward silence followed.
Ikael pointed out Shirone because of Anke Ra's will.
She also came here to disarm the rebels and negotiate.
Yet, she couldn't bring herself to speak.
'What am I missing?'
From the moment she saw Shirone, a scratching sensation continued
endlessly.
The Light of Sector 73. The boy she personally taught Ataraxia.
But why did she try to pass on the archangel's power?
As Ikael recalled her feelings from that time, her halo seemed to flinch
in surprise.
Her brow furrowed, and an unknown emotion consumed her body.
"Are you okay?"
Shirone asked worriedly.
"I'm fine. First, about the rebels…"
"Before that, I have something to ask."
Shirone needed to hear this first.
When Ikael blinked, as if waiting for an answer, Shirone took a deep
breath and carefully began to speak.
"I've been curious for a long time. Do you have anything to tell
me?"
Ikael tilted her head slightly.
There was nothing specific she needed to convey to Shirone.
Yet, she couldn't deny the faint, nostalgic feeling that washed over
her, similar to when she saw Babel.
"No."
Ikael's voice was firm.
It was a cold voice, desperately trying to deny something.
"I have nothing personal to say to you."
Shirone's heart sank.
He had risked his life to come to Heaven, certain that he would hear
something important from her.
But…
"Nothing at all? You have nothing to tell me?"
"I don't know what you're talking about."
At that moment, Ikael's voice trembled.
Even though she had no memory of it, her heart had already been given
away.
She struggled to find the words, but the more she tried, the more an
overwhelming sadness engulfed her.
With a pained expression, she finally managed to say,
"I have nothing to say to you."
Ikael stared intently at Shirone, as if searching for the source of the
torment in her heart.
In that moment, clear tears began to flow from her beautiful, sparkling
eyes.
She didn't understand the meaning of the tears she was shedding.
