Chapter 247:
Magic Clash (7)
"Shirone is a strong kid. He's not the type to be discouraged by a
single loss; if anything, he'll gain even more from it. A true teacher should also teach how to learn
from defeat."
Olivia raised her hand as if to block out Alpheas's words.
"I will take this as an excuse from a 4th-rank loser."
"Kukuku, well, in any case, today I'm just a spectator today.
Shirone and Dante. This will be really interesting."
Finally, the time for the match arrived.
As Sienna, who was in charge of the referee, walked to the center of
Heaven's Trial, the atmosphere in the training ground became solemn.
In a moment, the greatest talent of Alpheas Magic Academy and the
greatest talent of the kingdom will clash.
Mark, who was trembling as if he were having trouble breathing, looked
over at Iruki sitting beside him.
"Ugh, I feel like my blood is drying up. Senior, do you think Senior
Shirone can win?"
"I think both of them will have their opportunities. Shirone has a
wider variety of offensive magic, but Dante's tactical execution can't be underestimated. If the number
of magic circles increases, it could put Shirone in a tough spot."
Even from Iruki, who could be called his closest aide, a hopeful
assessment did not come out. Just as Mark was about to complain with a gloomy expression, the students
suddenly erupted in cheers.
Shirone and Dante were walking up to the side of Heaven's Trials.
Sienna, who was in charge of the refereeing, greeted the former and
current principals with courtesy, then called Shirone and Dante to the center of Heaven's Trials.
The faces of the students who had been watching the two people meet with
interest turned pale. This was because Shirone was approaching Dante beyond the designated
location.
'Oops!'
Shirone quickly snapped back to reality and stopped walking, but by
then, Dante's face was already right in front of him. He had been so deep in thought that he hadn't
noticed.
The students, interpreting Shirone's actions as a provocation, became
even more excited, but those who knew Shirone's personality had a different take.
It was obvious that he was nervous.
In response to the unusual situation, Kilain gave a sympathetic
smile.
"Hoho! How is that good? In this day and age, who still tries such cheap
provocations?"
There had been many challengers who had tried similar tactics against
Dante in the past. Each time, Dante had calmly countered, leaving his opponents humiliated.
'Huhu, Dante, I wonder what kind of performance you'll start with today…
Huh?'
A look of confusion appeared on Kilain's face. Dante wasn't moving
either; he was just staring intently at Shirone.
Even though Sienna was explaining the rules, it didn't seem like either
of them was listening.
Dante's friends were also puzzled, as they had never seen him like that
before.
Savina, biting her nails, spoke up.
"Dante, don't you think he looks a little nervous?"
"That's not like him. He was in good condition just this morning,
right? Let's wait a bit; maybe it's just part of his showmanship."
Iruki, who was listening to their conversation, chimed in.
"He can't help but be nervous."
"Huh? He can't help but be nervous?"
Mark turned his head with shocked eyes. He was so nervous that he was
surprised by whatever Iruki said.
"Shirone has never lost in competition. Although he's only been
learning magic for a short time, he's been overcoming highly difficult obstacles to get here. Of course,
Dante has also spent a long time at the top, achieving an almost impossible record. Now, these two are
about to clash."
Amy said.
"Defeat is a record engraved in a person's life. It changes the
direction of their life in some way. But neither of them has experienced defeat yet. They've both
maintained an abnormal career where even a single mistake wasn't allowed. They don't want to lose
that."
Iruki answered.
"And today, one of the two will have their careers completely ruined.
They will live their lives with the record of defeat engraved on their minds."
Mark was dumbfounded. Was this the world where geniuses lived? Having
been defeated at the age of fifteen, he was in a state where he couldn't even grasp the feeling.
But he didn't feel any regret. He only hoped that Shirone, who had
defeated him, would win again, and his heart pounded so hard it almost hurt.
"Ugh. This is driving me crazy. When are they going to
start?"
Sienna, who had explained the rules, instructed everyone to keep their
distance. But neither of them moved. It was as if they couldn't hear her.
"Both of you! Return to your designated positions and
wait."
All sorts of thoughts crossed Dante's mind. Who on earth is this boy? At
first, he said he didn't want to fight, that he didn't care about power or fame, but now he was glaring
as if he was ready to devour him.
'No. Let's not think about it.'
Dante suddenly came to his senses. Having never lost in 472 matches, it
was easy to assume he wouldn't lose now. He just had to crush Shirone like he had with every other
challenger.
"Shirone! Dante! Follow the referee's instructions!"
Only after Sienna shouted fiercely did the two come to their senses and
back away without breaking eye contact.
Upon reaching his designated position, Dante took a deep breath and
moved his body to shake off the tension.
The 60-meter distance seemed farther than usual.
But he has defeated all the strong mages and climbed up. If he fought
like usual, it is a match he cannot lose.
'Yes, I just need to fight my way. His only option is to strike first.
Let's use that to my advantage. As a Target type, I have the speed advantage.'
He accelerated his Four-Way Zone to the maximum and deployed a
teleportation magic circle under Shirone's feet. It wouldn't cause much of an impact, but it was the
best way to disrupt his balance. After that, he would simply adapt to the situation as he always
had.
"And now, Shirone versus Dante, Dante versus Shirone…"
Sienna's words were buried in the loud cheers.
In ten seconds, they would collide.
It felt as though the very air temperature had risen from the heat of
the moment.
Sienna gave up on giving a signal by voice and raised her hand instead.
The students' cheers grew even louder, and both Shirone and Dante locked their gazes on her hand
signal.
"Begin!"
Dante unleashed a super-fast Sequencing. The numbers began at one and
quickly reached ten thousand as his Target Type Spirit Zone extended, engulfing Shirone.
'Got him…'
Just as Dante thought that, his eyes widened in shock. Dozens of Photon
Cannons had already reached right in front of him.
Dante quickly switched his omniscience and deployed a defensive magic
circle. The barrage of Photon Cannons pounded against the magic circle. It was then that he realized
what was happening.
'Immortal Function!'
From the start, Immortal Function was opened. In addition, Photon Cannon
was fired nonstop without regard.
The physical force was on par with Earth Magic, and the speed was just
as fast. Experiencing it firsthand, Dante found it an incredibly annoying magic.
Every time a crack appeared in his defensive magic circle, information
was accumulated and repaired, but he could no longer endure it.
When Dante teleported to escape, Shirone, who had been rapidly firing
the Photon Cannons, also used photonization to follow him.
Two streaks of light painted the sky above Heaven's Trials.
The students were stunned by the barrage of attacks—no, it was more like
a one-sided display of firepower—right from the start.
Iruki burst into a hollow laugh.
Of course, it was a good strategy. However, he didn't expect that
Shirone, who has a cautious nature, would stake something so early.
"It seems like he's trying to prevent Dante from even thinking about
casting a magic circle from the beginning."
Mark asked in a trembling voice.
"This is amazing. Is this really the same person who had a
teleportation contest with me last semester?"
"Probably not. Anyway, not giving the opponent any time to use
their skills shows that Shirone is serious. With things like this, the outcome is
unpredictable."
Shirone pressured Dante with dazzling movements. Using the Patrol
technique to block him from the sides, he then cast Rainbow Drop in reverse, soaring into the sky.
Dozens of photon clusters were created every second beneath him, trailing behind.
Shirone fired them all. As Photon Cannons dominated the space, Dante had
no choice but to descend to the ground as his only escape route. Though aerial bombardments followed, he
didn't dare look up.
'Damn it! Let me use some magic too! This guy is completely
insane!'
No, maybe this was better.
Anyway, Shirone couldn't maintain Immortal Function for long. If Dante
could just survive the initial onslaught, victory would be his.
'Yes, at this rate, it won't last more than 30 minutes at most. If I can
just hold out for 30 minutes...!'
Photon Cannons raked the ground in a line as they fell. Dante changed
direction and checked Shirone's position.
Dozens of photon clusters were pouring down on the spot where he
expected Shirone to be.
Realizing his mistake, Dante felt a chill down his spine.
'Damn it! Forget 30 minutes, I can't even hold out for 5!'
After blocking the first bombardment with a defensive magic circle, he
used the delay between the first and second bombardments to find an escape route and slip through the
barrage. However, there was nowhere else to move. Without realizing it, he had been pushed to the
outskirts of Heaven's Trials.
Shirone used the Patrol technique to dominate the space, cornering Dante
at the very edge. When it looked like Dante might escape, Shirone hurled a compressed photon in his
hand. A streak of light shot out.
Dante, unable to escape the corner, hastily deployed another defensive
magic circle. The weight of this attack was on a whole different level compared to the previous
ones.
When the second and third attacks struck the same spot, cracks began to
form even as Dante tried to reinforce the magic circle.
Iruki gave a sympathetic smile.
"No matter how nervous he is, has his mind gone rigid too? He
should know after facing someone like this. There's no way you can stop that with a hastily made magic
circle."
As the magic circle that had been blocking Dante shattered into pieces,
Shirone rushed forward in a straight line as if compressing space into a point.
Dante, rather than trying to dodge, chose to stay put to buy himself
time to prepare. He spread a magic circle in all directions to block the approach, but Shirone lowered
his body and unleashed Photon Explosion.
"Ugh!"
As the barrage of 30 pulses per second pounded the magic circles, Dante
felt as if his mind was being torn apart.
He had seen Photon Explosion used in tactical training, but since it was
only used for defense, he didn't think it would be that powerful. However, after experiencing it
firsthand, he realized that it was truly a large-scale destruction magic.
His mind was shaken as all seven magic circles were shattered. Dante
covered his face and looked ahead over his wrist.
But Shirone was nowhere to be seen.
As he lowered his gaze, he locked eyes with Shirone, who was crouched
down with a photon compressed in his hand.
Shirone darted into Dante's range, swinging his hand. The afterimage of
the photon trailed behind him.
For a mage, two seconds is enough time to enhance an attack's power.
Dante, without time to think of anything else, quickly deployed a defensive magic circle.
Photon Cannon, thrown from close range, smashed through the magic circle
in one strike and hit him in the abdomen.
Dante felt dizzy for a moment, but after quickly checking his condition,
he realized the impact wasn't as severe as he had feared.
'Tch! At least I managed to block it! Now I just need to...!'
At that moment, Shirone grabbed Dante's collar with both hands and
activated photonization. Without even having time to register the unexpected physical confrontation,
Dante was forced to move at the speed of light.
As the photonization wore off, Dante found himself disoriented, unable
to tell where he was. He frantically searched for Shirone's location.
When he turned around, Shirone was nowhere to be seen, and countless
photon bullets filled his field of vision.
Kwakwakwakwakwakwakwakwakwang!
As Photon Cannon struck, the spot where Dante had been was filled with
blinding white light. The students tried to shield their eyes with their fingers, but they still
couldn't make out what was happening.
Shirone stopped his attack and caught his breath. Only five minutes had
passed since the battle began.
Dante, with seven magic circles spread out in front of him, covered his
face with both arms. However, the light from the magic circles soon dimmed and shattered like glass. One
of Dante's knees buckled, hitting the ground with a thud.
The students' eyes widened in shock. While Dante had managed to deploy
his magic circles, it was clear they hadn't completely blocked the attack.
Iruki's eyes lit up.
"It finally hit. This one's triggered Impact judgment."
Dante was known for never giving up the initiative in any
situation.
Once the battle began, his genius-level tactics would usually neutralize
the opponent's attacks, allowing him to finish them off with a counter.
However, Shirone succeeded in delivering an attack that could be
considered an effective hit, breaking through the iron wall-like defense barrier.
Of the 500 people gathered at the venue, not a single person uttered a
word. Even Kilain, who had watched Dante since he was a child, and his friends wore expressions of
disbelief.
472 matches, 472 wins.
Dante, who boasted an undefeated record, had been brought to his knees
within just five minutes. A gust of wind swept through Heaven's Trials, reflecting the brutal intensity
of the battle.
