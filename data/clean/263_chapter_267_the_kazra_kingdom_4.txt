Chapter 267:
The Kazra Kingdom (4)
Shirone opened the window and looked around.
They were deep in the forest, and only the torch fixed to the coachman's
seat illuminated the path ahead. Eerie shadows of trees rushed in the opposite direction of their
movement. It was fortunate that a full moon was out, but clouds were gathering somewhere, soon covering
even that.
Shirone's anxiety deepened further.
At some point, the carriage began to move slowly and then came to a
halt.
The sound of the horses' snorting echoed through the stillness of the
night air.
The coachman opened the door and whispered.
"Lady Reina, we've arrived at the rendezvous point."
Reina wrapped herself in a hooded cloak. The others followed suit,
covering their faces with hoods as they stepped out of the carriage.
The place they arrived at was a vast wasteland with a view of the
horizon. As Shirone mentally mapped the location, he figured they were somewhere on the Dawn Plateau in
the northern part of Thormia.
It felt like they were being extremely thorough. It would be very
difficult to attempt an assassination in a wasteland with open visibility in all directions.
Of course, if someone really wanted to, they could dig an underground
tunnel, but thinking that way would lead to endless concerns.
The rendezvous point was 300 meters away from where they had arrived,
and in the distance, they could see the silhouette of the escort unit from the Kazra Kingdom.
Louis, the head butler of Ogent Family, signaled them.
After Reina exchanged the signal in the same manner, the two groups
finally met at the midpoint.
Louis, who approached with a torch, bowed his head to Shirone's
party.
"Welcome. We've been expecting you."
"You've done well. I hear the preparations for entry into the capital
are going smoothly."
"Yes. We haven't detected any suspicious movements. But we can't afford
to be careless. Please get in for now. I'll explain as we go."
Following Louis, the group moved forward. A massive carriage, similar to
the one Shirone had seen at Alpheas Magic Academy, was waiting for them.
The leader of the escort, named Godin, introduced himself.
"I am Luzen Godin, tasked with escorting Prince Shirone, the first
prince candidate. Given the circumstances, I understand your concerns, but there's no need to worry. The
kingdom has assembled the finest escorts, so you'll reach the royal capital safely. Please, step
inside."
A soldier, armed with a silver sword, opened the carriage door.
Sirone was astonished even before entering. A glowing crystal orb was
attached to the ceiling, making the interior as bright as daytime. In addition, it was equipped with not
only a bed but also facilities for having refreshments.
"I'm sure it won't be up to your standards as esteemed individuals, but
please bear with it a little longer. We'll soon escort you to the royal capital."
As soon as Shirone boarded the carriage, he lay down on the bed. It was
just like any other bed he had rested on, but the fact that it was inside a carriage made it feel
strangely unfamiliar.
"Haha! Amy, this is really fun."
Amy also sat on the bed opposite him, smiling.
"It's called a 'house carriage.' It even has a shock-absorbing
mechanism, so it can endure a journey of about 10 hours without any trouble. They say the maintenance
cost is astronomical, but royalty sure is different."
Reina said.
"But it does mean the travel speed will be slower. It might actually be
safer if we arrive quickly. Either way, we need to stay fully prepared."
Amy raised her hand.
"I'll take the first watch. Mages can stay awake for about a day without
any problems."
Shirone shook his head.
"No, let's take turns together."
"It's fine. You'll be busy the moment we arrive, so you should rest
while you can."
Even with an escort unit present, they couldn't afford to let their
guard down. Someone had to keep watch over the group, and Amy thought she was the right person for the
job. Besides, it didn't make sense for Shirone's parents to stay up all night.
Reina frowned, clearly displeased.
"What's this, are you ignoring me? I can handle Schema too, you know!
Back when I was in my apprenticeship, I'd stay up for several nights. How about the three of us take
turns instead? We don't know the situation in the capital, and having just one person tire out isn't a
good idea."
Amy thought Reina's suggestion was reasonable, so she agreed without any
objection. Vincent and Olena asked to be included as well, but it was impossible for ordinary people to
ride a carriage for 24 hours and stay up all night.
"Please, get some rest. If you're already exhausted now, it'll only be
more of a disadvantage later."
Vincent and Olena had no choice but to lie down on the bed. But it
seemed that fatigue was something no one could resist, as they soon fell asleep.
The remaining bed was to be shared among the three in shifts.
Reina and Amy could sleep together, but Shirone, being male, couldn't
share the space with them and instead leaned against the wall to rest.
How much time had passed?
Shirone had fallen asleep while watching Reina take her turn as the
lookout, but soon, Amy approached and woke him.
"Shirone, do you want me to take over if you're still tired?"
Shirone opened his eyes instantly, without even a preparatory movement.
The fact that it was his turn meant four hours had passed.
His mind was unusually clear, and he didn't feel fatigued at all. He
could understand why nobles would spend so much money on a luxury carriage like this.
"No, I'm fine. You should sleep some more. Nothing happened,
right?"
"Yeah. It's so quiet that it's unsettling. If the soldiers were talking,
we could at least hear something, but they haven't said a word, probably because they're wary of
Schema."
Shirone nodded and moved to the center of the carriage.
"I see. Thanks, Amy."
"What's there to thank me for?"
Amy crawled back to Reina's side to catch a bit more sleep.
Then, as if something suddenly came to her mind, she turned to look at
Shirone while still lying down.
Shirone couldn't help but laugh at her cat-like appearance.
"What is it? Can't sleep?"
"Well, that too…"
Amy trailed off.
Ever since the emissary from the Kazra Kingdom had visited, there was
something that had been weighing on her mind.
"Shirone, if you do become royalty..."
Amy asked as if she had finally made up her mind.
"You won't be learning magic anymore, will you?"
"Huh?"
"Well, you know. You'd become the First Prince then. Naturally, you'd
have to undergo training to be a successor, and with that, you wouldn't have time to learn
magic."
Shirone snickered.
So that's what she'd been thinking all this time?
Come to think of it, he'd been so overwhelmed with just managing himself
recently that he hadn't paid much attention to what others might be thinking.
"Amy, I'm not going to become a prince."
Amy's eyes widened.
"What are you saying? Don't tell me you're planning to run
away?"
"If it comes to that as a last resort, then yes, I'll have to run.
But I'm going to try to persuade them so it doesn't come to that. Even if they are the ones who brought
me into this world, I only have two people I consider my parents. The reason I'm going to the royal
capital isn't to become the successor. It's to convey my intentions. I'm going to return home and
continue attending the magic academy. And…."
Shirone hesitated for a moment, then mustered up his courage and
continued.
"I will definitely keep the promise I made to you."
"Shirone..."
Amy felt a lump in her throat, but at the same time, she was
worried.
Until now, Shirone had managed to navigate life according to his
convictions, and it had always worked out, but this time, the opponent was different.
The royal family wasn't as rational as Shirone thought, nor were they
easily swayed by emotions.
Royalty was an intuitive existence.
Those who held the power to sway the world only considered simple
decisions of whether to act or not.
But when it reaches the level of a king, their judgment evolves so
profoundly that these simple choices can advance or regress the course of history.
To change the mindset of such a person with mere words was nearly
impossible.
Even so, Amy didn't say anything.
She wanted to keep the lingering emotion from Shirone's last words
trapped inside the carriage for as long as possible.
"Then, later, Shirone."
Amy lay down beside Reina and drifted into a short sleep.
@
Reina woke everyone up with a whisper.
"Wake up. It looks like we've arrived at the capital."
Amy rubbed her sleepy eyes and sat up. She peered through the crack of
the window, and it was still before dawn.
However, the carriage wasn't shaking anymore, and the sound of horses'
hooves was crisp, as if they were traveling on a paved stone road.
Shirone opened the window and looked outside. It was too dark to see
clearly, but he could tell that they were passing by buildings. They had entered the city. However, not
a single house had its lights on.
A soldier on horseback approached and spoke to Shirone.
"I apologize, but since we're conducting a nighttime patrol, you're
not permitted to look outside. I'll be closing the window."
The guard closed the window with a bang.
Shirone smacked his lips and turned around. Reina and Amy both had
bewildered expressions.
Amy pouted.
"Tch! The moment we entered the royal capital, they suddenly changed
their tone."
"We can't jump to conclusions. This is the first time that man has
spoken to us. It's something we should assess carefully."
Shirone also agreed with Reina's way of thinking.
From this point onward, it was like walking on a tightrope. Even the
smallest misunderstanding or error could put them all in danger.
By the time they entered the inner castle, dawn had broken. One of the
escort soldiers knocked on the carriage and informed them that they were now free to look around.
Shirone felt a sense of alienation in a foreign country. It seemed that
during the night, not only was it forbidden to go outside, but even peeking was restricted.
'It's definitely more rigid than Thormia. I'll have to be mindful all
the time.'
The carriage crossed a massive moat surrounding the castle and entered
the inner castle.
The Kazra Royal Palace wasn't as beautiful as the buildings in Heaven,
but it was enormous in scale.
As they were about halfway across the bridge, fanfare resounded.
A military band appeared, and the lower officials of the royal palace
came out, clapping to welcome them.
Shirone turned to Reina at the unexpected hospitality.
"What's going on?"
"I'm not sure. I didn't expect them to openly welcome us like
this."
Even Reina found this situation quite strange.
Given that the Kazra Kingdom grew under the protection of Terraje,
Shirone's existence would undoubtedly be a thorn in the side of the Empress's faction.
'And yet, there hasn't been an assassination attempt, nor any incident.
On top of that, this level of hospitality...'
Realistically speaking, there were only two things I could think
of.
Either Orkamph was putting everything on the line regarding Shirone, or
Terraje didn't consider him worth her concern.
Both the former and latter had their chances, so it was impossible to
definitively say which was more likely.
She checked Shirone's expression, but he remained calm.
Even though this situation must have been more nerve-wracking for him
than anyone else, his unease from the beginning had completely disappeared upon arriving at the royal
palace. This was precisely the strength in Shirone that always impressed Reina.
It was the same within Ogent Family. While he appeared like any other
innocent boy of his age, when faced with danger, he became startlingly composed.
'Well… he is now the kingdom's greatest aspiring mage.'
Shirone's essence hadn't changed, but everything about him had. After
reading about him in the Spirit magazine, Reina had asked a mage she knew what an Unlocker was.
She was told it was the most profound mental state a human could
achieve.
Knowing this made it hard to see Shirone as just a child like
before.
The Kazra Royal Palace spanned an area of about 20,000 pyeong
(approximately 66,115 square meters) and housed roughly 1,000 residents.
Its design was more militaristic than lavish, and perhaps because of
that, the entrance was narrower than other castles.
However, once inside, a massive grand hall unfolded before them, worthy
of being called a royal palace.
As they passed the red carpet lined with high-ranking officials
according to their ranks, they saw the throne atop the stairs, where Orkamph IV sat.
Though it wasn't proper etiquette to stare directly at the king's face,
Shirone's group couldn't take their eyes off him.
He had the same blonde hair as Sirone and an elegant appearance, making
it hard to believe he was the ruler of a nation. However, the aura emanating from him was anything but
ordinary.
No matter how powerful a king may be, he was still just another person
under the heavens, but there was something undeniably different about a man who held the lives of
millions in his hands.
'That person….'
When Orkamph and Shirone were placed side by side, one could easily
believe they were father and son.
Their resemblance wasn't identical, but given that they had been
separated for 18 years, the differences in their upbringing had to be considered.
Shirone's group performed the ceremonial greetings as dictated by
protocol.
However, Orkamph showed no reaction, focusing his gaze solely on
Shirone's face.
"Raise your head."
Shirone met Orkamph's eyes.
The king's gaze seemed to draw Shirone in, shaking him to his core. It
was the aura of a sovereign, something that couldn't be felt from a swordsman or a mage.
However, Shirone quickly composed himself, returning to a calm state. It
was the state of "Indestructible Diamond," where one's will is unshaken by others.
Seeing this, Orkamph's eyes gleamed with surprise.
'He truly is my son...'
