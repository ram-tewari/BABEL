Chapter 536:
The atmosphere in the temple grew heavy with tension.
"Answer me. What did you try to do to me? And what do you mean by
slave?"
The Arbiter himself lacked direct combat ability, but he had more than
enough powerful warriors who could protect his light-based powers.
"Priest, summon the Guardians of Ra."
The priest rapidly swirled his hands through the air, grabbing codes
from different directions and linking them in front of him.
Sparks burst from the connection point, and in an instant, the priest
vanished.
So he can do something like that too...
Before long, the priest returned, and with a loud clank, the doors swung
open and four figures entered like the wind.
"O Avatar of Ra, we heed your command."
Pointing at Shirone, the Arbiter issued his decree.
"Purge the heretic who dared to speak falsely in the name of the god.
The Language of Light is not meant for a slave."
Sensing the solemn intensity in the Guardians' prayer, Shirone tensed,
focusing his mind.
They're different from the others...
The settlement's elite were all born from the Life Tree—genetic
anomalies with a probability of just 0.001%. Their powers spanned biological, mental, and phenomenal
abilities, and they reigned supreme over the Sun Children.
"So you're the Ancient. I heard you rejected Ra's baptism," said the
only female Guardian, Yoa, as she approached.
Her green hair was intricately knotted atop her head, with straight-cut
bangs just above her eyebrows.
"It wasn't a baptism. It was brainwashing. What did I do to deserve
this?"
"There's only one crime," she said, aiming her palm at him. "Your
existence."
Silver Mirror.
Her arm transformed into silver-colored metal, and with a clang, it
shattered into shards.
"What the—!"
The fragments swirled into a storm and surged toward him. Shirone evaded
by blinking to the temple's edge.
"I'll go."
Standing next to Yoa, a man named Karof rolled his neck from side to
side.
He was of average height and muscular build, with the sides of his head
shaved and the top grown long.
"Grrrrhh...!"
Karof's face twisted ferociously, and fur began to grow over his body.
He transformed into a fierce canine beast and lunged toward Shirone's afterimage.
"RAAAAWR!"
When four claws slashed toward him, Shirone released his blink and
rolled along the ground.
"Dammit...!"
Even as he regained balance, Yoa's metallic shards rained down.
Pulse!
A shield of light deflected the shards. At the same time, Karof was
struck by a shock as if he'd been clubbed by dozens of blunt weapons.
Photon Cannon!
Shirone hurled a sphere of light, which expanded into a beam and struck
Karof square in the gut.
"Gah!"
Sent flying, Karof didn't touch the ground again until he had landed
back where he started. He collapsed to his knees in human form, puffing his cheeks and widening his
eyes.
"Ghhhrrrraaah!"
Even in his transformed state, boasting immense strength, he couldn't
withstand the impact. Blood spewed from his mouth.
Yoa formed a seal with her hands, and the silver fragments returned,
reforming her arm.
Checking her reflection in her metallic palm, she canceled the
transformation and muttered,
"Hmph. You could've dodged that if you wanted."
Karof shook his head.
"That's not the issue... That light…"
It had physical force.
Faced with the inexplicable, the two remaining Guardians who hadn't
fought stayed silent.
To the Sun Children, light was life itself.
Even without the doctrine proclaiming eternal life to those who
worshiped Ra, they were a species that literally could not survive without it.
"Hm."
In this situation, only the priest—keeper of doctrine—could pass
judgment.
Among the mutant-born, those who wield the Language of Light have always
existed.
Though the reason remained unclear, it was likely tied to the Life
Tree's light-based nature.
And now, at this moment, a new Language of Light has appeared—in an
Ancient, no less. Is this a revelation from Ra? Or something else...?
After contemplation, the priest bowed his head toward the
Arbiter.
"O Avatar of Ra, no settlement may harbor two suns. Though it may
discomfort you, I ask that you wield your divine authority and offer guidance to ease the hearts of the
children."
Things had escalated too far. Now, the people would have to choose which
light to follow.
"Very well! At the hour when Ra sleeps, I shall bestow a second
sun!"
The Arbiter knew the nature of power well.
If he backed down now, only death awaited him.
"In three hours' time. I will inform the priests to prepare."
The priest turned to Shirone.
"You who have disturbed the peace of the settlement, I grant you one
final chance. At the darkest hour, you and the Arbiter shall clash with the power of light. If you win,
you shall become the settlement's new leader. If you lose, you must take your own life."
"I have no interest in being a leader. And I won't be killing myself
either."
"Don't get cocky. You may wield strange powers and claim the name of Ra,
but what you've seen is not the full strength of the Guardians. If you want to survive, you'll have to
prove yourself with all your might."
Though the people would decide the outcome, the priest had no doubt the
Arbiter would triumph.
After everyone left, the Guardians remained to exchange their
thoughts.
"What do you think about this match?" Yoa asked.
Karof replied without hesitation.
"Of course the Arbiter will win."
"But the light that slave uses... it's strange."
"So what? The reason we serve the Arbiter is because light is life.
Destructive light has no place in the settlement."
As Karof turned and walked away, the others followed suit. Only Yoa
remained, murmuring as she looked toward the path Shirone had taken.
"Destructive light, huh..."
Meanwhile, Shirone was being escorted by a gatekeeper to a place where
he could rest.
From what he'd seen, life in the settlement was simple.
The Sun Children did nothing, while the Ancients took on nearly all the
labor.
Leaving the slave quarters and descending a few floors, he found Woodga
and Hamei sitting in a small room, basking in the light.
"What are you two doing here?"
Woodga, visibly elated, opened his eyes.
"Huh? Oh, you're safe. Thank goodness."
"I'm not out of the woods yet. I asked what you were doing."
"What else? We're basking in the sacred light. Huh? You're a slave,
aren't you? Shouldn't you be speaking more politely? Wait... if you can understand me, then Ra must've
blessed you too."
"That... was just postponed."
"Hmm, is that so?"
Woodga didn't seem too concerned. He figured it had something to do with
the Arbiter's upcoming Sun Ritual in three hours.
"Only those who've done something worthy of blessing can enter the
sacred room. It's where you can bask in the light up close. Today was supposed to be Hamei's blessing,
but she let me share it."
"Basking in light is a blessing?"
Hamei's eyes sparkled with bliss as she replied.
"It's the greatest blessing of all. When the light shines on you this
close, you feel this amazing energy. It's the best feeling in the world."
"If you stay too long, it's bad for you, but only the Arbiter can make
this kind of light. The slaves... they don't even know what it feels like."
Strong light, huh...
As he arrived at the room, Shirone fell deep into thought.
He could probably escape now—but maybe that's exactly what the Arbiter
wanted.
There is a way. Or… maybe not. I'd need to test it…
Just then, a strip of code linked up in front of him, and the priest
reappeared.
"...It's time. You will ascend to the top floor."
When the priest connected the code to Shirone, the scenery shifted in an
instant—they arrived on the settlement's rooftop.
The Arbiter was flexing, preparing his body, while the four Guardians
stood in a line beside him.
Looking down, Shirone saw hundreds of Sun Children gathered below, all
gazing up at the roof.
"O Avatar of Ra! Grant us eternal life! Grant us an everlasting
future!"
"Listen well, people of Ra!"
The plaza fell silent in an instant.
"The one who dared to impersonate Ra with false words—I shall punish him
myself. Now, I bestow upon you the power of blessing!"
The priest handed two devices connected with fiber optics to the
Arbiter, who grasped them in both hands and turned to face Shirone.
"Watch closely the power of my authority. If you cannot produce a
greater light than mine, I will sever your neck."
Shirone didn't answer, but examined the device.
The fiber optics were linked to the ceiling of the settlement, and the
trapezoidal handles were faintly glowing.
Is it a device that transmits light?
The Arbiter raised the device high into the air and released his
power.
"Even in darkness, the sun does not fade! I shall prove it with my own
body!"
As both his fists shone brightly and light began to flow through the
fiber cables, the crowd erupted in cheers once again.
"Uwaaaaahhhh!"
Glass orbs installed around the settlement began to glow, and
eventually, the entire structure lit up.
Without exception, the Sun Children turned their foreheads to the light
and began praying.
"Ra! Ra! Great Ra!"
"Amazing as always."
Karof nodded with satisfaction at the dazzling spectacle that drove back
the darkness of night.
As long as the Arbiter existed, the Sun Children would not
perish.
Even without combat ability, the reason one with the Language of Light
was always chosen as Arbiter was evident.
Feeling he had displayed enough of his strength, the Arbiter released
the device and the light faded.
Even so, the lingering waves of awe drifted on the night air, and the
people trembled in ecstasy under the afterglow.
"Now, it's your turn."
The Arbiter stepped aside with a sneering grin, as if mocking
him.
A light strong enough to illuminate the entire settlement...
As Shirone stepped forward, the crowd buzzed with murmurs.
Their words were muddled, but the waves of sound carried clear
hostility.
I have no choice.
Shirone's eyes snapped open as he focused his mind.
"Heretic, take the dev—"
The priest, who had approached to hand over the device, turned
pale.
Above Shirone's head, a small point of light traced an arc and formed
into a massive circle.
Ultra-Power Amplification Array: Ataraxia.
It was a technique that surfaced with the opening of his Immortal
Function—something from someone else's memory.
As Ataraxia completed its rotation, the Arbiter instinctively sensed
danger and shouted,
"It's the language of heresy! Seize that slave immediately!"
To the others, this may have seemed just a contest to decide Ra's
successor, but to the combatants, it was a matter of life and death.
"The Arbiter is right. Eliminate the heretic."
Just as the four Guardians lunged forward—
Shirone began gathering a small cluster of photons in both hands.
"Light?"
It was definitely light.
But it was barely enough to illuminate its surroundings, and even that
dimmed over time.
I have to align the polarization direction...
Growing anxious, the Arbiter mocked Shirone.
"That's all? You dare impersonate Ra with such a pitiful display?
There's nothing more to see! Destroy the heretic at once!"
The Guardians prepared to move again—but Yoa held up a hand.
"Wait. Something's... off."
The speck of light, barely visible in the dark, left Shirone's hands and
began to rise.
And then—just as it passed through Ataraxia—
BOOOOOM!
An unimaginable explosion of photons erupted in the sky.
The light emitted from the massive sphere—far larger than the settlement
itself—was enough to swallow everyone's vision.
A blinding world of pure white engulfed them, and cries of joy and shock
echoed through the air as people were struck by the overwhelming ecstasy of light.
"Waaaaahhhh! Aaaaaaahhhh!"
Woodga howled in overwhelming pleasure, tears running down his face as
if his very soul had been shaken.
It felt like his blood was evaporating, like his body might explode from
the overwhelming energy.
"Brother... Brother..."
Overwhelmed by emotion, Hamei clasped her hands together and looked up
with tearful eyes.
As the photons dissipated, the people finally saw the true form of the
light that had risen into the sky.
"A... aaaaaah..."
"Ra... Ra has descended..."
A sun was floating above Shirone's head.
