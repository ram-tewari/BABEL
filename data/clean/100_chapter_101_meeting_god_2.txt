Chapter 101:
Meeting God (2)
"Shirone……."
Emi, her eyes swollen from crying, clung to Shirone's hand. With the faculty currently
incapacitated, no one came to check on them. Or perhaps they had already accepted that Shirone was dead.
But Emi shook her head in denial. She couldn't accept that Shirone was dead. There were
too many inexplicable details for it to be mere obsession.
Nade offered words of comfort.
"Senior, don't worry. Shirone will come back."
"Exactly! It doesn't make sense. Why would he die? If mass teleportation was fatal, it
shouldn't have worked in the first place. Something strange must have happened to Shirone."
Iruki frowned, displeased.
"I don't care either way. Dead is dead. There's no room for emotions here."
"No. It's different."
Arin shook her head.
"Because right now, you're crying with such a sorrowful expression."
Iruki couldn't respond.
As Arin had said, before the mirror, a hundred words were meaningless.
***
That night.
The infirmary door slowly creaked open.
Emi had cried herself to exhaustion on a cot, while Nade dozed in a chair in the corner.
Iruki approached Shirone. Sleeping and death felt different from the start. Seeing
Shirone's cold, lifeless face made his heart ache.
By morning, the faculty would come to inspect Shirone. They would prioritize embalming
him for the funeral, but once that happened, there would be no way to bring him back.
Suppressing his emotions, Iruki pulled a scalpel from his pocket.
'I'll definitely save you.'
He checked on Emi and Nade, but neither showed signs of waking. After two days of extreme
stress, it was only natural. Finally, Iruki pressed the scalpel against Shirone's skin.
The blade glinted in the moonlight as it pierced Shirone's flesh.
