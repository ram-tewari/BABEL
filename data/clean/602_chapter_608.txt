Chapter 608:
Winter had arrived.
Alpheas, who was looking out at the snow-covered school scenery, turned
from the window and asked, "Did a notice come from the association?"
"See for yourself."
Olivia pointed to the stiff document on the desk.
It stated that graduating students from the five prestigious magic
schools had to personally deliver their graduation exam applications to the association.
"This could be a variable."
It was right when the final 30-week evaluation was starting.
At this time of year, students' minds become as sharp as blades, and
they adjust their biorhythms to peak on the day of the graduation exam.
Going to the capital's magic association in the midst of this would be
quite stressful.
"Are they using students for political purposes?"
This happened when Lupist, who had temporarily taken Gaold's place,
officially became the head of the magic association.
"That's how it seems. Lupist is an elitist. This is likely part of
a plan to manage future association members from now."
That's why the scope was limited to the five prestigious schools.
"Even so, isn't it too much? Changing something that was handled by
the school until last year like this. There must have been a lot of backlash."
"It's a show of power. Lupist's connections are excellent, but he
hasn't completely taken over the association."
"Gaold's influence must have been significant."
"He was a madman, but the kingdom's strongest mage. Many mages
secretly supported him despite publicly criticizing his actions."
Gaold was that kind of man.
"Right. Let's leave politics to the politicians and focus on our
students."
Collie, who had been listening, spoke up.
"To send 30 students to the association, we need to fill the slots.
Currently, one spot is vacant due to Anchal's withdrawal, and Aider needs to decide."
Aider, who had suffered multiple fractures from Shirone's Ataraxia
during the hill occupation evaluation, was still in the school infirmary.
The treatment was over, but the mental issue remained.
"According to the school nurse, the trauma is almost healed. Aider
is eager to take the graduation exam."
"Hmm."
Graduating students are entitled to take the graduation exam, and the
school can't stop them if they choose to exercise that right.
"But isn't he too young? Taking more than half a year to heal means
the trauma was severe."
Alpheas agreed with Olivia.
"Let's check one last time. If there are no issues, we'll fill the
slot with a volunteer from the advanced class based on grades."
"Yes. I'll leave it to Teacher Siina."
Collie bowed and left the principal's office.
As usual, Shirone sat cross-legged on the floor of the research meeting
room.
Since the upper part of Istas no longer existed, he came here every day
to perform the sequence ceremony.
"Believe in the power of the day."
Nade, who had recovered from his heartache, was being evaluated
normally, and the students paying the price for the Scramble Royal were steadily dropping in
grades.
There were only 10 days left until the graduation exam.
All the training up to now was just a process to see the results on that
day.
"Ugh!"
As the sequence ceremony surpassed 200 quadrillion, Shirone's face
twisted in pain.
He had manifested Temporal Reversal at the quadrillion level, and now he
was 200 times further.
Even so, entering the realm of the sun beyond the quadrillion was still
far off.
"It's immense. There's no end."
No matter how much he reached out, all he saw was an endless tunnel of
numbers.
-There is an end to numbers.
Recalling Goffin's words, which he still couldn't understand, Shirone
accelerated the sequence ceremony, causing the time energy filling the research room to swirl and
distort the scenery beyond recognition.
"Here I go!"
He would extend endlessly.
"Shirone, are you in there?"
At that moment, there was a knock, and Siina entered the room.
"Oh? What's the matter?"
Having perceived the future one second ahead, Shirone had already
stopped the sequence ceremony and greeted Siina calmly.
"Yes. Teacher Collie asked me to. Can you come with me for a
moment?"
"Of course."
Siina felt nostalgic as she watched Shirone stand up.
"You've really grown."
She recalled the memory of him catching the teachers' attention during
the entrance test.
The boy who only knew Spirit Zone and no magic had grown into the top
graduate, unmatched by anyone.
"Well, that's why we teach."
Shirone, wearing a winter coat, asked, "Where are we
going?"
"To the infirmary's rehabilitation training center. I need your
help with something. I'll explain on the way."
As they headed to the infirmary, Siina explained the situation to
Shirone.
"Oh, so it will take two days."
"Yes. But the travel time won't be long. We'll use the spatial
transfer magic circle at the Creas branch of the magic association to go to the capital."
The long-distance spatial transfer magic circle was a key security
device for the kingdom, and no one could use it for non-official purposes.
"The students being evaluated must have heard from Teacher Collie.
Make sure to prepare the clothes for the ceremony in advance."
The rehabilitation training center was located on the second basement
floor of the infirmary.
The 80-pyeong space was equipped with various rehabilitation tools along
the walls, and Aider, wearing patient clothes, stood next to the school nurse.
"Aider."
A student with a cute smile hiding a snake's malice.
The memory of him beating Maya unilaterally for fun was still
vivid.
"Oh? Long time no see, Shirone."
Aider greeted with the same smile, but Shirone, who had already seen his
true nature, couldn't greet him warmly.
"Are you still holding a grudge over that? I'm really sorry. I was
being foolish."
Sometimes Shirone felt he had been too harsh on Aider, but that was also
a regret born from the luxury of having many more choices as a stronger person.
"Yes. I heard you want to take the graduation exam?"
"Of course. I'm a graduating student too."
Shirone understood his feelings.
Giving up here meant repeating the year from the beginning.
"Coincidentally, I'm here to help with your rehabilitation
test."
Since Shirone was the source of Aider's trauma, he was the right person
for the job.
"Yes. I heard. Please take care of me."
Meanwhile, Siina and the school nurse were reviewing documents in the
corner.
"The rehabilitation was successful. No more nightmares or
hallucinations of light. But…"
"But?"
"It disappeared too cleanly. There were no issues during the
interview, but it's a doctor's intuition."
"Are you saying Aider is hiding something?"
"That's why we called Shirone."
Siina nodded and turned around.
"We'll start the test now. Shirone will recreate the situation, and
the school nurse will check Aider's condition."
Aider's face stiffened slightly.
"The situation back then?"
"Don't worry. It's not a real attack. Can you do it?"
"Yes, of course. I'm fully recovered."
Thus, the final test began, and Aider and Shirone faced each other from
opposite ends of the rehabilitation room.
'Ataraxia.'
As the colorful magic circle appeared above his head, Aider's confident
expression turned noticeably pale.
"It's okay, Aider. It's natural to feel fear. Just endure it, no
need to be nervous."
From a teacher's perspective, it was natural to want to give Aider, who
had worked hard to reach the graduating class, a chance.
Shirone moved to the next step.
'Photon Cannon.'
As the golden sphere appeared with a pop and vibrated, Aider's facial
muscles twisted with emotion.
"Aaaah!"
Aider, who had left his spot, buried his head in the corner and curled
up, screaming.
"The light, I see the light! Teacher!"
The hallucinations had disappeared, but he couldn't overcome the real
light.
Shirone, who had dispelled Photon Cannon and Ataraxia, looked at the
school nurse with a bitter expression.
After a moment of thought, she shook her head and wrote
"Rehabilitation Needed" in the personal information section.
"Good job."
Collie spoke to the 28 graduating students gathered.
"With this, the one-year course for the graduating class is
over."
There was no applause or cheers.
The only emotion felt by those who had trained their minds every day for
30 weeks was a nauseating tension.
"As you know, there are currently two vacant slots in the
graduating class. After a week of selection, two students from the advanced class have joined us. Please
welcome them."
As Collie turned his head, Mark and Maria, the top two students in the
advanced class, stepped onto the platform.
"Hello, I'm Mark. It's an honor to take the graduation exam with
you, even if it's late."
"Hello, I'm Maria. Please take care of me."
There was no concept of seniority in the graduating class, but Maria
bowed politely as was her nature.
No one paid attention.
The evaluations were over, and only the real competition remained, so
relationships were not important.
"The top two from the advanced class. Isn't that too
much?"
Anyone who had gone through the one-year evaluation process of the
graduating class would think so.
"But you never know. Those two have been personally tutored by
Teacher Thaad."
If a certified 6th-class mage had personally guided them, they wouldn't
have only undergone advanced class training.
'Senior Shirone.'
Mark met Shirone's eyes.
Experiencing the graduation exam in advance was a significant advantage,
but it also posed considerable psychological risks.
Even so, Mark chose to join the graduating class because of
Shirone.
'If not now…'
When would he get another chance to compete with Shirone?
The reason he had been able to rise so quickly from Class Seven to the
top of Class Four was because he had been chasing after Shirone.
'I will learn from you, senior. And…'
Mark looked at Maria.
'We will graduate this time.'
That was the reason he had been able to persuade Maria.
With the combination play honed through Thaad's special training, they
believed they could aim for at least the top 10, if not the top ranks.
"Now, let me explain tomorrow's schedule."
After Mark and Maria stepped down from the platform, Collie held up the
official document.
"Departure is at 6 AM, and preparations must be completed by 5 AM.
Wake-up time is 4 AM."
This was due to the security regulations of the long-distance spatial
transfer magic circle.
"Everyone must wear formal attire. Skirt lengths must be exactly 5
centimeters below the knee. Men will wear white dress shirts, women will wear white blouses, shoes must
be matte, no accessories other than the school badge are allowed, and all women must wear their hair
up."
Sabina raised her hand.
"Why do only women have to wear their hair up? There are many men
with long hair too."
Given the early wake-up time, the girls, who had to spend more time on
their appearance, were understandably frustrated.
"This is a rule set by the association head. If you have
complaints, you can formally appeal to the association."
This alone gave a glimpse into Lupist's character.
"Your attire must not be wrinkled by the time you arrive at the
association. Do not run, bend your waist, or swing your arms."
The students, already stressed by the graduation exam, looked even more
exhausted.
"We might as well be taken there as mannequins."
"Exactly. You must arrive at the association as if you were
mannequins, without any disarray. As you know, by noon tomorrow, all 150 graduating students from the
kingdom's five prestigious schools will be gathered in one place."
Only then did the students' expressions change.
"Of course, there won't be many people scrutinizing you closely,
but flaws are always noticeable. Do not show any signs of disarray. This is related to the school's
honor."
They couldn't afford to appear inferior to other prestigious
schools.
"Rest well today. Go to bed early and see you at dawn."
"Yes!"
The students' voices were louder than usual.
