Chapter 23:
Cold Boy and Hot Girl (1)
Shirone was overwhelmed by the variety of subjects he had to study. While he could choose
a major in his graduation year, until then, he had to complete all courses, so the scope was enormous.
First period was Basic Chemistry. Entering the Class Seven lecture hall, students were
settling into their seats. And standing at the podium was a familiar face.
The authority on ice magic, Olivert Sienna.
The second daughter of the Olivert family and a graduate of the Olivert school. Age 26. A
genius who reached 6th-rank magician with just ice magic. Beautiful face. Cold personality.
She had left a strong impression on Shirone during the entrance test with her icy
demeanor.
But surprisingly, she was popular among male students. Her beautiful appearance, teaching
position, and older-sister-like prickly personality—it was every student's dream.
Adjusting her glasses, she spoke with her long purple hair neatly tied up.
"Hello. Starting today, I'll be teaching Class Seven's chemistry. I'm Olivert Sienna."
Applause erupted like wildfire. Some even whistled or outright asked if she had a
boyfriend.
Ignoring them, Sienna opened her textbook first. Then, she subtly raised her gaze and
looked somewhere.
Shirone felt a chill. It felt like she was looking straight at him.
He thought it was his imagination, but Shirone's intuition was correct. The reason she,
who was in charge of Class Five, had taken on an additional subject was solely for Shirone.
'Talent rots if left stagnant. It's best to promote him as quickly as possible.'
Talent would follow once pushed. She planned to promote Shirone to crush her rival Sard's
spirit and uphold her principles as a teacher.
Regardless of her motives, the students were simply happy to be taught by the most
popular teacher.
"Teacher, is it true you're dating Teacher Sard? No, right?"
"Tell us about your first love. It's the first class."
"Please, Teacher. Please!"
As the students' demands continued, Sienna furrowed her brow. Then, a 30-centimeter ice
crystal formed beside her.
Crackle! Crackle!
The ice rotated and split, sending snowflakes scattering in all directions. The cold
particles instantly lowered the classroom's temperature.
The students, who had initially laughed it off, soon froze stiff as the temperature
dropped endlessly.
"Teacher! It's c-cold……"
"Let's begin the lesson."
Sienna stepped down from the podium and approached the students. As the ice's rotation
slowed, the temperature rose. But it was still below freezing.
"What principle do you think the ice magic I just cast operates on?"
She held out her palm, and an ice crystal floated above it.
"What is coldness?"
With her question, she clenched her fist, and the ice crystal shattered.
"Coldness is a state of energy deficiency. There's no such thing as cold energy. Thus,
while temperature can rise infinitely, it can't drop infinitely. The state of zero energy. We call this
absolute zero."
Shirone frantically took notes.
"By stealing energy from the atmosphere, you can create ice. Of course, it's ordinary
ice. But if you break it down to the particle level and scatter it with wind, what happens? Even simple ice
can lower the temperature to lethal levels, as you just saw."
The students paled at the word "lethal."
"So, how do you absorb energy? Or rather, how do magicians cast magic?"
Sienna extended two fingers and folded them one by one.
"Omniscience, omnipotence. Magicians cast magic through omniscience and omnipotence. The
Spirit Zone aligns the magician's mind with the world. Thus, within the Zone, the magician holds a divine
position. That's why this is called omnipotence. But that alone isn't enough to cast magic. Saying 'get hot'
won't raise the temperature inside the Spirit Zone. Here, the knowledge to raise the temperature is needed.
That's omniscience."
Shirone nodded involuntarily. The reason he hadn't been able to cast magic until now was
because he had only trained in omnipotence, severely lacking in omniscience.
"From now on, you'll study various subjects with me and other teachers. The Spirit Zone
is only half of magic. Without omniscience, you can't become an outstanding magician. Now, let's begin Basic
Chemistry."
Sienna finally turned and started writing on the blackboard. The real lesson had begun,
and the students' concentration was higher than ever.
'Indeed. Being popular as a teacher isn't just about looks.'
Trust in the teacher fueled the students' desire to achieve, so Shirone also lost track
of time during the lesson.
Some parts were hard to understand, but he didn't mind.
The backbone of his knowledge was already complete, and from today, he planned to devour
all other subjects.
At 5 p.m., the last class ended.
Basic Physics was over. Packing his bag and leaving the lecture hall, Shirone let out a
deep sigh. It had already been ten days since he started running errands for Amy.
He bought bread and coffee from the school store. Compared to the support funds from the
Ozent family, the cost was negligible, but it wasn't about the money—it was the principle. And that was
exactly what Amy was targeting.
"Why is she doing this to me? She's the one who wronged me first."
Walking without spilling the coffee required intense concentration. Well, he could
consider this part of his training. Adjusting his gait to prevent the coffee from spilling.
"As if! How long do I have to put up with this?"
Shirone felt pathetic.
"Oh my! There he goes, the devoted lover."
"He must be going to confess to her again today. It's practically an illness at this
point."
Passing female students pointed at Shirone and whispered. This was the biggest problem.
When he delivered bread and coffee to Amy, she would gracefully accept it and head into
the library.
Rumors had already spread among the students that Shirone had a crush on Amy. The more
the rumors flourished, the more coldly Amy treated him.
Seeing Amy relentlessly settling a six-year-old grudge, Shirone's illusions about women
were completely shattered.
"Hey! Here comes your prince."
"Prince? More like a servant."
Amy was waiting for Shirone at the library entrance with Seriel. Surrounding them were
students who had taken time out to witness the spectacle.
As Shirone climbed the steps, the watching girls giggled and clapped. His face turned
beet red.
Seriel smiled pitifully and said,
"But he's kind of pitiful. At this point, why not just accept him?"
Amy's eyes widened.
"What? What are you talking about? Why would I accept him?"
"Playing hard to get. You won't even make eye contact with guys, but you never miss the
coffee and bread Shirone brings. Some are even whispering that the Goddess of Alpheas has finally found her
match."
It was absurd. Her plan to provoke Shirone was backfiring spectacularly.
She had thought it would last a week at most. But for some reason, Shirone never showed
displeasure and diligently delivered the bread every time.
'Ugh! If this keeps up, weird rumors will spread. I need to stop this.'
As Shirone handed the bread and coffee to Amy, cheers erupted. His devotion to not
spilling a single drop for his beloved was touching. Of course, it was a complete misunderstanding, and the
rift between the two only deepened.
"Senior, here's your coffee."
"Ugh! So annoying!"
Amy raised her hand. To dispel the absurd misunderstanding, humiliation was the best
method.
She planned to slap his arm and spill the coffee. Then, Shirone would join the ranks of
countless rejected boys, and the strange rumors would die down.
"Who asked for this?"
Amy's hand trembled as she glared at the coffee.
'At least show some dislike. Idiot? Moron? Do you have no pride?'
Unexpectedly, her conscience pricked her. But this was Shirone's strength.
If he had to do something, he preferred to think positively. His attitude made Amy feel
guilty.
"Here, Senior, your coffee."
Amy, who had studied harder than anyone to reach Class Four, knew better. The time
Shirone was wasting on this was too precious.
Realizing this, she felt genuinely sorry. It wasn't like she avoided guys because she
cared about others' opinions—she just found them bothersome. The noble-born boys who acted high and mighty
despite having no skills didn't suit her temperament.
'Let them think what they want. As long as it's not me, who cares?'
