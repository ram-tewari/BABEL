Chapter 96:
The Genius Who Lost His Light (5)
"The Grand Sorcerer... is it you, Viltor Akein?"
The man was covered in blood. He had handsome features, and his eyes shone sharply like
gun barrels.
"You don't seem like the type to be swayed by money. Who are you?"
"My name is Mirhi Alpheas. I had no other way to meet you, so I came directly."
"Oh?"
Akein's eyebrows rose. It made no sense for someone holed up in a dungeon to be
completely ignorant of the outside world. Through secret channels, he had already learned of Alpheas' photon
theory.
"A light mage seeking out a dark mage. How peculiar. Sit down first. Let me heal
you."
Akein activated a life-sustaining device. Alpheas entered the hemispherical device filled
with green liquid and immediately fell into a deep sleep upon submersion.
Three hours later.
"Gah! Hah!"
Alpheas jerked his upper body above the water's surface. He had nearly suffocated. But
how had he been able to sleep? As he turned his head in confusion, he saw Akein sitting at a desk, writing a
thesis. The man looked more like a scholar than the grand sorcerer who had once thrown the continent into
chaos.
"Thank you for the treatment. This is quite a device. I'm curious about its
principles."
"No need for small talk. That doesn't change the fact that you're an uninvited
guest. Tell me why you sought me out."
If this was a man driven by curiosity and interest, there was no need to waste time.
Alpheas got straight to the point.
"I want to learn dark magic. Please take me as your disciple."
"Oh?"
Alpheas, currently the hottest topic in magical circles, was asking to become a disciple?
Even for someone like Akein, this was undeniably intriguing.
"Do you have a special reason?"
"I have a wife. But her intellectual abilities are lacking. I heard rumors that
you're researching memory storage through dark magic. I want to give my memories to my wife."
Akein's eyes flashed. Since they were both well-versed in each other's fields, no lengthy
explanation was needed.
"I've done considerable research on memory manipulation. But what you're asking is
on a different level. There are issues with personality transfer, and above all, it would require
transferring an entire lifetime's worth of memories."
"I believe it's possible. By combining the absorptive properties of dark magic with
photons' information-transfer capabilities, we could potentially create artificial intelligence."
"Hmm, using photon magic to construct a neural network and imprint memories onto
it?"
It was certainly a novel idea. Until now, photon magic had been limited in utility, often
referred to as time magic. But with the photon theory now revealed, Akein expected it to make breakthroughs
in information fields.
"Before we examine the theory, I have one question. Did your wife consent to this
experiment?"
"She doesn't know yet. But she will agree."
"There must be a story behind this."
Alpheas explained the circumstances that had led him here. After hearing his story, Akein
understood his feelings. Moreover, the proposal was undeniably genius. For the next day, the two discussed
its feasibility. Once they agreed on the theoretical foundation, even Akein conceded it wasn't mere fantasy.
"Fine. If you're truly prepared to stake your life on this, I'll take you as my
disciple. But convincing your family won't be easy."
Though becoming the grand sorcerer's disciple was an honor, Alpheas was also the scion of
a prestigious family. Abandoning a guaranteed position in the Thunder Magic Department for a dimly lit
laboratory seemed unlikely.
But Alpheas had no other options in mind. If Erina was unhappy, so was he. He wanted
nothing more than her happiness.
"Thank you. I'll bring my family here soon."
Upon returning home, Alpheas discussed it with Erina. Since it was a life-altering
decision, Klump was also present. Alpheas emphasized that this could cure Erina's intellectual disability.
Klump immediately objected.
"Alpheas, what are you saying? Why would you seek out such a person? He's a
criminal!"
"That doesn't matter to a mage. What truly matters is that he's the most
knowledgeable about dark magic on the continent. He's also an expert on human memory and brain structure. It
won't be a loss."
"No, it will be! You're bewitched! Implanting memories into a human brain? That's
impossible!"
"Klump, the reason you think that way is—"
"Yeah! I'm stupid! Not as smart as you, not a genius! But even I know one thing:
you're throwing away a blessing. Associating with someone like him will get your position in Thunder
revoked. What about your family? They'll disown you!"
Alpheas knew that all too well. But after countless deliberations, this was his
conclusion.
"I don't care. Those things can't dictate my life. Thunder, my family—none of it
matters. If Erina can be happy, I'll be happier than I am now."
Erina hesitated. But Alpheas's final words had already decided it. If her husband was
happy, so was she.
"Darling, I'll do it."
"Sister-in-law! This needs more thought!"
"I'll do it. Because I trust my husband. Alpheas will make me happy."
"Of course! Who do you think I am? A mage who earned the Gold Circle. And my master
is the grand sorcerer. Together, we can easily fix your shortcomings."
Klump visited daily to persuade Alpheas, but the couple's resolve was unshakable.
Eventually, Alpheas liquidated all his assets and entered Akein's dungeon with Erina.
"Welcome. I'm Viltor Akein."
"Hello. My husband is indebted to you."
Erina bowed respectfully. Now that he was her husband's master, she couldn't be careless.
But Akein comforted her as if it were nothing.
"Don't worry too much. I've devoted decades to this field. You've made a big
decision. If we succeed, we can cure countless mental illnesses. Your husband will also gain fame beyond
what Thunder could offer."
Becoming the grand sorcerer's disciple. And if success meant reclaiming everything her
husband had sacrificed, Erina thought it worth trying.
For the next two years, Alpheas stayed in the dungeon, researching memory transfer for
his wife. Akein, who operated his own dungeon, was an expert in experimentation. Under his rational care,
Erina lived there without much discomfort.
"Hey, sister-in-law. How are you?"
Klump visited weekly with food. Even if the two were busy with experiments, they couldn't
let Erina eat poorly.
"I'm fine. The experiment will end soon. Then my husband can return to the
world."
Klump laughed heartily. Despite enduring various tests for two years, she had persevered.
It made him bittersweet. So what if her intellect was lacking? Her willingness to sacrifice for her husband
was anything but inferior.
Hearing of Klump's arrival, Alpheas came to the room. As Erina had said, he looked
increasingly gaunt, as if pushing himself to the limit.
"You're here? Thanks, as always."
"How's the experiment? Any progress?"
"Almost done. The clinical trials were successful. Next month, Erina will be free
from those condescending stares."
"Next month? That soon?"
"What are you talking about? It's been two years. The master and I worked day and
night. Anyway, you must come that day. Bring alcohol instead of food."
The month felt endless to everyone.
But time passed, and the long-awaited moment arrived.
Erina lay on a bed, waiting. Akein and Alpheas were too busy checking the machinery, so
Klump kept her company to ease her nerves.
"Sister-in-law, how do you feel? After today, you'll have Alpheas's memories. Even
the time he wet the bed as a child. Think hard if he ever glanced at another woman. Hahaha!"
Erina laughed at Klump's joke. Though nervous, the mood was good. It felt like everything
would go well, and the equipment had been deemed flawless.
"Let's begin, Erina."
Alpheas lay beside her, stroking her hair. Because he loved her more than anyone, he
could give her his memories. Even the embarrassing or shameful ones—none mattered if they were hers.
"Darling, thank you."
"Don't mention it. I'm the grateful one. You endured so much. Let's live happily.
Have children too."
Alpheas worried if his smile looked forced. Honestly, he was so tense he felt like
fainting. Akein approached and attached devices to their heads. This experiment combined the pinnacle of
magical engineering, magical biology, alchemy, photon dynamics, and human biology.
"We'll begin now. Relax. It'll be over soon."
As Akein prepared to activate the device, Klump stepped aside, nervously wringing his
hands. With a clank, the machinery descended. Dozens of components whirred, and crystals emitted chaotic
light.
Alpheas unresistingly accepted the dark magic penetrating his mind.
The core principle was simple: absorb Alpheas's memories using dark magic, then transmit
them via photon output to overwrite Erina's memories.
"Gah!"
Erina's eyes flew open. As massive amounts of information flooded in, sparks flew from
her eyes. From Alpheas's earliest memory to their recent conversation, every moment of his life was
transmitted at light speed.
Erina smiled faintly. It felt like truly becoming one with Alpheas.
The excitement of their first meeting, the anticipation of marriage, the joy of newlywed
life. His love for her, free of any prejudice, came through intact.
'Darling, thank you. And I love you.'
Erina closed her eyes. No regrets. She had received more love than she deserved in this
life.
Meeting Alpheas... was her greatest fortune.
As the light faded, the experiment ended. Akein checked the gauges. 100% information
transfer rate. All memories had been perfectly delivered.
"Darling! Darling!"
Alpheas turned to Erina. Her pale face was covered in cold sweat.
