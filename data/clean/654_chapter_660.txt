Chapter 660:
The War Chariot Guild's master, Jordic, arrogantly raised his chin and
approached Ackman.
He was in his mid-50s, with a sharp, stern face, and wore a brown robe
embroidered with a carp winding around his body.
"Silver Ring Guild's gone soft. Figures. Chasing gold and nothing
else—you're bound to fall behind."
Ackman glared at him, but after being so thoroughly defeated, opening
his mouth would only make things more pitiful.
"Hahaha! That's why you need conviction to run a guild. You can't
last long just trying to fatten up your guildmates."
Master Evian of Blood Rose joined in, seizing the moment.
She wore a tight-fitting top that revealed her cleavage, a short skirt,
and a crimson neck cape like blood.
"They say if you can't cage the bird, let it fly. This just proves
Silver Ring isn't qualified to hold an Ivory Tower candidate."
It was infuriating to hear such things from sworn rivals, but Ackman
wasn't without something to say.
"So you think you could manage it, then? I'll give you a chance.
Why don't you prove it right here?"
The two of them clammed up.
They had watched the battle. Even they couldn't deny that Shirone
couldn't be bound by strength alone.
As the exchange ended, Shirone asked what had been on his mind.
"Who are you?"
Evian lowered herself slightly in a respectful greeting.
"Hello. I'm Evian, master of Blood Rose—strongest guild in the
kingdom."
"Ah."
As expected, they were the masters of rival guilds.
"And you?"
Shirone turned toward the man with the bushy sideburns, who extended his
hand.
"Jordic. Master of War Chariot. Even if you're a rookie, you've
heard my name, right?"
He hadn't. And as someone affiliated with Silver Ring, he had no reason
to shake hands with a rival guild master.
"Ahem!"
Jordic withdrew his hand, visibly annoyed. Evian, on the other hand,
went with a friendlier strategy.
"That duel—clearly a master verification, wasn't it? But Blood Rose
won't impose any conditions on you. We'd love to welcome you—no strings attached."
If they couldn't have it all, she was aiming for half.
Which left Jordic in a bad spot.
A war-monger who ruled by brute strength, he had no plans to offer
Shirone the position of master.
"Don't be ridiculous! Isn't it an unspoken rule that you can't join
multiple rival guilds? Hell, let's just settle this with a war. Let's see who's the strongest of the Big
Three!"
The problem wasn't Shirone himself—it was that his presence tipped the
balance of power.
In that sense, Shirone felt a bit bad, but not enough to shoulder the
responsibility.
'Actually, this is fine. It might help me cut down the time.'
Just as the masters were thinking of their guilds, Shirone was focused
on his mission.
"What if we did this?"
The three guild masters turned their eyes to him.
"I'm planning to organize a private mercenary unit."
Evian lit up.
"If that's the case, leave it to me. Blood Rose has plenty of
capable mages."
"No, I won't take just anyone. This could get people
killed."
The Radum operation was confidential, but at the very least, he needed
to make the difficulty clear.
Jordic, sensing a chance to step in, pushed forward.
"If you're scared of dying, you won't last in this world. Danger
doesn't mean anything to War Chariot's members. What really matters is this—"
He rubbed his fingers together, clearly asking about the reward.
But even Shirone didn't have a clear figure in mind yet. Even if Lupist
had entrusted him with full authority, gauging the right amount without knowing the guilds' current
standing was difficult.
"Well, I guess…"
"Ten thousand gold."
A voice called out again from the building.
"Huh? Miss Aria?"
Shirone's eyes widened in surprise at the unexpected person, but Aria
approached him calmly, as if it were all planned.
"Hey, Shirone."
"What are you doing here?"
"I told you, didn't I? That I'd see you again."
Jordic narrowed his eyes.
"I don't know who you are, but ten thousand gold? Is that the job's
reward?"
To a guild member who would risk their life for a hundred gold, it was
like golden rain falling from the sky.
"You're wrong. To be exact—it's ten thousand gold per
person."
Not only the masters, but even members of the Silver Ring Guild stirred
at that.
Shirone looked flustered.
"Miss Aria, what are you…?"
"Don't worry. I've already cleared it with the VIP."
If she had Lupist's approval, it was fine—but why it was Aria still
wasn't clear.
As if reading his thoughts, Aria spoke.
"My boyfriend told me. You're forming a mercenary unit, right? I've
decided to join. I think I can help."
Shirone rejected her immediately.
"It's too dangerous. You could die. And you're not even
combat-capable."
"What are you talking about? I'm a certified diplomat, you know. I
can handle myself. Want to see?"
She demonstrated the self-defense moves she learned at the
capital.
"Hah! Hah!"
Her strikes and kicks were clumsy at best. Shirone wasn't the type to
judge something he didn't know, so he looked to Rian.
"She's hopeless. She couldn't even take down a dog."
Hearing his verdict, Aria stuck out her tongue.
"Hehe, busted? I practiced last night. But don't worry, I've got a
secret weapon up my sleeve."
"Why are you so insistent on coming?"
The smile disappeared from Aria's face.
"Because it's my job. Just like you're working hard as a mage, I'm
doing what I'm meant to do."
Radum was under the Ministry of the Interior, but involving their agents
carried a high risk of leaks.
On top of that, since Radum was outside the kingdom's legal reach, Aria
could move more freely there—and her status as a low-level official made her ideal in Lupist's
eyes.
She clung to Shirone's arm.
"And you'll protect me, right? If you're the one protecting me,
even Hell wouldn't be so scary."
Ever since the Gold City terrorist incident, she had been digging into
rumors about Shirone, eventually finding out he was the kingdom's top talent and an Ivory Tower
candidate.
Thinking of Brooks, Shirone quickly pulled his arm away.
"If you're only coming because you think I'll protect you, then
don't. You never know what could happen."
"Hehe, I told you not to worry."
Already having planned everything out, Aria turned toward the three
guild masters.
"Let's make this official. I'm issuing a commission to Silver Ring,
War Chariot, and Blood Rose. Starting now, we'll be forming Shirone's mercenary unit and recruiting
members. There will be a first-round public test, and a second-round closed evaluation. It's a
confidential mission. Payment is ten thousand gold per head. Three thousand gold will be paid upfront as
a signing bonus. Please spread this through your guild networks immediately. The first test will be held
here, one week from today."
None of the masters objected. In fact, they were even considering
participating in the first test themselves.
Not for the money, but because how many of their guild members passed
could shift the balance of power.
"Understood. We'll spread the word."
As the masters stepped outside, Shirone pulled Aria aside.
"Aren't you making this way too big? It's supposed to be
confidential."
"That's exactly why it has to be big."
"What do you mean?"
"You really thought the VIP would entrust all of this to you alone?
Through this test, they'll send in their own candidates. Meanwhile, I'll screen out unknowns through
paperwork and interviews."
"Hmm."
Her responsibility was enormous.
'Well… the Association can't move openly either, I guess.'
Aria placed a hand on Shirone's shoulder.
"Using the guilds was the right call. Without at least ten thousand
gold, the real talent wouldn't show up. Even so... most of them are going to die."
Despite knowing that, Aria still took on the task without hesitation—her
boldness wasn't ordinary either.
"I understand. I'll do my best to protect you, Aria. But... I can't
promise anything."
"That's enough. Don't try to take on too much. Everyone's here by
their own choice. Whether they live or die—it's not your responsibility. That includes me."
She spoke like someone used to high office in the capital, and for the
first time, she truly felt like a graduate of the Magic Academy.
"Okay. I'll be counting on you."
During the week they waited for the test, Shirone and Rian cut off all
outside communication and received briefings from Aria at Brooks' mansion hideout.
They were taught the estimated population of nonhumans, their various
races and characteristics, and memorized the complex layout of Radum.
"Once we're inside Radum, high-level concealment facilities will
distort the structure. So don't rely too heavily on the map."
When it came to work, Aria was a flawless professional. But the moment
the session ended, she would immediately start flirting again.
For Shirone, rejecting her was the hardest part of his day.
"If you change your mind, just say the word. I won't eat you, so
don't feel pressured."
And yet, right after saying that, she'd sneak off to enjoy another
rendezvous with Brooks. It was clear she lived by different values than most people.
Even a notorious womanizer like Brooks had started to grow a bit
obsessed with Aria, and because of that, the way he looked at Shirone wasn't always friendly.
"Finally, today's the day. Let's go."
On the morning of the test, the three of them left Brooks' mansion and
headed for the Silver Ring Guild.
The ground that had collapsed under the Photon Cannon fire had been
repaired, and tables had been set up for the open test.
By the time the sun rose, applicants staying in Bashka had arrived in
waves, filling the guild to the brim.
A total of 273 applicants showed up, each wearing a badge marked with
their registration number.
"Alright, let's begin the entrance test for Shirone's Mercenary
Unit! The first round will be an open test evaluating your basic skills. Do your best to show us what
you've got."
As the applicants entered the arena one by one, Aria put on her
glasses.
She handled the paperwork. Shirone oversaw the magic tests, and Rian
evaluated physical combat ability.
Since this was an open test, they focused more on fundamentals than
flair. For mages, it was about Spirit Zone quality; for swordsmen or fighters, they were given about a
minute to show off a specialty skill.
Aria leaned over and whispered to Shirone seated at the center.
"Excluding the three of us and the helpers from the VIP's side, we
can only pass about 13 to 15 people. That means we have to eliminate over 200 in the first
round."
Shirone nodded, mentally setting a rough benchmark.
'Two hundred, huh. Isn't that too harsh?'
But once the test actually began, that thought vanished.
Even if they were chasing the ten-thousand-gold prize, many applicants
were far below the standard—they'd likely die in Radum before even doing anything useful.
"Ta-ha! Ta-ha!"
A muscular man wielding twin hammers swung them over his head with
gusto, but Rian remained unimpressed.
'Nope. Good strength, but his technique is terrible. He'll die like
that.'
He checked the "Fail" box on applicant 27's file and handed it to
Aria.
'Hmm, 27 in, and not a single viable one yet.'
Just as she started to worry, applicants 28 and 29 entered
together.
Aria skimmed their files, then her eyes lit up and she looked up.
'A support team.'
She subtly signaled Shirone that these were from the Association, and he
watched them closely.
They were a man and woman in plain black masks, but their presence was
clearly out of the ordinary.
"Greetings. Applicant number 28."
"And I'm number 29."
They each bowed in turn, and Shirone's eyes narrowed slightly.
"...Yes. Hello."
The voices belonged to Lupist, of the Magic Association, and his chief
secretary, Jane.
