Chapter 704:
Shirone stared intently at the young woman's face.
"She's not Middle Eastern, is she?"
The crystal ball on the table confirmed she was an astrologer, yet she
was Caucasian with blonde hair.
"Now I understand why she doesn't have any customers," Shirone
remarked as he took a seat.
"The Akad Desert is the birthplace of astrology. If you're not
Middle Eastern, people won't trust you."
It was admirable that she had decided to set up a tent in Vanguard
despite the odds.
"Still, why sit here all day? If no one comes, might as well just
daydream about striking it rich," Shirone said.
Although his own appearance might not inspire trust, he felt a sense of
camaraderie as a fellow outsider.
"Let's give her a chance. Maybe she's skilled but being overlooked
because of her race," Shirone suggested.
Kido and Rian dragged over chairs to sit on either side of him. Shirone
cleared his throat.
"Excuse me, we're customers."
A soft snore was the only response.
"Hey! You've got customers!" Kido banged on the table.
The woman twitched as if electrocuted and mumbled groggily.
"Sea…?"
"Sea?"
"Seafood pasta…?"
Her reply was in Continental Common, and she was clearly still
half-asleep. When she fully opened her eyes and realized they were staring at her, she quickly
straightened up.
"Oh my! Welcome!"
She hurriedly grabbed the face covering sprawled across the table and
put it on.
"Take your time. We've already seen your face anyway," Shirone
said, amused.
"Hohoho, my apologies! I haven't been getting much sleep lately.
Normally, astrologers aren't supposed to show their faces."
She seemed good-natured enough.
"You speak the Common Tongue. Where are you from?"
"Tormia," Shirone replied.
"Nice to meet you! I'm from the Kingdom of Märchen," she
said.
Märchen, a small nation below the Mediterranean, was also the birthplace
of Chagall.
"I've been fascinated by astrology since I was a child, so I
studied abroad in Kashan. I apprenticed under Master Logy."
The name didn't ring a bell for Shirone.
"He told me not to return until I earned 10,000 gold in Vanguard.
It's a kind of training."
"How much do you charge for a reading?"
"Two gold. I've made 360 gold in the past two months," she
said, sticking out her tongue playfully.
It wasn't a bad income compared to the cities, but considering the harsh
conditions of the desert, it wasn't great either.
"You might not leave anytime soon, then," Shirone
teased.
"Haha, that's okay. Once word of mouth spreads, customers will line
up. I'll hit 10,000 gold in no time," she said optimistically.
"Covering your face might help with that," Shirone
suggested.
She chuckled, understanding his point.
"True. But some customers come because they find it interesting.
Though, as you can see, business is pretty dead now," she sighed.
"I lack the knack for it. People like hopeful interpretations, but
I tend to get so absorbed that I blurt things out. That's probably why Master Logy sent me
here."
If she had formal training, Shirone was intrigued.
"So, who wants to go first?"
"I'll go," Shirone said, placing two gold coins on the
table.
Astrology's allure lay in its ability to predict the future.
"Astrologers are supposed to foresee what lies ahead,
right?"
"Of course! That's my job."
She adjusted the crystal ball's stand so Shirone's face was reflected in
it. Placing both hands on the ball, she began.
"Let's get started."
Her playful demeanor vanished as she focused intensely on the crystal
ball.
"Does the future appear in the crystal ball?" Shirone
asked.
"No, it's more of a feeling. When I concentrate, your face fades
like ripples on water, and unique emotions begin to flow through."
After a long pause, she tilted her head, puzzled.
"That's strange. Why don't I feel anything? This has never happened
before."
For a moment, Shirone wondered if she was a fraud.
"Oh, wait… I feel it now…."
Her words trailed off. She stared at the crystal ball as if in shock,
her body trembling.
"A… ahhh…"
Even after the reading ended, she remained dazed. Finally, she tore off
her face covering and spoke.
"I'm sorry, but I believe there are some futures best left
unknown."
For someone who claimed to lack skill, she suddenly seemed very
convincing.
"Haha! Don't worry. Just tell me the truth. I won't get mad,"
Shirone assured her.
"But it's not that simple. This is…"
Biting her lip, she seemed to make a decision.
"Very well. Your future holds immense despair," she said
grimly.
Despair?
"An endless, pitch-black void where you can neither see nor act.
That's all I saw in your future."
It felt less like a prediction and more like a curse.
"A massive enemy will entangle you, and in the end… you'll strangle
yourself to death."
"What? Shirone will strangle himself?"
Even Rian, who had been listening quietly, was dumbfounded.
Dying in battle was one thing, but suicide was the last thing anyone
would associate with Shirone.
"I'm sorry. I don't know what else to say."
'That's it?' Shirone thought, unimpressed. Her lack of tact seemed
evident now.
"Kikiki! What a fantastic future!" Kido laughed.
Shirone managed a smile, though he couldn't deny his legs felt a little
weak.
Rian stepped forward next.
"Can you read my future too?"
After placing two gold coins on the table, the woman stretched her neck,
preparing herself.
"Let's begin."
As her gaze sank into the crystal ball, her expression turned
pale.
"I… I can't believe it…."
Rian frowned.
"What? What did you see?"
"You will experience endless suffering. No matter how much you
struggle, you'll never escape it."
"Hmm, that sounds intense. So, what happens next?"
"Nothing. Just pain—endless pain. And eventually, you'll meet the
most horrific death imaginable."
It seemed like she was eager to deliver the harshest verdict
possible.
"I… die?"
Rian had always thought there might come a day when the whispers of
Smille would stop, but this was hard to believe.
"Puhaha! Creepy, but I like it! Alright, it's my turn
now."
Kido tossed the gold coins he had gotten from Woorin onto the
table.
"Tell me, how do I die?"
The girl took a deep breath, trying to steady herself, and peered into
the crystal ball reflecting Kido's face.
Minutes passed.
As Kido drummed his fingers on the table and stifled a yawn, the girl
remained utterly still, staring at the ball.
"Hey, what's the holdup? Struggling to make up a goblin's grand
finale?"
The girl's expression slowly shifted.
"Why… what's wrong?"
Tears welled up in her eyes as she gazed at Kido with overwhelming pity.
A single tear rolled down her cheek.
"Hey, what's the deal? Why are you crying?"
"You…"
She choked on her words, her voice trembling.
"You'll experience a heartbreak so excruciating, it will tear your
soul apart."
"Heartbreak? A goblin falling in love? Don't make me
laugh."
Goblins were a race that thrived on chaos and pleasure, not
romance.
"A love so agonizing that your heart shatters, leaving you hollow.
You'll wither away, twisted and decayed. Yet, despite the pain, you won't be able to let
go…"
She couldn't continue and covered her face with her hands,
sobbing.
"This is insane! You're telling me I'll die because of some stupid
love? And why are you crying, huh? If it's my pain, I should be the one crying!"
Somehow, her tears made the prophecy feel even more unsettling.
After a few moments, the girl snapped out of her daze, wiping her
face.
"Ah, I'm so sorry. I must have lost my mind for a
moment."
Slapping her cheeks lightly, she gave a sheepish smile.
"I'm really bad at this, aren't I? Do I just not have any
talent?"
"…"
None of them knew how to respond.
"Ugh, whatever! To hell with it! Is this even astrology? If cursing
people out is all it takes, I could do that!"
Kido stormed out of the tent, making sure his voice carried back to the
girl.
"Stop it, Kido. You're just scaring her. Don't take it to heart.
Honestly, is there even such a thing as a happy death?"
"Who said I'm scared of dying? It's just absurd! You're the one she
said would strangle himself! Who kills themselves like that?"
Shirone felt just as uneasy.
"Enough. Let's forget about it. How about we find a place to drink?
After a good night's sleep, none of this will matter."
Sometimes, Rian's simple solutions were what they needed, and Kido
enthusiastically agreed.
"Yeah! Let's get the strongest stuff they've got!"
The three of them ended up at a bar on the first floor of an apartment
building.
Loud, chaotic music played, and masked dancers performed on a stage,
their movements entrancing the crowd.
"Bring out the best food and the strongest drinks!
Now!"
After Rian translated Kido's enthusiastic order, a feast large enough to
collapse the table was served.
"Perfect! Let's eat, drink, and forget!"
Kido downed strong liquor as if it could wash away the day's events, and
even Shirone and Rian joined in, gulping down their drinks.
"She's a fraud, a complete fraud!"
Kido slammed his glass down.
"She just makes stuff up, getting lost in her own little world.
People fall for it because it feels like a prophecy. Humans and their delusions!"
It wasn't entirely impossible.
"Hey, you guys went to Claris for a reading, didn't
you?"
A drunk at the next table leaned over, grinning.
"Claris?"
"The astrologer with the crystal ball."
"Yeah, why?" Shirone asked.
The drunk burst into laughter.
"Puhaha! That explains your sour faces! Claris is infamous in
Vanguard for her ruthless readings. Nobody believes her, so don't take it to heart."
"I'll strangle that girl myself!"
The drunk raised a hand to stop Kido.
"Just let it go, man. Forget it and have a drink. Hey, looks like
you've got some good booze. Mind sharing a glass?"
Shirone poured him a drink, and the drunk offered a sly smile.
"Forget the astrology nonsense and enjoy yourselves. You've got
money, so why not tip the dancers? You'll see quite the performance."
"I've got money to burn!"
Kido pulled out his coin pouch, but Shirone frowned.
"Kido, impulsive spending isn't a good idea."
"So what? I'm destined to wither away anyway! Let's live it up.
Watch the guy with a suicide reservation have some fun."
Kido tossed gold coins onto the stage, and the dancers scrambled to pick
them up.
"Here you go! Let's party tonight!"
The dancers, now holding the coins, knelt before Kido in gratitude.
Cheers erupted from the crowd as the dancers let their clothes gracefully slip to the floor.
When the masked performers struck provocative poses, Kido laughed and
cheered.
"Khehehe! So this is what it's like? Hey, you girls…"
But when Kido turned around, Shirone and Rian were frozen, wide-eyed,
with drinks spilling from their mouths.
"What's with you two? Don't tell me this is your first time seeing
this?"
It was. And it was bizarre.
"They're completely different from men…" Shirone thought, his
face burning.
"Hah, you're both so naive. How are you supposed to survive
traveling like this?"
Shirone wiped his mouth, averting his gaze.
"Who said it's my first time? It's just… unfamiliar, that's
all."
In the chaos of a battlefield, seeing a woman's body was one thing.
This, however, was something else entirely.
"Hohoho! Another great night of business!"
From a distance, Momodo observed the lively bar, her fan covering her
smile.
Selling pleasure amidst emptiness—that was Vanguard's purpose.
"Manager, the Noskarta will arrive in an hour."
"Shut down Vanguard now. Activate the evacuation measures and drain
Luna's waters. Don't forget to cut the source underground," Momodo ordered.
A watchtower soldier climbed down a ladder.
"Manager! About forty people are quickly approaching from the
north. They'll be here in ten minutes."
"Late arrivals? Lucky for them. Let them in."
"But… they're not merchants."
"They're not?"
"Judging by their flag, they seem to be the Maga
Bandits."
Momodo's eyes turned icy.
"Oh?"
The most infamous bandit gang in the central Akad Desert had
arrived.
