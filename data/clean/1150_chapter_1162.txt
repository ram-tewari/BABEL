Chapter 1162:
Green Ocean.
At the outskirts of the elves' homeland, three hundred angels, with the
help of the fairies, had arrived.
Their faces, having lost their Divine Radiance, were filled with fear of
the unknown.
'Hmph.'
The Fairy King, Crown, rested his chin on his hand.
'Useless.'
He had suspected something was off when Uriel, the Archangel of
Destruction, requested the fairies' aid.
'But not to this extent.'
Angels, having fallen to the status of humans?
There was no longer any benefit in receiving their help, but in another
way, this was an opportunity for the fairies.
'Becoming the King of Humans… The greatest obstacle in that path has
vanished.'
Moreover, Uriel was still intact. If used wisely, he could become a
powerful asset.
'If we take the angels as hostages, even Uriel won't be able to act
recklessly. Strength is key here.'
The reason why angels could not go to humans.
'The minds of fairies do not change easily. That's because their concept
of justice has been built over countless ages. But angels are different. If God's perspective changes,
so do they.'
Even so, he still couldn't quite believe that such a situation had
actually occurred.
Uriel silently observed the confused angels as they hesitated upon
touching the ground.
Crown's lips curled upward.
'Of course, this must be miserable for him.'
Angels, now like lost children, would have to be personally tended to by
Uriel.
"The task you requested is complete. Since we are in a time of war,
extracting the fairies was quite troublesome."
"… I appreciate it."
Even as the Fairy King, such words would have been unthinkable in the
past.
"However, I am concerned. Since the angels have fallen to the status of
humans, they will now require everything necessary for survival."
They would have to eat, and they would have to sleep.
"Can you take care of them?"
"Of course, but there is a slight issue… At dawn tomorrow, we will
launch an assault on the elves' homeland. It will be a decisive battle, and we cannot afford to divert
our forces."
"I will fight."
Crown rejoiced internally.
"If I take to the battlefield, how many troops can you spare? The angels
need support until Satiel restores the Divine Radiance."
Crown understood the meaning behind his words but dismissed it as
irrelevant.
'God's justice has faltered. Even if he is an archangel, this matter has
already slipped beyond his grasp.'
With those thoughts, he replied.
"That is a difficult question. Uriel's strength is unmatched compared to
the fairies. However, considering the war, we can spare about three hundred."
One fairy caretaker per angel.
It wasn't much, but right now, even a single fairy was desperately
needed by the angels.
"I accept."
"Thank you. Your participation in battle will be of great help. Guards,
escort the angels to the barracks."
At Crown's command, a senior fairy led his subordinates to guide the
angels.
"This way, please."
The angels, lined up and heading toward the fairy headquarters, looked
uneasy.
"Could you… Could you turn on more light? It's too dark—I can't
see."
For beings who had once transcended space and time, now bound to the
human perception of vision, it was akin to being blind.
"We are at war. We cannot light the area any further. Please bear with
it for a while."
"O-okay."
Another angel spoke.
"My stomach feels strange. I sense… pain. Bring a fairy with healing
abilities."
A growling sound echoed.
"That's called hunger. Here, have some fruit. It will help."
The angel accepted the small fruit that the fairies ate and
waited.
The fairy, looking exasperated, gestured toward his mouth.
"You have to put it in your mouth and swallow it."
"Ah, right. Sorry. This is my first time."
Watching the angels awkwardly attempt to eat the fruit, the fairies
sighed.
'This is a nightmare.'
Caring for superiors who couldn't do anything was far more exhausting
than fighting elves.
Then, suddenly, the fairy smirked.
"Alright, everyone! We are in a location where enemies can easily spy on
us. We should crawl on the ground to avoid detection."
"Oh, really?"
The angels hesitated, wondering if this was humiliating behavior.
But with the absolute authority of God now gone, they no longer had the
courage to choose for themselves.
"I suppose we should?"
One by one, the angels dropped to their knees and began crawling
forward.
This time, the fairies were the ones taken aback.
'They're actually doing it?'
Like prisoners who had followed the commands of their jailers their
entire lives, only to suddenly be thrown into the real world.
'They cannot bear the fear of making decisions themselves. So instead…
they follow my standards…'
Watching the angels crawl away, the fairy grinned.
'Heh! Amazing! The angels, who always looked down on me, are now obeying
my every word.'
A higher-ranked fairy, Jin, flew down.
"What are you doing?"
"Jin!"
Jin's face was hardened with anger.
"Are you insane? Even if the angels lost their Divine Radiance, Uriel is
still standing. Do not push your luck."
"I-I'm sorry! I didn't actually think they would listen! I just thought…
it would be funny."
Jin let out a sigh.
Fairies were infamous for their mischief, not because they were
malicious, but because they committed evil acts without realizing it.
"I understand, but be mindful. If the angels ended up like this… we
could be next. Do not forget why Crown is waging this war."
"Yes… I'm sorry."
As the anger faded from Jin's expression, the fairy turned to watch the
angels again.
"It's terrifying, really. The absence of standards. Will we… eventually
become like them?"
"Most likely. That is why Crown seeks to become human. Human life has no
inherent standards. They must constantly think, decide, and choose for themselves. Those who make the
wrong decisions perish, while those who make the right choices survive—a merciless system."
The fairy stared blankly, mouth slightly agape.
"We must become like that as well. The future of the fairies rides on
this battle. Inform the others—no more pranks on the angels. Even the smallest thing could have
consequences."
"Yes, Jin."
As the two fairies followed the angels, Uriel finally ended his remote
observation.
Watching the fairies play tricks on the angels, a thought arose in his
mind.
'Is this truly our reality now? Or perhaps… this too was always meant to
be.'
From the Era of Gods to the Era of Angels, then passing through the Era
of Fairies…
'And finally, the Era of Humans.'
The world was moving toward uncertainty.
Ymir's Depths, Layer 1.5.
In the virtual world created by Luber, Shirone simply gazed out at the
sea.
A year had passed since his arrival, but perhaps that day had only been
a few hours ago.
Standing behind Luber, who was watching Shirone from the shore, were
Gaold and the others.
By now, they understood that this place was a dream, yet it did not feel
like a lie either.
"It's like one of those spot-the-difference puzzles," Luber said.
"If you look at only one picture, it seems perfect. But when you compare
two, you start noticing inconsistencies. The biggest problem is that you can't tell which picture is the
real one."
Sein asked, "Wouldn't the real one be the one we call reality?"
"Yes, that would be the case—for a human. But that's only true when
you're in reality. The reason you're feeling confused is because this world is perfect. The Five Great
Systems that make up this world have no flaws."
Luber turned to face them.
"In reality, logical reasoning is processed through Dreamo, while
irrational emotions drift into the Under World. We call these two ideals and desires."
Though they might seem similar, they were fundamentally
different.
"Dreamo merges the two, sending necessary information back to reality
and discarding unnecessary data into The Abyss. In the end, reality is nothing more than one of many
systems where a specific logic applies. Dreamo is merely a buffering system that mediates this
process."
Sein nodded.
"So that's why time doesn't flow here."
"Exactly. Even in the Under Coder, time flows, albeit slower than in
reality. But Dreamo is different. If no information is being processed, time does not flow."
Kangnan asked, "Then how does the Under World's information reach
Dreamo?"
"It comes through the Under Coder. The REM realm exists in a half-sleep,
half-awake state. Therefore, there are two ways to access it—leaning more toward sleep or leaning more
toward wakefulness."
The difference between the two was paper-thin.
"The first is entering a lucid state while dreaming.
The second is entering a dream-like state while awake.
Even demons sleep, but they barely influence Dreamo because their ideals
are weak. On the other hand, the second type of Under Coders are highly active. They bring in massive
amounts of Hidden Code."
"So, because the nature of the information is different, the pathways
are different too."
"Exactly. In truth, the Under Coder is merely a passageway for
information. There is no administrator governing it. Anyone can create a new world within it. If logical
Under Coding is something exclusive to humans, then illogical Under Coding is mostly created by demons,
since they specialize in Hidden Code."
Luber turned back to Shirone.
"Reality isn't everything. A battle is being fought over all Five Great
Systems. If you walk out there as you are, you will be killed by Ymir's Incarnation. Unless the Five
Stars regain their will to live, that is…"
But Shirone showed no signs of moving.
"In reality… he lived a painful life. He doesn't have the strength to
dive into his thoughts anymore. He's lost the drive to move forward. There's nothing wrong with finding
peace in a dream, at least for now."
Living in this world.
None of them had particular complaints, but their lingering attachment
to reality remained.
And that attachment would only grow stronger, eventually driving them
mad.
Miro, who was holding Arius, spoke up.
"He's not giving up."
"Hm?"
"You said he's waiting. If he had truly given up, he wouldn't be staring
at the sea."
Sein nodded.
"That's possible. Then, Miro, what do you think he's waiting
for?"
"Hmmm."
Miro thought for a moment before replying.
"A dream."
"Haha! That's a wonderful answer. But this place is already a dream.
Even if I still find it hard to believe myself."
"He's waiting for a dream."
Sein's expression turned serious.
"Why?"
"Because having nothing to do means having nothing at all.
That brother has been completely emptied out.
If he wants to fill himself up again, he has to take something from
outside himself.
And what can do that?
Only a dream."
Everyone froze.
They weren't looking at the nine-year-old Miro standing before
them.
They were thinking of the Miro from reality.
'Dreams and reality.
No matter which is the real picture, her insight remains the
same.'
Sein gently stroked Miro's hair.
"When you put it like that… I suppose we have no choice but to dream for
now."
Luber stroked his beard.
"A dream… A dream, huh…? Could it be?"
There was another dream within Shirone, one that wasn't his own.
Kangnan asked, "What kind of dream is he waiting for? A dream of meeting
a lover? A dream of the world he once longed for?"
Luber finally spoke.
"Perhaps…
If it is something passed down,
It might be the dream of a single flower."
Demonic Sword, Armand.
A dream that belonged to the man who once dreamed the grandest dream of
all,
And the Fireborn who loved him.
