Chapter 673:
The Secret of Radum (1)
The brains in the capsules were released, and the team members regained consciousness one
by one.
No lives were lost, but the mood after coming to their senses was devastating.
It was the overwhelming military power of the Lord of Terror, and Shirone was equally
disturbed.
'What was that? That memory…….'
Though it felt like it wasn't his own, seeing Ikael's face made his heart feel like it
would burst.
'Ra Enemi.'
Apart from the Ivory Tower test, he now had one more reason to meet him.
As the 17 members, having finished their preparations, formed ranks and began moving,
Kargin approached Joshua.
"Are you okay?"
Joshua had grown quieter after seeing the vision of his son.
"Don't worry. I'll stay alert."
"That's not what I meant……"
"It wasn't love."
Joshua spat out the words.
"A child who didn't even know who his father was. I always resented him. I just felt
guilty. That's all."
The words sounded almost like self-brainwashing, and Kargin didn't press further.
On the way into Radum, no residents were visible.
Perhaps they were still trapped in the shadow world, drifting somewhere to evade
Galloper's blades.
"We've arrived."
Kuan, who had reached the boundary separating Radum's exterior and interior—known as the
Siren—spoke.
"From here on, it's a different dimension. You'd better brace yourselves."
There were no visible distinguishing features, but the sensation transmitted through the
Ultima system confirmed this was a world unlike any before.
'Incredible.'
Within the informational veil encircling the entire area, countless circuits each secured
their own domains.
To Bakji's senses, it was a space burning with light.
"I've heard the rumors. A high-level concealment facility. Even the best information
mages can't fully decrypt Radum's internal security systems."
Even if Shirone could identify the nature of the signal, bypassing it was an entirely
different matter.
"How do you plan to break through?"
At Arkman's question, Jane spoke.
"There are two ways to reach Radum's core. One is to decrypt the codes one by one. The
other is to cut off the power supply."
"Cut off the power?"
Ibian suddenly seemed to realize something.
"Now that you mention it, it's strange. Radum is an isolated area. Where does the power
for the concealment devices come from?"
"The sun."
Rufist answered.
"What I'm about to tell you is a top-tier state secret."
The team members swallowed hard.
"Right now, we can see the sky. That's because something in Radum's core is allowing
light to pass through."
"What the hell are you talking about?"
"At first, it takes the form of a small sphere."
A steel orb the size of a human head materialized above Rufist's palm.
"A kind of seed. You plant it in the ground."
With a thud, the orb embedded itself into the ground.
"It's made of a substance called photic ore, which transmits, absorbs, or reflects light.
It doesn't exist on this planet, and of course, I can't synthesize it either. But for the sake of
explanation……"
The orb trembled, and vines sprouted from its surface, intertwining as they grew.
"It absorbs sunlight and grows on its own. Like a living thing. The photic ore is
presumed to have a special design inside, akin to a genetic blueprint."
Buds formed on the vines, blooming like flower petals.
"It collects photons and releases energy. Think of it as similar to photosynthesis—it'll
be easier to understand."
"No, I don't understand at all. What the hell is this?"
Jane spoke.
"The Torumia Kingdom has long isolated Radum from the capital. Why do you think that is?"
"Because terrorist groups and non-humans caused trouble. It's basically the capital's
dumping ground."
Shirone had initially thought the same.
"That's not wrong. But it doesn't explain the original cause of Radum's existence. Let me
show you now—the secret of Radum."
Jane took Nemesis out of the Cubic.
"Let's begin."
Following Jane's lead, Shirone inserted Nemesis and activated the Ultima system,
manifesting the Siren—Radum's internal boundary—into reality.
Jane used a simple information spell to materialize an electrical circuit, and Aria
approached it.
"By inputting the top-tier security code, we'll cut off the power to 'Sanghwa.'"
Aria's role was Radum exploration and code transportation.
The code had been imprinted directly into her subconscious in Yongroe, so even she didn't
know it.
Yet, because she held a top-tier secret, Rufist had assigned Kuan, the best bodyguard, to
protect her.
Jane knocked on Aria's mind.
Whether to accept or reject was Aria's choice, but the moment she refused, she would be
killed for security reasons.
'I grant access.'
The moment she thought it, Aria's eyes snapped open, and the keyword hidden in her
subconscious activated.
The Siren's boundary veil blurred, and the landscape of Radum's interior began to reveal
its true form.
Weeeeeeeeee—!
An ear-splitting alarm sounded, and a skyline of buildings blending the traits of
countless races unfolded before their eyes.
"Wh-what is that?"
Wooden, stone, mechanical—even structures too esoteric for human comprehension.
But what shocked the team was the massive iron tower, hundreds of meters tall, standing
at Radum's deepest core.
Rufist gazed up at the bud at the tower's peak and spoke.
"It's grown quite a bit."
The rumored base of the non-human alliance was, in truth, a flower.
"The reason the kingdom left Radum untouched. Sure, it was to isolate the trash, but in
the end, they were just fertilizer. Radum is a flowerpot. A flowerpot to cultivate an ancient weapon."
"An ancient weapon?"
Yordik turned to Rufist, his face pale.
"The ultra-large interceptor, Sanghwa. A strategic defensive weapon that collects solar
energy and releases high-energy beams. Its power is immense enough to transmit energy to all of Radum's
concealment facilities through its roots."
Rufist finally revealed the team's true mission.
"From now on, we infiltrate Sanghwa, take control, and steal its seed."
'Damn, so that's it!'
When Sanghwa was first reported to the Torumia Kingdom, Radum was isolated because once
the seed took root, it couldn't be moved.
Grow Sanghwa, obtain a new seed.
With that decision, the kingdom sealed off Radum, and non-humans gradually infiltrated,
leading to the present.
Boooooooooom—!
The ground trembled, and the team looked up at Sanghwa.
"It's…… opening."
The flower bud unfurled, casting a shadow over Radum.
Each petal spanned 200 square meters, and dozens of them bloomed—a spectacle beyond
words.
As the flower fully bloomed, a colossal shadow fell over the opposite side of Radum.
That area was likely plunged into night-like darkness.
"Ughhhhh!"
The ground swayed as if floating on the sea, and within moments, photons from a radius of
several kilometers were sucked into the petals.
As the surroundings suddenly darkened, the team glanced around nervously, and Rufist
shouted.
"It's firing! Get inside!"
"Damn it!"
A beam shot from Sanghwa slammed diagonally into the western region.
Scraping along the boundary between the exterior and interior, the beam triggered a
tremendous explosion.
"They already noticed."
The fact that they counterattacked the moment the Siren's boundary was disabled was
proof.
Mayray covered one ear and shouted.
"I hear Ra Enemi's voice! He gave the order!"
Kon yelled.
"Damn it! Do something! We're all gonna die at this rate!"
The beam's destructive radius was so vast that escaping in time seemed impossible.
"Jane!"
At Rufist's call, Jane reacted instantly.
'Cosmic Rail!'
A mental-type spell activated, and Jane's voice echoed in everyone's minds.
\- No tickets required for allies.
"What the hell does that mean……!"
Bicon's body, despite his protests, floated up and landed in what resembled a passenger
car with wheels.
"What?"
All 17 members were gathered inside, and beyond the illusory passenger car, a
one-meter-long rail was being laid at incredible speed.
The mass-transit spell, Cosmic Rail.
Its speed of laying rails corresponded to its movement speed, providing passengers with
an environment identical to real-world physics.
This was designed to allow passengers to focus on combat, a feat impossible without
simultaneous environmental, mental, and physical control.
'As expected of the Magic Association's secretary.'
The algorithm for rail movement, the aerokinetic force transporting all 17 members, and
the mental control ensuring passenger autonomy—it was the pinnacle of customer service.
"Too slow! Faster!"
Despite being more efficient than teleportation for a large group, Rufist complained like
a spoiled brat.
'16 is a bit tough.'
Jane blew off the ceiling, and the rail-laying speed skyrocketed.
Meanwhile, the energy beam from Sanghwa swept the ground like an unfolding umbrella,
closing in.
The rails were laid at unprecedented speed, the gale-force winds making it hard to
breathe.
A building's peak, higher than the beam's trajectory, exploded, raining boulders.
Jane curved the rails sharply, and the passenger car veered violently.
"Whoaaaaa!"
"Hold on tight!"
The rollercoaster algorithm activated, and the rails rose almost vertically as the team
clung to the walls.
The energy beam narrowly missed them.
"Huff! Huff!"
Bicon, exhausted, slumped against the wall and spoke.
"This is one hell of a transport spell."
"For now, we'll get as close to Sanghwa as possible."
"What do you mean? We'll be there in no time."
As Yordik, the master of the war chariot, pushed himself up, goblins on a rooftop leaped
off on hang gliders.
"Kill to eat!"
The goblins, part of Radum's middle class, were short, relished human flesh, and combined
agility with cruelty.
Jane sent a warning.
"You might feel dizzy. Be careful."
Rufist glanced at the team.
"Hey, mercenaries. Time to earn your pay."
Joshua nocked an arrow, his eye technique flashing, while Wig drew his twin swords, ready
to fight.
"Here we go!"
As the Cosmic Rail's path twisted like a rapid, a hail of arrows rained from the goblin
squadron.
"Pathetic fools!"
Mages teleported out of the passenger car, countering with their specialties.
As goblins fell one by one, Jane's eyes widened.
"Danger ahead!"
An electrical net unfolded from the skyscrapers, blocking their path.
"Sanghwa's power is restored. Should we descend?"
"No. Get as close as possible."
As anti-air defenses reactivated one by one, the Cosmic Rail pressed on perilously.
'Just a little more! Just a little more!'
By the time they'd crossed half the concealment zone, photons began gathering on
Sanghwa's petals again.
The sudden nightfall prompted Jane to shout.
"Let's land! This time, it won't miss!"
"Keep going!"
Every second—no, every fraction of a second—counted if they were to reclaim Sanghwa in
time.
As a massive orb of light exploded from the emplacements on the stem, Rufist roared.
"Jump!"
"You've gotta be kidding……!"
As they surrendered to gravity, a deafening flash streaked overhead.
