Those who passed the standards of "good" didn't even make up 1% of the total

Are humans evil beings?
If so, how would one explain the 48,893 humans still remaining in this world?
As long as even a single good person remains, humanity cannot be defined as purely good
or evil.
Rather, as beings who choose between good and evil themselves, they will endlessly strive
for the future of humanity.
"Humans are not yet ready for extinction. By the judgment of this Great Judge, I

The incarnation of the Great Compassionate One changed gestures and swung its arms wide,
causing an immense number of light sources to overlap.
Teraphose Incarnation Art—Compassion's Demon Extinction.
A clear and pure sound, inaudible to human ears, spread across the land.
The law that weakens the power of evil by borrowing the power of good was delivered to
the entire world through the mighty incarnation of Teraphose.
The balance to be restored at a ratio of 50,000 to 3.1 billion was not large, but it was
powerful enough to erase the incident of La Enemi.
"Ah..."
Shagal stared blankly at the sky.
The horrific memories that oscillated between reality and illusion gradually vanished,
leaving only the original memories behind.
There were no new memories to fill the void left by the erased ones—only the time spent
wandering in darkness remained.
"Uwaaaah! Uwaaaah!"
Watching Shagal scream madly in frustration and resentment, Shirone grasped the severity
of the situation.
'This is the aftermath Maylay mentioned.'
Likely, people all over the world were reacting the same way as Shagal.
As the memories of La Enemi disappeared, the "Kahr" value he had acquired
through human experiences also began to drop, albeit slightly.
The army of the Reverse World ceased fighting, and Igor and Varshiba, who had been locked
in fierce combat, distanced themselves from each other.
"What... just happened?"
"Varshiba, the masters of this world are none other than us. If we lose control, we
can protect nothing."
La Enemi had promised Varshiba the independence of the Reverse World, but even that
memory was now gone.
Igor turned to Shirone and spoke.
"We shall return. Your affairs are yours to resolve. Today, we fought together, but
next time..."
Igor left his words unfinished.
The beings of the Reverse World and the humans of reality were fated to never coexist
from the beginning. If such an incident were to repeat, war would be inevitable.
With the retreat of the Reverse World's army, all that remained were the gruesome corpses
and Shirone's group, covered in blood.
Maylay, having concluded the incarnation art, descended to the ground.
"I have erased the incident of La Enemi. As the representative of humanity, you bear
the responsibility for this matter."
"...You're quick to say that."
He had already prepared himself for it.
"But why do I still remember?"
"Compassion's Demon Extinction erases all that is controlled by evil. If you were
not controlled, your memories remain intact."
"I see."
As the incident of La Enemi vanished, the scenery of the Reverse World slowly faded.
The brain in the vessel was released, and Maylay, having returned to her girlish form,
covered one ear.
Through the divine frequency, the voice of La Enemi reached her.
-Find me, Hexa.
La Enemi would not reveal himself to the world until he reached 100% Kahr.
-I will be where you seek.
The voice faded, and Shirone checked the status of the survivors.
Venetia was dead, and Shagal was nowhere to be seen, having likely already left.
As Etela, Kuan, Rian, and Rufist approached Shirone, Maylay spoke.
"I shall return. I do not know what future awaits you, but I hope you make the right
choices."
Rufist interjected.
"Why not stay a little longer? It's not like La Enemi is completely gone yet."
"Teraphose protects the order of the universe through sound. La Enemi manipulated
cosmic events, disregarding that order, which is why I intervened directly. Physical interference like this
won't happen again unless another cosmic-scale incident occurs."
"Hmm."
Rufist neither agreed nor disagreed.
Though he longed to exploit Teraphose for the Kingdom of Tormia, they were far too
dangerous to handle.
"Hexa, Teraphose has acknowledged you as humanity's representative. I leave the rest
to you. I will contact you if necessary."
As her words ended, Maylay's pupils returned to those of a normal human.
"Huh? Where is this?"
Her clueless expression left Shirone's group staring blankly at her.
"You remember nothing?"
"I think I was blown away by the explosion while defending against the bio-energy
beam..."
'She was replaced since then. We were completely fooled.'
Though it would likely remain classified, Maylay, as the only psychic capable of
communicating with Teraphose, would be recruited by the kingdom.
Etela spoke up.
"What do we do now? The memories of La Enemi are gone, but he still lingers in this

Kido replied.
"Do we even need to find him? There's no point in him manipulating events

The method La Enemi used to collect experiences was on a scale impossible without design

Shirone added.
"But his ability to manipulate sensory memories is still useful. La Enemi might find
another way to use it."
"You're really planning to find him?"

"Didn't this incident teach you anything? Carelessly, you could turn La Enemi into a

The Kashan Empire.
Wu Orin woke up in a secret chamber of the imperial palace, Agnos, known only to her
closest confidants.
Feeling the cool touch of the waterbed, she turned her head to see Gando, biologically
male but spiritually her son, reading a book.
"Are you awake?"
Gando handed her a glass of water, and Wu Orin stretched.
"How long was I out?"
"Roughly..."
Gando regretted using the word "roughly."
"One hour and forty-two minutes."
"Hmm, that's quite long."
After drinking, Wu Orin slipped on her slippers and walked past Gando.
"You've been spending a lot of time in the Under Code lately."
Wu Orin had been frequenting the Under Code recently.
"It's fun. I'm getting stronger quickly."
The program she linked to was "High Gear," a virtual reality world created by
professional coders.
One of the most popular paid contents in the Under Code, High Gear required a "Dream
Star" pill to enter, each costing over 2 million gold.
There, users could modify their bodies at will, with some even undergoing 100%
mechanization.
"Is modifying your body fun?"
Gando, who had never entered the Under Code, found the idea of body modification
masochistic.
"The stronger you get, the more competitive it becomes."
Wu Orin pointed to her ear, grumbling.
"I need to upgrade the low-frequency detection function. Stealth mode is so
annoying."
She wanted to buy it outright with real gold, but High Gear's operators strictly
prohibited cash transactions.
"Then again, if I bought everything, I'd get bored quickly."
Gando, watching her immersed in the game, asked worriedly:
"...What do you do there?"
"Just chat with people, share item or modification info, fight against opposing
factions, stuff like that."
Wu Orin suddenly remembered something.
"Oh right, I leveled up this time. Level 260. That means I can equip a Magnan."
Gando didn't know what a Magnan was.
"Is that so?"
"Breaking into the top 10 rankings will be tough, though. The top 10 are absolute
monsters. Especially 'White Snake'—their control is so mesmerizing it's hard to look away."
Wu Orin's current ranking in High Gear was 17th, with the username "Little
Witch."
Pointing at Gando excitedly, she asked:
"Want to join me? I'll support you fully. Our faction is short on attackers
anyway."
It was the first time Wu Orin had invited him to do something together.
Though it should have been touching, Gando only felt bitterness.
'Because it's fake.'
In reality, there was nothing he could share with Wu Orin.
"I appreciate the offer, but I'll decline. I'll support Your Majesty in the real
world."
Wu Orin blinked.
"Huh? The real world?"
Reading her thoughts, Gando added:
"Though even this 'real world' might just be someone's dream—all fake."
Lost in thought, Wu Orin smiled and shook her head.
"No, that's not it, Gando. You mustn't think this world might be fake."
"Then...?"
Wu Orin raised a finger.
"The correct answer is to think: 'This world is fake.'"
"Fake...?"
Wu Orin, amused by Gando's reaction, sat on the sofa and crossed her legs.
"Linking to High Gear lets me admire technology far beyond our world's, but not all
of it was created by coders. They borrow countless modules drifting in the Dream Sea."
Such technologies couldn't be applied in Wu Orin's world.
"So while it might seem crude, I don't notice the difference. Because of the brain
in the vessel."
The brain only perceives what it accepts as real.
"But High Gear has an interesting system. Before modifying your body, you can
preview the changes on screen."
Wu Orin tapped her head.
"It's called an AI simulation. A clone with all my info appears and shows how I'd
react in various situations."
Gando just listened.
"It's so precise, I can't tell if it's the screen's me or the real me. The problem
is this: if I hand control to the screen's me and order it to create a new AI simulation, what
happens?"
Wu Orin spread her arms wide.
"Virtual worlds are created endlessly. The beings in those worlds would live without
knowing they're just programs. With billions of worlds already made..."
Wu Orin asked:
"What's the probability that this place is the real world—the origin of
everything?"
Gando couldn't answer.
"Almost zero. It's more rational to think we're just a midpoint in an endlessly
nested system. 'Might be fake'? No. This world is 99% likely to be fake."
Seeing Gando's stunned face, Wu Orin chuckled.
"Of course, there's a tiny chance this is real. Believing that is fine too. But I
propose a different approach."
"What is it?"
Wu Orin smirked and pointed at the bed with her thumb.
"Stop overthinking and just join me in High Gear."
