Chapter 1092:
What is God? (2)
Shirone gazed at the eye that seemed to be overlooking the world of High
Gear.
Though it lacked emotion, the intent to see was clear.
'Why an eye?'
When life first emerged, the ways to sense this world must have been
nearly infinite.
'Why choose to see?'
To see.
Even a blind human, his eyes clearly see the abyss.
In that sense of "seeing."
'Seeing light. Seeing the world. Seeing the principles and systems
within it. Seeing the heart within me.'
If they hadn't chosen to see, their thought system would have changed,
and with changed thoughts, their hearts would change too.
'In other words, an entirely different universe would be born.'
Returning to the question.
'Why an eye?'
Shirone could guess the identity of the eye beyond the veil of
signals.
'That is will.'
The All-Seeing Eye.
The only will of the outside world to see this world, projected in the
form of an eye.
'Therefore, it is inevitable that living beings see.' To see
what?
'God.'
So where is God?
'Ah.'
Shirone realized.
'The question itself was wrong. From the moment life, humans, first
opened their eyes, they were seeing God.'
Even with God right before them, they searched for God's existence as if
they were blind.
'We opened our eyes to see God.'
In the moment of entering the torn veil, Shirone reached the final
question.
Ultimately.
'What is God?'
Time.
Pervading the entire universe, inescapable by anyone, independent in all
dimensions.
'Therefore, it is an eye.'
Living beings wanted to see time.
'A sensory organ that receives signals of light. To directly confirm the
grand rhythm of this world…'
That will is the beginning and end of intelligence.
'Yet humans search for God. Why do they think they can't see it?'
Because it is too vast.
'The size of time is the size of space.' Angels can confirm information
from very distant places through "overlooking."
It's called time dilation.
If someone could see a planet 10 billion light-years away with their
eyes…
'Would time almost stop?'
If someone could obtain a view that encompasses the size of the universe
in one glance…
'Would God be there?' At that point.
'We would have seen God with our eyes, thus God exists, and we finally
know about God.'
Would we be able to say that?
The thought ended there.
'Nane.'
Gathering his thoughts that had passed like a flash, Shirone exited High
Gear.
'I see what you see.'
The veil closed.
"What, what?"
As Yahweh suddenly disappeared, users watching from the ground
murmured.
"Where did he go? Did he jump through the code?"
The Operator received a message.
The user's code name has been erased. It has completely
disappeared from the database.
It would take time to understand how such a thing was possible, but one
thing was clear.
"Yahweh."
Everyone in this world hoped he would return.
An undefined space.
Close to nothingness, it was known by seeing one's reflection in the
darkness without light.
'Gagiyo…'
Shirone spoke.
"Gorakesekatba?" (Author's note: For readability, the method
of expressing reversed thoughts in text will not be used except in special cases.)
Currently, his thoughts were flowing backward, but he didn't feel it was
strange.
"This is me."
The reflection in the darkness was not the machine of High Gear but the
real Shirone.
"No, that can't be known either."
The form of the real Shirone was just a shadow projected in the
idea.
'How far must I go?'
He walked aimlessly.
He felt like he was walking, but whether he was actually moving through
space was questionable.
"What is this?"
He stopped walking.
'If there is no space, there is no time. Then what is this place made
of…'
His consciousness was faint.
"Huh?"
Shirone looked around.
"Nengiyo?"
His thoughts reversed again, based on the outside world, but there was
still no sense of incongruity.
"It's the home of iron?"
The memory of first entering High Gear and catching Metal Rat was vivid
in his mind.
"Level 3."
At that time, he turned a right angle into a pile of scrap metal and
heard the sound of metal scraping.
This was also familiar.
"Could it be?"
Running there, he saw a massive machine swarming with Metal Rats.
'Yes, that's right. I was trying to hunt that…'
As Shirone held the grenade in his hand, a familiar face appeared from
behind the metallic terrain.
"Hey! Are you insane?"
There was no need for AI confirmation; Shirone immediately recognized
Destruction Machine707's gear.
'What's going on here?'
As the metal debris scattered, Destruction Machine707 let out a yell.
Along with him, two more familiar users emerged.
'Death Princess. Supreme Codename.'
Just as Shirone stared at them blankly, Supreme Codename approached,
aiming a rifle at him.
Shirone vividly remembered.
'Back then...'
The two spoke simultaneously."Are you picking a fight with
us?"
In an indefinable space, Shirone suddenly found himself back in the
outer world, lost in thought, his expression grave.
'Something doesn't make sense.'
The reality around him inverted again.
'There's no space, so how can there be movement? What did I just
experience? Was it a dream?'
No, it wasn't.
'It wasn't a dream. It was something closer to a void—born from
nothing...'
A kind of illusion.
'What kind of illusion was I experiencing?'
His thoughts wavered.
"...Backtracking. To understand..."
Before he could process further, the door to a familiar gear opened. It
was Aegis, and inside sat Yolga's son on a bed.
'Fermi?'
The name lingered in his memory, but Shirone, maintaining his composure,
entered the room.
"It really is Yolga's son?"
Shirone overheard Destruction Machine707's astonished exclamation as
Fermi gestured for him to sit.
"Take a seat. Though... you've brought company?"
"I need to talk to you," Shirone said coldly.
The sharp tone startled Destruction Machine707's group, who exchanged
nervous glances.
"Why so serious?"
"I've already experienced this," Shirone replied.
"Experienced what?"
"This situation. This moment. Every word you're about to say to me—I've
already heard them all."
If it were anyone else, they would've dismissed him as insane. But
Fermi's expression turned icy.
"Sit. Everyone else, leave the room."
The fact that Shirone hadn't even introduced the group emphasized the
gravity of the situation. Once the door closed, Fermi asked,"Time reversal?"
"No, it's not that. Honestly, I'm not sure. Maybe it's just another
illusion."
Shirone recounted everything.
"The Solar War started. And then..."
When Shirone finished his confusing tale, Fermi rested his chin on his
hand.
"I don't understand," he admitted.
Perhaps that was the correct answer. Fermi's insight, though sharp,
reached its limit here.
"You left the outer world, and yet, you're standing in front of me while
still being part of it. It's as if... you exist in both."
"Something like that."
"What if you killed me?" Fermi leaned forward.
"Would killing me change anything? Could you still encounter me under
these circumstances? Even if I ceased to exist in reality, could you meet me here?"
"Yes, I could."
"How can you be so sure?"
"Because I already have. Like I said, I've heard everything you're
saying before. This is the third time."
With no space, time didn't exist either.
"I've cleared the Solar War multiple times. I've killed, been killed,
watched Dr. Martin counterattack or not... I've eliminated countless variables."
Fermi leaned back slowly, silent for ten minutes.
"Multiverse," he concluded.
"From the perspective of the outer world, infinite universes exist
inside. Every time your mind moves, a new universe is born."
Shirone hung his head, looking defeated.
"Yeah."
He had tried everything possible. There was nothing left to
attempt.
"Why did you come to me?" Fermi asked.
"You've heard everything I can say, haven't you? Why did you seek me
out?"
"Why?" Shirone chuckled bitterly.
"Ha."
This was how it was.
"That's the difference between the inside and the outside, Fermi. You
understand my words, but you can't grasp their essence."
Shirone clenched his fists.
"What am I supposed to do in this situation? Should I just pick a random
universe and live there? What about the original world? What about everyone waiting for me
there?"
That world had Fermi too.
"Do you think I can return to it? You're wrong. Even if everything is
the same, that place isn't the world I gave my heart to. It's just a copy."
"Still, you could..."
"No, you don't understand. You're too busy applying this information to
reality. That's why you're trapped."
"Calm down," Fermi urged.
"It's fine. I don't need to be calm," Shirone snapped. "Do you know what
all this is? No matter how rationally I think! No matter what I say to you!"
In this indefinable space...
"...it all ends like this."
It was all just an illusion.
In a place where every event became a delusion, what could possibly be
achieved?
'Where is my heart?'
Was the outer world a place of infinite freedom, or was it a prison
within that infinity?
'I want to go back.'
Seated, Shirone leaned against something that felt like a wall, as
though the void was pushing back against him.
He had experienced countless illusions.
'Nane.'
If High Gear was like this, how must Nane have felt after leaving the
photon system in reality?
'It must've hurt so much.'
Perhaps Nane had shed tears of blood, screaming in anguish.
'We'll never meet again. No matter what illusion you have of Shirone,
that Shirone isn't me.'
Returning was impossible.
Even if he escaped to reality from one of High Gear's universes, even if
he fought there...
'Could I ever give my heart to that place again?'
Could he abandon the Amy in his heart and love a new Amy? What about
Ikael? What about Goffin?
Goffin?
"That's it! Goffin!"
Shirone leapt to his feet.
"He left the photon system! But how did he send Hexa? Did he just fire
it at any random world?"
If Goffin's conviction had been so shallow, he wouldn't have sent Hexa
in the first place.
'I can return!'
Racing through the indefinable space, Shirone muttered endlessly to
himself.
"I need to find a way. There has to be something. If I retrace my steps,
I'll figure it out. I need to flip my heart... what does that even mean?"
He repeated it over and over.
"Reverse the illusion. What was I thinking at that moment...?"
Even as he sobbed, Shirone pressed on, until suddenly, he froze.
"Wait."
A subtle unease he had never noticed before.
"I... right now..."
The insight of Yahweh, on par with the Buddha, flashed through his
mind.
"What is this strange realization?"
