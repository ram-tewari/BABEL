Chapter 716:
Imperial City Aganos.
Uorin hurriedly returned to her room, slammed the door shut, and leaned
on the dressing table.
"Ha! Ha!"
Her heart was still racing.
'Why is my heart…?'
It was the first time in 800 years that a Teraje's heart rate exceeded
normal levels.
"No! No!"
It was an empire built over thousands of years and the finest body
created from the genes of thousands.
What about her mind?
Her mind, which transcended humanity, controlled all emotions in this
world, and no one could touch her emotions.
"I won't give it. No one can have my heart!"
Gando entered the room.
"Your Majesty! What on earth is happening?"
It was the first time Uorin had shown such distress, and Gando was
almost terrified.
"We must end this now. If we miss this moment, we will lose the
means to subdue Shirone."
"……"
Uorin looked into the mirror on the dressing table.
'Will he like it?'
The thought of Shirone judging her appearance made her dizzy and her
heart flutter.
"Your Majesty."
Uorin suddenly turned around.
"Gando, am I ugly?"
"What?"
Gando frowned, puzzled by Uorin's flushed expression.
"Am I pretty? How pretty am I?"
'What on earth is happening?'
Uorin had gathered all the superior genes in the world to achieve the
most beautiful appearance.
'Absolute confidence. Perfect human.'
It was unbelievable that such a Teraje would doubt her appearance and
even ask someone else.
'This is dangerous. No, it's not dangerous. She is still powerful and
perfect.'
A Teraje is a Teraje.
Gando did not know what consequences a tiny crack in the glorious empire
might bring.
Shirone followed Panika into the temple.
In a circular hall resembling a small chapel of the Yor Church stood a
statue presumed to be the god of Codename.
"Ikael?"
With exquisite features, wings of light spread wide, and a halo above
its head, it was clearly an angel statue.
'Or am I mistaken?'
Most angels were beautiful, and the statue before him lacked detailed
expression, making it hard to be sure.
'Why does it feel so familiar?'
It felt as if he had touched and polished this statue every day.
Codename 1, Drine, stood beside Shirone and looked up at the
statue.
"Our goddess Ataraxia."
Shirone's head snapped around.
"Ataraxia?"
Suddenly, he remembered the statue that the tomb raider Arius had tried
to steal by infiltrating the maternal psyche.
It was the original concept of Ataraxia that Ikael had placed directly
into Shirone's mind through possession.
'But why is it here?'
Unaware of Shirone's confusion, Drine continued to explain.
"Codename is a city governed by the goddess Ataraxia. That's why
people from other cities don't have codes. Like you."
Drine, the most beloved by Ataraxia, smiled kindly and placed a hand on
Shirone's shoulder.
"Don't worry. As long as you're in this city, anyone can be
embraced by the goddess. With her grace, you can perform miracles in a human body. Extend your lifespan,
heal diseases, or gain divine knowledge."
Codename 9, Gale, scoffed.
"The biggest problem is that not everyone can receive it. Pray hard
to the goddess. You need to be at least in the 200,000s to live like a human."
Shirone retorted, glaring at Gale.
"It's not because their rank is low that they're unhappy. They're
unhappy because people like you torment them."
"Hahaha! You're getting talkative. Just admit you're scared.
Whatever code you get, you'll be below me."
Receiving the ninth highest code among over 420,000 people was certainly
difficult.
While the presence of Ikael gave hope, without knowing the criteria, it
was impossible to be sure of the rank.
"How do I receive a code?"
"It's simple. Kneel before the goddess and pray sincerely. We call
this 'Gwoncheong.'"
In Codename, where life changed based on the code received, Gwoncheong
was the most important ritual.
Panika whispered softly.
"Only rankers know this, but you can perform Gwoncheong multiple
times."
"What? So you get a different code each time?"
"Yes. That's why rankers gather at the temple. If a middle-class
person came here, they'd die."
Shirone understood why Gale had been hostile when he learned Shirone was
an uncountable.
"But rankers' ranks rarely change. The gaps aren't big. More often,
ranks drop."
If you were in the top 1,000 out of 420,000, there was no need to
gamble.
"But it's different for the middle class. They all gather here to
prevent a reversal of ranks."
Shirone wouldn't have been able to set foot here if not for Codename 97,
Panika.
Panika spoke cautiously.
"The standard way to perform Gwoncheong is to kneel and pray. But
there are rumors of special methods to get a high code. Some people strip naked, and some bow with each
step from the temple entrance."
Her voice lowered further.
"I fasted for ten days before performing Gwoncheong."
Without knowing the exact criteria for Gwoncheong, it seemed people
tried everything.
'No, this isn't about that.'
For them, Codename was the only world, but for Shirone, it was just one
of the 19,000 sealed by Goffin.
If the existing space-time had developed into a completely different
world, this place was from the era when Ikael lived long ago.
'Ikael…'
Shirone knew the terrifying history of Ikael, the archangel trusted by
Anke Ra, who had destroyed the human world.
'What was Goffin thinking when he sealed that era?'
As Shirone hesitated, Gale urged him.
"Hey, hurry up. Don't try to do the same as those born in Codename,
uncountable."
Gwoncheong's method might be superstition, but Gale, wanting Shirone to
get a low code, wouldn't wait forever.
"Gale! You know how important this is! Give him time to
prepare."
Panika sided with Shirone, but he didn't care about Gwoncheong.
"I'll start."
Shirone approached Ikael's statue and reached out his hand.
"Hey, that's…"
Gale stepped forward, as irreverent Gwoncheong was forbidden, but Drine
shook his head and stopped him.
"It's okay. The goddess will judge."
Thinking it would only harm Shirone to incur the goddess's wrath, Gale
didn't argue further.
Shirone looked up and spoke.
"Ikael, it's time to go back."
The artificial brain vibrated, translating Shirone's words, but to the
people of Codename, it sounded like a strange incantation.
"Hey, what nonsense is this? And Ikael? To the benevolent goddess
Ataraxia…"
As Gale couldn't hold back and stepped forward again, the statue
trembled, and the temple shook.
"What, what's happening!"
Rankers looked around anxiously at the unprecedented event in
Codename.
"The goddess…"
Drine, who had never lost confidence, stood stunned, mouth agape, as the
statue wept.
"Ugh!"
As a code began to be engraved on Shirone's forehead, rankers entered
the temple.
"What's happening?"
No one could answer, and Drine, Gale, and Panika stared intently at the
glowing code.
'Ikael! This is…!'
Feeling an uncontrollable amplification of his emotions, Shirone gritted
his teeth and looked up.
"Ahhh!"
The statue shattered, and a massive Ataraxia unfolded above Shirone's
head.
'It's expanding endlessly!'
Nothing passed through the magic circle, but the concept of
amplification alone shook the world.
'I see.'
As the code on his forehead became clear, the secret of Codename seeped
in at the speed of light.
'This world is…'
While Shirone was lost in thought, the rankers stared wide-eyed at the
code on his forehead.
"What is that?"
427,621
The rankers murmured as they confirmed Shirone's code.
"He's a bottom-tier citizen? Wait, isn't 427,621 the last
number?"
Drine, who had been anxious about the unprecedented Gwoncheong reaction,
sighed in relief.
'Of course. Was that the goddess's curse earlier?'
"Pfft…"
Gale burst into laughter.
"Hahaha! Last place! He's last place!"
Even the rankers couldn't help but laugh at the dramatic number.
"This is crazy. I've never seen such a number. Now I'll show you
what kind of person you are."
Panika blocked Gale.
"Stop it! He's already struggling!"
There was no need to torment someone sentenced to the most miserable
life in the city.
"No, it's not that. Bringing the last place to the temple is a
rebellion against rankers. I'll make an example."
Gale pushed Panika aside and gave Drine a look, who stepped back.
'This is punishment for surprising me.'
Mercy was only possible when maintaining the top rank.
"Now, where should I start breaking you?"
As Gale reached for Shirone's collar, the rankers suddenly looked around
in confusion.
"What's happening?"
The codes on their foreheads began to glow, and even Drine was no
exception.
'This is code conversion…'
Before he could finish the thought, he felt his power drain and a
massive headache hit.
"Ahhh! My head! My head!"
The rankers saw the new number on Drine's forehead and were
shocked.
427,622
"No way!"
The chain reaction that started with Drine reached Gale, changing his
code to 427,630.
"It hurts! My head hurts!"
Rankers with high codes fell to the ground, clutching their heads and
screaming.
"No! My power! How did I get this power!"
The codes of all 427,620 residents of the city were being pushed back
one place, starting from Shirone.
No one could use their powers, meaning everyone in Codename was now
equal.
As the screams that spread throughout the city subsided, sobbing could
be heard in the temple.
"No, it must be a dream."
Those who had been rankers just moments ago were now crying with the
numbers of the despised commoners engraved on their foreheads.
"Even if it's just a dream…"
As Shirone approached, Gale scooted back on his bottom.
"Eek!"
"This is not how it should be."
Ignoring the terrified Gale, Shirone looked at the spot where the statue
had been destroyed.
The original Ataraxia was gone, but another Ataraxia remained.
'This is Ikael's dream.'
The standard for Codename 847, isolated by Goffin, was the existence of
Ikael itself.
"And now…"
Looking at the people collapsed outside the temple, Shirone
spoke.
"I am the standard."
In Shirone's world, there was no such thing as power.
