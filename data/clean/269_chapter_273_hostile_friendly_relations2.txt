Hostile Friendly Relations(2)
Arius approached Shirone and asked for a handshake, saying,
"I must be going now. I can't be away from my post for long. Since
Shirone is also an Unlocker, being aware of these matters will help you in your role as a unique in the
magic society."
Shirone quickly grasped Arius's hand.
"Yes. It was incredibly helpful. Thank you."
"Haha! There's no need to thank me. From now on, I plan to dig into
your Ataraxia."
Arius flashed a cold smile, but Shirone couldn't see his expression. His
head had already drooped deeply, succumbing to sleep.
High-level sleep magic cuts off consciousness so cleanly that it leaves
no awareness of the mental waves being severed. Even if Shirone's consciousness revived and tried to
connect with the lost memory, he wouldn't feel anything unusual.
"Well then, shall we begin?"
Arius, a Scale Mage adept in handling the psyche realm, used a magic
called Dive to penetrate the human consciousness.
He can infiltrate anywhere in the 12 levels of the human psyche, except
for the 12th level shell which is the realm of intellect.
However, the inside of the human mind is a perilous environment. Diving
recklessly could cost a person their life, even if they had ten lives to spare.
Thus, most divers use a method of throwing a net from the surface to
extract only the desired information, and the keyword plays a crucial role in that.
Having suddenly implanted the keyword into Shirone, prompting him to
recall the impression of Ataraxia, Arius did some stretches before casting the Dive magic.
Even now, dozens of divers lose their lives each year in the human
psyche realm, but as a mage of Seven Prodigies, he cast the magic without hesitation.
"Dive. Ataraxia."
The Deviate-Type Spirit Zone shrank to an extreme scale and penetrated
Shirone's mind. At the same moment, Arius' body vanished from the waiting room.
He reappeared after about five minutes, kneeling, his clothes drenched
as though he had emerged from water.
Arius caught his breath and slowly stood up. He gazed at Shirone with a
troubled expression for a long moment before moving, but then a voice called from outside the
door.
"Sir Shirone, the trial will begin shortly. Please come and
attend."
Clicking his tongue, Arius used Flicker, a signature move of Scale
Mages, and disappeared from the waiting room.
"Huh?"
Shirone blinked and looked around.
Although he had been asleep for five full minutes, the clean cut of his
consciousness made it feel like those five minutes had never existed.
In the state of consciousness that had been severed and then sutured, he
could only think that Arius had suddenly disappeared while shaking hands.
'He really moves like a ghost. Well, Armin was like that too.'
When there was no response from Shirone even after some time, the
attendant opened the door and entered.
"Sir Shirone, you must come out now."
"Ah, sorry. I'll be right there."
Shirone followed the attendant into the courtroom.
The prominent nobles of Kazra sat in the jury seats, and two chairs were
placed side by side below the judge's seat.
Instead of evidence, various tools were laid out in the evidence storage
area, and an alchemist and a mage were inspecting them.
Shirone sat in the chair on the right and waited. From outside the
courtroom, a booming voice echoed, and the door swung open.
"His Majesty Orkamph is entering!"
As the nobles stood to greet him, Orkamph strode in with a dignified
gait.
He paid no attention to the other nobles and observed Shirone's
condition. Though Arius had already made contact, it was impossible to know whether he had successfully
replicated Ataraxia.
"Let's begin."
When Arius, seated next to Shirone, gave the signal, the judge initiated
the examination.
The mage used a syringe to draw blood from both individuals.
The moment the needle pierced the king's body, the atmosphere in the
courtroom turned chilly.
After Shirone's blood was also drawn, an alchemist mixed both samples
into a substance called Oxstamine. As he shook the beaker, the liquid changed from a deep purple to a
transparent water-like substance.
The alchemist explained.
"The blood mixed with oxytamine will be stored in a vacuum state
for 3 days. If a positive reaction occurs, it's a biological parent, and if a negative reaction occurs,
it's not a biological parent."
As the alchemist handed over the blood, the judge put it into an alchemy
box called a Caspi.
When the lid was closed, the air was expelled, creating a vacuum
state.
It was perfect for security-required inspections because it would break
if even a little air seeped in.
The judge held up the Caspi and announced,
"This Caspi will go into the vault and be placed in the inner
palace garden. The royal guard will monitor it 24 hours a day, and any noble with access to the inner
palace may check it at any time. However, crossing the restricted line is strictly forbidden. If a
separate observer is assigned, their identity must be registered within 1 hour."
Shirone nodded at the Inspection Department's decision.
If the vault was exposed to an unspecified number of people, they would
become each other's observers, preventing any mischief.
"This concludes the examination regarding the verification of the first
prince of the Kazra Kingdom."
The procedure ended with the judge striking the gavel.
Orkamph left the courtroom as if he had urgent business to attend to,
without even saying a word of appreciation to Shirone.
Shirone, who knew Orkamph's personality, no longer minded. Rather, he
was more concerned about the fact that he hadn't heard from Reina since last night.
He understood that, as a legal representative, she had to approve all
administrative work, leaving her incredibly busy. It was also the reason he could spend his time
leisurely like a nobleman.
Still, wasn't that why he had brought Amy along? It was strange that
there had been no message from her, no matter how far the lodging was.

"You failed?"
Orkamph's face distorted.
It was the first time he heard those words since hiring Arius. And they
were words that should never have been uttered.
The only reason he had endured the criticism and recruited a criminal
from the Black Line was because he believed in his skills. But he failed?
"I apologize. I succeeded in casting the net, but I couldn't
replicate it."
Divers refer to the process of using keywords to pull up concepts that
are sunken in the deep sea of the unconscious to the surface as "casting a net".
"What on earth is the reason? Didn't you say it was guaranteed to
succeed if you just planted the keyword?"
"Replication is possible even now. It's just that there's no need
to do so."
"No need?"
Arius recalled the situation at the time and made a bitter
expression.
"Ataraxia was encapsulated."
"Encapsulated? Is it a type of code?"
"No. Code is about rules. You write 100 characters on a 1-meter strip of
paper, wrap it around a rod of a specific thickness, and read the characters that align on one side. If
a sentence is revealed, it is a code. Now, what is encapsulation? You write a clear sentence on the
1-meter strip of paper and then crumple it up randomly. There's no pattern, and it cannot be unraveled.
What I saw wasn't Ataraxia, but rather a tangled cloud of information, like a ball of yarn."
Arius raised his index finger and continued.
"Then why is there no need to replicate it? Keywords make the other
person think of a certain concept. For example, this: Apples are delicious. Then, Your Majesty, an apple
comes to Your Majesty's mind. However, that is the apple that Your Majesty thinks of, not the perfect
apple. The perfect apple exists in the unconscious, beyond language. In the end, the thought revealed
through keywords is just a shadow. Usually that's enough, but when it's encapsulated like this time,
it's useless. Because even if I replicate the information cloud, it will remain just that—an information
cloud."
Orkamph couldn't blame Arius. He understood. And blaming Arius would
only expose his own ignorance.
"Then what do you plan to do now? We absolutely need
Ataraxia."
"First, I suggest speaking with Shirone. To find a solution, I need
to see Ataraxia directly—learn what kind of information system it is, and understand its activation
principle. Only after such information is collected can I provide an answer to Your Majesty. Of course,
I've installed a door so I can go back in at any time."
"A demonstration of Ataraxia..."
Orkamph did not like Arius' proposal.
Shirone was a clever child, so there was a high probability that he
would guess that he wasn't simply invited to the royal palace for a paternity test.
In that situation, if the original purpose was brought up, the damage
Shirone would receive would be tremendous.
If it was to protect the kingdom, he could bear the stigma of being a
despicable father as much as he wanted. But he wanted to avoid a situation where Shirone would refuse to
cooperate because of that.
"Isn't it too blatant? He'll definitely be
suspicious."
"Your Majesty, this is a major matter that will change the first prince.
The Terraje faction will surely make their move within the three days before the test results are out.
If we do not take advantage of having priority in negotiations with Shirone, the initiative will be
taken by the Terraje faction."
Orkamph fell into deep contemplation.
Should he fully embrace Shirone, or use him and discard him? Either way,
the sooner he made his decision, the better.
"If we're going to do this, we might as well make it grand,
right?"
Arius answered with a sly smile.

Shirone, who was heading to his parents' quarters, stopped in his tracks
on the 2nd floor corridor. An unknown attendant, a messenger of Zion, was blocking the way. It was
simply amazing how he could find him like a ghost in this vast royal palace.
"Sir Shirone, you must come with me."
Shirone straightened his back and lowered his chin.
It was clear he wasn't being called for a pleasant matter, but avoiding
it wouldn't help.
Rather than getting hit in the back of the head while running away, it
was more effective to meet directly and observe the reaction.
"Please lead the way."
"As expected, an excellent choice. Please, follow me."
The attendant took Shirone to the basement. It was a place where
warehouses were concentrated, so there was very little foot traffic, and perhaps because expansion
construction was frequent, the layout was complicated like a maze.
They arrived at the outermost part, where a door was installed into the
wall, leading even deeper inside.
It was not a room that had originally existed.
"This is the place. Please come in."
Shirone grabbed the doorknob without lowering his guard.
Contrary to his expectation of a room, what lay before him was a
rectangular cave lit by torches, stretching out with another door visible 15 meters ahead.
A double entrance.
It didn't seem like a structure chosen for fun, but no reasonable
explanation came to mind either.
When he was about halfway through the cave, the attendant slammed the
door shut with a thud. It was clearly to alert someone.
Startled, Shirone stopped in his tracks, but after taking a deep breath,
he opened the second door.
It was a completely white room.
Not painted, but made entirely of marble, and along the walls, shelves
were lined with various objects on display.
There didn't seem to be anything particularly luxurious, but knowing
Zion's nature, the room still gave off a sense of unease.
Zion and Uorin were drinking tea at the table. Seeing Uorin, he felt a
little relieved, but realizing that even such thoughts were dangerous, he erased them from his
mind.
"You're here. What are you doing standing there? Come
in."
Shirone hesitated to move. Zion and Uorin were looking at him with
playful eyes.
But he couldn't just stand at the door, so he cautiously took a step
forward, being mindful of his surroundings.
At that moment, the floor suddenly sank with a whoosh, and his body
toppled forward.
"Ugh! What the—!"
Shirone hurriedly raised his head. The floor was soft, so he didn't feel
any impact, but hearing Zion and Uorin's giggles made his face turn red with embarrassment.
'Why is the floor so soft?'
Crawling and groping around, he felt a solid floor at some
boundary.
"Are you okay, Oppa? I'm sorry for teasing you. But it was fun, wasn't
it?"
Uorin approached and held out his hand.
Grabbing her hand and standing up, Shirone looked back at the spot where
he had fallen. There was nothing distinguishing that part of the floor from the rest.
"What's this?"
"It's <Fluffy Everywhere>."
"Fluffy... what?"
