Chapter 21:
Learning Magic (4)
The Spirit Zone was a device that required not only countless magitechnological
advancements but also an enormous amount of financial backing to create.
However, if one could see the Spirit Zone with their own eyes, the achievement rate of
the lessons would skyrocket. It was no coincidence that the Alpheas Magic Academy was one of the kingdom's
top five prestigious schools.
***
Etra walked comfortably, unlike someone who had just entered the Spirit Zone, and
continued her explanation.
"Now, this is the basic form of the Spirit Zone. It uniformly dominates the entire space.
But in actual combat, except for special situations, there's rarely an opportunity to use the entire space.
Let me give you an example."
Etra snapped her fingers, and a target appeared 20 meters away.
"With a sphere 10 meters in diameter, you can't apply magical effects to that target.
You'd have to either move directly or expand the Spirit Zone. But if you change its shape, this method
becomes possible."
Etra's Zone elongated toward the target. The volume remained the same, but by altering
its form, she had captured the target.
"If you change the shape of your spirit, the Spirit Zone's form changes as well. Then,
like this……"
Etra extended her hand and spoke.
"Fire."
A burst of flame shot out and incinerated the target 20 meters away. The first-year
students' eyes widened. Etra, who they had underestimated due to her gentle appearance, suddenly seemed
awe-inspiring.
"But this method has a fatal flaw."
At Etra's follow-up words, the freshmen looked awkward. It was embarrassing to realize
that the demonstration they had just seen was actually a bad example.
"The Zone's form can be varied in many ways. But in the heat of battle, if you have to
calculate the shape every time, it would be highly inefficient. Therefore, magicians patternize several
forms. That is the four patterns of the Spirit Zone, known as the Four Directions Method."
Shirone's heart raced at this newly introduced concept.
"The first of the Four Directions Method is the Defensive Type."
Etra's Spirit Zone tightened, transforming into a polyhedron with 20 triangular faces. It
was a crude shape, but from a distance, it resembled a sphere.
"The foundation of the Defensive Type is the polyhedron. You establish a mental framework
as the skeleton. The more frames there are, the stronger the durability, and the more allies you can
protect. Next is the Offensive Type."
Etra changed the form of her Spirit Zone. The spherical surface area seemed to shrink,
and dozens of long spikes stretched out in all directions.
"The Offensive Type is called the Star Shape. It reduces the central area but extends
numerous Zones outward. It covers all directions, so there's no need to consider the target's position, and
it's also useful when facing multiple enemies. Next is the Target Type."
Etra's Zone transformed again. Extending east, west, south, and north from her center, it
formed a cross shape.
"The foundation of the Target Type is the cross. Among the basic forms of the Four
Directions Method, it has the longest range. While its firepower is weaker than the Offensive Type, it can
easily capture targets. You rotate the Zone around the magician and lock onto the target."
She snapped her fingers, and targets began moving around her. Etra followed them with
sharp eyes.
"Flank, 45 degrees. Fire."
The rotating cross shot flames each time it caught a target. Magic was cast
simultaneously in all directions, as if she had eyes in the back of her head, with no margin for error.
"What you must master in the Target Type is the rotation speed of the Spirit Zone.
There's an extreme form called the Linear Type, which has the maximum range, but unless you're a
sniper-style magician, it's not used. Because the magician is positioned at the end of the linear form,
changing direction is extremely difficult."
After demonstrating three forms, Etra briefly wiped her glasses.
"Lastly, the Separation Type involves moving the Zone's center. In other words, you could
say you're moving your brain."
Shirone didn't understand her words. The Zone's center was the magician's head. How could
that be moved?
Etra returned to the basic form.
A moment later, the Spirit Zone began moving while maintaining its spherical shape.
After 10 seconds, the Spirit Zone completely detached from Etra's body.
Shirone was astonished. If the Zone was the magician's spirit, what on earth was at its
center now?
"This is the Separation Type. Among the Four Directions Method, it's a special form. If
the Spirit Zone is unity with the mind, the Separation Type is unity with the object. The farther the
separation distance, the smaller the Zone becomes, and it's so difficult to master that there are
specialized magicians for it. But among the Four Directions Method, it's the fastest to move and can be
activated instantly."
Finally, Etra demonstrated the Four Directions Method in sequence. The Zone solidified
into a polyhedron, turned into a star shape, elongated into a cross, returned to a sphere, and then detached
from her body, swirling rapidly around her.
"Whoa……"
The Class Seven students finally realized it. Mastering the continuous transformation of
the Four Directions Method wasn't something achievable with just a year or two of training. The teacher
before them wasn't some clueless novice but an officially recognized 6th-rank magician.
"From now on, you will train in this Four Directions Method. Repeatedly practicing the
four forms. Now, let's begin class-by-class exercises."
The students received differentiated lessons based on difficulty. Shirone also attended
Etra's class with the Class Seven students.
"First, let's learn about the Defensive Type. Magicians may specialize in certain areas
based on their inclinations, but the Defensive Type is something everyone should polish consistently. It
allows you to maintain focus even in chaotic battles and, most importantly, grants resistance to
anti-magic."
Shirone recalled the caution he had heard the day before. The staff hadn't answered, but
the question still lingered.
"Teacher, I have a question. Ah! Um, is it okay to ask?"
"Hoho, feel free to ask anything. Surely your question isn't whether you can ask a
question, right?"
"Yes. Why is anti-magic a forbidden magic? Many magicians are legally researching
anti-magic, aren't they?"
"Hmm, let's see. There are two types of anti-magic. Magicians' anti-magic and artifacts.
Do you know this?"
"Yes, I know."
"Anti-magic isn't so much magic as it is a Spirit Zone with special properties. That's
why its efficiency varies depending on the magician's spirit. That's the difference from artifacts."
"So, approaching it clumsily could be dangerous."
"Exactly. If you cast it carelessly, accidents can happen. Magicians who major in
anti-magic train in it for their entire lives. Most of them have ruthless personalities. The school doesn't
want to encourage such methods for students like you."
Shirone nodded. Anti-magic, which destroyed each other's spirits, wasn't a subject to be
researched within the school.
"Alright! Let's return to practicing the Four Directions Method."
The Class Seven students began their individual training. Shirone also practiced the
Defensive Type. The trick was to first establish a mental skeleton and then connect it.
After practicing for a while, Shirone felt his Spirit Zone solidify and opened his eyes
wide. Though it wasn't an Image Zone and couldn't be visually confirmed, he had clearly achieved something.
His natural inclination being the Convergent Type helped, but even accounting for that,
it was an impressive result. It was the reward for six years of dedicated training in the Spirit Zone.
"Whoa! Look at that!"
At that moment, his classmates pointed at the Image Zone and shouted. The Class Four
students were taking turns training, and now Amy was putting on a dazzling show.
Amy was using the Target Type. Her cross rotated 90 degrees to the right, then 270
degrees to the left, twisting at unpredictable angles while shooting flames.
The Zone's length was about 20 meters, a modest level for the Target Type, but its
rotational speed was unimaginable.
Even as targets appeared from all directions, the number of flames shooting from the four
directions was at the level of rapid fire.
After instantly annihilating 120 targets, Amy retracted her Zone. With her beautiful
appearance and exceptional skills, the underclassmen looked up at her with sparkling eyes.
"Is that senior Carmis Amy?"
"First-class, stunning looks, and on top of that, her skills. If I could date a senior
like that, my school life would be perfect."
"Give it up. Over 100 guys have tried to hit on her, and she hasn't given any of them the
time of day."
"No surprise. The Carmis family is renowned even in the kingdom."
Hearing the Class Seven students' chatter, Shirone realized something anew. Why had a
girl from such a prestigious family been hanging out with delinquents six years ago?
As Shirone pondered this, his shoulder twitched. Amy, descending from the Image Zone, was
staring straight at him. Naturally, the other students also turned their gazes toward Shirone.
"What? Do they know each other?"
"No way? He just enrolled yesterday."
"Could it be she's into him? She's the type who won't even make eye contact with guys."
Hearing the whispers, Amy quickly averted her gaze. She had made a careless mistake. But
honestly, she was so bothered she could scream.
To Amy, Shirone was the only one who knew about her past foolish behavior. Of course, she
hadn't exactly matured now, but since she began studying magic, she had left all her past behind.
'This won't do. I'm about to enter my graduation year. If rumors spread here, everything
could go up in smoke. What do I do now?'
Amy returned to her seat, but no one dared to use the Image Zone. She was undisputedly
the top-ranked student in the advanced class, and anyone who stepped forward would only humiliate themselves
in front of the underclassmen.
Shirone saw this as an opportunity and approached Etra.
"Teacher, if it's okay, may I give it a try?"
"Huh? In the Image Zone?"
Etra tilted her head. Why would a student who had just started learning the principles of
the Four Directions Method want to use the Image Zone?
"Wait a moment… The rule is that upperclassmen go first."
Etra waited briefly. But still, no one stepped forward to use the Image Zone. Deciding it
was better to let him gain experience than let the expensive device sit idle, she agreed.
"Alright. It's good to try things in advance. But don't summon targets. You could get
hurt."
"Yes. I thought it would help to visually see the Defensive Type's image."
"Huh?"
Etra asked as if she didn't understand, but Shirone didn't hear her and left his seat.
As he stepped onto the Image Zone, the upperclassmen whistled and clapped. It was less
encouragement and more like they were expecting a spectacle, but Shirone ignored them and walked to the
center.
Amy, who was drinking water, turned her gaze at the commotion. The moment she spotted
Shirone, she spat out the water she was holding.
"Pfft!"
"Amy, are you okay? What's wrong?"
"What the hell is he doing? A Class Seven student trying to show off there?"
The unexpected incident turned the class into something resembling a break. But Shirone
was seriously focused.
As he entered the Spirit Zone, the sphere rapidly expanded. The moment he saw it
visually, he felt suffocated. Overwhelmed by the image, Shirone unconsciously stopped the expansion.
The noisy students fell silent. The Zone's diameter was a full 20 meters. Considering
that Class Seven's average diameter was 13 meters, this was the arrival of a prodigious freshman.
"Wow! He's got size."
"Hmph. Just because it's big doesn't mean it's magic. It's just an air bubble."
Shirone was flustered. The frame he had successfully created before entering the Image
Zone was now bending like taffy.
'Why isn't it working?'
Had seeing the Spirit Zone visually disrupted his focus? That was certainly possible.
But Shirone didn't consider himself that weak. There must be a fundamental issue.
'Ah! I see!'
In his current state, attempting the Four Directions Method would inevitably cause the
Spirit Zone to collapse. As a beginner, Shirone needed far more mental energy for shape changes than an
expert.
It was like trying to build a structure without enough materials. A diameter of 20 meters
couldn't support a frame as solid as steel.
'I need to maximize it.'
