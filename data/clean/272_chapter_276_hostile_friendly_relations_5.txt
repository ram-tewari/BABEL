Chapter 276:
Hostile Friendly Relations (5)
"Equivalent exchange of luck and misfortune?"
"No. It's not a problem with the Objet, but with the human being. The
first owner of Daily Gift was a shoemaker who lived in the lower class. He probably made a lot of money.
But he couldn't overcome his obsession. On the days when he received a modest gift, he became extremely
depressed. Such days were 75 out of 100. Eventually, he developed a mental illness, spent all day
spinning the roulette, and ended up losing his mind."
It was a phenomenon that happens when humans become obsessed with
probabilities.
In reality, people who sink to rock bottom due to gambling don't do it
to make money. What they seek is the thrill of winning against the odds, and money was merely a tool to
achieve that.
"As time passed, Daily Gift ended up in the hands of a noble. Obviously,
not someone who would be staring at the roulette out of desperation for money. But one day, they hit
miracle gift. And then..."
"And?"
Uorin pouted her lips as she imagined the situation at that time.
"The family was ruined."
"Ruined? Why?"
"When you hit a miracle, it gives you an Objet. The Objet that the
noble received is a small stone called Nemesis. When someone comes into contact with it, the person's
imagination becomes reality within a 20-meter radius. The nobles who acquired Nemesis became lost in
their imagination every day. You know how it is, Oppa. The thing about human desires..."
Uorin waved her index finger and clicked her tongue, perhaps embarrassed
to say it out loud.
Shirone understood right away. And he didn't necessarily think it was
all bad.
After all, every human lives with desires, and through imagination, they
find balance with reality.
The problem arises when that imagination turns into reality.
"Anyway, that's how they were ruined. And Nemesis left to find a
new owner. Even now, it's very popular among enthusiasts. But it seems no one can hold onto it for long.
It's been known to return to auction houses within a month. Of course, it's true that Objet are
dangerous. But that's a human problem, not the Objet. In fact, most owners of Daily Gift led comfortable
lives."
If that's the case, Shirone had nothing to say. But he felt even
stranger.
If it's not particularly dangerous, then what exactly are these Objets?
Why do such things even exist in the world?
Shirone glanced at Daily Gift.
The idea that one Objet could generate another was like a goose that
lays golden eggs for collectors.
But on the other hand, he thought it was terribly destructive. If there
was no limit to Objet production, the world would eventually be destroyed, if not immediately.
When he shared his thoughts, Uorin also agreed to a certain
extent.
"That's a possibility too. Maybe not in our generation, but still.
I think we'll be fine. Even though Objets are special, they aren't necessarily more durable than other
items. They eventually wear down and break over time. The most dangerous Objet that's come from a
miracle so far is Nemesis. The Curia Auction classifies Nemesis as Grade A. Daily Gift is also Grade A.
It seems like it can't offer anything beyond that level of a gift."
Curia was a world-class auction house, and he had heard about it from
Nade before.
But what caught Shirone's attention wasn't that.
Daily Gift cannot give a gift higher than its assigned grade. While it
sounded plausible at first, logically, it didn't make sense.
"How is that possible? The grade is decided by the auction house, but
there's no way the Objet knows those criteria. Couldn't it be a coincidence? Even after 240 years, the
frequency of hitting a miracle is low. If you consider the probability of a Grade A item coming out on
top of that, there's not enough data to compile statistics."
"That may be true, but…."
Uorin didn't fully agree nor disagree, leaving her words vague. While
Shirone's reasoning was logical, once you've owned more than 40 Objets, you naturally come across
knowledge that the average person wouldn't have access to.
One of them was a VIP membership card issued by the Curia Auction House.
On the back of the card, there were precautions written, and the first clause had this phrase:
<Document> Objets do not guarantee 100% safety to users.
Members would often ask their curators to explain this first clause. The
curators would share various cases that speculated on the origin of Objets, and from those stories, you
could tell that the warning in the first clause wasn't just an intimidating phrase.
But Uorin was tight-lipped about this issue.
Becoming a Curia VIP member wasn't just about having money. You had to
possess a sense of camaraderie regarding Objets.
Abusing privileges to non-VIP members was an act of lowering the value
of Objets yourself.
Yet, Uorin's pride as a Terraje wouldn't let her stop the game, so she
gave Shirone the chance to realize it on his own.
"Come this way. I'll show you other Objets."
Uorin guided Shirone to the shelves. The first thing she introduced was
a magnifying glass that could be found anywhere.
"This is an Objet called Just Once!. It shows a person's bare
skin."
"You mean it lets you see through clothes?"
"Yes, but it only works within a distance of 5 centimeters."
With a playful "Hey!" Uorin aimed the magnifying glass at
Shirone's lower body.
Despite having heard the Guideline, Shirone instinctively crossed his
legs. Then, Uorin stuck her tongue out and handed him the magnifying glass.
"Hehe, you try it, Oppa."
Shirone shone the magnifying glass on his arm.
At first, all he could see was the fabric of his sleeve enlarged, but as
he brought it closer, his bare skin came into view.
"That's fascinating. But if this is how it works, you might as well just
look openly."
"Exactly! If you tried to peek at a woman, you'd get caught right away.
That's why it's probably called Just Once!."
"Haha! That could be. But depending on how it's used, it could be
useful. It seems like it could see through armor too, so it could be used for inspections."
Shirone returned Just Once!.
"How does it work? Seeing through things like that."
"I haven't fully checked it yet. The ability of an Objet doesn't
necessarily depend on its function, so it could be the ability of the handle or frame. Honestly, it's
not a high-grade Objet. I got it randomly, just to round out the collection."
Shirone thought so too. Compared to Daily Gift, this one seemed a bit
lower in grade.
The next Objet Uorin introduced was a bookshelf. It was densely packed
with works by renowned scholars.
"This is Zion Oppa's. It's an Objet called Philosopher. If you put a
book on this shelf, the contents enter your head without having to read it. You can store up to 124
books in Kazra print. But keep in mind, once you remove a book from the shelf, the knowledge vanishes
from your memory too."
Shirone was dumbfounded. The idea that just placing books on a shelf
could implant their knowledge in your head was a whole new level of shock, different from Daily
Gift.
"How can a bookshelf connect to a person's head?"
"Hmm, that's surprisingly simple. Some Objets have a tendency to be
bound to the owner. At auctions, this is called Contract Type. In short, Philosopher is a Contract Type
Objet that is currently bound to my Zion Oppa. This is Grade B according to Curia Auction House
standards."
Shirone looked at the Philosopher in rapture.
He didn't know about the others, but he really wanted this Philosopher
at least. He felt like he understood a little why collectors became obsessed with Objets.
Uorin also introduced several other objects, including a headband called
Beauty that, when worn, causes the wearer to exercise for an hour while losing consciousness.
The Guideline said that you can lose weight effortlessly, but since you
often make unsightly movements, you should lock the door if possible.
There was also an Objet called Soulless Massage. It consisted of a pair
of stones, one red and one blue. When held in both hands, they would target any aching area, contracting
and relaxing the muscles to relieve pain.
Shirone tried it himself and found it incredibly refreshing. When the
arms, legs, shoulders, ankles, and soles of the feet were all massaged simultaneously, it felt so good
he almost lost consciousness. Although he couldn't feel human warmth, the effect was top-notch.
'So that's why it's called Soulless Massage? This is probably around
Grade D.'
Shirone's eyes lit up as he gained realization.
The names of the Objets he had encountered so far. Moreover, the grades
that even an outsider like himself could judge. These two facts revealed the secret of Objets.
'I see. Objets are...'
Daily Gift does not give Objets above Grade A. However, in order for the
Guideline to be established, one of two hypotheses must be followed.
Either the Objet knows the auction company's criteria, or the Objet
itself has an intrinsic value of its own.
The answer was the latter.
Objets are born with inherent value. Because their functions are
invariably tied to human desires.
The fact that they had such clear purposes implied they weren't
naturally occurring objects—they were artificial creations, born from the mind of an intelligent
being.
The desire to become more beautiful, the desire for knowledge, the
desire to receive gifts every day, the desire to make one's imagination come true.
In that sense, Nemesis, which fulfills the highest level of desire,
deserved a Grade A rating no matter what anyone said.
'Then what is Grade S?'
If you consider the fact that Daily Gift cannot give anything higher
than Grade A, it implies that there must be something classified as Grade S.
Shirone thought it was quite likely. Humans have not only animalistic
desires but also higher-level aspirations.
If Nemesis, the pinnacle of animalistic desires, was Grade A, then Grade
S was likely to be an Objet containing rational aspirations.
'Huhu. Looks like you've got a bit of a sense for it.'
As Shirone remained lost in thought for a long time, Uorin had a
satisfied smile. This was the joy of collecting Objets.
On the other hand, Zion was burning up inside.
It was the first time Uorin had kept someone other than a Curia Auction
House VIP member in the gallery for so long.
'That bastard dares...'
To Zion, Uorin was more than just a little sister. She was the most
powerful weapon he possessed, even more than the royal blood that flowed through his veins.
'Why? What's so special about that bastard child that she's fussing over
him?'
Unaware of Zion's seething emotions, Shirone quickly looked at the other
items.
Looking back now, it was truly an enormous amount of wealth. Most of the
Objets lined up along the shelves were Grade C or B, but there were also some Grade A objects that
caught his eye.
This was a level of luxury even a king couldn't afford. It was a
testament to how immense Uorin's power was as a direct descendant of Terraje Family.
Shirone, who had examined all the shelves on the east side, turned his
gaze to the north. However, the space there was barren, with only a single sword on display.
"Huh? Uorin, what's that sword?"
"It's Demon Sword Armand. It's the Objet that Zion Oppa treasures the
most."
It was the most treasured item for Zion, who even considered gold
trivial. For someone like him, it wasn't particularly flashy.
It wasn't adorned with jewels or covered in elaborate ornamentation, and
it didn't even have a scabbard. Yet, there was something subtly captivating about it. It had a unique
design that was almost imperceptible to the average person.
But even that assessment vanished the moment he saw the jewel embedded
in the center of the hilt. An overwhelmingly imposing presence was conveyed to the point that he
couldn't look away.
As if Armand were calling him, his feet moved on their own. When he came
to his senses, he had already arrived at the north wall.
Just as he was about to reach out and grasp the hilt, Zion
shouted.
"That's mine! Don't touch it!"
Even after Shirone stepped back in surprise, Zion's distorted expression
showed no sign of relaxing.
Demon Sword Armand was a Contract Type Objet that belongs to its owner.
However, its presence is so strong that it sometimes reaches out to another people.
The exact Guideline for selecting the owners has not yet been revealed,
but the very act of seducing someone else in the presence of the owner was unpleasant.
Shirone came to his senses and asked Zion.
"Ah, sorry. I didn't mean to. But this sword, it's Grade S,
right?"
"What does it matter to you? You can't even afford Grade F, so stay away
from my sword. Who do you think you are, touching a royal treasure? You're just a bastard."
Shirone turned around with a frown. No matter how much of a Grade S
Objet Demon Sword Armand was, he didn't lack so much pride that he would remain interested after hearing
such words.
Watching him, Zion changed his mind. Wasn't this a good thing? It was a
good opportunity to show the gap between royalty and a commoner.
