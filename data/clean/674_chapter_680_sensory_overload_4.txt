Chapter 680:
Sensory Overload (4)
Shagal had only one dagger left, but his attacks on Etela came in relentless waves.
Etela, protecting her vital organs while maintaining a defensive stance, had dozens of
cuts on her limbs. Her superhumanly trained muscles prevented even a single drop of blood from spilling, but
even that had reached its limit.
As her Schema's functionality rapidly declined and her consciousness faded, Shagal
delivered a frustrated kick. Instead of the sharp, piercing strikes that had been carving into her flesh, he
shoved her entire body, sending her sprawling backward.
"Why?"
Though Shagal was among the top hundred most brutal individuals in the world, he wasn't a
born psychopath, and his frustration simmered.
"Why aren't you fighting back?"
The Grass Leaf Circus Troupe had never existed in this world.
Even though his senses held vivid memories of it, the reason he couldn't scream that it
wasn't real was perhaps because, deep down, he acknowledged the possibility.
'Only the scent remains clear.'
He possessed a superhuman sense of smell beyond ordinary humans, but the fragrance of the
past didn't provide visual information.
Only the certainty of remembering existed—when he tried to delve deeper, even Tia's face
refused to surface.
"Archbishop Raphael didn't kill you."
Shagal, snapping out of his thoughts, glared at the fallen Etela.
"If that was my master's final wish, I will honor it."
"Final wish? He didn't kill me?"
Shagal strode forward and pressed his dagger against Etela's throat.
"Do you really think saying that will somehow elevate your master's honor? I killed
him. Why would the world's greatest assassin spare me?"
Etela recalled Archbishop Raphael's teachings.
"Having the power to destroy evil doesn't make you righteous. Isn't that true for
you as well?"
Unable to move her limbs, Etela could only turn her head to meet Shagal's gaze.
"Sacrifice. The courage to burn oneself for righteousness and even embrace evil—that
is the true value of righteousness, as my master taught."
"Shut your mouth."
There had been a time when he forgave.
He believed someone else might have forgiven him too.
But the one who always committed wrongs was evil.
Eliminating all possibility of evil was the only way to purify the world.
"Can you tell me what your master said right before he died?"
"I don't remember."
Shagal shook his head as if his neck had stiffened.
"It doesn't matter. I realized the moment I saw you."
"What? What could you possibly know about me?"
The urge to kill her as soon as possible surged within him.
"If you kill all the evil in the world, will a righteous world emerge?"
Shagal pressed the dagger harder against Etela's throat, exhaling roughly through his
nose.
"The battle between good and evil isn't that simple. Only when all evil turns to
righteousness will peace truly come. My master wanted to convey that grand aspiration."
Shagal's voice was strained, as if wrung out.
"No... You don't know anything...!"
If one day, all humans suddenly turned to kindness, the world's suffering would vanish
like magic.
An undeniably possible thing.
Yet, why couldn't humanity reach it—why did they even consider it impossible?
"You have no idea what kind of pain I endured!"
Shagal grabbed Etela by the throat, reversed his grip on the dagger, and drove it toward
her forehead.
***
-"Forgive."
The blade stopped abruptly before Etela's eyes.
'Master.'
Why had Raiden said those words at the brink of death?
'What? What am I supposed to forgive?'
Shagal raised his arm again.
"Uaaagh!"
-"You must forgive, Shagal!"
"Ghk! Ghk!"
As if someone had grabbed his wrist, his arm refused to descend, and Shagal screamed,
tears streaming down his face.
'Why did you kill him? Why?'
Had Raiden realized everything was a lie?
Was that why he had mercilessly erased even Tia, whom his disciple had loved so dearly?
'It's too late now! There's no going back!'
He had lied to Etela, but not once had he forgotten since killing Raphael.
-"The path back lies behind you."
"What the hell do you want me to do?!"
After losing everyone he loved, he had killed countless humans.
Because anyone could choose evil, he had eradicated all possibilities, regardless of good
or evil.
"And now you're saying it was all fake? That Tia never existed? That my entire life
was an illusion?"
That was unforgivable.
"No! Tia was real! My life was real! I'm a murderer! The worst of the worst!"
Shagal threw the dagger aside, climbed atop Etela, and shook her shoulders.
"You're Tia, aren't you? Tell me the truth! You're Tia, aren't you?"
If she said yes, he could die right now with a smile.
"No. I don't know you."
"Shut up! You're Tia!"
Just as he had with Tia, Shagal kissed Etela fiercely.
'Yes, she's Tia.'
The familiar scent of saliva, sweat, and skin.
"Damn it! Why can't I remember?!"
Only the scent remained.
'Ra Enemy.'
As she accepted Shagal's kiss defenselessly, Etela recalled Ra's chilling will.
'I don't know why you're going this far, but things won't go as you plan.'
"Tia! Tia!"
Nemesis materialized in Etela's hand, turning imagination into reality.
"Ti...?"
Shagal, his expression stunned, lifted his upper body and looked around.
***
-"Shagal! Clean up quickly! The performance starts in two hours!"
-"Wow, your aerial silk skills have improved so much!"
Memories of the Grass Leaf Circus Troupe flashed by rapidly.
-"Shagal, from now on, you're the ace."
The troupe members, precious as family, Tia, even the audience—none of them retained
human forms, dissolving into black smoke and dissipating.
"Uuugh...!"
Shagal's mouth slowly fell open, his eyes rolling back.
"My master was like a father to me."
Etela's voice snapped Shagal back to consciousness, and he looked down at her.
Tears streamed from behind her glasses.
"I hate you so much it hurts. But... I will love you just as fiercely as the hatred
I feel."
The moment one is consumed by anger, good succumbs to evil.
"So come back. Return and undo everything."
Shagal, his spirit shattered, staggered to his feet.
'Come back?'
He wasn't afraid of death.
Just as Etela could endure any pain for righteousness, Shagal, too, had abandoned
everything at the extremes of evil.
'I can't.'
Before paying the price, there was something he had to do.
'I'll kill them.'
He would drag Ra Enemy, the one who had thoroughly mocked his life, before him and stab
them.
'Stab, stab, stab, stab, stab!'
Just the thought amplified his rage, making his hands tremble violently.
'I'll kill them! Kill! I'll kill them all!'
"Teacher Etela!"
Shirone unleashed a barrage of photon cannons.
The impact was powerful enough to collapse walls, but Shagal had already leaped onto the
building's roof.
Seeing Etela, Shirone's eyes hardened.
"You! What did you do to the teacher?!"
Etela raised her upper body and spoke.
"It's fine, Shirone."
"Teacher!"
"Let him go. I have an idea."
Hearing this, Shagal turned his head slightly from the railing.
"...What's your name?"
"Lomi Etela."
"Etela."
Tia had never existed in this world.
"Don't wait. I won't come back."
As his words ended, Shagal vanished.
Shirone, glaring sharply at the rooftop, relaxed his expression and rushed to Etela.
"Teacher, are you alright?"
Though her Schema's ability kept her standing, her wounds were too deep for immediate
recovery.
'For Teacher Etela to be pushed this far...'
Now that he thought about it, everyone was dead.
Goblins, Pidu, Tubo, even his own mercenary squad members.
'Did he do all this alone?'
As Etela leaned against the building wall, Shirone snapped out of his thoughts and asked,
"Teacher, why did you let him go?"
"He's someone I can't forgive. He'll commit countless more killings from now
on."
"Then all the more reason—"
"Shirone, you must have realized by now too."
Shirone fell silent.
"Everything that's happened so far isn't a coincidence. It's the design of someone
existing beyond causality."
"Ra Enemy."
Etela nodded.
"My master already knew. Humanity is already Ra Enemy's puppets, and we can't even
distinguish what's real or fake. Three of the Five Senses have already gathered in Radum. If we consider
Mayeray's case, it's possible they're a manipulator on a cosmic scale."
Was Mayeray awakening from her oracle truly a coincidence?
Humans make puppets dance, but the puppets believe they're dancing of their own will.
"Honestly, even I don't know. Was letting Shagal go my pure will, or part of Ra
Enemy's design? If I had taken revenge on Shagal, would that have been an event outside Ra Enemy's
plan?"
In the end, nothing could be known.
"Ra Enemy possesses a strength entirely different from anyone we've fought before.
If he wanted Shirone dead, it would have been effortless."
"Probably."
He would have died.
Whether it was Miro, Gaold, or the entire world rising to kill Shirone—Ra Enemy had the
power to orchestrate such an event.
"I don't know his intentions—that's my honest judgment. That's why my master didn't
kill Shagal. To understand what Ra Enemy is truly after, he decided to die first."
It was a judgment befitting someone who had reached the pinnacle of detachment.
"I intend to follow my master's will. If no choice can free us from Ra's snare, then
all we can do is watch and wait for an opportunity. When the totality of human perception—the Five
Senses—gather, an opportunity will surely arise."
"Can we win?"
Can humanity break free from Ra Enemy's shackles?
"It's not a matter of choice. We need a compass. Someone who can judge and act by an
absolute standard, beyond emotions, beliefs, events, or variables."
That someone is Karl.
"It will be a difficult fight. But it's not without hope."
Etela looked at Shirone.
"Go to the Holy Flower. Even if we're under his design, the only thing we can do is
face Ra Enemy."
Shirone turned his gaze toward the Holy Flower.
Reflecting on why Ra Enemy hadn't killed those who stood out among the Five Senses, it
was clear: those meant to come would come, and those meant to die would die.
'Unacceptable.'
Humans are not puppets.
"I'll be back, Teacher."
Though injured, Etela could protect herself.
"Never break."
With a smile, Shirone offered a moment of silence for his fallen mercenaries before
soaring into the sky.
As Shirone disappeared, Etela's expression sank back into sorrow.
'Master, I'm sorry.'
She had said she hated him.
The words had slipped out as her will broke, and the sincerity terrified her. She wasn't
sure she could face the overwhelming evil ahead.
'You shouldn't have left like this. The burden is too heavy for me. Can I really do
this?'
Could she truly forgive Shagal?
As raindrops began to fall on Etela's face, she looked up at the gray sky.
