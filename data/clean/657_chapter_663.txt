Chapter 663:
"Che. Stubborn as ever."
Lupist grumbled as he sat down at the table, and Jane smiled.
"Why don't you try offering again? You were really disappointed,
remember?"
When Shirone was snatched away by the Ivory Tower, Lupist had been the
most furious, cursing the kingdom like no one else.
"If he was the type to accept an offer, he would've done it
already."
He still vividly remembered Shirone tearing up the acceptance letter
when the five great magic academies had gathered under the association's banner.
"Well, it's for the best. As long as he doesn't end up in the hands of
another country."
That was the only reason Lupist had held back.
Meanwhile, even after the evaluations of numbers 28 and 29 ended,
Shirone couldn't call in the next participant.
Rian and Aria were the same—still dazed from witnessing the spell
demonstration.
"That was…"
Rian couldn't even find the words, but Shirone nodded as if he had heard
them.
"That was the magic of a First-Class High Mage."
Even though it had been powerful enough to shake the building, not a
trace remained. It was a demonstration of both destructive power and finesse.
'And Jane's magic was incredible too.'
Because it was a specialty evaluation, some spells had to be
demonstrated directly to the judges. Shirone had clearly realized just how advanced her mental magic
was.
Aria spoke.
"Let's move on. We've still got 52 people left."
Shirone, now focused again, turned toward the door.
"Next person, please come in."
A moment later, the door opened, and a bald man with a build rivaling
Rian's stepped in.
"Greetings! Applicant number 59, Maghart!"
He was 43 years old, had trained in martial arts for 20 years, and was a
veteran mercenary affiliated with a martial guild.
He hadn't stood out particularly in the first evaluation, but his
fundamentals were solid, and his raw strength had earned him a pass from Shirone.
"Alright. I hope to see good results. Please show us your
specialty."
Maghart took off the huge pack strapped to his back and pulled out a
solid brick.
'A breaking demonstration, huh.'
Normally, breaking bricks would involve a stand to help with impact
transfer, but he stacked fifteen bricks directly into a tall tower.
Gazing down arrogantly at the brick stack that reached his waist,
Maghart nodded and spoke.
"The fiercest technique of the Tiger Fist style: Wild Tiger Headbutt. I
will shatter this in a single strike."
Brick-breaking—a boy's eternal dream.
Momentarily setting aside his role as a judge, Shirone's eyes sparkled
with excitement as he nodded.
"Fuuuuuu!"
Maghart took a horse stance, swung his arms, and held his breath.
Finally, he thrust his chin forward and shouted with all his might.
"Uraaah!"
He leaped into the air and came crashing down headfirst onto the
bricks.
The cracking of stone echoed in quick succession as his face smashed
through the bricks all the way to the ground.
"Wow! He broke them all!"
Shirone stood up and clapped for Maghart, who was bowed over and covered
in dust.
"Amazing! Are you alright? That must've hurt your head."
There was no response.
"Um… Mr. Maghart?"
Still motionless with his head buried in the ground, Shirone's tone grew
worried.
"Mr. Maghart, can you hear me?"
At that moment, Maghart's back gave out and he collapsed to the ground,
limbs twitching in convulsions.
"Oh no! I think he passed out!"
Aria jumped up and shouted at the door.
"Master! Master! We've got a medical emergency!"
A Silver Ring guild member rushed in, hoisted Maghart over their
shoulder, and bolted out of the room.
The scene was cleared up in a flash, but Shirone still sat there
blankly, staring after them.
"…What? What just happened?"
Rian clicked his tongue.
"He was too tense. His body was stiff, so the force wasn't focused
properly. He blacked out the moment he hit."
Shirone quietly sat back down.
"Next person, please come in."
Though there had been a mishap, most of the applicants who passed the
first round delivered solid specialty performances.
'Hmm, this is tricky. I'll apply a deferred decision.'
It was hard to set a clear line, so judging too early could
backfire.
Applicant 73 was the Silver Ring Guild Master, Ackman, who demonstrated
his signature Fire Snake magic without holding back.
'That spell is definitely convenient.'
It relied heavily on magical artifacts like the Flame Robe and the Fire
Prison, but since this was a practical evaluation, gear counted as a strength.
'Just like how I'm wearing Armand.'
When the fire snake coiled and landed, the three judges clapped, and
Ackman gave a sheepish bow.
Though he had already lost in a previous battle, making this
demonstration slightly awkward, from Shirone's perspective, it wasn't easy to find a mage of Ackman's
caliber.
'I'll lean toward passing him for now.'
Once Ackman left, the next to enter was Jordic, Master of the War Wagon
Guild.
Unlike in the first round, he was equipped with magical gear—a large
glove with a crystal embedded in the palm, similar to Armand.
"Number 74, Jordic."
Though a participant, his attitude remained as haughty as ever.
"Yes. Please show us your specialty."
Jordic introduced his equipment first.
"This is the Ice Glove. When combined with my ice magic, I can do quite
a bit. For example…"
When he held out his palm, the crystal glowed blue, and the air
instantly froze.
"I can absorb energy."
Then, clenching his fist, the ice rippled like water before forming into
a whip with jagged spikes.
"With this, I behead anyone who dares to oppose me."
Swinging his arm wide, the ice whip cracked through the air and lashed
in front of Shirone before retracting.
'Ah, so it doesn't stay in one shape.'
Ice magic was known for its great shaping potential but suffered in
fluidity.
However, with the Ice Glove, he could absorb energy in real time,
allowing even rigid ice to be used like a whip.
"Well… I'll stop here. The real killing techniques aren't fit to show
here anyway."
Jordic raised a cold smirk, expecting a reaction from Shirone—but
Shirone's eyes were already locked onto the paperwork.
"Thank you for your effort. We'll notify you individually about the
results."
"…."
Standing there awkwardly, Jordic clicked his tongue and turned around as
Aria called for the next person.
"Tch, these damn kids nowadays."
Applicant 75 was Evian, Master of the Blood Rose Guild.
Her attire clearly prioritized visual impression over
practicality.
"Hello, applicant number 75, Evian."
With her fingertips, she lifted her skirt and curtsied politely, one leg
tucked behind her.
"Yes, hello. You look beautiful today."
"Oh my, really? That's strange, I just dressed as I usually do.
Hohoho!"
Her exaggerated laugh caused her chest to bounce, and Shirone snapped
back to his usual self.
"Well then, shall we begin?"
Having achieved the desired first impression, Evian wiped away her smile
and demonstrated her specialty.
"Magnetic Control."
Evian's specialty was electric magic—specifically magnetism, and her
metal manipulation skills were top-notch.
She tossed a black metallic sphere known as the Dragon's Eye into the
air. It dissolved into iron powder and formed a visible magnetic field around her.
"Ooh."
Intrigued by the flowing iron dust, which resembled a silver river,
Shirone gave an interested expression. Gaining confidence, Evian shaped the iron dust into a rose and
sent it floating toward him.
The rose hovered in front of him, so realistic that its only unreal
aspect was its black color. As he reached to touch it, it dissolved into mist and returned to
Evian.
"That's impressive."
"My specialty is shaping, but I hope you don't underestimate the power.
When I focus, I can overcome 200 kilograms of resistance."
It was enough force to manipulate dozens of longswords
simultaneously.
'This spell will be useful. Let's place Evian in the passing group
too.'
Lupist, Jane, Ackman, Jordic, Evian—the elite had assembled.
With this kind of lineup from the start, it was safe to say the
foundation was taking shape.
The numbers jumped ahead, and it was finally number 121, Etella's turn.
What she had prepared for the evaluation, like Maghart, was a breaking demonstration.
'Well, there's hardly anything more impactful than this.'
She was skilled enough to fight Satan in Heaven, yet the fact that she
was using props for the evaluation was a clear sign of respect toward the judges.
"I'll demonstrate a secret technique of the Yin-Yang Wave
Fist."
"Yes. We look forward to it."
Shirone bowed respectfully to his former teacher, and Rian, still
haunted by the eerie impression from her first test, watched cautiously.
What she pulled from her bag was a solid iron sphere about the size of a
human head. To check its density, she dropped it from eye level, and it landed on the floor with a heavy
thud.
"She's going to… break iron?"
As Aria looked on in disbelief, Etella lowered her waist and readied her
fist.
"Fuuu..."
She inhaled deeply and then, with astonishing speed, slammed her fist
into the iron sphere.
Thud-thud-thud-thud-thud!
A sound far too violent to believe it came from a human fist rang out
repeatedly, and with each sound, waves of impact surged into the iron sphere.
'Thunderous Palm!'
Spreading her palm wide, Etella delivered the final wave of
force.
With a groan, the iron ball trembled, then shattered like it had
exploded from within.
"Whoa!"
Shirone exclaimed, impressed as always by her incredible skill.
It was hard to believe she had destroyed something that couldn't be
broken even by advanced magic—using nothing but her own body.
Etella crouched down to clear the fragments, then stood and politely
bowed.
"Thank you for your time."
Wearing her usual gentle smile, Etella left the courtyard, and Shirone
turned to Rian.
"What do you think? Is it a pass?"
"Huh? Oh, of course."
Her skill left no room for argument. The only reason Rian hesitated to
judge so quickly was due to his own self-doubt.
Back in his knight training days, winning had been the only thing that
mattered. But seeing how far today's applicants had progressed in their techniques left him with
complicated feelings.
Whether Shirone understood those thoughts or not, he moved on to the
next participant.
"Number 147, please come in."
Only after calling did Shirone check the file.
'Ah, so it's him.'
Whirlwind Wigg.
With twin swords strapped to his back in an X-shape, he walked in with a
cheerful smile.
"Hello, I'm number 147. Some people call me Wigg of the
Whirlwind."
"Hmph."
Rian crossed his arms tightly, lips pressed in a thin line.
Wigg had been giving off a challenging vibe ever since the first
evaluation.
"Alright, show us your specialty. I assume it's dual
swordsmanship?"
"Well, honestly, the weapon doesn't really matter. I just use
whatever I can get my hands on. What really matters is..."
Drawing both blades in a flash, Wigg closed one eye and pointed his
swords directly at Rian.
"...who you can beat."
Rian, his expression unchanging, asked calmly.
"So... who can you beat?"
As expected, he wasn't the type to back down. Wigg, as if waiting for
this moment, made his proposal.
"You. I challenge you to a real duel, Knight of Mach."
