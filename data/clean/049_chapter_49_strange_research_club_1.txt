Chapter 49:
Strange Research Club (1)
Shirone floated through a dark space.
In some places, countless stars were visible, while in others, only endless darkness
stretched on.
In the darkness, it felt as though time had stopped, and a fear that he might never
escape crept over him.
One by one, stars began to appear and converge toward a single point. It felt as though
space itself was contracting.
He shuddered at the unimaginable scale of the stars. He couldn't fathom what would happen
when they finally collided at that last point.
But the collision never came. The stars began to collapse, disintegrating into dust
before being sucked away.
Time was flowing backward.
By the time he realized it, Shirone's physical body had already decomposed and vanished.
Yet Shirone did not disappear.
Though he didn't know what this last remnant of his existence was, he remained conscious
as he journeyed toward the beginning of the world.
A writhing, vibrating string of light came into view. Within it, light, time, gravity,
and universal forces were unified.
Finally, upon reaching the end—or rather, the beginning—the entire world was filled with
light.
The presence that made up Shirone began to fade, and an overwhelming sense of unity
washed over him.
It was a unification akin to a colossal explosion.
***
"Aaaah!"
Shirone screamed and bolted upright in bed. His clothes were drenched in cold sweat, as
if he'd been caught in the rain.
The surroundings were silent, and the moonlight cast a desolate hue over the windowsill.
The familiar scent of books lingered in the air. On the desk, books about photon theory lay scattered in
disarray.
"Hah… Another nightmare…?"
He gulped down water from a kettle, then opened the window to let the early summer breeze
dry his sweat. Only then could he calm down enough to sit in his chair.
'Why does this keep happening every night? What's wrong with me?'
Ever since experiencing the Immortal Function during the Speed Gun test, he'd been having
similar dreams. Though not exactly nightmares, the emotions he felt in them were no less terrifying.
"Ugh…"
Shirone hugged his trembling body and rubbed his arms. It felt as though something
fundamental within him had gone awry. The problem was that this phenomenon wasn't limited to dreams—it was
seeping into reality.
One of his senses had become unnaturally sharp. But he couldn't figure out what kind of
information that sense was even receiving.
It wasn't that his sense of smell or touch had improved. His vision and hearing were
unchanged, too.
Yet, without a doubt, a new sensory organ had emerged.
"Don't tell me… it's something like a ghost?"
The sound of a piano signaled the arrival of morning. Seeing the dawn break outside,
Shirone headed to the bathroom to wash up. With the advanced class's practical evaluations over, normal
classes would resume today. As the warm water touched him, the strange sensations gradually faded. If this
persisted all day, he'd surely suffer a nervous breakdown. Partly relieved, Shirone packed his bag.
***
With the exam period over, the school regained its lively atmosphere. Students who had
once competed fiercely now chatted cheerfully as if nothing had happened.
The recent topic of discussion was Shirone's Speed Gun test. Unlike written exams,
practical evaluations had a sporting element, so it was natural for students to be excited when someone
delivered a spectacular performance.
Usually, each class had its own standout students, but the impact of the Speed Gun test
on the first day was so overwhelming that everyone else was overshadowed.
For the first time in the magic academy's history, two students had scored in the
1200-point range.
The difference between Shirone and Iruki's scores was a mere six points, and the match
had been an unbelievable comeback. With such a legendary showdown happening on the first day, even
outstanding performances from later participants couldn't compare.
Shirone had skyrocketed to stardom. Though he'd been famous since enrollment, it had been
as Amy's boyfriend or a promising rising star. Now, he was undeniably the face of Alpheas Magic Academy.
Even on his way to class, Shirone was greeted warmly by students—some of whom were
seniors from Class Four. In a magic academy with an early advancement system, the hierarchy between upper
and lowerclassmen was strict because progression was difficult. But given Shirone's historic achievement,
treating him as just another underclassman felt awkward. In hindsight, Amy's decision to acknowledge him as
an equal had been remarkably foresighted.
By the time he reached the advanced class building, Shirone was already exhausted from
exchanging greetings with everyone.
"Huh? It's Shirone-senpai! Hello, senpai!"
"Shirone-senpai, I'm your fan! Can I follow you around from now on?"
Class Six girls approached him warmly. Originally, they'd been the most resentful of
Shirone's rapid advancement. Jumping from Class Seven to Class Five had left them feeling slighted. But now,
even Class Four seniors saw him as a future rival among the graduating class, so they had no choice but to
change their attitudes.
"Senpai, what are you doing after class today? We're holding a magic demonstration with
friends—want to join?"
"We're going out on the holiday. Senpai, come with us! I'll be your partner."
"Sorry. I haven't been feeling well lately."
Between the nightmares and the eerie sensations that struck him even while awake, he
didn't want to go anywhere. Knowing about the insurmountable mountain named Amy, the girls didn't press him
and left, promising to invite him next time.
"Shirone, Shirone, Shirone! Shirone, Shirone!"
The moment he opened the classroom door, a classmate yanked him inside. Without even a
chance to set down his bag, Shirone was dragged in, bewildered by the stares of his friends.
"What's going on? What happened?"
"The semester's almost over—join our research club. We're making one just for Class Five
students. The final project is a free topic anyway. It'll save time and be fun. So, join us."
"Hmm."
Shirone hesitated. He'd received many offers, but if he were to join a research club,
he'd always assumed it would be the Supernatural Parapsychology Club, where Neid and Iruki were members.
"Shirone, come on, think about it. The reason you haven't joined a club is because of
personal study, right? There are plenty of students here like you. But we still have to do the final
project. So we're making a temporary one. Since it's not a major grading factor, we can finish it quickly
and disband. That way, you'll have more time for personal study."
"Ah. That makes sense."
It was a solid strategy. The final project was a free topic, so it carried less weight
than written or practical exams. Joining an existing club just to complete the project and then leaving
would be shameless. Forming a temporary club with other unaffiliated students was a clever workaround.
"Hey! What are you scheming? Shirone's off-limits! Shirone's mine, got it?"
Neid hid Shirone behind his back and shouted. The students stared at him in disbelief.
Those who knew Neid's true intentions glared at him.
"That's ridiculous. You just want to drag him into that weird club of yours, right?
Shirone, you absolutely cannot join that one. Even if you don't join ours, pick any other club. In all my
years here, I've never heard of Neid actually completing a final project."
The Supernatural Parapsychology Club boasted a long tradition, but its official
activities were rare. Most students didn't even know Neid was part of a club.
"Huh? Neid, you're in a club?"
Neid rolled his eyes and deployed his signature survival tactics to deflect the
situation.
"Hmph. Of course. Did you really think the school's most popular guy wouldn't have a
club? Anyway, Shirone's coming with me."
Neid grabbed Shirone's wrist and headed for the door. The student who'd suggested the
research club yelled after them.
"Hey, Shirone! Think carefully! Don't you dare join that club! And Neid, stop interfering
with Shirone's studies and let him join a normal one!"
"Hahahaha! Your words mean nothing! Shirone's mind is already filled with only me! We've
already crossed the forbidden line!"
Shirone's face turned pale.
"Wait, Neid. What are you talking about…?"
Neid didn't give him a chance to argue and slammed the door shut. The students in the
classroom stared blankly at the scene.
"…What did he just say?"
The student who'd yelled at Neid scratched his head furiously.
"This is bad. That bastard's seriously planning to drag Shirone into his club. We can't
let him get swayed."
"Isn't it too late? He's already being dragged around. And where the hell is he taking
him? Class is about to start."
***
The moment they stepped into the hallway, Shirone shook off Neid's hand. He knew it was a
joke, but the eerie feeling lingered.
"Hey! Why would you say something like that?"
"Hahaha! It's not wrong, is it? You're the only one who's seen the true nature of the
Supernatural Parapsychology Club."
"The true nature? There was nothing to see."
Neid glanced around at the people passing by before pushing Shirone into a crouch.
"First, sit down for a sec."
"Fine. Stop being so pushy."
Shirone crouched and leaned against the wall. After checking their surroundings again,
Neid lowered his voice.
"You're the only one who's actually been inside the club. So you absolutely can't tell
anyone."
"Who's even going to ask?"
"Anyway! The higher-ups are already talking about disbanding the club because of the lack
of members. We have to keep a low profile, so don't even mention the word 'supernatural.'"
"Got it. I wasn't planning to."
To Shirone, the Supernatural Parapsychology Club seemed like a lost cause, but its true
value was known only to a shadowy group of researchers who were always looking for an opportunity to exploit
it. The teachers, meanwhile, were desperate to disband it.
"By the way, are you really joining the club?"
Neid had known about the strategy of forming temporary clubs at the end of the semester
for a long time. While it was efficient, it didn't seem like the right fit for Shirone's situation.
"I was considering it. Why? Is there a reason I shouldn't?"
"Of course. Even if the final project isn't a major grading factor, teaming up with just
anyone is a waste. Besides, you're aiming for the top of the school, right? Every point matters. You'd be
better off working alone or joining a proper club."
In Class Four, where students aimed for the graduating class, perfect scorers in certain
subjects weren't uncommon, so even a one-point difference in the final project could be significant.
'Hmm. But doing the final project alone would take too much time.'
After pondering for a moment, Shirone spoke.
"Neid. What if… I joined the Supernatural Parapsychology Club?"
Neid's face lit up. If Shirone joined, the club would have its three musketeers. Though
they often hung out together, Iruki frequently skipped class, leaving their group incomplete.
But Neid couldn't immediately agree. Alpheas Magic Academy had been founded 45 years ago.
While most students took graduation exams and entered society, there was another hidden society where all
sorts of people engaged in bizarre activities. The Supernatural Parapsychology Club was one such place.
"Hmm. Are you sure? Our club is… closer to the shadows. Supernatural parapsychology is
already niche. I'd love to have you, but honestly, I wouldn't recommend it to a friend."
Shirone had guessed from his classmates' reactions that Neid's club had secrets. But he
didn't want to join a club just for grades. Supernatural parapsychology had been an interest of his since
childhood.
"It's not just about that. I've been… feeling off lately. I was actually going to ask you
guys about it. Maybe supernatural parapsychology could explain it."
