Chapter 1170:
Leaving the plaza behind, Fermi and Seriel made their way into a tavern
near the marketplace.
As he opened the door, Fermi asked,
"Are you sure you're okay with this?"
"You think I can't even handle a drink?
Just get in already. I'm starving."
It was a little early for dinner, but skipping lunch had left them both
famished.
"Let's just grab something quick."
Seriel took a seat and placed an order.
After an hour, the alcohol started kicking in, and their faces had taken
on a faint flush.
They weren't particularly close, but as final-year students, there was
always something to talk about.
"It's always tough."
Seriel rested her chin on the table, gazing out the window at the
evening streets.
"The hardest part is… Once you get used to the routine of senior
year, you stop realizing what's difficult. You need to recognize your flaws to grow, but when you're
stuck in a rut, you don't even see them."
"You are improving. One day, you'll look back and see how far
you've come. Trust me. I've been here longer than anyone."
Hearing that from a veteran gave Seriel a sense of relief, but it didn't
last long.
'A graduation exam broker.'
He was more than capable of graduating.
So why was he still here?
'Should I ask?'
Fermi smiled knowingly.
"You've got something on your mind. Go ahead, ask."
The conversation had been pleasant, and talking about graduation wasn't
so bad.
"Agh!"
Seriel hesitated, torn between asking and not ruining the mood.
In the end, she just downed her drink in one go.
After three hours, Seriel's eyes started to glaze over.
"Hey, Fermi. You can't keep living like this, you
bastard."
It was the fourth time she'd said this, but Fermi just smiled quietly
and let her rant.
"Listen, I know you're amazing. I know that! But you shouldn't live
like this. Do you have any idea how hard the other students work to become mages?"
"You mean, how could I take money and sell diplomas?"
"Yeah, you bastard. Taking mone—… huh?"
Half sobering up, Seriel's eyes widened as Fermi lit a cigarette.
"I've taken the graduation exam for years. I've helped many pass
and failed even more."
A long plume of smoke drifted up.
"But you're wondering if I took money? No. I've never taken a
bribe. No one's ever paid me."
"Then… why?"
"Because this is my belief. Controlling the system. Some people
think winning through combat is all that matters. But that's not enough for me. Just like you compete to
prove your abilities, I fight my own way."
Was he lying?
'He's saying he's not a broker? Does he want me to defend him at school?
No…'
Fermi was untrustworthy, but after spending half a day together…
'This guy doesn't lie.'
He'd rather stay silent than make something up.
"Cigarettes?
I never saw you smoke on campus."
"I don't particularly like them. I just don't want concepts like
'taboo' to control my life. If I need something, I'll do it. If I don't, I won't."
This was his way of controlling the system.
'What kind of life has he lived?'
The more Seriel learned about him, the more confused she felt— And the
more curious she became.
"Fine, I'll believe you. But at this point, you've already proven
your skills. So why are you still at school?"
Fermi thought of his mother.
Of Yolga, who still wandered the upper echelons of Istas like a
ghost.
"Who knows?"
A sudden chill ran down Seriel's spine.
Fermi's expression was completely transparent—
And yet, it looked like he was on the verge of tears.
"You…"
At that moment, she could think of nothing else—
She just wanted to hold him.
"Let's go. It's late."
They paid their bill and stepped out into the darkened streets.
Should they just part ways here?
The time, the atmosphere—everything felt uncertain.
Then, Fermi asked,
"Would you…?"
Seriel nodded slightly.
"Yeah."
—
Seriel woke up in a nameless inn at 4 AM.
The world outside was silent,
and beside her, Fermi was still breathing softly in his sleep.
'…Huh?'
Memories of the past day flashed through her mind like a reel of
film.
'What the hell… Did I lose my mind?!'
To her own surprise, it didn't feel as bad as she expected.
No—if she was being honest, it had been really good.
But now, with her mind clear of alcohol…
Emotions weren't everything.
He was a rival in the final-year rankings.
The leader of an opposing faction.
She had just spent the night with someone who, by all accounts, should
have been her enemy.
Seriel slowly turned over,
slid off the bed like a snake, and gathered her clothes.
'Careful. Careful.'
Kneeling on the floor, she put on her clothes, checked her belongings,
and made sure not a single sound escaped.
She gently opened the door—
and silently closed it behind her, not even allowing one decibel of
noise.
After checking that the coast was clear, she bolted down the hallway and
ran out of the inn.
'This stays a secret forever.
Not even Amy can know.'
—
One minute after Seriel left,
Fermi opened his eyes.
He had been awake.
Gazing at the dark ceiling,
he let out a slow breath and covered his eyes with his arm.
"…Haa."
In the hallways of Delta, Seriel was lost in thought.
That year, during the graduation exams, Amy had been brutally lynched by
the Golden Wheel and eliminated early.
'I passed, though.'
Unlike Amy, who was the top candidate for graduation, Seriel hadn't been
a direct target of the Golden Wheel's schemes.
Yet, that event had left an unresolved question lingering in Seriel's
mind.
'Why did Fermi let Amy fail? Was it just a tactical decision? Or… was it
because of me?'
Even now, facing him, looking at that face that always seemed to
unsettle her, she couldn't bring herself to ask.
"Fermi, by any chance—"
"Sorry."
Fermi cut her off and turned away.
"I've got something urgent to do. I'll send you the Angel Formula
soon. It won't take long."
Watching his back as he walked away, Seriel felt relieved—
And yet, her chest ached.
'What was that…?'
The expression he had just made—
Now, after hearing what Shirone had told her, she finally understood
what it meant.
'He's lost someone important again, hasn't he?'

8:00 AM. The Holy War Begins.
The leaders and officials of various nations gathered once again.
But unlike yesterday, the air was completely different.
The nations that had suffered assassination attempts were seething with
rage.
The ones that had escaped were eyeing the others with caution.
Albino stroked his beard.
'This atmosphere is tense. No doubt they already know. Today's session
won't go smoothly.'
The leaders of Moon Kingdom, Thormia, Jincheon, Corona, Paras, and
Gustav were absent.
Shirone thought to himself,
'Even Moon Kingdom's Woonryong was taken down. So the only ones who came
out unscathed were the Tribal Alliance?'
But something felt off.
'Kitra being hit doesn't make sense. He's an OOparts. Even if he was
targeted, there's no way it would have affected him.'
Was it a strategic deception?
As Uorin pondered the same thing, Corona's think tank, the Ivory Tower,
finally spoke.
"2-star Ivory Tower resident, Byulya Tzuoi."
True to her nickname, the Black Mars, her booming voice echoed across
the hall.
"Last night, there was an assassination attempt in the Corona
Kingdom. According to our investigation, at least four other nations suffered the same attack. And our
prime suspect is…"
Her eyes shifted.
"The Moon Kingdom."
The chancellor of Moon Kingdom shot to his feet.
"This is a setup!
I'll speak frankly!
We were victims of this incident as well—"
"That's irrelevant."
Tzuoi raised a hand to silence him.
With Woonryong gone, the Moon Kingdom no longer had a vote.
They were no longer a factor.
"The details can be investigated later. What Corona wants to know
is how the attack was carried out. Our findings indicate that the method used was Sals— a technique that
is impossible without the aid of the Under World."
The Moon Kingdom's chancellor slowly sat back down.
"It wasn't just ghosts. Someone amplified their emotions. In other
words, Satan is involved. And as far as we know…"
Tzuoi's gaze shifted again.
"Isn't Satan currently in Kashan?"
All eyes turned.
Uorin stood up, smiling.
"First of all, I would like to express my condolences to the
nations that have suffered. Kashan has done everything in its power to prevent this crisis, And we wish
to make it clear— We have no intention of breaking any peace treaties."
She lied with such confidence that it almost sounded believable.
"Then, what about Satan?"
"It is true that Kashan and Moon formed Code One. However, Moon did
not request me, they requested Satan. Let me be clear— Kashan had no involvement in this
incident."
Albino smirked.
'A clean severance.'
Just then, the doors of the Holy War Chamber swung open.
Havitz entered.
"I'll tell you the truth."
Half the officials jumped to their feet.
The presence of an unshackled Satan was overwhelming.
"Call the guards!"
Havitz raised a hand.
"Now, now."
Vanishing activated.
It took thirty seconds before the officials even registered that he had
reached the podium.
'Damn it!'
The fear that their heads could roll at any moment sent the officials
into a panic.
They turned to Uorin.
"Kashan! You promised to keep Havitz under control! You
said—"
Havitz flickered in and out of Vanishing, causing gaps to appear in
their memories.
"Listen up."
The room fell silent.
"Moon Kingdom was the first to break the rules. Before that, I
never touched anyone in this chamber."
That much was true.
"Moon Kingdom tried to launch Sals against all eleven nations. What
Uorin negotiated in Code One was a reduction in targets— in exchange for handing me over. It was a deal
to minimize casualties— and, more importantly, to protect Kashan."
International law permits preemptive strikes for national
security.
"So, Kashan is also a victim. I was forced into this
situation.
I wanted to follow the Holy War's rules. But Moon blackmailed me and
took away my control."
Or at least, that's what Uorin wanted me to say.
Havitz was simply reciting her words in real time.
"This is what I know. And now, I have reclaimed my
freedom."
The Moon Kingdom's chancellor gritted his teeth.
'So this was their plan all along. If they frame Moon as a war criminal,
They'll have a justification to use Havitz. And there's nothing we can do to disprove it.'
Even if this went to the International Tribunal, Moon's chances of
winning were slim.
Uorin 's lips curled.
'They really did use Sals. I may have spiced up the story, but they
can't deny the core truth.'
The power of the real perpetrator.
"Enough talk."
Before the officials could regain their wits,
Havitz cut off Uorin's voice and spoke.
"This is a complete mess. Everyone's killing each other. Even if
you survive, you won't last long."
"So I have a proposal. And trust me— it's a deal you'll want to
take."
"What…? What right do you have to control twelve
nations?!"
Even without hearing his proposal, they were already terrified.
"You don't have to accept. But you won't have a choice. This isn't
about rights— It's about power."
And for the first time in eternity,
Satan's eyes reflected Yahweh.
"Kill Shirone. Until Yahweh is dead— I will kill one person every
hour.
And that includes every single one of you."
