Chapter 369:
Special Training (4)
Shirone began training in Laser Guidance Magic in earnest.
While he had no issues with the execution, the theory was beyond
advanced level.
In this regard, Isabel was a savior.
As the head of the magic library, her explanations were easy to
understand and always hit the mark.
Light is a type of electromagnetic wave. The Laser Guidance Magic
developed by Jacquebain used an active homing system, where the laser observed the waves emitted upon
collision with an object to guide the projectile to the target.
For this to work, a laser-seeking magic called Seeker had to be used
first.
"Ugh! I don't get it! This is just information magic!"
Shirone looked up from his book, frustrated.
The page he was on detailed the algorithm for equipping the Seeker,
which to him was like a foreign language.
From across the training ground, Flu, who was reading a magic book,
clicked her tongue.
"That's why you should have considered the difficulty when choosing
a magic book. You have a month left. Why not give up and find something else?"
Shirone glared at Flu with half-closed eyes. She was so annoying.
"You said it was fine at first."
"Well, I'm not saying it's impossible now. If you push hard, you
might manage it in a month. But there's always Plan B, right?"
"Hmm, Plan B..."
Flu was right. If he was going to give up, now was the time.
If he invested more time in this, he might end up returning to school
with only Scatter Movement mastered.
"I'll stick with it. I've learned a lot already, like radio
waves."
As soon as Shirone finished speaking, Flu closed her book and stood
up.
"Alright, if that's your decision, let's get serious."
Shirone looked up at Flu as she approached.
"The most important thing right now is the Seeker. It's the linking
magic that fuses the laser and Photon Cannon."
"Yes, but it's really difficult. I'm not good with information
magic."
"Not only information mages use algorithms. For example, I can use
them too."
"Really? You?"
Shirone squinted in disbelief. Flu grabbed her magic staff and moved to
the center of the training ground. She struck the ground with her staff, showcasing her skill.
"Phoenix Formation."
A giant firebird rose from the crystal orb at the end of her staff,
hovering in the air like smoke.
Since no one was within its range, the firebird flapped its wings
silently.
Flu pointed to herself and said,
"What do you think my specialty is?"
"Uh, fire magic, right?"
"No. While I do handle fire magic, I'm actually a
zoner."
Thinking of Etella, Shirone tilted his head.
If a zoner's characteristics were a massive Spirit Zone and highly
sensitive senses, Flu didn't fit either.
"It's a special field, so you might not know. When people think of
zoners, they usually think of search-type mages. But a zoner is simply an expert in the Spirit
Zone."
Flu held up two fingers.
"Zoners are divided into search-type and manipulation-type. I'm the
latter, a manipulation-type mage."
"Ah, so you control that firebird."
"Yes. The Phoenix Formation fires flames at all targets within the
Spirit Zone. It reacts much faster than shared perception because it uses the Dawkins algorithm. It's an
automatic response of nerves and mind. Blocking the vampire's attack at the Gold Tower was also an
automatic response."
Shirone's expression turned blank.
A top graduate of the Royal Magic School and a specialist zoner.
No wonder she could precisely adjust the golden balance of the Spirit
Zone.
"The same goes for search-types. If durability is the virtue of
combat mages, zoners are obsessed with density. But since the mind isn't a particle, even 100% density
doesn't mean it's completely full. It just means intuition is 100% accurate. That's why it's really hard
to go beyond 95%."
Flu moved her staff behind her and changed positions. Shirone's head
followed her.
"Of course, a zoner should ideally reach 100%. Then, to enhance
clarity, we use a unit called specific gravity. If you consider a normal Spirit Zone as gas, a zoner's
Spirit Zone can reach the specific gravity of liquid, clay, or even rock, depending on their
level."
When Shirone turned to face her, Flu stopped and held up three
fingers.
"Still, the minimum standard for a zoner is 60% density.
Search-type Spirit Zones are incredibly large. If you expand your Spirit Zone to a 2-kilometer diameter
without Immortal Function, the density drops to 0%. That's why zoners focus solely on
density."
If the density was 0%, the Spirit Zone wouldn't form. But the comparison
made it clear.
"So, how do you search a 2-kilometer radius with 60% density?
That's where algorithms come in. For example, using the Pisarski algorithm, you can search for metal
substances within the radius. It captures the reflectivity of light, so it's less efficient at
night."
Shirone was struck by the novelty. While regular magic affected the
external world, algorithms were meta-magic that gave unique functions to the Spirit Zone.
Though not as powerful as schemas, mages had developed magic to counter
other magic.
How vast is the world of magic?
His heart began to race. It was wide and deep, a giant playground he
could immerse himself in for a lifetime without getting bored.
With Flu's help, Shirone gradually learned the algorithm for the
Seeker.
It was a series of basics, and Isabel provided intensive guidance on the
advanced, complex theories.
Time flew by, and with four days left before leaving the Magic
Association, Shirone finally completed the theory stage.
"Finally. It's done."
Shirone closed the magic book and lay flat on the floor. He had never
studied this hard, even at school.
It wasn't a matter of effort but biological limits.
Humans need sleep, but the Magic Association's sleep device helped him
overcome the impossible.
"I did it. I really did it."
Shirone mumbled in a daze, feeling like steam was escaping from his
head.
Flu, who had worked just as hard, looked haggard after a month.
"...You're really stubborn. I told you to give up."
Flu had been enthusiastic at first, but by the middle, she was reaching
her limit.
Though she had to handle the association's work during the day,
Shirone's determination was impressive even considering that.
"Hehe! How about it? I did it, right?"
Flu maintained her last bit of pride.
"No. Do you know that for an average person, this would have been
physically impossible? You've only just finished the theory. So my judgment was correct."
Shirone acknowledged that fact.
Just mastering the theory didn't mean the magic would work.
Still, he persisted with Laser Guidance because, unlike others, he
already had the execution down.
"Shall we start then?"
As Shirone slowly stood up, Flu shook her head.
"No, you need to rest today."
"But there's no time."
"No means no! If you practice in that state, you'll really die. And
not just you, I'll die too!"
"Then at least use the sleep device..."
"That's not allowed either! Go to your room now! If you don't sleep
for at least eight hours, I won't help you anymore!"
Flu pushed Shirone towards his quarters.
Shirone's steps were heavy as he walked back. There was no time. He was
so anxious that he didn't even feel tired.
"Just a bit more. At least review before sleeping."
Shirone lay on his bed with the Laser Guidance magic book.
And fell asleep in one second.
The next afternoon.
"Ah! This is bad!"
People in the hallway turned their heads at the sound coming from
Shirone's room.
The door burst open, and Shirone, with wet hair from a recent shower,
ran to the training ground.
There were only three days left.
He had to practice today and get evaluated tomorrow to achieve his
goal.
When he arrived at the training ground, Flu was already prepared.
Shirone's eyes widened. Despite oversleeping, Flu should have been at
work.
"Huh? What's going on?"
"Did you sleep well?"
Flu checked Shirone's condition.
"Yes. I slept so much it was annoying. But what about your
work?"
"I took leave. I'll focus on helping you for the next three
days."
"Ah..."
Tears welled up in Shirone's eyes.
Using her precious leave to help a junior train. Today, she looked like
an angel.
"Senpai..."
Seeing Shirone approach with a strange expression, Flu recoiled like a
turtle and held out her hand.
"W-What are you doing with that look? Stay back!
Creepy!"
As Shirone continued to approach, she backed away in a hurry. Only when
he stopped did she speak.
"Anyway, since I'm here, I have to see you succeed. So let's do
this properly."
Shirone's face turned serious.
"Yes, please take care of me!"
The training continued until midnight, extended to the next morning with
the help of the sleep capsule.
They corrected numerous algorithm errors overnight, but there was still
a long way to go.
"At least you've equipped the Seeker. Now let's try a proper
fusion. Can you do it?"
"Yes, I'll try."
Shirone mentally prepared himself and entered the Spirit Zone.
As his mental sequence accelerated, his eyes widened.
'Homing Photon Cannon!'
Eight Photon Cannons formed around Shirone, glowing with a pale,
beautiful light.
The difference this time was the red dot in the center of each
sphere.
A laser pointer, the Seeker.
"Fusion successful. Now for the target aiming
algorithm."
Following Flu's instructions, Shirone manipulated the Seeker, then asked
with a puzzled expression,
"Uh, where's the target?"
Flu clapped her hands together.
"Oh, right! There should be a test drone somewhere."
She rummaged through a supply box in the corner of the training ground,
then turned around with a new idea.
"Actually, never mind. I'll be the target."
She moved to the east wall and stood against it.
"You'll be the target, senpai?"
"Yes. We don't have much time. Let's skip the small
stuff."
Shirone agreed that it was more efficient.
He aimed at Flu with his Spirit Zone, and the Seeker targeted her
according to the algorithm.
Eight laser points marked various spots on her body.
Flu looked at the floating spheres in front of her and swallowed
hard.
The Seekers embedded in the Photon Cannons stared at her like the
emotionless eyes of a monster, making her feel extremely uneasy.
'This is... much scarier than I thought.'
As she slowly tilted her body, the eight laser points moved in unison,
following her.
When she straightened up, the Seekers adjusted their aim again. She felt
a slight chill.
'It's already a fast spell. And now it's tracking me?'
Shirone, ready to start, said,
"Senpai, I'll begin..."
"Wait a moment."
Flu raised her hand. Shirone stopped the magic and looked at her
blankly.
She cleared her throat and averted her gaze.
"On second thought, let's just use the drone."
