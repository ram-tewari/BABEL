Chapter 34:
Instant Movement (5)
Just as one can count the leaves of a tree through the Spirit Zone, the speed of light is
also perceived through insight.
This was exactly what Alpheas wanted. A magic that anyone could learn, but whose mastery
varied starkly depending on talent.
Shirone gained confidence. Though he lagged behind in general knowledge, when it came to
pure ability, he had honed it daily since he was twelve.
There were thirty days left until the exam.
Shirone did his best to internalize Kergos's photonization theory. After classes, he
buried himself in books all night in his dorm. After twenty days of this, he reached a point where reading
alone no longer provided new material to study.
From that day on, he sought out the Class Seven training grounds. His classmates seemed
to be conducting private training elsewhere, leaving the place eerily empty.
Shirone combined the photonization theory's general knowledge with the pure ability of
the Spirit Zone. At first, it was difficult to grasp the sensation.
Shirone stood between general knowledge and pure ability. The moment he erased himself
from that state, the two would merge, activating the magic.
In a trance-like state, Shirone cast the spell. A sensation of his body disintegrating
washed over him as light flashed. The moment he nodded forward to advance, the world melted away and
engulfed him.
"Gaaah!"
After moving five meters, Shirone tumbled to the ground upon arrival. It felt as if every
bone in his body had shattered.
"Ugh...!"
Gritting his teeth through the pain, the word "failure" etched itself into his
mind. Unless the photonization perfectly shed his mass, the impact of near-light-speed movement would strike
him in full.
This was too dangerous a spell to attempt alone.
But without a private tutor or even an upperclassman to consult, Shirone had no choice
but to learn through trial and error.
'I won't give up. I'll definitely succeed.'
From that day on, Shirone went through countless trials, correcting his mistakes. It
might have seemed like a brute-force method, but in the end, the only thing that remained was the memory of
learning through his body.
After a few days, an unidentified person appeared in the training grounds, observing
Shirone. They were likely a spy sent by someone, but he paid no mind. There was no better place to practice
anyway.
How many times had he rolled on the ground or scraped his lips against it? Bruises
covered his body, and his joints ached every morning.
Finally, the last day arrived.
With ten minutes left until midnight, Shirone staggered back to his starting position.
"I'll never... give up...!"
Dust filled his throat, making it sting. His half-lidded eyes fixed on the center of the
training grounds as he stood there, swaying.
Most of the errors had been corrected. All that remained was the perseverance to endure
the pain and the will to overcome fear.
Shirone couldn't move. The thought of casting the spell again terrified him. His stamina
was depleted, and even the slightest touch sent sharp pains through his bruised bones.
"Let's do this... I have to...!"
Muttering to himself in a daze, Shirone wondered if this might be for the best. Perhaps
he could forget himself without even trying.
"Instant... Movement."
At the极限 point where his upper body leaned forward, Shirone's physical form stretched ten
meters. Though incomparable to light, to human eyes, it was as fleeting as a flash.
Upon reaching his destination, Shirone barely caught himself from toppling forward.
"......"
He stood there for a long time. He felt nothing. This time, the usual excruciating pain
was absent.
"Hehe, I did it."
The chime of midnight rang out.
Listening to that solemn sound, Shirone collapsed to the ground, still in his standing
posture. The scent of earth tickled his nose.
In thirty days, the distance he had covered with instant movement was a mere ten meters.
But it was the first ten meters he had ever leaped.
'How far can I go with this?'
To cross the 700-meter "Uncrossable Bridge," he would need to cast the spell at
least seventy times. And if it were a race, stamina would also be a concern.
Casting instant movement once was far less efficient than casting an offensive spell
once. For moving ten meters, it would be better to just run quickly.
The key lay in chaining the spells.
If he couldn't cast them consecutively, instant movement was worse than useless.
If he arrived at his destination in a perfectly photonized state, inertia would vanish.
This prevented the mage's body from being shattered, but chaining spells required the same level of focus as
the first.
'No time for this. I can't lie down. I need more practice. At least the chaining part
needs work...'
His will remained strong, but he couldn't move a finger. His consciousness was sucked
into the abyss, and Shirone's eyelids slowly closed.
The next morning arrived.
***
Mark opened the window and welcomed the refreshing morning breeze. Stretching lazily, he
headed to the bathroom to wash his face. A smile never left his lips as he changed clothes.
Today would be a historic day in his life.
'Fast-tracking from Class Seven. Hehe. I can't wait to see how the Class Six kids treat
me.'
Mark's physique was impressively robust for a fifteen-year-old. In the world of magic,
physical strength wasn't paramount, but younger kids often cowered before sheer size. Combined with his
innate magical talent, no one in Class Seven dared to challenge him. This allowed him to push Shirone aside
and seize this opportunity, fueling his pride even further.
"Young master, how are you feeling?"
A slender man opened the door and greeted him. He was the mage who had privately tutored
Mark in instant movement for the past month. Though hastily hired, he was a certified 9th-rank mage and
skilled in espionage.
"I'm fine. What about Shirone?"
"Still the same. Even if instant movement is easy to learn, there's a limit to what
one can do alone. Even if he takes the promotion exam, he won't stand a chance against you."
"Hmm. Still, I shouldn't let my guard down. His Spirit Zone is larger than
mine."
Mark was satisfied with his own words. They often said a genius's weakness was laziness,
but he was different. With innate talent and hard work, there was no way he could lose to Shirone.
"By the way, how many are participating in the operation?"
"As of yesterday, four."
"That's quite few. The chance to receive private tutoring from a certified mage is
rare."
"Perhaps they don't know their place or are blinded by petty pride."
Mark scoffed as he recalled the students who had refused to join the operation. Those
with no talent had kicked away their own chance for early promotion, blinded by their delusions.
'Whatever. The promotion will be mine anyway—Slider Mark's.'
The Slider family belonged to the third tier of the noble hierarchy, not the highest, but
their influence in the magical society was considerable, as most family members worked in the Magic
Association.
The haughty first-tier heirs might sneer, but those kids wouldn't even enroll in the
academy if they lacked confidence in their magic, let alone linger in places like Class Seven.
In any competition, there were upper and lower ranks. Mark had gathered the middling
students to form his operation squad. The offer to help them secure jobs in the Association after graduation
had swayed a few. Facing the needle-thin chance of passing, they found it more practical to make a deal.
"Four is enough. We'll crush him early on."
"What about instant movement?"
Mark gave a thumbs-up as he opened the door to leave.
"Perfect. I could cross 700 meters blindfolded."
"I wish you success, young master."
The certified 9th-rank mage treated Mark with respect until the very end. Though Mark was
a junior far beneath him, he was also the son of his employer—the father who served as a technical advisor
for the Magic Association.
Mark headed to the advanced class building. Though classes were canceled for the
promotion exam, students not participating in the test remained for self-study. Mark went there first to
meet with his operation squad.
As expected, the expressions of the self-studying students were grim. The guilt of not
even qualifying for the exam weighed on them.
Mark reveled in his superiority as he scanned their faces. Those left in the classroom
today would likely spend their lives polishing his shoes—a thought that made him laugh.
"Hey, Maria! Did you give up on the exam too?"
Mark waved at Maria as she approached from afar. Her face paled at the sight. After
reporting Shirone's bullying to Siana, she had become an outcast herself.
Mark looked at the flustered Maria with disgust.
'Tch. A bat-like girl. She was all for bullying when it suited her, but now she's trying
to slip away alone? Useless, just useless. Why does someone like her even attend school?'
Trembling, Maria spoke first.
"Mark, aren't you going to the training grounds? Are you here to study?"
"Haha! As if! Unlike you, I'm not here to study. I came to meet my friends since I
have time to spare."
Being talked down to by someone four years younger left Maria feeling gloomy. But with
her lack of skill, she had no one to blame.
"What are you doing here, then? Do you think studying today will change anything? No
wonder your grades are terrible. You should be at the training grounds observing. Learning from those better
than you is also studying."
"Yeah, right..."
Maria maintained an awkward smile as she endured Mark's lecture. She just wanted to end
the conversation and leave.
"Well, good luck. I hope you pass."
"Kick! Do you really mean that?"
Mark's sneer made Maria's heart sink.
"I've tolerated you so far because we were classmates. But once I become your
senior, you'd better be prepared. I won't forget how you tattled to the teacher."
Maria tried to defend herself in panic, but her mind went blank. In the end, she bowed
her head and scurried away.
Mark snorted and entered the classroom where his squad waited. Three boys and one
girl—students who had trained for a month to hinder Shirone. Their focus had been on scenarios within 100
meters.
Shirone would surely expect interference, so they had to eliminate him early. Deliberate
collisions could lead to penalties, but Mark didn't care. Compensation would cover any sacrifices.
"So? Is the plan solid?"
"Yeah. Two will latch onto Shirone right at the start. The other two will cover you.
If Shirone pushes past that, we'll have to resort to the last resort."
They only had two chances to take Shirone down. But for a showdown within 100 meters, it
was satisfactory.
Shirone's leap distance was likely the standard ten meters. If so, the squad only needed
to make him fall within the first ten leaps.
"I won't forget today's help. Once I fast-track, I'll take care of you."
"Right. We're counting on you."
Mark was satisfied. Everything was going according to plan. He would be the proud
protagonist of a double promotion.
"Alright! Let's go!"
