Chapter 103:
Golden Victory (2)
On the fifth day since Shirone's death.
All the students had regained their memories. The children were particularly confused.
But since they were also reincarnates with exceptional mental resilience compared to ordinary people, the
aftereffects weren't as severe as expected.
But the real challenge was just beginning.
The incident where the school was occupied by monsters exposed the vulnerabilities in its
security systems. Especially for a school operating a dormitory system, safety was the top priority.
The biggest problem was that a student had died. And not just any student—Arian Shirone,
one of the school's most promising talents.
At Iruki and Neid's request, Shirone was placed in the infirmary instead of the morgue.
Common sense dictated that a funeral should be held, but the faculty were hesitant.
The reason the student council hadn't yet moved to assign blame was that Shirone's death
hadn't been officially confirmed.
In essence, they were planning to avoid responsibility by turning Shirone into a ghost.
Of course, many teachers also felt pangs of conscience about this approach. Currently,
they were gathered in the faculty meeting room, emphasizing the urgency of the situation.
"We must hold Shirone's funeral immediately. We also need to inform the parents. If
we don't handle this now, the school's policies will be criticized in the future."
"This isn't something to decide lightly. If word gets out that a student has died,
the repercussions will be enormous. We could lose the reputation we've built over the years in an
instant."
"So you're suggesting we lie and say the dead student is alive? It'll come out
eventually! The school's stance now should be to make a conscientious declaration. The issue isn't
reputation—the school might have to close its doors."
"Who doesn't know that? We're just saying we need a little more time to think."
"Why can't you see that the longer we delay, the worse it gets? Currently, even
Arcane's disciples are missing. Letting criminals escape is also a critical issue. We need to act now."
The teachers delaying Shirone's funeral were divided into two factions. Those making
political calculations wanted to buy as much time as possible to manage the students' anger.
On the other hand, some teachers genuinely couldn't accept Shirone's death. Notably,
Ethela, Sad, and Siena, who had been directly involved in the incident, were skeptical about Shirone's
death.
"Headmaster, please speak! I heard the student council is convening today. The
faculty must also take action."
The faculty's urgency was also influenced by the student council. If students backed by
numerous families began to denounce the school, they would miss their chance to act.
"I'm in favor of observing."
Most of the teachers stood up.
"Headmaster!"
Alpheas understood their feelings. The longer they delayed, the worse the school's
position became. But for him, something was more important than the school's survival: determining Shirone's
exact condition.
Was Shirone dead?
It was an absurd agenda to propose at a magic school, the symbol of intellect.
That made him even more cautious. If the deceased hadn't been Shirone, would they have
even entertained such doubts?
'We must think rationally. But I can't.'
Perhaps Shirone was receiving special treatment. But even knowing that, Alpheas couldn't
ignore the subtle feeling lodged in the corner of his mind.
***
"Let's not jump to conclusions about Shirone. We're currently lacking information
about Unlockers."
Ethela, who shared Alpheas's view, added.
"Shirone entered the Immortal Function three months ago. And in this battle, he
opened it to an extreme degree. The monastic order recognizes the Immortal Function as a process of
spiritual sublimation. It might be a matter separate from life."
"What nonsense is that? We're not here to listen to the monastic order's stance. We
want to resolve this situation as quickly as possible."
Some teachers didn't understand Ethela's words. The Immortal Function was a state, not a
level. No matter how deeply learned a teacher was, they couldn't confidently claim to understand the realm
of enlightenment better than a beggar on the street.
Ethela had been rebuked by a teacher who spoke directly to Alpheas.
"Headmaster, change your mind. Frankly, this all started because of your personal
grudges. For us, nothing is more important than the school. If this continues, the worst might happen."
Alpheas looked around the room. Dozens of eyes silently pressured him.
"The worst? What exactly do you think the worst is?"
"The school might be ordered to close."
"No, the worst isn't that."
A teacher, seemingly frustrated, struck his chest and said.
"I can't understand your stance, Headmaster. What could be worse than the school
closing?"
"The worst for a school is when all its students and teachers die."
The meeting room fell silent. The gazes fixed on Alpheas began to waver.
"If it weren't for Shirone, no one would have survived. And that list of casualties
would have included all of you."
Alpheas was disappointed by the teachers' tendency to exploit a student's death rather
than honor it.
"Isn't there even a sliver of possibility left? Even if it's less than one percent,
didn't that child give his life for us? Then shouldn't we at least try until the very end? Even if the
school closes, isn't it our duty as teachers to do everything we can to bring Shirone back?"
"But Headmaster, what is the school to us? I pity Shirone too, but if we close like
this……."
"The school won't close. I'll take full responsibility. So please, work for
Shirone's sake. Shouldn't we at least make this sacrifice for that child?"
The teachers couldn't refute Alpheas's words. Whatever the reason, the fact that they
owed their lives to a student was undeniable.
With that, the meeting ended, and the teachers left the room with dark expressions.
***
The Magic School Student Council.
Representatives from each class gathered in the large meeting room. Unlike the teachers
worried about their rice bowls, these sensitive students felt betrayed by Alpheas's past.
"Greetings. I am Amira, the student council president. I've convened this meeting to
discuss the current situation. Please share constructive opinions."
Amira from Class Two declared the meeting open from the podium. Specializing in climate
magic, she was twenty-two this year and one of the top candidates for graduation.
As a prominent left-leaning figure in the student council, the tone of the discussion was
predictable. As expected, the first to request the floor were the conservatives defending the school's
stance.
"Seriel from Class Three. I'm aware of the serious criticism against Headmaster
Alpheas within the school. But I believe we must be cautious. The school's honor is the students' honor.
Attacking the faculty in this situation could have negative repercussions for future graduates."
"Kas from Class Four. Seriel's opinion has merit, but this agenda concerns the
faculty's morality. Even if it happened forty years ago, it was a criminal act. If we don't hold them
accountable, the school has no future."
Naturally, Amira sided with Kas. But as the chair, she only nodded without showing it
outwardly.
"Olivia from Class Six. The main issue now is whether Shirone is dead. As we know,
Shirone is dead. But a minority of students and teachers are distorting that fact. Because of this, the
school has let two suspects allied with Arcane escape, and the headmaster's indictment is being delayed.
This is clearly evasion of responsibility, and the student council must step in."
Amira was angry for the same reason. The teachers were delaying Alpheas's indictment
under the pretext that Shirone's condition was uncertain.
Seriel retorted.
"Aren't you overinterpreting the faculty's intentions? Shirone's heart isn't
beating, but his body isn't decaying either. Regarding the Immortal Function, I understand there's much
debate even in the Ivory Tower. Considering these factors, the faculty's judgment isn't entirely
unreasonable."
The students reacted indifferently. Even the teachers struggled to understand the
Immortal Function, so it was impossible for the students to grasp. Some didn't even know what the Immortal
Function was.
Amira was no different. The highest state a mage could achieve. But so what? Magic was a
knowledge-based phenomenon. No matter how lofty it sounded, its significance didn't resonate.
"Um, Veronica from Class Ten."
An eight-year-old raised her hand. The student council elected representatives by class,
so even a child's words carried equal weight.
"Yes. Go ahead, Veronica."
"What did Alpheas grandpa do wrong?"
Her voice sounded on the verge of tears. Amira frowned and sighed.
'This is why I suggested separating by class.'
Veronica sniffled and said.
"Even if Alpheas grandpa did something wrong, can't we forgive him?"
"That's not possible, Veronica. This concerns the school's honor."
"But…… everyone makes mistakes, right?"
The room buzzed. This statement caused quite a stir. Seeing an unexpected opportunity,
the conservatives listened intently to Veronica, monitoring the situation.
"I get scolded by mom a lot too. But never too harshly. She says everyone makes
mistakes. Alpheas grandpa made a mistake, but he's led the school well until now. So can't we forgive
him?"
Seizing the moment, Mark raised his hand and stood.
"Mark from Class Seven. I agree with Veronica. Of course, those who commit crimes
must be punished. But if we, who trusted the school's security and maintained the status quo, act
aggressively, it's overreach. Unless the faculty intends to condone the situation, the student council must
also have the wisdom to wait."
'Well done, Mark.'
Seriel gave a thumbs-up under the table, and Mark nodded in acknowledgment.
The mood shifted, and opinions became evenly divided.
Amira predicted the votes based on the speeches. Overall, the lower classes sided with
the school, while the graduating classes criticized it. The upper classes were split.
'This isn't good. I might lose.'
At this rate, there would be no way to indict Alpheas. She needed to find a way to turn
the tide, but the younger students, more emotionally driven, were hard to sway.
"Ardino Fermi from Class One."
All eyes turned to Fermi, who had been silent until now. As the top-ranked graduating
student and the leading candidate for graduation, everyone was curious about his thoughts.
Seriel watched Fermi uneasily. A young man with neatly combed dark brown hair, dressed in
formal attire. But beneath his authoritative appearance lurked a mischievous streak.
Though capable of graduating, he repeatedly failed for some reason. A strong candidate
for student council president, he eventually stepped aside, handing the position to Amira.
Frankly, no one knew what went on in his mind. The only thing known was his love for
chaos and enjoyment of others' conflicts.
"Everyone here owes Shirone a debt. Without him, we'd already be dead. So it's
natural some can't accept his death."
The students grew solemn. They all knew how hard Shirone had fought for them.
But Seriel grew anxious. Fermi wasn't the type to feel gratitude toward anyone. She
couldn't understand why he brought this up now.
"I'm also grateful to Shirone. But the faculty are using him as a shield. So our
judgment isn't overreach. It's a fight to honor Shirone's spirit."
"What do you mean by honoring his spirit? Shirone isn't even confirmed dead
yet……!"
"It's already been five days. Shirone's heart isn't beating. None of you here fail
to miss Shirone. Isn't that right?"
Most nodded. Even if they didn't feel that way, mourning someone's death wasn't hard.
"Everyone misses Shirone and is grateful to him. So let me ask again. Does anyone
here truly believe Shirone is alive?"
Seriel realized Fermi's strategy. Separating emotion from reason, guilt would vanish, and
reality would become clearer. The students would judge coldly.
"Of course, no one does. It's not cruel—it's common sense. Yet the faculty still
insist Shirone is alive. Like a cult."
Seriel slammed the table and stood.
"That's not true! Shirone's condition is clearly unusual……!"
"What exactly is unusual? I've read the report on the Immortal Function. But the
state of magic has nothing to do with biological death. I've also received intelligence that Shirone's body
has been embalmed."
"That, that's……!"
It was Alpheas's order. If the Immortal Function involved the mind, the physical body
needed preservation here. But how Fermi knew this secret, known only to a few, was a mystery.
Amira's eyes flashed sharply as she asked.
"Embalmed? Is Fermi's claim true?"
Seriel glared at Fermi. Seeing his innocent smile only irritated her more.
'You jerk. You have no stake in this. You just want to stir trouble.'
"Seriel, please answer."
Seriel sighed. A fight they could have won had become uncertain due to a single misstep.
"I invoke my right to remain silent. If you want further answers, please convene a
formal hearing."
Remaining silent implied affirmation, but it was worlds apart from outright admission.
The importance of the right to silence was leaving room for rebuttal. There was no need to drive in the nail
prematurely when unknowns could flip the situation.
Amira didn't delay. If the tide was turning, now was the best time to act. She called for
a vote.
"Then let's begin the secret ballot."
The result…… was as Seriel expected.
***
"Alpheas must resign! Submit to the disciplinary committee's investigation!"
"Hold Shirone's funeral! Playing with the dead is no act for a teacher!"
A hundred students, aligned with the student council's decision, held up picket signs and
protested in front of the infirmary.
Amira, at the forefront, rallied the students.
"The school must disclose the full details of this incident. Indicting Headmaster
Alpheas is the only way to secure the magic school's future!"
"Right! Alpheas must resign! Hold Shirone's funeral!"
Their shouts reached Amy and the others through the infirmary window. Then the door burst
open, and Seriel rushed in.
"Amy, it's bad!"
"What's going on? Why are they suddenly like this?"
Amy's panic was understandable. She thought the student council meeting was ending, but
now they were gathered, demanding Shirone's funeral.
"The graduating class is conspiring to indict the headmaster. I thought we could at
least protect Shirone, but Fermi stirred up chaos……."
Amy could guess the situation without seeing it. Fermi was more than capable of such
mischief. The student council was moving to oust the headmaster. But she couldn't blame them. If the
school's leader had moral flaws, cleaning house was good for the future.
But this was too extreme. That's why Fermi's antics were annoying. While the faculty's
weak response had ignited the student council's anger, Fermi had lit the fuse.
"That jerk. He owes his life to Shirone too."
"What do we do, Amy?"
Amy bit her nails.
"What can we do? We hold out."
Alpheas wanted to observe Shirone for now. It was miraculous he'd convinced the teachers,
given his responsibility for the incident.
Neid, peering out the window, snapped at Iruki.
"Happy now? A mob of kids who think like you is here. Shouldn't you be out there
with them?"
"Want me to join them?"
Iruki shot back without backing down. The two had been awkward since the battle.
Seriel frowned and said.
"Enough. Fighting among ourselves won't solve anything. We need to unite now."
"Tch! You think Iruki here will cooperate? Heartless jerk."
"I said enough. And Iruki, restrain yourself. The situation is serious. One word
could sway public opinion, so stay here for now."
"Wasn't planning to leave anyway."
Iruki buried his face in the table, lost in thought. It had already been five days since
Shirone's death. Much longer than he'd expected.
'If the student council is moving, the faculty won't last long. Maybe 48 hours…….'
The infirmary's atmosphere grew icy. Everyone was on edge, worn down by the situation.
Amy stroked Shirone's forehead. Common sense said he was dead. But clinging to hope
now—was that even normal?
'Please, please come back, Shirone…….'
Amy couldn't be sure Shirone would return. But with his heart stopped for five days,
certainty was long past. Now it was a matter of faith.
