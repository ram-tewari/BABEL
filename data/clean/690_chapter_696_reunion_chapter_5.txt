Chapter 696:
Reunion Chapter (5)
Shirone and Rian jumped down, and the slowly rising priest opened his eyes wide.
It was impossible for a human to lift their eyelids while their pupils were slashed.
"No way… the High Judge of Teraphos?"
"Yes. I'm borrowing this body to deliver my voice. Humans call it an oracle."
"Can you possess anyone?"
"Not exactly. You need to connect to the Teraphos channel, which is only possible in a
trance state. Usually, dreams are used. But this guy showed no signs of entering a trance."
Shirone looked at the priest with pity.
"The stronger the channeler, the higher the density of the mind they can transmit. Among
humans, Maylay would be the most superior, but even someone like this can manage small talk."
"So… you came here for small talk?"
Was the High Judge of Teraphos living such a boring life?
"I came to let you know that any human connected to the Teraphos channel can communicate
with me at any time. This human's channeling was too weak, so it only succeeded at the brink of death, but a
powerful channeler could receive my entire mind."
Shirone recalled the execution of the Avalokitesvara Annihilation through Maylay.
"You should've said that sooner."
"The council just approved it. Also, this means the Teraphos species have recognized you
as humanity's representative."
"Hmm."
As Shirone fell into thought, a groan echoed.
"Ah, ahhh…"
Emma, still unable to move, was curled up in a corner, trembling.
"What about her? Can she channel?"
"Her spiritual senses are too weak. She wouldn't hear my voice even in her sleep. The
only one here capable of channeling is this human."
'Even if she's pathetic, she's still a mage, huh?'
Emma clasped her hands and asked,
"Are you… really the god of Teraphos?"
"We don't have a word for 'god,' but I am what humans call one."
"Then… you know everything about me?"
"Your name is Emma. The biological partner of Adoks, a centurion in the Kazura border
guard. But by human standards, you betrayed your vows and had biological relations with Miguelan, Adoks'
subordinate, 63 times over a month. Recently, you even planned to poison Adoks—"
"Stop! Stop it!"
The horror of having her secrets exposed was overwhelming.
"Please forgive me! I'll live righteously from now on! My husband drinks all the time,
breaks things, and hits me when he comes home… And he was the one who cheated first! With that 38-year-old
bar hostess!"
As Emma's eyes filled with venom again, Kido clicked his tongue.
"This household is a mess. Well, life's like that."
Teraphos had classified Emma as evil, but that was because they observed humans from
outside their lives.
Once you step onto the stage of life, only the emotions of the moment feel vivid, and
it's impossible to tell what came first.
"I am not a being who forgives."
Emma was half out of her mind.
"I'm sorry! I'm really sorry! Please forgive me. If my husband finds out I came here,
I'll die!"
Shirone reassured her.
"Don't worry. I won't tell anyone. But… could you let us stay at your place for a few
days?"
"Huh? Oh, yes! If that will atone for my sins…"
She seemed to mistakenly believe Shirone had the authority to forgive her sins.
Of course, after witnessing the miracle of Teraphos standing there with slashed pupils,
it was a natural reaction.
"What's the plan?"
When Rian asked, Shirone turned to the High Judge and said,
"Tell the king of Kazura, Orkamp, that I've arrived. Through an oracle."
Kido clapped his hands.
"Aha. That's one way."
A sort of communication satellite.
If Shirone's words were received from space and transmitted to the faithful, they could
deliver the message to any country in the world.
"Is that so? Wait a moment."
The High Judge closed his eyes, and the locations of all Teraphos faithful around the
world flashed in his mind.
"There aren't many Teraphos channels in Kazura. Even if there are, most are low-level
like this one."
'So it won't work.'
Each faithful was like a relay station, but compared to humanity's numbers, they were
few.
"I found the closest channel. There's one Teraphos faithful within a kilometer of
Kazura's royal palace."
"Within a kilometer… A bureaucrat?"
The higher the rank, the more convenient it would be.
"A military officer serving in the royal guard. He keeps his faith in Teraphos a secret
from everyone but his family."
"That's fine. They'll have no choice but to believe. First, send the location of Emma's
house. Say that Shirone requests an audience. And…"
Shirone chose his words carefully.
"Tell him I have something to say about his son."
The High Judge's eyelids trembled slightly, and that was all.
"Transmission complete. The oracle will be received tonight after he falls asleep. It
should be delivered by dawn tomorrow."
It was an incredible time-saver, but Kido suggested an easier method.
"Why not just possess the officer like you did now? Write it down on paper and leave."
"Forced trance states disrupt order. Only humanity's representative, the Hexa, can summon
me."
Shirone nodded.
"Got it. Then I'll wait for the palace's response."
With that, the communication ended. As the High Judge's expression faded, the fake priest
covered his eyes and screamed.
"Aaaah! It hurts! It hurts! Where am I?"
'He doesn't remember.'
Maylay had been the same.
Those whose legs had been severed had already died from excessive blood loss, so escaping
the cave without sight would be difficult.
'Spirit Zone. But is it possible in this state?'
Rian placed a hand on Shirone's shoulder.
"Let's get out of here, Shirone. Staying longer won't do any good."
"…Right."
As Shirone's group followed Emma out of the cave, the fake cult leader's screams echoed
behind them.
***
After descending the mountain while avoiding the border guards, Shirone could gauge
Kazura's current situation.
The number of soldiers had been reinforced, but their morale had weakened, with some even
napping sporadically.
The king's authority had eroded, and their self-esteem had hit rock bottom.
"That's the village."
It was a village of about 40 households, with all buildings made of wood except for the
government office.
Looking down at the village illuminated by torches, Kido said,
"I think I'd rather stay in the mountains."
In a city, it might be different, but to the rural villagers, a goblin was just a
monster.
"Are you sure? It might take up to two days if things run late."
"Heh, what are you talking about? Goblins aren't as weak as humans."
Shirone and Rian were superhumans who had trained relentlessly, but most people couldn't
match a goblin's physical abilities.
"The mountains are more comfortable. It's easier to find food, too."
Human food wasn't bad, but the taste of blood-soaked raw meat was irresistible.
"Sorry. I'll send a signal when someone arrives from the palace."
"It's fine."
Kido spun his spear and vanished from sight in an instant.
'Kido won't get caught by the guards.'
As Shirone finished his thought, Emma nervously spoke up.
"Um, Your Holiness, what should I tell my husband?"
Bringing two strange young men into the house would enrage him.
"Introduce us as merchants. We can store our weapons in rings. And we'll pay for your
hospitality."
Seeing the pouch of gold coins, Emma sighed in relief.
Money wasn't her priority, but her husband Adoks would sell his own family for it.
As they passed the wooden fence, chickens flapped their wings, and dogs barked at the
strangers' scent.
Arriving at the thirteenth house on the village's east side, Emma unlocked the door and
turned to Shirone's group.
"Come in."
The 14-pyeong space had no separation between the kitchen and bedroom, with a staircase
leading to the second floor in the corner.
Adoks, sitting at the table, snapped at her.
"Where the hell have you been?"
Emma's shoulders tensed at the sight of her husband, who was unusually home today.
"Honey, you're back already?"
Adoks was a rough-bearded man in his mid-30s.
"It's been dark for ages, and you're spouting nonsense? Do I look like some drunkard who
drinks all day?"
With a hangover-induced stomachache, Adoks scowled and asked Shirone and Rian,
"Who are you? Never seen you before."
"We're merchants. Could we stay here for two days?"
"What kind of merchant comes to this backwater? Are you… heretics? Be honest."
Reports had come in that the Teraphos cult was approaching nearby villages to extort
money.
"Haha, of course not. We're from a new merchant group looking for places to deliver
goods. We'll just survey the market for a day and leave."
Such things weren't unheard of.
"What merchant group?"
"We'd rather not say. This business is sensitive to rumors…"
"Are you kidding me?"
Shirone hastily added,
"The Neid Merchant Group. Please keep it a secret. Emma told us you're influential here…"
When he took out a gold coin, Adoks' pupils shook.
'That much money…'
Misah, the bar hostess, would faint with joy.
'They're just trying to butter me up, huh?'
But as a soldier, taking money outright could cause trouble later.
"The hardware store across the street also takes lodgers. Why not go there? It's just an
attic room, so it's no different from here."
Shirone understood Adoks' psychology.
"Hehe, staying at a store would draw attention from other merchants. You know how this
business is."
"Even so, this amount is too much."
"It's only right to pay more for the inconvenience. Think of it as a merchant's
sincerity."
"Well, I guess so."
Adoks took the gold coin from Shirone and confirmed it was the international standard
issued by the Red Line Currency Bureau.
"Fine. Treat them well, make sure they're comfortable. I'll be staying out tonight."
Emma could guess where he was going, but today, she had no choice but to let him go.
"Yes, come home early."
Despite her gentle tone, Emma gritted her teeth the moment Adoks left.
"That bastard. He's going to that hostess again. He'll flaunt that gold coin and show
off."
There was nothing to say to comfort her.
"Let's eat first. There should be some alcohol, too."
Perhaps unable to stay sober, Emma downed the remaining half-bottle of alcohol.
"Honestly, that Misah is only better than me at entertaining men! My husband's an idiot,
but that woman's worse!"
As Emma ranted, Shirone listened without showing annoyance.
"Sigh, I wish my husband was half as good as you, Your Holiness."
When she drunkenly slumped onto the table, Rian carried her to bed.
"Are you sure about this, Shirone? Wouldn't it be better to wait at an inn?"
The fact that Emma's husband was a centurion in the border guard was concerning.
"Adoks is fine. He can't file an international lawsuit based on speculation. The real
risk is Emma."
"Right. If Emma talks, things will spiral out of control. That's why you told the High
Judge we'd stay here."
Because he couldn't bring himself to kill Emma.
"Yeah. Until someone arrives from Kazura's palace, we can't let our guard down. If
there's even a hint she might talk…"
"Then what?"
Shirone's gaze turned cold.
"Then I'll leave it to you."
Mages were cold-hearted people.
"…Got it."
Shirone wanted to eliminate the risk before it became uncontrollable, yet he hadn't
abandoned Emma in the cave.
He wanted to find a path where everyone could be happy.
'Walking the tightrope between ideals and reality. Is that what it means to be a mage?'
Like Jane, who had to claw at her own heart to save Aria.
'Life is really hard for them.'
Rian thought he could never become a mage, even in another life.

The next morning.
Adoks left for work, rubbing his stomach.
"Lieutenant! Miguelan!"
Miguelan opened the door and entered.
"You called, sir?"
Unaware that Miguelan was his wife's lover, Adoks pretended to sip from a cup and said,
"You still have some honey, right? Mix it in and bring it to me."
"Did you drink again?"
Adoks had sworn off alcohol yesterday and left work early.
After gulping down the honey water, Adoks wiped his beard and said,
"Ah, I feel alive now."
"You went to Misah, didn't you?"
"Heh, shut up. I hit the jackpot yesterday. It was an amazing night."
As Adoks reminisced, his expression darkened.
"Oh, and you, go check on my house."
"What's wrong?"
"Something's off. Some merchants came yesterday, and I think they're up to something."
"Are they heretics?"
"Hey, have you ever seen heretics spend money? Ah, no."
Adoks hastily shook his head.
"Anyway, check it out. I was out last night. See if anything happened with Emma."
'What a piece of trash.'
While he partied with a bar hostess, he sent his lieutenant to spy on his wife.
"Understood."
Seeing Emma would be a bonus, so Miguelan rushed to Adoks' house.
'That guy's suspicious.'
The so-called merchants looked noble enough to be believed, and they were even handsome.
"Miguelan? What's wrong?"
Emma opened the door with a startled expression, but Miguelan kept his composure.
"The centurion sent me. He has an urgent message."
He wanted to say he loved her.
"You have guests. Sorry, but could you give us a moment?"
"We'll be upstairs."
As Shirone and Rian went up the stairs, Miguelan couldn't hold back and hugged Emma.
"I missed you, Emma."
"Why did you come now? What if we're caught?"
"Your husband sent me. That bastard. He spent the night with Misah again. Let's go
through with the plan we talked about."
The plan to poison Adoks.
"I can't. If we're caught…"
"Don't worry. No one cares if someone dies in this backwater."
Terrified at the thought of the gods listening, Emma hesitated.
"Trust me. We'll see this through together."
Miguelan found her even more endearing and leaned in for a kiss, but the door suddenly
opened as Adoks entered.
"Emma! Those merchants who were here— Huh?"
Miguelan and Emma quickly pulled apart, but it was too late.
"What? Why are you holding my wife's shoulder?"
"S-sir, why are you home?"
The gold coin Shirone had given him was the problem.
Most traveling merchants used the international standard, but Kazura's borders had been
closed for over a year.
'They might really be heretics.'
But now, Adoks' mind was filled only with rage toward Miguelan.
"How dare you touch my wife!"
As his superior drew his sword, Miguelan raised his hands.
"Sir! It's a misunderstanding!"
"Misunderstanding? What misunderstanding? Was there honey on my wife's shoulder?"
"Th-that's…!"
Miguelan's mind raced.
"The merchants! They tried to assault your wife!"
"What nonsense!"
"Why would I lie? I was just calming your terrified wife."
Adoks' eyes narrowed with murderous intent.
"…Where are those bastards now?"
"They're barricaded on the second floor. I blocked the exit, so they're trapped."
Miguelan planned to escape with Emma if Adoks rushed upstairs, but things didn't go as
planned.
"You damn brats! Get down here now! I'll slice you alive and eat you!"
The house shook from his roaring. Shirone and Rian calmly descended the stairs.
Unlike yesterday, Rian now carried a massive greatsword on his back, freezing Adoks'
face.
"Wh-what the hell are you?"
Rian gripped the greatsword's hilt and asked,
"Shirone, is it time?"
"……."
If Emma talked, Adoks and Miguelan would have to die too.
'Is there no other way?'
Then someone knocked loudly on the door.
"Is anyone home? I'm looking for someone."
"I'm busy! Get lost!"
The door swung open despite the rude dismissal, and a giant old man strode in.
"You little brat, are you ignoring me— Huh?"
The moment Adoks saw the emblem on the old man's armor, his face turned pale, as did
Miguelan's.
"Th-the royal guard?"
Sensing the tense atmosphere, the old man scanned the room and locked eyes with Shirone.
"Long time no see, Arian Shirone."
He had expected a quick route due to the urgency, but this was faster than anticipated.
'I knew it'd be him.'
Shirone, having reined in his killing intent, smiled at the familiar face.
"Yes. It's been a while."
The captain of Kazura's royal guard, Ritni Walker.
