Chapter 410:
If you travel about two hours by carriage from the city of Creas, you
will reach the small port town of Sanperos.
Galliante is a world-renowned resort, but outside of vacation season,
this place was just a quiet fishing village.
Arriving around noon, Shirone bought a handful of dried fish from a
stall.
The slightly aged dried fish was chewy like jelly and had a deep sea
flavor.
There were no regular ferries, so he would have to hire a captain
separately.
The fare was expensive, but the money he had saved up so far would be
enough.
When he asked a villager, they said it would take about an hour for the
boats that went out at dawn to return with their catch.
With nowhere else to go, Shirone walked to the breakwater and looked out
at the distant sea.
It wasn't his first time traveling, and his team members were waiting on
the island, but he felt like he was alone in the world.
'It's probably lunchtime at school by now.'
Shirone didn't think about it.
There was no place to return to. There shouldn't be.
"Are you Arian Shirone?"
A woman, dressed in attire completely unrelated to the Kingdom of
Tormia, stood behind Shirone.
Despite the warm weather, her sleeves extended below her wrists, and the
hood draped over her back was thicker than winter clothing.
The sight of her sucking on a pacifier was extremely odd, but her eyes
were deep and mysterious, creating an atmosphere that made it impossible to look at her
strangely.
Shirone turned his gaze to the strange creature floating above the
woman's head.
It was a squishy, transparent monster, with a rotating sphere inside
generating an electric field.
The auto-prism scattered light, creating a transparency effect within a
certain range.
It had almost no combat power but was an incredibly useful utility
monster, listed as a Tier 4 summoning magic.
Shirone looked at her warily.
In the worst-case scenario, he would draw Armand and fight, but if she
could summon a Tier 4 monster, she was likely more powerful than him.
"Who are you? How do you know my name...?"
"I am Zulu. Gaold told me to bring you."
"Huh? Da?"
Shirone was momentarily taken aback by her strange way of speaking, then
widened his eyes and looked directly at her face.
"What? Zulu?"
There was no way he wouldn't know the name of the world's only and
strongest summoning mage, capable of commanding a Tier 1 Lich.
Unable to believe it, Shirone pointed at her with his finger and asked
again.
"...Zulu?"
"Yes, Zulu."
Zulu nodded nonchalantly and said, "We don't have much time. We
need to cross the border before it gets dark, so follow me."
"Where is the president?"
"He has already arrived in Galliante and is preparing with Kangnan.
Are you coming or not?"
"I'll go."
Abandoning his fruitless search, Shirone approached Zulu, who then
deactivated the auto-prism and cast mass teleportation.
The scenery seemed to be pulled into a single point before expanding
into a new view.
They were now halfway up the mountain behind the port.
The breakwater where they had been standing was a small dot at the edge
of their vision, and below the mountain, the fishing village was spread out.
Zulu summoned the giant bird Kaidera there.
With smooth, hairless skin and a beak as long as an awl, its massive
claws seemed to grip the mountain.
Despite its fierce appearance, Kaidera nuzzled Zulu like a child, then
knelt and lowered one wing.
Shirone followed Zulu up the wing.
Kaidera's spine protruded from its skin like a shark's fin, reducing air
resistance and providing a stable place to sit in the hollow of its spine.
"Be careful. We'll be flying high."
Kaidera flapped its wings and pushed off the ground with its legs,
soaring vertically into the sky.
Shirone, pale-faced, stared at the rapidly approaching sky.
When they began flying on a normal trajectory, the ground was just a
distant dot.
"Kaidera is a lift-based giant bird. It doesn't need much oxygen,
so it can fly at higher altitudes than muscle-based creatures. This increases the chances of avoiding
the search algorithms of reconnaissance drones."
Reconnaissance drones with a basic diameter of 2 kilometers in their
spirit zone could easily search the sky.
However, the higher the altitude, the more they had to transform their
spirit zone into target or sniper mode, reducing the search range.
Shirone finally felt the reality of the situation.
The person in front of him was indeed Zulu.
"Thank you for coming to get me. Honestly, I didn't expect someone
as great as you to come."
"Gaold said the association would move soon. It seems there are
enemies within. We decided to prepare quickly and head to Heaven. That's why I'm in charge of
transportation. We're on our way to pick up a woman named Siena."
"Oh, I see."
"Of course, I would have come even if it wasn't urgent. Sein said
he would use whatever he could to achieve results. I'll be in charge of utility in the team, so let me
know if you need anything."
Zulu's words made sense.
From now on, they had to move organically as a team. Regardless of
seniority, everyone had to do their best in their roles.
"Yes! Thank you very much!"
Shirone bowed energetically.
Kentra City.
The meat produced in Kentra, Tormia's famous livestock area, is of such
high quality that it is designated as a tribute to the royal castle.
The restaurant business was also thriving, with entire streets filled
with restaurants.
People wearing cowboy hats were common, leather clothing was in fashion,
and whips were selling like hotcakes.
But the reason Siena chose this place for the meeting wasn't because
the meat was delicious or to buy Kentra specialties.
It was because this was the exact midpoint between Creas and
Bashka.
Siena checked the restaurant sign and opened the door.
Compared to Creas, it had a more casual atmosphere, and although she had
made a reservation, there were still some empty seats.
She drank the cup of water the waiter poured for her and waited
anxiously for the person she was meeting.
There were about 20 minutes left until the appointment, but she still
couldn't be sure if he would come.
No, if he was sane, he wouldn't come.
After a matchmaking meeting, she hadn't heard from him, and now she was
asking for a lunch meeting just because she received a congratulatory letter for becoming a certified
5th-level mage.
And she sent the invitation via express telegram.
No one would rush to Kentra City for lunch after receiving a telegram at
dawn.
'Nothing to lose.'
The door opened with a chime, and a man entered.
Parca Kuan, an instructor at the Kaizen Swordsmanship School.
His cold eyes, observing the atmosphere, were the same as during their
matchmaking meeting, but his attire was much more polished this time.
Kuan's icy gaze softened slightly when he saw Siena sitting at the
table.
As he moved with a limp, all the nobles' eyes focused on him.
He had never tried to hide his limp, but at times like this, he always
felt like an outcast in the crowd.
Especially when the people's gazes shifted from Kuan to Siena, he felt
an urge to cut down everyone present.
Siena stood up and raised her hand.
True to her nature, she didn't offer a warm smile, but she didn't care
about the people's stares.
There was also Armin, who had sacrificed his eyes for her.
Siena wasn't bothered by Kuan's limp at all.
Prejudiced gazes couldn't be hidden, so Kuan could sense Siena's
sincerity.
Perhaps that's why he had come this far, despite himself.
"Thank you for coming. I'm sorry for the sudden
request."
"I thought there must be a reason for the urgent telegram. Please,
have a seat."
The food arrived, but they still didn't speak. They just focused on
cutting and eating the meat.
Neither of them was used to such situations.
But Kuan, being the man, mustered the courage to speak first.
"Congratulations. A certified 5th-level mage at such a young age is
truly impressive."
Siena couldn't delay any longer.
She didn't have much time, and if Kuan refused, she would have to leave
for Galliante immediately.
"I sent the telegram because I have a favor to ask."
It was an odd thing to say in response to a congratulatory message, so
Kuan blinked, holding his utensils.
Then he nodded and said, "Yes, please go ahead."
Siena explained the project.
She withheld information that could endanger the team, but since they
would no longer have to worry about the world's scrutiny after tomorrow, the content was straightforward
and unfiltered.
"...So we need a hitman in the team. I think you're the right
person for the job."
Kuan, who had been motionless throughout the explanation, dropped his
utensils onto the plate with a clatter. He then gulped down water as if he had lost his appetite.
Siena waited calmly.
It was no different from asking him to go to his death. It would leave a
stain on his name in history.
Persuasion was impossible.
From now on, it was entirely up to his judgment.
Kuan stared at the restaurant floor, lost in thought. Then he looked
back at Siena and spoke.
"May I ask one question?"
"Anything. As long as it's information I can disclose."
"Why me?"
Siena's gaze turned cold.
"As you can see, my leg is not in perfect condition. And I'm a
swordsman who relies on my legs. Why does it have to be me?"
"That's because of your skill, Mr. Kuan..."
Siena swallowed the flattery she was about to say.
She knew why Kuan had come to this meeting. No man would travel a long
distance to have lunch with a woman he didn't like.
Although she had let Armin go in her heart, she didn't want to use
Kuan's feelings.
She would simply tell the truth and wait for his decision.
"Mr. Kuan, it's because it doesn't matter when you
die."
Siena spoke firmly.
"I heard you have no parents, siblings, or connections. If you die,
there's no one to take action. Even if things go wrong, there's no need to worry about the
aftermath."
Kuan stared at Siena with a blank expression.
No matter what, saying such things to someone's face...
He had thought things were going unusually well since he received the
urgent telegram.
"But it's not because I underestimated your skills. Your
swordsmanship is..."
"Heh. Heh heh heh!"
Kuan dropped his head and laughed, his shoulders shaking, causing Siena
to stop speaking with a tense expression.
"No need to worry about the aftermath? If you really thought
that..."
Kuan's eyes gleamed with a chilling light as he grinned.
"You've come to the right person."
Now he understood why it was this woman.
No prejudice, no pity.
For someone tired of the hypocritical lives of people, her blunt honesty
was refreshing.
Kuan retracted his earlier thoughts.
It seemed that God was still watching over his life.
Heaven.
What better place to serve as the grave of a sword demon?
Kuan placed his hands on the table and stood up.
"Let's go. It seems urgent. There's nothing to prepare. I settled
my affairs ten years ago."
"I appreciate it, but at least a word of notice..."
"Don't worry. This is the decision of a swordsman. If you resolve
yourself at the moment of death, it's too late. There's no need to look back on the way to the
battlefield."
It was an extreme resolve, but it was hard to find someone more suitable
for the role of a hitman.
Regardless of his motives, he was part of a suicide squad carrying the
future of humanity into the flames.
Siena sincerely respected his resolve.
"Thank you."
Kuan also gripped his sword handle and bowed his head, swearing as a
swordsman.
"The pleasure is mine."
