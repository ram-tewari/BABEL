Chapter 106:
Vajrayana (6)
Amira's steadfast eyes wavered for the first time. Even her iron will melted at the sight
of Alpheas's face.
How could it not? He was the man she had followed since she was a child wetting her bed.
"I'm sorry. For putting you through this."
Amira bowed her head, struggling to hold back her tears.
"I didn't mean to cause you pain, Principal. The more respected a person is, the more
they must face their mistakes. Otherwise, the school will…"
"I understand. I don't blame any of you here. You are the school's future."
Alpheas looked at Amira with pity. Every student gathered here was like a child he had
raised. He was sorry for hurting them.
Amira raised her head, her eyes glistening as she looked at Alpheas. He smiled kindly,
just as she remembered from her childhood.
"Will you trust me one more time? Once Shirone's matter is resolved, I'll handle
everything transparently. Even if punishment awaits, I'll face it proudly before my beloved students. I
won't run away."
Hot tears streamed down Amira's cheeks.
"You'll really come back? The person I admire…"
"Of course. I'll ensure no deceit in my punishment. I promise you this on my lifelong
honor."
Amira wiped her tears with her hand. She couldn't cry in front of Alpheas. She wanted to
show him how much she had grown since her childish days.
"Understood. Since you've said that, the student council will reconsider the matter.
Please don't do anything to tarnish your honor."
Alpheas smiled warmly. The child who once called him "Pwincipal!" while running to him
had grown into such a fine young woman.
'Erina, it seems my life wasn't entirely in vain.'
As the student council president made her decision, the protesters disbanded immediately.
The conservatives blocking the entrance also scattered, leaving the building's front desolate in an instant.
Only Fermi remained, scratching his head sheepishly.
"Tch, everything was going so well until the end."
Alpheas burst into laughter.
"Kekeke! Seems things didn't go your way this time, Fermi."
"Can't be helped. Honestly, the principal stepping in feels like cheating."
"Shouldn't you graduate soon? You'll be twenty-two next year."
"Who knows? Nowhere else pays as well as here."
"Is that so?"
Alpheas knew Fermi was the mastermind behind this incident. But to him, Fermi was just a
grown-up pupil he was proud of.
"Besides, it's still fun here. Well, I'll be going."
Without further farewells, Fermi saluted and walked away. After today, he likely wouldn't
step foot in the student council again. Peaceful places weren't profitable, after all.
***
Iruki and Neid walked to the infirmary in silence. It was the first time they had argued
so seriously in five years, leaving an awkward tension between them. But Neid, as usual, couldn't stand the
atmosphere.
"Hey! How can you not say a word? You didn't have to hide it from me! Did you just want
to look cool?"
"Funny. How could I discuss strategies with someone swimming in a sea of grief? Besides,
if the information leaked, they'd take countermeasures. Why can't you think straight?"
"Yeah, I'm an idiot! You're just so great, aren't you?"
Whether angry or cursing, the ice was broken, and the awkwardness vanished instantly.
Iruki asked Neid the most pressing question.
"By the way, how are you? No matter how angry you are, was revealing your old personality
really necessary?"
Neid, full of regret, clutched his head.
"Ugh, I don't know. I didn't have time to think about anything else. At least we didn't
fight."
"Well, I was so cool it probably didn't matter. You might've already forgotten."
"Funny. So you had it all planned out?"
"Heh, what's it to you? Bow to me already."
Their conversation ended as the infirmary door opened. Aimi came out to greet them. Iruki
scratched his head awkwardly. Unlike Neid, he still found it hard to act naturally around Aimi.
"Well, that's how it is. At least we protected Shirone…"
As he tried to brush it off, tears welled up in Aimi's eyes.
"Thank you. Thank you, Iruki…"
Iruki's face twisted in horror.
"Tch, whatever."
Weak to emotions, he fled into the infirmary. But there, another emotional
wreck—Seriel—waited with open arms.
Iruki dodged Seriel's embrace with boxing footwork and took refuge next to Shirone.
'Ugh, this is why I can't stand girls.'
At the door, Seriel and Aimi hugged each other and jumped up and down. Neid joined in,
and soon the infirmary was spinning with their joy.
"Woo-hoo! He's alive! Shirone is alive now!"
Amid the rare cheerful atmosphere, a faint smile touched Iruki's lips. But his heart
wasn't entirely at ease. The report had only delayed the acknowledgment of Shirone's death. Nothing had
truly improved.
Shirone still lay motionless, as if asleep. The promise to stay together after graduation
felt vivid, but the reality of his death was hard to accept.
'Come back. I'll wait for you, no matter how long.'
***
Shirone regained consciousness. More precisely, he went from madness to madness again.
His mind was like a coin spinning rapidly between the poles of sanity and insanity.
He had gone mad a thousand times. Desperately trying to hold onto his unraveling mind,
the overwhelming stimuli invaded his essence, altering his nature.
"You're a child someone picked up. Your parents would've been fine with anyone but you.
Did they ever regret raising you? They must have. They must have thought, 'Why did we pick this one?' If you
were their biological child, they wouldn't have felt that way."
"No! That's not true!"
"You're a bastard. Anyone in this world could've been an adopted child. They aren't your
real parents."
Shirone's particles trembled as a soul-wrenching cry echoed.
Ooooooh!
"Your essence is a vile predator. With superior power, you trample everyone. Your
strength inevitably brings someone's downfall. You're a tumor the world doesn't need."
Ooooooh!
Shirone went mad again. His mind reeked of foulness. If this fusion ended now, what would
he become? A demon? A yaksha? A devil? It was impossible to know.
The speed of his madness accelerated. As he reached his limit, two opposing natures began
to coexist simultaneously in his mind.
It was a new world.
In this supreme mental state, unreachable by any drug, Shirone finally found the only
solution to maintain his sanity.
The origin point.
If the boundary of the cycle was the extreme, the origin was the starting point of the
extreme. But it held the potential to encompass the entire world.
Some called it a spot. Some called it Spirit Zone. Some called it the Taiji. But
magicians referred to it as this:
Infinity.
Shirone's eyes snapped open. His Spirit Zone was sucked into his mind, and the countless
particles scattering at superluminal speeds began reconstructing into his spiritual form.
At the center of infinity, Shirone stood unshaken. The voices tormenting him vanished as
his spiritual form hardened like diamond.
The state of Vajrayana, attainable only after enduring thousands of trials.
Indestructible Vajra.
Shirone gazed at the exit with his brilliantly burning body. There was no more confusion.
As he stepped forward, the warm, bright light at the exit merged with him, becoming one.
A small, transparent glass orb floated before him. Inside it was the temple he had just
left, and the woman looked up at him.
"You're leaving after all, Shirone."
"I'm sorry. But I want to go back."
"No need to apologize. Honestly, I didn't think you'd succeed. You have the
qualifications."
Shirone hadn't expected her to let him go so easily, making his steps heavier. Perhaps
that was why. Even though it no longer mattered, he couldn't help but ask.
"Were you lonely?"
The woman blinked in surprise, then smiled warmly.
"You're a kind person, Shirone. But gods don't feel loneliness. I'm just a little sad to
see you go. Just a little."
"Thank you for reviving me. I wanted to say that."
Her expression turned solemn.
"Remember this. There are no second chances. Having reached the state of Vajrayana, you
can no longer receive invitations from me."
Shirone nodded firmly and stepped toward the exit. As his consciousness was pulled into
another dimension of spacetime, he felt a warm light.
'I'm finally going back.'
The woman's voice echoed in his fading consciousness.
—Give my regards to the principal.
Even in his dazed state, Shirone found it strange. How did she know Principal Alpheas?
But the thought didn't last long. His consciousness melted away and was drawn back into his body.

Day 10 since Shirone's death.
"Ugh."
Aimi rubbed her sleepy eyes and woke up. Stretching in the chair, her back cracked. After
nights of staying up, her body moved, but her brain still felt asleep.
Looking around, her friends were still unconscious. After drinking water and washing her
face, she approached Shirone with a towel.
"Shirone. Today, you'll come back…"
Aimi gasped and froze. The sound of her heartbeat echoed in her eardrums. A shiver ran up
her spine, making her shoulders, neck, and face tremble.
Shirone's eyes were open.
"Huh? Huh?"
Aimi called out to Neid and Iruki, who were sleeping on the floor.
"H-Here… here, here…"
Words failed her. At the very least, she needed to make a sound, but her throat felt
blocked. Aimi resorted to her last resort. Taking a deep breath, she clenched her eyes shut and screamed.
"Kyaaaaaaaah!"
The ear-splitting shriek jolted Neid and Iruki awake.
"What? What!"
