Chapter 1050:
Fine Adjustments (1)
A massive pit of unfathomable depth formed where Bardvelve had vanished.
Shirone, who had been watching the darkness, turned to face Nane.
"Why are you here?"
Nane, who had also been lost in thought with a serious expression,
approached with a smile."Because I have legs. I can go anywhere, can't I?"
"Don't dodge the question."
Shirone spoke firmly."If your heart doesn't move, your legs don't
either. You've never wavered before, so why did you suddenly come here?"
"There's something… strange there."Nane pointed toward the inner
fortress of the northern front."A being with universal constants different from this world. That
also means… micro-adjustments have been applied."
"Micro-adjustments?"
"There's a limit to the outcomes of events in the universe. For example,
if you create a world where a small ball moves, you can't set the ball's speed, mass, and direction
infinitely."
"Because it would exceed the scope of creation."
"Exactly. Therefore, if all characteristics were quantified, the limit
of those values would match precisely with the limits of this world's settings. The being closest to
that limit is Ymir."Nane raised his index finger."Of course, if you only look at power,
supernova explosions or black holes are stronger. But they are slaves to the natural order and its laws.
When you consider the overall sum, Ymir ranks higher. Free will, after all, is an overwhelmingly
powerful attribute."
"Even a mere child could set a mountain ablaze… is that what you
mean?"
"Precisely. But the neo-humans exist outside that boundary. Whether it's
physical, sensory, mental, or logical attributes, they are beings subjected to micro-adjustments. They
won't be easy to stop."
Shirone also came to understand this through his battle with
Bardvelve.'If it hadn't been for Nane, the battle would have been much tougher. The same goes for
him. But still…'
There was one question that remained unanswered.
"Why are you helping me? Closing this world was supposed to be your
goal, wasn't it?"
"The situation has changed. If the world remains closed, I'd be the sole
truth. But you've opened the ceiling. In this open state, the truth of this place loses its
absoluteness. Even for me, a Buddha, that's an undesirable outcome."
Nane began walking toward the inner fortress."Let's go. Before
making any decisions, we need to confirm something. You should see it with your own eyes as
well."
The horrific truth.
When Borbor opened her eyes, she found herself hanging with both hands
tied by ropes to the ceiling. As she blinked, her vision filled with the grotesque faces of dozens of
insect-like grasshopper men.
"Ki-iii… an enemy of our kin."
Though they carried no special weapons, their prominent jaws and claws
were threatening enough.
Borbor's lips twisted in a bitter smile.'I'm still alive.'
If life was suffering and death was fear, what could you call being
dragged back to life after dying?
One of the grasshopper men approached her.
"Don't expect a quick death."
This one was larger than the others, likely their leader. His grotesque
jaw jutted forward menacingly."I'll tear you apart piece by piece. Watch as your flesh gets
devoured by our throats, you pathetic livestock."
'Again…'
All human deaths are destined to be lonely deaths.'So, I'm
supposed to keep living.'
A lonely existence.No matter how desperately you live or how
quickly you vanish, nothing changes. Life feels like endlessly shoveling into the void.
'It's not that living is sweet. It's just that dying is
infuriating.'There's no honey-coated world out there.
"Bark! Go on, bark!"When the grasshopper man drove his claw into
her side, sparks danced in Borbor's vision.
Yet her expression didn't change.
"Heh. You're holding up well. Then how about this?"
As her flesh split and her bones scraped against something, a scream
erupted from Borbor's mouth."Ahhhhh!"
The grasshopper men burst into laughter.
"Good! That's the sound we wanted!"
Fueled by his comrades' laughter, the leader drove his claws even
deeper."Now, how's this, you—!"
The leader stopped mid-sentence when he saw Borbor's eyes, still cold
and unflinching.
'What the…?'
In her pupils, the grotesque face of the insect leader reflected
clearly, without a hint of emotion.
As the leader tilted his head slightly, Borbor mirrored the motion at
the exact same angle.
'No, that's not right.'The leader was confused.'Why do I
feel like I'm copying this livestock?'
Borbor, who could only find her identity by borrowing the emotions of
others, spoke."Gouge out my eyes."
For a moment, the leader couldn't tell whether the voice he had heard
was Borbor's or his own.
"Y-Yeah!"
Standing at the boundary where self and other blurred, he reached toward
Borbor's eyes with both hands."I'll gouge them out!"
"Huh? H-Hey?"The other grasshopper men recoiled in shock.
"Hey! Wait a second! What are you doing?!"
Even as they shouted, the leader grabbed his own eye with his clawed
hands.
"Arrgh! Die, filthy livestock!"
Borbor screamed back."Arrgh! Die, filthy livestock!"
With a sickening pop, the leader's eye burst, spilling thick, viscous
fluid onto his hands. He staggered back, clutching the mangled remains of his eye."Hah… hah… I did
it! I got my revenge!"
"What the hell…?"
While the other grasshopper men stood frozen in disbelief, Borbor slowly
turned her head.
"Next."
As Shirone and Nane opened the door, they were met by a young girl with
a simple, innocent appearance. However, the scene quickly turned unsettling—the girl's eyes were pitch
black, gleaming with small specks of light, and she was gnawing on a severed arm.
Shirone felt a chill run down his spine.'I can't tell what she
is.'
Even Shirone, possessing Omega, and Nane, armed with the Akashic
Records, couldn't categorize her.
"She's certainly peculiar," Nane remarked with a grin as he approached
her."No matter how the laws of the universe bend, something like this shouldn't exist. You, being
beyond truth, what are you?"
Chewing on the flesh with satisfaction, the girl responded with a single
word."God."
No further proof was necessary.
"Since the moment I came into existence, everything has been beneath me.
Now, hand over your arms and legs."
The moment her words ended, an unprecedented killing intent filled the
room.
'A disaster…'
The reason Yahweh and Buddha could divide philosophy between them was
because they operated within a closed world, a world clearly defined by humanity.'Philosophy is
for humans, defined by humans. Beings beyond that boundary shouldn't have the right to define
it.'
Nane drew his sword of sermon and approached."Let's see what
you've got."
Shirone shattered the Hexa and summoned his Hand of God, but the girl
remained unfazed."Hmm, you want to fight me?"
"Fight? I wonder if there's even a point where you and we can truly
clash."
"True…"
After gnawing off the last bit of flesh, she tossed aside the bone and
stood up.
"Come at me."
As her lips twisted into a grotesque smile, Shirone and Nane lunged
forward simultaneously.
'Please…'
Gritting his teeth, Shirone swung his arm, Hand of God mounted over his
shoulder.
'Let something remain.'
Amid the silence that transcended the roar of explosions, the northern
city ignited in blinding flashes of light.
Boom! Boom!
At an altitude of 500 meters, Asraiker and Gaitan watched the battle
unfold below. This was not a fight they could interfere in.
Holding the unconscious Borbor by the wrist, Gaitan spoke with a grave
expression."She has unusual eyes."
Asraiker nodded in agreement."Yes. It seems like she's
interpreting photon signals and converting them into quantum signals, ignoring causality
entirely."
A mountain was cleanly sliced in half. It seemed the girl was imagining
an enormous pair of scissors, shredding the landscape at will.
"Her ability is like a combination of Yahweh's subjectivity and Buddha's
objectivity. But even so…"
Despite her power, the girl was the one being overwhelmed.
"Their coordination is astonishing. If there were even a slight mental
gap between them, this battle would already be over. It's ironic—no one understands Yahweh better than
Buddha."
Asraiker's eyes glowed as a magic circle formed within them, magnifying
the scene on the ground.
"…It's over."
"Hah… hah…"
The girl, Piri, was in a wretched state—her left eye was gone, both legs
severed below the knees, and her right arm grotesquely bent and broken.
"How… how did you…"
Given her ability to defy this world's signals, it was abnormal for her
to suffer any injuries at all.
"You can speak, can't you?"
To Piri, 99.99% of the language spoken by humanity sounded like the
barking of beasts.
"Did you understand that?"
Only Shirone and Nane used a language she perceived as logical.
"Well, that's an honor."
A massive sword formed behind Nane, while Shirone unleashed a miraculous
stream of energy.
As the Hand of God grasped Nane's sword of sermon, it pointed directly
at Piery from the heavens.
"N-No…"
She couldn't accept it."You're nothing but livestock! You can't
harm me, the leader of the Neo-Humans! This is all a lie!"
"Sermon."
Nane declared."Heart."
Shirone invoked.
A red flash descended from the sky, slicing through the world and
transcending time itself.
"Gah!"
Piri's death throes ended abruptly as her severed head rolled across the
ground.
"Everything… is a lie."
Even in her final moments, she couldn't acknowledge her defeat. Shirone
understood her feelings."She was strong… If another being like her appears, we won't be able to

"This was just a prelude, Shirone."

"If the goal was simply to defeat you, they could have created an

Perhaps it was because it was Nane speaking that Shirone finally

"What are you planning? Why did you come to the northern front? You said

"The world has opened. Because of that, I need to find the answer to one

question. I will go to the outer world. If I still remain human there…" Nane smiled faintly."Then
perhaps I too can become a god with a heart. I will eliminate the suffering of all beings."
"Nane…"
Perhaps, beyond this world, the two of them sought the same
thing.
"But…"
The smile vanished."If I am merely a program, the human known as

Amy.
Nane didn't say it, but Shirone understood.'I may have opened the

Nane shook his head."Only I, who swallowed Anke Ra's dream, can

"But…"
"I opened the spirit realm. This pain is my burden, so allow me to end

"Who cares about who goes first now…"

'I will.'
Until the very end, one face remained etched in his mind.
'Regret…'
He was leaving with a heart burdened by longing.
