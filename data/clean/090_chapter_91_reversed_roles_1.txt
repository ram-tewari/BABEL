Chapter 91:
Reversed Roles (1)
Kanis glared at the white radiance burning in Shironé's palm. Its overwhelming presence
was undeniable. Of course, he had the belligerence to charge toward death without hesitation. But he also
believed that even in death, he should at least break even.
- Habi. I say we fight. Let's go.
- That magic... How fast is it? We might not be able to dodge.
- Then you block it.
- If I can't block it, you die. If you die, I disappear too. We'll have to fight
eventually, but there are too many variables.
Exchanging thoughts at the speed of thought was a tremendous advantage in battle. But
when opinions diverged to extremes, it became impossible to decide what to do.
"How did things come to this?"
An unexpected voice shattered the tension. Shadows poured like water from the cliff, and
a gaunt old man rose from the surface of the dark waters.
The Archmage Akein.
His face twisted as he took in the scene at a glance. The only reason he'd been able to
focus on Etella was his confidence that the situation here had already been resolved.
Akein had trusted Kanis. Or more precisely, he'd trusted Habist, who was bound to Kanis.
But upon arriving, the reality was completely different from his expectations.
Lucas lay in a pathetic heap, while Kanis and Habist were being threatened by a single
rookie.
"Forgive me, Master."
Akein was not a forgiving man. Ignoring Kanis's apology, he turned coldly and glared at
Shironé.
'I knew he looked familiar.'
He resembled Alpheus. Not in face or appearance, but in the aura he exuded. His audacity
to speak boldly even under the Archmage's gaze was the same.
"Did you plan all this?"
"You're quite insolent for a brat. What's your name?"
"I don't want to give my name to a murderer. Release the mind control and leave the
school. If you don't, I won't let you go."
"Hohoho."
Akein burst into laughter. He wasn't angry. But his criteria for killing someone wasn't
rage—it was how much amusement he could derive. In that sense, Shironé was a ripe fruit, ready to be
plucked.
"Sorry, but I can't lift the mind control. Because Alpheus's disciples must
die."
Feeling the killing intent, Shironé shuddered and instinctively fired a Photon Cannon.
It was clearly a misfire.
Akein dodged it effortlessly. Only after confirming the magic had dissipated did he turn
back.
"Spirit Zone radius of 52.7 meters. Impressive. Quite advanced for your age."
Even if it was light, as long as it had mass, its speed couldn't exceed the limits of
physics. But the basic speed of photon output was still far faster than any flying object. Dodging a Photon
Cannon in an instant and measuring the Spirit Zone's radius was beyond human senses.
Shironé gritted his teeth and compressed photons again. Facing an unfathomable master, he
had no choice but to fight to stop the hundreds of students from free-falling off the cliff.
"Stop, Shironé."
Etella emerged from the mountains. Her clothes were torn, revealing her skin, and she was
covered in dirt from head to toe.
"Don't act rashly. Akein is dangerous."
Akein's brow furrowed. Their battle had been enjoyable, but he'd long since discarded the
memory of food trampled underfoot.
"How shameless of the defeated. If you've been spared, you should cherish your
life."
Shironé and the others were shocked. Could it be that Etella, their teacher, had lost to
this skeletal old man? Bishop of the Carusis Council. Joner, an authority on Spirit Zones. A publicly
recognized 6th-class mage. None of her titles were easily earned.
"This can't be. Teacher Etella...?"
Neide wondered if such a thing was possible. Of course, there were 5th and even 4th-class
mages in the world, but what kind of realm surpassed Etella? It was unimaginable.
Shironé supported Etella and asked, "Are you okay, Teacher?"
"I'll manage. But it'll take time to recover."
"What happened? Who is that man? Why is he doing this?"
"Viltor Akein. An Archmage who reached unofficial 3rd-class forty years ago."
The group's eyes wavered. Any magic school student knew what an unofficial 3rd-class
meant.
Mage rankings were divided into ten tiers, but the fame and achievement points required
to advance skyrocketed at certain intervals.
Specifically, from 7th to 6th-class, and from 4th to 3rd-class.
If 6th-class was the executive level of magical society, 3rd-class was a position capable
of leading a kingdom's core facilities. Considering Alpheus, a publicly recognized 4th-class mage, was only
the headmaster of a private school, it was clear how high Akein's standing was.
"Akein holds a grudge against the Headmaster. He won't back down until he achieves
his goals."
"Enough blabbering. Whining to your disciple will only make you more pathetic."
"I have no interest in胜负. I'm a teacher. To protect my students, I'll do
anything."
"Oh? And what can you do? Even at full strength, you couldn't defeat me. Do you
really think you can stop me now?"
"Your bluff is excessive, Akein."
Akein's expression darkened.
"Clearly, I couldn't stop you. But you don't have the mental energy left to focus on
battle either, do you?"
Etella's words were half-right. Akein's magical power, after casting the Dark Servant,
was only a tenth of his normal state. But a tenth of an Archmage's power was more than enough to sweep away
the exhausted Etella and her fledgling students.
"Times have changed. I indulged you because you were cute, and now you dare to yap
at me?"
Akein summoned his magical power. The tenth he'd reserved to kill Alpheus—it didn't
matter. Troublesome calculations didn't suit his outlook on life.
"Master, leave this to me."
Kanis intervened. Abyss Nova consumed an immense amount of mental energy, something
ordinary mages wouldn't even attempt. Having cast such a spell and fought afterward, even his master
couldn't be in normal condition.
Akein frowned at Kanis. Even a disciple challenging his authority was unacceptable. But
after a moment's thought, he turned to Shironé with an intrigued expression.
"Hey, brat. You're also Alpheus's disciple, right?"
"Don't you dare speak the Headmaster's name, murderer."
"Keukuku! Is that so? You seem to respect him greatly."
Shironé didn't even feel the need to answer. How could he not respect him? Alpheus was
the one who opened his eyes to magic, the benefactor who enrolled a commoner like him in magic school.
"Whether you're an Archmage or 3rd-class, it doesn't matter. Compared to the
Headmaster, you're not even a mage."
"Puhahaha! PUHAHAHA!"
Akein roared with laughter. But his eyes burned with fury.
'Alpheus. How hypocritically have you lived? Don't you know? You have no right to be
respected.'
Having made his decision, Akein nodded.
"Fine. Watching disciples fight is entertaining too. Kanis. Duel him."
"Yes. Thank you, Master."
Kanis finally relaxed. This was his chance to redeem his earlier failures. But Habist
thought differently.
- Kanis. Reconsider. It's better to leave this to Akein.
- What are you talking about? I've already disappointed Master. Even if I die, I have to
defeat Shironé.
Disagreement in the heat of battle was never good.
- Habist. Answer me. Not even you can defy me this time. We're fighting, no matter what.
Habist remained silent. This had never happened before, and it was unsettling. But with
Akein watching, he couldn't show weakness.
Using Dark Port, Kanis instantly closed the distance to Shironé. Shironé teleported to
the edge of the cliff, avoiding collateral damage to the students.
"Hmph! Planning an aerial battle?"
The night sky, shrouded in darkness, wasn't unfavorable for Kanis and his Dark Port
either.
A thousand meters above the cliff, the two clashed like birds of prey, unleashing their
firepower.
Shironé condensed seven photons and fired them simultaneously. The Photon Cannons from
the Infinite Realm were several times more powerful than before, but the recoil was equally immense.
With each cast, his consciousness flickered. The Infinite Realm was indeed a double-edged
sword, threatening both his enemy and his own mind.
"What on earth...?"
Etella stared blankly at the sky. The photon output, generating shockwaves, was something
she'd never seen before.
The dazzling beams of Photon Cannons split the night sky. It was like slashing a golden
pen across a black canvas.
Iruki and Neide were mesmerized. Firing Photon Cannons indiscriminately with such power
was akin to turning Shironé into a weapon.
"He's incredible. But is this really okay?"
"We have to trust him. He did well during the presentation too."
"But this is different. It's a life-or-death situation now. He might cross the line
without realizing it."
Etella turned to them. "What do you mean? Has Shironé entered the Immortal Function
before?"
"Huh? Well, actually... he's been doing it casually lately."
Etella sighed. Even after warning him about the risk of death, he didn't seem to care.
But then again, without such passion, his rapid growth would've been impossible.
***
Kanis, dodging the barrage of Photon Cannons, was more flustered than ever. Why wasn't
Shironé tiring? It was as if his mental limits had been erased.
- Habi! We'll lose at this rate!
No response came from Habist. This had never happened before, and it was ominous. Worse,
he didn't know why. Habist wasn't foolish enough to stay silent during battle just because of a
disagreement.
- Habist! Answer me!
A Photon Cannon grazed Kanis. He twisted his waist, but it was clearly a delayed
reaction. He'd avoided being hit, but only because Shironé had missed.
"What's this?"
Over a dozen beams shot toward him. But again, their aim was off. Kanis finally realized.
The flashes were eliminating all the darkness he could use for Dark Port.
'This is insane...!'
If he couldn't land a hit, he'd trap and pummel his opponent instead. It made sense, but
wasn't it too brute-force? He'd already fired over 200 Photon Cannons, a spell notorious for mental strain.
Yet Shironé showed no signs of exhaustion—in fact, the number of beams was increasing.
'Just how vast is his mental power?'
The thought sent a chill down his spine. Dozens of Photon Cannons filled his vision, and
despair welled up inside him. The cluster of beams blocked all possible escape routes via Dark Port.
'I can't dodge this. It's over.'
He was more frustrated and resentful than afraid of death. The thought of losing to a
light mage in front of his master was terrifying.
- Annoying. Akein.
- Habi?
For the first time since the aerial battle began, the mental channel opened. Habist
blocked the Photon Cannons with his body. The consecutive shockwaves made the demonic creature tremble,
emitting eerie groans.
Realizing surface absorption wasn't enough, Habist opened his body wide and swallowed the
Photon Cannons whole. His slender waist ballooned like a inflating as he struggled to digest the photons.
Gradually, his body shrank back to its original form.
"Guh...!"
Kanis bent forward, his face twisted in shock. The reason Habist could absorb the Photon
Cannons was that he'd drawn on Kanis's mental energy. But doing so without permission violated their
master-servant contract.
- Habist?
- Focus and prepare!
Having drained Kanis's mental energy, Habist lunged at Shironé, slashing with his claws.
Unaccustomed to close combat, Shironé was cornered. Habist's speed matched Shironé's teleportation. How was
this possible? He seemed far stronger than Kanis.
Etella, sharing Shironé's thoughts, glanced at Akein. His emotionless gaze as he watched
the battle told her everything.
'Viltor Akein. You're truly cruel.'
The reason he'd given Kanis the ultimate demonic creature wasn't out of love for his
disciple. To Akein, consumed by vengeance, disciples were mere tools. Kanis was nothing more than a fuel
tank to maximize Habist's power.
As Shironé increased his teleportation speed, Habist moved even faster. Kanis's face
turned as pale as a corpse. Deprived of mental energy, he couldn't even think. Only betrayal and deprivation
echoed in his mind.
- Habist. Why?
Silence was the only reply. Realizing who the true master was, the light in Kanis's eyes
faded. His entire life flashed before him, and tears welled up.
'Kanis...'
Arin covered her mouth, crying. Kanis had always been like this. A pitiful child who
could only hurt himself for the sake of those he loved.
"Master! Save Kanis! Please!"
She didn't even see him as a master anymore. But to save Kanis, she swallowed her pride
and begged. Akein, however, coldly ignored her, issuing orders to Habist.
"Finish it. Habist."
Habist, saturated with energy, couldn't respond. Vibrating to speak risked explosion. And
that was exactly what Akein wanted.
- Kanis. Listen.
- Habist.
- Akein plans to detonate me. Everyone here will be caught in the blast. Then it's over
for both of us.
Arin released the mind control on the students. Sobbing, she rushed at Akein.
"Save Kanis! Or I'll never forgive you!"
"Arin!"
Kanis struggled to his feet. "How dare you attack Master? Stop this at once."
"Kanis! This man is...!"
"I know. Master saved us. He pulled us out of Radum. That's enough. I'll fight to
the end."
Was Kanis sincere? It didn't matter now. His rage burned so fiercely that even his own
death felt like relief.
"Fine! Let's all die together!"
Kanis screamed at the sky. Hot tears streamed down his cheeks. His life had been filthy.
But if this was his fate, he'd meet his end in the most revolting way possible.
"Graaaah!"
Molecules boiling, bubbles swelled across Habist's body. Now everyone understood. Akein
intended to blow up the entire school.
"Shironé! Stop! Habist is going to explode!"
Lost in his trance, Shironé barely heard Etella's warning and halted his energy output.
But the chain reaction within Habist had already begun. His grotesquely twisting form foretold secondary and
tertiary explosions.
"Hahaha! Farewell! Must be disgusting to die with trash like me! This ends
everything!"
- Kanis. I'm sorry.
Kanis's face froze.
- I'll release the contract.
Habist absorbed the last remnants of Kanis's mental energy and severed their bond. The
emptiness of a departing soul overwhelmed Kanis, and he collapsed.
"W-why... Habi...?"
Habist soared into the sky. How many seconds until detonation? Three? Two? Memories
embedded as vibrations resurfaced as he raced toward his end. Habi. Habi. Habi. The voice that had called
out incessantly would never be heard again.
'Kukuku. Kanis. That's your problem. You pretend to be so strong.'
Having no life to begin with, death held no fear. But he was worried. The betrayal he'd
felt through their mental link still resonated as if they were one. But now, with no way to convey it,
Habist could only hope.
That Kanis wouldn't be too hurt.
At an altitude of two kilometers, where no voice could reach the ground, the shadowy
face's mouth twisted grotesquely.
"But it was fun, wasn't it? Kanis."
BOOOOOOM!
A colossal explosion, capable of annihilating the world's darkness, erupted.
