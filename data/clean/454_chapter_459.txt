Chapter 459:
"Phew!"
Rider of Iron Wall exhaled.
Moments later, his electric barrier shattered like glass.
Blood spurted diagonally from his shoulder to his waist, and his upper
body slid to the ground.
'Defense magic is done.'
Gaold pulled his dislocated shoulder back into place.
For someone with heightened sensitivity to pain, it was excruciating,
but his eyes scanned his surroundings.
The remnants of Cage B Team moved like a storm, but it was clearly a
coordinated effort.
'What are they aiming for?'
The dealers hadn't used their strongest attacks yet.
They knew that ordinary attacks wouldn't work.
'Are they betting on the Terminator?'
That was likely accurate.
'They wouldn't include more than two irregulars in a Cage-level team.
Which means…'
This was their last chance.
Cage B Team was aiming for one perfect attack, and if Gaold could block
it, the victory would be his.
With that thought, Gaold's lips curled into a smile.
"Talking nonsense."
Gaold aimed at the metal mage Roche.
Bang! Bang! Bang!
The air gun fired explosively, and Roche hastily erected a metal
barrier.
Clang! Clang! Clang!
The deafening noise created air bullet marks on the steel
surface.
"Grrr!"
Roche gritted his teeth against the vibrations.
He kept thickening the barrier, but the air bullets quickly chewed
through the metal.
The metal barrier, crushed like clay, finally shattered, and dozens of
holes pierced Roche's body.
It took just one second for the air gun to penetrate the
20-centimeter-thick barrier.
But that one second bought Rose enough time to approach Gaold.
'This will do.'
Pleasure Magic.
A high-level scent magic that stimulated the brain to evoke the most
intense nostalgia.
Gaold's face twisted cruelly.
The already fragile emotional impact grew, tormenting him.
"Grrr!"
The gates of hell in his heart opened, and his extreme endurance turned
the surroundings into hell.
Though it made the already violent beast even wilder, Rose had a
different plan.
They couldn't overpower Gaold with force. Instead, they would excite him
to create a cognitive blind spot.
That blind spot was the only opening to subdue Gaold.
"Roche!"
But Rose overlooked one crucial fact.
Though it wasn't intentional, Roche's death was a perfect trigger for
Gaold.
Tarvan, who had been friends with Roche since childhood, was consumed
with rage, and that emotion clashed with Gaold's fury, creating a new gap.
"Guh!"
Rose's neck was severed by a blade of wind, and Gaold's upturned white
eyes locked onto Tarvan's movements.
'It's over.'
Horkin moved towards Gaold, separate from the plan.
Tarvan and all the mages present were young and talented.
'For the future of the kingdom, this must end here.'
Horkin activated his explosive magic.
Suicide, a spell that caused a temporary magic assimilation effect by
self-detonation, unleashed a powerful explosion that swept the area.
Unlike the Battle Arena, the scale wasn't reduced, so the radius was
immense.
Cage B Team, having quickly moved away, stared blankly at the
smoke-filled area.
Horkin's message was clear: he had ended his own life.
As the eldest member of Cage B Team, he had been a father figure to many
mages.
"The old man…"
Tarvan gritted his teeth.
It was a mission he should have carried out.
But Horkin had been the ultimate strategist until the end.
The power of Suicide was on par with Tarvan's Kaiser Blast.
"Is he dead?"
Saint Morak's voice was low.
As the smoke cleared, a dark silhouette emerged.
Gaold stood there, blood dripping from his forehead, looking
displeased.
"He's alive."
The mission had failed.
Rose, who could strategize, was dead, and they had lost dealers Roche
and Horkin.
"There's only one thing left."
Communication mage Weygan spoke.
"Do we go all the way, or do we retreat?"
They had no intention of retreating.
But Horkin's wish for some to survive weighed on their minds.
"We go all the way."
At Tarvan's words, insect mage Beam stepped forward.
"We need to report to the higher-ups. Not everyone can go."
"Pick anyone. I'll stay."
Weygan raised his hand.
"I'll go. I'm not much help in combat anyway."
No one blamed Weygan. To be part of Cage B Team, one had to have pride.
Choosing to live with shame was a sacrifice the remaining members appreciated.
"Let's go."
As the remaining members turned, Gaold flew towards them.
He realized further combat was pointless.
"You were a great team. For what it's worth."
Tarvan's eyes filled with killing intent.
"You don't really think it's over, do you?"
If he could withstand Suicide, Kaiser Blast wouldn't work either.
But he had to try. That was the Terminator's way.
"Stop."
Gaold relaxed his body and stood straight.
"The game is over. I let you have your fun, didn't I?"
"Game? Fun?"
Vibrations gathered in Tarvan's hands.
Gaold continued, uninterested.
"And… you can't go back anyway."
Cage B Team looked puzzled, and Weygan asked,
"We can't go back? Why?"
"You planned to return using the Meta Gate provided by the association,
right?"
No one answered, but there were no secrets from Gaold, the former head
of the Magic Association.
"Miro's dimension is probably gone. The Meta Gate coordinates would be
disrupted."
Weygan's brow furrowed.
Heaven's army couldn't threaten humanity because Miro's dimension
disrupted information between the two dimensions.
Only Miro, the owner of the dimension, could decode the
encryption.
The Meta Gate had fixed coordinates, remembering the coordinates of
Miro's dimension.
But if Miro's dimension was gone, the recorded coordinates would likely
be null.
"What are the chances Miro's dimension is gone?"
Saint Morak asked.
With no way to kill him, Gaold had no reason to lie.
But the Meta Gate activation time was only one minute.
If Gaold was wrong, they would lose their chance to return.
"Not 100%, but over 80%."
"That high?"
Cage B Team began to murmur.
"According to my information, Miro was captured by Archangel Kariel. The
Archangel of Creation would have found a way."
Miro was in Heaven.
The new information complicated everyone's thoughts.
If Miro's dimension was destroyed, Heaven's army would come to
Earth.
The association would soon learn this, but by then, it would be too
late. Every second counted.
"We have to check."
Morak looked at the team, and Tarvan asked,
"How?"
"We have to go in ourselves."
"So it's a matter of who goes?"
"I'll go."
Weygan raised his hand again.
There were no objections, and Weygan activated the Meta Gate.
Standing before the black sphere, Weygan looked back at his
comrades.
With over an 80% chance of death, emotionally, it felt like 100%.
"If the coordinates haven't changed, I'll come right back. Be ready to
leave."
With such a plan, even one minute was tight.
Tarvan nodded.
"Come back quickly. Don't get sidetracked."
"Ha ha! Who knows."
Weygan gave a cute smile and entered the Meta Gate.
A minute passed, and he didn't return.
The Great World War.

her mind.
Miro's brow furrowed slightly.
The restraint was on a different level from human-made magic control
devices.
It was a dangerous magic circle that could explode just by entering the
Spirit Zone.

"Don't worry. It'll be over soon."
Even with Miro's dimension destroyed, Heaven's army wouldn't move

To annihilate the detestable humans, an independent force was
needed.
So Miro was still useful.
The Archangel Council had already been convened. If he could get the

With Gaold's attack on Heaven, even Anke Ra wouldn't be able to remain

Even with her life hanging by a thread, Miro showed no fear.
'Not killing me means the final war is postponed. But why?'

To realize something and start acting, she needed someone's help.
'Gaold has come?'
Then Sein would be with him.
She had caused Gaold a terrible tragedy with her mistake, but with her

'But still…'

The Seventh Heaven, Araboth.
Ikael sat on the floor, meditating quietly.
Ashur had gone to gather information on Kariel's actions but hadn't
returned yet.
Even he, who could manipulate signals freely, found it difficult to
observe the Great World War without Kariel noticing.
It would take much longer than usual, as he had to assemble information
using signal interference.
Ikael's anxiety grew.
Something unusual was happening in Purgatory. What was going on?
As she sighed softly and calmed her mind, a voice echoed in her
head.
"Ikael."
Her eyes snapped open.
It was the voice of Anke Ra, one she hadn't heard in a long time.
"Sinner Ikael, you are summoned."
"The time has come for an answer."
"An answer?"
Anke Ra did not elaborate.
"Come before me. Your sins are forgiven."
Her eyes, wide open, began to shine with a light as brilliant as the
Milky Way.
'Finally…'
The time she had spent in penance, her powers restrained, for her
unforgivable sin, had passed quickly.
She felt neither excitement nor joy.
Her only thought was to reclaim her position as the Archangel and
restore order to everything that had been disrupted.
"Understood."
Ikael stood up abruptly and left the room.
Her body blurred as she soared to the high spire of Araboth in an
instant.
