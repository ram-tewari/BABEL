activated.
They probably wouldn't have used such methods just because they were
afraid of humans.
Amy thought of a frog caught in a lab.
The reason they tie up the limbs before dissecting them is not because
they're afraid of frogs.
The thought gave her goosebumps.
At the very least, it meant that what awaited them was something against
which it was natural to struggle.
Tess and Arin also remained silent, perhaps thinking the same
thing.
As if the current tense atmosphere would last forever if no sound was
made.
The only small comfort was that there was no drone on Tess's

'What happened? Why can't I see anything?'

The drone was able to guide Shirone and his group to arrive as quickly
as possible, but nothing appeared on her retinal screen.
She didn't know that the space itself was isolated by the Meca System's
functions.
Kariel was checking a machine about 70 meters away from the globular
cluster.
It was a huge pillar that reached the ceiling, and thousands of panels
were emitting various colored lights.
Kariel floated ten meters in the air, arms crossed, watching the
screen.
The screen was filled with complex symbols that Amy and her friends
could not understand.
Amy observed Kariel's halo.
It was much larger and clearer than Ikasa's.
Diagrams and formulas appeared and disappeared like afterimages inside
the halo, and each time they did, the machine's screen changed rapidly.
- System check complete. Initiating the Elixir of Life. Please input
the molecular structure of the genetic material.
A chilling female voice came from the ceiling.
The machine's voice was impossible to analyze, even for Arin. Trying to
use mental communication with Kariel would have been madness.
Kariel, who had been staring at the screen for a while, flew towards the
women.
They could hear low-pitched vibrations like a contrabass.
It was a young man. He was two and a half meters tall and was wearing
white robes. His halo was so bright it seemed to punch a hole through space.
Amy mustered up her courage and glared at him.
If something had to happen, she intended to be the first to face it. She
didn't want to imagine Tess or Arin suffering first in this situation.
"Aren't you ashamed to call yourself an archangel? Aren't you going
to take off these handcuffs?"
"What a base, human-like thought. I have restrained you to fulfill
my duties as an archangel."
Arin, who was staring intently at Kariel, bit her lip.
This was the first time she had seen such a scene.
All she could see was a vibrating string. The vibrations were shaking in
a complex manner, forming shapes, but even those were unclear.
Kariel existed purely as probabilities.
Amy snapped again.
"Duty? What do you plan to do to us?"
"Come to think of it, I forgot to introduce myself. I am Archangel
Kariel. I am tasked by Ra to carry out the Elixir of Life in occupied territories. I will now administer
the Elixir of Life to you as well. It is an honorable mission, so you should accept it
gladly."
"That's ridiculous! What kind of nonsense are you talking about
while tying up a person! Release us now!"
Amy shook her body. The handcuffs were fastened at waist height, so all
she could do was stomp her feet like a child throwing a tantrum.
Tess, mulling over Kariel's words, asked back as if wondering.
"Wait a minute. Occupied territory? Does that mean Heaven attacks other
worlds?"
Kariel was lost in thought. The law is always right. But to persuade
humans, a different approach was needed.
"I am merely conveying Ra's will. God loves humans and desires
their eternal existence. Your world will soon submit to Ra's will as well."
Tess was gripped by fearful thoughts.
The idea she had just conceived was that Ra was the tyrant of the
universe. It occurred to her that the subjects of Heaven were also residents of an occupied territory
somewhere.
Amy screamed.
"We saw the Elixir of Life! Is that how you love humans? You're no
different from a perverted murderer!"
"You don't understand. Humans confuse love with power. Don't think that
being loved by someone is some great ability. Ra cherishes you simply because you are beings who can
give names."
Amy recalled the book of lifespans. Fairies control the lifespans of
subjects through names.
But isn't a name something anyone can give? What significance does
giving names have to Ra?
Tess retorted sarcastically,
"So, what do you plan to do with us? Are you going to show your
love by whipping us now that we're handcuffed?"
"You have an important mission."
"Mission? What mission?"
"It is to conceive my child."
Amy, Tess, and Arin's faces turned red at the same time. The idea was so
horrifying and infuriating that it made their hair stand on end.
Amy couldn't control the trembling of her jaw and screamed,
"You, you pervert! Just try touching my body. I won't let you get away
with it!"
"What a pathetic reaction. Don't worry. I'm just transmitting angel
information to your bodies."
Tess was even more annoyed by the words. It was like the precise
language of perverts who insisted on expressing things accurately.
"In short, you want to have a disgusting time with us?"
Kariel showed a displeased expression.
"Human expressions are so vulgar. The Elixir of Life is a highly
advanced art that only I, among the archangels, can perform. Do not compare it to the reproduction of
lowly humans."
- The preparation of the Elixir of Life is complete. Information
accumulation rate: 100%. Would you like to proceed with the replication?
At that moment, a female voice was heard from the ceiling.
Kariel flew upward as if no words were needed.
"Replicate."
- Replicating genetic material. Estimated time: 2 minutes and 30
seconds.
Kariel's halo expanded into a ring over 20 meters in diameter.
When he brought his index and middle fingers together, a bead-sized
light was born at his fingertips.
Although small in size, its intensity was enough to illuminate the
entire Great Hall of World.
Kariel returned the halo to its original form and came back to the
women.
At the tip of the finger, where the light had disappeared, a violet
diamond-shaped glass panel was created.
"This panel contains near-infinite life information. Do you
understand? In order to create a human, we need information on all life forms that existed before
humans."
"I don't care! Whatever it is, we won't do it!"
Amy screamed. She was already so angry that she was about to die, but
the explanation only made her even more irritated.
However, Kariel seemed to have considerable pride in the Elixir of Life
and continued his explanation without an audience.
"The information of an angel is delicate and complex. This is why
women who have already given birth have a significantly lower success rate for bearing Nephilim. In that
sense, you are fortunate. You have the honor of carrying the offspring of a great angel within
you."
"Nephilim…What are you going to do by giving birth to them?"
"They will be sent to other worlds. In this case, it will be your
world."
"To convert the humans there and offer them to God?"
Kariel closed his mouth at the pointed question.
In the end, that was it. He made the women of the occupied territory
give birth to Nephilim, educated the humans there, and then led them to God.
Tess realized that the Nephilim were some kind of spies.
The fact that they were free from the law meant just that.
Because they would never impose their country's laws on combatants
hiding in an enemy country.
Kariel held out the panel floating at his fingertips.
"Don't worry. It's only about transferring information, so you won't
feel ashamed."
When the glass panel is attached to the forehead, glass spikes protrude

Through these roots, the angel's information penetrates and creates life

Kariel brought the glass panel to the forehead of each of the three

All their foreheads were smooth and beautiful, seemingly suitable for

"Hmm, very well. Shall I let you decide? Who will be the first to

Three women shouted at the same time.
"I'll go first!"
The women looked at each other, as if they hadn't expected the reaction.
The fact that they were fighting together gave them renewed courage.
Kariel was pleased.
To make the Elixir of Life successful, the angel's information needed to

The success rate was roughly one in thirty, and failure would result in

However, the stronger the mental and physical condition, the higher the

In that respect, all three women were excellent choices.
- The replication of genetic material is complete.
As a female voice came down from the ceiling, Kariel raised his
hand.
Panels of identical shape were created on the left and right of the

"Sisters with a strong bond. Since I can't tell which is better,
I'll plant them at the same time."
The three panels left Kariel's hand and floated toward the women.
Amy and her group were gripped by uncontrollable rage. They finally
understood.
Archangel Kariel was the worst kind of pervert.
