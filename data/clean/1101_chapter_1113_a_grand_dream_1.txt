Chapter 1113:
A Grand Dream (1)
The gathered guards felt the world sway with each step Kuan took.
His steps were neither fast nor slow, almost pitiful in a way.
'It's tilting.'
It felt like being on a ship in a storm, hard to keep their
balance.
"Hmm."
Natasha touched her lips with an intrigued expression, while the Wind
Guards looked displeased.
Leader Yula spoke.
"Block his approach."
Among all the murderous intent directed at them, the strongest was aimed
at the Wind Guards.
"Yes."
Just as 99 swordsmen were about to scatter like the wind, Kido stepped
forward.
"Stop."
Kuan halted.
"You're a swordsman from Tormia? This is a restricted area. You
can't enter without a permit. Go back."
Tormia couldn't be hated.
"I have business at the main building."
Kido glanced at Delta's building and then back at Kuan, shaking his
head.
"Then I can't let you through. Once the Holy War begins, no one can
enter or leave."
Rai interjected.
"He can enter. He's part of Tormia's special forces and has a
pass."
Kido bit his lip.
'He has killing intent but no hostility. If that changes, it would be
terrifying…'
If he had a pass, it might be fine to let him through.
'He must be killed.'
Yula was resolute.
The Wind Guards, though not as close as Kido, was also in a position to
gauge Uorin's intentions.
'That man is a threat to the Empress. This might be a good chance.
Killing him here won't affect the Holy War.'
Kuan had crossed the line.
"I don't know why you're late, but this is a restricted area. You
can't go through here."
"Then where should I go?"
"That's up to you. But the only way to Delta's main building is
through here."
They intended to trap him.
Knowing Yula's nature, Kido pleaded with Kuan.
"Go back, please."
Kuan calmly met his gaze and murmured.
"You're lucky."
"Huh?"
The ground suddenly tilted sharply towards Kuan, creating a slope over
70 degrees.
Simultaneously, the guards expelled external force.
"Catch him!"
Natasha fell naturally, Kido stayed grounded, and the Wind Guards flew
like the wind, drawing their sword.
'Can't let him escape.'
Kuan seemed to fall off a cliff, and losing sight of him for even a
moment would be the end.
Then the 70-degree tilt flipped to the left.
"Damn it!"
North and south reversed again, now spinning like a sphere.
Only Kido, grounded like a spirit, could track Kuan's movements.
"Phew! This is really strange."
Meanwhile, Natasha enjoyed the spinning world like a ride.
'It's not easy.'
Using the initial tilt on a single target made it easy to escape their
sight.
'But it's hard to shake them all off.'
Hundreds of swordsmen, all top-tier in the kingdom, were present.
A member of the Wind Guards reported to Yula.
"Captain, he might have a plan. Moving as a group could create gaps
in our defense…"
The fact that everyone was being led by a single swordsman was
humiliating enough.
'But he's that kind of man.'
She remembered young Kuan, crawling up a mountain path with a torn
Achilles tendon.
'You've come this far.'
Suppressing the urge to applaud him as a swordsman, she gave her
order.
"End it in one go. Form the death formation."
"Yes."
The 100 members of the Wind Guards crossed like a net and began rotating
around Kuan.
'If caught, not even bones will remain.'
It wasn't an exaggeration.
But Kuan, facing the best swordsmen of Kashan, looked colder than
ever.
'I'm just doing what I must.'
He refused the position of guard captain because protecting someone
didn't suit him.
'I am a clown.'
A pierrot who could do any foolish thing to make her laugh.
'What was I expecting?'
Still human, did he fantasize about being with her?
"Heh."
In the dark curtain of the Wind Guards tightening net, he thought of
Siina's face.
"Smile."
Because it's fun.
"Strike!"
A hundred blades created a storm, devastating the spot where Kuan
stood.
The landscape returned to normal, and the Wind Guards members reformed
their formation.
Kido looked dazed.
"He's gone."
He didn't see it.
In an instant, the scene distorted, and Kuan's body vanished.
"Move."
Rai rushed over.
At the center of the swirling grass, red drops of blood fell.
"Did we cut him?"
Yula didn't lie.
"No. None of us cut him. He disappeared just before our swords
reached him."
"Then this blood?"
"Probably…"
Yula pointed to her head with her finger.
"His ears."
By rupturing his own eardrums, his hearing was now half-gone.
'Mr. Kuan.'
Facing the situation where he single-handedly broke through the guards
of 12 countries.
'Are you truly becoming a ghost?'
Rai had a glimpse of what one must sacrifice to become the strongest
swordsman.
The Pope delivered a speech.
"Lamiism is a global religion. However, not everyone in the world
believes in Lamiism."
The atmosphere was solemn.
"Faith always comes with a price. It is an act of burying the soul
and knowing that there is a higher transcendence beyond any reason."
The Pope's gaze turned to Shirone.
"What can be gained from that price is strength. The peace that
comes from believing that I am not my own. Isn't that what this world needs?"
His gaze shifted away.
"The value of Lamiism lies in the courage, sacrifice, and hope that
those who cannot fight can lean on."
Gis murmured.
"Oh, God."
"No one is a god. As the Pope of Lamiism, I earnestly pray to the
God of Cria that a Leadership Council will emerge to fight for all humanity."
Applause filled the conference hall. For the Kingdom of Tormia, which
relied on the power of Yahweh, it was an uncomfortable situation.
"It can't be helped."
Lupist leaned his temple on his fist.
"As the official religion of the Holy War Leadership Council, it
has a strong influence. Above all, it has a justification that the kingdom lacks."
Plu agreed.
"It's not blatant, but they're subtly using wording that denies
Yahweh. It's intentional."
Everyone's inner thoughts might differ, but officially, the atmosphere
was one of admiration.
"Next, we will begin the proposal from the International
Tribunal."
Chief Socrates, a large, obese man, climbed onto the podium, wiping his
sweat.
"Ugh. Phew, it's hot. Or is it just me? Wearing this fat
suit."
No one responded, so he burst into a near-shout of laughter.
"Just kidding! Hahaha!"
The atmosphere was the worst.
"Ahem. Then I will propose. I, Chief of the International Tribunal,
Bebeto Socrates…"
He glared and spat out.
"Resign as of today."
While the officials from various countries had no reaction, those
working in the Holy War were in an uproar.
"Mr. Socrates, what do you mean?"
"Exactly what I said. Resign. I'd rather eat another loaf of bread.
Eating is what remains. I recommend Mito Shirano as my successor. That's all."
"Shirano?"
Socrates stepped down, and a thin, middle-aged woman climbed onto the
podium.
Nicknamed the Swamp Witch, she was once the head of the Magic
Association in the Kingdom of Stang.
"Thank you, Mr. Socrates, for passing on this crappy position to
me."
True to her character, her words were harsh.
"Then I'll propose. I resign as of this moment."
"What is this! Is the Holy War a joke?" Finally, some
officials protested, but Shirano was unfazed.
"Shut up. Don't look down on mages, and don't drag us into this
childish political game. Everyone knows, right?"
In the ensuing silence, the chairman spoke.
"But… if you resign, there will be no one to make proposals. Isn't
it courteous to present an agenda before resigning?"
"Proposal."
Shirano spat out.
"The strong eat the weak. That's all."
As she stepped down, the politicians were stunned, but some
smiled.
They were mages.
'They have their own stubbornness. They never swallow what they find
repulsive.'
King Munryong of the Kingdom of Mun stood up.
"This is outrageous! To raise the banner of survival of the fittest
at a gathering for world peace!"
The chairman tried to calm him.
"Please calm down. It's just the words of a single chief. Besides,
she's no longer a chief…"
"Do you think that will work? The International Tribunal oversees
all the rules and laws of the Holy War!"
The intensity of his words was strong.
"First, they allow the villain Havitz to attend the Holy War, and
now they say the strong eat the weak. Does the Holy War want to smell blood? If taken literally, it
means they won't intervene even if a king is beheaded!"
"That's too much!"
Mentioning the assassination of a king made even the observers react
sensitively.
"This is a gathering for peace. Isn't King Mun, who harbors such
vile thoughts, the one with ulterior motives?"
"What did you say? How dare you…!"
The topic was so sensitive that everyone stood up, and the atmosphere of
the Holy War turned combative.
'Assassination attempts will happen anyway. The side that loses here
will be the first to get hit.'
Amidst the boasting, bluffing, and threats, the door to the conference
hall opened.
The mere fact that the door opened surprised everyone, and silence fell
instantly.
"What, what is this?"
A one-armed swordsman bowed his head.
"Sorry if I interrupted."
As he limped down the stairs, the onlookers were even more
bewildered.
'Mr. Kuan.'
Siena watched with mixed emotions as the chairman stopped Kuan.
"Wait. Who are you?"
"I am Parka Kuan of the Tormia Kingdom's special forces. I was late
due to circumstances."
"Are you serious…"
The chairman, more curious than scolding, stopped and asked
again.
"How did you get in here?"
Once the conference begins, the guards of each country guard the only
exit.
Kuan looked back at the door and answered.
"Walked in…"
"That's not what I mean! How did you get in? All the doors of the
main building are closed, and you can't enter without passing the guards…"
At that moment, the chairman realized.
"Could it be?"
Did he break through the guards of 12 countries?
At that moment, blood gushed from one of Kuan's ears and fell to the
ground.
Siena's eyes widened, and everyone who knew him looked shocked.
'Pressure. He sacrificed one ear.'
The realization made the audience fall silent as Kuan walked
again.
He stopped at Tormia's council.
"Excuse me."
"Oh, yes."
Quick-witted Thad moved aside, and Kuan slowly sat in his chair.
Next to Siena.
"Why…?"
"This is my seat."
Albino and Uorin knew what Kuan intended.
'The country that touches Siena… will be the first to die.'
The unspoken threat weighed heavily on the room as Albino nodded.
'If he can't protect her, he'll cut them all down. If he's truly given
up everything…'
Kuan was now a sword demon.
