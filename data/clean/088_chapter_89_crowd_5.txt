Chapter 89:
Crowd (5)
The mansion of the Bastard family, one of the wealthiest in the capital, was so opulent
it skirted the law forbidding nobles from outshining the royal palace.
Three hundred crystal chandeliers adorned the hall's ceiling, and marble tables with gold
trim were laden with delicacies from around the world.
To the musicians' tunes, nobles danced with partners, while elsewhere, elder statesmen
chatted over wine.
"Hello? I'm Ozent Clump. Are you here alone?"
"Eek!"
The woman screamed and fled at the sight of Clump's creepy smile. It was literally a
killer's smile. Stunned, Clump froze as Alpheas burst out laughing and clapped his back.
"Pfft! You actually did it! Oh, my sides."
"Damn it! I'm an idiot for believing you. And where were you?"
"Lovely ladies kept talking to me. Just keeping them company."
Clump glanced where Alpheas pointed. Sophisticated city girls were blushing as they
sneaked peeks at him.
"Damn it. Even your backside is better-looking than my front. They're all staring at
you."
"Haha! Relax. Isn't that how it is? Let's eat till we burst."
With delicacies unseen in the provinces, Clump was eager. Since he was alone anyway, he
might as well eat his fill without worrying about girls.
As Clump devoured food, Alpheas sweated, entertaining nobles who came every five minutes.
Especially when the Bastard family head personally greeted him, all eyes turned.
"Pleasure. You must be Alpheas, the rising star."
"The honor is mine. Splendid party. Thank you for the invitation."
"Hohoho! Mingling with young intellects is my joy. Let's meet again."
"Of course. I'd be delighted to attend anytime."
Though brief, the Bastard head exchanging more than pleasantries with a rookie mage was a
mark of great favor.
Satisfied, Alpheas turned to see Clump stuffing his face like a beast. His unhinged jaw
was a marvel.
"How does your jaw not tire? Where does all that food go?"
Clump pointed to his biceps while chewing. Alpheas shook his head and walked off.
Countless female gazes followed, but to him, it was just boring. Lips twitching, he scanned for amusement
until a girl darting between tables caught his eye.
"Huh?"
His first thought: definitely not a mage. Her simple dress defied the gypsy trend, and
her waist-length black hair rejected the intellectual short style. A plate in one hand, a fork in the other,
she hopped around—utterly out of place.
"Huh? That's the family head's eldest daughter."
Clump's words piqued Alpheas's interest. Without looking away, he tilted his head.
"The family head's daughter?"
"You came without even knowing that? No manners, as expected."
"Don't care. Just explain."
"Her name is Bastard Erina. Age 19. The Bastard family's sole headache."
"Headache? A wild personality?"
"No. She's… a bit slow."
Only then did Alpheas turn to Clump. Likely true—the bear-like friend wasn't one for
cruel jokes.
"An accident in childhood. Mental age around 10. Supposed to be confined, but somehow
she's here."
Alpheas looked back at Erina with mixed feelings. But she, oblivious to stares, laughed
innocently, greeting young men.
Like any ball, she seemed to seek a partner. Yet no one smiled back. At best, they
awkwardly greeted her before fleeing—only possible due to the Bastard name.
Alpheas glanced at the family head across the table. As expected, he was red-faced,
suppressing rage before snapping at his wife.
"I told you not to bring Erina! Take her away!"
"Dear, bear with it. Erina loves parties. Causing a scene would be worse. Leave her—I'll
sneak her out."
"Ugh! The shame. Our family's disgrace!"
Alpheas pouted. He understood the sentiment, but calling your daughter a disgrace? Then
again, as a first-class noble himself, he knew their societal standing.
'Just like the old man at home. Nobles, huh.'
For three musical changes, Alpheas didn't take his eyes off Erina. He noticed she
genuinely enjoyed the party. Just greeting people seemed enough.
But mages were a proud lot. They smiled to her face but mocked her behind her back.
"Clump. Hold this. No, you drink it."
As Alpheas turned to the dance floor, Clump urgently stopped him.
"What now? Please, no trouble here."
"These idiots, just because they've read a few books, are rejecting the lady's kindness.
As Bashuka's heartthrob, I'll restore her dignity."
Clump's bear-like strength spun Alpheas around.
"Alpheas. That's cruel. Pity could humiliate her. Those people are enough. Don't make it
worse."
Clump was serious. His usual dull eyes burned with righteousness. Alpheas smiled and
patted his shoulder.
"So simple. Do you think I'd miss that? All evil stems from discrimination. Heard that?
Starting to pity her cages her life. Trust me."
"You serious? Really? Hey!"
As Alpheas crossed the floor, he flashed his signature killer smile at Clump.
"Relax. I'm everyone's lover."
