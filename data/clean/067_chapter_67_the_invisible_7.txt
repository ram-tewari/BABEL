Chapter 67:
The Invisible (7)
The students were still arguing to uncover the trick Shirone and his group had used.
In an instant, the central park had turned into a massive debate hall.
But the more they discussed, the more they found themselves lost in a maze. The students
of Class Four, in particular, were displeased. They had been completely fooled by their juniors' trick and
couldn't even figure out the mechanism, which wounded their pride more than they cared to admit.
"Hmph! There's no need to think about it! They must've pulled some ridiculous stunt. The
point is, this presentation was a farce! Showing us ghosts that don't even exist and calling it proof? Don't
make me laugh!"
The students of Class Four united in agreement.
"Exactly! This method is unacceptable! This isn't spiritual science—it's just a show!"
As the voices of criticism grew louder, Neid stepped forward as their representative and
asked them:
"Everyone, do you still refuse to acknowledge the existence of ghosts?"
"Of course! Ghosts? This is just some kind of illusion magic! It's not real ghosts!"
"Then let me ask you this: If you truly believe that, why are you all still here right
now?"
"Huh? Well, obviously…!"
The student fell silent, and a hush spread.
Among the hundreds of students, no one could refute Neid's words. If they truly believed
ghosts didn't exist, why had they fled like fools?
The answer was simple. Deep down, they couldn't completely deny the possibility of
spirits.
"The emotions you're feeling right now are the essence of supernatural spiritual science.
Humans live their entire lives with questions and curiosity about the unknown. If we dismiss what hasn't
been verified as unnecessary to know, then what reason is there for intellect to exist? Isn't this precisely
why the Supernatural Spiritual Science Research Club deserves its place in Alpheas Magic Academy, the cradle
of intellect?"
The students were left speechless.
Even if no real ghost had appeared, what did it matter?
What was important was that everyone here now entertained the possibility that ghosts
might exist. And if they thought that way, then someone had to strive to uncover the truth.
Neid's words made the students consider the presentation seriously for the first time. It
wasn't just a prank to disrupt the school. The Supernatural Spiritual Science Research Club had delivered a
flawless presentation in their own way.
"With that, we conclude the Supernatural Spiritual Science Research Club's presentation.
Thank you all for staying late to attend."
Shirone and his group bowed politely. The venue fell silent, and no one spoke.
***
But shortly after, an inexplicable sense of catharsis welled up in the students' hearts,
and they burst into laughter.
"Pfft—hahaha! This is a masterpiece! You guys really did it!"
"Yeah! Honestly, I've always been interested in this stuff! Hold more fun presentations
like this in the future!"
"Haven't sweated like that in a while. You guys won! This was amazing!"
The teachers were taken aback by the students' sudden change in attitude, but Shirone had
already anticipated this. Any rational human being harbored curiosity about the fundamental questions of
existence.
Where do we come from, and where do we go?
Perhaps that was the most important question for humanity. And maybe that was precisely
why the Supernatural Spiritual Science Research Club needed to exist.
Alpheas nodded in satisfaction.
"Showing the invisible without revealing it. So that's how it's done. The kids did
splendidly."
"Headmaster, does this mean you'll allow the Supernatural Spiritual Science Research Club
to continue?"
"Hmm. That's not for me alone to decide. Well then, does anyone here wish to raise a
counterargument to their reasoning?"
None of the teachers stepped forward. Even Siena had no choice but to remain silent this
time.
Frankly, she felt like she'd been thoroughly outplayed by her students. The Supernatural
Spiritual Science Research Club had to exist. The hundreds of students gathered here were proof of that.
Sad raised an objection.
"But what if their violations of school rules are exposed? There will be considerable
backlash. It's uncomfortable to approve the club like this."
Among the teachers, quite a few, like Siena, had confirmed the illegal intrusion into
restricted areas. With multiple people pointing out the same issue, an impromptu faculty meeting was held.
Siena was the first to voice her opinion.
"It's true that the kids broke school rules. But I believe that's separate from the
question of the club's continuation."
The teachers looked puzzled as Siena, who had been leading the charge for disbandment
just moments ago, now defended the club.
But Siena considered this settled. It was her promise to them. Shirone and his group had
met her demands, and all she had to do was acknowledge their achievements.
Separate from the teachers' discussion, members of underground clubs were secretly
conversing in a secluded corner of the park.
"Even a rotten fish can swim. It seems this isn't over yet. The graduating seniors left
behind some bold juniors, didn't they?"
"There won't be a war. The Spiritual Science Club will dominate for a while. Our Ant
Language Research Club won't interfere."
"Hmph! They're just Class Five. They'll rise to the graduating class eventually, but by
then, I won't even be at this school."
"Don't be so sure. If they fail this year, they might compete with us next year."
"What? Are you saying I'm scared of them?"
"Everyone, stop. It's ridiculous for us, who usually avoid each other, to be arguing like
this. If you want to fight, then fight. Of course, our Hide-and-Seek Research Club won't get involved."
"Says the one who'll be watching their every move. You guys would dive into sewage just
to hide, wouldn't you?"
"Let's end it here. We're drawing attention. Gathering like this only tarnishes our
clubs' reputations."
The leaders of the academy's underground reached an agreement and headed toward the
graduating class building. Siena frowned as she caught sight of them moving together.
'Ugh, those pathetic losers.'
She was sick of the sight of them. They were the worst troublemakers, tormenting her
relentlessly from the lower to upper classes and now into the graduating class.
The reason she couldn't do anything about them was their talent for vanishing like
ghosts, abusing the gifts heaven had given them.
'Just how many of these kids are there in this school? It's all because we keep approving
these absurd clubs.'
The ingenious tricks passed down from seniors to juniors, and then from those juniors to
their own juniors—how could teachers possibly keep up?
The reason Siena had tried to disband the Supernatural Spiritual Science Research Club
wasn't because she hated Neid and his group, but because she wanted to cut off the root of the problem from
the start.
'But it ended up like this anyway.'
Clicking her tongue, Siena pushed through the crowd and shouted at Shirone and his group
standing on the rooftop railing.
"The faculty meeting has reached a decision."
The students' applause and cheers died down.
"The Supernatural Spiritual Science Research Club was slated for disbandment due to a
year of inactivity and insufficient membership. However, following today's presentation and after
discussions with Headmaster Alpheas and the majority of the faculty, it has been decided that the club,
represented by Neid and consisting of three members, will be allowed to continue for the time being. But…."
Siena's voice caught as irritation surged within her. Unable to hold back, she pointed
and yelled.
"You three are suspended for a week! Consider it probation, you idiots!"
Bwahahahaha!
The students doubled over laughing. The idea to prove the club's legitimacy was
brilliant, but the crime was installing unauthorized machinery on school grounds.
Shirone, Neid, and Iluki stood dumbfounded, mouths agape. Then they looked at each other.
Their lips curled into grins, and their eyes sparkled with joy.
"We did it! We succeeded!"
"Wahahaha! We did it! We saved the club!"
They didn't care about the suspension. Watching the ecstatic students embrace, Siena was
at a loss for words.
But they had no regrets. They had given their all, leaving nothing behind, and the result
was genuinely worth celebrating.
"Hahaha! Hey, congratulations! Keep up the good work!"
"Where's your lab? I'll drop by sometime!"
Students congratulated them. Among them were Shirone's fervent admirers, Mark and Seriel.
"Uwaaaah! Shirone-senpai is the best! I knew it wouldn't end like this from the start!
Senpai is always one—no, two steps ahead!"
"Right. I had no idea the club's fate was on the line. But you pulled it off brilliantly
again, didn't you? Right, Amy?"
Amy, who should have been the happiest at this moment, was unexpectedly sulking.
"What's so great about getting suspended? Why do something so stupid? You should be
studying hard to join the graduating class as soon as possible."
Seriel looked at Amy affectionately, understanding her unspoken feelings. Amy wanted to
be with Shirone in the graduating class more than anyone.
"But isn't it cool? I could never do it myself, but everyone thinks about it at least
once—the desire to astonish the world."
Seriel gazed at Shirone with envy.
Shirone's face, beaming under the students' cheers, showed not a trace of worry.
Amy's eyes held a longing she couldn't express. Deep down, she knew she wanted to stand
by Shirone's side more than anyone.
But she shook her head as if rejecting her own emotions.
'Right, I don't have time for this if I want to graduate. I won't ever look up to you.'
She wanted to be with Shirone. To climb to the highest place and smile the happiest smile
in the world. Suppressing those thoughts, Amy turned away.
'So… hurry up and follow me, you idiot.'
