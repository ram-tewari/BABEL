Chapter 1201:
Number Seven, driving a massive drill machine, spotted a barrier of Zets
blocking the road ahead and pushed the throttle to its maximum.
Marsha, gripping onto a support bar, shouted.
"Are you crazy?"
"Then do you want to fight them? The only way through is to break past
them."
The next moment, the drill of the excavation machine bore into the wall
of Zets.
Every time an android was crushed like a tin can under the machine's
treads, the cockpit jolted violently.
"The whole machine is shaking this much? Its durability is no
joke."
Finally, the road ahead cleared, but on the other side, just as many
Zets were gathering again.
"Eliminate delusions."
As bullets rained down, Number Seven yelled.
"Emergency escape!"
The mining team was ejected into the sky just before the drill machine
exploded, launching them ten meters through the air.
"Is everyone alright?"
At Number Seven's question, Marsha gritted her teeth.
"Great job, idiot."
"There was no other choice. But we're almost there."
Staring at the tallest building in the city, just twenty meters ahead,
Operator asked.
"Are you sure? There's no guarantee this is the main
system."
After all, assuming the biggest building housed the core was just a
human way of thinking.
"There's no other option. We have nowhere else to go."
All around them, the silver glow of androids shimmered ominously.
"By the way, where's the entrance?"
There was no visible door.
"Let me check."
Lollipop Mark inspected the wall, while Freeman pointed toward the
back.
"Hey, over there."
Zets were already gathering again.
"We'll hold them off!"
As the elite members of the Parrot Mercenaries charged forward, Lollipop
Mark focused on the system.
"What kind of code is this? It's completely different from the era
Mucus was in."
The future had shifted from one where fairies ruled to one where the god
of law had directly intervened.
Operator spoke.
"We don't need to fully decrypt it. We just have to jam the signal
and force the entrance open, right?"
"No, the problem is…"
Before Lollipop Mark could finish speaking, a wave of heat surged toward
them.
The Parrot Mercenaries were being pushed back by the bombardment of the
Zets.
Even though they were formidable warriors in reality, their skills were
useless against the overwhelming technology of the future.
Lollipop Mark clenched her teeth.
"I'm sorry… I can't do it in time."
"There's one way."
Number Seven pulled out an Under Coder card, prompting Operator to
ask.
"You brought other equipment?"
If the engineer who had uploaded the data of the Excavator Extra into
the Apocalypse could do it, then there was a chance.
"Please… let it be something big and strong…"
They were hoping for something on the level of a high-gear boss
creature, but Number Seven looked hesitant.
"The data capacity isn't infinite. I had to be
selective."
"Fine, whatever, just do it. What is it?"
Number Seven turned to Operator.
"Don't get mad."
The moment he pressed the button, a sharp gust of wind roared from the
sky above.
"Huh?"
As Operator looked up, her eyes widened—then with a loud thud, a
mechanical device engulfed her whole body.
Lollipop Mark blinked in confusion.
"Mid Gear?"
It was a unit designed to charge and preserve high-gear mechs.
"I was saving it for a more fitting moment, but…"
As the locks of the Mid Gear disengaged, Operator emerged—her body
radiating a dark aura, moving with a sultry grace.
She no longer looked like a girl, but a woman.
"The moment was just fine."
It probably wasn't the romantic atmosphere Number Seven had
envisioned.
Scratching his head, Number Seven sighed.
"Tch, whatever. Just make sure to manage your energy output. You
can pull data, but there's no way to recharge here."
They had only one shot.
"That's enough."
Watching as the mercenaries were pushed to the brink, Operator kicked
off the ground and dashed forward.
Moving like the wind, she weaved through the swarm of Zets, drawing her
black-bladed tachi.
"Collapse."
A magnetic field expanded into a black sphere, pulling in all androids
within its radius.
The mining team instinctively covered their ears, but the metallic
shockwave vibrated through their bones, rattling their eardrums.
When Number Seven finally dared to open his eyes, he saw a massive heap
of crushed androids, compressed into a perfect sphere.
"Operator!"
His concern was unnecessary. Operator spun elegantly in midair before
landing beside him.
"Why the panic? Did you forget who I am?"
Before Yahweh had seized control, she had been the undefeated legend of
High Gear.
Staring at the towering structure before her, she spoke.
"Step aside. I'll break it."
Lollipop Mark backed away.
"Yeah… we might have no other choice. Honestly, at this
rate…"
Hacking into the main system was uncertain, but he didn't voice that
thought out loud.
"I'm going all out."
As Operator raised her black tachi, preparing to strike, Number Seven
turned pale.
If she failed to break through, her mech would be destroyed.
"Are you sure about this?"
"Yes. Using half-strength would just waste energy. I have to finish
this in one shot."
As the group watched in tense silence, Operator increased the power in
her lower half, ready to launch forward.
Just then, a rectangular outline appeared on the building's wall, and a
door slid open to the sides.
The hum of Operator's engines quickly faded.
Marsha asked,
"What the hell? Why did it open on its own?"
"Could it be a trap?"
Operator shook her head.
"No. If even Lollipop Mark couldn't open the door, there'd be no
point in setting a trap here."
Strapping her black tachi onto her back, she stepped forward into the
darkness beyond the door.
"Let's trust it. We're not the only ones in this city."
"… Huh?"
Marsha gazed beyond the city.
"Shirone should have arrived by now."
But he was nowhere to be seen.
An emergency assembly was convened within the Holy War.
Havitz's influence had grown strong enough to disrupt the very
foundation of the assembly's purpose.
"This cannot go on any longer."
The kings of various nations—or rather, those who were still able to
attend—gathered as discussions continued.
"How long are we just going to stand by and take this? Though weakened,
the demons are still a significant threat to humanity. We must elect a Global Governing Body as soon as
possible and unite our strength."
Even those who had not so much as blinked while watching people die had
now begun to realize the gravity of the situation.
'The tides have shifted.'
Albino stroked his chin, lost in thought.
'Twenty minutes ago, Havitz attempted to assassinate the King of Corona.
Now that he has begun hunting down world leaders, the grand cause of peace among humanity has become
meaningless.'
Expecting a king to fulfill the duties and responsibilities of their
title was nothing more than a collective delusion.
'It's no different from believing in a god that doesn't exist and
assuming it to be benevolent.'
The chairperson spoke.
"There are no objections, I presume. In that case, when would be the
best time to hold the vote?"
As soon as the discussion turned to specifics, the national leaders fell
silent.
In truth, they wanted to hold the vote immediately and get it over with,
but if they did so in haste, no one would recognize the legitimacy of the governing body that was
elected.
'But it can't be delayed too long either.'
A pervasive thought filled the room—any one of them could become
Havitz's next victim within the next hour.
Thus, the agreed-upon time was set: 7 a.m. the following morning—the
absolute minimum authority the leaders were willing to concede.
The chairperson summarized,
"Then, by unanimous agreement of the nations present at the Holy War, we
will proceed with the vote tomorrow morning at 7 a.m., ahead of the original schedule. Officials from
each department, please make the necessary announcements to your respective nations within the next two
hours."
There weren't many major events left on the schedule.
The only noteworthy item was a final night of performances—a
collaborative show by artists from around the world.
"So, it has come to this. Now, no one can predict what will
happen."
At Albino's remark, Lupist nodded.
"The one who reaches into the mud first and seizes the treasure wins.
Thormia still has a chance."
"Everyone gathered here is, in a way, a professional gambler. They won't
make a bet unless they're certain. Becoming a national leader is not something one achieves through luck
alone."
"There's no room for bluffing anymore. People will favor certainty over
high-risk plays."
"In the early days of the Holy War, there were some sure bets. Uorin's
Kashan, the Moon Kingdom's divination, Kittra's astrology… But now?"
Uorin was unable to attend the assembly, the Moon Kingdom had been
annihilated, and Kittra had withdrawn from the front lines.
"Keshia."
Albino muttered.
"In this situation, the faction holding the most reliable information is
Keshia."
He turned his gaze toward King Fermi of Keshia, who remained seated,
unmoving.
'I wish I could crack open his skull and see inside.'
Albino's eyes narrowed.
'Son of Yolga, what exactly do you know?'
Fermi glanced at his watch.
'Tomorrow at 7 a.m., huh? In that case… Ymir will move first.'
Up to this moment, his future had not changed.
Ymir, Deep Layer 1.
Gaold's group had arrived at the primal subconscious, the place where
the unconscious mind of birth lay dormant.
Beneath the realm of numbness stretched an immeasurable land—formed from
the bodies of ten billion Gaians.
"Even if there were ten billion humans, it wouldn't be enough to
create a landmass of this scale. That means the symbolism here is…"
Arius spoke.
"It represents his everything."
The very body of Ymir, born from the unification of ten billion
Gaians.
The group examined the ground.
It was distorted, as if molten lava had cooled into grooves resembling
fingerprints.
Here and there, arms jutted out from the surface, and faces sculpted
into the terrain gazed blankly at the sky.
Arius observed.
"As expected, this is different. None of the usual mental
constructs found in an ordinary person's subconscious are here—no cities, no sanctuaries, no climate.
That means Ymir never perceived any of these things. He was the strongest from the moment of
birth."
Miro frowned.
"The air is thick."
"Yes. It's the atmosphere of desire. The fact that the Gaians
became land means Ymir has completely dominated the body. From that control, desire fumes rise and fill
this entire world."
"The volcanic eruption in Deep Layer 2 must have been caused by
this atmosphere igniting."
"Correct. The gas from Layer 1 erupted into Layer 2. Given Gaold's
power, even that was an extremely rare occurrence."
Arius turned around.
"In any case, the structure of this world is simple. The land
represents the body, the atmosphere represents emotion, and the mind…"
The ultima of ten billion Gaians.
"It should be over there."
Everyone lifted their heads, following Arius's gaze.
"My god."
Kangnan stood in stunned silence, mouth slightly open.
At the center of the sky, which was filled with light, an enormous hole
had been torn open.
"A black hole," Miro murmured.
Arius nodded.
"Yes. If the light spread across the sky represents the minds of
the Gaians, then that black void is swallowing all of it. That means it is…"
A voice came from directly ahead.
"That's right. What you're looking for is the Ultima
System."
Startled, everyone lowered their gaze to see Ymir sitting before them,
resting his chin on his hand.
"It's the power to rewrite the law."
In other words, gravity.
The very force that dictated how fiercely something existed within the
vast stage of the universe.
