Chapter 778:
Near midnight.
All the districts of Aganos, the imperial palace of Kashan, were
brightly lit, and a grand festival filled the streets.
Nighttime Kashan.
The dazzling energy emitted by the city was like witnessing an unknown
civilization hidden deep within the universe.
Flying beside Jet, Shirone asked,
"Are we going straight in again this time?"
While the card players sat in a row behind Jet, Minerva was lost in
thought.
This is different from Nameimond.
As one of the Five Great Stars of the Ivory Tower, she had no reason to
hesitate even in Kashan, but Uorin was a special case.
Mitochondrial Eve…
Her ability to read global affairs through History Search made her the
most influential among the Three Emperors.
She was also the commander of the Valkyries, the elite anti-Heaven unit,
and the Holy War itself implicitly regarded her as their leader.
"Kashan's military isn't a concern. The real problem is the Aganos Royal
Guard, who are world-famous."
Minerva held up three fingers.
"Their code of deployment goes like this: Far guards protect the
Empress's buildings. Middle guards protect her space. Near guards protect her person."
"So the Far unit is outside the palace, the Middle is inside the palace,
and the Near unit is on the floor where Uorin resides."
Minerva bit down lightly on her pipe.
"The Aganos Royal Guard – Near. They say even the weakest among them is
on par with a general. If we trespass into their zone, they will attack."
Even though Uorin had requested the meeting, without specific orders,
the guards would have no choice but to draw their swords.
"Let's land just outside the palace. That's for the best."
"You've got it backward. I'm saying they're tough—so we need to smash
our way through from the start. We're going straight to the Empress's chamber."
Shirone narrowed his eyes.
"Why are you always like this?"
"As I always say…"
A blast echoed from Jet's ejection port.
"You do you, I do me!"
Tearing through the sky at terrifying speed, Minerva's eyes gleamed with
intensity.
You really tricked me good.
Back then, she hadn't even considered the possibility that Uorin was
Teraje.
Wait… it's quiet?
Even though the Far Guard of the Aganos Royal Guard should have
mobilized by now, there was no sign of movement from the ground.
"Hold on a second!"
Shirone shouted while hurrying after her, but the rising fury in
Minerva's chest only burned hotter.
Whatever you're thinking—you picked the wrong person to mess
with!
As Minerva and the card players rose into the air, Jet launched like an
arrow.
With a thunderous boom, the wall exploded.
"What is wrong with her?"
Shirone grumbled, but it was already too late to undo the
situation.
"Hmph."
Having breached the palace through the collapsed wall, Minerva pulled
Jet from where it had embedded in the floor.
"No one's here?"
Shirone entered with the card players following behind, holding out a
hand.
"Wait a second. This doesn't seem right…"
At that moment, Gando entered through a door.
"You've arrived. Her Majesty is waiting."
Despite their unannounced entry and the destruction of royal property,
Gando didn't mention a word about it.
"What? Where's the Royal Guard?"
"There was a special order. We were instructed to ensure the guests are
met without inconvenience."
In any empire other than Kashan, this might have seemed like an
over-the-top welcome, but this was Mitochondrial Eve they were dealing with.
Was this all planned too?
As old memories came flooding back, Minerva clicked her tongue and
followed Gando, who guided them deeper into the palace.
The room they arrived at was completely different from the one Shirone
had been shown on his last visit.
"You're here, Shirone."
Inside a room sparkling with crystal chandeliers, Uorin stood with her
hands clasped together.
"……"
Minerva's jaw dropped, and even the card players were stunned into
silence.
She wore light makeup, a red ribbon around her neck, and a black dress
that suited her silver hair perfectly.
"So beautiful…"
Shirone realized it was the most beautiful thing he had ever
seen.
Well done, Gando.
Seeing the uncontainable joy in Uorin's eyes, Gando finally let out a
sigh of relief.
It's up to you now, Your Majesty.
In Gando's eyes, what Shirone found beautiful was indeed the most
beautiful of all.
Minerva, on the other hand, had doubt in her eyes.
What is this? What the hell is going on?
It was such a far cry from the Teraje she knew that her thoughts
scrambled.
This is like…
The look of a woman in love.
It's not strange for Teraje to want Shirone.
But if it were truly urgent, she could have simply extracted his seed by
force using any means necessary.
Trying to win it with love… it's too inefficient. No—it's not even about
efficiency. Teraje would never care about that.
Shirone spoke up.
"Sorry about the mess. I wanted to come in quietly."
"It's fine. I knew you were coming and prepared for it."
When Uorin learned that Minerva would be accompanying them, she had
considered the possibility that Minerva might ruin everything.
"As expected of Uorin. You look amazing today."
Uorin almost lost consciousness from dizziness.
What do I do? This is worse than I expected.
This wasn't some passing crush—this was someone she'd carried in her
heart since the prehistoric age.
Her desire had grown so immense, it felt like her rationality was being
crushed under it. Her body felt feverish, almost beastlike.
Minerva approached with a smile.
"Long time no see, sis."
"Ugh."
With that one cold splash of water across the heat, Uorin's expression
crumpled.
"Sis?"
Tilting his head in confusion, Shirone remembered the contents of
Uorin's letter and realized:
Come to think of it…
Minerva pointed to Uorin.
"Of course she's my big sister. Or maybe I should say senior? She taught
me all sorts of magical delights back in the day."
Before becoming Kashan's Empress, Teraje had lived the life of a
witch—but Uorin had no desire to bring that past back up.
"It's just a part of history. But as you know, Teraje isn't just one
person. The one who mentored you is no longer in this world."
"Must be nice—living so conveniently."
Teraje had changed bodies repeatedly over generations, whereas Minerva's
body was still the same from her witch days.
Uorin gave her a pointed look.
"I do regret what happened back then. But I didn't guide you into
witchcraft. We just met by chance—"
"Hoho! That's right! We met back then! It's all coming back now."
Minerva placed a hand on Shirone's shoulder and looked at Uorin,
whispering in his ear:
"Do you know how incredible Teraje was as a witch? I think her name was
Electra or something. Wasn't it?"
"Hey, can we save the old stories for another time?"
But Minerva didn't stop.
"I learned so much from working with her. I was honestly shocked. At one
point, she—what was it? Had nearly a hundred men at once—"
Minerva was chattering away in high spirits when suddenly her eyes
wavered.
"……"
Uorin was trembling, her lips tightly sealed and her eyes filled with
tears.
From Gando's body, a murderous aura began to rise.
Should I kill her?
He might have to summon the executioner after all.
"Uorin, are you okay?"
As Shirone reached out a hand, Uorin suddenly turned away and stormed
out of the room.
Shirone stared blankly after her, then furrowed his brows and turned to
Minerva.
"Why do you keep bringing up things she doesn't want to talk
about?"
"What did I do? If she wanted to talk about it, she could have. Either
way, it's all true, isn't it?"
Shirone couldn't argue any further, even if he understood Minerva's
position.
Gando stepped forward with his chin slightly raised.
"You thought carefully before running your mouth, I hope?"
"Try jumping in when it's your place to speak, rookie. Anyway, go calm
your Empress down and bring her back. We've got work to do."
"I'll go."
Shirone turned to Gando.
"Please take me to Uorin. This happened because of me. If I talk to her,
I think I can calm her down."
Gando paused, deep in thought.
If he sees Her Majesty as even the slightest bit impure…
Then he would call upon the full military might of Kashan to erase
Minerva from existence.
"Please."
Gando stared into Shirone's eyes for a moment, then turned away.
He's not that kind of person.
He had realized this from the very first time they met at the Tormia
Magic Association.
"Follow me. She's probably in the annex."
Guided by Gando, Shirone arrived in front of Uorin's room and knocked
gently.
"Uorin, are you in there?"
There was no reply.
"I'm coming in for a moment."
Nodding to Gando, Shirone opened the door and stepped inside—then
blinked in surprise, hand still on the doorknob.
Uorin sat cross-legged on the bed, smiling as she gestured him
in.
"Ah…"
Mindful of Gando, Shirone quickly closed the door behind him and
entered. Uorin stood up from the bed.
"You came. Sit here."
"Are you feeling any better?"
"Not at all. I really can't stand that woman. But… it's not like I don't
understand her."
Uorin offered him a drink.
"Have some. No sleeping pills in it, don't worry."
Only then did he feel reassured.
"To be honest, I wasn't exactly composed either. I got emotional,
thinking I was finally going to see you. Minerva helped cool me down."
"Then why did you run off in tears?"
"There were things I wanted to say to you in private."
As Shirone set the glass down and waited, Uorin averted her gaze and
spoke.
"Will you make love with me here and now?"
"Uorin, that's…"
"I know. There's someone else in your heart. And you think that feeling
will never change, right?"
Shirone nodded honestly.
"I have a great responsibility to Kashan. So let's do this, right here.
Let's make love and end it all with that."
"I can't. That would…"
It would be a betrayal of Amy.
"I'm saying this for your sake."
Uorin looked him in the eye.
"I can't tell you what it is, but I know a way to make you love
me—without fail. If I use it, you'll have no choice but to become mine, whether you like it or
not."
He had never believed it was possible for someone's heart to be forcibly
taken.
"Gando worries about me. But I'm not afraid. I've spent long years
preparing, and the way to win your heart is already perfectly laid out in my mind."
Coming from Mitochondrial Eve, the architect of history, it couldn't be
brushed off as a bluff.
"In the end, I'll have you—regardless of your sorrow. In fact, I want it
that way. So this is the last mercy my emotions will allow. If you love me just for today, I'll let you
go."
When Shirone didn't respond, Uorin added,
"Let me be clear. There won't be a second chance. This is your last
chance to love Amy."
The last chance.
Will I really end up choosing Uorin?
He had no idea how that would happen, but he knew she wouldn't tell him
even if he asked.
"Nothing will change."
Shirone said firmly.
"I don't want to hurt Amy. If I truly loved you, that'd be different.
But unless that happens, I'll never change how I feel."
"…I understand."
Uorin closed her eyes.
So it ends like this.
Even if foundational events didn't matter due to the Great Reset,
Mitochondrial Eve's foresight was beyond human.
By consolidating all events that had occurred thus far, she could
vaguely foresee what would happen next.
Above all, Uorin was a Teraje whose future sight ability was the
strongest of them all.
"Alright, Shirone."
Uorin smiled.
"Let's go. We have work to do."
Because she believed he would eventually fall into her arms, Uorin was
able to let him go—with a smile.
