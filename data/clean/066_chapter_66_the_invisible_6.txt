Chapter 66:
The Invisible (6)
"Whew. It's already midnight. Time flies."
Maria, who had been studying in her dorm, closed her book and stretched lazily.
The faint sound of the bell echoed outside.
Unconsciously, she recalled the presentation. She shook her head quickly, trying to erase
the thought.
But the more she tried to forget, the more it nagged at her.
Neid's words about the hungry ghosts appearing at midnight lingered in her mind.
"Ugh, what's wrong with me? Why can't I stop thinking about it?"
Shirone had anticipated this. Once a keyword was planted, the imagination would take
over, no further persuasion needed.
'If you match the frequency of souls, you'll see ghosts?'
Thoughts spiraled. Maria shuddered and turned toward her bed. At the same time, her heart
plummeted. Her pupils shook as an electric jolt shot up her spine.
"Uh… uh…"
A rotting corpse was staggering around her room.
Maria's jaw trembled uncontrollably.
The skeletal soldier turned toward her, raising its arms as it approached.
"Kyaaaaaaah!"
Her scream shook the windows. But no one could hear her. The same screams echoed through
every hallway.
***
Crash!
A flower vase shattered against the wall.
Amy, who had thrown it, pressed her limbs against the opposite wall, panting heavily.
"What the hell? What the hell?"
The moment the bell rang, the ghosts had appeared. Was this the resonance Neid had
mentioned? At first, she thought it was a hallucination. But the figures before her were undeniably real.
"Grrrrr…"
As the ghost let out a guttural moan, Amy activated her schema and bolted for the door.
She flung it open with enough force to break it and found girls screaming and running through the hallway.
Amy froze in horror. Dozens of ghosts were chasing them, their jaws clacking.
"Amy! Help me! Ghosts appeared!"
Seriel cried, tears and snot streaming down her face. Amy paled. She had never believed
in ghosts, but fear was a primal instinct.
"Ugh, ugh!"
Swept up in the panicked crowd, Amy began running toward the building's exit.
***
"Grrrr…"
A grotesque, transparent figure broke the silence of the secluded training hall.
Etela emerged from meditation. She stood and turned to face the approaching specter.
Though the thought of a ghost crossed her mind, her ascetic heart felt no particular fear.
Squinting, Etela examined the ghost now inches from her face. The ghost tilted its head,
studying her in return. It was proof of how precise Iruki's equations were.
"Grrrr…"
"Grrrr?"
Curious, Etela raised her hand and poked the soldier's nose. Of course, her finger passed
straight through to the back of its skull.
***
"Haah! Haah!"
In the frozen veil of steam, Siena clutched her naked body, her breath ragged.
It was her habit to bathe in the women's dormitory's public bath on duty nights. But
tonight, the midnight bell had startled her so badly she thought her heart would stop.
"What in the world?"
The first thing that came to mind was the soul's frequency. Did that mean Neid's words
were true? Impossible. But she couldn't deny what she was seeing.
"Grrrr…"
The ghostly soldier phased through the ice barrier. Strangely, this calmed Siena. It
wasn't that she had figured out it was a hologram—her pride as a mage told her no being could so easily
nullify her ice magic.
"How dare you…!"
Siena strode forward confidently. As expected, the ghost passed harmlessly through her.
Realization dawned.
She spotted two devices installed on the ceiling and walls. Tiny, hemispherical units
emitted thin beams of light.
Siena sighed and stepped out of the bath.
The moment she entered the changing room, the water on her body froze with a sharp crack.
A light shake sent diamond-like ice shards scattering. Not a drop remained on her.
"These little…!"
Siena's eyes narrowed sharply.
***
Alpheus Magic School was in chaos.
Darkened windows lit up one after another as students screamed and spilled out of
buildings.
The same scene played out everywhere.
The holograms, programmed to anticipate human behavior, moved freely, and the terrified
students fled blindly, unaware they'd been tricked.
"Aaaaah! Help!"
"Ghosts! Ghosts are here!"
Human behavior under extreme fear was surprisingly predictable. With rationality
paralyzed, only instinct remained.
Lower-class students cried for their mothers, while upper-class students, separated by
gender, merged into the largest crowds.
From the higher grounds, graduating-class students—mostly girls with a few boys mixed
in—poured down like a flood.
The ghosts appeared to chase randomly, but from above, it was clear they were herding
everyone toward one place. Soon, nearly 400 students had gathered in the central park.
Trapped between people and buildings, the students finally realized: ghosts surrounded
them on all sides, leaving no escape.
"Kyaaaaaah! They're coming! Do something!"
"Aaaaah! Bring it on! Come at me, you bastards!"
Amid the screams, hundreds of soldiers raised their swords and lunged. The
students—whether screaming, crying, or defiant—all shut their eyes tightly.
At that moment, light burst from the soldiers' bodies. Their forms flashed like strobes,
then shot skyward toward a single point.
"W-what…?"
Every gaze turned to the rooftop of the central building. Shirone stood on the railing.
Neid and Iruki arrived late, taking positions on either side.
"Thank you all for attending our presentation."
The students stared blankly. They still couldn't grasp the situation.
Teachers rushed into the park, panting. Faculty members of all levels—lower, upper, and
graduating classes—had mobilized.
Siena pushed through the crowd, fuming.
"You! Get down here right now! What do you think you're doing, turning the school upside
down?!"
Neid winked at her cheerfully.
"Ah, Professor Siena. Lovely night, isn't it?"
"Lovely night my foot! Get down here now, or I'll come up there and kill you!"
"But this is still a presentation. Let us finish before we go."
A presentation? At this hour? The students murmured in confusion.
"Wait, so it was all fake?"
"We've been tricked! Damn it! I almost had a heart attack!"
Most were still dazed, but the quicker-witted few pointed and shouted at Shirone's group.
"You con artists! How dare Class Five pull a stunt like this? This isn't a
presentation—it's a scam!"
"They should get zero points for this! Ghosts don't exist! You're finished!"
Despite the barrage of insults, Shirone's group remained unfazed. When the jeering showed
no sign of stopping, Neid raised his hand to quiet them.
"The Supernatural Spiritual Science Research Club has proven the existence of ghosts
through this presentation. Yet you still refuse to believe."
"Of course we don't! You can't just make up ghosts and expect us to accept it! You're
definitely failing!"
"Yeah! This is just a cheap prank! Anyone could do this! If I wanted to—"
A Class Four boy trailed off, blinking. Others followed suit, murmuring as they looked
around.
"But… how did they even do this? Do you know?"
"No, I can't even begin to guess. It felt so real. And they fooled all 400 of us."
The students realized this was no ordinary prank.
Creating hundreds of holograms was impressive enough, but how they had been controlled
remained a mystery.
"Those bastards… what kind of trick did they pull?"
As unrest grew among the students, Siena pinched her brow and closed her eyes. Honestly,
even she couldn't yet figure out their methods.
It was impossible to immediately deduce the combination of Neid's engineering, photon
emission from the infinite realm, and Iruki's countless equations.
"Hohohoho. It seems we've been outplayed this time."
The teachers turned.
Alpheus laughed heartily as he gazed up at Shirone's group.
Siena shook her head and approached him.
"Headmaster, this is no laughing matter. Whatever they did, they illegally trespassed
into restricted areas. There's evidence of hologram devices—"
"Oh? So even Professor Siena doesn't know?"
Alpheus found it understandable. Had he not specialized in light magic, he too might have
missed the information transmission techniques woven into their strategy.
But was this truly possible for students?
In a way, it had been a gamble for Shirone's group. Alpheus wanted to praise them for
that.
Siena admitted frankly.
"It's embarrassing, but I don't know yet. However, just because I don't understand the
mechanism doesn't mean they've convinced the faculty."
"Hoho, I misspoke. Being outplayed wasn't what I meant."
"Huh? Then…?"
Alpheus held his tongue and looked back at the students.
"Let's watch for now. The presentation isn't over yet."
