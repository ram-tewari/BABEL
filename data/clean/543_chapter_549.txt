Chapter 549:
There was no choice but to pick either left or right.
Even with grotesque senses, it was impossible to detect Rian's movements
because his actions were perfectly split between left and right.
Existing with exactly a 50% probability on either side was a feat
impossible without the power of the law.
And that movement forced Paknyeo into a life-or-death gamble.
'Left!'
As the signs of an explosion on the ground approached her, Paknyeo
twisted her body and raised her blade.
Bam! Rian's greatsword slammed down.
There was no time to assess the force behind his strike; the moment of
collision, Paknyeo leapt back to create distance.
"Grrraaah!"
Rian, who had swung his massive sword, staggered.
At the same time, his thigh and calf muscles tore, producing sickening
sounds.
Pain surged through his remaining nerves, but Paknyeo was equally out of
breath.
'Blocked it. Did I block it?'
She had managed to twist to the left and parry Rian's strike, but she
knew deep down it was purely a matter of luck.
Her decision to act, trusting a 50% chance rather than hesitating in the
face of danger, showed how honed she was as a swordswoman.
But looking at it from another perspective...
'I almost died.'
A 50% chance.
Someone who had lived for over 10,000 years had nearly been cut down by
the blade of someone who had lived for barely 20.
For a Yacha, whose longing for life had long faded into ancient memory,
the humiliation was immense.
'I will cut him down!'
Paknyeo unleashed her Nirvana Energy to its absolute limit and charged
at Rian.
'All I need to do is avoid that.'
The power of Deny combined with super-regeneration was stronger than any
Axing, so she didn't give him the slightest chance to recover.
Rian, on the other hand, moved his nearly skeletal arms using the power
of the law to fend off her attacks.
If Paknyeo was acceleration, Rian was pure speed.
The gap in skill and experience was undeniable, but he endured by
compensating for it with his speed advantage.
"Rian…"
Shirone, who had been watching the battle in a daze, muttered.
"You've become stronger."
Though his memories were chaotic and mixed up, watching Rian's
devastatingly beautiful combat stirred a sense of joy deep within him.
"Lord Consul."
At that moment, Shirone's heart sank.
He recognized the voice immediately because it was drenched in the waves
of death.
Turning his head, he saw Yuga, exhausted and on his knees, with smoke of
desire escaping from him.
"Yuga…"
"Lord Consul, the colony's sun…"
Before he could finish his sentence, Fermi's air blade sliced Yuga's
neck.
"Yuga!"
Shirone's heart pounded wildly.
She was a woman who once seemed to own the world with a single shining
spell.
Despite being born a slave, she was the first to believe in and follow
Shirone.
'They're all dead.'
The bodies of the guardians piled up beneath the pyramid, and Fermi and
Marsha advanced past them.
"Lord Consul."
Shirone cursed having ears, for he had to hear what he didn't want to,
but his head turned involuntarily.
Priest Bebeto was smiling kindly with his eyes.
"Lord Consul, you are one who possesses the language of the gods. That
is why I proposed the duel."
Because of that, Shirone's life was spared.
"Please, create a better world."
"No!"
The moment Shirone shouted, Bebeto connected his code and
vanished.
Appearing before Fermi and Marsha, all Bebeto could do was use his body
as a flesh shield to buy time.
Shirone watched as Bebeto was slashed repeatedly by the dagger of
betrayal.
"Please, create a better world."
Someone had said that before. Bebeto had said that.
Even if all of this was a lie, could he call their pain a lie
too?
It can all be undone, human.
As the voice of the Digital Ra pierced his mind, Shirone looked down at
his feet.
At some point, new mucus had climbed up his body.
"Undone?"
You can save them. You can erase all the world's
pain.
The mucus reached his waist.
Accept me. Become a god and recreate this world.
"Rian…"
Even as Rian continued his desperate battle against Paknyeo, it felt as
if his entire being was telling Shirone not to give in.
"I'm sorry."
Shirone calmly closed his eyes and accepted the Digital Ra.
"Haah!"
When the fusion was complete and the Immortal Function activated, an
avatar with wings of light emerged from Shirone's body.
As if soaring toward the sun, the mucus lifted Shirone into the air
above the pyramid.
"It's begun!"
Shura, who had been fighting Miro, widened her eyes and turned to the
pyramid.
As light more intense than the sun enveloped the ground, everyone looked
to the sky.
Shirone, now merged with the La, completely seized control of
Apocalypse's time and space.
"Ah…"
The human agony Shirone saw through the Digital Ra was a combination of
countless random variables.
Within that combination were war, famine, torture, and every conceivable
kind of pain.
What are you seeing?
A voice he'd heard somewhere before.
From the deepest recesses of his memory, the voice of a Gaian surfaced
and struck his mind.
'Pain.'
What are you seeing?
'Death. The end of life.'
WHAT ARE YOU SEEING?
Shirone's eyes flew open.
'The ultimate!'
As the Ultima System and Elysion activated, another voice, inscribed in
the deepest history of humanity, reached Shirone.
What do you see at the ultimate end?
Shirone answered.
"... Void. (Form is emptiness, and emptiness is form)."
Rumble... Rumble...
As the world trembled, Shura activated the pyramid.
'Show me what you have.'
Miro also needed to understand what collateral Shirone had used to
enable the equivalent exchange of the Valhalla Axion.
'What exactly happened?'
The world began to dissolve into digital code.
While it was expected for unique codes and numbers to flow like rain in
the Under Coder, what came next was astonishing.
'The codes are simplifying.'
The unique codes, which were infinite decimals, started simplifying,
shrinking from billions to mere decimal numbers.
'Base-5. Base-3. Base-2.'
By the time Shura analyzed this far, her mind went blank, and she
stared, her mouth agape.
"Base-1..."
One.
All the codes had been reduced to 1.
Even in Base-2, Shura could decipher information about the sun or the
clouds, but now it was impossible.
Even if the sun's data was "11" and the cloud's data was
"111," there was no way to distinguish between them in the stream of "11111."
'No boundaries. This is...'
The Gaiaians' insight—Elysion.
Furthermore, Miro realized:
'Its ultimate simplicity allows it to reach the ultimate end.'
And beyond that lay the realm of infinity.
Elysion was the sole key capable of unlocking the door from a trapped
state into an open one.
As the corpses of the guardians and the subterraneans were swept into
the flow of the great "1," Shura snapped out of it and lunged toward Paknyeo.
'The void is closing in!'
Pulling Paknyeo back by her collar as she dodged Rian's blade, Shura
shouted.
"We have to get out! It's resetting!"
"Wait! The fight isn't over yet...!"
Shura adjusted herself and Paknyeo to Shirone's protocol, decomposing
their bodies into the code of "1."
Rian watched the cascade of code flowing like a waterfall, with the
"1" collective rising against the current.
For a brief moment, as if by an illusion, he thought he saw the
boundary, but it quickly dissolved, indistinguishable from other codes.
Meanwhile, Shirone remained in the dark space of the microcosm, still
gazing beyond the ultimate.
"Why do you leave?"
The Mucus Man appeared behind Shirone.
Unable to connect with Shirone through digital signals, he had assumed a
human form to chase him.
But he could not touch Shirone.
Even though the distance between them was less than a meter, the
boundary of the void was an impassable divide for La.
"Have you forgotten the value of this world? After seeing its endless
pain and the entanglement of life, can you still leave?"
"There is no truth or falsehood."
Shirone turned and spoke.
"It's all just a fleeting dream that arose from nothing."
With those words, Shirone slowly turned back and walked into the
darkness.
"Wait, Shirone! You owe me an answer. Why...!"
La could not hold Shirone back.
That was the vast gulf between the trapped state and the open state,
farther than the ends of the universe.
"Shironeee!"
Apocalypse Reset.
Shirone erased (departure from the information system).
"What just happened?"
Miro and the others found themselves left in a pale, empty space where
the code had vanished.
While Marsha and Fermi looked around, Rian collapsed to the ground, his
strength gone.
Only Miro understood what had occurred.
"Rian."
Cutting through the white landscape, Shirone appeared. Rian dragged his
injured body up and rushed toward him.
"Shirone, you..."
"Yes. I remember everything now. I'm sorry for making you worry."
Rian bit his lip.
For Shirone to say he remembered everything meant he had realized that
even he was merely information.
"I'm sorry. But to save you..."
"Rian, I'm fine."
Shirone was sincere.
"Thank you for coming all this way to save me."
He turned to look at the others.
"Miro, Marsha, thank you both. Fermi, you too."
The lack of hostility in Shirone's voice toward Fermi suggested the
state he was currently in.
"Well, no need to thank me," Fermi said with a shrug, stepping forward
and manifesting the depreciation deal contract in his right hand.
"First, just sign this."
Shirone looked at the contract, but it was as if nothing registered in
his mind.
Taking the pen, he wrote his name on it.
"Is that all?"
Without a word, Fermi turned and walked away.
As he stared at the contract and disappeared into a space where no one
could see him, his shoulders trembled slightly.
'Heh heh, heh heh heh.'
Though the contract still needed to be transferred to reality to be
valid, Fermi's face was already twisted into a grotesque grin of triumph.
'Finally scored it! 1.9 billion gold!'
Watching Fermi's retreating figure, Shirone smiled—a hollow yet
beautiful expression.
He hoped reality would not trouble him too much, but it no longer
mattered.
"Well, goodbye, everyone."
"Shirone, wait!"
Before Rian's cry could end, the scenery shifted, becoming the mirrored
chamber that housed the final exit of the Under Coder.
"Shirone..."
Miro placed a hand on Rian's shoulder.
"Don't be upset. For Shirone as he is now, this is natural. The real
issue lies with Shirone in the real world."
"What do you mean?" Rian asked.
"The Ultima System integrates all information into one. Therefore,
Shirone's Elysion has no boundaries. In other words, there's now the potential to control the Immortal
Function to its extreme."
Fermi's face twisted into a frown.
"He was already a tough opponent before reaching heaven, but now this?
It's going to be a nightmare."
"Whatever it is, Shirone getting stronger is a good thing, right?" Rian
asked.
Miro shook his head.
"No. Elysion is a double-edged sword. Beyond the ultimate lies nothing.
I suspect the void it entails is beyond what any human can endure. Can Shirone return to reality after
facing that void?"
Rian's expression turned blank.
"But that means..."
"I know. The Apocalypse's world cannot surpass the limits of human
imagination. So we can't guarantee it'll be possible in reality either. But if something like today's
situation happens again..."
After a brief pause, Miro spoke.
"Shirone will never come back."
