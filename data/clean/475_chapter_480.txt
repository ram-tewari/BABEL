Chapter 480:
"Absolutely cannot win?"
Shirone didn't dismiss Mirka's words lightly.
The reason she could remain unscathed despite being hit directly by the
photon cannons was likely due to her use of the fairy's Unregulated Form. Therefore, Shirone thought her
words were not mere bluff.
"What on earth is it?"
Shirone fired the homing photon cannons once more.
As expected, Mirka didn't dodge, and the photon cannons exploded with a
powerful impact.
Seeing Mirka's slight smile, Shirone realized something new.
She wasn't blocking the attacks; she was immune to them.
"Then can you withstand this too?"
Shirone fired an even more powerful laser, amplified by magic.
No matter how durable a material is, it can't withstand a laser that
causes molecular vibrations forever. But once again, Mirka received the magic without the slightest
tremor.
Only then did Shirone stop his attack.
"This is troublesome."
He couldn't understand the activation principle, but Mirka's Unregulated
Form was a very simple yet powerful ability.
"Invincible… is that it?"
Mirka nodded calmly.
"That's right. You are weaker than me, so you can never defeat
me."
Mirka's Unregulated Form, Razor of Truth, activates by receiving the
first attack imbued with clear intent to kill.
Once activated, it begins a comparative superiority judgment of the
strengths of the two entities. If Mirka wins, she becomes invincible to any attack from the
opponent.
The comparative superiority judgment is based on the existence of the
avatar, not measurable values. Razor of Truth eliminates all anomalies and variables in battle, making
it impossible to reverse the invincible state.
In practical terms, the winner is considered strong, but Mirka's
Unregulated Form twists this concept to mean that the strong one wins by eliminating all variables in
battle.
To establish this powerful concept, a strict equivalent exchange is
required. If Mirka's gaze is broken, the judgment is canceled, and even if the opponent is kept within
her gaze, the duration is only about 30 minutes.
The most extreme risk is that she must remain defenseless during the
initial attack with clear intent to kill.
If Mirka loses the comparative superiority judgment, she not only
suffers the attack but also activates Razor of Truth in reverse, making the opponent invincible against
her.
In other words, facing an opponent stronger in pure avatar strength
almost guarantees death or defeat.
This extreme condition for implementing invincibility is a well-balanced
fatal condition.
But Mirka never doubted her ability.
And she was currently ranked third among the 72 ranks of fairies.
"Shall we begin?"
Mirka attacked Shirone without hesitation.
Unregulated Form - Law of Submission.
Another Unregulated Form linked to Razor of Truth activated.
As Mirka quickly struck Shirone's solar plexus, a tremendous impact
spread through his body.
"How?"
Even as he struggled to breathe, Shirone felt doubt.
Ringer's Skin, a type of armor, wasn't activating.
Moreover, the physical force was too powerful to be from a fairy.
"This is also an ability."
Shirone's guess was accurate.
The Law of Submission, which activates when Mirka wins the comparative
superiority judgment from Razor of Truth, nullifies all the opponent's attacks and repels the force
generated by the counteraction.
In other words, it delivers a critical hit with double the impact,
making Mirka's physical attacks, in her invincible state, unbearable for Shirone, who was essentially
defenseless.
Mirka continued to physically attack Shirone.
As long as she was facing Shirone, her body was invincible, so her
attacks were relentless.
As Shirone endured the impact of being struck by a heavy rock, one
thought flashed through his mind.
He couldn't win.
Having encountered many Unregulated Forms through experience, various
potential countermeasures came to mind.
One correct answer was to get out of her line of sight, but that wasn't
the core issue.
She was a fairy ranked second among the 72 ranks.
Without a strength that surpassed the avatars of other fairies, Razor of
Truth was an unbeatable ability.
As Mirka immobilized Shirone, numerous fairies followed and unleashed
all kinds of magic.
If it wasn't Mirka's attack, most of the impact was blocked by Armand,
but no defense could withstand continuous strikes indefinitely.
"The problem is the Unregulated Form."
An ability that completely blocks variables in battle.
For Shirone, who had defeated stronger opponents with powerful insight
and adaptability, it was an almost natural enemy.
"This is the end."
Mirka dropped vertically and struck Shirone's abdomen with a physical
attack.
"Ugh!"
He heard the sound of his abdominal muscles tearing and couldn't
breathe.
The sky darkened from the edges, and two-thirds of his vision turned to
darkness.
"Now! Pour everything on him!"
As Armand resonated with the caster's consciousness, its functionality
weakened as Shirone's consciousness faded.
Seeing the tentacles droop like wet grass, the fairies poured all their
magic on him.
They intended to completely sever his consciousness, and his life would
be extinguished before he even hit the ground.
The fairies' total attack entered his narrowed vision.
Watching the dazzlingly colorful magic, Shirone squeezed out his last
bit of strength.
Judicial Halo - Valhalla Action.
As the halo spun, it quickly computed a single spell.
Judicial Halo - Super Magic Amplification Array Ataraxia.
As the massive halo unfolded in front of him, the fairies targeting
Shirone clutched their heads in shock and screamed.
The only one who could look at it was Mirka, already in an invincible
state.
"Damn…"
Seeing the Ataraxia accumulate instantly, she shouted to the surrounding
fairies.
"Everyone, get away!"
As the photon cannons passed through Ataraxia, an enormous flash filled
the sky completely.
The magic pouring down was swept away, and the fairies trapped within
the radius evaporated like flies in the flames.
"Ugh!"
When the flash disappeared, no fairies remained except for Mirka.
Mirka looked around for Shirone, who had vanished from sight, then
clenched her teeth in resignation.
Ataraxia, the signature move of Archangel Ikael.
Even knowing she was invincible, she couldn't act due to the instinctive
fear rooted in the beginning of her consciousness.
"He can't be alive. Falling from this height…"
She wanted to think that, but she couldn't be sure.
"Find him. Don't stop searching until you tear his body
apart."
At Mirka's command, all surviving fairies rushed to the ground.
The fact that they were flying over the most treacherous valley in
Shehakim, known as the Whale's Gorge, made it even more frustrating.
"I should have finished him off for sure."
The face of the third-ranked fairy was severely tarnished.
But it was in the past, so Mirka quickly let go of her regrets and
followed her subordinate fairies, flying swiftly into the gorge.
"Stay conscious. Don't lose consciousness."
As he fell into the gorge, Shirone desperately tried to stay
conscious.
It was an incredible concentration created in a situation that would be
near-fatal for an ordinary person.
As long as he remained conscious, Armand would heal him to some extent
even if he fell.
But if he lost consciousness here, no one could guarantee what would
happen next.
Thud! Thud!
He didn't feel pain.
Only a dull impact struck the back of his head.
Shirone, colliding with the cliff of the gorge, bounced off and hit the
opposite wall again before falling.
With his eyes closed, blood flowed from his forehead.
He was only 500 meters from the ground.
If he fell headfirst, his neck would surely break.
At that moment, a fairy flew towards Shirone at a terrifying speed,
skillfully avoiding the sharp rocks of the valley.
It was Feope, the spiral fairy.
"Ugh!"
Grabbing Shirone's robe, she pulled with all her might, but the inertia
of the fall was too strong.
Using spiral magic to disperse the falling force, she could only slow
the speed.
"Ugh!"
Feeling the pain of her shoulder dislocating, Feope gritted her
teeth.
Thud!
As Shirone hit the ground, her body bounced off and rolled on the
floor.
But without time to feel the pain, she flew to Shirone.
"Shirone! Shirone!"
Seeing no movement and losing consciousness, Feope, terrified, quickly
pressed her ear to his chest.
Thump, thump.
His heart was beating weakly.
It would soon stop, but she felt a slight sense of relief.
"Oh no, this isn't the time for this."
If the other fairies started searching the Whale's Gorge, it was only a
matter of time before they were discovered.
Grabbing his robe and looking around, Feope found a cave just big enough
for one person and dragged Shirone inside.
"Phew, this should be okay for now."
Wiping her sweat, Feope stared blankly at Shirone's face, who seemed
dead in his sleep.
The reason she could track him this far was that she hadn't seen
Ataraxia.
Somehow… she knew what Shirone was going to do.
"Of course."
Feope smiled sadly.
"We're friends."
Did he know that she wanted to choose him over the Ministry of Internal
Affairs?
Would he understand the heart that had to choose the opportunity to rise
from the lowest rank of the 72 ranks of fairies to a mid-level position?
"Shirone, I…"
Feope's eyes welled up as she chose her words.
"Meeting you changed me."
She wasn't saying she loved him.
No matter how pure her words were, if she knew it was an act of throwing
her heart, she wouldn't have said it.
She learned more emotions from the high-ranking fairies.
The more she thought about it, the more her words, like a spectrum
passing through a prism, spread into a multicolored rainbow with various attributes, shimmering in her
heart.
"Even so, I don't regret my choice."
Choosing the Ministry of Internal Affairs was the right decision.
"Igilin said that humans and fairies can't love each other. Because
I am…"
Feope transformed her sorrow, which felt like it would explode her
heart, into laughter and smiled brightly.
"Too small."
But her voice was choked, and hot tears endlessly welled up in her large
eyes.
Armand's Vajra Armor was released and transformed into the form of a
magic sword.
Shirone had completely lost consciousness.
His body was in terrible condition.
His broken bones were bruised, and his organs were failing, heading
towards death.
Feope looked at him with pity and flew to his face.
She knelt under his chin and caressed his cheek.
The time they had spent together was short, and she couldn't say she
knew much about him.
But having already thrown her heart, there was no more room for
regret.
"I'm sorry, Shirone."
Feope closed her eyes and kissed Shirone's lips as her body began to
burn with a radiant light.
She was pouring all her life force into a single act of giving
life.
"I love you."
With a concept she understood more clearly than a year ago, Feope once
again threw her heart to Shirone.
