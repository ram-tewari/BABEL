Chapter 54:
Overflow (4)
Siena steeled herself. Shirone, having awakened the Immortal Function, might succumb to
Overflow. Etela believed he could overcome it, but if the worst happened, Shirone's life would be ruined.
Lost in thought, Siena barely noticed when Isthas stopped. She entered without delay.
Walking down the hallway, she flung open the iron door labeled "Supernatural Psychic Science Research Club."
Through the haze of smoke, the kids sat unfazed, smiling leisurely. The sight reignited
her anger.
This was all too familiar. The only differences were that the troublemakers she'd clashed
with had all graduated—and Shirone had taken their place.
'Why do students change when they join this club? Even Shirone.'
Normally, Shirone would greet her with a bright smile, but now he maintained a perfect
poker face. It was expected, but still hurt. Neid sat with his legs crossed arrogantly, while Iruki lounged
in his chair, head tilted back. Their dismissive aura was palpable.
"No greetings for your teacher? You little…."
"We were deep in discussion. Welcome."
Neid's tone was icy. Student-run clubs fiercely guarded their autonomy, forming a pillar
of the magic school's community. By barging in unannounced, Siena had crossed a line.
'Hmph. School is school, and the club is the club, huh?'
But their arrogance ended today. The faculty had approved the club's dissolution, and
Etela, the advisor, had agreed.
"So, what brings you here, Professor? We've been keeping quiet lately."
"Official notice about the club. I came to deliver it personally."
"Then come in. It's humble, but…"
Neid brushed off a spot on the sofa. His demeanor showed no guilt.
"Thanks. How about some tea?"
"Would you trust tea from me? You know no one can find this place once Isthas moves."
Siena raised an eyebrow, amused. She knew this shabby warehouse didn't even have cold
water, let alone tea. Though their antics were irritating, their wit made it hard to dislike them.
"Fair point. I suppose even I'd be wary. Let's get to it, then. I'd like you to vacate
the lab within a month."
Neid's eyes bulged. The bolt-from-the-blue demand left him speechless.
"What? Vacate? Why?"
"Paperwork review showed you failed to meet the minimum membership this semester. The
school already cut funding. So, clean up within a month."
"Wait! Shirone joined! Three members qualify as an official club!"
"But you've done zero activities. If you want to save the club, produce research results
within the month. Though I doubt you can in 'supernatural psychic science.'"
"That's unfair! Clubs don't have to hold presentations!"
"This is special. The lab's location in Isthas, your notorious reputation—few clubs are
as inactive as yours. The one-month grace period is only because Professor Etela pleaded. Be grateful."
Shirone was stunned. He'd just started growing fond of the club, and now it was being
disbanded.
"But there's one way to save the club."
Neid leaned forward eagerly.
"How? What do we need to do?"
"Withdraw Shirone. Then I'll turn a blind eye until next semester."
"Whaaat?"
As expected, Neid shot to his feet.
"No way! We didn't force Shirone to join—we're friends who stick together everywhere. Why
tear us apart?"
Iruki added:
"If it's about my skipped classes, don't worry. We each have our own study methods."
Shirone couldn't stay silent.
"I agree. I know the club's reputation and that Iruki and Neid aren't diligent in class.
But they're doing their best in their fields. They're not wasting time. Please reconsider."
Neid hoped Siena's fondness for Shirone might sway her. But she stood firm.
"It's no use. Shirone, this decision is also for your sake."
"For me? I'm doing my best."
"Shirone, what's happening to you lately?"
Shirone's heart sank. Neid and Iruki averted their eyes.
Siena's intuition was right. They knew everything.
"You're terrible. Especially you, Iruki. You knew Shirone could fall into Overflow. Why
didn't you tell me?"
Shirone turned to Iruki.
"Iruki. What's Overflow?"
"It's… nothing much. Just a phenomenon when you awaken beyond your current level. I had
it at eight."
Siena wasn't satisfied.
"Overflow can shatter your mind. Etela told me about the Immortal Function. If you miss
the timing, recovery might be impossible."
Shirone bit his lip. He'd been having nightmares and hallucinations nightly. Sometimes,
he worried he'd lose his mind. If this continued, there were no guarantees.
"How was it for you?"
"Hmm. Mine was nothing. A bit scary, though. When I told my dad, he said, 'You've always
been like this. Just live with it.'"
Siena was appalled. That was all the comfort he'd offered his crying son? But given who
Iruki's father was, it made sense. His neglect had undoubtedly shaped Iruki's eccentricity.
Desperate, Siena asked Neid:
"Neid. As a friend, what do you think?"
"Hahaha. Dunno? I've never had Overflow."
Hoping their experiences would change Shirone's mind, Siena sighed. At least she'd
impressed the danger upon him.
"Anyway, Shirone will withdraw. If you care about him, accept it. His mental state is the
priority."
Neid scoffed.
"Isn't that your judgment? I disagree."
Frustrated, Siena exhaled. Insisting despite knowing their friend's condition wasn't
friendship.
"You don't know Overflow's terror. Iruki would understand."
"Nope. I'm with Neid. It's Shirone's choice. Why are you so invested?"
The cold reply seemed unfriendly, but it was precisely why they were friends. Shirone
wasn't upset—their rationality aligned.
"So you're fine if Shirone goes mad?"
"No reason not to be. But I believe Shirone can overcome it. If you aim for the top in
this field, it's a risk you take."
Neid added:
"Same here. Honestly, aren't you favoring Shirone too much? We don't mind, but he might
find it burdensome."
After a pause, Neid's eyes gleamed.
"Rumor has it you see Shirone as a man."
Of course, no such rumor existed. But this was the most effective way to rattle a
teacher. Siena wasn't easily shaken—years of clashes with underground clubs had hardened her.
"Suppose it's true. Then I can take Shirone now, right?"
As a teacher, she couldn't forcibly withdraw a student unless they violated rules. The
club president's consent was crucial.
"Stop forcing it. So you admit the rumor?"
"Think what you want. I won't back down."
"Really? Then can we date too?"
Siena recoiled.
"No. Never."
"See? Your logic's flawed. You're favoring Shirone."
Neid's jab hit home. Siena swallowed hard. Perhaps Iruki was right. The darkness that
had once consumed her left deeper scars than Overflow.
"This seems to be my issue."
Shirone broke the silence.
"I'm fine. It's scary, but I'm more curious. What will become of me? Can I overcome it?
Even if I fall into Overflow, I'd never blame you."
Siena was reminded of bittersweet memories. The person who'd saved her from hell had
said the same.
"Siena. Face what's coming head-on. No matter how scary, don't look away. Closing your
eyes won't make it disappear."
"Go away! You don't understand!"
'If only I'd had that courage…'
