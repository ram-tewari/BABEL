Limitless (4)
"No need. Just call me, and I'll come anytime."
Shirone realized once again who he was learning from. Even Samuel, who had already
graduated, was only an unofficial 10th-class mage. But the female teacher before him was a 6th-class mage,
and a nationally certified one at that.
'Just how strong is our teacher?'
The other students seemed to have realized this as well, maintaining a reverent posture
even after Samuel left.
"Now, everyone. Did you all see the demonstration of verbal magic?"
"Yes!"
The answer echoed louder than usual, and Etella nodded with satisfaction.
"The reason I showed you verbal magic in advance is because it's closely related to
what we'll learn today: strengthening the Spirit Zone. Now, you should be able to answer this question:
What's the best way to strengthen your thoughts?"
The students answered in unison.
"Speech!"
"Correct. It's speech. Because thoughts are formed through language. Can anyone
express this in another way?"
The students from Class Four, who had been taught by Etella for a long time, answered.
"It means thoughts can be seen."
"Exactly. Everyone, try to recall any thought right now. You'll probably be able to
see it with your eyes."
The students nodded as if they understood what it felt like after trying it themselves.
"Why is it important to see thoughts with your eyes? Because it allows you to
reflect. In other words, a verbal mage absorbs the words they've spoken to strengthen their thoughts."
Etella raised her index finger and continued.
"The basic system of verbal magic is simple. Speak, reflect. Speak, reflect. By
continuously amplifying meaning this way, you strengthen your magical power."
"Teacher, does that mean we'll learn verbal magic too?"
When a question came from Class Seven, Etella smiled.
"Do you want to learn verbal magic?"
"Yes. That demonstration earlier was so cool."
"Finding something that suits your aptitude is very important. But unfortunately,
verbal magic is a specialized field, so it won't be covered in advanced classes."
"But then how do we strengthen the Spirit Zone? Isn't it done through verbal
magic?"
"You've pointed it out very well. What I'm about to explain is exactly that. Verbal
magic is used to enhance the power of specific spells. There are countless spells in the world, and each has
its own language. But the Spirit Zone is a pure mental activity, so you don't need to learn verbal or
ultrasonic magic. So, how do you strengthen the Spirit Zone?"
Etella paused and stepped onto the Image Zone.
"It's a unique concentration technique called the numerical sequence. Raise your
hand if you've ever counted from 1 to 100, either in your mind or out loud."
More than half raised their hands.
"Good. Now, raise your hand if you've counted from 1 to 1,000."
The students craned their necks and looked around. The entire Class Four raised their
hands, but hardly any from the lower classes did.
"A numerical sequence is literally the technique of listing numbers. Unlike
language, numbers are symbols with an immutable status. That's why they pair well with the Spirit
Zone."
"Does counting numbers improve concentration?"
"Yes. But simply counting isn't enough. The basics are the same as verbal magic:
speak and reflect. However, in the case of numerical sequences, it's more like think and reflect. You can't
succeed unless you focus on each number."
This was why Etella had introduced verbal magic first. While advanced techniques differed
entirely, the basic concept of strengthening was almost identical between numerical sequences and verbal
magic.
"The faster you unfold the numbers, the stronger the Zone becomes. Performing a
numerical sequence can enhance activation probability, power, precision, and more. But remember, numerical
sequences aren't a cure-all. The Spirit Zone can only be elevated through rigorous mental discipline. So,
don't focus too much on technical strengthening. Now, I'll demonstrate a numerical sequence."
After finishing her explanation, Etella expanded a Spirit Zone about 10 meters in
diameter for teaching purposes.
"Starting now, I'll perform a numerical sequence. I'll strengthen this Spirit Zone
using only the sequence. Since it's not verbal magic, I'll only announce every 10th increment. Here we go.
1."
The moment Etella uttered the first number, the Spirit Zone swelled as if filled with
air.
"100."
"100? Did she just say 100?"
For the numerical sequence to be effective, each number had to be precisely savored. Yet,
she had counted to 100 in just two seconds—an astonishing speed.
"400."
The children's eyes widened. The acceleration had begun, and the
"zero-to-hundred" time for certain intervals had shortened dramatically.
"1,100. 1,700. 2,400."
Shirone's jaw dropped. Could a human count numbers that fast? In less than 20 seconds,
she had added 1 a staggering 2,400 times.
The Spirit Zone had grown into a sphere 40 meters in diameter. All achieved solely
through the numerical sequence.
When the count reached 10,000, Etella stopped. The Spirit Zone quickly shrank back to its
original size.
Her ability to count to 10,000 in one go was proof of why she was the youngest bishop in
the Carthis Council.
'This is incredible. How much mental training does it take to reach such a level?'
Students witnessing Etella's numerical sequence for the first time were shocked. Even her
beautiful appearance paled in comparison to her talent.
"Did you see? The faster you perform the numerical sequence, the greater the
strengthening effect. Since it's merely a technique, you won't gain much efficiency if your Spirit Zone's
discipline is lacking. But I believe you'll all be able to use it effectively."
Hearing her words, Shirone cheered inwardly. With the numerical sequence, he could use
active magic through mental concentration stronger than the Spirit Zone's durability.
"The numerical sequence starts with counting from 1 to 100 as the baseline. The time
it takes to reach 100 is called the 'zero-to-hundred.' Before you get used to it, don't drag out the numbers
too long; focus on shortening the zero-to-hundred."
After class, Shirone found a secluded spot. The numerical sequence, simple as it was,
proved difficult in practice.
It took him 7 seconds to count from 1 to 100. A decent result for a first attempt.
What felt best was that he'd reached 100 without breaking concentration despite the rapid
counting.
While Class Five's overall level was high, Shirone still excelled in mental endurance.
In reality, most students performing the numerical sequence for the first time lost
concentration as the speed increased.
'Counting itself isn't the problem. Should I try higher numbers? No, she said to focus on
shortening the zero-to-hundred first.'
Given Etella's nature, she must have tailored the numerical sequence's difficulty
precisely to the students' level.
'That means there's a much higher level. What is it? There must be a special method.'
Lost in thought, Shirone snapped his fingers.
'I don't need to count one by one. Instead, I should process numbers in chunks while
savoring them.'
Unbeknownst to him, this was the basic concept of information magic: modularization. It
involved grouping individual numbers into units for batch processing.
Shirone immediately began experimenting. To integrate numbers, he needed insight, not
calculation. As the number of digits processed within the same time increased, the Spirit Zone's performance
noticeably improved.
After breaking through the zero-to-hundred at an incredible speed, Shirone switched the
Square Method to offensive mode. The moment his numerical sequence reached a thousand, he took magical
action and cast a spell.
A flash of light burst forth, drawing the students' attention. A mass of photons shot out
from Shirone's palm.
Strictly speaking, it was a sphere of light, but its near-light speed made it appear as a
streak of brilliance to the naked eye.
"Whoa! What was that?"
"Photon output. Maybe he's testing targeting with it."
"But why learn that? Photon output isn't practical in real combat."
"Doesn't matter. The Image Zone's target activates any spell. Even healing removes
the target. It might actually be advantageous for the exam. Honestly, that was incredibly fast."
Even without physical force, among the basics of active magic, nothing was faster than
photon output. It was perfect for hitting fast-moving targets.

Etella followed the murmurs to where Shirone was. Light continued to emit from his hands.
"Shirone, you seem well-prepared."
"Yes. I just succeeded, actually."
"Hoho. Really? The numerical sequence must have worked."
"I think so."
Shirone focused on photon output again, as if there were many errors to correct. Etella
watched carefully, lips pursed. She had expected active magic to be quite difficult for him, as his
tendencies leaned too heavily toward defense.
'Yet he succeeded…'
That meant he'd incorporated modularization. She had planned to advise him during
personal tutoring, but since he'd figured it out himself, the opportunity was gone. Instead, she made a
suggestion.
"Shirone, you've never tried hitting targets, have you? Want to go up to the Image
Zone?"
"Yes, I'll try."
The students stopped practicing and watched Shirone. How many points he could score was a
major point of interest for his competitors.
Shirone waited for the targets to rise. Though it was just practice, speed gun training
was new to him, making him as tense as in a real battle.
Etella activated the magic barrier device to prevent accidents. Like Iruki before,
casting spells without a magic screen could damage equipment and injure students.
At the signal, targets rose from all directions. Shirone deployed the numerical sequence
and switched the Square Method to offensive mode. Countless spikes extended in all directions, capturing
many targets at once.
Without hesitation, Shirone activated photon output. The moment light flashed, the
targets turned to ash and vanished.
"Whoaaa…"
The children erupted in admiration. Though it was an active magic without physical force,
its brilliance was overwhelming.
'Not bad.'
Shirone continued casting photon output. Targets disappeared the instant he cast the
spell. The lack of time lag over distance was a tremendous advantage.
'I can do this!'
Gaining confidence, Shirone fired photons in all directions. As his ideas fell into
place, he began craving records.
He'd eliminated 172 targets so far. With 20 seconds left, breaking 200 points would be
easy.
'He's mastered modularization.'
Watching the demonstration, Etella was certain. The insight gained from the
"unbridgeable gap" had led him to the numerical sequence—a good sign.
Currently, Shirone's numerical sequence grouped 10 numbers into a single unit, processed
all at once. At this rate, he could break through the zero-to-hundred in just 2 seconds.
After a minute, the device stopped, and Shirone descended from the Image Zone, panting.
The students' eyes fixed on the scoreboard. Final tally: 342 points. He'd eliminated 342 targets in one
minute.
"How does he never miss? Maybe I should use photon output for the exam too."
"Think it's easy? Shirone can do it because he's mastered teleportation."
The students shared their impressions. Neid and Iruki sat side by side, discussing their
thoughts.
"Shirone's amazing. Specialized in defense, yet incredible at offense too. Maybe
he'll climb to the top group after this exam."
"I don't know, 342 points isn't much, right?"
"But it's good for a first attempt. He couldn't even do photon output until
yesterday."
"Whether it's his first try or not doesn't matter. What's truly important is the
ability to grasp the core, right? That's what's impressive."
"Huh? What's gotten into you? You're praising Shirone?"
"Give credit where it's due. Anyway, this'll be a good experiment. We'll see who's
superior in this exam."
"What's your plan?"
Instead of answering, Iruki stood and walked to the Image Zone.
"Teacher Etella, may I try?"
"Iruki, you?"
Etella asked uneasily. Given the previous incident, she couldn't predict what accidents
this child might cause.
"I'll just practice and come down. The magic barrier's active anyway."
Since it was exam prep, restricting specific students would be unfair. Reluctantly, she
agreed.
"Alright. Go ahead. But no accidents."
Iruki bowed and entered the Image Zone. His appearance drew everyone's attention. After
preparing, he switched the Square Method to evasion mode. The children murmured. No one expected him to use
evasion mode for a targeting exam.
Shirone was equally surprised. Evasion mode allowed the fastest movement among the Square
Methods, but capturing multiple targets required at least offensive mode to score. Evasion mode meant
abandoning spatial sense and tracking targets solely with the eyes—a perfect one-to-one match.
Metaphorically, it was like moving a dot to catch another dot.
'What's his plan?'
As targets rose, Iruki's Spirit Zone began moving. It had shrunk to about the size of a
human face but moved even faster.
Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang!
Precise. Like elastic rubber, the Spirit Zone bent sharply at angles, eliminating targets
one after another. The students' admiration was inevitable. Even Etella couldn't hide her surprise.
'Using evasion mode to eliminate targets. Quite a unique method.'
The count had already surpassed 100 points. Shirone realized how extraordinary Iruki's
skill was. He was eliminating targets while watching them. Considering the speed, his dynamic visual acuity
was incredible.
'No, it's beyond that.'
The Servant Syndrome's astounding computational ability. He was predicting the targets'
trajectories and positioning the Spirit Zone in advance.
'Don't be too surprised, Shirone.'
The moment Iruki's lips curved, his Spirit Zone moved at a speed beyond comparison.
"Huh? What?"
The students gasped. They couldn't follow the Spirit Zone's movements with their eyes.
Each time something blurry flickered through space, targets exploded from all directions.
Whooshwhooshwhooshwhooshbang!
By minimizing the atomic bomb's power, the detonation reaction took almost no time. While
Shirone's photon output dominated range with speed, Iruki moved the Spirit Zone to strike directly.
The count broke 200 points. Faster than Shirone's modularization. Seeing the results,
Shirone could acknowledge it. But how Iruki strengthened the Zone remained a mystery.
"Did he use my method?"
"No. His is different."

"Iruki sees you as a rival. Winning without the Servant Syndrome would be

Shirone's eyes widened. Counting to a million in a minute? Feeling a millisecond was one

Iruki finished his minute and descended. Final score: 548 points. Far surpassing
Shirone's.
The students buzzed. This was the first time Iruki had taken a class so seriously.
"This is insane. 548 points with evasion mode. That's not human."
"He calculated all the targets' trajectories with his Servant ability. Isn't that
cheating?"
Etella also saw Iruki differently. Using evasion mode to capture targets could be
attributed to mathematical computation. But the Spirit Zone's movement speed defied explanation.
'Could it be… that child…'
Iruki descended and went straight to Shirone. Winking, he said:
"What do you think? Pretty impressive, right?"
Shirone had to admit it.
"That was amazing. The Servant Syndrome—I had no idea you could calculate targets'
paths mathematically."
"Hmph, is that all? Disappointing."
"What?"
Shirone frowned, offended. Even praise seemed insufficient for Iruki. But he continued
unfazed.
"Grouping numbers into modules to speed up the numerical sequence. Definitely
something only you can do."
Shirone was shocked Iruki already knew. And uneasy. Because he had no clue how Iruki had
done it.
"Want me to tell you my method?"
Shirone's pride stung.
"Are you just giving it away? I thought you were seriously competing with me."

Typical Iruki, grating on people's nerves.
"Honestly, it's nothing special. I just used logarithms to reduce the units."

There was such a method. And as Iruki said, knowing didn't mean replicating it.
"Logarithms can simplify astronomical numbers. This was just practice. The real

Logarithms could express any unit of number simply—100 million, 1 billion, 10 billion,
all the same.
But logarithms were symbols meant to simplify complex calculations. Applying them to
something as simple as a numerical sequence made it impossibly complex for even mathematicians to compute
mentally.
Yet Iruki could do it. With his Servant Syndrome, he converted all natural numbers into
fractions and calculated them.
'Performing a numerical sequence under logarithms? Is that even humanly possible?'
For the first time, Shirone felt he'd underestimated things. Carthis Amy, the Ozent
family members—he'd met many geniuses, but none as extreme as Iruki.
"How about it? Still think you can beat me?"
"I'm not studying to beat anyone. If I do my best, it doesn't matter who wins."
"Textbook answer. Whatever. I always knew you were like that. But watch your mouth
next time. It's really annoying hearing idealistic nonsense from someone with no skill."
Shirone gritted his teeth. What was so dissatisfying? He just wanted to say life was
precious. If that was idealism, he'd have to prove it.
"Fine, got it! Let's compete. I'll definitely beat you in this exam."
"Haha! Try all you want."
Shirone was about to retort when classmates swarmed Iruki, leaving him silent.
"Iruki, you're amazing. How did you do it? Can you teach me?"
"With skills like that, why hide them? Want to join our research club? With you, we
could finish our project in a month."
Praise poured in. Shirone understood. Iruki's demonstration in the Image Zone was that
shocking.
"Shirone, the insight you showed at the unbridgeable gap was truly impressive.
But…"

"My computations are faster than insight."

such a formidable opponent.
Neid patted his back in consolation.
"Cheer up. Irresponsible as it sounds, it's fascinating to watch. I'm friends with

"987?"
Forget his own score—even Iruki, using logarithms, had barely passed 500. How fast did
one have to be to score 987 in a minute? It was unimaginable.
"That's insane. Who did it? Must've graduated, right?"
