newly added clauses in the contract.
Shirone.
The person who had given her a second chance.
I hope you're happy. You're the one who gave me back my family and my
life.
So...
I won't say a word. If it's what you want, I won't tell you no matter
what happens.
Marsha signed the contract.
Even though she was behind the main system pillar, the distance to the

But even the sounds of combat felt faint, as she was deep in her own
thoughts.
What should I do?
The file containing the history of Omega 999.
According to the contract, she had to send it directly to Fermi. But the

How much of humanity's future is left? What if that 83% only covered the

I have to look. This is the only chance I'll get to undo things. As long
as I don't tell Shirone, it's fine.

time difference.
Alright. If there's any information dated beyond today, then go. If not,

Records written in human language were packed tightly in chronological
order.
Flip. Flip.
She barely registered the words.

There it is.
There wasn't much, but the record clearly included today's date.
Okay, this is enough— Then, a single line of text caught her eye.
What...?
Before she could even form a thought, her brain absorbed an entire
paragraph of events.
Marsha slowly lifted her head.
Even in shock, her hand automatically shut off the device.
So this was it.
Fermi had been right when he said she'd know as soon as she saw
it.
Shirone.

So that's how it was? You knew everything… and still chose to erase your

"Is everyone okay?"

It really never ends.

"Yeah. It's not perfect, but we achieved our goal. Once we send it

"There's nowhere to hide. Zets will be on us soon. Let's get out of

"You all go ahead. I'll move one more time, then I'll go

'Why?'

'You take on everything. You fight for the world… so why? Why can't you

'Shirone.'
A signal flare announcing the end of Omega, the final chapter of

"Why do you look so sad? Don't worry. I'll find a safe place to get
