Alpha Fish (1)
The Ex Machina was a cube measuring four meters on each side, with a
weight reaching seven tons.
It was made of smooth metal, with golden Angelic script, Hena, engraved
on its black surface.
"It looks like a giant Meta Gate."
At Shirone's words, Minerva nodded in agreement.
"Even if it was made by the Archangel Kariel, the technology is still
fundamentally based on the Mecha System."
Uorin added.
"The Mecha race really likes cubes. It might be for technical reasons,
but the shape itself could be called Mecha."
Thinking of the Mecha civilization he'd encountered in both the magic
warehouse Istas and Heaven, Shirone couldn't help but nod along.
"Let's begin."
When the Reaper opened the Ex Machina, there was space inside for five
people.
Those who had already tried the card game entered first, and Iruki was
the last to step in.
"Iruki, good luck."
Four people sat in a row on the second floor, while Iruki took the
center seat on the first floor.
When he placed his palms on the hemispherical glass attached to the
armrests, a device descended from the ceiling, resembling a drone.
The brainwave sensor came down to cover his nose, and Iruki flipped his
right hand and gave a thumbs-up.
"No need to overdo it from the start."
At the sound of the Reaper's voice, the Ex Machina's entrance closed,
and shortly after, a terrifying mechanical noise roared to life.
As the noise filled the wide warehouse, Shirone covered his ears and
slowly stepped back.
The sound grew louder by the second, shaking even the seven-ton machine,
and the Reaper shouted out.
"Damn it! I said don't push it!"
His lips seemed to move in silence through the deafening noise, but the
gravity of the situation was clear.
"Iruki…"
Twenty intense minutes passed before the hellish noise finally stopped,
and the exit opened.
With a loud hiss of steam, the iron door opened and Iruki came tumbling
out.
"Uuugh! Blech!"
He collapsed to the floor, vomiting stomach acid, prompting everyone to
rush to him.
"Iruki! Are you okay?"
Staring blankly at the ground with dazed eyes, Iruki had yet to grasp
that he was back in reality.
"What happened?"
As Shirone turned with a glare full of worry, the people who played the
card game came down with grim expressions.
Gudio looked down at Iruki and spoke.
"He passed."
His brain function could only be described as monstrous.
"The tutorial we presented was to manipulate the outcome of a card game
played by four people."
"And?"
"Iruki jumped in early. Said it was boring or something? So we raised
the stakes."
Ness explained.
"The real test was this: in a card tournament with 300 participants,
manipulate the outcome so the winner ends up being the one we designate."
Agaya added.
"You had to manipulate every win and loss, track the real-time funds of
all 300 players, and even rig the random draws. Greedy fools, naïve idiots, beautiful women—seventeen
designated people in total had to win in a specific order. That was the test. And he passed."
"…You're saying he ran that kind of simulation in just 20
minutes?"
It was a data load that would easily take a month in real life. Just
hearing it made Shirone's head throb.
"Ugh, I'm dying. My head's spinning."
As Iruki stood up, wiping his lips, Shirone supported him—and
snapped.
"Be honest! You went into Overdrive, didn't you?!"
If sparks from the cortex were visible through his pupils, it meant no
one could survive doing that for long.
"I'm fine. This much is nothing."
Transcendence meant breaking through the barriers of a particular
domain, and Overdrive was one such case.
However, Iruki's transcendence wasn't in the natural world—it was in the
biological realm, through his own body.
"How can you say this is nothing?! This isn't even a real battle—it was
just a test! You can't push yourself like this! I…"
The memory of Iruki going into Overdrive during the graduation exam
still haunted Shirone.
"If something happened to you…!"
Shirone's eyes brimmed with tears.
"Shirone, my father once told me. There are so many people in the world
who want to help—but can't."
Just because something comes to you doesn't mean it belongs to
you.
"If those who can don't step up, we—humankind—can never move
forward."
Shirone recalled the day he first met Iruki.
"Yeah… you've always been that kind of person."
In the solemn silence, even the card game players quietly acknowledged
him.
"I thought it was just a reckless move, but…"
A young man who willingly bore a burden as heavy as his immense talent.
A worthy partner in battle.
Iruki patted Shirone on the shoulder.
"Anyway, I've adjusted now. No more embarrassing moments like that, I
promise."
Ness chimed in.
"No, seriously, you did great. When we first activated the Ex Machina,
we were knocked out for almost an hour."
Agaya added.
"Real training starts tomorrow. For today, let's go over the briefing.
Come with us."
Now that he was accepted as part of the team, Iruki had to know
everything the card game players knew.
"See you later, Shirone."
Following behind them, Iruki looked back and winked.
"……"
It meant: Don't run away.
From the top of the spire, the imperial capital looked as dark as the
deep sea, save for a few scattered lights that flickered like fireflies.
"Amy…"
Knowing why Iruki had insisted they meet tonight, Shirone couldn't
sleep.
"Sorry. The meeting ran late."
Iruki slid down from the spire's peak like it was a slide, landing just
short of a fall as he planted his feet on the railing.
"Whew. So this is your room?"
Lying back on the slope with his arms for a pillow, Iruki gazed up at
the countless stars sparkling in the night sky.
"Nice. Not suffocating at all."
Shirone welcomed him with a smile.
"Did the meeting go well?"
"It's a pretty meticulous strategy. The problem is, Alpha Fish isn't
easy to find."
"The one who loves Havitz the most?"
"Yeah. Alpha Fish is also called the pilot fish. It's the one you put
into an aquarium first to establish the ecosystem."
Iruki raised his index finger.
"Getting the first step right is key. We might be able to find the Beta
Fish, the one that hates Havitz, but…"
Realizing this would turn into a long conversation, he cut the topic
short and moved on to what Shirone really wanted to talk about.
"The others are doing well. Nade is working on tech development at
Yongroe, and Dante got a job at the Tormia National Intelligence Service. We had a few meals
together…"
He missed their days at Alpheas Magic School.
"Maya turned down offers from a big agency abroad and signed with a
performance agency in the Tormia Kingdom. Fermi seems to have left the kingdom. And then…"
Since Yongroe handled national affairs, Iruki was well-informed about
their former classmates.
"Everyone's living their own busy lives."
But the one person Shirone truly wanted to hear about—Amy—was the only
one Iruki hadn't mentioned.
"…How is she doing?"
In the end, Shirone had to ask.
"Haaah."
Iruki let out a heavy sigh as he finally opened up.
"After you said goodbye through that dream and left, she passed the
public officer's exam. Then, while training as a lieutenant officer in the Tormia Magic Forces…"
"While training?"
Shirone swallowed hard.
"Seems like she met someone and started dating. If she ever meets you,

"W-what? Amy did?!"
Shirone's face turned pale.
"Pwahaha! I'm joking! There's no way Amy would do something like that
without even telling you."
Shirone was genuinely furious.
"You jerk! Even as a joke, that's not funny!"
"I mean, wouldn't it be better if that was the case?"
Iruki's tone suddenly turned serious.
"She left the training midway and came to me. She had an enlistment

one for her."
"The Holy War? You mean the Valkyrie?"
"Yeah. You get what that means, right? Tormia isn't in a crisis yet, but

Amy's voice echoed in his mind like a dream.
—"I'll go."
"She wanted to see me…"
"Exactly. You called me because you needed me. But Amy… she's throwing
her life away trying to become someone you need."
Iruki sat up and looked down over the capital.
"I wanted to stop her, but I couldn't. I understood how she felt. I was

you."
Shirone said nothing.
"Why don't you… try visiting the Valkyries? You could even talk to the

"It'd be the opposite of helpful."
Shirone knew Uorin's feelings. Expressing any interest in Amy wouldn't
go over well.
No… she probably already knows.
Uorin wasn't someone petty enough to ruin everything out of jealousy,
but there must've been a reason she never brought it up.
"It's not confirmed yet. Sure, she has a recommendation from the

Tormia."
"She'll pass."
Even without invoking the name of the Carmis family, Amy wouldn't give

"…Yeah. And that's the problem."

"Iruki, you said it made you happy when I called for you."
"I did."
"Same here. I'd feel the same. If the day comes when I can face Amy

A light returned to Shirone's eyes.
"…then that means I truly need her. And I believe Amy will make it
happen."

All the applicants were elite talents favored by royalty—but that meant

"It's no different here either."
They were all used to competition—born from elite schools and polished
by exams.
"It's just about climbing even higher."
Amy repeated the cadence commands with a calm expression, and the other

Standing in line with her name tag on, Amy watched as the instructor
