Chapter 612:
After the submission of the applications was completed at noon, an
association guide went around to the rooms where the students were waiting to inform them of the next
schedule.
"You will have lunch until 2 PM, followed by a tour of the
association's museum. The dining hall is located in the annex on the first floor, so all students should
move there promptly."
"Do we have free time until 2 PM?"
"Yes. You can rest or explore the building. However, if you cause
any disturbances, you will be punished according to the association's regulations, and leaving the
premises is prohibited."
Being allowed to roam freely was quite a privilege, but no one was
likely to act recklessly.
"Let's eat first. I'm hungry."
As Iruki and Nade followed the procession to the dining hall, Shirone
said, "You guys go ahead. I have somewhere to go."
Having stayed at the association for over two months, there were quite a
few people Shirone needed to greet.
"Alright, see you later."
Shirone moved against the flow of students and headed to the desk in the
middle of the third-floor hallway.
"Shirone, long time no see."
An association staff member greeted him warmly.
"Hello. How have you been?"
"Well, the work at the association is always the same. Make sure
you pass this time."
The students heading to the dining hall glanced over in surprise at
Shirone's casual conversation with the staff.
Although it was due to Gaold's orders at the time, it was indeed a
special experience for an outsider to stay at the association for several months.
"I'll do my best. By the way, where is Senior Plu?"
"The manager? She's probably resting in the staff quarters. She
worked really hard for two days."
The staff had likely been more stressed than the students during this
inspection.
"Go ahead. The manager talked about you a lot."
"Okay, see you later."
Shirone bowed and headed to the staff quarters.
'She'll definitely be there.'
"Ah, I'm tired."
Plu, who had entered the quarters she used during night shifts, relaxed
and took off her uniform.
She had been so busy checking the security devices in all areas of the
association in cooperation with other departments for a week that she barely had time to sleep.
'I need to take a quick nap.'
She changed into a comfortable top, took off her skirt, and went to the
wardrobe.
"My pants…"
She opened the wardrobe and took out her pants, bending over to put one
leg in when the door suddenly opened.
"Senior! I'm here!"
"Ah! Ah!"
Startled, Plu lost her balance, hopping on one leg before falling
over.
"…"
As Shirone clenched his fists to hold back his laughter, Plu glared at
him and stood up.
"Hey! Do you want to die? You come in without knocking while I'm
changing?"
"Oh, I'll turn around."
"Forget it. No point pretending to be polite after seeing
everything."
"I'm sorry. I didn't think… I was just so happy to see
you…"
Plu understood that feeling.
"Well, it's been a while. Have you finished preparing for the
exam?"
Of course, the first question was about the graduation exam.
"Yes. I trained with Miro during the break. I'm in good
condition."
After returning from Heaven, Plu had left for the association
immediately, but she had heard many things through her own channels.
"I heard you gave up the evaluation. I think that was the right
decision."
Although not publicly known, the Heaven Project had affected the entire
world.
"How about you, Senior Plu? I was really worried after we parted
like that…"
As a member of Gaold's personal guard, she would have been viewed
unfavorably by Lupist.
"I'm fine. Although I had conflicts with the former head, the
current head only values skill. Thanks to that, Isabel has been reinstated."
Shirone knew what sacrifices Isabel, the head of the magic library, had
made for Gaold.
"That's really good to hear."
"We're fine, but the problem is you. Be honest. Are you confident
about the graduation exam?"
Shirone shook his head.
"I'm not sure yet. I heard the strategy from Miro, but whether it's
feasible…"
Considering Shirone's situation, there weren't many organizations that
would take him in just because he passed the graduation exam.
'It has to be at least the level of the association.'
Plu made a decision.
"It's lunchtime now, right? Come with me. Let's go to the fourth
floor."
"The fourth floor? Why there all of a sudden?"
"There's someone I want to introduce you to."
Following Plu to the fourth floor, Shirone entered an office with a sign
that read "Mage Personnel Management Department."
With the graduation exam imminent, the staff were busy sorting
documents, and the two of them passed through to a meeting room at the end of the office.
"Haven't you had lunch yet?"
Three people were sitting at a rectangular table, reviewing
documents.
Shirone glanced over and saw profiles of Alpheas Magic School students
and the applications they had just submitted.
The man sitting at the head of the table had a sharp, cynical look with
black hair, while the woman on the left had a calm demeanor with long eyelashes, and the woman on the
right had freckles and brown hair tied in pigtails.
"What is this? We're in the middle of a meeting."
The man snapped, but Plu led Shirone in without hesitation.
"I have a junior to introduce. Shirone, greet them. This is
Evaluator Baikal, who will be observing the Alpheas Magic School graduation exam as a
scout."
'A scout!'
Shirone was instantly alert.
"Hello. I'm Arian Shirone from the Alpheas Magic School graduating
class."
Plu introduced the women on either side.
"This is Analyst Elizabeth, and this is Recorder Lara."
Lara, with her cute freckles, smiled and waved.
"Hi. We've never met, but I've seen your records a
lot."
Baikal looked displeased as Shirone entered.
"What is this? Bringing in a graduation exam participant. This
isn't lobbying, is it?"
"It is lobbying. Give him a good score on the exam. I'll buy you
lunch."
Shirone's face turned red at the blatant request, but Plu didn't care
and pulled out a chair to sit.
"Baikal is a certified fourth-class mage. He scouted for the Royal
Magic School for over ten years, and I was recruited by him to join the association."
'Ah, so that's the connection…'
Baikal leaned back, looking uncomfortable.
"I just picked the most capable student. After all, she was the top
of the Royal Magic School."
From the short conversation, Shirone could tell that lobbying wouldn't
work on him.
"Still, graduation rank and scout evaluation are separate. Since
you're in charge of Alpheas Magic School this time, it wouldn't hurt for Shirone to make an
impression."
"Yes. Please take care of me."
Being straightforward, Shirone's sincerity softened Baikal's
expression.
"Alright. I'll keep an eye on you. Do your best."
Raising the scout's expectations would certainly help the evaluation,
but failing to meet them could lead to disappointment, a double-edged sword.
"You've been in charge of the Royal Magic School for over ten
years. Why the change this time?"
Elizabeth answered Shirone's question.
"Because it's the hottest place in Tormia this year. The
competition is unprecedentedly fierce, and the students' skills are said to exceed the graduating class
average. So the evaluator requested the change directly to the association."
It was an honor for the entire Alpheas Magic School graduating class,
not just Shirone.
"It was decided based on the documents. But numbers don't always
reflect reality. I hope you don't disappoint."
Sitting in the seat Elizabeth offered, Shirone pointed to the profile
documents on the table.
"Are these the grades for the year?"
"No. They are all the records from admission to the present. We
can't make hasty judgments about the kingdom's future. We try to be as objective as possible. Have you
ever watched a graduation exam?"
"Yes, last year."
"Then you know what scouts do."
"Well…"
At the time, Shirone was immersed in the fierce battles of the
graduation exam and was shocked by Amy's early elimination.
"It's okay. Knowing how scouts evaluate will help you. When the
graduation exam starts, analysts like me observe the participants' detailed abilities."
Elizabeth's eyes glowed blue, revealing a target-like mark on her
retina.
"…Is that a tattoo?"
"Yes, a magical tattoo. The most important thing for an analyst is
to assess the students' abilities in detail. Whether the repeated teleportation distance is 10 meters,
10 meters and 1 centimeter, or 10 meters and 1 millimeter."
"…I see."
Shirone was stunned by the idea of seeing that with her eyes.
"The analyzed information is immediately transmitted to the
evaluator here. The evaluator then grades the students' abilities based on the situation and discretion.
Judgment B grade, nerve transmission speed A grade, and so on. Then Lara records the evaluations of all
30 students."
When Lara made a V sign, dozens of notebooks stacked beside her floated
into the air and fluttered.
Then, an equal number of pens moved automatically, mimicking
writing.
A scribe capable of recording in thirty notebooks simultaneously.
As Shirone watched in awe, Lara shrugged with a pleased
expression.
"Being highly skilled doesn't guarantee a good
evaluation."
Baikal offered his first piece of advice.
"The personnel the kingdom needs are mages who can perform their
assigned tasks 100 percent. Most students focus on power, but from the kingdom's perspective, if one
isn't enough, they can send ten. It costs more, but it's a replaceable number. What we want is talent
that can't be quantified."
Baikal pointed to Plu.
"Plu's final evaluation in the graduation exam was an overall A. If
we expanded the scope to other countries, there would be A-pluses and S-grades. But within the kingdom,
Plu was the best student in Tormia."
"Is that a compliment?" Plu asked, but Baikal ignored her and
continued.
"One reason I rated Plu highly was because she wasted nothing. She
optimized time, mental effort, psychology, and actions for maximum efficiency. This is far more
important than casting powerful spells or overwhelming someone because it's not something that can be
replaced with money or numbers."
"I see."
Shirone had to acknowledge that individual achievements and what the
kingdom required were different.
'I'm just one of many mages.'
Baikal continued.
"If you want to score well in evaluations, reduce waste. Even if
you're smarter than the scout, the scout can still evaluate you because they measure the results of your
decisions."
Shirone realized why Plu had introduced him to Baikal.
"Thank you. This has been very helpful."
Lara clapped her hands and said, "Well, it's time for lunch. After
all, we work to eat, right?"
Elizabeth invited Shirone, "Shirone, you haven't had lunch yet,
right? Join us."
"No, I'll eat with my friends. Eating separately feels like a
special privilege."
Lara looked puzzled for a moment, then burst into laughter.
"Why, what's so funny?" Shirone asked, his face turning
red.
Elizabeth, covering her mouth as she laughed, said, "Numbers don't
reflect reality. You're very different from what I imagined from the documents."
"What do you mean?"
"You're pure. When you become a mage, you'll understand what real
privileges are. Let's go."
Following the scouts, Shirone had lunch in the staff dining hall.
Having used it often before, he quickly relaxed and gained a lot of
information from Elizabeth.
Then the dining hall door burst open, and a man in uniform appeared,
looking around before heading straight to Shirone.
"What are you doing here? Do you know how long I've been looking
for you?"
Startled, Shirone put down his utensils and said, "I'm sorry. I'll
go back now."
As he stood up, the man shook his head.
"Not there, go to the 18th floor."
"What? The 18th floor?"
The man's voice lowered noticeably.
"The head of the association wants to see you."
