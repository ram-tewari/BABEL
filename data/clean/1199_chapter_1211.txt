Chapter 1211:
Delta Headquarters.
Kitra of the Kingdom of Paras gazed indifferently at Exoverse, now
transformed into a black sphere.
'A singularity with zero volume and infinite density.'
If "nothingness" held all possibilities within a space of
zero, then even the concept of existence might be nothing more than an illusion.
"Do you defy the will of God?"
Though seven Archangels had reversed the laws, the pyramids scattered
across the world still remained intact.
If the number 100 represented the outcome, the ways to reach that number
were infinite.
What the Archangels had done was merely eliminate seven-eighths of the
causes. Nothing more.
"You cannot change the result."
To God, probability only came in two forms: 0% and 100%.
Until all eight source concepts were gathered, the overwhelming victory
still belonged to God.
As a blue light flickered in Kitra's eyes, the pyramid's magnetic field
began to expand.
Simultaneously, those affected by the magnetic field also had that same
blue light flash across their vision.
They were people who had lost their hearts amid the quantum fluctuations
expanding like bubbles.
"Kill Maya."
In Omega Year 999, someone's name had become the most important law in
the universe.
At the sun's core, the seven Archangels felt the laws of the universe
shifting.
It was taking a different path—but leading to the same result.
"How...?" Satiel asked.
Ikael responded. "They bypassed what we had enforced. One way or
another, anything can be changed."
"But they still kept their hearts," Satiel said. "At
least the Regular Angels won't lose their status over this."
But... would that really be the case?
On a personal level, it was a relief. But from the perspective of the
entire universe, nothing had truly changed.
They had failed to defy the will of God. And now, God was trying to
close this world.
'You can't stop what doesn't exist.'
The breath of God was seeping in—through every gap in every material, in
every law.
If the universe were a sealed box, then even a single hole would fill it
with air. Just the same.
'The only way is to seal it completely. Unless every being rejects God
with a singular will...'
In the end, it was a game where God always won.
Having absorbed the Phoenix's essence, Amy rode the solar wind to where
Shirone was.
"Shirone."
The moment she dismissed her Flame Incarnation within the Miracle
Stream's barrier, Amy was left completely naked.
"Ah!"
Blushing, she hurried behind Shirone to hide her body.
"Don't look," she said.
Shirone smirked. "I can make you some clothes, you
know."
That would have been fine, but when she saw how much Shirone was working
with the Shirone Sphere, she couldn't bring herself to ask.
"It's fine. They'll just burn up again anyway."
For a moment, Amy was entranced by the display of matter pouring from
Shirone's hands.
"Incredible..."
"Yeah. Even though my mom reversed the laws, the world of
Apocalypse hasn't changed. God's way of thinking is simply beyond human understanding. This is
probably... my last method of standing against God."
Shirone didn't say "our" method.
'He knows.'
He knew how much more difficult it was to unite all of humanity than to
manipulate matter with the Shirone Sphere.
Amy hugged him from behind.
"Stay strong."
It felt as though her soul was piercing through his back. His heart
swelled with emotion.
"Let's not be the only ones who find happiness."
When they had been left alone after Pisho's funeral, she had said the
same thing.
"Huh?"
And just like back then, tears flowed from Shirone's eyes.
"Don't worry," Amy said gently. "The only man I've ever
loved is you. That will never change."
Shirone wept quietly.
Feeling the full weight of his sobs, Amy recalled a distant
memory.
"Do you remember? The first time we met?"
It was in the back alleys of Creas City, where she'd found a lost,
awkward boy.
"You got roughed up pretty badly that day. And I wasn't so kind
either. Told you to strip or something—"
"Haha..."
"But you know, I went home that day and told my dad I wanted to go
to a magic academy."
It was the day Shirone succeeded at magic for the first time.
"That's how we met again. And we went through so much. Sometimes I
wonder... what if I hadn't gone into that alley that day? What if I'd stopped for ice cream? Or been
five minutes late? Or said something different in that moment..."
Looking back, human fate was terrifying.
"I wouldn't be here now. I wouldn't be hugging you like this or
saying these words."
Shirone remained silent.
"If I'd lived a different life, I probably would've just gone on
living that way. But Shirone, the me who stands here now... I think that moment was a miracle. The
miracle that changed my life."
And so—
"I'll never change it. Even if time was turned back—thousands,
millions of times—I'd never trade that moment for any other future."
Shirone lowered his head.
'I need to remember this moment.'
He still didn't know why.
But if the beginning and end of the world were already decided, then the
only thing humans could do was—
"A life lived throwing your heart in with everything you've
got."
"Shirone, I'll never forget you. Even if I have to defy every
probability in the universe—I'll keep loving you."
"...Right."
Turning to face her, Shirone gently touched Amy's tear-streaked
cheek.
"You'd better remember me too. I'll come find you. Even if I have
to search the entire universe—I'll be there."
Shirone knew nothing of what would happen six hours later.
But in Omega Year 999, at the endpoint of human history set by God, they
had made a vow to walk beyond it.
The two of them turned to face the sun.
"I'll be back."
Whatever was happening at the Core, Amy was sure she could help.
"Be careful."
"Don't worry."
Gently holding his face, Amy kissed Shirone before casting Flame
Incarnation.
As she drifted outside the Miracle Stream's barrier, she smiled.
"No one can kill me."
Using spontaneous combustion magic, her body instantly teleported into
the sun.
"Yeah..."
Feeling her warmth linger for a moment, Shirone's eyes sharpened
again.
"Mika."
"Construction progress is at 42.0148%."
'I have to finish this.'
For the first time, he thought—
'Maybe unifying humanity is impossible.'
Delta Headquarters Spire.
Seated at the highest point, Shirone looked down at the crowd gathered
at the front gate with sorrowful eyes.
'It never ends.'
After Gis's press conference, the citizens of Rodenin had split into two
factions, staging protests.
'No… that's not quite it.'
If you looked deeper—into the parts the eye couldn't see—no one was
actually willing to listen to anyone else.
"Let's punish Gis under the kingdom's law for tarnishing Jive's honor!
This is the right of the citizens!"
In the democratic monarchy of Jive, the citizens of the capital wielded
considerable power.
"Who's the one who tarnished anything? We don't even know the full truth
yet! If we divide public opinion like this, it'll only hurt us in the Holy War!"
"Don't try to threaten us! A king who's lost his moral compass has no
right to represent the people!"
One voice after another shouted their opinions, and the noise reached
all the way into the inner halls of Delta Headquarters.
Standing by the window, Albino stared out at the hundreds of torches
burning at the gate.
Lupist said,
"Everything's going just as Gis wanted. Those flames seem to represent
the anger of the citizens."
"Anger isn't good. Emotions are visible—and what's visible can always be
controlled."
Albino continued,
"The content of the press conference never really mattered. Once a
signal is sent out, people who receive it react in one of two ways. They either agree, or they
don't."
"That's how division begins."
"Binary thinking is almost a curse for humanity. If it's not right, it's
wrong. But the 1.5 generation of humanity is different. They have one more option."
Albino said,
"Right, wrong—or no right answer."
"There is no 'correct' in this world. Everything is contradiction.
People fear that. So they desperately try to insist they're right. But the 1.5-gen humans? They accept
contradiction. Even if they don't understand it, even if they can't justify it, they just act. Their
focus is solely on solving the problem."
"In a way, it's a lack of emotion."
"Very cold. But they've evolved enough to reach the top of
civilization's food chain. By now, they've already split up the big spoils—money and power—among
themselves. What's left, they'll toss to the citizens: the thrill of victory, a sense of achievement.
Empty scraps with no real nutrition."
"They're still admirable values, though."
"Heh. It's like slapping a 'Best' sticker on something you don't even
understand and selling it. I don't really get it—how any of that's supposed to make my life happier. But
people keep buying, so I guess they keep selling."
Deep in thought, Lupist asked,
"What if the citizens had stayed silent after the press
conference?"
"Then Gis probably would've been panicking. When you can't predict the
public's reaction, it's terrifying. But it's tough to achieve that. Integration at the pre-conscious
level—that's what they call Ultima."
"I see."
"If there was a fallback, it would've been for the anti-royal faction to
unify the citizens. But since the purge already happened, that's probably why he held the press
conference now."
Public declarations always come last.
The citizens continued to clash.
"Let's wait. Let's hold off judgment until after the Holy War."
Even within the pro-Gis camp, moderates and radicals were now splitting
off.
"Lady Rebecca has passed away. Do you think the king isn't in pain?
Let's be patient for now."
The moderates then split into emotional and rational factions.
"That's not the point! Come on, don't you understand the situation?
We're saying we should wait out of respect for the current crisis!"
Then the emotional faction split into the angry and the
sympathetic.
"How can you say that?! Don't you have kids?! Are you a psycho or
something?!"
"What the hell did you say?!"
Shirone could only watch with sorrowful eyes as the citizens continued
to fragment.
'There's no right answer.'
There are only those who wander in search of one, and those who charge
forward, clutching the wrong answer like it's gospel.
And under the current system, where the first to arrive gets to plant
the flag and rule over all—
'Right now…'
The real victors were already raising their glasses in celebration at
the top of the world.
"Your Majesty Gis, congratulations."
In a luxurious bathhouse prepared by Arachne, the leaders of Jive's
allied nations were gathered together.
As Gis entered the bath, he said with a grin,
"Ha! Come now, was that really so big a deal? Like pulling out a rotten
tooth—feels so much better now."
Sitting at the edge of the bath with her chest bare, the leader of
Yoeumbang puffed on a pipe.
"Raymond was more sentimental than I thought. I heard he called out his
wife's name before he died."
The King of Iron burst into laughter.
"Puhahaha! Why? Afraid of going to hell? And that guy thought he could
be a king… Please."
Gis's eyelid twitched ever so slightly.
"Ohh, this is nice. Very relaxing."
They were predators of civilization—creatures who let no emotion
interfere with getting what they wanted.
"Your Majesty, please accept my love too."
And yet, even this cold, calculating '1.5 generation' of
humanity—
"Heh, shall I ask you something?"
—was still ruled by something primal and animalistic at its core.
"Ahh!"
That… was the world of Omega Year 999, as seen through Shirone's
eyes.
