Chapter 1234:
The Definition of Love (1)
Gise's eyes flew wide open.
"Guh—!"
Plu's arms, wrapped tightly around Gise, moved like machinery under the
force of the Algorithm.
Crack.
His neck twisted completely, and his gaze locked on the royal guard
outside the bed.
'……Why?'
A member of the hyper-intelligent and politically evolved 1.5th
generation of humanity—capable of manipulating even angels.
Plu spoke.
"Not yet."
But even such intelligence was ultimately fueled by the pursuit of
greater pleasure.
Instinct still reigned supreme.
Gise's lips parted, as if trying to speak, and his fingers twitched
faintly.
But in the end, his breath stopped.
At that moment, the captain of the royal guard drew his sword and
charged forward.
'As expected, fast.'
True to Jive's reputation for having the strongest swordsmanship corps,
there was no hesitation or confusion in their eyes.
They would think about the consequences after killing Plu.
'This is the end.'
As she faced their overwhelming reaction speed, Plu realized just how
far her body lagged behind her thoughts.
Just as the Algorithm began to activate, the blade had already closed
in.
And then—
Something unperceived by all slashed the guard captain from the
front.
Plu blinked.
'What… is that?'
This sense of dissonance.
As the sword flew toward her neck, she suddenly realized.
'This... this is that! That thing!'
She couldn't define it clearly—but ever since she entered the Holy War,
she had never forgotten its presence.
That faint recognition triggered the Algorithm—and it saved her
life.
"Gghk!"
Her body twisted in a direction her brain hadn't anticipated, and her
muscles screamed in pain.
"Hoo?"
Havitz released his Vanishing.
"You lived?"
Plu dropped to one knee, clutching her aching side.
'I have to run.'
She had survived only because the guard's attack coincided with
Havitz's—she wouldn't be able to sense Vanishing a second time.
"Interesting."
Havitz activated the divine frequency.
"You pass."
"What?"
"I'll let you live for now. I'm still setting the stage."
The real game would begin after acquiring Uorin's History Search
ability.
"More importantly…"
Stepping over corpses, Havitz walked toward the window.
"What's that now?"
Outside Delta HQ, warriors from the South and holy knights were locked
in fierce battle.
'Kill Maya.'
That was the only message resonating through the divine
frequency.
"Heh."
As Havitz's killing intent surged, Plu shivered despite her
vigilance.
"This'll be fun."
The window opened.
And once again, Plu forgot Havitz.
The Pyramid of Truth.
Located in the Kingdom of Paras, the relic of an ultra-ancient
civilization radiated a powerful magnetic field.
The astronomical concepts etched into its inner walls distorted
space-time, overlapping countless events.
"Guhh!"
Ares, Amy's brother, had eyes glowing like they were set ablaze.
"Damn it…" Even as he endlessly searched his memories for
something to ground him, the wave of Humans—In—was too strong.
'No…'
Regret and hesitation.
'I had no choice.'
All the reasons behind his regrets came flooding in through every one of
Ares's senses.
'If only I'd done that back then…'
Within the wave of In, the future could be changed.
"Rghhh!"
But Ares rejected every hypothetical and shattered every "what
if" with his will.
"Life."
It means taking full responsibility for oneself.
'The moment you deny that, the moment you stop taking responsibility,
life ceases to be life.'
It becomes no different than the orbit of a planet, the fall of a
droplet, or the blowing of the wind.
"Aaaaargh!"
Ares clutched his head.
"Leave me alone!"
Who wouldn't want to change things?
If he'd known it would end up like this—if he'd known there was a better
life—he would've chosen it in a heartbeat.
"What do you want from me?!"
As he screamed, a storm of In surged down the corridor.
"Uuugh…"
His eyes rolled upward—and then the flashes of the Red Eye began to
slow.
'It's over.'
More than the terror of turning into an object… was the euphoria of the
entire future existing solely for him.
"Heh heh heh…"
Honestly, it was a beautiful experience. All the trouble he gave his
parents, the first love he couldn't confess to, the teammate he lost on an expedition due to a poor
decision—
'I can change it all.'
Trapped in a world with no regret or remorse, Ares wept.
He knew.
'I can't escape this place.'
A blind man can't live in a world where he can't see even a second
ahead.
And just as he began to lose himself…
"Erga."
A shriek from the dead pierced his eardrums from far away.
"Hahh!"
Lightning struck him at the same time.
"Aaaaargh!"
Even through the searing pain, Ares instinctively knew it wasn't
fatal.
Still convulsing, he wrapped his arms around himself and looked up at
the woman before him.
"Zulu." She stood in the darkness, pacifier now removed, her face
calm but sorrowful.
"One moment later, and it would've been too late-yo."
"Tch! Says who?"
Having regained some strength, Ares looked at the lich floating behind
Zulu.
"You okay?"
"What do you mean?"
Zulu was still indifferent, but Ares, who had experienced the wave of In
directly, was curious.
"This pyramid is… bizarre. It violates your mind. It submerges your
entire life—every moment from birth until now—into an infinite realm of space-time."
Zulu remained silent.
"So how are you still fine? Is there a way to resist the wave of
In?"
"No. There's no such thing. The same thing is happening to me right now.
Even at this very moment... an infinite number of futures are unfolding before me."
"Then how—"
Zulu turned her head and spoke.
"Because I have no affection-yo."
Ares couldn't fully understand.
But the moment he looked into her eyes, he understood something.
'This woman… may already be one of the In.'
He felt nothing.
Zulu spoke.
"No regrets, no lingering attachments, no remorse, no desire to change
the future, no longing for the past—I don't know any of that-yo. When I killed Erga, I must have died
too."
"That's not true."
Her words were too sad, so he denied them—though, deep down, he had no
strong reason to believe otherwise.
"But Zulu, you fought for humanity. You're here helping Shirone even
now. I don't believe someone like that has nothing left inside."
"I don't mean I don't feel emotions. I don't mean I don't think. But the
essence of all of it is affection. If what you say is true… does that mean In are alive too?"
"Th-That's…"
"The foundation of life is love. At the very least, you stay alive
because you love yourself. If you elevate that philosophy to the scale of the universe, you get
Yahweh."
"Is that why you help Shirone?"
"No."
Zulu shook her head.
"I don't have affection for anything. Not even myself. If you love
someone, that person has life—because they'll remain in your memory forever. But the countless people
you've met and now can't remember, no matter how hard you try? They're already dead-yo."
Time only lingers in memory.
Whether something existed or not, we simply accumulate memories.
Ares thought:
'So that's it.'
Losing your mind to the wave of In… means losing all affection for
life.
Suddenly, Zulu lifted her shirt just below her chest.
"Ack!"
As Ares turned his head in embarrassment, he spotted a strange creature
attached to her abdomen.
"What is that?"
"An armored beetle. Amoros. My summoned creature-yo." A being with
layers of carapace, its snake-like tail wrapped around her waist.
He could guess where its head was—blood was seeping from her
navel.
"What are you doing?"
"Amoros injects venom into internal organs. In the process of producing
that venom, it secretes a rare chemical compound. I'll give it to you. Drink it—you'll be able to hold
out for a while."
"But then what about you…?"
"It's fine. I'm used to it. Amoros only lives inside the pyramid, so it
can't be summoned outside. Strange, isn't it? This creature hasn't evolved in ages. Likely since the
pyramids of the ancient civilization first appeared."
Zulu pulled the tail of Amoros, revealing a much longer body than
expected.
"Ugh…"
She inserted her thumb into its maw and tilted the creature like a
teapot.
A stream of greenish slime began to drip.
"Here."
It was supposedly good for him, but it didn't look appealing.
"Uh… how do I…?"
"Lick it with your tongue."
"...Okay."
The moment he tasted it, the bitterness nearly stung his nose, but his
mind quickly began to calm.
"Whoa. What is this?"
His Red Eye flashed, analyzing his internal changes.
'Incredible. I can't even analyze the compounds, but this is
revolutionary. But how…?'
Zulu spoke.
"Long ago, this ancient ruin may not have been such a mystery. Many came
here, understood something, and left."
The countless pyramid replicas across the Kingdom of Paras were proof of
that.
"Amoros likely lived inside those pyramids back then. Through the
changes in law or the wave of In, it must have developed these abilities. And the fact that it hasn't
evolved since then means…"
It had already adapted to changes in time and space.
"Let's go. We have to give Amoros's fluid to others too. I can't make
much."
Zulu's complexion was pale.
"Next time, use me. I'm sure in ancient times, they offered some kind of
sacrifice to obtain this liquid."
Zulu shook her head.
"I've tried. It doesn't work. I was raised inside the pyramid, so I
developed an ancient constitution. Only I can control Amoros-yo."
Ares couldn't understand.
"Why go this far? Even with a special constitution, it's still a
venomous beetle. You won't last long."
"…You're probably right."
Zulu admitted plainly.
"But it's okay. I don't remember myself. It doesn't matter what happens
to me-yo."
"How can you say that?!"
Affection or not—offering your own body to save others, he believed, was
selfless.
"Come here. Let's treat the wound first." Ares dug through his
bag, applied medicine to her abdomen, and wrapped her slim waist with bandages.
"You're bandaged now. Don't overdo it for a while."
"Okay."
Waiting without emotion, Zulu turned first and began to walk into the
darkness.
Ares called out.
"Wait."
And just as she turned around, he suddenly leaned in and kissed
her.
'No more hesitation.'
From the wave of In, he learned that if you're unsure—do it.
But Zulu stood there like a doll, unmoved, not even breathing
heavily.
Frustrated, Ares held on a few seconds longer, but finally had to pull
away, face burning.
Zulu spoke.
"Let's go."
"Hey, I just kissed you. Shouldn't you at least slap me or say
something?"
"I don't remember."
Because her heart was empty.
Ares watched her back disappear into the darkness—with a sad
gaze.
