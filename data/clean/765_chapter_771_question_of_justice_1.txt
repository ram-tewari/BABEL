Chapter 771:
Question of Justice (1)
They had arrived in the Corona Kingdom.
So this is what it feels like.
Maybe it was because he had returned after completing his mission, but
even the cold, unfamiliar landscape felt comfortable—like coming home.
"Let's grab a meal before we go. What's the point of being one of the
Five Stars if you don't even have time to eat?"
They stepped into the White Inn, where Shirone had once stayed.
"Welcome! Oh my—!"
The middle-aged woman brightened at the sight of Shirone and Minerva
entering side by side.
"You two seem very close now. Don't tell me…?"
"It's business. He's a Five Star too."
"I see, huh…"
She looked slightly disappointed.
"Just something quick to eat."
As Minerva made it clear she was in a hurry, the woman disappeared into
the kitchen without further comment.
Seated, Shirone looked toward a table in the corner.
"……"
The same four people were still playing cards in the exact same seats as
back then.
No way…
Maybe they had gone home and come back, and by chance all wore the same
clothes. That had to be it.
Noticing Shirone's expression, Minerva turned toward the four and
asked:
"Who's winning?"
A bald old man replied.
"No one is. If the game doesn't end, there's no winner or loser. Only
the game wins."
Shirone listened closely to the old man's words.
Only the game wins.
It eerily echoed the current state of the world, where Yahweh and the
Buddha stood at odds.
"How long are you going to keep playing?"
"…Until the game ends."
Minerva dug through her coat and pulled out a stack of bonds from South
Eymond, waving them in the air.
"Mind if I join this round?"
The four players all froze at once.
"What are you doing?" Shirone asked, dismayed by the talk of gambling.
But Minerva was already grabbing a chair.
"Let me introduce them properly. These are the Card Players. They're
kind of a legend around the Ivory Tower. Tourists even use this table as a photo spot."
The innkeeper called from the kitchen:
"They're the pride of the White Inn!"
Minerva glanced over the game board.
"Fourteen years ago, four gamblers—the best in the world—came to the
Ivory Tower. They made a bet: Who would win in a game?"
"What kind of game?"
"Doesn't matter. A test, life itself—it could be anything. But the
players all have different dispositions. One must take, one must give, one wants to end the game, and
one wants to keep it going."
Shirone studied the Card Players again.
"Who do you think will win?"
"Obviously the one who must take—"
Shirone shook his head.
"There is no winner."
"Ironic, isn't it? But that's life. For the game to end, one side's
balance has to break."
Minerva laid the bonds on the table.
"I'll bet on the winner of this round. If I lose, you can take all of
this."
The bald man asked, "…And if you win?"
"Then the game stops. There will no longer be any Card Players in this
world."
A red-bearded man across the table spoke.
"Fine. Who wins this round?"
"Hmm, let's see…"
As Minerva studied the hands carefully, Shirone swallowed.
Breaking the balance.
If someone were to disrupt this game, perfectly balanced between good
and evil, justice and love…
Who would win then?
After carefully examining their hands for a long while, Minerva
scratched her head.
"I have no clue. I've never had a knack for gambling. So this is where
instinct comes in. Who's the one trying to keep the game going?"
Altruism—or Shirone himself.
"That would be me," said a gaunt man, his frame skeleton-thin.
"Alright. I'll bet on you."
The Card Players were the greatest Savants in the world—victorious in
every kind of game.
Whether it was chess with pure logic, cards with probability, or even
physical sports influenced by emotion, they had mastered them all.
Now, after fourteen years of playing simulations, Minerva had introduced
a new variable.
So what happens next?
She'd bet a fortune on the one trying to preserve the game. If Minerva
won, the game would be destroyed.
It's definitely complex.
The one trying to end the game would likely lean toward Minerva's
win.
That's how the chain reaction begins. Everyone's thinking will
shift.
"Let's begin," said the man with ink-black pupils.
The players' hands moved with blinding speed, drawing cards, laying them
down, swapping them out.
What kind of rules are these?
It was too fast to follow, and the game lasted over three
minutes.
"The result is in."
Minerva stared at the table with interest, and Shirone stood as
well.
"The winner of this round is…"
The bald man looked toward the red-bearded one.
"You. Gudio."
The one who must take—Evil.
"……"
It was just a game, but those playing it had reached the pinnacle of
action and reaction.
"Sigh, what a shame. I could've won that," Minerva said, clicking her
tongue as she returned to her seat.
Shirone stared at her in disbelief.
"Is that all you have to say? What about the money?"
"It can't be helped. That's gambling. Win, you take it all. Lose, you
give it all."
"What was the point if you were going to…"
"Let's go. We've got mountains of work to do. What's food going to do
for us?"
Minerva grabbed her Jet and left the inn. Shirone glanced back at the
Card Players.
None of them even looked at the bonds. They had their heads down,
already analyzing the previous round.
Shirone shook his head.
"I really don't get it."
As he followed Minerva outside, the innkeeper emerged belatedly with
their food.
"Oh, they already left?"
The bald man looked up and asked:
"Do you all agree?"
Everyone nodded.
"It was very slight. Seemed like it wouldn't affect anything, but now…
things might just tip."
Gudio said:
"At this rate, the game ends."
In other words, someone's chips would run out just before they could
recover.
"Who's the final winner?"
Since none of them were simulating the others, they'd have to actually
play it out to see.
"Let's begin."
As the four players' hands moved at astonishing speeds and cards flew
across the table, the bonds fell to the floor.
The innkeeper, watching quietly, dipped a finger in the soup and tasted
it, murmuring:
"What a waste. Can't throw it away either…"
After parting ways with Minerva at the Ivory Tower, Shirone arrived at
the Grand Earth Temple where the Great Star resided.
"You look tired," the Great Star greeted with a knowing
smile.
"Minerva is truly a challenging person," Shirone
replied.
"I've heard. But I need your confirmation—are you really planning
to register the 'Lawslayer'?"
"Yes. I'd like to use it."
Since Shirone was the owner, the Great Star had no objections.
"Understood. Also..."
Her voice turned cold.
"We need to discuss the rules of 'Breaking the
System.'"
Anticipating this, Shirone silently awaited her next words.
"As you may have heard, 'Breaking the System' is
dangerous."
"Because it infringes upon higher systems?"
The Great Star's expression grew somber.
"I don't want to hear you say that, Shirone. As I've mentioned
before, nothing is predetermined. All life among the stars simply lives according to its own
will."
"Then what is the problem?"
"Because we don't exist within a specific system; we create the
system."
The Great Star gestured toward the ground.
"Humans live by their own will, but when those wills converge,
certain patterns inevitably emerge. That's the system, and it might lead to the imagination that we are
part of a program. But strictly speaking, it's an imagination that reverses cause and
effect."
Of course, that imagination could be correct.
"The reason 'Breaking the System' is unacceptable isn't because
someone decided it shouldn't be done. It's because it inherently threatens the system we've
created."
As the Great Star spread her arms, numerous stars were born in a
fan-like formation.
"Within the backdrop of the universe exists the natural world, and
from that natural world, the biological world emerged. Humans occupy a certain point within that
biological world."
One of the stars exploded, creating a hole in the universe.
"The most powerful force of the natural world can pierce a hole
into the higher system, the universe. This is called 'Breaking the System.'"
"Then, from a human perspective..."
"Yes. From the position of the biological world, it would be a
force capable of destroying the natural world. The most potent magic you could wield before reaching
Yahweh's realm was Divine Punishment. But even that doesn't compare to 'Breaking the
System.'"
An illusion of the star Shirone inhabited appeared, flickering like a
tiny firefly.
"No matter the magic. Typhoons, volcanic eruptions? Stars are
unaffected by such natural disasters. They aren't even destroyed by most asteroid collisions. Even if
ten thousand of your Divine Punishments were unleashed, it would only scratch the star's surface—akin to
a human getting a skin abrasion."
Even if all the resources of humanity were mobilized, what would be
destroyed is their civilization, not the star itself.
"It's not because someone decided it should be this way; it's
because it was born this way, becoming the system. But you've broken that rule."
Shirone could destroy a star.
"That is 'Breaking the System.'"
"Of course, I can withstand your power, but Theraphos is different.
Unlike the natural world, what governs the biological world is the mind. If one wills it, it's believed
that a star can be destroyed."
"Is it Ymir?"
The Great Star nodded.
"Even Anke Ra doesn't tolerate 'Breaking the System.' It's like a
foul in a game. Think about it—if a being with intent could destroy a star..."
If higher systems could be destroyed at will.
"Who would want to live here?"
The value, reason, and purpose of existence lose meaning in the face of
someone with the power to break the system.
"There is controversy."
The Great Star turned away.
"Theraphos is an existence that far surpasses human intellect. They
believe that fouls shouldn't occur in this fight. If it's not a fair contest, distinguishing between
winner and loser becomes meaningless."
The Great Star raised her index finger.
"Hence, 'Man'—the limit of the biological world. You've transcended
that 'Man.' In the universe's caste system, you're no longer a living being. You're a form of
nature."
Anyone aware of what Shirone was capable of could only nod in
agreement.
"Then what should I do now?"
"Nothing. You've transcended 'Man' by your own will. I just wanted
to tell you that there are those who consider it a foul."
"Theraphos."
"Yes. Their council will judge. It seems the Theraphos's high
justice is exerting effort, but it's not easy. Stay at the Ivory Tower for a while and observe the
developments."
"What if it's dismissed?"
The Great Star turned back to Shirone.
"Nothing will change. But if you ultimately refuse to comply with
the ruling..."
Her gaze grew colder than ever before.
"Theraphos will excise you from the system to protect the
inhabitants of this star."
