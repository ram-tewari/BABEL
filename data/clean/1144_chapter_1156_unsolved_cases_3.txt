Chapter 1156:
Unsolved cases (3)
"Nade!"
Before his friends could stop him, Nade deflected the bandit's longsword
to the side.
"Tch! Not bad!"
Even though he couldn't use any special abilities, his combat instincts
and real-world experience were top-tier.
Twisting the dagger into the bandit's side, Nade smirked as the man
groaned in pain.
"Guhhh…!"
'Damn, the sensation is disturbingly real.'
Driving another stab into the man's abdomen, Nade flipped the dagger
upward, piercing the bandit's throat.
"Grrrhh…"
He held the weapon firm until he felt the full weight of his enemy, then
stepped back.
Like a broken tree, the bandit collapsed.
"How many points?"
- Your current Crime Points: 58P.
'It cost 18 points to take down one guy. Even defense counts as a crime?
This is seriously inefficient.'
If he had targeted civilians instead, he could have completed the
mission for just 5 points per kill.
"You arrogant bastards!"
Turning his head at the shout, Nade saw Iruki and Eden dodging the
swings of another longsword.
"You're good at running, huh?!"
Iruki ducked under a sword swing, twisting his body almost horizontally
along the ground.
Then, whipping his right arm like a lash, he kicked up dirt straight
into the bandit's face.
"Aaargh! My eyes!"
At the same time, Eden took a running start and delivered a powerful
kick between the man's legs.
"Guhhhh—!"
He couldn't even scream.
His pupils rolled upward, and as he collapsed, Nade stepped in and
slashed his throat.
"Points?"
- Your current Crime Points: 17P.
"What?"
They weren't sure who had used how many points, but it had consumed far
more than simply stabbing someone to death.
Nade turned to Eden.
"You…"
"Sorry."
She stuck out her tongue and shrugged just as Shirone's voice rang
out.
"Nade!"
A third bandit had wrapped his arms around Shirone's neck, wrestling him
to the ground.
"Ughhh! Let go!"
Reaching for a fallen longsword, the bandit raised it toward Shirone's
face.
"Shirone!"
Without hesitation, Nade flung his dagger.
But the moment his fingers released it—
- Insufficient Crime Points to execute a crime. Current Crime Points:
14P.
'Damn it!'
Throwing a weapon seemed to cost even more points.
"Die!"
As the bandit lifted his sword to strike, Shirone roared.
"Hyaaaah!"
Summoning all his strength, he twisted the bandit's head.
CRACK!
With a sickening snap, the man's head spun grotesquely to the
side.
His arms went limp, and the gruesome sight left everyone
speechless.
"Haa… Haa…"
Breathing heavily, Shirone shoved the corpse aside and stood up.
"Shirone, are you okay?"
Even though his endurance far surpassed that of ordinary people, his
back muscles throbbed in pain.
"Yeah… Just give me a moment."
The private investigators stood in stunned silence, watching a group of
young men take down the entire bandit gang with just knives.
'Who are these guys? They look so young…'
Knowing that rookies were only given 100 Crime Points at the start made
it all the more baffling.
"You guys…"
Just as one of them was about to speak—
- Mission complete. Reward event initiating.
Time froze.
Nade waved his hand in front of the frozen people, but nothing
happened.
"Another hidden code?"
Iruki nodded.
"Most likely. This system is designed to prevent us from knowing
exactly how many points we earned. Fair, in a way—since points mean everything."
- Bonus! You have completed an Honorable Crime. Your earned Crime Points
will be doubled.
As the message ended, two Crime Dice dropped at their feet.
No one moved to pick them up.
Nade spoke first.
"A bonus, huh? Guess it's because we completed a hidden event.
Double points—seems like a good deal."
Iruki shook his head.
"Not necessarily. Finding a hidden event is a hassle, and as we
just saw, killing through direct combat is a huge waste of points. Every action—defense, dodging,
striking vital points—consumes points."
Eden added,
"Honestly, I see what Curtis meant now. This method is
inefficient."
"… Honorable crime?"
Shirone muttered under his breath.
"They called it an 'Honorable Crime.' But we just killed a gang of
vicious bandits."
It felt as if the system was telling them: "You're still criminals.
You're no different from the rest."
Nade patted his back reassuringly.
"Forget it. This is a game built by villains. You know how it
works—hesitate for even a second, and you're done. This is exactly the kind of thing the Catacomb is
banking on."
"Yeah… I know, but…"
Maybe that was also the truth.
"Nade, I'm sticking with this strategy."
"Of course."
Steadying himself, Shirone bent down and picked up the Crime
Dice.
Beneath their feet, the ground shimmered like rippling water.
'A randomized structure.'
If this system was built by Havitz, then it would be flawless.
- Reward Summary:Base reward: 100 Crime Points.Bonus reward:
Receive Crime Points equal to the total sum of three dice rolls. Special reward: Honorable Crime
Bonus—All rewards doubled.
Iruki commented,
"It looks like Shirone still has ownership of the Crime Dice. Under
normal circumstances, we would have earned 100 points, plus up to 36 additional points from rolling the
dice."
Nade clicked his tongue.
"That's ridiculously low. No wonder that serial killer was being
stingy. Even breaking even seems impossible."
Eden nodded.
"But Curtis had three Crime Dice. And his dice had more sides than
ours. The more you enhance your dice, the bigger the rewards."
"Guys… listen."
Shirone turned to his friends with a dazed expression.
"I think… I can roll a 'double.'"
"What do you mean?"
"There aren't any restrictions on magic or Incarnation Techniques
here. So… I can do it. Quantum Collapse."
It was the same method he had used in High Gear to obtain a
legendary-tier item.
Iruki checked the surroundings.
"You're right. Since time is frozen, there's no signal restricting
us."
It was a world governed purely by probability.
"Alright, I'll try."
Rolling the dice in his palm, Shirone focused on the trembling,
thread-like surface beneath him.
"Whoa…"
Absolute observation.
As the quantum state collapsed, the ground froze over, and two dice flew
into the air.
Completely observed. In other words, 100% probability.
The dice bounced off the uneven terrain before coming to a stop, spread
apart.
Both showed a six.
"It worked!"
With a thrilling impact, the numbers combined into twelve, and the dice
reappeared before them.
"Double achieved. You have gained one additional roll."
"Nice!"
On the second roll, Shirone once again got six and six, but this time,
the system voice remained silent.
"Hmm, I see. The bonus roll from a double only applies once per
session. But since we still have two rolls left, we effectively get four total chances—assuming we hit
another double."
"No problem."
The moment the dice fell in front of him, Shirone grabbed them and
hurled them far.
The additional reward amounted to seventy-two points.
Adding that to the base reward, they had one hundred seventy-two points,
and with the double bonus applied, they earned a total of three hundred forty-four Crime Points.
"Hell yeah! Jackpot!"
As Shirone's group cheered, Nade jumped up and down excitedly.
"This method is amaziiii—ah…"
Time resumed.
Nade, still grinning, snapped back to reality.
"Huh? Wait, what?"
Hawk Eye, the private investigators, stared at them with blank
expressions.
"Ahem."
"What just happened?"
"Uh, nothing. Nothing at all."
Instinctively, Nade kept the information to himself, but Shirone spoke
honestly.
"We received our rewards. Sorry about that."
He could have explained the brief descent into the mental realm, but the
investigators didn't ask.
"No, we're the ones who should be grateful. Thanks."
"Are you going to wait again? Until another bandit
raid?"
The investigator's face darkened.
"We can't afford to waste any more time. We know how Melkidu's
rules work. That criminal has fled far by now. So we have no choice but to…"
… Kill the villagers.
"They aren't real people, and we've confirmed that those who die
resurrect. I don't even know why I'm saying this. I just… don't know anymore. We have to
go."
Shirone clenched his lips.
This is why it's difficult.
Unlike extreme good or extreme evil, justice and compassion cannot be
easily defined.
Are humans truly evil?
Perhaps. But before assuming that, one had to ask:
Is the system itself flawed?
Maybe humanity, society, and we ourselves were forcing each other toward
wickedness.
Because we must survive.
We compete, we pull others down, we feel jealousy and resentment…
I hate criminals too. I feel the same frustration that Miro does toward
injustice.
But a perfect system…
If there were a world where rational intelligence prevailed, where we
didn't have to worry about disease or energy, and people still harmed and deceived one another—
Then, we would have every right to condemn humanity.
But that's not the case. We just haven't reached that level of
intelligence yet.
Humans feel hurt when called evil and warm when called good.
Why don't they realize how remarkable that is? Placing value in goodness
over evil means that…
One day, far in the future,
We will reach that ideal.
Just as history has shown time and again.
We can't define humanity yet. We can't give up on ourselves in
advance.
One day, evil will disappear.
Because I, too, am human.
He wanted to believe that this wasn't all there was.
"Alright. Good luck."
Despite the countless thoughts swirling in his head, Shirone kept them
to himself.
My ideals are mere delusions to others.
Because at this very moment, all of humanity is playing a game of
passing the burden of suffering.
Nane is right.
If he considered only the people of the present, then closing this world
wouldn't be wrong.
As the private investigators limped away, Eden approached and
whispered.
"Let's do what we need to do."
… Until the concept of justice, evil, and compassion are unified.
"Alright, let's move out. One person can't roll the dice
consecutively, right? Who's next?"
Iruki pointed at Nade.
"Only Shirone can manipulate the dice for artificial doubles. So it
doesn't matter who rolls."
"Ah, right. Damn, I could've gotten free points."
Eden comforted him.
"Still, we have three hundred fifty-four points now. Normally, we'd
only have around one hundred at best. For a start, this is a great position. Meeting Curtis was lucky
too."
Nade took the dice.
"Alright. Let's go all out. The god of dice is watching over
me."
"Yeah, yeah, just roll already."
"Here we go! Shine, our brilliant fate!"
With all his might, Nade threw the dice.
From the distance, two glowing red numbers appeared.
One and two.
"Oh, for f—!"
Before he could finish cursing, Shirone's group flashed into light and
advanced three spaces forward.
