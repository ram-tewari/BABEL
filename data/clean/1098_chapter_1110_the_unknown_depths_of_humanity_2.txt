As long as Maya wasn't there to see it.
'I should go back to Tormia. What kind of knight am I? Staying here will

Kaiden's heart sank.

"It's fine. It's not the first time."
Maya knew about Kaiden's uncontrollable right hand.
What she didn't know was...
"What's your true intention?"

"No way! Inappropriate thoughts? I'd never dream of such a

Kaiden waved both hands, but only his left moved. His right hand formed
an "OK" sign with his thumb and forefinger.
Maya's expression turned blank as Kaiden's right hand gave her a
thumbs-up.
"Would you stop already!"
Kaiden yelled, hitting his right arm.
As another battle between his hands broke out, Maya spoke.
"Kaiden, I've been thinking a lot since your confession. But honestly,

Kaiden's right arm fell limp.
Whenever Shirone's name came up, his right arm would always go
still.
"I-I see. I wasn't expecting anything anyway…"
"But," Maya interrupted.
"I'm trying to move on. Shirone has Amy, after all. I can't cling to him
forever, can I?"
Kaiden's heart raced.
"I can't make any promises. But… can you give me a little time? Would
that be too much to ask?"
"No, not at all. I can wait. I'll wait forever—no, for eternity if I
have to."
It was a foolishly grand answer, but Maya smiled.
"Thank you for always caring about me. Good night."
"Oh, yeah. You too…"
After Maya left, Kaiden sat frozen for a long time before collapsing
onto his bed.
"She's just pitying me."
How pathetic and pitiful he must have seemed.
"Ugh."
His eyes welled up.
"But she didn't say no."
For someone crushed by the weight of the Red Cross Star's destiny, it
was the first glimmer of hope Kaiden had ever known.
"She said it's okay to wait."
He felt himself breaking free from the confines set by God.
'Maybe… maybe I can do it.'

covered his eyes.
