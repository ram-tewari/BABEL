Chapter 63:
The Invisible (2)
"Do you have something in mind?"
"Let's set up a psychological trap. Like this."
Shirone gathered his friends and explained the plan. After hearing it, Neid and Iruki
fell silent. Sensing their lack of confidence, Shirone added,
"It doesn't have to be exactly this way, but doing this will increase our chances, right?
For example, using keywords or…"
Iruki shook his head.
"No, Shirone, it's fine. More than fine—it's bound to succeed. This is quite profound.
With this method, we can use the presentation as foreshadowing."
Once the big picture was clear, Shirone and his friends began detailing the plan.
"The remaining problem is this: how do we create a ghost?"
The technical part was Neid's specialty. Rising from the sofa, he cast Lightning Shadow,
and an electrical afterimage of himself appeared.
"With Lightning Shadow, I can create a realistic ghostly form. But no one would fall for
this. It can't move, for one."
"Then let's think from there. How do we make the ghostly form move?"
"The first thing that comes to mind is this: to make Lightning Shadow move, you can cast
the spell at an incredibly high speed."
Neid cast Lightning Shadow repeatedly while walking. Though disjointed, the afterimage
appeared to be walking.
"I see. So, we can use optical illusions to make it seem real?"
"Right. But magic alone has limits. Plus, at my level, I can't increase the number of
entities."
Shirone asked.
"How about using mechanical devices?"
"It's not as easy as it sounds. Even if we make the device, there's a mountain of things
to consider. The biggest problem is that just moving won't fool anyone. To truly look like a ghost, it needs
to react to external stimuli."
Neid, who had once built a hologram device, knew creating a ghostly illusion wasn't the
issue. But if it couldn't react to people's actions, they'd quickly see through it.
Iruki, deep in thought, spoke.
"What if we predict people's reactions in advance?"
"Predict reactions?"
"Apply complex systems theory. Analyze the behavioral patterns of the crowd and program
them. Make them think they're getting feedback. But the amount of information is enormous. We'd need a
medium to transmit the data and a control device."
"Hmm. Data transmission. My electrical power definitely has limits. Then… ah!"
Neid turned to Shirone.
"Photon Output! Shirone's Photon Output!"
"Ah. Photon Output…"
Shirone also saw the potential. Though he'd learned the spell for targeting tests, he'd
heard that research on transmitting information via Photon Output was booming in advanced magical societies.
"Now it's coming together. Neid will build the hologram device. I'll write the program
for it. Shirone's Photon Output will transmit my program to the device. It'll require massive output—can you
do it?"
"I'll make it happen. This is our chance."
It was a ray of light in the darkness. Just having hope for what seemed impossible moved
Neid deeply.
"We can do this. We can save the research club. Assuming nothing goes wrong, of course."
"If we succeed, the school will be turned upside down, right?"
"More than that. It'll be chaos. I'm all in. Plus, it sounds fun. Hehe. I can't wait to
see the teachers' reactions."
Shirone and his friends exchanged meaningful smiles. With the fate of the Supernatural
Parapsychology Research Club at stake, an unprecedented event in the magic school's history was about to
unfold.
***
Shirone devoted his time to increasing the power of his Photon Output. The key was to
boost its intensity and duration. Since photons had no mass and couldn't exert physical force, they couldn't
be used as offensive magic, but this very trait became an advantage now. He could train in any of Istar's
storage rooms.
Shirone raised his concentration with a magical action, extending both hands, and cast
Photon Output. A beam of light spread in a circle on the wall. First was duration. If he couldn't sustain it
for 10 minutes, increasing the output was a pipe dream.
Maintaining Photon Output for 10 minutes was beyond Class Five, but the same went for
Neid's hologram device and Iruki's complex systems equations.
'I have to do this. I have to…'
Shirone gritted his teeth and held on. After 3 minutes, his consciousness began to blur.
During the speed gun test, even a single burst of Photon Output had barely lasted a minute. Compared to
that, this was remarkable growth, but it still fell far short of the goal.
"Gah! Huff!"
The time he managed to hold on with all his might was 3 minutes and 20 seconds. He had to
increase this to 10 minutes within 15 days. After recording the time, he rested for exactly 20 minutes.
Neglecting rest in such training could be life-threatening.
As Shirone slumped down, he noticed the noise from the next room. Neid seemed to have
started welding. During his break, he decided to check on him. Opening the door, he saw Neid buried in
various parts.
"Hey, you're here? How's the output time?"
"So-so. Increased by 20 seconds. How about you?"
"As you can see, still working on the engine."
"Can you make it in time?"
"I have to. Don't worry. Prototypes always take time. Once production starts, fixing
errors becomes hard. That's why we take our time to be thorough."
"I see. By the way, what about what Iruki mentioned?"
"Oh, that? Got it yesterday. Here."
Neid threw a finger-thick cord at Shirone's feet. The outer layer was rubber, and inside
was a transparent membrane like quartz. It was something Iruki had specially requested from his father, the
head of the Thunder Dragon faction.
With 300 hologram devices to be installed around the school, it was impossible for
Shirone to cast Photon Output on all of them alone.
At first, Neid thought of mirrors. Shirone would cast Photon Output, and the light would
reflect off mirrors to reach the devices. But in reality, the light would bend too much.
Neid wondered if more mirrors would work. By attaching mirrors to small hoses, he could
create tunnels for the light, mimicking the effect of light bending as if differentiating a straight line.
Proudly, Neid announced this. If they could make it, they'd be rolling in money, he
added.
But Iruki shattered his excitement. His father's monthly letters often included updates
on magical society, and a few months ago, they'd described something identical to Neid's idea—called
"optical fibers."
Neid was utterly disappointed. Even in the ivory towers of magic's cutting edge, this was
a newly developed field, so even Iruki felt it was a waste.
But upon analyzing the actual fibers, Neid shook his head. The engineering was so
delicate it was beyond his current skill.
'Still, I thought of the same thing as the ivory tower. Neid, you're amazing.'
Shirone picked up the optical fiber and examined it closely. To the naked eye, it didn't
seem special.
"But you managed to get it."
Neid, having finished welding, rubbed his reddened eyes and stood.
"The ivory tower is still in the early stages, so the performance isn't great. Let's test
it first. I made a tester."
Neid handed the modified optical fiber to Shirone. Unlike before, it had a transparent
orb attached at the end.
"What do I do with this?"
"Just hold it and cast Photon Output. I'll measure the efficiency. Pour everything into
it—we need the maximum output."
Since his break was over, Shirone didn't hold back. As light gathered in his hand, the
orb glowed faintly. The needle on Neid's connected device began to sway. After watching the gauge until the
3-minute limit, Neid spoke dejectedly.
"Sigh, Iruki was right. This is a total early-stage prototype. Well, I should be grateful
we even have this much."
"That bad?"
"Efficiency is only 18%. Only 18% of the light you send reaches the destination."
"18%…"
Shirone also felt hollow. With maintaining Photon Output for 10 minutes already a distant
goal, 18% efficiency meant he'd need to increase his output fivefold just to match 100%.
"Shirone, can you do it? If you can't increase the output, we'll never succeed.
High-output magic energy is impossible to achieve with machines alone."
Shirone nodded resolutely.
"I'll try. Practice will make it possible."
"You're sure you can do it?"
"Yeah. Don't worry. I'll figure it out. I'll go practice now."
"Really? You're really going to do it?"
As Shirone left, he turned and shouted.
"I said I got it!"
***
Shirone lay sprawled in the storage room, gasping for breath. Half a day of Photon Output
had taken its toll, both mentally and physically.
"Huff! Huff!"
His last record was 3 minutes and 20 seconds—no change from the morning. But pushing
himself to the brink of exhaustion counted as progress. An hour ago, his time had dropped to 2 minutes and
34 seconds, so his final push had paid off.
'Still, it's nowhere near enough. How can I reach 10 minutes?'
Maybe it was impossible from the start. Photon Output, being a sustained spell, drained
mental energy far faster than single-action spells. Maintaining it for 10 minutes was something only pros
could do.
"Ugh, I don't know. I'll try again tomorrow when I'm fresh."
Neid opened the storage room door and entered.
"Shirone, done?"
"Yeah. Can't go on today. Need to rest."
"I'm exhausted too from just sparks. It's past midnight. Let's head back to Iruki."
Time had flown. Shirone had spent over 7 hours on Photon Output alone—a life-threatening
amount of training. If the school found out, they'd surely ban him from magic practice.
Shirone struggled to his feet. Neid looked no better—his eyes were sunken, and his face
was red from prolonged exposure to electricity.
Back at the research club, Iruki was swiftly moving his pen over the school's blueprint,
calculating potential variables for the day of the operation.
Neid watched worriedly. Like Shirone and himself, Iruki hadn't moved since sitting down.
"How's it going, Iruki? Calculations okay?"
Without looking up, Iruki replied.
"So-so. We'll make it in time, so don't worry."
Neid's heart ached. His ambition was hurting his friends.
"Was it too much to ask? Calculating the entire school's behavioral patterns…"
"I can do it. I said I can. Don't distract me. I'm busy."
With that, Iruki fell silent. Equations filled the notebook at a terrifying speed.
