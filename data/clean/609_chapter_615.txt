Chapter 615:
Arian Shirone.
The name that was already imprinted in the minds of the students shone
brightly. Since defeating Dante in the 2000th duel, Shirone had been regularly featured in Spirit
magazine. When this promising student suddenly decided to forgo the graduation evaluation, it became a
hot topic in schools nationwide.
"What's going on? Did something happen?" Iruki and Nade
approached Shirone.
"Where have you been? We've been waiting all day."
"Sorry, the head of the association called me. I had to meet with
him, and he was late…"
"The head of the association?"
The students began to murmur. Did this mean Shirone had a private
meeting with the head of the association? It wasn't a pleasant thought for the others, knowing that out
of 150 students, only Shirone had been called.
But as Elizabeth had said, thinking of meeting the head as a privilege
meant there was no future as a mage.
"Why did the head call you?" Curtis approached, representing
the students. His eyes were filled with dissatisfaction, and the relaxed expression he had earlier was
gone.
"Why should I tell you that?"
It was a personal matter, and since he had already rejected the head's
proposal, there was nothing to feel guilty about.
"Hmph, since when did Alpheas Magic School become so arrogant? Are
you acting up because of the vice principal?"
Hearing Curtis's words, Shirone finally grasped the atmosphere in the
room.
"Whatever it is, let's not fight. We all have important exams
ahead, and wasting energy won't do us any good."
It was the right thing to say, but the Royal Magic School students,
already in a bad mood, felt they were at a disadvantage.
Attention, students.
Lupist's voice came from the ceiling.
'It's starting.'
Only Fermi smiled meaningfully as the broadcast continued.
Seeing the future of the kingdom enjoying themselves
makes me proud as the head of the association.
The students checked the video recorders installed around the room. They
knew they were there for security, but they never imagined the head would be watching.
'And he's proud of this?'
It meant he had anticipated the students' conflicts.
In that spirit, I've prepared a special event.
The students waited anxiously for what would come next.
Hidden in your location is a certified mage
license.
Dozens of eyes scanned the room.
Of course, it doesn't have a name on it. So, whoever
finds it owns it.
Shirone's eyes sharpened. He knew this event wasn't planned from the
start, as he had just met with the head.
'This event is targeting me.'
The person who finds the license should go to the
registration office on the 7th floor of the association. From that moment, the student will be a
certified mage and an employee of the Magic Association.
"A Magic Association employee?"
It seemed too extreme to be just an event.
Enjoy your time. That's all.
As the students debated whether to search for the license or not,
someone spoke up.
"Let's just enjoy the party. No need to search
frantically."
"Yeah, whoever finds it by chance will be lucky. It's just a simple
event, like a raffle."
Thinking of it that way made it seem less significant, but the words
"certified mage license" were already stuck in their minds.
As expected, the students scattered, and Iruki nudged Shirone.
"What happened? You can tell us."
"It's exactly what you think. This event was actually proposed to
me. I refused."
"Hmm. Is it love or hate? It's quite complicated."
Nade asked, "What should we do? Everyone's already searching, even
if they pretend not to. Should we look too?"
"Do as you see fit. But I won't."
The kind of mage Shirone aspired to be wasn't like this.
"Let's wait and see. They probably didn't make it easy to
find."
The strange atmosphere of the event continued to build. While only a few
openly searched, most pretended to enjoy the feast while discreetly checking under plates or
tables.
'It's not here. Where could it be?'
No matter how large the annex was, with so many people, visible areas
were quickly checked.
"They can't find it," Iruki said.
"If this event wasn't planned, it means they hid the license after
the students arrived. There are two possibilities: it was hidden with magic, or the staff hid
it."
The students nearby flinched and hovered within earshot.
Nade wasn't oblivious, but he didn't care.
"As a savant, you remember all the staff movements,
right?"
There were 23 staff members helping with the party.
"None of them showed any suspicious behavior. Of course, I couldn't
see what happened in blind spots."
"Maybe the staff have it?"
"That's possible."
'It's the staff!'
The students scattered, questioning the staff.
"Do you have the license? Did someone give you something? Be
honest!"
The staff shook their heads in confusion, but the students' questioning
was relentless.
"Come on, give us a hint! Is it a code? Do we need to say a
keyword?"
Watching the commotion, Iruki commented, "This head isn't normal
either."
If there was one thing that could emotionally manipulate the best
students in the country, it was a mage license.
"They wouldn't have entrusted it to the staff. It's hidden with
magic."
Shirone's voice lowered, and Iruki and Nade became more aware of their
surroundings.
"Right. The staff never left the annex, and no one was
replaced."
Nade asked, "No blind spots?"
"Of course. I watched everything from start to finish."
Iruki was capable of that.
"But how did they hide it with magic? The magic control device is
active."
Shirone said, "That's the hint. Lupist is testing us."
"Get lost! This is my spot!"
"I was here first!"
Voices began to rise from all directions.
"At first, you can ignore it," Iruki said, turning to them.
"But as you see others moving, you get anxious. What if they find the license? Even though it's a 1
in 150 chance, it feels like losing something that's yours."
Nade added, "The human pleasure center is more stimulated by
others' failures than by their own success."
"It's a crowd control method politicians use. Once the numbers
increase, it can't be stopped. Individual will is swept away, and the desire not to let others succeed
takes over."
The value of the part is absorbed by the whole.
"I don't like it."
Shirone glared at the video recorder in the corner.
It seemed to say he was just a slave to the system.
"Annoying country bumpkin!"
A fistfight broke out.
"You hit me first!"
As the conflict between schools escalated, the party turned into
chaos.
"Heh. Heh heh."
Fermi, leaning against the wall, grinned.
"It's a beautiful night."
Those who understand and use the system.
"The license is mine!"
"Shut up! I'm getting into the association!"
Those consumed by the system.
"Stop it."
Shirone clenched his fists.
"I said stop!"
Those who oppose the system.
Not knowing who offered the red and blue choices.
And the meta-system that rules over it.
'I have to stop this somehow.'
Lupist's intention was no longer in the minds of the fighters.
Shirone focused to enter the Spirit Zone but shook his head.
'It's no use.'
The magic control device disrupted specific brainwaves when humans
concentrated, making it impossible to enter the Spirit Zone despite being able to think.
Facing the technology that rendered mages like ordinary people, Shirone
widened his eyes and observed the world.
'Direct Perception!'
As the Ultima System activated, the information from the magic control
device was broken down into its smallest units.
'Wow…'
It felt like standing in the middle of an endlessly vibrating world of
information.
Following the real-time changing gaps, Shirone finally reached the focal
point.
"Everyone, close your eyes!"
A sphere of light compressed above Shirone's head, and his friends, who
had experienced it before, shut their eyes tightly.
'Shining Impact!'
With a flash, a massive white light exploded, erasing the scenery of the
annex as if it had returned to nothingness.
"Oh?"
As the light from all the monitors in the annex burst out, Lupist,
resting his chin on his hand, smirked.
"A Gaia user…"
Simultaneously, the monitors went dark, capturing the students' shocked
expressions.
"What… what is this?"
The students stopped fighting and turned to Shirone one by one.
"Stop it. What good will come from us fighting each other? We can
get the license with our own strength."
Rational words shamed the emotional students, who quickly rationalized
their feelings into anger.
"Who are you to tell us what to do? Do you think you're some noble
being?"
"Yeah! Do you think we're fighting over a mere license? It's a
chance to get into the association!"
Shirone spread his palm.
'Photon Cannon!'
As the photon with mass was born and vibrated fiercely, the students'
faces turned pale.
"H-how is he using magic?"
Confirming that the magic control device was still active, they were
shocked.
"Did he bypass it?"
Curtis, ranked first at the Royal Magic School, muttered in
disbelief.
They had tried to bypass it during the fight, but the idea of a single
student breaking through the association's security was unthinkable.
'Impossible! He must have cheated. Otherwise…'
The difference couldn't be this vast.
With the Photon Cannon floating, Shirone approached Curtis.
"Move."
"Ugh!"
Curtis felt a surge of anger but had no choice, being no different from
an ordinary person now.
As Curtis slowly stepped back, Shirone's gaze pierced the floor beneath
him.
'It's here.'
Through direct perception, Shirone sensed a magically relocated
box.
'Break through the security device set by the association.'
That was the minimum requirement to obtain the certified mage
license.
'This is so frustrating!'
Gritting his teeth, Shirone slammed the Photon Cannon into the floor,
shattering it with a loud crash.
"He really destroyed it with light."
It was the moment when the students saw the reality of the divine
particle they had only read about in magazines.
The space beneath the floor was empty, and Shirone reached in, opened
the box, and took out the license.
"Ah…"
The students looked at it with envy and desperate longing.
'I want it, the certified mage license.'
And it was a license that guaranteed a position at the Magic
Association.
'Lupist, this is my answer.'
Facing the video recorder, Shirone used Elysion to cast multiple light
explosions.
Pop! Pop! Pop!
As three small light explosions struck the object in the middle, the
license twisted and eventually shattered into pieces.
"What…!"
The students were stunned by the unexpected turn of events, while
Shirone glared at Lupist and scattered the fragments of the license into the air.
The video recorder watched impassively.
