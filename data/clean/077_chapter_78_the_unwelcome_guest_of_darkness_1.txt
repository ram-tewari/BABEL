Chapter 78:
The Unwelcome Guest of Darkness (1)
It was the last day of suspension. Shirone and his friends, who had studied at the inn
until dawn, woke up long after morning had passed. After teasing each other about their disheveled
appearances, they took baths.
By the time they returned the books to the library and entered the school, lunchtime was
over. The campus was quiet during class hours, with cleaners sweeping the stone paths.
"This is it, our last day of 'vacation.'"
"Vacation? Suspension is a vacation?"
"We still had fun, right? What's next?"
Shirone turned toward the dormitory.
"First, I'll clean my room. Classes start tomorrow, so I should tidy up today."
"You're so meticulous. Alright, we'll be at the research club. Come by if you're bored."
"Sure. See you later."
After parting with his friends, Shirone returned to the dormitory. His room was a mess
after neglecting it during the preparation period for the presentation. Rolling up his sleeves, he started
cleaning and finished by organizing his textbooks on the shelf.
An unexpected week-long suspension.
But he'd gained something from it—no, something far greater than what he'd learned at
school so far.
*'Finally, regular classes. I wish tomorrow would come sooner.'*
Falling behind was inevitable, but he planned to focus on classes, even if it meant
cutting back on research club activities.
*'Neide and Iruki must feel the same.'*
His friends' eyes had burned with academic fervor at the inn. They now had a clear goal:
advancing to Class Four before the semester ended.
At Alpheas Magic School, promotions were decided at the end of each semester based on
grades, with the cutoff being a staggering 80 points or above in every subject. Not an average of 80, but 80
or higher in every single subject—a terrifyingly high bar.
It wasn't impossible, but it was undeniably difficult for any student. Everyone had weak
subjects based on their aptitudes.
Yet the school's educational philosophy was firm:
Master all fields of knowledge to a certain level before specializing.
Thus, a graduate of the magic school was expected to be proficient in the basics of all
magic types. This was why graduates were highly regarded in magical society.
Not all current sorcerers were graduates. Some became apprentices to sorcerers, gained
enlightenment, or learned magic through dark paths.
Their advantage was abundant field experience and combat-suited magic. But compared to
graduates, their understanding of magic was lacking, and their growth potential was limited.
Hiring decisions depended on the job, but graduates undeniably had an edge. The school's
80-point standard reflected modern societal demands.
*'Haah. So what? In my case, I need to raise my average by 30 points.'*
No matter what insights Shirone had gained, this was still school. To advance, he needed
an all-around score of 80 or higher.
With his newfound framework, it wasn't impossible. Strengthening his knowledge from the
presentation and studying divine particles would boost his grades significantly.
But even considering that, his chances of promotion were roughly 30%.
*'No, that's amazing, isn't it? Advancing to Class Four in half a year with a 30%
chance?'*
Shirone thought positively. Wasn't that a challenge worth taking? If Neide and Iruki
advanced while he remained in Class Five… the thought alone was horrifying.
Moreover, entering Class Four would make him eligible to apply for the graduate class.
That way, he could keep his promise to Amy.
*'Gasp! Right! Amy!'*
He hadn't seen her since the presentation. Back then, he'd been too excited to even think
of looking for her.
*'Ah, I should've at least said hello. I was so thoughtless.'*
Amy had been a supportive friend during his toughest challenges. This time, he'd be the
one to support her. With that thought, a playful glint appeared in Shirone's eyes as he left the dormitory.
*'Hehe! She'll be surprised!'*
***
Shirone carried two cups of coffee to the graduate class building. One for Amy, the other
for Seriel. The bittersweet memory of running coffee errands for Amy brought a wry smile to his face.
*'Back then, I thought my world was ending.'*
What a strange connection it had been.
Who would've thought the delinquent girl he met in an alley at twelve would now be his
friend?
Shirone climbed the hill to the graduate building. The steel archway, nicknamed the Iron
Gate, stood imposingly at the entrance. From afar, he hadn't realized how massive it was.
*'This is the pinnacle of the magic school.'*
Passing under the structure symbolizing a sorcerer's spirit, Shirone's heart grew solemn.
When the sixth-period class ended, graduate students streamed out. Shirone spotted Amy
and Seriel chatting near the central entrance and called out.
"Amy! Amy!"
The graduates turned their heads, but Amy and Seriel, still deep in conversation, didn't
seem to hear. Playfully, Shirone sneaked up behind them and held out the coffee.
"Ta-da! A surprise!"
Amy's eyes widened in shock. Shirone grinned broadly and offered the other cup to Seriel.
"Here, this one's for you, Senpai. Drink up and stay strong!"
Seriel tilted her head. Amy blinked, then frowned.
"What? Who are you? Why are you giving us coffee?"
Shirone thought she was teasing him back.
"Sorry. Surprised? I didn't say anything to catch you off guard. How's graduate class?"
Amy's eyes turned wary. When Shirone tried to step closer, she hastily slapped his arm.
Both cups of coffee flew to the ground.
Shirone stared blankly. This wasn't teasing. Amy's face was flushed with genuine anger.
"Who do you think you are? And why are you using informal speech with me but honorifics
with Seriel?"
Seriel also looked displeased.
"Are you from the advanced class? No matter how interested you are, this isn't the way to
approach someone. And you should use honorifics with seniors. What's wrong with you? You're not some
delinquent."
Shirone was stunned. Amy and Seriel were acting as if they didn't know him.
"Seriel Senpai, it's me, Shirone. Don't you remember? Amy, you really don't know me?
You're joking, right?"
"Why would I know you? If you're a student, go study. Don't annoy people when and where
you shouldn't. Do I look like a pushover?"
Amy grabbed Shirone by the collar. She was genuinely irritated. She'd thought she was
done with pesky guys after entering the graduate class, but here was another one chasing her.
*'He was quiet for a while, and now this. It's all because of that guy. Hanging out with
weirdos and causing trouble…'*
As she muttered to herself, Amy paused.
*'Huh? That guy? Who?'*
She felt like there was someone connected to why the male students had backed off, but
she couldn't remember.
Shirone felt like his chest had been hollowed out. Amy's eyes held no familiarity.
"No… this can't be."
Shirone shook off her grip.
"No! Stop lying!"
"Ow."
Amy clutched her wrist, wincing. But Shirone, as if even that sight disgusted him,
yelled.
"Why are you pretending not to know me? What did I do wrong? If you hate me, just say so!
This is just cruel!"
"Hey! Get lost!"
Seriel shoved Shirone. Against her, he couldn't even resist and fell on his backside.
"Why are you bullying my friend? And a senior, no less! What class are you in? Do you
want to get in trouble?"
Shirone looked up at her with a face that had lost everything.
*'Seriel Senpai…'*
Amy's best friend. A top-ranked student with exceptional skills, yet one who cried over
romance novels. Shirone remembered. He remembered how she'd cheered him on during his toughest challenges.
But now, she was a completely different person. Her eyes burned with hostility, and her
clenched lips were full of resentment.
Shirone shook his head. Not because he couldn't believe it—because he didn't want to.
This couldn't be happening.
"No! This is a lie!"
Shirone scrambled to his feet and ran. If he stayed another second, he'd lose his mind.
"Don't come back! If you pull this stunt again, I'm reporting you to the teachers!"
Seriel cupped her hands around her mouth, still frowning as she turned to Amy.
"Ugh, what's wrong with him? He looks innocent but is so persistent. Hey, Amy, are you
okay?"
"Huh? Ah, I'm fine."
Lost in thought, Amy snapped back to reality. But an odd discomfort remained. Watching
Shirone run away, she felt an inexplicable sense of longing.
"Who was that? He acted like he really knew me."
"Probably just trying his luck. Guys like that are everywhere these days. Overestimating
themselves."
"You think?"
"Anyway, I'm heading in. Ugh, I wanted to chat with you, but that weirdo ruined our
break."
Even as classmates, their different majors made it hard to find time to talk.
As Seriel entered the classroom, Amy followed. But before stepping inside, she glanced
back at where Shirone had run.
"……"
She stood there until the bell rang.
***
*'Why are they doing this? What's the reason?'*
Shirone ran like a madman. Passing through the Iron Gate, he reached the advanced class
building as the bell rang and students filed into classrooms.
"Mark! Mark!"
Mark, who'd been chatting with Maria, turned his head. Shirone rushed over and grabbed
him.
"Mark! You know me, right? Who am I? Tell me!"
Mark glanced at Maria in confusion. Maria shrugged, equally clueless.
"Uh… who are you?"
Shirone's heart pounded wildly. He had no idea what was happening to him.
"It's me. Me."
"That's what I'm asking. You have to tell me for me to know."
"Me! Shirone! Class Five! We took exams together! You really don't know?"
Mark frowned. But hearing "Class Five," he couldn't argue and bowed politely.
"Ah, sorry. I didn't recognize you."
"That's not it! Why are you acting like this?"
What did he want him to do? Mark wanted to snap back, but with others watching, he
couldn't.
"Hey, class started. Why aren't you inside?"
Ms. Siena walked down the hallway. Shirone ran to her like a lifeline.
"Teacher! It's me, Shirone!"
Ms. Siena wouldn't forget him. She was strong. A certified 6th-rank sorcerer. But her
response was brutally cold.
"Shirone? There's no student by that name in the advanced class. Who are you?"
"Teacher, you don't know me? It's Shirone."
Ms. Siena searched her memory. But the name and face were unfamiliar.
"There must be some mistake. I haven't heard of any new students. Are you really from
this school?"
Mark and Maria's eyes turned sharp. Shirone, now suspicious, panicked.
"S-sorry! My mistake!"
"Hey! Wait!"
Ignoring Ms. Siena's voice, Shirone fled desperately. Now he understood. What was
happening.
Everyone had forgotten him.
"Hic… sob…"
He felt like a lost child. Weak and helpless. The world seemed terrifying.
Entering the East Building, Shirone got lost three times before reaching the Supernatural
Psychic Science Research Club.
*'My friends will remember. Of course they will. Why wouldn't they?'*
Hadn't they joked around just hours ago? Even if the whole world forgot, they should
remember.
Opening the door, he saw Neide and Iruki chatting. They looked surprised when they saw
him.
"Neide, Iruki, it's me. Shirone."
His friends blinked without answering. The dam holding back Shirone's emotions broke, and
his face twisted.
"Why aren't you saying anything? You remember me, right? You haven't forgotten?"
Neide glanced at Iruki, then scratched his head and approached Shirone.
"Uh… who are you?"
Tears welled in Shirone's eyes.
"You really don't know me? We were just together. We studied together yesterday and the
day before!"
"Sorry, but we don't know you. Are you spying for another club? Don't cause trouble and
just leave."
Iruki suddenly exclaimed.
"Wait! Maybe it's possible. How did you find your way here? Tell me! Who are you?"
"You… you guys…"
Shirone's lower lip trembled. He couldn't hold back his tears anymore. No, he didn't want
to.
"Pfft…"
Air escaped Neide's lips. Then he clutched his stomach and burst into laughter.
"Pffahaha! Did you see that? That expression! It's priceless!"
"I saw it! I saw it! 'You, you guys…' Pffahaha! Shirone, what's wrong with you?"
Neide laughed so hard he staggered, holding his aching stomach.
"Hehehe, hehehehe! Ah, the more I think about it, the funnier it gets! Why would you…
Kuhahaha!"
Shirone couldn't process the situation. He couldn't tell if this was reality or a dream.
"What? You remember me?"
"What nonsense is this? Did you have a nightmare? Do you know how ridiculous your face
looks right now? Pffahaha!"
Shirone's tension dissolved. The weight on his chest finally lifted.
"Ah, damn it! Then you should've said something! Why were you pretending not to know me?"
"Your face when you walked in was too funny. We had to mess with you. 'Me, it's me,
Shirone.'"
Neide mimicked Shirone, sending Iruki into another fit of laughter. Shirone, too
exhausted to scold them, slumped onto the floor and covered his face.
"Whew, I was scared to death."
"Sit down first. What's going on?"
Sitting on the couch, Shirone passionately recounted what had happened. At first, the two
chuckled in disbelief, but their expressions turned serious when Ms. Siena was mentioned.
"Hmm, even Ms. Siena didn't know you? She wouldn't play such a prank."
Neide couldn't believe it.
"Is that even possible?"
"If it's mental magic, yes. Though manipulating the memories of the entire school is
questionable."
"It's possible. I experienced it firsthand."
