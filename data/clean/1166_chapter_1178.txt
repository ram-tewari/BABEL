Chapter 1178:
The first section of the outer track is the only village that newcomers
to Melkidu cannot enter.
Village No. 1: Vital.
The reason is simple—it prohibits attacks between users and does not
contain any missions.
On the contrary, it is filled with various convenience facilities,
making it a common place for users to stay.
"We finally did it."
Seated in a restaurant, Shirone and his companions spread out a
hand-drawn map and began their discussion.
"Crime Points: 100 million."
At Nade's words, memories of their hardships flashed through everyone's
minds.
Eden let out a sigh.
"That was rough. Since we aimed for the special route, we had to
clear missions only through 'Honorable Crimes.'"
The 'Honorable Crimes' privilege would be revoked if they committed even
a single ordinary murder.
Iruki spoke up.
"Completing seven full laps around the outer track and reaching 100
million Crime Points is a perfect strategy. Some missions rewarded as much as 10 million points for a
single bomb. But the real challenge starts now."
The map they had created meticulously detailed the outer track of
Melkidu.
"A total of 468 sections. We've personally explored and documented
207 of them. At this rate, accumulating more points efficiently is impossible."
Some missions, for instance, required rolling a die to chip away at an
enemy's health.
However, since taking damage deducted an overwhelming number of points,
swift elimination was key.
"The Lord of Depravity... That was the only mission where we
actually lost points. We need better items and stronger dice. It's time to move to the inner
track."
Shirone made his decision.
"The price to enter Melkidu from the inner track is 10 billion
points. If we keep going at this pace, we'll get there eventually, but we don't have that kind of
time."
The best course of action was to strengthen their dice in the inner
track and rake in points at an accelerated rate.
Nade nodded.
"For now, let's double our 100 million points first. I saw a casino
on the way here."
Gambling was available in other villages, but in Vital, the casino was
run by the system itself.
"At least we don't have to worry about getting involved with other
users. But we can only bet once per crossing of the starting point, and there are strict limits on
betting amounts—1 to 99 points, 100 to 999 points."
This was precisely why they had come to the first section after
accumulating 100 million points.
"Gathering 10 billion would be impossible at this rate. Even
winning just 100 million points in one go would be huge."
Eden hesitated.
"But what if we lose? We'd be throwing away almost
everything."
Nade burst into laughter.
"Haha! But we have Shirone with us. He never loses at gambling.
Unless we're up against Satan himself."
A sudden silence fell over the group.
"Melkidu is Satan's..."
"Hahaha!"
Nade forced out a laugh and stood up.
"A-Alright! Let's put unnecessary thoughts aside and get going.
We're about to make 100 million points!"
The others sighed but followed suit.
Feeling somewhat uneasy, Shirone and his companions left the restaurant
and headed toward the casino in the center of the village.
There were about ten betting booths, with long lines of people waiting
for their turn.
'We never saw this many people in other villages. As expected of
Vital.'
Just then, a man emerged from the first booth.
"Jackpot!"
Judging by his reaction, he must have won, and envious gazes followed
him.
"How much do you think he made?"
Nade wondered aloud, and Iruki replied.
"Probably not a huge amount. Even with a 50% chance, this is a
life-or-death gamble. No one would bet as much as we plan to. Unless they're point tycoons."
The line for the sixth booth was the shortest, while the seventh had the
longest queue.
"So superstition runs rampant in places like this. Which booth
should we choose?"
There was no need to ask.
"Let's go with number six."
Shortly after, the door of booth six opened, and a woman walked out with
tear-filled eyes.
"Hic, hic..."
Her shoulders trembled as she walked before she suddenly lost strength
in her legs and collapsed.
"Aaaaah! What do I do?!"
She must have bet a significant amount, but no one spared her a
sympathetic glance.
When Shirone's group entered the booth, the space shifted as if they had
completed a mission.
"Welcome to the Devil's Casino. Please set your betting
amount."
Shirone exchanged glances with his friends and then spoke.
"100 million points."
"-Points deducted. Remaining balance: 6,878,300
points."
Shirone caught his Crime Dice—two six-sided dice—as they fell.
"-The side with the higher total wins."
Since the system also used identical dice, upgrading them offered no
advantage.
"Here goes."
Holding their breath, the group watched the dice tumble onto the
floor.
6 and 6.
"-Double. You have one more roll."
Without hesitation, Shirone rolled again.
The result was the same.
"Whew, that was nerve-wracking."
Iruki remained cautious.
"We can't relax yet. The system could roll the same, leading to a
tie."
"Come on, the odds of three consecutive sixes..."
Before Nade could finish, the system rolled its dice, and two sixes
appeared.
Nade's eyes widened.
"What the hell?"
If another 6 and 6 came up, the total would be 24—tying with
Shirone.
"What happens if it's a tie?"
A system message appeared.
"-A rematch will be held, and the double bonus will reset. However,
if there are accomplices, the participants will change."
A chill ran down their spines.
Had this truly been a mere 50% gamble, they wouldn't have dared to bet
100 million points in the first place.
'Please, please... I'm the one rolling next.'
With his heart pounding, Nade watched as the two dice glowed.
One landed on 6. The other on 4.
"-24 to 20. Congratulations. You have won the game. Your bet has
been doubled."
Nade nearly collapsed.
"Haaah, how many times did a 6 come up?! My stomach's churning from
the stress."
Shirone chuckled.
"For an ordinary person, this would have been terrible luck. But
for us? This was practically free money."
-As a victory reward, 100 million points have been credited. Your
current balance is 200,687,830 points.
"Alright, only 9.7 billion more to go."
Leaving the casual joke behind, they stepped out of the sixth booth,
feeling the weight of the onlookers' stares.
'No good will come from standing out.'
While duels between users were not forbidden, encounters could happen
anywhere.
"You guys."
As they turned around, the woman who had gambled in booth six was
following them.
"Did you win?" She shrugged her shoulders.
"Don't be so wary. I'm broke now, so it's not like I can do
anything. You could at least tell me whether you won or lost, right?"
Shirone replied, "We won. Why do you ask?"
"Hmm, well…"
The woman suddenly fidgeted.
"I'm really out of points, so... could you buy me just one thing
from the shop that I want…?"
"Let's go."
Nade bluntly pushed his friends forward, causing the woman's eyes to
widen in anger.
"You bastards! Do you even know who I am?!"
"And who would that be? The 'Point-Leeching Ghost'
Penna."
At the voice from behind, Penna's eyes went wide, and Shirone turned his
head.
"Huh?"
It was Curtis, the man who had helped them when they first arrived in
Melkidu. He raised his hand in greeting.
"Long time no see."
Since this place was somewhat of an underworld, time flowed differently
from reality.
"Curtis? What are you doing here?"
"The God of Dice led me here… Just kidding. I was waiting for you.
I figured you'd stop by the casino at some point."
He was right.
Penna scoffed.
"Tch, what? You guys know each other? If you're hanging out with
Curtis, you must be just as worthless."
Curtis grinned.
"You might want to rethink that. These guys are professional mages
in the real world."
"Oh?"
While Penna re-evaluated the group, Shirone turned to Curtis.
"You two know each other?"
"Not exactly… just a familiar face. She's not particularly strong
or weak, just someone who circles the outer track."
"Don't lump me in with you! I'm already pissed off from losing at
gambling!"
"Haha. And just so you know, don't believe a word this woman says.
She didn't get the nickname 'Point-Leeching Ghost' for nothing. Even if she lost, I bet she still has
her original stake."
Penna frowned.
'What the hell?'
More than having her identity exposed, she found it strange how freely
Curtis was giving away information.
'Why is he talking so openly? Does he believe these guys won't ever lose
out?'
Curtis asked, "How many points do you have right now?"
As expected, no one answered.
"Heh, quite the experienced players. Then, how many laps have you
done around the outer track?"
"We can't say. That would let you estimate our points."
"Exactly why I'm asking. There's no such thing as a perfect
alliance, but we trust each other enough to exchange information, don't we?"
"Are you saying the information you have is worth the risk of
revealing our points?"
"Possibly. What I know is a hidden method to enter
Melkidu."
Shirone exchanged glances with his friends.
"What do you think? Drinks are on you guys," Curtis said,
pointing to a restaurant.
"I-I'll come too! Huh? I can, right? I won't eavesdrop or anything.
I'm just starving."
'Now I see why she's called the Point-Leeching Ghost.' Curtis
chuckled.
"Let her come. She's got 27 fraud convictions in the real world,
but she's a reliable friend in her own way."
The contradiction in his words was obvious, but if he was insisting on
bringing her along, there must be a reason.
"Alright. Let's hear it."
As soon as they took their seats, Penna opened the menu and started
ordering various dishes.
"And a beer, please. Oh, and the lunch bread is free,
right?"
From the buyer's perspective, she was annoying, but at least she didn't
order anything too expensive.
"This should be fine, right? I'll take some leftovers with me
too."
"Sure."
Since they had just won 100 million points, they were in a good enough
mood to treat her to a meal.
Curtis returned to the conversation. "How many laps have you
done?"
"Seven."
"That's fast. If you've been sticking to the Honorable Crimes
route, you should have around 50 million points, give or take."
Curtis hadn't expected Shirone's group to be so efficient.
"Well…"
"Alright. Not denying it means you've got at least 50 million. In
that case, there's a possibility."
Penna reached out her hand.
"Hold on. We're eating. Should I even be listening to
this?"
"It doesn't matter. Knowing won't change anything. Plus, the number
of people involved is important."
Curtis took a drag from his cigarette.
"You can either gather 10 billion points, get extremely lucky, or…
there's another way. I have to go bankrupt."
Eden frowned.
"But if your points hit zero, the guards will come after you.
You'll be thrown into prison."
"That's the key. Once you serve your sentence, you restart from the
starting point. But there's a way to avoid returning to the start."
Shirone caught on.
"Bribery."
That, too, was a crime.
"Exactly. Break me out of jail. In this place, where all crimes are
absolved, you'll become criminals once again."
"What happens when we become criminals?"
Curtis glanced out the window, exhaling smoke.
"The backdoor opens."
