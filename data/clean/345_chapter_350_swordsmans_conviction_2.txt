Chapter 350:
Swordsmans Conviction (2)
Clump, seated at the head of the table, had his arms crossed and wore a
serious expression. Next to him sat Kuan, who had come from the swordsmanship school at Clump's
summons.
Rian, standing before them, kept his back straight and met Clump's gaze
without wavering before speaking.
"This is my decision. It's strange to ask for permission for
something I've decided, but please accept it."
Clump let out a deep sigh.
The blue-haired lineage was known for being free-spirited and
hot-tempered. He roughly understood how much Rian had struggled and could empathize with his frustration
at not being able to use Schema. But it had only been a year. It was too early to judge the success or
failure of his studies.
"...Are you running away? What do you plan to do after dropping out
of school? Are you thinking of returning to the family for personal training?"
"No. I'm going on a knight's quest."
Clump's jaw tightened, causing his beard to twitch. A mix of anger and
exhilaration surged within him. How could Rian think so much like he did when he was young?
Even so, he had to oppose it.
When Clump was young, many embarked on knightly quests. The world was in
turmoil, and unifying it with the sword wasn't just a dream.
But times had changed.
The sword was a tool for killing. While real combat was the best
training, it was a double-edged sword that could end one's life with a single mistake.
"I won't say you're too young. You're almost nineteen. But can you
truly say there's nothing left to learn at school? Is the school really that insignificant to
you?"
"I've never thought of it that way. I just want to become
stronger."
This time, Clump also showed his anger.
Being pleased that Rian resembled him was one thing, but following the
same path as his predecessors was another. Above all, he couldn't push his beloved grandson into
danger.
"You want to leave on a quest to become stronger? Do you think you
can get away with such childishness?"
"It's not childishness! I'm serious about risking my
life..."
Clump was firm.
"No means no. You can experience real combat when the time comes.
First, you need to build enough skill to face the world."
"No. I've already submitted my withdrawal. The decision is
made."
"I canceled it. This old man, nearing seventy, begged your junior
to have it rescinded."
"Grandfather!"
Rian raised his voice.
It wasn't just about the withdrawal being rescinded; it was frustrating
that Clump had to plead with someone on his behalf.
Just as Rian was about to speak out of his burning emotions, there was a
knock at the door.
Clump frowned, annoyed.
"What is it? We're in the middle of a conversation. Come back
later."
"Sir, Shirone is here to see you."
Rian turned to the door in surprise. His heart suddenly began to
race.
His closest friend and the lord to whom he had sworn fealty. For
whatever reason, he was the one person Rian never wanted to show this side of himself.
Clump also knew what Shirone meant to Rian. After a moment of thought,
he made a decision.
"Let him in."
Rian's stubborn personality wouldn't be swayed by family, but Shirone
might be able to change the situation.
The door opened, and Shirone entered. Tess peeked in to gauge the room's
atmosphere, but Louis quickly closed the door, giving her no chance.
Rian instinctively avoided Shirone's gaze. But Shirone, unfazed, first
greeted Clump.
"Hello, sir."
"Yes. Reina told me. You've been through a lot."
He already knew what Shirone had gone through in Kazra. Shirone's aura
had become noticeably stronger after his trials.
But now wasn't the time to focus on Shirone's changes. Shirone also knew
why Clump had called him and didn't beat around the bush.
"Rian dropped out of school."
Clump exaggeratedly scratched his head in frustration.
"Yes. Try to persuade him. He's being so stubborn."
"I support his decision."
Clump's hand stopped scratching. Even Kuan, who didn't know Shirone,
looked surprised.
"Support? Are you saying it's okay for Rian to drop
out?"
"Yes. I don't know the details or the reasons, but if Rian decided
that, I believe it's the right path."
Clump tried to maintain his composure.
He had hoped Shirone's arrival would be a turning point, but if Shirone
was going to defend Rian, it would be even more troublesome. He would be a very difficult
advocate.
"Alright. As Rian's lord, your opinion is worth considering. But if
you think that way, you must have a valid reason."
Shirone turned to Rian. Seeing Rian still unable to meet his gaze,
Shirone continued, looking at Clump.
"There is no valid reason."
"What?"
"I don't know which choice is more efficient for Rian. But I can
guarantee one thing."
Shirone spoke with conviction.
"The Rian I know never runs away from anything."
Rian clenched his fists. Those words gave him more confidence than a
thousand others.
"It might be a foolish decision. But it's not an attempt to avoid
anything. As his friend, I believe supporting him is the right thing to do."
Clump couldn't help but laugh, feeling a mix of frustration and
admiration.
It was a beautiful friendship. And it's human nature to want to protect
beautiful things.
'Clever boy. He already knows my weak spot.'
Dealing with the family head Bishoff was different. Clump, a man of
honor, couldn't firmly oppose such an argument.
But as Shirone already knew, Clump wasn't someone who acted solely on
emotions.
"I understand your feelings. And your argument is persuasive. But
Shirone, that's just your and Rian's perspective. You two don't know the world yet. I've walked the path
of a swordsman for decades. Do you think it's just about school? Not at all. One day, this dropout will
come back to haunt Rian."
Kuan raised his hand.
"I have something to say."
Everyone turned their attention to Kuan.
Shirone had never seen Kuan before, but he could tell from his aura that
he was no ordinary person.
"I didn't come here just for a teacher-student meeting. I needed to
deliver this personally."
Kuan took an envelope from his inner pocket.
It was Rian's withdrawal letter.
"I respect and want to help you, sir, but I can't allow the
withdrawal to be rescinded. Teaching a student who has already given up on school goes against my
educational philosophy."
"Ugh."
For Clump, it was one obstacle after another.
Kuan's stubbornness was well-known at the swordsmanship school. It
didn't matter if his opponent was a certified third-grade swordsman general.
"Please, just this once. Can't you let it slide?"
'Grandfather...'
Rian felt suffocated. Seeing his grandfather plead with a junior for his
sake made him feel his grandfather's love.
But Kuan was not an easy opponent.
"As a teacher, it's an irresponsible opinion, but Rian has no
future at the school. There are technical aspects he can learn, but the main evaluation criteria are
Schema. There's a growing opinion that brain enhancement falls under Schema, but it's not something the
school can teach. As you know, education and practice are worlds apart. And practice and real combat are
entirely different realms. Winning a wrestling match among cadets doesn't count as a score."
Kuan's words didn't sound particularly unpleasant. He was simply giving
his honest opinion as someone who had closely observed Rian.
The room fell silent. No one spoke.
Then, the head butler, Louis, announced from outside the door.
"Sir, the second young master has arrived."
Rian's face twisted in anger.
Ozent Rai. Of all times, his lifelong rival had to come home now. He was
someone Rian didn't want to see, just like Shirone, but for different reasons.
As Rai entered confidently, Shirone quickly stepped aside. He had only
seen Rai once when he worked as the Ozent family's librarian, but his impression was vivid.
Rai had black hair and a cold face. His demeanor was still icy, but his
presence had grown significantly stronger.
When Reina followed him in, even Louis couldn't close the door this
time.
Reina, glaring at Rai, shouted,
"Hey! Why are you so busy that you don't even show your face? Do
you know it's been a month since you came home?"
Even the cold Rai couldn't help but frown at his sister's
nagging.
"I was busy. I couldn't help it. I'm here to pack for a deployment
soon. I'm hungry, so make me some food."
Reina, fuming, glared at the back of Rai's head before whispering to
Louis to prepare a meal. After all, her brother had come home after a long time, so she wanted to send
him off with a warm meal.
Rai approached Clump and bowed formally. Now that he was also a soldier,
he couldn't be casual even at home.
"Grandfather."
"Hmm, you seem to be doing well. Ah, introduce yourself. This is
Kuan, a teacher at the swordsmanship school."
Rai's eyes changed.
'Kuan? The Death Magician Kuan?'
After passing the certification exam and joining the military, he had
heard stories. Although unconfirmed, the only swordsman who had survived a battle with Feng Zhang was
said to be Kuan.
Rai turned to Kuan and bowed.
"Hello. I'm Ozent Rai."
"Yes, I've heard about you. You have a good
disposition."
Kuan received the greeting as politely as possible, showing a different
attitude compared to when he dealt with Rian.
'So this is the genius swordsman of the Ozent family.'
Last year, he had achieved the 10th rank certification, and now he was
already an 8th rank sergeant. His aura was cold and sharp, reminiscent of Kuan's younger days.
Rai stood at attention, allowing Kuan to fully assess him, then turned
back to Clump and asked,
"But why is a high-ranking official from the swordsmanship school
here?"
Reina, crossing her arms, replied,
"Rian dropped out of school. Grandfather called him to try to
change his mind."
"Dropped out of school?"
Rai turned to Rian to confirm, and Rian glared back. He didn't want to
lose even a staring contest with Rai, unlike with Shirone.
But Rai just scoffed and said indifferently,
"What's the big deal? If he wants to quit, let him
quit."
Reina clicked her tongue in disapproval.
"How can you be so indifferent? Aren't you worried about your
brother's future?"
Rai walked past Rian towards the door.
"He was never going to make it. It's better to give up now. Hurry
up and get me some food. I'm hungry."
As Rian followed Rai with his eyes, he spoke up.
"Wait a minute."
When Rai turned around, Rian pointed a finger at his forehead.
"You didn't go to school either."
"That's different. I don't need to, and for you, it's a waste of
time."
Rian's teeth ground together in frustration.
