Another Belief (4)
The Duke's Laboratory
Lethe meticulously examined her surroundings, maintaining an awkward
silence.
There were no traces to be found.
"Mercede, it's been a while. How have you been?"
Hiding her intense nervousness, Mercede knelt on both knees.
"Hello, ma'am." The fact that she greeted Lethe instead of the
duke was an unusual start.
"Duke."
Lethe's gaze turned cold again.
"What's the reason?"
"What do you mean? If you cut off the context like that, even I
can't understand."
"Why are you helping Yahweh?" They could deny it, but a more
embarrassing situation wasn't their style.
Lethe spoke.
"There is an error in the purification system. A human broke the
chains of Gehenna. I sent the chief secretary to investigate, but he got sidetracked, so I came
myself."
Marguerite stroked his beard.
'So that's what happened. If Monoras had arrived first, I would have
naturally sided with the Fire Management Company.'
It wasn't a matter of first come, first served.
'The concept of coincidence is merely human logic. This situation is
just the result of some cause that started 500 million, no, a billion years ago.'
The duke originally had two choices.
'To uphold Lethe's will and maintain hell, or to uphold Satan's will and
reverse the situation.'
Shirone arrived first.
'The breaking of Gehenna's chains is an error to Lethe, but an
opportunity to Satan.'
If Jin Seong-eum's karma could be purified, the spiritual realm would
reopen.
"Lady Lethe."
Marguerite chose war.
"The breaking of the karma chain means human will has denied hell.
It's a matter of persistence, not resolution. The era is coming to an end. Can't we watch as Satan
persists?"
If one assumes the unspoken premise as fact, the duke's words make
sense.
"Since when has hell accepted Yahweh? Or rather, why are they so
obsessed with them?"
"There were those who couldn't end their hatred even for Lucifer or
Behemoth. The duke is the same. I can't understand it."
"Because you're not a demon."
Lethe was a manager.
"When I was first born in hell, I was a demon with the highest
abilities among the intelligent types. I fought, conquered, and researched without envy."
Marguerite recalled those times.
"I thought it was a wonderful place. A world where those who proved
their strength took everything. To me, philanthropy was an illogical and even violent
concept."
Lethe listened silently.
"Then why do we hate? Because we are the illegitimate children of
philanthropy. It's a systemic aspect. Before demons are born, human emotions must exist. It's a fate
that arises from the priority of process handling."
As they entered the realm of management, Lethe's eyes became
mechanically indifferent.
"That's fine up to that point. The problem is that Yahweh denies
it. He is a being that reverses the process. A terrorist of the system. In layman's terms, he denies
what he has done, even claiming he has done nothing wrong. That shamelessness is
infuriating."
Marguerite gave a bitter smile.
"Even if they can't analyze like the intelligent types, demons have
that feeling from birth. But sometimes they repent. Why is that?"
Lethe didn't answer.
"Because they are shameless. That brazen confidence without a hint
of remorse is so disgusting, hateful, and makes you want to tear them apart… But also so shameless
that…"
"It makes you wonder if it's really true."
"Is Yahweh really not at fault? Are we misunderstanding? If they
didn't abandon us, then maybe demons too…"
Marguerite pointed to the ceiling.
"By reversing the process of this system, can't we reach the true
world?"
They call it 'salvation' with feelings of disgust.
Oris was bewildered.
'That child defeated the intruder? He was a deserter who ran away in
fear.'
Ishak introduced him.
"This is Yupi. He inherits Satan's spirit, a royal
bloodline."
The expressions of the Seven Generals changed drastically.
Royal bloodlines could rise to the rank of army commander or duke in
hell's army.
Itaka looked at Oris.
"Do you know him?"
"Uh? Well…"
Shirone quickly answered in her place.
"I received help from her earlier. She taught me the true path of a
demon when I was running away in fear."
"Well, it wasn't that much."
As Oris scratched her nose with her cat claws, Moonom squinted his one
eye.
"So, what do you want? A reward for defeating the
intruder?"
Ishak bowed his head.
"I want to recommend Lord Yupi as the captain of the Special Task
Force. Please let him lead us."
"Nonsense!"
Moonom smashed the table.
"Croseide was the strongest warrior. Do you think that weakling can
replace him?"
"Lord Moonom."
Ishak glared.
"You can belittle me all you want. But please watch your words with
Lord Yupi."
Though it was a natural thing for a demon to say, Moonom was still
displeased.
"Hmph! That's only if he really becomes the captain. Do you think
Yupi is as strong as the Seven Generals?"
Ishak remained silent.
Though a royal bloodline would eventually rise to the top, he lacked
experience compared to the Seven Generals.
"I think the same."
Itaka spoke with his fingers interlaced.
"I acknowledge Yupi's achievement, but the defeated enemy was an
illusion. There's only one real one."
'Huh?'
Shirone looked back in surprise.
'Execution Commander Itaka.'
His earlier words proved he was the only royal bloodline among the Seven
Generals.
'He defined it as an illusion, not a clone. His precision in detection
matches my senses.'
Though it was limited to senses.
"Let's do this. There are illusions spread throughout the city.
Among them, there's one real one. I'll recommend a medal of honor to the first one who kills
it."
Itaka looked at Shirone.
"And if Yupi captures the real one, I'll recommend him for the
captain position in addition to the medal."
"Heh, that sounds good."
Moonom readily agreed.
"Good for you, kid. You wouldn't have stood a chance in a direct
duel with us, but this involves luck too."
Itaka's voice remained cold.
"Competition is fine, but don't overdo it. If it hinders the
mission, I'll discipline you. If necessary, consider cooperating as four."
"Six, actually."
The Seven Generals fell silent simultaneously.
"There are two more in the shadows over there. They have a very
quiet presence."
As soon as Shirone finished speaking, a voice came from behind
Itaka.
"He's got good instincts."
It was likely Night Guard Captain Ilgak and Baeksa, but they still
didn't reveal themselves.
"But we're out of the bet. Don't worry too much. If it looks like
you'll die, we'll help."
Their presence vanished.
"Is it a full mobilization after a long time? With Croseide gone,
the medal of honor will be mine."
Moonom prepared to deploy, but Shirone was the first to leave the
tent.
"Let's go, Ishak."
The Seven Generals were stunned, and Ishak, following Shirone, was
equally surprised.
'His demeanor has changed.'
Compared to when he was branded a deserter, he now exuded a strong
will.
'Well, as a royal bloodline, it's natural for him to be passionate about
competition.'
Though the interpretation was wrong, Shirone was indeed taking this
seriously.
'I have to find Son Yu-jung.'
The strength of the Seven Generals he had felt was not weak, but it was
still overwhelming.
'If it's Son Yu-jung, it won't take her three days to devastate
Laviette.'
It would also disrupt Marguerite's plans.
"I'll skip the fakes and find the real one. You probably can't keep
up, so gather the troops."
Before he could hear a response, Shirone's body turned into a flash and
shot into the sky.
"Lord Royal…"
Ishak trembled with emotion.
As the Seven Generals were fully deployed, Son Yu-jung's clones began to
decrease rapidly.
"Hahaha! What? They're nothing!"
Moonom's body, undergoing continuous biological evolution, was becoming
increasingly grotesque.
"Hmph!"
As he shot spikes from his body, Son Yu-jung's clones within a 30-meter
radius were impaled.
"Screech! Screech!"
The clones, writhing and screaming, soon turned into light.
"Tch! All duds?"
Moonom's face twisted.
'Even if they're fakes, they're too weak. Did Croseide really fall to
these?'
Though demons had no special camaraderie, they recognized and respected
each other.
"Unforgivable."
As his toothless jaws ground together, a loud noise erupted from the
other side of the building.
Sensing the abnormal impact, he turned to see a seven-story building
collapsing.
"There?"
"Haaah!"
Oris, with her claws dug into the ground, bared her teeth,
bleeding.
In her eyes, Son Yu-jung, burning with golden light, was slowly
approaching.
'She's really strong.'
Her combat abilities were all top-notch, to the point where it was hard
to say what was strongest.
Ricera laughed loudly.
"Hahaha! Serves you right, you wretched demons. Lady Son Yu-jung!
Quickly tear them apart."
Despite the human's mockery, Oris remained silent.
'The one carrying that human is the real one. She's on a different level
from the fakes. If left alone, the city will be destroyed.'
Determined to die with the enemy, Oris braced herself, her fur standing
on end.
"Huh?"
As Son Yu-jung tilted her head, a black shadow charged at terrifying
speed.
"Meow!"
The time it took for the body to reach was less than half a blink of an
eye.
"Too slow."
Son Yu-jung had already spun around and was swinging her weapon at the
back of Oris's head.
'Damn it.'
As she lost her will to fight and resigned herself, letting go of all
strength in her body…
"Huh?"
In Oris's cat-like eyes, the image of a red demon appeared.
Without time to react, Shirone pushed Oris aside and lunged at Son
Yu-jung.
'If this is the real one…'
Ordinary force wouldn't even scratch her.
'I'll hit her directly with my true power.'
As Miracle Stream compressed into a Photon Cannon, it struck Son
Yu-jung's abdomen directly.
"Ugh!"
Hit by an irresistible force, she screamed and flew out of sight.
Watching from where she had fallen against the wall, Oris slowly turned
her head.
"Yupi?"
The young demon extended a hand.
"Are you okay?"
