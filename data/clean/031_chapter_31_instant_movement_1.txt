Instant Movement (1)
During class, Shironé was so focused on the teacher's face that it felt like he was
drilling holes into it, making his current demeanor unfamiliar.
"Is school life difficult for you?"
"No, it's fun. I enjoy studying."
"Why didn't you say anything? I heard the kids were bullying you."
Shironé had already guessed as much. In fact, it would have been strange if he hadn't
known. Lately, the children had been openly tormenting him.
"It's okay. You don't need to worry."
Siena, who had heard detailed accounts of the children's misbehavior from Maria, didn't
believe Shironé's words.
"You can be honest about these things. There's no shame in it."
"I'm really fine. It's disappointing that I haven't made friends, but if they dislike me,
there's nothing I can do about it."
"Excluding a student is a bad thing."
"You can't force feelings. Of course, it was hard at first, but I'm okay now. If the kids
in Class Seven dislike me, so be it. It doesn't mean the whole world hates me."
Siena understood. For some, the group they belonged to was their entire world, but for
those who looked beyond, such groups were merely temporary waypoints.
"I want to meet more people. I believe there are people out there who will take my story
seriously."
Shironé was clear about his stance on early advancement. He believed that in Class Five
or above, he would find friends who would accept him without any sense of inferiority.
"Shironé, I've heard your resolve clearly. I'll take it into consideration at the faculty
meeting."
***
"With that, I conclude my remarks on Shironé's early advancement."
In a meeting attended by Principal Alpheus and senior class teachers, Siena submitted
documents related to the Class Seven bullying incident and took her seat.
The teachers frowned as they reviewed the contents. The details revealed how persistently
Shironé had been excluded, with the pretext that he couldn't use magic.
"Does this make any sense? Bullying a classmate for such a trivial reason?"
"Such things are common in lower classes. There are many young children, and some
students stay behind despite their age due to lack of talent. The classes themselves lack distinction."
"Then it would be best to advance Shironé quickly. He could graduate within four years.
With neighboring schools challenging Alpheas Magic School, the more early graduates, the better."
The age of graduates was one of the metrics proving a school's excellence. Even if they
didn't excel in society, the reputation would suffer, but rumors were circulating that other schools were
competitively producing early graduates.
It was a standard tactic to elevate a school's prestige. Once the prestigious title was
secured, talented individuals would flock, and only then could the quality of education be improved.
In the face of fierce competition from other schools, Alpheas Magic School's strategy was
to produce not only early graduates but also qualitatively outstanding students. In that sense, the teachers
had high expectations for Shironé.
"I'm somewhat opposed."
Sad raised his hand and cast a dissenting vote. Siena's expression twisted. It was
infuriating to hear objections when early advancement was practically a done deal. But Alpheus showed
interest in the first opposing opinion.
"You're opposed. Why is that?"
"I know Shironé is talented. But the core of this incident is that Shironé can't use
magic. The children's backlash stems from that. Shouldn't we address that clearly?"
Siena responded, "I've already mentioned this. Shironé has been learning general
knowledge and hasn't had the chance to focus on a single discipline. Personally, I'm confident this is the
optimal path for his growth."
"I'm not questioning the method. The problem is that you're favoring Shironé too much,
Teacher Siena."
"Excuse me?"
"Regardless, the kids in Class Seven are also studying hard after entering the school.
Whether Shironé had time to acquire knowledge or not, that should also be part of the evaluation criteria.
Otherwise, there's no reason not to advance other students early, even if their Spirit Zone is weaker than
Shironé's, since they're theoretically superior."
"Don't spout nonsense! I didn't judge this matter emotionally. My conclusion is based
strictly on school rules. The main evaluation criterion for lower classes is clearly the Spirit Zone."
"The problem is the students' backlash. This incident could demotivate them. Teachers
aren't here to pour everything into one genius, are they?"
"It's not a teacher's job to neglect a talented student because of the children's
misplaced jealousy!"
The two simultaneously slammed the table and stood up. It was a familiar spectacle
whenever there was a meeting.
