Chapter 261:
The Revealed Truth (6)
The students unconsciously took a step back.
The voice of the elderly man, who seemed like he could enter a coffin
tomorrow, was louder than a young man's shout.
However, once they grasped the meaning behind the old man's words, the
shock was even greater than his volume.
The Kingdom of Kazra was a nation located north of the Kingdom of
Thormia.
What business could an official emissary, who carries the king's orders,
have at a provincial academy instead of the Royal Magic Academy?
The students' confusion was soon cleared up by the next words of
Ordos.
"Is Arian Shirone present in this place? If so, come forward and
heed the command of the king!"
The students, acting as though this was a grave duty, started searching
for Shirone.
But Shirone was nowhere to be seen.
Once the initial shock settled, a strange thought occurred. Why was an
emissary from the Kingdom of Kazra looking for Shirone?
Alpheas turned to the student next to him and said.
"Could you go and fetch Shirone? It seems like an urgent
matter."
"Huh? Ah, yes!"
The student hurriedly darted off to search for Shirone.
Of course, he had no idea where Shirone could be, and finding him in
this vast campus seemed almost impossible. But in the rush of the moment, his legs moved before he could
think.
"Arian Shirone! Is Shirone not here? This is the king's command!
Come quickly and receive the royal decree!"
Ordos shouted as if deeply irritated.
Naturally, being an emissary of the Kingdom of Kazra, charged with
executing the king's orders, meant he held a position beyond reproach by anyone present.
No matter how renowned Alpheas was in the magic society, his authority
was granted by the state. No organization could outrank the state itself.
However, since it was as if the king of a foreign country had personally
stepped forward, the students of the noble families who had read the treatise on imperialism over and
over again were unable to make a sound.
"Shirone! Why are you not coming forward quickly!"
"I've brought Shirone."
A beautiful voice cut through Ordos's harsh shout.
Olivia, the current principal of Alpheas Magic Academy, walking hand in
hand with Shirone.
Since Shirone always visited the Supernatural Psychic Science Research
Group after classes, finding him hadn't been difficult.
Iruki and Neid followed closely behind.
Shirone, wearing a bewildered expression, scanned his
surroundings.
Nearly the entire academy had gathered, and standing at the forefront
were formidable-looking bodyguards and a large carriage.
On the way, Olivia had only mentioned that someone was looking for
him.
"Shirone, go on. That man is an emissary from the Kingdom of
Kazra."
"Huh? An emissary?"
Shirone, finally realizing what was happening, looked up at Olivia in
shock.
While Shirone had come to trust Olivia, especially after she had helped
when Killain caused trouble, hearing that a royal emissary was involved made him anxious.
Olivia gently patted his back, reassuring him.
"It's alright. You'll find out once you go."
Trusting Olivia, Shirone took a deep breath and walked toward
Ordos.
Ordos glared at Shirone with hollow eyes.
Though his eyes were hollow, it felt as though his brain was being
penetrated. Shirone had the eerie feeling that he couldn't tell a lie. As soon as he had that thought,
Ordos asked in his characteristic raspy voice.
"Are you Arian Shirone?"
"Yes, I am."
"Lying in front of me is to insult the Kingdom of Kazra! I will ask
again! Are you certain that you are Arian Shirone?"
"Yes! I'm certain!"
Shirone found himself shouting without realizing it.
He didn't understand why Ordos had to shout at such close range, but the
impact was undeniable.
As his mind turned blank, he responded automatically as if he was under
a trance.
Ordos stretched out his hand toward the assistant standing behind
him.
The assistant handed him a scroll bearing the royal seal, which Ordos
unfurled, raising his head arrogantly and shouting.
"Arian Shirone! Before delivering the king's decree, I will ask you
a few questions. Be aware that lying or giving false testimony may cost you your life. Do you
understand?"
"Yes, I understand."
Although Shirone was uncomfortable with Ordos's threatening attitude,
there was no choice but to comply since it was the king's command.
Of course, he had nothing to be ashamed of since he had not committed
any sins in his life.
"Arian Shirone, son of a hunter living on the middle slopes of the
Valley of Dawn, is that correct?"
"Yes, that's right."
"Arian Vincent and Arian Olena's son, found in a stable, is that
correct?"
There are no surnames that have any particular meaning for
commoners.
Vincent and Olena shared the same surname because they belonged to the
Arian ethnicity. Though their people were little-known, any ethnographer would immediately recognize
that they were commoners.
Ultimately, Ordos's words showed no consideration for Shirone.
There was no need to mention that he was found in a stable when the
entire kingdom already knew Shirone was a commoner.
"Yes, that's right."
Shirone answered with a sigh.
He could feel the disgusted looks of the other students, but he had
already braced himself for this.
However, Ordos seemed obsessed with humiliating Shirone, relentlessly
highlighting the most demeaning details.
"You were raised at two months old by the hunter Vincent, who lives
in the Thorn Mines area on the middle slopes of the Origin Mountains. You were abandoned in a stable.
From then on, you learned hunting skills from your father. Later, you entered the service of Ogent
Family as a low-ranking butler for two years, and it was through them that you became a guest and gained
admission to this magic academy. Is all of that true?"
One of the students burst into laughter.
"Puhahahaha! A low-ranking butler!"
The laughter died down quickly as others stared, but it was clear the
other students were also snickering internally.
A low-ranking servant was essentially a servant who mopped floors in a
noble household.
Nade looked around, trying to memorize the face of the student who had
laughed earlier.
Iruki also frowned, seeming offended by Ordos's words.
Shirone sighed again.
When he first stepped forward, he had been flustered, but by now, he
fully grasped his situation.
Everyone was mocking him.
It was horrible to see the friends who had laughed and talked with him
not too long ago now looking at him as if he were a bug.
"Answer me, Shirone! Is all of this true?"
"Yes, it is... true."
As soon as Shirone finished speaking, the students began to
murmur.
Most of the responses were scornful and mocking, and only a few looked
at Shirone with pity.
Among those few were Canis and Arin, listening from a distance away from
the crowd.
"That's unexpected. Shirone was a commoner?"
"Hmph, so what? Nobles are always annoying. All they know how to do
is flaunt their family name."
Canis and Arin had no parents either. They had been abandoned as
children and spent their childhood in Radum, the underbelly of the capital, so it was only natural for
them to be displeased by the nobles mocking Shirone.
"But not everyone is like that. Iruki and Nade, as well as Dante's
group, are also upset. Especially Dante. It's surprising; I thought he didn't like Shirone."
Arin had the ability of Initial State, so what she said was likely
true.
Canis looked at Dante with disgust and spat out as if it didn't matter
at all.
"Tch, what does it matter where they come from to those
magic-crazed lunatics? That's what's normal for aspiring mages. No, it's even more annoying. They're
born with a silver spoon in their mouth, so who are they to pity anyone?"
Arin snickered.
At least Shirone had the title of "commoner," but they were
nothing more than rootless bastards. Though Canis spoke harshly, it was clear that Dante's attitude of
treating people based solely on their magical abilities had also shaken his resolve.
"Hey... Amy is coming too."
Two women were hurriedly running toward the place Arin was pointing.
They were Amy and her best friend, Seriel.
"Amy, what the hell is going on?"
Even though Seriel was gasping for breath, she couldn't stop talking for
even a moment.
The student sent by Alpheas first sought out Amy, who was known to be
Shirone's lover.
Of course, her inseparable companion, Seriel, wouldn't miss out on
witnessing such a shocking situation.
"I don't know! We'll see when we get there."
Amy and Seriel pushed their way through the crowd of students.
Since Shirone's official lover had arrived, the students quietly made
way without a word.
"Shirone! What in the world is...!"
Amy, pushing her way through the crowd, swallowed her words
abruptly.
Numerous knights were standing guard with fierce glares, and an old man
with a stern expression was shouting.
Shirone was slightly bowing his head, listening to the old man's words,
as if he were waiting for a judgment after committing a crime.
Ordos's insults toward Shirone were escalating even further.
He started rambling on about stories published in magazines and, when he
finished, he moved on to recount private anecdotes gathered through his personal channels.
He spoke of Shirone spending time with the children from Hwajeon
Village, as well as wandering the mountains for days on end to find a single meal.
To the students, all of this was new and astonishing information. And
this made Shirone's status as a commoner sink in even more.
The scroll Ordos held would likely only contain the king's
command.
At this point, it was almost admirable how Ordos had memorized every
detail of the last 18 years.
Shirone, the subject of all this, was on the verge of losing
consciousness.
Having all of his private stories exposed in front of the friends he'd
spent the past year with felt more humiliating than being naked.
"Is all of this true? Answer me, Shirone."
Shirone was so agitated that he didn't even realize he was clenching his
fists. By the time he came to his senses, he was on the verge of exploding.
Forcing himself to calm down, he nodded, resigned.
"Yes, it's true."
The students burst into laughter.
As the barrage of absurd questions continued, no one held back
anymore.
It wasn't even that funny of a story. But for those who grew up in noble
families, the backstory of Shirone, the top student in the advanced class, was endlessly amusing.
Seriel looked at Amy with a worried expression.
Of course, she herself had only learned through the magazine that
Shirone was a commoner.
To be honest, she was deeply shocked. She also felt hurt that Amy hadn't
told her such an important fact.
But Seriel decided to choose the path that would make her
happiest.
There were too many precious memories of the time she had spent with Amy
and Shirone to allow herself to resent them.
But other students had no such memories.
To them, Shirone was an untouchable genius, a mere obstacle in their
lives, someone they wished would just disappear after encountering some misfortune — nothing more,
nothing less.
"Amy…."
Amy ended up exploding.
"Those bastards! What's so funny that you're all laughing?"
Amy rolled up her sleeves and marched toward the students.
She was also angry at Shirone's friends, who were just standing by and
letting this happen.
At that moment, Ordos delivered the king's decree.
"Arian Shirone, kneel and receive the decree!"
Amy stopped in her tracks, and Shirone placed one knee on the ground,
listening to the royal emissary.
Ordos, as if he had only been whispering until now, shouted at the top
of his lungs.
It felt like his voice might actually tear.
"King Orkamp IV of Kazra decrees! All of Arian Shirone's actions
match the information we have received in Kazra. Thus, there is a strong possibility that you are the
first prince, who went into hiding during the kingdom's crisis 18 years ago! Therefore, we invite you to
the kingdom. Respond without hesitation and prove your rightful claim as the first prince! That is all!
Orkamp IV!"
Iruki and Nade shouted simultaneously.
"Whaaaaat?!"
The shock felt by other students was similar.
When the foreign emissaries came and started digging into Shirone's
past, everyone thought it was just a simple scouting offer.
Although rare, it's not unheard of for kingdoms to recruit talent from
other kingdoms depending on their needs.
But Shirone was a royal. Not just any royalty, but the first prince, a
direct heir to the royal bloodline.
