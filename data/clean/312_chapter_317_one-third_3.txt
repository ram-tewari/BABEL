Chapter 317:
One-Third (3)
Although retired from active duty, the influence of a first-class noble
was immense. Many parents approached him to greet him.
Shirone led his friends to Shakora and took the opportunity to greet him
when other nobles weren't around.
"Hello."
"Ah, Shirone. How have you been?"
"Well, thanks to you."
Isis approached with a worried expression.
"Shirone, how is our Amy? Do you think she can pass?"
"How would Shirone know that? Don't stress the boy out,"
Shakora scolded, making Isis pout.
"Haha! It's okay. I saw her yesterday, and she seemed in good
condition."
"Hmm, that's a relief."
Shakora's face showed a hint of relief. Despite trying to appear calm,
he was clearly nervous. Even a first-class noble was just a parent when it came to their child.
Iruki and Neide, who were standing behind Shirone, also greeted Shakora.
Shakora showed interest when he heard the name Merkodain.
"So, you're Iruki. How is your father?"
"He's... still around, I guess."
It was a cheeky remark for such an occasion, but Shakora just
laughed.
Honestly, Iruki was quite polite compared to his notoriously eccentric
father.
"Shirone, bring your friends over to our house
sometime."
"Yes, I will."
After finishing their greetings, Shakora and Isis headed to the entrance
for parents. Shirone entered the Colosseum through the student entrance.
Although juniors and classmates gave him wary looks, he didn't feel
lonely. Students who were only interested in magic, not status, gradually gathered around Shirone. After
all, to discuss the exam, you needed to be on the same level.
Dante, Closer, and Sabina approached. On the other side, Canis and Arin
appeared, followed by Boyle and Pandora. Finally, Mark, who knew how to use his connections, confidently
brought Maria to the best seats.
Except for Mark and Maria, the top ten candidates for the next
graduation class were all gathered. Since none of them could afford to be left out of the graduation
class, their classmates frowned at their gathering.
Dante smirked and asked, "How does it feel to send your girlfriend
to the battlefield?"
Shirone looked down at the arena.
"I don't know yet. What do you think?"
Dante also looked at the arena and said, "There seems to be a
difference in skill levels among the participants. Based on objective abilities, she should be in the
top ten. But the exam subjects will play a big role. Unexpected situations usually arise from
asymmetrical power."
As an information processing major, Dante's analysis was hard to argue
with.
While they talked, Sabina waved at Neide.
"Hi."
"Oh, hi."
They weren't close enough to warrant a special greeting, so Neide
responded politely.
Sabina stood there for a while, waiting for more conversation, but when
Neide showed no interest, she sulkily sat next to Dante. Pandora's scoff was audible.
"Everyone, it's starting."
The 30 students participating in the graduation exam stood in a line in
the center of the Colosseum, all wearing 2000-series bracelets.
However, the audience didn't need bracelets to watch. The exam venue was
designed to the highest national standards, making the entire Colosseum a giant Image Zone.
Arin clasped her hands and prayed for Amy's success. Canis frowned and
said, "Do you think that helps? If prayers worked, anyone could become a mages. Besides, you don't
even believe in God."
"Still, I'm nervous. Don't you want Amy to pass?"
"Why should I care who passes? It's not my exam."
Arin glared at Canis, feeling hurt. But his indifference was only on the
surface. As he rested his chin on his hand, watching the arena, he saw Amy, ready for battle, and smiled
slightly.
"Do your best, pumpkin."
The faculty, led by Alpheas and Olivia, took their seats. Parents sat on
the left, while scouts and reporters sat on the right.
After Alpheas's opening speech, Olivia explained the exam evaluation
criteria.
The exam was divided into two parts, with two out of six subjects chosen
by roulette. Since they didn't know which subjects would be chosen, students had to train in all
areas.
This rule was established to ensure fairness for non-combat mages and to
give repeaters a slight advantage, as a courtesy from the Magic Association.
The preliminary event ended with the students pledging to compete
fairly.
The 30 participants stood in a circle along the Colosseum wall, and the
announcer, Saad, shouted from the podium.
"We will now begin the Alpheas Magic School graduation
exam!"
A giant circular hologram appeared in the sky above the Colosseum. The
six subjects were evenly divided, and the central pointer began to spin rapidly.
The participants clenched their fists and performed the water
ceremony.
The 2000-series would activate the moment the pointer stopped. They had
to move instantly.
Finally, the pointer indicated the first exam subject.
"Wow!"
The crowd's cheers filled the Colosseum.
Night fell.
People poured out of the Colosseum's exit, all with excited faces.
Shirone's group also remained silent until they were outside.
At the exit, Neide turned to Shirone.
"What now, Shirone? Iruki and I are hungry and need to eat
something."
"Go ahead. I'll stay with Amy."
"Alright. See you tomorrow."
After his friends left, Shirone exhaled white breath, warming his cold
insides. He turned as light from the Colosseum shone on the scoreboard displaying the names of the
successful candidates. The top ten students had become mages and were ready to venture into the
world.
1st: Alt Amira
2nd: Ludwig Aconel
3rd: Connie Kale
4th: Tanto Selena
5th: Ekins Samuel
6th: Aresa Depo
7th: Fortress Seriel
8th: Lara Coril
9th: Andres Raul
10th: Dolin Rickston
Amy's name was not there.
She had been the third to be eliminated as soon as the first exam
began.
The first evaluation was combat power measurement. Since it favored
combat mages, the students focused on targeting Amy. It was practically a one-sided lynching. Seriel
couldn't even intervene before Amy's mental gauge hit zero.
It defied expert analysis, but such variables were part of the
graduation exam. Amy must have anticipated this and prepared thoroughly.
Still, it felt off.
Collusion in combat is natural. When one side grows stronger, the weaker
ones band together. But did it just seem like the strong were colluding?
Initially, it was chaotic. Amy wasn't the only one aspiring to be a
combat mage, and with 30 participants, the firepower was dispersed.
But when Fermi's group started acting, the tide turned sharply.
They targeted only Amy, avoiding all other students' attacks.
After about a minute, even the initially unaware students realized that
siding with Fermi's group made things easier.
Eventually, everyone except Seriel attacked Amy. Even the mighty Amy
couldn't withstand such an onslaught. Fermi's group's combat power had to be superior to the combined
power of all other participants for such a tactic to work.
Could this be called natural collusion?
The strangest part was that none of Fermi's group passed the exam. It
even seemed like they deliberately lost to Samuel.
'Samuel.'
Most students noticed that Fermi's group seemed to support Samuel during
the first exam. But the feeling was so subtle that it was hard to judge.
The harshness of the graduation exam? There were too many oddities to
dismiss it as that. Most suspicious was that Fermi, who had never lost first place in six years, was at
the center of it all.
'Maybe it's just a misunderstanding. There's no guarantee such things
won't happen on the battlefield. And even if it's not a misunderstanding, what can I do about
it?'
Simply put, the stronger side won the combat power measurement. Even if
it was the result of artificial collusion, it didn't change the evaluation criteria.
'Unless there was a personal deal.'
There could have been an agreement between Fermi and Samuel.
But Shirone quickly dismissed the thought. If it wasn't true, it would
be a great insult to Samuel. Besides, if such a thing had happened, the faculty would have already
investigated.
'Yeah, no matter how much I think about it, there's nothing I can
do.'
Right now, he was only worried about Amy.
Since she was eliminated early, her mental strength should have
recovered. But thinking about how she had to watch the exam from the waiting room for a long time made
his heart ache.
Amy's parents emerged from the parents' exit. Shirone approached them,
unsure of what expression to make.
"Shirone..."
Isis greeted him with tearful eyes. Both Shakora and Isis, a famous
dancer, must have noticed the oddities in the combat situation.
"Shirone, how could this happen? Amy was eliminated. It's
like..."
"Watch your words. How do you think Amy would feel hearing
that?"
Shakora cautioned his wife.
He also found some parts suspicious. But nothing was so strange as to be
beyond reason.
Except for Fermi's group's elimination, the battlefield's flow was
rational and logical. And since the second exam was a non-combat subject, Fermi's group's elimination
couldn't be deemed unnatural.
Shirone swallowed dryly as he saw the eliminated students leaving. When
he saw Amy among the middle group, his mind went blank.
Amy glanced at Shirone before approaching her parents. She smiled and
stuck out her tongue.
"Hehe, Dad, Mom. Sorry, I failed spectacularly."
"It's just an exam. You did your best, so don't be too
disappointed."
"Disappointed? No way. I think I made a mistake in my judgment.
I'll just have to try again next year."
Shirone felt relieved by Amy's bright demeanor but also worried.
She couldn't be feeling normal. She was putting her family's feelings
first.
Now the successful candidates were coming out. Seriel approached Amy,
crying her eyes out.
"Amy, Amy..."
Amy was genuinely happy for her friend's success.
How could she not be? Today, Seriel graduated from magic school and took
her first step as a mage.
"Congratulations, Seriel. Do your best for both of us. I'll catch
up soon."
Seriel shook her head.
Why wasn't it Amy instead of her? Anyone could see that Amy should have
been the one to pass.
She should have realized it sooner. If she had quickly sided with Amy
when Fermi's group attacked, the battle might have turned out differently.
"Amy... I..."
Amy grabbed Seriel's shoulders.
"Seriel, get a grip. You passed the exam. Do you understand? You
took your graduation exam, and I took mine. And you passed. Nothing else matters."
Seriel swallowed her tears.
She couldn't cry. How could she cry when Amy wasn't?
