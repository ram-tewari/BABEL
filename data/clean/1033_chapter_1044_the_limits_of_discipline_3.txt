Chapter 1044:
The Limits of Discipline (3)
"Brother! Brother!"
Wizard called out as she left the training ground, but Shirone seemed
not to hear her and quickened his pace.
He arrived at the most secluded spot in the school, a place not designed
for rest.
Having lived in the mountains since childhood, Shirone always sought
nature when he wanted to relax.
"Haa."
Sitting on a small rock, he exhaled but couldn't release the frustration
in his heart.
'What is the right thing to do?'
To kill Satan Havitz, Wizard's power was essential, but what about
Wizard's life?
It was a situation where a seven-year-old child had to kill the most
evil and cruel person in the world.
Shirone chuckled.
'Maybe I'm the one who's crazy.'
He had known this for some time, but this time it was different.
"Brother."
At Wizard's voice, Shirone looked up.
She must have followed him out of concern, but seeing her with a splint
added to his guilt.
"You got hurt badly. Wizard, I need to tell you…"
"I'm sorry, brother."
Wizard's eyes welled up with tears.
"I didn't think about how much Mom and Dad would hate it. Learning
is so much fun that I got too excited…"
'Fun?'
Shirone realized something he had forgotten.
'Oh, right.'
No matter how hard and painful it was, humans could endure it.
'As long as they have the certainty that they can become
stronger.'
Nothing was more thrilling for humans than that.
"Wizard, why do you find magic fun? Do you want to be the strongest
in the world? Do you want to surpass me?"
"I want to surpass you. But now it doesn't matter if I win. Magic
is just fun."
"There are many ways to be happy in life. You can enjoy life
without being the best. So why do you want to learn magic?"
Wizard blinked her eyes.
"Well, that's because…"
And as always, she smiled the brightest smile in the world.
"Because this is the world I will live in."
Shirone felt a lump in his throat.
"Yes, you're right."
No matter what others said, this was the only world we would live
in.
Only we could understand ourselves.
"Today's training was a bit weak. I told Mom and Dad. It'll be fine
from tomorrow."
"Haha!"
Shirone wiped away his tears.
Since diving into the world of magic, he had never experienced
defeat…
"Yes, let's grit our teeth and run."
All the suppressed frustrations were being comforted for that one
result.
Wizard approached Shirone and wiped away his tears with her small
hand.
"You must be having a hard time too."
It hadn't been long since they intensified their training, but Wizard's
insight had deepened considerably.
"You've grown a lot. Being able to understand others' feelings is
an excellent talent."
"Hehe. So, brother, are you in love with Amy?"
"Huh? Well…"
"Then if I become stronger than you someday, will you like me
more?"
'She's still a child.'
Shirone held back his laughter and said,
"There are many kinds of love. If you really surpass
me…"
If she became the world's greatest magician…
"Yes, there might be times when I think you're more precious than
Amy."
It was an adult's way of categorizing things, but Wizard was delighted
by the possibility.
"Great! Then I'll surpass you tomorrow!"
"Oh really?"
Shirone pulled Wizard's broken arm and healed it with Miracle
Stream.
The next day, the atmosphere at the training ground was tense.
Despite witnessing yesterday's heartbreaking scene, Wizard's parents
insisted on observing again.
"Hoo, today will be intense too."
As teachers were already tense, Shirone and Wizard warmed up in front of
the Image Zone.
"Is it combat practice?"
"No. Today is basics."
Wizard's determination gave Shirone enough courage, but he couldn't
ignore her parents' perspective.
'Seeing their child in pain is the worst for parents. I was too
thoughtless.'
Even if it's our world, we must consider the feelings of those watching
from outside.
"Today's lesson is mental pulsation."
It was optimal training that wasn't overly intense while maintaining its
rigor.
"I'll demonstrate."
As an innate awakener, Wizard instinctively cast spells but lacked
theoretical knowledge.
'That's even more impressive, but to face Havitz, I must teach her
everything I can.'
When Wizard's creativity clashed with Havitz's chaos, her body would
remember this moment.
Shirone stood at the center of the Image Zone installed at Stang
National Magic School.
Memories of training to improve his grades in his final year flashed
through his mind like a lantern show.
'I must do well.'
As his heartbeat unfolded without warning, teachers watching widened
their eyes in amazement.
"What… what is this…"
In the Image Zone, mental activity became visible, but there was no sign
of back-and-forth movement.
'A giant sphere…? No, it's definitely oscillating. The count keeps
rising.'
Each time it reached its maximum radius, a buzzer blared through the
speakers as if tearing them apart.
'Heart rate: 6,000 beats per second.'
Whoever was ranked second in the world as a magician couldn't match this
speed by several times over.
The Ten Mages of the Ivory Tower shook their heads in disbelief.
'We can't compete with that.'
For someone who had mastered mental techniques, it was an expected
result. But Wizard was different.
'She can't reach it. Even if she does, she'll usually die. The Five
Great Stars are serious.'
As Bartok turned his head, he saw Wizard watching intently without
blinking at all during Shirone's demonstration.
"Aha."
The Ten Mages simultaneously realized something.
'1 minute 17 seconds.'
It took Wizard 1 minute 17 seconds to grasp what she saw from Shirone's
6,000 beats per second heart rate demonstration without knowing any basics beforehand.
"Got it?"
Without needing further explanation, Wizard nodded and entered the image
zone.
"I'll give it a try."
Wizard positioned herself next to Amy, and behind them stood the Ten
Mages of the Ivory Tower. Though it was officially part of a training exercise, there was a genuine
curiosity about how far Wizard could push herself.
"Hoo…"
After taking a deep breath, Wizard suddenly widened her eyes and
deployed her Spirit Zone.
As the buzzer sounded, the teachers and students fixed their attention
on her rapidly increasing heartbeat.
Bartok felt his pulse quicken.
'800 beats per second. This is far beyond a professional mage's level.
However, we're looking for someone who can reach the pinnacle of the universe. At this level, it's still
not enough…'
"Whoa! What's going on?"
As the students gasped in shock, Wizard's heartbeat rate continued
climbing.
2,000 beats per second.
The pupils of the Ten Mages of the Ivory Tower trembled, but the
situation was still within acceptable limits.
'She's maintaining control.'
The heartbeat continued to rise, and blood trickled down from Wizard's
nose.
"Dear…"
Wizard's mother grabbed her husband's arm nervously.
'Faster… I can go faster.'
Perhaps unaware of the extreme changes happening within her own body,
Wizard kept pushing herself further.
At 3,000 beats per second, blood began seeping from her eyes. Her
mother, unable to bear it any longer, rushed toward Shirone.
"Stop! She's going to die if this continues!"
Bartok, however, understood why Shirone remained silent.
'I am… a one-star resident of the Ivory Tower.'
Although skilled enough to be a magic association president in any
kingdom, he still fell short of the two-star, three-star, four-star, and five-star levels above
him.
'Who wouldn't desire to climb higher? Who wouldn't crave it? But simply
wishing for it doesn't make it possible to reach.'
Wizard's father stepped forward.
"That's enough. As we told you yesterday, our daughter's safety is our
top priority."
Bartok thought to himself.
'They don't understand.'
They couldn't comprehend the desperation of those obsessed with becoming
stronger in this world.
Mental pulse: 4,000 beats per second.
'It's not about defeating someone else. It's not even about gaining
something by becoming stronger. It's the pure joy of rising endlessly, the happiness of knowing that you
can continue to grow.'
In other words:
'All humans are…'
Wizard's eyes gleamed with fierce determination.
"Aaaahhh!"
Mental pulse: 5,000 beats per second.
'I can get stronger. I can do better. Believe. The moment you start
doubting, everything crumbles.'
Her tears mixed with the blood streaming from her eyes—tears of pure
longing.
'I… want to soar through the sky.'
As Wizard reached her physical and mental limits, her Spirit Zone began
to distort and collapse.
"If you lose your rhythm, it's over," Shirone said calmly.
"Your thoughts are too rigid. Let go of everything—your desire to be
recognized, your obsession with success—release it all and immerse yourself in the present. Don't
consolidate your thoughts; let them flow outward. Pour yourself out."
"Arghhh!"
When her mental pulse reached 6,000 beats per second, Shirone felt a
thrill of awe.
'She immediately understands and shifts her mental state.'
There was no room for jealousy—what Shirone felt was akin to witnessing
a masterpiece, pure and inspiring.
But to everyone else, it looked like a gruesome sight—just a
seven-year-old child bleeding from her eyes, nose, and mouth.
"Please, wizard…"
Wizard's mother pleaded tearfully.
"Please stop this. We understand that our daughter is talented, but…
what's the point of success if it means enduring so much pain?"
"Talent?" Shirone spoke for the first time.
"Do you think she's enduring all this pain because she has talent? If
that's what you believe, then you're gravely underestimating Wizard."
"But… but…"
"Yes, Wizard is a genius. Maybe talent does exist. But, ma'am…" Shirone
turned toward her.
"There's no such thing in this world as talent that allows someone to
excel without effort."
The mother fell silent at the conviction in his voice.
"All humans are the same. Do you think geniuses feel less pain when
pricked by a needle? No. The difference lies in how they perceive and process that pain. Are you
watching Wizard's talent? I see something different. I see a seven-year-old child who endured immense
suffering just to reach this point."
Shirone turned his gaze back to Wizard.
"She's here because of the sheer amount of pain she's been processing
since she was in her mother's womb."
How much had she cried as a fetus, trying to process such
incomprehensible sensations?
"Our daughter…" The mother looked devastated.
"You said you're farmers," Shirone said gently.
"I don't know much about farming, but I heard farmers remove rotten
seedlings in advance. It must not feel good. Nurturing life on a grand scale can sometimes be
cruel."
The father's expression hardened.
"We can't expect you to understand our world, just as we can't fully
understand yours. But within that world, we dream and compete. For us, that's the only true
world."
This sentiment was universal.
"Wizard wants to be the best. Not because she has talent, but because
the immense suffering she's endured won't allow her to accept her current state. That's why, the moment
she met me, she gave it her all."
Shirone made a heartfelt plea.
"Please, entrust her to me. If I judge that she truly cannot endure any
longer, I will stop."
"But she's already bleeding so much…"
"Be quiet," the father said, silencing his wife.
"I understand. I'm a man, too. I've dreamt of standing at the top of the
world. But you're telling me… that our daughter can be the best?"
"Yes. If she can continue processing this kind of pain endlessly, she
will be."
The father looked at Wizard, who was gritting her teeth, blood streaming
from her eyes, yet still holding on. His eyes grew moist.
"Damn it! Of course, she wants to fly. I wanted to fly too. So go
ahead—give it your all. I'll protect you."
Closing his eyes tightly, he bowed his head and shouted, "Please, take
good care of her!"
