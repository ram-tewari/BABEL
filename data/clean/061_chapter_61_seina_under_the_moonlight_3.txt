Chapter 61:
Seina Under the Moonlight (3)
"Aha! So you're an educator. Pleased to meet you. I'm Cosine Silver, fourth-rank
noble. My father serves as Creas's magistrate. Forgive my boldness, but I fell for you at first sight. If
you'd grant me the honor of escorting you home, I'd be eternally grateful."
Shiira wriggled upright. But instead of looking at Silver, she held her forehead and
sighed.
'Ugh, annoying. I just wanted to get drunk today.'
Nothing seemed to go right today. Showing weakness in front of her students, being
dismissed by Keira, and now strange men pestering her—wasn't it too much?
A bitter smile crossed Shiira's lips. Maybe she looked exactly like a heartbroken woman
right now.
'Heh. At least someone likes me. A temperamental, unkind woman like me.'
She'd never once felt love for anyone. When Armin appeared with Keira three years ago,
she hadn't felt betrayed. But perhaps there'd been a sliver of hope—a faint thought of what if they met
again. Could that even be called love?
"My introduction was too grandiose. My apologies. Meeting a woman I can't let slip
made me eager to impress. Forgive my rudeness."
"It's fine. I'm tired, so please leave."
Shiira was exhausted. Though a certified 6th-class mage could sober up easily, she still
needed the alcohol's dreamy haze.
"Leaving a lady alone goes against chivalry. Let me escort you by carriage. Lean on
me."
As Silver grabbed Shiira's shoulder to help her up, Shirone's group immediately
protested.
"What are you doing? Don't touch Teacher."
"Seems there's a misunderstanding. I'm just offering an escort. Kids shouldn't
interfere. The teacher can take the carriage, and you can go back."
Silver tried to force Shiira up. Shirone grabbed his wrist. Silver's face, which had been
polite for Shiira's sake, twisted instantly. Being ignored by a woman was tolerable for his goals, but being
treated like this by a kid wounded his pride.
"Hey, brat. Where'd you learn such insolence? Let go nicely while you can."
"Teacher said no. Please leave. We'll take care of her."
"Damn brats getting cocky. Earlier, I saw you getting her drunk—what's your game?
Which school are you from? Want to get expelled?"
Shirone's expression turned cold. Bringing up school was clearly pressure—a teacher
causing a scene here would invite gossip.
Iruki spoke.
"So you've been watching us all along. Then it's even less your place to interfere.
And let me add—the teacher here is beyond your reach."
"You? You dare use 'you' with me?"
"Should I call you something else? Pervert, perhaps?"
"You little—!"
Silver's drunkenness turned to rage as he grabbed Iruki's collar. But a sharp pain in his
side made him release his grip. Clutching his stomach, Silver staggered back.
"Guh! What the—?"
Nade generated electricity in his palm.
"Scram. Next time, I'll fry your lower half."
The burly man, who'd been ready to intervene, stepped forward with a serious expression.
"Hey, they're magic school students."
"Magic school? Alpheas Magic School?"
Silver's face paled. Magic schools were on a different level from regular noble schools.
And Alpheas was one of the kingdom's top five prestigious institutions, attended only by the highest nobles.
As Silver's eyes darted, assessing the situation, Iruki scoffed and approached.
"Heard you'll get us expelled?"
"No, that's…"
"I'm Mercodain Iruki. Expel me if you can."
"M-Mercodain?"
The alcohol seemed to evaporate from his system. No noble in Tormia Kingdom was
unfamiliar with the name Mercodain.
A first-rank noble family, they had led the king's advisory council, "Dragon
Thunder," for centuries without interruption.
As Silver's pupils shook, Iruki smirked, unsurprised. Nobles who flaunted their lineage
were everywhere. But he'd intimidated Silver this way to protect Shiira. Though he knew the man lacked the
backbone to retaliate, he might exploit her position as a teacher. Not that the Olipher faction's Shiira
would falter over such a thing.
"Let's go, Silver. Hey, Silver!"
The burly man dragged Silver away. Silver, grateful for the out, fled like a whipped dog.
Even after they left, Shirone's group seethed. Their blatant disrespect proved they
hadn't taken the students seriously—especially with their small statures.
"This place has terrible vibes. Never judge by appearances. Let's never come
back."
"Agreed. Let's just leave. It's getting dark."
"Teacher. Are you okay?"
"Yeah. Let's go."
With Nade's help, Shiira stood unsteadily. The unwanted attention had irritated her, but
she wasn't in a bad mood. With her students protecting her, what was there to fear? Even a certified
6th-class mage was just a woman who needed escorting under noble etiquette outside.
She found her students oddly endearing. After paying the bill without fuss, Shiira
staggered out.
The moon hung in the sky.
Under the cloudless night, bathed in moonlight, Shiira let out a euphoric sigh.
She felt inexplicably happy. Maybe it was the alcohol, or perhaps she'd taken a new leap
today. For the first time, she felt free from Armin's shadow, ready to live her own life. Probably the
alcohol, though…
"Ahh, I feel great. So great today."
Like a girl, Shiira spun under the moon. Shirone's group, just exiting the restaurant,
gaped.
Had the alcohol briefly melted her frozen heart?
Her purple hair cascading loose, she was beautiful under the moonlight. Her twirling
trench coat was amusing too. Passersby glanced at her, but none frowned or clicked their tongues.
She was that lovable.
Shirone's group didn't stop her. Instead, they watched her drunken antics with
fascination.
"Teacher's like this? Unexpected."
"Did we… just hit the jackpot?"
Iruki's eyes gleamed with an idea.
"Hmm. Could this be used as leverage? To stop the research club's dissolution."
"If we play it right, maybe. Teacher isn't the type to lie. She'd probably be too
embarrassed to remember anyway."
As Nade and Iruki schemed, Shirone pointed and shouted.
"Hey! There!"
Shiira, spinning in place, stumbled. Like a top losing momentum, she was about to fall.
"Grab her!"
Letting her fall in public would be disastrous. Shirone caught her back while Nade and
Iruki held her arms.
"Teacher, snap out of it. We need you sober to get back to school."
"Ugh. Dizzy."
Shiira staggered in zigzags. Downing a bottle of cognac in an hour and spinning dozens of
times—no wonder she was out of it. It was less drinking and more punishment.
"Hey, hey! This isn't working. Shirone, try carrying her."
"What? Why me?"
"Out of us, you're the strongest."
Shirone sighed. Though all three were 18-year-old boys, their magic training left them
weaker than average.
At least Shirone, having grown up climbing mountains and chopping wood, was stronger than
his friends.
"Fine. Then help her onto my back."
"Don't look so reluctant. How often do you get to carry Teacher? I'm jealous."
Nade's expression showed no envy. Instead, he smirked in relief.
"Tch. If you can't say it, don't."
Hoisting Shiira onto his back, Shirone started walking. As they left the noble district,
the crowd thinned. For commoners living hand-to-mouth, leisure was a luxury.
"Hey… Teacher's surprisingly small. She seemed as tall as me, but she's tiny."
"Probably psychological. Teachers always seem bigger."
"Really? But she's heavier than I thought. It's getting hard."
Iruki nodded, laughing.
"Heh. Of course she's heavy. Women are made of bones and muscle too. Did you think
she'd be light as a feather?"
He hadn't thought that, but he'd vaguely expected her to be lighter. Not that he'd admit
he'd never carried a girl before—or even held hands.
"No! I knew that much. But this is really tough. I might not make it to
school."
"If you can't, neither can we. Just keep going."
"Can't we use magic? Float her with air magic."
"Even so, she's our teacher. That'd be…"
Now that he pictured it, it'd look odd. Resigned to his role as pack mule, Shirone
grumbled at the unfairness.
"Three guys can't carry one girl? Pathetic. Now I miss Rian. He could've carried me
while I carried Teacher. Should've learned Schema."
Nade and Iruki stayed silent. Honestly, failing to carry a petite woman was demoralizing.
But women were heavier than expected, and bookish types like them had no strength.
"What can we do? The only exercise we get is breathing. I'm confident in mental
tasks, though."
Iruki flicked his fingers.
"Then use your mind. Learn Schema right now. Shirone, think you can?"
"How's that possible? Schema out of nowhere?"
"You can try. I mastered Spirit Zone in one go. Don't tell me you couldn't?"
Nade said.
"No, I got it on my first try too. Once I got the feel, it worked. Shirone, what
about you?"
Struggling under Shiira's weight, Shirone looked up.
"Huh? Oh, yeah, first try. Didn't even know it was Spirit Zone at the time."
Could someone enter Spirit Zone without knowing it? That was so like Shirone, Iruki
giggled.
"Interesting. Anyway, that's my point. The basics are the same, right?"
Now that he thought about it, Iruki had a point. Aptitude existed, but trying couldn't
hurt.
"Maybe? Should I really try? My arms are killing me."
***
Nade urged him on.
"Do it, do it. First, enter a trance and project your body's image. Then…"
"I know the theory. Let me focus."
As his friends fell silent, Shirone closed his eyes. The trance-like state before
entering Spirit Zone. From there, project the body's image and awaken the senses through insight!
"Ugh… Ugh! No, it's getting worse."
Iruki stifled a laugh.
"Idiot, did you really think it'd work? Excelling at Spirit Zone means you're
hopeless at Schema."
"Pfft—hahaha!"
Nade burst out laughing as Shirone glared.
"Screw this! I quit! Switch!"
They took turns carrying Shiira.
But Nade collapsed after half the distance Shirone had covered, and Iruki couldn't manage
ten steps.
Shirone, hoisting Shiira again with trembling arms, was exasperated.
"Don't we look pathetic?"
"Some thoughts shouldn't be spoken aloud, Shirone."
With Nade's words, the group fell silent. Not out of shame—they were just too tired to
talk.
As they neared the school, Shirone suddenly spoke.
"Hey… let's drop that."
"Drop what?"
"Using today to negotiate with Teacher."
"Hmm."
Nade also felt guilty. But the research club's survival was at stake.
