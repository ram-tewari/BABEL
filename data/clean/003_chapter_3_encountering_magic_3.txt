Chapter 3:
Encountering Magic (3)
One child, seeing Shirone's attire, frowned.
"Principal, he's not a noble. He looks like a commoner."
"Commoners aren't allowed in here. Hey, you, get out quickly!"
Alpheus, who hadn't expected him not to be a noble, scratched his eyebrow awkwardly. But
as a magician who pursues knowledge, he didn't discriminate by status and beckoned him to come.
"It's alright, come over here. So, which of this old man's words caught your
attention?"
Shirone stood hesitantly. He wanted to approach Alpheus, but the children's gazes blocked
his approach.
They seemed to be saying: Don't invade our territory, you insignificant commoner.
"Show me magic."
"Hoho, have you never seen magic?"
"I've read about it in books, but never seen it directly."
One child pointed and shouted:
"Liar! How could a commoner read books?"
Alpheus stared deeply into Shirone's eyes as if discerning the truth. It didn't seem to
be a lie. But children at that age often deceive adults with innocent faces as easily as eating.
"Alright, what kind of magic would you like to see?"
"Anything is fine. Just show me something, please. I beg you."
Shirone, realizing his position, bowed his head respectfully. Alpheus, with a hearty
laugh, waved his hand. It wasn't particularly difficult to perform magic at a level suitable for playing
with children.
"This old man's only joy is showing magic to our cute little ones. Good! Then this
time I'll show you a magic that creates wind."
"Wooah! Wind, wind!"
While the children were clapping, Shirone, gripped by tension, clenched his fists. It was
natural since he knew nothing about magic.
The moment Alpheus waved his hand, Shirone's eyes widened at an unexpected floating
sensation. The world sank below, and his vision cleared. He could see all the buildings of the magic school
at once.
"Uwaaaaah!"
Shirone unconsciously screamed. Meanwhile, the children were enjoying Alpheus's magic,
even performing somersaults in the air.
Shortly after, Shirone plummeted at an incredible speed. He closed his eyes tightly and
curled up at the sight of the ground rushing towards him.
But the desperate impact didn't come. Gently opening his eyes, he found himself floating
near the ground.
The children pointed at Shirone and laughed. Alpheus was also watching with a wrinkled
smile.
"How is it? This is magic."
All Shirone could hear was the sound of his heart beating in his chest. Magic,
experienced for the first time, surpassed the vague dreams of the boy.
"What is magic?"
"Hmm, let's see. Magic is..."
As Alpheus pondered with a mischievous look, an impatient Shirone added:
"It's okay if I don't understand. Please tell me exactly as you think."
The children's faces hardened. Alpheus was a famous magician known even in the kingdom.
That's why even noble children behave like gentle lambs. So for the children, it was astonishing that
Shirone would speak so impertinently.
Alpheus found it bold, but seeing desperation in it, he wasn't displeased. The beautiful
appearance, unlike a commoner, also helped dispel discomfort.
"Haha! No need to be so tense. In fact, it's not even a difficult story. But if you
want, I'll raise the difficulty a bit. Magic is an act of breaking common sense. Conversely, it's a mental
process that explores the truth of phenomena."
Shirone blinked and was lost in thought for a while.
"If you don't know, you can say you don't know."
"It's different from common sense, but is it the truth in reality?"
Alpheus pushed out his lower lip. It was a habit that only emerged when he was truly
surprised.
Understanding the true meaning from a short sentence is only possible when one completely
comprehends something.
And that was precisely the insight that Alpheus had emphasized.
"Where did you learn such a thing?"
"Uh, well, from books."
"Are there books with such content?"
"Not the content, I just thought of it while reading books. The idea that common
sense isn't necessarily right. I also thought that to reach the truth, one must ultimately fight against
common sense."
Alpheus nodded.
It was a reasonable statement.
'No, not reasonable, but the truth. Does he really know what he's saying? Anyone can
recite what they've heard from someone else. But if that's not the case...'
Was he even 12 years old yet?
When a thought ascends to the rank of common sense, it becomes extremely difficult to
destroy it. The fact that he was already aware of the first objective of magic, which is essence, indicated
considerable talent.
