Chapter 113:
End-of-Term Report Card (5)
Kaizen Swordsmanship School.
Located in the capital of the Tormia Kingdom, the Kaizen Swordsmanship School boasted a
200-year history and had produced numerous knights of great renown.
Over ten were certified first-class swordsmen, three of whom held the title of Sword
Master.
The Iron Creed. The Symbol of Courage.
Any aspiring knight in Tormia dreamed of enrolling at Kaizen.
But there was no entrance exam. Instead, a one-month probationary period assessed
candidates' potential, with only 30 of 300 aspirants admitted each term.
Among the current students was Rian, who had secured his place through sheer skill.
Urban Combat Training Grounds.
The training ground, replicating cityscapes, was Kaizen's pride.
A gaunt-faced instructor glared at the first-years seated on the steps.
"Today's practical is movement. For you first-years, the focus is center-of-gravity
shifting."
The instructor, Parkua Kuan, was a sixth-class swordsman. Before a war injury crippled
his leg, he'd been known as the Death Magician.
"Center-of-gravity shifting is essential. It's the foundation of the sword's triad:
speed, power, and precision."
Limping up the steps, Kuan pointed at a burly boy.
"You. Where's a swordsman's center of gravity?"
"Sir! The navel!"
"Wrong. You."
The shark-eyed cadet stood immediately. Hesitation meant punishment, exam or not.
"Sir! The soles of the feet!"
"Wrong. You."
"The sword, sir!"
"Drop. 200 push-ups."
"Sir!"
Push-ups before the test were brutal, but Kaizen's instructors didn't care.
Kuan drew his broadsword. Its tip fanned out like a spread, while the blade narrowed
unusually.
