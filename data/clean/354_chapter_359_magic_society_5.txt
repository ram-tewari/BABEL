Chapter 359:
Magic Society (5)
The anti-terrorism unit was giving Shirone sharp looks. It was natural
since he was serving as an excellent shield for the terrorists, but from the hostages' perspective, it
was frustrating.
The terrorist holding Shirone with a flail had immense strength.
Realizing that he couldn't escape without magic, Shirone began to feel increasingly anxious.
'What should I do? Am I really going to die like this?'
At that moment, Plu appeared, pushing through the anti-terrorism unit.
Even in a life-and-death situation, Shirone felt a pang of fear seeing her fierce expression.
'Why is Plu here?'
Plu pointed and shouted,
"Hey!"
All the Black Revolutionaries turned their attention to her. Unexpected
situations were never good.
The terrorist holding Shirone asked threateningly,
"Who is that woman?"
"Uh, well... she's my superior."
Plu continued angrily,
"Are you out of your mind? I told you to go to the alchemy shop, so what
are you doing here?"
Shirone hesitated, unsure whether to respond.
Of course, it made sense for her to be angry. But it was a ridiculous
thing to bring up in this situation.
'Why is she doing this? Something feels off.'
As Shirone pondered, she pressed on,
"I clearly told you to buy parts for the 'magic control device'! Because
of you, 'innocent people' are getting hurt! How are you going to 'take responsibility'?"
Shirone's eyes lit up.
'Magic control device?'
He noticed that Plu's eyes were unusually tense.
'Ah, this is a code.'
Piecing it together, it seemed to mean:
They would disable the magic control device. He was to take
responsibility for the hostages.
'So there's one chance. But when?'
Plu couldn't convey that part.
They could create as many codes as they wanted, but if they were too
long, it would arouse suspicion. Also, in an operation where success or failure was decided in an
instant, timing could be a double-edged sword.
From that moment, Shirone began to prepare his mind. There was no set
time. The key was not to miss the opportunity when the magic control device was destroyed.
On the rooftop of an eight-story building 200 meters from Gold Tower, a
gunner aimed a crossbow loaded with a magic bolt at Gold Tower. Unlike a regular crossbow, the tiller
was long enough to rest on his shoulder, and it used a trigger mechanism.
As the gunner blew a bubble with his gum, a voice came through his
communication device.
-Alpha. Do you read?
"This is Alpha. Awaiting orders. Over."
-Your orders are to destroy the magic control device in Gold
Tower.
The bubble popped with a loud snap. Destroying the magic control device
wouldn't help much with hostages inside.
-This is your one chance. Don't miss.
The gunner stopped thinking and focused on the magic control device
through the crossbow's scope. Strategy was for the brains, fighting was for the muscles. He was the
nerve of the anti-terrorism unit.
Recalling the internal layout of Gold Tower, lines in his mind formed
into a three-dimensional structure. He rotated it freely, calculating the trajectory.
The magic control device was embedded in the wall, invisible to the eye.
He needed to use his spiritual vision.
"Roger that."
Plu exchanged glances with Shirone and bit her lower lip. There were too
many uncertain variables. Even if the magic control device was destroyed, she couldn't guarantee Shirone
could evacuate the hostages.
'Please. You have to do something. One mistake and it's over.'
Shirone was desperately trying to reshape his mental state.
He chose mass teleportation as his method. He would evacuate the
hostages as soon as the magic control device was destroyed.
'Can I time it right?'
He had to bypass the terrorists and isolate the hostages in the Spirit
Zone. Given its unusual form, it was crucial to prepare his mind in advance. For a mental-type magician
like Arin, it would be easy, but for Shirone, who excelled in endurance, it was a time-consuming
task.
'This is harder than I thought. Nothing should happen now.'
Meanwhile, the gunner finished his preparations. The rhythm of his gum
chewing slowed, and he began to crush it slowly with his teeth.
Slowly... even more slowly, his finger on the trigger tightened.
Click.
As his teeth met, the trigger was pulled. With a ping, the magic bolt
shot out like a swallow catching a bug.
'Just a little more!'
Shirone did his best to reshape his mental state while staying aware of
his surroundings.
A flash of light passed over the anti-terrorism unit and through the
front door, narrowly missing Shirone.
The sharp metal bolt pierced the wall next to the vault and embedded
itself in the magic control device's circuit board. Electricity surged from the bolt, melting the wires,
and the overheated device exploded with a loud bang.
'Now!'
Shirone's eyes snapped open as he entered the Spirit Zone. The hostages,
including Aria, were captured in the amorphous Spirit Zone.
"What's happening?"
The sudden attack startled the Black Revolutionaries. But they quickly
regained their composure, having undergone rigorous training.
"They tricked us!"
As the flail came down on Shirone, his eyes flashed. His body turned to
light, and all the hostages were caught in the photon transformation spell.
'Mass Teleport!'
Countless flashes of light erupted simultaneously, twisting like a
dragon and escaping outside Gold Tower.
Shirone arrived 20 meters from the front door. He wanted to get farther,
but unfamiliar with the area, he was limited to visible space.
Confirming the number of hostages, Shirone used precise control to cut
the ropes with Armand. The anti-terrorism unit was arresting the terrorists near Gold Tower.
Werewolves, subterraneans, goblins—their races varied.
The giant with the flail was a Gader, known as a forest keeper. With his
developed jaw, he screamed in a deep voice as the knights twisted his arms.
'One is missing.'
There were only seven captured terrorists. Judging by their sizes, the
leader was missing.
"Argh!"
Hearing a sound from above, Shirone looked up. Plu was floating in the
air, swinging her magic staff.
With a thud, an invisible object fell to the ground. Moments later, the
leader appeared, trembling from the blow to his back.
Plu landed lightly and said,
"Where do you think you're going?"
"Damn annoying magician!"
The leader clutched his chest and tore off his robe.
A man with pale purple skin was revealed. He was bald, with knife-like
ears and sharp fangs protruding from his clenched teeth.
"If it's come to this, I'll at least kill you."
"A vampire, as I thought."
The ability to turn invisible for a short time was unique to vampires.
But since he wasn't suffering from the sunlight, he wasn't pureblood.
'If he were pureblood, everyone here would be in trouble.'
Plu kept her eyes on the vampire and spoke to the anti-terrorism
unit.
"I'll handle him. Block his escape routes. Vampires are experts at
escaping."
The unit immediately blocked the paths around Gold Tower. They knew that
if they moved clumsily, the vampire would vanish without a trace.
Plu twirled her magic staff out of habit, then gripped the end and
pointed it forward.
"Come at me, scum."
"Arrogant woman!"
Blades extended from the vampire's ten fingers. His body blurred into an
afterimage, moving like a torrent through space.
Vampires were fast. It wasn't physical strength but the unique power of
their half-spirit, half-demon transformation.
Plu half-closed her eyes, moving only her gaze left and right.
Wham! As she twisted her body and thrust her staff, the vampire's claws
were blocked midway.
"Grr! This...!"
"You underestimated me. A mutt like you can't be faster than the shared
senses of the Spirit Zone."
"Shut up! Shut up!"
The vampire slashed his claws at a rate of dozens of times per
second.
As the sharp friction sounds rang out, Plu retreated, moving at an
invisible speed around her. She gave up the chase and struck her staff into the ground.
"Phoenix Formation."
A giant firebird erupted from the crystal orb, spewing fireballs in all
directions.
The firebird, resonating with the Spirit Zone, shot fireballs at
everyone within its radius. It couldn't distinguish friend from foe, but its reflex speed was
overpowered.
"Argh!"
The vampire, hit by a fireball, rolled on the ground, his body burning.
By the time the flames were out, he had no strength left to move.
Plu pressed her staff against his chest and said,
"You can withstand the sun, but not fire. Surrender quietly."
"Don't talk nonsense! Do you know our pain? Do you understand the misery
of living in the sewers?"
"I don't. Humans can't even save themselves. We can't afford to care
about your situation."
"Foolish and arrogant humans! I was once human too. How long do you
think you can rule the world? One day, you'll pay a great price."
"Maybe someday. But not today."
"Heh, we'll see."
The vampire's body began to break apart like a jigsaw puzzle. The
grotesque transformation made Plu step back, removing her staff.
"What the...?"
"This is the result of your actions!"
The vampire's body scattered into hundreds of black bats, flying towards
the hostages.
Plu immediately aimed her staff. But she couldn't use the Phoenix
Formation near the hostages.
"Ahh! Please save me!"
The hostages clung to each other as they saw the swarm of bats.
At that moment, Shirone stepped in front of them and brought his fist
down.
'Radiant Burst!'
Pop! Pop! Pop! Pop! Pop!
The barrier of light pulsed rapidly, repelling the swarm of bats. The
bats, thrown outside the barrier, tangled together and reverted to the vampire's original form, tumbling
to the ground.
"Argh!"
The vampire's fingers, stretched out towards the sun, slowly
relaxed.
When he finally stopped moving, the emergency response team rushed in to
arrest him.
The hostages slowly opened their eyes. The terrorists were being
transported one by one to a steel prison.
"It's over. They've all been captured."
"It's over! We're saved!"
The hostages cheered and clapped.
The hero of the moment was Shirone. He had bought time as a hostage and
saw the situation through to the end, earning him their gratitude.
"That was amazing! Justice prevails!"
"Such bravery! You deserve to work at the Magic
Association!"
Shirone's face flushed with embarrassment. He was uneasy about people
mistaking him for a Magic Association employee. Bowing his head, he felt a tap on his shoulder and
turned to see Aria.
"Are you okay?"
"Yes, I'm fine."
"Thank you so much for today. I'd like to repay you..."
"Oh, no! That's not necessary!"
Aria didn't want to let Shirone go. Not only had he helped her, but she
also felt a fateful connection, remembering him from the carriage ride.
"I'm Aria from the Teshia family. My father is the kingdom's top
diplomat. If I tell him about today, it could be very helpful..."
At that moment, Plu grabbed Shirone's head and pushed him down.
"That's not necessary."
"What?"
Plu bowed politely to the bewildered Aria.
"I'm sorry. This boy is still a trainee, not a full employee. The
Association organized this operation to capture the terrorists, so please understand."
Shirone, still bowing, glanced sideways.
Strictly speaking, he wasn't even a trainee. But seeing Plu's stern
expression, he remembered what he needed to say.
"Yes, I'm sorry. This won't happen again."
Aria didn't care whether Shirone was a full employee or a trainee. She
just wanted to get to know him better.
"No, I just wanted to express my gratitude..."
Plu firmly cut her off with a wave of her hand.
"He was just doing his duty. Personal rewards are strictly
prohibited by the Association. Thank you for understanding. Let's go, kid!"
"Ouch! That hurts!"
Shirone was practically dragged away by Plu, leaving Gold Tower.
Aria watched them go, feeling a pang of regret.
