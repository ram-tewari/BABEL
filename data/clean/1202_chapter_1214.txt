Even as the minister of Kesia reported on state affairs, Fermi remained
trapped within the solitude of his own mind.
"…So, after consultation between the departments, we sent the official
document. What was it—oh, the International Tribunal."

"Handling that was a real pain. With the former king having passed,

Fermi's eyes narrowed.
'Just how much of the drug did they take?'
When used properly, Angel could reduce pain to a manageable level for
daily life.
Of course, it didn't make it disappear entirely, so the discomfort was
still intense—but…
'Why do they keep ignoring the dosage?'
The fact that such weak-willed people were sitting in ministerial
positions was exasperating.
Sigh.
Fermi masked his emotions.
"Thank you for your efforts. What about Lanstein's whereabouts? We need
information within the next six hours."
There was no reply.
When Fermi raised his gaze, the ministers only returned it with
indifferent expressions, remaining silent.
"Is there a problem?"
"Well, we haven't had a single day of rest since the Holy War began.
Everyone is exhausted."
Exhausted?
'You've got to be kidding me.'
Sure, human ability has its limits. But weren't these people supposed to
be the heads of their respective departments?
The chief secretary added, "Your Majesty, the workload is too much.
Everyone's worn out. At this rate, they won't hold out much longer."
'Right.'
That's what defeat looks like.
If they'd at least admitted it and swallowed their resentment, Fermi

'So what are they expecting?'
Wanting problems to be solved without backing it with actual work—wasn't
that a form of violence?
Because they're hoping someone else will do it.
'They're using the fact that we're "in this together" as leverage to
guilt me. "If you don't want us to sink, you have to sacrifice more."'
The contradiction of an organization where the ones who work the hardest
suffer the most.
'Should I fire them?'

overall efficiency of the organization.
Above all, time was running short.
'It'd be satisfying—like gaining a thousand more years of life—to beat

But that wouldn't solve the problem. So Fermi put on his usual polite
smile.
"You're right. I've been too demanding. I know how hard each of you is
working for the kingdom."
He wasn't the least bit moved.
Knowing full well they didn't actually believe those words made Fermi
sick to his stomach.
Hold it in.
He had known since before he could even read that human beings were
inherently self-centered.
'It's fine. I'll do it. There's no need to blame anyone. I'm doing this
because it's what I want to do.'
It's not about whether something is possible or not—if it must be done,
you find a way.
'That's what winning is.'

'I'll have to leave finding Guitar Man to the field staff. The

'How…'
How can they say they're tired so easily?
'Winning is supposed to be hard. Even a kid knows that. You're fighting

Fermi clenched his fist tightly.
Stay calm. If you let emotion control you, you'll ruin everything. You

The former king, Manolka, once described Fermi with a single
word:
Capable.
A man who could resist being swayed by emotion and endure anything until

"Any non-essential tasks. They're not particularly important. Please

you."

"Ghhh…!"

'Damn it.'

'I can't get entangled. Not even slightly.'

Crack. The bone snapped, and pain shot through his brain—but it would

desk.
Mother.

While everyone only looked out for themselves, what allowed her to keep

The Law of Large Numbers.

That's why she had left her son's side.
