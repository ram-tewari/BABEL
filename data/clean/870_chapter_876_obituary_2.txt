Chapter 876:
Obituary (2)
The capital of Tormia, Vashka.
In the administrative district of Alrog Street, there was a luxury hotel
where high-ranking officials stayed.
Hotel Desika, Room 1403.
Dante sat at the table by the window, looking down at the peaceful
morning street scene.
He was naked, with his arm draped over his knee, holding an iced coffee
in his hand.
"Pisho..."
What woke him from his sleep was an obituary delivered through the Magic
Association.
The sound of water from the bathroom stopped, and a woman came out,
shaking her hair.
"What's this? Setting the mood so early in the
morning."
Having spent a hot night together, she didn't bother to put on clothes
and sat at the vanity.
The woman, brushing her hair, looked at Dante through the mirror and
asked,
"Did you have a nightmare?"
Dante chuckled.
"Who knows? Is there a nightmare scarier than reality right
now?"
The woman disliked philosophy.
"You're really strange today. Aren't you going to wash up? You need
to go to work."
The woman thought Dante worked at the Royal Records Management
Department.
But in reality, he was the Deputy Director of the National Intelligence
Service, Code Black.
The fact that he had access to the highest-level encryption system,
known as "Rope," meant that all the nation's information passed through his mind for
storage.
The woman, applying makeup, said,
"I'll be late today. I'm meeting a friend. Don't wait up for me,
just go to bed."
"There's no need."
Dante turned to look at her back and said,
"Let's stop seeing each other."
The woman's hand, holding the lipstick, froze, and she turned around
with a frown.
"Are you... talking to me?"
"Both of you."
Another woman in the bed kicked off the blanket and sat up
abruptly.
"What! Me too?"
She approached Dante and protested.
"How can you do this? We haven't been seeing each other for long.
You should at least give it a month!"
The woman at the vanity sighed.
"Already tired of us?"
She knew from the start that he was a man she could never have
forever.
"It's not that. Life itself is boring."
"Then what's the reason?"
Dante smiled, bathed in the morning sunlight.
"The real one is coming."
It was Tormia's turn.
Sadness filled Shirone's eyes.
"Pisho is dead..."
It was an event foretold from the moment he became the host of the alien
organism Argones.
But questions remained.
"What does 'dead' mean?" Amy spoke up.
"From what Pisho told me, a person who becomes the host of Argones
has no lifespan limit. Of course, they would be buried under the infinite proliferation of cancer cells,
but they couldn't be declared dead."
"After the graduation exam, Pisho left. He must have been confined
in a quarantine facility.
Many experiments would have been conducted there, and what happened
afterward, no one knew except a few in the kingdom.
Maya asked,
"Is that all Iruki's letter said?"
Amy checked again.
"Yes. The use of the words 'immediately' suggests it's quite
urgent. There's nothing else besides the obituary."
"Iruki wouldn't miss that. It might be classified. We'll find out
when we get there."
Shirone looked at Maya.
"Your schedule must be busy. Can you go? To Pisho's
funeral."
"Of course. He was one of the people who stayed the longest with me
in the graduation class."
Even when Maya was the troublemaker of the graduation class, he always
treated her equally, a quiet older brother.
"Alright. Let's leave right away."
Although the Holy War's permission was granted, Amy was worried about
leaving her unit behind.
"Will it be okay? Without a commander..."
Tess pounded her chest.
"What are you worried about? I'm here. I'll take care of everything
and join the main force, so go without worry."
Rian spoke up.
"I'll stay here too. Is that okay?"
Tess, surprised, asked,
"What? You're staying here?"
"From now on, we'll be inside the Holy War's defensive wall, so
Shirone won't be in danger. On the other hand,  Hell's army is still advancing south. At this rate,
Tormia will be within reach."
Rian intended to be a shield.
"Yes. Thank you, Rian."
Amy said,
"The three of us will take Kaidra. We'll jump to Tormia from Kazra,
then to Creas."
"And then?"
Iruki hadn't specified the exact destination.
"Well, there should be some arrangements. If not, we'll go to the
school. The teachers will attend the funeral too."
Maya nodded.
"Oh, right. Everyone will gather."
It seemed like it would be a somber reunion.
Shirone's group arrived in the city of Creas at dawn the next
day.
For Shirone, who had been wandering the battlefield, the peaceful
morning preparations felt unfamiliar.
'It used to be like this.'
They finished a simple meal at a dawn restaurant next to the Tormia
branch of the Magic Association.
"No one is coming out."
There was no word from the association either.
"It might be thorough. Those related to Pisho should know, but it's
better if others don't."
Leaving the restaurant, Shirone's group headed to Alpheas Magic School
as planned.
Seeing the main gate, Shirone felt a lump in his throat.
'This is where it all started.'
Climbing over the wall of Alpheas Magic School at the age of twelve felt
like a distant memory.
Amy and Maya stood beside Shirone, looking up at the arch with nostalgic
expressions.
"Let's go in."
The guard, checking Amy's ID, opened the gate.
"What is this?"
The familiar scenery was the same, but the atmosphere had changed
significantly.
Everyone walked in rows, and even the young students had a fierce look
in their eyes.
"The militarization of the school. It's inevitable during
wartime."
With a heavy heart, they went to the principal's office, where Alpheas
and Olivia stood up in surprise.
"You..."
The surprise was brief, and their expressions softened as they thought
of Pisho's death.
"You're here. Come in."
The three bowed their heads.
"Hello, Principal, Vice-Principal."
Alpheas smiled warmly at the three protagonists of the golden
generation's graduation class.
"Yes. Have a seat."
Drinking the tea Olivia served, Shirone's group listened to the school's
situation.
"The graduation exam has been abolished starting this year.
Students are drafted into the army right after finishing regular classes."
"That's unfortunate."
"Yes. The benefits of competition are gone, but more importantly,
the education itself has become quite brutal."
Without exams, students had to undergo intense practical training before
being sent to the battlefield.
"You've heard about Pisho, right?" Shirone nodded.
"Yes. What happened?"
"I don't know. The Holy War is controlling the information. The
funeral is scheduled for three days later."
"Why three days later?"
"It's a political issue. But the main reason is probably... because
of you, Shirone."
"Me?"
Olivia spoke.
"It's not confirmed, but there's a rumor that Pisho left a will for
you. It must not be disclosed until you arrive."
Shirone blinked.
'Pisho left something for me?'
If they didn't have a deep friendship during school, there was only one
thing to think of.
'One of the Five Great Stars of the Ivory Tower. Or Yahweh.'
It was an issue that could be handled at that level.
"Since you're here, why don't you look around the graduation class?
Though now it's more of a memory."
Trainee would be a more fitting term.
Following Alpheas through the steel door, they saw thirty students
receiving guidance from Siena and Thad.
"Focus more!"
The pale-faced students were casting their specialized magic towards the
front.
"This is the morning class. The key is to keep casting magic
without rest."
Siena shouted.
"Karna! What are you doing! Get your head straight!"
Pointing at a girl whose power had weakened, she bit her lip and sat
down defiantly.
"Karna! You..."
"This is unreasonable!"
Karna's shout brought silence.
"What is this, every day! The same training, the ridiculous
intensity! We can't even recover!"
"Get up. This is your last chance."
Seeing Siena's stern look, Mark helped Karna up.
"Karna, let's try again."
"Let go! I have to say this! This isn't magic. It's just
overworking us! We came here to get stronger!"
Siena took out a document.
"Mino Karna. As of today, you are expelled from Alpheas Magic
School..."
Shirone approached.
"Get up."
Siena turned her head hastily, and the graduation students' eyes filled
with shock.
"Sh-Shirone senior..."
These were the students who had dreamed big after hearing Shirone's
inspiring graduation speech.
And now, he stood before them as the world's greatest mage.
"Get up and start training again. Not magic? No, this is
magic."
Karna, on the verge of tears, said,
"No. This isn't magic. There's no change, no creation. Was it like
this for you too?"
"Of course, it was different in your time. But the essence is the
same. Instead of complaining, cast magic one more time."
If Karna had to name the person she respected the most, it would be
Shirone without a doubt.
That's why it hurt even more.
"Why? What changes if I do that? What do I gain from casting one
more time right now?"
"Even the memory of having done it one more time will
remain."
Karna's lips tightened.
"Accumulate that memory endlessly. In the end, that's what will
protect you. That's all there is."
"That's all there is..."
"Yes. Don't leave room in your mind for the despairing thought of
having given up because it was hard. It will catch up with you someday. But if you get up now, there are
no limits."
Karna's eyes regained their cold clarity.
"Of course, there are times when you need to rest. I did too. But
looking back, I realized something."
Shirone smiled.
"That it's all pointless."
"...Haha."
"A mage doesn't rest when they're tired or exhausted. A mage rests
when a single break can increase the chance of victory."
Shirone extended his hand.
"You can control it, right? You're a mage too."
"Yes, senior!"
As Karna stood up with a smile, the students' determination flared up
like fire.
"Alright! Let's do this! I'll never give up!"
Shirone watched with satisfaction and bowed to Siena as he returned to
his place.
'Shirone...'
No longer a student, Siena also bowed in gratitude.
'Thank you.'
Mark's magic exploded with more power than ever.
'Senior Shirone is watching!'
Maria followed, and the other students poured all their mental energy
into their magic, forgetting their fatigue.
'Please give me advice too.'
The world's strongest mage was watching.
That fact alone brought the students' concentration to an all-time
high.
