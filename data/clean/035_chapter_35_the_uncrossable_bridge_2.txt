Chapter 35:
The Uncrossable Bridge (2)
The intense psychological battle was invisible to the distant spectators, who only saw a
chaotic rush. The competition was decided in split seconds.
'You haven't fallen until you've fallen.'
Even as the cliff loomed one step away, Shirone's body showed no glow. The watching
students' hearts pounded. Leaping from the edge, Shirone soared as far as he could. The overwhelming
sensation of floating. Strong winds rocked his body, and birds flew in flocks far below.
Shirone focused solely on instant movement. At the critical point of his leap, his body
finally flashed and shot forward like a spark.
With Shirone last, everyone completed their first turn. Naturally, by the second turn,
the rankings had flipped. Those who had started early now lagged behind.
Shirone made up for the gap from the initial run. Most importantly, he managed to
narrowly overtake Mark.
Mark was shocked to see Shirone surge ahead from behind. How late had Shirone started to
overtake him in the first turn? Though the lead group's gap was still within ten meters, making the rankings
fluid, his plan had already failed.
"You bastard! Don't stop there!"
A hair's breadth apart, Shirone and Mark cast their instant movement spells almost
simultaneously.
***
Ten minutes before the promotion exam began.
A girl trudged toward the training grounds with a gloomy expression.
Erlang Maria.
The third daughter of the Erlang family, a third-tier noble house, she had awakened to
her magical talent early and enrolled in Alpheas Magic Academy at nineteen.
Though now stuck in Class Seven and mocked by Mark, four years her junior, she hadn't
always been unnoticeable.
She had fought fiercely since enrolling at ten, spending nine years in relentless
competition. But at some point, it all felt meaningless.
She couldn't find a reason to claw her way to the upper classes. Promotion would only
lead to new competitions. Her life was a marathon with no finish line.
"Haah, what am I even doing now?"
Perhaps she had missed her timing. The moment she realized she was the oldest in Class
Seven, everything felt hollow.
The probability of a Class Ten enrollee reaching Class Seven was about forty percent.
Breaking through such odds was something to be proud of.
Maria had been overjoyed when she entered Class Seven at fifteen. But the happiness was
fleeting, and the narrowing gaps between promotions filled her with dread.
Ironically, her best performance had been in her first year in Class Seven.
But her grades had declined since, and now even freshmen looked down on her.
She feared competition. She had to stake her life on it, be more tenacious, more perfect.
Such thoughts suffocated her.
Was inertia keeping her in school, or was there still a spark of passion left? Or was it
just stubbornness over the time she'd invested?
Maria, who had entered the academy out of love for magic, sighed as she watched her
identity fade.
The promotion exam was someone else's affair. Yet staying in the classroom with kids for
self-study wounded her pride.
Lost in thought, she wandered aimlessly. Then, on a whim, she decided to head to the
training grounds.
Perhaps the dazzling talents up there were engaged in fierce competition? Watching them
would only deepen her inferiority, but she could feel her passion waning.
Grasping at straws, or hoping for some small incident to stir her numb heart, she took
those hollow steps.
"Huh?"
A blue signal flare shot up from the peak—Ethela's magic. The exam preparations were
underway.
The students' cheers echoed. Lacking the confidence to join them, Maria stopped. Their
cheers weren't for her.
'My place isn't there. Only the best of Class Seven would be there.'
At that moment, a demonic thought took root in her mind, guiding her steps.
At first, she just didn't want to ignore the possibility.
But by the time she regained her senses, she was already at the control room of the
Uncrossable Bridge.
Perhaps it wasn't over yet—that hope.
Clutching that hope, she opened the control room door. A staff member sat in a chair,
yawning. Normally, a teacher would supervise, but today, with the bridge's mechanisms off and all staff
mobilized for student safety, no one was around.
The relaxed staff member stretched and whistled. But soon, his eyelids drooped, and his
head nodded.
Maria stood behind the staff member, now deep in slumber.
A sleep-inducing spell was nearly ineffective against mages but worked well on ordinary
people.
Maria examined the control panel. The air net's safety mechanism was active, and a rotary
switch adjusted the bridge's difficulty.
Her finger hovered over the safety switch. Her body trembled. Was this the right thing?
Beyond right or wrong, was it even within her capabilities?
Just press the button. The horrific consequences were unimaginable for her usual self,
but when possessed by an idea, only the sweet reward filled her mind.
Maria pressed the button. In execution, it was laughably simple. No horror, no
tragedy—just a button press.
The warning lights of the air net at the cliff faded from red.

Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang! Bang!
Explosive sounds erupted from the group of students. Twenty of them flickered as they
raced across the 1,000-meter高空.
By the third turn, the gap between the lead and the rear was still narrow.
The massive human swarm moved as one.
But differences in skill were apparent. Most of those who had started early couldn't
withstand the void beneath their feet, their focus wavering.
"Gasp! Are we really falling? Here?"
Looking down, the abyss stretched endlessly. Was the safety net really there? Even if it
was, the thought of falling 100 meters was terrifying.
As their Spirit Zones weakened, their leap distances shortened. The gap with the lead
group widened instantly.
