Chapter 71:
At the Summit (5)
The mountain animals were torn apart, their remains hanging from branches like laundry.
Among them were even fierce predators.
The arriving children covered their mouths. Not screaming was commendable enough for
hunters.
"A-Altol. What happened? Did the Cowbear really do this?"
Martin's voice trembled, and Lumina turned away, eyes tightly shut. Shirone was the first
to act.
"Let's go. This isn't something we can handle."
"No, we need to see more. We have to know what's going on."
"Altol! This is serious!"
"That's why we're going! If this thing stays in the mountains, the game will flee! We'll
starve!"
"I get that, but it's too dangerous. Let's report it to the city."
"Report? Have you gone soft hanging out with nobles? Who'd care about hunters?"
"Idiot, that's what I'm saying! This isn't some minor incident. Look at those animals.
Don't you see how dangerous this is?"
Martin interjected. "S-Shirone. Altol. Look over there."
The bushes rustled. Shirone and Altol nocked arrows simultaneously, aiming at the sound.
Rustle. Rustle.
The shaking leaves heightened the tension. The children readied their weapons, waiting
for the mystery creature to appear. When it did, their arrows instinctively aimed higher.
"T-this can't be…"
Altol's voice, usually fearless, was laced with terror.
"Grrrr."
A wolf-like monster over two meters tall hunched before them, muscles bulging like
boulders. Its waist was slender, arms reaching its knees, and its hooked claws were as long as a human
pinky. Reverse-jointed legs supported its body like a cat's, making it agile.
Martin barely managed to speak. "A… monster."
Evolutionists believed all animals, including humans, descended from a single origin. But
monsters came from an entirely different lineage. They were stronger, faster, and even intelligent.
'Wolf-like, bipedal… an Ulk.'
Shirone recalled his studies. Ulks lacked language but could express hundreds of emotions
through sounds. They lived in tribes, exhibiting social behavior.
With their claws, speed, and strength, they were balanced monsters even hunters avoided
when in groups.
'But why here? This region has no monsters.'
Only one explanation came to mind: the Ulks had lost a tribal war and migrated here. The
wounds covering the creature's body supported this. Its fur was matted with blood—impossible to tell how
much was its own.
The children's ragged breaths reminded Altol of his duty as leader. He had to act.
'It's still a living thing. A headshot will kill it.'
Altol took a deep breath and fired. The Ulk, aware of the threat, simply tilted its head,
dodging effortlessly. Then it laughed, scratching its throat.
"Grrr. Grrrr."
The children gasped. But the real horror was yet to come.
Crack!
The Ulk shattered a tree trunk with its jaws. Then it roared at Altol.
"Grrraaah!"
"Damn it! Run!"
Seeing the display of raw power, Altol abandoned all thought of firing again.
They fled toward the summit, Shirone lingering to fire one last arrow.
"Grrraaah!"
The provocation enraged the Ulk, but it bought the others time.
The creature lunged, claws aimed at Shirone's skull. Shirone's body flashed, teleporting
him into the air.
"Grr?"
The Ulk stared as Shirone hovered above. Teleporting consecutively in the deep mountains
was risky.
'Not here. The summit.'
Altol urged the struggling children onward. Choosing not to descend was wise—they'd have
tumbled downhill.
"Faster! Run!"
They abandoned their gear, sprinting uphill. Lumina cried out.
"Shirone? Where is he?"
"He's buying time! Just move!"
Altol worried for Shirone, but panic wouldn't help. His priority was the others.
"Grrraaah!"
As they reached the summit, the Ulk's roar echoed from below. Altol's face twisted. If
the monster was here, Shirone had either escaped or died.
"Damn it! Nowhere to hide!"
The summit was a flat clearing ending in a cliff. Twenty meters wasn't insurmountable,
but they had no time to climb down.
"Altol! It's coming!"
The Ulk scaled the slope, claws scraping the ground. Sunlight glinted off its muscular
frame. Altol stepped forward as bait.
"Over here!"
But with the cliff at their backs, escape was impossible. Altol turned, fists raised.
Even his trained muscles were child's play against a monster.
"Screw this! I don't care anymore!"
As he swung, the Ulk vanished.
"Run!"
Altol whirled around. Shirone was suddenly there, grappling with the Ulk.
"H-how?"
He tried to help, but Shirone teleported again, leaving him stunned.
"Whoa. That's magic, right?"
The children gaped as Shirone blinked around the Ulk, dodging every attack.
"Grrraaah!"
Frustrated, the Ulk turned toward the group.
"Aaaah!"
The terrified children scattered—except Lumina, frozen in place.
"Lumina! Run!"
"A…ah…"
Shirone reached out, combining photon output with omnipresence. But Lumina was outside
the Spirit Zone's range.
'No! Too far!'
He reshaped the zone into a cross, finally sensing the Ulk.
'Photon Output!'
A flash struck the Ulk's eyes. It howled, shielding its face.
"He did it! Shirone beat it!"
The dazzling light made them think the monster was dead. But Shirone knew photons had no
physical force.
"Lumina! Run! Now!"
Lumina tried but collapsed, legs locked. The children realized the truth.
"What? It just shoots light?"
"Grrraaah!"
The Ulk recovered and pounced. Its claws snagged Lumina's hair tie, freeing her locks.
Shirone grabbed the Ulk's waist and teleported.
"Shirone! No!"
"Grrraaah!"
They soared skyward, then plummeted toward the cliff.
The Ulk thrashed, but Shirone kept teleporting, disorienting it. He'd set the
teleportation vector downward—a forbidden technique.
'What now? If we fall, it won't die.'
Teleporting canceled kinetic energy, making the fall harmless. But releasing the magic
meant facing the Ulk's fury.
'I need mass…!'
Then, a shiver ran down Shirone's spine. An overwhelming sensation flooded his
mind—fragments of thought: the universe's birth, gauge symmetry, a swirling vortex above his bed. Mass
wasn't force. It was the power to breach cosmic symmetry.
The void he'd felt upon returning from infinity—the missing piece was gravity.
As omnipresence aligned, the dissonance resolved. The condition for magic was met.
Shirone photonized the Ulk and hurled it downward.
"Grra…!"
The impact silenced its cry. The body bounced with a hollow thud.
Shirone landed gracefully with Rainbow Drop, staring at the corpse.
Instant death. The broken bones proved one thing: light had weight.
"Haah. Haah."
Matter wasn't born with mass. Another element granted and erased it—a particle beyond
human perception.
The Higgs boson.
That was the truth Shirone had grasped in infinity, the reason for broken symmetry.
"Got it. I finally understand."
His fists clenched, trembling with emotion so intense it brought tears.
"Kyaaaah!"
A scream echoed from the cliff. Shirone looked up.
"No way…?"
Ulks were social creatures. It was unlikely to travel alone.
"No!"
He teleported back to the summit, freezing at the sight. Dozens of Ulks surrounded the
children.
'I underestimated their society.'
The kids whimpered.
"W-what do we do? We're dead."
"I didn't even get married. I don't want to die."
Shirone didn't want to die either. But now, he had the means to fight.
"It's okay, guys. I'll handle this."
"How? Just run. You can escape with magic."
"It's fine. Now, breaking the rules doesn't matter."
He stepped toward the pack.
"Shirone! It's dangerous!"
Altol's cry went unheard. Shirone focused, recalling his epiphany.
The silver-furred chieftain nodded, and its lieutenant charged.
"Grrraaah!"
Shirone gathered photons in his palm.
'Not enough. More… more…'
The light paled, then flared blindingly white. The Ulk closed in.
Shirone gauged the distance, altering the photons' properties. As the children's screams
peaked, the Ulk swung.
Shirone teleported a meter back, eyes wide.
'Now!'
He hurled the compressed photons. A pale flash struck the Ulk's stomach, crushing it
before erupting into a golden beam.
"Grrraaah!"
The creature flew back, colliding with others and tumbling down the mountain.
The children stared in disbelief.
"No way. Did you see that? That huge monster just flew dozens of meters."
"How? Is that magic?"
The chieftain sniffed, its intelligence evident as it spoke in broken human tongue.
"Ma…gic…user…"
Countless eyes fixed on Shirone. But he only glared at the chieftain.
He could win.
The photons' mass was minuscule, but near-light speed granted them immense force.
This was a magic only Shirone could wield—the Photon Cannon.
