Chapter 553:
D-63 Days.
The harsh winds of competition swept through the entire graduating
class, and even Dorothy was no exception.
At school, her perpetually absentminded expression had earned her the
nickname "The Fourth-Dimensional Girl," but her eyes, magnified behind her large glasses as she
dismantled a tin robot, were sharper than ever.
The tin robot she was working on was named Hickory.
To Dorothy, a controller-class mage, Hickory was as precious as her life
and the core of her magic.
'I need to evolve the algorithm.'
Despite finishing the first half of the graduation program in the top
tier of Class Two, the barrier to Class One still loomed high.
The thought of several first-years from Class Three already advancing to
Class One kept her awake at night.
Connected to her through the Spirit Zone, Hickory began clanking as it
roamed around the room.
Working with algorithms meant controlling the processes leading to
results, so there was no need for combat simulations to confirm functionality.
Hickory, which looked like a piece of junk no matter how you looked at
it, hovered its heavy frame in midair and lit up its round eyes.
'Applying Dorothy Algorithm. Version 4.3.'
With a blaring siren, Hickory's neck rotated rapidly before crashing to
the ground with a thud and a vibration, its neck now broken.
Watching Hickory collapse, Dorothy's expression shifted to one of blank
indifference, devoid of thought or emotion.
"..."
Annoyed to the point where she couldn't muster the will to pick Hickory
back up, she crawled into bed and pulled the blanket over her face.
A muffled groan escaped from beneath the blanket.
D-61 Days.
"Faster! Faster! You're too slow!"
"Ughhhh!"
As Screamer unleashed a relentless flurry of strikes, the sandbag,
reinforced with thick iron cores, swayed heavily.
The leather bag filled with sand had circular target points marked on
it, with numbers indicating the sequence of strikes.
"Is that all you've got? How are you going to become a champion at this
rate?!"
Screamer's father, Pyrocker, who had appointed himself as Screamer's
coach, yelled directly into his son's ear.
Pyrocker, a martial arts fanatic, held a prominent position in the
Kingdom of Tormia's heavy infantry and even possessed a professional license in the Colosseum, boasting
a record of 23 fights, 21 wins, and 2 losses.
However, he had temporarily withdrawn from the martial arts world after
suffering a humiliating defeat to a mage in a kingdom-hosted event match. The story of how Screamer,
disgusted by that match, declared his intent to enroll in a magic academy was well-known within their
family.
"Damn it!"
Screamer's face bore clear signs of pain as he continued Pyrocker's
"Iron Fist Project."
The seven-strike combo, which had to be completed within a split second,
was endlessly repeated over thousands of cycles.
"Arghhh!"
Though the sound of the strikes was sharp and powerful, Pyrocker grew
even more irritated.
"Your accuracy is off! Faster, more precise, and stronger!"
It was an unreasonable demand, especially given that Screamer was
struggling just to move his arms.
"Don't stop! Keep punching! You need to ingrain a combo so devastating
that anyone caught in it is guaranteed to fall!"
As he repeated the same motion endlessly, pain shot through specific
parts of his body as though they were being torn apart.
'How am I supposed to punch faster than this?!'
Even as he teetered on the edge of his pain threshold, Pyrocker remained
unshaken.
"Does it hurt? What a shame—because I'm not hurting at all."
'I swear, I'm going to punch him one of these days!'
"Want to know a harsh truth? No one cares about your pain. No one cares
how hard you've worked. A fighter who doesn't deliver results is nothing more than a punching
bag!"
"Ughhhh!"
Losing control, Screamer threw a powerful straight punch and stopped his
training, but the heavy sandbag barely moved.
"Huff! Huff!"
He was so out of breath he felt like he might throw up.
"Who said you could rest? Keep punching!"
"... Just one minute. Let me rest for one minute."
Pyrocker's expression softened, and he spoke in a gentler tone.
"You want to get stronger, don't you? Want me to tell you how?"
As Screamer looked up, his father's face transformed back into that of a
demon.
"There is no such thing, idiot! The very idea of looking for a way is
just making excuses! Want to know why? Because the path to strength is so simple even a three-year-old
could understand it! Punch! Get stronger! Punch! Get even stronger!"
'My arms won't move...'
The one-minute break had only caused his muscles to cramp.
"Can't do it? Then admit it! When the champion walks by, just clap for
them from the sidelines!"
"You damn...!"
Screamer, boiling with rage, shoved the sandbag away to create distance
before charging in again with a flurry of punches.
"Do it properly! Accuracy! Accuracy!"
D-59 Days.
Sitting cross-legged on a rock overlooking a stream, Shirone looked
gaunt and frail.
The only thing he had eaten was Miro's herb porridge, and all the
nutrients he had consumed had been drained by his brain. His already light body had lost another three
kilograms.
'I can't think about anything.'
He wished he could just take his brain out and toss it into the
trash.
Apart from three hours of sleep, this was his only moment of
rest.
At first, he had doubted how meditation could help his training, but
recently he had realized it was essential.
Without the four hours of meditation, he would have either died from
exhaustion or gone insane and taken his own life.
'Nothing is meaningless. We just don't bother to look.'
Recalling Miro's words, Shirone stared at the scenery before him, though
he still didn't understand what he was supposed to be looking for.
'Leaves hanging on branches. They sway in the wind.'
He noticed a spider building its web between the branches.
'I wonder what that spider is thinking as it lives its life.'
As he watched aimlessly, the sun began to set.
'Night is falling. I wonder if the spider managed to catch something to
eat.'
The spider had likely eaten something.
It was such an obvious fact, but since he had never bothered to think
about it before, it felt oddly fascinating.
"Shirone, it's time for night training."
Miro's voice, calm and steady, sent a heavy thud through Shirone's
chest.
The four hours outside of hell felt shorter than the one hour within
it.
"... Yes."
With a quiet sigh, Shirone followed Miro toward the training
hall.
D-55 Days.
The annex in the royal palace of Tormia was as grand and luxurious as a
noble's mansion.
Pony, the only royal at the magic school, threw herself into the
specially modified pool in the annex.
As she floated to the surface, dripping wet, the maids anxiously held
towels and fidgeted.
"Miss, please rest for today. What if you catch a
cold?"
"It's fine. That's enough for today. Go and rest."
"But…"
"That's an order."
Startled by her cold tone, the maids put down the towels and left the
pool area.
"Phew!"
Pony parted her blonde hair to the sides and entered her Spirit
Zone.
As the propeller installed in the pool began to spin, numerous
whirlpools formed.
Her specialty was hydromancy, specifically the torrent branch.
The goal of this training was to completely reverse the flow of water
created by the machine.
'I will become stronger.'
Royals are noble and therefore do not labor.
But that was only a privilege of the direct line, and it was not a life
goal for Pony, who could not ascend to the throne.
'Royalty. I don't need authority given by others.'
As she cast the torrent spell, the water's flow changed rapidly, and the
force of the machine set higher than the limit surged, causing her vision to blur.
'I will do it! I will establish my own authority!'
The sight of foam rising to the surface made it look as if the water was
boiling.
D-51 Days.
Shirone's sequence method finally began to surpass the trillion
mark.
As he passed 1.2 trillion, the light of his incarnation shimmered
brilliantly, and Miro carefully observed the change.
'The change is definitely noticeable. But it's still far from reaching
the singularity.'
When one reaches the state of unity of mind and body, the uniqueness of
the incarnation affects the laws of the world.
Although it was still unclear what that uniqueness was, the magnitude of
the change was significant.
"Grrr!"
In the midst of repeated training, Shirone's sequence method extended
endlessly.
Surpassing 1 trillion, reaching 2 trillion, and then 3 trillion, the
growth rate spoke of Shirone's talent, but the quintillion mark was still far off.
"Gasp!"
With a final cry, Shirone fell to his knees.
It felt like something snapped in his head, and he couldn't think of
anything.
"Sob…"
Unconsciously, his face twisted into a grimace.
'It's hard. It's so hard.'
He had fought many battles, but fighting against himself was a
completely different concept.
Defeating oneself meant killing oneself.
In a state where he could neither live nor die, he had to progress even
by one from the previous day.
Having counted up to 3 trillion, he was at his limit.
"I don't think I can make it in time."
"That again? I told you not to think about it."
"My head is spinning. Let me sleep for a bit and then…"
"Do it!"
Miro's shout made Shirone lift his head with eyes full of
hostility.
The beautiful Miro now looked like a vicious witch.
"You say there's no time and then want to sleep? Keep going. All
other thoughts are delusions."
"I might really die."
"I told you not to whine."
Shirone's lips trembled with sorrow.
"When you think it's unfair, remember this. Even at this moment,
someone is training with the determination to die. There are countless people who want to become
stronger, even if it means selling their soul to the devil. There's no reason you should claim the glory
of victory over them. If time is short, reduce your sleep."
Even though he was already sleeping only three hours, Miro's words were
as sharp as a knife.
"It's fine, even if you reduce it by an hour…"
"You won't die! You won't die, Screamer!"
"Aaah!"
As if trying to erase Pyroca's voice, Screamer screamed while hitting
the sandbag.
But the more he did, the harsher his father's whipping became.
"Don't rely on talent! Don't take pride in your efforts! Fight only
for yourself!"
"Aaah! Aaah!"
As the seven consecutive hits repeated without rest, the heavy sandbag
creaked and swayed.
'I will become stronger! Stronger than I was a second ago!'
Tomorrow's me will be stronger than today's me.
With veins bulging in his upper body, Screamer gritted his teeth and
threw his punches.
D-48 Days.
Shirone desperately suppressed the feeling of his mind drifting
away.
'4.3 trillion. 6.7 trillion. 11.9 trillion.'
A small thrill appeared on Miro's face, which had been indifferent until
now.
'It's accelerating.'
It was proof that his mind and body were beginning to withstand the
current training.
As he surpassed 12 trillion, Shirone collapsed with a thud.
"Again. Start from the beginning."
"Aaah!"
As fierce emotions surged, he screamed involuntarily, but his body
responded to Miro's words as if hypnotized.
"Shirone, success is the sum of failures. You pile up failures to
leap over the wall of success."
'14.4 trillion! 19.8 trillion! 24.6 trillion!'
Blood dripped from Shirone's nose, but Miro paid no attention, looking
at a point higher than his head.
A golden angel with wings spread wide was shimmering in the
light.
'The world is watching you. It's do or die. You can do it, Shirone. If I
can do it, so can you.'
Ten days later, Shirone's sequence method surpassed the first target of
1 quadrillion.
Time remaining until the start of the magic school.
