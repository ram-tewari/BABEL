Chapter 552:
As Shirone tried to grasp the concept of (10^{64}), he blurted out,
"Is that a number humans can count?"
"It's not a number. It's a state," Miro pointed to her
head.
"The sequence method is just one way to expand your mind. If you
can accurately feel the rush of numbers, you don't need to read them in your mind."
Shirone, whose sequence method far exceeded the average person's speed,
could understand this.
However, interpreting Miro's words in reverse meant that unless he could
feel it accurately, he had no choice but to read through it.
'Feeling the number at the level of a quintillion.'
Upon reflection, it wasn't entirely impossible.
If he ran calmly at a speed that wouldn't shock his mind, he would
eventually reach it.
Speed could be considered later.
"There are 73 days left until school starts. The goal is to elevate
your incarnation to the state of unity of mind and body within that time. What you can do with it is
something to think about then, but it will definitely help with magic."
Shirone was also certain of that.
It was a difficult task, but if he succeeded, the enhancement of his
mental strength would be tremendous.
"Yes, I'll try. What should I start with?"
Above all, the great Miro was guiding him.
The thought that she wouldn't assign an impossible task gave him a bit
of confidence.
"To reach the state of unity of mind and body, what you need to do
from now on is…"
As Shirone swallowed hard, Miro spoke.
"Work like crazy."
The answer came quite some time later.
"What?"
"What? Was that your answer?"
"No, I mean, what method… We only have 73 days left…"
Miro chuckled.
"Right. I won't tell you to think of it as having 73 days left. But
Shirone, that's not something human strength can change. There's no need to think about what can't be
changed. If you have 73 days, you have to solve it within that time."
It was a suffocating statement.
"I understand how you feel. But that's the barrier of reality given
to everyone. If you overcome it, you win; if you don't, you lose. There's no such thing as finding a way
to make it happen. Haven't you always done that?"
Shirone nodded heavily.
"You can do it. Believe in the power of a day, Shirone. If you
achieve one today, in 73 days, you'll have built up 73 times that."
That was also a clear reality.
"People like to focus on talent, but in reality, I also reached
this point by building up each day."
It was somewhat comforting.
"The body is controlled by the brain, and the brain is controlled
by the mind. The mind moves only by looking at the result."
Miro pointed to Shirone's chest.
"If you reach the state of unity of mind and body, your incarnation
will also exhibit its singularity and become much stronger than it is now. Just think about
that."
'I will succeed. I will definitely do it!'
Seeing the change in Shirone's eyes, Miro stood up.
"Good, it seems you understand. Let's move on to the next step.
Follow me."
Shirone arrived at a stream not far from the cabin.
Miro tapped a flat rock in the shade and said, "Come and sit
here."
Although he did as instructed, Shirone, who had expected some grand
training, was bewildered.
"What do we do here?"
"Do nothing."
"What?"
"You can't do the sequence method all day. When you want to clear
your head, sit here and look at the scenery across the stream. No sequence method, no entering the
Spirit Zone."
Shirone looked across the stream.
There was nothing special about the common mountain scenery except for a
single curved tree planted prominently in front.
It felt good, but he was anxious that his determination might
fade.
"Can't I just practice the sequence method?"
"No. And you can't rest in another place either. Rest for at least
four hours."
The sound of the mountain breeze brushed his ears.
Could staring at nature for four hours without doing anything be
considered rest?
'I feel like I don't have time for this.'
Noticing Shirone's skeptical expression, Miro smiled slightly.
"Do you think it's meaningless?"
"Uh, yes. Actually…"
Miro, who had placed a hand on Shirone's shoulder, looked across the
stream and said, "Shirone, nothing is trivial. It's just that people don't try to
look."
People don't try to look.
"Even a leaf rolling in the wind or a pile of trash pushed out of
sight contains stories as long as the time they existed. Humans are the same. Just as the years you've
lived are immense to you, all people in the world are creating immense stories in the same time. But
most people don't know that. Because they don't try to look."
"…I see."
Shirone began to understand Miro's words a little.
"From now on, you need to look at every part of the world. Only
flexible thinking like water can surpass limits. Look at nature here every day. Clear your head while
listening to their stories. It probably won't be boring."
'Let's do it. Believe in the power of a day.'
Imagining his transformed incarnation 73 days later, Shirone clenched
his fists.
In the training hall of the Carmis family estate, explosive sounds rang
out without a day's rest.
The grand training hall, spanning 1,000 pyeong, was usually opened only
to accommodate troops attending bloodline meetings, but currently, only Amy remained, training
fiercely.
'This won't do!'
The Spirit Zone in sniper mode, spanning 2 kilometers, was rotating like
a compass.
Unlike the cross shape where the mage is at the center, in sniper mode,
the mage stands at the end of a straight line, making the rotation intense enough to numb the
mind.
Targets flying beyond visible range transmitted explosive sounds, but
the hit rate was only 60%.
'What kind of sniper is this!'
To hit fast-moving targets, spatial coordinates must be combined with
temporal coordinates.
If the hit rate was only 60% with targets following a set pattern, it
was nearly impossible to snipe a living creature with free will from 2 kilometers away.
'Maximize my specialty!'
Amy didn't give up.
The key was to shorten the rotation speed of sniper mode and the time it
took for Fire Strike to reach the target.
'One path!'
As she focused again on the pinpoint center of her sharpened
concentration, her mental strength was squared in power.
"Haaa!"
The 2-kilometer straight line made a large circle.
The number of targets caught in the process was 32.
Predicting all the target patterns and casting Fire Strike in all
directions, sixteen explosive sounds followed shortly after.
With a hit rate of 50%, lower than before, Amy withdrew the Spirit Zone
and knelt.
"Huff! Huff!"
She wanted to think it was because she had reached her mental limit, but
a fire boiled inside her.
There are no jack-of-all-trades.
If she couldn't succeed in sniper mode, graduating would only leave her
as an average mage.
"One failure is the stepping stone to success. Two failures are a
disgrace to the family."
Reciting the family motto, Amy stood up, her eyes burning red.
The recent failure was accurately backed up in the red-eye self-image
memory.
This was why the Carmis family had no masters.
D-69 Days.
300 meters underground in the grand mansion of the capital city,
Mercodine, there is a bunker designed to withstand ancient weapons classified as map weapons and
city-destroying level magic.
In this place, which stores supplies for over 100 people to survive for
three years, Iruki, the son of the head of the family, was boldly attempting a detonation spell.
'Now!'
Sitting in his room with his eyes closed, Iruki opened his eyelids, and
electricity sparked from his pupils.
At the same time, something seemed to stir in the air at the center of
the underground bunker.
"Detonate!"
He shouted and jumped up, but the expected earthquake did not occur, and
Iruki sank back onto his bed.
"It's not as easy as I thought."
The spell he was attempting was a yet-unnamed ultimate move, a nuclear
fusion detonation spell possible because he possessed two Spirit Zones.
"Are you here again today?"
The head of the family, Albino, entered the room, but Iruki didn't even
look at him, lost in thought.
"So, how's your plan to blow up the Mercodine mansion with its
ancestral breath going?"
"I haven't made any progress yet. It's starting to get
frustrating."
Albino chuckled as he watched his son for a moment.
"Don't overdo it with overdrive. Your brain's durability can't keep
up with your mind's circuit speed. You'll shorten your lifespan."
"I don't care. As long as I succeed."
"That's what I'm saying. It looks like your lifespan will run out
before you succeed. That seems a bit foolish."
Iruki stood up abruptly.
"Why are you here? Aren't you busy?"
Albino smiled broadly.
"No matter how busy I am, I have time to see my son."
"I don't have time to see you. Leave. I need to re-establish my
theories."
Waving his hand dismissively, Iruki walked over to a blackboard covered
in countless erased and rewritten equations.
As Albino watched him scribble furiously with chalk, he spoke.
"The human brain is an incredibly well-made organ."
The chalk stopped moving.
"What? What did you say?"
"It's so well-made that it can't distinguish between what you've
experienced and what you've imagined. Whether you eat food or imagine eating food, your brain's
electrical patterns are the same."
Iruki finally turned around.
"It's an interesting phenomenon, but it's science. That's why they
say if you want to succeed, imagine the moment of success. Then your brain will align itself
accordingly."
Iruki's eyes blinked rapidly.
"Well… that's just how it is."
As Albino raised his hand and left the room, Iruki turned back to the
blackboard.
And he began erasing equations like mad.
'It's not too late. I'll start again today.'
D-65 Days.
Numbers raced wildly through Shirone's mind.
'No! It's too big!'
The key was not reading thoughts but making thoughts race quickly.
However, after surpassing 100 billion, it felt like his brain was convulsing.
"Take it slow. There's still plenty of time."
Miro's words didn't resonate with him.
Several days had passed, yet he had only reached 100 billion.
At this rate, reaching numbers beyond trillions into quintillions seemed
impossible.
"Grr!"
At around 120 billion, Shirone's mind snapped back.
'I've been counting numbers for eight hours straight…'
Miro consoled him.
"You went further than yesterday. Let's try again."
"I can't do it today. My head feels like it's going to
explode…"
"Do it."
Miro's voice turned cold at times like this.
"You're not there yet. A day has 24 hours; only 15 hours have
passed. With four hours of rest deducted, that's 11 hours left."
Shirone had been through many trials but breaking through his limits in
real-time was unbearable pain.
"But… I also get tired…"
"That's not my concern."
Shirone's eyes hardened, but Miro remained cold.
"Don't be mistaken, Shirone. This training is for you to get
stronger. No one is forcing you to become stronger. If you want to quit, pack your bags and go home
now."
"I'm not going back."
"Then do it. That's reality. Does it seem unfair? Do you wonder if
there's any need to live so hard? Do you want me to tell you why?"
Miro spoke firmly.
"Because someone else is doing it."
The venom disappeared from Shirone's eyes.
"What you're unwilling to endure is something someone else is
enduring right now. That's competition. I did it; Gaold did it too. Sain, Zulu, Plu—they all endured
that pain and rose above it."
Shirone gritted his teeth hard.
"Don't think those above you got there by chance. They all suffered
as much as you did. So don't whine about limits or anything like that. If you don't want to do it, just
quit."
With determination rekindled in his eyes, Shirone let out all his
pent-up frustration in one breath.
"Phew!"
It was excruciatingly hard but not something he wanted to give up on; he
had no choice but to do it.
'Endure! Just a little more!'
And so another day piled up.
