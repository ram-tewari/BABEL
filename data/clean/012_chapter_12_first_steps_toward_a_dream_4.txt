Is that really the case?
He knew she had an artistic and open-minded personality. But this was potentially the
most serious situation that could arise between a noble and a commoner.
"So your name is Shirone. Don't worry. If Rian says you're a friend, then you're a

Shirone didn't drop his suspicious gaze. While she might have felt offended as the

"Hehe, I'm sorry. My brother is just immature. But I'm relieved he has a friend like

Rian walked to the table and poured water. Between siblings, words often fell on deaf

"Oh, no. I'm not really..."
Reina stared at Shirone, who was scratching his head shyly. At 17, he should have been
grown, but he was truly a pure child.
"Hehe, you're really cute."

Shirone's face turned red as a persimmon at Rian's mischievous joke. The reason he didn't

Reina's body leapt up. Shirone felt dizzy at her jumping power, which easily surpassed a

Reina was once a swordsmanship prodigy on par with Rai. As such, the strength

Rian grumbled continuously while rubbing the top of his head. What wind brought this

"Why did you come back anyway? You already took your vacation before."

"When have we ever cared about him?"
"Rai is taking the certified swordsman exam this time. I came to pick him up. I left

"Certified exam?"
Rian's eyes shook with shock. He had acknowledged the difference in skill. His brother
was a genius, after all.
But had the gap widened this much?
The certified qualification was the dream of all who lived in the Kingdom of Tormia and a
standard for noble ranking.
The exam is held once a year, and the number of applicants reaches a thousand.
But among them, fewer than thirty people can obtain certification.
For comparison, there's the non-certified qualification. It's issued by institutions
designated by the royal palace; for instance, in the city of Creas, Alpheus Magic School is a representative
example.
Upon graduating from the magic school, one becomes a non-certified 10th-grade magician,
and thereafter, they can raise their grade by performing various achievements through associations or
guilds.
The difference between certified and non-certified ultimately comes down to whether they
receive recognition from the royal palace. Therefore, there are non-certified individuals with skills that
don't lag behind the certified ones, but since people tend to gravitate toward money and honor, the overall
level of the certified is much higher.
The fact that more than half of the applicants in the annual certified exam already have
non-certified qualifications illustrates this difference.
"Hmph, good for him. As expected, geniuses are different. He gets a lot of support
from home too."
Reina looked at Rian with pity. Whether he had talent or not, he was her beloved youngest
brother. She hoped he wouldn't be hurt by this news.
"I should go prepare dinner. The seafood from the royal palace needs to be prepared
directly. Shirone, it was nice to meet you. Let's greet each other next time we see each other."
"Yes. Goodbye."
Shirone bowed with a regretful heart. Since he was leaving the mansion tomorrow, he would
never meet Reina again. But the exhilaration of playing the piano with her would be something he'd never
forget.
"Rian, about your sister."
"Hmm? What about her?"
"Well... does she have a boyfriend or something?"
Shirone regretted asking as soon as he did. Emotions are like beasts caged in iron; once
released, they can lead to uncontrollable consequences.
