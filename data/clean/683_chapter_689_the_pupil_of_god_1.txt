Chapter 689:
The Pupil of God (1)
At that moment, Shirone's voice was heard.
"Rian!"
Shagal, his eyes wide with madness, charged forward, with Shirone and his group following
close behind.
"Ah, ahhh…"
As Venezia trembled and let out a groan, Rian, Kuan, and Etella turned their heads
simultaneously.
A handsome man with dark skin, towering height, and Middle Eastern-style clothing stood
there, his long hair flowing like a woman's.
"Finally."
His senses merged into one.
"Ra…!"
Shagal snarled, drool dripping from his teeth, as he hurled a bag filled with rapid-fire
swords with all his might.
"Enemiiiiii!"
Ra Enemy, who turned to look back, smiled beautifully—but that smile was directed not at
Shagal, but at Shirone.
"It's been a while, Shirone."
For Shirone, it had only been 20 years, but for Ra, it had been billions of years of
waiting.
"Why! Why! Why!"
As always, Shagal took the lead.
Dozens of rapid-fire swords flew toward Ra's vital points.
A technique that had taken the lives of countless strong warriors.
But Ra Enemy evaded every attack with graceful, dance-like movements, spinning in a full
circle.
"What…!"
It was such a baffling sight that Shagal, plunged into utter despair, lost even the will
to continue his assault.
'This is impossible!'
His genius intuition screamed at him.
"Shirone! Now!"
As Rufist shouted, Shirone came to a stop.
'I won't let you escape!'
**Quantum Superposition—300 Overlaps—Incarnation—Punishment of the Angel.**
The radiant incarnation of the angel bloomed magnificently, launching a relentless
bombardment toward Ra Enemy.
The sheer power of the attack could have killed everyone present, but even so, sacrifices
were unavoidable in this situation.
'If we can just eliminate Ra!'
Since everyone shared the same thought, no one resented Shirone for casting the spell
without hesitation.
A deafening roar shook the heavens and earth, piercing through eardrums.
Debris rained down first, followed by a dust cloud that spread like a monstrous entity,
devouring the city.
"Phew! That was insane."
Rian emerged, lifting a brick larger than a person with ease.
The spears of light Shirone had mercilessly unleashed were far more powerful than
anything they had witnessed during their graduation exam.
'If I survived, the others must have too.'
Those gathered here were the elite of the elite.
The first thing that caught their eye was a pitch-black hole, dozens of meters in radius,
its depth unfathomable.
And above that hole, Ra Enemy hovered, unharmed.
"……He's fine?"
As the others emerged through the smoke, their expressions mirrored disbelief at the
current situation.
Only Shirone could fully recall the precise moment when 300 spears descended in sequence.
"This… is impossible."
The emotion Shirone felt was identical to what Shagal had experienced moments ago.
'There was no way to dodge that!'
It wasn't that Shirone had overestimated his own abilities or underestimated Ra Enemy's.
**Movement Zero.**
Despite the 300 spears perfectly blocking Ra's path, he had still evaded them.
'Was there a path to escape?'
Shirone shook his head violently.
'No, absolutely not. Even if I were in his place, there'd be no way out.'
In a perfectly checkmated situation, finding a way to break free was impossible—not
because it was difficult, but because there was none.
Mayray approached Shirone.
"Hexa. Ra is a perfect random variable."
"A perfect… random variable?"
"Humans can't create true randomness. Even when trying to set something randomly, the
very intention of 'setting it randomly' creates a new pattern."
Can we imagine perfect randomness?
"But Ra is different. He is perfect chaos. He moves outside patterns, making you believe
he doesn't exist."
Waving a hand, blinking an eye, shouting—when you imagine all the near-infinite actions a
human can take, Ra can perform something beyond that scope.
"Just because you can't imagine it doesn't mean it doesn't exist."
"Right. If Hexa is a 100% product of inevitability, Ra is a 100% product of chance."
If everything transcends patterns and is triggered by pure randomness, it's no wonder
humans can't comprehend it.
"So, in short, he's annoyingly strong. What do we do now? Pure firepower doesn't seem to
work."
Mayray spoke.
"There is a way to eliminate Ra Enemy. But it's only possible if we steal his blueprint."
Kido adjusted his glasses as he glared at Ra Enemy.
"If we devour him, we can devour his imagination too. Perfect chaos doesn't necessarily
mean invincibility. So… chaos is like that, right? Sometimes weak, sometimes strong…"
"Yeah, I get it."
Shirone took a step forward.
"I'll create an opening. That's when we devour Ra Enemy."
"How?"
"……I don't know."
With a smile, Shirone dashed forward, leaping over the pit with a teleport. Ra Enemy
spread his arms wide in welcome.
"Can you do it, Shirone?"
He didn't know.
But the answer lay there.
**Quantum Superposition—Wheel of the Void.**
At first, it was a double overlap.
Then, as Shirone activated Quantum Superposition again, the number of overlapping events
began to soar infinitely.
The only way to control the fractal of the void was the Ultima System, but Shirone
abandoned even that, multiplying the numbers relentlessly.
"My god…"
Shirone's numbers swelled like bubbles, forming a massive spherical body that completely
trapped Ra Enemy.
"How do you plan to endure this?"
If the numbers kept increasing, control would eventually become impossible—but Shirone
wasn't worried.
'It doesn't matter. Let them multiply endlessly.'
Neutralizing inevitability and chance.
The reason Shirone's events could multiply endlessly was that Ra Enemy's events would
cancel them out in equal measure.
"The bubbles… are disappearing."
Finally, as one Shirone grabbed Ra Enemy, all the other Shirones began converging toward
that single event.
***
The events that had multiplied like bubbles through the Wheel of the Void were advancing
toward every possible pattern.
An infinite number of patterns reacting to a single randomness was like throwing
countless pebbles to fill the sea.
Every possible event occurred.
Fighting, dancing, torturing, kissing…
Of course, these events weren't actually happening.
They might have happened, but more importantly, Ra Enemy's randomness was gradually
diminishing.
As Shirone's patterns increased, the number of possible reactions Ra Enemy could take
relatively decreased.
Some events became impossible from the start, and thus the bubbles of the Wheel of the
Void began to pop.
Eventually, all events vanished as "events that could never have occurred," leaving only
the true Movement Zero.
Grabbing Ra Enemy's shoulder in this state was trivial.
"Haa, haa."
Shirone gasped for breath as he glared at Ra Enemy.
'I've got you. I won't let go.'
Even if it meant dying here, he'd hold on.
'It was a battle of probabilities.'
As Rufist lost himself in thought, Ra Enemy spoke.
"You've finally arrived, Shirone."
Even in this dire situation, there was no tension in his voice.
*Piiiri.*
A black shadow whistled past.
*Thud.* Blood sprayed as Ra Enemy's right arm was severed.
"Kido."
Shirone turned his head to see Kido hunched over, holding Ra Enemy's arm, one foot
slightly lifted.
"Kekeke, Speedkiller makes no mistakes. Now, I'll thoroughly examine your memories…"
"Just eat it already."
Shirone urged impatiently, but contrary to expectations, Ra Enemy watched with a faint
smile.
"Well then."
As Kido bit into Ra Enemy's arm and began chewing, time seemed to slow.
Everyone watched with bated breath as something *gulped* down Kido's throat.
"Hmm…"
Kido looked up at the sky, waiting for the "taste of the event" to activate—but his eyes
soon filled with confusion.
"Huh?"
Through <Nemesis>, Ra Enemy's memories were transmitted to everyone.
Shirone's eyes shook with shock, and Ra Enemy tilted his head slightly as he watched him.
This is a story.
The tale of a being born from perfect randomness.
"T-this can't be…"
Ra Enemy's memories began in a distant era of the universe, beyond human experience.
On a planet, life existed.
Their forms, habits, language, and communication were utterly unlike humans, but that
wasn't the issue for Shirone and his group.
Because he—had nothing.
"It's coming! I can see the head! Just a little more!"
A life born under everyone's blessings began a tragic existence without granting his
family even a second of joy.
"No! No!"
The parents wept upon seeing their newborn.
The child, born a mutant, inherited nothing from them.
To be precise, all the sensory organs their species used to perceive the world were
broken.
"Why our child? Why ours?"
Yet, the parents decided to raise him.
At that moment, Shirone felt a chill.
'This is insane.'
The child could demand nothing, and his condition was beyond comprehension.
With no visual organs, he didn't know what light was.
—Ra Enemy is blind.
With no auditory organs, he couldn't even hear sound.
—Ra Enemy is deaf.
He couldn't smell, taste, or even feel his digestive organs moving due to paralyzed
nerves.
'How is this possible?'
Thus, his ego, devoid of any characteristics, was trapped in nothingness, living a
wretched existence.
He might have had thoughts, but he couldn't even recognize them as such.
Time was meaningless, and a single second in the photon realm stretched into 10,000
years, 100,000 years, even 100 million years.
"Gyaaaaah!"
Kido screamed as the memories threatened to burst his skull.
Venezia, too, shed tears of blood as she relived the memories that had killed two of her
brains.
"Grrr!"
Even Shirone, whose mental strength far surpassed theirs, grimaced as he desperately
endured.
It was suffocating.
So suffocating he felt he'd go mad.
Likely, the child's mind was filled with nothing but this oppressive void.
100 million years became 1 billion, then hundreds of billions…
In the endless expansion of time, the nature of nothingness, unable to bear the
emptiness, began to twist.
"Kyaaaah!"
The parents, who had come to nourish the child as usual, fled in terror upon seeing his
body contorting like a pretzel.
His bones crumbled, and organs that had never been needed began to dissolve.
He was transforming into a random mass of flesh, devoid of any functional beauty.
By the time 40 years had passed in the photon realm, no one came for him anymore.
"……."
In the silence, the world was utterly still.
Without time, there was no aging.
Destined to last forever like an unhatched egg, containing only the concept of
nothingness.
Thus, it was pure chance.
A signal without motive, intent, or purpose sent ripples through his world.
"……!"
The flesh mass's skin split horizontally, and for the first time, it began to move.
Doubt.
That was doubt.
The shell split open, and the newborn pupil was flooded with an overwhelming light.
—In the beginning, there was light.
A deluge of information followed, filling the world of nothingness with presence.
He called this world the universe.
—I am existence.
Anke Ra (The Eternal, Immortal Ra).
As Venezia collapsed to her knees, Kido vomited what he had eaten, dizzy.
'So this is…'
Ra Enemy smiled sadly.
"Yes, this is me."
Though Kido had vomited everything, the digested flesh was already transmitting new
memories.
In a dark space, Ra Enemy sat like a puppet with its strings cut.
'This is real!'
Not an event, but Ra as he existed somewhere in reality.
"Where? Where are you?"
"……."
"Answer me!"
Amid the flood of memories, Shirone desperately clung to reason and shouted.
Ra Enemy shook his head and stepped back.
"I will become a god."
As the memories fully transmitted and Kido grasped Ra's true intent, he clutched his head
and screamed.
"Shirone! It's dangerous!"

'Damn it! So this was his plan!'
He intended to become Anke Ra again in a human body.
If he couldn't become a god as a collective concept, he'd transform into the most

'In the end, Ra Enemy is…'
The totality of events Anke Ra had experienced since ancient times to become a perfect
human.
**1st Place. Ra Enemy (19 years old): 98.7%.**
Recalling the figure he'd seen during the Ivory Tower test, Shirone felt his hair stand

Venezia had been right.
The 98.7% Karma value built through the experience of events.

Through Shirone, Shagal, Kido, Mayray, and Venezia—five individuals—Ra Enemy's Karma

"Kill him! If he completes it, it's over!"
