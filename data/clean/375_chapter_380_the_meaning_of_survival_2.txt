Chapter 380:
The Meaning of Survival (2)
"Then, of course, I'll go with Shirone."
Amy moved to the red team, high-fiving Shirone, Iruki, and Nade in turn
before taking her place.
"Tch, eager to stand out. That's why freshmen are no
good."
Screamer didn't like that Shirone had been the first to make a
move.
Even those who had been in the senior class for two or three years were
cautious about choosing teams. For Shirone to boldly raise the team's flag was both a disregard for
hierarchy and a display of self-importance.
'At least that guy is a bit better.'
Screamer looked at Dante.
Seeing him still deep in thought, it seemed that the top ranks
understood each other.
Although he still didn't like him, letting emotions influence
evaluations was a shortcut to failure.
"Hey, let's join Fermi's side together. I know from last year. With
Fermi, victory is guaranteed. Of course, I won't be easy either."
If he could bring Dante over, he could isolate Shirone. That would
attract even more team members.
"Hmm."
Dante didn't even listen to Screamer.
Lost in thought, he moved with his companions.
"Let's go. I've decided."
Screamer's face twisted.
Even Dante, said to be the kingdom's best in talent, was joining
Shirone's team.
'What the heck? Are they kids or what...'
When Dante, followed by Canis and Arin, crossed the line to the red
team, it was beyond absurd—it was infuriating.
'Are they out of their minds? Do they even know what survival is? If
they had just checked last year's results, they'd know.'
Last year's survival evaluation had a ratio of 22 to 8 in favor of the
red team.
In the end, the majority's choice was correct.
Naturally, Fermi was among the 22 and became the final survivor after
enduring up to the fifth stage.
'Annoying. I have to do this again.'
Screamer shuddered.
The experiences in the Virtual Zone today would be extreme situations
that wouldn't even appear in nightmares.
Although the difficulty was prepared up to the seventh stage, there was
no need to go that far.
Only one person in the history of Alpheus Magic School had passed the
seventh stage of survival.
Even that was just a rumor.
"Fine, whatever. Be a sacrifice for my points. Let's go,
Pony."
Screamer turned and headed to the blue team. Pony, who had been watching
Shirone, followed him.
As more students chose their teams, Boyle made his decision after much
deliberation and spoke to Pandora.
"I'm going to the blue team. You should choose quickly too. You
don't want people saying you just tagged along."
Pandora tilted her head.
"Blue? Do you have a reason for being confident?"
"In my own way. But I won't tell you. I don't want to influence
your judgment."
"It's okay, tell me. I won't be swayed."
Finally, Boyle looked around and whispered in Pandora's ear.
"As you know, survival evaluates endurance. It's about patience,
not skills."
"So?"
"I heard only one person in the school's history has completed
survival. We probably won't last till the end either. So it's best to leave it to someone specialized in
this and focus on our records."
"Hmm..."
"You know, Fermi has been the final survivor for four consecutive
years."
"I get what you mean. But Shirone isn't easy either. He's an
Unlocker. His mental strength must be considerable. He actually showed good results in cognition and
judgment."
Boyle sighed regretfully.
Why was the difficulty of the survival evaluation set so high?
If even one person could endure, the competition wouldn't exist in the
first place.
If someone could definitely pass the seventh stage, everyone would join
that person, and the team balance would be 30 to 0.
So, whether it's Shirone or Fermi, they would both be eliminated.
Therefore, Fermi, who had taken the survival test for over four years,
was much more advantageous.
The fact that students above Class Two were heading to the blue team
without much doubt also supported Boyle's thinking.
"Well, it's your decision. Anyway, I'm going to Fermi's
side."
Boyle didn't share his thoughts.
It was a value judgment. Sharing information out of camaraderie was
fine, but trying to persuade would crack the alliance.
Pandora watched Boyle join the blue team.
She was also conflicted, but she had seen Shirone's skills with her own
eyes.
Most of Class Three being on the red team probably had similar
thoughts.
"Well, it's either this or that."
Pandora followed Boyle to the blue team.
Even in the ruthless senior class, she realized the importance of
allies.
'If it's 50%, let's live or die together.'
By the time Pandora chose the blue team, the teams' outlines were
becoming clear.
Students who hadn't scored high in evaluations were being extremely
cautious until the end.
One of them was Maya.
Having been in the senior class for a long time, she knew it was
advantageous to join Fermi's side.
'Hmm, I guess...'
Maya turned towards Fermi's side. Then Screamer, already on the blue
team, raised his chin and spoke.
"Hey, are you really coming here?"
Collie sternly warned.
"Screamer, no external pressure in team selection."
"I was just asking."
"That's a warning. One more and you're out."
"Tch!"
Screamer clicked his tongue and turned away. But this was also part of
his calculation.
Maya was worth pushing away with one warning.
He had suffered significant point losses by being in the same group as
Maya in strategy and tactics.
If they were on the same team, the point difference would remain, and he
couldn't avoid being in the same group in strategy and tactics.
But if the teams were divided and he won, he could escape from Maya with
a 40-point gap.
'Let's split here. You go to Class Three, and I'll go to Class
One.'
Feeling dejected, Maya moved to the red team.
Even if it wasn't external pressure, Screamer's hostile attitude made
her unwilling to be on the same team.
She approached Shirone.
Since he was the first to choose the red team, she thought of it as
Shirone's team.
"Can I join?"
Shirone smiled brightly.
"Of course. The more, the better. And it's not me accepting you;
you chose our team."
Iruki and Nade stepped aside, making room for her.
It was a strategic decision to give her a sense of belonging, as well as
being angry at Screamer's pettiness.
'Endurance is influenced by psychology. The more who can hold out
longer, the better.'
In the end, those with strong endurance would remain, but being with
teammates was a powerful weapon in itself.
"I'll join this side too."
Dorothy, with sleepy eyes, joined the red team.
Then Luman followed, and the timid Suabi cautiously moved over, watching
the situation.
'Oh, this is unexpected.'
Nade glanced at the three who had just joined.
Luman, who didn't like Shirone, choosing the red team likely meant
Dorothy influenced his decision.
Dorothy was almost the only person Luman was somewhat close to, but
Suabi, who was indecisive like she had decision paralysis, joining immediately was worth
reconsidering.
'Some in Class Two have considerable trust in Dorothy's judgment. So...
is Iruki right about the brainwave?'
With everyone having chosen their teams, only Aider remained.
Being the youngest was just as difficult in this situation.
His older friends and sisters, whom he was close to, were split between
the two teams, making it hard to decide where to go.
'Hmm, which side should I join to be seen as making the right
choice?'
Shirone was looking at him with a kind smile.
Feeling a sense of trust as a team, Aider also smiled and ran
over.
"Hmm! Hmm!"
At that moment, Screamer covered his mouth and cleared his
throat.
With one more warning, he'd be out, so he had to signal with a
sound.
Fortunately, it seemed effective as Aider turned his head.
With Collie's sharp eyes watching, Screamer pretended to be indifferent,
stalling for time.
'Screamer...'
Screamer was the one who bossed Aider around the most in the senior
class.
But he also took care of him a lot, making him someone Aider couldn't
afford to offend.
Changing his mind, Aider bowed to everyone on the red team and then ran
to the blue team.
"Teacher! I'm sorry! I'll change teams."
Collie sighed.
But he couldn't give a warning based on suspicion alone, so he concluded
the procedure.
Coincidentally, the red and blue teams ended up with exactly 15 members
each.
'Hmm, a close match. It's been a while.'
No, it was the first time since Fermi had excelled in the survival
test.
'Shirone's presence is that significant.'
The advanced class and the senior class were entirely different worlds,
but the title of the kingdom's top prospect was powerful.
The fact that he had earned the title by defeating the former strongest,
Dante, also played a part.
"Now that the teams are decided, enter the Virtual
Zone."
As the 30 students moved to the center of the Virtual Zone, Collie
followed and explained.
"Listen carefully. This is a matter of life and death."
Class Three students, experiencing the survival test for the first time,
looked particularly tense.
"The Virtual Zone is not the second training ground. It doesn't
transmit information through anti-magic. The runes engraved on the floor use sophisticated magical
engineering to inject specific sensations into your Spirit Zone as if they were real."
If the body is under the control of the mind, it's not strange for
someone trapped in a room-temperature freezer to freeze to death. This time, however, it was a method of
directly injecting information into the extremely sensitive mental state of the Spirit Zone.
"Even though it's virtual, it can still strain your mind. Once you
enter the survival test, your brain will go into a semi-conscious state due to shock. Therefore, it's
impossible to consciously exit the Virtual Zone."
A dark shadow fell over the faces of Class Three.
"Especially from the fourth stage onwards, it becomes really
dangerous, so the teachers will monitor your condition through vision. But remember, since this is an
official Red Line evaluation, they won't be able to intervene to save you even in dangerous
situations."
Collie held up two fingers.
"There are two ways to exit the Virtual Zone. First, deactivate
your Spirit Zone. Second, if deactivation is impossible, shout 'Toako.' Remember this well. It's
'Toako.'"
'Toako' is a word with no meaning, created as a code for the survival
test.
Using meaningful words could lead to misuse during the test.
"Teachers will not respond to any words other than 'Toako.' Whether
you say 'Help me,' 'I give up,' or 'The Virtual Zone is malfunctioning,' they won't be able to save
you."
All the senior class students showed determined expressions.
"What I want to emphasize is, do your best but don't be stubborn.
This is just a rehearsal. Also, if you feel any abnormalities after exiting the Virtual Zone, report it
immediately to receive mental treatment. Understood?"
"Yes!"
The students' voices echoed through the mountains surrounding the
training ground.
