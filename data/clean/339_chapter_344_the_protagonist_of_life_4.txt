The Protagonist of Life (4)
As the law was lifted and the tourists regained their human forms, they
were horrified to see their naked bodies. Chaos ensued. Women screamed and covered themselves, while men
fumbled around looking for places to hide. Shirone and his group managed to evacuate everyone to an inn
with the help of people outside the city walls.
Sitting on the railing of a café terrace, Shirone watched the tourists
filling up the buildings.
Some were threatening to sue the tourism board, while others were loudly
demanding to see the manager.
They too had encountered a being from heaven. The implications of
today's events were unpredictable.
Shirone looked back at Kangnan. She too was watching the tourists, but
her mind seemed elsewhere.
"What do we do now? Should we try to convince the
people?"
"Is that necessary?"
"Pardon?"
"We don't need to bother with trivial matters. The association will
send people. They'll handle it."
"But what if it gets harder to manage over time? What if rumors
spread?"
"Just announce it as if the myth of the Ice Queen was true. Whether
they believe it or not, it contaminates the information. But it doesn't matter if we tell the truth
because it's not something ordinary people can handle anyway."
Kangnan had often encountered such situations. Despite the potential for
chaos if information about heaven spread worldwide, she was certain nothing significant would
happen.
People would still worry about their daily meals, lose sleep over
important contracts the next day, and fret over how to raise their growing children.
Kangnan looked back at the group following her. Despite having no
specific orders, they hadn't left her side.
They knew it was a situation beyond their control to manage.
But to Kangnan, it was merely an annoyance.
"The association will decide your fate too. So stop following me.
Run away if you want."
The group hung their heads dejectedly.
They understood what it meant to be adversaries of the Magic
Association. Even if they went to prison, as long as they were part of the Red Line, their magician's
licenses wouldn't be revoked. But if they ran, the Black Line was their only refuge—a world inhabited by
those lost to madness. The group didn't have what it takes to survive there.
"Yes, we're sorry. We'll turn ourselves in when the association
people arrive."
Amy silently watched the departing group. This was as far as they could
share their lives. Whatever happened, they had to continue living, fighting fiercely to be the
protagonists of their own lives.
After ensuring follow-up measures were in place, Kangnan prepared to
leave. Before speaking, she gave Shirone a hard look.
He looked much younger once the Diamond Armor was deactivated. Of
course, she wasn't foolish enough to judge someone by their appearance.
However, she considered that such features could be problematic within
the association.
Gaold's proposal was indeed radical.
"Shirone. It's time to leave. I'll wait for you in the carriage.
Hurry up and come along."
"Yes, I'll be right there."
Shirone was ready to follow Kangnan to the Magic Association. The chance
to meet Gaold was rare, but he was also curious about the situation with Miro.
'I might meet Rian and Tess in the capital.'
Shirone grabbed his backpack and approached Amy.
"I'm sorry it turned out this way."
He felt ashamed facing Amy. He had wanted to enjoy a proper vacation
this time, but once again, he had put her in danger.
"I'll tell you plainly, I won't travel with you again."
"Ha ha! I'm really sorry. I don't know what to say."
Amy smiled and shook her head.
"Thanks, Shirone. Because of you, I've gained confidence. I think I
can work hard again now."
Shirone felt it too. Not only had he overpowered the group, but the
extreme heat of the Inferno was a spell difficult for others their age to execute without Amy.
He hadn't asked about her enlightenment during the fight. There are as
many enlightenments as there are people, and Amy's style was unique. Perhaps they would discuss it
someday.
"I'll contact you when I get back. And Dante, thanks for looking
after Amy."
Dante shrugged. Unexpectedly tasked with escorting a rival's girlfriend,
it wasn't a bad deal. Both the talks with heaven and Miro seemed to suggest that Shirone and Amy knew
something. He planned to ask about heaven on the way.
"Don't know what this is all about, but go well."
"Yeah. See you at school."
As Shirone left, Dante and Amy also turned to leave. Near the exit,
Dante pointed to a café.
"How about a coffee before we go? You're buying, of
course."
"Trying to cling to the very end! Buy your own! You've got plenty
of money!"
"Ah. I'm escorting you home, a coffee wouldn't hurt…
huh?"
Dante turned around at a noise. Liria was standing there as she had when
they first met, a large backpack on her back.
With the Lawmaker sealed, she should return to the Akeanis order to
report. Likely, after today, she wouldn't meet Dante again. Magicians and sorcerers, beings of light and
shadow, naturally walked different paths.
But Liria was left with regrets. If she hadn't met Dante, this mission
would likely have ended in failure. It was hard to imagine a wandering sorcerer sharing affection with
someone, but because she had relied on him considerably, she couldn't simply cut off her
feelings.
"I'll buy the coffee."
"Hmm."
Dante read the affection in Liria's eyes. Truly, she was a woman who
couldn't hide her feelings. He wasn't one to enjoy flings, but he wasn't in the habit of turning away
those who came to him. He pulled her close and pointed somewhere.
"Okay, forget coffee. How about we find somewhere quiet to go
in?"
Liria's face flushed as she looked where Dante pointed. A big sign for
an inn hung prominently.
"Not understanding the ways of the world, Liria was shocked by the
proposal for an instant style of romance.
"Are you... what are you... saying...?"
"Ha ha! No need to back down. We're all adults here. Let's not
waste time."
Liria groaned. She could tolerate his self-centered personality to an
extent, but his promiscuity was unacceptable.
"Such a flirt! Let go!"
When Liria pushed him away, Dante easily stepped back.
"A flirt? How is it flirting when we haven't done anything
yet?"
"Forget it! I must have lost my mind! I'm going back to the
order!"
Dante chuckled. If this was where their paths ended, there was no need
to hold her back.
"Alright. Take care."
'Indifferent bastard…'
As Dante truly let her go, Liria felt slighted. Yet, she didn't look
back as she walked away. Turning around felt like it would make it truly irreversible.
"Write if anything fun happens!"
Liria blinked at Dante's voice echoing in the distance, then let out a
chuckle.
Airhain Dante. 18 years old.
Second in line among the magic aspirants of the Kingdom of
Thormia.
'Heh. I'll remember that.'
With a bright expression, Liria walked towards the horizon as Dante and
Amy headed back to the exit.
Eager to leave the now tiresome ruins, they encountered Jokre's group
waiting near the ticket barrier.
Their faces were surprisingly defiant. Unable to completely hide their
fear, they seemed unwilling to show weakness.
Vivian approached Amy.
"I'm sorry."
Amy waved her hand. This wasn't child's play. Their arrest seemed just
in balancing the situation.
"Forget it. We both fought with conviction. There's no need to
apologize just because you lost."
"No. I mean for saving me at the end. If I had died then, there
wouldn't have been a next time."
"Well..."
Amy awkwardly turned away. Truthfully, her actions weren't solely for
Vivian. They were necessary to succeed with Okwangbakyojin, and the last shout was something she wanted
to say to herself.
"I don't know why you said that, but try hard. Make sure you become
a magician at the next graduation. You definitely have what it takes to be a protagonist."
Amy was moved. It was a cruel thought, but words from the lips of a
defeated opponent gave her more confidence than any consolation she had heard before.
Vivian limply dropped her shoulders and returned to her friends.
Watching the miserable departure of a loser, Amy spoke up.
"Hey."
As Vivian turned around, Amy unbuckled one strap of her bag and rummaged
through it as she approached.
"Here. Take this."
In Amy's hand was a doll of the Ice Queen, a prize from a mini-game
arcade. Holding it with trembling hands, Vivian stared at the Ice Queen for a long while. The face of
the Ice Queen, who had enveloped her in solitude, smiled innocently like a child.
"Sniff. Sob."
Tears streamed down as Vivian hugged the Ice Queen doll close. Jokre
approached and comforted her back. After a moment, he led her away with a glance at Amy.
Amy watched them leave for a long time. They would continue to fight in
their own lives. Perhaps, someday, they too could become protagonists.
'Yeah. It's just one failure. If this is my life, I'll fight and win as
much as I need.'
Firmly clenching her fist, Amy turned around. Her steps towards Dante
were now unhindered compared to yesterday.
Divine Transcendance (1)
In the capital city of Bashuka at the Kaizen School of
Swordsmanship.
The current certified level 6 swordsman, Kuan, once called the Sorcerer
of Death before injuring his Achilles tendon, was battling the nightmares of an irretrievable
past.
Born into a military family, Kuan lost all his family on the battlefield
at the age of six. His uncle took him in only to seize his fortune and send him to a swordsmanship
school in another land.
However, he harbored no resentment.
Although he had no one to rely on, his genius allowed him to become a
certified level 6 swordsman at just 20 years of age.
The nightmare began with an event ten years ago.
At 21, Kuan was appointed as the second company commander of a search
battalion tasked with capturing the founder of the Dark Order, Kointra.
The soldiers did not welcome him. It was natural for a 21-year-old
rookie to join a veteran unit with an average age of 38.
Kuan didn't care either. To him, a commander, soldiers were merely
expendable resources needed to win wars.
Without even time to exchange greetings, Kuan led his troops into the
Ghost Forest immediately upon his assignment.
Numerous search parties had entered before but all had lost contact
without exception.
The previous company commander had fled back to his homeland citing
illness from fear, prompting Kuan's emergency deployment.
But Kuan was confident.
Even if there really were ghosts in the forest, there was nothing in the
world his sword couldn't cut.
'I'll capture it. Even if it's a ghost.'
The first day passed without incident.
But disaster struck in the early hours as the second company was
reorganizing at camp.
"Attack! It's an attack!"
The sentry's cry was the last sound before disappearing. Realizing the
enemy had breached, Kuan grabbed his sword and rushed out of the barracks.
Other soldiers, each armed, were also alert to the approaching enemy in
the dim dawn light.
A strong wind blew. Or rather, it seemed only to resound in everyone's
ears. Kuan reflexively cut through the darkness, but his blade caught nothing.
