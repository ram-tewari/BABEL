Chapter 295:
Great Secret (4)
Having been hurt by Shirone's refusal to give her gold, Amy felt a bit
better seeing her school friend and said,
"I'd like to take a look at some weapons. Mind if I browse?"
Nade smiled kindly and encouraged her to shop.
"Sure, take a look. We've got all sorts. However, I'm surprised to
see people out in this weather. I was thinking of closing early because I'm feeling a bit down. Must be
the rain—it makes everything seem gloomier."
Amy clasped her hands behind her back and wandered around the store,
examining various weapons.
The basic equipment was all there. Most fortunately, there was no mage
equipment.
If there had been items for magic users, she would have felt ridiculous
clinging to her pride about not wanting to buy anything with someone else's money. It was easier this
way.
While casually browsing, Amy noticed a weapon encased in glass and
displayed on the wall.
"Hm? What's this?"
Below the case was a paper labeled <First Thunderstrike>.
Nade's face lit up with pride as he spoke.
"Ah, that's the pride of our shop. When you swing it, it makes thunder
sounds. Amazing, right? I tried it once and it's so powerful. Any monster would run away in
surprise!"
Amy recalled a story she had heard from Rian during the carriage ride to
Galliant about Shirone's childhood.
Successfully created Thunderstrike at age twelve, and it seemed this was
quite a precious memory.
Amy looked at the price tag. Her eyes widened in disbelief.
"5.9 billion gold?"
"Hahaha! Of course! It's the pride of our shop! It would take at least
that much to have it."
Armin stepped up behind Amy and added,
"Probably no one can buy this through normal means. I'm not sure
how the economy works in this world, but even if we combined all the gold Miss Reina and I have, it's
only 17 gold in total. No one, not even Shirone's parents, could withdraw an amount as absurd as 5.9
billion."
Amy understood. But seemingly unable to let go, she stroked her chin and
asked,
"Hmm, what if a grave robber tried to steal this?"
"They could. Since it's an object filled with Shirone's intense
thoughts, it would be easy to convert into an Objet. They would gather funds through illegal means or
theft, I suppose. But if I were a relic hunter, I wouldn't bother stealing this."
"Because it's not particularly useful."
Armin nodded in agreement.
"Right. Objets are valuable when they break common sense. The idea
of a thunderous sound coming from an axe isn't particularly unique—it's merely about successfully
creating a Thunderstrike. Most items at this eleventh level are probably like this. This is the surface
layer, so there's no room for deep human desires to manifest. Therefore, rather than desire, <First
Thunderstrike> is better interpreted as just a precious memory."
Amy had come to understand the nature of Objects and, with it, just how
vile a person Arius was.
This world belongs to Shirone. Regardless of what others think, it's his
most precious mindscape. For Arius to commit murder just to plunder that world meant he had to be
stopped.
Armin walked over to the window, peering outside as he spoke.
"Anyway, as law-abiding citizens, we don't need to be greedy. If
we're not going to rob, these are just dream items. No need to be picky. Just arm yourself enough for
basic self-defense."
Amy tilted her head and asked.
"Self-defense? Aren't we choosing weapons to fight Zion and Arius?
Wouldn't we need perfect armament for that, and even that might not be enough?"
Armin, still by the window, replied,
"I'll explain that once we're outside. Just choose for
now."
Reina propped her chin on her hand, lost in thought.
Ogent Family revered swords. And the sword was her most confident
weapon.
"Do you sell bows here, by any chance?"
A smile spread across Armin's lips.
"Good choice."
Currently, it's too dark outside to distinguish anything. In such
conditions, a ranged weapon would be more practical than a melee one. Especially given Reina's acute
hearing, her accuracy with a bow would likely be excellent.
Reina examined the bow Nade selected. The draw weight was solid, and the
bow's curve wasn't warped.
She had learned during martial arts training that bows could create
variables. And being from a martial family, she had strong fundamentals from frequent hunting.
"I like it. I'll take this. And a quiver and arrows
too."
"Great. I'll give you good quality ones. Just wait."
While Nade searched for items in the storage behind the counter, Reina
turned to Amy and asked,
"There don't seem to be any magical weapons. Should we check somewhere
else?"
"It's fine. Mages don't really need tools anyway."
Amy summoned a Jack-o'-Lantern next to her face.
"Besides, I have the weapon Shirone bought me."
Amy regretted the words as soon as they left her mouth. Emphasizing that
Shirone bought it would only make her feel more miserable.
But it was already spilled milk, so she pretended nothing had happened
and turned her body away.
Armin leaned against the window with his arms crossed.
'Jack-o'-Lantern. That's ancient magic. She obtained quite a rare
item.'
Having visited Heaven himself on a certain mission, Armin was familiar
with the general market values in the community.
Ancient magic artifacts like the Jack-o'-Lantern could only be acquired
with an elixir, and something of this caliber would likely require a White Elixir.
Shirone and Amy must be quite close.
Yet she can't withdraw money. And that last comment reveals her hurt
feelings about Shirone.
'Hmm, so that's how it is.'
It might be dangerous. No—within the confines of this mental world, this
might be the most significant obstacle.
'I should warn them ahead of time.'
Just as Armin made his decision, Nade returned with two 20-piece
quivers. When asked about the total price, he said 4 gold.
Amy approached Armin and whispered.
"Isn't that too expensive? A single bow set is worth half your
entire trust fund!"
"It is expensive. But it must be top-notch. It might even have
additional capabilities. After all, the 'Master of this World' isn't the type to swindle people. Of
course, the boy Nade might be someone who usually scams people, but considering he was entrusted with
the <First Thunderstrike>, he seems to be a friendly projection."
The reason Armin referred to Shirone as the 'The Master of this World'
indirectly was because Nade was present.
Having experienced at the inn how sensitive projections were to
keywords, Amy immediately understood and nodded.
"Yes. Nade isn't the type to carelessly sell low-quality
items."
Reina paid the 4 gold. Money wouldn't be needed anyway once they met
Master of This World.
Reina strapped one quiver to her back and another to her thigh. She also
held three arrows between her fingers, resting them against the bow's back, ready for action.
Amy floated the Jack-o'-Lantern beside her and looked at Armin.
They were only a group of three, but their resolve was as firm as an
army preparing for battle.
"Are you ready? We're going to meet 'this world's master'
now."
Reina asked.
"But where do we start looking?"
"We don't need to go far. After all, he exists everywhere in this
world."
Armin raised his head and looked toward Nade. Amy and Reina stepped
aside to clear the way.
"Do you know where Shirone is?"
Amy gasped.
Though it wasn't a major incident, she didn't want to experience again
the kind of intense focus and stares from others like at the inn.
As expected, Nade fixed his gaze on Armin without even blinking. But
perhaps being a complex projection, he didn't completely play dumb like the innkeeper.
"Why are you looking for Shirone?"
A smirk tugged at the corner of Armin's lips. As expected, this weapon
shop was a place where Shirone's consciousness was particularly concentrated.
'Then I can't miss this opportunity.'
Armin raised the intensity of the keywords.
"Shirone is about to die. We're here to help him before it's too
late."
Amy swallowed nervously, anxiously awaiting Nade's reaction. How would
Shirone's consciousness interpret Armin's statement?
Nade stared at Armin with an expression devoid of emotion. Then, in a
voice completely different from before, he issued a warning.
"Leave. This is not your world."
Armin realized he had connected with Shirone's avatar and pressed
forward.
"Is that a refusal? Shirone, think again. You need our help. Please
tell us where you are."
An unusual sign occurred in Nade's body as he remained silent. His
pupils vanished, replaced by pitch-black voids, and his entire body started to turn a deep, inky
black.
"Tch! A failure?"
Armin hastily stepped back.
"What is it? "What's happening?
"This is where it starts! Get outside!"
Armin shoved open the door with his shoulder, and Reina, with her bow
nocked and ready, followed close behind. Amy hesitated for a moment, looking back.
The Nade she knew had vanished, replaced by a rapidly approaching black
shadow. Her heart nearly stopped as she left the shop, seeing similar entities converging from all
directions.
Amy immediately cast Shining, illuminating the surroundings. A burst of
light illuminated the pouring rain, making every drop stand out clearly.
Silhouettes of shadows lingered within the curtain of falling
rain.
The figures resembled humans but with unnaturally elongated torsos and
arms.
The most unsettling part was their faces. A thin spiral of light swirled
like a vortex, as though pulling everything into an abyss.
"Armin-ssi. What exactly are those..."
"They're Egoists."
Amy tilted her head slightly, puzzled.
"Egoist?"
"Don't be deceived by their appearance. Their forms are
ever-changing."
"Swiooooo!"
An Egoist lunged, but Armin quickly cast Flicker, slipping through the
encirclement. People poured out of nearby buildings, only to immediately transform into Egoists and join
the chase.
Armin cast Flicker repeatedly. His form vanished, leaving only
flickering shockwaves crossing space to reveal his trajectory.
Meanwhile, Reina finished combat preparations and instantly shot three
arrows between her fingers.
Each arrow found its mark, and the Egoists burst apart with a pop,
crumpling to the ground. This was definitely something that could not happen in reality.
As one Egoist fell, the others quickly adapted. When Reina shot arrows,
they twisted their waists thread-thin to dodge. However, Amy's Fire Strike engulfed one's head in
flames.
Even while burning, the Egoists pressed on, attacking relentlessly.
Contrary to their frail appearance, their physical strength was immense, comparable to Tier 3 monsters
from the real world.
Reina was quickly becoming accustomed to her bow. Ignoring the sound of
the rain, she focused on tracking their movements by sound alone and made a predictive shot, hitting the
Egoist as if the arrow was curving.
"Sheuooooooo!"
While Armin and Reina effectively worked together, Amy was locked in her
own battle against one of the Egoists and separated from the group.
This one was the strongest among them, the transformed form of Nade from
the weapons shop.
Nade's Egoist shifted forms for tactical advantage, pressuring Amy. Its
arms split into four, stabbing like drills, while its flexible torso contorted to dodge her rapid-fire
Fire Strikes as if dancing through the air.
The number of Egoists pouring into the battlefield grew, waves of
shadows surging in from all sides. Armin's Flicker magic, though effective for evasion, wasn't viable
for holding the battlefield, and time was running out.
Amy cast Fire Wall to divide friend from foe. Concentrating, she raised
the fiery wall to twice its usual height, burning several Egoists caught along the line.
Nade's Egoist paused to look back at the flames, then stopped its
assault and stared intently at Amy.
Amy recalled hearing Shirone's voice in the shop earlier and shouted
desperately.
"Shirone! It's me! Amy!"
The Egoist didn't answer, but it was clear that it had understood. Its
head tilted slightly, about 45 degrees.
