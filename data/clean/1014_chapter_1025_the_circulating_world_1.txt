Chapter 1025:
The Circulating World (1)
Shirone stared at the giant eye that had appeared between the gates of
the Maika ruins.
Though obscured by an opaque barrier, it clearly looked like an eye and
seemed alive.
"Who are you?"
There was no response, but Shirone realized that communication might not
be possible.
As he walked sideways along the gate, the eye surprisingly followed
him.
'It's watching me.'
If this were an entity from the outside world, it would shatter all
known truths of humanity.
"You know I'm here. Show me some proof, anything."
Just because the gate of an ultra-ancient civilization had opened didn't
mean it was proof of the outside world.
'I need to hear it directly. Even if it's not a sound, anything that can
serve as evidence.'
Unless something beyond a mechanical response came from inside the gate,
it was meaningless.
At that moment, the eye blinked.
It might or might not have been a special signal, but Shirone asked with
anticipation.
"Is that the outside world? If you can't speak, blink twice."
There was no response.
No matter what he said, the eye seemed to operate independently of
Shirone's presence.
Frustrated at being unable to take a step closer to the final truth,
Shirone grew angry.
"Tell me. Why do we exist? Where did we come from, and where are we
going?"
Looking at the indifferent eye, he wondered if it had ever been
interested at all.
'I can't give up.'
Shirone tried every method that came to mind.
The result was a complete failure.
None of his attempts elicited a meaningful response, leading Shirone to
mental exhaustion.
"This is hard."
Perhaps everything Shirone tried was just one simple thing to the
eye.
'No matter what a bug does, to a human eye, it just seems to
squirm.'
Sitting down, Shirone looked around the Maika ruins, devoid of human
traces.
'It's a lonely place.'
The feeling that there was truly nothing there seemed like evidence of a
god's indifference.
'There was a god with a heart.'
Lost in thought for a moment, Shirone asked the eye with a bitter
smile.
"Is Goffin there?"
He didn't expect anything, just felt a longing.
"McClain Goffin. He's my dad. I hope he's doing well. I miss
him."
At that moment, the eye blinked twice for the first time.
"Huh?"
For the first time since arriving, a groundbreaking change occurred, and
Shirone stood up abruptly.
"Did you just answer? If you know something…!"
Before he could finish, the eye disappeared behind the barrier, and
Shirone bit his lip.
'Come back. Come back.'
Whether or not his thoughts were conveyed, the eye reappeared.
Screech…!
A powerful high-frequency sound burst forth, causing Shirone to grimace
and step back.
"Ugh!"
It seemed to be trying to communicate, but even the Ultima System
couldn't decipher it.
'Don't try to understand.'
Even the smallest fragment of the signal from the eye would surpass the
teachings of the Ultima System.
Emotions close to questioning were conveyed, and after a moment, the
high frequency stabilized.
"Sanctuary."
Shirone's eyes widened.
'I can interpret it.'
Like the information received during the Gyorgi incident, it was clearly
a protocol of this world.
"…Forbidden Silverfinger Inraklemac."
As soon as he heard that, a tremendous roar erupted, blowing Shirone's
mind away.
'No! I can't end it here…!'
He tried to endure, but the entire Maika ruins were disintegrating into
dust.
"Ugh!"
As Shirone, enveloped in a magnetic field, returned to reality, Poine
rushed over.
"Messiah! Are you alright?"
"Gasp! Gasp!"
Barely aware of reality, Shirone replayed the recent events in his
mind.
"I heard it."
"What? What do you mean…"
Nothing was clear yet, but the shock of the moment was vivid in his
memory.
"It was trying to convey something to me."
As Poine and Kaos struggled to understand, Kalt's voice was
heard.
"…Is that so."
When Shirone looked up, Kalt was leaning back in his chair with an
ecstatic expression.
"What happened?"
Behind Kalt's chair, brain matter and blood were splattered like shotgun
pellets.
Poine spoke.
"His head exploded. Then you reappeared immediately."
Climbing the steps, Shirone reached Kalt, whose dying gaze moved
slowly.
"Did that eye… speak to you?"
Since gaining the ultimate sense through OOPArts, one question had
endlessly tormented him.
What is there?
"Kalt."
Shirone felt a sense of kinship.
Though he didn't know Kalt's philosophy or thoughts, the emotions
conveyed through the electric field were the same.
'Where did we come from?'
Perhaps since opening the Spirit Zone, he had been running solely to
find that answer.
'It's frustrating.'
Living each day, eating, sleeping, reproducing, yet not knowing who we
are.
'Humans, humanity…'
That fact was unbearable.
"At least I'm glad. To confirm it before dying. No, even dying is
funny."
The life faded from Kalt's eyes.
"Did I… really live in this world?"
It was his final words.
Shirone, setting aside the fact that Kalt was a pirate, gently closed
his eyelids.
'It was a dangerous journey.'
When forcibly cut off from the information of the ultra-ancient
civilization, Kalt's brain had exploded…
'What if it were me?'
He might not have been easily defeated, but he couldn't guarantee safety
against the outside world.
'I need to find it myself.'
He needed a way to safely connect without being dominated by
OOPArts.
"Brother, brother."
Freed from confinement, Jakra approached Kalt with disbelief.
"He's really dead?"
Confirming the headless body behind the chair, he raised his
hands.
"Hahaha! My brother is dead! It's over! From today, I'm the king of
Dionas!"
Other pirates joined in.
"Damn! I thought I'd die of frustration! He kept spouting nonsense every
day!"
Watching the scene, Kaos asked.
"What should we do?"
"Arrest them all… and handle the rest as you see fit."
Hearing Shirone's words, Jakra pressed his temples, revealing his
killing intent.
"Haha! It's too late! My power… huh?"
Other pirates mimicked Jakra, but the spatial jump didn't work.
"OOPArts was only with Kalt. Without his cognition, you're just ordinary
humans."
As the pirates stood frozen in shock, Shirone spoke to Cairns.
"Bring in the refugees. I'll handle the diplomatic procedures. And
Langi…"
Cairns, quick to understand, bowed.
"Nothing will happen to her. If she wishes, I'll send her back to the
homeland immediately."
Was Langi a problem?
If Arachne could exercise authority over Dionas, it was a tremendous
opportunity.
'Of course, it will be a debt in the Holy War…'
Politics is like that.
As Shirone left the castle, passing the pirates, Poine hurried after
him.
"Messiah, what should we do?"
"For now… how about taking a break?"
They hadn't had a single peaceful day while protecting 20,000
refugees.
"A good decision. But what next?"
"We do what we can."
Finding evidence of the outside world didn't change today's
tasks.
"It would be a lie to say I'm not troubled. But someday, we'll find the
ultimate answer. And…"
Shirone looked at Poine.
"To reach that day, we must live first. Day by day, doing our
best."
And so, they would continue living.
"Indeed."
Realizing that the heart of philanthropy still existed in the world,
Poine smiled in relief.
Lunar Surface
Nane looked down at the human planet.
'Looked down?' Direction was meaningless on the moon, floating alone in
space, but it felt that way.
"What is important?"
From the moon, the tragedies on that blue planet seemed like someone
else's problem…
"Why must there be suffering?" Nane felt even more sorrow.
"Buddha."
Shura saw the swords of the sermon, densely planted within a 100-meter
radius, begin to tremble.
"It's getting dangerous." The sermons provided an environment for living
beings to survive in space, and their destruction would be disastrous.
A screech pierced the invisible barrier.
"Kiaaa! Kiaaa!"
The space creature 'Destura,' made of mental energy, was drawn to Nane's
thoughts.
"Grrr! Bu…ddha!"
As its scythe-like hand broke through the barrier, golden electricity
spread like cracks.
"Is the universe rejecting me?"
Destura, which materialized through others' thoughts, was the main
obstacle to humanity's escape from the planet.
Watching the monster gradually penetrate the barrier, Shura swallowed
hard.
'When specific thoughts perceive the universe, these creatures
inevitably gather. Truly a terrifying species.'
According to her research, even dragons attempting to escape the planet
600 years ago were captured and torn apart by Destura.
"Kraaa! Kraaa!"
As the giant creature pushed its upper body through the barrier, air
began to escape through the cracks.
"I'll stop it."
Shura crossed her hands, ready to use her Gestalt ability, when Nane
spoke.
"A guest is coming." Shura maintained her stance and looked towards the
planet.
"What?"
Like a meteor piercing the atmosphere, something glowing red was heading
towards the moon.
'Escaping the planet's gravity alone would be difficult.'
Watching a bit longer, Shura recognized the figure engulfed in flames
and sighed.
"Ah, of all times…"
The stone monkey's avatar, Son Yujeong, was extending her staff
infinitely as she approached.
Drawn by the moon's gravity, she landed and retracted her staff,
speaking harshly.
"Finally found you. Damn it."
Nane, understanding her lip movements, smiled.
"If she's the granddaughter of the Thunder Monkey, it's not surprising
she reached the moon…"
The Destura, which had been targeting Buddha, shifted their focus to
her.
"Do you know where you are?"
Burning like heated iron, she observed the monsters and pulled out
another staff.
Previously split in two by Rian, she could adjust its length,
effectively having two.
Nane chuckled.
"Dual staff?"
Son Yujeong, holding a staff in each hand, tilted her head and
muttered.
"What a bunch of trash…"
As the 8-meter-tall Destura raised its arms on the lunar surface,
"Kiiii!"
Son Yujeong, eyes emitting a golden glow, bared her monkey fangs and
leaped.
In the near-vacuum silence, her two staffs began to relentlessly beat
the Destura.
