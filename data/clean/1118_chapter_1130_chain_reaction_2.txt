Chapter 1130:
Chain Reaction (2)
In front of Delta headquarters, where the holy war was taking place, 200
citizens were protesting.
"Impeach the King of Jive!"
This was the backlash from an article by a journalist named Michael,
published in a daily newspaper, about the collusion in the holy war.
"A king who has lost his morality has no qualifications. Citizens
must unite to rebuild the country." This was an unusual sight, not seen in other kingdoms, as the
king was elected by vote.
While such a system had made Jive a wealthy nation, it was maddening
from the king's perspective.
Gis's aide, Myron, observed the protesters as he left Delta
headquarters.
'It's more serious than expected.'
The fact that 200 people had gathered in just a few hours indicated the
extent of the citizens' backlash.
'Of course, there are probably opposition members among them too.'
Moving people requires money, and it's difficult without a big shot.
The streets were also busy.
"The world is ending! Everyone, repent! The god Kria will save
us!"
The massive pyramid that suddenly appeared in Jive's territory was
visible even from here.
Naturally, the citizens were shocked, and doomsday preachers took to the
streets.
"The glory of Teraphos has arrived! Now all humanity will face
divine judgment!"
The pyramid's appearance helped divert attention from Gis, which was a
good thing.
Young men who had been boldly robbing stores were seen being arrested by
the security forces.
"Pathetic."
The system doesn't shake easily.
'Unless there's a certainty that the world will end tomorrow…'
Humans can't let go of today.
"Whew, let's change clothes first."
His face was bruised from being hit by Gis, and his clothes were stained
with blood.
When he entered the mansion guarded by bodyguards, his wife and daughter
were in the hall.
"I'm home."
"Oh my, what happened to your face?" His wife asked with a
surprised expression, and Myron made an excuse.
"You saw the article, right? I fell while getting through the
protesters. It's not serious. Get me some new clothes."
He heard his daughter sigh.
"Sigh."
His twelve-year-old daughter, who didn't even greet him and was reading
a magazine, made him frown.
"Your dad's home after a few days, and you don't even acknowledge
him?"
"Whatever. You're so annoying."
She slammed the magazine shut and headed to her room, making Myron raise
his voice.
"Hey, kid! Watch your mouth…!"
"Honey, leave her alone."
His wife brought new clothes and said,
"She's a teenager. Things are rough outside right now. And she's
about to enter a prestigious school."
"Teenagers are so dramatic."
"But she got first place in the multilingual ability test today.
The teacher says she has talent."
Myron raised an eyebrow as he changed clothes.
"Really?
This is why I live.
"Good girl."
A smile spread across his face as if nothing had happened.
"Well, she is my daughter. She should do that well. What else did
the teacher say?"
"She has good judgment and excellent social skills. They said
she'll do well in school."
For the child of a politician, school was a special-purpose
institution.
"Support her well from behind. Don't provoke her unnecessarily.
That age is the most unstable."
"You should come home more often. Talk to her…"
"Nagging again."
As always, he was more emotional after being beaten by Gis.
"Do you think I'm playing outside? You know I'm busy. Take it easy
today."
His wife didn't want to argue with an injured man.
"Okay. Are you going to the holy war?"
"It's fieldwork. I won't be home for a while. Stay inside and
always have a bodyguard with the kid."
Gis's words couldn't be ignored.
"Yes. But you need to eat."
"I don't have time. I'm busy. Bring Toby. I want to see his face
before I go."
Their four-year-old son.
"He's sleeping. It's nap time."
"…Couldn't you have waited a bit? Missing one nap won't be a big
deal."
His wife's voice had a sharp edge.
"How would I know when you'd come home? And naps are important for
his growth…"
"Okay, fine."
No matter how long the conversation, the result was the same, so Myron
headed to the door as if escaping.
"I'm leaving. Take care of the house."
His wife's voice softened, perhaps feeling guilty about not seeing their
son's face.
"Are you really okay?"
"Of course. I'm Myron. I won't break. Just take care of the
kids."
In truth, he wasn't sure.
'If this incident escalates, the boss's reputation will change. And I
heard the pyramid weakened the angels' power.'
'Let's solve it one by one.'
Stopping journalist Michael from writing more articles was the
priority.
"Sigh."
Leaving the mansion, he looked at the pyramid's peak beyond the city's
skyline.
"Who cares."
He could barely take care of himself.
A man stood in front of a VIP-only bar in the city.
"So you come to places like this."
The bar, frequented only by the powerful, was naturally closed, but that
wasn't a problem.
When he knocked, the door opened.
"Who are you?"
"I'm Baekgu."
His head was shaved, hence the name Baekgu, but his real name was
Travis, a gang leader.
"Come in."
In the cold air, with no preparations for business yet, there were
dozens of rooms.
'He must be a big shot.'
A person who rented the entire bar, which even the powerful couldn't
enter easily, in broad daylight.
The manager led him to the most private and spacious room.
At a table that could seat dozens, with a bottle of high-end liquor, a
man sat deep in thought.
"Sir, I've brought him."
"Leave us."
Myron, who had arrived earlier and was waiting, adjusted his glasses and
scrutinized Travis's demeanor.
A burly, straightforward man.
"Take a seat. Handsome fellow."
Travis, bowing respectfully, walked to the end of the long table and sat
down.
"Greetings. I'm Travis. Age 43. I've heard a lot about you from the
boss."
In truth, he knew nothing.
The so-called "boss" who had resurfaced after three years had
only said that he'd find money here if he went along.
Pouring him a drink, Myron spoke.
"I'm planning a big operation lately, but I'm short on people. I
heard you're strong."
"I'll give it my all."
"Good. What do you do now?"
"I run a brothel at Edia Street No. 2. I've got five men under
me."
"Five, you say."
Travis hurried to add, "They're all dependable guys."
It was an attempt to mask the inferiority complex of running a small
gang, but Myron thought otherwise.
'Five misfits? That works just fine.'
A king doesn't hire thugs because he lacks better options.
'It's the Holy War season. If it's revealed there's political
manipulation, other nations will tear into us. But if it's just some brothel thugs, even if it's
exposed, it'll be easy to cover up.'
The reason Myron, an advisor to the king, personally came was because of
an iron rule: "The longer the lineage, the more likely it is to get caught."
"Take this first," Myron said.
He pushed a heavy briefcase on the table toward Travis.
"Is this for me?"
"Open it."
As Myron crossed his legs and sipped his drink, Travis unlatched the
briefcase.
"W-What is this?"
It was filled to the brim with large gold coins.
"One million gold."
"One million?!"
It was an amount Travis could never have even dreamed of touching in his
lifetime, no matter what he did.
"You need money to do the job, right? Take it and use
it."
A flood of thoughts rushed through his mind.
'This is definitely dangerous. But does that matter? What could I
possibly refuse for this much money?'
"Don't just stare at it—go ahead and check it. With large coins,
you can count it easily."
"I trust you, sir. It's just…"
For the first time in his life, Travis felt intimidated by the sight of
money.
"Whatever the job is, I'll do it. But honestly, I don't need this
much money for what I do."
Myron chuckled.
"What are you talking about? This is just the advance."
"W-What?"
"I don't know what kind of world you're used to, but in my line of
business, it's a different level. Think of this as a gesture of good faith. If you handle the job well,
I'll give you ten times this."
"Ten million gold?"
Travis shook his head.
'No, no, this can't be real. Even most nobles wouldn't have this much
money.'
He finally realized what was bothering him.
'Even if I sold my skills—or my life—it wouldn't be worth this
much.'
Myron had the same thought.
'It's a bit much.'
Normally, it was best to apply low pressure over time, but right now,
even an hour was precious.
'I can't help the morning papers, but I need to stop the evening
edition. I'll have to cook him quickly.'
Click. The briefcase snapped shut.
"…I'll do it," Travis said.
It was inevitable.
'Who would take the long road when you can end it all with one shot?
People aren't that complicated.'
Money is what complicates things.
"Then I'll leave it to you."
"Um… do I need to sign a contract or anything?"
The amount was so large that Travis's conscience pricked at him, but
from Myron's perspective, that was out of the question.
'Why else would I bring cash?'
Pointing to the briefcase, Myron said, "That is your contract.
Don't worry about it. With this kind of money, you'll be able to make plenty more in the
future."
At that moment, Travis realized.
'He's a politician. Am I finally going to see the light?'
He didn't know the name or face of the person he would serve, but he
felt he could give them his very soul.
"By the way, the person I'll be working for…"
Sigh.
Taking off his glasses, Myron let out a frustrated groan, letting his
annoyance show.
"What's with gangsters and all these questions?"
For a fleeting moment, Travis had a vision of the one million gold
disappearing before his eyes—a horrifying image.
Perhaps it was instinct.
Travis leapt to his feet and bowed at a perfect 90-degree angle.
"I'm sorry. I overstepped. It won't happen again."
Myron poured a strong drink into a glass and handed it to him.
"Take a drink. I'll be in touch."
"Yes, sir."
Travis downed the drink in one go, grabbed the briefcase, and bowed
deeply.
"Call me anytime."
Stepping out of the bar into the sunlight, his head spun from the
alcohol.
But he didn't feel drunk.
'Or am I drunk?'
Even while holding a briefcase full of gold coins, his arms and hands
felt numb.
Taking a deep breath, Travis kept a poker face as he walked into the
street.
His heart felt like it was about to explode.
'This is insane.'
He worried if someone would snatch the briefcase, if he was being
followed, or if lightning would suddenly strike him.
'Please, just let me make it home.'
Usually, he walked the streets with his chin up, full of confidence, but
at this moment, he felt like the weakest man in the world.
"Extra! Extra!"
News about the massive pyramid erected in Jive was being distributed by
magazines.
"Repent! The end of the world is near!"
Every religion seemed to have poured into the streets to preach their
doctrines.
"Reveal the truth about the Jive pyramid! We don't want a corrupt
king!"
Citizen groups marched, spreading their messages like wildfire.
But Travis?
'Honey, wait for me! Son, your dad did it!'
The only moment that felt real was when he imagined slamming the one
million gold down in front of his family.
