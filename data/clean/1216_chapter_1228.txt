Chapter 1228:
Uriel asked again,
"Longing… you said?"
Asriker recalled the history of Heaven.
"You could have ended the war—back when Ikael stood with you. Only you
could've ended it. But you didn't destroy Ikael. Even though angels are reborn in the sun as pure
spirits, as long as their concept still exists…"
"You could have destroyed Ikael, but you couldn't destroy your longing
for her. That's why Kariel obsessed so much, and why you remained silent… isn't that right?"
"What are you trying to say?"
"O Archangel of Destruction who cannot destroy—you're more human than
Kariel. And you still have the power to end this war. Don't dance to the tune of divine
providence."
"…Perhaps."
Uriel didn't deny it.
"But even if I go to war with God, what would change? Wouldn't I simply
be used by you instead?"
"You love Ikael."
Even within the abyss, Uriel's energy could be felt.
"That feeling you constantly deny… the emotion that still weighs on
you—that is love. Not the kind Kariel held, but a different kind. A love that exists only for
her…"
As the Judicial Halo: Ragnarok condensed, the darkness dispersed, and
Uriel emerged into view.
"White Soul Dragon. Do not mock me."
In this moment, he was the Archangel of Destruction—capable of ending
the war in an instant.
"Then all you have to do is say it's not true."
Is it…?
Uriel had never once considered that the Archangel of Destruction could
love someone.
He had always believed he lacked the function for such a thing.
"I…"
He wanted to deny it. But in the end, Uriel couldn't bring himself to
utter those few short words.
"Do you think Ikael doesn't know?"
Asriker continued,
"You deny it, so everyone else pretends not to know. But I'm sure
someone has already seen the weakness in you."
A woman's face appeared in his mind.
'Miro…'
Was that why, whenever they clashed, that serene warrior of light always
left his heart in disarray?
"The Silent One once said: 'Uriel will end the war only when he can
destroy without hesitation.' Accept it. That is your true battle."
As Abyris's breath—Shadow of the Abyss—faded, the scenery
reappeared.
The forest, ravaged by Wrath of the White Bones, was withered as if
scorched by radiation.
Uriel dispersed Ragnarok.
"I won't hesitate anymore."
Wings of light unfurled behind him.
"If God intends to use me, I'll destroy Him and raise a new god in His
place. If longing is what chains me—then I'll destroy that too."
And with those words, he shot off like a flash of light, leaving only
the echo of a grand vibration behind.
Asriker lowered her gaze and muttered,
"…So stubborn."
Then again, perhaps it was that very stubbornness that made Ikael rely
on him—despite knowing everything.
"Well then."
Asriker stood.
"Shall we resume the war?"
The eleven Apostles waiting in the sky confirmed that the Shadow of the
Abyss had lifted.
Many fairies had aged into extinction, and the angels looked utterly
exhausted.
The Light Dragon Peyton said,
"The forest's gone, but it worked. What now? Should we keep hammering
them with breath attacks? Or…"
The Poison Dragon Poine responded,
"Don't show all our cards at once. This war isn't ending just yet. What
are we missing?"
The Draconic Knight Argan asked,
"Do we have to be missing something? You sound like you're expecting us
to lose."
Blitz said,
"That's the kind of war this is. The outcome won't be decided by us—but
by the Messiah. And so far, no major variable has emerged. But assuming we'll win just because we're
fighting well? That's a dangerous mindset."
The Twelve Apostles silently watched the forest below.
'What are we missing…?'
As the abyssal shadow vanished, the vast wasteland revealed elves and
fairies still locked in battle.
"Hah… hah…"
One elven woman, crawling from the rotted swamplands, dragged her filthy
body along the ground.
"P-please… help me!"
"Erin?!"
One of the elves firing on the fairies recognized her.
"Cover her!"
An elf dashed forward with his bow, grabbed Erin's arm, and pulled her
into their lines.
"What happened? We thought you were dead!"
Tears flowed down Erin's face.
"I was taken to Crown's tent… I should've died, but Grid… he gave his
life for me… Aah… aaaah!"
The elf, knowing their relationship, asked no further and simply
embraced her.
"It's okay. Just breathe. Let's report to Enox."
As Erin was escorted into the forest, her expression suddenly turned
cold.
'At last… we meet.'
From above, the Twelve Apostles merely turned their gazes away
indifferently.
'What… is happening in that forest?'
No one knew.
Xing, as well as Umji and Yangji, all stared at the man who had emerged
through Mimeon's body.
"Argones…?"
The first human—Gaian. The source of all living creatures, even before
dragons, before humanity itself.
Baal approached with a cold gaze.
"Yeah, I've heard of you, even in Hell. But I thought Administrators
didn't interfere with human affairs."
Great Star had the same doubt.
'Why?'
By the mechanics of the universe, the two were bound as a couple, but
their way of thinking was entirely different.
'I'm a program created to protect humanity. But he… once tried to
annihilate humans.'
Because they had trespassed into the domain of the gods.
'Could it be… he's here to protect humanity?'
Great Star shook her head.
'Impossible. For me, humanity is the one and only outcome. But he—he can
create life from any base. That's why I feel maternal toward humanity.'
'And yet…'
It was Argones who had named the first humans Gaia and had forgiven them
at Great Star's pleading.
Baal spoke.
"Unlike Lady Lethe, the two of you are complementary. Don't tell me
you're here to protect Great Star?"
"That's right."
"Huh?"
Great Star blinked in surprise.
"If the planetary program fails, it affects me too. And besides, I can't
allow myself to be defeated by a mere creation—especially not one who doesn't even recognize its
parents."
"…I can't accept that."
Baal's expression twisted.
"You created humans, not us. Well, I suppose since demons came from
human emotion, we're like your grandchildren. But that doesn't make you superior to Lady Lethe."
"Why are you here?"
Great Star still couldn't fully believe it.
"You said you don't interfere with human matters. Weren't you the one
who said you'd give us a chance?"
She feared Cell Buster—the apocalyptic protocol Argones might
unleash—even more than her own erasure.
"This isn't just a human problem. The Dreamer Program, the Outer World
System—none of them are functioning properly. If this continues, the balance of the Five Systems will
collapse."
Argones turned his head.
"This is your final chance. Go back to Lethe. We'll handle things
here."
"Heh."
Baal's gaze sharpened with power.
"You really think I'll do that?"
His chest still ached from Argones' previous strike, but it thrilled him
as well.
He had reached an unprecedented peak in demonic history—and now, he was
eager to test his strength.
'The end of the world… I thought it'd be over too quickly. But
now…'
Good.
'I'll get to swing my blade for real.'
In the next instant, Baal and Argones clashed, and the outer walls of
the Ivory Tower crumbled into dust.
"Graaagh!"
Everything around them was breaking—except Great Star, Xing, and the
others, protected by Law.
Xing thought to herself:
This must be the final battle of the world.
"…Even an Administrator's not much."
With a single slash of Baal's power, Argones' upper body was cut
diagonally in half.
But balancing on one hand, he transformed—his arm turning into a leg,
his form flipping midair as he returned to his original shape.
Baal rested his sword on his shoulder.
"So I still can't kill you?"
Unlike Mimeon, Argones had infinite cellular regeneration. He was truly
immortal.
"But I've won. Fighting an unkillable enemy is just a waste of
time."
That was also true.
Twisting his body with full force, Baal gripped his sword with both
hands.
"I am the pinnacle of Maga."
It was a strike meant to cut down Argones—along with Great Star and
Xing—in one swing.
"Stop."
A voice they knew all too well.
Great Star and Xing's eyes widened, and blood vessels popped in Baal's
eyes.
"Grrrr…"
Even if he mimicked a swordsman, he was still a demon.
"Yahweh."
With a twisted snarl, Baal's face contorted into something demonic as he
spat out the name.
Xing couldn't believe it.
"Sh… Shirone?"
He was drenched in demon blood—completely unlike the Shirone she had
seen before.
"Heh… So you came after all? Must be painful. In the end, you couldn't
protect anything. And that demon's blood on you… you're just a mere human like the rest—"
"Shut up."
Shirone cut him off coldly.
"Don't talk to me."
In the next moment, Baal's body was crushed—half of it flattened into
the floor.
And by the time he returned to form—
He was already flying backward at incredible speed.
Baal summoned all his power to slow down.
"Graaaaagh!"
But his body didn't stop. The pressure threatened to tear him
apart.
"Aaaaaaah!"
And thus, he became a star in the sky.
"…Aaaaaaaah!!"
Moments later, he came crashing back, coughing blood as he hurled
curses.
"Detestable Yahweh!!"
"No."
Baal should have stayed calm.
"Even if I really am detestable… even if I'm just a hypocrite, like you
say…"
His pride as the pinnacle of Maga had been shattered. That must've been
unbearable.
"But you have no right to say it."
He shouldn't have come back without understanding how he'd been struck
in the first place.
"Photon Cannon."
What was born was a single photon.
And when mass was added to it and it was fired at 99.9999% the speed of
light—
'What…?'
There wouldn't be any sensation.
"Aaaaaagh!"
But the atoms that composed his body were twisted and torn apart.
And that…
"Infinity."
Spread endlessly into numbers beyond count.
"G-ggaaahhh!"
It wasn't a shock. It was sheer terror. A feeling like your soul being
sucked out.
As Baal's hideously warped body was flung backward again—
"Come here."
Shirone reached out, and as if caught in a current, Baal was pulled
forward.
Great Star realized it.
'The wave of particles.'
Not Yahweh, but a single human—this was the highest level a mage of
light could reach.
"G-gaah! Gaaaaah!"
Baal, collapsed on the floor, writhed like he was melting from
heat.
Standing over him with Baal between his legs, Shirone calmly raised his
fist.
"Photon Cannon."
Baal shook his head wildly.
"No! Nooo!!"
"…Infinity."
"Aaaaaaaaagh!!"
Countless light-speed particles poured downward, and Baal convulsed
violently.
It felt like the birth and destruction of the universe passed through
only his brain.
"Does it hurt?"
There were no words to describe the sensation. Baal could only tremble
and weep.
"This isn't revenge. Hurting you won't bring the dead back."
"Uuurgh…!"
Shirone looked down at him with sorrowful eyes.
"I hate that so much."
