Chapter 65:
The Invisible (5)
Siena threw down the evaluation sheet and apologized to Alpheus.
"I'm sorry, Headmaster. You came all this way to observe, and this is what you had to
see."
"Hohoho. It was a challenging task, indeed. Isn't that what supernatural spiritual
science is all about? Don't trouble yourself over it."
"I'll make sure to give them a stern warning."
Alpheus waved his hand dismissively and headed for the exit. But just before stepping
out, he turned back and glanced at the empty stage. The corners of his lips curled up beneath his beard.
'If this were truly the end, they wouldn't have started in the first place. Isn't that
right, Shirone?'
***
"We did it! Phase one is complete!"
Despite the audience's reaction, Shirone and his group hurried back to their research
lab. The real challenge was just beginning.
"Do you think it worked?"
"Yeah. I could tell just by their expressions."
They had anticipated the disappointment—after all, the grander the expectations, the more
underwhelming the reveal would seem. But Shirone had counted on this. The more dismissive the audience was,
the more susceptible they'd be to psychological manipulation.
Mass hypnosis.
The key was the midnight bell. Once the idea was planted, it didn't matter how absurd it
seemed. The keyword would trigger their imaginations and lead them straight into terror.
"Shirone, it's all up to you now. If you succeed, we win."
"Got it. I'll meditate here for a while."
"You have to succeed! We're counting on you!"
They had given it their all for the past month. Now, the final responsibility fell on
Shirone.
"I need to focus. Give me some space."
Shirone stopped Neid from patting his shoulder and sat down in a secluded spot. Six hours
remained. He had to elevate his mental state to its peak within that time.
'One. Two. Three.'
Shirone lowered his head and slowly began counting. Neid and Iruki tiptoed away, careful
not to make a sound.
The success of this project hinged on whether Shirone could sustain photon emission for
over ten minutes. But the others still had work to do.
Iruki had to set up the remote control for the holograms, while Neid fine-tuned the core
that would channel Shirone's energy. As everyone busied themselves with their tasks, night fell, and
midnight approached.
In the pitch-black darkness, Shirone's eyes snapped open. After five hours of meditation,
his mind was as sharp as a honed blade. Soon, the door creaked open, and Neid's voice whispered,
"Shirone, it's time."
Shirone stood up smoothly, as if it were nothing, and followed Neid out like a gladiator
entering the Colosseum.
***
The hologram core was installed in the sewers beneath the central park. Dozens of
fiber-optic cables stretched upward from the massive device, branching into hundreds more through
multi-adapters.
A staggering 300 hologram units had been deployed for this operation. Shirone was amazed
by Neid's production capabilities, but the pressure weighed heavily on him.
Though he had managed to sustain photon emission for ten minutes during practice, this
was the real deal. No one could predict what might go wrong.
With ten minutes left until midnight, Shirone approached a machine ten times larger than
the prototype.
Neid handed him the input terminal with a solemn expression. Shirone gripped the crystal
orb with both hands. Iruki stood by with a stopwatch, ready to time it perfectly. Too early or too late, and
the holograms would fail to sync with the bell.
"Ready? It's time."
Shirone took a deep breath and nodded. Iruki raised his hand, watching the clock
intently. The moment the bell was set to ring, his hand slashed down. Simultaneously, Shirone's counting
accelerated explosively. Light erupted from his hands with unprecedented intensity. His friends marveled at
how much his photon output had improved in just a month.
'More! Stronger!'
The light grew until it threatened to consume Shirone. Neid rushed to the core device to
check the transmission efficiency. His frozen expression made Iruki anxious.
"Well? What's the percentage?"
After a long silence, Neid answered in a trembling voice.
"Seventeen percent."
"What?!"
It was far lower than expected. Where had they gone wrong? But there was no time to
analyze. Failure now meant total defeat.
'The range is too wide. It's impossible like this.'
Shirone had sensed it immediately. The moment he began emitting photons, it felt like
pouring water into a bottomless pit—an endless void draining his energy.
The fiber-optic transmission efficiency was only 18%. The parallel connections had
weakened the signal further.
Shirone kept pushing, but no matter how hard he tried, the efficiency wouldn't rise
beyond 17%.
Iruki realized it was hopeless. Activating the holograms now would only shatter them into
fragments.
After five minutes, Neid smiled. Even if they failed, Shirone's effort to reach this
impossible goal was admirable. The fault lay with his own flawed design.
"Shirone, that's enough. We did our best. Let's stop here."
Shirone didn't respond. Truthfully, he was furious. Neid, Iruki, and the entire research
club had poured their hearts into this project.
And this was the end? To admit defeat when they hadn't even achieved anything?
'After coming this far…'
Shirone closed his eyes, steeling his resolve. He hadn't given everything yet. The light
in his hands swelled, engulfing his entire body.
"W-wait… are you serious?!"
Neid realized what Shirone was planning.
Immortal Function.
He was stepping into the forbidden realm again—a place where he could lose everything.
"Shirone! Stop! Don't do it!"
Neid and Iruki shook Shirone desperately, but his mind, racing toward nirvana, remained
unshaken.
The light had already consumed Shirone and was spreading outward. Terrified of what might
happen, Neid cried out,
"Shirone! Please stop! We can't lose you! We can give up on the research, just stop!"
Shirone's eyes snapped open. A faint glow leaked from his eyes, nose, and mouth as he
stared ahead.
"Na… Neid…"
"Yes, Shirone! Let's stop! Let's end it here!"
The corners of Shirone's lips curled slightly.
"I'm… not afraid anymore."
The moment Shirone closed his eyes again, a brilliant light flooded the tunnel. His
mental energy began expanding infinitely.
There was no stopping it now.
Neid and Iruki stumbled back, their faces pale. All they could do was pray for Shirone's
safe return.
In the infinite realm, Shirone felt a familiar sensation.
The feeling that everything constituting his existence was meaningless. But this time, he
refused to forget himself.
'I won't look away. I'll face it head-on.'
A monstrous entity lashed its tongue, trying to devour him. Shirone recalled Armin's
advice: never look away. Not everyone who held the key to infinity disappeared—Armin had proven that.
'I'll overcome this. I'll conquer it.'
The monster's maw gaped, revealing a dark throat as it lunged. Shirone stared straight
into the abyss, unflinching.
"Grrr…!"
His mind collided with the barrier of the Spirit Zone, sending a searing pain through his
skull. He had caught it. Gritting his teeth, Shirone endured. The overflowing energy surged into photon
emission.
"Shirone! Are you okay? Say something!"
"Check the output first!"
At Shirone's shout, Neid turned to the core. Transmission efficiency: 22%. The stubborn
gauge had jumped by 4%.
"It's rising, it's rising! Shirone!"
"Gaaah!"
Shirone focused harder. Infinite mental energy flowed through the Spirit Zone, channeled
into the crystal orb through his arms.
Neid cursed. Efficiency: 36%!
'More! I need more power!'
The Spirit Zone trembled. Even its sturdy defenses couldn't withstand the torrent of
energy. It felt like hundreds of compressed forces were about to explode.
"Gaaaaah!"
Efficiency: 59%.
"Shirone! Just a little more! Hang in there!"
Even the usually calm Iruki shouted in desperation. The gauge needle swayed wildly like a
ship in a storm.
Shirone transformed the Spirit Zone into a defensive form. The frame creaked as it
tightened, and a hallucinatory sound of grinding filled his ears. The moment the pressure converted into
output, a sense of deprivation washed over him—as if the world had vanished.
Efficiency: 87%.
"Ugh…!"
His entire body trembled, his mind shaking violently. Shirone realized just how
incredible Armin was. Harnessing infinite power for reality was insane. A slight misstep, and the
overwhelming force could crush him to death.
Shirone channeled all his energy through the only open path—his palms. Even with the exit
fully expanded, the photon intensity didn't diminish.
Efficiency: 100%.
"Gaaaaah!"
As Shirone poured out his last ounce of strength, the core lamp turned red.
Neid shuddered at the unbelievable result. The ravenous beast that had barely reached 17%
with ten minutes of emission was now screaming as if it would burst.
"It's done, it's done! Iruki, start it!"
Iruki, who had been watching in awe, fumbled with the remote control. His trembling
fingers pressed the button, and light shot up through the cables, spreading like a spiderweb across the
school.
Dong. Dong. Dong.
The midnight bell rang.
