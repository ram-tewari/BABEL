Chapter 104:
Golden Victory (3)
The woman watched closely the red light emanating from Shirone's body. If it vibrated
particles by releasing high energy, it could destroy any object.
"Shirone, stop this. That magic is too destructive. It doesn't suit your
nature."
"I don't want to fight either. But you intend to keep me here. I can't allow that. I
must return."
"Do you think you can win against me?"
Shirone shook his head. He was being honest. Even as a being of the same dimension, she
claimed to be a God with formidable power. At the very least, she was stronger than Arcane.
Who was this woman? And where was this place?
Shirone stopped thinking. At least, this wasn't where he belonged.
"Tell me how to return to my original world."
"Even if I told you, it's impossible. It would only lead you to death again."
"I still want to try."
The woman wasn't afraid of Shirone's magic. But the laser's power wasn't something to
dismiss lightly. She couldn't let this world be destroyed.
"Fine. Have it your way. But promise me this. If you fail to return and come back,
then follow my will."
"How do I return?"
"I can't concede any further. Will you still do it?"
No matter how daunting the obstacle, Shirone's resolve was unchanged.
"Fine. But I'll never give up."
"Your life is yours. Use it as you wish."
The woman created a glass orb before Shirone. Inside, black darkness swirled.
"Shirone. To return to your original world, you must reverse the process that
brought you here."
"I see."
"It won't be easy. Returning from pure spirit to human. Humans are aggregates of all
kinds of unpleasant elements. If you can't accept this, you'll end up as neither human nor anything
else—just a strange aberration."
The woman was certain Shirone would fail.
"This darkness is a spot piercing through spacetime. Commonly called a black hole.
You can return to your original world through it."
The orb vanished, and a massive darkness appeared before Shirone.
"What must I do?"
"It's simple. Enter. It's the spot connected to your body."
"Thank you. I won't forget your kindness."
Shirone meant it. Though hijacked, without her, he'd have had no chance to return.
"Don't thank me prematurely. I don't believe you'll succeed. If you want to give up,
just walk back to me."
Would that happen? If he died, he died—he wouldn't return. But since he hadn't
experienced it, Shirone couldn't say for sure.
"Then I'll go."
The woman half-closed her eyes sleepily.
"Have a safe journey."
Shirone took his first step into the orb. It felt similar to when he first entered the
spot. As expected, he couldn't find an exit. No matter how much he walked, nothing changed.
Turning back in surprise, Shirone saw the woman still watching him sleepily. Time wasn't
passing. He was trapped at the event horizon, where even light couldn't escape.
'I can't escape. It's absolutely impossible to leave this place.'
Shirone finally understood the woman's words. In a place where even time couldn't escape,
there was nothing he could do.
His intelligence made him face reality quickly. Shirone turned back in despair. Better to
live among people than be trapped in eternal darkness.
'Wait. Maybe…….'
Shirone suddenly stopped. At the event horizon, time was eternally trapped. But he had
the power to generate time: photon magic.
Sub-light, near-light, light, superluminal.
Photon mages classified speed into four types. By Shirone's calculations, surpassing the
event horizon required speed faster than light—superluminal.
'Superluminal…… Is it possible?'
Shirone's current level was barely sub-light. With divine particles, near-light might be
possible, but that was still far from superluminal.
'Let me think.'
Now that he had a method, time wasn't an issue. After long contemplation, his eyes
changed. It might be possible. Without a physical body, achieving superluminal speed was feasible.
'Immortal Function.'
Even light speed couldn't traverse the vast universe. But infinite expansion was
instantaneous. Perhaps the Immortal Function was the key to reaching superluminal speed.
Either way, returning was the priority. Shirone took a deep breath and activated photon
magic while simultaneously opening the Immortal Function.
The effect was immediate. Tremors from accelerating to sub-light, near-light, and light
speed shook the Spirit Zone to the brink of collapse.
"Ugh……!"
Yet he couldn't reach superluminal. Without comprehending speed faster than time, magic
through cognition was impossible. The only option was fully opening the Immortal Function. He might vanish
again into the unknown, but without hesitation, he expanded his consciousness to the whole.
Shirone's form burst apart, dissolving into particles. Finally, something faster than
light crossed the event horizon and raced toward the light.
'It's working!'
Shirone still retained consciousness. Even with the Immortal Function fully open, no
particle scattered inside the black hole. Far ahead, the exit awaited—where his body was.
'I'm going! Finally going!'
Suddenly, his speed began to drop. Unintelligible particles clung to Shirone's particles.
Combining, they created meaning, acting like voices that disoriented his mind.
-Feed. Feed. Feed. Feed. Feed. Feed. Feed. Feed.
-Breed. Breed. Breed. Breed. Breed. Breed. Breed. Breed.
As Shirone grew heavier, the exit receded. The particles intertwined complexly, evolving
simple meanings into higher ones.
-Ah, to be born. The lowest existence.
-I don't want to die. A mortal's life.
Shirone realized this was what the woman meant by becoming human. A being embracing both
desire and fear, cast into the world.
Perhaps all humans heard these voices in the womb.
"I won't lose! I will return!"
-To where? There's nowhere to welcome you.
"To my world! Where my friends await!"
-They'll kill you. Interfere with feeding and breeding. What awaits you is the clash of
desires, painful struggle, and inevitable death.
"Ahhh! I hear nothing! No words can stop me!"
Shirone screamed desperately. But as the combinations progressed, the voices grew louder.
Humans were ultimately beings composed of countless voices. Like a solo drowned in a
chorus, Shirone eventually lost his own voice.
-Feed. Feed. Feed. Feed. Feed. Feed. Feed. Feed.
-Breed. Breed. Breed. Breed. Breed. Breed. Breed. Breed.

On the seventh day since Shirone's death.
The student council was still protesting. Specifically, the radicals following Amira.
In opposition, conservative students blocked the entrance, barring access. Moderates also
split into factions, doubling the protesters.
Most of the school had gathered in front of the building, protesting day and night. The
friends guarding Shirone were on the verge of nervous breakdowns.
"Hold Shirone's funeral!"
"Hold it! Hold it!"
"Alpheas must face the Magic Association's judgment!"
"Face it! Face it!"
Seriel covered her ears. With the window closed, the noise wasn't loud, but she couldn't
take it anymore.
"Aren't they going too far? Why are they so obsessed with Shirone?"
On the auxiliary bed, Amy, who had been dozing with her face buried, lifted her head at
Seriel's words.
"They have their reasons. Some probably never liked Shirone."
"Is it Shirone's fault for being exceptional? They're just narrow-minded."
"That's how people are. For every person who acknowledges Shirone, there's one who
rejects him."
At the table, Iruki, who had been lying face down, spoke.
"Did you have an epiphany? Just yesterday, you said you'd chase them down and beat
them up."
Amy didn't even have the energy to get angry. Iruki was probably the same. After a week
of stress, they couldn't be bothered to lift a finger.
Amy rested her chin on the bed.
"Seeing things now, I get it. At least some are fighting for Shirone."
Neid, watching the protest from the window, chimed in.
"But our side is mostly lower or upper class. The protesters are mostly graduating
class. If they really charge, we can't stop them."
Seriel said.
"We're fine for now. We have the faculty. The headmaster, Siena, and Ethela are on
our side."
"You don't know, Senior. They've been acting suspicious since yesterday."
"Huh? How?"
Seriel turned to Neid, but Iruki answered.
"Some are missing. Especially Amira and Fermi. At least one should stay to
agitate."
Amy flared up.
"Hey! You use honorifics with Seriel but keep dropping them with me!"
Iruki looked away, annoyed. There was no particular reason. If Shirone's girlfriend was
his friend, then so was she.
The infirmary door opened, and Alpheas entered with Siena and Ethela. The teachers'
faces were haggard from the student council's persistence.
"You've worked hard. How is Shirone?"
"As you can see…… unchanged."
"I see. It can't be helped."
Even Alpheas was starting to doubt. A week had passed. If some absolute being claimed
Shirone would return, he'd wait a year or even ten. But no one could prove Shirone would come back. It was a
battle against time.
"I'll cast a spell on Shirone."
Amy and Seriel made way. Alpheas placed his hand on Shirone's chest and cast a slow
spell. It was preservation without harming the body.
Finished, Alpheas stepped back. Shirone would last another day.
But how much longer? The teachers were silently observing now, but complaints were
inevitable.
Siena stared at Shirone, then stroked his head. The more she thought, the more unfair
and bitter it felt. A promising mage, cut down before blooming.
'I told you to stop.'
Shirone overcame Overflow. But the cost was frequent contact with the Immortal Function.
Back then, Ethela vowed to take full responsibility. She didn't want to criticize now. If Shirone didn't
make it, she'd spend her life mourning his spirit.
"Teacher! It's bad!"
Seriel shouted. Amy and Neid also looked out the window, unable to hide their anger.
Siena walked to the window. Amira and Fermi, absent yesterday, were now waving documents at the infirmary.
"On behalf of Alpheas Magic School students, we submit indictments against all
faculty! Hand over Headmaster Alpheas!"
Siena's head throbbed. Her worst fears had come true. The student council had played
their strongest card. They weren't just targeting Alpheas—they were demanding punishment for all teachers.
Once the indictments were submitted, the situation would spiral out of control. With the
students' families involved, the school would collapse beyond recovery.
