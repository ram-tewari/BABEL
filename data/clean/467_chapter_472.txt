Chapter 472:
Plu, running through the alley to escape the Kergos hunters, flinched as
a powerful blow struck her from the side.
As the Mecca weapon, Signa, slammed into the ground, a shockwave several
meters high erupted.
Pushed by the force, Plu was thrown against the building wall along with
the shockwave.
"Ugh! What the…?"
"Damn it. Did I miss?"
Clank. Clank.
The metallic sound pierced her eardrums like a sharp blade.
A man equipped with a Piper appeared, holding a sword and shield.
It was Raun, the immortal.
At 1,010 years old, he had achieved divine transcendence at 450 and
perfected his physical synchronization with machinery at 900.
"Well, this isn't so bad. It's a woman, after all."
Raun scrutinized Plu's appearance and gave a sly smile.
Despite enjoying countless pleasures over the ages, the strongest desire
for any living being remained reproduction.
For him, who had lost the concepts of love and affection, only the dry
memory of pleasure remained in the back of his mind.
"What are you babbling about?"
Hearing Plu's voice, Raun tilted his head and pressed a button on the
Piper attached to his head.
His Mecca-level 1 equipment was perfectly customized for him, and its
translation function far surpassed that of the Night Watch's devices.
"Ah, I see. You're a feisty woman. The kind that doesn't easily
fall into a man's arms."
As Raun's words were translated through the mechanical sound, Plu
frowned.
Meanwhile, Raun abandoned his previously gentlemanly demeanor and
revealed a face of pure, wicked desire.
"That's my specialty."
As Plu shuddered at Raun's conveyed emotions, the ground shattered with
a bang, and Raun's body shot up.
Reflexively looking up, Plu saw Raun floating much higher than expected
and sensed something was wrong.
'How is that possible?'
Having been part of the rebel headquarters, she had a rough
understanding of the Mecca combat system.
No Piper could enhance its functions to that extent.
It wasn't a matter of performance; the human body couldn't withstand
it.
Pipers were meant to assist muscle strength. If they operated faster
than nerve transmission speed, just throwing a punch would dislocate a human's joints.
But Raun's movements defied all such logic.
Perfect understanding of machinery.
In reality, Raun's Piper was 30 times more powerful than the devices
used by the rebels.
It was a device that couldn't be operated without perfect
synchronization of nerves and mind with the machine.
Mega Smash!
As the powerful drive of the Piper, divine transcendence, and the
amplified power of Signa combined, a shockwave erupted that seemed to blow the building away.
Recognizing her opponent's capabilities, Plu drew Phoenix and fought
back with all her might.
Raun's acrobatics were so dazzling that predicting his movements was
impossible, but Plu, as a professional zoner, was no pushover.
Setting up Phoenix and countering with the Dawkins Algorithm, the
surrounding buildings shattered.
'I can't lose here…!'
Plu gritted her teeth and dodged the attacks with all her
strength.
While both the Piper and Dawkins Algorithm involved involuntary
movements, Raun's understanding of machinery far surpassed human limits.
Every time Signa's shockwave swept through, Plu's body staggered.
'He's using some interesting techniques.'
Raun quickly grasped Plu's abilities.
Based on the Nor race's abilities, but with the principles of the Mecca
race.
'As expected of hybrids, they're quite a mix.'
Raun licked his lips.
"This should be fun."
Raun sheathed Signa at his waist and raised his fist.
Electricity flowed from his spine, and his fist glowed with a blue
light.
Electric Shockwave!
As he approached Plu and slammed his fist into the ground, a wave of
electricity spread across a 20-meter radius.
"Ugh!"
Unable to handle the wide-area attack with the Dawkins Algorithm, Plu
immediately disengaged the algorithm and went into defensive mode.
Raun, with explosive propulsion, lunged forward and raised his
fist.
Plu crossed her arms to shield her face, layering defensive air shields
to their limit.
Boom, boom, boom, boom!
Raun's fist easily pierced the heavy air barrier and struck Plu's
guard.
Plu's vision blurred from the impact, and she heard the sound of her
left arm breaking.
Boom!
When she regained her senses, she was rolling on the ground, not knowing
how far she had been thrown.
"Sob!"
Plu let her limp left arm hang down and stood up.
Raun entered the building with a meaningful smile.
"If I had hit your stomach, your organs would have burst, and you'd
be dead."
Despite trembling, Plu glared fiercely at Raun.
"But do you know why I hit your guard?"
Raun's face lit up with the anticipated thrill of pleasure.
"Let me teach you."
Inside Paradise, the combined combat power of the three was turning
everything to ashes.
In his Vajra Armor state, Shirone was far stronger than without Arman,
but the combined assault of the immortals Murka and Deina was formidable.
Murka's divine transcendence was at a level no mortal could achieve, and
the magical power from Deina's mental transcendence was overwhelming, even for Shirone, who had
experienced many battles.
"Hahaha! What, was it this easy?"
Deina spread wind seals in all directions, as if she had become one with
the air.
Even if they could be seen, they were too fast to follow.
Murka swung his sword through the gaps in the wind seals.
With divine transcendence, there seemed to be nothing he couldn't cut,
and Shirone was pushed back 100 meters in an instant.
The buildings they passed collapsed, proving the devastating power of
their attacks.
'This is combat based on incarnation techniques.'
Shirone realized something new in his battle with them.
He had met many strong opponents, but this was his first time fighting
enemies using incarnation techniques.
They manifested power through will.
It wasn't about enhancing strength but twisting the very nature of
existence.
Humans, born as humans, made all decisions within the human
realm.
But those who mastered incarnation techniques transcended human
limits.
If humans had twelve fingers, the entire world would be different.
Similarly, those who mastered incarnation techniques had a fundamentally different sense of
combat.
This created countless variables, and how they were utilized determined
the vast differences in combat effectiveness.

could defeat us? Our experience and skill are on a different level!"
Murka, already feeling victorious, spread his arms wide and
charged.
Imagining himself cutting Shirone's neck, the position of an official in
the Immortal Council seemed within reach.
The Immortal Council, a group of monsters incomparable to these two, was
composed of the most powerful immortals.
The ten leaders of the council had lived for at least 10,000 years, and
the average age of the officials was over 3,000.
And now, he, a mere 1,000 years old, would become an official.
All immortals would look up to him, and he would live an even stronger
and more splendid life, existing eternally in this world.
'It's not over yet!'
Shirone glared at Murka and Deina charging from both sides.
First, he restrained Deina with Akamai, then unleashed the Photon Cannon
on Murka.
The flash's power, far beyond anything before, made Murka's face
pale.
'Mental transcendence?'
Even as a master of Skima, the flash's beam was so intense it left
afterimages.
It was as if Shirone had caused an explosion, sending everything around
him flying.
After sweeping the area with a scatter movement, he fired lasers,
causing massive explosions.
But what truly terrified the two was not the explosions but the heat
that seemed to melt their flesh.
'What is this?'
Shirone noticed the enemy's counterattacks weakening and observed with
curious eyes.
Unlike their previous expressions of enjoyment, they now looked
serious.
The biggest difference was their movements.
In a battle that should have been growing more intense, their activity
had decreased.
'I see.'
Shirone, as if finding the solution, pressed the attack even
harder.
His prediction was accurate, and Murka and Deina couldn't even think of
counterattacking, only focusing on evasion.
Finally, as Shirone's Photon Cannon struck Murka's side, he flew back
with a look of shock.
Boom!
Hitting the wall, he couldn't even think of checking Shirone's position,
focusing instead on healing his side with Skima's regeneration.
Deina retreated towards the exit, her eyes darting as she wondered what
to do.
"Now I understand."
Shirone, with Akamai floating beside him, walked slowly.
Looking down at the grinding Murka, he said,
"You are weak."

They weren't fighting Lena, Kanya, or Crude.
