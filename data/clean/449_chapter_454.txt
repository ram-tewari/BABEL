Chapter 454:
Flash! As ten or so people simultaneously cast teleportation spells, the
surroundings briefly lit up as bright as day.
'Rampaging Evolution!'
Beam's insect magic, Kraygus, attacked Gaold.
Its basic form was a mantis, standing two meters tall, with the deadly
traits of eleven different poisonous insects.
Pooof!
Gas bombs exploded, followed by flames, and various poisonous stingers
shot out.
With Rose's pheromones enhancing their power, the effects were
multiplied. Black, a crowd control mage, cast petrification magic on Gaold's legs.
Curse magic Influenza was cast on Gaold, and Iron Wall Rider erected
barriers in all directions to restrict Gaold's movement.
Kraygus's poisonous gas filled the enclosed space, obscuring
vision.
Gaold likely didn't expect anyone else to be in the toxic gas chamber
with him.
The key player was the carnivore addict, Benifis.
'Just one bite, that's all I need.'
Benifis's flesh began to rot.
Already poisoned, he wouldn't live for more than 20 minutes, but he was
the only one among the team who could avoid the mage's shared senses.
'Hurry up and show your skills, you damn bastard.'
Once a spell is cast, it becomes a phenomenon that affects even the
caster.
So why was Gaold unaffected by the air press?
Because he cast his magic outside his own range, and Benifis aimed for
that moment.
"Ha ha, it's been a while since my blood boiled like this."
As soon as Gaold's voice was heard, an immense atmospheric pressure
crushed the entire space.
Screeech!
Kraygus was flattened, and the gas settled on the ground.
In the now-clear landscape, Benifis charged at Gaold from behind.
'Now!'
As his teeth sank into Gaold's shoulder, the remaining 20 moved
simultaneously.
'Bite!'
At that moment, Gaold's lips curled into a grin that reached his
ears.
"Guh!"
Crunch!
Benifis's jaw clenched involuntarily. Before he could swallow the flesh,
his neck twisted rapidly.
Crack! Thud!
His body fell away, leaving only Benifis's face on Gaold's
shoulder.
Gaold grabbed the bald head, tore the face off, and threw it to the
ground.
"One down."
In the landscape filled with afterimages, Gaold grinned wickedly.
Cage B Team had 20 members left.
"Gaold was…"
Sein continued.
"He was the gentlest and most foolish person I knew. He was inherently
kind. He was raised in the Yore faith, but even without it, he would have been the same."
Religion doesn't define a person's character.
Shirone knew many who had oppressed others in the name of
religion.
"Gaold loved flowers."
"Pfft!"
Plu couldn't help but laugh.
"Oh, sorry."
"He loved tending to flower beds. Whenever his grades in the graduation
class were bad, he would go to the flower bed and plant flowers."
Kangnan's eyes grew somber.
She didn't know Gaold during his school days, but she had many memories
of him.
Sein's gaze turned to the night sky.
"The only thing worth acknowledging was his stubbornness, which was
almost foolish. Even in the graduation class of the magic school, he never cast an offensive
spell."
"Gaold loses. Three points deducted. Sein wins. Three points
added."
After the graduation class's highlight, the duel, Gaold gritted his
teeth.
Sein found it amusing.
Honestly, Gaold wasn't particularly talented in magic. The only thing he
was somewhat good at was air magic, a basic form of omnipotence.
In such a situation, only defending with non-combat magic, who could he
possibly beat?
"There's no need to feel wronged. Even if you had cast offensive magic,
you wouldn't have been a match for me."
"I'm not upset because I lost. I'm frustrated because I couldn't prove
my faith."
Sein sighed.
Gaold's best magic was air press. To be blunt, it wasn't even a
technique, just a way to increase atmospheric pressure to protect himself.
If he could last ten minutes, it would be a draw.
That was the only victory Gaold sought.
'Foolish. The world is full of people who will do anything to
win.'
Gaold wouldn't graduate.
He was the epitome of the illogical thinking Sein despised most.
Then one day.
"Hello? Long time no see. You two still getting along?"
Miro approached while Gaold and Sein were in the middle of a religious
debate.
The bizarre incident on the first day had led the three of them to often
hang out together in the graduation class.
All three were unusual individuals, but they formed a strange golden
balance in their precarious relationship.
Recently, however, Miro had been frequently absent from school.
It wasn't against the rules, but not knowing what she was up to
frustrated Sein.
"Getting along? Me with this guy?"
While Sein grumbled, Gaold welcomed her with a broad smile.
"Hello? Where have you been this time?"
"Oh, I've been busy. Getting called here and there."
Miro's skin was tanned.
Since it wasn't a particularly sunny season, Sein guessed she had been
outside Tormia.
"Ha ha! Sounds like fun."
Gaold simply liked Miro.
As he got to know her, he realized she was far more talented than he had
thought.
She was the first person in the school's history to pass the seventh
level of the survival test last year.
But what he really liked about her was her open-minded thinking.
"Oh, right. Miro, listen to this. Is it really such a strange logic that
God knows love?"
"God doesn't know love. Love is a purely human emotion. If God exists,
it would be a being with feelings and thoughts completely different from what we know."
Gaold didn't back down.
"Even if that's true, it doesn't fit your definition of God. God is a
perfect being, so it's natural for God to understand human emotions. Therefore, it's only natural for
God to know love."
Gaold looked at Miro.
"What do you think?"
Miro didn't answer for a long time.
Gaold, who thought she would naturally side with him due to her
endlessly free thinking, found the situation unexpected.
"Gaold, there is no God."
"…What?"
"God doesn't exist. Everything is determined by dimensions. Within the
algorithm of dimensions, hierarchies are divided."
Sein also found it strange.
It was the first time she had made a definitive statement about
something, especially something metaphysical.
"What are you talking about!"
Gaold felt betrayed.
Even if everyone else denied God, Miro shouldn't.
Because…
"Determined by dimensions? Miro, you haven't seen all dimensions. How
can you say that?"
Miro smiled bitterly.
It was only later that they speculated she had met Goffin around this
time.
"Yeah! No one knows! Sorry. Let's stop talking about this and eat. Who's
buying me lunch?"
"Forget it! I'm leaving."
As the still-angry Gaold left, Miro pouted.
"What, is he sulking? So sensitive."
Being kind-hearted was good, but the problem was his
narrow-mindedness.
It was probably because he was soft-hearted, but Miro felt unnecessarily
guilty and didn't like it.
"Hey, you."
Sein spoke.
"For the first time, I like you."
Miro sighed and unfocused her eyes.
As with any school, Alpheas Magic School had its infamous survival test
day.
The 30 graduation students were divided into teams, with a ratio of 29
to 1. It wasn't surprising that they wanted to at least break even since Miro had passed the seventh
level last year.
What was truly surprising was that Gaold had given up even that.
Seeing Gaold stubbornly standing with the red team, Sein was annoyed
just by looking at him.
Of course, he thought Gaold might stand out in the survival test.
He was a sincere believer, and even Sein acknowledged his
perseverance.
But there are situations where perseverance doesn't work.
No student could match Miro's level, which even the kingdom's
professionals acknowledged.
"Gaold, are you really okay?"
Some students asked with concern.
His skills weren't enough to be considered a rival, but his personality
was good, so they showed some kindness. But Gaold was resolute.
"Hmph, I will never side with someone who changes their words like a
bat."
Miro, receiving Gaold's sharp gaze, smiled brightly.
"Ho ho ho! Fine, I'll show you the spicy side of this genius
girl."
"Grrr!"
Gaold gritted his teeth.
He was furious at how she could be so confident after hurting his
feelings.
The survival test began, and a gruesome hell unfolded in stages.
As people were ejected from the virtual zone, they soon entered the
fifth stage, 'Torture.'
When Sein mentioned that he had been ejected at the fifth stage to
Shirone's group, he hesitated.
But Shirone thought that just the fact that the logic-focused Servant
had crossed the threshold showed the potential of Equilibrium, which manipulates both emotion and
reason.
And Gaold… surprisingly reached the sixth stage, 'Extreme Heat.' Even
the graduation students couldn't believe it.
"God, sacrifice this lamb to save everyone. Bring love and peace to the
world."
In the hellfire, Gaold knelt and prayed with his hands clasped.
The terrifying heat seeped through his teeth, and juice, not tears,
flowed from his eyes, but his prayer didn't stop.
He couldn't lose.
To surrender in the survival test he entered in God's name was
unthinkable.
The teachers watching through the vision sweated nervously.
Gaold's physical condition in reality was abnormal. A normal person
would have already lost consciousness, but somehow he was enduring.
"Give up, Gaold. You'll really die if you keep this up."
Miro spoke calmly.
Even in the extreme heat, her expression remained composed.
"God, please… burn me…"
Miro couldn't leave Gaold like this.
If his body was completely burned away, his spirit would leave his body
as well.
"Why are you doing this? This is just a magic school test. What are you
trying to prove about God's existence here?"
Gaold finally opened his eyes.
"I'm not trying to prove God. I can endure because God resides within
me."
Miro let out a hot breath.
How could someone be so stubborn?
"And…"
Gaold added.
"I don't want to lose to you."
Miro understood.
It wasn't just Gaold; countless mages in the world longed to surpass
her.
"Alright. But you don't have to do this. The survival test isn't
everything. You can work harder and beat me in the graduation exam."
Gaold closed his eyes again.
"God, I believe in the power of infinite love. Grant me…"
"Why are you doing this!"
Miro grabbed Gaold by the collar and lifted him up.
Just that action made Gaold's body jerk in reality.
"You live under constant criticism anyway! Whether God exists or not, is
it worth risking your life over a single word from me? Do you want to die and make me feel guilty? If
so, you're mistaken. No matter what…"
"Because I like you."
Miro's words stopped.
All the students watching through the vision were stunned. Only Sein
watched with a serious expression.
"What… what did you say?"
Miro asked, bewildered.
Gaold, knowing everyone was watching, didn't shy away.
"I said I like you. So I want you to understand me. I want you to say
I'm right."
Miro let go of his collar. She had no words for the confession she heard
in hell.
"So… you want to date me?"
Gaold nodded.
His eyes burned with heat and passion, but Miro responded simply, as
usual.
"But I don't like you."
Boom!
The intense heat exploded from Gaold's head, and he lost
consciousness.
Thus, Gaold failed the survival test, and Sein recalled that day,
saying,
"In a way, Gaold saved face. He didn't fail because of his religious
beliefs. But he did become the biggest fool. Everyone laughed at him, calling him the first man to get
rejected in hell."
In the end, Gaold's pure love was brutally rejected by Miro.
But before moving on to the most important event, Sein added one more
thing.
The truth is… he still doesn't fully understand it.
