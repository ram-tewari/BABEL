Chapter 427:
In front of the Military Community Gate.
"Heh, they're all scared. What should we do with them?"
"Kill them? Or maybe take them alive?"
The guards' voices were thin and raspy, as if their vocal cords were
strained.
But more surprising was the aura they emitted.
When facing a creature with murderous intent, whether it be a knife,
fangs, or claws, one feels a strong presence, but each has a different nature.
A murderer? A beast?
The aura from these guards was entirely different.
An unknown creature. Or perhaps a complex mix of existing
creatures.
'I can't be humiliated again.'
Kuan was ready to strike at any moment.
If they revealed their true nature, he would shoot external force in
three directions.
First, he would cut down the ones on the left, then use the recoil to
accelerate and swiftly eliminate the rest.
Tick. Tick. Tick.
A faint sound, barely noticeable to anyone but a Schema user, reached
Kuan's ears.
It was the sound of claws extending from the right hand of the guard
facing Armin.
"Everyone, stop!"
A loud voice boomed from beyond the gate.
Kuan immediately grounded himself and neutralized the external force,
but his sword had already tasted blood.
Thud! Thud!
The heads of two guards on the left fell to the ground.
Siina shuddered as she realized that Kuan, who had been standing beside
her moments ago, was now among the enemy ranks.
She hadn't noticed when he moved or acted.
While most swordsmen use propulsion for surprise attacks, Kuan moved by
suction.
An instant acceleration with no preparation or warm-up needed.
She felt fortunate that Kuan wasn't an enemy.
"You bastard! I'll kill you!"
As the guards charged at Kuan, he raised his sword horizontally, ready
to fight.
"Stop, you idiots! Didn't you hear the boss?"
The guards froze in place.
Their faces, though not yet transformed, were twisted into monstrous
expressions.
With a clanking sound, the iron gate opened, and a large middle-aged man
stepped out.
He wore a coat with long, soft fur like mink, and his shoulders were
adorned with the taxidermied heads of terrified weasels.
His unkempt hair hung to his shoulders, and he wore dark glasses with
pointed lenses.
This was Frankwine, the leader of Yamaeng, who controlled all the
hunting grounds near the mainland.
Armin walked up to him and asked, "Are you the person in charge
here?"
"Hmm."
Frankwine stroked his beard and examined Armin, then grinned, revealing
gold teeth.
"Young and full of energy. Yes, I'm the boss here. What do you
want?"
"We have a request for the military community."
"Ah! So you want to do business. Business is essential. Come in,
come in."
"Boss! They killed two of our men! Are you just going to let them
in?"
Frankwine glanced at the two headless bodies and shrugged.
"So what? Men fight, and sometimes they die, or get their heads cut
off. What's the big deal?"
"But boss…!"
Frankwine's face twisted in annoyance.
"Are you going to keep arguing with me? Are you dead? No, you're
alive, right? Then stop worrying about others and do your job."
The guards glared at Armin's group but ultimately had to step
aside.
Violent and absolute.
That was Armin's first impression of Yamaeng.
As they walked to the office, Frankwine briefly introduced the
factories.
Seeing it in person and hearing about it confirmed the immense
scale.
They produced food made from Elixirs, weapons, hunting supplies,
medicines, and more. The vast amount of Elixirs obtained from renting out nearby hunting grounds made it
the largest community on the mainland.
'They're definitely different from anyone we've met before.'
Feeling cautious, Armin probed gently.
"Thank you for your time. I heard you were busy."
"Ah, I drank too much yesterday. Didn't feel great. But what's the
point of resting? Might as well do something useful, haha."
Frankwine proudly opened the office door, like a hotel manager showing
off a room.
It was a cramped, filthy room, reeking of cigarettes and alcohol.
"Wait here for a moment. I'll finish up and be right
back."
After leading Armin's group into the office, Frankwine turned and
entered a room at the end of the hallway.
A handsome blond man with slicked-back hair was lounging with his legs
on the desk. He jumped up in surprise.
"Boss, you're here."
"I told you, if you sit at my desk again, I'll kill
you."
"Who knew you'd catch me? Anyway, how are they?"
This was Meatgun, Yamaeng's second-in-command.
Though Yamaeng had over 300 employees, Meatgun was the only one
qualified to guard Frankwine.
Frankwine lit a crumpled cigarette.
"They're capable. Let's give them a try. Do you have the estimate
Raesis sent? Give it to me."
Meatgun pulled a paper from his pocket.
Frankwine unfolded it, revealing a list of about ten creatures.
They were ranked in order of difficulty, all challenging to hunt.
"Heh, I'm crazy, but that woman is insane. How are we supposed to
get these? Our men wouldn't even come back with their bones."
"But it's a bold offer. She promised to raise the telomere levels
of 100 members to level 5."
"Hmm, level 5."
Telomere levels represent the pure energy needed to sustain life.
Higher telomere levels mean greater combat power.
At level 5, one could live up to 400 years without explosive biological
activity.
With 100 such subordinates, Yamaeng's future would be bright.
But it was curious why Raesis, who was always cautious, would make such
a bold offer.
"What's her plan? She listed only the top predators in the local
food chain."
Survival favors those best adapted to their environment, but that alone
doesn't make them the best.
Top predators have powerful traits that cover all weaknesses.
"She's a pervert, but this is too unique. Such distinct traits
aren't easy to combine with other creatures."
"She must have found a way. She's untrustworthy but not clumsy. Her
offer will benefit Yamaeng."
Frankwine crumpled the memo and stuffed it in his pocket.
"So, do you think we should accept this deal?"
"Yes. Whatever she's planning, it's a great opportunity to
strengthen Yamaeng. If we aim to dominate purgatory, we must ally with her."
Frankwine swung his arm and punched Meatgun's jaw.
Meatgun flew like a cannonball, crashing into the wall. As he fell to
the floor, a huge crack appeared in the wall.
As Meatgun clutched his forehead and stood up, Frankwine opened the door
and said, "Sit at my desk again, and I'll kill you."
Thud.
The door closed.
Armin's group sat in the office, all frowning.
The stench of alcohol and cigarettes was mixed with another foul
odor.
Siina waved her hand fan and said, "What is that
smell?"
The men had an idea but remained silent.
Etella tilted her head and said, "It smells like fish…"
Armin quickly changed the subject.
"Isn't it strange that the head of the military community is
suddenly so friendly?"
Siina agreed.
"We can't let our guard down. The guards were odd, and Frankwine's
aura was really strange. Something might be happening that we don't know about…"
Etella and Kuan nodded.
But even if something was happening elsewhere, they couldn't
know.
Frankwine entered with a cheerful smile.
"Sorry about that. Got a bit behind."
Siina immediately complained.
"Why is this office so dirty? And what's that smell? It's giving me
a headache."
Frankwine looked at her as if wondering why she was asking, then burst
into laughter.
"Haha! Right! I had a wild night yesterday…"
Armin gestured for him to sit.
"Please, have a seat. We have something important to
discuss."
Frankwine sat down, looking sheepish but calculating quickly.
All four were highly skilled, and there were two innocent-looking young
women among them.
Such people were rare in purgatory, where survival was
everything.
'They're from another world.'
That made things easier. Frankwine wasn't from purgatory either.
"So, what brings such formidable people to this shabby
place?"
He hadn't forgotten his subordinates' deaths.
But Frankwine's feelings were more businesslike than mournful.
"We want to rent your factory."
"Oh, starting strong. What do you want to make?"
Armin provided only the necessary information.
The goal was to produce a large Tagis.
They would supply the Elixirs and materials and pay for the factory
rental with Elixirs.
Frankwine licked his lips.
He didn't dislike the offer. Every deal was golden to a
businessman.
But something felt off.
Raesis's sudden estimate, and now the 1st Headquarters' request to make
a large Tagis.
'Something's happening with the rebels.'
Armin emphasized again.
"We'll make sure you're well compensated."
Frankwine snapped out of his thoughts and smiled broadly.
"Of course, you should come to us for such a big job. But Elixirs
alone won't do. We're doing too well lately."
"Then what?"
Elixirs were the most common currency for obtaining things on the
mainland.
Armin didn't understand why Elixirs wouldn't suffice.
"Hmm, what to do."
Frankwine pretended to ponder, then scribbled something at his
desk.
He tore a page from his memo book, crumpled it, and tossed it on the
table. Armin slowly unfolded it and read the contents.
His eyes, covered by a bandage, glowed.
