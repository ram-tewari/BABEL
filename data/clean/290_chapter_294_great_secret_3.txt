Chapter 294:
Great Secret (3)
Reina said.
"Behavioral instructions? Can something like that really
work?"
Shirone was already strangled by the steel wire. It didn't seem like
planting thoughts to trigger certain actions would improve the situation.
"We have no choice but to try. Honestly, we're not in a situation where
we can afford to hesitate. While it might take time for his neck to be fully severed, even a severed
artery would result in a fatal injury. By then, the unconscious mind will probably collapse at
tremendous speed."
Amy grew anxious.
"Then what should we do?"
Armin's solution was simple.
"We need to find Shirone."
"What? But this is Shirone's mind."
"To be precise, everything here is a shadow created by projecting
Shirone's core essence. We're going to find the core, not the shadow. We call it an avatar."
Amy recalled what she had heard from Godlock in the Valley of the
Whirlwind Serpent. The enormous shadow of the Whirlwind Serpent had turned out to be nothing more than a
smooth, small salamander. The people of Heaven had called it an avatar.
"Would this avatar be in the 11th level surface?"
Armin shook his head.
"It's unlikely. Which is why we'll have to summon it."
Reina asked.
"How do we summon it?"
Amy noticed before she heard Armin's answer.
"Couldn't we just ask the people here? After all, everyone here is a
reflection of Shirone, right?"
Armin nodded approvingly. It seemed the two of them were beginning to
grasp the nature of this world intuitively.
"That's right. We just need to ask people. We call it a keyword.
While it's quite difficult from the outside, here, we can just ask. If we inject Shirone as a keyword,
the avatar will rise to the 11th level. Now, let's get started."
Armin led the two women to the counter and asked the middle-aged woman
who was knitting.
"Do you know someone called Shirone?"
As soon as Armin spoke, the bard's music stopped as if on cue. The
people's murmuring could no longer be heard.
It wasn't until all sound vanished that they realized just how much
noise had surrounded them before. It was now perfectly silent.
Amy felt a chill as she looked around.
The previously despondent people had snapped to attention, their eyes
clear and focused on Armin.
The woman at the counter was the same. She had been motionless as if
frozen but now opened her mouth after a delay.
"Why are you looking for that person?"
Armin let out a soft chuckle and smiled.
'That person,' indeed. Not saying Shirone's name showed his
consciousness was appropriately cautious.
From here, he would have to tread carefully. They couldn't be too
obvious, and statements showing hostility were forbidden.
"I have something important to tell them."
The woman thought for a moment before continuing her knitting.
"Don't know. Don't know who that person is."
"I see."
Armin exhaled with a sigh, thinking this wouldn't be easy.
Well-trained indeed, he thought. No matter how specialized a mage was in
psyche, a scenario where their neck wason the line usually causes a crack in their composure.
Despite the thunderstorm raging outside, the fragments of Shirone's
consciousness still maintained their vigilance. This was a testament to how disciplined Shirone's mind
had become through constant refinement and discipline.
"I understand. Then we'll be going."
The woman at the counter spoke without looking up.
"Pay the roof fee before you go."
Even on the brink of death, it seemed he wouldn't be satisfied until
collecting what was due. This too was the mindset of a mage seeking 100% efficiency.
'Huhu, you're a very thorough person.'
It was customary for those who stayed at an inn without lodging or
meals, merely to shelter from the rain, to pay what was called a 'roof fee'.
But Amy had no money. Even if she did, it was questionable whether
real-world money would work here.
At that moment, Armin rummaged through his pocket. He promptly pulled
out and paid with a gold coin.
"Here you go."
"Huh?"
Amy observed the gold coin he handed over. As expected, it wasn't the
currency of the real world but a cog-shaped gold coin unique to this realm.
The woman at the counter examined the coin and furrowed her brow.
"This is too much."
"Keep the change."
"I can't do that. Here, take it back."
"Really, it's fine."
When Armin declined with a smile, the woman coolly accepted.
"Alright then. Blessings to you."
Amy pouted.
Though the innkeeper's manner, appearance, and gender were different,
somehow they reminded her of Shirone's usual demeanor.
"Now then, let's get going."
Armin said, his expression growing serious.
They left the inn but didn't dare step beyond the eaves. Water was
rising from the ground like a flood.
Amy shivered as a cold chill swept over her.
"If this continues, the whole world will be submerged."
Armin shook his head.
"No, the world will disappear before that."
Amy turned to look at the inn's interior through the window.
The warmth blocked off by just a single pane of glass made her feel
colder, but seeing the people inside resting peacefully brought a small sense of comfort.
If Shirone dies, all of them will disappear.
Even though they weren't real people, Amy found herself wishing they
could continue living in this world for a long time.
"First, since we've injected the keyword 'Shirone', Shirone's
avatar should surface. Divers call this process 'netting'."
"I see."
"But that's where the problem lies. There's not much time, so I'll
explain as we go. Once the water rises further, even moving will become difficult."
Armin dashed into the downpour where nothing was visible. Amy caught up
with him and asked.
"Where are we going? We don't even know where Shirone
is."
"First of all…  We'll need weapons, most likely."
Armin stopped in front of a two-story building and looked up. He saw a
sign with crossed axes and swords.
The three once again took shelter under the weapon shop's eaves. This
time the eaves were narrow, so they had to press against the wall.
Armin turned to the two women beside him and asked.
"Do either of you know how to use any weapons?"
Reina spoke first.
"I learned some swordsmanship when I was young..."
"I'm a mage."
When Amy said it sheepishly, Armin smiled. It seemed she was embarrassed
to confidently admit she was a mage after the scolding she had received earlier.
"I see. Hope we can find something useful."
As Armin was about to enter the weapon shop, Amy hurriedly stopped
him.
"Wait, but what's with the money?"
Even if there were good weapons available, they couldn't be purchased
without money. Moreover, the money Armin used at the inn wasn't from reality.
Since Armin hadn't been in Shirone's consciousness for long, Amy
wondered if she might also find a way to procure money herself.
"Ah, I withdrew it."
"Withdrew? From where?"
Armin pointed to the sky.
"From Shirone, of course. Since this is Shirone's consciousness, you can
create anything you need. Just think to yourself that you need money. The amount you receive will
correspond to the level of trust Shirone feels toward you. Let's see…"
Armin searched his pocket and pulled out gold coins.
"I have 9 now, so I received 10 gold total. If these are even
called gold, haha."
Looking at the cog-shaped coins in Armin's hand, the two women both
closed their eyes and thought about needing money.
Reina searched her pocket. Her face lit up with surprise and
said.
"Huh? It worked! Seven coins, seven gold."
Amy looked on with wide eyes.
Considering how long she had known Shirone and the fact that she had
never heard of this Armin before, Amy expected Reina to receive far more coins than Armin.
Yet, contrary to her expectations, Armin won.
Did this mean Shirone thought little of Reina? No, it was more likely
that he trusted Armin that much.
'Who is this person? What connection does he have with Shirone?'
Anyway, Amy felt somewhat expectant too. These coins were useless in
reality, but in this mental world, they were the only valid currency.
'Hmm, sir Armin got 10, sister Reina got 7. So maybe I'll get around
20?'
Amy stuck out her tongue and searched her pocket, then switched hands
again and searched the other pocket.
Her expression gradually worsened.
"Huh?"
Reina tilted her head and asked.
"What's wrong?"
"No, hold on."
Amy frantically searched every pocket in her clothing. But no matter how
thoroughly she checked, she couldn't find anything.
Her face flushed red as she spoke in disbelief.
"There aren't any gold coins?"
Armin nodded as if understanding the reason.
"Hmm, I see. If Shirone refuses the withdrawal, that's entirely
possible."
Amy was stunned.
The number of coins was supposed to represent how much Shirone trusted
someone. But how could she end up with nothing at all?
"N- No, I'll try again."
Armin dissuaded her as if it was useless.
"It's no use trying. Once it fails, no matter how many times you try,
the result will be the same. Let's focus on buying what we need with what we have."
Withdrawing gold coins is an extremely simple task. If gold coins didn't
come out once, it would be the same no matter how many times you try.
Reina consoled Amy and led her to the weapon shop.
"Come on, Amy, let's just go. Sixteen gold should be plenty."
Amy shook off Reina's arm, stopped in her tracks, and shouted.
"That can't be right!"
Tears welled up in Amy's eyes.
The seven gold coins Reina received represented Shirone's feelings for
her. She didn't want to buy her weapon with such a thing. If she had to buy something, it had to be with
the money Shirone gave her.
Armin fully understood her sentiments. This was exactly why grave
robbing within consciousness was universally forbidden.
When someone learns about the innermost parts of another person's
psyche, it's easy to feel as though everything they knew was a lie.
Even though that was actually human nature.
"Miss Amy, there's no need to feel betrayed. Shirone is desperately
holding on. When emotional fluctuations occur, such things can happen. Perhaps you conveyed some
negative emotions to him before coming here..."
Amy vehemently shook her head.
"No, that can't be. I..."
Amy cut her words short.
Honestly, how could she be sure? No matter how much she thought she knew
Shirone, she couldn't claim to understand every corner of his heart.
Perhaps it was because she couldn't properly respond to Zenogre's
attack. Perhaps Shirone had hoped she would have melted the web with Ignite to save him, only to be
disappointed by her lack of action.
Armin sighed. In the end, situations like this were inevitable when
navigating Shirone's consciousness, and it wasn't a question with a clear-cut answer.
"Let's go in now. We can't delay any longer."
When Armin firmly cut off the conversation, Amy had no choice but to
follow. Reina, looking at Amy's dejected expression, felt sympathy but knew there was no way to comfort
her in this situation.
"Welcome."
The shop owner put down his newspaper and greeted them.
Amy glanced up, only for her eyes to widen in shock. He looked a bit
older, but he was undoubtedly Nade.
"Nade? How are you here?"
"Hm? You know me? But I'm seeing you for the first
time?"
Amy turned to Armin with eyes demanding an explanation. Armin was also
acquainted with Nade. He was one of Shirone's friends who had hidden in his house before.
"It's Shirone's projection. The concept of 'weapon shop' and the
concept of 'Nade' have combined. It seems Shirone associates Nade with an interest in this kind of
thing."
Reina said.
"Sometimes that happens in dreams too. You fight someone you think
is a villain, only to wake up and realize they were a family member you quarreled with the night
before."
"Yes, it's a natural mental process. And having a complex
projection means there exists a more impressive concept here than elsewhere. Shall we look
around?"
Nade stared at Amy intently and furrowed his brow.
"I really don't know you. Perhaps you're mistaking me for someone
else?"
"Ah, maybe so. The person I know is much younger. I'm
sorry."
"Hahaha! I do get told I look younger than my age quite often."
Amy pretended not to know to avoid stimulating Shirone's mind
unnecessarily. The human mind is so intricate that even the smallest disturbance could trigger a
butterfly effect.
