Chapter 474:
The Second Heaven, Lakia.
As Gaold walked towards the walls of the Hall of Corruption, Miro was
lost in countless thoughts.
Ever since she heard his desperate cries 20 years ago, she had always
felt that Gaold might never give up.
'Foolish human…'
Miro suppressed the urge to curse out loud.
She looked at Gaold's muscular body, hidden under the
bloodstains.
His once slender frame was now completely different.
His skin was covered in scars, with no space left unmarked.
Anyone could see that his body had been honed through relentless
training.
Was it the contrast that pleased her?
Even in the face of a monumental event that could determine the fate of
humanity, Miro wanted to make a joke.
'You've become quite sexy?'
Words she would have easily thrown out before now stayed
unspoken.
Perhaps she was a little angry.
Reflecting her feelings, Miro's eyes grew cold.
He had really come.
He had crushed the fire giants, torn apart countless Maras and fallen
angels, and made it here.
'But even if he's here…'
What difference does it make?
Just to have me? To possess me? To hear whispers of love meant only for
him while lying beside him?
Any human might desire that, but Gaold had already crossed the
line.
This was the heart of Heaven's enemy territory, and with her magic
sealed by Kariel, her value was reduced to that of a powerless hostage, weighed against the lives of
humanity.
'And…'
There were two archangels here.
Kariel and Uriel.
Even if Kariel's power was weakened, teaming up with Uriel, the
Archangel of Destruction, made them the worst possible combination.
The conclusion was that even if he came, he couldn't leave.
Gaold would die here, and so would she.
The rest of humanity would be slaughtered by Heaven's army.
"Why did you come?"
Miro coldly spat out.
She could control the rising anger, but she couldn't let herself show
any affection for Gaold.
Reminding the enemy of the hostage's value would only increase the
number of victims.
"Because of you, everything is a mess. Do you even know what you've
done?"
Miro desperately wished.
'Go back. Please, even now, go back, you fool!'
Seeing Gaold looking up at her in a daze, Miro added some emotion to her
words.
"Put it back! You've ruined everything I've dedicated my life to.
So put it back…!"
Thud!
Gaold's eyes lost focus, and his knees hit the ground.
His body trembled as nerves fired along his spine.
At the end of the terrifying pain was a profound, emotionless
calm.
His head, which had been hanging limply, slowly rose again.
A small voice escaped his cracked lips.
"I'm sorry."
Miro clenched her lips, struggling to hide the shockwave exploding in
her heart.
"I'm sorry. It took too long, didn't it? At first…"
Gaold's words and breaths tangled as his body trembled again.
The 20 years that had been cut off reconnected, and only his feelings
for Miro remained clear.
"At first, I thought it would be quick, but it kept getting
delayed. I wanted to come sooner, but I couldn't…"
As Gaold's eyes reddened, Miro's eyes also filled with warmth.
The emotions condensed to the extreme in one human couldn't be expressed
in mere words.
Even without words, the fact that Gaold had come this far spoke volumes
about his 20-year journey.
Realizing this, Gaold stopped explaining and looked up.
His eyes, which had been half-conscious, finally returned to
normal.
No one knew, but Miro recognized this as the Gaold from 20 years
ago.
"Let's go back, Miro. Let's just… go back."
Miro gritted her teeth.
She couldn't even identify the emotions surging within her.
Anger, resentment, or perhaps something else she wanted to disguise as
those feelings.
'I must die.'
Her rational mind immediately offered a solution to block her
emotions.
She had to die here.
Even though the situation was dire, if there was even a slight chance to
prevent the final war, some of those dispatched to Heaven had to survive and leave Lakia.
"I…"
As Miro steeled herself and began to speak, a massive flash of light
erupted from the rear of the Hall of Corruption.
The immense energy wave traveled dozens of kilometers to reach them,
causing Kariel and Uriel to turn their heads.
The shockwave was coming from Araboth, where Anke Ra was.
"Ikael…"
Kariel muttered with a twisted expression.
Currently, the archangels who governed the four fundamental forces were
testing Ikael's qualifications as the archangel.
If the archangels of the four forces subdued Ikael, it would be
beneficial for him.
They would act according to their own judgments outside the archangel's
authority, and his decisions would align with theirs, making them less significant.
On the other hand, if Ikael subdued the archangels of the four forces,
the situation would become complicated.
She wouldn't tolerate the angels' reckless behavior, and Kariel, having
lost the fire giants, would also be threatened.
Therefore, he had to hope that the archangels of the four forces would
defeat Ikael, but Kariel found himself unable to choose.
A part of him couldn't accept the idea of Ikael losing to anyone.
'No, that's not true.'
Kariel shook his head.
She had to be defeated.
But the one to do it had to be him, and no one else.
'I will crush her. Only I have the right to crush her.'
An even stronger energy wave swept through Lakia.
The high spire of Araboth shook from deep within the ground.
Boom!
The area where the wave passed was left spotless.
It was the epitome of purity, unseen in the human world.
"Huff, huff."
Ikael knelt on one knee, breathing heavily.
Around her, the archangels lay as if dead.
'She's strong.'
The thought simultaneously crossed the minds of the archangels of the
four forces.
'Indeed, the supreme law of Heaven, Ataraxia.'
Her amplification was unique, beyond the reach of intellect, a concept
before existence and non-existence.
The archangels of the four forces were each powerful in their own right,
but even with all four of them combined, they could only bring her to a groggy state.
"Phew, let's consider this test concluded."
Metatron was the first to rise.
"For now, Ikael, you are still the archangel. We trust you and
leave the judgment regarding Shirone entirely to you."
Shirone.
Ikael thought of that name as she slowly stood up.
Perhaps she could have persuaded them with words alone.
But she had defended her judgment regarding Shirone, even if it meant a
show of force among angels.
Why?
What was that boy to her?
The remaining three archangels rose from their positions.
"We agree with Metatron's opinion."
"But as archangels governing Heaven, we cannot overlook human
recklessness. We will not engage in the war by Anke Ra's command, but we will intervene through our
Maras."
Ikael didn't stop them.
She couldn't, and as the archangel on Heaven's side, it didn't make
sense to prevent the punishment of humans.
"Anke Ra's command was only to prohibit angelic
activities."
As Ikael indirectly expressed her agreement and emphasized once more,
the archangels of the four forces nodded briefly and looked at each other.
The battles were taking place in all six heavens except Araboth.
Each would oversee one and handle it.
"Then, let's go."
As the four archangels simultaneously spread their wings of light, a
sacred vibration created a harmony that shook the world.
Watching the archangels disappear in an instant, Ikael turned coldly and
headed for the spire of Araboth.
The only one she had to protect was Anke Ra.
Thud.
Uriel moved for the first time.
As he jumped down from the wall and landed on the ground, the entire
scenery seemed to bend towards him.
As he took a step forward, Gaold stepped back at the same pace.
Having never retreated before, Gaold's followers, including Sein, could
guess the pressure he was under.
'So this is how it ends.'
Sein controlled his emotions.
They had made it this far, but the true challenge wasn't the fire
giants, the Maras, or the fallen angels.
It was the archangel standing before them.
Gaold had managed to deal with his three-horned Mara, Shiva, with
extreme endurance, but Uriel's power, based on the law of his followers, was expected to far surpass
Shiva's.
"Gaold."
"I'll handle this."
Gaold, still not taking his eyes off Uriel, responded to Sein's
voice.
"Rescue Miro in the meantime."
Sein knew it was an impossible plan.
Even if all ten of them attacked Uriel, it was doubtful how long they
could hold out, let alone Gaold facing him alone.
'But we have to try.'
As everyone, led by Sein, steeled themselves, a powerful aura exploded
from Uriel's body.
Pure destruction.
The aura was so overwhelming that any being, no, any existence, buried
in it would be utterly destroyed, leaving no bones behind.
"No one can pass me. The only thing you can do here is
die."
Gaold spoke.
"Go."
Zulu moved first, followed by the others.
They might be annihilated upon approach, but without trying, there was
no way to save Miro.
The seven members scattered left and right, passing Uriel.
'What is this?'
Without any time to gather his thoughts, Kangnan broke through the
castle gate with a powerful stomp and charged inside.
Just before entering the Hall of Corruption, Sain turned
around.Uriel was still staring solely at Gaold.
'Was he testing us?'
Though he clenched his teeth in frustration at the sense of mockery,
missing such an opportunity would have been foolish.
Sain led everyone in their infiltration of the Hall of
Corruption.
Gaold slowly curled his lips into a grin."Are you offering mercy,
befitting a great archangel?"
Uriel gave no reply.
Even knowing they will die, they charge forward.That is
humanity.And among all humans, the one standing before Uriel now—Gaold—gave off the most bizarre
impression.
"What are you dreaming of? There is no hope for you."
"Hope?"
A concentrated sphere of atmospheric pressure formed in Gaold's right
hand.It was the Vacuum Press, powerful enough to block even the Photon Cannon of Ataraxia.
"Hope is nothing more than an illusion born of despair. What drives
humanity isn't hope. It's something far more brutal..."
Gaold's body shot forward like an arrow.
In an instant, he reached Uriel's flank and swung his right hand,
unleashing the Vacuum Press.
"It's despair."
