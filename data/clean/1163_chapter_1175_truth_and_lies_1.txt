Chapter 1175:
Truth and Lies (1)
Siena twisted her body with all her strength as Yula, the leader of the
Wind Guards, held her captive.
"Kuan! Kuan!"
"Don't try anything foolish. Killing you here and now would be
effortless."
Yula's words were likely true, but Siena also knew she wouldn't be so
easily disposed of.
"It's enough already! I'll go with you—just stop attacking
Kuan!"
Yula remained silent.
'He must die.'
From the beginning, Siena had only been taken hostage to neutralize
Kuan.
'What greater shackle exists than death itself?'
If this could be the end of it.
That was the thought lingering in Yula's mind—until she heard the most
horrific sound.
'What is that?'
She turned to see a storm of flesh and blood, spinning like it had been
fed into a machine.
"Stop."
The formation of forty Wind Guards halted, and Siena's face went
pale.
"Kuan…?"
How many human lives had been thrown into the grinder to create the
scene before her?
"Siena!"
Kuan tore through the Wind Guards' defensive formation, and Yula quickly
assessed the casualties.
"Thirty-seven."
A battle lasting no more than ten seconds—
And in that time, he had slaughtered more than half of a sixty-man
unit.
"Kh…!"
As Kuan spun through the air and landed, he swayed—
A lone swordsman continuing his dance, as if trying to quell the fury of
his blade.
With one leg limping and only one arm wielding a sword, his form should
have looked absurd.
But—
"Siena… protect…"
With each staggering step, the world trembled.
With each rotation of his sword, nausea churned in the gut.
Yula understood.
"I can't predict him. There's no logical connection between one
movement and the next."
This was the domain of the Fool.
That was why Kuan seemed to disappear and reappear within her
vision.
The surviving Wind Guards regrouped.
"We failed…"
It was not something that could be blamed.
'Yes, failure is not tolerated in this world. The Wind Guards are the
greatest swordsmanship force in existence.'
Achieving fluid mechanics through the human body wasn't something mere
effort could accomplish.
'Even with the losses suffered in the Great Purification, the new
recruits filled the gaps with sheer genius.'
Their performance in formation combat rehearsals had been near
indistinguishable from veteran warriors.
'But to Kuan…'
Even the smallest gaps seemed like vast chasms between life and
death.
'Thirty-seven died because of it.'
Lost in thought, Yula slowly lowered her sword and removed her owl
mask.
'Parca Kuan.'
He was limping toward her now,
But there was no certainty he would still be limping a moment
later.
Sweat formed on her forehead.
'This is an utterly monstrous presence.'
Whatever happened next, Kuan would make it reality.
'He is—'
Her equal.
A mirrored existence at the peak of the summit.
"Stop."
As her blade pointed at Kuan's forehead, for the first time, he came to
a halt.
The very air around them quivered, and the Wind Guards clenched their
jaws.
"Kh…!"
In terms of pure precision, Yula's accuracy in pointing her sword was
likely at an atomic level.
'He's not caught.'
For Kuan to move his body with the same level of precision—without
moving at all—was the only way to match it.
'He seems still, but in reality, he's moving. A movement no human could
perceive.'
The only thing that changed was perception itself.
"Kuan, I offer you my applause. Today, you have reached the
pinnacle of technique."
"For that reason, I will make this proposal again—this is the first
time in my history. Join the Wind Guards."
Conviction burned in her gaze.
"I will grant you the authority to lead the First
Division."
She was the leader of the Wind Guards.
"Captain!"
One of her subordinates protested, but Yula ignored them.
"You once asked me—doesn't it disgrace me to call formation
swordplay the strongest? You lost a leg in exchange for that answer. But now, you are worthy of speaking
those words. Lead the Wind Guards. This position is proof that it is the greatest swordsmanship in the
world."
Yula had never once betrayed her convictions, so every word she spoke
was sincere.
Kuan looked up at the sky and answered.
"Siena… protect…"
His voice was slow, uneven—
And for a moment, not just the Wind Guards, but even Siena felt a
chilling thought creep into her mind.
'No… that can't be it.'
This was just… another level of mastery.
'I'm in danger right now, so he's doing something extreme. So…'
This could still be undone.
Siena clung to that belief, but Yula finally understood
everything.
"Is that so?"
It was inevitable.
'What a tragedy.'
To hide the emotion on her face, Yula put her owl mask back on.
"Kill him."
All Wind Guards except for Yula scattered like an explosion, launching
toward Kuan.
At Delta Headquarters' garden, Shirone and Kido faced each other with
lethal intent.
"Shirone."
Kido barely moved his lips.
"I don't want to fight you."
"Same here."
Though they stood as enemies, they both understood too well why the
other was fighting.
"I just want to go to Uorin. That's all."
All the nations involved in the Holy War would be desperate to kill
her.
"Then why are you here?" Shirone asked.
"If protecting Uorin is all that matters, why not stop her? Why are
you going along with this?"
"Uorin…"
Sadness flickered in Kido's eyes.
"She doesn't love me."
"This is all I can do. If I don't give her what she wants… She'll
leave me."
'That's not true.'
Uorin wouldn't abandon Kido,
But Shirone also understood his desperate feelings.
Shirone spoke.
"I'm worried about Teacher Siena. And Kuan, too."
Thormia had already abandoned Siena.
Shirone was the only one who could save them.
'I've wasted too much time here.
Kuan can't last much longer.'
Through Omega, he knew the combat capabilities of all 100 Wind
Guards.
'Especially Yula…'
As Shirone withdrew his killing intent and shifted his stance, Kashan's
Royal Guards began moving in response.
Although it was a truce born out of necessity, Kido said this as he
passed Shirone.
Though it was a ceasefire out of necessity, Kido still spoke to Shirone
as he passed by.
"I owe you one."
Shirone's eyes burned.
"Kido."
The two turned back toward each other, and Shirone, smiling,
asked—
"We're friends, right?"
Kido kicked off the ground, calling out—
"Of course!"
None of the Wind Guards screamed, but Siena wanted to block her
ears.
The sound of flesh being sliced, bones snapping, organs
rupturing—
Their bodies were crying out in agony.
"Please… just stop!"
The storm of blood before her—how much of it belonged to Kuan?
"Siena!"
Soaked in crimson rain, Kuan emerged from the carnage like a
specter.
Only twenty-four Wind Guards remained.
He had cut down seventy-five on his own.
Their annihilation was already certain.
Yula muttered—
"Astonishing."
Wind Guards were the wind itself.
The reason they were the strongest was because no one could escape the
wind.
And yet—
"He's dodging it."
Kuan was slipping through the wind itself.
A cripple, missing an arm, an eye, and an ear—his mind was surely
shattered as well.
Or perhaps…
'He has no need to think.'
Watching the swordsman's dance, Yula shuddered at the memory rising from
the depths of her mind.
'That was it.'
She had never been able to envision the perfect sword technique,
But she had once felt something beyond her senses.
'The swordsmanship I dreamed of as a child.'
A pure killing technique that ignored all physical limitations—
A technique capable of cutting down any foe.
'I wanted to be the strongest swordswoman.'
She had become the leader of the Wind Guards, commanding ninety-nine
prodigies like they were mere limbs.
But now, the one genius she had crushed was shattering everything she
had built.
"Siena…"
With every staggered step, with every swing of his blade,
The black curtain of the Wind Guards was dyed red.
'I was the one who cut him down.'
She had severed his Achilles tendon.
That should have ended his path as a swordsman.
Yet he had found his way here—
'To protect her.'
If a sword existed only to take lives,
Then Yula was undoubtedly the strongest swordswoman in the world.
But Kuan's sword—
'It exists to protect someone.'
"Let's end this."
Setting Siena down, Yula stepped forward to face the man who had cut
down ninety-nine of her warriors.
'His back is exposed.'
Just as Siena was about to enter Spirit Zone,
Yula spoke first.
"Don't make a mistake."
How keen was her perception?
Siena asked herself.
'I know how sensitive swordsmen are.
This isn't an unusual reaction.
But even so…
She's too fast.
Is it just my imagination?'
If the unknown was merely a feeling,
Then Yula's sense of it was something beyond monstrous.
"If you want to fight, go ahead.
But think carefully—will your actions help him, or harm
him?"
Siena had no choice but to stay silent.
'I don't even know how to help him.'
Yula took another step forward.
'I can reach him.'
Wind Guards embodied aerodynamics,
But Yula's skill was far deeper.
'My senses remain in absolute stillness.'
Even a calm surface of water was, in truth, a sea of countless atoms in
chaotic motion.
"The pinnacle of motion in stillness."
Though Kuan seemed to flicker in her vision,
Her blade was aimed straight at his heart.
"I'm coming."
Kuan and Yula moved at the same time.
Their speed seemed shockingly slow compared to their true skill.
Siena was puzzled—
And when they clashed—
She never saw what had happened.
A gust of wind passed between them,
And then—silence.
They stood with their backs to each other, unmoving.
The owl mask split in two, revealing Yula's pale, smiling face.
A thin line of blood trickled down her forehead, over her nose, past her
lips, and finally dripped from her chin.
"Hah… Hah…"
A single, flawless cut—
From her chest to her abdomen, slicing through her core.
Blood sprayed from the wound like a fountain,
And the force of it knocked her off her feet.
"Kuan!"
Siena rushed to his side as he collapsed to his knees.
"Kuan! Are you okay? Kuan!"
"I… missed."
Siena flinched, turning toward Yula with wary eyes.
"I was so close… but I didn't reach him."
Why?
'Ah…'
The confusion in Yula's gaze faded, giving way to clarity.
'Is it because he had something to protect?'
To Yula, Uorin was merely an object—
A symbol of her convictions, nothing more.
"Lucky you."
Yula's expression turned peaceful.
"You've become the most noble person in this world. Even nobler
than the Empress of Kashan."
Her breath ran out.
'I'm sorry, Your Majesty.'
She could only hope that, one day,
Uorin would meet someone who would truly fight for her.
And then, Yula died.
"Kuan… Kuan!"
Siena shook him again.
"Siena."
"Kuan! Are you conscious?"
Relief spread across her face—
But the moment she saw his condition, her expression turned ghostly
pale.
"…Protect."
She shook her head.
"No. It's over now. You won. You can come back to me
now."
"Siena… I…"
"No!"
Siena couldn't accept it.
"This isn't fair! You promised me! You said you'd stay by my side!
Never again… never again…!"
Never leave me again.
The contradiction in those words finally struck her,
And she buried her face into his chest.
"Aaaaaah! Aaaaaah!"
Shirone looked at the scattered remains of the Wind Guards,
At Yula's lifeless body,
And at Kuan, muttering the same words over and over.
"Teacher…"
Before Siena's weeping figure,
He found no words of comfort.
