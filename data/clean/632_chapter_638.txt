Chapter 638:
Even after the administrator's announcement, many people remained in
their seats, still lingering in the aftermath of the graduation exam.
Baikal also stayed, gathering the voices of scouts from other
countries.
As expected, the name most frequently mentioned was Shirone, and
Elizabeth felt proud of that.
"Coming to Alpheas Magic School was worth it. I don't know how the
exams in other regions went, but none received as much attention as this one."
Now, the scouts had to write their reports.
Detailed evaluations of all 30 participants would be recorded,
categorized by grade, and submitted to the association. The kingdom's magic department would then set
contract terms and begin negotiations.
"We must secure Shirone. Scouts from other countries have already
started moving. They're preparing huge signing bonuses and options."
"We still have a chance. According to the Red Line Agreement, the
magic department of the country where the student graduated has the first negotiation
rights."
This applied even to international students.
"Convincing Shirone won't be easy. He's proven his skills. If he
invokes his right to refuse negotiations and announces it to the Red Line, countries worldwide will
competitively raise their offers. He has no reason to accept ours."
"…He calculated this from the start and approached the graduation
exam accordingly. Now the tables have turned."
Lara said,
"We need to offer not just the best contract in the kingdom but
something extraordinary. Considering the Empire might make an offer, it's not a loss."
Elizabeth agreed.
"If it's reasonable, Shirone wouldn't want to leave Tormia. After
all, it's where he's lived his whole life."
Leaving a place filled with one's life history isn't easy.
"The problem is defining 'reasonable.' Even extraordinary offers
are based on experience. The current highest salary in the kingdom is 24 million gold, signed ten years
ago. Even without additional benefits and options, it caused quite a stir among the nobles. After all,
it was just a fresh graduate without certification."
24 million gold was an amount that an uncertified mage in Tormia's
mercenary guild couldn't earn in 10,000 years of average economic activity.
Even considering the immense value magic creates, the income disparity
was hard to accept.
The monopoly of human resources with no alternative industry, the issue
directly tied to national defense, and the competitive psychology of countries integrated into the Red
Line Network created the unique nature of the magic field.
In reality, countries shared a common sentiment of wanting to retain
well-known mages, even if it meant spending billions of gold annually.
"But the person in that contract easily passed certification and is
now the commander of the kingdom's elite magic unit. Certification isn't an issue if the contract is
renewed."
"Then how much should we offer? 100 million? A billion? The Empire
can afford it. It's not that Shirone's magic is 100 or 1,000 times stronger. It's because only Shirone
can perform that magic. If they throw money at it, the kingdom can't compete."
Veteran Baikal's prediction was likely accurate.
"We need to offer more than money in the initial negotiations.
Since he's a commoner, granting him a noble title is one option. But I'm skeptical if the magic
department will do that."
"Why not? We must secure Shirone."
"That's because we're mages and scouts. There are many
considerations, like the nobles' opposition and the royal family member Pony's elimination. From now on,
it's politics."
Baikal's wrinkles deepened.
"And politics always betray individual common sense."
The participants, who had rested in the waiting room, left the colosseum
only after the audience had cleared out.
Some spectators who hadn't left the school yet clapped from a distance,
and many parents searched for their children.
"Mom!"
Shirone spotted Olina and Vincent standing with the Ogent family and ran
over.
"Shirone!"
The first to embrace him was, of course, Olina, who buried her face in
her now-grown son's chest, crying.
"It was tough, wasn't it? Are you hurt anywhere?"
Despite having overcome the massive hurdle of passing the graduation
exam, her first concern was her child's health, making Shirone's eyes well up.
"I'm fine, Mom. I'm sorry."
He didn't want to show her how fiercely he had fought.
"You did well. You really did well, Shirone."
Vincent hugged both his son and wife, while Rian and Reina watched with
pleased expressions.
Other participants also reunited with their parents, and Amy's mother,
Isis, pointed to the colosseum.
"There's Amy."
Amy's face, looking thoughtful and dazed, revealed no emotions.
"Amy, over here."
Hearing Isis's voice, Amy snapped out of it and approached with her head
down.
Final graduation rank: 4th.
Though she had placed high, it was uncertain if her pride, having never
missed first place since childhood, would accept it.
"I'm worried. She must have had high expectations, especially since
she repeated the exam."
Amy's father, Shakora, said sternly,
"Don't show it. Pretending not to know is helping."
Amy stopped in front of them, her shoulders trembling.
"Mom. Dad."
Not knowing what to say, she burst into tears and made a V sign with her
fingers.
"I… I did it. I passed."
Overwhelmed, Shakora couldn't contain his emotions and hugged his
daughter.
"Yes! Well done, my daughter! You're the best!"
Though she was 4th now, it didn't mean she would always be 4th.
How much pressure must she have felt taking the exam a second time,
bearing the honor of the Karmis family?
'This is just the beginning, Amy! Be proud of what you've
achieved!'
Having done her best and feeling satisfied, her rank didn't matter to
her parents.
"Our daughter passed!"
Shakora shouted, forgetting his dignity, making Isis blush.
"Dear, don't. You're embarrassing…"
"Haha!"
Amy, wiping her tears and snot, laughed brightly.
Unlike the affectionate Karmis family, the Mercodine family was more
reserved.
"I never expected you to graduate. My mind must be
rusty."
Albino's first words made Arganes nudge him.
"Is that what you say to your son? Congratulations,
Iruki."
Arganes spoke formally to her son, and Iruki just nodded.
Though she was his biological mother who had nursed him, the Mercodine
bloodline separated emotion and reason, making it harder for Arganes to relate to her son as he
grew.
"You're definitely getting rusty. Should I open it up and clean it
for you?"
"Heh heh heh!"
Albino chuckled at Iruki's typically emotionless Mercodine joke,
touching his nose as he asked,
"So, what will you do now? If you really want to clean my head, I
won't stop you."
Iruki looked around the colosseum.
"I'm quite satisfied. I'll go to Yongrye."
Albino's lips curled meaningfully.
Machines are used and discarded, but humans are replicated.
Iruki would be more than adequate as his successor.
"Are you sure? This field isn't easy. Being a mage is a good life
too."
"I said I'm satisfied."
Asking twice was enough to confirm his resolve.
"Graduate and come to the capital. I'll make the
preparations."
Each family had its own tendencies, but in the end, they were
family.
Meanwhile, elsewhere, a family reunion was awkward and strained.
"Congratulations."
Bolum extended his hand for a handshake, but Nade turned his head away,
not wanting to speak.
Feeling awkward, Bolum withdrew his hand and nudged his wife.
"Say something."
Teria glared fiercely, but Nade wasn't scared like before.
He had gained the freedom to do anything.
"Are you going to become a mage?"
Teria, though uninterested in her son, was well-versed in which mages
made a fortune.
She knew that being the runner-up in the graduation exam could lead to a
lucrative contract.
"No. I won't become a mage. No association would take someone like
me."
Teria's face twisted in frustration.
"Why did we send you to magic school then…!"
"Fine. If you don't want to, don't."
Bolum's words made Teria's eyes widen.
"Dear! What are you saying?"
"You stop too. We didn't do well either. So, Nade, if not a mage,
what will you do?"
Even if they didn't want to speak, family made them open their
mouths.
"I'm not quitting right away. I'll work as an uncertified mage for
now. Then I'll save money and open an alchemy shop. Under my name, not West."
Though his words were filled with denial and distrust of his family,
Bolum nodded and pulled out a document from his inner pocket.
"Take this. It's land I saved for you. Sell it to help open your
shop."
Nade looked up in surprise, but Teria was even more shocked.
"Dear! How could you do this without consulting me…! And where did
this money come from?"
"It's not your money. It's not mine either. Grandfather left it for
you. So don't worry and take it."
"How can you say that now! Look at how we're living!"
Bolum took Nade's hand and placed the document in it.
"People have opportunities. This might be my last chance to act as
a parent."
Nade, staring at the envelope with his grandfather's signature, handed
it to Teria without emotion.
"Take it. I don't need it."
Teria's face went pale at his unexpected words.
"I want to try things on my own. So use it. I'll repay the money
spent on me someday."
Teria, needing to put out immediate fires, had no choice but to accept,
though her pride was shattered.
"Are you cutting ties? Never seeing us again?"
"It's not like that. I'll visit when I'm stable. Take
care."
As Nade turned to leave, Teria spoke softly.
"…I was going to leave."
Nade stopped walking.
"I was going to go back too."
"I know."
For the first time in his life, Nade could smile in front of his
mother.
"Don't worry. I'm not scared anymore."
He wouldn't resent anyone or hate the world.
If he swam desperately and reached the shore, maybe then he could truly
smile.
While Nade was parting with his parents, Shirone was reuniting with Rian
after a long time.
"Thank you, Rian. I heard everything."
Without Rian's bravery, he would still be wandering in the hell of the
apocalypse.
"Hahaha! It was my duty! You'd be amazed at how strong I've
become!"
Just from his aura, Shirone could tell Rian was no longer the
same.
"In that case…"
Rian's eyes sparkled.
"Let's have a duel. I need to avenge my past defeat…
Ow!"
Reina couldn't hold back and smacked him on the back of the head.
"Why are you always so childish? Is that important right
now?"
"It's important to me! I need to beat Shirone!"
'He's still the same.'
Though his body had grown, Rian was still Rian.
"Rian, I can't beat you with a sword anymore."
Feeling awkward, Rian cleared his throat.
"Well, that's not the answer I was looking for…"
"So."
Shirone smiled.
"Take care of me from now on."
It was the answer he truly wanted to hear.
"Of course, Shirone."
Cut down anyone who threatens Shirone.
That was the first law engraved in the Kingdom of Conviction.
