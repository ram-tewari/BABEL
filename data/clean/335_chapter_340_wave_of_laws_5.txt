Chapter 340:
Wave of Laws (5)
"Fire Call?"
Jocre scanned the direction from which the rock had flown. Amy stood at
a distance that blended into the scenery if not looked at closely.
"Do you dare look down on us?"
Jocre activated teleportation and raised Speed Gear to level 10. Amy
hastily fired a Fire Call, but he easily dodged it, able to control inertia.
"Hahaha! Is this all you've got?"
Amy's lips curled into a faint smile. Just as Jocre felt puzzled, dozens
of Fire Calls rained down from the sky, striking him.
"Ugh!"
A sniper shot at an angle of 88.97 degrees.
The Fire Calls, which had stayed out of perception, suddenly attacked
from a blind spot, something even Speed Gear couldn't defend against.
Without stopping, Amy charged at Vivian. The Radius of the Spirit
provided a favorable environment for allies but had the fatal flaw of petrifying the user. Vivian
couldn't do anything and was hit in the stomach by Amy, lifting her off the ground.
"Ugh! Ugh!"
As Vivian lay on the ground, vomiting, Amy dusted off her hands and
scoffed.
"Hmph, you're lucky it ended with just that."
She wanted to teach her a lesson, but the situation didn't allow for it.
Explosions were echoing in the sky as Shirone's firepower battle began in earnest.
"Where's Shirone?"
Shirone was floating in the western sky. The Ice Queen was nowhere to be
seen, but a cloud of cold air was visible on the opposite side.
"Huh? What is that...?"
Amy's face was filled with shock. The sight of the Ice Queen emerging
from the mist was beyond her imagination.
Shirone swallowed hard, his face tense.
As the cold cloud cleared, the Ice Queen's enormous eyes moved
around.
It would be hard to find her easily. Humans also struggle to spot
objects smaller than their pupils.
Dust, mosquitoes, hairs.
Honestly, he didn't know.
Nor did he want to know.
Seeing an eyeball larger than a person, with pupils and irises moving at
the same speed as human eye movements, made his mind go blank.
If the Ice Queen's eyeball was the size of a person, her face was as big
as a house. Cold air leaked out between her neatly aligned teeth, each several meters long.
The fact that only her face had grown was the most bizarre.
A small body attached to a giant face. If this were a dream, it would be
the worst nightmare.
Why did she make such a choice?
If the goal was to shock and stop his heart, it was a decent strategy,
but that wasn't the primary reason.
'She increased her brain capacity.'
That was the only explanation.
For the first time, the Ice Queen's face, which had shown almost no
expression, was filled with emotion.
Fear and anger.
At the moment she experienced the threat of annihilation from the photon
cannon barrage, she reached the most intense emotions a human could feel.
'Humans are strong.'
The Ice Queen realized through Shirone.
'Then I will become stronger.'
Shirone's strength came from his brain. Therefore, she needed to become
larger than Shirone. It was a somewhat crude method but also the most intuitive and clear
solution.
"Ooooooo!"
Cold air spread from the Ice Queen's mouth. As the air contracted, it
felt like the sky was closing in.
The impact reached the ground. The air cooled so much that it became
hard to breathe as the ground froze.
Dante and Liria shivered in the intense cold. Even the monsters with
ironclad skin were losing their activity. Amy, twisting Vivian's arm behind her back, ran over and
shouted.
"Dante! What's happening with Pakyojin? If we don't act fast, we'll
all freeze to death!"
Liria shook her head gloomily. The Ice Queen's transformation was
grotesque. Regardless of power, it seemed impossible to defeat such a monster. It was better to die here
than to let the Ice Queen escape.
"Amy! Let go of Vivian!"
Jocre and Ludvance, regaining their senses in the cold, ran over. But
even they stopped in their tracks, stunned by the Ice Queen's transformation in the sky.
"What... what is that?"
The Ice Queen Jocre knew was gone. Only a monster with a giant face
floated in the sky.
Amy, using Vivian as a shield, shouted.
"Do you see now? Do you understand what kind of monster you've
awakened? Surrender and help us!"
Vivian struggled and shouted.
"Who cares! Jocre, attack!"
Jocre didn't move. No, he couldn't move. The temperature was dropping to
a critical level. Attack or not, they would all freeze to death.
"Damn it! Stay still!"
"Ah! Hey! Handle me carefully!"
Unable to bear it, Amy threw Vivian aside and joined the group. She cast
a Fire Wall, surrounding the area with a wall of fire, bringing a moment of warmth.
"Jocre! Do something! We'll die like this!"
Jocre, also a fire mage, snapped out of it and cast another wall of fire
inside Amy's Fire Wall.
But it was futile. The fire, created with magic, had a much lower
ignition point than reality, but even that couldn't withstand the extreme cold and went out.
The extreme cold swept over them. Their lungs froze, and their vision
blurred. It felt like the end of the world, like the day the Ice Queen froze herself 800 years
ago.
For the first time, Jocre felt the fear of death. His face contorted,
and a desperate scream burst out.
"Save me! I can't die like this!"
Ludvance grabbed his throat and knelt.
"Ugh! I can't breathe!"
The current ground temperature was -120 degrees Celsius.
It was a temperature that could kill everyone in three minutes.
'Shirone , please...!'
Amy looked at Shirone with desperate eyes.
The only one who could withstand the extreme cold was Shirone, protected
by the power of the Diamond Armor.
"Shirone! We'll all die like this!"
Armand's auditory sensors collected Amy's voice and transmitted it to
Shirone's brain.
But Shirone already knew without hearing it.
Even with Armand enhancing all his body's heat-generating organs, the
cold was unbearable.
The heat consumption per second was a staggering 1 kilocalorie.
Even the organic robe, designed to maximize insulation, had hardened, so
those on the ground likely had no strength left to move.
'There's no choice but to confront her.'
Shirone pushed his Light Wings back and charged at the Queen.
As the headwind increased, the perceived temperature dropped
further.
It was a suicidal attempt, but Armand used all available means to
maintain body temperature without complaint.
But even that reached its limit.
Tissue necrosis. Organic generation impossible. Remaining
life support time: 4 seconds. 3 seconds. 2 seconds.
"Ugh!"
Shirone gritted his teeth and charged. His whole body felt like a cold
stone.
And finally, at the last moment of his life, he plunged into the Ice
Queen's mouth, the source of the cold.
Amy shouted in shock.
"He's crazy! Shirone!"
Entering the Ice Queen's body was even more insane than facing the
extreme cold head-on. It wasn't just about dying. Who knew what was inside?
Dante, exhaling, spoke.
"Amy. The temperature is rising. Cast Fire Wall."
The Ice Queen, who had swallowed Shirone, didn't even blink. For some
reason, she seemed to be in a temporary state of inactivity.
Shirone's decision was ultimately wise. If he intended to save everyone
at the cost of his own life.
Amy's expression was grim as she cast Fire Wall. It already felt like
Shirone had left the world.
"He'll come back. Shirone will definitely come back."
Dante didn't offer hopeful words. The odds of Shirone returning from the
unknown were incalculable.
"We've bought some time. I don't know how much, but we need to do
something. Gather around, let's plan."
While Dante's group discussed their strategy, Jocre stared blankly at
the sky.
'That's the true form of the Ice Queen.'
The Ice Queen's giant face was horrifying. It was like someone outside
the world was shining a magnifying glass on her face.
And Shirone had jumped into such a place.
Maybe he could have held out a bit longer. With his incredible flying
ability, he could have escaped with his friends in the worst-case scenario.
But he threw himself into the jaws of death.
To save the world? To become a hero? Or was it just arrogance?
"Why?"
Jocre asked sincerely, but Shirone, who should have answered, had
already disappeared into the Ice Queen's law.
Shirone floated in the world of law.
Countless stars, whose distances couldn't be measured, flickered
incessantly, exchanging signals.
Light and darkness were 1 and 0. The binary signal system was executing
the Ice Queen's law.
But that wasn't the important information right now.
The truly important information was that there was no air here.
Remaining oxygen consumption time: 2 minutes 37
seconds.
Shirone was terrified. The time Armand conveyed was literally his
lifespan.
'Stay calm. If I panic, I'll use more oxygen.'
He tried to regain his composure, but nothing changed.
There was nothing but the 1 and 0 signals. Therefore, he couldn't create
any variables to change the situation.
Would he die when the oxygen ran out? Would his body become a corpse,
drifting forever in the cold world?
Then the stars began to flicker rapidly.
It felt like ants that had been slacking off suddenly sped up when food
fell to the ground.
