The central city of Sadia is the region where most of Thormia's local
nobles reside.
As a famous granary, its finances were solid, and while external forces
were few, internal competition for territory was fierce, causing the hierarchy to change frequently. The
West Family, to which Nade belonged, was no exception.
During the break, Nade practically lived in his workshop. His eyes were
hollow after staying up for two nights straight.
The workshop looked like an extension of the Supernatural Psychic
Science Research Group. Tools of unknown use rolled on the floor, and engine parts of some mechanism lay
on the table.
"Phew, should I call it a day?"
Though the season had turned to autumn, the workshop was stuffy. When he
took off his shirt, scars crisscrossing his back were revealed. It was an old wound, but sometimes it
stung so much that it's unbearabale.
Nade pulled a tarp over the table. If his family found out, there would
be an uproar, saying he was spending money on something useless.
Though the family had fallen, just three generations ago, half of the
Sadia granary belonged to the West Family.
Nade never learned the reason for their decline. However, after living
for 17 years, he felt there wasn't any special reason. They had simply spent it all.
"Nade, nade."

stop.
It's ironic that the voice's owner is his mother, but to Nade, she was
the scariest being in the world.
"Yes, Mother."
"You said you were good with machines, right? Go to the Jones Family and
fix something for them."
"Let me just take a quick nap and then I'll go."
His mother's expression twisted horribly like a monster's. It was like a
lock that made it impossible for Nade to move.

against their parents.

"Do you not see how hard your mother working? Do you know how much it
costs to send my son to that prestigious magic academy? If you're going to be like this, just disappear

no such memories. Only the excruciating scars on his back ached.
No one in West Family ever tried to start even a little business with

The main house of Seriel's family, Fortress, was in Creas City, just
like Amy's.
Although their noble rank was not very high, they were a prestigious
family known for producing many great doctors, and they were well recognized in the kingdom.
At school, she is Amy's best friend and a model student, but at home,
she is a wild and unruly brat who puts her younger brother, who is an aspiring doctor, take the front
while she stayed in the background.
Today, she was hiding for the third day to avoid her mother's nagging.
Her younger brother's room, who was expected to become a great doctor in the future, was a nagging-free
area where her family didn't go.
"Uwaah! What do I do? It's so moving."
Seriel, who was reading a romance novel under the covers, couldn't hold
back her tears any longer and started crying.
Her younger brother, Vale, who was sitting at his desk, was about to
lose his mind.
"Ugh, be quiet! I'm trying to study! Go back to your room! Or at least
stop crying so loudly."
Hugging the novel that ended in tragedy, Seriel savored the lingering
emotions. If she were to rate it herself, it was one of the best stories she read during
vacation.
"It was such a sad love story. Oh, where can I find a man like
Joseph?"
Vale paused his writing and looked up.
"There is."
"Really? Who is it?"
"Joseph."
"You're asking for a beating! Are you teasing your sister?"
"Why not? If there's no one like him in reality, you might as well live
with a fictional character, kekek."
Seriel glared at her brother's back, her lips twitching. She hadn't
gotten angry because she had been indebted to him for a few days, but now he was trying to climb all the

Just then, as the door opened, a letter fell out with a thud, along with
his mother's nagging.
"You've got a letter! I won't nag you to study, so come out and stop
bothering your brother. If you're going to play, at least take a bath. How are you going to get married

She thought it was Amy, but the sender was an unexpected person.
"Huh? It's Shirone! Shirone!"
Seriel quickly opened the envelope and read the letter. Her eyes
instantly filled with affection. She smelled the scent of the paper and held the letter in her arms,
murmuring.
"Ah, where can I find a man like Shirone?"
Vale's eyes, which had been diligently reading a book, became wide
open.
'Shirone is a real person, you idiot. Just go date him.'
These words were on the tip of his tongue, but he knew the woman
wouldn't listen to him. She had been so engrossed in novels for days that she was clearly not in her
right mind.
