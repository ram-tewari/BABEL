Chapter 520:
The Nameless Swordsman(4)
Rian arrived at the foot of Arch Mountain, lost in thought.
'You can't save everyone in the world.'
Moreover, wanting to save someone without the ability to do so was akin
to suicide.
"I know, Shirone."
This wasn't like him.
Charging into the enemy camp to die a glorious death wasn't his
style.
"Yeah, that's not it."
Rian smiled.
"Just go where your heart leads you."
The scenery was beautiful, with clear birdsong filling the
valley.
But the aura of death waiting for him halfway up distorted all the
scenery to the point of dizziness.
The faster he walked through the underbrush, the more he wanted to get
it over with.
Living five more minutes wouldn't change the history he had lived so
far.
As he approached the bandits' hideout, two bandits on patrol drew their
swords and blocked his path.
"Who are you? Do you know where you are?"
Rian didn't slow down and kept moving forward.
Seeing his trained body and the large sword strapped to his back, the
bandits' eyes filled with murderous intent.
"Stop. If you don't want to die. Who are you?"
Instead of answering, Rian reached for the handle of his sword.
"This bastard…!"
The two bandits charged down the slope, swords raised.
"I am Rian."
Rian's face twisted with determination, and hot air escaped through his
clenched teeth.
"I am Shirone's sword."
The bandits' eyes widened in shock.
A massive flash surged like a tidal wave rising from beyond the
horizon.
Sylvia was gripped by a strange fear, feeling pressured to say
something.
"But why are you looking for him?"
Lambda leaned back in his chair, twirling his mustache.
"Hmm, rumor has it that he has reached divine
transcendence."
"Divine transcendence?"
"Moving the body with the power of will. Of course, it's unclear if
that's even possible, but it's an intriguing topic for swordsmen. I left my knight training at this age
to confirm the reality of divine transcendence. So I want to meet him. To see if the Knight of Maha can
cut me down too."
Liz glared and asked,
"Are you going to kill him?"
"Haha! Who knows, little lady? Whether I kill him or he kills me,
we'll only know if we fight. If he's truly the mythical Yaksha, my black iron armor will be like
paper."
Lambda then fixed his gaze on Sylvia.
"Now, tell me. Where is the Knight of Maha?"
He didn't seem the type to harass women.
But his more sinister nature implied that he would kill if she kept
silent.
"He's a benefactor to our family. He killed Fox, but he didn't seem
that strong. It might be someone else."
Sylvia deflected because she genuinely didn't know where Rian
was.
"Yes. That's a reasonable excuse. You've done your best. Where is
he?"
Sylvia, on the verge of tears, said,
"I don't know. I asked for his help last night, but he refused and
left in the morning."
"Oh, how pitiful. If true, it's truly sad that the Knight of Maha
was just a coward."
Despite his words, Lambda's cold face made Liz shout,
"It's true! Brother abandoned us and left!"
"Haha! So you're saying it's a lie that he cut down Fox with one
strike yesterday?"
"That's not… but…"
Rocking back and forth, lost in thought, Lambda asked again,
"Then tell me. You were there. Did you notice anything strange when
the swordsman cut down Fox?"
Liz's face froze as if struck.
She couldn't remember the scene.
Not because she was scared, but because the world seemed to warp at that
moment.
Lambda saw Liz's expression as confirmation.
"You saw it, didn't you? The Deny."
"I don't know. I didn't see anything."
"It's not that you didn't see it; you were shocked. It's like a
shockwave that destroys human common sense."
Lambda grew increasingly excited.
"Swordsmen call it Axing. The Axing that the Knight of Maha uses is
called Deny."
As Lambda's killing intent grew, Liz clung to Sylvia's clothes and
said,
"I don't understand any of this! And it has nothing to do with
us!"
"Yes. But it matters to me. So you need to know what kind of person
the Knight of Maha is."
Lambda slowly stood up and leaned forward.
"His sword denies the principles of the world."
"Stop him! It's just one guy! Archers, hold your
positions!"
The 120 members of the Red Spear Bandits moved in perfect
coordination.
With a leader who was a former soldier, their organization was different
from other bandit groups.
Arrows rained down from 16 watchtowers, and infantry armed with swords
and spears repeatedly formed and broke ranks, targeting Rian's life.
Swords were threatening, spears were too far, and arrows fell from blind
spots.
"Yaaah!"
Swinging his greatsword in an X, Rian scattered the troops and struck
the pillar of a watchtower built from thick logs.
The greatsword sliced through, causing the watchtower to tilt and then
crash down.
"Aaagh!"
Finding an archer in the wreckage, Rian drove his greatsword into the
man's back.
At the same time, eight spears thrust at him from different
directions.
"Damn!"
Rolling on the ground to evade, Rian looked around.
No matter where he looked, all he saw were enemies.
'So this is what 100 men look like.'
Anyone with long battlefield experience knew the vast difference between
imagining 100 men and facing them in reality.
He had taken down three in ten minutes, but that meant he could never
wipe them all out.
Even if he took down one every three minutes, it would take 300 minutes
to eliminate them all.
In other words, he would have to fight harder than a full-speed sprint
for five hours.
Of course, this was just an ideal minimum.
"Surround him! Don't give him a moment's rest!"
The enemies knew this too, so instead of charging recklessly, they
cornered Rian, waiting for the right moment to strike.
'I can't win like this.'
He had to finish it within an hour.
The enemies, sensing his plan, widened the distance.
But Rian moved faster, charging like a beast.
'What…?'
The world seemed to warp around Rian, and the bandits' eyes
widened.
A movement that defied the laws of inertia.
How could he land faster than he jumped?
"Yaaah!"
Swinging his greatsword, he cut through three bodies.
An unstoppable attack that even shields couldn't block.
The bandits were terrified, but the overwhelming numbers gave them the
courage to advance again.
"Kill him! If our numbers dwindle, we'll be in more
danger!"
The deputy's words had a strong impact, and the bandits steeled
themselves as if facing a monster attacking their village.
"Damn! He wasn't just a kid!"
To the bandits, Rian's balance was exceptional.
But with 200 eyes watching him, some found and exploited his
weaknesses.
"Ugh!"
Two arrows pierced his shoulder and back, and a spear cut his
side.
'There's no end.'
He had killed 40 enemies so far, but he couldn't feel the difference
between 120 and 80.
'This isn't it.'
As fatigue set in, an insatiable thirst tormented him again.
His thoughts were so skewed that everything felt like the wrong
answer.
'I don't mind dying. But…'
As a swordsman, he wanted to experience a perfect cut just once.
"Yaaah!"
Rian charged with a roar.
If he couldn't grasp it with his mind, he would accumulate countless
failures through his body.
'…What?'
How much time had passed?
Rian realized the number of enemies had significantly decreased and was
puzzled.
'How many times have I swung?'
He couldn't remember the battle.
No, he hadn't been conscious of it.
The moment he felt the sensation of cutting, a thrilling shiver ran down
his spine.
'This is it!'
For the first time, he achieved a perfect cut while conscious.
And the conclusion he reached after accepting it with every cell in his
body…
'It wasn't skewed.'
Nothing had been wrong from the start.
The sword just moved forward.
So the one who was skewed wasn't the sword…
'It was me.'
Swordsmanship doesn't save the world.
A sword doesn't kill people.
Saving the world and killing are done by humans.
So a sword is just something humans wield as they wish.
'I move forward for myself!'
As the greatsword passed, fountains of blood spurted from five
necks.
"The cutting sensation has become sharper."
Faiken, watching the battle from the hideout's platform, spoke.
He was a giant, making the stone chair seem small, and a spear stained
with real human blood leaned against the armrest.
"It could be luck. Enlightenment doesn't come easily."
Breeze, one of the deputy leaders and the only magician in the bandit
group, said.
He was an old man in his mid-60s with a roughly grown beard.
"There's no such thing as luck, especially with a
sword."
Faiken was sure of Rian's transformation.
"If realizing before swinging is genius, then realizing after
10,000 swings is dullness. But those 10,000 swings are terrifying. It's like the difference between a
skilled gambler with 1 gold and a foolish gambler with 100 million gold. Who will come back with money?
Being able to swing 10,000 times is an asset that geniuses can't have."
Breeze stroked his beard and said,
"Then I should step in."
With the sword's momentum at its peak, it was too much for the infantry
alone.
With the sword's momentum at its peak, it was too much for the infantry
alone to handle.
Agreeing with this thought, Faiken gestured for Breeze to step
in.
"Go ahead and take him down. With someone of his skill, we can
afford some losses."
Breeze stepped forward on the platform, raising his hands as a fiery
blaze gathered above his head.
If he unleashed it now, his subordinates would also be sacrificed, but
if he didn't stop the beast-like Rian now, it would be even more troublesome later.
"Impressive for someone so young…"
Breeze performed a magical action, extending his hands forward to cast a
massive fireball.
"You've picked the wrong place to play."
"It's a fireball!"
As the bandits shouted, Rian turned his head, crouched low.
Seeing the massive blaze scorching the air as it flew towards him, his
eyes widened.
Divine Transcendence - Yaksha.
As the air expanded and the avatar's afterimage appeared, Breeze, who
was watching Rian, felt his hair stand on end.
"Rrrraaah!"
Twisting his body with all his might, Rian swung his greatsword at the
incoming fireball.
Axing - Deny.
With a thunderous roar and a gust of wind, the fireball lost its
momentum and split in half.
Breeze stared in disbelief.
"My… my magic…"
Even Faiken, who had been watching, leaned forward with a look of
incomprehension.
"…He cut through magic?"
In his decades on the battlefield, he had never seen such a sight, not
even in rumors.
