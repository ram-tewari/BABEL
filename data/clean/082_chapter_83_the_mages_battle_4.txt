Light and Darkness, Good and Evil (1)
***
Buried in the underbrush, Canis couldn't move a finger as the shockwave from his abdomen
lingered.
After realizing Arin had a talent for telepathy, he had focused on training in
dark-physical hybrid magic.
Force absorption was also physical, and thus his Dark Skin's shock absorption was
considerable. An adult's hammer strike would feel like a child's punch against it.
But the Photon Cannon's power, struck by surprise, far exceeded expectations. Worse,
analyzing how light could carry physical force was impossible.
"Kekeke. You got me good, Canis."
A murky voice grated on his ears. Though no one else was in the mountains, the voice was
real.
"Harby... What the hell hit me?"
"No idea. But I felt mass. A mass moving at incredible speed struck your gut."
"Mass? Light has no mass."
Studying opposing attributes was basic for combat mages, so Canis knew photon theory.
"Light has no mass, but it has energy."
Photons in motion indeed had negligible mass, called relativistic mass—like solar wind.
"But that's just mathematical reduction. Like how strong winds feel like being hit
with a stick."
"Your point stands. But theoretically, it's possible. So not entirely
impossible."
"No, something's missing. To deliver this much force, there must be a link between
mass and energy. But nothing like that exists."
The voice, Harby, sounded indifferent.
"Guess you found your match."
Canis didn't reply. Though distinct entities, he and Harby shared thoughts. Sensing
Canis's irritation, Harby reluctantly conceded.
"Impressive. Unique, too. Didn't expect such talent at the magic school."
Finally satisfied, Canis stood. Accurately gauging an opponent's strength was the first
step to victory. His first true rival. His body felt feverish.
"Can we win, Harby?"
"Kekeke. Canis, if you wish..."
The eerie voice seeped into the darkness.
"There's no enemy we can't defeat."
Canis dragged his abnormally long shadow as he returned to the mountaintop where Shirone
waited.
***
Shirone stared at the forest's darkness for a long time. Though unsure if Canis had cast
Dark Skin, his retina registered a warning signal.
Canis emerged, parting the trees. As Shirone's suspicion was confirmed, Neid frowned.
"Dammit. He's really unharmed."
"Neid. The sun's set."
With nightfall, dark magic's power would peak. Iruki cast an illumination spell. Though
dim compared to Fire Sun, Neid amplified it. Two light sources glowed ten meters above. But given mages'
speed, a full-frontal battle was still impractical.
"Tch. Still too dark."
At that moment, Canis cast an illumination spell. The light magic from a dark mage
unsettled the two.
"What's he thinking? Illumination magic?"
"What's the problem? All schools learn photon theory. It's essential for
teleportation."
"That's not the point! Light is a dark mage's weakness! Why would he weaken
himself?"
"Hahaha! Hahahaha!"
Canis laughed uproariously.
"Magic school graduates are so dumb and rigid. No magic is without weaknesses. What
matters is compensating for them. This proves it. Arin, fire another."
Even the cautious Arin complied without hesitation.
With four light sources, the brightness resembled daylight. But unsatisfied, Canis turned
to Shirone. The light mage with unique techniques could surely create the perfect stage.
"Hey, why not fire one too? I'm waiting."
Shirone didn't know illumination magic. Mastering teleportation, Rainbow Drop, Photon
Output, and Photon Cannon left no time for other spells.
"What? You can't? Unbelievable. A light mage who can't cast illumination
magic."
Occasionally, such cases existed—short magic threads excelling in one spell through sheer
luck. They might shine in a circus, but magic was killing art. In battle, where psychology and tactics
reigned, specialists like Shirone were easy prey.
"I misjudged you. Thought you'd be a worthy opponent, but you're just a half-baked
brat."
As Canis spoke, a light orb burst above Shirone's head. Its brilliance dwarfed the
others.
Neid and Iruki gaped. Shirone had cast illumination magic without learning it. His
insight was limitless, but his anger was palpable.
Shirone stepped into Canis's Spirit Zone. He couldn't forgive the man who took the school
hostage while preaching about a "good match."
"Happy now? I played along. Do your worst."
Canis reconsidered. Not just a circus act. But did it matter? No matter how strong the
opponent, he was stronger. This was the perfect stage to prove it.
"Anything? You'll regret those words."
Casting the Power of Darkness, shadows surged toward Shirone like a torrent. Shirone
soared, photons glowing in his palm. Another predictable attack.
'Just bluffing to piss me off?'
Shirone fired a Photon Cannon. Canis vanished into his shadow—nothing new, just textbook
movement magic. Bombarding the mole-like Canis, the mountaintop was devastated.
'Got you!'
Analyzing Canis's patterns, Shirone predicted his reappearance and fired. The Photon
Cannon struck as Canis emerged—inescapable, and too powerful for Dark Skin to block.
As all seemed lost, a shadow rose from Canis's feet—elongated like a human shadow—and
caught the Photon Cannon with its hands and abdomen. The photons trapped between its palms were absorbed
with a pop.
Shocked, Shirone landed. The shadow had destroyed the Photon Cannon. Darkness had
overpowered light.
"Kekeke, flashy entrance. How was I?"
"How is this possible? Darkness swallowing light..."
Neid couldn't believe it. No matter how strong the dark power, it shouldn't suppress
light. Like lighting a torch only for the surroundings to remain dark.
The monstrous shadow, connected to Canis, moved unhindered despite the illumination.
Its face was small, but its shoulders and chest were massive. Its waist was slender like
a panther's, and its arms were long like an ape's. Its palms were shield-wide, fingers blade-thin. The
unnatural proportions marked it as artificial.
"Kekeke, told you I was no joke. Hey, brat. Your photons were delicious."
The shadow spoke, leaving Shirone dumbfounded. Its use of "photons" instead of
"light" hinted at intelligence.
Canis introduced his shadow.
"The essence of dark magic. The ultimate arcane organism—Harvest."
"Arcanic organism?"
The pinnacle of alchemy and arcane engineering. Seeing what was only in textbooks was
surreal. Moreover, no book had mentioned an intelligent one.
"Kekeke! He's totally stunned! What's with this guy, like a bumpkin seeing magic
beasts for the first time?"
Harvest laughed, shoulders shaking. A frivolous, eccentric monster? No—a being both
profoundly human and transcendent.
"Harvest is immune to light. And linked to my life. As long as I live, Harby won't
perish."
"Yep, yep! We're two but one! One but two! Though I'm way better-looking! Seriously!
Wish I could show you. Kehehehe!"
Harvest joked, but Shirone had no time for it. Immunity to light erased dark mages' sole
weakness.
'Can I... win?'
No external help was coming, and Alpheas's whereabouts were unknown. Hundreds of lives
depended on those present.
Canis relished Shirone's dismay. Revealing Harvest after illumination magic was masterful
strategy. Harvest's playful demeanor was an act—it was far more intelligent than Canis, having inherited
Akein's knowledge. Even now, it exchanged brainwave patterns with Canis.
- Canis. Let's move into the forest. We're at a disadvantage here.
- Will they follow?
- They're the desperate ones. Plus, Lucas is strong. Getting him to act would boost our
side.
- I don't like it. Don't want help from that bastard. Would've fought earlier if you
hadn't stopped me.
- It's for you, Canis. He's strong and not stupid. In raw power, you're outmatched. Can't
let you get dragged into petty squabbles.
Lucas was another face of the bullies who tormented Canis in Radum. The thought of
relying on him made his blood boil.
