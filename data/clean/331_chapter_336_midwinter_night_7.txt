Chapter 336:
Midwinter Night (7)
The Ice Queen, pondering Jokre's words, bent down slightly to look at
him.
"Do you want to become stronger?"
Jokre blinked, not understanding the Ice Queen's intention.
In the legend, she was a powerful mage, so much so that even the kingdom
couldn't handle her, leading to her exile here.
But survival was his priority now. He couldn't risk angering the Queen
with a wrong answer.
"I want to become stronger!"
Suddenly, Vivian shouted.
Jokre turned to her in shock.
'This fool!'
Was she still not sober?
He could understand her feelings, though.
How much frustration had she endured all her life?
If a god appeared one day and offered to grant a wish, people in their
field would forsake wealth and love for magic.
But this wasn't the right time.
What could they expect from a woman who had frozen everything around her
and even herself?
Maybe the situation felt too unreal.
Perhaps her theory of life as a play was resurfacing.
Vivian, unaware of Jokre's inner turmoil, looked up at the Ice Queen
with desperate eyes.
Even if this was a trap, she knew it was a rare opportunity.
"Teach me, Your Majesty. Teach me magic!"
Her suppressed emotions surged, and her drunkenness hit her all at
once.
But her mind was clearer than ever.
The Ice Queen raised her hand silently.
The information she had absorbed as Optros was now stored in her current
memory.
Having created herself, she accepted the world's rules as pure
information and could control them.
The Wave of Law.
A strong wind spread, forming concentric circles in the air.
But Jokre and his friends couldn't feel the wind. They only realized
that something within them had changed.
They looked at their hands, trembling in disbelief.
The world they perceived was completely different from a moment
ago.
The Ice Queen spoke indifferently.
"Is this the law you desired?"
Overwhelmed with emotion, Jokre could only nod repeatedly.
You can't feel or understand it until it happens.
But you will feel and understand it when it does.
That is the change brought by the law.
'This is unbelievable. How is this possible?'
Jokre clenched his hands.
Vivian was right.
Today's event was the most significant and perfect twist in his
life.
The Next Morning
Shirone and Amy visited the old ruins' prison.
Time had eroded and exposed it, now half above ground.
The history of magic is ancient, but its academic definition is
relatively recent. 800 years ago, there was little understanding of omniscience, and primitive magic was
prevalent.
At that time, the Holy Order's power was immense, laying the foundation
for modern holy magic.
As a result, mages were persecuted.
Abilities not attributed to God were considered demonic, and many mages
were abandoned by their families.
They entered the prison where mages were tortured.
Standing before a device that stretched the spine, Shirone
shuddered.
"How could they come up with such a device? It must have been so
painful."
"Mages had it tough too. Magic isn't something you can do just
because you want to. Now it's a blessed talent, but back then, it was practically a curse."
Shirone and Amy exited the underground prison.
As they climbed the stairs to the surface, they saw everyone looking at
the sky. A massive storm cloud was rolling in.
The winter in the Les Mountains is known for clear weather due to the
westerly winds rising along the mountains, creating high pressure.
Occasionally, localized low pressure brings snow, but even regular
winter festival visitors had never seen such thick storm clouds.
"What is this? It doesn't look like rain."
The wind wasn't strong. Yet the storm cloud approached rapidly, like
smoke, covering the sky.
The temperature began to drop sharply.
Even those dressed warmly felt the biting cold, and the tourists' faces
showed signs of unease.
"What the... Is the weather going crazy?"
Shirone and Amy tightened their collars, watching the situation.
The temperature drop was too abnormal to be a natural
fluctuation.
"Amy, something's wrong. The temperature can't drop this fast
naturally."
"Yeah, it feels like magic."
Thunder rumbled from the Ice Queen's castle. A powerful wave surged,
pushing the storm cloud away.
The air folded visibly along the wave.
"Watch out!"
Shirone stepped in front of Amy.
There was no time to dodge. The wave swept in from the outskirts of the
crowd, approaching rapidly.
"Move!"
A woman in a robe stepped in front of Shirone and Amy.
It was Lyria, the mage.
She quickly formed hand signs.
The three basic seals of exorcism—prohibition, reversal, and
fragmentation—flowed seamlessly, creating a barrier of law.
As the powerful wave swept over them, a shockwave exploded in front of
Lyria.
"Ah!"
Lyria's body flew back like a cannonball.
The tourists all looked at her. The wave had passed, but only she seemed
affected.
"What the... What's going on?"
Even after hitting the ground, Lyria rolled several times.
Dante appeared, lowering his stance. He caught her precisely in his
arms.
"Are you okay?"
Leaning against Dante, Lyria bit her lip. She had performed many
exorcisms, but this was the first time encountering a mental entity using such powerful law.
Shirone and Amy saw Dante and ran over.
They instinctively knew that the woman with Dante had protected them
from something.
"Dante, what's going on?"
Dante delayed his answer and helped Lyria up.
The law, unlike magic, had no physical impact. Whether Shirone and Amy
were unharmed was for Lyria to determine.
"How did it go? Did you succeed?"
"It was close. How about you?"
The Wave of Law had spread quickly, and Lyria couldn't protect both
locations. No matter how skilled a mage, without mastering the will of prohibition, reversal, and
fragmentation, blocking the law was impossible.
Dante pulled out the totem hanging around his neck.
"Thanks to this."
The pendant, shaped like a monster's face, hung on a necklace made of
twisted birch bark.
Though it looked menacing, it was a guardian spirit that repelled
evil.
A diagonal crack ran across the guardian spirit's forehead, a scar from
exposure to the Wave of Law.
As he placed it in his palm, it finally split in two with a
crack.
Dante handed the broken totem to Lyria with an awkward
expression.
"You said it was the only one, right? Sorry."
Lyria shook her head firmly.
"No need to apologize. That's what totems are for. It means the law
was strong enough to split the guardian spirit. It's a relief we blocked it."
Shirone couldn't understand their conversation. But the situation seemed
serious, so he intervened.
"Dante, what's going on?"
Lyria answered.
"We were lucky. You should be fine."
Amy asked in frustration.
"What do you mean, fine?"
Lyria looked between Shirone and Amy.
It was already predicted that a self-aware mental entity would change
the surrounding law to survive. In such a situation, Lyria could only save 2-3 people in an
instant.
There were hundreds of people gathered here.
Among them, there were surely those with the abilities needed for this
exorcism.
Was it the right choice?
She couldn't predict the outcome of saving a couple not even twenty
years old while abandoning everyone else.
"Dante, who are these kids? I didn't follow your lead to save your
friends."
Dante glanced at the Ice Queen's castle and spoke.
"I told you, second place."
"Second? What's second?"
Dante squinted one eye.
After spending a night together, he realized Lyria was quite
oblivious.
He didn't want to explain in detail. What would he gain from a
long-winded explanation?
"He's first. The kingdom's number one."
Lyria looked at Shirone in shock.
Even though he wasn't a professional yet, she had allied with Dante
because he was known for his exceptional skills in the kingdom.
Sorcerers didn't have a competitive system like mages, so hearing
"second place" didn't impress her much.
In a kingdom with so many people, what did it matter if someone was
first or second?
Second place just meant a very talented student.
But here they were.
In this vast kingdom, having the first and second place in one spot was
shocking to her.
'Did this kid really beat Dante?'
He had a kind face. Except for his lively eyes, there was no aura of the
nation's top student.
She had experienced Dante's skills firsthand early that morning.
His practical response, judgment, and exceptional skills were comparable
to professionals.
Honestly, as a mage, she couldn't imagine what skills surpassed
his.
Dante pointed to the Ice Queen's castle.
"She's here. Shirone and Amy, get ready. You might not understand,
but stay alert."
A woman in the same dress as the Ice Queen flew across the sky towards
them.
"Isn't that the Ice Queen?"
The Ice Queen descended slowly to the ground.
A flash of light twisted through space and landed beside her.
Amy's eyes widened.
"Jokre? Why are you...?"
Jokre sneered and bowed behind the Ice Queen. Rudvans and Vivian landed
next.
No one understood the situation.
At least Shirone and Amy felt a sense of crisis, but the tourists seemed
more relaxed, even smiling.
"Haha! It's really the Ice Queen! Is this some event?"
"Wow, she looks just like her! How did they find someone so
identical among the mages?"
It was natural to think it was part of the festival.
Otherwise, how could the Ice Queen be walking around in front of
them?
Shirone would have thought it was a performance by the organizers if he
hadn't seen the shockwave explode in front of Lyria.
As the Ice Queen stepped towards the crowd, the tourists applauded and
cheered.
The winter festival is held every year, but this year's special event
was considered innovative even by their standards.
Reviving the legendary Ice Queen was quite an idea.
Even the wax figure of the Ice Queen had garnered numerous fans due to
its beauty. However, the appearance of the woman playing the role was incomparable to the wax
figure.
It was the radiant power of life.
"Whew! Ice Queen! Please shake my hand just once!"
A chubby man grinned and extended his hand, but the person next to him
stopped him as if to say it was inappropriate.
"Hey, that's disrespectful. You might freeze."
People burst into laughter. No one realized that this was a real
situation.
Amidst the jokes, the Ice Queen continued to walk with an indifferent
expression.
Liria watched the Ice Queen's every move with her hands clasped.
'What is it? What law did it touch?'
The wave of law had altered the laws of everyone except Shirone's
party.
Nothing special had happened yet, but considering the power of the wave,
the extent of the change would be enormous.
