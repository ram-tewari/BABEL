Chapter 80:
The Dark Intruders (4)
Ethella remained in the training hall, wearing the same outfit as yesterday. Her eyebrows
twitched as she sat cross-legged without moving a muscle. Her forehead wrinkled, and her nose bridge
twisted. The veins on her neck bulged as she gritted her teeth, and her body began to shake violently.
Thud thud thud thud thud.
The building shook along with Ethella's body. The walls and ceiling vibrated, sending
dust raining down. Finally, her eyes snapped open, and she exhaled a rough breath before collapsing forward.
"Hah! Hah!"
Ethella, who had been battling a mind demon since dawn, was drenched in cold sweat.
'What was that?'
She couldn't pinpoint it exactly, but it was a type that infiltrated human minds and
blocked memories. Dark magic belonged to the orthodox school, but the malice embedded in the magic was
intense enough to push Ethella's vigilance to the extreme. Had it not been for the laws of the Karsis
Diocese, she too would have lost her memories without a fight.
'It's dark magic. But it's strictly forbidden within the school.'
Ethella checked the time. Though it was summer and still bright, it was past the time
when school classes would have ended.
'Does that mean I was trapped by the mind demon for over 12 hours?'
Considering Ethella's supreme mental strength, it was an incredibly powerful magic. What
was even more astonishing was that no one had come looking for her despite her skipping classes entirely.
She wasn't the only one affected.
Once that thought crossed her mind, Ethella rushed out of the training hall without even
changing her clothes.
***
A flash of light passed over the magic school's sky. Then, as if drawn by a magnet, it
curved toward the ground and plummeted. Without even a sound of impact, a single man landed.
"Dammit! Am I too late?"
Sade, who had ridden a carriage all night to reach Creas, used spatial teleportation to
arrive at the main gate as soon as he entered. As expected, the guards stood motionless, showing no
reaction.
It was dark magic.
And an extremely high-level one at that.
'I need to find the master first.'
Sade turned his steps toward the faculty quarters. But before he could take ten steps,
the distinctive roar of spatial teleportation echoed from the sky.
As Sade's vigilance triggered, entering the Spirit Zone, Ethella descended in a beam of
light.
"Teacher Ethella! What's the situation?"
Sade, who had just returned from a trip, asked as if he already knew about the incident
at the school, making Ethella puzzled. But since every second counted, she cut straight to the point.
"It seems we've fallen victim to dark magic. Everyone—students and teachers—has lost
their memories."
Sade had expected it, but facing the reality was still shocking. No matter how insane,
launching a surprise attack on Alpheas Magic School, one of the kingdom's top five prestigious schools, was
unthinkable.
"There's no time. First, tell me what's happening."
"Scanning with the Spirit Zone, the entire school is heading up the mountain. Judging by
the direction, it seems to be toward the Uncrossable Bridge. There are three escorts, and three others are
tailing them. Among the teachers… it seems I'm the only one who managed to escape."
Arcane's dark magic was at the level of a grand mage, but Ethella was also a bishop of
the Karsis Diocese, so he couldn't have dominated her mind. That alone was a relief, Sade thought as he
explained what he knew.
"Let me brief you quickly. The one behind this is Viltor Arcane. A grand mage who threw
the world into chaos 40 years ago."
"I've heard of him. Why would such a person target the magic school…?"
Sade bit his lip. It was related to the personal history of his master, Alpheas. But the
only person he could rely on now was Ethella, and given her character, she would accept the truth without
prejudice.
"Viltor Arcane… is the headmaster's master."
Ethella blinked. But as Sade expected, she didn't imagine anything beyond what she'd
heard.
"I see. But Arcane wasn't among the ones leading the students. What's the situation?"
"We can't. If you're affected by mental magic, it's not easy to break free. Trying to
save the students one by one will only backfire."
"But we can't just leave them like this."
"You mentioned three people are tailing them. Do you know who they are?"
"I couldn't confirm exactly… but it might be Shirone."
"Shirone?"
Hearing Ethella's words, Sade also remembered. Arcane's magic was likely cast around 24
hours ago. The only students who could have been outside the school at that time were Shirone and his group,
who were on suspension.
"I didn't see their faces, so I'm not sure. But judging by their aura, it seemed like
Shirone, Neide, and Iruki."
"That's probably correct. If it's them, they'll be fine for now. They're smart kids, so
they'll know what to do. Let's leave it to them for now, and Teacher Ethella, please find Arcane."
"What will you do, Teacher Sade?"
"I'll look for the headmaster. Finding the master and dispelling the dark magic is the
priority."
Ethella nodded in agreement.
"Understood. Then I'll find Arcane."
Sade looked around the mountain ridge. The school grounds were so vast that he had no
idea where Arcane might be.
"Hundreds of people were affected simultaneously. A magic of this scale would have
drained a significant amount of mental energy. He's likely hiding in a secluded place. The mountain is the
best place to search. How far does your Spirit Zone extend in Jonah Mode?"
"Hmm, roughly a diameter of two kilometers."
Sade nodded after a rough calculation.
"Marking about five points should suffice. Patrol around the training grounds. I'll head
to the school."
"Alright. I'll capture Arcane and then save the students."
"Don't push yourself too hard. Arcane is…"
Sade swallowed his words. Telling her how strong he was would only fuel her sense of
duty. If he couldn't trust the bishop of the Karsis Diocese, a state-recognized Jonah, who could he trust?
"No. I leave it to you."
Feeling the responsibility as Alpheas's disciple, Sade bowed his head on behalf of his
master. Ethella smiled shyly and shook her head.
"I don't know the details, but I'm also a teacher at this school. I'll do my best for the
students."
"Yes. Then…"
From now on, it was a race against time.
As Sade teleported toward the school, Ethella took a deep breath and focused her mind.
Her sequence formula rapidly ascended, expanding her Spirit Zone. The unique expansion
type exclusive to Jonah unfolded, allowing her to sense everything within a two-kilometer radius through
synesthesia.
'First, the 26th Training Ground.'
Ethella's body transformed into light as she flew toward the mountain peak. With a single
teleportation, she covered one kilometer.

The Mage's Battle (1)
Canis and his group gathered the memory-blocked people at the starting point of the
Uncrossable Bridge.
Arin's face was pale after completing her task. Even though they were under Abyss Nova,
mentally controlling hundreds of people to move was an exhausting ordeal.
Neide, who had been watching Canis's group from the bushes, spoke.
"Why here of all places? There's just a bridge and a cliff."
"No idea. Maybe they're waiting for someone?"
Iruki counted on his fingers as he spoke.
"Cliff, mental control, villains. With just these three elements, the answer is obvious.
They plan to throw them off the cliff."
"What? Why would they do something so insane?"
"Logically, it makes no sense. There's nothing to gain from killing students. But what if
it's an emotional issue? Like resentment or revenge."
Shirone thought it made sense. Also, if they really planned to do such a thing, waiting
like this wasn't the best approach.
"We need to act before that happens. We might even have to fight."
For ten minutes, nothing happened. The students and teachers stood frozen under mental
control, while Canis and Arin looked over the cliff, waiting for their master.
"Are you sure this is okay, Canis? We searched the entire school but couldn't bring
Alpheas."
"We have no choice. The master told us to be especially careful if we encountered
Alpheas. We just need to do what we can."
"Still… I feel like we'll get scolded."
Canis looked at Arin with a displeased expression.
"Arin. The master is kind. He'd never scold us for something like this, so don't worry."
Lucas, who had been listening to their conversation, was growing increasingly annoyed.
He'd come along for the hefty payment, but he hadn't expected to carry out a terror attack on the entire
magic school.
Considering the mission's difficulty, the advance payment was meager. Moreover, Arcane
was long overdue but still hadn't appeared.
"Hey. When is your master showing up? Did he run away or something?"
Lucas made the responsibility clear. It might come up when the final payment was due.
Canis didn't evade the question either. As the leader in Arcane's absence, it was his duty.
"Arin, step back."
"But Canis…"
"You don't need to intervene. Focus on controlling the people. I'll handle this."
It was impossible to maintain mental control over hundreds of people and fight at the
same time. As Arin hid among the crowd, Canis approached Shirone's group, feeling unburdened.
"Who are you? How did you break free from the mental control?"
Iruki spoke.
"Dark magic is already outdated, isn't it? There are plenty of ways to counter it."
"Nonsense. If it were that easy, the ones behind us wouldn't be affected either."
'True…'
Canis looked up at the sun. At the mountain peak, the light was still strong. But as the
disciple of a grand mage, there was no need to negotiate terms with students.
Lucas, who had been watching with his arms crossed, shouted.
"Hey, what's the plan? This wasn't part of the contract. I can handle it if you double
the pay."
"I don't need your help. I'll handle it myself."
"Suit yourself."
Lucas clicked his tongue and stepped back. Though he acted indifferent, his true
intentions were the opposite. He'd pretend to stay out of it, then strike from behind once the fight
started. That would be the end of it.
"Hurry up and come at me. I'm busy."
Shirone and Canis slowly closed the distance. Through synesthesia, Shirone sensed Canis's
Spirit Zone as a sphere roughly 50 meters in diameter, similar to his own.
"I'll handle this. You guys stop that swordsman."
At Shirone's words, Iruki looked at Lucas.
"That guy? He doesn't seem like he'll interfere."
"You never know how things might turn. He's vicious enough to throw Teacher Shina off a
cliff."
Shirone glanced back with cold eyes and said.
"Don't believe a word he says."
