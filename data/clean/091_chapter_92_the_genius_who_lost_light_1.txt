Chapter 92:
The Genius Who Lost Light (1)
"Your name is Harvist."
At Arkane's words, the black orb vibrated. As the molecular bonds loosened, the black
substance billowed up like smoke. The monstrous entity born from darkness spoke in a murky voice.
"I am Harvist."
"I will conduct a few tests. For now, stay inside the test tube."
Arkane pointed to a three-meter-tall glass tube. For a human, it would be too cramped to
endure even ten minutes, but for a magical creature, the concept of temporal permanence did not exist.
That night, Kanis and Arin sneaked into the laboratory. Since they didn't have Arkane's
permission, their nervousness was evident. However, those feelings vanished the moment they discovered
Harvist.
A humanoid shadow, two meters tall.
The slender waist and dynamically broad shoulders shook Kanis's heart.
"This is the master's……."
The pinnacle of magical biology, perfected by the Grand Mage over 150 years. Moreover, it
was a magical creature that had inherited Arkane's memories, endowed with intelligence.
Unlike Kanis, Arin trembled as if she had seen something grotesque.
"Ugh. It's really weird. I thought it would look more human-like."
"If a magical creature didn't look terrifying, who would be scared of it? To me, this
form is amazing. It's combat-ready."
"Still, I find it strange……"
Kanis paid no attention. If Arin disliked it, that was even better.
'Wait, Harvist. I will become your master.'
Arkane had said he would choose between himself and Arin as Harvist's master. Of course,
Arin was a precious friend, but there could be no concessions in this matter. Through fair competition, he
would prove his worth.
"Kanis, let's go. The master might come."
"Huh? Oh, right."
Arin turned off the light and followed Kanis out. Then, she flinched and looked back. The
explosive emotions of the vibrating Harvist reached her.
"Mommy!"
Arin fled the room as if running for her life and never set foot in the laboratory again
after that day. However, having spent her childhood locked in Ladum's warehouse, she couldn't leave Harvist
like that.
When everyone was asleep, Arin mustered her courage and returned to the laboratory.
Harvist remained trapped in the glass tube, unchanged from before.
"Are you feeling stifled?"
When no answer came, Arin examined the tube's mechanisms, searching for a switch.
"I can let you out for a little while if you'd like."
"Why do you assume I feel stifled?"
Arin flinched and stepped back. The voice was chilling, instinctively evoking fear.
"You've been trapped here for a month already."
"It is humans who find immobility uncomfortable. I cannot perceive time. I could remain
trapped like this forever, and it wouldn't matter."
Arin tilted her head skeptically. The wavelength she sensed from Harvist was filled with
suppressed and stifled energy.
"……But maybe you're right."
"Did something occur to you?"
"Well. Curiosity, I suppose? That's not quite the right word. From the moment of my
existence, I was designed to learn. But now, that's impossible."
"That's exactly what feeling stifled means."
Harvist fell into thought. Was that truly the case?
"How can you read my emotions?"
"Hehe. It just turned out this way. I've lived in Ladum for a long time."
"Ladum. The slums of Bashka, the capital of Tormia. A high-level concealment facility
with extreme population fluctuations. Presumed to be a den for displaced demi-humans who lost their
territories on the continent."
Even Arin, who had lived in Ladum, was hearing this for the first time.
"Harvist, you know a lot."
"What information do you have?"
"Are you asking to converse?"
"I see. Conversation is the only option, then. I currently lack a master to share my mind
with."
Arin talked with Harvist all night long. For her, who had spent most of her childhood
alone, it was the first time she had ever had such a long conversation with someone.
After two months of experiments, Harvist was finally granted freedom. Arkane opened the
glass tube. But Harvist still didn't move.
"Come out. You will resonate with Kanis. However, you won't be bound by the constraints
of subordination. I'll explain the reasons in due time."
Harvist remained silent. For a magical creature with superhuman computational speed, the
delayed response was unexpected, prompting Arkane to turn his head.
"Why? Is there something holding you back?"
"Why Kanis?"
"Hmm. Do you dislike the idea? It's strange that you, who haven't even formed a mental
resonance, already have preferences."
"I understand there's no merit in me, who inherited your memories, being subordinated to
you. But Kanis is stubborn and prideful. He's unlikely to heed my advice. Such traits would be a weakness in
battle."
"Of course, that's true."
"On the other hand, Arin is different. She lived in Ladum for ten years and never met
anyone but Kanis. As a result, her mental sensitivity is incredibly sharp."
"Are you referring to the 'First Sight'?"
First Sight was a unique brainwave pattern that perceived everything as if seeing it for
the first time. Arin had grown up seeing nothing since infancy, and her brain had solidified in that state.
Thus, she couldn't distinguish objects like ordinary people. However, her ability to
grasp the essence of things was absolute. While it came with many inconveniences, it was the ultimate talent
for a mental-type mage.
Even Arkane knew Arin's talent was more special than Kanis's.
"Arin is out of the question. That child is too soft-hearted."
"I don't understand. First Sight outweighs any other shortcomings. With her, my abilities
could be maximized."
Arkane smiled bitterly.
Even with shared memories, differing forms led to differing judgments.
"Perhaps your reasoning is more logical. But Arin is not an option. You will go to
Kanis."
***
"Kekekekeke!"
Harvist, plummeting, twitched.
"So that's how it was, Arkane."
Only in the final moments of life did he realize it. The reason Arkane had abandoned
Arin, who possessed exceptional magical talent, and stubbornly chosen Kanis instead.
'You were just as stubborn.'
Harvist's fall carried no weight. That made it all the more pitiful.
"Harvist!"
Kanis rushed to Harvist. The creature's body, crumpled like thin paper, was riddled with
holes, making it painful to look at.
"Why! Why did you do this! I wouldn't have cared if I died! If you were going to do this,
you shouldn't have betrayed me in the first place! Why!"
"Keke. How should I know? You're my master."
Kanis clenched his teeth as if realizing something.
"Magical creatures… just do what their masters want."
"Harvist……"
Kanis's head dropped. He felt resentful and wronged, wishing he could just die. But part
of him also wanted to live. Why wouldn't he? That was human nature. Harvist seemed to know it, which was why
he severed the master-servant contract and chose self-destruction.
"I'm sorry, Harvist. I……"
Harvist didn't mind. Magical creatures were designed to do what their masters wanted.
Death wasn't shocking because of that. Instead, he was more intrigued by Shironé's combat prowess, which had
overpowered his and Kanis's combined efforts.
'Hmm. Strange magic. But it wasn't just magic that overwhelmed us.'
The opponent was undoubtedly strong. There was no hesitation in subduing others. Yet,
they weren't aggressive by nature. Rather, their battle style was ruthlessly result-oriented, resembling
that of a magical creature.
'Emotions recover at an incredible speed. Likely due to superhuman insight. The time from
problem to solution is so short that they appear unshakable.'
After finishing his analysis, Harvist twisted his lips. They were absolutely specialized
in something. He didn't know what, but it wasn't a trait commonly found in nature.
'Keke. Arkane, you're in for a rough time.'
Arkane's expression was grim. Neither this one nor that one would move as he wished.
Feeling disgusted by the current situation, he slowly stepped forward and spoke.
"Pathetic, Kanis. Even if you disgrace my face……"
"Shut up."
Shironé cut him off. The violent tone, unusual for him, made his friends' eyes widen. In
contrast, Arkane turned his head with interest. When things were too absurd, anger didn't even arise.
"Kekeke! Such a spirited child. How about this? I'll make you an offer……"
"I told you to shut up. Can't you hear me?"
This time, even Arkane's face stiffened.
"I'm barely holding back as it is. So shut up. One more word, and I'll blast you to
pieces."
Shironé's body glowed with a faint red light. A laser. Its power had already been
demonstrated. If it could make Harvist, specialized in energy absorption, explode from energy overload, even
a Grand Mage wouldn't stand a chance.
But the watching friends felt their blood run cold. The opponent was a Grand Mage who
once ruled the world, while Shironé was just a student. Provoking him like this was tantamount to suicide.
"What's he trying to do? If this keeps up, something terrible will happen!"
"No. It'll be fine."
Neid and Iruki simultaneously turned to Etela.
"Teacher. What do you mean? Shouldn't we stop him now?"
"Arkane is in a tricky situation. He's at a disadvantage in compatibility. If Shironé's
magic focuses energy, the current Arkane can't block it. Now that there's a chance he might get hurt, he
won't act recklessly. Shironé knows that."
Neid looked back at Shironé. Even if that were true, only Shironé could dare to act so
boldly against a Grand Mage.
'He's as cunning as ever.'
Arkane conceded gracefully.
"Fine. As you are now, you might overpower me."
For a moment, the surroundings fell silent. It was hard to believe such words had come
from a Grand Mage. But it wasn't born from cowardice. He even smiled leisurely.
"Red wavelength. It seems to vibrate particles, making it a natural counter to dark
magic. Moreover, I'm exhausted, so stopping you would be quite difficult."
Arkane observed Shironé's reaction closely.
"What do you think? Hearing this from a Grand Mage."
"What do you mean? Didn't I tell you to shut up?"
"Magic is fun, isn't it?"
Shironé frowned. He couldn't tell what Arkane was getting at.
"That's what magic is. It's neither fair nor bound by rules. Without limits, it's
unpredictable. A meteor mage capable of obliterating a city could die at the hands of a petty mercenary
mage. That's the world of magic. So even if I lose to you, it wouldn't be strange."
"So what's your point? Are you saying you'll atone for your sins?"
"You could take it that way, but……"
Black smoke rose from Arkane's body. The whites of his eyes were visible, and his
expression was ghostly. A blade-like killing intent exploded outward, spreading in all directions.
"The point is, you only know the outcome of a fight after it happens."
Shironé instinctively stepped back. This wasn't about skill or level—it was pure fighting
spirit. The aura of death that only one who had crossed countless battlefields could emit.
"No matter how favorable the compatibility, it's useless if you can't win. So, what do
you say? Will you face me?"
For the first time, Shironé's lips, which had been so confident, fell silent. Honestly,
he didn't feel like fighting. Arkane's fighting spirit was that overwhelming.
'As expected of the real deal.'
He had persistently exploited weaknesses, but the moment they vanished, he switched
tactics instantly. He had no stubbornness. His thoughts flowed like water, and his vision was open in all
directions. It was a glimpse of why Kanis had lost.
