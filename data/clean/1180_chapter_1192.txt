Chapter 1192:
The administrator spoke.
"Administrators are classified as non-player entities. While we
hold a higher rank, we are still part of the system. As such, our emotions are also governed by the
system. If you gain the upper hand using Crime Dice, you can persuade me."
If human emotions were based on probability, then the administrator's
emotions followed the probability of law.
"However, the method of persuasion differs based on the
administrator's personality. Some are rigid, others are cold, optimistic, or critical. In my case, I
fall under the optimistic category. If the number on your Crime Dice is high, I will be
persuaded."
"Do we only need to win once?" Shirone asked.
"Even within the same personality type, the degree of persuasion
varies. In my case, a single loss will cause a 40 percent emotional shift. Once the shift exceeds 100
percent, persuasion is complete."
That meant they only needed three consecutive wins.
Shirone was guaranteed to get at least a draw, so the real challenge lay
with the other two.
Shirone asked, "What do we need to be responsible for?"
"Each time you attempt persuasion, 1 million Crime Points will be
deducted. Additionally… your Crime Points are continuously decreasing."
Iruki checked their balance.
"About 10,000 points have already been deducted. It seems like a
system to prevent players from hiding indefinitely within the inner track."
"That's partly true," the administrator replied, "but
more importantly, this is a place where criminals cannot enter. The reason you are being accommodated is
because your Crime Points are being deducted. If your points reach zero, my personality will shift to
hostile, and I will summon the guards."
The administrator added, "This rule applies to all administrators
under the jurisdiction of Melkidu's royal city. If you encounter guards in the outer track, your Crime
Points will also be deducted."
Shirone asked, "The Crime Points required for persuasion—does it
vary by administrator?"
"Yes. The deduction amount differs based on personality type and
intensity."
Shirone turned to his companions.
"Alright. Let's head out for now."
Leading his friends out of the system services department, Shirone
suddenly came to a halt.
"I think I understand now."
"Understand what?" Nade asked.
"The hidden mission. I think I figured out how the bribery program
activates."
Shirone raised his index finger.
"Administrators' emotions are converted into dice rolls. That
process is what we call persuasion. And each administrator demands a different amount of Crime Points.
What had been bothering me was this—why did Curtis say we needed at least 50 million points? At first, I
thought it was a requirement for obtaining a certain item, but that wasn't it."
Eden nodded.
"It's persuasion. Someone in the government office needs to be
persuaded, and the cost is at least 50 million points."
"Exactly. The rules will vary depending on their personality. If
you win in a single attempt, then 50 million should be enough. But if the match drags on, we'll need
even more points."
Nade clicked his tongue.
"Even if it only takes five wins, that's 10 million per roll. Can
we even do this?"
Iruki added, "At least the large amount makes things simple. We
only have enough points for a single attempt anyway. And if we're gambling on dice rolls, then we
definitely need to strengthen our dice. Let's first check out the types and prices of items. Our points
are being drained as we speak."
Since time literally equaled Crime Points, they quickened their pace
toward the shopping section.
There were vending machines scattered around and three stores operated
by non-player entities.
Eden examined one of the vending machines.
"Looks like you can change the type of Crime Dice here. Shape,
color, material, number of faces. There are even decorative ones with gemstones. Oh, this one's
beautiful—a sapphire-embedded crystal die."
"How much is it?"
Eden read the price.
"3.7 million points. Plus, persuasion is not allowed."
"Alright, alright! Focus on the mission!" Nade said, turning
away without a second glance.
Eden smirked. "It's not like I was asking you to buy
it."
At the first shop, a bespectacled man in his mid-thirties greeted
them.
"This is a specialized store for dice enhancement
items."
Instead of merchandise, the shop displayed only cards, all of which were
related to dice enhancements.
"There are more options than I expected," Iruki noted.
"This is just for beginners," the shopkeeper said. "If
you want high-end items, you'll need to visit at least the 100th sector."
"They don't sell beginner items there?" Nade asked.
"No. There's a standard of fairness. It wouldn't be right to
operate that way."
It was clear that this setup was meant to prevent fast-tracking.
"So we have to buy something here since we won't be able to
later," Nade muttered as he scanned the items.
"Grit. Sprint. Runner's High."
One increased a die's faces by one. Another increased all dice faces by
one. The last increased a single die's faces by three.
"Sprint isn't bad. One-time use items are unreliable due to the
nature of dice rolls, so Sprint is more stable than Runner's High."
The price of 800,000 points seemed reasonable. Shirone asked, "Do
you only sell dice enhancement items here?"
"Yes. The shopping section is divided into four categories: items
that enhance dice faces, items that enhance dice calculations, non-dice-related items—mainly
movement-related—and vending machines."
Aside from what Nade had checked, there were quite a few other items,
but Shirone wasn't interested.
"Can you be persuaded?" Shirone asked.
The shopkeeper laughed. "Of course. If you persuade me, I'll give
you a five percent discount on items. My personality type is critical, and the challenge is based on odd
versus even dice rolls. The cost is 100,000 points per attempt."
The discount rate seemed to vary based on the administrator's
personality, but this wasn't what Shirone was after.
"No, not that."
"Huh?"
"The underworld. I heard there's a bribery program that grants
access to Melkidu's Core."
His companions, who had been browsing items, all turned toward Shirone
and the shopkeeper at once.
The shopkeeper fell into deep thought, then let out a small chuckle and
shrugged.
"Well now, do I know about that or not? There's only one way to
find out—you'll have to persuade me."
Shirone was certain now.
Persuasion applied to everything. It wasn't just used to get discounts
on items.
The shopkeeper continued, "I'm critical, and my challenge is odd
versus even rolls. One attempt costs 20 million points."
His companions' eyes widened.
"Wait, 20 million points?"
They had to wager a massive sum without even knowing if the shopkeeper
actually had the information.
So this was how it worked. Not just anyone could open the backdoor. At
this rate, they could lose all their points in no time. This must have been how Curtis gathered
information as well.
The result was bankruptcy.
As the group hesitated, the man pointed at Eden and said,
"But for this cute young lady, I'll give a discount of 10 million
points. Want to try persuading me?"
Shirone blinked.
'Wait a second. This…'
"Think about it carefully. You won't get an opportunity like this again.
To persuade me for just this small amount of points—"
"We'll come back later."
As the shopkeeper stared blankly, Shirone shoved his friends out of the
store.
"What was that? Why'd you stop us? We finally found a lead!"
"I was mistaken."
Shirone continued,
"I thought it was just part of the system—this whole persuasion
mechanic. But that shopkeeper had emotions, just like us. The difference is that their emotions shift
according to the laws of the system."
"So?"
"Let's try the next shop."
They entered the store next door, where a woman with a tattooed right
arm greeted them.
"Welcome to the Computation Enhancement Item Shop."
Noticing that they were younger than the average Melkidu resident, she
smiled.
"Hey there. Having fun?"
Shirone answered,
"Well, more or less."
Inside the glass display cases were items that could modify the
mathematical properties of dice rolls.
Bonanza. Death Gross. Accountant's Mistake.
While not as powerful as Wind God, they were items that could shine
depending on their usage.
After checking the prices, Shirone asked,
"We're looking for the bribery program that grants entry into Melkidu.
Can you be persuaded?"
"Oh my."
She didn't even need to think about it.
"For something like that, of course, you came to the right place. My
personality type is hot-tempered, and the rule is designated numbers. One attempt costs 20 million
points."
Shirone smoothly responded,
"That's a bit expensive. I think I'll have to think about it. Instead,
I'd like to become your lover."
"Hey, hey!"
His friends stared at him in shock, but the woman grinned in
amusement.
"Oh? Trying to woo me? Well, lovers do get a bigger discount. So…
you?"
Shirone clenched his fist.
'It worked.'
If emotions were mechanical, increasing the probability of attraction
could give them an advantage in persuasion.
"Well, I think all three of us like you. The required points for an
attempt must be different for each of us, right?"
"Of course. Let's see… If you want to be my lover, blondie, you'll need
1 million points."
She pointed at Shirone, then listed the amounts for the others—
"You, 4 million. You, 2 million. And this girl, 3 million."
Nade raised his hand.
"Hold on a second. Shirone, fine. But how the hell am I more expensive
than Iruki? And why is Eden, who's a woman, only 3 million?"
She giggled.
"What's wrong with that? Taste is personal, you know."
Even Shirone hadn't seen that coming.
As Nade was left speechless, Iruki stepped forward.
"I'll do it."
Since Shirone needed to handle the final persuasion attempt, it was
better to cycle through turns beforehand.
"I love you. Please accept my heart."
The shopkeeper nodded.
"I'll use two dice. Pick four numbers between 2 and 12. If the result
lands on your chosen numbers, you win. If it doesn't, you lose. If we roll the same result, it's a draw,
and we go again. Of course, you'll still have to pay the points."
Iruki exhaled.
"You first."
Avoiding the same numbers as the opponent was crucial to prevent an
unnecessary draw, which would cost extra points.
"Hmm. I choose 4, 7, 9, and 12."
"Then I'll take 3, 5, 8, and 11."
The surroundings faded, and a vast subspace for the dice roll
appeared.
'No tricks work here.'
Except for Shirone.
"Here goes."
As Iruki placed his bet, the shopkeeper wasted no time throwing her dice
high into the air.
The shopkeeper's result was 6. Iruki's was 3.
"I won."
With Iruki's victory confirmed, the subspace vanished, and the store
returned to view.
A blush spread across the shopkeeper's face.
"My heart's been racing since the moment I saw you. Alright, we're
dating now."
Her attitude flipped at a terrifying speed.
'Winning with dice really makes anything possible. In the inner track,
dice are truly king.'
She beamed with joy.
"So, where should we go for our date? I can close the shop for
you."
Since their Crime Points were still being drained, they had no time to
waste.
"Before that, I have some questions. First, I should at least know your
name and age, right?"
"Oh, you're right. I'm Kara. I'm twenty-eight. I'm older than you, but…
now that we're lovers, that doesn't matter."
Iruki's gaze turned cold.
'Successfully persuading her gave us this much information. It wasn't an
easy win, after all.'
The odds of a draw had been much higher, meaning Iruki had gotten
lucky.
"Alright, then one more thing. About the bribery program you
mentioned…"
Kara's expression stiffened.
"I'm not asking you to tell me. I just want to confirm that you actually
have the information."
She let out a deep sigh.
"Oh man… What should I do…"
Under the watchful eyes of the group, she suddenly lifted her
head.
"Yeah."
Then, she spoke clearly.
"I have the information."
