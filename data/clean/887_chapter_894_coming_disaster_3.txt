Chapter 894:
Coming Disaster (3)
The Kingdom of Bekros was the smallest of the Ten Island
Kingdoms.
With a population of about 2 million, the island thrived on fishing and
exuded a laid-back southern charm.
In the southeastern village of Arar, Shirone woke up feeling
refreshed.
"Ooh..."
The warm sunlight and the sound of cool waves seemed to invigorate his
tired body.
"That was a great sleep."
As he muttered this, Shirone suddenly sat up, realizing
something.
"What the...!"
He didn't understand.
"What happened?"
As he got out of bed and opened the door, children with bronze skin
stopped playing ball and waved at him.
"Shirone, you're up?"
The foaming sea was refreshing just to look at, but his mind grew more
tangled.
'Here again.'
After meeting Fermi in the southern continent, he had crossed over to
the Ten Island Kingdoms to return to the Ivory Tower.
'Using the Bekros terminal is the fastest way to jump to the Kingdom of
Corona.'
That much was certain.
'But...'
It had already been ten days since Shirone arrived.
"Why can't I leave?"
He was trapped in the village.
"It's the same today. It's because you won't leave, Shirone. You
had that dream again, right?"
Shirone looked down at a boy about ten years old.
"Maicon."
He remembered.
When he first arrived in Arar village, Maicon was the son of a couple
who had given him lunch.
"Me?"
"Yeah. Every day you say you don't have time and end up staying
another day."
Did I?
"Oh..."
Shirone recalled partying with the villagers until past midnight last
night.
'That's right.' Torches lighting up the night, the smell of sizzling
fish, drinks and feasts, people laughing and chatting.
Everything was vivid.
"Hahaha! You're really fun. I thought magicians were cold and
aloof."
Village chief Begpa handed him a beer mug.
"Oh no, you have to have fun when it's time to have
fun."
Already drunk, Shirone's nose was red as he clinked mugs hard enough to
break them.
"That's right. Even during the world war, this village is safe.
We've never been invaded."
Begpa always said the same thing when he drank.
"Do you know what Arar means? It's an onomatopoeia for God's
breath. This place is specially protected by Beka God."
Shirone nodded along.
"Wow, really?"
"Beka God's breath has great power. It creates waves that sustain
our livelihood but becomes sharp as a blade when angry. There's still a place on Mero Cliff where Beka
God's breath is engraved."
"Yes, I saw it too. It was amazing. It didn't look like something a
person could carve."
"Of course! What have you been listening to? It's definitely Beka
God's breath!"
Shirone didn't believe in superstitions but saw no need to argue while
drinking.
"Oh, right! I must be drunk."
A hearty voice was heard.
"You should be drunk!"
A fat man holding grilled fish skewers in both hands walked over with
his beautiful daughter.
"Uncle Yaska." The best fisherman in the village and
incredibly strong.
"In Arar, you can forget about war! Come on, eat this and get drunk
again!"
As Yaska handed over a fish skewer, his daughter Lua approached with a
smile.
"Hi, Shirone. Looks fun?"
She was tall like her father, with a bronze body that was as strong and
slender as a wild horse.
"Yeah. It's been a while since I've felt this relaxed."
Lua was very popular in the village, but Yaska liked Shirone.
"Yes, happiness is most important. Why don't you settle here with
my daughter?"
"Dad..."
Lua didn't seem opposed either.
"What's wrong with that? I'd feel safe with Shirone. He's kind and
a magician. And he fixed all our broken houses with his amazing magic skills."
"I knew it. Fixing houses means getting married? Don't worry about
it, Shirone."
Shirone laughed it off.
"Haha! I like it here too. This village is wonderful. And now I
have no responsibilities." Yes, everything was over. 'Forget it all. Fermi will handle it well. The
war and Yahweh's name...'
Though he had turned his back on the world, it couldn't compare to the
joy of laying down humanity's enormous burden.
"What?"
At this point, Shirone felt an unease about his memories for the first
time.
'Yahweh's state...'
He had definitely talked about it with Fermi.
'What did we talk about? I can't remember. Did we make a deal? No way.
Yahweh is still...'
Yahweh was vividly alive in Shirone's mind.
'Time explosion, resonance... I can feel them all. Nothing has been
handed over to Fermi yet. So why?'
The memories continued.
"Hallariole! Reierola!"
Arar's unique folk music played as people paired up and danced
together.
Shirone and Lua held hands and spun around.
"Hahaha! Hahahaha!"
The world spun around as they drank more alcohol.
"Shirone, this way."
Lua grabbed Shirone's wrist and ran towards the end of the beach from
the party venue.
As the sound of the drum faded into the distance, the two gazed at each
other in the moonlight.
"Shirone."
Lua, with sparkling eyes, suddenly approached and kissed Shirone.
"I like you. I really do."
The people of Arar village are honest with their emotions.
"Lua…"
For a moment, Amy's face flashed through his mind, but Shirone
forcefully erased her presence.
"I'm sorry. I can't go back."
He didn't want to take even a single step back into the outside world,
where a dreadful life awaited.
"I'll live here forever…"
In the village of Arar, protected by the gods.
"Let's live."
Shirone pulled Lua close and kissed her, as she had been waiting for
him.
The two collapsed onto the sand, and their barely-there clothing was
easily removed with a touch.
The drumming continued throughout the night.
"No!"
Shirone yelled out loud.
"It can't be."
Although he had to accept his memories, there were parts he couldn't
understand.
"Me and Lua…"
The touch of her lips, the feel of her skin, even her scent and the
sounds of the environment were vivid.
"It can't be. I still love Amy. Even if I was drunk… could it
be?"
A thought crossed his mind.
"Memory distortion?"
Setting aside Ra Enemy, it wasn't impossible for a mental
magician.
"But who could it be?"
Could someone manipulate the mind of an infinite magician?
"Moreover, it doesn't make logical sense. In my memory, I entrusted
everything to Fermi. But in reality, Yahweh's realm is with me. That's the key."
It was just a hypothesis, but if it wasn't the work of an amateur
magician, it was quite malicious.
"Or some other unknown…"
Maicon shrugged and sighed.
"If only it weren't for this. Normally, he's calm, but it was
strange that he was so angry every morning."
"He must be having nightmares every day."
Coming from a world at war, Shirone must have his painful
memories.
"Just calm down and come have breakfast at our place. We need to
hurry to Mero Cliff."
"Mero Cliff?"
Maicon pointed to the cliffs by the sea.
"You know, the place you go to train every morning. You're there
from sunrise to sunset."
Shirone looked towards the cliffs.
"I don't remember this."
Memories are meant to be forgotten, and we only realize which ones are
erased when we encounter a keyword.
"More information has been deleted than I expected."
If he visited the cliffs every day, there must have been a clear
reason.
"Maicon, I'm sorry, but today…"
"I know. You're not eating. You'll say you don't have time?"
Maicon said, grinning as Shirone turned his head away.
"Yeah. I don't have time."
Shirone teleported and dashed toward the cliff, leaving Maicon with his
mouth open.
"Amazing…"
No matter how many times he saw it, he never tired of it.
"Here."
Looking down at the cliff from the sky, Shirone saw circles of various
sizes etched into the ground.
"They're incredibly large. You couldn't draw these circles without
measuring from the sky."
No, you would need to view them from the end of the universe.
"They're perfect."
The villagers call these circles evidence of God's breath, no different
from an angel's halo.
Shirone turned to look at the forest inland.
"Hmm."
His transcendent senses, open up to the ninth sense, craved the forest
like a thirst.
"Yes. My body remembers."
Entering the forest, Shirone opened his spirit zone and searched for
traces of the past.
"There's something there."
Among the synesthetic sensations coming through his spirit zone, one
object particularly stood out.
Arriving at an ordinary tree, Shirone reached into the trunk and found a
notebook.
"It's made of material."
As a marker, one page was made of heavy metal. Flipping the cover, he
found pages filled with codes.
"This is… Shirone's code."
While it might seem childish, Shirone created codes from his own
secretive secrets.
For instance, he would substitute "Amy's heart" with the
letter X and decode it as "unknown."
Even if someone extracted the cipher, depending on the person, it could
be interpreted as "touch" or "desire," making it difficult to decrypt.
"A code governed by subjective feelings. I used to create these
when I was bored, not just for journaling…"
In this situation, it was helpful.
"Let's see…"
Shirone decoded the multitude of strings of codes all at once.
The contents were shocking.
"First note! Do not extract the chip in my head. It could lead to
death? What does this mean?"
Shirone quickly touched his head.
"There it is!"
He felt a small incision behind his ear.
"They implanted a chip?"
It was probably true.
"A physical device would be unrelated to mental power. That's how
my memories were manipulated."
But who?
He rifled through the notes, but the Shirone of yesterday hadn't
confirmed it either.
"Wake-up time every day at 8:30 AM. Right, it was the same
today."
He continued reading.
"Escape plan from Arar village. Time vibration. Requires at least a
24-hour amplitude."
If the time line is snapped back 24 hours, he could perceive events from
12 hours ago as happening now.
"Then it would be last night at 8:30 PM. The events and timing in
my memory match exactly."
It appears he had been training here.
"When should I implement this?"
Checking the last page, Shirone finally realized what memories he had
lost.
"Today."
And it was happening now.
"Time Vibration!"
An immense surge of temporal energy unlike any before rippled through
Shirone's body.
