Chapter 878:
Obituary (4)
"Who's there?"
Maya cautiously asked towards the door.
"Ah, well..."
The man's voice sounded, and his tone seemed extremely nervous.
'Who could it be?'
Though she too was a graduate of a prestigious magic school, Palmer
always advised her to be wary, especially when alone in a hotel.
"I'm sorry. I don't really know how to explain this. I just saw you
from outside, and, well..."
As the man rambled, another voice interjected.
"Move aside, you idiot."
Clearing his throat with a cough, he said,
"We're fans of Ms. Maya. We happened to see you and wondered if we
could possibly get an autograph?"
Normally, Palmer would supervise such interactions, and she would
happily oblige, but now it was inconvenient.
"I'm sorry, but..."
Maya began to politely decline but then stopped herself.
'I can't live like this.'
Instead of harboring any expectations, she wanted to bring a new breeze
into her life.
"Just a moment, please."
Maya, donning her coat, gently opened the door, and two men around her
age stepped back in awe.
"Wow, it's really Maya."
Even if they had seen her from VIP seats during a concert, they wouldn't
have been this close to her face.
'Her face seems so unreal.' The aura of someone they admired seemed to
elevate her from a mere human to a deity.
"Hello! We're fans!"
Seeming harmless enough, Maya unlocked and fully opened the door.
"Nice to meet you. Um... about the autograph?" They weren't
actually prepared to receive an autograph, having made up the excuse on the spur of the moment.
Thinking quickly, one man turned his friend around and slapped his
back.
"Please sign here. I promise I'll never wash it and keep it
forever!"
Laughter broke out.
After signing for both men, Maya capped the pen and held the
doorknob.
"Take care then."
"Oh, wait!"
The man reached out hastily but, realizing it was inappropriate,
retracted his hand and scratched his head.
"If you haven't eaten, would you like to join us? We'd love to
treat you, especially after asking for an autograph."
"Dinner?"
"If it's inconvenient, you can say no. It's just, this feels so
unreal..." Maya looked at the man, who was anxiously awaiting her answer, with a hint of sadness in
her eyes.
'Shirone...'
If the person standing there was Shirone, who could sway between heaven
and hell with just a word from her.
'I would be speechless with joy.'
But that was unlikely, so it remained a fantasy in her head.
'I need to move on.'
Taking the silence as a rejection, the man bowed his head.
"I'm sorry for bothering you..."
Maya smiled.
"No, it's fine. Since we've met like this, let's have dinner
together. I was just getting hungry."
"Really?"
The man was stunned, having believed there was a 99 percent chance of
being rejected.
"Are you serious?"
Maya quickly stopped herself from imagining Shirone in the man's place
again.
"Why would I lie? And what's wrong with eating
together?"
"But you're a celebrity..."
"Haha! Do celebrities not get hungry? Come on, just make sure it's
something delicious." Are you watching, Shirone?
'I'm popular too. All these handsome noblemen hang on my every
word.'
A bit of victorious pride.
"Alright! I'll treat you to something top-notch!"
With a sliver of hope that she might forget Shirone, Maya left the
hotel.
Maya, staying at a hotel about 200 meters from a bar where Shirone and
Biscoff were sitting.
"How much can you drink?" Biscoff asked as he poured himself
another drink, a plate of cut meat in front of him.
"Not much."
If the limit was how much his mind could handle, Shirone could drink
infinitely.
"Alright."
Biscoff gave a bitter smile as he raised his glass, and Shirone clinked
his with both hands.
Looking down at his glass, Shirone spoke.
"Lord Biscoff, about Guy..."
"No need."
It wasn't just about receiving consolation.
"How was he? I hope it wasn't disappointing. I wanted to know how
the eldest son of the Ogent family fared against an endless sorcerer."
"He was strong."
Shirone could assert that.
"Not just his skill with the sword but his spirit too. There was
not a hint of hesitation in enforcing his convictions."
"Yes."
Biscoff reminisced about Guy.
"You're wrong, Shirone. Guy was weak. That's why he died."
Shirone silently drank his liquor.
"If surviving by fighting is what strength means, then there are no
strong beings in the world. Humans are weak. Just a lightning strike can kill them."
"It must have been a mistake. Everyone makes them."
"There's no excuse. If he really was strong, he wouldn't have clung
to such visible forms of strength. He was devoured by fear. He lost to you and to Rian."
Biscoff looked out the window.
"... As the eldest son of the Ogent family, he must have carried a
burden. Perhaps it was heavier than he could manage. Much of it is my fault."
Unable to see his face, Shirone felt Biscoff was crying.
Though the atmosphere was heavy, it wasn't despairing; they continued to
drink in memory of Guy
As the sun set, Biscoff stood up.
"I should head back first. Reina must be pondering a lot too. I can
give her a day, right?"
"Of course. I'll come in tomorrow."
"I'll take care of the bill."
After Biscoff left the bar, Shirone soon got up to leave as well.
'It's tough.'
As he walked, contemplating, he noticed people peering through the bar
windows.
"Is that just someone who looks like her?"
"That looks like her? It's really Maya."
Shirone stopped in his tracks.
"Maya?"
"That explains it. I've felt moved by her lyrics from the first
time I heard them."
"No, the lyrics aren't really that good..."
Maya quickly waved her hand.
"There's no need to be modest. Sincerity resonates, you know.
Hearing your story, I've become an even bigger fan."
One of the two fans who had come to the hotel, a man named Mason, poured
Maya another drink.
"To think it was unrequited love, I can't believe it. It's so
frustrating to hear."
Mason's friend, Tito, chimed in.
"Right? There are plenty of people who like Ms. Maya. Have you
received many confessions?"
"Confessions? No, not really..." Suddenly, a man flashed
through her mind.
'Kayden.'
Sometimes, when life was tough, she would reminisce about her graduation
exam and a question would always follow.
'Why did he give up?'
Initially, she thought she was just lucky.
But after experiencing society, she realized such absurd events don't
just happen.
'He was strong enough to be a member of the Golden Wheel. He definitely
gave up. And I passed.'
Maybe there was a definite reason.
'Is he doing well?'
If she met him at Pisho's funeral, she wanted to ask about that
day.
"Yeah."
Maya smiled.
"I've forgotten him now. What do I care about that guy? I'm ready
to meet someone new."
Mason brightened up.
"Haha! That's the spirit. Here's to Ms. Maya's new
love!"
"Cheers!"
Excited, Mason didn't know what to do with himself as Tito chuckled and
got up.
"I should be going."
"Already?"
"I forgot I had plans with my girlfriend. Sorry, Ms. Maya. Let's
drink together again next time."
Mason was nearly moved to tears.
'Thank you, my friend.'
Tito winked.
"Take good care of Ms. Maya. See you next time."
After settling the bill and opening the door, Tito passed by Shirone,
who had just entered.
'Can he really handle all that drinking?'
From a distance, he saw that many bottles were already on the
table.
"Maya, you were here?" Shirone approached the table, and
Maya's smiling face instantly turned serious.
"Shirone."
As if she felt guilty.
"Just passing by and saw you. Who is this?"
Catching the mood, Mason stood up to shake hands.
"I'm a fan of Ms. Maya. I was just treating her because I admire
her so much."
"Pleased to meet you. I'm a school friend."
"Ah, I see."
Mason realized immediately, and shadows fell over Maya's face.
'Yeah, just a school friend.' That was all.
"Shall we head back? I'll take you home."
Normally, Maya, being a famous artist, was conscious of the prying eyes
outside.
"Why?"
Her tone was colder than usual.
"Why would you take me home? I'm having fun. Don't worry about me;
just go."
"No, I'm worried because..."
"What are you worried about? I'm not caught by a bad person, nor
can I not handle my drink?"
"You've had too much to drink." Maya's eyes hardened.
"Don't underestimate me. I'm a certified sorcerer who passed the
graduation exam. I don't want to be interfered with in these matters."
Lost in thought, Shirone nodded.
"Alright. Be careful on your way in."
As Shirone left, Mason sat down next to Maya, watching her
carefully.
"Are you alright?"
Maya gulped down her drink and laughed heartily.
"I did it! Did I do well just now? Finally shook him off. Ah, it
feels so liberating."
"If Ms. Maya thinks so, then it's good.
But are you sure you're okay?"
"Yes! Let's drink more!"
Maya and Mason continued drinking until midnight, and he helped her back
to the hotel.
"Ms. Maya, try to stay on your feet. You're
staggering."
"It's fine. I'm a sorcerer, you know? Ever heard of the Spirit
Zone? Just concentrate, and this little alcohol will be nothing."
She didn't want to sober up.
"Mason, would you come in for another drink?"
"What? Well, that's..."
Maya stared at him intently.
"I don't want to be alone. I feel like I might go
crazy."
Mason hesitated for a moment, then gritted his teeth and led her
inside.
"Let's go upstairs. It's better than the lobby where people are
passing by."
After climbing the stairs to Maya's room, he let go of her, breathing
heavily.
"Phew. Ms. Maya, where's your key?"
"Sob. Sob."
Leaning against the door, Maya's shoulders trembled.
"Forget it..."
When Mason turned to look at her, Maya's face was covered in
tears.
"I can't just forget!"
"Ms. Maya..."
Her mournful cries echoed down the hallway, and Shirone listened from
behind the wall.
After finding the key, Mason opened Maya's room and handed it back to
her.
"Go in, Ms. Maya. I should be going too."
Maya quickly wiped her tears.
"Ah, I'm sorry. I..."
"I may be presuming too much, but I have something to
say."
As Maya looked up with tearful eyes, Mason smiled and said,
"Unrequited love is still love. If forgetting is harder than the
pain of watching from afar, there's no need to force yourself."
"Mason."
"Stay strong. I'll always support you."
With a firm fist, he didn't look back as he left.
"Sigh."
Once outside, the spring air was chilly.
"Is it this again?"
Tito was standing in front of the hotel.
"You didn't go?"
"I just stopped by. How many times has this happened? That's why
you've been solo your whole life."
"Stop it. I'm already regretting it enough as it is."
"Ha, you'll regret it your whole life."
"But... she'll remember, right?"
Looking up at the hotel, Mason boasted,
"It wasn't embarrassing, at least. Honestly, I was pretty cool. Can
you imagine?"
Tito chuckled and slapped Mason on the back.
"Let's go get a drink."
Someday, spring will come.
