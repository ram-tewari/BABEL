Alpheus Magic School, Southwest Closed Training Ground.
Although this training ground was no longer in use, it wasn't considered
out of bounds for the battlefield, as the boundaries were based on 30 years ago.
Iruki stopped at a place where old training equipment was piled up, and
his teammates arrived one by one.
Amy, who had been chasing Frings, walked over, panting.
"Ugh! He slipped away like a slippery eel! Did he grease himself up
or something?"
Iruki greeted her, saying, "Good play. Thanks to you, we managed to
stop Eden."
"I went because I had a Triumvirate. If my hand was weak, I could
have been counterattacked."
Dorothy spoke up, "I collected the Scramble that Iruki gave
up."
Even without a means of communication, they moved organically by just
looking at the coordinates and status.
"So, Dorothy, you collected four cards?"
"Yes. I have a Triumvirate, and the last card is still
unopened."
"Open it now. We need to see the hand to plan our
strategy."
Dorothy took out the Random Card and said, "Open."
A Black card appeared, completing the strongest fourth-stage hand, ○●○●
(Democracy).
"Nice. This will work. On the other participants' Master Cards,
Dorothy's status will show as ⓡⓡ●ⓡ. If we matched the strongest hand with three Random Cards, we can
aggressively call Kangs."

Infinity."
"That's why I called this meeting. From the next round, give up
collecting Scrambles and find Eden. Once you find her, signal me with Shining."
"Are you sure? That would be a huge loss for us."
"I know. But let's endure just for today. With Dorothy holding
Democracy, we can call Kangs and at least destroy one of their cards. That way, we won't be at a huge
disadvantage in terms of card count. And Shirone might also have Democracy…"
Iruki looked around.
"Isn't Shirone here yet?"
"No. Based on the coordinates, he seems to be on the opposite
side."
"But he knows the layout of the hideout. If he uses spatial
movement…"
At that moment, footsteps were heard outside the training ground.
Everyone turned cautiously, and Shirone appeared, looking
exhausted.
"Shirone? What happened to you?"
His face was red with blood from his forehead, and his body was covered
in dirt.
Shirone passed his friends silently and collapsed onto a pile of
equipment, taking a deep breath.
"I'm late, sorry."
"What happened? Did you fall off a cliff or something?"
"Kaiden… he didn't call a Kang."
Everyone looked at each other, not understanding at first, then Shirone
raised his weary eyes.
"I think he's crazy. We've been fighting for six hours
straight."
His friends were shocked.
"You've been fighting all this time? What about the
Scrambles?"
"I've been collecting them steadily. But somehow, he kept finding
me right away."
"That's Hershey's ability."
Sonar's location tracking was the best in its class.
"So… how was it? You look injured."
Shirone touched his forehead.
"At first, I was surprised because he's a swordsman. But after
that, I think I can handle it. Of course, it's just a reconnaissance battle, so we'll see."
Since they hadn't seen Kaiden use a sword even in the graduation exam,
they couldn't gauge his skill based on Shirone's words alone.
'Fermi's group are all professional-level mages. If he only used a
sword, there's room to observe further.'
Shirone looked up.
"By the way, what about Eden?"
Shirone also guessed the enemy's strategy.
"I stopped her. And we need to keep stopping her. Dorothy completed
Democracy."
Iruki explained the strategy.
"Eden currently has Saint (○○). As long as we have three cards,
we'll win unless it's a dead hand. But don't call a Kang. She won't fight anyway."
If she broke her pacifism, her powerful defensive omniscience would
weaken.
"From the next round, their routes will be more complicated.
Conversely, Hershey will figure out our locations."
Dorothy said, "When we're gathered like this, even Hershey can't
pinpoint us. But we can't gather every round."
Iruki nodded and looked at Shirone.
"In that sense… how did it go?"
Shirone took out his cards.
"I currently have three cards. I chose Random for all of them, and
my hand is Triumvirate (○●○)."
Neid grinned.
"How many times did you spin it?"
"Three times at the end. Black came up twice in a row."
This confirmed it.
Shirone's time manipulation could revert a confirmed card to
Random.
"Thinking about it, one second is quite a long time."
"Right. Decisions are made in zero seconds. Anyway, as long as we
can collect Scrambles, we can hold the strongest hand."
Sabina asked, "What are you talking about? Did you guys lose your
minds?"
Neid patted Sabina's shoulder.
"I'll explain later. We're still in the lead. Let's block Eden's
solo run today and come up with countermeasures starting tomorrow."
Iruki said, "We can't collect Scrambles, so Shirone and Dorothy
should aggressively call Kangs."
"Got it."
Ruru's voice was heard.
"Ten minutes until the fourth Scramble summoning."
"Principal, it's Pandora."
After knocking on the principal's office door and waiting, Alpheus's
voice was heard.
"Come in."
Inside the principal's office were Alpheus, Vice Principal Olivia, and
Collie, the head teacher of the graduation class.
It was unusual, but Pandora guessed it was related to the mass absence
of students today.
It was also related to why she had come.
"Thank you for taking the time to meet with me."
"Of course. So, what did you want to discuss?"
Pandora took a deep breath and approached the desk.
"I request a thorough review of the fairness of the graduation
evaluation."
The teachers remained silent.
Pandora, who specialized in fragrance magic, had once been a promising
student competing for the top spot in the advanced class with Boyle. However, with the arrival of
Shirone's team, Dante's team, and other strong competitors, she was now struggling at 23rd place.
"I'm not complaining about my low rank. It's my own fault. But… how
is this possible?"
Her question was valid.
"I spent my vacation researching. I collected data from the five
prestigious schools in the kingdom."
"Pandora…"
Olivia started to speak but then closed her mouth.
"Do you know what the numbers show? The average ability of Alpheus
Magic School's graduation class is 3.8 times higher than the average of the other five prestigious
schools. 3.8 times."
The teachers were already aware of this situation.
"This is unfair. Alpheus Magic School faces much tougher
competition than the Royal Magic School, which has 20 graduation slots. Either increase the slots
or…"
"It's not that simple," Alpheus said.
"Slots aren't determined arbitrarily. Any school can have a year
with an influx of talented students."
"You know this, Principal!"
Pandora's voice rose.
"This isn't just this year. Fermi's team, Frings, Anchal, Eden—they
all have skills that should have made them professionals already. Last year, Anchal didn't even unseal
her Magic Eye. She has no intention of graduating."
Collie said, "But that's up to the individual."
"Of course. But the accumulation of such skilled individuals in the
graduation class every year is clearly a problem. If you look at Alpheus Magic School's records, the
graduation rate of those in the top ranks has been below 30% for the past seven years. It's an abnormal
figure, indicating a breakdown in the graduation cycle for some reason."
"That's true," Alpheus said.
"But Pandora, your argument has a logical leap. You can't combine
the low graduation rate of top students with the high average skill of this year's graduates. The reason
this year's graduation class is so skilled is because the incoming students are exceptionally strong.
The same students who were on par with you."
Indeed, sophistry wouldn't work.
In fact, Dante, Amy, and Iruki were competing in the top ranks.
Tears welled up in Pandora's eyes.
"Honestly, it's too… difficult."
The teachers' eyes softened with pity.
"You may think it's cowardly, but it doesn't feel fair. Is it so
wrong to want to compete on the same level as other prestigious schools? If this continues, no one will
want to enroll in Alpheus Magic School."
That was also a matter of individual judgment, but Olivia remained
silent again.
A student who had steadily climbed the elite course, competing for the
top spots since the lower classes.
Being 23rd in the graduation class was a number she had never seen in
her life.
"Alright, Pandora. I'll bring it up in the teachers'
council…"
"There is a problem," Alpheus interrupted Olivia.
"There are always problems, everywhere."
Pandora looked up with teary eyes.
"Alpheus Magic School might be wrong. But Pandora, this isn't about
your graduation. Everyone in the graduation class is competing under the same conditions. The only
problem I see is this."
Alpheus sighed.
"There are too many people better than you in the year you're
taking the graduation exam."
True competition begins when you accept that reality isn't fair.
That's why everyone except Pandora was silently fighting.
"Harsh as it may sound, you know that the world doesn't grant
wishes just because you ask. Organizations can't move for individuals. It's not that they won't; they
can't. So, to achieve what you want, you have to make a choice. Fight the school on behalf of the
majority, or fight yourself. Any other decision is just a delusion. You understand, right? You're a
magician too."
"…Yes."
You have to overcome it.
Someone is overcoming it and running towards success.
Watching the sorrowful Pandora, Collie thought,
'It's a disgustingly brutal, damn competitive system.'
"Thank you for your time. I'll be going now."
With a dejected farewell, Pandora left, and the three teachers finally
spoke their true thoughts.

"I also think this year's graduation class is too
intense."

"They're aware but turning a blind eye. They don't want to bring it

"You knew that and still spoke so harshly? You could have been more

"Miro is already out in the world. It's time to shut down

ahead."
