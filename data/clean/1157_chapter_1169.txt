Chapter 1169:
That day, at Seriel's favorite bookstore. Was meeting Fermi there just a
coincidence? Or was it a terrible twist of fate?
"Y-You…"
Or perhaps… was it an even worse one?
"What the hell are you doing here?"
Seriel, looking momentarily stunned, quickly hid the book she had been
reading—"Primal Snare."
'Ugh… This is so embarrassing.'
She had never been ashamed of her tastes, but this time was an
exception.
Fermi chuckled.
"You don't have to hide it. 'Primal Snare.' It's popular among
women. Not exactly my taste, but I know of it."
"Huh? You know this book?"
Seriel's interest spiked, but she quickly forced her face back to
neutrality.
"Anyway… why are you here?"
"It's a bookstore. And my family runs a publishing business. We've
probably stocked quite a few books here."
"Ah, right."
The Ardino family was ranked among the top 20 wealthiest in Thormia's
financial sector.
Of course, their main income didn't come from general literature— but
from high-end information like magic and alchemy.
'Well, that makes sense.'
Fermi continued,
"I'm looking into something for my father. Then I saw you. By the
way, what do you think?"
"What do I think about what?"
"The sequel to 'Primal Snare.' It's getting a great reaction from
readers. I heard the roles are reversed this time."
Since he already knew about it, there was no point hiding it.
More than that, she wanted to talk about it.
Seriel held up the book.
"Yes! It's amazing! This time, the male protagonist gets ensnared
by the female lead. And then…"
She suddenly coughed, realizing that saying it out loud would be too
embarrassing.
"Ahem! Anyway, that's the gist."
Fermi's eyes curved like a seagull's wings.
"So you're into that kind of stuff? SM?"
"None of your business! Besides, it's not about the act itself—
it's about who the partner is. Mayton is a noble man."
That was the male lead's name.
"Really? He was forced into an arranged marriage with a 62-year-old
woman, sexually exploited for years… Then he met the female lead, a maid, and discovered his true
nature."
Fermi recited the entire plot.
"And then he started getting tied up in every way possible. The
philosophy behind restraint was interesting, but let's be real—he's a pervert."
"Hah! Like you're any better? And you're conveniently ignoring why
Mayton ended up like that. Since he was fifteen, his wife, Madeleine, has been—"
Seriel abruptly stopped talking.
'Why the hell am I discussing this with him?'
Fermi smirked.
"You really love this story. Well, I suppose it makes sense. He's
popular with women, just like me."
"…Huh? What did you just say?"
"A man who overcame childhood trauma, cold and intelligent, and
above all, handsome. Sounds just like me, don't you think?"
Seriel didn't say a word.
But the murderous intent in her eyes was answer enough.
Fermi quickly raised his hands.
"Sorry. Please don't kill me."
Seriel sighed.
"Forget it. Just leave, okay? This big sis is enjoying her precious
day off."
(Fermi was actually older than her.)
"Come on, let's go together. You look like you've got nothing
better to do. Let's grab some food."
"Are you insane? Why the hell would I eat with you?"
"Because it's better than eating alone? We see each other at the
school cafeteria every day anyway."
Seriel's lips curled into a mischievous grin.
"Aha, now I get it. So you're hitting on me right now,
huh?"
"Well… if you put it that way."
'This bastard.'
She wasn't sure if the rumors were true, but she had heard that the
Golden Wheel had quite the reputation with women.
"Haha! What, are you scared?
Afraid I'll eat you up—"
"Fine! Let's go."
Just imagining where this conversation was going made her
shudder.
She forcefully spun Fermi around and shoved him forward.
"But I pick the restaurant. Actually, this works out. I had
something to ask you anyway."
A chance to hear directly from the leader of the Golden Wheel about all
the corruption happening at school.
'Amy, hang tight. I'm bringing in top-tier intel.'
When she placed her book on the counter, the bookstore owner glanced at
Fermi.
"Your boyfriend? He's handsome."
Seriel immediately waved her hands.
But before she could speak, Fermi beat her to it.
"Thank you. Here's the payment."
He casually placed silver coins on the counter.
Seriel stared at him in disbelief.
"…What the hell? Why are you paying for my book?"
"Trying to impress you. You know, a small unexpected gesture— just
like Mayton."
"Ha."
She couldn't even scoff properly.
"You can't be Mayton. He might seem cold on the outside, but deep
down, he's gentle and delicate."
Seriel jabbed a finger at him.
"Unlike you, who's rotten to the core!"
The bookstore owner chuckled.
"You guys fighting over 'Primal Snare'? Happens a lot with couples.
If you're fans, why don't you go to the plaza? I heard the author is here today."
Seriel's head snapped around.
"Wait. You mean Sirius?"
"Yeah. He's touring Thormia on weekends, holding signing events. I
think it's starting right about now—"
"Thank you!"
Grabbing her book, Seriel bolted out the door.
Fermi hurried after her.
"Hey! What about lunch?"
"Who cares about lunch?! Just hurry up and follow me!"
When they arrived at the plaza, a huge banner hung overhead.
A long line of people—mostly women, from various age groups—stretched
out beneath it.
"Whoa, it's real. 'Primal Snare' Author: Sirius. Book Signing Event
for the Sequel."
Reading the banner aloud, Seriel clutched her book and bounced on her
heels.
"What do I do!? I'm so nervous! What kind of person is he? He must
have a cold, charismatic aura. The name 'Sirius' alone has that kind of vibe, right?"
"He's over there."
Fermi pointed at a table.
A huge, burly man was seated, signing books.
"…A hairy old man?"
With a thick, unkempt beard, he looked nothing like a romance
novelist.
Fermi barely held back his laughter.
"Mayton's reincarnation, huh?"
Seriel glared at him, then took a deep breath.
"It's fine. A writer's strength is in their words. He must be a
delicate soul."
But as the signing event dragged on,
Sirius kept chatting at length with each fan.
"See? I was right."
Seriel puffed up with confidence.
Until…
"Next!"
When a couple approached for a signature, Sirius didn't even make eye
contact with them—a stark contrast to before.
"Author, could you please write my name as well…?"
"Next!"
The man, irritated by the blunt dismissal, frowned and confronted
him.
"How long does it take to write a name? You just spent five minutes
chatting with that other woman!"
"So what? You think I'm a joke? Oh, I see. You're a couple, huh?
Just because you have a partner, you think you can flaunt it in my face?!"
The boyfriend was completely baffled.
"Wait, what? Are you okay?"
"Get lost! My readers are women— and not just any women! Only
single women! Am I right, ladies?"
Though a few booed, their voices were drowned out by the overwhelming
cheers.
Seriel stood there, jaw hanging open.
"What the hell is happening?"
"He's a novelist, after all. From a publishing perspective, authors
like him aren't that uncommon. Think about it—you only immerse yourself in a book for two hours at a
time. But this guy lives in his stories all day, every day."
"So it's like an occupational hazard?"
"Haha! Yeah. For him, the characters in his books matter more than
his own life. All he can do in reality is drink, smoke, and cause a scene at book signings."
Seriel frowned.
"Do you think he'll react badly to us? We're not a couple, but what
if he misunderstands?"
Unfortunately, her fears were justified.
After waiting for an hour, just as their turn was finally
approaching—
The event host grabbed a microphone.
"Alright, everyone! That's the end of today's signing! Please
continue to support Sirius!"
A disappointed sigh rippled through the crowd.
But Sirius himself didn't hesitate—he left the table immediately.
"Damn it. If he'd just signed for three more people, I would've
gotten one."
Fermi didn't say a word.
His eyes followed Sirius as he slipped into a café near the
plaza.
"Give me the book."
Without waiting for a response, Fermi snatched 'Primal Snare' from
Seriel and took off.
"Hey! Fermi!"
By the time Seriel caught up, a swarm of fans had already gathered in
front of the café.
"Does Mayton really love Elgia? If he does, why doesn't he divorce
his wife?!"
Readers were bombarding Sirius with questions.
Meanwhile, Fermi pushed through the crowd.
"Excuse me."
As soon as he opened the café door, the event host blocked his
path.
"Apologies, but the signing is over. The author is exhausted, so
please wait for—"
Fermi handed him a business card.
It bore the crest of the Ardino family.
The host immediately stiffened and stepped aside.
"This way, sir."
Before Fermi even reached the table, the host whispered something
quickly to Sirius.
"Hah! So what?"
Seeing his reaction, Fermi immediately knew.
'Haah. This type.'
Most authors were polite to their readers.
But every once in a while, you ran into someone like this.
"Good afternoon. I'm Fermi of the Ardino family."
"Yeah. I saw you waiting in line."
Of course, Sirius only remembered because of Seriel.
"My friend is a big fan of yours. She's not my girlfriend, by the
way. Would you be willing to sign her book?"
"Hah! Oh, really?"
Sirius let out a mocking laugh.
"You think I'm an idiot? Do you seriously believe a romance
novelist wouldn't recognize a guy trying to impress a girl? You can't fool me."
…He wasn't wrong.
"That may be true, but we're really not a couple. Could you
consider this a favor?"
Fermi was always respectful in business.
"I understand your situation, but I don't make exceptions. I may
not be some 'great literary master,' but I have my own writing philosophy. And without principles, you
can't do this job."
'Ah. So it's that type of issue.'
This was a nightmare for a publisher.
"Here. A small token of appreciation."
In the end, Fermi resorted to his last option.
He discreetly slipped a gold coin into Sirius's hand.
Even for a bestselling author, this was a difficult sum to
refuse.
"Oh? Money."
Sirius clenched the coin.
"Money's great. I like money. Who doesn't?"
Then he tossed the coin back into Fermi's pocket.
"But you're misunderstanding something, businessman. I don't write
to make money. I make money to keep writing."
"A noble paid me a fortune to change a story. I refused. Do you
know why?"
Sirius grabbed his iced coffee.
"The only time I change something…"
Then, with zero hesitation, he splashed the drink directly onto Fermi's
face.
"…is when it benefits my readers! And that has nothing to do with
money!"
As coffee dripped down his face, gasps erupted from outside.
"Oh my gosh! What just happened?!"
Most onlookers weren't shocked—they looked amused.
But Seriel went pale.
'…He just did that to Fermi?'
Anyone from Alpheas Magic Academy who knew Fermi would have the same
thought.
"……I understand."
To everyone's surprise, Fermi didn't even wipe the coffee off.
He simply turned around and left.
Outside, he noticed something.
His copy of 'Primal Snare'—
now soaked with coffee.
"Ah…?"
Seriel saw something unexpected.
For the first time, Fermi's eyes flickered with genuine
uncertainty.
"S-Sorry. Uh… You know,"
Fermi awkwardly held up the book.
"Should I… go buy a new one?
I can go alone if you want."
Silence.
And then—
"Pfft—"
Seriel doubled over, clutching her stomach.
"PWAHAHAHAHA! You… You're hilarious! Pwahaha!"
"What? What's so funny?"
He probably didn't realize it.
Yes, Seriel loved this book.
But at the end of the day, it was just a book.
'Back then… Fermi…'
Even though he didn't get the signature,
he knew what really mattered.
'Honestly… he was kind of cool.'
In Seriel's eyes— he was actually a pretty decent guy.
