Chapter 2:
Encountering Magic (2)
"I'll try again."
Even after Vincent withdrew, Shirone continued to observe the notch in the tree for a
long time without moving.
Currently, he was feeling a slight thrill.
Holding a line of technique in one's head is knowledge. But accepting it with the entire
body is sensation.
Strike the same spot, but twist it slightly.
This simple knowledge, passed down through the mouths of many people, was being absorbed
by Shirone as a much greater realization than Vincent had expected.
"Dad."
"Yes?"
"Should I try to break this in one go?"
"Hahaha! You haven't even chopped halfway, how could it break?"
"If I'm lucky, it might happen."
No tree breaks because of luck. Skilled lumberjacks relentlessly exploit the weakness of
the grain and topple them with just a few axe strokes, but Shirone couldn't possibly do that.
"Alright! Let's believe in our son's luck!"
Vincent heartily played along with his son. He was just grateful that he showed interest
in wood chopping.
"If I break this, grant me one wish."
"Hm? A wish?"
A sudden unease crept in. What if he wants to learn writing? Or asks to be sent to school
like children from rich families?
"When you go to the city to sell things next time, take me with you."
To be honest, Vincent was immensely relieved, but he didn't show it and laughed
boisterously.
"Haha, if that's all, sure. I'll grant it!"
Having received permission, Shirone took the axe behind his shoulder and looked at the
tree. Soon, as the smile on his lips disappeared, Vincent felt a chill. His son, not even blinking, was
staring at the tree as if searching for something invisible.
Shirone suddenly swung the axe. It seemed to hit the notch precisely, but mixed in at an
extremely subtle level, imperceptible to human recognition, was a slight twist.
Crackboom!
Vincent opened his eyes wide at the sound like lightning striking. Cracks like an
earthquake occurred at the spot where the axe entered, and the tree broke with a loud creak, unable to bear
its weight.
"Hooray! I did it!"
Vincent couldn't believe it. It was the "Thunder Chopping" passed down as a
legend among lumberjacks. Neither Vincent nor any lumberjack he knew had ever seen or experienced this
mythical technique.
But that's because lumberjacks' work is focused on livelihood. In reality, there is one
profession where the Thunder Chopping phenomenon can be encountered quite frequently, and that's
swordsmanship.
Swordsmen, who wield their swords with their lives at stake, hone techniques that are
incomparably more systematic and profound than lumberjacks.
Thunder Chopping was among these techniques. But it was clear that even for such
swordsmen, it wasn't an easy technique to master.
"Hooray! I did it!"
Shirone, who would now be able to visit the city as promised, jumped up and down with
both arms raised.
Vincent, watching him, was confused.
He couldn't decide whether he should raise his son as a lumberjack or not.
***
Vincent held the reins of a twin-horse carriage with a luggage cart and passed through
the gates of Creas City. Shirone, sitting in the cargo area, looked around with sparkling eyes.
Leather goes to the weapons shop, meat to the grocery store, and entrails to the herb
shop or magic shop. They had to visit several stores, and if they included bargaining, it would take at
least 4 hours.
Upon arriving at the store, Shirone jumped off the cart. As Vincent placed a bag of meat
on his shoulder, he said:
"We need to return before sunset."
"Don't worry. I've memorized all the roads."
"Never go into obscure places, stick to the main roads. If someone comes and asks
why you're alone, point to the nearest store and say you're waiting for your dad."
"Okay. But nothing happened the last time I came, you know."
Vincent felt pained at the thought of leaving his son. But the family's livelihood
depended on how well he negotiated, so it couldn't be helped.
Shirone reassured his worried father and left the shopping district. Vincent expected
that he would go to places children typically visit like performance halls or toy shops, but Shirone had no
interest in such things.
The place Shirone arrived at was the largest library in the city. Looking up at the vast,
majestic building, his heart pounded.
Could all the knowledge since the beginning of time be accumulated in this building?
There was no way to confirm. The library, being a high-end cultural space, was
inaccessible to anyone who wasn't a noble.
When two female students walked out of the library holding books, Shirone quickly stepped
aside.
Vincent was an affectionate father, but he always threatened like an ogre when talking
about nobles.
Never defy them. Their words are always right. If you make eye contact, bow your head.
Shirone didn't care how great the nobles were. He just wanted to read books.
'Why can't I enter?'
Shirone decided to follow the female students. Not only was there nowhere else he wanted
to see, but he was also curious about how nobles differed from commoners.
After walking for about 20 minutes, the scenery changed dramatically.
Shirone, forgetting his original purpose, was overwhelmed by the enormous structures
rising in all directions. The crown jewel among them was a school that boasted a vast area even when seen
through iron bars.
The building name was inscribed on the arch of the main gate.
Alpheus Magic School.
If there was one word that Shirone, in his life, couldn't understand, it was magic.
It appears consistently in numerous stories, but he had never seen a book that explained
its principles. It was a kind of disregard. It was also arrogance, suggesting that those who aren't
magicians don't need to know.
A guard spotted Shirone and shouted:
"What! What are you doing, kid?"
And waved his hands back and forth as if shooing flies, seeing Shirone's attire was that
of a commoner.
"Get out of here! This isn't a place for vermin like you."
"Ah, I'm sorry."
Shirone fled in fear. His knees weakened when the burly-looking guard glared at him.
But when the guard was out of sight, he stopped walking again. Surprisingly, he was still
under the walls of the magic school.
How vast is this school anyway?
While thinking this, he heard an old man's voice from beyond the wall.
"Now, let's talk about what magic is."
"Ahh, no! Show us magic. Just one more!"
"Fire! Show us the fire, Principal!"
Shirone looked up at the towering tree standing beyond the wall. The principal of the
magic school seemed to be conducting an outdoor class under the tree's shade.
The voices of the children were all younger than Shirone. Since nobles are educated from
the moment they're born, it wasn't strange for young children to attend magic school.
"Hohoho, lighting a fire at school would get me in trouble. Instead, if you answer
the question correctly, I'll show you an interesting magic."
"Yes! What's the question? Ask quickly!"
Shirone put his ear to the wall.
"What is the most necessary talent for learning magic?"
Silence followed for a while.
Everyone must have been speechless. Even to Shirone, it was a question requiring quite a
subjective answer.
But the children, recognized for their talent in magic and admitted early, were geniuses.
Rather than giving up, they each shared their own answers.
"Effort. Since magic can't be fully learned even if studied for a lifetime, effort
is important."
"I think it's knowledge. I've read over 100 magic books."
Other plausible answers like concentration and memory followed.
But the principal's voice was still not heard. Perhaps he was smiling kindly while
watching the children?
"Money. Because there's so much to buy to learn magic."
The children burst into laughter.
The principal's laughter was mixed in as well.
By this point, Shirone was also curious. If not effort, knowledge, or money, what is the
most necessary talent for learning magic?
After hearing all the children's answers, the principal finally spoke.
"The most necessary talent for learning magic is insight."
A second silence fell.
"What's insight?"
The principal made a troubled groan. But soon, in a kind voice, he continued his
explanation.
"Insight is something more accurate than knowledge and faster than effort."
"Wow! Then it's totally like magic?"
"Hoho! It could be. No, that's right. All magic in the world stems from insight. Let
me give an example. Do you know what 1 plus 1 is?"
"Of course it's 2."
The children answered in unison, as if asking how he could ask such a thing.
"I see. Then, can you explain why 1 plus 1 is 2?"
"Huh? Well, obviously..."
The children, who were about to speak confidently, closed their mouths. They couldn't
grasp where to start the explanation or how to explain it.
Alpheus's voice carried a hint of amusement.
"That strange feeling you're experiencing right now is insight. Long ago, there was
a time when people didn't know that 1 plus 1 is 2. It was proven through knowledge and effort. But you
understand perfectly that 1 plus 1 is 2 without going through that process, don't you?"
Shirone became increasingly engrossed in the story. The children beyond the wall were
probably also listening with their mouths agape.
"Magic is a phenomenon that has always existed. Just like how the answer is 2 even
when people didn't know that 1 plus 1 is 2. Someone uncovered that fact through effort and knowledge, but
you don't need to do that. Thus, insight is the fastest means to understand the rules of something."
"So there's no need to study or make effort?"
"Is that what I'm saying? But to be honest... yes, that's the case."
Common sense is knowledge believed by the majority. But sometimes, it's common sense
because it's easy to accept. Shirone could guess Alpheus's feelings of having to tell the cruel reality.
"So we don't need to go to school?"
"Hohoho! Insight isn't something easily obtained. The reason why 1 plus 1 is 2 was
proven by scholars over a long period. Of course, there are people who grasp the answer without going
through the verification process. We call such people geniuses."
"My mom said I'm a genius?"
"That's not wrong either. Every human is born with talent, and if they hone that
talent, anyone can become a genius."
Anyone can become a genius. Those words lodged deeply in Shirone's heart.
But is that really true? Would the opportunity to cross this high wall come to him as
well?
"Yes. You, the child standing behind the wall. I'd like to hear your thoughts."
Shirone hastily retreated from the wall.
Should he run away? No, should he answer? But does a commoner have the right to answer?
"Don't do that, come over here. I want to see your face."
Shirone approached the wall, hearing the sound of his beating heart. Though still young
and unaware of the ways of the world, one thing was clear: if he didn't cross this wall now, he would regret
it for the rest of his life.
Shirone climbed the wall. Alpheus, the principal of Alpheus Magic School, was smiling.
The first impression was of a white-haired old grandfather. But in reality, he was a
well-respected figure, a certified level 4 magician recognized by the kingdom, known even in foreign
countries.
Alpheus, sitting on a flat rock, waved his hand.
"Come on over. Have you come to keep this old man company?"
Gaining confidence from Alpheus's kindness, Shirone jumped over the wall. As expected,
children around Shirone's age or younger were sitting in a circle.
