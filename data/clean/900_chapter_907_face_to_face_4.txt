Chapter 907:
Face to Face (4)
A day had passed.
"Ugh."
When Amy woke up in bed and moved her body as usual, she felt muscle
pain all over.
'Feels like I fell off a cliff.'
Holding her aching back, she hobbled to the window and looked
outside.
She saw Nane and Shura working in a small garden since early
morning.
'What are they doing?'
The world was heading towards destruction, yet the so-called Buddha of
the era was tending a garden.
Sensing her gaze, Nane straightened up and looked back at the
cabin.
As Amy took in Nane's smile, she recalled what he had said the previous
day.
'He needs to make sense.'
She understood his desire to become like Shirone to understand
him.
'Is love something you can achieve just because you want to?' As she
questioned herself, she suddenly realized.
'Why do I love Shirone?'
Maybe, as Nane said, love is just about giving your heart.
"Ah, whatever! It's not something I should worry
about."
What concerned her more was what Shura had told her the previous
day—that the heavenly army had arrived.
'Havitz alone is overwhelming. Iruki must be having a hard time. No,
it's beyond that...'
It would be the worst war in history.
Thinking this far, Amy looked at Nane with a slightly different
perspective.
His face was covered in tattoos, making him look fierce, but his smile
was genuinely innocent.
'He doesn't seem like a bad guy.'
She didn't even want to imagine what would have happened if she had been
captured by Havitz instead of Nane.
'If he sides with Shirone...'
Moreover, Nane had swallowed Anke Ra's dream, which controlled the
heavenly army.
As if reading her thoughts, Nane shook his head with a mischievous
expression.
"Hmph!"
He had said he loved her, but now...
'No, no.'
Expecting anything from him was dangerous, so Amy shook her head.
'I have to fight.'
Her body was still too weak to run, but magic didn't require physical
strength.
When she opened the cabin door and stepped out, Shura took off the scarf
she was wearing and stood up.
"Lazybones. Come and help."
"Why should I?"
"If you don't work, you don't eat. Don't you know that? If you
don't want to starve, at least pick up some stones."
Not eating for a day wouldn't kill her, but she didn't want to be
stubborn about it.
"I'll get a hoe..."
Nane interrupted.
"Are you feeling okay?"
"Don't worry. I'll earn my keep."
Seeing Amy pout and puff out her cheeks, Nane smiled with his
eyes.
"Guests don't need to work. Leave this to us and do what you want.
There's a good place in the forest for mental training."
"I'm fine. And I'd prefer if you didn't act so friendly. It's
creepy." Nane didn't seem offended.
"Just go. You're a soldier, and the world is on the brink of
destruction. Tending a garden would only frustrate you and be bad for your health."
Remembering her mission, Amy regained her composure.
"You're right. What am I doing here? Whether you feed me or not,
I'm going to train." Without waiting for a response, she turned and walked away, and Shura threw
down her hoe in frustration.
"Ugh, so annoying! Such a fuss over nothing."
"The fire element isn't nothing."
Shura fell silent.
Amy might have been just a strong magician once, but now she was a
system.
'That pillar of fire.'
The massive flames that had pierced the sky at the Kanian Plateau were
still vivid in her mind.
"Tsk!"
Shura glared at Nane.
"Then shouldn't we treat her even better? She'll be our enemy
someday."
"I told you. If I don't understand Shirone, I can't become a true
Buddha. The same goes for Shirone. He's probably realizing the truth of emptiness right
now."
"Isn't it just because she's pretty?"
"Haha! I can't deny that."
Shura's eyes darkened.
"What kind of Buddha are you?"
Nane patted Shura's head.
"Pretty is pretty. The first virtue in facing the truth is not to
ignore facts."
Since the Buddha's words were always right, Shura accepted them this
time too.
'Why am I like this?'
Since Nane confessed to Amy, Shura felt a tightness in her chest.
'Buddha, why am I by your side?'
For the first time, that small fact felt more significant than the
secrets of the world.
Shura didn't ask further, and Nane continued to work in the garden with
his usual kind smile.
'Ignite!'
As the ignition circuit of the fire magic was activated, flames erupted
from Amy's body.
"Wow."
The power was so overwhelming it even surprised her.
'Is this the Fire Avatar technique?'
She felt like she had become the fire itself, not just a magician
controlling it.
"But this isn't enough."
She needed the power of the magic she had cast at the Kanian Plateau to
fight the heavenly army.
"Phew. Phew."
Having already faced death once, she was afraid to take the first
step...
"Here I go."
Amy was a soldier.
'Crown of Emptiness.'
As her focus endlessly delved into emptiness, her mind seemed to open
up.
'I can't give up!'
If she couldn't control the inertia of emptiness, she would die like at
the Kanian Plateau.
"Grrr!"
Her golden eyes flashed 30 times per second, but control was impossible,
and the flames grew endlessly.
'No! No!'
Realizing she had lost control, the flames flared up as if fueled by
oil.
"Sermon. Calm."
As Nane's voice echoed through the world, the flames of the fire element
subsided as if pressed down.
"Gasp!"
It felt like being pulled out of water just before drowning.
"Huff! Huff!"
Amy's mind snapped back to reality.
Panting with dilated pupils, she slowly turned her head.
Nane scratched his eyebrow with a troubled expression.
"This is difficult. I suggested mental training, not training with
a life-or-death resolve."
"What's the difference?"
She wanted to argue fiercely, but her voice was small because his sermon
had helped her.
Nane walked over with his hands behind his back.
"Your Fire Avatar technique is dangerous. It's rare for a human to
awaken an element as an avatar. Only someone like Shirone could do it."
Hexa itself was light.
"Of course, you're unique too, Amy. It seems the spirit of fire
resides in your mind."
'Jack O' Lantern.'
The fire spirit Shirone had given her outside heaven had grown with
Amy.
"So it manifested as an avatar?"
"You've maintained a high affinity for fire. It's a level that
requires tremendous training. It's a double-edged sword. If you can't control it, it will harm
you."
'The Fire Avatar technique isn't simple.'
Unlike magic, it didn't get stronger with repeated training, as she had
just experienced.
'Enlightenment.'
It required a special realization that could control not just the mind
but the heart.
'But I can't start meditating now. Or can I?'
Amy knew better than anyone that she was far from being an enlightened
seeker.
Nane asked,
"Shall I help you?"
"No, I'll figure it out myself."
If she hadn't heard his story yesterday, she might have accepted, but
she didn't want to owe him more.
"Don't overthink it. Helping someone lost in their mind is natural.
It has nothing to do with Shirone. And I might be a pretty good teacher."
He was the closest to righteousness in this world.
"I know. But I still don't want it."
Nane laughed helplessly.
"You're really stubborn. Then I'll give you a hint. You're not far
off. There's no big or small in being wrong, but enlightenment is like that. A moment of insight. A
slight change in direction can solve it instantly."
"Instantly?"
"Yes. It depends on you. But to do that, you need to set aside your
stubbornness for a moment."
Amy pouted.
"Shall we try again? Don't worry and fully open your
avatar."
Nane spread his right arm.
"Sermon Wall."
A yellowish wall spread out like a fan, blocking the surrounding scenery
like a net.
When Amy glared suspiciously, Nane shrugged.
"At least I have to protect my land."
"Hmm, fine."
She hadn't heard the mantra, but she felt something from his words to
fully open her avatar.
'I was scared.'
This time, she would control it.
'Crown of Emptiness.'
As her golden eyes lit up, flames roared around Amy again.
"Grrr!"
This is why it's difficult.
'I understand it in my head...'
She thought the heart was lighter than a feather, but fear still
remained.
Her mind was being sucked in.
"Gasp!"
As the flames grew uncontrollably and hit the ceiling of the barrier
Nane had created, Amy thought, 'I need to stop here...'
At that moment, Nane's voice echoed in her mind.
"Don't stop."
'What?'
"You are fire. You won't stop until you burn everything. Grow
bigger. Keep growing endlessly."
"Endlessly?"
A flash of insight struck Amy's mind.
'I see!'
The only thing that can control fire.
"Yaaah!"
Amy broke through her fear and increased her firepower in a state of
selflessness.
'Yes! Burn! Burn it all!'
As if she had become a bomb, her mind was engulfed by the will of the
fire.
"Gasp!"
As the will of the fire pierced through the speed of emptiness, a
massive catharsis surged through her.
"That's it."
Nane smiled.
"That's exactly it." When Amy regained her senses, she saw
flames surrounding her.
"This is..."
The flames were small but pointed in all directions like the quills of a
hedgehog.
"The will of the fire."
Nane said, raising his hand.
"The true meaning of the fire element is to become fire before it
consumes you. Remember, the only thing that can suppress fire is an even greater fire."
"I..."
Listening to Nane's words in a daze, Amy suddenly raised her hands and
shouted.
"I did it! I did it!"
She had mastered the fire.
She had finally reached the level she had dreamed of since her days at
the magic school.
"I did it! I...!"
As she jumped around with tears in her eyes, Nane watched with a pleased
smile.
"You really love magic. Congratulations."
Amy's movements stopped abruptly.
"...In the end, I received help."
"You did it. I was a magician once, so I know how desperate you
were. And just because I said a few words doesn't mean it was help."
"If it were anyone else but you, I wouldn't have risked my life to
follow them."
"If you put it that way..."
As Nane scratched his head awkwardly, Amy felt a bit sorry.
'Right, I should accept kindness as kindness.'
"I really have no ulterior motives. You risked your life, and no
seeker would ignore someone lost on their path..."
"Thank you."
Amy said with a smile.
"This time, I'll accept that I received help."
Surprised by her unexpected response, Nane blinked for a moment and then
realized.
'I see.'
He felt he understood Shirone a little better.
