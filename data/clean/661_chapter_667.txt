Chapter 667:
Of course, Shirone knew they were different from the corpse-eating
monsters said to inhabit the Middle East.
These people were undeniably human, yet they had chosen
cannibalism.
One didn't need to recall the tale of Canis eating filth to imagine what
kind of place Radum was.
In a space made of iron and stone, the only things they could eat were
either stray birds that lost their way—or the human beside them.
The thought of eating human flesh made his skin crawl, but as the leader
of the mercenary corps, Shirone remained calm.
"Let's move in. Maintain alert formation in case of any
emergencies."
As the twenty mercenaries passed through the barricade and entered the
alley, a sticky bloodlust could be felt rippling through the air.
It was only darkness, yet it felt like being submerged in something damp
like paint, and like drool from the starving, thick black water seemed to drip from the shadows.
"This is where Radum begins."
After passing through the barricaded straight alley, Lupist
spoke.
The reason the ghouls hadn't approached the corpses recklessly was
because this alley was still under the guards' control.
Jane whispered.
"Shall we take care of them?"
If they left the area now, the guards' corpses would surely end up in
the ghouls' throats.
"No, we proceed."
Recovering the bodies and returning them to their families was the duty
of the Magic Association's head, but right now there was a more important mission.
'Tools of the kingdom.'
Things to be used and discarded if needed to achieve a goal.
"Light the torches."
They didn't want to provoke anything with Shining magic, so Baikon, the
heavily armored swordsman, and Bromark, the survival specialist, lit torches and scraped the edge of the
shadows.
Along the outer rim of the light that pushed back the darkness, shadows
flickered and darted into the corners.
"Strange."
Lupist halted his steps.
The ghouls' obsession was far worse than expected.
No matter how hungry they were, they weren't foolish enough to fail in
recognizing apex predators.
Normally, if a twenty-man mercenary corps appeared, they'd retreat far
beyond range. But now they were keeping to the minimum distance and staring them down.
"This is annoying. Want me to wipe them all out?"
Toto, the spearman, twirled his weapon.
He was known for his violent and uncontrollable temper and was
blacklisted in many circles, but his athleticism using four schema and skill in spearmanship earned him
a place on the team.
"Hey, maggots! If you're coming, come out already!"
His voice echoed through the alley, and Shirone frowned.
"Lower your voice. If you provoke the enemy without permission, I'll
take disciplinary action going forward."
Toto shrugged.
"What, over a bunch of ghouls…"
It was common knowledge that Radum was dangerous, but their unit wasn't
weak enough to be intimidated by starved wretches.
"There's a trap."
Bromark, kneeling, held the torch toward the alley.
"A tripwire trap, connected by thin wires. These things are luring
us."
"Raaaagh!"
Suddenly, a ghoul charged from the darkness, screaming as though its
throat had torn open.
'They're attacking?'
Given their primary survival instinct, it was a shocking
development.
"No need to panic! They're just ghouls!"
As the twenty readied formation, the residents of Radum charged in from
every direction.
Without any prompt, the mages cast Shining magic, and the floating
globes of light revealed the scenery of Radum in full.
"Ugh!"
Corpses lay everywhere in horrific states.
Gutted bodies, long-rotted skeletons, corpses with hollow eye
sockets—just the sight of it all sent shivers down one's spine.
"Ha! This is why I love being a mercenary!"
Toto was the only one enjoying himself, and his spear sent ghouls flying
with every swing.
While the mages still hesitated to act, Baikon and Wigg played the role
of gatekeepers, slicing down ghouls on either side of the formation.
'They're weak, but…'
Something was off.
The ones that looked terrified were the ghouls themselves—it barely felt
like a fight.
"This is just like…"
Like animals leaping off a cliff to escape a wildfire.
"Gah!"
As Wigg drove both blades into a ghoul's abdomen, it flinched and
reached out with trembling hands.
"Hmph!"
He twisted the blades, about to disembowel it when the ghoul muttered
with a dying voice,
"Please… kill me…"
"What?"
"This place… is hell…"
With vacant eyes, the ghoul opened its mouth and screamed.
"Daaaah!"
"Grrgh!"
Wigg backed off and sliced downward with both blades, cleaving the ghoul
in two.
"Meirei!"
At Lupist's command, Meirei covered her ears and activated the Divine
Frequency.
<The Nemesis>, worn as a ring, activated and transmitted the sound
she was hearing to all the core members.
"Dammit! What is this!?"
A massive wail crashed over them like a tidal wave.
It was the kind of sound you'd expect if ten thousand people were locked
in a room and set on fire.
Amid the wave of brain-numbing noise, some voices could be heard—but
they were unintelligible.
—Karte mu Ciel! Kartision be Raveka Purvel!
'It's Ra Enemy's voice!'
Deep, muffled, as though spoken while swallowing stones, and powerful in
its resonance.
'If you want to find me… seek the hell within you.'
Only Meirei, once a priestess of Theraphos, and Shirone with the Ultima
System, could decipher it.
—Struggle in the agony of illusion! Is there meaning in meaninglessness?
Become a god!
"What the hell is he saying?"
Even deciphered, the words were impossible to fully understand.
Rian, blocking the way in front of Shirone and cutting down a ghoul,
asked,
"The ghouls have gone mad. What does Ra Enemy's message mean?"
These people had surely encountered Ra Enemy.
"I don't know yet. There's something beyond just language."
In under five minutes from the start of the battle, the ghouls were
annihilated.
If anything, the fight had been underwhelming, but none of the
mercenaries looked pleased.
Even they now sensed something disturbing.
"What the hell's wrong with them? That kill felt dirty."
Baikon muttered, flicking blood off his blade, and Bromark pointed
toward the alley under the light of Shining magic.
"Look over there. All traps."
Dozens of tripwires were strung like spiderwebs.
"When did they set all that up?"
"Hm, a veteran like me could do it in under an hour."
"An hour…"
Shagal would've already passed through by now.
Bromark turned to Shirone.
"Two options—disarm or detour. Which route is this?"
"This way."
Lupist pointed at the trap-riddled path.
"I see. What's the plan?"
It was Shirone's call.
"How long to disarm them?"
"Disarming is harder than setting them. About an hour."
"Hmm…"
As Shirone hesitated, Bromark grinned.
"But if it were me, I could do it in 30 minutes."
"Then let's disarm them."
Their goal was to reach the central area as fast as possible—deviating
from the route now would be costlier.
"Then sit tight."
Bromark bit down on a dagger and crawled into the trap-laden path with
his toolkit.
While the knights stood guard and the rest rested, Joshua suddenly stood
up and aimed her bow at a rotting wooden crate.
"What is it?"
"Someone's there."
Her eyes blazed red.
"Nightshot?"
It was a kind of x-ray vision detecting heat—an eye technique she hadn't
even shown during evaluation.
Peering into the crate with Nightshot, she spotted a human heat
signature lying prone.
"Come out. My arrows pierce steel."
"Eeek!"
The crate rattled, and a man crawled out through a hole in the
back.
"P-please spare me!"
Baikon grabbed him by the collar and yanked him forward.
"Who the hell are you? Why were you hiding?"
"I-I'm not a Radum resident! I'm a trap engineer!"
"Traps? Then…"
As Baikon glanced over at Bromark, who was still disarming the traps, he
asked again.
"So you're the one who set those up? Why? Were you trying to trap
us?"
"N-no! I don't know the reason! Someone hired me just a few days ago. He
asked me to set traps at Radum's entrance."
Shirone asked, "Who was it?"
"He didn't give his name. He was a young man, but I don't think he was
from this country."
Meirei covered her ears with both hands, but no voice came—only
static.
"Hm, in that case…"
Just as Baikon was about to ask another question, the man suddenly
trembled, as if something had shocked him.
"What the—? What's wrong with this guy?"
"Shi…ro…ne…"
A thick, guttural voice came from deep in his throat.
"Watch out! Get back!"
Rian raised his greatsword to sever the man's head, but Shirone held out
his hand.
"Wait!"
The man, who had been arching backward, slowly lifted his head and
stared directly at Shirone.
"You… must answer the question left behind."
"The question left behind?"
Shirone asked, but the man gave no reply—his gaze turned upward.
His pupils began to rotate rapidly in opposite directions, and then,
finally, his real voice burst forth.
"No! Please! Save me! Aaaaaagh!"
His eyeballs spun so violently that his optic nerves were shredded, and
bloody tears streamed down his face.
"A human trap…?"
The ground shook as the trap's steel wires snapped with sharp, whiplike
sounds.
"Bromark! Get out of there!"
The mercenaries shouted, but Bromark was already bleeding from being
struck by the wire.
"Damn it! What the hell is this!?"
Before they could even attempt a rescue, the trap was triggered.
"The space is folding."
The alley closed like sliding doors, and Bromark, who had fallen to the
ground, reached out his hand.
"Gaaaah!"
As the walls connected, the sound of bones being crushed echoed.
Kargin winced and muttered, "Some survival expert…"
There wasn't even time to process the shock. The other members scanned
their surroundings.
Radum's layout had completely changed. On the walls, fibrous gunk like
organ residue clung thickly.
The corpses that had somehow stood upright were now motionless, like the
dead. On either side of the newly formed alley stood statues of nude women fused grotesquely with
monsters.
"The entrance is gone too. How is this even possible?"
"Reality through the five senses," Lupist said, looking up at the
sky.
The Shining magic had faded, yet they could still see—because the sun
was up.
But it wasn't the brilliant sun of their world. It was a burning red
sun, like one on the brink of death.
"We've been trapped."
"What do you mean, 'trapped'?" Shirone asked.
Lupist turned toward him. "Even if our brains were removed and put in a
tank, we wouldn't be able to feel it."
"No way…"
"Yes. That's exactly what's happened. This must be the scenery the
ghouls were seeing. They used the brainwaves of that man turned trap to project hell into our
reality."
"Goddamn it! What kind of sick joke is this!?"
Lemo, a non-certified 5th Class defensive mage, shouted.
"You think we followed you here to listen to this crap!? You're supposed
to have a solution!"
"Calm down. I'm thinking."
"Cut the crap! I had a bad feeling from the start! You—you're the Magic
Association's head, aren't you!?"
Lupist didn't answer.
"I don't care about the money or anything anymore! Let me out of here!
If you don't, I'll sue—! Guh!"
Lemo's eyes rolled back, and he collapsed, blood pouring from
them.
"Shit!"
Kargin rushed to him and tried to cast healing magic, but it was too
late—Lemo was already dead.
"What happened?"
Lupist glanced toward Jane, who shook her head indignantly.
"I didn't kill him. I was thinking about it, but I didn't."
"Struggle in the agony of illusion."
Meirei stepped forward.
"That's what Ra Enemy said. He also said: 'Seek meaning in the
meaningless.' If this is a kind of divine oracle, then perhaps it means this—"
She raised a finger.
"The moment you recognize death… is when it truly happens."
