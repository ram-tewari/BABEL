Chapter 873:
All That Conflict (3)
As Havitz fully recovered, demons from around the world were gathering
in Central Continent.
Led by six legion commanders, 220 million troops of the army of hell
assembled in the Kingdom of Galron.
220 million.
An unbelievably reduced number compared to the first army.
"We underestimated the annihilation," said Balkan. The
capability to destroy the system was producing results beyond mere arithmetic figures.
Gaold, who obliterated the South, and Shirone, who brought down
Gustav.
"Rian is also troublesome." As Balkan pondered over a world
map, Natasha approached him.
"Is he that strong?"
"Strong? If belonging to strength includes the inability to be
approached conceptually, then yes...
When simply calculated, no human surpassed Natasha's combat
abilities.
"He's like a joker in a card game, governed by different rules from
the existing ones. It makes him difficult to contend with."
Smordo approached.
"Having a joker doesn't necessarily mean winning the game. It's an
advantage, of course."
"Yes. And now we also have a joker."
In the commander's tent, Havitz was sitting on the bed, feeding a bottle
to Deris's child.
"Peekaboo."
Havitz seemed fascinated by the child.
"An entity connected to the law from outside the law. Havitz's
banishing definitely belongs to destruction."
Smordo asked, "What will we do?"
With the Gustav Empire annihilated, there was no place for them to
return.
"We advance. Central Continent is the key. If we break through
here, we can reach Zion via the Southern Continent."
By then, the humans should be largely subdued.
Hell's army, 220 million strong, reorganized into six legions and
commenced war.
Although Valkyrie had gained time due to Havitz's temporary constraint,
the situation remained critical.
"General! The 13th Brigade has been annihilated! The army of Hell
is moving south over the Argant Mountains!"
"The 8th Division is destroyed! Casualties reach
30,000."
As bad news arrived consecutively, commanders recalibrated the strategic
situation in real-time.
Ilruki bit his lip.
"There's no way."
Commonly, it's said that humans have no limits.
But Ilruki knew how arrogant and trivial that logic was.
"There really isn't any way!"
No matter where he looked, there was no place ringing with
victory.
Even a fool with 220 million demons at his command could win any
battle.
"Balkan."
And the enemy Ilruki faced was the best gambler who had beaten 'everyone
playing card games.'
"Kekeke, I can see it, clearly."
Though they had never faced each other, Balkan felt like he knew
Ilruki.
Smordo asked, "Is this the limit for Valkyrie's
military?"
"No. He's a genius. Honestly, if it weren't for the crowd tactics,
he might have been reversed."
"You're joking. With such a difference in troop
numbers..."
Of course, it was a joke because it wasn't reality, but honestly, Balkan
couldn't guarantee the outcome.
"There's a weakness."
Balkan moved troops on the map.
"They calculate the efficiency of war in human values. That's why
they can't win. 100,000 lives. Certainly, it would take a lifetime to recount their lives
but..."
The six legions formed a radial deployment.
"In war, they are just 100,000. You can say it in a second, just
100,000 lives."
Like putting chess pieces back in the box after a game, in war, humans
were merely tools.
"See, I moved like this. Then the other side can't even breathe
without suffering."
The third legion advanced 100 kilometers.
Smordo shook his head at Balkan's dark humor.
"Kekeke. How about that, kid?"
Balkan looked up, targeting Ilruki.
"War is fun, isn't it?" Ilruki slammed the table hard.
"Damn it!"
Even as he did so, reports of Valkyrie's defeats kept arriving.
"The capital of Galron has fallen. Casualties are estimated to
exceed 300,000."
"300,000."
The immense weight of lives lost clouded his mind.
"It's me killing them." Grey hairs proliferated, and now
Ilruki's hair was almost entirely grey.
"Me, I..."
It was strictly forbidden for a general to show signs of defeat, but
Ilruki was already beyond that point.
"General."
An aide by his side approached Ilruki and handed him a bottle of strong
liquor.
"Please drink. Cool your head first."
Ilruki chuckled.
"You want me to drink? Do you realize that hundreds of thousands of
lives hang on my command?"
Who would follow the strategy of a drunk?
"That's why you should drink." The aide pushed the bottle
towards him.
"If a person holding the lives of hundreds of thousands gets drunk
from a little alcohol, then he doesn't deserve to be general."
Ilruki looked back at the aide.
"Your strategy is brilliant. No one in Valkyrie can think like you.
But... this is war."
The aide spoke up about what was weighing on him.
"It doesn't matter how many die. If we don't win, we all die
anyway. Valkyrie follows you not to live but to win."
Ilruki slowly took the bottle as the aide bowed and pleaded.
"Please win."
Ilruki stared at the aide for a moment before opening the bottle and
taking a deep drink.
"Huh."
One must become a demon.
'If it's for victory...'
As his thoughts expanded, elements previously unseen started to combine
in his mind.
There still seemed no way to win, but at least a chance for mutual
destruction appeared attainable.
"The problem is time. If we don't make it quickly
enough..."
He had to trust in Nade.
"It's been a while, Nade."
Richard, the chairman of the Ainka Foundation, greeted Nade stepping out
from a luxury carriage.
Nade, neatly dressed in a suit, was flanked by secretaries from Nade
Munitions.
"Changed a lot, haven't you, alchemist Richard?"
Richard shrugged his shoulders.
"Kekeke, embarrassing to still use my school nickname. Anyway,
let's go. I heard you're busy..."
Richard had arranged the meeting to gather investments from wealthy
individuals around the world.
Funds required for developing an elemental bomb were astronomical, but
currently, Valkyrie lacked time.
Properly designed, it would take over three years to complete; they
needed to finish in a few months.
The only way to shorten the time was money.
"Everyone is waiting."
As they entered the VIP room of a high-end bar, the world's tycoons
arrogantly sat waiting for them.
"Sorry for being late. I am Ainka Richard, chairman of the Ainka
Alchemy Foundation."
Nade bowed.
"I'm West Nade of Nade Munitions. Pleased to meet you."
After introductions, Nade placed various documents on the table.
"As stated, the value of the elemental bomb could change the future
of humanity. If you invest in Valkyrie, we guarantee a return of at least 1,200 percent."
Nade finished speaking calmly.
They had likely reviewed all possible risks through private
channels.
The investment value was enticing, but the concept of an elemental bomb
itself might not be appealing.
"Ilruki's theory of elemental decay could become a revolutionary
technology that changes the future."
A bomb with power surpassing magic.
Once commercialized, the existing powers ruling the world would
inevitably experience a seismic shift.
A bald man spoke.
"Ah, finished? I was impressed by the briefing. So, you want us to
invest?"
"Yes. The world is under threat. Please lend your strength for the
cause."
It was unlikely to persuade them.
"Right, right. I understand. By the way, aren't you a graduate of
Alpheas Magic School?"
"Yes, I am."
"There's a singer named Maya from there, right? You know, she's
quite the talk among the young folks these days."
A long-haired man across interrupted.
"Oh? Mr. Todd, do you know Maya too? I've seen her perform once,
and she was amazing."
"Hahaha! Isn't she the top idol among the soldiers right now?
Anyway, you know her?"
Nade blinked and responded.
"Yes, she's a schoolmate of mine. But why Maya?"
Todd gestured towards Nade.
"Can you bring her here? Some event planning agency is being
ridiculously snobby. They think they can mess around because they rely on Valkyrie, not realizing they
could end up dead."
"I'm afraid that's difficult with Maya..."
"What's this? Isn't it better to help each other out for humanity's
sake? If you bring Maya, I'll treat you well too."
Nade suddenly heard a sound as if something inside him snapped.
"Oh, shi..." As electricity surged through his body, Richard
suddenly stood up and approached Todd.
Without a chance to speak, he grabbed a bottle of whiskey from the table
and smashed it over Todd's head.
"Aaaargh!"
As the heavy bottle shattered, Todd clutched his head and fell under the
table.
Nade watched blankly as Richard, shaking off blood from his palm,
said,
"Do you think Alpheas Magic School is a joke? Who dares to mess
with my classmate?"
Todd pointed his finger.
"You, you bastard! Do you know who I am? Ainka Foundation! Do you
think you'll be safe after this?"
"You better grasp the situation correctly. Do you think because
she's a singer, she's trivial? Do you know what Maya's classmates are doing now?"
Shirone's class was known as the golden generation at Alpheas Magic
School.
"You... you..."
As several figures flashed through Todd's mind, he was speechless, and
Richard headed for the door shouting,
"Nade, let's go! Why entrust the future to such
people?"
"Uh, okay."
As they left the upscale bar, Nade caught up to Richard, who was
storming ahead.
"Hey, wait. What are you doing?"
"And you? It's lucky for the bald guy that I didn't smash his head;
you would have killed him."
Nade couldn't deny it.
"How's your hand? Let's get it disinfected first."
"No need. I'm alchemist Richard, remember? Anyway, let's dust off
our hands. The world isn't just rich with those guys."
"Right. Let's look for other investors." Because it was none
other than Maya, Nade cleanly let go of any lingering feelings and walked to the carriage.
"Excuse me."
A sharp-looking man with horn-rimmed glasses approached briskly.
"Mr. West Nade, correct?"
After a glance at Richard, Nade responded.
"Yes, that's me."
"I heard you're gathering investors."
"Who are you?"
It might no longer be confidential, but knowledge of the elemental bomb
was extremely rare.
"We might be able to provide some funding. If you have a moment,
could we talk?"
Richard asked.
"You're providing funding? I don't know what you've heard, but it's
not an amount one person can invest."
"It's possible, no matter the amount." The man smiled and
pulled a coin from his pocket, tossing it to Nade.
"This is my business card."
"This is...?"
As Nade examined the coin, his eyes sparkled, and Richard opened his
mouth in astonishment.
"Golden Wheel."
Fermi's face came to mind.
