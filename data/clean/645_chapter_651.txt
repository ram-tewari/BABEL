Chapter 651:
The woman's face, draped in ragged cloth, looked truly unwell—partly due
to the harsh winter cold.
"Are you alright? Come inside for now."
At Shirone's invitation, the woman nodded obediently and stepped toward
the carriage.
"Tch, kids these days…"
Austin grumbled as he extinguished the flames on his hands.
Though she was bruised in places, the woman was voluptuous and
strikingly beautiful.
That alone made her a tempting target for bandits—but more worryingly,
it also made her appearance the perfect bait to deceive hot-blooded youths like Shirone and Rian.
"Thank you. Really… I was so scared."
As Shirone boarded the carriage, the woman pressed in close and grabbed
his arm, tears welling in her eyes.
"If you knew what they did to me, you'd be shocked. The bandits,
they—"
Shirone gently patted her back.
"You don't have to say anything. More than that, what I'd like to
ask—"
"Hold on, Shirone."
Rian called out from outside the carriage.
"I don't want her sitting next to you. Of course, only if you
agree."
Helping someone in danger was a given—but when it came to protecting
Shirone, Rian would not yield an inch.
Realizing the cold reception from both Austin and Rian, the woman clung
tighter to the only one who had shown her kindness—Shirone.
"Please sit across from me. The carriage is spacious anyway."
"Ah… okay."
Recalling Austin's warning, Shirone said this gently, and the woman
shifted to the wall seat across from the two.
"Ow…"
Even as she loosened her clothes and gently rubbed her stinging skin,
neither Shirone nor Rian reacted with surprise.
'Are these guys even men? Normally, when you go this far, they at least
pretend not to resist, right?'
If they had really doubted her, they wouldn't have let her board in the
first place—"cautious" was the more accurate word.
'Well, they did say he's a strong mage, even if he's young…'
Only after the carriage departed—its window on the driver's side left
open—did Shirone begin to ask questions.
The woman, who introduced herself as Mari, said she had been kidnapped
by bandits while traveling with friends from the capital.
"How many were there?"
"I'm not sure. But I'd say more than 100."
Austin snapped his head around.
"What? Over a hundred?"
A force of that size indicated a large, well-established group of
bandits.
And more importantly, it likely meant they had mages among them.
"Where are they now?"
Mari pointed to the side of the mountain.
"If they haven't noticed I escaped yet, they're probably still beyond
that ridge. I climbed straight down."
We're going to run into them…
Avoiding them would be the safest option, but as a veteran, Austin
remained cautious.
"What do we do? If we want to avoid the bandits, we have to decide
now."
Shirone asked Mari,
"Is it possible they haven't noticed your escape?"
"There were a lot of people captured besides me. And my friends…"
As Mari broke into sobs, Shirone fell into thought.
Should they avoid combat, or attempt a rescue?
"Let's go. We need to try and save the captives first."
"Huh?"
Mari's eyes widened.
No matter how strong a mage Shirone might be, charging into a force of
over a hundred with just three people was reckless.
"We'll save your friends too, Mari. Don't worry—we're not taking you
into danger."
"N-no, that's not what I meant… It's just…"
The reason for Mari's shock lay elsewhere, but she quickly adjusted her
tone, trying to appear sincere.
"Don't worry. We'll get your friends back."
She should have been worried about them, not her nonexistent "friends."
But Shirone showed no signs of fear or hesitation.
'Is he really that strong? He's younger than me…'
Mari stared at Shirone's side profile as he looked out the
window.
Atop the ridgeline of the Underground Mountain Range, 89 members of the
infamous Red Blade Bandits—known across the capital—were encamped.
"What's taking so long? Is she doing it right?"
"Reporting!"
A messenger galloped up on horseback.
"The carriage carrying Arian Shirone is headed toward the rendezvous
point!"
The bandit captain, Coldpark, scowled.
"Tch. So it was a bust after all."
As the mastermind behind this operation—who even used his beloved Mari
as bait—he had just lost a shot at a massive fortune.
"Let's move. We're joining up with the alliance."
The fact that every member was mounted hinted at the Red Blade Bandits'
scale.
As they rode along the mountain path, their strategist and mage, Banker,
rode up beside Coldpark.
"If they're heading toward the alliance, it means they chose to fight.
Based on the rumors, Shirone's a powerful mage."
Whether Shirone chose to fight or take a detour, the bandits had both
routes locked down.
This was only possible because, beyond the Red Blade Bandits, many other
groups were participating in the operation.
The idea had been for Mari to scare the group into veering toward their
trap, giving Coldpark's unit a chance to claim all the loot—but with Shirone choosing combat, that plan
had failed.
Alphasa, the vice-captain, approached from the left.
Carrying a massive double-bladed axe on his back, he rode using only the
muscles in his thighs, arms folded across his chest.
"They say he's an Ivory Tower candidate. Even if we've joined forces,
isn't this a bit dangerous?"
Even among the three major bandit groups in Tormia, made up of elite
Skima users, this operation was near-suicidal.
"We're doing well enough without taking on someone this high-profile.
The men don't look too convinced either."
The Red Blade Bandits prided themselves on discipline comparable to a
real army—but if Alphasa had doubts, there was no need to ask the others.
"Banker, tell them why we have to fight."
Following Coldpark's order, Banker pulled out a scroll—a compilation of
intelligence from guilds across the region.
"Arian Shirone. Confirmed graduate of Alpheas Magic School. Suspected to
be carrying an artifact called Armand. Rumored to have received it from Kazra royalty. Estimated value
based on appraisal: at least 6 billion gold."
"Six billion gold…"
The eyes of Alphasa and the others lit up.
"Now do you get it? Even if we lived ten lifetimes, we'd never make that
kind of money thieving. They say even royals chase after the Ivory Tower. But in the end, it's all about
power. If we can't take a single finger off a twenty-year-old, then what kind of bandits are we?"
From all gathered intel, Shirone's Armand was suspected to be hidden
within a Cubrick.
Coldpark shouted boldly:
"Money, women, pleasure—you bastards live for this! Since when did we
care about staying alive? If you're not in it for a massive payday, get lost now!"
Thundering hooves filled the air, but none veered off course.
Coldpark reined in his horse at the mountain peak, looking down at the
237 troops stationed below.
"The greatest war in Red Blade history begins now."
The Ear of the Deep, Beast Lynx, Spear and Death, and Blood Pillar—four
of the most notorious bandit groups near Bashka had gathered, forming a 237-strong allied force below
the ridge.
"Let's ride, boys! Six billion gold is waiting for us!"
As the carriage neared the rendezvous point, Mari closed her eyes with a
weary expression.
'I need to take them out here…'
Her light clothing wasn't just to play the damsel in distress—it was
part of her seduction tactic, meant to lower their guard.
After all, no man in his right mind would volunteer to search a
half-naked woman's body.
Once the sleeping gas was released, arrows loaded with mana rounds would
obliterate the carriage. That's why she believed Coldpark could still claim the artifact for
himself.
'Bet you've never seen a scheme like this before, huh?'
Mari, who had risen this far on her charm alone, had long mastered the
art of reading her targets—even with her eyes closed.
'Heh… as expected…'
She cracked one eyelid open, startled by the carriage's vibration—but
Shirone was still sitting there, resting his chin on his hand and gazing out the window, just like
before.
'Classic rookie. He's kind, sure—but if he lets this chance pass, he's
gonna regret it forever.'
Chuckling to herself, Mari pretended to fall asleep, turning her body
slightly away.
'Time to begin—'
But then she noticed Rian, watching her without so much as a twitch in
his expression.
'What the—? Was that… a coincidence?'
Now alarmed, she opened her eyes fully and made deliberate eye
contact—but Rian's stare remained unmoved.
'He's watching me. Shit.'
Her every move was refined through years of training—enough to avoid
suspicion. But at this rate, deploying the gas was out of the question.
'No more chances. I have to make my move now…'
Mari, now in her most dangerous posture, lifted her eyelids ever so
slightly—just enough to peer through her lashes.
It was another advanced seduction technique, but Rian maintained his
guard without the slightest change in expression.
'So annoying. What the hell are these guys?'
Beauty never fails.
Though it only worked on half the human race, history itself had proven
its near 100% success rate.
'So I'm not even a woman to them?'
Her pride was deeply rooted in her mastery of such techniques, so her
ego took a blow. But now, she was truly out of time.
'The bombardment will begin any second now.'
Beep beep. Beep beep.
Birdsong echoed from the mountaintop. Austin frowned and looked up at
the sky.
'Not good.'
Bandits traditionally used birdsong-like flute signals to communicate,
and the problem was—it was nearly indistinguishable from real birds.
'Ten seconds left.'
Mari, who understood the meaning hidden in the signal, started counting
down to the bombardment.
'Nine… eight…'
"Mari."
For the first time, Shirone turned from the window and looked directly
at her.
The face she saw through her lashes was unrecognizably cold—nothing like
the boy from before.
"What are you hiding?"
At Shirone's question, Mari's fingertips twitched.
'Breath. Control your breathing.'
It was a level she'd reached after years of practice. But in front of
Shirone's unwavering eyes, her heart refused to obey.
'Damn it! I'm off by a beat. He's already figured it out.'
She shut her eyes tight, twisted her body suddenly, and launched herself
out of the coach through the driver's window.
'Four… three…'
Even as Mari threw herself out of the carriage, Austin didn't yell in
anger. Instead, he glanced up at the sky.
Archers riding hang gliders were soaring in from beyond the mountain
heights.
"It's an ambush! Move, now—!"
But by the time he turned around to shout, the passenger compartment was
already empty.
'Two… one…'
As dozens of arrows rained down, Shirone clutched the back of Austin's
collar and leapt from the carriage.
At the same moment, Rian, who had exited through the opposite door,
beheaded Mari with blinding acceleration.
BOOOOOM!
The magic-loaded arrows struck the carriage in full force, triggering a
massive explosion.
"Urgh!"
The shockwave hurled Shirone and Austin toward the slope, and the
thunder of galloping hooves filled their ears.
Dozens of mounted bandits charged down the mountain path.
"Engage! Kill them all!"
Austin, still holding fireballs, growled.
"Tch! Knew it was a trap!"
"At least no one got captured."
"You call that—?!"
Before he could finish venting, dozens of spearmen from the Spear and
Death bandit crew leapt from the slope with weapons raised.
"Six billion is ours!"
"Watch out!"
As Austin reached out, hundreds of eyes flashed open from within
Shirone's robe.
Akamai – Full Bloom.
"Argh! What is this?!"
Stripped of their will to act by the anti-thesis magic, the spearmen
collapsed like marionettes with their strings cut, tumbling helplessly to the ground.
