Chapter 48:
Limitless (7)
Shirone's mind expanded like a supernova, spreading endlessly. Perfect liberation. Beyond
modular limits, all numbers in existence compressed into a single point.
Whoosh whoosh whoosh—Bam!
His targeting speed skyrocketed. Two streaks of light shot from Shirone's Spirit Zone
simultaneously.
"Senior's doing double too!"
"No, double's impossible without Servant Syndrome. This is beyond that."
Seriel couldn't believe her eyes. The flashes multiplied—from ten to twenty, twenty to a
hundred.
Shirone felt pure liberation. A sensation of permeating the world. The stronger it grew,
the fainter his thoughts became. He could think of nothing—only sense everything.
His Spirit Zone expanded endlessly. Surpassing 80 meters in diameter, the flashes merged,
filling the zone with light. A massive photon—even Shirone's form was engulfed.
Targets exploded upon appearing, accelerating the step progression.
'Ah… Ahhh…'
Shirone shed tears as he felt his existence fading. He had transcended numbers. It was
over. All that remained was dissolving into the world.
'No! It's not over yet!'
Iruki gritted his teeth. Shirone's surging score didn't matter. If Shirone sped up, he'd
just run faster.
'Servant calculations have no limits! A trillion, a quadrillion—I'll compute them all!
I'll see the end of numbers!'
Iruki's Spirit Zone moved at inhuman speeds. He'd win. He could! Time left—1 second! 0.9!
0.85! Damn it! Why won't time move!
Beep!
The eternal minute ended. As targets vanished, Shirone and Iruki collapsed
simultaneously.
While Iruki gasped for breath, Shirone lay motionless—unconscious.
Iruki looked up at his score.
1,247 points.
An unprecedented record in the magic school's history, surpassing Amy's by 260 points.
'Did I win? Why's it so quiet? What's Shirone doing?'
Turning, Iruki saw Shirone kneeling, face buried in his chest, unmoving. Though he seemed
to breathe, his presence felt hollow.
Iruki checked Shirone's scoreboard.
1,253 points.
"Se—Senior! Senior Shirone…!"
"Shirone won!"
Seriel cut off Mark and rushed forward. Shirone had defeated the formidable Iruki. She
wanted to kiss him on Amy's behalf—if she'd allow it.
Juniors swarmed the image zone, congratulating and shaking Shirone.
"Gasp!"
Shirone jolted awake, eyes wide. His mind, submerged in meaninglessness, reconstructed
itself—names, emotions flooding back.
While Shirone was mobbed, no one approached Iruki. Only Nade placed a hand on his
shoulder.
"You lost. But it was a great match."
"Not much consolation. The point is to win."
Nade chuckled and glanced at Shirone. Truly a remarkable talent. While others saw just a
targeting test, knowing Iruki's prowess made Shirone's epiphany unimaginable.
'Congratulations, Shirone.'
Surrounded by students, Shirone wore a sorrowful expression despite the glorious victory.
Etella watched him solemnly, lost in thought.
The 6-point difference was minuscule, but in terms of mental speed, it was vast.
'Shirone… Don't tell me…'
In the final moments, Shirone's Spirit Zone had filled completely with light—impossible
for photon output alone. Except for one scenario.
'Did you transcend numbers?'
Numbers have no end. Even if Iruki spent 10,000 years on sequence formulas, he'd never
leave the starting point of their journey.
'Shirone, how long have you known?'
Sequence formulas expand numbers to strengthen the mind. But what happens when you
transcend numbers?
Infinite expansion.
Mages call this phenomenon the "Immortal Function," but as a monk, Etella knew
another name.
'Nirvana.'
A concept too dangerous to teach at the magic school. Infinity lies beyond the
finite—entering it erases one's existence.
Tears welled in Etella's eyes.
'Shirone, you mustn't. No matter how radiant, no matter how splendid, entering infinity
means…'
You disappear.
Monks attain Nirvana through asceticism, relinquishing all worldly attachments.
But Shirone wasn't one. An 18-year-old boy with dreams to fulfill—infinity was too
perilous for him.
'Passing out means it wasn't complete. But if he truly entered…'
Etella wiped her tears and approached Shirone. His sorrowful face said it all.
"Shirone, you…"
"I'm sorry, Teacher."
"How long?"
"A very long… journey."
Their exchange resembled a monk's koan. Only Shirone knew its true meaning.
Etella could offer no advice. Shirone had realized and stopped himself—the choice was his
alone.
"Don't worry, Teacher. I didn't expect it either."
All Etella could do was gaze at him tenderly and say:
"Shirone, I love you."
Students gasped.
"Whaaaat?"
A teacher confessing to a student? The situation was absurd, and the timing bizarre.
Shirone bowed deeply, moved by her words.
"T—Teacher! Did you just say you love him? With so many in the Teacher Fan Club, how
could you betray us…!"
Etella tilted her head.
"Fan club? What's that? I love all of you."
"Huh? That's it? Hahaha! I thought—I love you too, Teacher~!"
"Me too, Teeeacher!"
Boys competitively confessed their love. Etella patted them affectionately.
"Alright, let's wrap up. Gather around."
Students lined up before the image zone. Despite the grueling test, their faces were
refreshed.
"Good work. At least tests mean early dismissal, right? Rest well, and let's resume
training next time. Well done, everyone."
Etella bowed like a monk, and students followed half in jest, half in respect.
"Good work, Teacher!"
Etella left to report the results, while students lingered, savoring the match's
aftermath.
"Shirone! This is great! Amy will love it. Or maybe she'll be mad her record was
broken? Who cares! Her boyfriend won! Hohoho!"
Shirone endured Seriel's chatter with exhaustion. He'd do anything to make her stop.
Miraculously, she paused and looked around.
Iruki was approaching.
Students watched eagerly. How would the arrogant genius save face this time? But his
words were unexpectedly humble.
"I lost. A complete defeat."
Shirone shook his head.
"No. I don't see it that way."
"No need for modesty. Especially when a genius like me admits defeat."
"I really don't feel like I won. This was a targeting test. But what I did at the
end hit the entire space."
"Pfft. So old-school. That's still targeting. You just amplified photon output for
area clearance. You won—don't overthink it."
"By that logic, you could've done the same with explosions."
Iruki laughed, clutching his stomach.
"True. But large-scale explosions take time. Though I was desperate, so maybe I
could've shortened the reaction. Still, results would've been lackluster."
Shirone conceded. But Iruki's unusual demeanor left him speechless. If he could explain
so amiably, why had he picked fights?
"Did you do it on purpose? To provoke me?"
Iruki blinked, then brought up something unrelated.
"I study explosions. Using your words—explosions that could kill everyone in a
city."
Shirone frowned.
"But Shirone, you're mistaken. Explosions aren't violent. Violence requires intent
to harm. Explosions have no intent—just energy beyond human scale."
"Lack of intent doesn't prevent harm. People control that energy."
"Exactly. So I define explosions like this: uncontrollable energy. But Shirone,
energy is life force. What if humans could perfectly control explosions?"
Iruki spread his arms wide.
"A new era would dawn. Explosions powerful enough to level cities could feed 100,000
people. Control them, and wars over resources would end. Everyone could live abundantly."
Shirone's expression softened.
"You should've said that from the start. We wouldn't have needed to fight."
Iruki smiled bitterly.
"I told you. Ideals are just ideals. One mage can't change the world. Even if I
controlled explosions, someone would use them to kill thousands. But Shirone, I'll still do it. Someone has
to."
"That's not the way. I get it, but that choice will make you miserable too."
"Ha! Right! That's why I need you!"
Iruki sat down, gazing at the sky.
"Maybe I should quit here. Play around in Class Five, and when that gets boring,
delve into dungeons. Maybe someone like me shouldn't have been born."
Shirone looked down at him, conflicted. What was this boy? An idealist? A realist? Or
just an indefinable oddity?
"So from now on, you're responsible for me."
"Huh? Responsible?"
"You can stop my rampage. If someday my methods lead to tragedy…"
Iruki spoke with utmost sincerity.
"Stop me. If you deem me unworthy of existence, end my life with your own
hands."
Iruki's mindset was cold. But he saw clearly the light and shadow of his pursuits.
If someone had to do it, it should be Iruki, Shirone thought.
"Alright. I'll stop you. No—I'll make sure you never make such a terrible
mistake."
Nade, seemingly moved, trembled as he hugged both and led them down the mountain.
"Waaah! I really like you two! Let's do our best from now on!"
Shirone and Iruki, caught in Nade's embrace, exchanged smiles. The day Class Five's trio
was formed.
