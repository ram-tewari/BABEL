Chapter 617:
The 30 graduating students who arrived in Creas via the magic circle
from the capital were brimming with confidence.
"Did you see the looks on those Royal Magic School guys' faces?
They looked like they were chewing on something bitter. That's what they get for picking a fight and
losing."
"I didn't realize the difference in level until now. It's clear
we're on another level."
"It's thanks to all our hard work."
Competing with the best students naturally pushed their potential beyond
its limits.
"At least we won something before the graduation exam. It gives me
a boost of confidence."
The skills they had built up through hard work wouldn't disappear, even
in fierce competition.
As each student expressed their confidence, Collie's expression grew
darker. By the time they arrived at Alpheas Magic School, it was already past midnight, and the
graduation exam was the next day.
"Good night. See you in the morning."
As the students dispersed after roll call, Collie spoke up.
"Could you spare a moment?"
The students turned back, puzzled. In all their years under Collie, this
was the first time he had made such a request.
As the students gathered around, Collie gestured for them to sit
comfortably.
"I'm sorry to bring this up at such a sensitive time, but there's
something I need to say."
"There's no need to apologize, sir. You've worked so hard for us
all year."
For the first time, a gentle smile appeared on Collie's usually stern
face.
"You've all worked your way up from the lower classes to get
here."
The students focused intently.
"That means none of you are here just to become mages on a whim or
because you dislike competition."
The graduating class wasn't a place for complacency.
"So, I want to ask you now."
Collie hesitated before speaking.
"Why not give up on becoming mages?"
In the silence, the students had many thoughts. They were human, after
all, capable of fear and pain.
"Watching you shine today, I wondered what we're doing. The most
important thing in the world is happiness. Are you happy? Fighting, competing, envying, and being
jealous every day—do you really want to become mages that badly?"
For the first time in his teaching career, he revealed his true
feelings.
"Competition is a pyramid built with human blood and flesh. To
build that pyramid, you need the bodies of the losers. Only one person can reach the top by stepping on
others."
Out of 30, only 10 would pass.
"Even if you pass, it's not the end. The higher you go, the more
losers you'll need. So I'm asking you to find happiness instead of just trying to win."
No one spoke, but the students' eyes burned with determination.
'Of course, there's no such thing as giving up in your
dictionary.'
If they needed sympathy, they wouldn't have made it this far.
'I guess it's time for me to retire.'
As the head teacher managing the iron rules of the graduating class,
expressing such weakness was a sign of aging.
"If you still won't give up…"
Having watched their journey for a year, he could say this.
"Fight and win! Without blood, tears, or sympathy! Crush your
friends and climb to the top!"
If becoming a mage was their only happiness.
"Become mages, no matter what!"
"Yes!"
Their voices echoed into the night sky.
The next morning.
For the first time in a year, they had a day with nothing to do.
Some students slept in to recover, while others woke up earlier than
usual.
"Yawn."
Of the 30 students, Nade was the only one who could stretch
leisurely.
"I'm hungry. Do we get breakfast today?"
As the day approached, he felt that throwing away the mage license would
be the most liberating thing.
"Hmm?"
While washing his face, he noticed a letter slipped under the
door.
"What's this?"
With a towel over his shoulder, Nade's face twisted as he saw the
sender.
The West family.
He threw the letter away as if it were something vile but eventually
picked it up and opened it.
What had he expected?
Even though the situation he had hoped for had come true, anger surged
within him.
"That wicked woman!"
Nade tore the letter to pieces.
"How dare she show her face here now!"
His mother had never cared about his talent, only talking about
money.
'And now she wants to attend the graduation exam?'
She wanted to take everything he had built through pain and
despair.
'Fine. I'll crush her hopes. I'll fail the exam right away. No, I'll
die! Right in front of her!'
"Nade, are you in there?"
Hastily composing himself, Nade shoved the letter pieces under the bed
and opened the door.
"Yeah. Come in."
Iruki was with Shirone.
"How do you feel? The exam is tomorrow."
"Of course, I'm thrilled. What's up? I was about to head out. Don't
we get breakfast today?"
"We do. But before that, we wanted you to try this."
Shirone pulled out a piece of paper.
"Iruki and I made a bet. We're guessing the top 10 ranks for the
graduation exam. The loser buys dinner."
The paper listed their predicted ranks, with each of their names at the
top.
"Ah, so the loser buys dinner."
"Join us. It might help you focus."
"Hmm, the top 10, huh."
Nade realized that if he didn't include his name, Shirone and Iruki
would notice.
"I'll pass. It might just add pressure."
That was why Shirone had suggested it.
"Nade, you don't mean…"
"Haha! Don't worry. I won't be the only one left behind. I need to
use the bathroom. Go ahead to the dining hall. My stomach hurts."
Shirone sighed and turned away.
"Alright. We'll talk later."
As his friends left, Nade called out to Iruki just before the door
closed.
"Iruki."
"Yeah?"
"Make sure Shirone doesn't get hurt…"
"No. You do it. I don't know what you're thinking, but if you
disappoint Shirone, I won't see you again."
"Iruki…"
"I don't care if you fail. One less competitor isn't bad. But do
you know? If you beat Shirone, you'll be the top in the kingdom. You'll gain wealth and fame. Why are
you doing this?"
He could gain wealth and fame.
Even his gambling father and indifferent mother would acknowledge
him.
"Think rationally. What's best for your life? If you're consumed by
hatred, you can't do anything."
Nade laughed bitterly.
"Haha! What a joke. How could I beat Shirone? He's so
strong."
"Are you serious?"
Iruki's stern look froze Nade's smile.
"Do you really think that? That you can't win even if you try your
best?"
"Iruki, I just want to have fun with Shirone…"
"Answer me clearly. If you betray us later, I won't let it
slide."
Nade hung his head.
"See? You think you're stronger. Then fight. Shirone and I have
worked hard. If you give up, you're disrespecting our efforts."
"Iruki, what are you doing? Let's go."
Shirone's voice called from the hallway.
"I'll answer clearly."
Nade lifted his head, determined.
"Shirone is stronger than me. I'll never fight him."
"…See you at the dining hall."
Iruki closed the door.
After breakfast, Shirone parted from his friends and strolled through
the garden.
A year ago, on this day, he had passed through the iron gates to meet
Amy and Seriel.
Now, as the protagonist, he finally understood how nervous they must
have been.
'It feels like I have a lump of iron in my chest.'
Amy, facing her second exam, must be fighting even harder against the
tension.
'Repeaters have the lowest pass rate.'
Given the comprehensive nature of the exam, even the dining hall
situation could be analyzed.
Out of 30, only two skipped breakfast: Richard, the alchemist, and
Amy.
'Richard can substitute food with something else, but…'
Amy not coming to the dining hall likely meant her tension was at its
peak.
'Should I go see her?'
Shirone shook his head.
With the need to maximize focus, worrying about a friend was out of the
question.
"But…"
Amy wasn't just a friend.
Amy tried not to give special meaning to skipping breakfast.
She had achieved much higher growth than last year through hard work, so
there was no reason to be nervous.
'Focus. Let's focus.'
After washing up, she sat on her bed, closed her eyes, and
concentrated.
'I need to feel the magnetic memory more precisely.'
The competition rate for the graduating class was the highest in the
school's history, and even a minor mistake could mean another wasted year.
"Ugh!"
Covering her mouth, Amy ran to the bathroom and vomited into the
toilet.
"Ugh! Ugh!"
Even though nothing came out, her stomach churned, and the anxiety she
had been suppressing exploded.
Failure. Failure.
Only negative thoughts filled her mind.
'Get a grip, you idiot! If you panic, it's over!'
"Amy, are you in there?"
Hearing Shirone's voice, Amy turned but couldn't bring herself to open
the door.
"Yes. What's up?"
"You didn't eat breakfast, so I was worried."
"Sigh…"
She should have gone to the dining hall.
When she opened the door, Shirone saw her pale face and looked
shocked.
"You're not well."
"Haha! It's nothing. I just threw up."
"…"
Realizing her mistake, Amy looked away.
"My stomach's upset. It's nothing serious, so don't
worry."
As Shirone stepped over the threshold, Amy sat on the bed, looking
defeated.
"Honestly, I'm a mess. I don't know why I'm like this."
"I understand. I'd be nervous too."
Only then did Amy remember that Shirone was also facing the exam.
"Sorry for making you worry."
"What are you talking about? We're not just friends…"
He didn't finish the sentence, but they both knew the truth.
Otherwise, he wouldn't have come here before the most important exam of
his life.
'Come to think of it, he's been so patient with me.'
She recalled how she had ignored Shirone's feelings last winter at the
Snow Crystal Inn.
"Can I sit?"
Though the future was uncertain, this question clearly suggested another
possibility.
Thinking this made her heart race.
"Yes."
A shy voice escaped Amy's lips.
