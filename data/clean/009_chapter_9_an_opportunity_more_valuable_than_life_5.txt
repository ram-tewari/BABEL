Chapter 9:
An Opportunity More Valuable Than Life (5)
"Aaaagh! Aaaagh!"
Dust swirled in the great training yard. It was the dirt kicked up by Rian's legs. His
heart felt like it would burst. Air wouldn't come in, and the contents of his stomach were rising up.
"Ugh! Ugh!"
What he had eaten for lunch came up through his esophagus. Rian just let himself vomit.
The only thing he cared about was his two legs.
"A hundred laps! Passed!"
Sword instructor Kite shouted heartily. He couldn't help but admire how Rian seemed
different today.
"You've broken your record! But what's gotten into you? Completing your training
without complaint?"
"Damn it! You call this training?!"
"What did you say?"
Kite raised his eyes in astonishment. He was about to give some rare praise, but as they
say, a leopard can't change its spots—Rian was complaining without hesitation.
But today's complaint was different from others.
"I'm still standing, aren't I? Master, don't you have anything tougher?"
"Hooh?"
Kite was quite surprised. It had been a long time since Rian had burned with such
passion. It seemed like the first time since he had knelt before Ozent Rai's genius two years ago.
"You... something must have happened?"
Rian, with his hands on his knees, raised his head while dripping with sweat.
"Nothing happened."
Kite didn't believe him. In his disciple's eyes, which stared fiercely ahead, there must
be the shadow of someone lingering.
'Is it Rai again?'
No matter how he thought about it, the only person who could make Rian like this was the
second son of the family. Had he accomplished something again? But what? Hadn't Rai already mastered his
schema?
It didn't matter anyway. His disciple was Rian, not Rai. And right now, that disciple was
offering his neck, asking to be trained to the limit.
"Alright! Let's properly kill you today! I can't take responsibility if you actually
die!"
"As much as you want!"
Rian repeatedly brought down an iron rod twice as heavy as a longsword. If one couldn't
realize the schema through insight, the only way was to force the body to its extreme limit.
"Ha! Ha! Ha!"
Kite watched with pleasure as Rian repeated his vertical slashes. But as time passed, his
expression hardened. Finally realizing something, he opened his eyes wide and ran over.
"You crazy fool! Stop! Your muscles will burst!"
"They haven't burst yet!"
Kite stopped in his tracks and looked at his disciple. Tears were streaming down from
Rian's eyes as he gritted his teeth and swung the rod.
"Damn it! Why won't my muscles burst? Why won't my arms break? I can do more! This
isn't the end! I'm not finished yet!"
Tears welled up in Kite's eyes too. He was as frustrated as his disciple. Why doesn't it
work? Even pushing the body to the limits of limits, why can't he open the schema?
Kite wiped his tears with his thick arm. This wouldn't do. If the master showed doubt,
what would his disciple Rian do?
Kite received Rian's iron rod with his palm as it cut through the air. Even Rian couldn't
insist on continuing when he hit his master's hand and stopped his movement.
"Rian, that's enough. Let's stop for today."
At the warm voice, Rian's madness disappeared. The feeling in his arms had long since
vanished. Without even realizing the club had fallen to the ground, Rian bowed his head deeply and said:
"Yes, thank you."
***
Kite placed an ice towel on Rian's shoulder. The fact that his bones weren't damaged
showed he was born with a strong physique.
Rian sat on the training ground hill, looking at the distant mountains. Kite, having
finished treating him, sat down beside him.
"What are you thinking about?"
"That my arms are killing me."
A smile crossed Kite's lips.
"Rian. In the reports I submit to the head of the family, I've never once written
that you have talent."
"Tch, who asked?"
"But I think you do have talent. Achieving something quickly isn't the only form of
talent. Challenging something persistently despite seeming impossible—that too can be called talent."
"You don't need to comfort me. That's just a nice way of saying I have no talent,
which is why I have to work to death."
"Is that really so? Geniuses know they are geniuses. Therefore, they clearly
understand what they can do. Have you ever thought about that?"
"Well... I know well what I can't do."
"Yes. That's why you're not a genius."
Rian looked at Kite with an incredulous expression. Instead of helping a burning house,
his master was pouring oil on it, making not just his arms ache but his heart as well.
"Stop it. I understand enough already."
As Rian pouted, Kite smiled.
"But, Rian, do you know what geniuses fear most? It's effort. Making an effort
ultimately means that you don't have something to begin with. You say you put in effort because you lack
talent? Not at all. In this world, the only people who can make an effort are those who make an
effort."
Kite grabbed Rian's shoulder and gently pushed strength into it.
"Geniuses fear you. Because you have the power to seize what you don't have. Those
who make an effort are the natural enemies of geniuses."
The natural enemy of geniuses.
Rian liked this phrase. Even if it was just to comfort a poor disciple, it didn't matter.
Yes, he simply didn't have it. If he never had it to begin with, he just needed to obtain it with
persistence.
Rian clenched his fist. He intended to pour everything into the remaining month.
***
Shirone clearly knew what he was capable of.
Even abandoning any attachment to schema and seeking other methods showed his character
was the complete opposite of Rian's.
Nevertheless, talent finds the most efficient and shortest path to the destination.
Shirone set aside various techniques and delved only into the basics of swordsmanship.
A month passed quickly.
Eight-directional striking and eight-directional blocking. Having mastered these two
skills satisfactorily, Shirone ended his training.
And finally, he asked a question that encapsulated what he had learned.
"What is swordsmanship?"
The totality of the movement dynamics that arise with the purpose of killing life. It was
also a sophisticated psychology in the sense that it separates life and death.
Shirone imagined a virtual Rian and pointed his sword. The enemy would likely move from
every imaginable angle.
But that was just an illusion. Only the sword coming to cut him was real.
Shirone responded to his imaginary opponent with imagination. His sword extended like a
phantom, suppressing all eight directions. After one more exchange, it split into dozens of streams.
'It's not about counting numbers. It's about feeling the whole.'
If you focus on the trees, you can't see the forest. Savoring possibilities as a
whole—that was what insight was.
But it wasn't as easy as it sounded. The more diverse patterns spread out like fractals,
the more he found himself fixating on attacks from specific areas.
'It's okay if I can't achieve it. Let me observe.'
Gradually gaining momentum, Shirone completely let go of his thoughts at some point. All
possibilities began to be sucked into the infinite blank space.
"Uh? Uhhhhh?"
Shirone looked at the world with astonished eyes. There was no end. The entire world was
being filled with his and Rian's swords.
Unable to handle such immensity, Shirone dropped his sword and staggered.
"Haha, hahaha."
A hollow laugh. Like magic, swordsmanship was also infinite. He had experienced the
infinite world derived from a single sword.
'So this is the path swordsmen have chosen.'
Wasn't he also walking the infinite path of magic? An inexplicable sense of kinship made
him feel good.
'Mom, Dad, I'm sorry. I might not be able to come home.'
He had no regrets. Rian would be the same. Facing the crossroads of life and death, he
was thankful to have seen such a wonderful aspect of the world.
Outside the library, the sound of bells approaching midnight could be heard.
Rian, trying to possess what he couldn't have. Shirone, who had completed everything he
could do. The clash between the two boys, mirroring each other, was now just an hour away.
