Shirone began his detailed explanation.
"At an altitude of 6,000 kilometers, I'll cast dark sphere using

Sein's Iron Wheel Eyes spun rapidly.
Even with just this information, he understood what Shirone intended to
do.
Complex but precise calculations began to run through his mind.
Shirone spread his arms wide.
"It will probably take the form of a spear. The shape isn't

"Yes. That's it."
That was all.
And everyone was speechless.
The mechanism was much simpler than expected.
But surprisingly, it contained an idea powerful enough to destroy
Heaven.
"But is it really possible?" Plu asked.
Gaold looked at Sein. It was now up to his savant's brain to
judge.
"It's possible," Sein affirmed.
"Simply put, a mass of 10 tons will fall from an altitude of 6,000

exceed 14 kilometers per second."
Plu's eyes widened.
"14 kilometers per second?"
At that speed, it would be impossible to respond once it entered the
visible range.
And the result would be a spear of 10 tons impacting at 41 Mach,
quantified as an immense force.
"Exactly. The kinetic energy alone, converted to explosive power,
would be about 2.19 million kilobusters. That's beyond Heaven's durability. If it hits the central
Aravot, all seven heavens will be devastated."
If Sein said it, there was no error in the calculations.
Yet no one could fully grasp the numbers.
2.19 million kilobusters?
The idea that a mere student could generate such power was beyond
comprehension.
"Hehe, what do you think? Didn't I say it was a brilliantly insane

"What I liked most when I first heard this was that there were no
vague assumptions. It clearly distinguishes between what can and can't be done. I thought it was just a
graduation student's nonsense, but it wasn't. Shirone's spell doesn't destroy Heaven."
Sein added, "Gravity does. The planet's gravity pulls the spear

"Exactly. Those who worshipped the gods will be destroyed by the
gods' hands. It's called 'Divine Punishment.'"
Shirone looked at Gaold.
"I was going to call it Shining Spear."
"The name doesn't matter. The issue is the trajectory, impact
point, and timing."
Sein said, "I can calculate that. Once we reach Heaven, I'll create
a chart. It will take about three days to understand the planet's motion, and then we can set the launch
time."
Everyone began discussing new strategies and tactics to align with the
new variable.
Divine Punishment had the power to completely revise the project's
strategy.
While everyone engaged in heated discussions, Siena looked at Shirone
with pride.
Gaold liked that Divine Punishment utilized natural properties, but
Siena thought differently.
Everyone knew that dropping 10 tons from 6,000 kilometers would be
immensely destructive.
But no one attempted it because lifting 10 tons required potential
energy equal to the kinetic energy.
Thus, Divine Punishment was not just gravity's doing but Shirone's
magic.
Amplifying the laser with Ataraxia to transmit information to the
altitude, then activating divine particles to gather mass.
The result seemed simple, but it was an application that required
perfect understanding and analysis of what Shirone had.
'You squeezed out every last drop.'
The best result, compressed like a diamond, from ideas meticulously
refined to leave no gaps.
Siena could genuinely celebrate her student's significant leap.
"But there's a condition," Shirone said, interrupting the
heated discussion.
"I received Ataraxia from the archangel Ikael. So, I must meet
Ikael before casting this spell. And that was part of the contract."
No team member would be foolish enough to break a contract.
But it was natural to momentarily overlook it after gaining such a
powerful weapon as Divine Punishment.
Even the cold-hearted Sein felt the same.
For Divine Punishment to activate, Shirone had to meet Ikael.
Depending on the situation, it could be very easy or impossible.
Regardless of the difficulty, Sein disliked the uncertainty.
"We must honor the contract. You deserve our help. But is that the
only option? If you give up on Ikael, we can offer something equivalent. For example, prioritizing your
survival even if we all perish."
If uncertainty could be removed, that would be better.
To Sein, Divine Punishment's importance to the project was that
significant.
"No. I won't meet Ikael if it endangers everyone. But if it's not
dangerous, Divine Punishment isn't necessary. If it becomes dangerous, then I'll have enough motivation
to meet Ikael. In such a limited situation, there's no need to bind Ikael and Divine Punishment with
other constraints."
"Hmm, I see."
If they were in a situation to destroy Heaven, they were already
cornered.
If they had to grasp even a small chance, meeting Ikael was an
option.
"Fine. I'll be satisfied with the team's safety being independent
of meeting Ikael."
It was the best compromise Sein and Shirone could reach.
