Chapter 1213:
The Veron Problem (1)
King Basak of the Kingdom of Iron returned to his chambers after the
banquet.
"Phew, I'm exhausted."
Having indulged in endless days of pleasure and decadence, his stamina
was nearly depleted.
"I'm getting old. I used to be fine even after ten rounds, but now it
feels like even my soul is being drained…"
"Don't worry. A man's tool never truly dies until he does."
When a woman's voice echoed through the chamber, tightly guarded by the
royal guards, Basak shot up in alarm.
"Wh-Who…?"
He let out a sigh of relief when he recognized the pale, serpent-like
face of the woman.
"It's you."
It was Shura, ranked seventh among the Council of Ten.
Even after the death of the Buddha, she continued to pursue the secrets
of the world.
Her reason for staying in Iron was simple: the iron wedge of the Council
of Ten's leader, Veron, was embedded there.
The Veron Problem. —Can humanity truly change the future?
It was one of the most burning questions at the terminal point of
history known as Omega Year 999.
Basak collapsed back onto his bed.
"You startled me. What brings you here so suddenly? You haven't shown
your face in ages."
"With nerves like that, how do you manage to attend banquets? You're
such a strange man."
"Ha! If I didn't like meeting women, I'd have no reason to live. More
than that, this whole Holy War mess has things feeling grim."
People were dying at random every hour.
"So, where've you been?"
If Shura had been involved when all the nations were chasing after
Uorin, things might've turned out very differently.
"…I was visiting a friend."
Friend, huh.
Though Basak wore a vacant, absentminded look, his brain was spinning
rapidly.
What scheme is she brewing this time? He had heard the Council of
Ten had disbanded, but there wasn't much intel on their current status.
"Mind your own business."
When Basak came to, Shura was already staring at him with cold
eyes.
"Did I say anything? Of course I trust you."
"Heh… the god of lies, huh?"
"I'm a believer in lies. Because only when buried in lies can truth
truly shine."
Shura leaned against Basak's body.
"Then what truth is hidden in my lie?"
"That you could kill me." "But the fact that you haven't means you
still need me. Which means… we can still be good friends. That's the truth I've found."
"Excellent deduction."
"Ha! On that note, mind if I ask a favor? You've always made my dreams
come true, after all."
Shura was powerful.
But expanding the scope to the world stage, there were still those who
could potentially defeat her.
Even so, Basak trusted her—not just for her strength, but because her
strategic mind was second to none.
And… she was the direct disciple of the Buddha.
Shura asked,
"Let's hear it. What do you want?"
"The world."
His body, unresponsive to her touch until now, was suddenly aflame with
desire.
"Gis is a skilled politician, I'll give him that. But let's not forget—I
laid out the board for this game."
Basak turned his head, eyes gleaming.
"The leader of the World Government. You could bring that position to
me, couldn't you?"
Shura's eyes narrowed.
Truly insatiable.
She obviously knew that Jive was the most likely candidate for the
world's leadership.
If Jive wins, Iron will receive a favorable position as well. But he's
still not satisfied. He wants even more.
Basak continued.
"Give me the world. And in return, I'll give you what you truly
desire."
Truth that shines within lies.
"…I'll do my best."
As if time had turned back, she stepped away from the bed and headed for
the door.
It's not an easy task. But…
It wasn't completely impossible either.
In the royal quarters of the Tormia Sector, Shirone watched over the
groaning Pony.
'She's regained consciousness. Is it a sign of recovery, or…'
A deathbed rally.
When the doctor finished his examination and left the room, Shirone
placed his hand on Pony's forehead.
Blinking slowly, Pony recognized him and smiled like she had back in
their school days.
"Shirone."
"Are you okay? How do you feel?"
"I'm not sure. How do I look?"
Pony was dying.
"Just hang in there a bit longer. I'll find a way. I'll definitely save
you. Until then…"
Suddenly, Pony's eyes flew open wide and she coughed up blood.
The poison of emotion.
The deep resentment lodged in her heart was constantly reversing her
metabolism.
Shirone activated Miracle Stream.
'Damn it.'
Who could possibly soothe her?
A woman who had been surrounded by brutal men and forced to die in
horrific agony—how could her emotions be consoled?
'Even that must be unbearable. No comforting words could stop the
hemorrhaging.'
Her hatred, amplified to the extreme through the infernal system, had
become one of the most powerful forms of malice.
'The only way…'
He needed to reset the system of the Under World to purge the demonic
curse from Pony.
"Just hold on a little longer."
Pony gave a bitter smile.
"I don't deserve to."
"Since when did life require qualifications?"
"All of this happened because of my greed. If I hadn't tried to become
queen… none of this would've happened."
"You were just trying to survive. When the royal family was being
purged, that was your only option."
Pony shook her head.
"I hated my lineage back in school. Others might say I was privileged,
but it wasn't like that. A royal who couldn't become queen—like a mage who couldn't use magic."
"Why did I hate royalty? I gave so many reasons, but I think I finally
get it. The truth is… I wanted it more than anyone else. That title of queen."
"There's nothing wrong with that."
"Was I dreaming of some fairy-tale monarch? I thought I'd be different,
thought I could change things…"
Pony sobbed uncontrollably.
"I really wanted to do it right, Shirone! But I couldn't! And that's
what's so unfair!"
Watching her weep while coughing blood, Shirone bit his lip.
Contradiction.
Everyone accepted it as natural, but the more he thought about it, the
more terrifying the truth became.
'This world is wrong.'
A world where people can't even achieve what they truly desire—is that
even acceptable?
'It didn't have to be this way. A world where everyone could be happy…
that was completely possible.'
Is God some kind of twisted pervert?
'No…'
Perhaps God did create a perfect world, and only humanity was under a
massive illusion.
'Which means… the heart is the error.'
Truth lies in emptiness.
And yet Zet, knowing that truth, still wanted to pass his
"error" on to the future.
'The Tachyon Formula. If that's the key to connecting with the Outer
World… then even if it's just me…'
He could fight God.
As he steeled his resolve, the door opened and Albino, Lupist, and Dante
walked in.
"You're here."
Albino approached with a smile.
"I heard you called for us. You know the situation in Tormia. Since
we've made the time, I hope that—"
"I'll do it."
Albino fell silent.
"I want to help make Tormia the World Government. What do I need to
do?"
'Hmm… this is…'
Albino stroked his beard.
'More than I expected.'
Though his competitive spirit flared again, he said nothing and turned
to look at Pony.
She had lost consciousness again, perhaps due to the earlier
excitement.
That might be for the best.
"Have a seat, then."
After moving to the table, Lupist asked,
"When you say you'll help Tormia—what exactly do you mean? Does that
include war?"
"I won't go against Yahweh's will. But if war becomes necessary, I won't
shy away from it."
For now, that would do.
"I see. But what made you change your mind?"
"We need a focal point. I want to unify humanity with Tormia at the
center. More than anything… I can't allow someone like Gis to become the leader of the world."
It seemed the recent protests had made Shirone realize many
things.
Albino spoke up.
"Excavating Ymir's Ultima is unlikely, so political strength is
essential. But you already know, don't you? Even if we unite all of humanity, it's not true
integration."
"Ultima doesn't matter anymore."
The harsh tone made everyone blink.
"Of course, I haven't given up on unification. But it's time we looked
for a backup plan."
"Have you found it?"
He didn't mention the sphere surrounding the sun.
"I believe I've said enough. Now it's your turn to choose."
Albino spoke.
"Hmm. Currently, Tormia ranks 6th. The probability of securing enough
votes to become the World Government is 16.4%."
Then he turned to Dante.
"I'm curious. If Shirone gives us his full support, what would the
probability become?"
Dante finished his calculations and answered.
"…Lower."
Albino wasn't surprised.
"So it drops even more. I suppose siding with Yahweh is like holding a
live bomb."
"The initial variable was the scandal linking Yahweh's name to Arachne.
If it had blown over, it might not matter. But Gis fanned the flames, and it's spiraled."
Dante always considered every detail.
"Originally, Yahweh was just a symbolic image. But once that name enters
the political arena, the enemies pile on. Since the protestors are already split, half the citizens of
Jive probably despise Yahweh."
"But in the Holy War, the citizens' rights are limited to the vote
delegated to Gis. The voting sentiment of other nations shouldn't matter, right?"
"Of course. But…"
Dante's face grew serious.
"The real issue is the Pope's growing influence. If Yahweh is criticized
in the host nation, the world's largest religion—Lamism—will spread that message globally. And even if
it's just a formality, the World Government still needs the Pope's endorsement. Shirone will definitely
generate new variables, but politics plays by different rules. It'll be hard to win votes."
In fact, the odds were getting worse.
"I see."
Albino nodded.
"I agree with our intelligence chief. It feels awkward saying this in
front of the man himself, but… what do we do now? Do we reject Shirone's offer?"
"Of course not."
Dante said firmly.
"We must accept. We need to stake everything on Shirone."
Albino smirked.
"Why?"
"Because it feels good."
As silence fell across the room, he continued.
"Just because you have an ace card doesn't mean you win the game. You
still need a good hand and a solid play. But once you have that ace…"
"You feel good."
"Yes. Even if the rest of your cards don't look great, just knowing your
opponent might not have made their hand yet gives you the edge."
"Until the ballot box opens, nobody knows."
Dante nodded.
"Looking at the current state of things, the ace might be a bad card.
But if we can't close the gap with probability alone, then Tormia has to take the risk."
Lupist blinked, resting his chin on his hand.
"Sorry. A so-called intelligence officer talking about gut feeling…
That's just my opinion."
"No."
Albino smiled.
"You're the best intel officer we've got. You just shifted the 16.4%.
We're going with it. Let's give it a shot."
Now that a conclusion had been reached, Shirone asked,
"Then what should I do?"
"Unanimous vote. To suppress Lamism's global opinion, all 11 nations
have to vote for Tormia."
Lupist added,
"Use anything you've got—threats, bluffing, whatever it takes. Get those
votes from the kings. All of them."
