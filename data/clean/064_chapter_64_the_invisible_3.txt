Chapter 64:
The Invisible (3)
Obsession bordering on madness—and euphoria.
Shirone shuddered. Everyone had been working tirelessly for days. Calculating, their
average sleep was barely 1 hour and 40 minutes per day.
Even during his most intense study sessions, Shirone had never worked this hard. Yet,
oddly, he didn't feel as tired as before—as if the pain had numbed him.
'They slacked off so much before, but when it's time to work, they go all out.'
Their dedication, to the point of begrudging sleep, ignited a newfound drive in Shirone.
"Neid, tired now?"
"No, not at all. Wait, am I? Actually, I'm not sure. Feels like my brain's wide open."
"Same. Let's work on the script while Iruki's busy. We need slogans for posters too.
These small things take time."
"Hmm. Alright. Let's put up posters tomorrow."
"You sure? We can't even predict the workload yet."
"We'll have to risk it. Announcing early is better. We don't want to seem desperate. It's
psychological warfare."
"Right. I'll draft the poster text. You handle the design."
They spread paper on the table and began designing. Late as it was, their minds were
still sharp.

The next day.
By lunchtime, posters were up on the bulletin board—an announcement for the Supernatural
Parapsychology Research Club's presentation. Most students were baffled. The club, operating in obscurity,
was virtually unknown.
The posters were laughably absurd, distorting the school's scenery into something
eerie—like a circus performance.
