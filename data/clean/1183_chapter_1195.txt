"Wash up first. If we go like this, no restaurant will even let us

"I have an idea."

Penna's eyes widened.
"How did you do that?"
"It's just an ability I have. Since Corkolas doesn't use points,
things like this are possible."
His explanation was brief, but Penna didn't seem to care.
"I'll be quick!"
As soon as she entered the bath, they could hear the sound of her
gulping down water.
Shirone's face turned pale.
Iruki noticed and asked,
"What's wrong? This kind of thing shouldn't be hard for
you."
"Yeah, it's not… but…"
The aftermath of constructing Shirone Sphere near the sun was affecting
him.
Simultaneous events didn't mean splitting his presence—every version of
Shirone existed at 100 percent everywhere.
'But Armand is not me. I cover it with mental power, but there's still a
limit.'
As the silence stretched, Iruki understood.
'Something serious is happening.'
He didn't pry further. Knowing wouldn't help—there was nothing they
could do.
'We're experiencing Melkidu, but for Shirone, this is just one of many
incidents.'
Even now, he was controlling everything elsewhere.
'Stay strong, Shirone.'
Iruki patted his back wordlessly, and Shirone felt a tightness in his
chest.
'Thank you.'
They wouldn't know.
Of all the countless simultaneous events, this was the only place where
he could actually smile.
Not because his friends were strong.
Not because Melkidu was easier than the other places.
'Even if I fail, it's okay.'
To his friends, he wasn't the Infinite Mage, one of the Five Stars of
the Ivory Tower, or Yahweh, the guardian of humanity's fate.
'To them… I'm just Shirone.'
Just a fellow graduate from Alpheas Magic Academy.
'Even if I act sensitive sometimes, even if I stay silent… they don't
see me any differently.'
Because they understood why.
Feeling at ease for the first time in a while, Shirone looked up as
Penna stepped out of the bath, shaking water from her hair.
"Finally, I feel alive again. Let's go, I'm starving."
Shirone smiled.
"Yeah."
They headed to a nearby restaurant.
As Penna stuffed food into her mouth, she suddenly choked and gulped
down water before speaking.
"What happened to Curtis?"
"Ah, that guy. It worked out. Apparently, he was kind of a big deal
before. His crimes were so heavy that they didn't send him anywhere else—they locked him up in the
capital, Parme. He's in the highest-security prison."
That didn't sound like a good outcome.
"At least I avoided extra trouble. But when I realized I had no way
to contact you guys, I almost lost it. I could've just stayed in the capital, but I had no way to reach
you. So, I had no choice but to use "Loss of Will" to come here."
Shirone reassured her.
"You didn't waste anything. We had to come here anyway. I think

"Really? Hmm. Yeah, that makes sense. If an underworld organization

insane."
"What did you see?"
"Dismemberment. They were cutting up bodies like they were pork. No
hesitation at all. But thanks to that, I picked up some intel about the Three Great Gangs."
Penna's information was similar to what they had heard before but was
more up to date.
"The ones who took control of the supply shipments this time are
Johan Cartel. That's why Desperado is furious. There's going to be a huge war."
Shirone stood up.
"Alright, then we'll head to Johan Cartel. The ones controlling the
supplies must be in contact with the underworld organization."
"Ugh, Johan Cartel…"
Penna seemed like she wanted to say something but shook her head.
"Fine! Let's go! There's no such thing as a sane gang among the

Nade returned from paying the bill, looking annoyed.
"Dammit. Just for this meal, we had to spend a Bonanza item. Let's
just go outside and beat the info out of someone."
As they stepped outside, they saw that the entrance was blocked by gang
members from the Three Great Gangs.
A man aimed a machine gun at them.
"It's about time you made a choice. We're not patient
people."
Nade scanned their faces, then turned to his friends with a grin.
"This just got a lot easier."
As the others smirked, only Penna was trembling.
Johan Cartel.
Their headquarters was a monastery, filled with a solemn atmosphere—a
stark contrast to the sound of gunfire.
"Is he a new recruit?"
In a dimly lit room, a woman in a nun's habit was sipping from a liquor
glass.
She was Aria, the boss of Johan Cartel.
"Yes, ma'am. He's fresh—hasn't been through any other
gangs."
In front of several high-ranking gang members, a man in his mid-twenties
bowed his head.
"Greetings! My name is Boto! I will serve you with all my
devotion!"
Aria tilted her glass and asked,
"How exactly will you serve me?"
"… Excuse me?"
"How will you serve me?"
"Ah, well…"
Unable to handle Aria's sultry gaze, Boto shifted his eyes toward a
shelf.
It was filled with men's undergarments with openings, whips, and dog
collars connected by chains.
Aria snapped her fingers, bringing Boto's gaze back to her.
"Listen carefully. Johan Cartel is the most historically
significant organization in Melkidu. We are the only ones who directly inherit the legacy of Cain, the
founder of Melkidu. Since ancient times, we have controlled this place, ensuring that our brothers in
the real world remain strong."
"Yes."
"But then, at some point, the likes of Red Union and Desperado,
these nobodies with no lineage, started crawling in and acting like they owned the place. I don't like
it. Do you get what I mean?"
"Don't worry. I was a big shot outside. My hands are itching for
some action."
At that moment, there was a knock at the door.
"Boss, we brought them."
"Let them in."
As Aria looked over Shirone's group, her expression twisted in
disgust.
"… What the hell is this?"
They weren't just some gangsters. They were something entirely
different.
"So, I hear you've been snooping around? What are you? Cops? Or are
you looking for someone?"
Shirone spoke.
"Tell us about the underworld organization. We want to enter the
Core through the back door."
"Ah…"
Aria's gaze turned hazy.
"The back door. I like that."
"Heh."
Understanding the implication, Boto couldn't hold back his laughter,
making some of the gangsters turn toward him.
"Ahem, sorry."
Aria, who had been staring at him expressionlessly, placed her drink
down and stood up.
"Come here."
Boto hesitated before stepping closer. Aria leaned in, grabbing his
wrist.
"B-boss?"
"What's wrong? You like this kind of thing, don't you?"
She lifted the hem of her nun's robe and guided his hand between her

"Feel this. Yeah. Hold it tight."

"Wait… why am I—"

"Gah!"
He instinctively tried to push her away, but the blade had already sunk

"Aaagh! A knife! I—I've been stabbed!"
As he stumbled back, Aria grabbed a bottle of liquor and swung it full
force.
With a loud crack, the bottle shattered against his head, sending him
sprawling onto the ground.
"Aaaahhh! Aaaaagh!"
Blood gushed from his skull, and the knife in his stomach burned like
fire.
"M-my stomach! There's a knife in my—"
"Heh. Then pull it out, dumbass."
As the gangsters kicked him like a toy, Boto writhed and
screamed.
"Stop! No! I'm dying! Ahhh! Help me!"
Aria waved her hand dismissively.
"Shut up. Get him out of here."
Boto was dragged away by his legs, leaving a bloody smear across the

"Hah… Now then."
Settling back into her chair, Aria spoke.
"The back door is my specialty. Among the Three Great Gangs, no one

Melkidu's system was believed to have been created by Cain, humanity's

Of course, the truth was in the Core, but Johan Cartel had honored his

Both in the real world and in Melkidu.
