Chapter 107:
Back to Normal (2)
For Shirone, not much time had passed. After entering the Immortal Function, he met the
woman and escaped through the black hole. But in reality, ten days had already gone by.
Shirone smiled awkwardly and raised his hand.
"Haha. Hi?"
"Hi? You bastard!"
"Ow!"
Aimi punched Shirone's face. There was no pain, but the force knocked him onto the bed.
He grimaced and looked up.
"Ow! What was that for?"
Aimi shed chicken poop-like tears.
"You jerk, making us worry like this… You brat!"
Shirone looked dazed. Everything was sudden and confusing. But then, realizing something,
he smiled warmly. Aimi's tears told him what had happened in his absence.
"I'm sorry. Because of me…"
"Shirone's back!"
Neid and Iruki pushed Aimi aside and buried Shirone in the bed. Aimi, unintentionally
landing in Shirone's arms, turned red and shrieked.
"Eek! Hey, get off!"
"Ow! It hurts! It hurts!"
Shirone screamed in pain—more from embarrassment than actual pain. He couldn't even look
at Aimi later if this continued, so he twisted his body with all his might, sending his friends tumbling to
the floor.
"Phew, what was that?"
Sitting on the bed, Shirone looked like a war refugee. On the floor, Aimi rubbed her sore
back while scolding Neid and Iruki.
"Quite the welcome. Youth is wonderful."
Shirone's expression changed when he saw Alpheas. His presence alone showed how serious
the situation had been.
Shirone asked Neid the most pressing question.
"What happened to Arcane? What about the others? What's been going on?"
Neid looked at him incredulously.
"That's not the issue! You were dead for ten days!"
"What? Ten days? Dead?"
"Of course! Your heart wasn't beating—how could you be alive? Explain what happened."
Shirone fell silent, stunned. He needed to explain his experience but didn't know where
to start.
The infirmary door opened as teachers entered. Siena's eyes widened at the sight of
Shirone awake. Though she had prayed fervently for his return, seeing it firsthand was shocking.
She hadn't attained infinity like Shirone, but she vaguely understood how terrifying and
painful it was for a human mind to reassemble after dissolution.
'But you came back safely. Thank you.'
