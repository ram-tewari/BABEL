Chapter 463:
The Land of Fallen Giants, Muspelheim.
In a place where unquenchable flames stretched to the horizon, the fire
giants lived.
Among the giants born from the elixir of myths, those who harbored the
incarnation of hatred had nothing in their minds but burning hatred, with no clear purpose or
cause.
Kariel walked leisurely through the battlefield of brawling giants, who
were screaming and injuring each other.
They, too, had once been bound under Heaven's command structure, and
they knew that breathing fire at the beautiful archangel would result in instant annihilation.
Surtr, the leader of the fire giants, came to greet Kariel.
Standing at 7 meters tall and clad in armor of flames, his presence was
comparable to a deity, but even he knelt in the most reverent posture before the archangel.
"A humble being greets Lord Kariel."
The intense heat enveloped Kariel.
In Heaven, the biologically strongest being was undoubtedly Ymir, but
the fire giants were somewhat unique compared to other giants.
They wielded their own unique magic.
This was likely due to the unstructured freedom of thought inherent in
fire.
While Ymir's warrior could be compared to Ikael's three-horned Mara,
Ashur, the strongest swordsman in purgatory was undoubtedly Surt.
"I have a task for you."
Surt looked up in surprise.
The fire sword Muspel, which he carried, spewed massive flames and
dragged along the ground.
For an archangel to request help from a fire giant was a
once-in-a-lifetime opportunity for Surt, who dreamed of returning to Heaven.
"Entrust me with anything!"
Kariel nodded in satisfaction.
Anke Ra had entrusted all authority to Ikael, and she would end the
war.
If that happened, Kariel's purpose would be thwarted, and with his limbs
cut off, he had no way to oppose Ikael.
But they would come.
'Gaold.'
He would surely come, as long as Miro was alive.
'Though I hate to admit it…'
Miro was exceptional.
If humans had the most outstanding magician, the opportunity to change
Heaven's situation could come at any time.
Now was not the time to be picky about gathering forces.
"Prepare the elite troops and come to the Second Heaven, Lakia. I
will be waiting in the Hall of Corruption."
"It is the most fitting place for us."
The fire giants were banned from entering Heaven, but according to the
principles of the law, the city of fallen angels, Lakia, was an exception, which was why Kariel had fled
to the Second Heaven.
"There is no time. Depart immediately."
Having given his orders, Kariel flew straight to Heaven.
It was time to welcome the guests.
"You have nothing to tell me?"
Shirone couldn't believe Ikael's words.
No, perhaps it was true.
A vain delusion, or a selfish and utterly human expectation that it
would be so.
Such thoughts might have caused the misunderstanding.
But how could he explain the faint memory lingering in his mind, and the
feeling of his heart racing whenever he tried to touch that memory?
"Perhaps…"
Shirone couldn't bring himself to speak.
He couldn't bear to hear the same answer again, so he asked
indirectly.
"Who is Goffin?"
Thud.
Ikael's halo trembled again.
If the angels had seen this, they would have been shocked to the point
of losing their minds.
Ikael, realizing this, composed herself.
As Heaven's commander, she couldn't show any disarray while negotiating
a truce with the rebels.
"Goffin is the last of the Gaia. After losing the war with Heaven,
he left the photon realm. Of course, all information is now lost."
Shirone knew about Goffin's erasure.
But how did Ikael have memories of him?
There was only one possible reason.
Just as humans inferred Goffin's existence through his relics, she must
have personal memories of Goffin that had become objectified.
For humans who lived only about 100 years, this was impossible, but this
was Heaven, the place most deeply connected to Goffin.
"Were you close?"
Ikael shook her head.
Thinking about that time made her head ache.
"No. He was a stubborn human. He didn't know how to compromise. If
it weren't for him, the Gaia wouldn't have suffered so many sacrifices."
Her reaction was subtly different from when Shirone first met Ikael in
Heaven.
It was only natural.
According to Babel's records, Gaia and Heaven fought fiercely for each
other's annihilation.
"So you met as enemies."
"We clashed countless times and argued endlessly. He was strong.
Even as an archangel, I couldn't settle things with him."
Ikael's lips curled up unconsciously.
Even recalling the memories of fighting, her eyes shone with
happiness.
"Once, there was this incident…"
Ikael suddenly stopped speaking.
She couldn't remember anything.
It felt like a black hole had punched through the space filled with
colorful memories.
As she stared into that black space, all her emotions were sucked in,
and a crushing pain filled her chest.
Ikael widened her eyes and bit her lip.
'Not now. I can't be underestimated any longer.'
She amplified her voice and addressed everyone.
"I will change the negotiator. The person in charge, come
here."
Shirone became desperate.
She clearly knew, and therefore, she had to speak.
"Why are you deceiving me?"
"About what? I'm not deceiving anything."
"You're different from before! There must be a reason you taught me
Ataraxia!"
"It was to keep Kariel in check. Thanks to that, I was able to
return to the position of archangel."
"No, you have to tell me. You have that
responsibility."
"My only responsibility is to Heaven."
"You are…!"
Shirone stood up abruptly.
Both of them were avoiding the core issue, arguing with just their
energy.
"You are the one who…!"
Just as Shirone was about to shout, the door burst open.
Commander Crude, Gaold's party, and the officers, having heard Ikael's
message, rushed in.
"What's going on?"
As soon as Plu entered the room, she checked Shirone's condition.
Fortunately, the feared incident hadn't occurred, but the atmosphere was
tense.
Ikael was glaring at Shirone angrily, and Shirone was clenching his
fists and biting his lip.
Ikael turned her head in annoyance and spoke.
"I admit my mistake. I don't want to drag this out any longer.
State your demands. Heaven's only condition is the cessation of war."
Ikael had already reverted to the dignified archangel. And to Shirone,
that was all she was.
Everyone moved to the command control room and sat around the round
table.
Some people had no seats and stood, including Shirone and Plu.
Plu found it strange that Shirone, who had insisted on a meeting with
Ikael, was now staring at the wall, ignoring her.
It was puzzling why Shirone had set the condition of meeting Ikael, and
she wondered if there could be anything in the world that would make them both blush while sitting alone
with an archangel.
Commander Crude spoke.
"These are our conditions. Return the rebels to Heaven. Ban the
elixir of life. Also, strip the Kergoin of their sacrificial rights and elect leaders from all races.
These three things."
Ikael's eyes calmed down.
Why did humans love equality so much?
Nothing in this world is truly equal. Yet they hold equality as their
highest value.
'Because they are weak.'
Yes, they are weak.
Therefore, this is not subjugation but mercy for the weak.
"I accept. You will no longer suffer from the elixir of
life."
"That's not enough."
An officer who had been standing against the wall stepped
forward.
He had been glaring at Ikael with fierce eyes since he arrived, having
lost his family in the war with Heaven.
"Do you know how much we suffered because of you? And now you want
to end it as if nothing happened?"
"Debra, restrain yourself."
Crude tried to stop him, but Debra couldn't hold back his anger.
"Shouldn't you suffer the same pain? One of your angels should
apologize and commit suicide! That's my condition!"
Equality.
Ikael nodded. She couldn't go against Anke Ra's will.
"Understood. I will comply."
The round table murmured.
Debra might have lost his reason, but no one expected her to allow an
angel's suicide.
Then another officer, who had been watching, stepped forward.
"Give us immortality."
He was a man with a malignant tumor spreading through his body, with
little time left to live.
"You don't die even if you're killed, right? So what's the big deal
about one angel dying? Give us immortality! That's the condition!"
Equality.
Ikael couldn't hold back any longer after three demands for
equality.
As her eyes grew cold, tension filled the room. The command control room
began to shake as if an earthquake had struck.
"How far will you indulge, humans?"
Chiiiiing!
Ikael's halo expanded, and Ataraxia was instantly concentrated.
As Gaold pressed down with an air press, the round table
flattened.
Ikael, who had already left her seat, flew through the ceiling.
"Damn it! Chase her!"
Ikael's trademark Ataraxia amplifies all kinds of power in the
world.
That's why it's the strongest.
Even a gentle spring breeze could turn into a typhoon capable of
sweeping the world if Ataraxia was applied.
Sein activated Equilibrium with his iron wheel eye, but it wasn't enough
to reverse Ikael's information.
"Attack! If we don't capture her now, it's the end!"
Crude glared at the officers who had ruined the negotiations and then
rushed outside.
He couldn't blame them. Even he had momentarily hoped for
something.
Ikael was amplifying the underground water veins flowing beneath the
command center.
The amplification was at least 20,000 times.
The moment the water veins burst through, everything in the command
center would be destroyed.
"As the archangel, I command you."
Ataraxia shot a brilliant light into the sky.
"Annihilate."
The ground shook vertically, and cracks appeared on the floor.
As the iron buildings began to tear like paper, despair spread across
the humans' faces.
Gaold's assessment was accurate.
Ikael was not something to be defined by the concept of combat; she was
sheer power itself.
Target for elimination: Archangel Ikael.
Just as a massive explosion was about to occur, a black object flew
rapidly towards Ikael.
To everyone else, it was a blur, but to Ikael, it was clearly Babel, as
if it were etched in her memory.
Even though it wasn't an unavoidable speed, she couldn't evade it
because of the black hole in her memory.
'What is this? What did I forget that my mind is…'
After what felt like over ten thousand thoughts by human standards,
Babel reached Ikael.
She twisted her body leisurely.
At that moment, an unknown force restrained her.
As she quickly turned her gaze, she saw Shirone, transformed into Vajra
Armor, activating Akamai's Antithesis.
'Shirone…?'
Bang!
Babel's fist struck Ikael's face.
Exposed to the tremendous force, Ikael's body plummeted vertically, but
just before hitting the ground, she spread her golden wings to stop the fall.
"This… this can't be…"
The rebels, ready for battle, stood with their mouths agape.
Even Gaold was taken aback this time.
As Ikael gracefully rose from her prone position, Shirone landed on the
ground.
The tendrils of the Vajra Armor spread like a peacock's feathers, aiming
at Ikael, while Akamai's eyes on the tendrils locked onto her.
'Antithesis, huh.'
Ikael shrugged off the restraining force with a shake of her
shoulders.
'It could certainly bind a lower angel.'
He had become much stronger than before.
But what use was it? There was nothing in the world stronger than
her.
"Shirone, if you intend to oppose me like this…"
Thud!
The sound of the ground shaking stopped Ikael in her tracks.
The metal angel Babel stood beside Shirone, ready for battle.
Ikael frowned slightly.
The fact that a being resembling her was protecting Shirone was
extremely displeasing.
