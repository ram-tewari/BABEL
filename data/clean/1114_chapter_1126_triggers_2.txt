Chapter 1126:
Triggers (2)
The Pride of 1 o'clock frowned deeply.
"Ugh!"
Before the shockwave could tear Havitz apart, it was restored to its
previous state by her intervention, reversing the choice made in the past.
'This is dangerous.'
Because something created from nothing has no cause, the time to judge
and respond was fleeting.
'If only the Sloth of 4 o'clock were here...'
But what could be done?
A comrade had been shoved to their death by their own companions.
Hwoo-o-o-o.
As Wizard succeeded in her preemptive strike, the shimmering mirage of
the Void God rose again from her body.
The branches twisted into even more grotesque forms, and the long tongue
writhed like an eel.
"Now! Attack…!"
As Siok shouted, the form of the Void God once again dissolved into the
air.
"Negative Perspective. Converging World."
Wizard's hand signs grew more intricate, and secondary and tertiary
shockwaves erupted around Siok.
"Guhhhh!"
The Pride of 1 o'clock endured using a hidden code, but the strikes grew
faster and faster.
"Attack now! We can't hold on any longer!"
The Prejudice of 5 o'clock yelled, "You're nothing but an insect! A
worthless insect who can do nothing!"
They thought it was over.
Even if she denied it, the Stubbornness of 3 o'clock would counter her
claims.
The Revenge of 10 o'clock cast curses, and the Delusion of 9 o'clock
dragged Wizard into an illusion.
And Wizard's response to all this?
"Void God."
She merely reintegrated the grotesque avatar of the tree into the
world.
"White Rotation."
Dozens of whirlpools formed behind her, and branches extended like hands
from their centers.
The hands gripped the air, spinning like corkscrews, causing the faces
of Siok to twist in agony.
"Ugh!"
Their hidden codes were completely shattered.
'Reversing the laws.'
Wizard's integrated sensory system included the reverse side of the
world.
The hidden code may not have adhered to human logic, but neither did
Wizard's.
'Then, what is it?'
To Siok's question, Havitz answered,
"No one knows."
It was a world only Wizard could perceive, and from that world sprang
boundless creativity.
"…Impressive work."
Havitz didn't know who had raised Wizard as a weapon, but he felt a
shiver at its sheer efficiency.
'She's still young. She lacks experience. Physically, her strikes aren't
even that strong. But…'
The potential.
'That limitless possibility.'
It was as if something destined to bloom in the distant future had been
drawn forth into this very moment.
"Do you really want to kill me that badly?"
The fact that she was an unstoppable existence only made Havitz feel a
sense of kinship with her.
"Heh heh heh."
There couldn't be a more fitting finale to his life.
"Let's play!"
The muscles of Havitz's face were pushed back, revealing the true face
of Satan.
Faster than the Siok, sharper than a blade, his claws grazed Wizard's
cheek.
"First Void Rotation."
Wizard clasped her hands together at her core, and a massive ball of
compressed air plummeted downward.
Boom!
The force was enough to shake the ground, but Satan only bent his back
slightly in response.
"Heh. Your creativity is certainly something. It's impossible to dodge
and impossible to block. But…"
As Havitz's dull, amber eyes fixed forward, an overwhelming wave of
malice surged outward.
"This weak? That's disappointing."
Wizard's facial muscles twitched for the first time as the Wandering of
6 o'clock slammed into the ground.
'Now's the time!'
Assuming the effort of 200 years, the martial artist's kick drove toward
Wizard.
'Air Shield.'
The air barrier burst with a pop as the man's shin slammed into the
child's side.
Her ribs all shattered.
Without even groaning, Wizard flew sideways, using one foot to brake
herself.
The pain of her spilling organs caused her legs to give out, and one
knee touched the ground.
'Should I heal myself?'
Normally, unless death was imminent, attacking would take
priority.
'But I feel like I'm dying.'
Her body's reaction suggested her heart would stop in about three
seconds from the trauma.
'I don't know. Should I gamble?'
Despite countless trial-and-error scenarios with Shirone, there were
still limits to her experience.
'There's no other way.'
As the Void God was reabsorbed into the world, her injuries began to
heal rapidly.
Havitz wasn't one to miss the only opportunity when Wizard's chance to
counterattack disappeared.
"Still young."
Though death lingered in Satan's mind, assumptions held no meaning in
the face of chaos.
Havitz's body, now crimson as though stripped of skin, swung both arms
at once.
Just before Wizard's face could be smashed—
'What?'
Havitz—or rather, Satan—felt something he had never experienced
before.
'What is this?'
Wizard was suddenly far away, and two distinct lines were etched into
the ground.
'I was pushed back.'
What had hit him?
Lowering his gaze, Havitz realized only his solar plexus had returned to
the color of human flesh.
In the sky, Wizard rose, bending one leg and spreading her arms
wide.
"Void Creation."
From the centerline of her body—her brow, throat, chest, navel,
genitals, and perineum—her chakras began to glow.
Then, tens of thousands of chakras opened, and light radiated from
Wizard's body.
Havitz did not define it.
Therefore, he could not be caught off guard, nor fall into traps of
thought. But the one thing he had overlooked…
'Genius.'
It was the transcendent creativity of a human capable of imagining even
things that did not exist in the universe.
As sprouts of light grew along Wizard's arms, all 11 Siok
charged.
"Kill her!"
The sprouts rapidly transformed into branches, fractally expanding and
filling the sky.
'She's still imagining.'
Destruction. Change. Creation.
The mechanism of this destructive-creative cycle spiraled infinitely,
and the avatar of the Void God blossomed once more.
Its form was…
"…What?"
It didn't exist in the universe—a form so shocking that just looking at
it felt like it could stop your heart.
"Hyperform."
Havitz thought to himself:
'Form is everything. Every phenomenon in the universe begins from
forms—waves smaller than subatomic particles.'
'Then what phenomenon does that form create?'
As if answering Havitz's question, Wizard slowly clasped her hands
together and made a hand sign.
"Boundaryless Void."
The eleven Siok members saw crimson petals bloom swiftly before their
eyes, like bursts of light.
"Hyperwave."
The fruit of imagination was sublimated into flowers, which began to
bloom from the heads of all the Siok members.
'It's unstoppable.'
It was something Wizard alone had imagined—something that did not adhere
to the physical laws of the universe.
The Nihility of 12 o'clock gritted his teeth.
"Damn it…"
Everything reset.
Bang!
As his head exploded, the remaining Siok opened their eyes wide in
disbelief.
'She targeted 12 o'clock from the beginning.'
Even in the surreal reality of ten Siok members surviving, Wizard
remained calm.
To her, it wasn't surreal at all.
"Can we end this now?"
As Wizard landed on the ground and made a hand sign, the Void God
quivered and then vanished.
"Cherry Blossom."
Death.
"Graaaaah!"
Sensing his annihilation, Satan's body swelled to an enormous size, and
crimson flowers blossomed across his form.
The frenzied Siok charged forward.
"Satan, watch over us!"
Wizard traced a circle with her finger, aimed at Satan, and pierced her
thumb through the circle.
"Void Burst."
Thousands of flowers bloomed simultaneously, and an indescribable shock
reduced Satan's body to a smaller, diminished form.
"Graaaah! GRAAAAH!"
Shock, restraint, suppression, transformation—all forms of physical
forces worked to reduce Satan back into Havitz.
"No!"
As the Siok panicked, Wizard rushed toward the half-naked and staggering
Havitz.
'I'll kill him here.'
The time until Satan's annihilation? One second? Maybe 1.5
seconds?
'I've done it.'
It was an incredibly short amount of time.
'I'm going to kill Satan.'
But it was enough time for a human to dream, even when success was
within their grasp.
'If I kill Satan…'
There was no greed.
Even if the entire world were offered to her, it would mean nothing. The
only sweet thought that crossed her mind was:
'Shirone… brother.'
Even as her mind was forcibly elevated into the realm of adulthood, her
desires remained small.
'Would he praise me? Would he say I did well?'
But then, a faint voice of desire.
'What?'
At the moment Havitz vanished from her perception, her pupils trembled
in shock.
'What am I doing here?'
Havitz, realizing Vanishing had activated through her momentary doubt,
exuded killing intent.
"Grrrhhh!"
As he pushed off the ground and charged, Wizard couldn't pull herself
out of her confusion.
'Why? Why now?'
She didn't know.
But her extraordinary resilience snapped her out of her ignorance and
restored her to her original state.
'Havitz!'
As soon as Wizard recognized him, Havitz's fist struck her
abdomen.
"Ugh…!"
An adult's fist embedded into the body of a child left her
breathless.
"Guh! Guh!"
The Siok swarmed like demons.
But Havitz was faster, and his longsword rested against Wizard's
neck.
"Satan! You must kill her!"
Wizard still couldn't move, and the fact that Havitz hadn't already
struck her down filled the Siok with dread.
"Please kill her now! There won't be another chance."
But Havitz thought differently.
"…Hah."
The man who had narrowly escaped death exhaled deeply toward the sky,
then spoke to Wizard.
"Are you okay? That was a bit rough."
"…Kill me."
She had lost her sense of self.
She didn't think of fighting again or catching him off guard for a
surprise strike.
The moment she entertained such thoughts, Havitz would disappear from
her grasp.
"The fight's over. So kill me."
"Hmmm."
Havitz removed the blade from Wizard's neck and collapsed onto the
ground as if his legs had given out.
"That was close. You're remarkable. To extract one result from pure
chaos—that's something even I can't do."
Because it was a contradiction.
"Kill me! Just kill me!"
"But contradictions aren't a natural state. Failing to maintain it for
longer was your undoing. In five years—no, maybe even one year—you could kill me perfectly."
Wizard slowly straightened her back.
"…One day is enough."
"Maybe. Honestly, I don't even know what you're thinking. Did Shirone
send you?"
Wizard gritted her teeth.
'I'd rather die.'
Facing her master's disappointment, dying here seemed the better
option.
"What do you want? If you plan to let me live, then leave."
"Planning to kill yourself?"
Havitz stared silently at the child biting her lip, then asked
himself:
'What is this girl to me?'
He didn't care to find the answer, but one thing was certain—he didn't
want Wizard to die.
'It's strange.'
Even meeting the 4 Gustavs wasn't this thrilling.
'Ah, I see.'
He just wanted to play more.
'This girl sees me.'
Perhaps even offers him some form of comfort.
'Should I suggest tormenting people together? No… I'll ask her to fight
alongside Shirone instead. That would be fun too…'
Wizard's inner voice reached him.
'She hates me.'
Sadness briefly flickered in Havitz's eyes before he stood up slowly and
said,
"If you ever want to kill me, come find me."
"Satan! No!"
As the Siok sank into the ground, Wizard tilted her head in
confusion.
"Find you?"
"Yeah. Of course, just wanting to kill me won't be enough, but… you'll
try anyway, won't you? Better that than dying."
Wizard fell silent, lost in thought, but Havitz seemed to already
understand.
"Today was fun."
Even as Havitz disappeared into the forest, Wizard stayed rooted in
place for a long time.
"Sniff…"
The hypnosis broke.
"Waaaaah! Waaaaah!"
Having returned to her seven-year-old self, Wizard burst into tears,
sobbing uncontrollably.
