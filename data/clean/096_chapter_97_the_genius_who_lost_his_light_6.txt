Chapter 97:
The Genius Who Lost His Light (6)
"Master! What happened? Did the experiment succeed?"
"Yes, it succeeded! Our magic worked!"
But Alpheas couldn't rejoice. Erina's condition was clearly abnormal.
"But why is my wife like this? Darling! Snap out of it!"
Akein rechecked the gauges and was shocked. While individual metrics were normal, her
overall bodily rhythms were declining simultaneously, as if her very life was fading.
"How? This didn't happen in the clinical trials!"
"Darling! Erina! Open your eyes!"
Erina half-opened her eyes and smiled faintly.
"Darling... I'm okay."
"Erina! What's wrong? Where does it hurt?"
"A rejection reaction started from the terminal nerves. There must be something
different about human brains. But you've done something incredible. With a little more research...
Gah!"
Erina convulsed, gasping for air.
"Darling! Don't speak! I'll fix this! No matter what, I'll save you...!"
Alpheas cursed his own intellect for realizing it was already irreversible. But he had to
say it.
Erina slowly shook her head. What her husband knew, she now knew too.
"Darling... hold my hand."
Alpheas gripped her hand tightly. If there had been even a 1% chance, he would have
tried. But now, all he could do was stay by her side.
"Erina, this can't be. How can this...?"
Erina smiled sadly.
"Darling, I'm sorry."
"For what? You have nothing to—"
"For being stupid."
Alpheas's heart shattered. How could he have forgotten her words—that she was beautiful
just as she was? Why had he tried to change her?
"No... why are you sorry? I'm the one who's sorry! I was the stupid one! I was the
fool!"
"Meeting you was my greatest fortune."
Erina finally understood her husband. The wonderful world ruled by reason. He had lived
there. She was happy to share it briefly, to carry his memories as she departed.
"Darling! Open your eyes! Please... I was wrong, darling!"
As Erina's eyes closed, Alpheas hugged her and wailed.
"Waaaaah! Darling! Darling!"
Akein bowed his head solemnly. What made human brains different from animals? If they had
conducted human trials from the start, this tragedy could have been avoided.
But it was a meaningless assumption. Erina had set one condition for the experiment:
never test it on others.
That was why he had poured his heart into it. The data gathered would serve humanity
well.
'I'm sorry. I won't let your sacrifice be in vain.'
A dull thud sounded. Turning, he saw Alpheas smashing his head against the wall.
"Waaaaah!"
Each impact produced a cracking sound—whether from the wall or his skull was unclear.
"What? The light of the Mirhi family? Light? Light!"
Thud! Thud! Thud!
After repeatedly bashing his head, Alpheas's body bounced back like a rubber ball. But he
didn't stop. The horror of surviving alone while his wife died twisted his insides.
"Arrogant Alpheas!"
Alpheas charged at the wall again.
Thud!
Klump grabbed him. If this continued, he would surely die.
"Alpheas! Snap out of it! What are you doing?!"
"Let go! Damn it! Waaaaah!"
Even Klump's strength struggled to restrain him. It was as if his soul was burning itself
out in a rush toward death. But eventually, shock took over, his pupils dilating as consciousness faded.
"Erina... Erina..."
Even unconscious, he called his wife's name, bringing tears to Klump's eyes.
