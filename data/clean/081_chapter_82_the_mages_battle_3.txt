Chapter 82:
The Mage's Battle (3)
Iruki gulped down his saliva. Shirone, who had switched the circuits in his mind to
combat mode, had a completely different gaze than usual.
Shirone began timing his moves. Photon Output boasted exceptional activation speed among
offensive spells. But dark magic wasn't far behind in terms of speed either.
No signals could be read from Canis's eyes. Only an uncertain future swirled within them,
like the black pupils of an orangutan.
These were not human eyes. Only someone who had survived countless times could possess
such intensely animalistic eyes.
What kind of life must one live to have eyes like that?
The two twitched their fingers. Then, the moment a bird fluttered its wings and took off,
they moved simultaneously. Photon Output and the Power of Darkness clashed in a dazzling display,
overwhelming the eyes.
Shirone teleported upward in an instant. The pursuing Power of Darkness, which had been
chasing him vertically, melted away under the sunlight.
Canis smirked. His target wasn't Shirone's main body but the shadow embedded in the
ground. Realizing the properties of dark magic too late, Shirone hastily teleported beyond the cliff. As the
shadow disappeared a thousand meters below, the Power of Darkness, having lost its target, vanished like
flames.
When Photon Output rained down like rapid fire, Canis cast Shadow Wall. A thick barrier
of shadow rose abruptly, absorbing all the photons.
'Incredible strength. To block light with darkness...'
The dark power defied the laws of nature. Shirone, who had amplified the power of Photon
Output through thousands of rebounds, could guess just how formidable Canis's mental strength was.
'Then how about this?'
Shirone concentrated photons in his palm, clenched his teeth, and hurled them. As the
Photon Cannon fired, Canis maximized the thickness of his barrier. His lips curled up briefly, but the
Shadow Wall exploded, and a flash of light surged in.
'What the hell is this?'
Feeling a chill, Canis instinctively cast Dark Skin. His body turned black just as the
Photon Cannon struck his abdomen.
"Guh...!"
The limit of shock absorption Dark Skin could handle had already been surpassed upon
impact, and the shockwave rattled his insides. Canis was sent flying, legs dangling, before being buried in
the forest.
"Canis!"
Arin couldn't believe it. Physical force imbued in light? Light was pure energy. If even
a small amount of physical force were embedded in it, any planet with a sun would have been shattered.
Lucas's expression also turned serious for the first time. A spell where beams of light
flew swiftly to strike the opponent. Even if he had been in Canis's place, the odds of dodging would have
been fifty-fifty.
'This isn't just bad luck—it's like stepping in shit.'
A murderous glint flashed in Lucas's eyes. It might not be a profitable deal, but
shouldn't the debt be paid? His twin swords trembled as he fixed his gaze on Shirone.
***
Etella was somewhere in the mountain range, about 800 meters from the impassable bridge.
And before her stood Akein, who had risen to the rank of unofficial third-class archmage 40 years ago.
For Akein, this was an unwelcome guest. Even if he had rested, the mental exhaustion from
casting Abyss Nova had only recovered about 60%.
But such thoughts were short-lived. Akein narrowed his eyes as he realized the true worth
of the woman blocking his path.
'Oh ho, what do we have here?'
She wore oversized glasses that made her look clumsy. Her ample chest and gentle,
sheep-like appearance made her seem unlikely to engage in combat.
But he could feel it. Just by standing there, every energy passing through her body
flowed without obstruction.
Her pupils, especially, were remarkable. They radiated the spirit of a sage, one who had
transcended the eight emotions of joy, anger, sorrow, pleasure, love, hatred, desire, and greed.
She was a seeker.
And one far too exceptional to be a mere teacher at a magic school.
'She might just be able to counter Abyss Nova.'
Etella was equally tense facing the authority of dark magic, a man who had made his name
as an archmage even before she was born.
"I've heard of your esteemed name."
"From the monastery, perhaps?"
Akein could guess Etella was a nun even without hearing it. But her next answer far
exceeded his expectations.
"I am Bishop Etella of the Carciss Monastery. I must ask why someone who once made a
name for themselves in dark magic would commit such atrocities."
"A bishop, you say."
Akein burst into laughter, as if finding it absurd. For someone not even thirty to hold
the position of bishop was unthinkable in his prime.
Especially since Carciss was one of the continent's largest monasteries, with over 2,000
parishes. A bishop was responsible for an entire parish, equivalent to the head of a city.
"Where is Alpheas?"
Etella remained silent. She didn't know his whereabouts either, but even that fact could
be information for the enemy.
"I can hear the gears turning in your head. Honestly, it doesn't matter. I never
expected that cunning brat to fall for my magic so easily."
Calling Alpheas, who was past sixty, a "cunning brat" gave a glimpse into just
how long the archmage before her had lived.
"Why are you looking for the headmaster? If it's a matter of grudges, the proper way
is to resolve it face-to-face. If you release the students from mind control and withdraw, I can arrange a
meeting."
"Hahaha! Seems the brat has built quite a reputation in my absence. But child,
Alpheas isn't the man you think he is. He's the most foolish and arrogant bastard in the world."
"His past doesn't matter. The headmaster now is respected by his students."
"Oh? Is that so?"
Akein playfully stroked his chin. The Alpheas in his memory was limited to a brash young
man in his twenties.
"I'm curious. What exactly did that brat do to earn such respect? Care to enlighten
me?"
Etella saw through Akein's tactic. The time when the sun would set was approaching. He
was trying to stall until night, when dark magic would be most effective.
"If you're waiting for darkness, you might as well stop now. As a teacher, I can't
stand by and let evil deeds continue."
"What did you say?"
Akein frowned in displeasure. A mere child, 120 years his junior, dared to treat him as
some magician who relied on stalling for time?
Casting the Power of Darkness, Akein's shadow began devouring the trees. Branches rustled
as trunks bent toward a single point.
"Did you think I was afraid just because the sun was out? If so, you've gravely
underestimated me."
Etella looked up. Trees within hundreds of meters tilted, weaving a massive dome-like
net. As the sky closed, the surroundings grew darker than night.
"Die, insolent wretch."
Akein vanished into his shadow—Dark Port, a signature dark magic technique. By erasing
the concept of distance through assimilation with darkness, he could instantly move anywhere a shadow
existed.
The absence of any presence was Dark Port's greatest strength. As Akein emerged behind
Etella and cast the Power of Darkness, her body turned into a flash of light and retreated. Dozens of
shadowy hands clawed at empty air.
A high-speed battle ensued, starting with Akein's repeated use of Dark Port. It seemed as
though dozens of Akeins and Etellas were fighting at once. As Akein cornered Etella in a narrow radius, she
teleported at extreme speeds from her spot. Her upper body appeared to multiply as shadowy hands pierced
through afterimages.
'Bishop of Carciss Monastery. Not bad at all.'
Even Akein, filled with rage, couldn't help but admire her. Though her mental magnitude
fell short of his, her skill and senses far surpassed her age.
"Then I'll just have to exploit your weaknesses."
Etella's pupils shook with confusion. As the Power of Darkness intensified, thousands of
hands flew at her simultaneously.
An unfathomable amount of magical power.
Jonah's detection ability was worthy of being called a spell in itself, but even that
couldn't evade thousands of hands.
When a branch's shadow grabbed her wrist, Etella abruptly stopped moving. Attempting to
teleport now would tear her arm off.
"Ugh...!"
The shadow's tension was rubber-like. But Etella's strength was no joke either. Gritting
her teeth, she yanked her arm, tearing the shadow apart.
'She knows martial arts too? Quite the sturdy one.'
Akein smirked bitterly.
"If that's your preference, I'll play along."
