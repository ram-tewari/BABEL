Chapter 88:
Crowd (4)
Alpheas's head was under the photon magic cast by Sad, whose forehead was already
drenched in cold sweat. Viltor Achain—an annoying man, but the power of his magic was beyond imagination.
'Phew. Such powerful mind control. This would still work even in this era.'
At first, he had thought it would be easy. No matter how grand the title of Grand
Sorcerer, forty years had passed. Magic had evolved over generations, and dark magic had become obsolete.
But Abyss Nova was so complex, even Sad, a beneficiary of cutting-edge magic, couldn't
analyze it.
After twenty minutes, he finally felt the light energy penetrate the core of the
darkness. From then on, Sad pushed the light in with his utmost mental strength. The dark veil lifted, and
Alpheas's memories began to unfold brilliantly along the flow of light.
***
Forty years ago. Capital, Bashuka.
The Kingdom of Tormia was swept by an unprecedented magic craze. The 12th king, Adolf
XII, unlike his predecessors who revered military might, was overflowing with intellect and kindness.
Upon ascending the throne, he separated the magic department, which had been limited to
military affairs, and gathered countless talents.
Talents from the provinces and even other countries came to Bashuka to study, filling the
capital's streets daily with the voices of intellect.
Fresh graduates of the magic school gathering in taverns to debate by day was a common
sight.
Gypsy-style clothing with bold colors was in fashion.
For women, hair as short as to expose the neckline was a symbol of intellect. Meanwhile,
men's hair grew long, reaching their waists.
Taverns saw factions form over academic clashes, with fistfights breaking out often
enough that security had to drag people away.
The most famous tavern in Bashuka was "The Homeland of Ancient Gods," a rustic drinking
hole.
Its grand hall held over 200 tables. At the central podium, people frequently climbed up
to expound their magical views.
All sorts of sounds converged, and the topics of chatter varied.
Though magical debates dominated, gossip circulating among nobles was also brewed in
real-time at The Homeland of Ancient Gods.
It was an era of romance.
Alpheas reminisced about his golden youth like that.
"Oh my, kids! He's here, he's here! Lord Alpheas has arrived!"
A woman at the tavern entrance made a fuss, and the girls at the tables shrieked as they
turned to look.
"Hello, ladies! It's as lively as ever here!"
The fair-haired, handsome youth, Alpheas, entered The Homeland of Ancient Gods. Beside
him was a young man with a buzzcut, out of fashion—Ozent Clump, Rian's grandfather.
Though Clump, a swordsman, wouldn't have cast invisibility magic, the girls treated him
like air and clung to Alpheas.
"We've been waiting, Lord Alpheas! What magic will you teach us today?"
"First, let's wet our throats. My tongue stiffens quickly if it's not soaked in alcohol."
"Hahaha! So funny. Lord Alpheas is really amusing."
If his current disciples heard this, they'd find it lame and mock him, but back then,
Alpheas was a popular figure who could say anything and be understood.
The heir of a first-class family, top of his class at the magic school, handsome, and
refined—was there any woman in the world who wouldn't like him?
Meanwhile, the men looked at Alpheas with distaste. Even those not narrow-minded enough
to envy others couldn't help but glare when discussing him, showing how much resentment he inspired among
males.
The Light of the Mirhi Family.
That was Alpheas's official title at the time, but among those who knew him, he had
another nickname.
Arrogant Alpheas.
***
Anyone in the magic world could tell Alpheas was a man chosen by the gods. The problem
was that he displayed his talent without regard for time or place.
Once an argument started, he'd see it through to the end, and if the opponent made even a
minor mistake, he'd dig into it relentlessly until they conceded.
Only Clump knew Alpheas meant no harm. Not arrogance, but purity. His passion was just
too much for people.
In truth, Alpheas was the type to admit his mistakes without hesitation and celebrate the
other's victory.
The only problem was that such occasions were rare.
As Alpheas waited for his beer, bored, he mingled with people at the tables. Clump
watched worriedly, but seeing no point in stopping him, shook his head.
"Sarof! Here for a drink in broad daylight?"
Sarof, a native of the capital and one of Alpheas's few rivals, was greeted with raised
hands. Though he always lost in debates, it was widely agreed that, aside from Alpheas, he was the top
prospect in the photon field. Naturally, Sarof's feelings weren't positive, but Alpheas approached anyway.
"Oh, right. Sarof, I heard you submitted a thesis to the royal palace? I skimmed it—quite
interesting."
"What? Why are you reading my thesis?"
"Isn't it obvious? My field is photons too, and the royal librarian is my ardent fan.
Anyway, good job."
"R-Really? How was it?"
Among young mages at the time, the highest honor was having a thesis recognized and
working under the royal magic department.
Once a thesis was accepted, you'd get a private lab and all research expenses covered—no
greater glory for a mage.
Sarof had staked his life on his magnum opus, submitted just a week prior, so he couldn't
help but be curious about his rival's opinion.
"You nailed the characteristics of light. Flawless, really. The part about energy
concentration hit me hard."
Sarof's expression brightened instantly. But Alpheas, as usual, didn't stop there.
"But there's one error. The wave nature of light. That's clearly a flawed theory."
"What nonsense? Light's wave nature is already experimentally verified."
"True, but I disagree. Recent results show too many gaps wave theory can't explain. Light
might be closer to particles."
"Hah! Resorting to gossip now, Alpheas? Countless critics have pointed out errors in
particle experiments! If light were particles, how would you explain waves? They're entirely different
concepts!"
Whether light was particles or waves was the hottest topic of Alpheas's era. Wave theory
dominated, but a minority of scholars, including Alpheas, constantly challenged it.
"You're the one trusting critics! I've seen papers debunking particle experiment errors
too. But their approach wasn't scientific. Isn't that the real error?"
Drunk and sleep-deprived over his thesis, Sarof slammed the table and stood. Alpheas was
fanning flames in a burning house.
"Enough! Who asked you to critique my thesis? If you're so confident, write your own! Let
the world laugh at your nonsense!"
"I'm just worried. Even if it's rejected now, you can revise it later. What matters isn't
the thesis but realizing the truth, right?"
Sarof couldn't take it anymore. Wasn't the real message, "You're getting rejected this
time"?
"Get lost! I won't even speak to you again!"
Alpheas returned to his table, licking his lips. Debating a rival in the same field was
mutually beneficial, so he wasn't upset.
But Clump was worried.
They'd first met at an inn in Bashuka. Clump, also from Creas, had bonded with fellow
provincials, and a year had passed since they started hanging out.
Frankly, it was unlucky. Even as a swordsman, he could tell—how much worse must it be for
rival mages?
"Alpheas, are you a fighting cock? If others can't do something, just let it be. Why
insist on fixing it?"
"Where's the 'others' or 'me'? Wrong must be righted. That's intellect."
"Honestly. Do you know what people call you? Arrogant Alpheas. That's your nickname now."
"Hahaha! I like that. Much better than 'The Light of Mirhi'—so cringe."
Magic was the domain of talent, and Alpheas had been blessed with overflowing talent. He
believed talent would shine no matter how hidden, so humility was just hypocrisy.
"This is the fate of geniuses. Come on, don't overthink it. Let's drink. Hey, Rafne,
bring snacks fast. This guy eats more than a bear."
"Hohoho! If Lord Alpheas wishes, of course!"
Rafne, the tavern maid, looked at him with adoration. Clearly, she was smitten too.
Alpheas treated every woman like a lover, giving Clump a headache.
Not that he was in any deep relationships. His superficial charm had countless men
missing their chances. Even beyond magic, Alpheas was public enemy number one among males.
"Even Rafne? At least date properly. What's wrong with you?"
"Haha, as you know, I'm everyone's lover. And I'm not even trying. They just melt at my
killer smile."
Alpheas stroked his chin and smirked. Indeed, it was the kind of looks women went crazy
for.
Would smiling like that really work? Clump, who had no luck with women, experimentally
curled his lips.
"How's this? Do I have a killer smile too?"
"Oh, yeah! A real killer smile—a murderer's. Pfft!"
"You wanna die?"
"Hahaha! Just kidding. It was actually decent. Use that smile at the party later. Girls
will swoon."
"Party? Do I have to go? I'm not a mage."
That evening, the Bastard family was hosting a party for young mages. Though unrelated to
magic, nobles then threw parties under any pretext to assert status.
"Who said only mages can go? All walks of life will be there—network a bit. Connections
matter as much as skill."
"Funny coming from you."
"What can I do? You gotta climb high to make a difference. Just come. Who knows, you
might find your other half there."
Clump, brimming with passion for swords, cared little for networking. But the last line
got him.
Who knew? A country bumpkin swordsman might fall for a sophisticated city girl.
Suddenly, as if catching a cold, Clump coughed repeatedly.
"Ahem! Fine, I'll tag along."
