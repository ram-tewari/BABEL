Chapter 515:
A New Journey (1)
Miro and her group returned to the unified rebel headquarters.
This was possible because none of the rebels knew about the existence of
God's punishment.
Zulu had changed the location of God's punishment's impact point through
the Metagate to the deepest part of Zion, 300 meters underground.
Even though the explosion was contained within a natural bunker, the
seismic waves swept through all of heaven. If it had struck the surface directly, the aftermath would
have been unimaginable.
'Mr. Crude…'
Plu awkwardly received the rebels' joyous greetings of victory,
recalling his face.
If he had listened to Plu and revealed everything to his subordinates,
Shirone's group wouldn't have been transported to the headquarters for treatment.
"For the night of the victors! For the future of
humanity!"
Drunken rebels shouted under the moonlight.
Thinking of their fallen comrades, they couldn't endure it
otherwise.
Rebels who disliked the noise gathered in quiet places to talk.
What puzzled them most was the massive earthquake and the collapse of
Aravot's spire at the last moment.
Some testified to seeing light falling from the sky, but no one thought
it was magic.
Hundreds of torches burned in the headquarters' open space, and the
drinking continued all night.
Meanwhile, Shirone's group was busy receiving treatment.
It was fortunate that Saint Moriak was still with Cage B Team.
"Aaagh!"
Arius's scream filled the infirmary.
He had second-degree burns all over his body and fractured ribs, but
Moriak's hands, casting healing magic, were mercilessly efficient.
"I'm sorry. Just bear with it a little longer."
The process of rapidly regenerating skin and mending bones inevitably
involved pain.
But Moriak's mouth twitched as she looked at Arius with pity.
"Just a little longer… Hehehe! Ah, sorry, hehehe!"
As Moriak's face twisted into a grotesque smile, Plu, waiting for her
turn, turned pale.
Tarvan had mentioned that her personality had changed after divorcing
her husband.
Nevertheless, her skills were excellent, and the emergency treatment was
swift.
Even Moriak, who had been laughing foolishly, became serious as the
patients' conditions worsened.
Sain's jaw was broken, Plu had bruises and fractures all over her body,
and the martial artists Etella and Kangnan had both hands shattered.
But the most severely injured was Kuan.
The swordsman had lost an arm.
"Isn't there anything we can do?"
Shina asked, looking at the arm on the makeshift bed.
Moriak, on the other hand, was examining Kuan's severed limb.
With such significant physical damage, magic couldn't restore it.
"It's a clean cut. The nerves don't seem too damaged. If we find a
skilled surgeon quickly…"
"No need."
Shina turned, but Kuan avoided her gaze, maintaining a cold tone.
"I cut off that arm myself. Reattaching it now would be
meaningless."
It would be a lie to say he hadn't struggled with the decision.
A limping man and a one-armed, limping man were worlds apart from
Shina's perspective.
But he ultimately decided to discard the arm.
What allowed him to protect Shina from Nameless was the realization as a
one-armed man.
What use was winning her heart if he couldn't protect her?
'The war isn't over yet.'
He needed to become stronger.
No one could read Kuan's thoughts, but Armin seemed to guess something
and smiled quietly.
"The emergency treatment is done. Not everyone is healed,
but…"
Moriak looked at the two people lying on the beds pushed
together.
Some wounds, etched into the mind rather than the body, were impossible
to heal.
Those were Shirone and Gaold.
Even at this moment, they were trapped in their own hells, paying the
price of their resolve.
Miro concluded the situation.
"It can't be helped. At least they're not dead, so let's think
about it slowly. For now, it's best to rest."
Everyone knew they wouldn't be able to sleep, but no one complained as
they headed to their rooms.
The shouts from the drinking party, continuing past midnight, sounded
like ambushes, and most whispered in somber voices under the night's silence.
It was natural to be exhausted as the tension eased.
But Miro, dragging Arius by a dog leash, was checking each of the
group's rooms.
After all, those who had come to rescue her deserved some
responsibility.
When she reached Gaold's door, she hesitated to knock.
"……"
But she soon took a deep breath and shook her wrist.
Knock, knock.
Without waiting for a response, she opened the door to find Gaold
unconscious and naked, sitting on the bed, while Kangnan was wiping his body with a warm, wet
towel.
Miro noticed the bandages wrapped around Kangnan's fists.
Her palm bones were almost shattered, and even Moriak's healing magic
couldn't fully restore them, but she focused on wringing the towel without showing any pain.
Probably because Miro was there.
"You're very devoted. Even if he wakes up, he won't recognize
it."
Kangnan's eyes sharpened.
"The chairman will return. Definitely."
Miro lightly brushed off Kangnan's hostility and smiled.
"Yes, he's not the type to collapse like this. But are you okay?
When he wakes up, he'll come running to me again."
"You…!"
As Kangnan's anger peaked, Miro changed her expression and raised her
hand.
"Sorry. It was a joke. I didn't mean to hurt you."
Arius, sitting in the corner like a dog, smacked his lips.
Only Arius, besides Miro, knew there was a deeper meaning to her
words.
Miro's mind, approached through Drimo, was the epitome of nobility
without a trace of trauma.
But even she had a tiny, almost imperceptible tremor.
It was engraved on the twentieth pillar of the temple built in Miro's
mind.
'Mikael Gaold.'
A tiny name that no one but the grave robber Arius could have
discovered.
It was the net that pulled Miro out of her deep concentration.
The human mind is inherently marred by countless wounds, making it a
speck of a flaw, but that made it all the more remarkable.
'Adrias Miro, the epitome of detachment…'
Had once had her heart shaken by Gaold.
-Then… save me…
Miro recalled that night, the only regret she had in her life, with a
bitter smile.
The only regret she had left in her life.
'But you… you really did it.'
Gaold had spent twenty years fulfilling Miro's wish.
It wasn't something achievable by mere strength, only Gaold could do
it.
Miro looked at Gaold's emaciated face, his beard grown thick, with
pity.
'You gentle fool. How do you plan to survive this harsh world with such
a soft heart?'
Well, that was Gaold.
Finishing her thoughts, Miro walked to the bed and stroked Gaold's
head.
"It may be a tough place…"
Then she slowly bent down and kissed his forehead.
"Have a good journey."
After holding Gaold for a long time, Miro straightened up and
said,
"Once he's cleaned up, prepare to leave. We'll return at
dawn."
She had already extracted the coordinates from Ingris, where she had
gone with Sain to understand Ra's will.
"What about Shirone?"
Miro stopped at Kangnan's question and turned her head.
"I'll take him. He's too valuable to leave as he is."
Not only did he cast the spell known as God's punishment, but he also
annihilated one of the archangels, Faiel.
A nineteen-year-old boy who hadn't even graduated from magic school yet
seemed a promising future for humanity.
'But that won't be easy…'
Leaving Gaold's room, Miro entered Shirone's room.
She saw Shirone in a deep sleep, and Plu reading a book on the opposite
bed.
"Oh, what brings you…?"
"Just patrolling. How is he?"
Plu closed the book and looked at Shirone.
"Still no sign of waking up. Armand hasn't been released
either."
Valhalla Action was a power installed in Armand.
Moreover, it was integrated with the avatar through the Unity of Mind,
so it wouldn't be released until Shirone paid off all his debts.
Having heard the general situation from Plu upon arriving at the
headquarters, Miro could guess as much.
"Valhalla Action, and for hundreds of years?"
"I don't know the exact details. But Archangel Ikael said it might
be a hint."
"What do you mean?"
Plu summarized what Ikael had said.
Humans can't normally live for hundreds of years.
But Ikael said if the result came out that way, the cause must have been
established somehow.
"I see. A reversal of causality…"
"Did something come to mind?"
Miro, who had been resting her chin and rolling her eyes, shook her
head.
"No, let's focus on returning for now. We're not in a comfortable
position either."
"That's true. So when do we leave?"
"Right now."
"What?"
"Since the emergency treatment is done, it's best to send Cage B
Team first. It's fortunate I brought multiple coordinates just in case."
"I see."
Once Cage B Team returned safely, who knew how they might change.
Of course, with the strongest party and Miro present, it was unlikely
they would make a move, but identifying the return point could narrow the encirclement, so moving
separately was the right choice.
"Then get ready."
After finishing her patrol, Miro left the headquarters to find Cage B
Team ready to depart at the front gate.
The war in heaven was over, but the human world would become even
busier.
Tarvan approached.
"You're here. Let's go."
Miro took Goffin's disk from her pocket and inserted it into the
Metagate, causing a brief flash of red light from the metal plate's gap.
"It's ready. Take it."
Tarvan, catching the Metagate Miro threw, asked,
"Where is it set to?"
"Who knows? It could be in the middle of a desert or inside a blue
whale's belly. I set it roughly, so find your way."
Tarvan didn't get annoyed.
Honestly, just being able to return was fortunate enough.
"What do you plan to do now?"
Miro, heading towards the headquarters, turned back.
"What do you mean?"
"Aren't you also a citizen of Tormia? Why not return with us and
cooperate with the kingdom?"
"A citizen of Tormia."
Of course, Miro's homeland was Tormia.
And that homeland had imprisoned her in a dimensional prison with no end
in sight.
"I understand you're upset. But you, as well as Gaold's team, are
involved in the events of heaven. You'll be monitored by various countries from now on. So why
not…"
"Hehe, I'll politely decline."
Miro turned with a beautiful smile.
"I'm a lover of all people."
Tarvan didn't make a second offer.
If the strongest scale magician in history said no, no one could
persuade her otherwise.
'Gaold, you've awakened a monster.'
Miro had returned to the human world.
The political landscape of various countries would change
rapidly.
'Not that it matters.'
When Cage B Team returned, the events in heaven would be reported to
Tormia, and this information could be used to exert influence in the international community.
"Let's go back."
When Tarvan activated the Metagate and threw it on the ground, a black
sphere formed, expanding the space.
"Surely it's not really inside a blue whale's belly,
right?"
As Cage B Team left heaven and the black sphere of the Metagate
disappeared, Miro's steps halted.
"Phew, then."
She took out another disk from her pocket.
"Shall we go home too?"
