Chapter 398:
Rapidly Changing Political Situation (2)
After the celebration for her promotion to a certified level 5 magician,
Siena arrived at her dorm, pleasantly tipsy.
Among the many bouquets she received, she brought in the flowers Shirone
had given her.
As she took off her shoes, she noticed dozens of congratulatory letters
from various institutions.
Gathering the pile of letters, Siena sat at her desk, pushing her hair
back as she looked through them one by one.
There were letters from the Tormia National Defense Magic Research
Institute, the Royal Magic Museum, the Climate Association, the Magician's Pension Fund, and many
more.
"Whew, I guess I've really made it."
Siena stood up, taking off her coat. The hangover wasn't a problem, but
she had sweated a lot and wanted to wash up.
As she threw her top onto the desk, she noticed a unique seal among the
letters.
"Kaizen Swordsmanship School?"
Born into the Olifer family and having lived as a magician, she had no
particular connection to swordsmanship.
"Did I meet someone at the teachers' association?"
Siena opened the letter and checked the name.
Parkar Kuan.
"Oh... Mr. Kuan."
Last year, under pressure from the Olifer family, she had gone to meet a
potential suitor and met Kuan.
She remembered it as a decent meeting. His tone was cold and his
demeanor frosty, but he hadn't crossed the line into rudeness.
"How did he know to send a letter?"
He might have heard through the teachers' association. But since
swordsmanship and magic are entirely different fields, he wouldn't have known unless he had been paying
attention.
"I should send a reply."
Still in her underwear, Siena sat back down. She opened a drawer, took
out some stationery, dipped her fountain pen in ink, and brought it to the paper.
Tap.
The dot on the blank page didn't move for a long time.
'Armin...'
The only person she wanted to receive congratulations from was
Armin.
The person who had given up his eyes for her when they were children.
Without him, she wouldn't have dreamed of reaching this position.
Siena put the dotted paper back in the drawer. She felt sorry for Kuan,
but there was no room in her heart, filled with the lingering image of Armin, who had already
left.
'Is he doing well? He doesn't seem to be releasing any new
works.'
Since leaving Creas, where he had been working, she hadn't received a
single letter from him.
He used to send letters at least once every three months, so she was
worried that something might have happened.
'Well, he's much smarter than me.'
Who could worry about whom?
Even as a certified level 5 magician, in front of Armin, Siena was
still the crybaby little sister from her childhood.
Siena looked up at the ceiling with a sad smile.
"Hehe. Brother, I did well, didn't I?"
It was the most terrifying dream of his life.
From a city-sized hole in the sky, countless Maras invaded.
Beings called demons, evil gods, and gods by humans were devastating the
creatures of the earth.
He lost everyone.
Among the corpses strewn like a field, all the people Shirone loved were
dead.
'No! No!'
Shirone wept in front of Amy's corpse.
Her unclosed eyes stared resentfully at the dozens of Maras floating in
the sky.
'Ahhhh!'
Ataraxia accumulated at a terrifying speed. When all the hair on
Shirone's head stood on end, a massive flash tore through the sky.
The Maras were engulfed in light and disappeared.
As the entire world filled with light, darkness quickly surged in from
the edges, covering the light.
Shirone was left alone in the darkness. For the last human left in the
world, even the name "loneliness" didn't fit.
Shirone knelt and wept, shouting into the darkness to return the lost
ones.
"Shi-ro-ne."
A murky voice pierced his eardrums.
A small, thin flash passed by, and the darkness split open, revealing a
giant eyeball.
Shirone's eyes widened. In the center of the giant eyeball, his own
face, twisted like a demon, was reflected.
"...! ...!"
Shirone screamed, coughing up blood. He had never felt such hatred in
his life.
But his voice sounded muffled, as if submerged in water.
The giant eyeball, embedded in the darkness, spoke in a murky
voice.
"You have the right and duty to answer the remaining
questions."
"...! ...!"
Blood tears flowed from his eyes. He hurled all sorts of curses and
insults at the eyeball.
Losing control even in his dream, he pounded the darkness with his
fists. Resentment, rage, and longing filled his trembling shoulders.
For the first time, the giant eyeball looked at Shirone. There was no
movement in the pupil, but it certainly did.
Suddenly, as if he had gained ears, Shirone finally heard his own voice
in the dream.
"What do you mean? What questions do I have to answer?"
"..."
The eyeball asked.
"Gasp!"
Shirone woke with a start, his upper body shooting up. He saw the
memo-covered desk divider.
'My room. I'm in my room...'
Shirone collapsed back onto the desk. His forehead was drenched in cold
sweat, and he felt exhausted, as if all his strength had left him.
"Whew, what was that? Why such a strange dream..."
Feeling the texture of the paper stuck to his cheek, he sat up. Among
the densely written notes, one word caught his eye.
Heaven's Destruction.
He had fallen asleep while researching magic all night. It was literally
about developing a mass destruction spell. The stress of having no solution might have contributed to
the nightmare.
It was already 5 a.m.
Shirone neatly organized his notes. He already had over ten notebooks
filled with ideas related to the Heaven's Destruction spell. His understanding of the Divine Particle
had deepened, but the possibility of meeting Gaold's proposed standards was still 0%.
'No, there must be a way. Maybe...'
If completed, it would be the most destructive spell in the world. He
couldn't give up because a faint resonance, like an answer, still echoed in his mind.
After washing up with warm water, Shirone packed his notes and pen in a
crossbody bag and left his dorm.
The graduating class that had started in winter was now heading towards
summer. The nights were shorter, and the air was refreshing, like sparkling water. The forest was filled
with the cheerful sounds of birds.
'Can I really reach heaven?'
When he met Gaold, a year had seemed like a short time. But living
through the busy schedule of the graduating class, all the promises related to him felt like a distant
dream.
"Oh? Shirone?"
Turning his head, Shirone saw Maya sitting on a garden bench, blinking.
She held a steaming mug of coffee in both hands.
"Maya, what are you doing up at this hour?"
"Huh? I always get up now. I have to play the morning music. What
about you?"
Shirone, who woke up to the sound of music every morning, hadn't
considered the small inconvenience it might cause.
"Oh, I see. I woke up early. I had some time, so I decided to take
a walk."
"I see."
Although they had grown closer since the hill capture, meeting alone
wasn't something that happened often, so an awkward atmosphere lingered.
Maya, looking down and pushing the mug with her thumb, gathered her
courage and pointed to the seat next to her on the bench.
"Want to sit?"
Shirone didn't refuse. He had come out to clear his head, but being with
Maya wasn't a bad choice.
While magicians drew sharp lines around their domains, Maya stepped
beyond those lines to soothe others' pain.
As a magician who disliked burdening others with his problems, Shirone
had initially found it perplexing.
But now, he had to admit it honestly.
Her kindness was an oasis that gradually softened Shirone's heart,
hardened by the intense schedule of the graduating class.
"Want some? It's still warm."
Maya offered him coffee mixed with sugar and milk. Stressed as he was,
Shirone had no reason to refuse something sweet. Of course, it was also because it was Maya offering it
that he could accept it without hesitation.
"Thanks."
As Shirone took a sip of the coffee, Maya's face blushed, and she smiled
shyly.
"Is something bothering you? You look troubled."
Shirone sighed and leaned back on the bench.
"I've been working on an idea, but I can't find a breakthrough. I
haven't slept for days, and today I had a terrible nightmare. So I'm not feeling great."
Maya could see that Shirone had lost a lot of weight. She had thought he
had no worries, given his success in the graduating class, but it seemed that even someone at his level
had struggles.
"Don't think too hard. Sometimes, when I try too hard to sing well,
it doesn't go right because I get too tense."
Shirone had also experienced that insight, where the mind doesn't flow
unless it's relaxed.
But that alone couldn't create a great river.
Only when the dam is broken after being squeezed to the limit does the
mind become a great torrent, reaching a genius idea.
"Hmm, I don't know. Whether it's easy or hard, it has to make
sense. I might go crazy at this rate."
Shirone tilted his head back. Busy birds were flying through the sky,
looking for breakfast.
Maya's expression turned sympathetic. How deep must his worries be for
such dark circles to form under his eyes?
"Shirone..."
Maya's hand covered Shirone's. Even then, Shirone's gaze remained fixed
on the sky.
She couldn't tell if he was unaware or simply allowing it, but just
holding his hand made her heart feel like it would burst.
"Shirone, can you tell me what's wrong? Even the most talented
people can miss small details. Maybe asking for help from someone else could lead to a
solution."
Shirone slowly blinked.
'Hmm, asking for help? That might be a good idea. But this is a state
secret, who could I possibly... huh?'
Shirone's eyes remained open and still. From a distance, one might think
he was dead, so static was his demeanor.
Maya didn't notice Shirone's change.
She wanted to comfort him. Not just anyone, but herself. To be his
woman.
She felt herself leaning towards Shirone. The thought crossed her mind
if this was really okay, but if he didn't want it, he would have rejected her when she held his
hand.
Maybe Shirone was waiting for what would come next?
Gaining objective courage from her subjective thoughts, Maya slightly
parted her plump lips and moved towards Shirone's.
"Shirone, maybe I..."
"Divine Particle."
"Huh?"
Incomprehensible words spilled from Shirone's mouth.
Receiving help. There are limits to what one can do alone.
The faint light in his mind amplified at an incredible speed, sending
chills down his spine. Finally, a refreshing clarity filled his mind as if a blockage had been
cleared.
"Maya!"
"S-Sorry! I just..."
Maya hastily curled up. Only after regaining her senses did she realize
what she had been about to do. It was a situation ripe for misunderstanding, or maybe she really was a
pervert?
Shirone set the mug down with a loud clink and stared intently at
Maya.
"Um, Shirone, you see, the reason I..."
Without giving her a chance to explain, Shirone hugged her.
Maya's eyes widened. Her head felt light, and a sense of euphoria washed
over her, as if she had the whole world.
"Thank you, thank you so much! I finally figured it
out!"
Shirone, shaking Maya's shoulders vigorously, grabbed his crossbody bag
and dashed off like a man possessed.
"Sorry! I have something to do! See you later!"
As Shirone stumbled and nearly fell while running at full speed, Maya
stood up abruptly. But before she could call out, he regained his balance and disappeared to the other
side of the park.
Maya heard the sound of her own heartbeat. Today wasn't just a lucky
day; it was a jackpot.
With a happy smile, Maya shouted.
"Shirone! Do your best! Fighting!"
Shirone responded by raising his fist in the air.
