Chapter 890:
Desperate Measures (4)
The Valkyrie 2nd Corps, stationed in various locations, rapidly marched
towards the Kanian Highlands.
Morale was at its lowest.
"Damn it! Is this the result of dedicating my life to training?
Becoming meat shields for the demons?"
"Didn't you hear the general? It's war, after all. We're not being
dragged with our hands tied; we're going to fight."
"That's nonsense. Those who will survive will survive. You heard
about the magic division lieutenant colonel, right?"
Transfers in the military are common, but in situations requiring noble
sacrifices like this, they were a potent poison.
"I heard she's connected to the top brass. Must be nice, fighting
when she wants and pulling out when she wants."
"She's a woman, right? No surprise there. How many guys did she
have to sleep with to save her own skin..."
Someone interrupted.
"Hey, infantry."
The members of the 6th Battalion of the Magic Division walking beside
them showed their hostility.
"Watch your mouth. Or you'll get it ripped open."
The infantry, seeing the insignia on their uniforms, closed their mouths
with displeased expressions.
'Damn mages.'
Even with the same rank, the treatment of infantry and mages in the
military was worlds apart.
"Did I say something wrong?"
Normally, it would have ended there, but the stress of impending death
made the infantry extremely sensitive.
"Just because you can use magic, don't get cocky. If your superior
ran away, you should be ashamed. Or what? Did you get some crumbs from that woman too?"
"What? That woman?"
Beric from the Magic Division broke military law and left the line to
grab the infantryman's neck.
"Say that again. That woman?"
"Ugh! Let go of me!"
In close quarters, the infantryman had ways to respond, but he didn't
want to get into a mess.
"Go back, idiot. If you want to die, die alone. Don't drag me into
it."
"Why? You were all excited to insult the lieutenant colonel, but
now you don't want to die?"
The company commander's voice thundered down.
"What's going on!"
Beric's comrades from the Magic Division restrained him, and the
infantrymen turned pale.
The infantry company commander confirmed that a soldier from the Magic
Division had left the line.
"Are you crazy! War isn't a joke! You two are out! I'll have you
court-martialed!"
With discipline being all the Valkyrie could rely on, the company
commander showed no mercy.
"Stop it."
All the commanders had the same thought, so the company commander
couldn't hide his anger and turned his head.
"Ah..."
Corps Commander Garcia was sitting on his horse.
"Salute!"
He had been patrolling the units along the long line and had stopped by
the 6th Battalion.
'So this is how it is.'
Even with the authority of the Valkyrie Commander, saving just one
person was all he could do.
'It would have been better if there were no exceptions...'
Through many battles with the demons, he realized that the Commander's
strategy transcended human limits.
'There must be a reason.'
Whether it was personal feelings or a larger strategic picture, he had
no complaints about the Commander's judgment.
"What's your position?"
Garcia asked Beric.
"Corporal Beric! 3rd Squad Leader of the 6th Battalion! My role is
long-range support and area elimination with fire magic!"
"Protecting your superior's honor is right. But breaking military
law won't get you what you want."
"Sorry!"
A soldier can't speak at length, so he poured all his emotions into one
word.
Garcia saw the determination in Beric's eyes to accept any
punishment.
"Return to the line. If this happens again, you'll be executed
immediately."
"Yes!"
As Beric quickly ran back to the line, his comrades sighed in
relief.
"Are you crazy? Why did you do that? Don't you know the unit's
morale is terrible right now?"
"I acted without thinking. Don't worry. I won't make this mistake
again."
"You... really liked the lieutenant colonel, huh?"
They had sensed it when Amy took his resolution letter, but they didn't
realize he was this serious.
"It's over. It's all in the past."
Beric's expression, lost in thought, returned to that of a
soldier.
"It's better this way. I can't protect her, and she's the lover of
one of the Five Great Stars of the Ivory Tower..."
Thinking their worlds were separate was enough.
"Engagement!"
A voice came from the front.
"Engagement! Engagement!"
Reports came from the front of the line, and all the soldiers
immediately took combat positions.
"What? Suddenly?"
They were moving while avoiding the demon units as much as
possible.
"45 degrees to the right!"
As the soldiers turned their heads at the repeated command, a loud
explosion was heard.
"Kiaaa!"
Demons suddenly burst out from the forest on the high hill, running down
the slope.
"Magic Division! Take positions!"
The number of demons was only a few hundred, and they were annihilated
by the army's firepower.
"Stragglers? But there hasn't been a battle?"
While keeping an eye out for additional forces, many eyes watched the
forest as the grass rustled.
"They're coming! Prepare!"
As the company commander was about to give the order to fire, the
branches parted, revealing Amy.
"Phew, found you."
In a place where thousands of lungs were breathing, not a single breath
could be heard.
"Lieutenant Colonel..."
The first to recognize her were her direct subordinates in the 6th
Battalion, and soon others spoke up.
"Who is that? The uniform is Valkyrie."
"Make way."
As Garcia, accompanied by high-ranking officers, rode forward, the
troops parted like water.
Garcia was also pleased to see her, but his expression remained
cold.
"Lieutenant Colonel, why are you here?"
Amy pulled out an official document from her pocket and saluted.
"Reporting! Lieutenant Colonel Karmis Amy! I have been ordered to
return to the 6th Battalion of the Magic Division as of this moment!"
"What?"
The soldiers of the 6th Battalion looked at each other and raised their
arms in cheers.
"Woohoo!"
It felt like they had won a war.
An excited Beric pointed at the infantryman he had grabbed earlier and
shouted.
"See that! This is the Magic Division! This is us!"
The infantryman, who had been watching Beric, smirked and
shrugged.
Seeing the visible rise in morale, Garcia also regained his
smile.
"Ten-minute break."
It was the most he could offer in such a busy situation, and the
division commander raised his hand and shouted.
"With this, there are no exceptions in the 2nd Corps! We fight
together, die together, and win together! We are one!"
"We are one!"
Though unintended, Amy's return breathed life into the despairing
unit.
As Amy slid down the hill and joined the line, her subordinates gathered
around her.
"Lieutenant Colonel! What happened? Are you really
back?"
Amy raised her fist.
"Of course. I said I'd be back soon. Did you really... not believe
me?"
"Haha! How could we? We firmly believed that only you could lead
the strongest 6th Battalion."
"Always with the words..."
Amy approached Beric, who was standing with a sullen expression, and
poked his side.
"Hey, did you miss me? Huh? Did you miss me?"
"Ah, stop it!"
Contrary to expectations, Beric responded grumpily, making Amy blink her
big eyes and ask.
"What's with him? Does he have a problem with me?"
"Haha! He's just too happy. Actually, he had a bit of a scuffle
with that infantryman over there..."
"Ah! Ah! Ah! Ah!"
Beric quickly shouted to cut off the conversation, frowning
fiercely.
"If you're here, you're here. Let's not make a fuss. It's
embarrassing."
Amy laughed.
"What's this? You're the one making a fuss. Anyway, be prepared. I
won't let you off if you fall behind."
As Amy took the lead, a comrade quietly approached Beric.
"What's wrong with you? I thought you'd be the
happiest."
"Who said I wasn't?"
He watched Amy's back as she walked away.
"Why?"
Why did she come back?
'We're going to die anyway. You have a glamorous life waiting for
you...'
He didn't know the reason, but Beric felt a different kind of victory
compared to the other soldiers.
