Chapter 487:
"Plu?"
Crude's eyes widened.
Plu was part of Shirone's team, which operated on a completely different
path from the rebels.
How had she ended up here?
As Crude ran towards her, Kanya and Lena followed, with Babel hovering
protectively over them.
"Plu!"
When Crude arrived, she was already collapsed on the ground.
"Damn…"
Plu was wearing the attire of a citizen, clearly stolen.
But even that was tattered, revealing numerous wounds.
'She went through hell.'
It looked like she had fallen from a great height, with bruises covering
her shoulders, thighs, and calves.
"Crude…"
Hearing her faint voice, Crude quickly shouted,
"I'm here. How did you get hurt like this?"
"Shehakim…"
"Shehakim?"
Crude's face turned incredulous.
Had she run that incredible distance in this condition?
"I have urgent news… very urgent…"
Plu couldn't finish her sentence and fainted again.
Realizing the severity of her condition, Crude turned to Kanya and
Lena.
"We need to move her. To the Nor medical room!"
Babel immediately carried out the order.
With the rebels united, skilled Norin mages were on standby at the
headquarters.
They filled a large tank with water and submerged Plu, casting the water
healing spell Orhiri, which began to heal her wounds.
After three hours, Plu was moved to the recovery room, her breathing
steadier than before.
Crude, who had rested for about an hour, opened the door to the recovery
room and looked at her.
'Plu…'
From the first time he saw her, she had occupied a place in Crude's
heart.
Was there a reason?
Maybe, but did it matter?
Her stubborn appearance matched her sharp personality.
Everyone on Shirone's team had a mysterious aura, but to Crude, Plu was
the most enigmatic.
A human perfectly specialized in a social system created by humans,
something not found in heaven. In Shirone's world, such people were called professionals.
But at this moment, the sharp edge he usually felt from her was
absent.
Perhaps that's why a sleeping woman is beautiful.
Crude's hand slowly reached for her forehead.
He justified it by wanting to check her temperature, but his wish was
thwarted when Plu's brow furrowed.
"Ugh!"
Quickly withdrawing his hand, Crude spoke as Plu slowly opened her
eyes.
"How are you feeling? Are you alright?"
"Where am I?"
As soon as she regained consciousness, Plu connected her awareness to
her memory.
Then she quickly scanned her surroundings.
A room with the characteristics of Mecca. And Crude looking down at
her.
'I've arrived. I don't remember anything after entering Matei.'
It was a distance she couldn't have covered with sheer willpower
alone.
Without the help of the algorithm, she would have been caught and killed
by the giants.
But that relief was nothing compared to the overwhelming sense of
failure.
She had failed her mission.
Her task was to safely transport Shirone to Araboth.
Failing to fulfill her role as a supporter shattered the pride of a top
graduate of the Royal Magic School.
Seeing Plu trembling with clenched fists and a tight-lipped expression,
Crude couldn't say anything.
Perhaps he had hoped for a word of thanks for saving her life, but Plu's
mind was consumed with guilt over her failure and how to make up for it.
Having made up her mind, Plu stood up abruptly.
Though her wounds had healed, the pain hadn't completely subsided, but
she gritted her teeth and endured it.
"What's the situation? How far have we pushed?"
As the conversation shifted to the war, Crude set aside his emotions and
spoke.
"We've pushed more than halfway. The distance to Araboth is about
40 kilometers. But the battles from now on will be different."
The lack of angelic activity and the rebels' unexpectedly strong
firepower had contributed to today's success, but it wasn't just a matter of brute force.
"To be honest, the enemy's defense perimeter was too wide. But as
we approach the center of the concentric circles, the defense range will narrow, and the enemy density
will increase."
"Probably. Can we break through?"
"In my opinion… it's impossible. Even if the advance distance is
the same, the enemy density will be at least three times higher. We won't be able to break through like
we did yesterday."
Crude spoke frankly.
This was also why he couldn't give Kanya and Lena hope for
survival.
"Hmm, I see."
"We can't afford to drag this out. Once heaven's interception
system Aegis is restored, bombardments will rain down from all sides. A quick, decisive battle is the
strategy I'm considering."
Realizing the weight of Crude's words, Plu turned her head.
"Do you have a plan?"
"We'll concentrate the rebels in Jebul. We'll use Titans to break
through heaven's walls and gather there. Then we'll advance to Araboth."
Jebul was a city of powerful angels, but currently, there was no angelic
activity.
It was a somewhat risky strategy, but it was the only way the rebels had
any chance of success.
"But didn't you say you had something to tell me?"
He had heard her say that just before she lost consciousness.
But Plu remained silent, deep in thought.
'Sein…'
Just before departing for heaven from Purgatory, Plu had received two
special orders from Sein.
One was to transport the Terminator to Araboth.
This mission was to be prioritized above all else.
Sein had also given her another task.
"If Shirone arrives safely at Araboth, or if any variables occur
and you fail to transport him or leave the team, you must find the rebel commander."
"Crude?"
"Inform him about God's punishment. If the second situation occurs,
the outcome of the war will likely depend on Shirone. It's best to use this information, but by then, it
won't matter if you reveal it."
It was a calculated yet human decision by Sein.
Once God's punishment was activated, there was no way to stop it.
Heaven would be destroyed, and a massive explosion would occur.
If the rebels were concentrated near Araboth, they too would be
doomed.
Despite this, the reason for keeping it hidden was a deeply selfish
desire to ensure the project's success.
Plu wasn't afraid.
No matter the criticism, a mage carries out their mission.
Knowing her personality, Sein had entrusted her with the role of the
villain.
"Before spreading the plan, I have something to say."
Without hesitation, Plu revealed everything.
Shirone's magic would destroy heaven, and the spell would be cast no
later than noon tomorrow.
Whether it succeeded or not, it was information the rebels needed to
risk their lives for.
Crude's face turned pale upon hearing the truth.
The land of victory, Araboth, had suddenly become a land of
death.
Advance and die, retreat and die.
They had been used.
Hundreds of thousands of rebels, all because of a few people from the
land of earth, were now marked for death.
"From the beginning… we were deceived."
Regardless of his true feelings, Plu didn't blink.
"As the chairman said, it's not a bad proposal for the rebels.
Didn't we risk our lives to win the war? If so…"
"Do you call that a proposal?"
Crude, abandoning his usual composure, shouted.
"Do you think sacrificing your life for a cause is the same as
being killed like a dog by someone else's hand? Then what have we been fighting for?"
Plu remained silent.
There was no way to refute it. That had been the plan from the
start.
"So what do you want me to do now? Tell all the soldiers? Tell them
that death awaits them at Araboth? Even now, they can't sleep because of fear! How am I supposed to tell
them that?"
"That is…"
Plu gritted her teeth.
Desperately trying to steady her mind, she looked Crude in the
eye.
"That's not my concern."
Crude's eyes filled with murderous intent.
Even under his deadly glare, Plu remained defiantly composed.
"Damn it! Damn it all!"
Crude knocked over everything in the recovery room and walked to the
door.
The sound of the door slamming shut echoed his emotions.
Only after Crude left did Plu grimace in pain from her injuries.
'How far has Shirone gone?'
Or had he even survived the fairies' bombardment?
If he was alive, she had to go to Araboth to complete her final mission
as a supporter.
She took out her phoenix from her cubrick.
Her stamina was severely depleted, but as long as she could move, she
couldn't stop.
'Just wait a little longer, Shirone…'
As Plu left the rebel headquarters, Crude stood before the voice
transmission device in the command center.
He had to speak.
They were fighting to win, but at least they should be able to choose
their own deaths.
"Attention all headquarters personnel."
Crude's voice was transmitted to each room.
The sound echoed through the night, reaching Plu's ears faintly.
"I have an important announcement. At dawn, the war will resume,
and we will ultimately win. But…"
Crude paused.
He couldn't bring himself to say it.
Once the order was given, the information would spread rapidly through
wireless communication, and tens of thousands of rebels would spend the night fighting the fear of
death.
Even as a commander, did he have the right to plunge so many people into
despair?
As the silence lengthened, the awakened soldiers looked at the speakers
with anxious eyes.
It was clearly not good news, and their eyes gradually filled with
unease.
"…Let's fight."
Crude's voice, sounding choked, continued.
"Let's fight to the end."
Gritting his teeth and squeezing out the words, Crude stared into the
darkness.
He couldn't give up here.
If he told the rebels the truth, despair would spread among them.
That would immediately weaken their strength, and those fighting with
the resolve to die would only face miserable defeat.
'I will take responsibility for everything.'
Even if he became the worst commander in history, even if he was called
a demon, he had to lead everyone into the fray.
With hot tears streaming down his face, Crude shouted,
"We will win! We will win and reclaim our freedom! Today is the
most glorious day in human history! Let's give up our lives and fight!"
"Yeahhhh!"
The resolve of the highest commander of the rebels was more effective
than a brief sleep, and the soldiers in countless barracks cheered.
The massive sound, as if the entire radius was shouting, reached Plu's
ears as she left the headquarters.
'We will fight. We will fight to the end.'
Despite the pain from her bruised body, she didn't slow down.
In the distance, she could see the faint light of dawn breaking over the
horizon.
The last night in heaven was coming to an end.
