Chapter 499:
Despair (2)
2nd-tier monster Furaka.
A giant reptile with three heads, each spewing fire, venom, and
electricity, it was the ultimate beast.
As the most powerful offensive monster in Zulu's summoning magic, Furaka
was not intimidated even by the three-sided Mara, Kali.
Venomous fumes spread, the colors of fire filled the air, and the white
light of lightning flashed.
Every time the scenery flashed, the wallpaper-like patterns on Kali's
shadowy body were revealed.
The patterns, resembling whirlpools, absorbed Furaka's magic and emitted
it as black smoke.
It was the aura of death.
"Foolish human."
Kali swung the sword of death, severing all three of Furaka's heads
simultaneously, and charged at Zulu.
Kali's four weapons—a sword, shield, whip, and skull—each symbolized
death, with effects of cutting, disease, pain, and decay, all deadly to humans.
"I will take your life."
As Kali's whip wrapped around Zulu's wrist, intense pain surged through
her.
The disappearance of Furaka's corpse meant the familiar had been
broken.
But Zulu's expression remained unchanged.
She revealed nothing.
Expression is a form of communication possible only when there is
someone to observe it.
Even while wrapped in the whip of pain, Zulu's calm demeanor made Kali
frown.
"What an absurd human."
It wasn't that she was enduring or hiding her pain.
It was as if there was a deep hole in her heart, sucking in all
emotions.
"Death comes to all."
Leading with the skull, Kali charged, swinging her sword and
shield.
As the aura of disease worsened Zulu's condition and the aura of decay
rotted her cells, the black sword traced a bizarre path toward Zulu's neck.
Phosmetry.
A golden temporal bird soared to the ceiling, and the blade passed
through Zulu's neck.
Having shifted to another space-time, Zulu freed herself from the whip
and retreated to the end of the trailer.
Focusing while wrapped in the whip of pain was impossible for a normal
human.
Kali reconsidered Zulu.
'Severely lacking.'
Absolute denial.
She denied pain.
She denied death.
She even denied herself.
Standing at the center of the cycle, she had no clear substance, making
even pain unreal.
It was the complete opposite of Gaold's extreme endurance and thus
inhuman.
Suck, suck, suck, suck, suck.
The only change in Zulu, as she looked at Kali, was the gradually faster
sound of sucking on the pacifier.
"Pitiful human. How did you fall to such a state?"
'I can't win like this.'
Faiel's three horned Mara was the nemesis of all living things.
'Must I abandon life?'
A ridiculous strategy, but it was the first thought that came to Zulu's
mind.
Suck.
With one sharp sound, Zulu stopped sucking on the pacifier.
As the thoughts that circulated everything ceased, Zulu's true face was
revealed for the first time.
'Truly remarkable.'
Kali realized that the opponent she had been fighting was merely the
shell of a human named Zulu.
The true essence of Zulu, hidden in the unbroken cycle, was akin to
Kali, the god of death.
Gloomy and, in some ways, mystical Middle Eastern words flowed from
Zulu's mouth like the first verse of a song.
"The greatest fear for humans is not death."
Kali understood Zulu's language as if it were her native tongue.
"Is that so? Then what is your greatest fear?"
"A life burdened by death."
Zulu summoned her familiar.
"Erga."
A black bird was summoned and perched on Zulu's shoulder.
It was the first 10th-tier monster she had summoned at the age of
three.
Kali looked puzzled at the small, unimpressive familiar.
"Do you think you can face me with just that?"
Zulu ignored her and continued.
"Humans are strange beings. They know death but deny it. In
reality, there is no death in their lives. Even knowing they could die at any moment, they believe it
will never happen."
"Are you saying you are different?"
"Death…"
Zulu looked at Erga flying above her head, then turned her gaze.
An indescribable gloom filled her eyes.
"Death has always been on my back."
"Nonsense."
Kali prepared to charge again, leading with the skull.
Erga blocked Zulu's path, opening its broad beak to mimic Zulu's
words.
"Death has always been on my back."
Kali's advance halted.
Erga's voice was identical to Zulu's.
"This monster was the first creature I successfully summoned. Its
name is Erga."
"So? Are you planning to die together?"
"Summoning magic is performed through a familiar. There are three
stages: imprinting, empathy, and annihilation."
Zulu looked coldly at Erga, who was mimicking her.
"Erga was the first creature to come to me. We were always
together. That's imprinting. We got to know each other well. That's empathy. And finally…"
When Zulu turned back to Kali, her face was no longer normal.
Extreme loneliness.
Her expression was as empty as the landscape of a planet visited by no
one since its birth.
Kali realized the source of the unease she had felt when Zulu first
revealed her true self.
There was no love.
She was a human created with perfect emotional deprivation.
"Shall I tell you something interesting?"
Tears of blood began to flow from Zulu's upturned eyes.
"I was three years old at the time. I'm not sure, but it was around
then. And I…"
Erga's black body began to crack and crush.
The black mass, like ground meat, writhed unpleasantly and began to
unravel into a massive form.
Screeeech!
A grotesque scream shook Zion as a 10-meter-tall lich revealed
itself.
The robe had an unusually long waist, and its head was bowed low.
The skeletal fingers dangling from the long sleeves were eerie.
The robe, absorbing nearly 100% of the light, made it seem as if a hole
had been torn in space.
Though called a 1st-tier monster, Zulu had never encountered a lich in
her life.
Thus, the lich was a projection of herself.
It was the embodiment of her twisted desire, reflected in the creature
she had first met and cruelly killed.
"Erga!"
Clutching her chest and shedding tears of blood, Zulu's face mirrored
the soulless face hidden within the lich's robe.
Whoooosh!
Kali felt an unfamiliar emotion from the lich.
A vast fear she had only experienced from her master, Faiel.
Death is the end for living beings, but it is not the end of
everything.
The true end lies beyond death. Eternal solitude.
The aura of that solitude emanating from the lich made Kali
tremble.
"Ergaaaaa!"
Zulu's voice, distant and haunting, seemed to come from the
underworld.
I killed it.
Believing that taking its life would keep it by her side forever, she
had cruelly strangled her first friend.
'This can't be.'
Kali's expression twisted in horror as she swung her four weapons
simultaneously.
'No human can overcome the fear of death.'
Death is the ruler of living beings.
"Perish!"
Kali's sword slashed through the lich's robe, but it was her sword that
was cut.
There was nothing to cut in the empty void.
Whoooosh!
The lich extended its skeletal hands, enveloping Kali.
"This is quite… interesting."
Nameless, the Nephilim born from the angel of fulfillment and Garas,
muttered outside the barrier of Armin's Stop.
In Armin's world, he was the only one presumed to have reached the realm
of light speed.
For Nameless, born with endless ambition, it was a state beyond dozens
of barriers.
But for someone with far superior insight than humans, the barriers were
only a matter of time.
"Oh. Aha. I see."
Armin shuddered as he watched Nameless nodding with his sword
lowered.
Though he hadn't taken any action, the constant gleam in his eyes was
unsettling.
Growth? Awakening? Evolution?
It was clear that he was rapidly devouring and approaching a state that
no human concept could explain.
'Damn it! At this rate…!'
As Armin considered taking a gamble, a dark wind swept in beside
Nameless.
Clang!
Nameless easily blocked Kuan's attack and countered.
Like a scholar taking a break from long research, Nameless's face was
serene as he pushed Kuan back.
'Damn bastard.'
Kuan was annoyed by his expression.
In a life-or-death struggle, Nameless looked as if he were
half-asleep.
To Nameless, was the clash of swords just child's play?
'Do you mock the sword?'
Kuan vibrated, drawing external force from all directions.
His body, vibrating left and right, suddenly passed by Nameless.
"Ugh!"
Dozens of wounds appeared on Kuan's body, and blood spurted out.
Rolling on the ground to regain his balance, he glared at Nameless in
disbelief.
"This… was too shallow."
Nameless shook the blood off his blade and turned his head.
"Next time, it will be deeper."
Kuan gritted his teeth.
The dozens of small wounds he had sustained in exchange for avoiding a
fatal blow shredded his pride in his swordsmanship.
As Kuan sprang forward, Nameless charged.
The two swordsmen clashed briefly, but within seconds, more wounds
appeared on Kuan's body.
Siena, watching the battle from within the Stop barrier, quickly stood
up.
"We need to go out!"
Kuan's skills were not enough to subdue Nameless.
Even if it meant risking their techniques being stolen, it was best to
try to win early.
At that moment, Armin grabbed Siena by the waist and pulled her to the
ground.
"Brother?"
"……"
Siena realized what Armin was thinking from his silence.
"Brother! Let go!"
No matter how much she struggled, Armin wouldn't release her.
Nameless, who had already mastered the flicker spell, wouldn't let any
of the three escape.
If Kuan could create an opportunity, the best option was to flee with
Siena.
"Brother! Brother!"
Siena struggled harder to break free from Armin.
But suddenly, she froze, eyes wide, staring ahead.
Nameless, who had just slashed Kuan, had a smile on his face.
"Ah, this is the taste."
Kuan stood with his back turned for a moment, then turned around with a
bitter expression.
Finally, a fatal blow—Kuan's side split open, and bright red blood
gushed out like a fountain.
"Sigh."
A sigh of frustration escaped his lips.
"This is driving me crazy."
