Chapter 24:
The Cold Boy and the Hot Girl (3)
Amy was handed bread and coffee. Then, as if watching a performance, applause erupted
from all around.
"Don't bring me this kind of stuff anymore since I won't need it from now on! Got
it?"
"Huh? Oh, yes."
Did this mean her anger had subsided? Shirone, who thought he wouldn't have to see her
again, sighed in relief.
But ultimately, Amy's words only caused greater misunderstanding among the students.
"She accepted it! Amy accepted Shirone's feelings!"
"She even said she doesn't need coffee anymore. Does that mean he can just come as
he is?"
Amy, as if unwilling to respond, entered the library. Seriel quickly followed her, unable
to hide her excitement.
"Hey! What are you planning to do? If it's true, it's great, but did you really mean
it that way?"
"Ugh, I don't know! Think whatever you want. Don't you have anything better to do?
I'm done with this once I graduate anyway. Let them play their childish games among themselves!"
Shirone, descending the library stairs, felt so lighthearted he could almost fly. Ten
days of hardship. Honestly, he hadn't expected it to end so quickly. From tomorrow onward, he could head
straight to the library without wasting time.
"Yay! I'll have time to read one more book!"
Shirone, who had supposedly captured Amy's heart. The male students, lost in such
delusions, watched Shirone's joyful sprint with envy.
***
11 p.m.
Amy closed her book and sighed. She had never found studying enjoyable, but she loved the
library. Compared to mingling with people who gossiped about others' love lives or cared more about others
than themselves, this place was far better.
Even as midnight approached, the library was still crowded with students. They had no
interest in who was dating whom. They were racehorses, sprinting madly toward their dreams.
Observing them made her realize that even getting lost in idle thoughts was a waste of
time.
Yet, she remained the top-ranked student in Class Four, and the thought of being
overtaken never crossed her mind.
Seriel, sitting beside her, turned her head.
"Huh? Why are you closing the book? Are you leaving already?"
"Yeah. I'm feeling a bit troubled today. I'll just rest properly and wake up an hour
earlier tomorrow."
"Really? Then let's go together."
"No. You don't need to rest just because of me. See you tomorrow."
"Alright, then. Get some rest. Make sure to wake up early tomorrow."
Amy smiled as she packed her bag. Fortress Seriel was the only friend she had made in six
years at school. Though as much of a chatterbox as any other girl her age, she knew when to get serious. Her
grades were also stable at sixth place in Class Four, ensuring she could safely enter the graduating class.
Amy liked Seriel, who enjoyed mature competition over shallow friendships.
After cheering her friend on, Amy left the reading room.
Except for the person on duty, everyone had left, leaving the hallway silent. For
students, the school was a place of dreams, but for the staff, it was just a workplace to earn money.
Amy stretched to relieve the day's fatigue as she descended the stairs. Then, in the
darkness of the stairwell, she noticed someone standing there.
Pale skin with dark circles under the eyes. Black hair covering the cheeks. Recognizing
the man's face illuminated by moonlight, Amy's expression twisted instantly.
It was Ardius Jake.
A noble of the second rank, his family head was the royal treasury executor. Given the特殊性
of managing the royal finances, the family's influence was said to rival that of the first rank.
Jake was arrogant and prickly. But the real reason Amy disliked him was that he was a
student with little talent who didn't even bother to work hard.
Though they attended the same Class Four, Jake was far from the level required to enter
the graduating class. Moreover, rumors circulated that he was the leader of a research group called the
Black Magicians.
In Amy's opinion, the Black Magicians' field of study was figuring out how to torment
students more talented than themselves.
Since it wasn't a school-approved research group, it received no funding. In short, it
was a dark circle of students plagued by inferiority complexes who bullied their betters.
Of course, they didn't openly pick fights or extort money. Most students were children of
high-ranking nobles, and acting like back-alley thugs would get them expelled immediately.
Yet, rumors persisted that some students had fallen victim to the Black Magicians,
proving they were undoubtedly active in some form.
"Amy, you finished early today. I was planning to wait until midnight."
"Why were you waiting for me? Don't you have anything better to do?"
"Don't be like that. I heard the rumors. You opened your heart to some nobody named
Shirone, right?"
Amy sighed, pressing her forehead. Was he out of his mind? Coming all the way to the
library to spout such pathetic nonsense.
Jake smiled lazily and held out a cup of coffee. Amy recognized it just by the
scent—coffee made from civet beans, popular among nobles.
"I warmed it up for you. Someone like Amy deserves nothing less. Would you dirty
your mouth with cheap coffee?"
Amy stared at the coffee, then looked up in disbelief.
"What are you doing?"
"Exactly what it looks like. Courting you. You know well the power of my family.
I'll eventually take over my father's role managing the royal treasury. Doesn't that make me the perfect
match for you?"
"Sorry, but I don't engage in private chatter in the library. So, you're wrong about
the place, the timing, and—oh, I forgot the most important part—the person. I dislike you. So let's both be
careful to avoid this kind of thing in the future."
Amy sidestepped him and continued downstairs. Jake blocked her path by pressing his hand
against the wall.
"What do you think you're doing?"
"Was I too gentle? I have my family's dignity to uphold, and this won't do. Isn't it
the same for you? Aren't you studying like mad to avoid tarnishing the Carmis family name? We share the same
struggles."
Amy scoffed. No matter how prestigious the Ardius family was, the Carmis family wasn't
one to be dragged into their petty squabbles. Above all, she disliked Jake's tone.
"A money-grubbing miser like you."
"What did you say?"
"My family lives by doing whatever we want. We're qualitatively different from
someone like you, who brags about counting money under your father's shadow."
Jake's eyes reddened with anger. Even a first-rank noble couldn't speak to him so
insolently.
My father is the royal treasury executor. Money solves everything. Amy was the one acting
like a spoiled brat.
"Is the Carmis family that great? How many of you are actually in power? With my
family's influence, we could crush the Carmis family with money. How about it? Want to test that if you
don't believe me?"
Amy shivered at Jake's murky aura. He was the kind of man who would torment others
endlessly for his own advancement. She finally understood why she had felt uneasy all day.
"Whew. Thanks to you, I figured it out. Why I accepted the coffee."
"What?"
"You're different from Shirone. In a word, you're a coward."
"Hah! You think I'm more of a coward than that pathetic guy? Want me to prove it
right now?"
"If you're so eager to do something, hurry up and do it. Don't waste my time
blabbering. Do you think I'll be scared just because you're glaring at me, you coward?"
Amy smacked Jake's mug. The expensive coffee splattered against the wall. The mug
shattered into pieces as it hit the stairs.
