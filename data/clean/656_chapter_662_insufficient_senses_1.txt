Chapter 662:
Insufficient Senses (1)
The members of the Leaf Circus, having completed their scheduled tour
around the Märchen Kingdom, had returned to their hometowns for the winter. Only Shagall and Tia, who
had no family, remained behind, living with Raiden.
"Shagall, come here for a moment."
Wiping the sweat from his brow after practicing circus tricks, Shagall
walked over to Raiden.
"What's up? Do you need me to do something?"
"No. I want to teach you something."
Shagall tilted his head in confusion.
"I thought I already learned all the tricks?"
He was only sixteen, but Shagall was undeniably the most skilled
performer in the Leaf Circus.
"Starting today, you're going to refine your juggling."
"Haha! Boss, did you hit your head or something? You know how young
I was when I first picked up clubs!"
"Bring them over."
At Raiden's serious tone, Shagall fetched a box of clubs. Raiden grabbed
three with one hand and tossed them.
"Heave-ho."
Without even getting into stance, Shagall began to juggle the three
clubs with dazzling precision.
He was so proficient that juggling on a unicycle during performances
came as naturally as breathing. Catching each club in turn, Shagall grinned proudly at Raiden.
"See that? That's a genius at work."
"Yeah. You're a genius."
Shagall scratched his cheek, embarrassed.
"Why the sudden praise? It's just basic juggling."
Of course, Raiden hadn't called him a genius just for spinning three
clubs.
'He absorbs any skill in the shortest possible time.'
Shagall's insight into the essence of a technique was too valuable to
let waste away.
"Do it again. Properly this time."
Sensing the change in mood, Shagall got serious and threw all ten clubs
from the box into the air.
He showed off cross throws, spins, order swaps—flashes of flair. Raiden
watched and then said:
"Pass them to me."
They started two-person juggling, exchanging clubs while continuing
their routines.
'Huh?'
Shagall noticed the clubs weren't coming back. He looked at Raiden and
widened his eyes.
'Is he... locking them in place?'
Raiden was alternating his hands to make the clubs float before his
face.
Two, four, six clubs hung in the air, frozen like pins. Eventually, all
ten were in Raiden's control.
Vwoooom!
The air vibrated like a swarm of bees as Raiden's hands blurred, weaving
between the clubs with afterimages.
"Khhh!"
A groan escaped through clenched teeth, and both Shagall and Tia stared
blankly at what could only be called the pinnacle of juggling.
When the wind suddenly stilled and Raiden's hands stopped, all ten clubs
dropped like strings had been cut.
"Phew. It's been a while—tiring."
"Boss… What was that?"
"Rapidblade Raiden. That's the name I was known by before I became
the leader of the Leaf Circus."
Raiden told the story of how he had once been a famous assassin.
Though he'd long faded from the public's memory, the story was still a
shock to Shagall.
"Boss…"
"Say whatever you want. I've put down the blade, and I intend to
spend the rest of my life here. Dying here wouldn't be so bad. But after watching you… I've changed my
mind. It would be a waste to let your talent end here."
"Talent…?"
"As you just saw, even juggling can become a killing technique. How
you use it is up to you, but I believe you'll make the right choice."
Shagall looked at Tia.
Though she didn't show it, she knew his heart, and she knew they would
eventually share their lives together.
"How can I become like you?"
Raiden raised a single finger.
"Schema."
"Hmm?"
Sensing a presence, Shirone opened his eyes and was startled to see Aria
leaning over him.
"What—what are you doing!"
He looked around in a panic, but Rian had already woken up and was doing
push-ups with his feet on the bed.
"Hehe, you're pretty cute when you sleep, you know?"
When Aria leaned in close, Shirone yanked up the blanket to cover his
bare upper body.
"Could you show some manners? You can't just barge into a guy's
room like that!"
"What? You think I'm gonna eat you or something?"
Judging by the mischievous glint in her eye, it was hard to say what
might've happened if Rian hadn't been there.
"Shirone, hurry up and get ready. Time to eat."
As Rian pointed to the door with his thumb, Aria—who had been staring at
his bulging muscles—snatched away Shirone's blanket.
"Hey!"
"Ahh! What are you doing!"
Shirone quickly covered himself, but then realized that was weirder, so
he just lowered his arms.
"Whoa. You're seriously skinny. You need to bulk up."
That had also been on Rian's mind.
"Yeah, Shirone. From now on, train with me. Even mages need to stay
fit."
Shirone looked at himself in the mirror.
'Am I really that thin?'
He'd never paid much attention before, but as Rian flexed his chest
muscles, the contrast was obvious.
He hadn't had time to train at the Academy, and most mages were skinny
anyway.
If you can fly by concentrating your mind, why run around?
"Strength is everything for a guy. Go on, take a
swing."
Rian thumped his chest and motioned for him to hit it. Shirone narrowed
his eyes, lips pouting.
He briefly considered launching a Photon Cannon.
At this rate, building muscle was hopeless.
"Forget it. I'll get stronger as we travel. I wasn't this scrawny
when I lived in the mountains, either."
He grumbled while grabbing his toiletries, and Aria giggled behind her
hand.
"Anyway, get cleaned up and come down. Today's the closed
evaluation—it'll take longer than yesterday."
Out of 273 candidates, only 54 had passed the first round. They would
have to cut that number by at least half again.
When Shirone arrived in the hall after washing up, all the qualifiers
were scattered around, preparing.
'The Chairman…'
He spotted the masked Lupist and Secretary Jane sitting face to face in
a corner booth.
Etella was sipping tea by the window, and across from her, Kuan was
eating breakfast.
Passing the first test meant you had some degree of expertise.
One person was sharpening arrowheads, another lifting weights, another
prepping traps for their specialty assessment—everyone was getting ready in their own way. But one
individual stood out.
"Good morning, Shirone."
Sitting on the floor with relaxed posture, a young man raised his hand
toward Shirone on the stairs.
'His waiting number was 147.'
Wigg of the Gale.
Twenty-three years old. Specialty: dual blades. A rising talent like the
Knight of Maha, Rian.
"Good morning. Were you able to rest well?"
"Haha, I couldn't sleep a wink. It's an honor to work with an Ivory
Tower candidate, after all."
Despite what he said, there was no trace of nervousness in Wigg's body
language.
And even though it was Rian who would evaluate him, the fact that he'd
approached Shirone instead gave off the faint air of rivalry.
"The Knight of Mach. Is that your sword?"
Wigg turned his head toward Rian, who was scarfing down meat, and
asked.
"Yes. But more than that, he's my one and only friend."
"Hmm, he doesn't look that strong. Do you know how many Schema overlaps
he has?"
Schema is often explained as a body diagram, but it's not like a
blueprint that appears in your head.
More accurately, it's the sensation of control over one's body.
For example, if someone can operate three Schemas, it means they can
simultaneously control three areas of their physical state during a specific action.
Even someone uncoordinated can memorize how to do a backflip with
practice, but a gymnast can instinctively sense the exact placement of fingers or the balance of their
muscles in an instant.
Ultimately, the body is one, but the number of diagrams that excellent
individuals possess is relatively higher—by that standard, Rian's motor ability was notably
lacking.
"Uh… well…"
Shirone hesitated to respond, and Wigg scratched his head as if
realizing something.
"Ah, I'm sorry. I shouldn't be evaluating a judge. That was out of
line."
"No, it's fine to be curious. If you pass, you'll be a strong ally
too."
"Thanks for saying that. Then I'll see you at the second
evaluation."
Wigg picked up the dual swords he had set on the floor and strolled off
to the corner of the hall, sitting down again.
Shirone felt a tinge of guilt after the conversation ended.
'Why couldn't I just say it?'
Rian was strong enough to stand his ground against any swordsman, and
Shirone was proud of his friend's accomplishments.
But he didn't like the idea of his friend being judged by the world's
standards, where failing to open a Schema meant having no talent.
'I'm sorry, Rian.'
Just then, Rian met Shirone's gaze while chewing meat, pointed at the
food, and gave a thumbs-up.
"Hahaha!"
Rian was the best, Shirone thought.
After breakfast, Shirone, Rian, and Aria took their positions in the
guild's training ground to await the second evaluation.
Since the test would showcase the applicants' main survival skills, no
one other than participants was allowed to enter.
"We'll begin. Come in."
At Aria's call, the doors opened, and the first two successful
applicants from the first test—numbers 28 and 29—entered in order.
Unlike before, there was no formal greeting, and while Shirone narrowed
his eyes at them, the two removed their masks.
"What were you thinking?"
Shirone snapped as Lupist chuckled and walked over to the table.
"Apologies if I startled you."
"I'm not surprised—you should've told me in advance. You were the one
who told me to form a mercenary corps."
"Nothing's changed. You're still the leader. Where we're headed is the
Iron Tower, the Living Flower, ruled by the upper class of Radum. We'll need overwhelming force just to
get in."
Shirone couldn't press further, especially since he'd already heard
about the Iron Tower from Aria.
"By the way, what happened with number 213?"
The Regulation Exception user, Aset Meirei.
He hadn't had time to ask her details during the first test, but she had
clearly said she could hear Ra Enemy's voice.
"According to intelligence, there was a massive inquisition in the
Garlon Kingdom four years ago. It was carried out under divine command from the Theraphos Order, their
high clergy. The goal was singular: to find and eliminate the one who hears the voice of God."
"You're saying that's number 213?"
Jane spoke up.
"Meirei's Regulation Exception ability—what we call divine frequency—is
assumed to be the power to detect electromagnetic signals that exist in the world. She heard Ra Enemy's
voice on a specific frequency. We believe this is tied to the inquisition."
No one knew exactly how the evil god Theraphos, known for destroying
entire universes, was connected to Ra Enemy.
Lupist continued.
"Her ability will serve as a compass to purge the lingering history of
Ra Enemy embedded in Radum. Either way, it's useful information for you to know. Make sure she passes
the second evaluation too."
As Lupist and Jane turned to head toward the back door, Shirone called
out.
"Wait. Where are you going?"
When Lupist turned, not understanding, Shirone gestured in front of
him.
"You still haven't demonstrated your main skill. This is a test,
remember?"
"I'm busy. Not really in the mood for jokes."
"I'm not joking. Even if you're the head of the association, no
shortcuts. Come back and take the test properly."
Knowing Shirone's stubbornness since his academy days, Lupist sighed and
walked back to his seat.
"Well then…"
RUMMMMBLE.
The Silver Ring Guild building shook, and the applicants waiting in the
hall looked around nervously.
"What the heck?"
Moments later, the masked 28 and 29 reappeared with annoyed expressions,
opening the door and walking toward a corner table.
