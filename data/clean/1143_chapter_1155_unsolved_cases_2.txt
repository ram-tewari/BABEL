Realizing the impact of Crime Points, Shirone's group looked up at the

As they walked through the village, Shirone noticed that he couldn't
make eye contact with any of the townspeople.
"We have to kill three people."
Dorian was a small village, and in just thirty minutes, they had
gathered all the information they could.
Back at the village entrance, the group sat under a tree to exchange
opinions.
Eden was the first to speak.
"They feel like real people."
Iruki responded,
"But they're not. More importantly, we're missing the key
detail—they resurrect."
Nade agreed.
"Yeah. If the consequences of our actions reset, is there any

Iruki turned to Shirone.
"You decide. We're here to support you, whatever you

Shirone spoke thoughtfully.
"Melkidu must be a world governed by a hidden code—a kind of
simulation."
The group listened attentively.
"High Gear was similar. Back then, players even killed each
other."
Shirone shrugged.
"But this time, it feels different. We're being told to repeatedly
kill unarmed civilians."
Iruki nodded.
"That's the rule of the murderers. It's definitely a system built
with malicious intent."
Nade stepped forward.
"Is that so? Personally, I find it interesting. I mean, it's not
like I enjoy killing people in real life, but as kids, we played war games, right? We'd pretend to slash

They had already experienced far too much brutality.
"Let's just do it. We don't have time to waste. Or should I just
grab someone right now and—"
Just then, Nade pointed to an alley where two young girls were laughing
and chatting.
Watching them for a moment, Nade slowly lowered his hand and pressed his
legs together.
Iruki gave him a scornful look.
"If killing civilians isn't an option, how about hunting criminals?
This place may be made for murderers, but not all of them are pleasure seekers. There must be cowardly
weaklings, mercenaries, and zealots who kill out of blind hatred. If their perspectives exist in this
world, then there must be a playstyle that suits us."
"Oh, right!"
Nade suddenly lifted his head.
"Curtis said the same thing. At first, he only hunted criminals to
earn points. He said it was inefficient, but we don't have the luxury of being picky right
now."
Shirone looked hesitant.
"If a criminal harms someone, I'd fight to protect the victim with
my life. But actively searching for criminals just so we can kill them? That doesn't sit right with
me."
Nade argued back.
"How are we supposed to clear the mission if you overthink
everything?"
Eden sided with Shirone.
"I agree with Shirone. We need to distinguish between intent and
outcome. What he's saying is that we shouldn't normalize this. Right?"
"Yeah. I understand that killing criminals is the best option. But
the moment we start rationalizing it, we become part of Melkidu's system. Eventually, it won't matter
who we kill."
Just like Curtis.
"What about Seina? We need to save her as soon as possible. She's a
real human."
Shirone finally revealed what was on his mind.
"Honestly… I can free everyone right now."
Iruki asked,
"Like in Andre's Labyrinth?"
"Yeah. By fully unlocking the Immortal Function, we can reset this

count as a crime. If we do that, Melkidu will be absorbed back into the real world."
Eden asked,
"What's stopping you?"
"To return with the Immortal Function activated, I'd have to

Shirone's eyes hardened.
"… Gyorgi"

humanity.
"No one understands Gyorgi better than I do. This is probably the

Iruki stood up.
"Alright, now that we've decided, let's begin. Let's ask the
