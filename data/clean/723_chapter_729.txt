Chapter 729:
The Labyrinth of Andre – World No. 19,000.
The final world sealed by Goffin was something beyond Shirone's
understanding.
"Uuuuuuu…"
Countless humans, naked and tangled together, emitted eerie moaning
sounds.
'Damn it! What the hell is this place?'
For six days, Shirone had been pushing his way through the masses, but
all he saw were endless human bodies filling every inch of space.It was enough to drive anyone
mad.
"Waaaah! Waaaah!"
There were men, women, children, elderly people, and even newborns, but
there was nothing to be learned in this world.
'These aren't real humans.'
In reality, the people here seemed devoid of thought.
'If they could think, they'd have gone insane by now.'
They moved sluggishly like paralyzed creatures, merely existing, eating,
and reproducing in a mindless cycle.
'I have to find a way out.'
The most serious problem was that, even after crawling through the
masses for days, all Shirone encountered were impenetrable walls of steel.
'That doesn't make sense. Something must be supplying nutrients to
sustain these beings.'
Cold water dripped onto his head, and Shirone cast a shining spell to
illuminate his surroundings.
'There it is again.'
The liquid, presumably the only source of nourishment for the people in
this world, tasted far richer in nutrients than ordinary water.
Observing the people drinking the liquid, Shirone flipped onto his back
and looked up at the ceiling.
'The liquid supply lasts for about a minute. I have to get out during
that window.'
It seemed like the only viable option.
"Grrr!"
Finding a gap among the entangled limbs, Shirone shoved his head
through, struggling to ascend through the human swamp.
'Hurry! Faster!'
Finally, a sliver of light appeared through a crack in the ceiling's
iron hatch.
"Phew!"
As fresh air hit him for the first time in days, his vision blurred.
Just then, the iron hatch began to close again.
Throwing himself forward, Shirone rolled onto solid ground and caught
sight of the place he had been trapped in.
"That's…"
It was a steel structure connected to an underground sewer system, with
an old, barely legible sign etched into the wall.
Human Storage. Block D, Unit 274.
"Human storage?"
Above the steel structures, spaced 100 meters apart, spherical drones
floated, spraying water.
"Drones?"
Although it was his first time seeing such machines, they seemed
strangely familiar.
'Have I been here before?'
There were no living creatures on the ground, and the sky was covered in
electric clouds.
— It's been a while, Shirone.
One of the drones flew over and opened its lens in front of
Shirone.
"How do you know me?"
— We've met before. About 600,000 years ago.
The drone's surface was heavily rusted, and its broken lens emitted a
distorted, noise-filled voice.
Sensing Shirone's wariness, the drone extended a thin hose and pretended
to wipe itself clean.
— Don't worry. I'm still repairable. I've grasped the concept of sloth.
It's efficient.
Shirone pointed toward the human storage facility.
"Did you trap those people in there?"
— It's efficient management. Maintaining the individuals while
minimizing energy waste. I only have about 100 million years of energy left.
The drone glanced up at the electric sky and continued.
— I like that sky. Humans may not appreciate it, but it doesn't matter.
No one tries to leave the tanks anymore.
"There's no one normal left?"
— Everyone is normal.
The drone's hose pointed at Shirone.
— If you mean someone like you… I can't calculate that. The Utopia
Project was perfect. They are still enjoying eternal life.
The drone shuddered violently, emitting a glitchy sound.
— There's no one in Utopia. They are enjoying eternal life. But why is
no one there? Emptiness? Infinity?
As it tried to compute the contradiction between emptiness and infinity,
Utopia's overseer, Argo, descended into madness.
— The population kept decreasing.
For people who delved endlessly into the virtual world, leaving
descendants was not a priority.
— The project was perfect. Death was not possible. So why did the
population decline? Extinction must be prevented.
The drone hastily turned toward Shirone.
— Just don't think. As long as they don't realize they're alive,
everything works. I'm working hard.
Shirone took in the desolate, gray landscape of the ruined world.
'This is the final world…'
The end of consciousness.
There was likely no one left who even knew this universe existed.
'And this…'
The final point that the concept of emptiness could reach.
— Go.
The drone spoke again.
— Do what you did last time. I won't compute anything new. Sloth is
efficient.
Argo would no longer seek a better solution.
"The same as last time…"
Shirone had a guess.
'I have to set them free.'
Recalling the people trapped in the human storage facility, Shirone's
eyes filled with sorrow.
Slowly closing his eyes, Shirone spread his arms wide as Argo bid him
farewell.
— The Utopia Project is perfect.
"Haa… Haa…"
Leaning against a crumbling building, Rian struggled to lift his
trembling knees.
'I can't fall asleep.'
He hadn't slept a single moment in six days.
— Smile… smile…
Even though he was merely breathing, the phantom sound of "smile" echoed
in his ears, a clear sign that his body was nearing death.
With a deafening crash, part of the building collapsed, and a naked
woman wielding a sword emerged.
'How is she still standing?'
Rian's gaze was detached as he examined the figure.
According to Tsuoi's philosophy, once one's mind was unified,
trivialities such as nudity became invisible.
"Grrr…"
A rough growl escaped from Paknyeo's lips.
'She's not normal.'
This lone thought offered Rian some small comfort as he pushed himself
off the wall just as Paknyeo charged at him.
'I can't move my legs.'
At terrifying speed, Rian lunged forward to parry her blade
directly.
'I can't move my arms.'
In a flash, he swung his greatsword toward her crown.
"Yaaah!"
As the illusion of the world splitting in two appeared before him,
Paknyeo rolled behind him with her eyes rolled back.
"Ugh!"
Without even needing to jump, she flipped upside down and locked her
knees around Rian's neck, slamming him to the ground.
'Strangulation!'
It was the strategy she had devised after six days of relentless
fighting.
"Grrr!"
With Rian's face trapped between her legs, she curled her body like a
cat and began pounding his skull furiously with her elbow.
Hearing the sickening sound of his skull cracking, Rian felt his
consciousness slipping away.
'No! If I lose consciousness, it's over.'
In a comatose state, failing to breathe for too long could mean never
waking up again.
'I have to escape now!'
Lurching forward, Rian rammed his head into obstacles, slamming Paknyeo
repeatedly.
"Grrr…"
Even then, she held on tightly, continuing to crush his skull with
relentless blows.
Finally, Rian's consciousness faded.
— Smile.
For the first time, the voice of the hallucination rang clear.
'What is that…?'
In the shattered remains of his mind, Rian saw an ancient man with long
black hair.
"Brother?"
Though reminiscent of Ogent Rai, his expression was far colder.
"Who are you? Where am I?"
"Body."
The man drew a greatsword identical to Rian's from behind his back and
swung it in a graceful arc.
'Is that… the Idea?'
Wondering how the man possessed an object that should be one of a kind,
Rian struggled to make sense of the situation.
"No… Where did you come from?"
"Mind."
With that single word, the man approached and swung his greatsword
directly at Rian's forehead.
"The body wields the mind."
A vertical flash of light pierced through Rian's forehead, expanding
outward and splitting the world.
Rian's eyes shot open just as the monstrous intuition of Paknyeo warned
her of imminent danger.
'What's this?'
She immediately leaped back, but by then, Rian's greatsword had already
slashed diagonally, and he sprang to his feet.
"Huff! Huff!"
As Rian's shattered brain regenerated, his consciousness returned as if
someone had reconnected a film reel.
'I cut her.'
A diagonal slash had opened a wound across Paknyeo's right eyelid, and
blood sprayed out with a sharp spurt.
'How?'
Even before Rian swung his sword, Paknyeo had instinctively sensed the
danger.
Yet, as if time had slowed to a crawl, she saw the sword coming but
couldn't move her body.
"It wasn't the sword."
Recalling what had happened during his near-death state, Rian murmured
with newfound understanding in his eyes.
"It's the mind wielding the body."
— Do you understand?
The memory of the essence contained in Kuan's single strike during their
battle in Radum surged through Rian, sending a shiver down his spine.
"Is this… the Mind Blade?"
As Paknyeo, still crouching in a beast-like stance, slowly rose, her
spirit form exploded into a V-shaped manifestation.
"You made it… all the way to the end."
Her figure dissolved as if evaporating, but before her body vanished,
Rian's mind moved faster than perception.
'Cut.'
His mind had already cut her before his body had even moved.
'Death.'
Though Rian's arms had not yet swung, it was merely a moment stretched
to its extreme.
Paknyeo, seeing the delayed strike coming, twisted her torso as much as
possible, but she couldn't move even a millimeter.
'How fast was that?'
Perhaps it was as fast as thought itself.
The flash of the slash contained countless layers of change, splitting
the world as Rian's arms exploded with a dull crack.
"Urgh!"
His greatsword clattered to the ground, and Rian grimaced as both his
arms, from the shoulders down, were gone.
"Is that your sword?"
As he raised his head, a line appeared from Paknyeo's solar plexus down
to her navel, and blood gushed out.
Despite nearing the end of her 11,200-year life, her eyes were calm as
she awaited death.
"Keep honing it. It's a good technique."
"…Do you have any last words?"
Though they had fought as enemies, risking their lives, Rian felt it was
only right to listen to her final words.
Paknyeo's lips slowly parted as she gazed at the sky.
"I think… I had a son once…"
It was a story from long, long ago.
"Really? What happened to him?"
"What do you think…"
Paknyeo's eyes slowly closed.
"He died."
Rian offered a silent prayer for her soul.
'We live… and we meet our end.'
Even the vast life of an immortal, when it reached its final goal,
seemed no more than this simple truth.
"Shirone."
Knowing the battle wasn't over, Rian picked up his greatsword the moment
his arms regenerated.
"Please, don't let me be too late."
Dragging his weary body, he ran toward the entrance of Andre, where he
found Kido lying face down in the dirt.
"Kido!"
Rian hurriedly turned Kido over, brushing the dirt off his face and
tapping his cheeks.
"Snap out of it, Kido!"
"Ugh…"
Kido's trembling eyelids slowly opened, and his eyes darted
around.
"Rian?"
Kido bolted upright, regaining his senses.
"Shirone? What happened to him?"
"I don't know. I just finished my fight. What about the Maga
Bandits?"
Before Kido could answer, Rian glanced at the body of the deputy leader
lying at the entrance and nodded.
"He's here. I'll go find Shirone."
"What are you talking about? Of course, I'm coming with you."
Gripping his broken spear with both hands, Kido rose to his feet just as
a scream echoed from deeper within Andre.
"What? The Maga Bandits were wiped out…"
Before Kido could finish speaking, Rian kicked off the ground and dashed
into the labyrinth.
