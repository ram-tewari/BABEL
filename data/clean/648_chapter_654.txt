Chapter 654:
"Ra Enemy?"
Shirone couldn't understand how the man before him knew Ra Enemy.
More than that, he was claiming Shirone had met him—when they hadn't
even met yet.
"How do you know that?"
Every time Shagal swayed his upper body, Rian flinched without meaning
to.
He was undeniably powerful.
Shagal, one of the world's Top 100 Most Dangerous Individuals.
He was born in the Märchen Kingdom west of the Mediterranean, an
illegitimate child in a picture-perfect tourist destination.
Some murderers are born without emotion, but Shagal was a killer
developed by nurture.
Sniff sniff. Sniff sniff.
What he was detecting through Shirone's scent wasn't molecular. It was
the memory of an event.
Just as a particular smell can recall a memory, Shagal could sense the
scent of events themselves.
And that meant one thing: Shagal had met Ra Enemy at least once.
If his memory was accurate, he had killed Ra Enemy more than seven
times.
If his memory was accurate.
"Where is Ra Enemy?"
His raspy voice echoed down the tunnel of the underground sewer,
amplifying its eerie chill.
"I don't know. We're here on a job for Mr. Brooks."
It wasn't just about skill—there was a grim, oppressive presence that
made you not even want to engage with him.
"Brooks?"
He'd been working in the underground sewer since being hired three
months ago, eliminating countless demi-humans.
It had been enjoyable work for Shagal. But delivering Brooks' letters?
That he still didn't like.
After all, the only reason he'd been hired was because there was
evidence of Ra Enemy in Bashka.
Shagal erased the mission from his mind.
"If you won't talk, I'll just ask your body."
"Kh!"
The sewer air, already thick with murderous intent, suddenly
expanded—like thousands of blades stabbing in all directions.
He's coming.
The swaying of Shagal's body grew faster, and in an instant, both his
arms seemed to vanish.
With a boom, as if the air had snapped, Shirone and Rian simultaneously
leapt down the stairs.
Crash! Shagal's twin daggers struck vertically against Rian's
greatsword.
It's fine. I can hold—
But the moment he thought that, the air trembled again, and a barrage of
phantom blades surged in from all directions.
"Grhh!"
Blood burst from his arms and legs, but Rian didn't flinch. He swung the
greatsword in a massive arc.
The sheer force of the strike filled the sewer, and Shagal was launched
like a rubber ball.
He doesn't have a scent.
Shagal twisted like a phantom past Rian, and Shirone readied himself for
battle, magic primed.
Even in a split second, the eerie sway of Shagal's torso left a strange
impression.
But then Rian stepped in front of Shirone.
A thunderous sound echoed down the sewer, and Shagal's eyes flickered in
brief confusion.
What?
It wasn't just speed. It was a reaction bordering on the impossible.
Shagal's sense of smell kicked in again.
An event he had experienced before—
A memory from when he killed Ra Enemy resurfaced.
"Shirone, go. Get to the surface."
There was something too dark, too foul in this man to be just a
mercenary. Rian made the call to send Shirone ahead.
"But…"
"It's fine. The mission comes first."
Knowing Rian's ability, Shirone met Shagal's gaze over his
shoulder.
Could he really get past that man and out of the sewer?
Now!
As Shirone shifted sideways, Shagal's gaze tracked him exactly,
following the trajectory perfectly.
Then his body seemed to unravel like smoke, and his dagger shot straight
toward Shirone's forehead.
What…?
But the dagger only cut through empty air. Shirone had already vanished,
escaping in the opposite direction with a Simultaneous Flicker Burst.
He blasted the iron gate of the sewer open with a Photon Cannon and shot
upward.
Even then, Shagal remained rooted in place, unable to process what had
just happened, staring in the direction Shirone had gone.
Rian rested the greatsword on his shoulder.
"Alright. Now we can go all out."
With no need to protect Shirone anymore, it was like Rian had cast off
mental shackles.
"Heh… Heheheh."
Shagal laughed, shoulders twitching.
He didn't know exactly what they were—but it was clear they had
undergone something similar to him.
"I'll kill you slowly."
No matter where Shirone ran, Shagal's sense of smell would eventually
lead him to his prey.
"Maybe save the boasting for after you kill me?"
Shagal's laughter stopped cold.
For a killer who had murdered over a thousand, there was only one
criterion to judge people:
Kill or don't kill.
And in that instant, Shagal made his judgment of Rian based on that one
criterion.
"You die."
The moment Shagal vanished, Rian leapt forward with his greatsword in
both hands.
A deafening impact shook the sewer.
Hurry! Hurry!
Inside the mansion, Shirone pushed his body to its limit using the
metabolically-enhanced functions of the devourer spirit Kuzen.
There were plenty of guards stationed throughout, but internal security
wasn't as tight as expected.
Likely because the only entrance—the sewer—was guarded by that strange
man.
What was with that guy? Who is he?
Even so, Shirone was worried for Rian.
Where is he?
By expanding his Spirit Zone, Shirone could detect people without
entering rooms. But the mansion was vast—hundreds of rooms.
Woof woof! Woof woof!
As Shirone raced down a corridor near the windows, patrol dogs outside
turned and barked.
Damn it!
You could avoid human eyes, but not a dog's nose.
Especially after meeting that guy who sniffed like a dog in the sewer—it
made things even worse.
"Intruder! Find them!"
An alarm blared throughout the mansion, and the pounding of dozens of
guards' footsteps echoed up the stairs.
Shirone swiftly circled the second floor, then rushed up to the third. A
guard spotted him at the stairs.
"Third floor! After him!"
The skilled guards began closing in fast, but Shirone spotted someone at
the end of the hallway.
Two people?
Had his Spirit Zone been any less sensitive, he might have missed
it—their presences were entangled.
"Protect the master!"
Guards burst out from the opposite side of the hallway.
As enemies closed in from both ends, Shirone dodged their advance and,
just before the blades struck, crashed through the door with his body.
Unlike the hallway, bright light instantly filled his vision.
As Shirone rolled and regained balance, guards stood just outside,
weapons drawn.
"What's all the noise?"
A voice came from the bed. Shirone turned to see a man in his
mid-forties getting out—completely naked.
On the bed sat a blonde beauty, wrapped in the bedsheet.
Brooks glanced at her, clicked his tongue in irritation, and slipped on
a robe.
"And who the hell are you?"
Shirone stared at the short-bearded man, unsure what to say.
He could just say he came to deliver a letter—but with the guards all
watching, handing it over felt too risky.
"Uh, well, the thing is…"
"Shirone?"
The woman on the bed asked with a surprised look, making Brooks turn
with a puzzled expression.
"What? You have another boyfriend?"
The woman said nothing. Still covering herself with the sheet, she
awkwardly got out of bed.
"Shirone! It really is you! Don't you remember me?"
Staring at her face, Shirone blinked in shock.
"Wait… you're…"
"Yes! I'm Aria! Aria, the one you saved!"
Teshia Aria.
Half a year ago, when Shirone had visited the Magic Association, he
happened to share a carriage with this open and outspoken woman.
He remembered her well, because during the terrorist attack carried out
by the Black Revolutionaries at Gold Tower, he had personally rescued her.
'But why is she here?'
Even back then, there were rumors she had two lovers. Still, seeing the
young and beautiful Aria with the middle-aged Brooks was undeniably strange.
Brooks asked, "How do you two know each other?"
"Oh, don't you know who Shirone is? He's a really famous magician now.
Remember that terror attack a while ago? He's the one who saved me."
"Hmph."
After a brief moment of thought, Brooks gestured for his subordinates to
leave.
Once the door closed and only the three of them remained, Shirone, who
knew the contents of the letter, still couldn't bring himself to state the real reason he'd come.
"So, what business brings you here? It doesn't seem like you came to see
Aria."
"I came on a mission from the Silver Ring Guild."
"A letter? Let me see."
Even with Aria standing right next to him, Brooks was completely
unfazed, and Shirone finally pulled the letter from his coat and handed it over.
After reading it, Brooks clicked his tongue, and Aria asked knowingly,
"It's that woman, right? The one from the provinces?"
"She says she's pregnant. Threatening to report me if I don't come down
there soon."
Aria laughed out loud.
"Oh, seriously. You really should be more careful. Poor guy. Looks like
the exciting days of a bachelor end today."
The letter was from one of Brooks's lovers, claiming she had gotten
pregnant after a recent reunion.
The fact that Aria could laugh after hearing such news showed just how
bold she was—and Brooks was thinking the same thing.
'Aria…'
Daughter of one of Tormia's top diplomats, and a brilliant woman who had
passed the national diplomatic exam herself.
He used to think of her as a bored noblewoman playing around—someone he
entertained on the side.
But now he wasn't so sure.
It suddenly occurred to him: maybe he hadn't been playing with
Aria—maybe she had been playing with him.
'I don't want to lose this woman.'
Brooks tucked the letter away and looked at Shirone.
"But why the ruckus? If you'd told Shagal, he would've brought you here
quietly."
Shirone suddenly snapped to attention and shouted, "Ah, right!
Rian!"
Had anyone been in the underground sewer, they would've screamed and
covered their ears at the sound of the deafening impacts.
Two shadows twisted and clashed like a whirlwind through the maze-like
tunnels.
Their offense and defense were almost instinctive, and as time passed,
more and more wounds opened up on Rian's body.
Smile… smile…
The familiar hallucinations whispered, and his wounds began to
regenerate—but each time, Rian felt a revulsion that weighed as heavily as death.
'Something's off.'
Shagal was feeling it too.
A massive greatsword against twin daggers.
At such close range, he should've had the advantage in speed and
reaction.
But Rian's movements ignored inertia. He was unlike any opponent Shagal
had ever faced.
'Then…!'
Shagal's eyes gleamed. He let go of his daggers.
From behind his back, two more daggers slipped into his hands—now four
blades in total flew at Rian all at once.
'Is that even possible?'
He couldn't afford to answer that question. Rian stepped forward to meet
the incoming blades.
Clang! Clang! Clang! Clang!
As metal clashed and sparks flew, their shadows danced like monsters
across the tunnel walls.
Then, in a flash of movement, both realized there was a third shadow
among them.
"Gah!"
Dozens of blades suddenly appeared in front of their eyes, cutting
through the air between them and slamming into the wall behind.
Boom!
As the wall collapsed, the blades vanished—clearly magical rather than
physical weapons.
"So that's who it is…"
A voice echoed from the shadows, accompanied by the sound of
water.
"A big shot's shown up."
"You are…?"
The man stepped forward—pale-faced, with a scar torn across the right
corner of his mouth.
It was Lupist, Head of the Tormia Magic Association.
