Chapter 1038:
Another Belief (1)
Emotion Diesease Case No. 13-465
Paimon's demon realm had expanded its reach beyond the Central
Continent, dominating the Southern Continent.
"Guh… Ugh!"
A hulking man lay sprawled on the grand staircase of a massive mansion,
groaning in agony.
Clovis, a magnate of the Kingdom of Kale.
Ranked second in the kingdom's wealth hierarchy and among the top 100
richest individuals across the Southern Continent, he was a man of immense influence.
"Why, why…"
Clovis's face twisted with anguish.
"Why does it keep hurting!"
First, he killed his maid.Then his friend. Then his 13 wives. Then
his 7 children.
All of it happened within a single day, and just moments ago, he had
gutted Cassie—the being he loved most.
"Could she still be alive?"
Clinging to a shred of sanity through the unbearable pain, Clovis
hobbled down the staircase.
His wives and children, their eyes wide open, lay lifeless on the cold
floor, staring at him.
"Ugh… It hurts. It hurts so much."
The pain was beyond human endurance.
"Cassie, are you still alive?"
A large dog, one of a giant breed, lay sprawled with its guts spilled,
its tongue hanging limply from its mouth.
"Just die. Just die already. I'm so tired."
Every stab with his longsword sent a jolt of pain through his body, as
if his bones were shattering.
"Aaaah! Aaaaagh!"
It wasn't until he could stab no more that Clovis finally confronted the
grim reality of the lifeless dog before him.
"What the hell do you want from me? I've killed them all! What else do I
have to give up to stop this pain?!"
In a fit of rage, he hurled his longsword. It ricocheted off the wall
and struck the shelf holding his priceless porcelain.
"Gah!"
His face turned pale as the porcelain wobbled before shattering on the
floor.
"No! No!"
Panicking, he rushed over and picked up a fragment from among the
hundreds of pieces, his hands trembling.
"Damn it! Do you know how much this costs, you idiot?!"
At that moment, Clovis realized his sense of touch had returned to
normal.
"Money…"
Seeing a glimmer of hope, he shook his head vigorously.
"No. How could I give up the money I've worked so hard for?"
Since the age of seven, he hadn't taken a single day off. He wouldn't
let anyone take his wealth.
"Bastards! Scum! You lowly creatures! Do you think I'd live like one of
you?!"
Yet even the worth of a porcelain shard was nothing but dust. His pain
surged again, sharper than before.
"No, no…"
Terrified, Clovis trembled uncontrollably.
"Please, someone, make it stop. I'll pay 100 million gold—no, 1 billion
gold! Just fix me!"
Please… Please…
"Arrrgh!"
As his threshold increased, the pain doubled in intensity, consuming his
entire body.
"Help me! Somebody, help me!"
Sixteen hours later.
By the time the World Health Organization arrived, the burning mansion
illuminated the dawn sky.
"Phew."
Seriel closed the file for Case No. 13-465 and looked at Clovis's
charred, hardened corpse lying on the bed.
It was like blackened coal.
"Immediate incineration?"
After signing the document prepared by the mental mage, Seriel stood as
the examiner covered the body with a sheet.
"Yes. We contacted everyone, but no one was willing to hold a funeral.
Understandable, really."
It was a lonely death.
"All right. Take care of it."
Seriel's expression remained composed as she left the morgue and
returned to her lab.
She was greeted by a wall covered in sticky notes and a large graph
tracking time and data.
Reality set in.
"Hah…"
The lives of the victims had long since been buried under the hundreds
of reports she received daily.
"Why can't it be cured?"
Her helplessness surged, and in an effort to deny it, she desperately
summoned her rage.
"Argh! This is so frustrating!"
She hurled anything within reach, smashing it against the walls.
'Calm down! You have to stay calm!'
Even though she knew that rage wouldn't solve the problem, her body
refused to obey her mind.
Suddenly, the door to the lab swung open with a loud bang.
"Seriel! What's wrong?"
Shirone stood there, alarmed.
When Seriel came to her senses, the room was in complete disarray, and
blood trickled from a cut on her palm.
"You're hurt! Why are you so upset? This isn't like you!"
As he approached to check her wound, Seriel lowered her arm and spoke in
a trembling voice.
"Shirone…"
Shirone saw the despair in her eyes and realized that the wound wasn't
the real issue.
"Seriel, it's tough, but…"
Before he could finish, Seriel buried her face into Shirone's chest,
clutching his collar tightly as she sobbed.
"Aah… Waaah…"
Her cries were heart-wrenching.
"It's okay."
Shirone gently stroked her back, repeating the only words he could
offer.
"It's okay. Everything will be okay."
How much time had passed?
By the time her tears soaked his clothes, Seriel's breathing had
calmed.
"... I'm sorry."
She held onto his collar tighter, as if she couldn't bring herself to
face him.
"You don't need to apologize. It's okay to cry when things get hard. No
one doubts how hard you're trying."
Maybe not everyone, but Shirone believed it.
Finally, Seriel lifted her head slightly and, with a pout, muttered, "If
you tell Amy, I'll kill you. Don't say a word until I do."
"Haha! Got it. Now let's take care of that hand."
Though Seriel specialized in healing magic, emotional wounds required
more than just skill.
The cut was long but thankfully not deep.
"What got you so worked up?"
Sitting in a chair while Shirone treated her, Seriel lit a
cigarette.
"I don't know. I've been having nightmares lately. Maybe I should cut
back on case reviews."
Shirone gave her a wry smile.
"Yeah. It's tough."
"Emotion Disease… Having to look into the deepest parts of a person's
soul—it's enough to drive anyone mad."
Seriel turned her head.
"You're incredible. How do you handle it? If I were in your shoes, I'd
have gone crazy by now."
"You're doing great too. You've analyzed countless cases and developed
guidelines to save people. Just remember, you've saved far more than you've lost."
"Yeah…"
Seriel was grateful.
"I called you here because I have a favor to ask. Are you up for a
trip?"
"A trip? For what?"
Seriel extinguished her cigarette and began rummaging through her
desk.
"Where is it…?"
She eventually sat on the floor, sifting through scattered
documents.
"Ah, here it is. Take a look."
Shirone glanced at the paper and saw a map marking the areas where
Emotion Disease cases were reported.
"One red dot equals a hundred cases. If we adjust the scale, it'd be
more precise, but this should do."
"I see."
It was clearly an abnormal distribution.
"The disease spreads through the air, like a windborne contagion. While
humans are a transmission vector, most nations have implemented quarantine policies, so the spread isn't
as rampant. Therefore…"
Shirone interjected, "The highest frequency of reported cases should be
in the Central Continent, gradually decreasing toward the southwest. But this map shows patches of areas
with no cases, almost as if the wind had stopped."
"Exactly. It's like there's a windless zone in the Southern Continent.
At first, I thought it might be due to a demonic invasion, but the Holy War confirmed that hell's armies
are blocked at Kale's borders. So, I investigated whether these areas were administrative cities or
military hubs."
"If they were, publicizing casualties could harm national
interests."
"But they're not."
Seriel pointed at the map.
"My research showed no reason for these areas to withhold reports. In
fact, most of them are just small, unremarkable fiefdoms."
"Did you send investigators?"
"I reported it to the higher-ups. If my theory is convincing enough,
they might issue an emergency directive, but prioritizing this over appeasing the nobility is
uncertain."
The confiscation of noble wealth took precedence.
"Waiting for approval is frustrating, so I kept digging. Then I had a
thought: what if it's not that the cases aren't reported, but rather that these regions are somehow
overcoming the disease?"
Silence lingered.
"Hmm. Even if that's true, the simultaneous occurrence across the entire
Southern Continent seems too large in scale. And it's happening in small villages, not major
cities…"
Shirone trailed off as he connected the blank areas on the map with an
imaginary line.
"A route of some sort."
"That's my thought. So, I pulled an all-nighter investigating. I checked
regional specialties, even farming tools, but nothing fit. I hit a wall until last night when I decided
to take a different approach. If there's a product being distributed from one region across the Southern
Continent, the biggest beneficiary would be its place of origin."
"And you tracked that down?"
There was only one way to find the source.
"I went through every report, from No. 1 onward, marking each one. About
two hours in, I realized I didn't need to. A specific place stood out for having no cases at
all."
Shirone leaned forward.
"So I dropped the map and kept digging through the files until I found
it. A region—or rather, a kingdom—that hasn't reported a single case of Emotion Disease."
Seriel drew a border around the southernmost part of the continent,
above the Iron Kingdom.
"Know where this is?"
Shirone's blood ran cold.
"The Kingdom of Kessia."
Where Fermi was.
"A drug route."
"Yes. It's a drug smuggling route. The drugs are produced in Kessia and
spread across the Southern Continent. And wherever these drugs circulate, there are no reported cases of
Emotion Disease."
"But… is that even possible?"
"That's the problem. We've tested every type of morphine, sedative,
tranquilizer, even experimental drugs, but nothing alleviates the symptoms of Emotion Disease. So what
is this? What kind of substance could counteract the disease?"
'It's too suspicious to be a coincidence. Besides, Fermi became a drug
dealer long before the demonic realm opened. There's no way he could have predicted this…'
Without realizing it, Shirone muttered aloud.
"Ah."
Maybe…
'Because it's Fermi.'
Shirone shook his head.
'I honestly don't know. But it's even harder to believe that he just got
lucky. If all of this was calculated from the beginning…'
On that day, the one missing from his memory—what exactly had Fermi
proposed to him?
'And why…
Why did I accept his proposal?'
"Go to Kessia."
Seriel, who had been watching Shirone, pressed the ends of her pen
together and spoke.
"No matter how I look at it, this isn't something an investigator can
handle. Fermi has already taken control of Kessia, so launching an official investigation is tricky. I
know it's a difficult request, but I'd appreciate it if you could go and confirm things
yourself."
"Got it."
Finding a way to suppress the Emotion Disease was important, but more
than that, Fermi was someone Shirone had to meet before the holy war began.
"I'll leave now."
And so, Shirone set off for Kessia once again.
