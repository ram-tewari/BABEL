Chapter 368:
Special Training (3)
The intensive training began in earnest.
Shirone focused on practicing Scatter Movement during practical sessions
and studied the theory of Laser Guidance Technology during breaks.
Thanks to an hour of tutoring from Isabel each day, he was gradually
getting a grasp on radio waves.
Flu would evaluate his daily progress after returning from her
duties.
However, no matter how hard Shirone tried, Flu's strict evaluations
never seemed to yield a passing grade.
"Too slow. Again."
Gritting his teeth, Shirone cast Scatter Movement. Each time, Flu would
appear in front of him, gripping his shoulders with both hands.
Despite hoping for a lucky break, it seemed professional mages didn't
make mistakes.
'How does she keep finding me? Can a human really react this
quickly?'
It was hard to believe it was just because Shirone was inexperienced;
Flu's sensory perception was incredibly sharp.
Regardless, Shirone couldn't complain about being guided by such an
excellent mage, and the days continued to accumulate.
"Ugh!"
With a dull thud, Shirone rolled on the floor, his back arching in pain
and his face contorted in agony.
Focusing too much on photon output, he missed the landing point of his
teleportation.
Flu looked down at Shirone, who had fallen at her feet, and said,
"Again."
A brilliant flash exploded in the center of the training ground.
Photon output scattered in seven directions, and Shirone safely reached
his destination. Flu's face was nowhere to be seen.
'Got it! This time it worked!'
It seemed Flu hadn't been able to track Scatter Movement. As expected,
she hadn't moved from her original spot.
With her eyes closed, she drew a line with her index finger.
"Again."
Panting, Shirone asked,
"Why? The Force was perfect, and you didn't catch me."
"Look where you're standing."
Shirone looked around his feet. The direction was correct, and there was
nothing unusual.
"What's wrong with here?"
"You only moved about 8 meters. Don't you usually set your
teleportation distance to 10 meters?"
"Oh..."
How could he make such a basic mistake?
"Listen. Your teleportation distance must never waver, no matter
the situation. In real combat, hundreds of turns pass in an instant, and you have to navigate complex
terrain. If your distance wavers, you die. That's why even high-level mages rarely change their set
distance during battle."
"Yes, I'm sorry."
Flu put her hands on her hips and exhaled sharply at his dejected
response.
"If your basics are weakening, it means you're becoming more
technically proficient. But you can't let your guard down. All applications ultimately come from solid
basics."
Shirone clenched his fists and took her words to heart.
Basics, basics are everything.
The next day.
Shirone finally succeeded in Scatter Movement.
Flu didn't move, and the direction was accurate. The teleportation
distance was also a perfect 10 meters.
"Ah... I finally did it."
He was so tense that he couldn't even fully appreciate the joy of
success.
But Flu still didn't smile. Instead, she approached with a colder gaze,
as if the real work was just beginning.
"Now, let's get serious. Your own Scatter Movement."
Understanding Flu's meaning, Shirone quickly adjusted his
expression.
Scatter Movement was a good spell on its own, but the reason he chose it
from among many magic books was its potential to be converted into an attack spell.
'Don't be nervous. Just replace photon output with Photon
Cannon.'
It had taken three weeks to master Scatter Movement. If he didn't pass
now, it would delay him by several more days, leaving little time to learn the advanced Laser Guidance
Magic.
"Go ahead. I'll take it directly."
Flu's gaze was entirely different from when she evaluated Scatter
Movement.
Photon Cannon, unlike photon output, was a complete attack spell. As
this was the final evaluation, he had to give it his all.
Shirone took a deep breath and timed his move. Assuming a one-second
delay, he cast teleportation at 0.5 seconds to disrupt his opponent's tempo.
As his body glowed, Photon Cannons shot out in all directions, and Flu
moved simultaneously.
Shirone arrived in the west. Therefore, the Photon Cannon heading
towards Flu, who arrived in the south, was on target.
As the Photon Cannon was sensed through the Spirit Zone, Flu's head
involuntarily tilted back.
The Photon Cannon whizzed past her face and crashed into the wall with a
thud, shaking the training ground.
"How was that? Will it work in real combat?"
"......"
Flu reviewed the situation.
The pressure of receiving the Photon Cannon was much stronger than
expected. The power came from its speed, which distinguished it from other spells.
Sub-light speed is a standard created to define the speed of light
implemented by magic.
While not used in physics, it's a speed indistinguishable from light to
the human eye.
If you shot an arrow with a light-emitting object at night, it would
appear as a flash.
But experiencing the Photon Cannon was much faster than expected. It was
closer to the speed of a bullet fired from a gun. This meant the ratio of mass to output was perfectly
balanced.
A larger mass than a magic bullet was propelled at bullet speed, causing
the target to stiffen.
Even if the original Scatter Movement didn't hit the target, it would at
most be a dud, but this was a bang.
"Pass. This will definitely work in real combat."
Shirone's face finally lit up.
"Whew, thank goodness. The three weeks of hard work paid off. Thank
you so much!"
There was nothing more gratifying for a mage than learning a new spell,
and Flu, understanding Shirone's feelings, replaced her strict expression with a smile.
"You should maintain this feeling. Keep practicing if you
want."
"Of course. Remembering the sensation of success is important. Just
don't overdo it."
From then on, Shirone practiced Scatter Movement until he was
exhausted.
After two hours, he could move to any of the eight directions without
any deviation in the power of the Photon Cannon.
"Ouch, my back."
Flu, who had been watching Shirone's training, massaged her back, which
had stiffened from dodging Photon Cannons.
If she had dodged with physical strength, it might have been different,
but moving with manipulation magic took its toll.
'Once he mastered it, he became proficient in the intermediate level
within two hours. Talent is talent, after all.'
According to academic journals, Shirone's strength was his insight. He
had an extreme tendency to delve into the core rather than accumulating miscellaneous skills.
This was likely what Gaold had high hopes for.
"Whew."
Finishing his training, Shirone looked up at the ceiling, his face
drenched in sweat.
The state of unity derived from a sense of achievement is a miraculous
period where skills can improve rapidly.
Mages call this state "fever."
"All done?"
Shirone looked satisfied.
"Yes, I think I've got the hang of it."
"Starting tomorrow, we'll move on to Laser Guidance Technology. But
there's something we need to address first."
The joy of success disappeared from Shirone's eyes.
He had to listen to whatever it was.
Living with the professional mages of the association, their mindset had
naturally rubbed off on him.
"Watching you for three weeks, I've noticed your balance is way
off."
"Huh? Balance?"
Flu twirled her finger, roughly describing Shirone's Spirit Zone.
"The biggest problem is that your Spirit Zone is too large. It
feels like it's over 80 meters in diameter."
"Is that a problem?"
"In real combat, the smallest details determine victory or defeat.
It's important to maintain the golden balance of density, volume, proportion, and durability. You tend
to overdo it. If your opponent's durability is 100, 100 power is enough. But you unconsciously pour in
120, 130."
Since the Spirit Zone is a manifestation of the mind, there's no lying.
Reflecting on all his past battles, Shirone realized this was true.
"It's the same now. When our Spirit Zones overlap, the tension
pricks at me. I've always wondered why. Unless you've only fought monsters."
Shirone couldn't deny it.
Looking back on the year since he started learning magic, he hadn't
faced many equal opponents. By his standards, all his enemies were monsters.
As Shirone remained silent, Flu understood. If he had grown through such
battles, it made sense that his Spirit Zone would be in a state of display, like a wounded beast.
"Anyway, that's not good. I'll help you find the golden
balance."
Flu went to the wall and turned on the Image Zone switch.
As Shirone's Spirit Zone became visible with a bluish light, she turned
off the training ground lights to enhance clarity.
Shirone watched his beautifully glowing Spirit Zone in silence.
At that moment, Flu hugged him from behind, pressing her body against
his.
"Uh, um..."
"Stay still."
Flu's calm voice stopped Shirone from speaking.
As she expanded her Spirit Zone, Shirone's Spirit Zone was clearly
conveyed through shared perception.
'He's really tense. Almost like a mage in a war zone.'
Flu closed her eyes and focused, then gave Shirone instructions.
"Close your eyes and take deep breaths. Reduce your Spirit Zone
until you feel completely comfortable."
Shirone relaxed his body and let go of his tension.
As his Spirit Zone, which had been taut like a balloon, gradually
shrank, his mind felt lighter.
"Good... keep going..."
As he reduced his Spirit Zone further, the tension he hadn't noticed
before began to reveal itself.
"A little more..."
Flu used all her senses to feel Shirone.
"More. More... that's it. Good."
At that point, Flu's voice faded away.
Shirone, with his head bowed, felt an overwhelming sense of warmth and
peace, as if he were in his mother's embrace.
Flu's eyelids slowly lifted as if waking from sleep.
"…Done?"
"Yes, it feels good."
Flu released her hands from Shirone's waist and quietly moved to the
Image Zone device.
She checked the gauge, which read a diameter of 62.8 meters.
When the lights came on, Shirone opened his eyes, feeling refreshed as
if he had just woken from a deep sleep.
"Remember this feeling. The size, shape, sensation,
density—everything. Everything contained within the 62.8-meter sphere is your current golden
balance."
Shirone understood Flu's words. The increased density made his
surroundings feel clearer, and his durability felt significantly stronger.
Most importantly, his thoughts flowed smoothly, like water.
"It feels really comfortable. But 62.8 meters isn't much larger
than a year ago. Is that okay?"
"Yes, that's perfectly fine. Unless you're a sniper, you don't need
an excessively large Spirit Zone. How often do you fight enemies at invisible distances? Even learning
the four-directional technique doubles your range."
"That's true, but…"
"Trust me. For a search-type mage, a Spirit Zone diameter of at
least a kilometer is necessary. Communication mages need an extended Spirit Zone to transmit information
over tens of kilometers. But some combat mages have Spirit Zones only 10 meters in diameter, focusing
instead on density and durability."
The key was to maintain a mental state that maximized the efficiency of
his magic.
"Don't be swayed by numbers. They can solidify your thinking. While
gauging an opponent's capabilities by numbers is essential for combat mages, preemptively imagining the
outcome is dangerous. No trait is an absolute advantage or disadvantage. The criteria for advantage and
disadvantage vary depending on the opponent, and your ability to adapt to various situations determines
victory or defeat."
Shirone nodded resolutely.
"Yes, I'll keep that in mind."
