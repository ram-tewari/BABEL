Chapter 601:
"Neide, it's so good to see you! How have you been?"
Even as Liz approached and shook his shoulder, Neide stood there with a
blank expression, unable to find his lost soul.
'How?'
How could she be here when she was supposed to be in the capital,
Bashka?
'No, that's not the issue.'
After the graduation exam party, Neide had confessed his true feelings
to Liz.
She had left the school, saying he was a good younger brother but not
someone she saw as a man, and she was sorry.
The memory of that day was still vivid in Neide's mind, but he couldn't
understand how she could talk to him so brightly as if nothing had happened.
"When did you come back?"
Shirone looked on curiously as Neide forced himself to speak.
"This morning."
"But I heard you got a job at the capital's armory…"
"Yes. I took a vacation and came down. I had some business."
"Oh, I see."
As Neide couldn't hide his dejection and lowered his head, Liz gave a
mischievous smile.
"But I think I'll be moving down here soon."
Neide's head shot up, but Liz's next words crushed him again.
"Actually, I'm engaged."
"What?"
Even though Liz's voice echoed in his mind dozens of times, Neide
couldn't help but ask again.
Liz linked arms with the neatly dressed man standing behind her and
approached.
"This is him. Say hello. This is Clid Oscar, a certified 8th-level mage.
He's been assigned to the Creas Magic Association."
"Oh, hello."
Shirone quickly bowed, but Neide was too stunned to remember to
greet.
'Engaged? What was engagement again? Engaged?'
Oscar didn't mind and greeted them with a friendly smile.
"Nice to meet you. I'm Oscar. You must be Neide. Liz has told me a lot
about you."
"Oh…"
It wasn't until Oscar extended his hand for a handshake that Neide came
to his senses and slowly took his hand.
It was a soft and warm hand.
"Instead of standing here, let's go eat. I should treat my juniors to
something nice."
At Liz's suggestion, Neide waved his hand.
"No, we need to get back to school soon…"
Just a moment ago, Neide had been adamant about not returning to school,
but Shirone quickly nodded in agreement.
"Yes, we need to go back to school."
Liz didn't listen.
"Don't lie! I know you're skipping. If you're out in the city at this
time, anyone can tell."
"It's true. We really need to go."
Shirone tried his best, but Liz ignored him and approached Neide.
"Come on, okay? I'll buy you anything you want."
"There's nothing I particularly want to eat…"
"I really want to introduce you."
Neide finally looked Liz in the eye and saw the earnestness in her
beautiful eyes.
'She remembers too.'
Maybe she thought the feelings had faded over time because they were
young then and much time had passed.
'But this is too cruel.'
What had Liz told Oscar about him?
Maybe she had said he was a junior who used to follow her around or a
troublesome younger brother.
'Damn, what is this?'
Liz wanted to introduce her fiancé to shake off any lingering burdens
from the past.
"Neide, come on."
The more he thought about it, the more unfair it seemed, but seeing her
face, desperate like a lost kitten, made his heart race, and he couldn't refuse.
"Alright. But I'm ordering something expensive."
"Of course! You're not just any junior; you're from the research
society."
A junior from the research society.
That explained everything.
Since it was morning, the upscale restaurant wasn't crowded.
The four of them took a table by the window.
"So, what should we eat?"
Watching her dance with the menu in hand, Neide felt a wave of nostalgia
that made his mind hazy.
'This is dangerous.'
Her voice, mannerisms, glances, and even small actions were inexplicably
endearing.
"Let me formally introduce myself. I'm Oscar, and this is my fiancée,
Liz."
Seeing Oscar's victorious smile made Neide's blood boil, but the shock
of reuniting with Liz made everything feel unreal.
"I'm Neide. This is my friend Shirone."
As Shirone bowed, Neide added.
"He's quite famous."
"Oh, really?"
There wasn't a student at the magic school in the Kingdom of Tormia who
didn't know Shirone's name.
Even if Oscar was a professional mage, he would understand what it meant
to be recognized by the kingdom.
Honestly, Neide wanted to wipe the smug look off Oscar's face, but Oscar
skillfully deflected the provocation.
"Haha! My apologies. I used to keep up with Spirit magazine after
graduation, but I haven't heard much about the magic school lately."
It was a way of saying they were just students.
Of course, that was true, but it was still a petty remark to make to
juniors, so Liz quickly changed the subject.
"If you're that famous, you must be graduating soon. Are you in your
final year?"
"Yes."
"That must be tough. Honestly, I still dream about my final
year."
"Well, it's not too bad for me. I gave up on the second half of the
graduation evaluation."
"Oh, so that's why you're in the city today. There were quite a few like
that in my time too. So, did you give up too, Neide?"
"No. I really had something to do…"
"Really? Then what's your rank now?"
Neide felt a wave of fear but couldn't bring himself to lie, so he told
the truth.
"Last place."
"Pfft!"
Oscar pretended to spit out his drink.
"Oh, sorry. That was unexpected. You didn't seem like it."
He hadn't expected much when he heard Shirone had given up on the
evaluation.
Even now, it was the same. When he attended the magic school, giving up
on the evaluation was a choice made when there was no other option.
'Though I didn't expect him to be last.'
Even without showing it, Neide could tell Oscar's gaze had
changed.
"Hmm, I see."
On the other hand, Liz looked at Neide with a sympathetic expression, as
if she understood something.
"There must be a reason."
"Not really. Last place is last place."
Having already lost face, Neide spoke as if he didn't care.
"Haha, what happened to your face? Did you get into another
fight?"
Neide remembered the scars on his face.
'Today is really the worst.'
As Neide lowered his head slightly, Oscar interjected.
"Giving up on the evaluation and having low grades is fine, but if you
want to be a mage, you can't go around fighting. Overconfidence in your skills leads to no
progress."
It was advice from a senior who had become a professional, but today,
Neide couldn't take it well.
"I didn't fight. I just… got angry."
Knowing he would regret his words after parting with Liz, he couldn't
filter his words.
Seeing the self-inflicted scars, Liz gave a bitter smile, as if she
understood.
"You haven't changed, Neide."
The meal continued in a slightly awkward atmosphere.
Liz led most of the conversation, with Oscar occasionally chiming
in.
The food was delicious, but Neide couldn't taste it.
"Oh, really? So, Shirone is also in the Supernatural Psychic Research
Society? It's really hard to get a member. You must be talented."
"Well, there are only three of us. You know Iruki, right?"
Liz clasped her hands and looked up at the ceiling.
"Oh, meeting you really brings back memories. We used to stay up all
night debating the secrets of the upper echelons. It's still not revealed, right?"
"Well, no…"
Having closed the upper echelons himself, Shirone had nothing to
say.
As their alumni reunion continued, Oscar, feeling left out, changed the
subject.
"The graduation exam is coming up soon. Are you prepared? It's the most
important exam of your life."
Even knowing Oscar's words were loaded with intent, Neide couldn't help
but feel like the ugly duckling.
"I don't care about exams. I don't even want to be a mage."
It was a rather arrogant statement, and Oscar didn't let it
slide.
"That's your choice. But staying in school while saying that is
contradictory. If you're running away like this…"
"I'll pass."
Liz interrupted.
"He'll pass. Neide is strong. Right?"
Facing Liz's confident gaze, Neide quickly turned his head to hide his
blushing face.
'Damn. What am I supposed to do when you look at me like that?'
"Ahem."
Oscar cleared his throat and wiped his mouth with a napkin.
"If we're done eating, let's go. I have things to do."
"Really? We haven't even had tea yet."
"Liz, you have an appointment with the association people,
remember?"
"Oh, right."
Liz reluctantly stood up.
"It was nice meeting you today. I'll take care of the bill."
"Yes. Thank you for the meal."
Shirone and Neide also stood and bowed.
"I'll visit the principal soon. See you then, Neide."
Holding her fiancé's hand, Liz's words made Neide's heart race
again.
"Yes. Take care."
As Liz and Oscar left the restaurant together, Neide finally came to his
senses and slumped into his chair with a sigh.
"Ah, I'm going crazy. Shirone, let's stop by a pharmacy. I feel like I'm
going to be sick."
"Who is she? She's not just any senior, right? I've never seen you this
nervous before."
Neide, who never got intimidated even in front of teachers as the head
of the research society, was visibly shaken.
"Well… it's a long story. I'll tell you on the way."
"Alright. Let's go."
As Shirone prepared to leave, Neide reached out his hand.
"Wait! Just 10 more minutes, let's stay here for 10 more
minutes."
"Why? We need to get to the pharmacy."
"Liz might still be around."
"…"
Shirone thought Neide must have lost his mind.
"Why did you do that?"
After walking in silence for a while, Oscar asked.
"What?"
"Why did you defend those juniors so much? I have my pride too. How
could you interrupt when I was giving advice?"
Liz smiled like a seagull.
"What? Are you jealous?"
"Who's jealous? Of those kids?"
"Then why do you care? Just think of it as treating some cute juniors to
a meal."
"Honestly, it bothered me. You know? Neide kept provoking me."
"Aren't you overreacting? Honestly, you were a bit harsh. They're doing
their best."
"See, you're defending them again."
Realizing they might actually fight, Liz decided to relent.
"Alright. I was wrong. I guess I got too excited meeting them after so
long."
Oscar's mood softened a bit as she conceded first.
"I'm sorry too. Hearing that he confessed to you before, I couldn't help
but be bothered."
"Oh, it's all in the past. It was three years ago. Neide was only
sixteen then."
"But you said he's strong? From what I see, neither of them will become
mages. They seem to lack perseverance."
"Neide is really strong. I don't know about Shirone, but Neide will
pass."
Oscar blinked.
He had known Liz for quite some time, but she had never used the word
"strong" to describe anyone, not even him.
"What kind of kid is Neide?"
"Well, I'm not really sure."
"Not sure?"
Liz looked up, trying to recall.
"Blue electricity…"
"Huh?"
"It feels like you're only seeing an afterimage, even though he's right
in front of you. That's the kind of junior Neide was."
Oscar couldn't quite grasp what she meant.
