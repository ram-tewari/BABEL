Hill Capture (1)
It was the day of the hill capture evaluation.
The first thing the 30 students did upon gathering at the training
ground was to check the members of the opposing teams.
Shirone also confirmed the Screamer team.
Pony was strategically allied with Screamer, and Suavi and Luman were
key factors in the martial artist killer strategy. Including the reliable Aider, they had formed a solid
team.
Among the six teams gathered, the evaluation instructor explained the
rules.
"Today is the hill capture. You should already know your opposing
teams. Six teams will be evaluated simultaneously, with no time limit. The first team to score 5 points
wins, and the losing team will have 20 points deducted. The battle will take place at the main hill
capture battlefield, number 2000."
Shirone examined the battlefield.
It was a rectangular area 200 meters long and 50 meters wide, surrounded
by 20-meter-high glass walls. Inside, each side had a 10-meter-high hill with a flag planted in the
center.
The evaluation would proceed in rounds, and once a flag was captured,
the round would end temporarily, moving on to the next round.
"To deepen strategic tactics, spatial movement is prohibited.
Flight height is limited to 20 meters, and exceeding this will result in disqualification. If you touch
the flag, you won't be able to use any magic due to the built-in magic control device. Remember this
well to avoid complaints later."
While holding the flag, one would be defenseless and vulnerable to enemy
attacks.
Therefore, capturing the flag required at least two people to occupy the
hill and cooperate.
The instructor continued the explanation.
Upon entering the battlefield, the teams would be divided along the
central 100-meter line, with each team member assigned a position to perform their duties.
There were four positions: forward, winger, linker, and guard.
The forward started at the 10-meter mark in their own territory, closest
to the center line, and their role was to charge towards the enemy's flag.
Wingers moved along the left and right lines, typically starting at the
more defensive 20-meter mark. However, they often moved faster due to less defensive pressure.
Linkers started at the 50-meter mark in the center line, balancing
offense and defense.
Depending on the opponent's strategy, they could assist a winger, charge
with the forward, or retreat to defend the flag with the guard, making them versatile players.
Finally, the guard protected the flag on the hill. Even if the enemy
forward infiltrated, the guard could defend the flag, as the forward couldn't use magic while holding
it, making it a defensive position.
