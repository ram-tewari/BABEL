Chapter 481:
Shirone sat in the grass, gazing at the lake.
The lake was white like milk and as calm as if it were in the middle of
a windless zone.
His eyes were fixed on the horizon above the water's surface.
"What are you looking at?"
'Ikael.'
The surface of the lake bulged in the center and transformed into the
shape of Ikael.
The milky liquid that smoothly flowed over the surface disappeared, and
Ikael's figure emerged as if real.
"What are you looking at?"
'Father. Mother.'
Ikael's form crumbled like a sandcastle, replaced by Vincent and his
wife.
'Rian. Amy. Friends.'
The calm surface began to ripple as if hit by a storm.
Countless forms of people emerged on the water's surface, filling it
completely with allies and enemies, loved ones and hated ones.
"What are you looking at?"
The milky liquid gathered into a giant shape of Mirka.
He had been completely defeated by her Unregulated Form, which
fundamentally blocked variables.
But was she truly an unbeatable enemy?
Marsha's advice that nothing is absolute in Unregulated Forms flashed
through his mind.
'Who is it?'
Shirone suddenly looked up at the sky.
Who could it be?
The voice that had been questioning him.
"To truly see is to observe something that exists there in its pure
state."
'In its pure state.'
The voice asked again.
"What are you looking at?"
Defeat. Feope's betrayal. Plu's safety.
All those emotions surfaced on the water.
No, I am not looking at that. It is merely a delusion created because I
wanted to see it or thought I had to see it.
"What am I looking at?"
All the forms on the water began to dissolve.
"I am not looking at anything."
The voice said.
"When you see nothing, you see everything."
The lake calmed down again.
Soon, the water level lowered, the white liquid disappeared, and the
world disintegrated into tiny pieces, scattering like cherry blossoms out of sight.
The Ultima System, which had seeped into his brain like a ghost, finally
became systematized and integrated into his neural network.
Simultaneously, Shirone's avatar briefly shone, signaling his mental
transformation.
If the blind directionality of the cerebral synapses created focus and
that focus manifested as a Spirit Zone, then Shirone's Spirit Zone, equipped with the Ultima System, was
manifesting as something unique and independent with a different temperament than before.
'This is…'
Direct Perception.
A single gaze that looks straight at the entire world.
The previously empty landscape regained its original place and unfolded
before Shirone's eyes.
The lake was still calm, but now Shirone could clearly see countless
possibilities changing like a living organism.
"What are you looking at?"
Shirone answered.
"The ultimate."
As if in response, the voice continued.
"That is the Ultima's…"
Flash!
A powerful flash struck Shirone's brain, and he opened his eyes wide in
shock.
'What happened?'
A faint memory like a dream.
But the image itself was so vivid.
The last thing Shirone remembered was hitting his head on the cliff and
losing consciousness.
"Huh?"
As he regained his senses, Shirone felt a warm sensation on his
lips.
A mysterious woman was on top of him, kissing him.
Blushing, Shirone pushed her away in surprise.
"What, what is this?"
The woman rolled to the side weakly, breathing painfully with her bare
body exposed.
Shirone quickly lit a fire with Shining Magic.
"Feope?"
He couldn't believe it even as he said it because the small fairy had
grown to the size of an adult woman.
But the small dress she wore was torn to shreds, and the fairy wings
lying beside her confirmed she was Feope.
"Shirone…"
Feope opened her eyes with difficulty and smiled faintly.
Reading the emotions in her smile, Shirone looked around in
confusion.
Why was she here? And what had happened to her?
"What happened, Feope?"
"I'm glad you're safe."
Shirone finally checked his own body.
All his wounds were completely healed.
Recovering from fatal injuries so quickly without the Vajra Armor was
impossible.
There was no doubt she had saved him.
"Why? I…"
Feope shook her head.
What Shirone thought didn't matter to her.
Facing the end of her life, the most important thing was something so
trivial it even surprised Feope.
"Shirone, how do I look?"
Shirone, not understanding her meaning, looked at her body.
The price of exploding all her life force at once was rapid growth and
the loss of her fairy essence due to mental collapse.
"Am I pretty?"
Feope's question was so pure that Shirone couldn't say anything.
Realizing he had nothing to give back but gratitude sent chills down his
spine.
But it was the truth.
One cannot create something out of nothing.
When Shirone didn't answer, Feope closed her eyes gently.
She felt both disappointed and relieved. There would be no lingering
regrets at the end.
"Don't look, Shirone."
Feope made her final wish.
"Please… don't look."
Feope's body began to shrink rapidly. The backlash of burning all her
life force was hitting her.
"Fe, Feope…"
Shirone watched in a daze as Feope transformed.
Her face, now the size of a fairy, was full of wrinkles, and her once
firm body had lost all its muscles, leaving only bones.
Beside her lay wings that finally matched her size, but they had already
fallen off and were decaying.
"Feope! Feope!"
Shirone held Feope in his hands and shouted.
Her lifeless body didn't move.
She couldn't die like this.
Realizing this in front of her corpse, Shirone brought Feope close to
his face.
She was still breathing faintly.
'She's still alive.'
But how could he save her?
"There is nowhere left to run, foolish human."
Hearing Mirka's voice resonate from outside the cave, Shirone grabbed
Armand and stood up.
Beyond the curtain of light at the cave's entrance, Mirka and her fairy
troops completely blocked the exit.
Shirone advanced slowly, holding Feope without any sign of fear.
He realized why the fairy troops had stepped back only after coming
outside.
An enormous army had completely surrounded the area.
There was no way to escape.
Mirka, having concluded this, looked at Feope in Shirone's hand with sad
eyes.
"Foolish one. I explained so many times what the price of loving a
human would be."
There had been fairies who loved humans in history.
But their ends were always tragic.
Fairies are pure, and humans are selfish.
No matter how passionately they burn, human greed knows no bounds, and
in the end, they drain the fairies dry.
"She's still alive. How can I save Feope?"
That was why Shirone had willingly entered the encirclement.
"Human, accept the aura willingly. If you do, I will ensure Feope's
life is saved."
"I asked how."
Seeing Shirone's unwavering gaze, Mirka answered coldly.
"There is only one being in heaven who can restore a fairy whose
life force has been exhausted. Archangel Ikael."
Shirone instinctively knew it was true.
The incarnation of amplification.
Ikael could amplify Feope's faint essence and restore her to her
original state.
"Alright."
"A wise choice. Hand over Feope to us and…"
"Vajra Armor."
Shirone grabbed Armand's handle and activated the Vajra Armor.
In an instant, the organic robe enveloped him, and the artificial brain
equipped with Akamai's Eye floated above his head.
The fairies tensed up and assumed battle stances.
Although he had been defeated by Mirka, he was a formidable opponent who
had shot down many fairies.
But Mirka, knowing there were no variables in her duel with Shirone,
smirked mockingly.
"You have a good weapon. Thanks to it, you could achieve so much
with your mediocre skills. But without that weapon, you are nothing but a weak human."
Having already overwhelmed him in avatar strength, it didn't matter what
techniques he used or how many functions he had.
The absence of variables was the scariest aspect of Razor of
Truth.
Without a word, Shirone placed Feope inside Armand's robe.
He created a pocket near his heart and safely protected it with Ringer's
minerals.
To reach Ikael as quickly as possible, he would have to fight
fiercely.
Realizing Shirone had no intention of surrendering, Mirka extended her
hand with an angry face.
"Kill him. Make him pay for the crime of defiling
heaven."
The fairies waiting all around flew at Shirone at terrifying
speed.
At the same time, Shirone's eyes focused forward.
Magic of unknown attributes rained down from all directions, and the
fairies moved swiftly like a storm, but Shirone ignored it all.
When you see nothing, you see everything.
As Shirone's Direct Perception activated, the boundaries of his Spirit
Zone began to blur.
Mirka, the highest-ranking entity, was the first to sense the
change.
"What is that…?"
The presence of the Spirit Zone was fading. Eventually, it completely
merged with the world, and the boundaries disappeared.
Boom! Boom! Boom!
As Shirone widened his eyes, dozens of photon cannons shot diagonally
from completely unexpected places.
It was an impossible angle unless Shirone had fired them from
mid-air.
The fairies charging from the sides were caught off guard and exposed,
swept away by the cascade of flashes and slammed into the ground.
"What is this? How is he using magic…?"
Shirone created and fired photon cannons in all directions.
The rampaging flashes intertwined like a net, bombarding the fairies
indiscriminately.
Even amidst the chaos, the fairies couldn't find the origin of the magic
and looked around in confusion.
There was no central point where the magic was being cast.
Shirone's mind, now merged with the world, had achieved a state of
perfect omnipotence. The photon cannons, bundled together, rapidly changed direction like schools of
fish in the ocean, sweeping up the enemies.
"Ugh!"
Mirka hastily moved her body out of the radius.
The complete annihilation of the first unit that tried to subdue Shirone
was shocking, but what came to her mind first was an ancient record.
"This is the Gaia's…"
The Spirit Zone of those who wield the Ultima System, 'Elysion,' had
resurrected, transcending the ages.
