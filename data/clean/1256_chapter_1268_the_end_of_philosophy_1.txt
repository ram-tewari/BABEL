Chapter 1268:
The End of Philosophy (1)
Is it living, or merely being kept alive?
—Can humans truly change the future?
Shirone opened his eyes.
What remained in his broken consciousness might have only been the faint
echoes of a desperate hallucination.
'Am I alive?'
His senses were gradually returning.
It felt like a very noisy, chaotic place, but even that noise was faint
and distant.
"Shirone."
The door opened, and Nade entered.
"Nad…"
Shirone moved his lips, then winced as he realized his throat was
hoarse.
How much had he screamed?
"You're awake. You were unconscious for three days. Here, have some
water. Miro said we could only watch over you, but things have been so busy here that we didn't take
proper care of you. Sorry."
Nade was talkative.
As Shirone took a sip of the water he offered, the dryness in his throat
eased just a little.
"Nade,"
With his voice still strained, Shirone asked,
"What about Amy?"
There was no answer. Realizing this wasn't a dream, Shirone closed his
eyes again.
"Shirone."
"No."
He didn't even know what he meant by that.
He just felt like if he didn't say it, the anger that had settled
momentarily would erupt again.
"Where are we?"
Shirone changed the subject.
"The Kingdom of Iron."
"Iron?"
That was an unexpected location.
"Remember how Nane—no, Anke Ra—erected altars to open the spiritual
domain? He gave orders to the Council of Ten. There are more than 200 such places."
"And?"
"There's a shelter underground, built in case of emergency. Though, I'm
not sure if 'emergency' is the right word. Anyway, it's safe here. Try to relax and rest."
Shirone was relieved that there was no immediate battle, but still full
of questions.
"Who… Who made all this?"
"Well…"
Just then, he walked in through the open door.
"Got your head on straight now?"
It was Fermi.
Still lying down, Shirone looked him in the eyes—then couldn't hold back
and asked first,
"Amy…"
"I knew."
After taking a short breath, Shirone spoke again.
"My memories…"
"I erased them."
Once again, Fermi cut him off.
"Of course, with your consent. You knew how much it would hurt. Just
like how you look now—you anticipated it."
That made sense.
"But why?"
What was certain was that Shirone would never have allowed Amy to be
sacrificed under any circumstances.
"Why did I agree? What did you say to me that made me go along with
it?"
Fermi thought for a moment, then asked,
"Can you move?"
Shirone nodded, but Nade stopped him.
"You're still weak. Getting excited now could make it worse."
"We don't have time. You know that."
After exchanging a silent stare with Fermi, Nade sighed and supported
Shirone.
"Fine. I'll take you."
Shirone didn't fully understand the conversation, but didn't argue right
now.
He really was too weak.
As they left the room with Nade's support, Liz, who had come with them,
was waiting.
"Ah…"
When their eyes met, Liz gave a stiff bow. Shirone nodded in return as
he passed her.
He sensed difficult emotions, and most people who passed him did the
same.
Once they exited the corridor and entered the plaza, they found medical
personnel tending to the wounded.
Amid the chaos, Shirone spotted Rian sitting off to the side.
When Nade guided him over, Rian, who was talking with Tess, gave a
bitter smile.
"Hey."
Shirone's eyes went to Rian's right arm.
"Oh, this?"
Though crudely built, something resembling a mechanical skeletal
structure had been attached.
"It's decent enough. I don't know how she did it, but Miss Ellis took
care of it. She said she's a friend of Master Kuan."
That wasn't what mattered.
Why had Rian's right arm ended up like this? Why couldn't it be
healed?
Guessing his thoughts, Tess spoke.
"Rian brought you here. Without him pushing that far, we wouldn't have
made it."
She couldn't completely hide her resentment.
"But Rian's just glad. Glad he could save you. So… it's okay."
Tess forced a smile.
"…Ah."
Shirone wanted to say something, but no words came to mind.
"Go on. I'm sure you have a lot you want to hear."
Her gentle tone felt sincere, but for some reason, Shirone felt like a
stranger now.
As they left the plaza, another long corridor stretched ahead, lined
with rooms on either side.
"Damn it! How long do you expect me to stay in this filthy place? You
expect me to eat a meal without even an appetizer? What am I, a dog? I'm the Minister of Internal
Affairs!"
Shirone paused and glanced back.
Of course, in such an urgent situation, they wouldn't have selectively
saved people—but the aides probably thought differently.
"I'm sorry! We've sent the request to the upper command—"
"I am the upper command! Who the hell outranks me in this
place?!"
As Shirone silently watched, a sharp voice came from the end of the
corridor.
"What's all the noise?"
It was someone from the upper command.
'Shirano…'
Mito Shirano, Chief Justice of the International Tribunal of the Holy
War, walked side by side with Bebeto Socrates.
As she spotted Shirone, she paused briefly, but then brushed it off and
entered the room.
"Could you please keep it down? Don't you realize there are injured
people here?"
"You! How could you treat me this way? Don't you know who I am?
Shouldn't I at least be given a bigger room?"
Shirano let out a hollow laugh.
"You lunatic."
"W-what did you say?"
"You're gravely mistaken. You're no longer a minister. Your country is
gone—forever. And you're no longer a noble either. Ninety-nine percent of humanity is dead. The people
left here are all that remains of the world. There are no policies for you to make, no documents for you
to approve."
"Ninety-nine percent…"
Shirone felt dizzy.
'What… what have I done?'
It was a result far too devastating to blame solely on his reckless
charge at Buddha.
The former Minister of Internal Affairs shed tears.
"S-still… it's just so cold. Can't I get one more blanket?
Please?"
Shirano sighed and turned around.
"Give him another one."
"Yes, ma'am!"
As her aide responded and Shirano walked back out toward the plaza,
Socrates asked,
"Shouldn't we avoid making exceptions?"
"Who cares? The world's ended. If a guy's crying over being cold, it
probably means he's not fit to survive anyway. Calming him down will make it easier on everyone
else."
"Hm. What you're saying is… we need people who can fight."
"Yahweh is awake now. We have to move. The new World Leadership Council
will be decided tonight. Rom, Gartor, Temica. The rules stay the same."
"Sounds simple, but it won't be. Even with just two people, one will try
to rule the other. Even in a collapsed world, politicians won't let go of their power. It's
instinct."
"That's fine."
Shirano didn't care.
"Now it's all about brute strength."
When she arrived at the plaza, the "brute strength" she spoke of—the
leaders of the national Magic Associations—were already in conference.
"Everyone."
The group stood respectfully as Shirano entered.
"Thank you for coming. We don't have much time. If we want to make use
of this final opportunity, we need to unite all remaining forces. To do that, we need a
representative."
Lupist stepped forward.
"That's exactly what we were discussing. We've already settled on
Tormia."
By the standard of "someone who can fight," it was a nation with strong
central power.
"Good. The vote will be held tonight. Please return to your countries
and persuade your representatives."
Plu interjected.
"If I may… will that be alright? If anyone refuses, things could get
messy."
Shirano's solution was simple.
"In that case, kill them. And come back with a new king to vote for
Tormia."
Plu shrugged.
"Well…"
It was hard to argue when she had already had Gis removed without
hesitation.
Albino spoke.
"The result of the vote doesn't really matter. What matters is that a
vote takes place at all. It gives the illusion that we're still civilized."
That was a biting comment.
"Mr. Albino, you're safe."
"Ha! Sorry for making you worry. I'm at an age where tripping on a rock
could kill me. Who knew the world would end before I did?"
"No, I mean it. We're short on military leaders. I hope you'll continue
to share your strategies."
"I guess so… My son's still out there."
Iruki, his son, was currently investigating military strength as the
leader of the Valkyries.
Just then, Socrates nudged his chin toward the plaza.
"Hey. Look over there."
Shirano grimaced.
"Ugh."
Gaold, his hair disheveled like a wandering vagrant, walked by in a
daze.
One of the Association leaders murmured,
"…Micaeh Gaold."
He had appeared at a few meetings back when he was an Association head,
but was always treated like a madman.
Not that it was any different now—but no one could deny what he had
accomplished.
"Move. That's my seat."
The Association leaders, intimidated, made way, but Shirano stood firm
in front of the spot.
'Ugh, this nutcase.'
They had clashed countless times when he was in office.
'Still, he's the real deal.'
Given the state of the world, they'd have to put the past behind them
and maintain a good relationship.
Gaold stopped in front of Shirano.
A long, awkward silence passed, and finally Gaold spoke.
"What?"
"…What?"
"What?!"
So much for a good relationship.
Annoyed, Shirano stepped aside. Gaold walked past her and headed for the
wall.
Lupist asked,
"What's he going to do?"
There was no answer.
"We need a clear answer. We have to know whether to count him in our
forces or not."
"Don't."
He barked the reply and suddenly turned back.
"Just remember this."
A flash of madness appeared in the eyes of someone who had reached the
end of philosophy.
"When I fight—don't be anywhere near me."
Those who knew what he meant swallowed hard, and Gaold slumped down in a
corner.
Plu, who had once followed him, looked saddened.
'I still respect you. But…'
He was broken—beyond the point where anyone could help him.
Meanwhile, Shirone, leaning on Nade, entered a wide room.
It had been arranged as neatly as possible with limited resources, a
sign of its importance.
"Shirone."
Seriel stood up first. Lying in the bed was Jin Seoug Eum, her eyelids
slowly lifting.
"…You've come."
She looked even more emaciated than when she returned from Hell. Shirone
was shocked.
Fermi explained.
"There are around 200 altars built in Iron. King Basak—or rather,
Shura—constructed 147 shelters beneath them. We evacuated the last of humanity into those shelters.
Using ether waves."
Just imagining it was staggering.
"We can't go back to the surface. Everything's burned, all buildings are
dust. We had to rally the commanders scattered across the shelters. Jin Seoug Eum led that effort.
Simple discussions can be done via Chong's communication magic, but…"
"Wait."
Shirone interrupted.
"You're going to fight again?"
"Yes."
"Why?"
Seriel and Jin Seoug Eum looked at him with sympathy.
"It's not us who'll be fighting."
"Then who?"
"You."
Shirone.
"This fight is for you alone."
For Yahweh, who gave everything for the world—this was the only thing
Fermi could offer.
