Chapter 57:
Gauge Symmetry (3)
"Siena! Open your eyes! Get a grip!"
"Oppa! S-save me…!"
Siena was in no state to regain composure. Yet expecting two children to swim across was
impossible. Armin realized he had to do it alone.
'If we can just reach the Delphons region, the river widens, and the current slows. At
this speed, we'll make it in 20 minutes.'
Twenty minutes? An eternity. Could he last that long in the rapids with Siena barely
conscious?
'Stupid. You have no choice.'
Armin steeled himself. The grotesque scene before him twisted unnervingly. Broken logs
bore down on them. He twisted desperately. Even as filthy water stung his eyes, as boulders loomed, as sharp
branches sliced his skin, he pushed forward.
'Just a little… a little more!'
Siena's tiny fingers pinched his waist. The fragile life relying on him was
heartbreaking, but a greater sense of purpose kept him sharp.
After what felt like hell, the distant downstream destination came into view. But the
path there was blocked by a tangle of broken trees.
"Siena, listen. We have to avoid that. The only way is to go underwater. I'll hold you
down. Hold your breath, then we'll surface. Got it?"
"Oppa! I'm scared! I can't!"
"You have to. You can do it. Trust me!"
"Oppa! I—"
Where two whirlpools collided, the current doubled in speed. Unprepared, Armin gritted
his teeth. Debris loomed like monsters in his limited view.
He kicked fiercely, his legs cramping. Sharp objects grazed his body. Blood loss and
dropping body temperature fogged his mind. Then they reached the blockade. Even up close, diving under was
the only way.
"Siena! Now!"
Armin timed it, watching the approaching log. Just before impact, he pushed Siena's
shoulders down.
"Now!"
Siena's scream was cut off as she went under. Holding her down, Armin surfaced just as
the log's jagged bark filled his vision. But all his focus was on the hand pressing Siena down. As she
passed under the log, he released her.
'Done! We made it!'
Armin smiled faintly as the log rushed toward him. Saving Siena was worth it. And until
the very end, he never closed his eyes.
"Oppaaa!"
Siena's voice, fading downstream, was the last thing he heard.
***
Siena's story ended there. The children bowed their heads solemnly.
"I was rescued by villagers in the Delphons region. Past the forest, there were no
dangerous structures, and Armin oppa drifted to where I was. The school's people treated him, but the
diagnosis was permanent eye damage. He lost his sight."
"I see. So that's your story…"
"Oppa taught me how to face fear. Thanks to him, I overcame Overflow. But he lost the
light. The most talented genius in Olipher's history… just stopped."
Shirone's chest tightened. Losing one's sight was akin to losing everything. Books,
magic—all would be limited. He could imagine the despair and pain without seeing it.
"Three years ago, I heard oppa came here. He's developing a style of painting light. So I
volunteered to model."
"That explains it. And here we were, misunderstanding…"
Misinterpreting their relationship as an affair was disrespectful. As the mood grew
somber, Armin returned with tea and snacks.
"Did I interrupt? Siena rarely talks about the past."
"Oppa."
"Have some tea first. It's nothing special, but my wife's secret recipe makes it tasty."
"Ah, thank you."
The trio accepted the cups humbly. Though he'd switched to painting, Armin had shown
genius-level talent in magic 20 years ago. Misfortune aside, as aspiring mages, they couldn't look down on
him.
Returning to the canvas, Armin gestured behind it.
"Please wait here. I need to work."
"Ah, of course."
They awkwardly moved, cups in hand. As Siena posed on the bed, Neid hesitantly asked.
"Sorry for asking, but you're not painting her nude, are you?"
Knowing Armin was an artist, they had no right to judge. But Siena might feel awkward
with her students watching.
"Hahaha! Even blind, I wouldn't ask such a thing of my dear little sister."
"But earlier, you said to take off clothes…"
"Ah, that was this." Siena opened a closet and pulled out a foil coat. Its reflective
surface was blindingly bright.
"Wow! That's dazzling."
"Without Spirit Zone, I can't see accurately. So I use light-reflecting materials to
sense shapes. The foil coat is perfect for that."
"That's unique. Painting by sensing light."
"Losing my eyes let me paint what others can't. But today's the last time I'll paint
Siena. A patron appeared, so I'm moving soon. I wanted to capture her true essence. The coat dulls her
beauty."
Siena's cheeks flushed slightly.
"Oppa, stop being ridiculous."
"Haha! What's wrong? In my mind, you're still that nine-year-old brat."
"Just get to work. If Keira comes back, you won't be able to."
The trio fidgeted under the oddly intimate atmosphere. They were like siblings, but not
by blood. Siena's strong reaction to the affair accusation might hint at lingering feelings.
"Then let's begin. Since I'm going by feel, it'll finish sooner."
As Siena posed, Armin picked up charcoal and began sketching. The trio marveled at his
skill. His rough strokes perfectly outlined her form.
"Wow! Amazing."
"Shh."
Iruki raised a finger to hush Neid. Blindness was a painter's致命 flaw, requiring extra
focus.
But Shirone frowned. Something was off. Without reflective materials, Armin was capturing
Siena's details with uncanny precision.
'How is this possible? Is his Spirit Zone that dense? Even then, this level of detail…'
A dense Spirit Zone could allow precise tactile feedback, like footprints in mud. But
even Jonain Etelazo hadn't reached that level.
The painting grew more detailed. Siena smiled beautifully, a rare sight at school. Armin
used bread as an eraser to perfect her smile.
Shirone marveled inwardly. Lips were among the hardest to paint due to subtle changes in
expression.
'This is impossible.'
No matter how dense his Spirit Zone, a blind painter couldn't capture such nuances. The
realization sent chills down his spine, and the eerie sensation returned.
"Um… Armin."
"Yes?"
Armin smiled slightly, tilting his head. Shirone hesitated. If he was wrong, it'd be
terribly rude.
"Can you… see?"
Armin's hand froze mid-stroke. Still smiling, he set down the charcoal.
"Yes. I can see."
Shirone was speechless. Could he really see? Then why didn't Siena know?
Turning fully, Armin unwound the cloth over his eyes. The scars around them were
numerous.
'He's clearly injured, so how can he see? Wait, he's been covering his eyes all along…'
"You must have questions. I'll show you."
Armin slowly opened his eyes. Shirone gasped. There were no pupils. Instead, bright light
filled the empty sockets.
"N-Neid, Iruki! Do you see that? Huh?"
Shirone grabbed Neid's thigh in panic. But the texture was odd—like touching an object.
Turning, he saw his friends frozen in place, smiling at the canvas.
"No way! How?"
Neid didn't move. Iruki sat smiling. Checking Siena, she too was motionless, like a wax
figure. Behind him, Armin spoke.
"It's been a while since I had guests."
"Is this…?"
"Yes. The Stop magic."
Shirone turned, stunned. Stop was a spell only light-speed mages could use, with few in
history achieving it. He was witnessing time magic's pinnacle.
"Sit. Let's talk calmly. We have plenty of time."
"Haha…"
Shirone laughed awkwardly. With time stopped, it was effectively infinite.
"I was nervous while painting. I didn't know when you'd enter this time field."
"How did you know I'd notice?"
Armin pointed to the light in his sockets.
"Eyes receive light and reconstruct visual information. Without eyes, I perceive light
itself. It's horrifying. The world looks entirely different to me."
"Like the world of ideas."
Human senses couldn't guarantee reality. A brick might feel like jelly. Fire could be
cold, and a lover's kiss might bring pain.
"Let me introduce myself properly. I'm Armin of the Radiant Eyes. Among those who know
me, I'm called the Eternal Contemplator."
"But you lost your magic as a child…"
"Yes. A tragic accident. I left Olipher and wandered for 15 years. In a world of
darkness."
