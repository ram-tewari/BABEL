Chapter 658:
"Captain… you were wrong."
Shagall murmured up at the night sky, pulled from his memories.
He was a killer by nurture, not nature.
And maybe that's why, tonight more than ever, the starless sky felt
unbearably lonely.
As he lowered the magician's knife, his hand swept upward—now gripping a
freshly forged, razor-sharp dagger.
A signature weapon of the quickblade style: twenty-three layered sheaths
stacked into a single dagger.
He stared at a tree ten meters away. Ra Enemy's face flickered in his
vision like a phantom.
He swung his arm vertically. One sheath flew off with a thunk, embedding
itself in the tree—right where Ra's forehead would have been.
He swung upward again. Another sheath shot off, sliding perfectly into
the first.
Thunk! Thunk! Thunk! Thunk!
With each motion, a blade flew. His arm blurred until it could no longer
be followed with the naked eye.
Thun-thun-thun-thun-thunk!
As the blades stacked in the same exact spot, the tree shuddered. After
releasing all twenty-two sheaths, he threw the final dagger.
Thunk.
It punched through the tree trunk, causing it to twist and groan before
tipping over.
The fused dagger clattered to the ground—exactly as it had begun.
"You were wrong, Captain."
Shagall was a killer by nurture.
And he was, without a doubt, the evil of evils.
The next morning.
Shirone left the inn, clutching the request form bearing Brooks' seal,
and stepped into the district packed with guilds.
Even from early morning, the streets bustled with countless experts,
each with unique and polished skills.
"Capital life, huh. This is nothing like the provincial guilds."
Mages, swordsmen, travelers, alchemists, blacksmiths—the main branches
of countless guilds all vied for dominance, stacking their buildings ever higher.
"It's good for us, though. We don't have a lot of time."
Recruiting a 20-man mercenary unit, as Lupist had requested, would take
more than a month if done one by one.
"He said to handle it as I see fit, but I still want to put together an
elite team."
Radum was a place the kingdom hadn't been able to touch for years—any
mission there came with no guarantees of survival.
At the very least, mercenaries would need to be strong enough to escape
alive even in the worst-case scenario.
"Let's finish the original request first."
As Shirone and Rian walked toward the Silver Ring Guild headquarters,
they shared no words—as if by silent agreement.
Someone was following them.
After surviving countless brushes with death, a person's senses become
as sharp as a cat's.
Rian, amidst the crowd, identified a few suspicious stares. Shirone,
operating Elysion, confirmed the presence of a tail keeping perfect distance and pace.
"Who is it?"
"No idea. If word's gotten out about the bandit raid, it could be
anyone."
Without breaking stride, Rian shifted his gaze.
"Want to catch and rough them up?"
"No. They won't act in a crowded place, and neither will we. Besides,
with the Ultima System, there's no way we'll be ambushed."
Elysion removed the boundary of the Spirit Zone, making it nearly
impossible to detect—even for a Zoner.
With the unease hanging behind them like a tail, the two arrived at the
Silver Ring Guild's towering six-story headquarters.
It was no match for the Magic Association, but as one of the kingdom's
three major guilds, it had its own impressive grandeur.
"Let's go."
As Shirone approached the entrance, Rian's brow twitched, and he sharply
turned his head.
Incoming!
Two hooded figures shoved through the crowd, rushing toward them.
Here? Seriously?
As Shirone readied himself and Rian reached for his greatsword, the two
figures pulled something from their coats and shouted—
"Now! Start!"
Assassins?!
Rian drew his blade, ready to strike before they could even use whatever
they'd pulled—just as the guild doors flew open.
"Welcome! To the newest member of the Silver Ring Guild!"
A beautiful manager stepped out, kneeling with a bouquet, while two
burly men flanked her and unrolled a banner.
Shirone blinked in disbelief as confetti poppers burst behind him—the
banner read:
"Welcome Ariane Shirone to the Silver Ring Guild!"
Before Rian could slice both "attackers" in half, he stopped
mid-swing.
"Eek!"
A young mage stumbled and fell on his butt, his hood flying off to
reveal a fresh-faced youth.
A girl around the same age rushed over.
"You okay?"
"Yeah? Hahaha! I just slipped, that's all!"
A surprise greeting, but to Rian—Shirone's blade—it was just plain
irritating.
"Who the hell are you two?"
The boy stood and held out his hand.
"Sorry for the scare. I'm Tyler, a mage of the Silver Ring
Guild."
"Elza."
Elza followed with a bright smile and a bow, but Rian's nerves were
still taut.
"Shirone, let's just go inside. No point making a scene."
"Yeah."
Without showing his emotions, Shirone crossed the threshold.
Inside, more than twenty people sat at tables, all with sharp, hardened
auras.
Whether officially licensed or not, it was clear this place was filled
with people who'd survived the front lines.
"And now, everyone! A miracle of a rookie has joined our guild—please
welcome Ariane Shirone!"
Michela, the Silver Ring Guild's manager, prompted applause. A few
tables clapped politely.
To the guild, Shirone was a prize catch—too valuable to lose.
To the rest, he was just another rival.
"Come on now, no need for jealousy among guildmates."
The voice came from the stairs, turning all heads sharply.
A tall man descended, wearing a black robe embroidered with red flame
patterns—the Silver Ring Guild Master, Ackman.
"Master, isn't it unfair to favor him so obviously?"
A gruff, bearded man—not someone you'd think was a mage—grumbled.
The report had come in from the Kentra branch, and the news of the five
bandit groups' annihilation had reached them that very morning through Austin.
But many, like the bearded man, still didn't like the guild bowing to a
rookie, even a prodigy.
"Sure, he's strong! But how long do you think a candidate for the Ivory
Tower is going to stick around? He'll either ditch us or stir up trouble!"
Trust only worked between equals.
As far as the guild was concerned, they had little to offer Shirone—and
too much to lose.
"Hey rookie! You really gonna give it your all for the Silver Ring? Can
you fight with loyalty and responsibility?"
The bearded man jabbed a finger at him.
A veteran ranked A, he was senior by far. Some mages watched Shirone
closely.
But Shirone didn't plan to stay long, and his silence gave the man
fuel.
"See? He can't even answer! This whole thing's a joke! I won't stand for
it!"
He began gathering his things, but Ackman stopped him.
"Kon. Today is about welcoming new members. As a senior, it's your duty
to stay."
"Hmph! Duty? He'll be gone in no time—"
"Shut up and sit down."
The killing intent in Ackman's voice made Kon flinch and slink back to
his seat.
'Fool.'
Did they think the Guild Master didn't know they couldn't hold
Shirone?
'If we let him go now…'
Then War Wagon or Bloodrose would snap him up with whatever it
took.
War Wagon had brute force, Bloodrose seduction.
Silver Ring had money—but to a candidate of the Ivory Tower, that meant
little.
"My apologies. On behalf of the guild."
"No need. They weren't wrong."
Ackman nodded at Shirone's honesty and called him forward.
"Well then—let's begin the welcome ceremony."
Amid the tangled mix of emotions in the room, he knelt before Shirone
and lowered his head.
"Become the new master of the Silver Ring Guild."
Kon's eyes widened in disbelief.
"Master! Why?"
It was an event that defied all logic, and the guild members finally
grasped the gravity of the situation.
This was Ackman—renowned flame mage, an unofficial fourth-tier magician,
and the pride of the Silver Ring Guild, respected across the magical world.
Only the manager, Michela, knew the full extent of Ackman's
intentions.
'Express the utmost goodwill. That's Phase One of the strategy.'
Even for a candidate of the Ivory Tower, a rookie's rank couldn't simply
be raised on a whim.
The only way around that… was to promote them to master.
It had never happened before in guild history—but that was exactly why
it would be so impactful.
'If he accepts, we proceed with verification procedures. If he
refuses…'
Kon spoke again.
"Master, are you serious? There's no precedent for this."
"There was no precedent for an Ivory Tower candidate joining a guild,
either."
That shut down all objections.
But what Shirone wanted was simple—to cooperate with the guild and
swiftly form a 20-man mercenary unit.
"I refuse."
The eyes of the guild members sharpened with hostility.
Rookies fought tooth and nail to rise through Senior and Veteran tiers,
all dreaming of one day becoming master.
It wasn't a dream granted to everyone, but for many, that dream was what
gave their lives meaning.
'Not even worth considering, huh?'
They had factored in the possibility of refusal, knowing Shirone could
get the same offer from any other guild—but to decline without hesitation was a shock.
"Then how about notarizing it? A pledge that you won't join any other
mage guild within the Kingdom of Tormia."
Even calculating it rationally, Shirone had no reason to agree.
"I don't want to do that either."
"You son of a—! So you do plan to betray us?!"
Ackman raised a hand to stop the guild member and stood, speaking to the
manager.
"Michela, disable the mana control system."
If they couldn't hold him, letting go might be the only option—but
Ackman still had something to confirm.
"Shirone, maybe this seems like petty stubbornness. But for us, the
position of guild master is non-negotiable. For you, this is just a choice. For us, it's our lives. If
you leave for another guild, dozens of members will suffer. All I'm asking is a written pledge."
"I'm not willing to offer anything beyond trust. I won't leave without
reason, but I can't accept notarization or the position of master. That's the limit of what I'm
offering."
At Ackman's signal, Michela gulped and flipped the switch.
'A boy with the potential to earn a Star Title.'
Did they really think Ackman never dreamed of that—of the highest honor
a magician could receive?
'Just a twenty-year-old kid…'
Beyond the duty to provide for his guild members, what burned in his
chest was something more personal.
A lifelong ambition. A test of everything he had become as a fourth-tier
mage.
'I have to know—what exactly is the difference between us?'
As Ackman activated his Spirit Zone, the flames embroidered on the hem
of his robe began to shimmer like they were alive.
'A magic artifact? Probably has options related to flame spells.'
If it was gear used by the master of a top-three guild, it certainly
wasn't anything easy to come by.
"Shirone, want me to handle it?"
"No, I've got it."
Wounded pride in his voice, Ackman raised a pillar of fire from beneath
his feet.
"Then, forgive me for this…"
"Master, don't tell me you're seriously going to—"
Before Kon could finish his sentence, the Hellfire swirling around
Ackman surged forward.
"You actually did it?!"
Startled guild members dove under tables, and the front door of the
guild exploded along with part of the wall.
