Chapter 560:
"Run!"
Knowing they couldn't overpower Shirone, Shuamin and her group fled down
the opposite hallway as soon as they launched their surprise attack.
'Are they out of their minds?'
Casting attack magic on a senior?
Deciding he couldn't let this slide, Shirone used teleportation to chase
after them.
Challenging Shirone to a movement contest was a mistake on the juniors'
part.
The distance closed in an instant, and realizing they couldn't escape,
Shuamin and her group ducked into a warehouse as soon as they rounded a corner.
Spotting the end of their invisibility, Shirone roughly opened the
warehouse door and looked around.
They had vanished like ghosts.
'Invisibility cloaks. Where did they get those?'
Shirone's voice echoed through the warehouse.
"Come out. I know you're hiding in here."
When there was no response, he deployed homing photon cannons and used
his Spirit Zone to locate the targets.
As the seekers rotated in three directions, the golden spheres trembled
as if ready to launch.
"See the lasers? These are homing missiles. Once fired, I can't
cancel them. I'll count to three. One."
Silence.
"Two."
The atmosphere wavered.
"Three…!"
Shuamin emerged from a corner of the warehouse.
"Hmph, do you think you can attack us?"
Alt appeared from inside a box, and Gerein crawled out from between
construction materials.
'They're really good at hiding.'
Gerein stood up and agreed with Shuamin.
"That's right. Magic is forbidden on school grounds. If you hurt
students, you'll face more than just a week's suspension."
Their shamelessness left Shirone speechless.
"You were the ones who attacked first."
"Hahaha! Fine, let's all get expelled together. I've always wanted
to try that."
'Bold little brats…'
They were exploiting the fact that graduation class students had more at
stake than advanced class students, but it was still an absurd argument.
"Alright, I won't use magic then."
Shirone thought it best to play along to get them to confess, but Gerein
already looked victorious.
"Good thinking. Let's go, guys."
Shirone moved to block the exit.
"Where do you think you're going?"
"What are you going to do? Use magic…"
As Shirone raised his right arm and opened his palm, Gerein fell silent
immediately.
With a rotation of the Cubrick slot in his retina, a sword appeared at
the top, and Armand materialized in Shirone's grip.
"Diamond Armor."
As he spoke the keyword, the blade split with a crack and quickly
enveloped Shirone's right arm.
"W-what is that?"
Shirone's appearance in a pure white robe was undeniably
intimidating.
The artificial brain's external unit glowed with a purple light as it
orbited him, creating an almost mesmerizing sight.
But for the advanced class kids, it was less frightening than seeing him
wielding a sword; their tense expressions began to relax one by one.
"What the heck? You scared us!"
Passing by a graduation class senior wielding a sword in a dead-end
warehouse was undoubtedly terrifying.
As Shuamin signaled with her eyes and cast Wind Cutter, Alt and Gerein
provided support fire.
'These little…!'
Shirone walked forward without even thinking of dodging.
With a metallic clink, the sound of breaking iron echoed as Shuamin's
group stopped in their tracks, horrified.
"What kind of armor is that?"
The spots hit by Wind Cutter had turned metallic.
It was Ringer's carapace armor, known for its unparalleled durability
even in heaven.
"When did you all start acting so recklessly?"
"What are you talking about? Old man."
They had been polite and well-mannered kids during integrated advanced
class lessons not long ago.
'There's definitely something going on.'
Feeling the need for interrogation, Shirone plucked Armand's tendrils
like peacock feathers.
"Ugh!"
Seeing the bio-material for the first time made Shuamin's group recoil
in fear.
"Spill it. Who put you up to this?"
"W-what are you going to do with that grotesque thing? Are you some
kind of pervert?"
Shirone's expression remained cold.
"That depends on how you behave."
"Guys! Let's do this for real!"
At Shuamin's signal, her group activated their specialties.
Fireball, Ice Dagger, Eruption.
These spells were on a different level of imagination compared to Wind
Cutter, making Shirone move seriously for the first time.
As molecules on the floor collided and exploded like a volcano, ice
daggers embedded themselves in the rising flames of Fireball.
But having already moved out of position, Shirone swung his tendrils and
grabbed all three by their necks, lifting them into the air.
"Gah! Monster!"
"This is what happens when you pull stunts like this. It's
self-defense. And I didn't even use magic like you said."
"Shut up! You're just a dropout who gave up on
evaluations!"
"Shut up?"
Shirone's eyebrow twitched.
Thinking back to how much he had admired graduation class seniors when
he was in advanced class made their arrogance infuriatingly absurd.
"Aaah!"
As the pressure from the tendrils increased, Shuamin's group's faces
turned red.
"Talk. Or your necks will snap."
"Kill us! We'll never talk!"
They might have been somewhat sincere, but Shirone couldn't shake the
feeling they still underestimated him.
They probably thought he wouldn't actually kill them; Shirone had no
intention of committing murder either.
'What should I do now?'
After pondering for a moment, Shirone came up with a good idea.
"So you absolutely won't talk?"
"That's right! We're proud mages! Our resolve won't
break!"
"Fine. Then I'll just have to ask Mark."
"W-what!"
As expected, Shuamin and her group's faces twisted in
frustration.
After losing to Shirone in the teleportation evaluation, Mark had become
his follower, training hard to secure a spot in Class Four and reigning as the advanced class's top
fighter.
"How can you use a junior's name like that? And you call yourself a
graduation class student?"
Alt's tone shifted back to respectful, showing how much they feared
Mark.
'Of course, a nearby fist is more intimidating than distant
rules.'
They were more concerned about Mark, who they might have to deal with
for years, than Shirone, a graduation class student.
"So, talk. Otherwise, Mark will ask you himself."
Gerein, glancing at Shuamin, cautiously spoke up.
"We're from the Hide-and-Seek Research Group."
Shirone had heard of this underground group from Nade, and their unruly
behavior made sense.
Underground research groups had a strong sense of belonging and operated
like a separate society within the school.
"Why is the Hide-and-Seek Research Group tailing me?"
"I don't know. The president told us to check on you."
Their earlier talk of resolve and principles quickly faded as they
spilled the truth.
"Who's the president?"
But this time, Shuamin's group spoke in unison.
"We can't tell you that!"
"Really? Then I'll ask Mark…"
Shuamin pleaded tearfully.
"It's true, senior. If we reveal that, the president will kill us,
not Mark. We don't even know why we're investigating here. Please forgive us this once."
Ironically, their sincerity showed in their fear of the president, not
Mark.
Retracting his tendrils, Shirone looked down at the coughing
juniors.
"Don't spy on other research groups again. If this happens again,
you'll be the prime suspects."
"Yes, sir."
"Now go back. And don't skip classes."
As soon as he finished speaking, Shuamin's group fled, and Shirone fell
into thought.
'Hmm, an underground research group is involved. Could it be related to
my supernatural sense?'
It was possible, but since he had never been in the research group
during class time, he couldn't be sure.
'I should ask Nade.'
Realizing it was almost lunchtime, Shirone headed to the dining
hall.
'I wonder if those kids will find their way out. It's quite a
maze.'
The dining hall's atmosphere was as lively as ever, the only time of day
when students could relax.
The main difference was the more diverse relationships compared to the
early days when students were more divided by class and faction.
What caught Shirone's eye was the 4D girl Dorothy sitting across from
Iruki.
'Dorothy used to only eat with Luman.'
To be precise, Dorothy was the only one who tolerated Luman.
Even now, Luman sat beside her.
Despite being a master of crowd control, his selfish personality and
overweight figure made him unpopular. He had gained even more weight over the break.
Boyle, a summoning magic major, looked disgusted as Luman stuffed his
face.
"Do you even feel hungry after eating so much? How much did you eat
over the break?"
"Hmph, you should ask how much I trained. I eat when I'm
stressed."
Iruki delivered a short jab.
"You get stressed because you keep eating."
Luman glared with wildcat eyes.
"What did you say? You're just weak because you don't eat
enough."
"Want me to tell you an easy way to lose weight?"
"Huh?"
As Luman showed interest, Iruki tapped the table with his index
finger.
"Always tap the table like this when you eat or read."
"Tap the table? What good does that do?"
"Your fingers moving burn calories."
Luman stood up abruptly.
"Are you mocking me for being fat?"
Iruki replied briefly.
"That's math."
Boyle and Dorothy turned their heads simultaneously.
"No matter how trivial, numbers clearly express it. Tapping the
table burns calories. Even a small amount of energy burned means losing weight."
Unable to argue with the logic, Luman sat down, though his feelings were
hurt.
'Crazy jerk. One day I'll…'
Then he noticed Dorothy tapping the table with her index finger.
"What are you doing?"
"Dieting."
As Shirone watched this scene, Nade approached and whispered.
"Those guys have been acting suspicious lately."
Startled, Shirone sighed in relief upon recognizing Nade.
"Who's suspicious? Iruki and Dorothy?"
"Yeah. You've noticed, right? Dorothy's been showing interest in
Iruki. So let's interfere."
"Why would we do that? Isn't it a good thing?"
Nade grimaced.
"I don't like it."
"Why not?"
Nade draped an arm over Shirone's shoulder, speaking like a
mentor.
"Listen. At our age, hanging out with girls means we can do so many
things. It's a complete betrayal! I can't stand to see it until I die."
Shirone looked at Nade with pity, pushing his arm away and turning
towards Iruki.
"Then you should die."
"Hey!"
With Nade following, pouting, Shirone reached the table, and Amy pulled
out a chair for him.
"You're here?"
"Yeah. How was class today? It was the specialty exam,
right?"
"It wasn't too hard. The real challenge starts about ten weeks from
now. Only Arin will probably get a master rating."
"Oh, I see."
As Shirone and Amy chatted, Nade whispered to Iruki.
"They seem to have worked things out. I was worried because Amy was
so cold when Shirone wasn't around."
"They'll sort out their feelings. Worry about
yourself."
"Everyone hates me!"
Just as Nade was about to shout, everyone suddenly fell silent, and his
voice echoed through the dining hall.
"Ugh!"
Nade's face turned red, but no one paid attention to him.
Everyone was looking at the entrance.
"It's Maya."
She had refused evaluations and hadn't been to the dining hall since the
first half of the term. This was her first appearance.
