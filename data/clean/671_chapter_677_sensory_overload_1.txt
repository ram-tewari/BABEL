After Rian deliberately stepped aside to give the two men some privacy, Shirone let out a
deep sigh and spoke.
"Go."
As soon as the permission was granted, Kargin and Joshua jerked their heads up to look at
Shirone.
His face wasn't as benevolent as they had hoped, but it wasn't filled with condemnation
either.
"If you manage to leave Ludum safely, go find Mr. Brooks and rewrite the contract. Return
the commission fee as well."
Overwhelmed by the thought that he might not die here, Kargin bowed his head repeatedly.
"Thank you! Thank you so much!"
Shirone, forcing himself to remain cold, turned away and flew up to the building, calling
out to Rian.
"Let's go."
Rian followed Shirone's rapidly retreating figure, stepping off the wall and climbing the
building until he landed on the rooftop.
"Are you going to find the other members?"
"I have to."
They might choose to leave Ludum like Kargin and Joshua, but for now, they were still
Shirone's team.
As they silently leaped across buildings, Shirone asked,
"……What do you think?"
"You know me. I don't judge."
"Not as a sword, but as my friend, Rian. Do you think letting those two go was foolish?"
After a brief silence, Rian chuckled.
"Don't be stupid."
Someone who doesn't value even one life would never care for ten thousand. That idea
itself is delusional.
Shirone was, and always would be, Shirone.

When Brooks arrived, he paled at the sight of Venetia's eyes, consumed by madness.
"Hey! Snap out of it! What's happening?"
Brooks existed in the present.

—Wasn't this what you wanted?
The Ra Enemy speaking to Venetia was from the past.
"No! Not like this!"
When the Ra Enemy had visited Brooks' mansion, Venetia had resolved to face him directly.
"What are you scheming?"
Having relinquished her position as the head of Spectrum, she believed she wouldn't be
used by him anymore.
"Why not see for yourself?"
Memories of the Ra Enemy began flowing into Venetia's brain, the one responsible for her
past.
'This is…….'
An incident.
A story from the Ra Enemy's distant past, so remote it couldn't even be called history.
"Kyaaaah!"
Tears of blood streamed from Venetia's eyes.
'How, how could this happen?!'
Was this a calamity or a blessing?
One thing was certain: this was an event so anomalous that even the Tricerebrate's brain
couldn't analyze or predict it.
"Ah, ah……"
Venetia's face, twisted in agony, suddenly relaxed into a dazed, euphoric expression.
'I see. God. So this is…….'
The shocking truth God had concealed.
'We can't win. No matter what we do, we can never defeat Ra.'
The Ra Enemy approached with a sad smile.
"That's right."
As he slowly reached out and closed Venetia's eyelids, she could feel her life fading
away.
"That's who I am."
Venetia prayed desperately.
'Get out of there, Shirone.'
Her body collapsed forward, and her three brains shut down one after another.
'You must not meet the Ra Enemy.'
No one should try to save humanity.
"Hey, Venetia! Snap out of it!"
Brooks flipped Venetia over and shook her shoulders, but her body was already cold.
"Damn it! Do you know how much she's worth? Call a doctor! Now!"
The Ra Enemy from the past gazed at Venetia's corpse before slowly turning and leaving
the room.
Brooks shouted again.
"Damn it! Do you know how much she's worth? Call a doctor! Now!"

While it didn't matter much to Rufist, Aria's absence was unsettling.
"Where did she go?"
Jane, who had followed Rufist, soared into the air and scanned the surroundings.
"It seems she's gone deep inside. She might be approaching Bioflora on her own. Let's
wait."
With Quan as her bodyguard, nothing major should happen, but the code imprinted in Aria's
subconscious was a matter of utmost importance.
"Find her. I'll head to Bioflora first."
Splitting up would ensure perfection.
"Understood."
As Jane boarded the Cosmic Rail to depart, Rufist added,
"Jane."
"Yes?"
"I won't tolerate a second mistake."
Nothing other than reclaiming Bioflora as a national asset should interfere with the
mission.
"……Understood."
As the Cosmic Rail sped away and disappeared beyond the buildings, Rufist glanced at the
spire of Bioflora.
"It's quiet."

"What are they plotting?"

"Kyaaah!"
Aria, crouched beside a building, screamed as she clutched her head.
Members of Vega, a subordinate organization of Spectrum, were battling Quan.
The Tuvos, a race with bovine faces and abnormally developed lower bodies, were among the
most formidable fighters in the lower ranks, but not a single one could surpass Quan's blade.
"Where the hell is she?!"
None of the Tuvos had even seen Quan's face.
The movements of the Pierrot were too precise, and those who did see his face didn't live
to tell the tale.
A gust of wind blew, and Quan appeared before Aria.
When she slowly opened her eyes, she saw countless bovine corpses strewn about.
"Are you alright?"
As long as Quan was guarding her, she was safe.
But the heat from the Golden Jade, which had ignited Bioflora, was intense, and without
special defenses, her wrists and ankles were covered in severe blisters.
"Yes, I'm fine."
Despite her words, Aria's face was pale.
'She's infected.'
Quan's sword was useless when it came to saving lives, though it excelled at taking them.
"I can go. The code……"
Aria's condition was deteriorating rapidly.
Ludum was a filthy place teeming with various demi-humans, making it hard to identify the
cause of her illness and treat her.
"Quan."
Jane descended vertically from the sky, leaping off the Cosmic Rail.
"Why are you still here?"
"As you can see, the subject is injured."
As Aria shivered from chills, Quan offered an excuse that wasn't really an excuse.
"I can't cut germs."
It wasn't Quan's fault.
"Jane, please…… take my code……"
Aria pleaded with desperate eyes.
If she lost consciousness, it was better to transfer the code to Jane as soon as
possible.
The problem was that transferring the code without accessing Bioflora's main system would
result in Aria's death.
"It's okay."
She had already made up her mind.
"I'm also a diplomat of the Tormia Kingdom. I know that in a national crisis, I'm the
first to be sacrificed. Take the code."
Jane looked at Aria with pity.
'A tool of the kingdom.'
According to Rufist's philosophy, humans were nothing more than expendable tools to
maintain the system.
***
—I won't tolerate a second mistake.
The guild master's voice seeped into her mind like a hypnotic suggestion.
"There's one way."
Aria stared blankly at Jane's words.
"What way?"
"I'll connect my mind to yours and infiltrate Bioflora. If we transfer the code in the
main system, there won't be any issues."
As a mental mage, Jane could maximize her Spirit Zone's tendrils to connect with
Bioflora.
"But then you'll be in danger!"
In that state, she wouldn't be able to fight at full strength.
Moreover, the place she had to reach was the heart of Bioflora, where the upper-class
demi-humans resided.
"You're right, Aria. We're tools of the kingdom."
Jane placed a hand on Aria's shoulder.
"But we're not suicide squads. If there's a way, it's only human to take it."
"Jane……"
As Aria's eyes welled up, Jane turned away.
"Protect Aria. It won't take long."
Recognizing the gravity of her resolve, Quan bowed respectfully.
'Cosmic Rail.'
After connecting her Spirit Zone to Aria, Jane soared back into the sky on the Cosmic
Rail.
"Impressive."
Despite the mental burden, the speed at which the rail unfolded was unchanged.

"Hweeee. Hweee."
The mournful tune fit perfectly with Ludum's desolate landscape.
"Who's there? Show yourself."
Whoooosh!

'What's with that guy?'
A goblin in a tattered red cloak, with a distinctive hooked nose and goggles, sat on the
rooftop railing, whistling.
The whistling faded into the air, and the goblin adjusted his goggles with a finger
before looking up at the overcast sky.
"Is there a need to fight, comrade?"

"I'll head to Bioflora now."

As leaders of their respective guilds, mission failure would deal a fatal blow to their

The magic race, Filkers.
