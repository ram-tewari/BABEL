Chapter 657:
Mercenary Recruitment (1)
Alpheas Magic Academy.
Spring, the start of the new semester, was still a long way off.
Most of the teachers had returned to their hometowns, and Bishop Etella
of the Karsis Monastic Order was deep in meditation at the training hall.
A great evil is approaching.
Even without hearing any news about Ra Enemy, her mind—sharpened and
purified—was already perceiving the shift in the world.
I must step forward.
Among the top ascetics of the Karsis Monastic Order, Etella held a
singular status.
She was the bishop overseeing one of the twelve dioceses and was the
direct successor of the Yin-Yang Pulse Fist, a martial art passed down from Karsis Yung, the founder of
the Order. It was a feat only achievable by a genius said to appear once every hundred years.
"Bishop, are you inside?"
As a monk and a zoner, Etella had sensed her visitor approaching from
over a kilometer away.
"Come in."
Opening her eyes slowly, she greeted the guest with a graceful
smile.
"It's been a while, Bishop Rodi."
A man in his sixties, shorter than Etella and leaning on a cane, entered
the hall.
His snowy-white eyebrows extended like wings across his kindly
face.
"You're still at the school, I see. You should begin your rounds of the
diocese soon."
"Yes, I plan to leave in a few days. I should have visited you
first."
Rodi waved his hand.
"Think nothing of it. You're busy. You can't be as idle as an old man
like me."
Knowing pleasantries could go on endlessly, Etella gestured for him to
sit.
"Please, take a seat. But what brings you here?"
Once they'd exchanged greetings and sat down, Rodi's expression darkened
significantly.
"This is not easy for me to say…"
It was clearly not good news, but Etella remained calm.
"Archbishop Raphael has passed."
"…What?"
Etella's normally unshakable composure wavered for the first
time.
"Forgive me, but did you just say—"
"He was murdered."
Etella felt her heart drop.
"There must be some mistake."
What reason could anyone have to bear a grudge against the Archbishop,
the head of the Karsis Monastic Order?
Even if there were, Raphael had taught Etella martial arts himself. As a
master of the Yin-Yang Pulse Fist, he wouldn't have been easily overpowered.
"At first, the Order couldn't believe it either. We assumed, as always,
that he had gone off on a spiritual retreat. But three months ago, his body was found in the central
mountain range."
"No… why? Why would Master…"
Though overwhelmed with emotion, Etella kept listening as Rodi
maintained his calm tone.
"The moment we confirmed the cause of death, there was only one
suspect—Shagall."
"Shagall…?"
"One of the world's 100 most dangerous individuals. Experts call him
'Shagall of the Rapid Blade.' Archbishop Raphael's body had 273 stab wounds. They said it was as if he'd
been pierced at incredible speed."
Etella's lips trembled.
"In any case, the Order is preparing to elect the next Archbishop,
and…"
"Where is he?"
Etella cut him off.
"Where is this man, Shagall?"
Surely they wouldn't bring this news to her without knowing.
"We've received word that he recently entered Bashka."
Having said this, Rodi quietly looked at Etella, who had her eyes
closed.
"Bishop Etella, revenge is merely the methodology of evil. The doctrine
of the Karsis Monastic Order that the Archbishop preached—"
"I know."
Etella opened her eyes again.
"I do not believe in repaying death with death. But as his disciple, I
cannot forgive myself for never hearing my Master's final will."
She had to meet Shagall.
She needed to know what kind of man he was, and why he would kill
someone like the Archbishop who had chosen the path of goodness.
"I'll go to the capital. Bishop Rodi, please look after the
Order."
Seeing the fierce determination in Etella's eyes, Rodi sighed and
stood.
"Please be careful. I know your strength, but he is extremely
dangerous."
Etella rose and bowed in the manner of a monk.
"Thank you for delivering the news in person. Take care."
Even after Rodi had left the training hall, Etella remained standing,
dazed.
"Master…"
The warm, gentle face of Raphael—who had treated her like a daughter
since childhood—floated in her memory.
"Hrk…"
Unable to hold back the tears, Etella fell to her knees.
"Sniff… sob…"
Soft sobs echoed quietly through the training hall.
Deep in the forest behind the royal capital, within the subterranean
Geoja Mountains, a campfire crackled and burned.
Seated on a rock, Shagall gazed at a dagger gleaming in the
firelight.
With a flick, the blade pierced his opposite palm—but it didn't go
through the back of his hand.
As he slowly pulled the dagger out, the spring-loaded blade, once
retracted into the hilt, extended again.
A magic trick knife that could hurt no one.
It had been a gift, given to him when he first joined the Greenleaf
Circus Troupe.
"No moon tonight."
Looking up at the pitch-black sky, he suddenly recalled a battle from a
few months ago.
It was just as dark then.
Archbishop Margo Raphael of the Karsis Monastic Order.
Even among the many formidable foes Shagall had faced and slain, Raphael
had been a man whose survival couldn't be guaranteed.
—"Whichever path you take, the road home will always lie behind
you."
What had been uttered mid-battle ended up being the man's last words—and
somehow, they stuck with him.
Perhaps it was because those words reminded him of someone important in
his memories.
"Captain…"
Twenty-five years ago, when Shagall was seven.
Abandoned as an infant with no knowledge of his parents, he began to
realize he was different from others.
The Scent of Events.
It was speculated to be an extreme development of olfactory cognition,
possibly dating back to his time in the womb before birth. But at the time, all he knew was that he
could remember things more vividly than others.
So when the Greenleaf Circus Troupe came through the Märchen Kingdom
again after two years, Shagall instantly recognized one of them.
"Are you performing today?"
While the other performers set up tents in the meadow, the ringmaster
Raiden, peeling an apple off to the side, noticed a filthy, stray-like child standing beside him.
Raiden looked ridiculous enough to be a clown without makeup—his
expression always a little vacant.
"Well, we'll perform once we've drawn a crowd, right?"
When Shagall hesitated, unable to keep the conversation going, Raiden
caught on to the boy's intention.
"Do you want to see the circus?"
Shagall shook his head.
"Then what?"
Backing away slowly, Shagall suddenly pushed off the ground and tumbled
gracefully through the air.
"Ho?"
For a kid, his athletic ability was remarkable.
"I want to join the circus. I don't need money—just let me eat."
Raiden, catching on to what kind of life Shagall must've had, gave him a
sympathetic look.
But being able to do a single tumble wasn't enough to join.
Just look at Tia, who was the same age and had already mastered solo
acrobatics, unicycling, ball balancing, and tightrope walking.
"Sorry, but I'll need to discuss it with the troupe first. It might look
glamorous, but this job is really dangerous."
Shagall didn't give up.
"I don't care if I die. Whether it's today or tomorrow—it's all the same
anyway."
Watching the tears roll down his cheek, Raiden gestured for him to come
closer and held out the paring knife he'd been using on the apple.
"If life's that unbearable… I'll end it for you."
It would only be later that Shagall realized the look in Raiden's eyes
at that moment was that of a true professional.
"…Do it. I'm not afraid of dying."
Staring coldly at the trembling boy, Raiden suddenly thrust the knife
toward Shagall's heart like lightning.
"Kh!"
The boy gasped, but didn't move a muscle.
As Raiden watched him standing firm with clenched fists, he finally
smiled and pulled the knife back.
With a click, the spring-loaded blade emerged from the hilt, and Shagall
collapsed onto the ground, his legs giving out.
"What… what was that?"
"Scared you, huh? It's a magician's knife."
"A… magician's?"
Raiden demonstrated by pressing his finger to the tip of the
blade.
"See? The blade retracts like this. But for sleight of hand, you need
quick hands. Like this."
He held it in reverse grip and stabbed at his forearm. With a swoosh,
the blade snapped in and out.
"See that? Looks real, right?"
Then Raiden held out the knife to the wide-eyed Shagall.
"Practice every day. The key is to stab quickly."
Shagall's eyes lit up.
"Then… I'm really in the circus now?"
"Let's just call you a trainee for now. Tomorrow the performances
begin—go into town with Tia and pass out flyers."
"Tia?"
Raiden chuckled.
"There's a stubborn girl about your age. Try to get along. She might be
a handful."
After joining the Greenleaf Circus, Shagall learned many skills from
Raiden and had mastered most circus acts by the age of fourteen.
"Tia! Hurry up! We've got flyers to pass out!"
"Hold on! I need to do my makeup!"
Tia stepped out of the tent in a short skirt, her clown makeup
finished.
Though they'd grown up together like siblings since the age of seven,
now that Shagall was hitting puberty, he started to see her differently.
Trying to play it off, he pointed at her and laughed.
"Pfft! That snot looks way too real! Wait, is it actually real?"
"Shut up! This is what kids like!"
Win the kids over, and the parents will bring them to the show—simple
logic.
"Is it that weird? Should I just wipe it off?"
"No, it's not weird. It's natural for a clown to dress like a
clown."
"You little… you wanna die?"
Tia clawed at him, but ever since puberty, catching Shagall had become
impossible.
"Waaah! The clown's coming to get me!"
Truthfully, Shagall liked her makeup.
Because of her role, Tia had to endure the leering eyes of adults. He
always wanted to punch them.
But as long as no one knew what her bare face looked like, he could
handle it.
"Hey, hey! Bark! Bark!"
Woof! Woof!
Their game of tag came to a stop at the sound of barking near the other
tent.
"What's that?"
Peeking around the tent, they saw three boys from town throwing rocks at
the dog guarding the Greenleaf Circus.
"Die, mutt! Take that! And that!"
Shagall shouted furiously.
"Hey! What the hell do you think you're doing? Knock it off!"
"Hmph! Who are you to boss us around, circus boy? Don't you know it's
our dad who gave you the performance permit?"
"I don't care whose kid you are—why are you attacking a dog that's done
nothing to you? Want to get smacked?"
Taller than the others, Shagall's threat made them flinch—but not quite
back down.
"You think you can take us on? Hey, clown girl! We'll be back to peek at
your panties later!"
Shagall's eyes flared.
"You little punks—!"
But before he could lunge, Raiden had already approached the boys with a
cold, piercing gaze.
"Ugh!"
Even the most fearless brats buckled under the stare that had tamed
beasts.
"Go home. The performance hasn't even started yet."
The boys hesitated, then scurried off. Shagall, still fuming, turned to
Raiden.
"Captain! Why'd you just let them go? You should've taught them a
lesson!"
"They're just kids. Once or twice, it's fine to let it slide."
"So what if they're kids? They're rotten to the core!"
"Maybe. Or maybe not. But how would you feel if someone said that
because of one of your own mistakes?"
"I wouldn't care. I don't make mistakes."
Raiden ruffled his hair.
"Shagall, everyone makes mistakes. Even ones we don't realize. But the
reason you can think like that…"
He bent to eye level and looked into the boy's eyes.
"…is probably because someone already forgave you."
As Shagall stood there, silent and thoughtful, Tia's painted clown face
curved into a quiet smile.
Because someone already forgave you.
Those words Raiden had once told him… they would remain in Shagall's
heart as a pillar of his beliefs.
Until the day he met Ra Enemy.
