Chapter 1204:
Heart and Error (1)
In Melkidu's Corkolas, after taking control of Johan Cartel, Shirone was
guided by its leader, Aria, to meet with the underworld organization.
The sudden appearance of a ship in a place where all sectors were
strictly separated was enough to surprise them.
"It's the system."
The fact that the bribery program was one of the legitimate ways to
enter the Core made it seem more plausible.
Of course, reaching this ship had not been a smooth process.
Desperado and Red Union gang members had attacked them, but against
Shirone's group, no weapon had any effect.
During the truce negotiations, the leaders of each organization accepted
Shirone's proposal.
"You're leaving?"
Since Shirone had no intention of ruling over Corkolas, the best choice
was to give him what he wanted and send him away quickly.
"Fine, we'll let it go this time. But if you ever set foot here
again, one of us is disappearing."
Of course, it would be the gangs that disappeared, but their aggression
had already been proven through Johan Cartel.
"This way."
The ship was stocked with weapons unavailable in the real world.
"120 million points."
The captain named his price, and Aria agreed without hesitation.
Shirone realized that if she wanted to, Aria could amass the 10 billion
points needed to enter the Core.
"She must be guarding Corkolas for the sake of Johan Cartel in the
real world. To ensure that real-world criminals can easily hide here."
There was no need to create unsolved cases when simply hiding in
Corkolas was enough.
"Evil seeps into everything."
For Shirone, whose mission was to save humanity, this was not something
he could overlook.
"Move it."
As Aria's orders sent the gang members carrying the weapons, the captain
glanced at Shirone's group.
"Who are these small fries? I haven't seen them
before."
Aria answered in an uninterested tone.
"They know about the bribery program. Said they wanted to meet
you."
"Oh?"
Even if they had obtained hints in the inner track, one still needed the
approval of the Three Great Gangs to meet the underworld organization.
"Impressive. You have enough points, right?"
The captain, being a system manager, wouldn't be swayed by anything
except points and dice.
"Yes. A friend of mine is currently imprisoned in the capital's
Parmé prison. I heard that meeting him might provide a solution."
The captain lit his pipe.
"…I can connect you. But Parmé is tricky. The head warden there is
no pushover."
Shirone remained silent as the captain pointed toward the cargo
hold.
"You can't enter normally. There are plenty of empty crates, so
hide in one. The smuggling fee is 10 million points."
"Ten million?"
It was far more expensive than the Royal Entry item.
For other users, this might be acceptable since they wouldn't have to go
through a "persuasion" process, but for Shirone, there was no merit in paying.
"Curtis said the minimum cost was 50 million. I expected additional
fees since we hadn't fully investigated the bribery program, but if things keep going like this, we
might run out of points."
Currently, their funds were twice the minimum requirement, about 100
million points.
"That's pretty expensive."
As Shirone remarked and turned his head, Aria let out a sigh.
"I'll pay for it."
As the points were deducted, the captain moved toward the bridge.
"We'll leave as soon as the cargo is unloaded. Get in the crate
now. It has to be sealed."
Before heading to the cargo hold, Shirone turned back to Aria.
"Thank you."
"Hmph. I'm only giving it to you so you never come back. This is
the end of my involvement."
She was telling him not to return even if things went wrong
later.
"This was a cheap price to pay."
Thanks to him, she had secured 120 million worth of supplies, so giving
away 10 million was nothing.
"See you next time."
As Shirone bid farewell, Aria, stepping off the ship, clenched her
teeth.
"Damn bastard. I thought he was a fool, but he's
sharp."
It was the kind of game she hated the most.
Once Shirone's group hid inside the crates, the crew nailed the lids
shut.
"Departure in ten minutes."
After the sailors left, Penna grumbled.
"This is suffocating. Do we really have to do this? Wouldn't Royal
Entry be enough?"
Iruki responded.
"This might also be part of the bribery program. Regardless, we
need to be near the captain to meet the underworld organization."
A buzzer sounded, and the ship rocked slightly on the waves.
Then, a dazzling light of multiple colors began rushing past their
vision.
"Spatial movement."
As the light faded, Shirone knew they had arrived. He turned to his
companions.
"Everyone alive?"
"Yeah. But do we really have to stay like this? Can't we just break
out?"
"Not sure. But since it's smuggling…"
Just then, the door opened, and workers began unloading the
cargo.
"Stay quiet. If we get caught, it's over."
Unlike in Corkolas, the sailors' voices were filled with tension.
After all the cargo was unloaded, the crate containing Shirone's group
was loaded onto a cart and taken to a secluded part of the port. A worker removed the nails and knocked
twice on the crate.
"Tomorrow morning."
Leaving behind what seemed like a code phrase, the worker disappeared.
Slowly, Shirone's group opened the lid.
Nade groaned.
"Ugh, it stinks. Did they store fish in here?"
As each of them grumbled about the short journey, only Penna remained
silent.
"What's wrong?"
"Is this… really Parmé?"
Among players traveling through Melkidu, the capital was one of the most
frequently visited locations, second only to Ruki.
Penna had spent a lot of time in the capital for various reasons, but
this was her first time at the port.
"I didn't even know there was a port. I've never heard about it
from other players either. Are we sure we're in the right place?"
"Probably."
Shirone spoke as he stepped out of the crate.
"It's likely an area that can only be accessed through smuggling.
In other words, this is the administrators' world. Penna, how much of Parmé have you
explored?"
"I've been everywhere. I know the capital's geography inside and
out."
Something clicked in her mind, and she turned to Shirone.
"All of Melkidu's spaces are blocked off by invisible walls, right?
But there's one exception—the royal castle's walls. I always thought it was just background scenery,
but…"
"There could be a port behind the castle."
Shirone scanned the surroundings. Darkness covered the sea, and several
docked ships glowed with dim lights.
"This must be the middle layer of the system," Shirone
concluded.
"All of Melkidu's essential supplies must pass through this middle
layer. If that's the case, smuggling could let us access any sector."
Iruki spoke up.
"Hmm… So, in the end, the outer and inner tracks aren't completely
separate spaces. The government buildings in towns—the area behind their doors was the inner track.
Instead of separate spaces, it's more like a hierarchy of permissions."
Eden added,
"If Curtis had been imprisoned in a different city's jail instead
of the royal capital, we probably would have ended up inside that city's government building—maybe in an
administrator's quarters or an item storage room."
Nade commented,
"That sounds insanely risky. And this is the capital, the biggest
hub of supplies. That means people could steal items, and there would be high-ranking administrators
everywhere."
Shirone nodded.
"Right. In a place where any crime is considered innocent, we just
committed another crime."
"If we get caught, it's over."
It wouldn't just end with imprisonment.
Nade smirked.
"But we've got some time until morning, don't we?"
"Wait, what…?"
As Penna stared blankly, Iruki chuckled.
"Checking things out doesn't sound like a bad idea. There might be
unreleased items or things that got censored and scrapped. What do you think, Shirone?"
Given how quickly their Crime Points were being spent, the risk might be
worth taking. But in truth, Shirone's curiosity about the middle layer of the system was the stronger
reason.
"Alright. Let's explore—just until morning."
As the three of them left, Penna looked terrified.
"Wait, are you seriously doing this? What if you get
caught?"
"There's no stopping them," Eden sighed as she
followed.
"Once they get obsessed with something, they won't stop until they
see it through."
The five stowaways vanished into the darkness of the port.
In a dream, time is determined by the quality and quantity of
information. As Miro and Ymir clashed, Shirone absorbed a massive influx of data from his transcendent
dream.
"Hup!"
Armand dove into the river with all her strength, desperate to save
Yorahan.
Through small-world resonance, she knew his exact location, but the
powerful current had already carried him far away.
'Humans can't hold their breath for long.'
On top of that, the shock of hitting the water had likely forced him to
exhale most of his air. Time was running out.
"River, give him to me."
The river's center reversed its flow, and Yorahan's body drifted into
Armand's arms.
She surged upward, breaking the surface and gasping for air as she
dragged him out.
"Hff!"
The moment their connection through small-world resonance reestablished,
Armand winced from the suffocating pain in his body.
'He's dying.'
Recalling all the things she had yet to tell him, she began performing
artificial respiration.
For someone who found it much easier to synchronize with inanimate
objects, it was fortunate that Yorahan was unconscious.
His lungs, reacting on instinct, convulsed and expelled a large amount
of water.
Then, as she pressed her lips to his again—
"Cough! Cough!"
Yorahan jolted upright, coughing violently.
"Huh?…"
His blurry vision slowly cleared, revealing Armand's face.
'She's beautiful.'
A loud smack echoed as stars burst in front of his eyes.
"Ow! What the hell was that for?!"
Armand's glare sharpened as the exhausted Yorahan groaned in
protest.
"Seriously, humans! I just saved your life, and that's the first
thing you think of?"
"I mean, well…"
How was he supposed to control his thoughts?
"Anyway, if you're alive, get up. We need to head back before it
gets dark."
She wasn't afraid of the night, but something in her instincts told her
she shouldn't spend it alone with Yorahan.
"Ugh!"
As he tried to sit up, Yorahan collapsed back onto the ground.
"Sorry, I can't. My body's too weak to move right now."
Armand sighed in resignation.
She could guess how he felt—his internal organs had been forced to work
while unconscious, so it was probably like getting struck by a hammer from the inside.
"Haa, seriously."
Two hours later, as the sun had completely set, a small campfire
crackled beside the river.
Armand had caught some fish, which Yorahan had cleaned and skewered on
wooden sticks.
"Alright, they're ready."
Still drying her soaked clothes, Armand sat curled up in a blanket at
the edge of the fire.
"I hate fire."
That was only natural for a member of the Fireborn.
'Right, even if she eats meat, she prefers it raw.'
But as a human, Yorahan had no reason to reject a properly cooked
meal.
"Still, you should come a little closer. You need to warm
up."
Armand shot him a suspicious look, making Yorahan flinch.
"No, I mean… you might catch a cold. Sorry."
Even if only for a brief moment, they had shared their thoughts through
small-world resonance.
'This is so awkward I'm going crazy.'
Now that his feelings had been laid bare, denying them would be
meaningless.
The only thing he could focus on was the fish, slowly roasting over the
fire.
"Hmm?"
Hearing footsteps, he lifted his head to see Armand approaching, still
wrapped in the blanket.
"Smells good. Let's eat."
The moment the firelight illuminated her face, Yorahan realized.
'It's her.'
A flower had begun to bloom in his heart.
