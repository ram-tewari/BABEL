Chapter 539:
Two women appeared on a ruined road.
They were Paknyeo, ranked 9th in the Council of Ten, and Shura, ranked
7th—both having just arrived in Apocalypse.
Paknyeo wore rags that exposed half her chest, barefoot with her hair
lazily tied in a turban. In stark contrast, Shura was impeccably dressed in a deep green dress and
matching shoes.
While Paknyeo's disheveled look suited Apocalypse more, it was Shura,
the apostle of Prajna, who truly belonged in a world of deception.
"There's no one alive. Where's Shirone?"
Paknyeo's calm voice carried clearly, even against the raging
wind.
"Are you doubting my coder?"
"There's no one here."
With that, Paknyeo abruptly stopped walking.
Shura glanced at her with suspicion, then looked down, hearing the
trickle of liquid hitting the ground.
Paknyeo was urinating, the stream running down her legs. Shura's face
twisted with disgust.
"Seriously? You stink. Do you even bathe?"
"When it rains. Everything gets purified."
Shura followed Paknyeo's gaze toward the city.
As if measured with a ruler, a thick band of filthy-looking storm clouds
hovered above the urban area, and water poured from them as if wrung out by some invisible hand.
From afar, nature's rhythm might appear beautiful. But up close, when
traveling beside someone like this, it was unbearable.
"Anyway, Shirone is here. I don't need to code the language for you,
right? Not that you'd need it."
"Words convey nothing."
It might have been an insight unique to Paknyeo, but to someone like
Shura—her complete opposite—it was an insult.
"Of course. With a beast's brain, I doubt you'd ever understand.
Honestly, how did I end up teamed up with you?"
The vote to include Shirone in the Council of Ten had resulted in a
tie—five in favor, five against.
Those in favor pointed to Shirone's formidable manifestation ability,
while those against emphasized that he was not an immortal, but merely a Prajna.
Still, the reason the Council had sent not one but two executives was
because they'd received intelligence that Miro had entered Apocalypse to restore Shirone's data.
If the mental construct of an Ancient—Elysion—could be reconstructed
here in Apocalypse, it would bring great gain to the Council. Thus, the ones sent were Shura and
Paknyeo.
"Adrias Miro…"
Shura, recalling her face, spoke firmly.
"I'll make this clear. The reason I'm here is to contain Miro. If I have
to fight her, even I won't be able to handle anything else. So don't get in my way."
"You think you can handle Miro?"
Paknyeo's blunt question brought a strange silence. After a long pause,
Shura answered.
"That filthy monkey… she's getting too bold."
"…."
Though the silence was the same, the air around them now trembled, ready
to explode.
Paknyeo spun on her heel. Her black blade split the air, tearing toward
Shura.
Shura raised her index finger in front of her throat and effortlessly
blocked the blow.
"I said no. Especially here. This is my world."
"…Want to see it through to the end?"
One of Shura's eyebrows twitched.
"Tch. I'm the idiot for trying to reason with a beast."
Surayacha Paknyeo.
She ranked 9th, the lowest among the Council, but once she ignited, even
the other executives were reluctant to face her axing.
"Enough. Let's split up here. Working with you is pure madness. Do what
you want."
With a burst of aura, Shura vanished without a trace. Paknyeo turned and
began walking toward the city as if nothing had happened.
"I'm hungry."
It was the only feeling she had at the moment.
"Curia, please! You've got to wake up!"
Malta's voice was loud enough to echo outside the engine room, but Curia
only stared at him as if she couldn't understand what he was saying.
"What are you talking about? Are you crazy?"
"I'm not the crazy one! This world is fake! It's a lie!"
"Humanity is extinct! I'm just grateful to live with you! That's all I
need! Please, just calm down!"
Malta gasped, his chest heaving from frustration.
Why couldn't she see it?
This world was nothing more than a virtual space beneath the Under
Coder.
At first, he'd thought she would understand if he simply explained it
all.
He believed that if she understood, she'd return to her old self.
And being the sharp woman she was, she had shown some doubt.
But no matter what method he used, she could never fully accept that
this world was virtual.
"Malta, I love you. I don't need anything else as long as I have you. So
please…"
Curia embraced his neck and kissed him.
Her scent. Her touch.
Malta's eyes closed involuntarily as her information washed over
him.
"Information! Damn information!"
He suddenly jolted upright and shoved her away.
"You're not my wife! You're fake!"
"Darling…"
Storming out of the room, Malta locked the door behind him to keep Curia
inside.
"Darling! Open the door! Please!"
Malta shut his eyes and tried to block out her voice.
When he first took her out of the cryogenic capsule, there had been hope
she could be restored.
But over time, her data had begun to sync with Apocalypse itself.
'I have to isolate her. Don't let her get exposed to anything
else.'
As always, once the knocking ceased, Malta sat down on the sofa.
He no longer felt guilty—because he knew she wasn't real.
'It's a curse, really.'
Sometimes he wondered if he was the one who'd gone insane.
"Huh? No one else here yet?"
Marsha stepped inside, the first to return from the field.
The group had agreed to split up and search different areas to speed up
the investigation.
"You're the first. Find anything?"
Marsha shook her head and went to the water dispenser.
"Nothing. I wonder if Miro found anything."
Malta stared at the back of her neck as she drank.
"I've been walking all day. I'm exhausted. I'll be in my room."
"Go ahead."
Even after she disappeared into her room, Malta didn't look away, as if
chasing her afterimage.
'She's real.'
A woman who came from the same world as him.
'There's no such thing as hope here.'
Malta stood and quietly opened her door.
"…Did you know?"
Marsha sat on the bed, smoking her pipe. She didn't lose her smile even
as she met Malta's lustful gaze.
"I'm used to that look."
Malta remained calm.
"You know my situation. Can't you show some mercy?"
"How about you control yourself? I don't like hurting people who've
helped me."
But that was a choice Malta's desire could never make.
"In that case… I have no choice."
As Malta's Spirit Zone surged with multisensory force, Marsha's smile
began to stiffen.
'This guy… He really is strong.'
Malta strode forward with heavy steps.
"Accept it. In the real world, I was a certified fourth-class mage.
Aside from that woman Miro, no one in our group could defeat me."
That was precisely why now was the time.
"Heh, aren't you underestimating me a bit? Sure, Miro is impressive, but
I had my heyday too, you know."
"Talking a lot is just a sign you're nervous."
Marsha bit down on her long pipe.
It looked like she was taking a puff, but in reality, she used the
motion to discreetly toss it aside.
Now!
She cast a teleportation spell, flying straight for the door—but Malta
was already there, blocking her way.
It was a reaction only possible through prediction.
"Not bad. But weren't you underestimating me a little?"
Malta raised both hands, unleashing a powerful air press.
Marsha was flung horizontally and slammed into the wall, crashing down
to the floor with a heavy thud.
"Ghk!"
Looking down at her sprawled form, Malta spoke calmly.
"I'd prefer it if you didn't resist. The man you're up against isn't
some street thug."
Then why are you doing something only a thug would do?
Marsha bit back a string of curses, knowing that reasoning was pointless
with a man consumed by lust.
That should be enough of a setup.
With a sigh that sounded like resignation, Marsha slowly raised her
hands and stood.
"Fine. Let's end this without any more trouble. I don't want to die
either."
When Malta looked at her suspiciously, she turned her back to him
entirely.
"What, are you scared? You think you can even touch me acting like
that?"
Rage flashed in Malta's eyes as he grabbed her from behind and pressed a
kiss to the nape of her neck.
"Just… don't be too rough."
Covering the tattoo on the back of her left hand with her right, Marsha
activated the Regulation Exception: Dagger of Depravity.
The tattoo glowed, and a solid black blade materialized in her right
hand.
I, on the other hand, might be a little rough.
She twisted her body quickly and swung the dagger, but Malta leaned his
upper body back with unflinching calm.
"I told you, I'm no street thug."
He caught her wrist mid-swing and used his other hand to slam her into
the wall by the back of the neck.
Sparks burst behind her eyes as her head hit.
He then pinned her delicate wrist against the wall, and before the
dagger could even hit the floor, it vanished.
"I knew you were a Regulation Exception user. And if that's all you've
got, it means you never learned even the 36 standard Regulation spells. Trash from the gutter. So what
happens if that dagger hits?"
"…Fine, I'll tell you. Just let me go."
"There's no need to know. I'm sure it's lethal. I'll just use a sleep
spell. That'll be easier for both of us."
Grinding her teeth, Marsha scratched at the tattoo on her right palm
with her fingers.
Another dagger materialized.
"Isn't talking a lot a sign of nerves?"
The moment Malta's eyebrow twitched, she kicked him hard in the groin
and drove the dagger into the back of his neck with all her strength.
"GRAAAGH!"
Startled, Malta hurled her aside. Gasping for breath, she scrambled out
of the room.
"Damn it! Bitten by a rat?!"
Malta's eyes widened in shock as he inspected the wound.
"What… what the hell is this?"
From the black gash in his neck, dark gas was streaming out
nonstop.
It's not a physical effect…
The dagger had vanished, and there was no blood—but it felt as if
something was being constantly drained from his body, and it was far from pleasant.
Regulation Exception: Dagger of Depravity.
It was modeled after the dagger Marsha had used to kill her adoptive
father. Its core concept was perceiving the human body as a container of desire, then carving a sheath
into it to drain everything.
Each sheath drained desire at a rate of 1% per minute. If left
untreated, it could bring even a powerful opponent to collapse within 100 minutes.
This is dangerous. If I want to undo the Exception, I need to find
her.
Malta realized the smoke pouring from his neck was flowing outward and
curving off somewhere.
Even a single stab from that dagger could incapacitate a superior foe,
but the gas would always trail toward Marsha, inevitably revealing her location.
The price of a Regulation Exception. If it works like this, the only way
to break it is to track and kill the user…
A wicked grin spread across Malta's face.
If he'd made a mistake, it was in holding back his offensive spells to
preserve her body—but now, there was no reason to hesitate.
Just wait. I'll kill you in one second.
Malta stepped out and turned toward the direction the smoke was
flowing.
It was leading out of the engine room—he heard a door slam shut.
Using the darkness?
If she'd gone into a pitch-black tunnel, finding the black smoke would
be much harder.
Only then did Malta realize her tactic, and he bared his teeth in
rage.
"You filthy little bitch!"
