Chapter 305:
Master of the Avatars (3)
As Shirone burst into laughter, Zion's face turned red.
A lowly commoner looking down on him and laughing? He had never
experienced such humiliation in his life.
Shirone, still chuckling, shook his head.
"Is that so? It seems a lot of time has passed. Do you really know
nothing?"
"What are you talking about?"
"Teraje has no sons. She only gives birth to daughters. You are
nothing but a shell of Teraje."
Zion's face went blank.
What did he mean by no sons? Only daughters? Then who was he? Was there
such a thing as a child without parents?
Zion's face twisted like a wounded beast.
"Shut up! How would someone like you know that? You're insane.
You've gone mad after being consumed by that monster! Who are you to speak about my mother like
that?"
"Didn't Teraje tell you? You must have been a truly pathetic fool.
She respects ability above all else, even if you're not her child. But it seems you weren't even worth
that."
"When did you meet my mother?"
Zion, losing his temper, charged.
He had lived his whole life with the pride of being Teraje's son. And
now he was being called a shell?
No, it was the ramblings of a madman. How could a commoner like him have
ever met Teraje, the emperor of the continent?
"I am royalty! A bastard like you has no right to speak to
me!"
As the blade of Zion's gauntlet approached Shirone's neck, a photon
cannon struck Zion's abdomen.
Zion spat out a mouthful of blood and was thrown 10 meters into a sack
of flour.
"Cough! Cough!"
The sack tore open, and flour poured over Zion like a waterfall.
His face, covered in flour and blood, looked like that of a
clown.
Armand reported: Combat ineffective.
It was the only information Zion could comprehend.
Shirone walked over, holding a photon cannon in his palm.
"Humans love such things. Kings, bloodlines, heritage, families.
But Teraje, whom you follow, once said this to me."
Zion struggled to lift his head. He watched the photon cannon in
Shirone's palm vibrate and grow.
Shirone mimicked Teraje's tone.
"Even if you are royalty, you are merely part of the human lineage.
But..."
Shirone's photon cannon grew stronger.
Half out of his mind, Zion seemed unaware that it was a spell meant to
attack him. He stared at the light with a dazed expression.
Shirone grinned, holding the photon cannon.
"Talent is bestowed by the heavens."
The photon cannon fell in a brutal arc. Tears streamed down Zion's face
as he watched the light's trajectory in despair.
A powerful shockwave shook the food storage. The photon cannon, buried
in Armand's fibers, tore through Zion's organs until it exhausted all its power and dissipated.
Flour spread in a ring, creating a thick fog.
Amy and Reina anxiously scanned the white smoke. A shadow appeared, and
Shirone emerged.
Amy couldn't bring herself to speak.
Even if that boy was Shirone, the aura emanating from him felt like
holding a ticking time bomb—dangerous and unstable.
"I'm going to the surface. You need to run as fast and as far as
you can. I'll handle things here."
The moment of truth had arrived.
Run fast and far? Such words were only spoken in the face of natural
disasters like typhoons or earthquakes.
"What are you planning to do? Whoever you are, you need to treat
your injuries. That body belongs to Shirone!"
"I am Shirone."
Shirone tilted his head back to look at the ceiling. His cold gaze
slowly descended to Amy.
"Are you disappointed?"
Amy couldn't breathe, unable to say a word.
Shirone channeled his immense rage to strengthen his mind. The sound of
blood vessels bursting was eerily audible beneath his skin.
As he cast a laser, a beam as thick as a log shot up to the ceiling. The
rapid accumulation of thermal energy caused the ceiling to shake.
"Shirone! Wait...!"
"It's dangerous! We need to run!"
Reina grabbed Amy's wrist and ran for the exit.
As they left the food storage, an explosion echoed from Shirone's
direction.
The shockwave sent them rolling on the ground. They turned their heads
to see a massive rock blocking the entrance to the food storage.
The court musicians' music turned discordant.
As an earthquake shook the grand hall, women screamed and collapsed. The
men also looked around in fear.
"An earthquake? Kazra isn't on a fault line, is it?"
"Everyone, get down! Get under the tables!"
At the guards' shouts, the nobles crawled under the tables. Those who
got there first fought with those who arrived later, resembling a bullfight.
The earthquake lasted about a minute before stopping.
The nobles, regaining their composure, crawled out from under the tables
with embarrassed expressions.
Orkamp and Eliza, supported by the guards, also looked pale.
There had never been an earthquake reported in Kazra's history. A sense
of foreboding crossed Orkamp's mind.
The north side of the grand hall exploded with a bang. Bricks flew in
all directions, and smoke filled the air.
A few nobles, already on edge, lost their composure. They fell to the
ground, flailing their arms and legs meaninglessly.
The first to grasp the situation were the palace guards and the nobles'
personal bodyguards.
Realizing it wasn't a natural disaster, the guards surrounded the king
and queen, and the bodyguards closed in on the nobles.
"An attack? What's happening?"
The guards searched for the head of security, Ritney Walker. He had been
there just moments ago but was nowhere to be found.
The chain of command was in disarray.
If it were terrorists from an enemy nation, the security level needed to
be raised to the highest. But there were too many factors to consider before making such a decision
without orders from a superior.
For a moment, nothing happened.
The nobles tried to maintain their dignity. They hid their true feelings
and peered into the hole in the floor.
But when a figure emerged, they fell back in shock.
"Is that... Shirone?"
Orkamp muttered with a puzzled expression.
The person who had wrecked the grand hall was none other than
Shirone.
He remembered seeing Shirone dancing with Reina just an hour ago. Why
was he doing this now?
"Shirone, what on earth are you doing?"
The guards blocked Orkamp's path.
"Your Majesty, don't approach. Something is wrong."
Even Orkamp could see that Shirone looked different from an hour ago.
His eye color had changed, making him look like a different person, but the most striking difference was
his expression.
Shirone never showed such emotional expressions. He always maintained a
calm poker face, but now he looked pained and angry.
"Modern castles are quite durable. Humans have come a long
way."
A guard approached, ready to draw his sword.
"Sir Shirone, using magic inside the castle is strictly
forbi...!"
Without turning, Shirone fired a photon cannon.
The guard dived and rolled on the floor. The photon cannon's beam chased
him, scraping the marble floor.
"From now on, no one speaks without my permission. I make the
decisions."
Shirone's seriousness was proven by his earlier attack. If the guard had
reacted a moment later, he would have been torn apart by the photon cannon.
The atmosphere in the grand hall turned icy. The nobles looked around,
confused and unable to understand.
For days, they had assessed Shirone as a herbivore—docile and honest, an
easy prey.
But now, even the most experienced nobles found Shirone's presence
ferocious and wild.
The guards, unable to find Walker, awaited orders from the deputy
commander. But someone who had always stayed out of responsibility couldn't make such a critical
decision.
"Deputy Commander, what should we do? Give us orders!"
The deputy commander anxiously rolled his eyes.
The main forces were stationed outside the inner castle, so even if
contacted, it would take time for them to arrive. It was abnormal for an incident to occur in the heart
of the castle, the grand hall.
"Deputy Commander! Orders! Should we activate the magic control
device?"
The deputy commander's face twisted.
"Do you think it's that easy, you fool?"
The magic control device disrupts a magician's concentration. But it
affects everyone, friend and foe alike. If activated, even their own magicians would be rendered
useless.
'Damn, what should I do? Should I activate the control device? But
making all our magicians useless just to catch one person? If it goes wrong, I'll have to take the
blame. Where the hell did Walker disappear to?'
Since it concerned the king's safety, he had to consider all possible
variables before making a decision.
But the pressure overwhelmed him, and his thoughts ended in frustration
and resentment towards Walker.
"The control device is a last resort. Why not leave it to
us?"
The deputy commander turned his head, feeling as if he had found a
savior. Kazra's head of magic, Aimar Bosun, was approaching.
"Ah, are you sure?"
"We've been closely monitoring Shirone's movements. It seems he's
finally showing his true colors. But we can handle it. With the security chief's permission, may we use
magic?"
"Of course. If you could take care of it..."
It was much better to share the responsibility than to bear it alone.
Besides, Bosun had significant influence in the palace, so he wouldn't ignore any fallout.
Bosun gave a fake smile and walked towards Shirone. One by one, his
subordinates emerged from the group of nobles and followed behind him.
"I'll stay by your side, sir."
The spot next to Bosun was taken by his closest aide, Mito
Ekkashi.
Standing at 153 centimeters, with thick eyelids and eyes that slanted
sharply, he had a childlike face.
Having roamed numerous battlefields since childhood, he had risen to the
rank of certified 6th-level magician in Kazra's magic department at the age of twenty-three.
Of course, there were extreme geniuses like Olifer Shina, who became a
certified 6th-level magician at the age of twenty. But such individuals were rare, even on a kingdom
scale, appearing perhaps once in a decade. Ekkashi was certainly considered the future of Kazra.
"This is an unexpected opportunity, sir."
"Hmm, unexpected events can easily turn into disasters. But this
time, you might be right."
Casting magic in the royal court was a serious crime. Moreover, Shirone
had attacked a royal guard and uttered treasonous words, effectively branding himself a condemned
criminal.
"So, what has made the first prince candidate so
angry?"
Shirone glared coldly at Bosun.
An hour ago, he had wanted to punch him. But now, he felt no such
desire. He only wanted to kill him.
"So, you're the one driving me mad."
Bosun's heart raced at the pure, 100% killing intent that didn't seem
human.
But he didn't lose his composure. He turned slightly and spoke to the
royal guard.
"Escort His Majesty to safety. I'll handle this."
"Understood. Your Majesty, please come."
Orkamp shook off the guard's hand.
He didn't know why Shirone had changed, but if he left, Bosun would kill
Shirone. That would mean handing over his position to the Teraje faction.
"Who said anything about running away? Who do you think is the king
here?"
"I-I'm sorry!"
Sensing his mistake, the guard knelt. In the urgency of the situation,
he had followed Bosun's words.
Under different circumstances, it might have been overlooked. But
considering Bosun was part of the Teraje faction, it was indeed a reckless move.
