Chapter 355:
Magic Society (1)
Leaving Reina's mansion, Shirone caught a carriage to the Magic
Association.
With Rian dropping out and leaving on his knight's quest, the schedule
had moved up sooner than expected. Although he could have stayed at Reina's mansion for another day, he
chose to head to the Association.
The spark Rian left behind ignited a fire in everyone's hearts. Tess
immediately returned home, saying she would start training right away, and Shirone also wanted to
immerse himself in the atmosphere of the Magic Association, the pinnacle of magic.
Upon arriving at the Association building, Shirone asked for Plu at the
reception desk. After the receptionist relayed the message through an internal line, Plu came down five
minutes later.
She frowned as soon as she saw Shirone. With Kangnan also out, there was
no one to keep her in check.
"Why are you back so soon? Acting like you're so busy."
"My business finished early. Can I stay here?"
Plu's gaze towards Shirone was far from kind. Without showing any sign
of hesitation, he bluntly asked for a place to stay.
This was the prestigious Magic Association, not a hostel for kids with
nowhere else to go.
However, since Kangnan had instructed her, she couldn't outright refuse.
With many eyes watching, Plu took Shirone to her designated area on the third floor.
"The Chief Secretary specifically asked me to look after you, but you
have to follow my instructions. If you disobey or wander around, I'll kick you out. Got it?"
"Yes."
From the start, Plu's attitude was prickly, leaving Shirone bewildered.
He hadn't done anything wrong, yet she had been unfriendly since their first meeting.
"Here, you can stay here."
Plu offered her own quarters, which she used when working late. The
small room had bunk beds attached to both walls.
Shirone cautiously unpacked his things. But before he could settle in,
Plu called him out.
"Follow me. I'll give you a tour."
Plu walked down the corridor with her magic staff held behind her back,
her knees straight, and her chin tilted up at a 15-degree angle.
"First, let me introduce myself. I'm Ravid Plu, 22 years old. I
graduated top of my class from the Royal Magic School and am currently a certified 8th-grade magician.
I'll be a 6th-grade in two years. My current position is the manager of the third floor of the Magic
Association's security department."
"Oh, I see. I'm..."
As Shirone began to introduce himself, she stopped and turned
coldly.
"I know. Arian Shirone. Attending Alpheas Magic School. Can use the
archangel's power, Ataraxia, and specializes in light magic. Your unique Unlocker ability is mass
attribution."
Shirone was stunned. Someone who didn't even know his face the day
before now knew everything about him. The most puzzling thing was her overly prickly attitude towards
him.
"You have a student named Dante at your school, right? He transferred
recently."
"Yes. Do you know Dante?"
"Why should I know Dante?"
"Oh, I just thought since he also attended the Royal Magic
School..."
Plu's sharp gaze made Shirone shrink. He couldn't figure out what to say
to please her.
"When I was a senior, Dante was a junior. I know you beat Dante. But if
you think that makes you special, you should stop. If I were three years younger, I'd be the top student
in the kingdom, not you."
'I never acted special...'
Shirone swallowed his words. He felt that no matter what he said, he
wouldn't get a good response.
As Plu patrolled the third floor, she occasionally introduced the
departments. Even the guards with graying hair bowed politely when they saw her. Each time, she nodded
briefly with a proud posture.
Plu was trying to show the successful side of a magician. The tour was
essentially a way to flaunt her position and train Shirone.
At that moment, a man drenched in sweat and carrying documents walked
down the corridor. Plu's confident face momentarily showed tension.
"Hello, Mr. Sakiri. Good morning."
Shirone looked closely at the man called Sakiri.
'So that's Sakiri.'
After the Arcane incident, when Alpheas and Kanis were summoned to the
Association, the investigator in charge was named Sakiri.
He looked like a tired old man, but his eyes were sharper than anyone
Shirone had ever met.
"Hello. Who's this? A new face."
"Oh, just giving a tour. Part of a special activity."
Plu lied smoothly.
The Magic Association, being the core institution of the kingdom's
magical society, received numerous tour requests from students. So Sakiri just nodded and walked
away.
Plu let out a small sigh and looked at Shirone. The atmosphere, which
had been stiff with her head held high, now felt awkward.
"That was Mr. Sakiri, a certified 5th-grade magician. He's very famous
in the inspection department. Any criminal caught by him won't even have bones left."
"Yes, I've heard of him."
"Really? Hmm."
For the first time, Plu smiled with satisfaction. Knowing that even a
mere magic school student was aware of someone she worked with restored some of her pride.
'Now I have a rough idea of how to handle him.'
Thinking this, Shirone licked his lips when a man got off the elevator
at the end of the corridor.
Seeing Plu, he approached, his eyes lighting up with interest when he
saw Shirone.
"Plu, is that Shirone?"
Plu greeted him with a bright smile.
"Oh, Mr. Gando. Hello."
The man's name was Klein Gando, a member of Gaold's elite guard like
Plu.
Known as "Gando of Thunder," he was a combat magician skilled
in electric magic, holding the position of head of security at the Magic Association and a certified
4th-grade magician.
He was tall, wore tight leather pants and studded boots, and had purple
hair slicked back with one strand left out. Despite his flashy appearance, his face looked
angelic.
Plu led Shirone to greet Gando.
"The Chief Secretary asked me to look after him. Hey, greet him."
Gando extended his hand first.
"I see. Nice to meet you. I'm Gando."
"Oh, hello."
As Shirone was about to bow, he quickly shook Gando's hand.
The awkward combination of a bow and handshake made Gando laugh.
"You look much younger than I expected. I thought you'd have a scary
face."
'Is that my image?'
Having rarely left Tormia, Shirone couldn't know. But recalling the
articles in the journals, he thought it was possible to be misunderstood.
"Are you on a regular patrol?"
"Yes. Starting from the top floor. How's the third floor? Any
issues?"
"No, as usual. Hohoho!"
Plu laughed, covering her mouth with pride as the third-floor
manager.
"Good. It seems Ms. Kangnan was summoned by the Chairman. She should be
back with him tomorrow. Just relax until then."
"Thank you."
Gando's kind demeanor made Shirone want to complain, "This sister
is bullying me," but he knew it would only cause trouble, so he kept it to himself.
As Gando headed back to the elevator, Plu's smiling face turned stern.
She resumed scolding Shirone.
"He's in charge of security at the Magic Association. My direct
superior. You've heard of Gando of Thunder, right?"
Since Shirone knew about Sakiri, she assumed he would know Gando
too.
"Yes, I've heard of him."
In truth, Shirone had never heard the name before. He only knew Sakiri's
name because of the major incident at Alpheas Magic School. But in this situation, it was best to
pretend he knew.
Plu was satisfied with Shirone's compliant attitude.
Feeling that the scolding was enough, she brought up what had been on
her mind.
"Do you know why the Chairman wants to see you? It's related to Heaven,
right?"
"..."
Shirone remained silent, his expression serious. Even if Plu was someone
Kangnan trusted, he had to maintain security.
Seeing his sharp gaze, Plu realized she couldn't treat him lightly. She
felt both impressed and suspicious, wondering if his previous demeanor had been an act.
"Anyway, it's my job to assist the Chairman. I'll definitely go to
Heaven. So don't get cocky just because you got invited once."
Shirone had never been cocky, nor did he intend to be. But her words
gave him a clue about her hostility.
"You must really respect the Chairman."
Plu's expression flickered like a fish out of water.
"Of course! Mr. Sakiri and Mr. Gando are impressive, but they can't
compare to Mikael Gaold."
"That's true. He's a certified 1st-class Archmage."
"Hohoho!"
Plu laughed condescendingly, making Shirone's blood boil.
"You really don't know anything. Listen carefully. There are four
1st-grade class mages in the Tormia Kingdom. But being the Chairman of the Magic Association isn't just
about being strong. It requires political skill and resourcefulness. So why was Mr. Gaold chosen as
Chairman?"
Shirone thought about it but couldn't grasp the level of a 1st-grade
Grand Magician, so he couldn't answer.
"Is he highly skilled in many areas?"
Plu shook her head.
"No, it's the opposite. He's not just strong; he's overwhelmingly
strong."
"Overwhelmingly strong?"
"Right. He can maintain his position as Chairman despite being labeled a
madman because of his overwhelming strength, which sets him apart from other 1st-grade Grand Magicians.
He's the strongest! To a combat magician like me, he's an idol and an ideal. How could I not respect
him?"
Hearing Plu's words, Shirone realized that the Chairman was an even more
extraordinary person than he had imagined. This made him even more curious about why such a person
wanted to see him.
Plu turned away without hesitation and said, "Anyway, I have to get back
to work, so stay in your room until dinner. The tour is over, so don't wander around without my
permission."
"Ah! Um..."
As Shirone called out urgently, Plu turned back.
"Before you go... can I try the elevator just once?"
Plu looked at Shirone and smiled, but then her face turned cold again,
and she replied curtly, "No."
Shirone watched her walk away, feeling bewildered.
"What did I do wrong?"
After spending a day that felt as monotonous as prison life, the next
morning arrived. Shirone decided to sleep in since he couldn't leave the room until Plu arrived.
As he tried to get more sleep, he heard bustling noises outside. In a
daze, he saw Plu burst into the room and shout,
"Hey, rookie! Are you still sleeping?"
Shirone groggily opened his eyes and sat up.
"Ah, good morni..."
"Do you think this is your bedroom at home? Get up quickly!"
Now Shirone was starting to get annoyed.
"Um, is there something I need to do?"
"Of course there is. Did you think you could eat and sleep for free?
Today, you'll follow me around and assist. I'll give you 10 minutes, so wash up and get ready!"
Shirone felt like he had been hit by a bomb while sleeping. Struggling
to wake up, he slowly leaned back and fell onto the bed again.
But he couldn't stay there for more than 10 seconds before he curled up
and got out of bed.
"Ugh, this is too much."
