Chapter 485:
"What are you thinking about?"
In the underground of the Hall of Corruption in Rakia.
Kangnan, snapping out of her recollection, looked up.
Torco, a subordinate of the fallen angel Mauriel, was staring at her
with a smile that seemed to reek of foulness.
"Are you preparing yourself for the upcoming torture? But blocking
out thoughts won't help at all."
As Torco grabbed Kangnan by the collar, there was a ripping sound as her
clothes tore.
"We are all slaves to time. If you stretch out your time, you have
exactly 18 hours left. Only pain and annihilation await you."
Torco wanted to make her aware of reality, but in Kangnan's mind, time
had already stopped.
"Gaold won't come."
This was the end. Whatever future awaited, it was already over.
"Hehehe, then all that awaits you is a desperate
scream."
When Kangnan responded with a final smirk, Torco's face finally lost all
expression.
"It's been a while since I've had such an interesting
subject."
As Torco slowly stepped back and extended his hands, his weapon, a
two-pronged whip, appeared in his hands.
"First, let's tenderize the meat."
As soon as he finished speaking, Torco's hands alternated in swinging
the whip.
Each time the whip struck, Kangnan felt a burning pain on her
skin.
'It hurts.'
The pain traveled through her nerves to her brain, transforming into a
new kind of mental agony.
'It hurts.'
At the end of the pain, she felt an even more poignant emotion.
'It must have always hurt like this.'
Breathing felt like inhaling glass shards, and every step felt like
walking on nails.
But Gaold had endured everything and made it this far.
So she could endure it too.
'I won't scream.'
Each time the fierce whip struck her cheek, memories of her time with
Gaold flashed through Kangnan's mind like lightning.
"The sun is setting."
Sein, seeing the darkening sky outside the forest, approached
Gaold.
His eyes, still gnawing at his thumb, were fierce enough to kill anyone
who approached, but Sein could no longer yield.
"Decide, Gaold. What will you do?"
With a click of his teeth, Gaold stopped his self-harm.
No one here knew what Kangnan meant to him.
How could they?
Only now did Gaold realize that he had completely forgotten what she had
given him.
Only Miro.
He had poured all his passion into turning back the stopped clock, never
once thinking about who was following behind.
'Pathetic.'
Miro, looking displeased, sat on a rock with her legs crossed.
Her left leg, draped over her knee, swung back and forth, reflecting her
dissatisfaction.
Seeing her displeasure, Arius crawled over to her and supported her
swinging foot with his head.
Whimpering, he brushed the dirt off her bare foot with his cheek and
began sucking on her toes.
If Arius was human, Miro was a god, and if Miro was human, Arius was
something less than human.
That relative status never changed, so Miro just let him be.
Then, annoyed, she pulled her toes out of his mouth and kicked his
face.
"Whimper! Whimper!"
Ignoring Arius as he rolled on the ground with a hurt expression, Miro
stood up and strode over to Gaold.
"Hey! Make up your mind! Is it me or that girl? I can't stand this
anymore!"
In Miro's mind, a grand map of heaven and humanity was drawn, but on a
more human level, Gaold's indecisiveness hurt her pride.
Of course, even if Gaold chose her, she had no intention of accepting
him.
But that made his inability to choose between two women even more
absurd.
Gaold slowly turned his gaze to look up at Miro.
The woman he had wanted so desperately, the one he had staked his entire
life on, was right in front of him, and he had no doubt that he loved her.
So why did his heart tremble?
The heart that should have been running blindly towards Miro shook like
it was driving on an unpaved road whenever he thought of Kangnan.
"I can guess a little about the life you've lived."
Seeing Gaold's reaction, Miro was convinced.
"You must have trained through countless pains. For 20 years, you
looked only at me and reached this point."
Gaold just listened.
Having the woman he desired more than life acknowledge his efforts was
overwhelming, but he finally realized.
He could never have made it this far on his own.
"You know, don't you?"
Miro recalled the look in Kangnan's eyes at the last moment.
"While you were running, looking only at me, that girl was running,
looking only at your back."
Gaold looked away from Miro and stared straight ahead.
It might have been the first time.
The moment Kangnan stood before him.
"I don't know what story you two have, but…"
"I'm going."
Gaold interrupted Miro and stood up.
Everyone's eyes focused on him, or rather, on his lips.
"I'm going to save the mutt."
No one disagreed with Gaold's decision.
It had been his fight from the beginning.
But a hint of sadness crossed Miro's face.
The price of saving Kangnan was Miro's death.
She had already accepted it, so there was nothing to fear, but now that
Gaold had made his decision, she felt a strange jealousy for the first time in her life.
But such human emotions were absorbed into her grand spirit, leaving no
trace, and she smiled again with a relieved expression.
"Fine, then let's go. If you save her after sending
me…"
"No. I'm going alone."
Sein frowned and asked,
"Alone? What are you going to do?"
"I told you, I'm going to save the mutt."
"But without Miro…"
Sein stopped mid-sentence as if realizing something.
"You, no way?"
Gaold intended to end it.
Having achieved something more impossible than dying, he had the resolve
to face it.
Whatever the outcome, Gaold had no future beyond this.
"The final war might happen. Miro must not die."
Sein, who knew this better than anyone, had only yielded to Gaold's
overwhelming emotions.
"Hmph, do you feel any responsibility for the chaos you've caused
humanity?"
"No."
Gaold turned his head, revealing a mocking smile.
"Why should I care about that? I'm just going to meet Kangnan. The
rest is up to you."
From God to Miro, and now to Kangnan.
A man who only knew how to run forward, perhaps that was Gaold, Miro
thought.
'Yes. That's why I…'
As she watched Gaold leave the forest, Miro spoke.
"Hey, after all this, you're not even going to say
goodbye?"
Instead of looking back, Gaold pushed aside the branches blocking his
path.
"Let's do that when I get back. I've already reserved
it."
Realizing he wasn't talking about a farewell, Miro chuckled.
It was an awkward situation to take back her own words.
"We'll see about that. If you come back in one piece."
"Hehehe."
Gaold shrugged and disappeared into the forest.
'You've become strong, Gaold. Really strong.'
Still stubborn and annoyingly single-minded, but he had truly reached
it.
He had become a man who could look her in the eye.
'Goodbye, Gaold.'
Turning to Sein, Miro's face no longer held a smile.
"From now on, we need to move quickly. Depending on whether we can
return or not, the risks humanity faces will change."
Sein spoke.
"What will you do? You stopped the explosion, but there might be an
archangel capable of breaking the spell."
It was a realization that came from seeing Uriel's Judicial Halo.
Ikael's Ataraxia also defied human understanding, but Ragnarok was an
equally extraordinary ability.
It enveloped the entire body in the concept of destruction, making
physical attacks.
The fundamental concept was unshakable, so once Ragnarok was activated,
there was no way to stop Uriel.
Miro rested her chin on her hand and said,
"The only one who can break the Stop spell is the Archangel of
Light, Raiel. His Judicial Halo can implement the speed of light. If the Stop is broken, my head will
fly off."
Armin spoke.
"If he can use the speed of light, he won't be trapped in the time
field. But breaking the spell is another matter. It won't be easy."
Sein asked,
"How long do you estimate it will take to break the
spell?"
Armin thought for a moment and then spoke.
"About five minutes."
Miro nodded.
"That's enough time. I'll handle it from here. You focus on your
own missions."
"What are you going to do?"
"Arius."
When Miro called, Arius wagged his tail like he had one and
approached.
"Stop doing stupid things. From now on, you are allowed to
speak."
Arius knelt on one knee as if nothing had happened.
"Arius. At your command, my master."
"There must be a door installed in my head. Enter the primal
consciousness again. It should be easier the second time."
"Yes, but… I can't break the archangel's magic circle with my
ability."
"Don't worry about that. I'll handle the magic circle."
Miro explained her plan.
"Kariel's magic circle is blocking my brain circuits, preventing
concentration. So you will become my nerves. Connect my nerves by bypassing the magic circle. As soon as
I enter the Spirit Zone, I'll infinitely reduce the magic circle with Scale Magic. I can't stop the
explosion, but I can handle it."
This was the true terror of Scale Magic.
By magnifying relative size, all the reasoning created by intelligence
becomes meaningless.
Sein also thought it was an excellent judgment.
"Can you time it right? If the Stop spell breaks right now, you'll
die."
"That's why we have to be quick. Once Arius enters consciousness,
time will flow differently. Five minutes. It should be enough."
"Alright, I have no choice but to trust you. Be
careful."
Worrying about the great Miro didn't make sense.
"Let's go, Arius."
Reading his master's thoughts, Arius cast Flicker Magic and moved Miro
to a secluded place.
With Gaold and Miro gone, Sein began to strategize to end the
war.
With the first goal of reclaiming Miro halfway achieved, he pondered the
unease he had felt before coming to heaven.
"Even though the war has started, the angels aren't moving. How is
this possible?"
Aside from the interference from Uriel and Kariel in the Second Heaven,
only fallen angels and Maras were visible.
Fighting a war without the regular angels was like fighting with both
arms tied from heaven's perspective.
"Something is definitely happening that we don't know about. Where
are they moving? Heaven? Or humanity?"
Regardless, the fact that the regular angels weren't moving was an
opportunity for the rebels.
"If there are no special communications, Shirone will activate
God's punishment by noon. We must take advantage of this opportunity."
Whatever the outcome, the war will end tomorrow.
