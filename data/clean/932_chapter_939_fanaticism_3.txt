Chapter 939:
Fanaticism (3)
"The representative of Zion has arrived." As Aromi's voice
echoed from the ceiling, the door burst open, and Miro entered.
She was flanked by Kangnan and Zulu.
"This woman!"
The head of the security team, who entered the room late, bowed deeply
to Iruki.
"I'm sorry! This woman insisted on barging in..."
Iruki raised his hand.
"It's fine. You may leave."
Iruki never blamed his subordinates for failing to handle unexpected
situations.
The head of security, knowing Iruki's disposition, felt ashamed for not
handling things properly.
"Understood."
Though he had much to say, he didn't want to stoop to Miro's level and
simply responded.
'What kind of paragon of virtue is she? She's an arrogant woman.'
Iruki didn't believe that actions taken for someone's sake were
inherently good.
It was more of an abstract concept.
'Good is law.'
An ironclad rule and belief without a hint of chaos, and in that sense,
Miro was the epitome of good.
"Welcome. You're later than expected. I thought you'd be the first
to arrive."
With Zulu, the strongest in utility, it wasn't a matter of distance or
speed.
Miro plopped onto the sofa, draped one arm over the backrest, and
crossed her legs.
"We got held up by a complaint. Took some time to handle
it."
"A complaint?"
"The Apostle of Time."
As Miro's group flew to the central continent on Kaidra, a vacuum
phenomenon occurred around them.
"Huh?"
Suddenly unable to breathe, Miro turned to the cockpit and asked,
"What's going on?"
Though no sound was transmitted, Zulu had already turned to
answer.
"There's no atmosphere. We can't fly."
After canceling the summoning magic and landing, they found themselves
in a rocky area with massive red rocks.
As they took deep breaths, humans emerged from the rocks.
There were twelve in total.
"Well?"
Zulu's eyes widened unusually, and Miro's smile seemed somewhat
forced.
'These aren't humans.'
You could tell by their eyes.
Even without special methods, their eyes were so different that anyone
would think the same.
"Greetings, paragon of virtue."
The speaker was an elderly woman with a kind smile.
'A dragon.'
Only a first-class dragon could change forms, making Miro even more
uneasy.
'Have all twelve apostles ever gathered before?'
As she slowly scanned the figures on the cliff, their ages and genders
varied, but none looked ordinary.
'Six men and six women.'
Of course, dragons didn't care about forms.
If their inherent traits were manifested as human characteristics, then
the old woman's true identity was...
'Poine, the Poison Dragon.'
According to legend, Poine was kind and benevolent but also incredibly
cruel.
Miro waved her finger forward.
"Uh, could you introduce yourselves? It's hard to tell just by
looking at your faces."
"There's no need."
The man standing third from the right on the rocky ground turned into
blue lightning and appeared before Miro.
As the afterimage of the lightning lingered on her retina, Miro examined
the man's face.
'Blitz, the Thunder Dragon.'
His pale face lacked eyebrows, and his sunken eyes added a sharp
impression.
"A message from Karatorusa."
Miro slightly bent her knees.
"Karatorusa. To hear that legendary name here. If it's a message
from such a great being, I must make time for it. Even if I'm too busy to wash."
Despite her sarcastic tone, Blitz calmly delivered the message.
"Do not go to the central continent."
Miro waited for more, but Blitz's mouth remained closed.
"Is that all?"
"Yes."
Miro burst into laughter.
"Of course, I understand what it means for all twelve apostles to
gather just to deliver that short message."
'They're trying to suppress me with force.'
Honestly, she felt a bit shaky, but Miro steadied herself.
"I'd like to hear the reason."
Giving her a chance to be convinced wasn't due to the dragons' authority
but pure curiosity.
"Miro, don't misunderstand."
A blond boy sitting on the cliff spoke.
"It's not our words, but Karatorusa's message. Just do
it."
Miro's gaze shifted to the boy.
'What kind of monster is this?'
He looked about twelve years old.
With a playful face, he wore sports shorts and a fashion band on his
wrist.
He looked like a boy who loved sports, but the light in his eyes sent
chills down Miro's spine.
'Phaethon, the Light Dragon.'
Behind the boy, she could almost see the golden dragon radiating
blinding light.
'He's notoriously violent.'
According to legend, even the archangel of light, Raiel, was overwhelmed
when fighting Phaethon.
"So..."
Sighing, Miro gathered her thoughts and raised her chin towards
Phaethon.
"Tell me the reason. Don't be annoying."
Even Kangnan felt a chill at Miro's words, sensing the tense
atmosphere.
Phaethon's head slowly lowered, and after a moment, his shoulders shook
silently.
When he looked up again, his eyes were filled with murderous
intent.
"Damn it..."
"Stop."
At Blitz's voice, Phaethon flinched and turned to him with the same
murderous look.
"Who? Me?"
"Yes, Phaethon. You."
As Phaethon slowly stood, golden smoke rose from his body, forming a
dragon's shape.
This time, it wasn't an illusion.
"Have you lost your mind? Just because the Immortal Dragon
entrusted you with this era, you think you're in charge?"
When Shirone sacrificed himself in the first world of the Labyrinth
Andre, Karatorusa appointed Blitz as his representative.
Remember Shirone's light. Without that single phrase, there would be no
hierarchy among the twelve apostles.
"I am merciful, so I'll forgive a mistake. Now, why don't you try
speaking again?"
Phaethon's information, conveyed at the speed of light, was so violent
it was uncontrollable.
"Fighting among ourselves is meaningless."
Karatorusa chose Blitz for this era because of his extremely cold
nature.
"We're trying to preserve Miro's combat power. What good is it if
we kill her here? Cool your head."
Miro smirked.
"Oh, such deep meaning. But it sounds like you're underestimating
me."
Despite Miro's provocation, Blitz remained calm.
"Karatorusa has protected time through three resets. Without him,
this world would have already been altered by a specific being's intent. We can't fathom his deep
intentions. But if you need a reason, I can share my personal thoughts."
Miro gestured for him to continue.
"Your opinion?"
"The reason we're ordered to stop you is that we can't guarantee
Shirone will align with time."
Miro blinked.
"What do you mean?"
Blitz explained the current situation.
Shirone was receiving all the information from Omega, which unfolded in
the order of time.
And soon, an Elemental Bomb would be dropped on Bashka, led by the Holy
War.
"An Elemental Bomb."
Miro, resting her chin on her hand, raised her finger.
"So if we're on the battlefield, we'll all die? Therefore, we need
to preserve our strength?"
Kangnan shouted.
"Gaold is going too!"
Blitz nodded.
"Gaold is moving with a member of the Ivory Tower. If you want, I
can tell you their location."
"The Ivory Tower?"
Miro had thought Gaold would never travel with anyone else.
'He can't move alone.'
How much had he deteriorated?
Miro, who had seen it firsthand, could imagine it, but it didn't change
anything.
"I don't care about Gaold's situation. I have my mission, and I
need to go to the central continent."
Kangnan glared fiercely.
"Are you serious? Gaold is fighting like this because of you! What
if he dies...?"
"He won't die."
If he were someone who could die from a bomb, she wouldn't have cut him
out of her heart with tears of blood.
"That's not the point. What I really want to know is how you found
out. The Elemental Bomb. The Holy War isn't lax enough to fail in security management. And if they did
fail, the plan would have been canceled. How did you know?"
"Nothing."
Blitz stated the fact.
"We don't need to do anything to know information. Karatorusa's
Omega is..."
The information Shirone was currently receiving.
"It's the log of the Akashic Records."
"A log? So it's all the records from the moment this world existed
until now?"
"Yes. All dragons can access the core, but only the log is
inaccessible. There was likely an agreement with Goffin before the first reset began. Omega is vast and
dangerous. That's why Karatorusa chose eternal sleep."
"Hmm. And Shirone is currently receiving that log."
Imagining Yahweh's omnipotence added to all the world's information,
Miro shuddered.
'He could literally be called a god. This might actually allow him to
capture Nane.'
Blitz spoke.
"Do not go to the central continent. All logs will now pass from
Karatorusa to Shirone. We will also follow Shirone. Help him protect time."
"Thanks. I understand now."
Miro raised her head.
"But I refuse."
Feeling the immense killing intent behind her, Blitz remained
calm.
"Why?"
"I won't help Shirone."
Though they were allies, Shirone and Miro's philosophies
differed.
"Shirone believes everything has value. He thinks even evil can
repent. But I don't. I will never tolerate evil."
"Shirone doesn't unconditionally forgive evil."
"I know. We might occasionally meet for a drink and chat. But
Shirone and I have reached extremes in different directions. Especially during war, there's no room for
compromise. I don't know how to fight while considering the possibility of repentance. If Shirone gets
in my way, then Shirone is my enemy too."
"So? You're determined to go to the central continent? Do you think
you can survive?"
"No, quite the opposite."
If the operation succeeds, it couldn't be better, but there are many
unknowns among the forces of evil.
If they even get a hint of the plan, luring over a hundred million to
Bashka would be nearly impossible.
"I'll detonate the Elemental Bomb no matter what. Even if it means
my death."
Miro had already given up on life.
As she finished speaking, the apostles in the rocky area all stood
up.
Each watched her with different emotions, and Miro swallowed
hard.
The moment Blitz gave the signal, an overwhelming attack would surely
follow.
"I'll give you a choice."
Blitz's unexpected words made Phaethon bare his teeth and snap,
"You don't have that authority."
"I do. I'll report directly to Karatorusa. Until then, I make the
decisions."
Ignoring Phaethon's displeased expression, Blitz turned back to
Miro.
"Since you've resolved to face death, here's my conclusion. If
you're going to die anyway, arguing is a waste of time. So I propose this: withstand a strike from the
twelve apostles."
Miro didn't speak carelessly.
"We won't combine our attacks due to contractual obligations. Even
so, there's a high chance you'll die. If you can't accept this, your sincerity is in doubt."
"...What's the other option?"
Blitz's pupils emitted a blue electric glow as he turned his
head.
"Kill all of us and go."
