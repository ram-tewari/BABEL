Chapter 531:
24 hours later, Miro's group still hadn't even made it into the Under
Coder.
To reach the Under Coder, they first had to enter Drimo. The
problem was that in order to find the Dreamer Luber, they needed the ability to lucid dream—awareness
within the dream itself.
Marsha had managed it in just two attempts, but Rian was
struggling.
"Do we really have to go through Drimo? Can't we just break out of the
dream directly?"
Fermi shook his head. "The Under Coder exists outside of the mind.
Drug designers have tried many approaches, but all failed. Dream Star only works within Drimo."
"You heard him. Let's try again, okay?" At Marsha's encouragement,
Rian nodded gloomily and stepped into the adjacent room.
They were trying to reduce the psychological pressure, but truthfully,
Rian still couldn't grasp what lucid dreaming even was.
"Damn it! Even dreaming takes effort now?"
And so, the four of them entered the dream again.
This time, under Arius's guidance, they found themselves in a maze-like
slum made of interlocking alleyways.
Miro, Fermi, and Marsha quickly located each other, but once again, Rian
was nowhere to be seen.
They finally found him at the edge of the alleyway in an open lot piled
with scrap. He was swinging a rotten plank and muttering to himself.
Miro shook her head. "He's slipped into a 'Rilpin' state."
Rilpin—essentially the opposite of lucidity, a state where one's focus
is completely lost inside the dream.
"If I'm going to slash like this, that's not good… but maybe it's fine
to go this way… though if I really have to, then—"
"Meaningless rambling," Fermi muttered.
"Hey! Rian! Snap out of it! You're dreaming!" Marsha cupped her hands
and shouted. But Rian was no longer capable of hearing her.
Frustrated, she turned to Fermi. "How do we snap someone out of
Rilpin?"
"Deliver a jolt strong enough to trigger lucidity—but it'll probably
wake them up."
Without waiting, Marsha stomped over and smacked Rian hard on the back
of the head.
"Hey!"
As the crack of impact echoed, Rian shot upright in the real
world. "Augh!"
Though it was only a dream blow, the sting still lingered. He rubbed the
back of his head just as the door burst open.
Marsha stormed in, fuming.
"You didn't have to hit me..."
But Marsha raised her hand and slapped him again—this time in
reality.
"Hey!"
But unlike in the dream, her hand now throbbed with pain.
"Why'd you hit me?!"
"Are you kidding me?! Are you trying to screw this up? Even regular
people can do this with the right help!"
"How am I supposed to know it's a dream when I'm dreaming?!"
Marsha let out a long sigh.
"Listen. If you fail, Shirone might never come back. Are you really okay
with that?"
Rian's face tensed with anxiety.
'Right. I have to save Shirone. But how...?'
Knowing full well Rian was the most frustrated of them all, Marsha
softened her tone and encouraged him.
"If you can't do it with technique, then do it with sheer will. Wake up
in that dream no matter what. Think about Shirone."
"I'll try again. Right now."
And exactly 24 hours later, the four finally gathered together inside
the dream—each lucid and aware.
When they asked the Dreamer Luber for a job, he offered them three
related to love, power, and fame. After they declined all of them, he gave them a cheeky smile and
opened a door in the dream.
"Go ahead. Dream all you like."
As the group passed through, the scenery instantly changed. The
door behind them vanished, and towering buildings shot through the sky in a misty world.
"Whoa. That's way taller than anything in Heaven."
"They're not real," said Arius.
Looking down, they found a fuzzy blue dog sitting calmly—Arius's
incarnation, born from his wish to become Miro's pet.
"What do you mean not real?"
"They're just background info. The buildings probably don't even have
actual details defined."
"So… props. Like theater scenery."
Suddenly—BOOM. The ground trembled.
Reflexively, they looked up. Every face went pale.
"Cotton candy?" Marsha said with a shaky voice.
"Someone wanted cotton candy? You could've just said. I'd have bought
you some..."
"No. It's not just our thoughts here. That's why Drimo is so dangerous,"
said Fermi.
"Sorry, but something worse is about to happen. The capsules are
dissolving. The Dream Star will kick in soon."
As predicted, the bodies lying in the real world twitched. The
Dream Star stimulated their brains just enough to activate physical senses, causing Drimo's landscape to
shudder violently.
"KYAAAAA!"
The cotton candy's color turned murky like sewage, then vaporized into
smoke and came crashing down on them.
"It's starting! Keep your focus and run!" Fermi shouted, bolting
forward. Miro followed.
"What the hell? Was it expired or something?" she yelled.
"It's the drug. We're now foreign entities in Drimo. We don't belong
here."
Rian jerked his chin forward. "Then what about that?!"
The very world was quaking—every visible structure shattered and
collapsed like shards of glass.
"The dream is collapsing!"
"This way!"
Fermi veered suddenly into a building. The others dove in after
him.
The scene changed again—and they found themselves aboard a ship on an
endless ocean.
"Damn it!"
There were no exits anywhere in sight.
"What now? This place is gonna collapse too."
"Just shifting dimensions won't get us out. We either need to reach the
edge of the sea or find a new exit."
"Wait! Something's wrong!"
The ground shuddered—waves surged as if fire had erupted underwater. Sea
creatures burst from the surface, soaring into the air and diving back down in arcs.
"We're not gonna make it in time," Fermi said.
The information might've ended just 10 meters ahead—but if they gambled
on that and were wrong, they'd be swallowed whole.
"There, look!"
Marsha pointed to the sky, unable to form the words.
A massive white pillar descended diagonally from the heavens, plunging
into the distant sea.
The group stood stunned.
A child's face hung over the sky—slightly off-kilter, as if missing
chromosomes—and the pillar was lodged in its mouth like a straw.
"That's a dimensional interference," someone muttered.
"Run! Get off the ship!"
Miro used Air Magic to lift them into the sky.
As they ascended, the child's cheeks caved in, and the sea level dropped
instantly—sucked into the white pillar.
To see an entire ocean swallowed in a blink was enough to shock anyone
into silence.
"I found it!" shouted Arius, riding on Miro's back.
"Where?!"
"That pillar—no, inside the straw!"
They'd found the only passage to another dimension—but didn't need to
reach it themselves.
With the sea gone, the air began to pull too, and the four humans and
one dog were dragged in.
"Whooooooooosh—"
The child exhaled. Bubbles formed—five in total—drifting gently on the
wind.
They popped.
The group looked around, startled. Pretty children were playing
with soap bubbles nearby.
But there was no time to catch their breath.
Darkness surged across the distant plains—frightened children began to
cry.
The earth trembled.
From above, sticky, mucous-like entities rained down in hordes.
"Nightmares! Don't let them touch you! If they do, this world becomes a
living nightmare!"
"KYEEEEEEE!"
One of the dark entities darted toward the group.
Rian reflexively raised his sword—but recalled Arius's advice and
instantly activated Deny.
His body twisted unnaturally, as if space itself warped—just enough to
make the Nightmare's lunge miss his chest.
"There's too many of them!"
Their speed wasn't particularly fast, but the way the Nightmares
moved—disregarding inertia—made each of their actions feel like they were using Axi.
As two Nightmares darted past her sides, Miro shouted to Fermi,
"Can't we teleport out of here?"
"Can you maintain focus?"
"Did you forget who I am?"
"Then do it—quickly!"
No sooner had Fermi finished speaking than Miro cast a teleportation
spell, taking everyone with her. Just as the sky collapsed, darkness came crashing down on the
land.
Boom! Boom! Boom! Boom!
The massive shadows chased the flashes of their teleportation like an
elephant stomping after ants.
"Where's the damn exit?!" Arius yelled.
"The Nightmares are causing the world to expand! At this rate, we'll
never escape!"
"For crying out loud!"
Miro stopped the teleportation and set the group down. On the far
horizon, a towering Nightmare loomed, extending a massive hand-shaped form toward them.
"Oh, so we're doing this all the way, huh?"
Staring down the advancing Nightmare tide, Miro pushed her clasped hands
forward.
Transcendent Mind: Avalokiteśvara — One Palm.
A massive avatar of the bodhisattva Avalokiteśvara rose behind her,
formed from radiant energy. As Miro thrust her hands forward, the avatar mimicked her, driving its palms
into the heart of the Nightmare wave.
When she spread her arms, the avatar did the same—ripping the monstrous
shadow apart down the middle.
"That's it! Let's go!"
Without caring that Rian and Marsha were staring at her like she was
insane, Miro immediately teleported them out beyond the Nightmare's reach.
"What's next?" she asked.
Fermi replied, looking unsettled, "The nightmares weren't the last
threat. Now comes the Nightmare itself."
RUMBLE RUMBLE RUMBLE RUMBLE—!
The field beneath them rippled as though they were floating on the
ocean. Then explosions burst from beneath the earth.
The group scattered, zigzagging through the chaos—but time itself
started to slow.
'Damn it! It's Nightmare Time.'
Explosions echoed from every direction. Pillars of fire erupted from
below, blasting through the ground.
And then, a massive explosion—far larger than anything so far—detonated
at the point where all of them had been aligned.
BOOOOOOOOOOOM!
In slow motion, everyone was launched skyward, spinning helplessly from
the force of the blast.
To Marsha, everything appeared in perfect clarity—time slowed to a
crawl. She saw Arius floating upside down, tongue lolling comically. Then her gaze returned to the
explosion, and an electric thrill surged through her spine.
Flames bloomed like enormous flowers—each blossom slick as if coated in
oil, gleaming gold, lighting the entire world as if it were midday.
"Aah…"
Marsha stared in a daze.
'This really is… one hell of a nightmare.'
Time snapped back to normal.
"Snap out of it!"
Right before she lost focus, Miro grabbed her waist and flew straight
for the horizon.
Everyone else had already regrouped around her.
The shockwave from the largest explosion had blasted them hundreds of
meters, and just as Marsha was about to slip into the floating sensation of a Rilpin state, Miro slammed
into the ground and rolled, snapping her out of it.
"Haah… haah…"
Marsha was still breathless, the vivid memory of that overwhelming blast
fresh in her mind. Rian looked equally stunned.
"We've arrived," Fermi said with a smile, pointing ahead.
"This is the end of the mind."
No explanation was needed.
The same sunset they had seen from the distant plains was now right in
front of them. It hadn't grown a single inch closer or smaller—just like a painting on a wall.
"The mind can no longer produce information. Cross this boundary, and
we'll be inside the Under Coder."
Miro rolled up her sleeves with a confident grin.
"All right, time to get serious."
Stopping in front of the sunset, she made up her mind and stepped
forward.
Her body vanished into the illusionary veil, as if swallowed by
mist.
