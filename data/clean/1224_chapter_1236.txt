Chapter 1236:
"The dream will be passed on."
Luber fell into deep thought.
"It's possible within the system. But it goes against the administrator
protocols."
Dreamo was not the exclusive property of any one species.
It was a realm meant to process the ideals of all dreamers, from the
most primitive lifeforms to the most advanced.
"Especially Theraphos…"
At present, they too were constantly pushing for channels that could
influence humanity.
"If I allow the Legacy Dream, I'll have to allow Therapho's channel as
well. Both have reasonable justifications… but still…"
Creating precedent would make managing Dreamo even more
difficult.
Monga bit his lip and called out.
"Luber…"
With Yorahan's dream about to flood Dreamo entirely, the time for
hesitation had ended.
"…Very well."
Luber nodded.
"Your dream will not be processed by Dreemo. Instead, it will appear in
other people's dreams—passed down to them."
"Thank you."
Yorahan smiled with such kindness that Luber could only sigh in
surrender.
"Then I'll store your dream in the database. But first, you must give it
a name."
"A name…?"
"You might not understand yet, but naming something is the most
important thing in the universe."
Once, even Shirone hadn't understood this.
But through his journey, he had come to glimpse the truth.
The reason naming something is important…
Because the universe we live in is a database processed and calculated
by God.
And when something is named, that definition links to the Idea through
the missing link.
"Only those with names can transcend classification."
Among the countless living beings, only those few with names could
become something that impacted the entire universe.
"A name…"
Yorahan thought for a moment.
"Yorahan and Armand."
Then he lifted his head and spoke to Luber.
"I'll call it… Yor."
"Understood."
The origin of the name didn't matter—what truly mattered was the life
that name would carry forward.
"Many people will dream your dream from now on. It will be passed down
across generations, perhaps even become a religion. You will become the god of a religion called
Yor."
"…I see."
Though he had achieved what he once longed for most, Yorahan remained
calm.
Then Armand spoke up.
"I have a condition as well."
Luber frowned but didn't deny that the two were now one and the
same.
"Speak."
"I want to protect my husband's dream. Send me to the real
world."
"But you're already—"
"I don't need life. I'm a Fireborn. Even if I become an object, I'll be
satisfied as long as I can witness Yorahan's dream come true."
"Hm…"
Luber knew how.
"I can extract her as an object. But the administration will never
approve…"
Ultimately, he would have to override them.
Dreemo began to tremble.
"…Very well. You'll be reborn as an object imbued with a unique concept
and sent to the real world. What do you wish to become?"
"A sword."
Armand didn't hesitate.
"I'd like to be a sword."
"…Approved."
Yorahan and Armand looked at one another.
Though they were now parting forever, their hearts would continue
on—connected to someone else's.
"I love you."
They kissed, and with that, Yorahan's avatar dissolved.
Monga turned to Luber.
"…Will it be all right?"
"This is their definition to make."
Luber looked around Dreemo, which had finally stabilized.
"We've avoided destruction."
Armand regained consciousness.
"Where am I?"
Though she had no eyes, nose, or ears, she could sense the space through
Small World Resonance.
A prison.
The king of Mikhas and his guards lay collapsed, and Yorahan's presence
could be felt near the wall.
"…Ah."
Though she had no legs, the sword shook—but she quickly figured out how
to move.
"Because I resonated with the Living Sword."
Using direct control, Armand—now floating—flew to Yorahan's lifeless
body and leaned against the wall beside him.
"My love…"
He wore the most peaceful expression, as if everything had been
forgiven.
"Such a kind soul."
She once hated humans, and she still couldn't say she liked them now,
but—
"I'm so glad I met you."
She wouldn't trade the time they spent in love for anything in the
world.
An illusion of Armand appeared around the demon sword, gently embracing
Yorahan's body.
"You were always too kind, too soft-hearted, always the one to get hurt
by others…"
He only ever wanted everyone to be happy.
"I'll protect you."
The blade of the magic sword split open like the legs of an insect and
swallowed Yorahan's body.
"So that no one can ever wound your heart again—I'll become the hardest
shell in the world…"
Clad in armor, Yorahan rose.
"I'll protect you."
Adamant Armament—金剛武裝.
Though it was Yorahan's voice, the one moving his vocal cords was
Armand.
"…Let's go."
To find the one who dreams Yorahan's dream.
The Demon of Flame.
Lethe roared at the avatar of the Great Sage, Equal to Heaven, which
came crashing down from the sky like a bolt of light.
"Graaaah!"
Once again, her body was pierced through, and the fires of hell rushed
in from all sides to mend the wound.
"Khh…!"
At that moment, she realized it.
'I can't go.'
There was no way to reach Yahweh.
'No…'
As a system administrator, she didn't have a personal attachment to this
world.
'But I…'
If there was one thing that set her apart from the other
administrators—
'I must protect the underworld.'
Reforming her body with hellfire, Lethe turned her flames to the earth
below.
"Fuaaah!"
For three full minutes, thick pillars of fire burst forth in concentric
circles, spreading out across several kilometers.
"Grrrr…"
When the flames subsided, Son Yoo-jung stood alone in the center of the
scorched land, holding her Yeoui out front.
'She's strong.'
Undeniably the Great Sage, Equal to Heaven.
'There's no time left.'
With that thought, Lethe's massive form shrank down and returned to her
appearance as the president of the Fire Management Company.
Son Yoo-jung lowered her Yeoui.
"Did you give up?"
"There's no point in fighting like this. We don't have much time left.
We have to make a choice."
"A choice?"
"Whether or not Ultima is still possible."
Son Yoo-jung fell silent for a moment, then raised her Yeoui
again.
"Leader of the demons, your crimes have crossed the line. Come at me. I
don't want to destroy someone who's already resigned to her fate."
"I can't die," Lethe said with a bitter smile.
"There's no such thing as 'clocking out' for Fire Management staff. Not
until this world is closed. But you know what that means too, don't you? That also means the end of the
real world."
Richella, whose face hung from the chain at Son Yoo-jung's waist,
screamed.
"What are you doing?! Kill her already! If she's gone, I won't have to
live like this anymore!"
"You be quiet."
Yoo-jung unhooked the chain from her waist, handed Richella to
Mortasinger, and approached Lethe.
"Is this a threat now? I don't compromise. My righteousness won't
change."
Lethe's eyes grew somber.
"Your righteousness, and my wrongdoing—those are just things someone
defined. If you truly want integration, this isn't the way to get there."
"I don't understand complicated words. I'm not like Yahweh. If you want
to convince me, you'll have to break me."
In that moment, light returned to Lethe's eyes.
Yoo-jung paused in caution, but Lethe stayed like that for a long
time.
'I have no choice.'
More important than her duties as an administrator was protecting the
world where the demons lived.
"What I am…"
For the first time, Lethe began revealing administrator-level classified
information.
"I'm a Heart Program."
Yoo-jung simply listened.
"The universe is made up of five systems: the Real World, Drimo, the
Underworld, the Abyss, and the Under Coder, which connects them all. All systems except the Under Coder
have administrators to some extent, but I am the only one who operates on quantum signals."
It was known as the Hidden Code.
"My name is Lethe of Forgetting. I am the only one who can manage the
demons. As long as I don't violate administrative authority, any crime committed gets erased through
oblivion. I offer second chances—thousands, tens of thousands of times."
Just as she had for Chief of Staff Monoras.
"If I weren't programmed like that, the chaos of demons, who don't know

adjust my parameters and respond actively to chaos."
Yoo-jung didn't understand the technical terms.
But even so, her instincts told her—this might be the most important
information in the entire universe.
'I don't get it. Why is this important? If Shirone were here instead of
me…'
Would he have responded differently?
Lethe continued.
"And now, because you've overpowered me, my Heart Program has changed. I

world. But if things continue like this, the universe will close."
If you flipped the perspective of good and evil, Lethe was fighting for

"Yoo-jung, don't trust her. She's lied to us again and again and dragged

"I know."
Yoo-jung had once been the embodiment of chaos herself. But the reason

'It's because I have a heart.'

"Yahweh understood both good and evil, but in the end, he couldn't

The condensation of that darkness had become Gyorgy of the
Catacombs.
"As an administrator, I'm certain—if we continue like this, we'll never

"Why? You hate Yahweh."
"I do. All administrators just carry out the tasks assigned by their

Lethe placed her hand over her heart.
"Only I am capable of loving him."
That, perhaps, was the key—something Son Yoo-jung understood with primal
instinct.
She asked,
"How long until the end of the world?"
"Two hours."
By temple time, that meant midnight.
"We're already far from Yahweh. Including the time it'll take me to get

Lethe opened both hands.
"Decide within that time. Will you send me to Yahweh—or keep me bound
here?"
Only 600 seconds.
An absurdly short time to bet humanity's fate on… but such was the world
they lived in.
Yoo-jung weighed the impossible choice over and over.
"Haaa…"
Her hair, wild like a mustang's mane, began to turn white.
"Y-Yoo-jung…"
Mortasinger called out anxiously, but even she had no words of
comfort.
We can never truly know someone else's truth.
'Should I trust Lethe?'
Yoo-jung shook her head.
'No. I can't trust anyone. Only I… can save myself.'
And suddenly, a thought came to her.
That unknown truth— might be the most terrifying thing in the
world.
