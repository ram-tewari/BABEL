Chapter 334:
Midwinter Night (5)
Shirone grasped Amy's hand firmly.
"Amy, you're strong. Stronger than anyone I know. You'll find a
solution. You always have."
Amy turned to look at Shirone. The moment their eyes met, it felt like
electricity coursed through her body.
The moonlit scenery, the sounds echoing off the walls, and the feel of
Shirone's hand made her heart race.
It was as if the discomfort from just meeting his gaze earlier had been
a lie, and now, courage surged within both of them.
'Shirone...'
Amy had to make a choice.
She was given another year, a time that felt like hell.
Having already experienced one failure, her focus had to be solely on
mastering magic.
But for the first time in her life, she wanted to rely on someone, to
lean on someone.
She didn't have the strength or confidence to overcome this
alone.
Even in this moment of silence, Shirone looked at her with unwavering
eyes.
She had to cross the point of no return.
If she turned away now, Shirone would leave her side.
To keep Shirone, she had to accept him.
Amy thought she had made up her mind. But at the same time, new
hesitation surged within her.
Selfish thoughts.
Was it too much to ask for him to stay by her side, even if she didn't
choose anything, even if she pretended not to know?
'Just one more year... Can't you give me just one more year? So I can
make a careful decision, and until then, stay with me like this?'
Amy slowly withdrew her hand. If she thought this would disappoint him,
she wouldn't have been able to pull away.
Amy felt a sneer rise within her.
'What? You said you made up your mind? Liar.'
She knew the truth.
Shirone would wait for her.
Even if it was selfish, she couldn't help it. She couldn't give up
either the graduation exam or Shirone.
"Thank you, Shirone. I'm glad you're here."
Amy wanted to hide somewhere after saying that.
The words she had carefully chosen felt so insincere.
But Shirone smiled gently, just as she had hoped.
"Me too. Let's do our best."
Shirone squeezed Amy's shoulder firmly, then went back to his spot and
laid out his sleeping bag on the floor.
Amy felt relieved. It was as if they had returned to a time before the
recent events.
But the look in Shirone's eyes, captured by her Red Eyes, reminded her
that it was all real.
How long would he wait for her?
Feelings of self-contempt and guilt towards Shirone tangled within Amy's
eyes.
Past midnight.
Jokre and his friends were quite drunk.
Still unsatisfied, they bought a large bottle of whiskey and snacks from
a shop and settled in an open space near the castle walls.
Jokre opened the strong whiskey and poured it into the glasses in front
of Rudvans and Vivian.
Finally, he filled his own glass and drank it down without even
toasting.
"Ugh, it's really strong. What are you doing? Aren't you going to
drink?"
Rudvans asked worriedly.
"Aren't you drinking too much? What about tomorrow?"
"Ugh, if I feel sick, I'll just stay in the inn."
Vivian, who had just wet her lips with the drink, looked up at the night
sky.
"Sigh, graduating isn't that great. I thought I'd have a lot of fun
once I graduated. But no guys are interested."
Jokre pointed to himself with his thumb.
"Aren't we guys too?"
"Oh, please. Do you think you can hit on me with that
face?"
"Haha! Vivian, you're not that pretty either."
Rudvans agreed.
"Yeah, to be pretty, you'd have to be like that Amy."
Jokre's face twisted in anger.
"Damn it! I thought of her again! How dare she look down on us?
They haven't even graduated yet!"
"But what happened with you and Amy? You didn't have to follow her
to the skating rink. Those little actions make them look down on us."
Vivian also looked curious, waiting for an answer.
Indeed, Jokre had seemed more agitated than usual at the skating
rink.
Jokre had intended to take the secret of what happened when he was
thirteen to his grave. But once he started drinking, he couldn't keep it in.
To others, it might seem trivial, but to him, it was an unbearable
humiliation.
"Just before I transferred to Eins, I confessed to
Amy."
Vivian reacted with surprise.
"Oh my, really? So? What happened?"
"She said no."
"No? Just no?"
"Yeah! She just said no! Not 'I'm grateful, but,' not 'You're a
good person, but,' not even 'I have someone else I like.' Just no!"
"Oh my, that's really mean!"
"Arrogant."
Vivian and Rudvans each added a comment.
Jokre's fists trembled as he recalled the moment.
"Do you know what the worst part is? I responded with, 'Okay,
sorry.' I'll never forget that humiliation."
Vivian clicked her tongue.
"Pretty people only think about themselves. Hey, hey! It's better
this way. Who cares about her? We're mages now. Jokre, you won! Come on, drink."
Jokre downed the drink Vivian poured for him. It burned his throat and
felt like fire in his stomach.
But he felt better. After all, friends were the only ones he could rely
on.
Who cared about prestigious schools?
He had found people who understood him, so he had no regrets.
Jokre and his friends kept drinking, using Jokre's painful past as a
topic.
Soon, the world started spinning.
The winter wind howled over the snowy field.
No one spoke anymore.
They all knew that no matter what comfort they offered, the truth
wouldn't change.
Jokre couldn't beat Amy.
Vivian leaned back and touched the ground. Her eyes, looking up at the
sky, were already unfocused.
She exhaled a breath full of heat.
"That's true. Alpheas Magic School is one of the kingdom's top five
schools. Graduating means you can at least get a job at an official institution."
Jokre and Rudvans silently agreed.
Becoming a mage didn't mean everyone had the same job opportunities.
Noble rank and connections mattered, but in a merit-based society, your background made a big
difference.
Anyone working in the magical society knew the competitive edge of
prestigious schools versus regular ones.
So it was only natural that they were treated differently.
Rudvans spoke.
"Anyway, we graduated from magic school and became mages. That's
something. There are plenty of mages out there who didn't even graduate and just learned a bit of
magic."
Whether you graduated from school or joined a guild, the issued
certificates were unofficial, so there wasn't much difference.
But students clung to schools to have the school's name engraved on
their certificates.
In reality, mages with guild names on their certificates were often seen
as inferior by people like Jokre and his friends.
But that didn't make them feel better.
Above school graduates were graduates from prestigious schools. And
their ultimate goal was the official mage certification.
Guild mages were considered professionals because they earned money with
magic, but the kingdom only recognized mages with official certifications.
But not just anyone could become an official mage.
It was one of the kingdom's top five exams, with fierce competition,
attracting the best mages from across the country.
Entering a prestigious magic school, advancing each year, passing the
graduation exam, taking the official mage exam, and passing it. Only then could you be considered elite
in the magical society.
So Jokre and his friends, starting from a different point, couldn't just
laugh at guild mages.
Jokre drank straight from the bottle and shouted.
"Damn it! Who cares if others get their certificates from guilds?
Once we get jobs, we're all mages. And our bosses will be from prestigious schools. Look at Dante. He
was beating professional mages even when he was at the Royal Magic School."
"But that's just gossip from the journal, isn't it?"
Jokre's speech became more slurred.
"Who cares? It's all the same. We have to compete with graduates
from the kingdom's top five schools. Will major institutions hire us? And we're not even top nobles.
We'll end up as lackeys, becoming 9th grade, working like dogs to become 8th grade, and after ten years?
The prestigious school graduates will all be 6th grade, sitting in management positions."
Though drunken ramblings, it wasn't untrue. Vivian nodded, accepting
reality.
"True. And even prestigious schools aren't the end. Those who
graduate at the top reach 6th grade in 2-3 years. What are we supposed to do?"
Jokre pointed repeatedly as he spoke.
"Do you know what's more annoying? Those geniuses become teachers
at prestigious schools. It's a cycle of talent. Genius teachers attract genius students. We can't even
dream of getting in."
Though Jokre's words were becoming exaggerated, they weren't entirely
wrong.
There were exceptions among regular school graduates who reached the top
positions in the magical society.
But such anomalies lost their significance against the average.
Regular school graduates were seen as less skilled than those from
prestigious schools.
This was the accepted truth.
The pessimistic stories from Jokre and his friends lingered around their
drinking spot.
In school, they thought becoming mages would solve everything. But once
they became mages, their desperate hopes vanished like a mirage, replaced by new worries.
Vivian, staring at the stars, voiced a thought.
"Have you ever thought that this world might be a play? We're
living our lives, and someone is watching us."
Jokre and Rudvans turned to look at Vivian.
"If that's the case, I would never be the main character. The
audience would never want me to succeed. They probably wouldn't even care. They might just want me to
exit quickly, or maybe even wish I were dead."
Jokre looked at Vivian with pity.
She had always been a sensitive friend. But what really hurt was knowing
that her words weren't just drunken ramblings.
Maybe she was right.
"Why worry about that? Life isn't a play. No one knows what's going
to happen. And no one thinks of you that way."
"But think about it. Shirone, Dante, Amy. Do they know how much we
want to be like them? They don't. They just stick to their own group. Maybe we never had a place with
them, like extras in a play."
The mood at the drinking spot grew extremely somber.
Especially Jokre, who felt pathetic for not being able to refute
Vivian's words.
"Damn it! What is this? This is supposed to be a graduation
celebration trip! We passed! We became mages! We won! It's just bad luck that we ran into those
jerks!"
Jokre put the bottle to his lips and drank deeply. The strong liquor
burned all the way down his throat.
