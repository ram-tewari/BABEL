Chapter 53:
Overflow (3)
"Hahaha! The latest issue of the picture book! How long have I waited for this day?"
Glancing at the book, Shirone's face turned red. The cover was far from a masterpiece. It
depicted a nude woman from the neck to the thighs, with the words "Female Anatomy Research Club" written
along the stitching line.
"What on earth is that weird book?"
"Ah. You wouldn't know. This is what we call a 'picture book.' A privilege only a few at
the magic school can enjoy."
Iruki added:
"You could call it the unproductive amusement of idle minds. Hehehe!"
The so-called "picture book" was a euphemism among students for a collection of seminude
drawings of women.
Naturally, it wasn't an official publication but an obscene book produced by students
majoring in art.
The publisher was the Female Anatomy Research Club, an unofficial club. Despite this, it
enjoyed explosive popularity among those in the know. It went beyond simple seminude drawings, featuring
characters from the magic school as models—female students, teachers, and occasionally even male students or
teachers.
The school was well aware and constantly confiscated the publications, but the club
operated like a shadow organization, and its core members remained unidentified. Rumor had it the club's
leader was a senior, but this was unconfirmed.
After hearing the explanation, Shirone was stunned. In a way, this club was far more
outrageous than anything Iruki or Neid had done.
"So you've seen this book too, Iruki? That's unexpected."
Without looking up from his chessboard, Iruki waggled a finger.
"Just gathering information. The Female Anatomy Research Club is resourceful—they collect
all sorts of rumors."
Neid flipped to the back of the book. As Iruki said, detailed descriptions were included.
Everything from the female students' backgrounds to trivial gossip was meticulously
recorded—far too much effort for a mere hobby.
The insistence on seminude drawings was also a clever workaround to avoid violating the
city's obscenity laws. Yet the poses were so lewd that even a layman could tell it wasn't art.
As Neid flipped through the book, he gasped in admiration.
"Wow! This month's issue is amazing! Iruki, can I tear out page 7?"
"Go ahead. I've already memorized all the info."
Shirone stared blankly at Neid. What on earth was he planning to do with a torn-out page?
"Instead of studying…."
Neid burst out laughing. This was probably a first—a model student like Shirone joining
the den of misfits that was the Supernatural Psychic Science Research Club.
"Shirone. You'll get used to it. You'll soon realize there are more interesting things
here than classes. Stick around, and you'll see it's worth it."
As Neid gleefully turned the page, his face suddenly stiffened. He glanced at Shirone and
said:
"Uh, Shirone. Don't freak out."
"What now? Is there something even more shocking?"
"Well, it's just… Amy's in this picture book."
"What?! Give me that!"
Shirone snatched the book. A risqué drawing filled an entire page—so realistic it could
have been a photograph.
"Shirone. Don't misunderstand. How accurate is this drawing? I've always been curious."
"Accurate? How would I know?!"
Iruki shrugged and giggled.
"Given your personality, I doubt you've even held hands with her, let alone anything
else."
"Hmph. Worry about yourselves. Why meddle in my love life?"
Shirone turned back to the book. Regardless of his feelings, the skill was impressive.
The figures on the page looked alive.
'Even I can tell this is incredible talent. But why waste it on this?'
Shirone tore out the page. The thought of Amy being featured in such a book was deeply
unpleasant.
"Ugh…."
Unable to bear looking at it, he averted his eyes as he ripped the page. Neid burst out
laughing, clutching his stomach.
"Pfft—ha! What are you doing? Don't be so sensitive. It's all imagination except for the
face. It's not like you've seen the real thing."
Shirone glared. Whether imagined or real, Amy was a friend who had fought alongside the
Black Magician. If possible, he'd track down the Female Anatomy Research Club and disband it.
"Anyway, stop looking at this book. If you're not going to study, let's do something
productive. This is a research club, but we're just lazing around all day."
Realizing that studying here was impossible from the start, Shirone decided to find
something they could do together.
"How about checking out Neid's inventions? The patent list of the genius Neid."
"Oh, right! I haven't shown you yet."
Neid crawled under the table again. Curious about what lay in the trash pile, Shirone
bent down to look. Surprisingly, a safe was hidden inside.
"Ta-da! What do you think, Shirone? Want to see?"
Shirone blinked blankly. Neid's hands were empty—but upon closer inspection, the scenery
seemed to shimmer along a certain boundary.
"Is this…?"
"Haha! This is the Invisibility Cloak!"
The Invisibility Cloak used electrical forces to bend light, creating the illusion of
transparency. Shirone had read about it but never seen one in person.
"Did you really make this? By yourself?"
Shirone was incredulous. Though not an expert in magical engineering, he knew the immense
value of such an item.
Electrical magic wasn't just potent in combat—it had endless industrial applications.
In fact, the most commonly used spells by electrical mages weren't lightning-based but
illusion spells.
The pinnacle of illusion spells was Invisibility, and the Invisibility Cloak could
achieve the same effect.
Though not Neid's original invention, replicating and producing the cloak required
engineering skills on par with professionals.
"I want to be a magical engineer. Of course, this is pretty shoddy compared to the
high-end cloaks on the market. The material is low-grade, and the charging tech is lacking—it only lasts an
hour after charging. But not bad, right?"
As Neid draped the cloak over himself, his figure faded away. The quality of the cloak
depended on its light refraction rate. Below 95%, it couldn't be called "invisible," so even a 1% difference
marked the gap in technology. Neid's current skill couldn't achieve 100%, but the invisibility effect was
undeniable.
When Neid moved, a faint outline was detectable. But when stationary, one wouldn't notice
him unless they looked closely.
Shirone was awestruck. Creating magical tools independently required not just magical
knowledge but alchemical expertise.
"This is amazing. If you put this effort into studying, your grades would be much better,
wouldn't they?"
"Hahaha! Maybe. But I'm happiest when making things, not casting spells. Here, look at
these."
Neid pulled out more items from the safe: a miniature electric carriage, a holographic
vision that projected images onto a crystal panel—all impressively crafted.
Some, like flashbangs and smoke bombs, were based on Iruki's ideas but paled in
comparison to Neid's patented inventions.
Watching Neid passionately explain each item, Shirone realized something. Neid wasn't
wasting his talent—he was ahead of his peers in his field.
"This carriage's engine has a rotation speed of— Huh?"
Mid-explanation, Neid paused as the room shook. The vibrations grew stronger, toppling
chess pieces left and right.
Iruki rushed out to check the hallway and returned with urgency etched on his face.
"Damn it! A teacher's here! Hide everything, now!"
"What? What's happening?"
There was no time to explain. Neid swiftly stashed the picture book and the club's
unofficial funds—like smoke bombs—into the safe. With the contraband secured, he dragged a bewildered
Shirone to the sofa.
"Shirone! Sit here! It's a surprise inspection!"
The Isthas mechanism activated. Red warning lights flashed in the hallway, signaling that
the warehouse housing the research club would soon be summoned to the first floor.
"A teacher's coming? But if I just sit here, how will I greet them?"
Neid forced Shirone onto the sofa.
"Never mind that! Act natural, like nothing's wrong. If they find the picture book or
smoke bombs, it won't end with just a warning."
"Huh? Oh, okay."
Shirone steadied his mind with his unique mental discipline. Even in the chaos, his
friends marveled at his composure. Prepared for battle, they waited for Isthas to descend.
"Ugh, this is annoying."
Siena massaged her throbbing head. It took at least three minutes for Isthas to bring
the warehouse to the first floor—plenty of time to flee or hide evidence.
In the past, some teachers had raided the Supernatural Psychic Science Research Club
without activating Isthas. But after wandering the building for over an hour, they'd failed to find the lab.
Meanwhile, the members came and went freely. Siena had a rough idea how: they'd analyzed
Isthas's movement patterns to deduce its routes.
Though Isthas was marketed as a magical warehouse, its manufacturer kept the blueprints
secret. Yet some student had cracked it decades ago and turned it into the club's hiding spot, much to the
teachers' frustration.
As Siena waited, she crossed her arms and tapped her foot. Three minutes felt
interminable.
'The more I think about it, the angrier I get. How did they figure out what even teachers
couldn't? And why waste it on this? What am I supposed to do with these clubs?'
This was the problem with underground clubs. If they were just misbehaving, the school
could discipline them. But their intellect often surpassed the school's control.
Take the Supernatural Psychic Science Research Club—if they put their minds to it, they
could toy with delinquent clubs like the Black Magician.
'Why did Shirone join? What help could these kids be, even with the Immortal Function?
And joining them will ruin his assignments.'
Neid, ordinary at school but sharp in the club. Iruki, a Class Five problem child who
skipped classes despite his Servant Syndrome. And now Shirone, the school's top model student. How did these
three, who seemed so mismatched, stick together?
'Does talent attract talent? Well, Neid and Iruki could interact as equals, but…'
