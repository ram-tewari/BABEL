Chapter 323:
Second Outing (1)
When Shirone arrived at the Karmis mansion, he checked his appearance
and walked through the arch of the main gate.
Through the white iron bars, the unique, free-spirited garden of the
Karmis family stretched out.
The guards, who were vigilantly watching the surroundings with sharp
eyes, were still the same.
As expected, when Shirone approached, the guard's right hand moved to
the handle of the sword at his waist.
"Stop. What is your business here?"
The difference from half a year ago was that they used
honorifics.
The guards of noble mansions typically rotate every four hours and
change positions monthly, so it was unlikely that the guards from back then were still here.
Nevertheless, the reason they couldn't treat Shirone carelessly was
because they sensed an extraordinary aura different from ordinary people.
After attending the magic school for a year, Shirone's appearance had
shed much of his rustic look.
Especially the high level of mental strength he had cultivated through
practical experience and training was radiating an aura that even the guards, who were not familiar with
magic, could feel.
"I'm Amy's friend. Is Amy inside?"
The guards tilted their heads in confusion and exchanged glances.
Shirone had entered Amy's room, which was a forbidden area for men, half
a year ago.
But since that day, there had been no instance of a male friend being
allowed in, so it was natural for the guards, who were unaware of the incident, to be skeptical.
"There are no scheduled guests today. If you have business, please
follow the proper procedures."
It was the expected response.
Shirone smacked his lips and turned around.
He had hoped to get in right away if he was lucky, but since things were
complicated, he had no choice but to write a letter and send it to the mansion.
"It's fine. Let him in."
A familiar voice came from inside the gate.
When Shirone turned back, the head butler of the Karmis family, Adel,
was standing there with a smile on his face.
The guards straightened up.
To those who lived in the mansion, Adel's kind face felt like that of a
grim reaper.
Adel, leaning on his cane, crossed the gate and stopped in front of
Shirone, looking into his eyes.
Recalling the memory from half a year ago, Shirone prepared
himself.
The eye technique, Pressing, which he had experienced for the first
time.
As the head butler of the Karmis family, Adel wouldn't have used his
full strength, so Shirone had to stay alert.
But Adel didn't use any technique. He simply bowed politely at a right
angle.
"Welcome, young master Shirone."
Shirone, who had been tense, relaxed and quickly bowed in return.
"Yes, have you been well?"
"Haha, an old man's life doesn't change much. You must be here to
see Miss Amy."
"Yes, I'm sorry for coming without notice. Is she
home?"
Adel scratched his eyebrow awkwardly.
"You must be aware of the situation. Because of that, Miss Amy has
been a bit unwell lately. Just the other day, Miss Seriel came by but had to leave."
"Oh, I see."
Seriel was Amy's closest friend. If even she was sent away, it meant Amy
was serious.
But Shirone didn't give up.
"Please just tell her I'm here. If she still doesn't want to see
me, I'll leave."
Adel hesitated for a moment, then nodded.
In fact, the atmosphere in the mansion had been tense since Amy failed
her graduation exam. Since she wasn't the type to show her frustration outwardly, no one had stepped up
to comfort her, just watching her mood.
But Shirone might bring a change.
After all, he was the only friend who could enter Amy's house as a
man.
"Understood. Please wait a moment."
Adel instructed the guards to treat Shirone with respect and got into
the carriage waiting in the garden.
'It's been about half a year. What has he been through?'
As the carriage started, Adel's expression turned serious.
Using Pressing on men approaching Amy was a small pleasure and hobby
only the head butler could enjoy.
But this time, he didn't even dare to attempt Pressing.
Shirone's mind had become much sharper than before.
No, 'sharp' wasn't enough to describe it.
'Chilling.'
The moment he tried to use Pressing and met Shirone's eyes, he felt his
hair stand on end.
Overall, his temperament didn't seem to have changed much.
He was still intelligent, bright, and kind-hearted, just as he had been
half a year ago.
But the energy in his eyes was subtly sharper.
'Something else is living inside him. Something very
terrifying...'
It was a fact that only someone of Adel's caliber, the head butler of
the prestigious Karmis family, could sense.
He couldn't guess what it was, but if he tried to harm Shirone, it would
surely come out and devour him.
In the brief moment their eyes met, Adel realized that such a situation
must never happen.
It was that violent and extreme a monster.
'Is it okay to let him in? Of course, he wouldn't have come to see his
friend with an uncontrollable demon inside him...'
Adel reserved judgment on this matter.
There was no way the head of the family, Shakora, wouldn't know what he
knew. And of course, the person in question would know as well.
Thinking that far, a bitter smile appeared on his face.
'...What an extraordinary boy.'
How long had it been since he felt his legs go weak, even for a
moment?
Shirone had sealed such a monster deep within himself.
'He's become even stronger in half a year. Truly remarkable
talent.'
The Karmis family was known for its lineage of geniuses. Especially
Amy's talent was outstanding even among her family.
But Shirone's growth was beyond imagination.
If Amy was the fastest sprinter among humans, Shirone was a giant with a
stride on a different level.
'What drives him?'
As the carriage stopped, so did Adel's thoughts.
With unanswered questions in his mind, he opened the mansion
door.
Although quite some time had passed since the graduation exam, a solemn
atmosphere still lingered.
For Adel, who had loved the diverse atmosphere of the Karmis family all
his life, it was a difficult adjustment.
"Master."
Shakora, who was reading the newspaper, turned his gaze.
"Shirone has come to see Miss Amy. However, Miss Amy has informed
the butler's office that she will not meet anyone..."
"Hmm, Shirone?"
Shakora understood Amy's feelings.
His daughter, who was full of pride, had messed up her graduation exam,
so how could she face anyone?
But with Shirone, there was room to gauge her intentions.
Although Seriel had been turned away, the emotions between a man and a
woman were different.
Isis, who had been listening from the kitchen, came out and
spoke.
"I'll go ask Amy."
Isis went upstairs to Amy's room on the second floor.
After a brief conversation, she came back out and approached the
railing, shaking her head.
Receiving Amy's message, Adel bowed without hesitation.
"Then I will send Shirone back."
"No, let him in."
At Shakora's words, Isis looked worried.
"But dear, Amy..."
"It's fine. Didn't I tell Shirone to come when I saw him at the
school? It's not polite to send him back like this. I want to see him. Let him in."
"Understood."
Thinking that at least Shirone wouldn't be disappointed, Adel left the
mansion with a brighter face.
Ten minutes later, Shirone arrived at the mansion.
Shakora was sitting on the sofa waiting, and Isis brought out a tray of
tropical fruits just in time.
Shirone walked over to Shakora and bowed.
"Hello, Father."
"Yes, welcome. Have a seat."
Sitting on the sofa, Shirone waited for the conversation to
start.
Having heard that the atmosphere in the house wasn't good, he didn't
bring up Amy first.
Like Adel, Shakora also immediately noticed the subtle sharpness in
Shirone's demeanor.
During the graduation exam, he hadn't had the time to observe closely
due to his concern for his daughter, but now that she had failed, he was interested in Shirone's
achievements as a parent.
'The kingdom's top prospect...'
Of course, this evaluation excluded the graduating class.
But even including them, he didn't think Shirone's evaluation would drop
significantly.
Although he hadn't told Amy, Shakora was an enthusiastic father who had
been clipping articles about the magic society since she entered school. The student he had heard about
the most was Dante.
When Shirone defeated Dante, the top student in the kingdom, even
Shakora was surprised.
But now, seeing him, it was clear it wasn't luck or chance.
In the eyes of this spiritually accomplished monk, there was a burning
desire that transcended the mundane world.
Adel had called him a monster, but Shakora thought differently.
That too was Shirone.
'It's not something you can get anywhere. Was it a latent trait that
manifested later? But how?'
He had already heard the story of Shirone's rough experiences in the
Kazra Kingdom. But even so, it was too transcendental a demon for an eighteen-year-old boy to
bear.
Realizing they hadn't spoken for a while, Shakora returned to being a
doting father and smiled wryly.
"Actually, she's not very sick."
"Yes, I know."
He had thought as much.
But the reason he didn't think it was a lie was that it might be a
condition more serious than physical pain.
Shakora scratched his head as if troubled.
"Honestly, I don't really know. She doesn't seem to be struggling
much, but she seems a bit deflated. You would understand, right?"
"Of course. I would feel the same way."
At first, it's bearable.
You might think, since it has come to this, let's start over.
But when you think about having to repeat the grueling training for
another year, the failure of the graduation exam hits you to the bone.
With each passing day, the regret grows heavier and the self-reproach
endlessly weighs down on your heart.
"Amy is strong. She has truly given her best since she was twelve
years old to get this far. That's probably why the wound is deeper."
"Yes, it's best to leave her be for now. Anyway, that's Amy's
current state."
Shakora shrugged his shoulders in a gesture of surrender. It meant that
with his daughter's mood as it was, there was nothing he could do to help.
Shirone glanced up at the second floor and confirmed that Amy's room was
firmly closed.
It seemed he could still hear the sorrowful sobs he had heard outside
the dormitory door on the day the graduation exam ended.
"May I go up and see her?"
Isis spoke with a troubled expression.
"I asked, but Amy said she was sick and told me to send you away.
If you push in recklessly..."
"Haha! I'll just get hit once, then."
Shirone replied playfully. But at the same time, he conveyed his
determination not to give up.
Shakora sighed deeply and fell into thought.
Only Shirone could fully understand Amy's feelings.
Also, the fact that a considerate child was being stubborn meant that
Amy's condition was much more serious than expected.
If there was no breakthrough, shock therapy might be a good
method.
"Go to Amy's room. I give you permission."
"Thank you."
Shirone got up from the sofa and went up the stairs to the second
floor.
Watching him, Isis asked Shakora with a worried expression.
"Dear, what are you going to do? With Amy's
personality..."
"It's fine. He understands Amy's current psychological state better
than anyone. Actually, Shirone can just leave if it doesn't work out. But the fact that he insists on
meeting her means that things can't go on like this."
"What if the situation gets worse?"
"It'll be fine. More importantly, he's a friend Amy
trusts."
Isis pouted her lips as she glanced at Shakora.
'Since when did you start praising boys? You doting father...'
