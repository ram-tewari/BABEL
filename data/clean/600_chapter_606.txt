Chapter 606:
Liz, who had stayed up all night waiting for Oscar, buried her head on
the table, leaving her cold coffee untouched.
"Sigh."
When she took a long vacation and came to Creas, she never imagined she
would feel this troubled.
'He met another woman?'
It was something she couldn't have imagined before hearing it from
Neide, but it was true that Oscar hadn't returned without any notice.
'Neide isn't someone who would lie.'
How did things end up like this?
She just wanted to see him one last time before committing to a lifetime
with Oscar.
"Don't move!"
In the alchemist Wiz's warehouse, Neide had quickly taken down six
mages.
As he was about to deal with the last mage, the concentric circles of
flames restraining Liz flared up.
"Don't move a finger. If you do anything, this woman dies."
For a guild mage, time-splitting techniques were out of reach, and the
only option left was to use Liz as a hostage to threaten Neide.
"Tch."
As Neide suppressed the electricity surrounding his body, a smile
finally appeared on Wiz's face.
"Good! Finish him off!"
With Liz as a hostage, the mage couldn't cast any other spells and
hesitated.
Admitting, "This is all I can do," would have been foolish.
'This is why street mages…'
Realizing the mage's hesitation, Wiz frowned and quickly found a new
solution.
'That's it!'
If he shot Neide with the Ultrix, which discharged electrical energy, he
would be incinerated without a trace.
As Wiz activated the Ultrix, Liz's face turned pale.
"Neide! Run!"
"Shut up."
The mage threatening Liz warned Neide.
"Don't move if you want to save her."
Even as he said this, he wondered if this deal was worth it, but
surprisingly, Neide completely disarmed himself.
"Do as you please."
"Neide, why…?"
Looking at Liz's confused expression, Neide gave a bitter smile.
"Who knows why?"
Even as Neide recalled that day, his decision remained a mystery.
Perhaps he wanted to end it all.
"Wiz! Hurry!"
The mage, thinking they needed to act before Neide changed his mind,
shouted.
Meanwhile, the Ultrix's discharge port condensed powerful electrical
energy, ready to incinerate its target.
"No!"
Seeing Neide exposed and defenseless before the Ultrix, Liz made the
only choice she could.
'I have to die.'
As she stepped out of the concentric circles of flames, the fire shot up
to the ceiling.
"Idiot!"
As Neide shouted, Wiz fired the Ultrix.
"Grr!"
The lightning shot out at the speed of light, striking Neide, and even
the ruthless Wiz couldn't bear to watch, closing his eyes tightly.
"Grr!"
Hearing Neide's continued groans, Wiz opened his eyes to an unbelievable
sight, even for his 30 years of alchemical experience.
"What is that?"
Neide's body was enveloped in massive electrical energy.
"Immune to electricity?"
It was impossible for any human or biological body.
There was only one possibility.
"Could it be?"
At that moment, Neide's body disappeared like lightning, cutting through
the mage's flames.
Liz, now in Neide's arms, looked up.
"Neide?"
"Grr!"
Neide's face was covered in veins bulging from his neck, as if he were
desperately holding something back.
"This is impossible…"
As the flames lost their target and died out, the mage realized.
The highest level of electrical movement technique, Spark.
Unlike photon-based teleportation, it burned the mage's body, making it
impossible to cast without special equipment.
"Run. I can't hold it back anymore."
Pushing Liz away, Neide turned, consumed by uncontrollable killing
intent.
"Run! Run!"
As Wiz and the mage scattered in different directions, Neide let out a
monstrous scream, unleashing his power.
The warehouse lit up, and thousands of electrical currents twisted like
sharp wires, pushing everything in front of them.
"You… bastard."
Hit by the Lightning Thunder, Neide bent over, trembling, then looked up
with terrifying eyes.
Meeting those eyes, Oscar felt as if all his blood had
evaporated.
"How can you…!"
People's reactions to Neide's unique phenomenon were always the
same.
Those unfamiliar with magic were terrified, but those knowledgeable in
magic were shocked and thought of one thing.
"Magic Synchronization?"
Also known as Magic Resonance.
It's when a mage's mind completely aligns with magic, famously
exemplified by the Ice Queen exiled to the Les Mountains.
But this phenomenon is impossible for a mage with a normal
mindset.
Mages are inherently rational, and Magic Synchronization is the most
irrational mental state, aligning the mind with natural phenomena.
Mages affected by Magic Synchronization caused disasters wherever they
went, and their ends were always tragic.
'No, this can't be.'
Neide had been talking to him normally just moments ago.
But now, approaching with electricity surrounding him, Neide was clearly
electricity itself.
"Die."
At Neide's declaration, Oscar hastily cast a teleportation spell.
Lightning struck where he had been, creating a deafening thunderclap and
scorching a 20-meter radius.
"Ahhh!"
Oscar screamed as the shockwave sent him rolling on the ground.
It wasn't human thought but electrical thought.
Like the spirit of electricity, the only emotion Neide felt was a
natural desire to destroy everything.
"Grr!"
His face completely twisted, veins bulging, and blue light shining from
his eyes.
'I'm going to die. I'm really going to die.'
A purposeless destructive instinct.
The sight of a natural phenomenon manifesting as a human, walking
towards him, was the same fear Prings had experienced, and Oscar's limbs trembled.
"Help…!"
As Spark was cast, Oscar's body was thrown 100 meters away.
"Grr!"
As Neide slammed into a tree trunk, a powerful impact struck the back of
his head, and a strained voice was heard.
"Why… did you do it?"
Hearing the question, Oscar, like Prings, found a glimmer of
hope.
'He still has some sanity left.'
He didn't know how, but it was clear that not answering would mean
death.
"What… what did I do?"
"Why did you bring Liz? If you wanted success so badly, you shouldn't
have brought her. You should have gone to that woman."
"I told her not to come! I was conflicted too… The only way for someone
not from a prestigious school to get into the association was that."
"That's not an answer."
"It's true! I loved Liz too!"
It was sincere but a packaged sincerity.
"Die."
Oscar's heart sank, and he hit rock bottom.
"I wasn't sure! Things might not have worked out, and I didn't want to
lose Liz, so I brought her!"
In the face of human weakness, Neide's power grew stronger.
"Please, spare me. If you kill me, Liz won't know I cheated. She'll
blame you!"
That didn't matter.
What Neide truly feared was the guilt Liz would feel for loving a man
who had killed.
"Grr!"
But the effort to suppress the Magic Synchronization was reaching its
limit, destroying Neide's mind.
"Go. Get out of here…"
As Neide's grip weakened and Oscar slumped, the final limit
broke.
"Grr!"
Oscar's screams were drowned out by the sound of electricity exploding
everywhere.
"Help!"
Electricity struck like a predator's fangs, following him.
He was no longer sane and didn't even realize his pants were wet from
fear.
As he turned his head, he saw Neide, fully enveloped in electricity,
charging at him with white light in his eyes.
"Ahhh!"
At the moment of his most pathetic scream, someone rushed in like the
wind.
"Neide!"
Shirone, blocking Oscar, cast Burst, and Neide cast Spark, moving at a
right angle.
Shirone was stunned by the abnormal reaction speed as Neide dodged the
first barrier.
"Iruki! Hurry!"
As Shirone called, Iruki landed and took Oscar away.
They had been waiting for Neide from a distance at the Dolphin Inn, and
when they saw Oscar, they teleported and rushed over at the sound of the explosion.
"Shirone! Get out of there too!"
With Magic Synchronization in effect, Neide couldn't distinguish between
friend and foe.
"Neide! It's me! Snap out of it!"
Shirone didn't give up and shouted, and Neide disappeared.
With a crackling sound, Neide appeared in front of Shirone and tore at
his face.
Hearing the sound of his face exploding from the powerful electricity
was a glimpse into the future.
'Time Leap!'
As Shirone hastily moved his face, he saw Neide's hand swipe
past.
Without time to think, Shirone repeatedly cast teleportation, retreating
30 meters and stopping with his hand on the ground.
"Gasp! Gasp! Gasp!"
His heart pounded wildly, and his whole body trembled as if
electrocuted.
One of his legs, supporting his weight, gave out and buckled.
'I told you it wouldn't work.'
Iruki, already out of range, clicked his tongue.
Neide was no longer a mage but magic itself, no longer human but
electricity.
Electricity twisted by killing intent.
'It's not fear. He's my friend.'
Anyone would feel absolute fear before Neide, but Shirone didn't give up
and stood up.
"Nade! It's me, Shirone! Please, snap out of it!"
"Shirone…"
One of Nade's eyes dimmed, and a human-like voice emerged.
"Nade! You're coming back to your senses…!"
"Graaaah!"
But it was only for a moment. Nade spread plasma thickly on the ground
and cast Spark Magic again.
Lightning flashed everywhere, and the distortion of natural phenomena by
time magic created a scene that seemed like it was happening in some distant part of the
universe.
"How can he do that?"
Oscar, who was watching the battle with a dazed expression,
muttered.
"A graduating student?"
He had gained a lot of pride as a mage after getting certified, but he
had never imagined such a monstrous and irrational fight could exist.
"Still, is he a friend?"
Even Iruki was staring in a daze this time.
Facing Nade's transcendent killing intent head-on proved that Shirone's
spirit was also at the highest level, beyond natural fear.
"But Shirone, you know, right?"
Even so, it was best to stop this fight as soon as possible.
"There is no limit to magical assimilation."
"Aaaaah!"
Nade, unable to catch Shirone, let out a monstrous scream, and the
electric shock spread explosively, burning everything.
"We're going to die! We're all going to die!"
Oscar couldn't even think of avoiding the wave of electricity rushing in
like a tsunami.
"I can't stop this!"
Looking at the massive natural phenomenon, Shirone reached back.
"Yes, Iruki. Seeing it in person is definitely
different."
He had no choice but to acknowledge that to stop Nade, he too had to be
prepared to die.
"Diamond Armor!"
At the moment Shirone, gripping Armand's handle, shouted, Nade charged
with thunder.
