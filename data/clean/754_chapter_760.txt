Chapter 760:
"Ugh!"
Jayce only felt the pain after flying more than twenty meters, clinging
desperately to her fading consciousness.
'Stop! Stop already!'
She tried using a reverse-thrust spell from the Blow category, but all
it did was make her bounce across the ground like a ball, multiplying the pain.
"Kyaaaaa!"
She had never imagined a person could tumble for so long—or so
violently.
She rolled endlessly, as if the end was waiting at some far, unreachable
point in time.
'I'm going to die!'
She was going to die from tumbling.
She felt something snap with a sickening crack, and just then, a
tremendous force stopped her cold.
"Ah... haah... haah..."
Lying in someone's arms and staring up at the sky spinning above, Jayce
quickly assessed the damage to her own body.
'Three broken ribs, fractured wrist, torn ankle ligaments.'
And after the damage assessment came a question: what kind of magic had
hit her?
'It was just light...'
There was a growing amount of knowledge about divine particles now, but
North Aymond was an exception.
'There's no such mage here...'
If a third-rank archmage was taken out with a single spell, the caster
had to be at least first rank—possibly higher.
"You got a real beating. But I'm sure it was a good experience for
you."
Minerva clicked her tongue, then leaned in and blew the Witch's Breath
into Jayce's lips.
"Ah..."
Jayce slipped into brief ecstasy. When she came to, all the pain in her
body had completely vanished.
"I only patched it together. Don't even think about fighting."
Jayce's eyes flew open.
"I can't do that! I still have thousands—tens of thousands—left to
kill...! Nngh!"
Her side throbbed, and she curled up in pain.
'Beautiful, that rage.'
Minerva watched her affectionately, then adjusted her expression and
stood up.
Shirone was standing right in front of her.
"What do you think you're doing?"
"You know what. If you don't by now, that's just embarrassing. North
Aymond conducted experiments that pose a threat to humanity."
"So what are you planning to do about it?"
"As you can see..."
Minerva gestured at the battlefield where countless demons had turned to
ash and disappeared.
"I'm ending them one by one. It won't be long before North Aymond
disappears from the world map."
Her refusal to compromise meant she had also realized the truth about
Igor.
"The Garas are one thing, but control of the shrine has already been
lost. You see those demons? Even now, more of them are pouring out. This can't be stopped without a mass
purge."
"That's barbaric. You'll drag innocent people into this too."
"Of course. But minimizing the damage is my job. And frankly..."
Minerva's eyes turned cold.
"There isn't a single person in this world who doesn't deserve to
die."
"..."
"If you're strong, you can choose—whether to be good or evil. But the
weak? They only have one weapon to become strong: evil. Do something evil, and people fear you. Because
they don't want to be the victim."
She pointed her pipe at Baseto.
"You think he's any different? If he were as powerful as you, he
might've turned out even worse than Igor. No one knows."
Shirone had learned what kind of person Minerva was back in the Ivory
Tower elevator, and he knew why from the story of Lawslayer.
Since compromise was off the table, he spoke plainly.
"Stop the slaughter. From here on out, I'll handle North Aymond."
The one who hated hearing that most was Jayce. Clutching her side, she
stepped closer to Minerva.
"You know each other?"
At least if that were true, she wouldn't feel so humiliated.
"Know each other? You could say we're coworkers."
"What...? Coworkers?"
Jayce narrowed her eyes, glancing back and forth between Minerva and
Shirone.
"He's one of the Stars too?"
"Yup. It's awkward. If he were from another branch, I could just chase
him off—but he's the same rank as me."
The same rank as Minerva.
"Wait, then he's one of the Five Stars?!"
"That's right. I'm with the Department of Human Safety Enforcement, and
that brat's with the Department of Integrated Cosmic Management. And the problem is..."
The Cosmic Department held slightly more sway with Great Star.
Jayce didn't understand the internal power dynamics of the Ivory Tower,
but she found Shirone fascinating.
"He looks barely twenty..."
Then again, Minerva herself was over seven hundred years old.
"Yeah, he's twenty."
Jayce gave Minerva a stunned look—but she wasn't joking.
"And the strongest mage in all humanity. Or is he ranked second?"
Minerva smiled teasingly, subtly referencing Nane.
"Fine, we'll call it a tie for first."
"Don't stir up drama like a child. I'll handle Nane. Right now, you're
the problem, Minerva. Nothing will be solved if you keep doing things like this."
"So what do you want me to do?"
"Cancel the disaster magic immediately."
The toxic gas she had spread was killing everything—demons, half-demons,
and civilians alike.
"You still have Lawslayer, don't you?"
Shirone frowned.
"Let's make a deal. Hand over Lawslayer, and I'll hand this incident
over to the Cosmic Department."
"I can't do that."
"Going to give it to Kashan? You're not in a position to play politics
right now."
"No. I just think if you have it, things will only get worse."
The poison gas vanished.
"Just because you sacrificed yourself for everyone doesn't mean you can
save them all. You and Nane aren't gods. And you're not Goffin either. Even if you've reached the realm
of infinity, it's still just human infinity."
Minerva pointed to the battlefield.
"See? Even now, people are dying to the demons. Sure, we can win. But we
can't save everyone! Stop pretending to be some half-baked god and focus your efforts!"
That's what pissed her off the most.
"Nane, Kashan, the shrine, humanity, the horrors...! You can't do it
all! You can't even protect the people right here and now!"
From the other side of the supply road, Verdi and her family
shouted.
"Dad! Dad!"
"Verdi!"
Baseto dove forward, and demons with vicious weapons were chasing down
his family.
Everything happened at once.
The second-generation demons were massacring the stationed troops, and
in the distance, Commander Godan of the 48th Military Facility was cutting down laborers with his
saber.
Minerva spoke.
"This is reality! Killing them all is the only way to minimize
casualties! So let me handle it and get out of the way!"
"And what is 'reality,' exactly?"
The moment you give up—that's reality.
"How can you say that if you haven't even tried?"
As Shirone focused his mind, Spirit Zone expanded to an unimaginable
scale, engulfing the entire military facility.
"No way..."
Even Jayce, a third-rank archmage, couldn't dare interrupt the divinity
of Yahweh's mind, which had surpassed human limits.
'Quantum Superposition!'
One hundred thousand overlays.
Far surpassing what he'd accomplished in Andre's Labyrinth, countless
projections of Shirone blinked across the battlefield with teleportation.
Blinding bursts of light exploded everywhere, and countless people
experienced miracles all at once.
"Gghhh...!"
A synesthesia of the five senses, time, space, and spirit swept through
them via Spirit Zone.
The transcendental data precisely analyzed every situation, and Shirone
rescued people just seconds before they would have fallen to the demons.
"Damn Yahweh! That cursed Yahweh!"
The demons were the only ones perishing, and the second-generation
demons froze, struck with fear.
"Ah... Ahhh..."
Verdi looked up at the thousands of arcs of light streaking through the
sky, tears running down her face.
She couldn't analyze it with an adult's mind, but the swell of emotion
in her chest was no different.
"Huuuk... Huuuuk..."
He would save everyone.
"Big brother told me so."
Inside the 48th military facility lived countless people treated as less
than human.
"You're the most valuable person in the world."
As something no one in human history had ever attempted became reality,
people one by one fell to their knees.
The sky lit up with radiant light.
"What is that light?"
As everyone whispered the same question, the demons turning to ash
screamed their final curses.
"Horrible Yahweh! I'll tear the ones you love to shreds! I'll watch you
weep blood in Hell!"
"Die, Yahweh! Curse you, Yahweh!"
And then people murmured back,
"Yahweh, huh."
A love no human could possess.
"Oh Yahweh..."
A hundred thousand lights left ten million afterimages as they blanketed
the sky, and finally, the entire world was filled with light.
Shirone collapsed to his knees, gasping for breath.
"Haa... Haa..."
Minerva stood in silence, deep in thought, as she took in the sight of a
battlefield where not a single demon remained.
"It's not over yet!"
What remained were a few of the second-generation demons.
Most were too injured to move, but Commander Godan still stood, charging
straight toward Shirone.
"Demon."
It was only natural for those filled with every human emotion except
love to hate Shirone.
"I'll kill you, you damned...!"
Minerva raised her hand. Her Jet shot forward at terrifying speed,
piercing straight through Godan's body.
"Gah!"
Still impaled, his body was dragged through the facility, crashing
through building after building until he finally went limp.
"...You alright?"
Minerva approached Shirone, who was steadying his breath with a
meditative technique.
Shirone raised his head in silence.
"That look says you think you're the only one who can answer."
"I can do this. You may have your own karma, but let me handle this
incident."
"You saved the 48th military facility."

"What next? Will you save the capital? Then all of North Aymond? Then

How far would it go?
"Just because you succeeded today doesn't mean your karma has been
fulfilled."
"I know. But..."
Shirone looked toward Verdi, crying tears of joy as she clung to her
family.
"That's not what matters."
If one person sacrificing everything could save the whole—then...
'That's why even Nane didn't try. But if—just if—there's even a 1%
chance...'
What better gamble could there be?
"Hmph. Talking about karma when you don't even have one."

"So, you're leaving it to me now?"
"Who cares? The Department of Human Safety Enforcement does what you say

They were, after all, the department with the highest kill count.

"More or less. That's enough to kill some petty thugs. But this time...

"Since you know Uorin, this should be easy to follow. Their tricks

'She's right. From North Aymond to the Gustav imperial palace, and

How would the Lawslayer's laws of fate have to shift to make that
