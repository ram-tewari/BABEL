In The Flower Field (3)
Following Anke Ra's orders, Satiel returned to the place where Ikael was
hiding.
"You're back. I was worried since I hadn't heard from
you."
There wasn't a hint of doubt in Ikael's smile.
'No, none of them are foolish.'
They must have already considered all variables and assumed the
worst-case scenario.
Yet they were still smiling...
'They trust me.'
They trusted the years Satiel had fought for humans and the desire for
everyone to become gods.
Satiel tried to appear calm.
"Yes. I'm sorry for being late. Actually... I haven't come up with
a name yet. It's a lot of pressure."
Ikael looked at her with a gentle gaze.
"Haha, it's okay. You're an angel with a kind heart, so I'm sure
you'll come up with a wonderful name."
Satiel couldn't respond.
"Come in. You're like my sister, so you'll be an aunt to our child.
You should see your nephew's face."
'Aunt?'
Her eyes welled up with tears.
Even if she couldn't be with Goffin, they could have been connected in
this way.
'No, this isn't what I wanted. I don't want this kind of relationship
with Goffin.'
But why did the tears keep coming?
"What are you doing? Come in already."
When she finally cut off her feelings coldly, even that small connection
felt incredibly precious.
'I should have just endured it. I could have watched over them and lived
my life.'
But it was too late.
"I'm sorry."
Seeing the tears of light streaming down Satiel's cheeks, Ikael tilted
her head in confusion.
"Don't forgive me."
A loud noise erupted in the distance.
Though the source of the sound was not yet visible, Goffin sensed the
danger from the wind pressure alone.
"Get down!"
As maternal instinct erased all other thoughts, Ikael flew in the
opposite direction of the sound.
The speed was immense, but it probably wouldn't have been enough.
If Ymir had targeted Ikael instead of Goffin.
"Found you."
Goffin, seeing Ymir, watched as a fist seemed to tear through space
towards him.
'Miracle Stream!'
Golden smoke wrapped around Ymir's arm, and a command that transcended
the world was input.
'Stop.'
As their signals clashed, vibrations occurred, but neither felt the
tremors.
The world shook entirely.
"Grrr!"
Though his face twisted, Ymir's eyes filled with joy at the resistance
he felt for the first time in his life.
'Is this what it feels like?'
To do something.
"Raaah!"
As he roared and extended his arm, an immeasurable force surged
forth.
The world's vibrations ceased, and Ymir's fist stopped right in front of
Goffin's face.
If Ymir's elbow had fully extended, Goffin's head would have been blown
off.
'He broke through Miracle Stream.'
Destroying Hexa, the artificial implementation of the Idea,
meant...
"Are you a Gaia?"
There was only one conclusion.
"I am Ymir."
Recharging his muscles with energy, Ymir lunged forward with one leg
extended.
"I am the king of giants!"
Goffin quickly created distance.
'Strange.'
Though it was his first time facing such a formidable opponent, there
was a strange sense of nostalgia in Ymir's appearance.
'...Could it be?'
He realized it from Ymir's eyes.
It was a single feeling, but it was deep enough to contain the emotions
of 10 billion beings.
"Is that what happened?"
Realizing he was the last Gaia left in the world, Goffin's eyes burned
with determination.
"I'm sorry."
Stopping his retreat, he pulled his arms back, gathering Miracle Stream
between his palms.
A beam of light shot towards the rapidly approaching Ymir.
'Photon Cannon.'
As the near-light-speed mass struck Ymir's abdomen,
"Grrr!"
Ymir grabbed the sphere of light with both hands and crushed its inertia
with his grip.
'Did he catch it?'
No, catching it wasn't the problem.
If a biological body moved at near-light speed, it should have vaporized
from frictional heat.
Seeing Ymir's arms glowing red-hot sent chills down Goffin's
spine.
'This is...'
As Ymir moved at near-light speed, a shockwave spread out 4 kilometers
around him.
Satiel, who had been standing in a daze after Ikael fled, was instantly
blown away...
"Grrr!"
In the flattened area, only Goffin and Ymir glared at each other.
"Strange."
Ymir brushed off his aching abdomen.
Though pain brought him joy, the problem now wasn't his body.
"I don't feel good."
He could be an enemy to everything in this world, but Goffin was the
only exception.
"It feels like... I'm hurting myself."
Goffin asked,
"Do you remember anything before you were born?"
"Not at all. But I can guess. Seeing you, I think I'm
right."
"Let me go. I need to protect my wife and child."
Ymir looked up at the sky.
"Family."
At this early stage of his existence, he wanted to sort everything
out.
"I don't have a family. I never will."
"If you help me, you could..."
Ymir extended his hand.
"No, I don't want to care about anything. I just want to
fight."
He had made his decision, and only one thing remained.
"If you don't have much time."
Ymir stomped the ground.
"Let's end this."
With one kick, the ground collapsed into an endless abyss, causing a
massive explosion.
From space, it would have looked like a matchstick light on the planet's
northern hemisphere.
Rumble!
The shockwave reached Heaven, and even the angels turned pale before its
destructive power.
'This giant is dangerous.'
Uriel turned towards the explosion.
'No compatibility. No natural enemy. No criteria. Just pure strength.
His existence is destruction.'
Even Goffin...
In Uriel's mind, surveying the battlefield, he saw a sea of molten lava
where all matter had melted.
Ten meters above the hot surface, Ymir floated in the air.
The external gravity caused by simple muscle vibrations showed no sign
of returning even after ten minutes.
'Goffin, I gave you a chance.'
Just before they clashed with their ultimate attacks, Ymir abandoned his
assault.
If Goffin had killed him with his strongest blow, he would have accepted
that outcome.
'But I survived. And... the unease that hindered my resolve has
completely disappeared.'
"I am Ymir."
He finally felt like himself.
Before leaving the mantle of the planet, Goffin spoke.
"Thank you."
As Ymir slowly rose from the lava, he smirked.
"Both of us were uneasy, huh? Anyway, this is the worst outcome for
you. As long as I exist, you can't win anything."
"That might be true."
Goffin didn't deny it.
"But hearts are endlessly connected. Someone will appear to take my
place. And..."
Goffin's body turned to light and became transparent, dissolving into
the air.
"They will free my people from the prison that is you."
Ymir snapped out of his recollection.
"Connected."
Waiting for the external gravity to return, he sat cross-legged in the
air, resting his chin on his hand.
"I hope so."
At the peak of a mountain, Nane sat in meditation, with clouds swirling
above his head.
Watching the scene, Shura muttered,
"A Buddha with worldly desires."
No, it was precisely because it was the Buddha's desires that it was the
last problem remaining in this world.
'Is it Amy? What was so impressive about her?'
Since Amy left, Nane had been lost in thought, never resting.
'It doesn't matter.'
Shura turned away.
'I just want to know the secrets of the world. If this leads the Buddha
to enlightenment, I have no regrets.'
Though she organized her thoughts, the feeling of not being recognized
remained.
"Shura."
Nane's voice descended from the sky.
"Come to me."
Flying vertically up the high peak, Shura knelt before Nane.
"You called?"
"I can feel your inner turmoil."
Shura gathered her courage.
"Forgive my rudeness, but why Amy?"
The Buddha of the era, the one who preached the emptiness of all beings,

Nane plucked a flower from its roots.
"I wondered how I could throw my heart out there."
Then, covering the flower gently with his other hand, he
continued,
"Praising the color, scent, and form of this flower, I couldn't

The flower was crushed between his palms.
"It was just emptiness."
Shura listened silently.
"No matter what method I used, everything was false before

Karmis Amy.

Shura's expression turned blank.

"I throw my heart into this flower. The color, scent, and form

'Gaold.'
The one who defeated Buddha.
