Chapter 112:
End-of-Term Report Card (4)
The summer was fading.
Chilly winds blew in the mornings and evenings, and plants prepared to don their autumn
colors. But the days remained hot, and the students were relentless in their final push.
Only one week remained. Classes had ended the previous week, leaving only the final
exams.
Exams were held sequentially, one class per day, starting from Class Ten. On the sixth
day, Class Five students soothed their pounding hearts as they headed to the exam hall.
Shirone's group picked at breakfast and arrived early.
The exam lasted exactly 12 hours, with only five-minute breaks. Compared to ordinary
schools, this was grueling, but for magic school students, maintaining focus was as crucial as knowledge.
The exams were divided into Types A, B, and C—same questions, different answer
arrangements—to prevent cheating and lucky guesses.
Shirone had Type A, Iruki Type B, and Nade Type C. Assigned to different halls, they
stepped into the corridor beforehand to calm their nerves. Even the troublemakers were tense—their half-year
efforts hinged on this.
Nade shivered despite the warm weather.
"Ugh. I'm dying. Shirone, how about you?"
"Feel like puking. One mistake, and it's over. Especially in math."
"Stay calm. We have time. Save the nerves for the exam paper."
At Iruki's words, the two steadied themselves. But when the starting bell rang, even they
paled.
"Let's go. See you with smiles later."
"Right. Good luck!"
The three parted ways, promising to reunite in 12 hours.
***
Supernatural Psychic Science Lab.
Shirone's group was busy grading their exams. Though the papers couldn't be leaked, Iruki
had perfectly reconstructed them.
A Servant's memory knew no forgetting. It was like storing documents in mental drawers,
retrievable anytime.
Iruki recited questions, Nade transcribed, and Shirone checked answers. They worked until
dawn to reconstruct all subjects.
Exhausted, they split up to tally scores.
"Ugh, I'm nervous. Think I bombed a few."
"Same. This is brutal. Even perfect scores in other subjects won't save us if one
falls short."
"But that's why we formed the study group. Let's finish grading first."
Hours later, their scores took shape.
Nade was the first to leap up.
"Yes! 82 average! Cleared 80 in every subject!"
No high marks, but balanced scores secured an 82. The former delinquent, who'd once
scoffed at Class Four, now couldn't be happier.
Iruki and Shirone stayed quiet. True celebration would come only if all three advanced.
Iruki leaned back, wiping sweat.
"Done. 87. Got one 80 in language. One more wrong, and it'd have been over."
"Whoa! You passed too? Congrats! Class Four!"
"Whatever. Feels good, though."
Nade and Iruki turned to Shirone. If he passed, their dream of advancing together would
come true.
But Shirone was silent. Iruki noticed his hand had stopped moving.
"Shirone? What's wrong? Did you fail?"
Nade pressed anxiously.
"What's the matter? What's your score?"
"86…"
"Whoa, that's amazing!"
Nade was genuinely surprised. They'd aimed for all three to advance, but hearing it felt
miraculous. Shirone, a commoner's son with no academic background, had averaged 86 after just half a year of
effort.
Compared to Iruki's 87—flawless in math thanks to his Servant ability—Shirone's
desperation was evident.
Iruki tilted his head.
"You… did well, right? Did you bomb a subject?"
"That's the thing—I'm not sure."
"Not sure? What do you mean? Explain."
Shirone bit his nails anxiously. No matter how hard he thought, it wouldn't come to him.
"Iruki."
"Yeah?"
"The last multiple-choice math proof—what was the answer?"
"Oh, that? The one linking two equations? The answer was super long."
Iruki's eyes widened.
"Don't tell me…?"
"Yeah, I can't remember which proof I picked. I redid it, but it's the same. I was
torn between two back then."
"What's your score? If you got it wrong, what's your score?"
"78…"
Iruki and Nade grasped the gravity. The last question was worth 2 points. One mistake
meant advancement or failure.
"How can you not remember? Which option did you pick?"
"Option 3. But I can't check—I had Type A."
Nade snatched the test paper. The last question's options were twenty lines long—a
nightmare. He'd surely gotten it wrong too. Probably only Iruki had aced it in Class Five.
Shirone clutched his head.
"I thought it was 2 or 3. With 10 seconds left, it hit me, but… I can't remember
what it was."
Last-second recalls happened. Nade and Iruki, having grown up in magic school, understood
the feeling.
But this decided advancement. Failing meant another half-year in Class Five.
Nade was frantic.
"Damn it! I can't take this!"
"What're you gonna do?"
"Check directly. Let's ask Ms. Siena. She's our homeroom teacher."
Shirone couldn't focus either. The three left the lab and headed to the exam hall.
Grading had taken a full day, so Class Four's exams were now underway. During lunch,
Shirone's group slipped in and waited anxiously for the teachers. Thirty minutes later, Siena appeared.
They rushed over.
"Teacher! Teacher!"
"Hmm? Why are you here? Class Five's exams ended yesterday."
"The last multiple-choice math proof in Type A! What's the answer?"
Siena tilted her head. Her specialty was chemistry, but all teachers reviewed exam
papers at least three times. Even non-majors could handle student-level math.
"Why the sudden question?"
"Please! It's really important!"
Seeing Nade's desperation, Siena recalled the question. Its difficulty had stuck with
her.
"Oh, that? For Type A, it's Option 2."
"Option 2…"
Shirone froze as if struck. He'd picked Option 3. His score: 78. A 2-point difference
meant failure. His breath caught.
"Option 2… Option 2…"
Muttering, Shirone suddenly clutched his neck. Then, he collapsed backward. Nade barely
caught him.
"Shirone! You okay? Snap out of it! You're Indestructible Diamond!"
"Option 2? How… How could it be Option 2…"
"Teacher, we'll go now! Thanks!"
As they dragged him away, Shirone kept mumbling. Siena blinked, then chuckled and headed
to the faculty office.
"Those kids never run out of energy, do they?"
The office buzzed with grading teachers. Siena sat and began grading. Then, on a whim,
she pulled out the Type A math exam.
"The last multiple-choice, was it?"
She skimmed the question. After a moment, she smiled brightly.
"Ah, it's Option 3."
Arian Shirone. Advancement confirmed.
