Chapter 16:
Alpheas Magic School (2)
"No, it's not like that. It's just that a good opportunity came up."
"Huh? A good opportunity?"
Shirone recounted the events that had transpired. He had already organized the story in
his mind, so the words flowed smoothly. But to the listener, it was nothing short of astonishing.
Vincent's expression gradually turned to shock. Honestly, at some point, he stopped
hearing Shirone's voice. He couldn't fathom what his son was saying.
Had his son befriended the youngest son of the Ozent family? And that friend had sworn a
knight's oath to him? Moreover, the family had accepted it and allowed them to remain friends?
With each successive breach of common sense, it felt like thunder was crashing in his
head—boom, boom, boom. When the story finally ended, Vincent silently scratched his head.
He knew his son was clever and deep-thinking. But even so, this was unbelievable. Had his
son's mind gone astray? Had the hardships here driven him into delusions of grandeur?
"Shirone, be honest with me. Your father would do anything for you. What exactly
happened to you? Who did this to you?"
"It's the truth, Father. Why would I lie about something like this?"
"Exactly! You're not a liar. So tell me. Who tormented you? Who made your life
miserable?"
Vincent was genuinely furious. He had seen countless examples of commoners who got
entangled with noble families and came out half-broken.
Even Shirone thought the story was absurd. But he couldn't think of a better way to
convince them than telling the truth.
As he struggled to find the right words, the door to the reception room opened, and the
direct family members of the Ozent household entered.
=======================================
"Pleased to meet you. I am the family head, Bishop."
Bishop, the family head, greeted everyone on behalf of the group. Though Klump and Rian
were absent, Lian and Reina had accompanied them instead.
Lian bowed deeply to Vincent, his heart filled with warmth.
"Greetings, Father! I am Rian, Shirone's sword. The future greatest swordsman in the
world! Hahahaha!"
"I'm Reina, Rian's older sister. I've heard much about you."
Vincent and Olina could only blink in stunned silence. Then, as if a circuit had suddenly
connected in their minds, they realized the situation and bowed their heads.
"Ah, greetings! I'm Shirone's father. To think esteemed individuals like you would
come to such a place..."
"Oh? Didn't Shirone tell you?"
"Tell me? About what?"
Vincent racked his brain, but the only thing that came to mind was his son's unbelievable
boast.
"So... that story was true?"
"Ah, I suppose it must be. We might have arrived a bit too early. Anyway, it's
exactly as Shirone said. My younger brother Rian and Shirone are bound by a knight's oath. That's why we
went through the trouble of bringing you here."
Finally grasping the full picture, Vincent's heart pounded. The joy was fleeting, quickly
replaced by fear. What kind of trouble had his naive son gotten himself into?
"Let's have a meal first. We can discuss the details afterward."
Bishop, true to his character, concluded the matter briefly and turned away.
The family head's attitude wasn't particularly friendly. But the fact that a noble of the
second rank used honorifics with a commoner was telling—it revealed the parents' feelings of having their
youngest son taken hostage.
Even after the family members left, Vincent remained seated for a while. Then, as Shirone
tugged at his sleeve with a smile, he snapped out of it and followed him to the living room.
'Is this really happening? Our Shirone? With the Ozent family?'
Everything was a question mark. Every question was a shock. Olina, trembling like a
frightened girl, clung tightly to her husband's arm. But even Vincent's legs were shaking.
Then he quickly pulled himself together. If Shirone's words were true, he couldn't afford
to show weakness. He had to hold his ground for his son's sake.
What should have been a simple meal felt like a battlefield to Vincent.
***
A rectangular table was laden with delicacies from land and sea. Even Vincent, a hunter,
saw dishes he had never encountered before. Some ingredients had been specially procured by Reina from the
royal palace.
Seating was arranged according to protocol, with the family head, Bishop, at the head of
the table. To his left sat Klump, and to his right, Reina.
Vincent, seated at the far end, finally felt the reality of the situation as he gazed at
the exquisite dishes before him. When the head butler, Louis, and sub-butler Temuran observed formalities,
he unconsciously straightened his shoulders.
But he couldn't simply enjoy the meal. Considering his son's dignity, he needed to finish
the meal quietly. Yet, the sight of over seven different types of utensils made his head spin.
Commoners could get by with just a fork, even if they were tackling a wild boar.
But the utensils before him varied in size and shape, and some were tools he had never
seen before.
The meal began, but Vincent's family still hadn't taken their first bite. The biggest
problem was that the maids merely stood by, making no effort to assist.
In the precarious psychological battle between nobles and commoners, recklessness was
unnecessary. Moreover, commoners who had suddenly risen in status were often met with ridicule and sarcasm.
Bishop set down his utensils and spoke.
"Please, eat. There's no need to adhere to noble etiquette."
"B-but still..."
"Etiquette isn't about actions but the intent behind them. Your current struggle
alone shows us enough courtesy. Please, eat comfortably."
Vincent was moved by the family head's words. Whether it was genuine consideration or
noble pride, such attention was a significant gesture.
The maids paled. The Ozent family's attitude toward Shirone was genuine. They truly
intended to accept him.
Flustered, the maids hastily explained how to use the utensils. The real issue was
ignorance—once they knew, eating wouldn't be so hard. Vincent's family, having picked up on the cues,
finally ate comfortably.
With his composure restored, Vincent grew concerned about his son. What exactly did this
family intend to do with Shirone?
He had heard the explanation of the knight's oath, but that didn't change anything.
Shirone was still the son of a hunter, and aside from being more clever than most, his future was far from
guaranteed.
Was there some ulterior motive? He hated suspecting those who showed kindness, but when
it came to his child, Vincent would stop at nothing.
"Thank you for looking favorably on my son, but I can't help but worry. The Ozent
family is one of the most prestigious in Creas, isn't it? It's hard to believe that such a family's young
master would swear a knight's oath to Shirone."
"Haha! You needn't worry about that. Shirone and I—"
Bishop cut off Rian's words.
"To be honest, I do feel a bit displeased."
The utensils around the table froze. But Bishop continued cutting his meat without pause.
"However, it's a knight's oath. For the military, it's the highest form of binding
pride and conviction. To harbor ill feelings simply because of a difference in status would tarnish Rian's
honor. So, we'll continue using honorifics—please don't feel too burdened."
Bishop's words resonated deeply with Shirone. Most nobles were arrogant and
self-absorbed. But precisely because of that, the values they revered surpassed imagination.
Nobles, whom he had always seen as oppressors, were now revealed to be individuals
governed by their own codes.
"Kukuku, our family head finally speaks sense. Must be lonely sending off your last
remaining son, eh? Why not just send him off next year?"
Bishop ignored his father's provocation.
"Rian has mastered the Schema and combined it with Ozent swordsmanship, so his
chances of earning recognition are high. Shirone, on the other hand, hasn't even grasped the Schema yet. We
have no choice but to pin our hopes on this swordsmanship school."
"Sounds like you're subtly favoring the black-haired one. Cold-hearted, aren't you?
But blue blood runs with undying youth. Underestimating the passion of the blue-haired might lead to
embarrassment later."
Rian, chewing on lamb, gave his grandfather a thumbs-up. Bishop snorted. He was truly fed
up with his father's antics.
"Blue-haired or black-haired, whoever succeeds is still my child. And that is
precisely..."
The direct family members set down their utensils and spoke in unison.
"...Ozent."
***
After dinner, Klump gathered the family in the living room to discuss Shirone's future.
Reina shared during the family meeting that Shirone was interested in magic, and everyone
agreed. Given his physique and temperament, swordsmanship had its limits.
In the end, calling Vincent had been the right decision. Despite the warm atmosphere
during dinner, Klump wore a solemn expression as he made the announcement.
"As you know, our family intends to support Shirone. I hear he wants to become a
mage, so how about sending him to Alpheas Magic School here in Creas? Headmaster Alpheas is an old friend of
mine, and the school is one of the top five in the Torumia Kingdom. As a state-designated institution,
graduating guarantees an unofficial qualification. In short, he can become a mage."
"A... a mage?"
Vincent swallowed hard. Mages were the epitome of intellect, rulers of phenomena. It was
a special profession, unrelated to status. Once certified, they were highly sought-after talents.
Some in the world were self-taught mages, while others learned as apprentices under
certified mages. Such individuals could join mercenary groups or associations to earn their qualifications.
But these cases were extremely rare. For most commoners, becoming a mage was a distant
dream.
Yet now, Shirone could become a mage—and not just any mage, but one from a prestigious
school that granted qualifications upon graduation. As a father, it was an offer he couldn't refuse.
Olina burst into tears, overwhelmed with emotion. Shirone hugged his mother. It was a
heartwarming scene, but Klump's expression remained dark.
Attending magic school was undoubtedly the fastest path to becoming a mage.
But the problem was Shirone's status. Magic schools strictly admitted nobles, and special
admissions were as rare as plucking stars from the sky.
Even the influence of a second-rank family like the Ozents couldn't easily change that.
After all, magic schools were filled with children from second-rank families—and even first-rank ones.
"You must already know this, but Shirone's current status doesn't meet the school's
admission requirements. While we can cover tuition and material costs, the admission method will inevitably
raise questions."
Vincent snapped out of his dream. The fantasy had been so sweet that he had momentarily
forgotten reality.
"Ah, of course. I know. Yes... I see."
Disappointment seeped into Vincent's voice. There was self-loathing, too. His son,
acknowledged by a noble family, couldn't attend school because of the status inherited from his worthless
father.
"There is one way. No, it's the only way."
At Klump's words, Vincent's head jerked up. Shirone, hearing this for the first time,
also paid close attention.
"We want to adopt... Shirone into the Ozent family."
Silence filled the living room.
Klump's proposal was, in essence, a request to sell his child. Of course, it wasn't a bad
offer—the son of a hunter would instantly become a son of the Ozent family.
Under normal circumstances, such an offer would be unthinkable. But the reason Vincent
hesitated was the matter of blood.
Shirone could become a noble. But from that moment on, he would no longer be his son. His
child would belong to someone else.
Vincent looked at Shirone. Annoyingly, his son's expression remained calm. Was it because
he assumed it would be accepted, or because he thought it was absurd? Vincent couldn't tell.
"After discussing it as a family, we concluded this is the best solution. Among our
cousins, there's one without children. Registering Shirone under him would grant him noble status, making
school admission possible. Of course, we have no intention of taking your son away. It's purely a paperwork
matter—family ties won't change."
Just a paperwork matter. Could it really be that simple? In this world, everything was a
paperwork matter.
Vincent wasn't confident. He couldn't guarantee he wouldn't feel resentful seeing Shirone
registered as another family's son.
'Shirone, say something. What should your father decide? What do you truly want?'
Vincent looked at Shirone with a miserable heart.
Shirone frowned. Vincent realized his son was frustrated by his indecision.
Given the sensitivity of the matter, everyone held their tongues until Reina spoke up.
"Shirone, what do you think? It's just paperwork. You can become a noble and achieve
your dream. Of course, we'll always consider your current parents as your real ones. Nothing will
change."
Shirone's frown deepened, his eyes narrowing. Then, as if he couldn't comprehend it, he
spoke.
