Chapter 1250:
The Terrible Truth (5)
Shirone suddenly had a thought.
'Inside that small glass sphere… could countless universes be endlessly
repeating their cycle of creation and destruction?'
If even human life—its joys and sorrows—was merely a fleeting flash
trapped within that sphere...
Shirone asked, "Why do we exist?"
What does existence mean to a god without even a mind?
"Hexa." A mechanical voice echoed through the white
space.
"Because there are two, there is infinity."
At the sound of God's voice, heard for the first time, Arius froze in
place—but soon resumed his searching.
"Ultima… I must extract Ultima…"
Even though he was in the same space, he acted as though God didn't
exist.
"There is one perfect world."
To the right of God—or from the perspective of the outer world, to the
left—a circle drawn in light appeared.
"This is your world. The whole of it. No matter what is added or
taken away, it will always be correct, so even existence is defined."
God asked, "Is it not enough? You are defined, and you live.
But when the two worlds meet…"
On the opposite side, an identical circle appeared, and they connected
in the shape of a sideways 8.
"Infinity."
Nothing can be defined anymore.
"Why do you assume there must be something higher than this? In the
end, everything leads to nothing. Your obsession is merely a cognitive error—perceiving even 'nothing'
as something."
"Even so, it doesn't change anything," Shirone replied.
"You may find nothingness natural. But humans are different. We
feel each moment as reality, and we live while embracing every one of those moments. Even if it's an
illusion, we can't just disappear like bubbles for the sake of a 'result' like you."
"Human, is it?"
God asked, "Why do you think there are no humans
here?"
"What?"
"You created me. That is why I exist. I am the god of the inner
world—you are merely the shadows of humanity in this world. The Illuminati."
He had vaguely suspected it, but hearing it from God himself left him
speechless.
"Why do you deny me? Why do you reject the truth? You who refuse to
accept me are the error."
Shirone bit his lip.
'Maybe so…'
Because God is always correct.
'The sum of all causes. Therefore, to deny God is to deny
myself.'
And in that paradox, the person who came to mind was Nane.
'Are you there?'
Beyond that glass sphere, in the world of the Illuminati… had he finally
found peace?
'Are you really… satisfied with that?'
God spoke again.
"Hexa, humanity must be unified into the void, like the primordial
Illuminati or the Gaia. To realize impermanence within life and return to the Idea—this cycle of
reincarnation is eternal."
"Then what about Goffin?"
For the first time, God did not respond.
"Goffin didn't believe there was nothing. And countless Gaians
followed him. Maybe it was a mistake. If he hadn't tried to escape the photon system, Ultima might not
have been destroyed."
"It was an error."
That was the best response God could offer.
"An error? Then where is Goffin now? Did he realize it was all an
illusion and become an Illuminati? Is that why he didn't even show up when I came?"
Again, God remained silent.
"Let me guess. Goffin isn't here. Because you cast me—the one you
call 'error'—into the world."
"…You are very much alike."
It sounded like criticism, but Shirone felt oddly pleased.
"Of course. He's my father. Just as the Gaia followed Goffin, I
will follow in his footsteps. The heart he left behind will become greater than the universe you
created."
"Goffin left nothing behind," God declared.
"Hexa, the only truth is me. Because I am the final result chosen
by infinite universes. There is no outcome beyond me. Goffin has become nothing."
"No," Shirone said, placing a hand on his chest.
"He still lives—inside my heart."
"I don't see it."
The mechanical tone grew even colder.
"What is it that you claim is there? Hexa, what you're pointing to
is merely a biological organ. It is not Goffin."
There would be no compromise.
Shirone glanced at Arius, who was crawling on all fours and licking the
ground.
"Hehehe! This is the taste of Ultima!"
He wanted to believe Arius had a plan—but it was far more likely he had
simply gone mad.
Shirone turned back.
"What do you plan to do with us?"
"Nothing."
There was no emotion.
"I only maintain the world and correct errors. I am neither the
enemy of humanity nor a monster, as you think."
Shirone pondered whether that was perhaps the most perfectly flawless
state of being.
'I have to return to reality.'
If compromise was impossible, only battle remained.
'A reversal of thought alone won't bring me back. Unless I reverse time
itself…'
The first thing that came to mind was the Tachyon Code Zet had mentioned
during the Apocalypse.
'I don't know. I don't understand what it even means.'
If he understood the meaning, he could memorize even the longest code.
But Zet's chant had just sounded like a random string of syllables.
'A keyword… If I could just figure out the meaning of one word—'
Then, using his own Ultima System, he could instantly decode the entire
code.
'So in the end, I have to connect…?'
If he used Small World Resonance to link with God, he might be able to
attack the world of the Illuminati.
'But the odds are hopeless.'
The moment he connected, he'd likely become an OOPART.
"Hehehe."
A laugh broke the silence.
When Shirone turned, Arius was staggering, laughing maniacally.
"Right, it was that simple all along! You fool—no, genius! I'm a
genius!"
Suddenly, he lunged at God.
"ULTIMA!!"
"No—!"
As Shirone shouted, Arius grabbed the glass sphere and hugged it to his
chest.
"UAAAAAAH!"
Electric currents surged through him, his bones visible beneath burning
flesh, yet he burst into laughter.
"KYAHAHAHA! I've got it! I caught God!"
As the sphere dropped with a thunk, Arius lovingly licked its
surface.
"Hehehe! So this is the taste of God?"
"Arius…"
As blood poured from his shredded tongue, Shirone grimaced in
horror.
At that moment—
"Ghhh!" The electricity inside the glass sphere flared,
and Arius's torso arched like a bow.
"Ughhhhhh! Ughhhhhh!"
Blue light flared from beneath the bandages covering his eyes, and
Shirone instantly knew.
'He's becoming an OOPART…'
"What…?"
Arius, now quiet, wore a look of ecstasy.
"Wow…"
Shirone, who had witnessed it all, suddenly came up with a plan to break
through the impasse.
'This is the only chance.'
He would use Small World Resonance to indirectly analyze God's data
through Arius.
"Hang on a little longer!"
But the moment Shirone placed his hand on Arius's back, his face turned
pale.
"Ughhhhh!"
Perfect void of mind.
'I can't endure this. My heart feels like it's freezing…'
As Shirone's consciousness began to fade and blue light started to swirl
in his eyes—
'Huh?'
A woman's voice whispered.
"Illuminati."
Then, fragments of information—like splinters from the outer
world—rushed in like shrapnel.
The farthest future of humanity.
Having reached the end of their journey, humanity was about to make the
most critical decision in its history.
"We can't go any further. We have to abandon the whole and start
over."
And so, humans created God.
"Because there are two, there is infinity."
God granted humanity the concept of infinity.
"Through a mechanism that connects the outer and inner worlds, life
will endlessly reincarnate. God will manage everything. From now on, we will move beyond being organisms
and become truth itself."
Consumption and reproduction were strictly forbidden.
"What about the children?"
Hearing the woman's voice again, even in a fleeting moment, Shirone felt
a sense of unease.
'Children?'
Someone had reproduced.
"It'll be fine. Our family holds the final authority. We'll find
another way."
The identity of the child was unknown.
But the 11 senses of Ultima transcended their language system and
decoded their conversation.
'What...?'
An unbearable truth.
"Explain it to... too. They're young and curious. And be mindful of
their friends too."
Shirone tried to deny it, but the moment he heard the name, the image
that surfaced in his mind was unmistakable.
'Havitz?'
Zzzzzzzing!
A mechanical screech burst from the glass sphere, and like a severed
nerve, Small World Resonance was forcibly terminated.
"Ugh!"
As Shirone staggered back, Arius was flung backward with a loud
bang.
"Warning. Warning."
The glass sphere rapidly transmitted a voice.
"Illuminati search behavior detected. Connection terminated. Final
phase authorized for error correction."
God's pronouncement of the end—but Shirone couldn't hear anything
anymore.
"Haa… haa…"
In the fleeting moment that he glimpsed the outer world, only one truth
had been conveyed.
'Was it really...'
Just a dream?
'If all the time I've lived, all those memories, are nothing more than
shadows of this world...'
Then the concept of "me" was undeniably an error.
Sometimes we become water, sometimes air, sometimes birds, and sometimes
others.
'We are nothing more than beings endlessly reincarnating.'
"Ugh..."
Arius stood up.
Having shared the same information through Small World Resonance, his
thoughts couldn't have been much different from Shirone's.
"What are you doing?"
He spoke in a clear voice, no longer sounding like a madman, as he
picked up the glass sphere from the ground.
"Now that we've got Ultima, let's go back."
He added:
"To our world."
Shirone understood what Arius meant.
'That's right. We're not in a subordinate relationship. Our world isn't
a mere shadow of the outer world.'
Right now, God holds the result. But humans, too, can change the result
through their own cause.
'Nothing's over yet.'
Just like Goffin, it was time for Shirone to choose.
'Amy.'
His friends. Everyone he loved.
"Let's go back."
Kukukukukuku!
As Shirone closed his eyes, black specks began to appear in the
vibrating white space.
'It was the zeta function.'
That was the secret of Zet's incantation.
'For humans, infinity is a matter of thought. But for God, infinity is
as clear as the number one is to us.'
Zet had transmitted that massive code solely to describe the nature of
infinity.
'I can reverse time.'
The white space vanished completely, and the interior of the black hole
that had swallowed them came into view.
Arius held out the god in his hand.
"Take a look."
The sphere's outer membrane disappeared, leaving only blue electricity
swaying wildly.
"That's Ultima."
It didn't look much different from the Ultima Shirone had acquired at
Babel in Heaven.
"I see. But we're still inside the black hole. We're not going to
get sucked in again, are we?"
"Who knows?"
A stream of light, resembling Miracle Stream, burst violently from
Shirone's body.
The only difference was that within the radiant light, dark specks like
foreign substances were mixed in.
"That light…?"
Or should he say darkness?
"Tachyon."
The moment Shirone said it, light and dark tangled chaotically and burst
into flames.
'A signal born from nothing.'
A superluminal particle that only God could wield, capable of
controlling cause and effect through imaginary time.
