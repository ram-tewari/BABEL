Chapter 545:
Grand Rite (1)
Having returned to the northwestern settlement, Shirone summoned Arbiter
Bebeto.
Since coming back from the Forbidden Sanctuary, Shirone seemed uneasy,
and Yo hovered nervously at his side, uncertain what to do.
Code twisted through the temple, and Bebeto appeared.
"Arbiter, you summoned me?"
"Prepare to leave the settlement."
The abrupt statement, delivered without context, made both Bebeto and Yo
stare at Shirone in surprise.
"You're leaving the settlement? Then where will you
go?"
"I'm not going anywhere."
Shirone spoke with certainty.
"I'm going to change this world. A world without war or hunger,
where everyone lives together in harmony."
Bebeto looked troubled.
When Shirone proposed alliances with other settlements, he had gone
along with it. But this? This wasn't something a sane person would say.
"What on earth happened?"
Bebeto asked Yo, but it was Shirone who answered.
"It's not time to explain yet. Once those sent to propose alliances
return—"
"The Guardian has arrived!"
Before Shirone could finish, the sentry cried out.
As Shirone rose from his seat and turned toward the entrance, the doors
swung open and a group entered, led by a figure flapping bat-like wings—Ekser.
"Arbiter, as you instructed, we've formed alliances with the
eastern and southern settlements. These are the Guardians of each."
It was what Shirone had hoped for, but the speed and ease of the
accomplishment felt unnatural.
Given that these people detest the idea of having two Suns in one
settlement, wariness toward the alliance proposal should've been expected.
"What happened?"
His single question carried many.
Ekser finally looked a little sheepish.
"Well, actually…"
"It was my doing."
Parting the Guardians, a woman in a green dress stepped
forward—Shura.
"I've already removed the Arbiters of the eastern and southern
settlements. Meaning, Shirone, you're the only Sun left in this world."
Shirone instinctively took a step back.
'Can no one else feel it?'
The snake-like aura rising from this woman.
It wasn't visible to the eye, yet a massive serpent seemed to coil
around her, flicking its tongue.
It must be a specific frequency only the Prajnas could perceive.
"Who are you?"
"I'm Shura. The one who's going to make you a god, at Ra's
request."
"Ra's request?"
"Well, it seemed like someone might try to interfere. You know who
I mean, don't you?"
Shirone recalled Miro's group.
"You're saying you're going to hurt them?"
"No, just the opposite. They'll be the ones coming to hurt us. And
I'll do everything I can to stop that."
After reaching the state of Prajna through Soulworld Manifestation,
Shirone finally realized how powerful Miro truly was.
If she so chose, she could easily erase this entire incident from
existence.
'But… Miro said it was all for my sake.'
Sitting wearily on the throne, Shirone asked,
"What if this world is a lie? What do you think of
that?"
Shura approached him quietly and leaned in with a warm smile.
She gently tapped his crown with her snake-like finger and whispered in
his ear.
"There is no such thing as truth in this world, Shirone. The only
truth is what you believe."
"……"
Shirone remained deep in thought for a long moment before lifting his
head.
"Then… what do we do now?"
"We begin the ritual to make you the Sun God."
As Shura placed a hand on Shirone's shoulder and looked back at the
Guardians, their shoulders flinched.
When she first arrived, calling herself Ra's wife and Shirone's mother,
they thought her a lunatic.
But after witnessing her powers firsthand, doubt became
impossible.
"Shall we change locations?"
Shura closed her eyes, and the landscape around them began to unravel
into lines of code.
Bebeto, a fellow Coder, trembled.
'The language of gods itself.'
Shura truly was a god in this world.
"Where are we?"
The Guardians of the three settlements looked around in
confusion.
The scenery had changed in an instant. They were now standing in the
middle of a desert beyond the city.
"Like it, Shirone? This is where you'll become a god."
"Why choose this place?"
"Even I can't fully protect you from Miro in the city. And
besides—don't you think it's more dramatic this way?"
"What kind of grandeur is there in a desert—"
Shirone trailed off and stared at the sky.
Shura raised her arms toward the sun and chanted. From the clouds of
tangled code, dozens of massive rectangular stones began falling, each weighing tens of tons.
What would take years for all the settlement's inhabitants to build was
completed in moments.
Before the colossal pyramid that soared hundreds of meters high, even
Shirone couldn't help but feel a twinge of fear.
'Who is this woman, really?'
Shura pointed at the pyramid.
"This structure will protect you from any threat. All my abilities
are embedded within it."
It would take at least this level of power to withstand Miro's
avatar.
She didn't say it aloud, but the pyramid also served another purpose: to
analyze the code generated by the upcoming fusion between Shirone and Ra.
"So that's why the desert. But you can't merge with Ra out here,
can you?"
"We'll just have to be patient. Ra will pull all the city's Mucus
out here. It should all arrive by noon tomorrow."
"I see."
Shirone looked up at the peak of the pyramid.
'By tomorrow… up there…'
He would save a ruined world.
'Still… something feels off.'
Shura bit her nail, deep in thought.
Stopping Miro wouldn't be enough. Miro's companions would surely try to
interfere as well.
'No choice. I hate asking for help, but…'
Suddenly frowning, she turned her gaze elsewhere.
"What the hell is that monkey doing?"
"Yaho! It's a hunt!"
Having given up on entering the Forbidden Sanctuary, the Undergrounder
combat unit let out a cheer as they spotted another human.
"She looks delicious!"
Paknyeo stared straight ahead, watching the motorcycle unit sweep across
her line of sight.
Behind the encirclement, Taejang sat in a jeep, arms crossed, wearing a
vicious scowl.
The motorcycles screeched to a halt in front of Paknyeo and
simultaneously raised their guns.
"Too hungry to hear your last words."
Paknyeo analyzed the emotions sinking into her chest.
'Hunger. Mockery. Death. Final sentiment.'
The moment her thought ended, thunderous gunfire erupted from the
barrels, accompanied by bursts of fire.
Then the muzzles of the guns trembled and slowly pointed toward the
sky.
"……"
Taejang blankly watched as his headless subordinates convulsed, their
rifles firing wildly into the air.
Moments later, the motorcycles toppled sideways, and Paknyeo stepped
onto the hood of the jeep.
"Not bad. What are you?"
'Recognition of me. Question of identity. Desire to understand.'
He was asking who she was.
Though she had never learned the language of the Undergrounders, the
meaning of Taejang's words transmitted clearly through his entire presence.
Paknyeo. Sura Yaksha, age 11,200.
Unlike most immortals who pursued something beyond humanity, her spirit
had instead burrowed deeper into the animal realm.
After thousands of years, she could no longer distinguish between what
made up her "self" and the natural world.
Through that process, she developed a sixth sense that surpassed simple
sensitivity—what could only be called a "grotesque sense," capable of predicting phenomena that defied
logic with nearly perfect accuracy.
"I am your queen."
Taejang's expression twisted at Paknyeo's words.
He, too, bore the genes of the wild and was acutely sensitive to any
signal that tried to dominate him.
"How dare you look down on me!"
Taejang sprang like a coiled spring, and Paknyeo backed off to create
distance.
As the surrounding Undergrounders opened fire in a crossfire, Paknyeo
landed and crouched low, wrinkling her nose.
"Khrrrrr!"
Axing-Nirvana E-Engine (Eight-Cylinder Nirvana Drive).
With a bang, her body swept through the surroundings.
Like blades of wind, her afterimages sliced through the Undergrounders
as she passed, their bodies cleanly slashed. Taejang roared like a beast and hoisted the jeep high above
his head.
"Eat this!"
It was brute strength at its peak, but to Paknyeo, the jeep's motion
seemed slower the longer she watched.
'Bend.'
Her Axing-Nirvana E-Engine used the power of Nirvana to push the
cylinders of Law.
The engine could arrange up to eight cylinders, each loaded with a
single action.
A straight-8 mechanism—eight processes per cycle.
The more the Nirvana Drive accelerated, the more powerful the Nirvana
energy became, the faster the cycle spun, and there was no upper limit.
'Accelerate!'
As she accelerated her Nirvana Drive, Paknyeo swung her blade toward the
falling jeep.
Having loaded her eight laws with eight directional slashes, her cycle
had now exceeded 80 revolutions—so fast that even Taejang, with his incredible reflexes, could barely
see her movements.
The steel body of the jeep shattered like stars in the sky, followed by
an endless barrage of slashes.
By the time Paknyeo had passed through the jeep, it had already been
disassembled like spare parts, torn into thousands of pieces rolling across the ground.
"Hoooohhh…"
Heat rose from her body, making her clothes flutter—she was the very
image of a Yaksha.
"Ah…"
Taejang stared at Paknyeo with eyes wide in shock.
Even if they couldn't communicate through words, the instincts of the
wild passed a singular emotion through his genes:
"Uwoooooohhh! Uwooooooohhh!"
A king! A king to rule over this jungle city had appeared!
Taejang let out a wild cry and strode forward, bowing his massive body
before Paknyeo.
"My queen, please rule over me."
Knowing that in the wild, power was everything, Paknyeo naturally became
their leader and issued a command.
"Take me to the one who used to be your king."
Taejang still couldn't understand her words, but instinct guided him,
showing him exactly where to go.
By the time the sun was setting, Miro's group finally discovered the
site where Shura would conduct the ceremony.
With Rian, the swordsman, holding position on the ground, the three
mages floated into the sky and looked down at the massive pyramid standing in the desert.
"That structure wasn't on any map. This must be the place."
"Yeah. That was fast."
"Are we too late?"
"There's still time. The mucous hasn't arrived yet—it won't reach the
site until noon tomorrow."
All of the city's mucous was slowly slithering across the sands toward
the pyramid.
Even at thousands of times the normal expansion rate, the distance was
still considerable.
As night fell, Miro's eyes glinted with magic light.
She was using a magnification spell that bent light, allowing her to see
far-off distances.
"Hm…"
The bluish, dome-shaped shield surrounding the pyramid was clearly a
law-based barrier.
"No breaking through that."
"Even for you, Auntie?"
Fermi had made a light jab, but Miro admitted it without
resistance.
"Maybe in the real world, but not here…"
"No other way?"
"The only choice is to go inside. It's a barrier meant to block external
laws. Once we're in, it'll be useless."
"What if there's internal protection too? Like anti-magic or something
that disrupts casting?"
This time, Miro couldn't hold back her irritation.
"Are you kidding? That barrier's meant to block me. No one would waste
the effort on extra defenses beyond that."
"I see."
If it wasn't enough to withstand Miro's full-powered assault, there was
no point in setting up a barrier in the first place.
Only someone like Shura, the strongest human Lawbringer, could pull off
something like that.
"Either way, this is getting complicated. Looks like she's united the
settlements. Can we even reach the pyramid past all the mutants?"
"Hold on. Something's coming."
From across the desert, hundreds of headlights lit up the
horizon.
Undergrounders were crossing the sands with various vehicles, a sight
that caught Miro's eye.
"So the Undergrounders have joined in too."
Marsha spoke up.
"To disable the barrier, we have to break in from the inside. Standing
in our way: a Council of Ten executive, the Sun Children's guardians, the Undergrounders, and the
mucous. Think we can do it?"
Miro shook her head.
"No. Prajna's power cancels out Prajna's power. As long as Shura's
active, leaving it to me isn't the best idea."
"Then what? Do we have any options?"
"Do we?"
Miro smiled and looked down at the ground.
"We have a Yaksha."
Everyone's eyes turned to Rian.
Grounded and unable to fly, he was sitting on the floor, leaning on his
sword, exhausted.
"…I'm starving."
