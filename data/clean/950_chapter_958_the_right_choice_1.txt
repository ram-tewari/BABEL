Chapter 958:
The Right Choice (1)
The commanders of the allied forces were masters in their fields, but
that alone couldn't secure victory.
The demons were strong not only because of their physical power but also
due to their different bodily organs, constitution, and mental abilities compared to humans.
They could reflect physical force, manipulate elements, or destroy minds
like Igor, the Lord of Terror.
The reason why the former First Legion Commander, the Great Swordmaster
Fidero, died was likely due to extreme mismatches in attributes.
In the end, anyone could die in the battle of the flower field.
"Advance! Advance!"
Despite the shouts, the allied forces were continuously pushed
back.
And finally,
"Ugh!"
The back of a soldier fighting at the front collided with the shield of
a soldier at the rear.
With their lives on the line, the anger in their eyes quickly turned to
despair.
"Already..."
It was the end of the flower field.
When they finally widened their view, there was nothing left standing
among the three hundred giant structures.
'We were pushed back this far?'
The flower field, planted with 300 giant structures, was vast, spanning
23 kilometers in a straight line.
Lupist gritted his teeth.
'Damn it...'
Changing the course of the demon army was nothing like turning the head
of a running horse.
The allied forces were always surrounded by demons and had to generate
rotational force within that.
In terms of power difference, it was like moving water currents below
the surface to rotate an entire lake.
'Even so...'
The allied forces had managed to turn the demon army's course by 14
degrees compared to when they first entered.
'According to the Holy War's predictions, if this much inertia is
applied, they should naturally change direction.'
This strategy was possible because the demons' behavior was different
from that of human armies.
'Humans use war as a means to achieve a purpose...'
For demons, war itself was the purpose.
They simply charged towards places where they could torment
humans.
'Therefore, the fact that inertia isn't working means human-like will is
at play.'
This was also related to why the legion commanders were not included in
the demon army.
Lupist unfolded a map in his mind.
'If they continue straight... it's still not enough.'
No matter how large the army, they would pass by Bashka.
'We have to do something here.'
Lupist shouted.
"Everyone!"
But seeing the soldiers' faces, Lupist couldn't bring himself to say
"charge."
Despair was evident in everyone's eyes.
"The flower field has been occupied."
The power of the flower field was undeniable, and the soldiers had
relied on it psychologically.
"It's over. We can't win now."
Seeing the soldiers who had lost their will to fight, Lupist couldn't
push them any further.
It wasn't because he was soft-hearted.
'They fought really well. They couldn't have done better.'
Once he understood this, even the cold-hearted Lupist couldn't do
anything.
"Everyone, retreat."
The unexpected order shocked the soldiers.
"Commander."
"Retreat. I'll handle this. Go back to Bashka and plan for the
future. You'll be needed."
A silence fell.
"We're sorry, Commander."
And as if on cue, everyone lit up their eyes and pointed their weapons
forward.
As the demons surrounding them chuckled, Lupist turned his head.
"What are you doing?"
"Well, we do want to live, but..."
The centurion laughed.
"We don't feel like running away. Our hearts aren't
moving."
Having fought together, no more words were needed.
"This can't be right, can it?"
That one sentence was enough, and Lupist glared at the enemy with the
most terrifying look.
"...That's right."
The allied forces, focusing their last remaining will, prepared to
charge.
"Everyone!"
Now he could say it.
"Charge!"
As if their souls were pulling them, the allied forces advanced,
forgetting how to run.
"Hahaha! The final feast!"
As the demons swarmed in, imagining the humans they would soon devour,
their attention was suddenly drawn to the sky behind the allied forces.
"Huh?"
Without anyone saying a word, their gazes turned to the sky behind the
allied forces.
"What, what is that?"
A towering avatar of Avalokiteshvara, reaching endlessly into the sky,
looked down at them benevolently.
"Prajnaparamita."
The hands of the avatar, clasped together, suddenly opened.
"Paradise Palm."
In an instant, countless afterimages filled the sky, spreading like a
fan.
The two palms, circling the ends of the world, collided where the demons
were.
Countless afterimages converged into a single point, and all the impact
exploded at once.
"Argh!"
The seemingly impenetrable wall of demons was torn apart without limit,
and Lupist shuddered.
'Cognitive dissonance.'
Every process was vivid in his mind, but looking back, it was
astonishingly instantaneous.
A path opened from the rear of the allied forces, and the formation
split in two.
"Why are you so surprised? Never seen a pretty woman
before?"
Lupist realized.
'Yes, we still have that.'
An ambiguous figure to call human, the strongest defender of humanity
and the ultimate good of the era.
"Adrias Miro."
Enjoying the attention of the allied forces, Miro shrugged as she
approached Lupist.
"If you're interested, contact me. I'm currently
single."
As Lupist watched, Dante approached and pointed.
"You're too late. The other leaders of Zion are already carrying
out operations elsewhere."
"You brat!"
Before he finished speaking, Miro placed her fist on Dante's cheek and
turned it sharply.
"Ouch!"
It didn't hurt, but the sheer absurdity of it left him stunned.
"How dare you compare me to those idiots? Don't you know Adrias
Miro? I'm a busy woman."
Dante's eyes lit up.
"It was your mission. And they risked their lives on difficult
tasks."
"Difficult tasks? No, you should call them impossible
tasks."
"...What do you mean?"
"Hehe. Kid, you act smart, but listen carefully. This sister will
tell you."
Miro placed her hand on Dante's head and smiled.
"No matter what you think, the world is much deeper and more
complex. Everything I do has a profound meaning."
Lupist interrupted.
"I'd like to hear about that profound meaning you
know."
"It means Satan isn't here."
Dante asked.
"Because Zion's elite diverted him?"
"No. He wouldn't have come anyway. The world's operation is beyond
human reasoning. You can't calculate it with thought. You need to understand the essence."
Lupist asked.
"What is Satan's essence?"
"The most cowardly being in the universe. He never gets involved in

this. Satan always gets what he wants. If you don't act with that in mind, you'll be swept away by the
laws of this world."
"So you sent your comrades to their deaths? They could all be
dead."
Miro was unfazed.
"Don't focus on humans, look at the whole. I didn't send just
anyone. To catch Satan, you have to meticulously handle the laws first. Zion's elite went to do
that."
Dante agreed.
Only with Miro's perspective, observing the world from the highest
point, could they trap Satan.
Seeing Dante's changed expression, Miro was satisfied.
"Of course, it's you who will change the world. If you hadn't
fought so fiercely, could I have arrived? In that sense, death is also life. All lives come together to
create this situation."
That is the law.
"...So what do we do now?"
"What do you mean?"
Miro stepped forward.
"We open a new future."
Kiaaaa!
From beyond the demon army, Kaidra flew in, carrying Zulu, Gangnan, and
Arius.

'He's taciturn, so the explanation should be brief.'

The 12 apostles would fight according to Shirone's will.
