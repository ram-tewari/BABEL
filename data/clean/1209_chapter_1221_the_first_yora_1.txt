Chapter 1221:
The First Yora (1)
"Cain?" Upon hearing Shirone's words, Iruki and Nade scrutinized
the king's face. He appeared middle-aged, yet he didn't feel old—likely because of the mischievous
glint in his eyes.
'No, that's not the issue here.'
If he was the first murderer of humankind, then he would've lived eons
before their time.
"How is he still alive?"
"Deep Time," Shirone said. "Time in Melkidu flows differently from
the real world. This place was created by delving into reality through the mind."
It wasn't something that could be done in a single step.
"From the real world to the Outer Track, from the Outer Track to the
Inner Track, then to the royal castle, to the Grand Hall, to the throne... He's created new worlds like
that, delaying time along the way."
Pena asked, "Is that even possible? You mean he can live
forever?"
"Not forever."
Even by delving endlessly into Deep Time, an endpoint still exists when
faced with the concept of infinity. What Shirone first realized:
'The 19,000th world of Labyrinth Andre.'
It was the reason the Utopia Project, started in pursuit of immortality,
had failed.
'The Deep Time program, Argo, couldn't compute infinity—it malfunctioned
instead.'
It had abandoned thought and began farming humans like livestock.
"Right," Shirone said, "Deep Time can't beat infinity."
Cain spoke. "But that only applies if infinity exists. Do you
really think this universe will last forever? From what I know, it doesn't have much time left."
By universal law, only five hours remained. Shirone asked, "Were
you waiting for this?"
From the beginning of mankind, to its very end.
"It's been a long and tedious journey. Can you even imagine how many
stages of Deep Time I had to go through? My world is nothing more than this chair. The moment I take a
single step out, I enter a higher world. A layer closer to reality."
Time would flow faster there.
"I've waited like that. In the deepest part of Melkidu—the place I
created—for this day to come."
"For what?"
"You want to reach the Core, don't you?" Cain gestured. "Release
him."
The knights sheathed their swords and stepped back. Curtis rubbed his
neck and muttered, "…I'm sorry."
There was a mountain of things Shirone wanted to ask him, but for now,
getting to the Core was the priority. Shirone approached Cain.
"How do we get a permit to enter the Core?"
"You already know, don't you? You have to persuade me."
"The rules?"
"Haha! This feels a bit awkward. Normally I'd pretend to be a non-player
character and all… My personality is 'Chaos.' That means the rules are mine to make. You don't need to
pay Crime Points. But you get only one chance. If you lose here, find another way."
Cain gave them a moment to think, then asked again, "Who's going
to try?"
After exchanging glances with his friends, Shirone turned back.
"I'll do it."
"A wise choice."
The scenery changed, and the vast phase space for dice duels stretched
endlessly.
Floating in midair, Cain looked down at Shirone's team. "The rules
are simple. Each of us rolls one die at the same time. If the number you roll matches mine, you win. Any
other result means you lose."
Iruki's expression grew tense. "Simultaneously…"
Shirone could manipulate the outcome with quantum collapse.
'But not this time. Not even Cain knows what number he's going to
roll.'
It was true chaos.
As Shirone watched with a displeased expression, Cain smirked and
said, "What's wrong, great persuader of half of humanity? I thought Yahweh was someone who had
mastered the heart?"
There was only one chance.
"Fine."
When Shirone responded without hesitation, Eden turned to him,
surprised. 'What are you thinking?' 'I'll win anyway,' Shirone thought confidently.
'In this place, Incarnation techniques work. That means Hand of God
will, too.'
All he had to do was see Cain's roll first, then use the Hand of God to
change his own result.
"Shall we begin?"
Cain rolled his 7-sided die, and Shirone snatched the die floating in
front of him.
A system message rang out. –For this round only, the countdown
begins. Countdown duration: 60 seconds. Judgment window: final 1 second. 60, 59…
As the timer ticked down, Shirone and Cain didn't take their eyes off
each other.
–3 seconds, 2 seconds, 1…
Both dice flew into the air at once.
'Now.'
Shirone focused intently on the dice rolling before him. Cain's
die stopped at 4. Shirone's die rolled to 3.
'Hand of God.'
The divine hand snatched Shirone's die and threw it again.
The original result vanished, and a new event unfolded before everyone's
eyes.
'There!'
The moment Shirone felt certain of victory, the die stopped.
"…Huh?"
Nade, staring blankly at the die showing 3, murmured, "…You
lost?"
It was undeniable. But those who knew of Shirone's abilities couldn't
understand how.
Shirone slowly lifted his head.
Cain was wearing an enigmatic smile as the phase space began to
collapse.
With the Grand Hall coming back into view, Shirone's group wore uneasy
expressions.
Cain spoke. "Looks like I won."
Shirone wasn't foolish enough to assume he was bound to win.
"How did you do it?"
"When you live through the early ages of human history, you hear many
stories. Was that the power of the Gaia, passed down only through myth? The moment I became certain the
die would land on four, that certainty became law. And you—you're capable of changing it. Truly... a
power worthy of being called a miracle."
"You're saying you weren't certain?"
Unless his eyes had been closed, controlling his thoughts to that extent
meant he was already at Yahweh's level.
Cain laughed.
"You give me too much credit. I'm just the first murderer. A state like
that? Impossible for me."
"Then…"
Only one hypothesis remained for Shirone.
"Yeah, it was a cheat."
—Warning. Main system data has been corrupted by an unidentified code.
Initiating quick recovery. A critical system error may occur.
"What the...?"
Cries echoed from beyond the palace walls.
"It was a global message?"
Cain said, "I altered the system parameters. Well, I'm sure some users
will benefit, and some will suffer."
In other words, total chaos. Iruki asked,
"You really didn't want us to reach the Core that badly? What's
there?"
"No."
Cain rose from his throne.
"I will send you to the Core. The reason I tampered with the system was
to uphold my own sense of justice."
"What justice?"
"The efficiency of evil."
Cain stepped out of Deep Time.
"In Melkidu, countless users fight with everything they have to reach
the Core. That's how I designed it. They treat the system as a divine, immutable law. But…"
Is the system truly perfect?
"Truth is, rules mean nothing. One changed parameter and everything
shifts. Even your precious heart, Yahweh. You desperately wished for a 4 on the die, but you got a
3."
The system dominates the mind.
"So you killed for that?" "You broke the rules just to get what
you wanted?"
For a moment, sorrow flickered in Cain's eyes—but soon returned to its
usual playful glint.
"Yeah, I'm evil. Or more precisely, I'm a scholar of evil. Why shouldn't
I kill? If breaking the rules gets you what you want faster and easier, what's the problem? Just
look—you came through the backdoor, didn't you? It was convenient."
Cain argued his case.
"Humans must eat. They must reproduce. To eat, we kill other species,
and among our own, we compete. That's who we are. Is that so shameful? So we make strange rules and say
we're good, we're righteous—hiding behind pretty words. But is there any good that doesn't consume? Is
there any good that doesn't reproduce? That's just emptiness. Yahweh, the love you speak of cannot exist
without evil. Evil must be part of it."
"Maybe so."
Shirone didn't deny it.
"I don't believe the system is perfect, either. But imperfection doesn't
mean it's wrong. It just means we have to seek better ways."
"Better ways to where?"
Cain scoffed.
"To what destination? Look behind you. We're animals. Unless we become
machines, humans will keep killing. They'll hurt rivals. Someone gains, someone loses. One finds love,
another is abandoned. Jealousy, greed, desire—there's no system that can contain all of it."
And if there were, that would be Ultima.
"Then why did you create Melkidu?"
Cain fell silent.
"If you were that confident, you could've just lived on as a murderer.
Why go out of your way to prove you were right? Shall I tell you? It's because you already know."
Shirone said,
"That no matter how you try to justify it—there is no sanctuary for
evil."
A long silence followed...
"…Heh."
Cain tilted his head back and burst out laughing.
"Ha! Wait, seriously? You heard that? Come to think of it, I think I did
say something like that. It was so long ago, it's all a blur."
"You remember," Shirone said. "If you didn't, there's no way you
would've endured the endless years here."
"Now I understand—why you made Melkidu. It wasn't to justify evil. It
was the opposite."
"What nonsense are you spewing?"
Cain's smile vanished without a trace.
"You want to erase it. What you did. That one mistake that disappointed
the ones you loved. The memory of losing everything."
A sob rang out.
"Ugh…"
Curtis collapsed to his knees and began to speak in a trembling
voice.
"I thought about dying. I didn't come here because I was afraid of
death. I just… I couldn't bear the thought of facing my daughter in the afterlife. I wished it had never
happened…"
From his tears, Pena sensed the truth.
So that's what it was. You were a mess too, weren't you...?
Shirone turned to Cain.
"I learned about you through Omega's records. And about what your
mother—Mitochondrial Eve—did."
Cain said nothing.
"If you're a scholar of evil, it means you're capable of seeing it
critically. Let us go to the Core. At the very least, don't you want to see for yourself the one thing
you've waited an entire human history to find?"
It felt like Cain would stay silent forever...
"…And the Lord said…"
Cain murmured,
"Agrippa, why do you weep?"
Agrippa replied: Because the woman I love has married my
brother.
It was Ecclesiastes, Chapter 3.
O lost lamb, cast away your jealousy and resentment. Slay your brother
and take his wife.
As he reached the final verse...
I shall be with you. You need not pay a price to obtain what you desire.
Take whatever you wish. From the sanctuary of murderers…
Cain slowly lifted his gaze and spoke:
"…Melkidu."
At that moment, the Grand Hall shattered like glass, and a violent wind
surged forth.
Beyond the broken scenery was pure darkness—yet it felt like they were
moving at the speed of light.
"What is this?"
Then finally, the darkness lifted, revealing a sunlit, dense
forest…
And the Core of Melkidu revealed itself.
