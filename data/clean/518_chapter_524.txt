Chapter 524:
"Shirone. Of course I know him."
Miro felt an eerie chill watching the brief flash of tenderness in
Uorin's eyes.
Biologically speaking, a mother's gentleness arises from a sense of
ownership over the child.
Uorin's expression, in that sense, was the pinnacle of a possessiveness
that had transcended desire.
She skillfully deflected, hiding her emotions.
"You mean the fact that he's in a non-functional state."
Information retrieved by Cage Team B was already spreading in fragments
across the world under the label of intelligence.
And the first place that intelligence would reach… was none other than
the Holy War.
Miro smacked Shirone lightly on the forehead.
"As you can see, he's unconscious. Completely out of it. I figured if
anyone could wake him up, it'd be you."
Uorin crossed her legs, clearly reluctant.
"And why would I do that? Now that you've returned, Shirone's role is
finished. He's not someone important enough to go out of my way for."
Miro smirked.
"Oh, don't be like that. We're both professionals here. His role might
be over, but the Armand is still a factor, isn't it? You need Shirone's power."
"Needing something and helping someone are two very different things. If
I really wanted, I could take Shirone anytime."
"Could you really? Even you won't be able to break Armand's chastity
lock that easily. This thing doesn't come off—ever."
Until the price for the Valhalla Action was fully paid, Armand wouldn't
be removed from Shirone.
Seeing Miro's confident response, Uorin realized this was a negotiable
matter and softened her stance.
"Fine. What's the issue? If it's not worth the effort, I'll back off.
All I want is Shirone, eventually."
"Work out your desires however you want, Your Majesty. Right now,
Shirone is paying the price for the Valhalla Action."
"Valhalla Action?"
Miro relayed the details she'd heard from Gaold's group regarding
Shirone's condition.
Fortunately, Plu had known the mechanics of the Valhalla Action in
detail.
Uorin nodded as she listened.
"I see. That explains your boldness. The power of an angel, huh? So
Shirone can use two abilities now?"
"The Valhalla Action is equipped to Armand. Since he's merged with it, I
suppose it doesn't matter—but for you, it changes things."
Miro had gifted Armand to Shirone not just out of debt, but also as a
calculated move to nurture his power and eventually inherit the ability in full.
Which was why it was tragic that one of the highest-tier angelic powers,
even among the Valkyries, had ended up sealed in a mere weapon.
Uorin, finally letting go of her hesitation, asked,
"So, how much does he have to pay, exactly?"
Arius answered,
"Based on my dive investigation, it appears to be around 127
years."
"Far beyond a human's lifespan."
Since Shirone was nineteen, it meant he wouldn't wake up until he was
146.
"Exactly. By then, everything will already be over. But Ikael said that
in itself is the key to bringing Shirone back."
Miro studied Uorin's reaction carefully.
If they were thinking the same thing, she'd remember something.
"Hm… I see."
Uorin leaned back, raising three fingers.
"There are probably three main ways to bring Shirone back."
"Oh?"
More than expected.
"The fact that the Valhalla Action activated means causality was
fulfilled. But Shirone paid far more than his credit limit. That's the key point."
Arius interjected.
"So something was paid that shouldn't have been possible to pay."
"Exactly. That kind of event defies logic, which makes it open to
analysis. My guess is that the Valhalla Action took something from Shirone as collateral."
"Collateral?"
"When I first heard how the Valhalla Action worked, my first thought
was: That's unfair. Sure, maybe the calculation included interest on future uncertainty, but lending 127
years without collateral doesn't make sense. So I don't think it's a matter of lifespan."
Arius said,
"It's time, then."
"Right. As long as he's human, his lifespan can't be extended. But if
the collateral was time, that's another story. Something like…"
Uorin pointed at Miro.
"Something like you."
Miro bit her lip.
"Twenty years ago—nineteen, to be exact—you haven't aged a day since
then. Why do you think that is?"
"Who knows? Maybe I just have a baby face."
She said it like a joke, but her thoughts were far from light.
Uorin was pinpointing the exact possibility that had been circling in
Miro's head ever since she'd heard Ikael's hint.
"Of course, ascetics like you might slow your biological processes
through spiritual training. But you didn't eat or sleep for twenty years inside the dimensional wall.
How's that even possible?"
Because she had known it would happen.
The problem was, no one in the world knew how she had known.
"Maclaine Goffin."
Uorin spoke the name.
"Miro, even before you, the dimensional wall existed. It stopped
Heaven's forces. After that, Goffin disappeared, and you were chosen. You're his successor. You can't
remember him because he's been erased, but you definitely met him."
Miro's gaze shifted to Shirone.
"And something Goffin passed on to me… might have been passed on to
Shirone too?"
Uorin lifted her head, recalling something.
"During the Third Reset—the Great Purification—I had one lingering
question. Why did Ikael pass Ataraxia to Shirone? And why did the avatar that manifested in Heaven carry
angelic wings? And finally…"
She turned a piercing gaze toward Arius.
"Why was Goffin's security protocol embedded in Shirone's primal
psyche?"
"…How do you know that?"
Arius had been the only person to dive into Shirone's depths.
And aside from Miro, whom he followed with unwavering loyalty, he had
never revealed that to anyone.
Miro frowned slightly.
"You found out after the Reset, didn't you?"
"Yes. During the Great Purification, I encountered Arius again in Kazra.
After failing to steal Ataraxia, he was chased by Walker and escaped via Apoptosis. I extracted some
information back then. You remember—the sleep spell you used on Shirone?"
Masters of sleep magic could slice consciousness so precisely that the
subject wouldn't even notice as their memories were rewritten.
As Arius recalled that moment, he swallowed, realizing something.
If she had witnessed the scene of him placing the psychic door inside
Shirone, then there was nothing she didn't know.
Miro narrowed her eyes at Uorin.
"This is why you're so infuriating, Teraje."
In the world connected to Goffin's name, there were only a handful of
people like Miro and Shirone.
The real problem, however, was that Uorin was likely one of them
too.
She knows. I'm sure of it.
Having lived through three iterations of human history, there was no way
Teraje didn't have a sense of déjà vu when it came to Goffin.
She might even be the only person alive who still remembers his
face.
But—
She'll never tell us.
That truth belonged to Teraje alone.
Until she opened her mouth herself, there was no way to uncover the
truth.
Miro gave up and redirected her focus back to Shirone's
situation.
"Enough with the commentary. Just tell me—the three ways to bring
Shirone back."
"The first method is very simple and easy."
Uorin raised a single finger.
"Kill him."
"Hm."
Miro nodded as if she understood.
"If you're convinced there's collateral, killing Shirone is the easiest
way. Something will definitely happen. And if we're lucky, maybe he'll even come back to life."
Miro, resting her chin on her hand, lifted her head in surprise.
"Has that ever actually happened?"
"In all of human history—about three times."
"Extremely rare, then."
"But still the simplest option. Even if he dies, it's not the end of the
world, is it? I'd be a little disappointed, sure, but when you weigh that against the effort it would
take to wake him… not such a big loss."
"True enough."
It had the advantage of being the fastest possible resolution. Killing
Shirone and waiting to see what happened wasn't the worst option.
"Alright. Let's hear the second one too. I can decide later."
"The second method is to have Shirone pay the debt himself. This is also
related to the collateral."
"I see. But how would he choose anything in a moratorium state?"
"There would need to be a separate method for that. But if the second
method is even possible, the third one might be better."
"And the third?"
"Have someone else pay the debt on his behalf."
"Oho…"
"The causality of the Valhalla Action belongs to Shirone. But the
collateral that connects that causality may not. Which means…"
The two women said it at the same time:
"It can be transferred to someone else."
Uorin rested her chin in her hand and looked at the wall.
"Regulation Exception. An Object. We might need both. An ability that
can bypass moratorium. And one that can extract and transfer magic. If we can secure those two
functions, we might be able to wake Shirone."
Miro pursed her lips, clearly thinking of someone.
"Hmm… Regulation Exception, huh?"
"You seem to have someone in mind."
"How about you just look into it for me?"
"I'll cooperate, but don't expect much. And really—if you're Adrias
Miro, the answer shouldn't be far."
At Uorin's cryptic retort, Miro clicked her tongue, realizing Uorin
already had everything figured out.
"You're so stingy."
"Bring me the collateral. I'll find someone willing to take on the
debt."
"It's 127 years. Think you can manage?"
"If we split it between ten archmages, we can clear it within a year. If
that's not enough, hire twenty."
Miro let out a small laugh.
"You're awfully generous all of a sudden. Weren't you just saying
killing him was the simplest option?"
"It still is. Hiring twenty archmages is one thing, but you'd
essentially be throwing their talents away. No matter how much I want Shirone, I don't think he's worth
that much."
"Really? Then why?"
Uorin shrugged casually.
"He's cute. I like this type."
Miro's eyes curved with exaggerated sweetness.
Liar.
She already had a good idea why Uorin chose the third method despite
having easier options.
To find out what Shirone's collateral was.
If it was related to Goffin, then even twenty archmages would be a cheap
price to pay.
A treasure chest that hasn't been opened yet, huh…
Having sorted out her thoughts, Miro stood up from her seat.
Arius quickly wrapped Shirone in the black cloth and lifted him onto his
back.
Time was short—they couldn't afford to waste it.
"Then I'll handle the extraction. You prepare for the transfer."
"If you need anything, just call. As long as it's part of the contract,
I'll help with whatever you need."
"Mm. I'll take you up on that."
Miro waved cheerfully as she left the room, with Arius following close
behind, carrying Shirone.
Outside the door, the leader of the Wind Guards glanced around the room
briefly, confirmed his lady's safety, and quietly shut the door.
At that moment, the smile on Uorin's face vanished in an instant,
replaced by a chilling coldness.
"Debts are best when they're piled high."
