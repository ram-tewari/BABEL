Final Chapter (6)
Year 6, September 27.
It had already been six years since humanity freed itself from the laws
of God.
The fact that there had been no official wars since the beginning of
this new era was a miracle that would remain in human history.
Experts called it an obsession with purity.
"Who will commit the first crime? Which country will produce the
first criminal? That's the issue. No one wants to be remembered as the first villain."
A reporter asked:
"So you're saying humanity hasn't matured—people just don't want to
break the illusion of perfection?"
"Humans are creatures of desire. It's possible crimes are already
happening in places we can't see. Some countries may even be suppressing information. Still, I don't
think this will last much longer. Once the first official act of evil appears, the second and third will
come quickly."
Three years ago, the first official murder occurred.
The perpetrator was a 32-year-old lawyer named Reaper. What he said to
the judge in court was simple:
"I wanted to be the first."
Humans are strange.
Just as the experts predicted, crimes began appearing worldwide—but
arrest rates remained notably high.
And so the world continued.
They had come down from 100 percent unified consciousness, but not by
all that much...
Back to ordinary life.
"I'm heading out!"
Amy, having taken off her uniform and wearing a dress for the first time
in a while, tripped and fell at the front door.
"Puhaha!"
Ares burst into laughter.
"You've really perfected the art of falling. Don't go around
calling yourself a beauty."
"Shut up. This dress is so uncomfortable."
"Just wear it however. It's not like you're pretty
anyway."
"Want to die? How long are you gonna keep lazing around at home? Go
get a job or something!"
"Nope. Your allowance is more than enough."
"You seriously said that to your little sister? Ugh, whatever. I'm
leaving. Make sure you eat."
Even while rushing out the door, Amy didn't forget to check her
reflection in the mirror.
Ares let out a bitter laugh.
"Geez…"
He understood his sister's feelings.
Returning to his newspaper, he read a front-page headline.
"Yahweh! Flying from the South toward Tormia! Attending his alma
mater principal's wedding?!"
Shirone...
Folding the newspaper, Ares looked up at the ceiling.
"Amy's going to cry at this rate."
Outside the gates of the magic academy, reporters from all over the
world were already gathered.
"This is so embarrassing!"
Today's bride, Vice Principal Olivia, grumbled as she tugged at her
white wedding dress.
"Why? Isn't it nice to be celebrated?"
Wearing a tuxedo, Alpheas was calm as ever.
"Are you kidding me? It's bad enough getting married at my age, and
people aren't even here for me!"
"It's an honor. One of our school's greatest students is coming
back."
Olivia couldn't deny it.
"It's not even certain. Even if he left the South yesterday, there
are dozens of countries between there and here."
It was believed that Shirone had returned from the realm of infinite
void three years ago.
Believed—because there had been no official announcements, and only a
few acquaintances had seen him since.
Alpheas said,
"We really don't know what it means to return from the infinite
void. Maybe it's something deeper than waking from a dream—something like facing pure
emptiness."
"He hasn't lost his heart. He's still helping rebuild the world
with the Twelve Apostles. Even Vincent says he seems the same."
"And that's what's strange."
If there had been rumors that he'd changed, that would be easier to
understand...
What did you see, Shirone? In that true nothingness?
Olina asked,
"Do you think he'll stop by home?"
Right now, Shirone's birth mother, Ikael, was staying with the Vincent
family.
"If he's coming to Tormia, he will. He's never once missed a
visit."
Vincent muttered,
"Geez... People talk like my son has to take responsibility for the
whole world."
Olina sighed.
"I'm more worried about Amy. Those two acted like they couldn't
live without each other."
"Maybe..."
Ikael thought to herself,
"Everything must have returned to nothing. Dreams, illusions—even
emotions. It takes time to rebuild all that. But in the end, he'll remember. He'll remember the most
important reason he belongs in this world."
Just like how Goffin once sent Hexa for her.
The South.
"Move those supplies to the open lot. This storage building's
full!"
Kangnan, having returned to her hometown, was helping rebuild southern
civilization.
"Mister! Don't climb over that—!"
Amid the chaos of the construction site, a familiar face
appeared.
"Huh?"

"Wow! It's been so long! Has it been a year? What brings you to the
South all of a sudden?"
"Something came up. Just thought I'd check in."
They moved to a quiet clearing and sat on some lumber, sipping
drinks.
Sein asked,
"How's Shirone?"
"He left last night. Blitz said he'd fly him there, so he should
make it in time."
"Has he made a decision?"
"I don't know. He's hard to read. But if he's going to the wedding,
that means he intends to see her."
"Yeah..."
Sein asked again,
"What about you? Are you okay?"
Kangnan understood what he meant.
"I'm okay. Gaold isn't in pain anymore. That's enough for me. What

"No. I'm not avoiding her or anything, though. Miro is finally
living her own life—not as some divine guardian, just as a woman. At least for now, I don't want her to
feel responsible for anything."
"Yeah..."
In the end, their feelings were the same.
"You're living a beautiful love, aren't you?" Sein said with a
smile.
"Maybe," Miro replied.
"Go," Gaold said.
"I'm not going," Miro answered.
"Go!" Gaold repeated.
"I said I'm not going, damn it! Why are there so many events? Now I

As key figures in world peace, the two traveled from country to country
attending ceremonies and events.
"Still, this time the event fee's pretty generous. We don't have a

"Don't get too caught up in the world."
"Look who's talking. It's because of all the trouble you caused.
What the hell did you do for the Magic Association to blacklist you?"

"I was busy saving you."
"Agh, seriously!"

ribs.
"Ah! That was a bone—definitely a bone!"
Grimacing in pain, Gaold collapsed onto the bed, prompting a sigh from
Miro.
"Why are you such a crybaby? What a waste of a body. Why do you
even work out?"
"Heh, you know why."
Gaold, now lying on his back, spread his arms and winked.
"Still got some energy left, right?"
Miro glanced at the clock for a moment.
"Only 30 minutes."
"Amy!"
When they arrived at Alpheas Magic School where the wedding was being
held, she spotted familiar faces.
"Tess! Seriel!"
Rian and Fermi were following close behind.
Tess said, "It's been so long! Sorry, I've been caught up with
promotion exams. How've you been?"
"Haha! Same here. You're here for the wedding?"
Though Seriel and Fermi had ties to the school, Rian and Tess
didn't.
"Of course! Shirone's coming, right? Just let him show up—I'm gonna
deck him."
"Come on, it's just a rumor," Tess said, though she clearly
looked hopeful.
As they entered the venue, they ran into more familiar faces among the
large crowd.
Canis and Arin, Maya and Kayden, Dante and Plu were also waiting in the
guest seats.
And then…
"Amy!"
Always a source of laughter, but also someone who brought too many
memories with him—
"Iruki! Nade!"

"No way?"
"Haha! Yep. She finally made it into the world. Daisy, say hi. This

'They haven't changed at all.'

"There are so many."

"Hey, is that…?"

"Hey."

"Shirone!"
"Haha! You guys been well?"

"Haha! Seems like she likes you. Just as I thought—she's my

'What the heck? I thought something serious had happened…'
He's exactly the same.

to Shirone.
"What are you doing? Go see Amy. Don't tell me you forgot
everything in that infinite void or whatever?"

She spoke of her gratitude, her regrets, and the responsibilities she

Alpheas added briefly:
"That's love in old age."
The guests burst into laughter, and the ceremony ended in a wave of

"Amy."

"It's been a while."

"What happened?"
"In the world of Infinite Nothingness…"
Shirone fell silent for a long time, but no one dared to even breathe

"I didn't exist. Because I wasn't there, there was nothing. No way

A chill swept through the crowd.
"It must have been terrifying. But maybe it's because there was no

world."
"I see."
What kind of heart must he have had, realizing only after existing again

"Even now, I don't know. I don't know what happened there that
allowed me to return here. I believe it was to meet you… but I had to be certain. Otherwise, the vast
fear of that nothingness would swallow me up again, and I'd lose my sense of self. So…"
"You traveled the world?" Amy asked.
Shirone nodded.
"I worked for humanity, I took care of my family, I carried out
every piece of my vision. One by one, I resolved everything… so I could meet you."
That's how he spent those years.
"And now, I'm sure. The reason I returned from the Infinite
Nothingness is..."

"I love you."
All the illusions he had wandered through in a world without time,
space, or even himself—
"You…"
