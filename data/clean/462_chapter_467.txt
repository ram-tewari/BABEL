The Seventh Heaven, Araboth.
While the war between Heaven's army and the rebels raged on the
outskirts of Heaven, Araboth, located at the center of the concentric circles, remained tranquil.
Under the spire, where neither the sound of cannons nor screams could
reach, Ikael stood, gazing at the distant rising smoke.
Her eyes held no emotion. Only the dry, unwavering will to carry out
Anke Ra's will remained.
As a piercing presence approached Ikael, her halo expanded
reflexively.
Chiiiiing!
The halo spun at an unprecedented speed, indicating that her thoughts
were also at their peak.
Few in Heaven could make Archangel Ikael this tense.
"What are you guarding?"
A handsome, androgynous figure approached from Ikael's left.
It was the Archangel of Light, Raiel.
"Or are you waiting for someone?"
From her right appeared Metatron, a being with shoulders twice as broad
as other angels, despite his short stature.
Then, in front of her, appeared Metiel and Satiel, the Archangels of
Fusion and Disintegration.
"So, the war has begun. And with a humiliating invasion at that.
Disappointing, Ikael."
Surrounded by four archangels with the spire of Araboth at her back,
Ikael's expression remained cold.
As she briefly glanced at each of them, only one thought crossed her
mind.
'Faiel isn't moving.'
The Archangel of Annihilation, Faiel.
Though the four archangels present were powerful enemies dealing with
fundamental concepts, Faiel's presence was special to Ikael.
In terms of sheer power, if Uriel, the Archangel of Destruction, was her
equal, Faiel was practically her nemesis in the concept of amplification.
Of course, this was true for Faiel as well.
In this uneasy situation, it seemed they had chosen to observe for the
time being.
'Is that fortunate?'
"Hey, Ikael."
Metatron approached with a creaking neck.
"I know what you're thinking. But aren't you underestimating us too
much? Even you can't take on all four of us."
"Why did you rebel? Is it a rejection of Anke Ra?"
As Raiel took a step forward, blue electricity crackled around
him.
A being whose essence was light, not magic.
Even Ikael wouldn't be safe if hit directly.
'They haven't summoned their Maras. They've already deployed them to the
battlefield.'
Raiel spoke.
"Our stance on Anke Ra is firm. But you are not."
Satiel approached.
"Is Archangel Ikael a thing of the past? Being swayed by a mere

For the first time, a murderous intent flickered in Ikael's eyes.
"Arrogant fools."
Angered by the provocation, Metatron activated his halo.
An immense gravitational field spread out, making the very landscape

"Perhaps you've been Archangel for too long. Even if you were the

Electromagnetic force, gravity, weak force, strong force.

Four shockwaves exploded simultaneously.
As the archangels disappeared, Ikael's nose wrinkled in anger.

Ataraxia shot up into the sky like a pillar of light.
The immense power swept through Araboth.
Flash!
Everyone in Heaven could see the massive surge of energy rising above
the spire.

kilometers.
But the deeper they went, the fiercer the enemy's counterattacks became,

"Damn it!"

kilometers.

myths.

"Uooooh!"
Bang!

The power of Skima in his strike surpassed Mecca's machinery, and Lena

"Lena!"

But Kuroi's response speed couldn't keep up with her urgency.
Why had she forgotten? She still had someone to protect.
'Idiot!'
Tears welled up as regret flooded in.

"Ugh!"

As the commander of Heaven's guard, he was well-versed in Mecca's

But the equipment blocking his path was something he had never seen

"Ba-Babel?"

But even the strength of a battalion commander couldn't scratch

The vibration transmitted through his hands made it clear how solidly

Target eliminated.

"Th-Thank you."

The complex tangle of emotions, like tangled blood vessels in her heart,

Babel was the enemy who killed her father.
But now, she had saved her beloved sister.
This left her confused.
How should she perceive the metal angel before her?
Ching!

'Sister…'
Babel turned and looked at Kanya expressionlessly.
Aiming the barrel was a clear sign of attack.
But her algorithm didn't include retaliation against Kanya.
Click!
In the end, Kuroi's barrel lowered again.

"Don't leave my side from now on!"

The thought of protecting Lena cooled her head, but the emotional knot
remained.
