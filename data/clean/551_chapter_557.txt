Chapter 557:
Shirone had returned.
For some, it was a moment of joy; for others, it brought despair.
Most of those who had fiercely competed during the first half of the
term felt the pressure of being pushed out of the mainstream just by seeing Shirone pass through the
Iron Gate.
"Shirone!"
With tears in his eyes, Nade ran up and hugged Shirone tightly.
"What happened? Do you know how worried we were? I
really…"
His voice choked up, and he couldn't continue.
Shirone had disappeared suddenly, leaving behind a farewell
letter.
"I'm sorry. I'll explain everything soon."
"Soon? Tell us right now! What happened?"
Iruki placed a hand on Nade's shoulder to calm him down.
"Calm down. Look behind you."
Turning around, Nade realized everyone was staring at him in a daze. He
cleared his throat and stepped back.
"Alright, let's talk later."
Shirone gently patted Nade's back and walked towards Collie.
On his way, he glanced at Amy and gave a small smile, but she turned her
head away coldly.
'Well, I guess I need to explain things slowly.'
He couldn't blame Amy for feeling betrayed; he would have felt the same
if he were in her shoes.
Approaching Collie, Shirone bowed his head in greeting.
"I'm here. I'm sorry for causing you concern."
"Hmm…"
Collie didn't know why Shirone had refused the evaluation.
However, he suspected it wasn't just a personal matter, given the
ominous atmosphere at the school at the time and the sudden departure of Etella and Siena.
If he were to speculate further, it might even be related to the Day of
Judgment of the 20, which had occurred when he was a young teacher.
"Well, I'm glad you returned safely. Will you resume the evaluation
from the second half?"
The competitors' eyes focused on Shirone.
In an already intense competition, Shirone's return meant another
predator entering the survival of the fittest.
"No. I will refuse the evaluation in the second half as
well."
The atmosphere buzzed with surprise.
For the competitors, it was a relief, but for Shirone, it was an
unexpected decision.
As Amy looked at Shirone for the first time, Nade asked, "Shirone,
are you really going to take the graduation exam like this?"
When you return to the magic school…
Instead of answering, Shirone recalled Miro's advice.
"Tell Mr. Collie that you will continue to refuse the evaluation.
Because you went to heaven, you've already missed over 100 points. Even if you try to catch up in the
remaining time, it's impossible to overturn. That's the structure."
Except for strengthening specialties or group evaluations, small points
had to be accumulated daily, so a few weeks of absence was a serious loss.
"But is it okay? If you refuse the evaluation, you can't even enter
the evaluation hall."
"You have friends. Even if they are competitors, they will help
you. In the graduation exam, factions form, so there's no reason to abandon your
connections."
Although it was a cynical view of friendship, Shirone felt lighter
knowing there was give-and-take.
"And most importantly, it's about you. It's much more efficient not
to reveal your skills than to gauge your competitors'."
Shirone couldn't deny that point.
"If the exam is conducted normally, you can easily be in the top
10. But to survive, that's not enough. Always remember, from now on, it's politics."
'From now on, it's politics.'
Shirone, coming out of his thoughts, said, "I'll do it alone. It's
hard to catch up on the points anyway."
Collie respected Shirone's decision.
"Understood. Then Shirone, go to the dormitory. The rest of you…
hmm?"
As Collie looked up, the students turned their heads.
A slender woman with brown skin was entering through the Iron
Gate.
As the students stared at the beautiful woman with her hair tied back,
someone suddenly blurted out a name.
"Maya?"
That was the cue for the students to start murmuring, and Shirone also
looked at Maya with a blank expression.
She was from the Mysterious Tribe and specialized in sound magic, but
she had always been at the bottom of the graduation class.
Although she had always been pretty, her weight loss had transformed her
appearance completely.
"Is that really Maya? Crybaby Maya?"
Screamer, who didn't know Maya before she gained weight, couldn't
believe his eyes.
They say a woman's beauty is power.
Although they had a contentious relationship, having clashed in strategy
evaluations and hill captures, her transformed appearance made it hard to treat her the same way.
"Um… sorry, could you move? The path is blocked…"
Maya's voice trembled, indicating her timid nature hadn't
changed.
"Oh? Ah, sorry."
As Screamer hurriedly stepped aside, she walked up to Collie and stood
next to Shirone, bowing her head.
"Sir, I've arrived."
"Yes. You've lost a lot of weight."
"I don't need to increase my vocal volume anymore."
Although she had sought to change her technique after Iruki advised her
to give up vocal techniques, that wasn't the only reason.
Collie recalled the night Maya had come to him in the pouring rain,
crying that she couldn't forget Shirone.
It was clear she had lost her appetite due to the heartbreak.
'Has she sorted out her feelings?'
Looking sympathetically at the student he had personally scouted, Collie
regained his composure and said, "So, you still refuse the evaluation?"
"Yes…"
"Alright. Go and rest."
As Maya passed Collie and headed to the dormitory, she hesitated for a
moment before turning back with courage.
Amidst the gazes of many boys, she found Shirone and gave a small smile
as a long-overdue greeting.
Nade, who had approached unnoticed, nudged Shirone's side.
"Hey, hey! She's become incredibly beautiful! Does she still have
feelings for you? Man, I'm jealous."
"What's there to be jealous about?"
Shirone asked, frowning, but Amy had already left the scene.
"We're running late. Let's go to the classroom."
Except for Aider, who was in the hospital, all the graduation class
students had arrived.
"Shirone, don't go anywhere and stick with the research group. I'll
make you cry with how much I'll beat you."
"Haha! Got it. I'll be waiting."
Watching the graduation class head off in a group, Shirone left the Iron
Gate and headed towards the magic warehouse, Istas.
As expected, without the nagging presence, even more dust had
accumulated.
Familiar scenery, familiar smell.
But now, he knew what Istas was.
"Now it really feels like I've returned from heaven."
Although he didn't get a definite answer from Ikael, he had achieved
some results.
Shirone recalled the time he spent with Miro the day before descending
the mountain.
'The meat was really delicious.'
Following the delicious smell for the first time from the cabin, he
found Miro grilling meat she had bought from the village.
To set the mood, she had even prepared some strong liquor.
"What's all this?"
"We need to have a party to celebrate descending the mountain. Of
course, it won't taste as good as the porridge I made."
"Oh, of course."
Shirone quickly answered, fearing she might change her mind, and sat at
the table as Miro placed the grilled meat and opened a bottle of liquor.
"Here, drink. You're old enough to drink now, right?"
"Yes, but…"
As Shirone hesitated with the glass, Miro aggressively proposed a
toast.
"Come on! Drink, drink! We're going all the way
tonight!"
An hour later.
Having emptied all the bottles, the two sat on a hill, looking down at
the mountain scenery.
The peace felt after the hellish schedule made their bodies melt.
"Shirone, do you regret going to heaven?"
Knowing she had something to say, Shirone's answer was natural despite
the sudden question.
"No, not at all. I came back alive."
Miro gave a sad smile.
"You had something you wanted to ask Ikael, right?"
As the silence grew, Miro asked again.
"Do you think she's your mother?"
"Yes. Though I didn't get an answer."
"Then you must have some guesses about your father
too?"
Shirone didn't know who Ikael had fallen in love with, so Miro's
question was more challenging, but he felt the presence of someone within him.
"Why are you asking that?"
"Aren't you resentful? Your biological parents abandoned
you."
Shirone felt he should answer before hearing her response.
"My parents live in Creas. I have no other parents."
Shirone looked up at the night sky.
"I just thought I should know. Who they are, and why."
Miro, who had been lost in thought for a long time, slowly spoke.
"Shirone, maybe I…"
As her voice grew heavy, Shirone turned his head, but Miro suddenly
changed her expression and playfully leaned on his shoulder.
"Hey."
"Why, why are you doing this?"
Shirone shuddered, but Miro wrapped her arm around his.
"Stay still. Why? Are you embarrassed?"
"Don't change the subject. You had something to say, didn't
you?"
Miro, still leaning on him, slowly shook her head.
"It's nothing. I just felt like I should say it. But if you truly
consider your current parents as your real parents, then it's not something that should come from
me."
Shirone, staring at Miro, seemed to reach a conclusion and turned his
gaze forward again.
"Yes. I only have two parents."
Some stories shouldn't be heard from others.
"Ah, it's nice. Leaning on a young man's shoulder like this. Is
this why people fall in love?"
"The chairman seems to think a lot about you."
Miro just laughed like a girl.
"We'll be busy from now on. Both you and I. We have to live hard,
not to waste the youth we've spent over the past 20 years."
"Thank you. You've given me new hope."
"Thank you? This is all for my retirement. Someday, I'll benefit
from my students."
Miro looked up at the full moon floating above the mountain.
"…Let's keep in touch."
Shirone shrugged and laughed.
"Yes."
A gentle smile appeared on Shirone's lips as he came out of his
recollection.
'Thanks to you, I've met many good people.'
But that was all in the past now.
Even Istas, which had once attracted the world's attention, was just an
ordinary research group.
'There are no more secrets. Training resumes today.'
As if to guard against the relief of being out of the competition,
Shirone adjusted his posture and began the sequence method.
The difficulty of moving from billions to trillions and from trillions
to quadrillions was like night and day.
Therefore, it was impossible to predict how long it would take to reach
the next level, the quintillion.
'I believe in the power of a day. Building up day by day.'
As the sequence raced and he entered Archangel mode, the incarnation of
light merged with Shirone's body.
He didn't feel any special changes, but perceiving three seconds in one
second was a given.
'Breaking the wall of time like this…'
At that moment, Shirone's face suddenly hardened.
'What is this? What's happening?'
An incomprehensible voice echoed in his ears like a
hallucination.
"Who's there!"
Feeling something pass behind him, he turned and shouted, but a piercing
scream came from outside the door.
"Aaah!"
"Chase them! Don't let them escape!"
Just hearing the sounds of a battlefield, he knew something was
happening in the hallway.
"What's going on?"
With a worried expression, Shirone kicked open the door and left the
research group.
"……"
But the hallway he saw was as quiet as ever.
'There's no one here. Then what did I hear?'
Realizing he was out of Archangel mode, Shirone re-entered the state of
unity of mind and body.
'Time Explosion.'
As the incarnation merged, he heard the sound of people running on the
ceiling again.
"Don't let your guard down! The opponent is the strongest
Prajna!"
"Reporting! Squad 1 is annihilated! Squad 1 is
annihilated!"
As Shirone listened to the hallucinations with a dazed expression, a
thought suddenly crossed his mind.
"Maybe…"
There might be something more left in this place.
