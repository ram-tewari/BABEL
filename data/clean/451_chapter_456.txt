Chapter 456:
After confessing to Miro and being rejected, Gaold became the subject of
ridicule among the students.
Although he wasn't particularly laughable, students would burst into
laughter whenever they saw him and offer mock words of comfort.
Gaold didn't care about their gazes.
Just as he had fully embraced the Yore faith, he didn't try to distort
or rationalize his feelings.
What troubled him was seeing the woman he cherished the most hanging out
with the person he disliked the most.
"Look at them, sticking together again today."
"Yeah, they couldn't stand each other at the beginning of the graduation
class. Are they really dating, as the rumors say?"
"What about Gaold? That's really sad."
Hearing the students' whispers, Gaold watched Miro and Sein walking
together in the park.
Rumors were rampant in the graduation class that the two were
dating.
Honestly, he didn't understand why Miro was hanging out with
Sein.
'No, it would have been the same with anyone else.'
Gaold turned away with a gloomy expression.
"Damn it."
He didn't want to run into either Sein or Miro.
"Paranormal Psychic Research Society?"
One day, as the distance between them grew, Miro and Sein made a
proposal.
Gaold didn't understand why they were starting a research society amidst
their busy graduation schedules.
And a Paranormal Psychic Research Society at that?
For someone like Gaold, who lived by the power of faith, it might make
sense, but it didn't suit Sein at all.
'Hmph, no principles? Giving up your beliefs for a girl?'
Gaold was annoyed by everything.
"I'm not joining. Find someone else."
Miro looked surprised and widened her eyes.
"Why? I thought you'd like it. It's one of the reasons you're at the
magic school."
"I don't know. I don't even want to talk to you."
As Gaold turned away, avoiding her gaze, Miro asked bluntly,
"Don't tell me, you still have feelings for me?"
Gaold couldn't say yes or no and just pouted his lips.
Seeing this, Miro said calmly,
"Alright then. If you don't want to, there's nothing I can do."
Gaold's heart raced.
He didn't think he would care, but when she decided to withdraw, he felt
he couldn't let it end like this.
"Fine. I'll join the society."
It took great courage for Gaold, but Miro smiled brightly as if she had
expected it.
"You should have said so from the start. Now we can finally collect
membership fees!"
"Wait, is that why you wanted me to join?"
"Huh? No. Well, partly. Hehe."
Miro stuck out her tongue and turned to fill out the application.
'If I was going to do it, I should have just agreed from the
beginning.'
Gaold felt a wave of regret.
As a member of the society, Gaold's tasks were simple and
laborious.
'Damn it, why me…'
He had to go around the magic warehouse Istas and record
coordinates.
Sein used those coordinates to calculate something and discussed it with
Miro.
Even in the society, Gaold always felt left out.
About a month later, bad rumors spread around the school.
It was said that Miro was breaking graduation rules and leaving the
school every night.
Some students claimed to have seen her leaving in a carriage adorned
with jewels that even the highest nobles couldn't afford.
The students concluded that Miro had found a benefactor.
What Miro was giving in return was obvious, even without saying
it.
Of course, Gaold didn't believe it.
Miro was a genius and free-spirited.
She might hang out with rich old men for fun, but she wouldn't seek a
benefactor.
However, the rumors grew like a snowball, and when Miro was degraded to
a woman who sold her body, Gaold couldn't stand it and confronted Sein.
"Hey, you! What's going on?"
Sein, who was writing formulas on the society's blackboard, didn't react
to Gaold's agitation.
"What?"
"Miro!"
Gaold shouted.
"Shouldn't we be doing something to stop these rumors? They're calling
her a prostitute!"
"So what?"
"What?"
Gaold looked at Sein in disbelief.
Even at that moment, Gaold didn't realize how much inner conflict Miro
was experiencing.
"You! How can you say that? Do you really care about Miro?"
Sein finally put down the chalk.
"Aren't you the one who doesn't trust Miro? Maybe deep down, you wish
the rumors were true. Then you might have a chance."
Gaold clenched his fists and trembled.
"Are you really dating Miro?"
"…Why should I tell you that?"
"You bastard!"
Gaold grabbed Sein by the collar and raised his fist.
Sein, offering his chin, said,
"How low. Are you so blinded by jealousy that you've abandoned your
beliefs?"
The Yore faith opposed violence and followed love. Gaold, who had
believed in Yore all his life, couldn't bring himself to throw a punch.
"Forget it. I won't associate with someone like you."
Gaold let go of Sein's collar and left the society.
A few days later, Gaold made a big decision.
He decided to follow Miro when she left the school after
midnight.
"Damn it. So it was true? Jealousy, my ass. Cold-hearted
bastard."
A large house carriage was waiting where Miro was heading.
There weren't many people around at that hour, but it stood there
proudly.
"Hmph, not afraid of anything, are you?"
Muttering sarcastically, Gaold followed the carriage.
Casting magic outside the school was forbidden, but considering the
situation with Miro, it wasn't the time to worry about rules.
He felt uneasy.
The rumors might be true, or they might not.
If they were true, what should he do?
If they weren't, he would just be a petty man snooping on someone else's
girlfriend.
'Whatever!'
Gaold decided to think about the consequences later and continued to
follow the carriage.
The carriage stopped in a plain far from the school.
Thanks to the bright moon, he could see everything clearly.
A more luxurious carriage than the one Miro had arrived in was parked
there, and an old man who looked nearly 100 years old got out.
'Damn! He's a complete old geezer!'
Gaold's disappointment in Miro doubled, but it was too early to
judge.
"Today is the promised last day. Have you made your decision?"
The old man's name was Gustav Havitz XVI.
One of the three emperors of the Holy War, the emperor of the Gustav
Empire.
"…"
Miro didn't answer.
A committee for the Judgment of the Twenty would soon be convened. It
was certain that Havitz would be one of the twenty.
The power of the three emperors would also have a strong influence
within the committee.
"You are truly beautiful. The most noble child in the world…"
She could avoid being trapped in eternal loneliness, bearing the weight
of humanity's fate.
But only if she became the beloved doll of Gustav Havitz.
Even so, it was something Miro had to consider.
Wouldn't it be better to be someone's doll than to bear the burden of
all humanity?
"From today, you are mine, Miro…"
Havitz extended his snake-like tongue and approached Miro's neck.
He had no more desires left as the emperor of the empire, but Miro was
different.
She was the most noble being in the world, the only hope beyond all of
humanity.
Having her would be the perfect final amusement for someone who had
ruled the world all his life.
"You can live. You can continue to be happy. I will give you
everything…"
Even as the snake's breath approached, Miro just stared into the
distance.
"You bastard!"
As Havitz's lips touched her neck and he was about to bite her earlobe,
a dull sound echoed, and his face turned.
With a groan, Havitz fell to the ground and looked up in
disbelief.
A young man with an innocent face was glaring down at him.
Someone had struck the face of one of the three emperors.
No, more importantly, where were his guards?
"You insolent fool! Do you know who I am?"
"Who cares? You're a perverted old man! And Miro, I'm really
disappointed. No matter how much you like money and power, how could you be with someone like
this…"
Miro blinked at Gaold.
"What are you doing here?"
"What do you mean? Of course, I…!"
Gaold couldn't bring himself to be honest.
"The strange rumors were bothering me!"
Havitz stood up and shouted.
"This worthless human! What are you all doing? Cut off his head! No,
capture him alive! I'll torture him for three years before killing him!"
Havitz's voice echoed and then faded into silence.
Only then did the emperor realize something was wrong.
Miro's eyes had turned cold.
"Let me make this clear."
"Ugh…"
Havitz's face turned pale as Miro suddenly seemed enormous.
It was the first time in his life he had felt such fear.
"I reject your offer. I am prepared to sacrifice myself for the future
of humanity."
Gaold asked,
"The future of humanity? What future?"
Miro ignored Gaold and turned away.
"And… it's best to keep today's events to yourself. Even an emperor
can't handle everything."
Havitz finally understood why his guards hadn't responded.
From the moment she arrived, he had been trapped in Miro's space.
"Let's go, Gaold."
"Huh? Oh, okay."
Sensing the gravity of the situation, Gaold followed Miro.
After a brief silence, Miro left the space-time field and turned to
Gaold.
The cold look from earlier was gone, and she laughed heartily, clutching
her stomach.
"You're really something. Do you even know who you just hit?"
"Who cares? Who was it?"
"The emperor of the Gustav Empire, Havitz."
"What! The Gustav Empire!"
Gaold, who had tried to maintain his pride, finally widened his eyes in
shock.
Hitting an emperor?
This wasn't just a matter of his family being wiped out; it could
directly impact the safety of the kingdom.
But Miro shook her head as if it was nothing to worry about.
"It's fine. He won't tell anyone."
"What on earth is going on? The future of humanity? What have you been
doing?"
Miro gave a gentle, somewhat troubled smile.
She hadn't wanted Gaold to know, but now there was no choice.
"Before I explain, can I ask you something?"
"Sure."
"If you had known he was the emperor, would you still have done the same
thing?"
Gaold hesitated, not because he was conflicted, but to genuinely
consider his answer.
"Of course. That filthy old man deserved it."
Miro didn't doubt Gaold's words. From what she had seen in the extreme
heat zone of the survival test, he was more than capable of such actions.
"But you used violence. Doesn't that go against the Yore faith?"
Gaold finally realized and looked at his hands.
It was the first time he had ever hit someone.
No, before that, if he really was the emperor, how had he managed to get
here without being caught?
"Did you know I was following you?"
Miro didn't answer that and turned away.
"Let's go back to school. I have something to tell you."
Even then, Gaold didn't know.
He didn't realize that what he would hear that night would change the
next 20 years of his life completely.
