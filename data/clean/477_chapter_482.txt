Chapter 482:
"He's just a human!"
Mirka refused to believe that Shirone's Spirit Zone was Elysion.
Anke La had feared the mythical humans, Gaia, who had driven heaven to
the brink of destruction.
But they had ultimately lost the war and left the world, hadn't
they?
The idea that their abilities, which could only be found in fragmented
records, would suddenly reappear now didn't make sense.
"Do you think a mere human can wield Elysion? You're just a poor
imitation."
As if to refute Mirka's words, Shirone widened his eyes and cast photon
cannons.
With flashes erupting from close range, Mirka couldn't even think of
using her Unregulated Form and had to dodge.
'This is definitely dangerous.'
If Immortal Function is infinite, Elysion is intangible.
A mind perfectly integrated with the outside world handles all
information detected by the Ultima System as its own.
It was similar to combining Shirone's Spirit Zone with ancient magic,
making Mirka feel like she was fighting a system rather than an individual.
To any being with individuality, the Ultima System's Direct Perception
was an utterly unfamiliar phenomenon.
This entirely different combat system threw the fairies into confusion,
and Shirone's magic, adapted to Elysion, began to devastate the surroundings with even greater
destructive power.
'We must stop him here.'
Seeing seven fairies vanish in just ten seconds, Mirka finally steeled
herself.
Whether Shirone was using Elysion or imitating it, the ability itself
was real.
"Everyone, follow me!"
As Mirka led the charge, Shirone fired photon cannons from behind,
bombarding them like a flurry of punches.
Simultaneously, Mirka activated her Unregulated Form, Razor of
Truth.
A chilling white light flickered around her, and photons with mass
struck her body.
Boom! Boom! Boom!
All the fairies who knew the power of the Unregulated Form paused their
battle to watch the outcome.
Shirone also observed Mirka with sharp eyes.
"Hah, hahahaha."
Mirka, unscathed, extended her hand and laughed.
Being among the top-ranked fairies, her avatar's strength still gave her
the upper hand.
But her smile was tense, almost rigid.
"I've won."
With her Unregulated Form activated, Shirone was now invincible to
her.
She activated another Unregulated Form, Law of Submission, and charged
at incredible speed.
"Tch!"
Shirone spread his wings of light and soared into the sky.
All the fairies shot up vertically, quickly leaving the Whale's Gorge
and entering the open sky.
"Catch him! If we capture him, we win."
Shirone's vision was filled with countless fairy spells.
Visible, invisible, material, and phenomenological, the types varied
according to the fairies' concepts of creation, but Shirone didn't dwell on any of them and just looked
straight ahead.
Elysion integrated all information into a single dimension and
transmitted it, while Armand's powerful functions heightened Shirone's reflexes to the extreme.
"How is this possible…?"
Shirone's movements, dodging what seemed like an inescapable barrage,
astonished even the fairies born with innate flight abilities.
Shirone, who had escaped the magical barrage that couldn't be avoided
without calculating millimeter-level errors, began his counterattack.
As Akamai's eye locked onto targets, photon cannons flew in from all
directions, delivering concentrated strikes.
Every time the focus of Antithesis jumped between fairies, they fell
without fail.
"What are you all doing?!"
As Mirka flew in, shouting in anger, Shirone stopped his attack and
focused on her.
'Stay calm. Think.'
Shirone knew Mirka was invincible, but unlike before, he didn't
despair.
All Unregulated Forms have activation conditions and costs.
'Just blocking the first attack can't make her invincible.'
The first thing that came to mind was a time limit.
She couldn't maintain invincibility indefinitely. But that alone wasn't
enough.
The advantage was so powerful that it made Shirone's judgment
easier.
'Spatial limitation.'
Having reached a conclusion, Shirone sharply changed direction to avoid
the charging Mirka.
Confirming Mirka's reaction as she tried to keep him in her sight, he
felt somewhat confident.
'I see. In her line of sight…'
Using the maximum functionality of his wings of light, Shirone performed
dazzling movements to confuse Mirka.
"Hmph! It's a good idea, but…"
Many enemies had tried to break Razor of Truth before.
But that was a misconception focused only on the ability to eliminate
variables.
With a bit of different thinking, one could understand why Mirka had
such an ability.
Ranked third among countless fairies.
Mirka was inherently strong, and the Unregulated Form was merely a tool
to maintain that strength at 100%.
"Do you think you can evade me?"
Mirka's body turned into light as she pursued Shirone.
Charging at a terrifying speed, she aimed to pierce Shirone's body,
caught in the Law of Submission.
With a bang, Mirka passed through Shirone, her eyes filled with
shock.
"What?"
Shirone had suddenly disappeared.
Not a teleportation, but literally vanished.
Hearing the sound of the wind being cut, Mirka quickly turned to the
side. Shirone's tentacles were approaching rapidly.
Having lost sight of him, the Unregulated Form reset.
Gritting her teeth, Mirka hastily leaned back.
Ugh! The wind pressure alone pushed her body back several meters.
Shirone didn't stop at swinging his tentacles but followed up with
photon cannons.
Even as she dodged, tracing complex trajectories in light form, Mirka's
mind was filled with questions.
How had he gotten out of her sight?
"Razor of Truth!"
Regaining some composure, she activated her Unregulated Form
again.
The photon cannons vanished futilely, and realizing she was invincible
again, she glared at Shirone.
Valhalla Action.
Seeing the fallen angel's magic circle behind Shirone, everything became
clear.
'Damn…!'
Reversing cause and effect.
If he had already reached a specific coordinate and then reversed time,
she couldn't keep him in her sight.
'This way, I can avoid the Unregulated Form. But I must refrain from big
attacks.'
However, Shirone also faced a delay as a cost for reversing
causality.
For Mirka to activate her Unregulated Form, she had to be attacked once,
and Shirone could be caught if he miscalculated the delay.
Both had to be cautious.
But this destroyed the overwhelming advantage of the Unregulated Form
and balanced the power.
Shirone fought fiercely among the fairy troops.
It was a one-against-many battle, but the tide was gradually turning in
Shirone's favor.
The magic emerging from Elysion's intangibility steadily eliminated the
enemies without giving them time to adapt, and finally, a deadly laser net was deployed.
A red line net, possible only without a central point, spread in all
directions, disrupting the fairies' movements.
As homing photon cannons slipped through the net and exploded the
enemies, screams echoed in the sky.
"This wretched human!"
As Mirka activated Razor of Truth, Valhalla Action triggered, and
Shirone disappeared.
'I must find him!'
Mirka looked around frantically.
If she could find him before his delay ended, she would win.
'Huh?'
Turning around, her eyes widened in shock. Shirone was right in front of
her.
Spinning and swinging his tentacles, his movements were domineering, but
not beyond Mirka's ability to dodge.
'Foolish human. Did he make a reckless move out of impatience?'
Mirka smirked and extended her hand.
If she blocked with her Unregulated Form, Shirone would be completely
defenseless.
"Ugh!"
But just before activating her ability, Mirka felt a powerful restraint
and shuddered.
Akamai's eye, embedded in the artificial brain, was targeting her from
the side.
The advantage of Valhalla Action was the ability to reverse the
causality of all linked events.
In other words, Shirone had simultaneously advanced two outcomes:
placing the eye on her side and then attacking from behind.
The delay was longer, but it was Shirone's unique variable to break the
balance of power.
'Is there no absolute outcome?'
As she reached this thought, a thud echoed in Mirka's head.
Her head shattered, she flew into the distant sky, and as the inertia
ended, she fell in a parabola.
"Lady Mirka!"
All the fairies in the sky couldn't believe what they were
seeing.
A noble spirit ranked second among the 72 ranks of fairies had been
defeated by a mere human.
Fury surged as their pride was trampled, and all the fairies
charged.
"Ugh!"
Shirone grimaced, waiting for the delay to end.
The waiting time was much longer than usual because he had reversed two
events.
When the delay finally ended, countless spells were already rushing
toward him.
'I have no choice.'
It wasn't his preferred method, but this was war. Sometimes, brutality
was necessary for victory.
Through Elysion's mental power, Shirone cast one of his specialties,
Berserk.
But this Berserk was entirely different from what he had shown
before.
It was a literal multi-explosion of Berserk.
Due to Elysion's lack of a central point, Berserk exploded in numerous
locations.
Previously, Berserk had merely repelled things, but this time, it
crushed everything with a storm of light.
Boom! Boom! Boom!
"Ahhhh!"
All the fairies trapped within Elysion's influence screamed in
agony.
The small bodies caught between the curtains of light made cracking
sounds as they broke.
From a distance, it looked like lightning was striking out of a clear
sky.
As the 30-meter diameter spheres of light flashed, the fairies trembled
as if electrocuted and fell in droves.
When the casting of Berserk, which could only be performed within
Elysion, ended, the sky returned to its calm blue state.
The fairy troops were annihilated, and Shirone, floating alone in the
air, took a deep breath as he looked up at the higher sky.
As his wings of light vanished, his body began to fall.
Accelerating towards the ground at a tremendous speed, Armand's robe
extended, flying dozens of meters to anchor itself to the ground first.
Simultaneously, it absorbed the impact, allowing Shirone to land lightly
as if he had jumped from a low height.
He was on the mountainside, and below the mountain range, he could see
the paradise where the subjects lived.
"Haa."
Shirone sat down for a moment to calm his exhausted mind.
Armand's nerves vividly conveyed the safety of Feope, who was nestled in
his chest.
He briefly wondered how much stronger he had become.
But what he felt most acutely at that moment was the devastation of his
mind and the harsh reality brought on by the war.
"I have to keep going."
That was the nature of war. There were also personal goals he had to
achieve.
"Just hold on a little longer, Feope."
As Armand restored some of his physical functions, Shirone stood up
again.
As he gently bent his knees, the massive wings of light spread out once
more from his back.
With a burst of dust, Shirone's body soared, quickly flying over the
mountain range towards Araboth.
