Chapter 93:
The Genius Who Lost Light (2)
"Good judgment. It's foolish to ignore nine things just because you've obtained one out
of ten. No matter the situation, you must never stop thinking. Everything in the world is determined by the
volume of thought. Battles are no different."
"Don't misunderstand. I will never forgive you."
"Kekeke! If you've got spirit too, that's perfect. Shall I take a guess? You're the type
who grows through real combat. A school that only teaches rigid things doesn't suit you. How about it? Won't
you come with me? I'll make you the greatest mage."
"Stop spouting nonsense. I respect the teachers here far more than a murderer like you."
"Then tell me. What have you learned here? Photon cannons? Red wavelengths? If not that,
did you at least get advice on the Immortal Function?"
Shironé's eyes wavered slightly.
"How do you know about that……?"
Arkane burst into laughter. This must be why people become teachers. Gathering clueless
children and spouting nonsense to their heart's content.
"How rare. Truly rare. Is the Immortal Function an ordinary achievement? But child, there
are terrifying monsters living in places you don't know. If you mingle with them energetically……"
Arkane narrowed one eye and smiled.
"Unlockers aren't that rare either."
Shironé swallowed dryly. Just how many life-and-death battles must one cross to say
Unlockers aren't rare? Regardless of skill, the accumulated experience was on a different level.
"I've met seven in 100 years. Four were decent, and I killed three. The uniqueness of
Unlockers lies in their distinctiveness. The potential for distortion is infinite. But untrained uniqueness
tends to die before greater stability."
It was frustrating, but undeniable. Individuality could be a variable that ignored
objective strength, but it could also helplessly lose its power in the face of straightforward methods.
"You have nothing to learn in a school that ignores individuality and teaches only
uniformity. You must sharpen your unique strengths. I can help you with that."
"I don't learn magic to defeat someone. Don't lump me together with you."
Arkane saw conflict in Shironé's eyes. Of course. Without an obsession-like drive for
magical achievement, he wouldn't have risen to his current position.
"You probably think everyone likes you now. But humans are naturally favorable to the
extraordinary. It's not because they like you as a person. How long do you think you can keep interacting
with such hypocrites?"
Everyone listening frowned at Arkane's words.
But Shironé remained silent.
"Grow stronger. So they can never ignore you. Otherwise, they'll trample you. Do you
think you can avoid conflict with pretty excuses when that happens?"
Arkane held up three fingers.
"Three years. I'll make you strong enough that no one on the continent can ignore you in
that time. Do you think three years is too short? I don't. If we maximize your uniqueness, it's far from
impossible."
At the very least, it wasn't nonsense to Shironé. As an Unlocker, there was little
benefit in staying at school and rejecting a Grand Mage's guidance. At best, it might make finding a job in
magical society easier.
"You're right."
Iruki and Neid's faces darkened.
"Shironé……"
If it was the truth, there was no need to deny it. Unlockers held a unique position even
in magical society, and the standardized education of magic schools had limits in nurturing true strength.
"Perhaps you're right. But I still won't follow you. School isn't a place for me to
fight—it's where my life is. Teachers, friends, a sense of belonging. I don't think you can give me any of
that."
Arkane scowled. Whatever tricks Alphaeus had used, his ability to brainwash students was
impressive.
"You're being deceived. When you actually live your life, such things won't matter. If
you weren't strong, you'd just be another ordinary kid at school. Proving your strength is the only thing
that matters in life."
"If that's really all there is, then why are you doing this?"
"What?"
"You became a Grand Mage through such battles. What do you have left now? Everyone hates
you. Aren't you lonely too? Because no one cares about you, this is the only way you can prove yourself."
Arkane's face reddened as he gritted his teeth.
"You're spouting nonsense. The strong are lonely by nature. The weak cling together out
of weakness. Look at the current situation. Everyone is kneeling at my feet. I rule over them. This is what
loneliness is."
"No. You're just a murderer no one cares about."
At the word "murderer," madness flashed in Arkane's eyes.
"Hahaha! Perhaps. But do you know? I once had something called comrades. But he led
everyone around him to ruin. That man is Alphaeus, the one you respect."
Shironé didn't believe it. Etela had told him the two had a grudge. The Alphaeus he knew
would never lead anyone to ruin.
"I don't know what happened in the past, but I won't listen to you. Because you're a
murderer. And because you're still killing now."
"A murderer. Would you say the same if Alphaeus… were the same as me?"
Shironé's eyes widened. This time, he couldn't help being shocked.
"That's a lie. What do you know about the headmaster?"
"Kekeke! Of course I know. That brat was my disciple."
"What……?"
Shironé was speechless. Alphaeus had been a pioneer of light magic since his youth. How
could a dark magic user be his teacher?
Feeling the irony himself, Arkane looked up at the sky with a distant gaze. The events of
that day felt as vivid as yesterday. Suddenly remembering something, he turned to Etela.
"Did Alphaeus ever marry?"
"No. He has lived alone his whole life, devoted to education."
"I see."
A fleeting sadness passed through Arkane's eyes. After 40 years of hating Alphaeus, the
only thing he could pity was Elina's story.
"You may not know this, but Alphaeus was once married."
Etela tilted her head. To her knowledge, Alphaeus was unmarried. Even if he had married
and divorced, as a union of noble families, it should have been recorded.
"But the headmaster is listed as unmarried in all records."
"So it was never recognized. But Alphaeus was married. They lived together for three
years. Her name was Elina. A remarkable woman. She shone brighter and was more beautiful than anyone."
"What happened between the two of you? Was it something worth holding a grudge over for
50 years?"
"Back then, Alphaeus was a rising star, recognized for his photon theory. He even
received the Gold Circle Award from the emperor—can you imagine his status? His position in the Thunder
Magic Department was secured, and he set up a household with a woman from the Bastard family. He had
everything the world could offer. But he had one worry."
Arkane tapped his head.
"His wife, Elina, was a bit… lacking in intelligence. About the level of a ten-year-old."
"I see."
"But Alphaeus didn't mind. He truly loved his wife. His love was never pity or pretense."
Arkane's memories traveled back 40 years. A time when he ruled the world, and Alphaeus
was hailed as a rising star in the magic world.
***
"This year's Gold Circle Award goes to… Mirhi Alphaeus!"
A fanfare echoed through the grand hall. As multicolored confetti rained from the
ceiling, Alphaeus shed tears of joy. The Gold Circle Award, given to the mage with the year's greatest
achievement. Winning it guaranteed entry into the Thunder Magic Department and support from numerous noble
families.
"Congratulations, Alphaeus. I knew you'd win."
"I'll be counting on you. You won't forget your fellow students now that you're
successful, will you?"
Dozens of mages swarmed around Alphaeus. With his stock set to rise endlessly, they
wanted to get on his good side early.
But not all gazes were friendly. Especially Sarof, who had fought tooth and nail for the
award until the end, wore a face like he'd bitten into something rotten.
"Hmph. He got famous by catching one idiot. With the Mirhi and Bastard families combined,
how could anyone compete?"
"Exactly. A poisoned chalice, truly. But what can we do? The results are what they are.
We'll have to watch our tongues around Alphaeus from now on."
Sarof knew it too. As long as he couldn't leave magical society, befriending Alphaeus was
the best course.
"What now, Sarof? It'll be awkward if we meet alone. Should we sneak in now and make
peace?"
"No, I'm not in the mood. You go ahead if you want."
"I feel the same. Let's do it later. Two is better than one. Want to grab a drink?"
"Yeah. I don't want to stay here any longer."
Sarof left the royal palace with his friends. He had been a finalist for the Gold Circle
Award, but no one paid attention to the loser.
Clump burst into hearty laughter, dragging Alphaeus through the crowd. Alphaeus thought
he could finally catch his breath, but his face was soon squished against Clump's arm as he was shaken
violently.
"Hahaha! You did it! My friend won the Gold Circle! I can't believe it!"
"Ow! That hurts!"
Alphaeus screamed, but Clump's celebration didn't stop.
"You were so annoying, acting all smart, but turns out you're a genius! You did it, man!"
Alphaeus felt dizzy enough to die, but he didn't try to escape. The Gold Circle. If not
today, when else would he taste such thrilling pain?
"Come on! It's a great day, so let's drink till our noses are crooked. I reserved a bar,
so just bring your stomach and leave your brain behind."
Finally free, Alphaeus gave an apologetic smile.
"Ah, I can't go right away. I need to stop by somewhere first."
"What? If the guest of honor leaves, what's the point of toasting?"
"I need to go home. I want to give the medal to my wife."
Even Clump couldn't argue with that. Though married just a year ago, due to opposition
from both families, Elina lived in a small newlywed home in the commoners' district without any support.
It started with the Mirhi family. They couldn't accept their son, sent abroad for
studies, marrying a "foolish" woman.
In response, the Bastard family refused to back down. Even among first-class nobles,
there was a clear difference between regional and capital families, so they wouldn't yield even if their
daughter was a bit slow.
In the end, Alphaeus and Elina had no choice but to hold an unofficial wedding with only
friends in attendance.
"I see. The wife. Why don't you bring her along?"
"No. What wife would like seeing her husband drunk? I'll just make a quick stop."
"You've got tact, huh? Then hurry back."
"I'll be quick. Go ahead to the bar!"
Alphaeus fled the palace like a man chased. Though exhausted from day-and-night research,
today, his breathlessness felt different.
'I did it! I really did it!'
He had won the kingdom's most prestigious award. With such an achievement, even the Mirhi
family would have to accept him, making a formal marriage ceremony with Elina no longer a dream.
"Honey, I'm home."
Elina knew today was the day the winner would be decided. Other families might have
prepared surprise parties, but she came out of the kitchen just like any other day.
"You're back? Have you eaten?"
