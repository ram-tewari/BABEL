Chapter 543:
"How are you here...?"
Shirone couldn't place the source of his own words.
He had gradually recovered fragments of memory throughout the countless
events he'd faced—but none had ever stirred such powerful nostalgia as this moment.
"Arbiter!"
At Yoa's scream, he turned his head and saw her being dragged away by a
Mucus Man, sinking into the wall's gelatinous slime.
"Damn it!"
As Shirone rushed toward the door, he collided with three others who had
arrived just behind Rian.
Something about them felt familiar.
"It's been a while, Shirone."
As Marsha delivered her line like it had been prepared in advance, a
sharp pain surged through Shirone's head.
"Urgh...!"
Miro, Marsha, Fermi.
Whether for good or bad, these were people who had left a deep
impression on him in reality.
Who are they…?
Their presence aligned seamlessly with the information in Shirone's
data, spawning new memories in his mind.
Descendants of the fairies.
Because they were part of the dwindling fairy bloodline within society,
they were capable of wielding special abilities.
"Help me! Yoa was taken! She went underground!"
No one moved.
Just as no adult would interfere in children's games, for those in the
open state, anything happening in this world was meaningless.
"Just as expected. Let's subdue him," Fermi said coldly.
"Fermi…!"
Shirone glared at him, grinding his teeth as memories of being
relentlessly bullied by Fermi in fairy society returned.
"There's no time to fight. If you won't help, I'll go save Yoa on my
own."
"Suit yourself."
As magic began to gather, Rian spoke up.
"Let's go, Shirone."
Fermi whipped his head around.
"What are you doing?"
Rian turned away from him entirely, facing Shirone.
"I don't know who she is, but she's important to you. Let's go save
her."
Fermi looked at Miro and Marsha.
"You're really going to let this happen?"
"We have no choice. This is Shirone," Marsha replied.
The Shirone she knew was not someone who could ignore suffering in front
of him, no matter what greater cause was at stake.
Fermi's last hope was Miro, but she too limbered up and moved to join
Marsha.
"That's a majority vote," she said.
"Aunt, but this is—"
"Let it go this time. You know what Shirone's like, don't you? And now,
we know what Rian's like too."
Fermi clicked his tongue.
"This is just maddening."
Miro cracked a grin and moved her hands. The Avatar of Kannon
materialized behind her in a halo of light.
Moments later, a massive double palm slammed down toward the
ground.
KUUUUUNG!
The shockwave shook the entire greenhouse, though all that remained on
the floor was the imprint of her palms.
Catching everyone's gaze, Miro chuckled awkwardly.
"Ahaha... this building's really solid, huh?"
Shirone exploded.
"You're laughing? I should just—!"
"Fuuuuu…"
As Miro exhaled deeply, Shirone's eyes widened.
The Avatar of Kannon expanded to tens of times its original size,
filling the greenhouse and pressing against the ceiling.
"One Finger."
With a single jab of her index finger, the giant avatar pierced through
floor after floor of the building.
BOOM! BOOM! BOOM! BOOM! BOOM!
Marsha screamed as she fell, and the rest followed, crashing all the way
to the tenth basement level—the bottom of the facility.
Fermi brushed off the dust and grumbled.
"Her damn temper..."
Shirone couldn't take his eyes off Miro.
"Who the hell are you?"
"We can talk on the way, can't we?"
Miro pointed toward the hallway, where a distorted trail of mucus was
slithering rapidly into the darkness—clearly where Yoa had been taken.
"Stop!"
Shirone and the others lunged forward, only to find the corridor ending
at a massive glass wall.
"What is this…?"
Shirone was the only one who knew.
A magnetic levitation elevator...
He placed his hand on the glass, and an LED line glowed, forming a
rectangular panel.
When he laid his palm on the biometric scanner, it lit up, and the door
opened.
So my records are still stored...
Naturally, cryo-sleep subjects would need access to the facility upon
waking.
They boarded the elevator behind Shirone, soft light illuminating from
the ceiling.
"Where are we going?" Miro asked.
The elevator suddenly shot downward at astonishing speed into the
darkness.
But after passing the initial acceleration zone, it became so dark and
smooth it didn't even feel like they were moving.
"Probably where Mucus's control core is," Fermi muttered.
Even before going into cryosleep, Shirone had never imagined such an
enormous underground facility could exist.
This probably isn't the only entrance. Each major institution might have
one of these buried beneath it…
Finishing the thought, Shirone finally turned to face the others.
"What happened to us? What's going on?"
The fact that he felt something was off was a good sign.
"Listen carefully now, Shirone," Miro said. "It'll be hard to believe,
but this is the truth. The world you're living in now…"
She gauged his reaction carefully.
"It's all fake. Even you."
Shirone fell silent for a long moment, but unlike Malta's wife, he at
least chose to hear it out.
"I don't understand what you're saying."
"Alright, then I'll start from the beginning."
Miro laid everything bare.
This time, even Shirone couldn't help but be shaken.
"It's all fake? This entire world?"
"Yes. So whatever happens here doesn't matter. What matters is restoring
the real you—"
"That's impossible."
Shirone shook his head.
"I'm not blindly loyal or anything. It's just... there's no way that's
true."
Miro understood how he felt.
"Maybe not. But you have to believe me. A descendant of the fairies?
No—I'm human. And your memories can't be that different from mine, can they?"
"Liar!"
Shirone finally snapped.
"This is all side effects from cryosleep! That's what the administrator
said! It's totally possible for long-term sleep to cause confusion!"
"That too is fake. Artificial memories designed to help you integrate
into Apocalypse's data stream."
"And you expect me to believe that?!"
Marsha spoke up.
"You have to, Shirone. If you don't, the real you will die."
"Damn it!"
Shirone spun around and paced restlessly within the elevator.
Rian approached him quietly.
"Shirone, I know it's hard, but—"
"I believe you."
Shirone turned back.
"Fine. I'll believe you. Maybe what you're saying is true. So yes, I
believe it."
He spread his arms and looked at the four of them.
"So now what? Is something supposed to change in me? Am I supposed to do
something else?"
"……"
This was the real problem.
Even if Shirone believed, nothing changed.
The difference between possibly believing something, and truly feeling
it is as wide as the ends of the universe.
WHIIIIIIIIIIIIINE
The elevator, which had been rushing downward, began to slow—then
suddenly dropped.
"Guhhh!"
Though the internal pressure adjusted automatically to prevent
zero-gravity, the sensation of his stomach lurching was nauseating.
How far are we going...?
Eventually, the elevator reached its destination, but Shirone had no
sense of how far from the surface they had come.
The glass door slid open.
Before them stretched a corridor lined with blinking panels and endless
cables.
The drop had jolted Shirone's focus. His eyes sharpened as he stepped
into the passage.
Whatever the truth was—saving Yoa came first.
Passing a sign that read "Special Containment Zone," they
heard a faint mechanical humming from behind an open doorway.
Shirone conjured a Photon Cannon in his hand and suddenly lunged
forward, with the others hastily following.
"This is...?"
Even his determination seemed to fade for a moment as he stared at the
object at the center of the domed space.
A massive lens, nearly two meters in diameter—eerily resembling a human
eye.
Its metallic body was tangled with thousands of cables, connecting to
every part of the surrounding chamber.
From Miro's lips, a word escaped before she could think.
"Ra…?"
Digital Ra (Local Energy Circulation System Quantum
Computer)
Fermi asked, "Ra? You're saying this scrap heap is that
Ra?"
"No. The real Ra was a living being. But... this one's just too
similar."
Fermi tried to imagine what the real Ra might have looked like based on
this digital Ra's appearance—but it was meaningless.
"Didn't you say Ra was erased?"
Erased data couldn't exist anywhere.
"Right. So this isn't the real Ra."
Myth had been destroyed—by a single boy.
"This is just another god, created by humans who couldn't bear the
absence of one."
As the lens within Digital Ra rotated, a Mucus Man descended from the
ceiling with Yoa still clutched in its grasp.
"Yoa! Are you alright?!"
A flicker of color returned to Yoa's face—until the Mucus Man yanked her
close again, more forcefully.
"Who are you? Why have you come here?"
Shirone stepped forward boldly.
"Who are you to ask that? The world's already ended. Why are you doing
this?"
"My name is Ra. I am a humanoid model stored within the quantum
computer's data."
Miro drew her own conclusion.
A kind of Avatar…?
"Frankly, I don't care what I'm called. I am a program that purifies the
world using solar energy. The ancient humans called Ra a monotheistic god."
"No matter what kind of computer you are, I won't accept anything that
harms humans."
"We are all just links in the chain of cycles. Erasing one life is no
different from creating another."
"You can only say that because you have no life. Have you ever mourned
someone's death?"
"Humans also celebrate the death of others."
Shirone gritted his teeth.
If only there had been a way to translate an AI's logic into human
speech, maybe the world wouldn't have ended like this.
There's no choice but to fight. But with Yoa in its grasp…
As Shirone hesitated, Marsha began walking toward Ra.
"Whatever it is you want from a human, how about me instead?"
"Marsha?"
When Shirone turned to her, she gave him a playful wink.
If I can just land a hit with the Dagger of Depravity…
Ra's arm stretched out like rubber, seizing Marsha by the neck and
pulling her in. Yoa shouted in shock.
"Are you insane?! You're offering yourself up?!"
"Yeah. Guess I am."
Marsha's expression went cold as she drew the Dagger of Depravity from
her left hand.
When she stabbed it into Ra's side and saw a burst of black smoke gush
forth, her eyes gleamed.
It's working! This might really—!
Marsha stabbed again and again, wildly, like a woman possessed. Ra
didn't react—only stared blankly at the smoke pouring from its body.
"What is this?"
Finally, Marsha stopped, sensing something was wrong.
"…Dammit!"
The wounds carved by the Dagger of Depravity were healing on their
own.
"It's rewriting its data," Miro said.
The backup capabilities of a quantum computer were beyond human
comprehension.
"I will now analyze your data," Ra declared.
From the mucus-coated wall, a beast-shaped entity emerged—its jellylike
form resembling a leopard. It lunged at Marsha.
It was based on a creature Ra had once digested before the world's
end.
"Watch out!"
Without thinking, Shirone dove and rolled with Marsha in his arms,
shielding her from the attack.
"Shirone…?"
Their eyes met, and Shirone's pupils trembled.
—You can hold me if you want.
"Graaaargh!"
As the beast leapt, its rear legs fully extended, Shirone's hand
instinctively shot out.
"Look out!"
Rian shouted as he charged forward.
BOOM! BOOM! BOOM! BOOM!
The sound of something smashing echoed from deep within the facility,
drawing Ra's attention.
CRASH!
A sword burst through the steel wall, slicing through the beast's cheek
like a clothesline, and landed squarely in Shirone's hand.
"…Diamond Armament."
At the same time, the blade unfolded like a spider's legs, encasing
Shirone's entire body.
