Chapter 25:
The Cold Boy and the Hot Girl (4)
Jake felt his blood boil. His hours of effort to meet Amy had been met with humiliation.
"You little...! Ugh!"
Jake clenched his teeth and recoiled. Amy had already entered her Spirit Zone. Likely a
cross-shaped targeting type.
"You little what? All talk and no action, huh? If you're so angry, come at me. I'll
burn you to a crisp right now."
Jake gritted his teeth. His assumption that she was just a studious model student had
been a mistake. She, too, had been a troublemaker who roamed the back alleys from a young age.
"Using magic at this hour is against the rules."
"You were the one who used magic first, weren't you? When you were warming up that
civet coffee or whatever."
"Crazy! You think that's the same as a cross-shaped spell...!"
"Haha!"
Amy couldn't help but laugh. He was just a petty coward like this. Most men she'd met
were like this—quick to back down when faced with real power.
"Do you know why I accepted Shirone's coffee? Because he seems like the type to take
responsibility for his actions. Unlike you, blabbering about family and rules, he does what he can. If you
don't disappear right now, I'll burn you to ashes. There won't even be a trace left."
Jake was certain. It wasn't a bluff. The rumors about the Carmis family's Crimson Eyes
were true. Her large pupils were glowing red.
"If you kill me..."
"I don't care. I take responsibility for my actions too."
Jake gritted his teeth so hard they might break. This was the first time he'd felt such
intense humiliation.
But even Jake, the notorious troublemaker, had no choice but to retreat under the
pressure of the Crimson Eyes' intimidation.
Amy's cross-shaped spell had locked onto him. As the top-ranked student in Class Four,
she could incinerate him with near-absolute certainty.
"We'll see, you arrogant brat!"
Jake gnashed his teeth and stormed out of the library. Only after he disappeared from
view did the glow in Amy's Crimson Eyes fade. Staggering slightly, she rubbed her eyes and muttered.
"Annoying. Disgusting."
She'd wasted her time on a worthless human when she'd planned to rest. That fact
irritated her unbearably. Because once time passed, it couldn't be reclaimed.
Why were the men who clung to her all like this? She wondered if there was something
wrong with her personality.
A soft laugh escaped Amy's lips.
"Well, there are plenty of problems."
Suddenly, she felt sorry for Shirone.
***
Shirone, freed from his servitude to Amy, was happy every day. There's a saying:
"You don't know what you've got till it's gone." Realizing how precious the hour taken by Amy had
been made studying even more enjoyable.
Though he endured stares for causing a scandal with the school's most arrogant girl, no
one paid him any mind in the library. This academic fervor was what made Alpheas Magic School prestigious.
Today, too, Shirone hummed a tune as he headed to the library after class. Then, near the
faculty quarters, he spotted a familiar face.
Seriel, Amy's friend. She was pacing restlessly, perhaps needing the bathroom urgently,
so Shirone changed his direction.
They had exchanged greetings during integrated classes, but he personally liked her.
Unlike Amy, who growled at him, Seriel always offered words of encouragement. Though he still had no idea
what she was cheering for.
Noticing Shirone approaching, Seriel's eyes widened. Then, as if seeing a ghost, she
gaped before suddenly sprinting toward him.
"Senior, hello..."
"Hey! What are you doing?"
Seriel grabbed Shirone by the collar and shook him.
"Why are you here? If I'd known, I wouldn't have let Amy go!"
Shirone tilted his head. It was common knowledge that she headed straight to the library
after classes.
"What's going on? You have to tell me for me to know."
"Read this! Are you really saying nothing happened?"
Seriel thrust out a crumpled paper. Shirone took it silently and unfolded it. It was a
short warning from a sender called the Black Magicians.
***
30 minutes earlier.
A man blocked Amy and Seriel's path to the library.
A Class Five student, a repeater over 23 years old. Though they knew his name from being
classmates a year ago, his gloomy personality meant they'd never really talked.
"Hello, Senior. Still soaring high, I see."
Amy snorted at his smarmy tone and stepped aside.
"I'm busy. Move. I have nothing to say to you."
"Aw, don't be like that. We were classmates once. Spare me a moment."
"Was I your only classmate? Hundreds have passed through Class Five. Go bother
someone else."
Though she addressed him formally out of respect for his age, she didn't hide the disdain
in her voice. She disliked him. Though not entirely untalented for Class Five, his mind was elsewhere.
Content with mediocrity, he preyed on junior girls—what was there to talk to such a scoundrel?
"Haha! Still as rude as ever, Amy."
Amy's eyes filled with venom.
"What? What did you just say?"
She'd addressed him formally out of courtesy, and now he was pushing his luck. But the
man, seemingly confident, handed her a note calmly.
"The boss wants to see you. You'd better comply. Unless you want to upset the
prince."
"The prince? What are you talking about?"
Frowning, Amy snatched the note and read it.
***
We have Shirone. Come to Training Ground 14 by 6 p.m. before we make him less of a man.
If you tell a teacher, you'll regret it for life.
—Black Magicians.
She immediately crumpled the note.
"So what? Shirone means nothing to me. Even if he did, you think I'd obey you?"
"Hehe, that's up to you. I'm just the messenger."
The man, having completed his mission, smirked and sauntered away.
Furious, Amy threw the note to the ground. Seriel picked it up, read it, and trembled.
Black Magicians. Though their true nature remained unknown, rumors about this delinquent
circle were rampant among students.
"Amy, what do we do? These guys are real scum. They say Tyra dropped out last month
because of them."
"Tyra?"
Tyra, a Class Four classmate, wasn't graduating-class material but was a solid mid-tier
performer. If they'd threatened her, they must have either ganged up on her or used underhanded tactics.
"I'll go. Whatever the case, Shirone was caught because of me."
"Let's tell a teacher instead. I'm worried about Shirone, but you could be in danger
too."
"A teacher? Hmm..."
Amy hesitated. Her past was one thing, but Shirone had a secret about his origins that
couldn't be revealed.
If this escalated, rumors would spread school-wide, and Shirone's school life could be
ruined. Knowing the nobles' sense of privilege better than anyone, she was certain of it.
"I'll go first. If I'm not back by 7, report it to a teacher then."
"What are you saying? We should tell them now!"
"Sorry. There's a reason I can't. I'll explain when I get back, okay?"
Seriel was flustered. Amy, always so confident, had never made such a request before.
"Then let's go together. Who knows how many of them there are?"
"You need to stay behind in case things go wrong. Don't worry. The leader's probably
Jake. With his skills, he can't do anything to me."
Seriel knew better than anyone how talented Amy was. Though held back by the
graduating-class barrier, even within Class Four, skill levels varied wildly. Amy was the undisputed top.
"Fine. Exactly one hour. If you're not back by 7, I'm telling a teacher no matter
what."
"Okay. Then I'm counting on you."
***
Hearing the details from Seriel, Shirone was stunned. Not only was there a delinquent
circle called the Black Magicians, but Amy had risked herself for him.
"So she went because of me? What am I doing here then?"
"I don't know! Amy told me to wait no matter what! Why are you even here?"
Shirone understood. The one-hour grace period showed her confidence in handling it
herself. And the reason for her actions included consideration for his secret. Leaving Seriel behind as a
messenger was a brilliant move.
"I have to go! Senior, please do as Amy said."
Shirone ran toward Training Ground 14. Though 30 minutes remained until the deadline,
distance was the issue. Training Ground 14, for live combat, was deep in the mountains.
Suddenly, the scenery blurred as buildings rushed past. Turning his head, he found Seriel
hugging his waist.
"Running won't get you there in time! I'm coming too."
It was the short-distance teleportation spell essential for mages. Though banned on
campus due to collision risks, this was no time for rules.
Students looked up at the popping sounds as Seriel, holding Shirone, blinked away toward
the mountains in 10-meter intervals.
Reaching the mountain's base, the two began climbing on foot. Teleporting in the
mountains was too dangerous, risking their lives.
They arrived at Training Ground 14 before 6. But Amy was nowhere to be seen. No sign of
the Black Magicians, either.
"Amy! Amy! What's going on? Why isn't she here?"
"Maybe they took her elsewhere? The note exposed the location, so they might've
anticipated us reporting to a teacher. We might've been tricked."
"Then what about Amy?"
Realizing the Black Magicians' cunning, Shirone was certain. They truly intended to cause
harm.
"Go down the mountain and tell a teacher now. You'll get there much faster without
me."
"Amy said to wait until 7 no matter what! She said there was a reason. What if she's
disappointed later?"
"That reason is me, so go! I said it's fine, so don't worry!"
Seriel stared blankly. Amy's desperate expression had been because of Shirone.
"Really? What's going on between you two? Are you secretly dating?"
"Is this the time for that? It could get dangerous if we're late!"
"Ugh, fine! You stay put too!"
Seriel turned and hurried back the way she came. If Amy wouldn't be harmed, there was no
reason not to report it.
Shirone ventured into the mountains, searching for Amy. Unlike other nobles, he'd spent
half his life in the mountains, so the dense forest felt familiar.
Expanding his Spirit Zone to its limits, he searched for traces of Amy. After 10 minutes
of climbing, his senses picked up the first footprint.
"Here!"
But there were no further traces. The flattened grass suggested she'd taken flight from
here.
If so, there was no way to track her. Shirone wandered aimlessly for a while. Then, a
popping sound erupted from the distant sky. His head snapped toward the source.
***
Black smoke rose from Amy's face. But a closer look revealed it hovered a finger's width
above her skin—an Air Shield spell, compressing air to create a localized barrier.
"Changing the meeting spot and ambushing me. Still as cowardly as ever."
Amy rose from her landing stance and turned. The man who'd handed her the note stood
there, grinning vilely.
"Hehe, I've got a bone to pick with you. If you'd just taken it, I could've had the
honor of your first kiss."
"Enough of your disgusting talk. Where's Shirone?"
Five figures descended around Amy, including Jake, the Black Magicians' leader. Surveying
the scene, he spoke, sounding surprised.
"You really came alone. Was that guy really that important to you?"
"What nonsense? I'm more than enough for the likes of you. Where's Shirone?"
"Hahaha! That weakling's probably still in the library. Clueless about what's about
to happen to his girl."
Amy realized she'd been tricked. But it was for the best. Without a hostage, these
lowlifes couldn't touch her.
"Calling me here means you're prepared to die, right?"
"Such confidence. But do you really think it'll be that easy? The list of those who
underestimated the Black Magicians and paid the price would disagree."
"Even for someone with no learning ability, this is pathetic. All bark and no
bite."
Amy entered her Spirit Zone. Transforming it into a cross-shaped targeting type, Jake,
recalling the previous day, immediately deployed his own cross-shaped zone.
But once again, the cost of missing the first strike was high. Amy ignored Jake and
targeted the others first.
Firing consecutive Fire Strikes—her specialty—forced the men to dodge frantically.
Strike-type spells added physical force, making them impossible to block with Air Shields alone.
Amy rotated her Spirit Zone rapidly to lock onto targets. But human movements were
unpredictable, preventing 100% accuracy like in practice. Her head throbbed as the zone spun and reversed.
Jake soared into the sky with a gust of wind. If fire was Amy's forte, wind was Jake's.
In one-on-one combat, a single lethal move often decided the outcome. Unleashing his signature Wind Cutter,
sharp blades of wind tore through the air.
Amy didn't sense the Wind Cutter's approach. The cross-shaped targeting type extended
range dramatically but was defensively weaker than an omnidirectional sphere. Aerial attacks were especially
deadly.
Feeling the Wind Cutter penetrate her zone, Amy twisted her body. But it was already
within a meter. As the Black Magicians cheered, her movements blurred impossibly fast. The Wind Cutter
grazed her red hair, severing a lock. Jake gaped.
"What the—? How?"
"It's called a schema, you idiot."
Amy raised her cross-shaped zone skyward. Jake, panicking, tried to escape with a gust of
wind. But her targeting speed was the fastest in the advanced class.
"Fire Strike."
A cannonball-sized fireball shot out. Certain he couldn't dodge, Jake encased himself in
Air Suit, an advanced air spell.
The fireball struck Jake, sending him crashing to the ground with a deafening roar.
"Argh! You damn brat!"
He staggered up, glaring at Amy. The flames, sucking oxygen from the Air Suit, blazed
fiercely. Exploding the suit scattered the flames like falling petals, but the physical force had already
struck, twisting his gut.
"I'll kill you."
"Hohoho! You thought you could beat me with that? You're not even fit to graduate,
let alone fail. No wonder you're in this state, wasting your energy on nonsense."
Amy, the Carmis family's genius. The difference in skill was as overwhelming as her
reputation.
But Jake smirked viciously.
"Really? Your confidence ends here."
As if to prove it wasn't a bluff, the Black Magicians simultaneously raised their hands.
Amy's face paled as an unfamiliar sensation crept in. Something was invading her mind, disrupting her focus.
Amy glared at Jake furiously.
"This is... anti-magic?"
"Hahaha! Surprised? This is the Black Magicians' specialty. The spell that's sent
countless talents packing."
"You pathetic scum."
It was truly despicable. Anti-magic, banned by school rules. But crossing that forbidden
line was like biting at a student's Achilles' heel.
The five resonance waves tangled Amy's Spirit Zone chaotically. It felt like someone was
gripping her head and shaking it violently.
Unable to endure, Amy dropped to her knees. Maintaining the zone any longer might drive
her mad.
Jake, wearing a victor's smile, cast Air Chains. Bonds of air coiled around Amy's limbs,
lifting her into the air.
Struggling with all her might, Amy couldn't break free. Even with her schema, the
restraint was unshakable.
"Finally caught you, you feisty little cat."
Jake drew a knife.
"Now, what should I do with you? For the record, I haven't forgotten what happened
days ago. I'm tempted to teach you a lesson."
"Let me warn you—if you harm even a hair on my head, you won't walk away
unscathed."
"Bahahaha! Even the great Amy resorts to family threats when cornered? I told
you—we're the same. Without your family, you're just a helpless little girl."
"I told you not to lump me in with trash like you. I'll destroy you with my own
hands."
Jake's eyes gleamed with malice. Her unyielding pride in this situation irritated him.
His knife-wielding hand trembled, as if ready to stab at any moment.
"You're in no position to boast. Should I carve up your face? Once you're ugly, no
one will bother you."
Amy shuddered at Jake's deranged gaze. His inferiority complex had no bottom. He was
truly capable of anything.
Jake pressed a finger against the blade. Heating metal was child's play for a fire mage,
even if it wasn't his forte. The blade's interior glowed red.
"Let's do this. The arrogant Amy lost her eyes experimenting with magic alone. She
dropped out and lived the rest of her life as a loser. How's that for a story?"
The blade, dripping with molten heat, inched toward Amy's left eye. She steeled herself,
but the searing heat forced her eyelids shut.
"Wait, you bastards!"
A sudden shout made the Black Magicians flinch. Shirone, fists clenched and panting,
stood there. Amy's eyes widened.
"Look! A knight in shining armor! Too bad—if you'd been a little later, your girl
would've been even prettier."
"What are you doing to her?"
"Why don't you ask her yourself? Or would you rather hear it from your lover?"
"You idiot! What are you doing here alone?"
Amy yelled. But Shirone was too stunned by the scene before him to listen.
A girl, bound and surrounded by older students. Jake, wielding a knife and cackling.
'Because of me...'
For the first time in his life, Shirone felt overwhelming rage. He picked up a log from
the ground and shouted.
"Let her go! Or I won't hold back."
"Bahahaha! Hear that? What's he gonna do with that stick?"
Ignoring the taunts, Shirone charged. At the very least, his swordsmanship had once
beaten even Lian. If he bought enough time, the teachers would arrive.
"Here I come!"
"Boys, he's coming. Open the door."
No one moved as Shirone ran toward them. They smirked, almost eager for him to come
closer.
Something felt off. The moment Shirone realized it, his mind reeled as if struck by a
hammer.
"Guh!"
"Idiot! Dispel your Spirit Zone! It's anti-magic!"
Anti-magic? Shirone understood. This was how the top-ranked student in Class Four had
been immobilized by delinquents.
The shock and vibration were overwhelming. The five resonance waves rattled his mind.
Shirone lifted his head. The world blurred like watercolors mixing. But he couldn't give
up. Clinging to his fading consciousness, he took a step forward.
"Senior, I'll save you."
"Stay back! You'll lose your magic forever! You'll become an idiot!"
"Because of me... this happened..."
"Get out of your Spirit Zone! Now!"
Shirone couldn't hear her, but screaming was all she could do. Each step he took to save
her shattered her heart.
Gritting his teeth, Shirone maintained his Spirit Zone.
'No! Hold on. Need more frames.'
His Spirit Zone gradually morphed into a defensive cube shape under his willpower. The
Black Magicians panicked. The anti-magic backlash was overwhelming, like being crushed under a boulder.
"Wh-what's this? Why is it like this?"
Shirone expanded his Spirit Zone to its limits. Though wobbling like dew on a leaf, he
poured all his focus into maintaining its form.
'Harder. Don't collapse. Don't break.'
Finally, the frames connected, stabilizing the zone. Then, like a seesaw, the Black
Magicians began groaning in pain.
"Gah! What's happening? My head hurts!"
In that instant, Shirone's eyes snapped open. The defensive cube solidified completely,
compressing the massive sphere into a polyhedron. The anti-magic's pain vanished, replaced by an unshakable
solidity. The pressure felt capable of crushing diamonds.
"Argh!"
The five anti-magic users screamed and collapsed. As if crushed by an invisible force,
they couldn't even twitch, noses bleeding.
This was why anti-magic was banned in school. Without a mind honed to near-inhuman
levels, it would backfire, destroying the user.
Amy stared in shock at the writhing Black Magicians. Shirone had crushed five anti-magic
users alone. A Class Seven student who hadn't even been enrolled a month.
Through her Spirit Zone, she sensed Shirone's—cold, solid, and heavy.
'My god. How is this possible...?'
Shirone's gaze remained sharp. Taking a step forward made Jake retreat instinctively. His
Spirit Zone clashed with Shirone's, triggering a primal fear.
But dodging a 15-meter Spirit Zone with mere steps was impossible. When the two zones
overlapped, Jake's face twisted in horror.
"You... what are you?"
Unbelievable. But the Spirit Zone's resonance didn't lie. A defensive cube—a massive
polyhedron with 88 faces—encircled Shirone.
