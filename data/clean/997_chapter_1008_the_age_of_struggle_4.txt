Chapter 1008:
The Age of Struggle (4)
At the sight of a man melting and eating bread like juice, the people
realized:
"This is real."
Many had saved alcohol as the last item in the royal action guidelines,
and many knew that this man was one of them.
Seeing him happily eating the bread, there was nothing harsh about
him.
"I will repent!"
People cried out on their knees one after another.
"True God, please give me rest too. Life is too hard."
"Please grant my wish. I want nothing else. I just want to eat to
my full."
The priest watched them with satisfaction.
"People mocked by false gods, now the true God weeps tears of joy
for you. Go ahead. Achieve what you desire. You are truly the lambs who put God's word into
action."
"Uaaaaa!" Encouraged, people started rushing in all
directions, snatching possessions from passersby.
They stripped clothes, stole food, and plundered all the wealth they
could.
"It's true! It doesn't hurt!"
Their hearts felt as if they were soaring as they reclaimed the lives
they had been forced to abandon due to the action guidelines.
"Yes, that's right! This is the true God! There's no reason for the
omnipotent God to give us pain!"
Those who had been starved to the bone devoured the stolen food.
In reality, the emotional disease did not manifest.
"Paimon's emotional disease manifests when trying to protect
something precious. But..."
The priest smirked bitterly.
"Something stolen from others isn't precious."
A fact overlooked by humanity.
Or perhaps it had been analyzed, but it was certainly something that
could never be publicly disclosed.
The square turned into a chaotic scene.
Simply by reversing the circuit of logic, humans could indeed become
stronger.
"That is our God," the priest said.
"It's Satan."
"Mr. Giyorgi."
The man from the tavern had been watching the situation and approached
the priest.
"Gultan?"
When the tavern man removed his hood, a face with red skin and perky
ears appeared.
"There's been a complication in the plan."
"I see."
Giyorgi wasn't flustered because he understood Yahweh better than
anyone.
Gultan reported.
"The man who approached Maya is another human. We might need to
move to another location..."
"Yahweh is here."
A philanthropist wouldn't be foolish enough to use someone as
bait.
"Above all, it smells. The only being in the universe that emits
such a foul odor."
Gultan sniffed around.
"Anyway. What should we do? Although we're of the same rank, you're
the head, Mr. Giyorgi."
Their organization was called Catacomb.
A secret society formed by the army of Hell, composed of seven legion
commanders tasked with the assassination of Shirone. They were chosen for their specialized abilities in
the Anti-Yahweh warfare.
Especially Giyorgi, being able to manipulate minds, was said to be
Shirone's antithesis.
"To kill Yahweh, we need to shake his mind first, even if it's
through anger. We'll send Miyo to Maya. She can provide the utmost misery."
Miyo was a succubus, among the most lascivious and powerful
demons.
"Let's convey that. And Yahweh?"
Giyorgi took in the scene of violence spreading beyond the
square.
"As the atmosphere has ripened..."
He pocketed the inverted cross pendant and sniffed the air, checking the
direction.
"Shall we greet him?"
Gultan raised an eyebrow as he turned.
"Is that okay? I know your abilities, Mr. Giyorgi, but the opponent
is Yahweh."
"Evil wins."
Holding the Bible of Hell, Giyorgi left the square, continuing,
"Always under the guise of goodness, but evil has always been the
winner. It will continue to be the same."
He disappeared into the rioting crowd.
"God is Satan."
Cayden knocked on the door of the inn where Maya was staying.
"Maya! Maya!"
No movement was detected, but through the Spirit Zone, he confirmed she
was there.
"It's me, Cayden! Please open the door!"
"Go away."
Maya's voice was tinged with tears.
"I don't want to meet anyone. Not you, not Shirone. Just let me be
alone. Please."
"You can sing again!"
There was no reply, but the quality of the air had intuitively
changed.
"Shirone said he could cure your emotional disease. So let's go.
You don't have to stay here anymore."
"Why?"
Maya asked.
"Why would Shirone cure my disease?"
"That..."
Although not magically talented, she was capable of rational
thinking.
"If he could have cured it, he would have cured everyone. Because
that's the kind of person Shirone is. But why is he specifically looking for me?"
Cayden spoke honestly.
"Shirone wants your help. He hopes you'll fight for the
world." After a while, a reply came.
"I can't help Shirone. No, I don't want to. So go
back."
"Maya! Your emotional disease can be cured! You don't have to
suffer anymore!"
"That hurts even more!"
Suddenly raising her voice, Cayden fell silent.
"I can endure this state, even if people mock me, because Shirone
doesn't see it. But if I meet Shirone..."
Crying was heard.
"No matter what I do, no matter how much I like him, Shirone
doesn't like me..."
Cayden realized.
Maybe the fate of the Red Cross is embedded in everyone, big or
small.
"Maya."
His heart throbbed, and his teeth clacked in fear.
"Maya, I..."
He knew he shouldn't, but his heart was pushing the words out.
"I love you. Since the first time I saw you, my heart has never
changed."
This wouldn't come to fruition.
"Damn it! Damn it!"
It felt like he was burning up, and screams of agony echoed in his
mind.
"Maya, it doesn't matter what you think. Even if you hate me, it's
okay. I just want to be by your side..."
Then there was a loud crash as the wall crumbled.
"Kyah!"
As Maya screamed and Cayden burst through the door towards the bed, he
saw her.
"What are you?"
A woman with her eyes covered with black bandages crossed in an X had
grabbed Maya from behind.
"Me? A beast crazed with lust, Miyo."
"A succubus."
Cayden sensed it, but it was quite different from the average feeling he
had learned about in magic school.
Displaying a physical beauty that seemed to exist only for
function.
"Cayden... Gasp!"
Miyo gripped Maya's neck.
"Spare your throat. You'll need that pretty voice to sing praises
for me soon."
"Let her go."
Cayden aimed his sword, but his arm trembled too much to bear the
weight.
"Damn."
Unable to let go of Maya.
"Are you sure? Your arm is shaking badly. Isn't it better than
fighting like that... What do you think?"
Miyo's tongue, red as blood, wriggled like jelly along Maya's
neck.
"Wanna have fun? It'll be great."
"You bastard!"
Despite his lack of strength, Cayden lunged and swung his sword.
"Magic doesn't even work."
Miyo easily dodged the commoner's sword and soared out of the building
with Maya.
"Hohoho! Follow if you dare."
"Stop there!"
Only then did Cayden manage to cast a flying spell, thanks to the mental
techniques he had been trained in from birth.
Chasing after a division commander-level opponent was tough, but Miyo
was adjusting her speed.
"Your heart's beating fast?"
Miyo kissed Maya's cheek.
"Don't worry. Once you surrender to me, you won't even realize you
exist."
Maya's eyes widened.
"Gasp!"
As sensations crawled up her spine, her consciousness seemed to bounce
out of her skull.
"Shirone..."
Her vision went dark.
"Cayden is late."
At a street cafe with a table outside, Shirone was waiting for
Cayden.
Amy said,
"Really. Is the talk taking too long?" Given the complex
circumstances, it was inevitable, but Shirone had a bad feeling.
"We can't wait. Let's go."
Amy had just paid for the tea when Shirone raised his arm to halt her
advance.
"Wait."
A man in priestly robes with a desperate look approached.
"A demon."
He appeared human, but the distinctive nature of a demon couldn't be
concealed.
"No, is he not even trying to hide it?" Such certainty
permeated the strange atmosphere, and Shirone's expression turned grave.
"Who are you?"
"Giyorgi."
Having answered, he stopped and opened the Bible of the Devil.
"I am God's representative and the head of the Yahweh assassination
organization. Currently, one of my operatives has Maya."
"Where is she now?"
"In the mountains. Miyo excels at manipulating senses. By now, she
might have lost her personality."
As Miracle Stream emerged from Shirone, Giyorgi turned aside.
"Go."
"What?"
"If she's important to you, save her. Moreover, that's truly in
accordance with God's will. But..."
Giyorgi's eyes seemed to contain an abyss.
"If you leave here, I'll kill everyone in the village. The choice
is yours."
Amy clenched her fist.
"Are you misunderstanding something? We can eliminate you and save
Maya, and that would be the end of it."
"That's impossible."
Giyorgi firmly closed the Devil's Bible and again blocked Shirone's
path.
"Yahweh, you can never defeat me. You already know that, don't
you?"
"If you keep spouting nonsense..."
Amy was about to argue, but suddenly realized that Shirone was still
silent.
"Shirone, what's wrong?"
"A demon."
Shirone's expression was not good.
"A demon is a collection of human emotions, taking a specific form
when extreme emotions converge."
"Suddenly stating the obvious..."
"It's me."
Amy blinked briefly, then turned ahead with a startled
expression.
"Could it be..."
"Yes. That demon is made of the emotions I discarded. In essence,
Yahweh's darkness."
Giyorgi said,
"There's a misunderstanding in saying discarded. It's not that
Yahweh discarded me, but that I left Yahweh."
Black smoke rose from his entire body, creating a strange contrast with
Miracle Stream.
"Repent, Yahweh. No longer mock humans under the guise of a false
god."
"Amy, please look after Maya."
"But..."
"This time, it might get rough."
Amy swallowed and nodded, then soared into the sky.
"Don't worry! I'll definitely save her!"
The roar of spatial displacement was intense, but Shirone had already
blocked out unnecessary information.
"Let's move the location."
"To avoid harming humans?"
"Yes."
Giyorgi nodded.
"That's right, Yahweh."
He leaped from the ground, and terrifying black smoke surged from his
body.
"That's why I win."
The collision of light and darkness caused a yin-yang reaction, covering
the city in a monochrome zone.
The citizens screamed.
"Aaah! What's happening to my body all of a sudden!"
A gravitational collapse caused matter to float, and the sequence of
time began to entangle chaotically.
"Whaaaat's happening! Aaaah!"
The bizarre sounds were unlike any human accent, sending chills down
Shirone's spine.
"Whatever will be, will be! Save me!"
Even the Ultima System couldn't perfectly restore causality.
"By the slightest touch. Believe in what is almost there. It's all
up to you."
