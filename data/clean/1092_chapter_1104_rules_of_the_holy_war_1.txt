Chapter 1104:
Rules of the Holy War (1)
The kings stood in stunned silence, gazing up at the host of angels
filling the sky.
"So it's true…"
Rumors that Heaven's Army had disappeared from Jive's borders had
circulated, but no one had expected this.
"They've aligned with angels?"
While humans have been known to bargain with even spirits for what they
desire, this revelation was shocking.
"Could negotiations have been possible? That many angels could
obliterate an entire nation."
Satiel's piercing gaze scanned the delegations of each nation.
"Where is Ikael?"
She didn't see her among the Nephilim.
"Could they have hidden him already? No, not while I'm here…"
Her eyes sharpened, and she suddenly froze.
"Satan!"
Gustav Havitz.
Though his appearance had changed since the battle in Heaven, the
essence of Satan's presence was unmistakable.
He was the one who had destroyed countless angels, including the
Archangel Metiel, and orchestrated Heaven's collapse.
"How dare you show your face here…?"
Sensing Satiel's rising fury, Havitz showed his first sign of
interest.
"Oh? An angel?"
He climbed onto a saddle, turned to face Satiel, and wrapped an arm
around Wuorin's waist, shielding his eyes from the sun with his hand.
"Hmm, it's Satiel. Familiar vibes. Yes, I consumed them."
Through memory and essence, Havitz had absorbed every facet of Satan's
past.
"Well, isn't that terrifying."
Hearing the angels' murderous intent through divine frequency, Havitz
nodded in mock appreciation.
"Such pure resentment. That's the problem with angels—they need
everything to be so absolute."
Satiel glared fiercely.
"Y-you…!"
Havitz extended his tongue mockingly, moving it up and down as if
licking the distant Satiel.
Satiel's divine aura trembled violently.
"You worm!"
"Heh heh heh. Ahh, you're just too fun!"
Even as he chuckled, Havitz exaggerated his tongue's grotesque
movements.
"I'll disintegrate you!"
Satiel's divine aura expanded into a radiant halo, and the angels in the
sky assumed an attack formation.
King Gith spoke calmly.
"Please, restrain yourself."
"Silence. A mere human dares give me orders? Should I kill you
first?"
"You're welcome to, but causing a commotion here would only please
Ikael, wouldn't it?"
The glowing halo retracted into a sphere.
"Satan is also part of the plan. Trust me—Ikael will suffer far more
than you could imagine."
"Ikael…"
Grinding her teeth audibly, Satiel turned her back and headed toward the
main hall.
"I won't wait long. Bring me Ikael, and make her shed tears of
blood."
"Understood."
As the lesser angels gradually disappeared, the foreign dignitaries
exchanged grim looks.
"One mountain after another…"
Shirone spoke up.
"Satiel has gathered over 300 lesser angels. With Mara added to the
equation, this might be the most powerful single nation's force we've ever faced."
These weren't the humans of old.
"The global population has dropped by a third. Thirty nations have
vanished from the map. With core personnel lacking, there are hardly any superpowers left."
Lupist turned to Shirone.
"Other nations have strong cards to play. Tormia has its strengths, but
your convictions may not hold up against this."
Shirone's cold, calculating eyes sharpened.
"A total war."
In the distance, the Truth Pyramid shimmered faintly on the horizon.
Shirone halted mid-step.
"Argan. Frigi."
As Shirone's companions exchanged puzzled glances, two apostles
immediately knelt before him.
"Yes, Messiah."
"Head to Jive. Immediately."
"What?"
It was unclear why he had changed the destination so abruptly,
especially with the Truth Pyramid so close.
"Why…?"
"I've located the angels. All twelve apostles will be summoned there.
Follow my instructions when you arrive."
At the mention of angels, the apostles' expressions hardened.
"Understood."
With those words, the apostles leaped into the air, transforming into a
dragon and an ice wyrm.
As the two dragons ascended into the sky, Ares and his party were
reminded of their true nature.
"Right, they're dragons," Ares mused, recalling how they had restored
the desolate Nag-Nak Oasis with immense magical power.
Glen spoke with concern.
"Will we be okay without them? The Truth Pyramid is an unknown,
mysterious space."
With Lucia's Emotion Sickness temporarily alleviated, the next priority
was safety.
Canis retorted sharply.
"If you're scared, go back. We've done just fine without
dragons."
Glen didn't back down.
"I'm saying we need to be prepared. Losing key forces can be a critical
issue in any situation. I thought magicians were supposed to be smart, but apparently not all of
them."
"What did you say?"
Their tension from the earlier Nag-Nak Oasis incident threatened to
reignite.
"And you? When your lover was—"
Canis flinched and stopped mid-sentence.
"Tch, forget it."
He, too, had someone precious—Arin.
Recognizing this, Glen softened but still felt the need to
clarify.
"It's true that Yahweh helped Lucia. I'd even risk my life to repay
that. But I won't forsake my faith. I still deny him."
"Who cares? Got it."
While expecting compensation might seem petty, it wasn't something
anyone could do. Begrudgingly, Glen acknowledged Canis.
"...Still, he's a magician."
As Canis muttered and returned to his seat, Arin smiled and took his
hand.
"You did well. Please, control your temper."
Their mental channel resonated with soft words.
"What? I wasn't even that bad. Sure, I get it, though."
If Arin had been in Lucia's place, the thought would've been
unbearable.
"Huhuhu."
As Arin gazed at Canis with a soft laugh, she leaned in and kissed him
lightly on the cheek in front of everyone.
Canis's face turned as red as a tomato, prompting Shirone to chuckle and
turn his gaze elsewhere.
"Still as inseparable as ever."
Though Shirone hadn't expected Canis to get so flustered over just a
kiss on the cheek, he mused:
"They're even worse than me."
Glen, who had been watching enviously, quickly averted his gaze, only to
be caught by Lucia, who raised an eyebrow at him.
"What are you looking at?"
"Huh? Oh, nothing, really!"
"Liar." Lucia pinched Glen's arm hard.
"Ow! Sorry!"
Though there was no doubt about their affection for each other, as
priests, they were clumsy in expressing it.
Ares placed a hand on Shirone's shoulder.
"There's this kind of love, and that kind of love."
"Ah, yes. I suppose so."
A brief silence followed, growing more awkward by the second, until Ares
leaned closer and asked in a hushed tone:
"Our little sister is doing 'just fine,' isn't she?"
"Uh, well…" Shirone stammered, unsure of how to define "just fine,"
prompting Ares to burst into laughter.
"Relax, it's a joke! I can tell just by looking. But as her brother, let
me warn you: once Karmis sets her sights on something, she won't stop until she's the best."
As Ares walked toward the Truth Pyramid, Shirone stared after him with
an exasperated expression.
"What kind of brother is he?"
Meanwhile, the couples—Canis and Arin, Glen and Lucia—held hands and
followed after Ares.
There were all kinds of love.
"I just hope everyone can be happy."
With that thought, Shirone trailed behind them, while the stoic Euphra
and Zulu brought up the rear.
"Whoa…"
The massive structure loomed overhead, its sheer size overwhelming the
human spirit.
While Ares knelt to perform a brief ritual, Arin's voice trembled as she
called out to Canis.
"Canis, this… This is…"
"What does it look like to you?"
Though Arin perceived every object as if seeing it for the first time,
her reaction now was extreme.
"I don't know. What is this? A massive light, but scattered with black
specks like noise…"
The light and darkness seemed mixed, but not in harmony.
"It's grotesque. Even looking at it is difficult. What kind of form
could possibly cure Emotion Sickness?"
Zulu spoke.
"The Truth Pyramid has existed since before human civilization. The
ancient Kingdom of Paras modeled countless pyramids after it, meant to serve as tombs for their
kings."
Shirone added, "It's meaningful. Did ancient kings somehow know that the
afterlife was connected to the outside world?"
Ancient civilizations often erased their own records, so even Omega
contained no data about them.
"We don't know how much they understood about the outside world," Zulu
said, "but considering Paras was the birthplace of astrology, it's hard to dismiss the possibility
entirely."
"Astrology…" Shirone pondered.
The power to transcend time.
"Done."
After finishing his ritual, Ares picked up his backpack.
"Let's head in. Whatever it is, we'll figure it out by exploring. Maybe
we'll even meet God."
"Oh, merciful God," Glen and Lucia murmured as Shirone approached the
pyramid.
Though small compared to the overall structure, there was a gap large
enough for them to enter.
Surprisingly, the interior wasn't dark.
"Strange…"
When Ares turned off his lantern, it became dimmer but still
visible.
"There's no light source, so how can we see?"
"Electrons, probably," Shirone said.
"Electrons in the air are faintly emitting light. It's
unsettling."
His skin prickled with a strange sensation.
"But electrons can't emit light without energy. Could it be…?"
"Yes. The pyramid itself might be generating energy."
"Is that possible? This is an artifact from before recorded
history."
"It's likely similar to OOPArts. Everything from ancient civilizations
seems to generate electricity."
"Then it's essentially a perpetual energy source?"
"By this world's standards, yes. More importantly, OOPArts often emit
electromagnetic fields that disrupt brainwaves. They might even erase data from the Akashic Records. Be
cautious."
"Understood. Everyone, stay alert."
Despite Ares's attempt at humor, the group's mood remained tense.
Navigating the maze-like passages, Canis surveyed their surroundings
with a grim expression.
"It's massive."
The overwhelming scale they had felt outside was nothing compared to the
experience of being inside.
"If we tried to explore it all, we'd never finish. But the problem is,
there's no sign of civilization in any of these rooms."
This was typical of ancient civilizations.
As Ares memorized their route with his red eyes, he suddenly stopped
mid-step.
"These aren't rooms. There's no discernible purpose here. It feels like
the size and shape of these spaces exist because they were needed to create this structure."
Shirone suggested, "Perhaps it's designed to control some sort of
flow?"
"A complex path. Rooms of varying sizes. Irregular patterns. If the
entire structure is meant to create an invisible flow…"
"Then that's how it generates electricity. Hmm…"
As Ares mused, Arin, glancing back, suddenly widened her eyes.
"Canis, look over there."
Blue glowing symbols on the walls of the corridor began to shine and
move toward them.
