Chapter 752:
The Republic of Aymond was split into North and South 200 years ago and
remains locked in a brutal war to this day.
In particular, North Aymond has become an extremely closed society under
the fear-driven rule of the dictator Igor.
It is the only nation that refused to follow the will of the Ivory
Tower. Even after the Altars were opened, no one knew how North Aymond had handled the aftermath.
"The entire village is a military base."
Shirone, who arrived in North Aymond three days ago, was making his way
south along the mountain range near the capital.
Since leaving the Ivory Tower, he had traveled through seven countries,
sealing their Altars—but in Aymond, he had made no progress.
The people here were extremely hostile to foreign intervention and
displayed a terrifying resolve, even going so far as to launch suicide attacks upon discovering
outsiders.
"Were they truly brainwashed…?"
It was impossible to brainwash over twenty million people with magic
alone, but watching them made it hard not to consider the possibility.
Aside from the difficulty of access, there was another issue: since
entering North Aymond, there was no way to use Theraphos channels.
The people trusted no one but Igor, and they weren't even allowed to
believe in anyone else.
"Guess I'll be sleeping in the mountains again tonight."
Since every human settlement had been converted into a military
facility, camping outdoors was the only option.
"Material."
Armand's final evolution, which had embraced Yahweh's Light, could
manifest any substance that existed in the world.
"Sleeping bag, utensils, toiletries…"
As Shirone visualized the molecular structures he'd learned through
omniscience, objects began to appear one by one before him.
Lastly, he summoned firewood. Thinking of Yahweh's Light, Shirone
muttered:
"Catch fire."
The logs suddenly warmed, and with a whoosh, a flame ignited, spreading
a gentle warmth.
Though deep in the mountains where patrols wouldn't likely come, he
still cast an illusion spell—just in case.
"Phew… I'm exhausted."
Leaning back against a tree trunk, Shirone gazed up at the sparkling
stars beyond the leaves.
"…Where am I, really?"
Though he had returned from the Realm of Infinity, the question that had
haunted humans since time immemorial remained unanswered.
"I won't think about it for now."
As long as Nane remained a threat, Shirone needed to focus on human
matters.
He pulled a letter from his coat.
"Searching for a needle in the desert, huh…"
It began with "To my dearest Shirone," handwritten by Uorin.
Shirone read silently.
First of all, congratulations. You've become a star of the Ivory
Tower. I wish I could run to you right now and throw myself into your arms, but I can't. It
saddens me.
No matter how many times he read it, the words made his scalp tingle
from embarrassment.
I believe that if it's my request, you'll listen.
A very Uorin-like shift in tone.
Can you come to me right away? It's urgent.
Because he owed Uorin so much, Shirone had wrestled with this line for a
long time.
But if you can't… if, for personal reasons, you're unable to
come…
She seemed to understand Shirone's circumstances—that he couldn't let
his emotions weigh on anyone.
There's something I want you to find on your journey to seal the
Altars. It's for the sake of humanity. I don't care how long it takes—but you must find
it.
For a request like this, Shirone would gladly oblige.
From now on, I'll tell you about the future I've foreseen.
Mitochondrial Eve.
Though there was no foundational event to compare it to in the past,
Uorin possessed another ability: future sight.
Shirone speculated that it was the ability to see timelines—something
exceedingly rare, even in all of Teraje's history.
It was also the reason the previous Teraje, Mistra, had killed countless
daughters and chosen Uorin as her direct heir.
Like the directions of space—north, south, east, west—this world will
begin to expand in four great directions.
Shirone studied the cross-shaped diagram drawn for clarity.
North and South were labeled Emptiness and Love. East and West were Good
and Evil.
I call this the "Compass of Law." From now on, all beings will
stand at a point somewhere within the combination of these four directions.
Where once the world revolved solely around Good and Evil, it now gained
two more axes—escape paths upward and downward.
At the very center of the Compass of Law was suffering: the human soul
endlessly agonizing, having lost its direction.
Mikaia Gaold. If Nane is the embodiment of Emptiness, you are the
embodiment of Love. Because you uphold the Law of Compassion, I suspected you would not be able to
come to me.
Even without future sight, one could infer the world's direction by
using the Compass of Law.
In the East, it's known as Yin-Yang Theory, or the I Ching (Book of
Changes). And the one who represents the axis of Good… is of course, Miro.
Because she inherited the will of Yolga, Fermi's mother.
By the time you receive this letter, Miro will likely have already
launched the Zion Project. In short, she has lit the torch of Goodness.
Now you understand what I'm trying to say.
Uorin's handwriting grew firmer from this point on.
According to the Compass of Law, it is highly likely that Extreme Evil
will soon appear in this world. Because Good and Evil call each other into being.
Even if you and Nane are locked in a close battle, the clash between
Good and Evil is a separate issue. A slight tilt in either direction will sweep away countless
beings aligned within the Compass of Law.
It would become a battle to claim the greatest share of the four
quadrants of law.
Zion is already operating at full capacity. At this point, I judge
it too difficult to push back Extreme Evil by force—so I've discovered a new method.
Shirone let out a long breath and read the next line.
Use Evil to destroy Extreme Evil.
If it were just about Shirone personally, perhaps this would trouble
him—but Yahweh didn't care about the dichotomy of Good and Evil, so he didn't feel particularly
conflicted.
This next part is important. Once you've committed it to memory,
tear it off and destroy it.
She had even drawn a perforation line for easy tearing. But since
Shirone could recreate anything with Material, he could restore it at any time.
Just like he had with this very letter.
The item you need to find is a dagger known as Lawslayer, classified as
an S-rank Object.
Just like Armand, the one I gave you, it's known as one of the strongest
weapons among Objects.
When Shirone saw the underlined phrase "the one I gave you," he couldn't
help but chuckle.
You're probably thinking this: If it's that powerful of an Object,
wouldn't it already be in the hands of some wealthy collector?
Correct.
But thankfully, that's not the case.
Although this might make it feel even more hopeless—no one knows where
Lawslayer is.
Why? Because… no one is looking for it.
That's when Shirone felt a surge of curiosity as well.
So here's a hint. Once you understand what kind of weapon it is, you
should be able to trace its route.
Lawslayer is, first and foremost, an extremely dangerous weapon. Because
this dagger is guaranteed to kill something—always.
That's simply the nature of the Object.
How does it kill? Through the law.
For example, if I hold Lawslayer and think of someone I truly want to
kill while pouring strong intent into that thought, then my will becomes embedded in the dagger.
It was chilling.
That's all it takes.
Once the will is embedded, the dagger will kill that target no matter
what, in any circumstance—even if it means bending the laws of causality itself.
At first, Shirone couldn't fully grasp the concept.
Let's say I have Lawslayer and fixate on killing a person named
A.
The means are infinite.
Maybe one day, one of my maids will steal Lawslayer. She might get run
over by a carriage.
A passerby finds it. Later, they get pickpocketed. The dagger continues
its journey, warping the laws of causality again and again, until it ends up in the hands of the
innkeeper where A is staying.
While cleaning rooms, the innkeeper examines Lawslayer and accidentally
enters the room where A is napping.
As they approach the bed, they trip over a wine bottle A dropped the
night before and fall forward…
Shirone turned the page.
Eventually, Lawslayer ends up lodged in A's heart—without a single inch
of deviation.
It was a law at work, something impossible unless the entire sequence of
events had been calculated from the very beginning.
Even though he had read it multiple times, Shirone couldn't help the
shiver that crept down his spine whenever he got to this part.
"This is insane."
Knowing how Objects were made, the words slipped out naturally.
Who could've done this?
How intense must their murderous intent have been to create such an
incomprehensible Object?
Almost as if having a conversation with him, Uorin had already written a
reply in the letter.
The reason I'm asking you to find Lawslayer is because its original
owner was Mirac Minerva, one of the Five Great Stars of the Ivory Tower.
The shock Shirone felt then was considerable.
When he realized that one of the stars of the Ivory Tower was
Lawslayer's owner, he briefly thought about asking Luber about it, but after finishing the letter, he
didn't even have the courage to look for her.
Minerva made her name known as a great witch about 700 years ago.
I met her a few times too, back when I was active as a witch
myself.
Of course, that was in a previous life—and soon after that period, the
great empire of Kashan was founded.
She's a tragic figure.
If someone like me, a Teraje, says she was tragic…
She wasn't a witch from the beginning, but in the end, she must have
become one.
When you observe human lives beyond the bounds of lifespan, you come to
understand something: that things inevitably turn out that way.
Because she was a woman. Because she was beautiful. Because of a mole
beneath her eye. Because of her personality. Because of the way the world flowed.
If reality is just the result of countless convergences, then the future
will simply unfold as determined by the wheel of law.
Veron of the Council of Ten departed to conduct the ultimate experiment
on this question, but it's not yet time to verify the results.
If free will truly exists, it should be capable of destroying the
future.
So for now, I'll call it a curse.
Minerva was born that way. Someone who makes you want to torment her,
hurt her, push her into despair—for no reason at all.
She was made to entertain bureaucrats from various countries starting at
the age of ten. It must've been a horrifying life.
Uorin spared her words.
A child too young to even fight back—the humiliation she suffered turned
into raw fury toward humanity…
And what was born from that was Lawslayer.
Mercury, Mirac.
A planet gifted to her directly by the Great Star, it had no life,
composed entirely of water.
A massive full moon filled the sky.
At its very center, where it tempted the hearts of witches, Minerva sat
on her broomstick, lost in thought.
She was just a ten-year-old girl.
"……"
Gazing up at the moonlight, she gave a faint smile and reached out her
hand.
"I need to cleanse my heart with moonlight."
As she gently stroked the moon, she pressed a hand to her chest and
wiped downward.
"I have to wash it clean."
Transparent tears fell into the sea, drop by drop.
"Clean… I have to be clean…"
As long as Nane doesn't close the world, the wounds carved into her will
never heal…
"I have to wipe it away."
Minerva endlessly rubbed her chest.
