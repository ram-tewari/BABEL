Chapter 551:
"Miro…?"
Shirone's eyes caught a face he could never forget.
Although they had fought against the heavenly army together, they had
operated in different regions, so he hadn't even known she was alive.
"It's been a while. How have you been?"
This was their second meeting since they encountered each other in
Miro's dimension.
Instead of the divine aura from back then, a more realistic presence
came first, but her beautiful appearance remained unchanged.
"What happened? Why are you here, Miro?"
"First, sit down. You've been starving for quite a
while."
As if in response, a growling sound came from his stomach.
Without a word, Shirone sat at the table, and Miro placed a bowl of
porridge in front of him.
"Eat. There's plenty."
"Thank you for the meal."
Shirone was so hungry he felt like he was going crazy, so he immediately
picked up the spoon and took a big bite.
'It's awful.'
The bitter taste was beyond imagination, and the porridge flowed back
into the bowl like a waterfall.
Miro propped her chin with both hands and smiled with her eyes.
"What's wrong? Is it hard to swallow?"
"Um… Is this some kind of medicine for my treatment?"
"No. It's just my breakfast."
With nothing to say, Shirone just stared at her, and Miro reached out
towards the bowl.
"If you don't want to eat, you can have it later."
"No, it's fine!"
Shirone hurriedly swallowed the bitter porridge.
As he ate, he thought that maybe she was concerned about his
long-starved stomach, but perhaps this was just Miro's cooking skill.
Anyway, the feeling of something entering his stomach was satisfying
enough.
While he ate, Miro told him what had happened so far.
The events in heaven, how Terraje and Miro had cooperated to enter the
Apocalypse to save Shirone, who was caught in debt due to the Valhalla Action.
"Rian…"
Shirone's nose tingled.
Although the Under Coder was just a copy of information and had nothing
to do with memories, the sight of Rian fighting for his life came to mind as if he had seen it with his
own eyes.
"Rian went through a lot. Of course, I had the hardest
time."
When Miro smiled, Shirone realized his mistake and added more.
"Ah, thank you. Miro, and also Marsha and Fermi. I owe you all a
debt I can't repay."
"Yes. But you'll have to repay it soon."
Shirone, who had finished a bowl of porridge, put down the utensils and
blinked.
"Repay…?"
"Did you think you were saved for free? Follow me. There's
something you need to do."
Miro didn't even clear the dishes and went outside the cabin.
When Shirone stepped out, the scenery of the mountains in late summer
unfolded before him.
"Where is this?"
"Where do you think? It's the place where you'll be rolling around
like a beast."
"Rolling around like a beast?"
Without answering, Miro entered a small training hall behind the
cabin.
Inside the wooden building, candles were lit along the walls, and there
were only two cushions.
"Sit down. From now on, follow what I teach you."
Although he sat facing Miro, Shirone was in a state of confusion.
It had only been two hours since he escaped the debt of the Valhalla
Action.
He hadn't even realized he had returned alive from heaven, and above
all, the faces of his parents and friends waiting for him came to mind.
"Um… Are we going to train?"
"Probably. Why, don't you want to?"
"No, it's not that…"
Shirone asked cautiously.
"Can I at least contact my family?"
Miro's smiling face suddenly turned cold.
"Don't be arrogant, Shirone."
With a whoosh, the candles tilted towards Shirone.
Facing her cat-like eyes, he swallowed hard.
"Whatever experience you had in heaven is already in the past.
What's left in front of you is the graduation exam of the magic school. Your competitors are training
hard even now. Or what, do you think you can graduate with your current skills?"
Shirone, deep in thought, looked straight at Miro and said.
"Yes."
Miro's expression softened, and she sighed.
"That's right. Actually, I think so too."
Shirone's magic had improved significantly in heaven.
The confidence that he could handle the graduation class was not
arrogance or delusion but a judgment based on a cold analysis of his own state.
"You are strong. Among your peers, there are few who can compare to
you. Of course, the Valhalla Action is sealed for now, but it's a function attached to Armand, so it's
separate from the graduation exam."
Shirone waited calmly for her to continue.
"You can graduate. But that doesn't guarantee you'll become a
mage."
"What do you mean?"
"Exactly what I said. The graduation exam only determines whether
you graduate. Whether you get a job as a mage and what kind of life you lead depends entirely on the
scouts' evaluation."
"I heard that from Professor Collie. So it's up to the individual
to handle the graduation class schedule."
"Exactly. And I can assure you…"
Miro twisted her lips slightly and said.
"You won't become a mage."
Shirone's eyes widened.
"A mage…"
"To be precise, the mage you want to be. Graduating from Alpheas
Magic School will get you an unofficial certificate. You could become a mercenary or join the Black
Line. But don't even dream of becoming an official mage in the association or rising to a high position
in the kingdom's magic department."
It felt like his heart was on fire, turning his insides upside
down.
"Why?"
"Because you killed a god."
"……"
"No matter what, the world is run by a few rulers. The world's
powers are watching you. Terraje won't abandon you, but even she is just one of the three emperors in
the Holy War. Do you think the other two emperors will stay still? I guarantee you'll be caught up in
all sorts of political strife, used, and discarded."
It didn't feel real, but it was probably true.
Miro, who had suffered from the checks of the world's leaders through
the Judgment of the 20, was telling him directly.
"Do you understand reality now? I also created a dimensional wall
and was trapped for 20 years. Of course, it was my choice, but if I had refused, I would have died. Just
like my family was exterminated."
Shirone suppressed the urge to cry bitterly.
"The strongest Banya who blocked the heavenly army. That's the
title given to me. Of course, as an individual, I'm strong enough. But Shirone, above humans is the
state. The system is stronger than force. Even Gaold, whom you know, lived in hiding for 20 years to
save me."
"Then what should I do from now on?"
"Get stronger."
Miro's answer was simple.
"Graduation isn't the problem. No country will make a move until
you graduate. Terraje will also use her power. The real storm begins after graduation. By then, you need
to be strong enough to at least protect yourself."
All the miscellaneous thoughts in Shirone's mind disappeared.
"I'll teach you about becoming a mage later. But that's something
to deal with after you get stronger."
"Yes, I understand. I'll start right away."
Miro thought it was convenient to talk to someone who could change their
stance quickly and then laid out the plan.
"I'll guide you, but what you need to focus on most while you're
with me isn't magic."
Shirone tilted his head.
"What else is there besides magic?"
"Incarnation techniques."
It was the incarnation techniques spoken of by the strongest Prajna of
humanity.
"Everyone has an incarnation. They just don't realize it. And even
if they do, mastering it to the point of calling it a technique is another matter."
Miro demonstrated it herself.
Behind her, the incarnation of Avalokiteshvara slowly emerged, with
twelve arms blooming like petals.
"This is…"
"Thousand-Armed Avalokiteshvara. If Prajna reveals their
incarnation outside their body through mental transcendence, the will of Prajna affects the laws of the
world."
When Miro widened her eyes, Avalokiteshvara's arms flew forward.
Shirone couldn't even react until the palm was about to pierce his body,
and his heart pounded belatedly.
"If I had done it for real, you would be dead."
Shirone nodded with a stiff expression.
"Up to this point, it's just a mental action. It's the
manifestation of that action through mental transcendence. You could call it a technique,
but…"
Miro closed her eyes, and the incarnation of Avalokiteshvara began to
grow larger.
As it filled the training hall, Shirone gaped and looked up at the
ceiling.
"If you refine it, you can do this too."
Mental transcendence - unity of mind and body.
When Miro clasped her hands together, following Avalokiteshvara's
movements, all the hundreds of candles were extinguished with a flick.
As the smell of burning wicks permeated the air, no one spoke.
The wall sparkled, and soon Miro was seen lighting each candle one by
one.
"How did you do that?"
"It's simple. Avalokiteshvara's many arms grabbed the wicks
simultaneously. You just couldn't see it."
"Can the power of the incarnation affect objects too?"
"Not everyone can do it. Incarnations differ from person to person
and have unique characteristics. The level is fixed, and I just applied it through scale
magic."
Miro, who had lit all the candles with Ignite, sat down.
"The basics of incarnation techniques are to strengthen the
incarnation. There are many methods, but let's use a sequence method for simplicity."
"Yes, I also learned the sequence method."
Miro chuckled.
"What you learn at magic school is how many seconds it takes for
zero to a hundred. But incarnations are on a different level."
Miro quickly drew a horizontal line with her finger.
"The speed of the sequence method increases mental strength. It
uses the concentration of counting to a hundred in one second. Therefore, unless the speed keeps
increasing, mental strength won't endlessly grow."
Shirone had also experienced this in practical exams.
"On the other hand, the important thing in handling incarnations is
the magnitude of numbers. You apply the size of the numbers to the incarnation. Let's try it now. Since
you're a Prajna, it should be easy to apply."
"Yes, I'll try."
Shirone took a deep breath and closed his eyes.
As he entered mental transcendence, the shadow of an angel with wings of
light appeared.
'I'll strengthen this with the sequence method.'
Using the modularization he learned at school, he quickly broke through
zero to a hundred and entered the thousands and tens of thousands.
'Still not enough?'
After five minutes, Miro yawned.
He couldn't tell how much he had to count to strengthen the
incarnation.
'In that case…!'
Driven by determination, Shirone activated the Immortal Function.
Although it was dangerous to fully expand and surpass the numbers, he
controlled it, and the magnitude of the numbers increased exponentially.
Shirone's incarnation began to become clearer, and as the shadow
disappeared, a distinct outline emerged.
"Gasp!"
At that moment, Shirone, who had deviated from the sequence, exhaled
heavily.
"Not bad. It's impressive to succeed on the first try. So, how far
did you count?"
Catching his breath, Shirone recalled.
"Um, 58,387,210,000…"
"That's enough."
Miro cut him off as soon as the first digit came out.
"It's fine if you're slow, but for ten minutes, that's too little.
You can't handle the incarnation with that."
"Then, how far did you go, Miro?"
"Me? Hmm. I didn't really use the sequence method. For example,
when I extinguished the candles earlier…"
Miro, who had been thinking with her arms crossed, said.
"It must have exceeded 10 quadrillion (10^16)."
"Quadrillion…"
Shirone couldn't think of it as a number a human could count.
"Why are you so surprised? That's the level you need to reach
during the vacation period."
Shirone's face turned pale.
"Me? I have to?"
"You can do it. I'll help you."
Miro had unprecedented talent in human history, but even so, she wasn't
entirely sure.
"I won't say it's easy. But it's a problem if you're scared
already. You've entered such a world."
Miro recalled a recent event.
"There was a situation where I had to stop Satan in heaven. It took
some time, but I eventually delivered the best strike. If you look at the incarnation technique at that
time in terms of the sequence method, how much do you think it was?"
When Shirone, who couldn't even guess, shook his head, Miro raised her
index finger and said.
"One."
"One?"
"Unfathomable."
Shirone began to count as he had read in books, not intuitively
understanding the unit.
'Trillion, quadrillion, quintillion, sextillion, septillion, octillion,
nonillion, decillion, undecillion, duodecillion, tredecillion, quattuordecillion, quindecillion.'
As the units grew larger, his eyes gradually rolled upward.
'Unfathomable…'
It was 10^64.
