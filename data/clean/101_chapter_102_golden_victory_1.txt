Chapter 102:
Golden Victory (1)
"God does not lie. No, God is a being who cannot lie."
An omnipotent God cannot lie because any word can be made into reality. But Shirone saw
it in the woman's eyes. She was hiding something.
"You said there was no way back when I asked. But that wasn't true."
"Whether there's a way back or not, since I said there isn't, there isn't. That's
the reason."
"That's a lie."
The woman flinched. Shirone didn't miss her reaction. As she panicked, the scenery of the
temple rippled as if submerged in water.
"Are you really a God?"
He had doubted it from the beginning. Of course, there was no reason why the form of God
imagined by humans had to be the real God, but even so, the woman's reactions were too human-like.
The woman nodded as if she understood something.
"Shirone. We have different concepts of God. That's why there's been a
misunderstanding. God is not as sublime as you think. Just a designer of the world."
As the woman extended her hand, a glass orb floated above her palm. Inside the orb was a
small village landscape with scattered people standing around.
"As you can see, I created this village. Of course, it's just a model. But I can
also move people like this."
The woman inserted her finger into the orb and moved a farmer feeding his horse to a
nearby field.
"See, the farmer has moved. Changing space means granting time. Can you find any
difference from the world you lived in? No. This is what God is. I've become the God of this village
now."
The woman pushed the orb closer to Shirone's face.
"God is ultimately nothing more than a designer who can create a dimension one level
lower than the one they inhabit. Yet why does God feel like an absolute being? That's the interesting part.
This farmer won't even realize that some force moved him to the nearby field."
The woman pulled the orb back and cradled it in both hands. As she spread her arms wide,
the village instantly expanded, covering the temple.
Shirone looked around. He had somehow entered the village. The two stood at opposite ends
of a path, facing each other. The other people remained motionless.
The woman approached the farmer tilling the field.
"This farmer doesn't know how imperfect the world I created is. Because he was born
here. There's only one way to find out."
"The Immortal Function."
"Correct. The Immortal Function expands the senses to the whole. This village is
contained within the glass orb. So it's no exaggeration to say there's no world beyond the orb."
The woman placed her hand on the farmer's shoulder. The farmer turned into light,
spreading across the sky. As the woman clenched her fist, the scenery shrank, and the entire village
returned to the orb.
Shirone silently stared at the orb. It was filled with light from the farmer.
"This farmer expanded into the whole through the Immortal Function. But even then,
he can't perceive my existence. The same goes for you, Shirone."
Shirone felt the farmer's state wasn't someone else's problem. If the woman hadn't
attempted hijacking, he too would have dissipated into meaningless light.
"What do you want from me? Knowing these facts, what can I possibly do?"
"This is it, Shirone."
The woman placed the orb between her palms and pressed. The orb shrank, compressing into
a black dot.
"Spot……."
"Yes. This is the true nature of the world we live in. The world you inhabit is
ultimately just a dot. But inside, endless worlds unfold. The spacetime matrix."
The woman demonstrated. The scenery in the orb disappeared into the dot, and a new world
was born. Worlds within worlds, and again within worlds……. Then, as she clasped her hands together, the orb
vanished. Billions of worlds disappeared in an instant.
"Please enter the spacetime I created. There, create your own spacetime. That's how
a perfect universe is born."
For some reason, she seemed to desire innovation in her world. It wasn't an unpleasant
proposal. Becoming a God of another world in exchange for death seemed like a decent ending.
"I can't do that."
The woman asked again, as if she couldn't understand.
"Why? You have no reason or justification to refuse."
"If I enter your spacetime, another person like me will have to be sacrificed."
A flicker of sadness passed through the woman's eyes. But the change in emotion was
brief, and she soon returned to a cold expression.
"What does that matter? It's your created world anyway."
"But you're not a God."
For the first time, hostility appeared in the woman's eyes.
"You're a human like me, inhabiting the same world. The proof is that you brought me
here before I was completely disassembled. If you were truly a God, shouldn't you exist outside the
world?"
The woman didn't deny it. But that didn't change the outcome.
"Shirone, you must follow my words. I called you here for a purpose. If you refuse,
I can put you through the disassembly process even now."
His mind was reconstructed by the woman's power. So perhaps she could freely revert it.
But now that there was even the slightest chance of returning to his original world, Shirone had no
intention of obeying.
"I won't be disassembled."
The woman extended her hand to Shirone and invoked the omnipotence of disassembly. But
Shirone didn't disassemble. He had already entered the Spirit Zone.
"You've solved another problem. An excellent choice."
The woman's arm slowly lowered.
"Now I understand. The Spirit Zone was also a spot."
A spot could be infinitely delved into by the principles of the spacetime matrix. That
was why Shirone, as a spiritual entity, could enter the Spirit Zone again.
"And this place…… is your Spirit Zone."
Shirone concentrated the energy of light. The woman frowned uncomfortably. The red glow
emanating from Shirone's body pressed against her with a menacing aura.
