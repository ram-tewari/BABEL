Chapter 56:
Gauge Symmetry (2)
While her friends were chatting, Shirone squinted and observed carefully. The atmosphere
felt oddly familiar.
"Wait… is that Ms. Siena?"
"Ms. Siena?"
Neid leaned forward and took a closer look. It was definitely her. She wasn't wearing
glasses, and her trademark neatly tied-up hair was loose, but it was unmistakably Siena.
"Wow. That's incredible. Can a person change that much?"
"Right? With makeup, she looks like a completely different person. By the way, Shirone,
how did you recognize her? You've got a sharp eye for women, huh?"
"Huh? No. She just looked like her."
The reason Shirone recognized her at a glance was that he had seen Siena with her hair
down before. But he couldn't bring himself to mention that the teacher had visited him late at night, so he
brushed it off.
"But where is she going? She's even wearing makeup."
"Today's her day off, and it's a holiday too. Maybe she's on a date or something."
"But wasn't Ms. Siena distant from men?"
"Still, she must have someone she meets. At 26, she's at the age where people start
thinking about marriage. Even teachers have to get married someday."
"Hmm. I guess so…"
Neid stroked his chin, lost in thought, when a sudden idea lit up in his mind.
"Should we follow her? I'm curious."
"Follow her? Why? It's her private life."
"We don't have anything else to do anyway. Sitting here won't solve anything, and my head
feels like it's about to explode. It's a holiday, so as long as we're back before dark, it's fine."
Iruki was skeptical.
"Do you even know how to tail someone without getting caught? She's a certified 6th-rank
mage. Even without Spirit Zone, her awareness is on another level. We'll be spotted immediately."
"Then let's do this. Iruki, you track the teacher, and Shirone will act as the relay.
I'll stop by the lab and come back."
Shirone saw through Neid's plan.
"The invisibility cloak?"
"Exactly! If we follow her under the invisibility cloak, even the teacher won't notice.
She has no reason to deploy Spirit Zone while on a date. What do you think?"
"That's not a bad idea. Alright, I'll start tailing her now. Shirone, you bring Neid to
me later."
As Iruki sprang up from the bench and began chasing Siena, Shirone sighed. These kids
were truly exceptional when it came to mischief.
"Then I'll go get the invisibility cloak. Shirone, don't lose track of Iruki. Can you
keep checking points while moving?"
Since the city's layout was constrained by artificial structures like roads, tracking key
points would give them a rough idea of her route. They couldn't let Neid fall too far behind, so it was
crucial to determine the rendezvous point midway.
Reluctantly, Shirone nodded. He hated how his mind had already calculated everything even
in this situation.
"Got it. But hurry. I can't guarantee more than 10 minutes."
"Haha! That's our Shirone! I'm counting on you!"
Neid dashed off to the research lab, while Shirone tailed Iruki and returned after 10
minutes to pick up Neid at the main gate. From there, they ran quickly and caught up with Iruki in another
10 minutes. When the two arrived, Iruki shook his head with a troubled expression.
"This is weird."
"Why? Did you lose the teacher?"
"No. But I almost did. She's unusually alert, as if she's on a battlefield."
"Oho. This is getting interesting. Maybe she's dealing with forbidden items for extra
cash, just like us?"
Shirone shot Neid a disapproving look.
"The teacher wouldn't do something like that."
"Anyway, let's go. Now that we have the invisibility cloak, we can get closer."
The three covered themselves with the cloak and continued following Siena. As they
entered the commoners' district, the streets were bustling with people setting up shop since dawn, making
the tailing easier.
Siena stopped at a two-story house. The trio peeked out from under the cloak, watching
to see who would open the door. When Siena glanced around, they ducked back in like turtles, their hearts
pounding.
"Why is she so cautious? Whose house is this? It's just an ordinary commoner's home."
Driven by curiosity, they peeked out again. There was no need to freeze like criminals
when they hadn't done anything wrong.
A young man opened the door and stepped out. The moment he saw Siena, they embraced
deeply.
"W-what in the world…!"
Shirone and his friends were shocked. Siena's nickname, "Frozen Siena," referred not
only to her magical specialty but also to her cold demeanor toward men. Yet here she was, secretly involved
with one. They exchanged glances, confirming this was reality.
"What's going on? That was definitely a man, right?"
"We'll find out if we go in."
Shirone was startled.
"What? How can we just enter someone's house?"
"But we can't leave now, right? We need to know for sure."
"The teacher is single, so of course she can meet men. Why are you making such a fuss?"
Iruki's thoughts were different. Given the circumstances, there were too many oddities.
"Something smells fishy. There's nothing wrong with the teacher meeting a man. But why
hide it? She's officially single at school. And her being so cautious is also suspicious."
"That's exactly it. So let's check it out."
Shirone asked nervously.
"What are you planning?"
"We'll go in through the basement storage. Don't worry. Even if we get caught, it's not a
big deal. It's not like it's a stranger's house since the teacher went in."
Shirone gaped.
"Isn't that… a very subjective way of thinking?"
"I don't know. Anyway, I'm going. I'm dying of curiosity."
As Neid and Iruki moved, Shirone sighed and followed. He didn't like going along with it,
but leaving the two alone was riskier.
At the basement entrance, Neid examined the iron door. It was horizontally installed and
firmly locked.
He pulled out a pen-like device from his pocket and pressed a button, causing a sharp
metal needle to extend. It was one of Neid's patented inventions: a lock-picking tool.
Picking a lock required precise pressure on its internal mechanisms, and Neid's device
could adjust the needle to a hundredth of a centimeter, making common locks easy to crack.
After fiddling with the lock, Neid twisted his wrist, and with a click, it opened.
Inside, a ladder led downward. Shirone felt a sense of futility. People trusted locks to
keep them safe, forgetting that locks were also man-made.
The scent of onions, typical of a food storage, tickled their noses as they descended.
While Shirone closed the iron door, Neid pressed his ear against the door leading upstairs to listen for
movement.
"Good. The first floor is quiet too. Let's go up."
Under the invisibility cloak, the trio floated like ghosts with only their heads visible.
The first floor had an open-plan living room and kitchen, but no one was there. Ascending to the second
floor, they sensed movement from a room at the end of the hallway. Siena's voice could be heard.
"What are you doing? Hurry up."
Shirone and Neid exaggeratedly moved their facial muscles, but Iruki pressed a finger to
his lips. A man's voice followed.
"Wait. I'm not ready yet. Take off your clothes first."
"When will your wife be back?"
"She went to the market. It'll take at least two hours."
Neid couldn't contain his excitement—or rather, his shock.
Shirone felt similarly. He never imagined the man Siena was meeting was married.
***
"Aren't you wearing it today?"
"No. Straighten your back a bit more."
"Like this?"
"Yeah, like that. Lift your legs slightly. Yes, that's it."
The trio's faces turned bright red. As adults, they understood romantic relationships,
but this was an affair. If the school found out, Siena's teaching career would be over.
"Shall we begin? Don't move."
"Don't worry. I've held out so far."
Neid looked at his friends with anger. Understanding his intent, Shirone and Iruki
nodded.
Love knew no borders, but considering the betrayed wife's feelings, they had to stop
this.
Neid's hand trembled as he gripped the doorknob. Waiting longer would provide irrefutable
evidence, but he couldn't bear to see their respected teacher in such a state.
The doorknob turned roughly, and the three barged in without hesitation.
"You scoundrel! Ms. Siena! I'm so disappointed!"
Neid's shout was met with silence.
Shirone and Iruki, who had braced themselves to close their eyes, stared blankly at the
scene inside the room. It was nothing like what they had imagined.
Siena, sitting on the bed, turned toward the door. Across from her was a large canvas.
"Huh? What?"
The trio gaped like fish. A single thought crossed their minds: this was a massive
misunderstanding, a distortion of reality.
"What? How did you all get here…?"
Siena was equally shocked. Her face turned red at the sudden appearance of her students
in a private setting, but her embarrassment soon turned to a frown.
"Did you follow me?"
"Huh? No, it's just… we were sitting in the park, zoning out, when you walked by. So we…"
"So you did follow me!"
"Eek! We're sorry!"
The three shrank back simultaneously. Given her personality, a scolding wouldn't be the
end of it. But contrary to their expectations, no further reprimand came. Siena seemed lost in thought.
'How did they manage to follow me?'
She had been vigilant, avoiding Spirit Zone but maintaining awareness. Yet her students
had tailed her effortlessly.
"What's that behind you? Hand it over."
Neid shook his head fearfully, but when Siena's eyes gleamed dangerously, he hastily
handed over the cloak. She snatched it angrily, then stared in disbelief as her arm disappeared when she
held it. Wrapping it around her waist, her torso vanished.
"Neid. Is this another one of your patented inventions?"
"Yes."
"You really made this yourself?"
"B-but I didn't sell it on the black market! I have some conscience left."
"Sigh."
At Siena's sigh, the trio flinched. But she didn't lecture them further. As a certified
6th-rank mage, being tailed was more a reflection of her own carelessness than her students' mischief.
"Why did you come into this room? You knew you'd get caught. And what's this about being
disappointed?"
Shirone spoke in a trembling voice.
"At first, we followed you as a joke, but when we saw you hugging a man, we came in.
After eavesdropping, we thought you were having an affair and…"
"What? An affair?"
Siena's face turned as red as a beet. It was the first time they'd seen her so
flustered.
"An affair? How could you think that of me!"
Neid interjected.
"But it sounded like that! How could we not misunderstand? Telling you to take off your
clothes, adjust your posture… Who is that man anyway?"
Neid pointed at the man hiding behind the canvas.
"Him?"
A warm voice replied, followed by the sound of a chair scraping. Moments later, the man
stepped out from behind the canvas.
The trio stared blankly. He was a handsome man in his late twenties with golden hair
reaching his waist. But what truly shocked them wasn't his looks—it was the ragged cloth tied over his eyes.
He was blind. And a painter.
'What's going on?'
Shirone's face paled. The familiar eerie sensation he felt every night began creeping
over him again.
'Why now? This never happens during the day.'
His hair stood on end, and his bones felt frozen. Clutching his trembling wrist, he took
a deep breath. Sensing his distress, Neid patted his back.
"Hey, Shirone? You okay? Don't worry too much. It's not like the teacher will kill us."
"Huh? Oh, no. I'm fine now."
Shirone gradually regained his composure. The chill was far more intense than usual, but
it also faded quickly.
"Siena, forgive them. They're your students, after all."
"That's exactly why I can't forgive them. Following me is one thing, but trespassing?
You've learned all the wrong things."
The man smiled kindly.
"But they're your students. They probably thought it was okay since you were here."
Seizing the opportunity, Neid chimed in.
"Haha! That's exactly what we thought! Really! Shirone doesn't lie, so you can ask him.
We'd never enter a stranger's house. We're not thieves."
Shirone nodded repeatedly.
"It's true. Neid really said that to me. I heard it clearly."
Siena massaged her temples, as if suffering a headache. Their innocent expressions were
almost infuriating.
'Such innocent kids, yet they pull stunts like this? Crafty little…'
Trespassing with an invisibility cloak, picking locks—none of this was excusable.
Yet the man kept smiling. Above all, he was happy to meet Siena's students.
"I'm Armin. Siena and I grew up like siblings. I also studied at the Olipher school."
As Armin extended his hand, Neid eagerly shook it.
"Ah, I see. We apologize for our foolish misunderstanding. And for barging in
unannounced."
Siena snapped.
"Trespassing! Don't sugarcoat it as 'barging in'!"
"Hehe. That's why we're apologizing. Honestly, we're relieved the misunderstanding about
you is cleared up."
"We're sorry, Teacher. It was a silly misunderstanding."
Siena placed her hands on her hips and exhaled sharply. The awkwardness of meeting her
students in a private setting was mutual, and silence lingered.
"Sit down and talk. I'll bring some tea."
Armin tactfully stepped out. Despite his blindness, his footsteps were precise, leaving
the trio amazed again.
"Teacher, who is he? And what was that about taking off clothes? That's why we
misunderstood."
Siena shook her head as if the suspicion was absurd.
"Armin oppa is my senior from the same school. He was once hailed as the most talented
genius in Olipher's history. Now he's a painter, but his talent far surpassed mine even when we were young."
"He must be amazing. But if it's okay to ask, what happened to his eyes…?"
Siena's expression darkened, making Neid regret his question. But she quickly composed
herself.
"He lost his eyes saving me."
"What? Because of you?"
"When I was nine, I fell into Overflow. I began vaguely understanding the world's
principles, and everything terrified me. I cried every day. Even now, I remember it as sheer torment."
Siena smiled bitterly.
"Armin oppa saved me. He pulled me from darkness into light. He was only 11 at the time."
Eleven was still a child. Yet he rescued Siena from Overflow, a testament to his
extraordinary talent.
"So his advice helped you overcome Overflow."
Siena shook her head.
"No. I wasn't that strong. I was just a brat who acted high and mighty because of the
Olipher name. Back then, I hated oppa. He always told me the same thing: 'Never look away from what comes to
you.' It annoyed me. If something's scary, you close your eyes. If you can't bear it, you run. Looking back,
I was such a fool."
Shirone couldn't imagine Siena, a certified 6th-rank mage, cowering in fear.
"Then how did you overcome Overflow?"
"I hated oppa finding me, so I'd hide in the mountains, fields, or riversides. He always
found me, but I'd run again. One day, during this game of hide-and-seek… the incident happened."
Siena winced in pain.
"It was the rainy season, and it had poured for days. Even in that weather, I wandered by
the river. Looking back, it might've been fine if I'd just passed by. But coincidentally, the dam upstream
collapsed. Before I knew it, I was swept away by the flood. It was massive enough to submerge half the
village."
Siena recalled the events of 17 years ago.
She was swallowed by the river without even screaming. When she surfaced, thrashing
desperately, the scenery was completely different. Debris and livestock floated downstream, while broken
trees drifted ominously.
For the first time, Siena tasted the terror of death.
'Help me. Please…!'
She knew no one would come. This area, far from the school, was a no-man's-land between a
stream and a swamp. Now a raging river, no one would expect a little girl to be here.
The water dragged her under rapidly. The opposite bank was impossibly far, the
once-gentle stream now a 25-meter-wide torrent. The water was black, with whirlpools forming where it
crashed against rocks.
"Help! Someone, help me!"
For the first time, Siena begged shamelessly. Her pride as the Olipher family's only
daughter meant nothing against nature's fury. Her innate talent couldn't overcome the flood. All she could
do was scream for help.
"Siena!"
Like a divine revelation, Siena mustered all her strength to look up. Armin was perched
precariously on a bent tree branch over the river.
"Oppa! Oppa!"
"Give me your hand!"
Armin reached toward the water. Just as Siena tried to grab it, a sudden whirlpool
dragged her under.
"Siena!"
When she resurfaced, it was her legs, not her face, that broke the surface. Without
hesitation, Armin dove in. Riding the current, he swam swiftly, grabbed Siena, and pulled her head above
water. Choking, Siena was barely conscious.
"Gah! Gah!"
"Siena! Snap out of it! We need to get out!"
"S-save me! Save me!"
Past the whirlpools, even deadlier obstacles awaited. Armin expanded his Spirit Zone to
navigate around them. At 11, it was his only option.
