Chapter 697:
Reunion Chapter (6)
Walker, a master swordsman who had authored the beginner's guide to Kazura's palace
swordsmanship, was unparalleled in both theory and practice.
Once, he had doubted Shirone and used the eye technique "Clear," but this time, he didn't
even dare attempt it.
'Is that really the same boy from back then?'
An overwhelming aura radiated from Shirone, making it clear he was untouchable.
'A candidate for the Ivory Tower. That's what makes it so frustrating.'
If Shirone had been the first prince, Kazura might still have had a glimmer of hope.
Since Kashan's Terraze had taken control of Kazura, the kingdom's power had rapidly
declined, and defeatism had spread among its people.
Kashan was unbeatable.
The certainty of the kingdom's eventual downfall had crushed the will of countless
talented individuals striving for the future.
But what about Shirone?
He now stood before Walker with strength rivaling any mage in Kazura.
"You've trained incredibly hard. You're almost inhuman now."
Shirone humbly replied,
"You flatter me. I haven't achieved anything yet."
"…Is that so?"
This marked the end of their personal reunion.
Walker's expression stiffened as he knelt before Shirone and formally carried out his
duty.
"Captain of the Royal Guard, Ritni Walker, delivers His Majesty's message. The king has
invited you to the palace."
Adoks and Miguelan's faces lost all color.
"Th-the captain of the royal guard?"
Compared to Adoks, a mere centurion, Walker was a figure at the pinnacle of power.
The appearance of Kazura's greatest swordsman alone would have shaken the village, but
his bowing to Shirone was a shocking event.
'Who the hell is that guy?'
Shirone turned to Emma.
"We'll be going now. Thank you."
With the king's approval, there would be no further diplomatic issues.
'I need to signal Kido.'
As Shirone headed for the door, Adoks blocked his path.
"Wait! No matter how esteemed you are, you can't just leave like this!"
Letting Shirone go without a word would disgrace him as both a soldier and a man.
"Respected Captain, I don't know who this person is, but he tried to assault my wife. We
need to get to the bottom of this."
Walker's face flushed with anger.
Asserting his own claims in front of a foreign dignitary, especially after the king's
command had been issued, laid bare Kazura's current state.
'No, this is just self-consciousness.'
Walker calmed himself and frowned at Adoks' words.
'He tried to assault his wife?'
It was highly unlikely given Shirone's character.
"Is this man's claim true, Lord Shirone?"
"Yes, it's true."
If he told the truth, their lives would change.
Only they could know what came first, so Shirone wanted to leave things as they were, as
if they had never happened.
Lost in thought, Walker nodded.
"Hmm, there must be circumstances. Let's leave now. The carriage is ready."
Adoks stared blankly.
"Please wait, Captain. How can there be circumstances for assault?"
"Shut up, you fool."
Walker glared at Adoks, his anger barely contained.
"If this were the Age of Glory, your head would've rolled by now."
How lax had the border security been to let Shirone infiltrate the village so easily?
The eye technique "Pressing" activated, and Adoks gasped for air.
"Guh… guh…"
It felt like someone was strangling him.
"P-please… spare me…"
Just before losing consciousness, Walker coldly turned away and headed for the door,
unable to suppress his fury.
"I'll wait outside."
Adoks collapsed to the floor. Shirone and Rian stepped over him without a second glance
and exited.
Walker's subordinates had brought a carriage.
Normally, a royal carriage would have been prepared, but speed was essential, so they had
minimized the weight.
Inside the carriage, Walker spoke.
"This is Kazura's current state. I'm too ashamed to show my face."
