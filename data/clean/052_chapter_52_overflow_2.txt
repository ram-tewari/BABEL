Chapter 52:
Overflow (2)
The faculty office was unusually busy.
Most teachers would chat or drink tea between classes, but now they were all sorting
through paperwork.
Classifying dozens of club applications, recording them in reports, and forwarding them
to the responsible departments was more labor-intensive than expected.
Siena, who'd just finished teaching, joined the effort without complaint.
"My, all these applications? They're pouring in this week."
"Hoho. It's the end of the semester. This always happens."
With the semester ending and vacation starting, students had to submit free-topic papers
by the next term.
But these projects could be replaced by club presentations, so even unaffiliated students
tended to join clubs temporarily around this time.
Sad, who was helping, chimed in.
"Kids these days are all about cutting corners. It wasn't like this before. Clubs and
projects used to be about passion. Nowadays, they're too focused on efficiency."
"Haha! Times have changed. Competition is fiercer than ten years ago, and the kids are
under more stress. Forming temporary clubs buys them time for personal study."
"That's true, but aren't they too fixated on results? School should have some romance
too."
The teacher could only offer a bitter smile in agreement. As he prepared to forward the
documents, Siena noticed one had fallen under the desk.
"Here's a missing one."
"Huh? When did this come in? Must've been left during class."
"Fufu. Someone must've been in a hurry."
Curious about which student had tried to cut corners, Siena examined the form. Upon
seeing the name, she adjusted her glasses in disbelief.
"This says… Shirone, right?"
The teacher peeked over.
"Indeed. Arian Shirone. I thought he wasn't interested in clubs. Well, with everyone
gaming the system, it's understandable."
Sad snatched the form and frowned.
"That doesn't seem right. This club—it's quite infamous, isn't it?"
"Let me see again."
Siena took the form back and checked the club Shirone had joined.
It was indeed infamous.
'The Supernatural Parapsychology Club.'
The name struck like lightning in a clear sky.
Recalling the annual battles with graduates from this club made her head throb.
'Wait… Now that I think about it…'
Hadn't most of the troublemakers from the Supernatural Parapsychology Club graduated last
year? She vaguely remembered hearing that funding had been cut due to a lack of members.
The teacher smiled.
"Come to think of it, Iruki's also part of this club. Maybe they get along."
Sad let out a hollow laugh.
"I heard they were impressive during the targeting test. But I wouldn't have guessed
they'd be close. They don't seem like a good match."
Siena massaged her temples.
"Yes. That's the problem."
If the genius Iruki had persuaded him, even the stubborn Shirone might've been swayed.
But Iruki?
The school's biggest troublemaker. Though both were geniuses, Shirone and Iruki pursued
opposite paths.
They might help each other grow—or one might consume the other, leading to mutual
destruction. The school would suffer a huge loss either way.
'Maybe it's for the best. Let's sever this cursed chain here.'
Siena flipped through the documents to check who the club president was.
As expected, it was Neid.
The two closest to Shirone lately were both in this club.
Now she understood why Shirone, who'd avoided clubs until now, had joined.
'Neid's homeroom teacher is…'
Siena scanned the teachers' names to find the responsible faculty member. Upon seeing
it, she sighed and rubbed her forehead.
'Sigh. Of all people…'

On the outskirts of the magic academy stood a training hall.
Etela sat cross-legged in meditation.
The Carthis Monastery, where she belonged, was the kingdom's largest, with over a
thousand monk trainees. Hundreds of children entered each year, but only a handful were recognized as
priests.
Etela stood out even among them.
She'd surpassed high-ranking nobles—even royalty—to become the youngest bishop.
The title of bishop wasn't earned by skill alone. Etela had caught the elders' eyes
because of her innate saintly nature.
To become the light and salt of the world.
With this vow, Etela had turned her back on wealth and fame to become a teacher at
Alpheas Magic Academy.
'Thank you. For allowing me to safely complete another day of education.'
Thus, Etela differed from other teachers. Her background wasn't a school or magic
association but the monastery.
If mages viewed the world critically, monks sought to embrace it whole without analysis.
Infinite gratitude to nature for her existence, and a sense of duty to share that feeling
with others—that was all Etela was.
She slowly opened her eyes. Her irises shimmered, piercing the void.
Through the Spirit Zone, she sensed a woman approaching.
Ordinary Spirit Zones couldn't discern gender, but Etela was a Joiner—a master of the
Spirit Zone—and her detection skills surpassed most mages.
If a normal Spirit Zone was like air, a Joiner's was like liquid. The denser medium
allowed for far clearer sensory perception.
Etela rose and waited. After a long while, Siena entered, and Etela greeted her with a
smile.
"Welcome, Teacher Siena."
Siena wasn't surprised Etela had been expecting her. She knew how advanced a Joiner's
Spirit Zone was.
"You were meditating. Am I interrupting?"
"Not at all. I'd just finished. Please, come in."
"Then pardon my intrusion."
Though kneeling on a cushion was unfamiliar, Siena sat respectfully and accepted the tea
Etela offered.
Following monastery etiquette, she remained silent until finishing the bitter tea.
Though summer days were long, the golden sunset had already deepened. Wanting to start
before dark, Siena emptied her cup quickly.
Etela opened the conversation considerately.
"You wouldn't come all this way for something trivial."
"Yes. There's something I'd like to discuss."
"You're Neid's homeroom teacher, correct?"
"Yes. He's bright and kind. His grades are above average, and he's talented. He gets
along well with others too."
"He's the president of the Supernatural Parapsychology Club, right?"
"Of course. Did Neid do something wrong?"
Siena straightened, her tone firm.
"Yes. I plan to disband the club he leads. As his teacher, I wanted to inform you first."
Etela sipped her tea thoughtfully. After a long pause, she spoke.
"If it's your decision, it must be for the students' sake. But this time, I'd like to
hear your reasons."
"The Supernatural Parapsychology Club has always been problematic. It was notorious even
before I arrived. Of course, its achievements matched, so the school tolerated it. But things have changed.
According to records, it hasn't held a single official presentation in three years. It's done nothing but
cause trouble deserving of punishment."
"But as you said, that's in the past, isn't it? The current members are Neid, Iruki, and
Shirone. Class Four-caliber students. Shouldn't we see what they do? Besides, the Supernatural
Parapsychology Club is one of Alpheas's oldest. Guildmaster Gauld was a member, wasn't he?"
