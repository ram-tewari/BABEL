Depression (1)
The army of hell split into two in the central city of Kashan.
Natasha and Zetaro led the 40 million troops heading towards the
central continent, where the Holy War was stationed.
The remaining 10 million, under Balkan's orders, charged towards
Kashan's capital.
In Aganos, the capital of Kashan, a grand party was held on the eve
of a war that would determine the empire's fate.
In the grand hall filled with lively music, all the prominent nobles
of Kashan gathered.
'Why are they holding a party in such times?' Their faces were
uniformly grim, and the bad news coming through their secretaries weighed heavily on their
hearts.
"Is this what a human party is like?"
Kido, looking at the long table laden with countless delicacies,
swallowed his saliva.
"Where should I start?"
He wasn't picky about food; he would eat anything edible.
As he walked from one end of the table to the other, stuffing his
mouth, the nobles frowned.
"What's with that goblin?"
"I heard he's the Empress's pet."
"Pet? But he's carrying a weapon?"
Weapons were strictly prohibited except for the guards, so Kido's
spear was particularly conspicuous.
"Burp!"
As he let out a loud belch, the noblewomen covered their noses and
scattered.
"Even if I die, I should eat before I go."
Even for Kido, today's banquet was unusual.
Though he had no interest in politics, rumors suggested that Kashan
would fall within seven days.
'Is this like a last supper?'
Since following Uorin into the capital, he had seen how much the
Empress possessed.
It was no wonder she seemed to have lost her mind, knowing all of it
could turn to dust.
"Her Majesty the Empress is entering!" At the guard's
shout, all the nobles stopped what they were doing and turned towards the door.
Slowly, the door opened, and Uorin entered with a bright expression,
wearing a black dress.
Adorned with diamonds and pearls, her beauty left the nobles in a
daze.
"Wow..."
Even Kido, whose aesthetic sense was inferior to humans, was
momentarily speechless.
'Beautiful.'
It was the first time he was captivated by a living form.
"Good evening, everyone."
Everyone focused on Uorin's words.
"You must be wondering why we're holding a grand party in the
midst of a war."
Kido glanced at Gando beside Uorin but couldn't read his
expression.
"To be blunt, the war situation is not good. And we might...
lose the war."
It felt like a lump in his throat.
"But don't worry."
Uorin smiled beautifully.
"Kashan will never fall. I will use every means to protect the
empire and continue its powerful history forever."
The name Teraje, which had never known failure since Kashan's
founding, inspired confidence.
"Enjoy yourselves. We are showing the world that we are not
afraid."
The nobles shouted in unison.
"Long live Her Majesty the Empress! Long live Her Majesty the
Empress!"
The musicians quickly resumed playing, and Uorin approached the
nobles, making eye contact with each one.
"How is it? Are you enjoying the party?"
"...What are you thinking?"
While boosting morale was important, Kido thought this war was
hopeless.
"Well, what do you think?"
Kido frowned, thinking of the only way to prevent Kashan's
destruction.
"Surely you don't mean..."
Uorin extended her hand.
"Shall we dance?"
Kido turned his head and snorted at her small, delicate hand.
"Hmph, a goblin dancing..."
"Come here."
As Uorin pulled Kido by the wrist into the ballroom, the nobles
looked shocked.
"Is she really going to dance with a pet?"
Only Gando and Wind Guards knew Kido's true identity.
They thought the Empress had gone mad, but no one could diminish
Uorin's beauty.
"I don't know how."
At Kido's grumbling, Uorin smiled and led him.
"It's easy. Just follow me and turn like this."
As Kido focused on the dance, he glanced at Uorin.
"Are you afraid of dying?" Uorin gave a sad smile.
"If the army of hell captures Kashan, Habitz will come for me
first."
Even Uorin, with Teraje's blood, couldn't imagine the disaster he
would bring.
"Even if my long life ends in death, I can accept it. But I
still have a reason to live."
"Shirone?"
Uorin didn't answer this time either.
"I'm not saying I'll give up Kashan for free. There will be
negotiations. Even if it means the fall of Teraje, Kashan will not disappear."
"Why not fight?"
Kido found himself feeling pity.
"I'll protect you. No one will touch you, so don't be afraid and
fight."
Uorin shook her head.
"We can't win. Only Shirone can do it. But Shirone won't fight
for Kashan."
'Only Shirone can do it.'
Uorin's words left an ache in his heart.
Tormia Kingdom Magic Association

and massaged his temples.
'The Holy War won't be able to hold them off.' The news was that the
army of hell had suddenly bypassed and was heading towards the central continent.
'They can't break through to Tormia.' As he was thinking this, there
was a knock on the door.
"President, the candidate for the next chief secretary is
here."
Since Jane's death, the chief secretary position had been vacant, but
with the war breaking out, he needed someone to assist him.
"Let them in."
The door opened, and a woman with a stern expression and blue hair
down to her waist entered.
She was Lavide Plu, a certified 6th-level mage.
"Hello. I'm Plu, the head of security on the third
floor."
Lupist had already reviewed all the details about Plu through her
profile.
Her magical skills and judgment were excellent, making her a suitable
candidate for chief secretary.
However, there was one thing he needed to address.
"You've heard that Gaold is active, right?"
"Yes, I have."
She had once fought alongside Gaold in the Gaold Guard against
Heaven.
"Between the Association and Zion, what is your
priority?"
Plu paused to think.
Such an attitude might hinder her rapid promotion, but she was
unbothered.
"I still respect Mr. Gaold."
Lupist's expression remained unchanged.
"But as always, my priority is the well-being of the Tormia
Kingdom. Personal feelings have no place in this."
'She resembles her.'
Lupist thought of Jane, who had passed away.
If possible, he wanted to keep the chief secretary position vacant
while he was president.
'There's no one as capable as you.' In a world heading towards
destruction, her name was even more missed.
"How's your sleep?"
At the unexpected question, Plu blinked and adjusted her
glasses.
"Well, I haven't heard any complaints."
Lupist chuckled.
'Yes, after all, we are all...'

The Grand Star, the incarnation of the stars.

They had heard he reached the level of Yahweh, but it was different

Like a ticking time bomb, his lower abdomen churned.
