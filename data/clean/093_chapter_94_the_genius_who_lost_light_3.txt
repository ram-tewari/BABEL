Chapter 94:
The Genius Who Lost Light (3)
Alphaeus loved that about her. Even with the intelligence of a ten-year-old, her thought
process wasn't childish. Her consideration—thinking he might be upset if he lost—made his eyes misty.
"Honey, I……"
With trembling hands, he held up the gold medal.
"I did it! I won! The Gold Circle Award!"
Elina was always calm and composed, but this time, her radiant eyes couldn't help but
waver. The day Alphaeus had talked about endlessly had finally come.
"Congratulations. I knew you'd win."
"Elina, it's all thanks to you. You made this happen. Come here. Let me put it on you."
Alphaeus believed Elina was the true winner of the Gold Circle. Of course, the blunt
Clump didn't understand. He thought Alphaeus was amazing for deciphering Elina's childlike words. But
Alphaeus knew. The small ideas from Elina's mind would become the core code shaping humanity's future.
"This is your award. Your thoughts amazed the world."
When Alphaeus leaned in for a kiss, Elina pulled away like a shy bride.
"We should eat. Come on."
"Ah, sorry. Clump's throwing a party, so I should go. We'll eat later."
"Oh, okay."
This time, Elina didn't hide her disappointment. Alphaeus grinned mischievously and
suddenly scooped her up.
"Which is why we should save time for eating."
"Eek!"
Alphaeus carried her up the stairs two at a time.
"Wait! The bread will burn!"
"Let it burn! Or I'll burn instead!"
***
After Alphaeus's win, dozens of invitations flooded in daily. All from prominent nobles
or institutions. Trying to please everyone meant pleasing no one. By refusing all but essential connections,
his reputation soared.
As part of this strategy, Alphaeus left home to attend a banquet hosted by the Alchemy
Foundation, an organization handling 40% of magical material distribution—an unmissable event.
But Sarof and his friends, unaware of this, camped outside Alphaeus's house before
sunset. Their anxiety grew as evening fell.
Sarof sighed. "This hurts my pride."
"Is pride the issue? His value has risen too much. Alphaeus wasn't just good at magic. We
should've seen it coming."
Sarof regretted it deeply. When Alphaeus won, he should've swallowed his pride and
mingled in the crowd. Just a month after the award, Alphaeus's fame had skyrocketed, all thanks to his
political savvy. Now, Alphaeus was picky with connections, leaving Sarof with even fewer chances.
'Damn it. Trying to save face only made things worse. Why does this keep happening?'
No amount of thinking would solve it. At least his friends were there to share the
embarrassment.
"Let's go in. I'll do the talking."
Sarof knocked on Alphaeus's door.
"Is Alphaeus home? It's Sarof."
To his surprise, Elina answered.
"Who are you?"
"Ah, the wife. I'm Sarof, Alphaeus's classmate. Is he home?"
"No. He's out. At the Alchemy Foundation."
They knew about the banquet. But since it wasn't a drinking party, it wouldn't last long.
Sarof thought it was for the best.
"I see. Would it be alright to wait inside? With all these people around…"
Waiting outside would only invite gossip.
"Sure, come in."
Sarof and his friends sat in the living room, fidgeting nervously. Feeling sorry for
them, Elina invited them to the table and served snacks and drinks.
"Have a meal. My husband will eat there."
"Thank you. You're too kind."
Embarrassed, Sarof regretted not bringing a gift. He'd bought one but couldn't bring
himself to carry it in.
'Damn it. Trying to save face only made things worse.'
He consoled himself that at least his friends were there.
As he drank, the alcohol hit him fast. At first, he tried to stay composed, but sensing
Elina's intellectual level, he unwittingly relaxed.
"You know, I was once called a genius too! Me, of all people!"
"Who says otherwise? The world's so unfair! Hard workers suffer, while some ride
connections to success! Magic is dead!"
Sarof's eyebrow twitched. His friend was targeting Alphaeus. But he let it slide. Elina
wouldn't understand anyway. Suddenly, the alcohol tasted sweet. For the first time, he felt like he'd won
against Alphaeus. To criticize him so boldly in front of his wife…
'Hah, after all the humiliation he put me through. This is just the beginning.'
Sarof went further, addressing Elina directly.
"Right, sister? Magic isn't just about skill. You agree, don't you?"
"I don't know much."
"Hahaha! Of course not. But it's true. Everything I say is true."
Sarof savored the thrill of victory. He'd criticized Alphaeus and mocked his wife. His
friends joined in, pounding the table.
"Sister, why not sing us a song?"
"Yeah! To celebrate Alphaeus's success! Sing us a song!"
Elina's smile stiffened. People assumed those with low intelligence understood nothing.
But reading emotions was always the weak's role. Elina knew Sarof's group wasn't friendly. Still, as her
husband's friends, she endured it.
"In my hometown, when the wheat ripens…"
As Elina began singing, Sarof's group clapped and whistled. Despite her trembling voice,
she persisted.
But three verses in, a cold voice cut through.
"What's going on?"
Everyone turned. Alphaeus stood there, fists clenched, face red. Sarof sobered instantly.
Realizing what they'd done, he paled.
"S-sorry. We drank too much."
"You bastards!"
Alphaeus stormed forward and punched Sarof, sending him sprawling. His friends jumped up.
"Alphaeus! How could you! Even if you're angry—"
"No, stop. Let it go."
Sarof raised a hand. He was ashamed. Was this all he was? A loser who mocked his rival's
wife for comfort?
"Get out, before I kill you. Now!"
"Sorry about today. I won't come again."
Sarof and his friends were thrown out. Silence filled the living room. Alphaeus's rage
was uncontrollable. He knew why they'd made Elina sing.
"What happened? Why were they here?"
Elina, terrified, couldn't speak. She'd never seen him so angry.
"Talk! Why did you let them in!"
"I'm sorry. I thought… since they're your friends…"
"What are you saying? Since when are they my friends? Can't you tell real friends from
fake ones? And you just sang for them? Are you really that stupid?"
Alphaeus turned away, his face twisted with regret. The words he'd sworn never to use had
slipped out.
'I'm trash. Not even human.'
He wished he could turn back time, but it was too late. Or maybe he'd have said the same
anyway. His anger was unbearable.
"Forget what I just said. I messed up."
"Honey, I'm sorry. I won't do it again."
Elina's apology hurt more. She had nothing to apologize for. But where else could he
direct this fury?
"I need air. I'll be fine later. Sorry. Go to bed."
Alphaeus left. It was the only way to handle this rationally.
He went to Clump's inn and drank all night. He couldn't get drunk. The more he drank, the
more Sarof faded from memory, leaving only Elina's pain.
"Damn it. I'm trash. I don't deserve to be her husband. She trusted only me, and I said
that."
Clump looked sympathetic. As a man, he understood Alphaeus's anger. But the mistake
remained.
"Marital fights are like cutting water with a sword. Your future's bright, and the bad
times are over. This could bring you closer."
When Alphaeus stayed silent, Clump broached a long-considered topic.
"Why not have a child?"
Alphaeus's eyes flickered briefly before dimming.
Clump's eyes widened in realization.
"Wait, you… haven't you two ever…?"
"Nonsense! We're married. Elina may be young, but she knows everything."
"Tch, who's talking about her? Maybe you're the impotent one. Look at you, so scrawny.
Kekeke."
Alphaeus wasn't in the mood to laugh. He stared at his glass bitterly before downing it.
Clump sighed. "So what's the plan? Complaining isn't like you."
Alphaeus swirled his glass, lost in thought. Then, at some point, his eyes gleamed
sharply, resisting the alcohol.
