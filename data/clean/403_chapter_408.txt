Chapter 408:
It was a gloomy day.
Shirone packed his clothes, dry food, and emergency supplies into a
military backpack, preparing for any situation.
He grabbed the fully packed backpack with his right hand and placed it
into the slot of his Cubrick.
Holding Armand, he inspected the scabbard and stored it in the second
slot of the Cubrick.
The total weight was 38.7 kilograms. It was packed optimally, leaving
some spare space.
"......"
Shirone checked the room to see if he had forgotten anything and then
checked the time.
Seeing that it was close to midnight, he took out Maya's letter from the
desk drawer and read it again.
Maya had fallen for Shirone on the day of the Paranormal Psychic Science
Research Society's presentation.
When Shirone stood in front of Maya at the graduation welcome party, it
felt like fate.
The question about who he wanted to kiss was half a joke, half a wishful
thought, hoping he might choose her.
Her feelings were fully expressed in the four-page letter. It was a
sincere confession, devoid of embellishments.
If you accept my feelings, come to the park at midnight in two
days.
Even if you don't come, I will never resent or hate you.
I hope you show your true feelings this time. I love you.
Shirone folded the letter along the crease Maya had made and put it back
in the envelope. He carefully placed it in his inner pocket, pressing it gently with his palm.
"Maya..."
Shirone left the dormitory early, not wanting to keep her waiting on
such a day.
He arrived at the park ten minutes early.
Under the only lit streetlamp, he waited for Maya to arrive.
Dong. Dong. Dong.
The bell tolled midnight.
Dark clouds thickened, and a storm rolled in. Thunder rumbled in the
distance, and cold rain began to fall.
Swoosh!
The sound of countless percussion instruments disoriented Shirone's
hearing.
Water streamed down from his wet hair, and his clothes grew heavier as
they soaked up the rain.
Shirone watched the path Maya would take.
Dong.
The bell tolled 1 AM.
Maya still hadn't arrived.
He heard the sound of someone walking through the puddles behind
him.
Only when the rain stopped falling on his head did Shirone turn
around.
Amy stood there, holding an umbrella.
They just stared at each other.
Amy hoped Shirone would speak first, but he had nothing to say to
her.
Amy's suppressed emotions finally exploded.
Resentment, anger, confusion, betrayal, and perhaps something more
desperate.
Clenching her teeth, she spoke in a trembling voice.
"Why are you here?"
Shirone didn't answer.
"Tell me. Why are you here? Are you really with
Maya..."
"This has nothing to do with you."
Amy's heart sank.
She knew Maya was going to confess.
She had agreed, confident in her understanding of Shirone's feelings.
She regretted it, fearing Maya's courage.
Unable to bear it, she had run to the park.
Shirone was waiting for Maya. And now, he was looking at her with cold
eyes.
"No, it can't be."
Amy's voice trembled.
"Something's wrong, right? You've been acting strange lately.
You're strange now. Tell me! What's going on? If you don't tell me, I won't see you
anymore."
"Fine. I don't care."
Amy couldn't believe it.
Why was he being so cold?
This wasn't the Shirone she knew. She was scared.
For the first time, she felt completely exposed in front of
Shirone.
"Why? Why are you doing this? Why won't you tell me anything? Is it
because I'm not like Maya? You fool, I... I also..."
Shirone hugged Amy tightly. He had no other way to hide his contorted
face.
Hot tears mixed with the cold rain as they streamed down his
cheeks.
"Amy, thank you for everything."
Overwhelmed with sorrow, Amy burst into tears.
She tried to push him away, but her hands slipped.
"No. Don't hug me. Tell me! Why are you doing this? You
jerk!"
She had no strength left.
Time pushed her forward, and a cliff awaited her. For the first time,
she wished she could turn back time.
Shirone gave her a sad smile and spoke his final words.
"Goodbye, Amy..."
Thud.
The umbrella fell from Amy's hand. The sound of the rain grew
louder.
Even as Shirone walked away, she stood there, unable to move, her face
blank.
Tears filled her eyes, blinding her.
"Ugh. Ugh."
As the sobs stuck in her throat burst out, the sky roared, crying for
her.
Thud! Thud!
Graduation class head teacher Collie woke up to the sound of knocking on
the annex door.
Hurriedly putting on a robe and rubbing his sleepy eyes, he opened the
door to find Maya, drenched in rain.
"Maya? What are you doing here at this hour?"
"Teacher..."
Unable to control her emotions, Maya threw herself into Collie's arms
and burst into tears.
"Waaah!"
Collie was taken aback but checked Maya's condition. Calming her
shivering body seemed the first priority.
Collie lit a fire in the fireplace, sat Maya down, and handed her a
towel.
She made cocoa and placed it in front of her, but Maya just stared
blankly at the flames.
'She went.'
Collie realized today was the day Maya had mentioned.
During the graduation schedule, she didn't show it, but she had a lot of
affection for Maya, whom she had personally enrolled and taught at the magic school.
'A delicate child. Always considering others' feelings, neglecting her
own.'
Collie first saw Maya at the opening performance of singer Canary's
outdoor concert.
Commoners had the chance to watch from outside the barricades, and the
nobles seemed to like this arrangement.
So the concert planner decided to have a commoner perform the opening
act to highlight Canary's excellence.
That's how 14-year-old Maya was recruited.
But the planner's scheme backfired. The 14-year-old's popular song was
so beautiful it gave everyone goosebumps.
The nobles were naturally displeased and began booing and criticizing as
soon as the song ended.
Some even demanded the tribe chief bring Maya to their bed for a large
sum of money.
The chief fought back and was beaten and driven away, and Maya gave up
her dream of becoming a singer that day.
Collie suggested Maya become a mage.
Though delicate, she had undeniable talent and succeeded in entering the
spirit zone within two years.
She didn't expect Maya to become a great mage.
If she could become a mage, uplift her tribe, and someday sing the songs
she truly wanted, that would be enough.
"Shirone didn't come out?"
Maya smiled faintly. It was the only happiness she could find in her
pain.
"No. He came out. Foolishly, I told him not to. He could have cut
ties with me, but he came out. He waited for me for over an hour."
Maya covered her face with her hands.
"And it hurts. Because I like him so much. Because I want to be his
woman."
"Why didn't you talk to him? Maybe he came out because he really
likes you."
Maya shook her head.
"I knew from the start I couldn't win Shirone's heart. I confessed
anyway. I liked him so much, I couldn't bear not to say it. So I walked into hell, knowing
it."
Maya looked at Collie with a terrified expression.
"But what do I do? It hurts so much. It hurts so much I feel like
I'm going to die. Can I forget Shirone? Can I go back to who I was before I met him?"
Collie spoke honestly.
"Maya, you can't erase past memories. It's something you'll carry
with you for life."
"I don't think I can. How can I live with this pain..."
Maya finally broke down, sobbing.
Collie approached and hugged her, patting her back.
"There's no need to be afraid. The scars won't disappear, but as
you live, you'll get so many new ones that you won't know which ones Shirone left. And then you'll be
okay. You'll just live."
Maya buried her face in Collie's chest.
"That's not living. How can I live without any hope?"
"Maya, we don't live because of hope. We create hope to live. You
may never forget, but it'll be okay. You'll create new hope."
"I don't want that. I want Shirone to come to me. I want Shirone to
love me."
When cut by a knife, it doesn't hurt immediately. The real pain comes
when you realize you've been cut.
So what Maya felt now was not pain but fear. Fear of the pain to
come.
'The more delicate the heart, the harder it is. But Maya, an artist is
someone who tells human stories. You can't see humans from high above. So remember today's pain. Sing
from the lowest place. Then someday, everyone will soothe your wounds.'
Collie patted Maya's back.
Her shoulders trembled pitifully, like a baby bird with broken wings in
a storm.
3 AM.
Shirone had never intended to sleep, so he returned to his dorm, washed,
and changed into new clothes.
Sitting at his desk, he tried to block out his thoughts, but a sigh
tinged with the remnants of tears escaped.
"Is everything settled now?"
He had left the Paranormal Psychic Science Research Society. Nade had
been very angry but eventually hugged him, telling him to come back no matter what.
And today, he had said his goodbyes to Maya and Amy.
He wanted to sever all ties before going to Heaven.
No, he had to.
If he had any lingering attachments here, he might hesitate at a crucial
moment. He might waver in a situation where he absolutely had to die.
In a short time, Shirone had managed to sever all his ties.
But the faces of his loved ones still floated in his mind.
Rian, Tess, and... his parents, who always wished for their son's
happiness.
He was glad he had told them he loved them before leaving home.
That was truly the best thing Shirone had ever done in his life.
He opened the drawer and took out a clean sheet of paper. The farewells
he hadn't been able to say would now have to be written.
Turning the pen in his fingers, Shirone finally began to write on the
paper.
Will.
"Sob. Sob."
Tears of sorrow streamed down his face.
He wanted to graduate from magic school. He wanted to experience the
world with his friends. He wanted to feel the excitement he had felt when he held Amy's hand once
more.
"Mom..."
To Shirone, his parents were Vincent and Olina. That fact would never
change.
He should have known before deciding to go to Heaven.
No, he shouldn't have met Ikael in the first place.
But he had found out.
He hadn't shown it to anyone, but the answer to the question he had
carried in his heart all his life was with her.
'I'm sorry, I'm so sorry.'
Wiping his tears with his sleeve, Shirone continued writing his
will.
He expressed gratitude to those who had helped him, shared that he was
happy to have received so much love, and conveyed his respect for his parents.
In this way, he was emptying everything he had held inside for 19
years.
